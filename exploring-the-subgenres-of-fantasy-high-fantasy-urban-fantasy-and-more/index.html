Exploring the Subgenres of Fantasy: High Fantasy, Urban Fantasy, and More


Fantasy is a genre of literature that allows us to escape into magical worlds, encounter mythical creatures, and embark on epic adventures. Within the expansive realm of fantasy, there are various subgenres that cater to different readers’ preferences. In this blog post, we will delve into two popular subgenres of fantasy – high fantasy and urban fantasy – and explore what makes them unique and engaging.

High Fantasy

High fantasy, also known as epic fantasy, is a subgenre that takes readers to entirely new worlds, often featuring intricate world-building, larger-than-life heroes, and grand quests. In high fantasy, the setting is typically a secondary world with its own rules, magic systems, and races. This subgenre transports readers into a realm filled with kingdoms, ancient prophecies, and mythical creatures, creating a sense of awe and wonder.

One of the defining features of high fantasy is the presence of a heroic protagonist who embarks on a perilous journey or quest. The hero often possesses special abilities or an important destiny that drives the plot forward. In high fantasy, readers can expect epic battles, the clash between good and evil, and the triumph of courage and virtue.

Examples of high fantasy include J.R.R. Tolkien’s ‘The Lord of the Rings’ series, which transports readers to the enchanting world of Middle-earth, complete with elves, dwarves, and wizards. George R.R. Martin’s ‘A Song of Ice and Fire’ series also belongs to this subgenre, immersing readers in the political intrigue, complex characters, and sprawling kingdoms of Westeros.

Urban Fantasy

In contrast to high fantasy’s grandeur, urban fantasy situates fantastical elements within modern, urban settings. This subgenre often incorporates elements of mystery, suspense, and crime, combining the supernatural with the familiar. Urban fantasy blurs the line between our world and the magical, unveiling hidden realms within everyday environments.

Typically, urban fantasy features a protagonist who navigates both the ordinary and extraordinary aspects of their lives. They might be detectives, witches, or ordinary individuals thrust into a secret supernatural society. This subgenre often explores themes of identity, hidden powers, and the clash between different magical factions.

An excellent example of urban fantasy is Jim Butcher’s ‘Dresden Files’ series, which follows the adventures of Harry Dresden, a wizard and private investigator in modern-day Chicago. Butcher seamlessly blends magic and noir-esque detective narratives, creating a compelling blend of genres. Another notable series is Seanan McGuire’s ‘October Daye’ series, which combines elements of faerie mythology and urban setting to create a captivating world of magic beneath the streets of San Francisco.

Other Subgenres

While high fantasy and urban fantasy are two prominent subgenres, the world of fantasy offers even more diversity. Here are a few other subgenres worth exploring:

  1. Historical Fantasy: This subgenre combines elements of fantasy with historical settings, often reimagining real-world events or introducing magical elements into a specific time period.
  2. Dark Fantasy: Dark fantasy delves into the grim and macabre, often featuring morally ambiguous characters, horror-inspired elements, and a sense of hopelessness. It can be both terrifying and captivating.
  3. Steampunk Fantasy: Steampunk fantasy blends Victorian aesthetics with advanced machinery, steam-powered technology, and a touch of magic. It offers a unique fusion of history, science fiction, and fantasy.

The subgenres of fantasy are as diverse as the imaginations that create them. Each subgenre offers a unique reading experience, catering to different interests and preferences. Whether you prefer the epic scale of high fantasy or the modern intrigue of urban fantasy, there is a fantasy subgenre waiting to transport you to a world of magic and adventure.

In conclusion, exploring the subgenres of fantasy opens up a vast array of storytelling possibilities. High fantasy captivates with its expansive worlds and epic quests, while urban fantasy blends magic with modernity to create a unique reading experience. By familiarizing ourselves with the various subgenres of fantasy, we can dive into the fantastical and embark on journeys limited only by our imagination. So, grab a book and let the magic unfold!
