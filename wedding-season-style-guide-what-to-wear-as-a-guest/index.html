Wedding Season Style Guide: What to Wear as a Guest


Summer is here and that means wedding season is in full swing. As invitations start rolling in, it’s easy to get lost in the excitement of sharing in the joy of the happy couple. But before you RSVP, there’s always that daunting question: what to wear?

Whether you’re attending an outdoor garden wedding or a black-tie affair, choosing the right outfit is crucial. You want to look stylish and put together, but not upstage the bride or groom.

To help ease your stress, we’ve put together a wedding season style guide that covers everything from dress codes to trends, so you can confidently strut your stuff as a guest.

Dress codes

Understanding the dress code is key to showing up to the wedding in appropriate attire. While the invitation may not explicitly state the dress code, clues can be found in the time of day, venue, and style of invitations.

Casual: Beach weddings and daytime park weddings often fall into this category. Women can wear a sundress or skirt and blouse, while men can wear khakis and a button-down shirt.

Cocktail: This dress code is for semi-formal weddings, and usually calls for a knee-length dress or dressy separates for women, and a suit or sport coat and dress pants for men.

Formal: For black-tie or evening weddings, women should wear a long dress or dressy separates, while men should wear a tuxedo or dark suit.

Trends

Now that you know the dress code, it’s time to pick out your outfit. Here are some top trends for wedding season:

Florals: It’s no surprise that florals are a go-to choice for weddings. This year, we’re seeing bold floral prints, mixed prints, and watercolor florals.

Jumpsuits: Jumpsuits can be a chic alternative to a dress, especially for a more casual wedding.

Matching sets: Coordinating sets are another popular trend this year, with many designers adding a touch of whimsy by incorporating prints or bold colors.

Accessories

No outfit is complete without accessories, and weddings are the perfect occasion to have some fun with your accessories.

Shoes: For outdoor weddings, opt for wedges or block heels to avoid sinking into the grass. For indoor weddings, strappy sandals or classic pumps are a great choice. Just make sure you can dance the night away in them!

Bags: A clutch or small crossbody bag is a must-have for a wedding, as it allows you to keep your essentials close while still looking chic.

Jewelry: Keep it simple and elegant with statement earrings or a delicate necklace. Just remember to avoid anything too flashy that may upstage the bride.

Final thoughts

Wedding season can be overwhelming, but with these tips in mind, you’ll be able to confidently choose an outfit that is both comfortable and stylish. And remember, the most important thing is to celebrate the union of the happy couple while looking and feeling your best. Cheers to a great wedding season!
