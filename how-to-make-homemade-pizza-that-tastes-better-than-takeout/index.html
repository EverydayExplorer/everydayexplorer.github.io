How to Make Homemade Pizza That Tastes Better Than Takeout


Do you ever find yourself craving a delicious slice of pizza? We all love the convenience of ordering takeout, but there’s something special about creating your own homemade pizza. Not only is it a fun and rewarding experience, but homemade pizza can also taste even better than what you get from your favorite pizza joint. In this blog post, we’ll share some tried-and-true tips and tricks to help you make a homemade pizza that will have your taste buds begging for more.

1. Start with the Perfect Crust

The foundation of any great pizza is a delicious crust. While you can buy pre-made crusts at the store, nothing beats the taste of a homemade crust. Making your own pizza dough may seem daunting, but it’s actually quite simple. All you need is flour, yeast, water, salt, and a little bit of patience.

Here’s a basic recipe to get you started:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Combine the flour, yeast, and salt in a large bowl. Gradually add the warm water and mix until a dough forms. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Let it rest in a warm place for about an hour or until it doubles in size. Once the dough has risen, punch it down, and shape it into a round or rectangular crust.

2. Sauce it Up

The sauce is what adds depth and flavor to your pizza. While you can use store-bought pizza sauce, making your own allows you to control the ingredients and customize the flavor to your liking.

Here’s a simple homemade pizza sauce recipe:

  • 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Heat the olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook for a minute until fragrant. Add the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer the sauce for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Taste and adjust the seasonings as needed.

Spread a thin layer of sauce onto your prepared pizza crust, leaving a small border around the edges.

3. Cheesy Goodness

No pizza is complete without a generous helping of cheese. While mozzarella is the most common cheese used on pizza, don’t be afraid to experiment with different varieties to suit your taste. From classic cheddar to tangy feta, the cheese options are endless.

Grate or slice your desired cheese and evenly distribute it over the sauce.

4. Load up on Toppings

One of the beauties of homemade pizza is that you can choose your own toppings and load it up with as much or as little as you like. The sky’s the limit when it comes to toppings. Here are some popular options to consider:

  • Pepperoni
  • Sausage
  • Mushrooms
  • Bell peppers
  • Onions
  • Olives
  • Fresh basil
  • Tomatoes
  • Spinach
  • Pineapple (if you dare!)

Remember not to overload your pizza with too many toppings as it can make it heavy and difficult to bake evenly.

5. Bake to Perfection

Now that you have assembled your masterpiece, it’s time to bake it to perfection. Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C) and place your pizza on a baking sheet or pizza stone. Bake for about 12-15 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.

6. Finishing Touches

Once your pizza is out of the oven, give it a few minutes to cool before slicing it into pieces. This will allow the cheese to set and avoid any burns.

You can also elevate the flavor of your homemade pizza by adding some finishing touches. Drizzle a little bit of olive oil over the top, sprinkle some fresh herbs like basil or oregano, or add a dash of red pepper flakes for some heat.

Conclusion

Making homemade pizza that tastes better than takeout may require a bit of time and effort, but the end result is well worth it. From the perfect crust to the flavorful sauce, creative toppings, and gooey cheese, homemade pizza can be a culinary adventure that satisfies your cravings and impresses your family and friends. So next time you’re in the mood for pizza, skip the takeout and give homemade a try. Trust us, you won’t be disappointed!
