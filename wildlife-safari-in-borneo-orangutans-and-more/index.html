Wildlife Safari in Borneo: Orangutans and More


Borneo, the third-largest island in the world, is a tropical paradise that is home to some of the most spectacular wildlife on the planet. One of the main draws for visitors to Borneo is the opportunity to go on a wildlife safari and get up close and personal with fascinating creatures such as orangutans, proboscis monkeys, and pygmy elephants. In this blog post, we will explore the incredible wildlife safari experiences that Borneo has to offer, focusing particularly on the enchanting orangutans.

Borneo is synonymous with orangutans, and rightly so. It is estimated that around 50,000 of these magnificent creatures call the island home, making it an ideal destination for wildlife enthusiasts and conservationists alike.

The first step in planning your wildlife safari in Borneo is to decide which part of the island you want to visit. The two main regions in Borneo for orangutan spotting are the Malaysian state of Sabah and the Indonesian province of Kalimantan. Sabah is often referred to as the ‘Land Below the Wind’ and is renowned for its lush rainforests and abundant wildlife. Kalimantan, on the other hand, offers a more remote and off-the-beaten-path experience.

In Sabah, the Sepilok Orangutan Rehabilitation Centre is a must-visit. Located in the Kabili-Sepilok Forest Reserve, this conservation center is dedicated to rehabilitating orphaned and injured orangutans before releasing them back into the wild. Visitors have the unique opportunity to witness the feeding sessions, where these gentle giants swing down from the trees to enjoy a meal of bananas and milk. It’s a truly unforgettable experience to watch these iconic creatures up close and personal.

If you’re looking for a more adventurous safari experience, head to the Kinabatangan River in Sabah. This river is famed for its diverse wildlife, and a river cruise here offers the chance to spot not only orangutans but also pygmy elephants, proboscis monkeys, crocodiles, and an incredible variety of bird species. Floating along the tranquil waters of the Kinabatangan River as the sun sets, with the sounds of the jungle as your soundtrack, is an experience that will stay with you long after you leave Borneo.

For those seeking a true jungle adventure, a visit to the Danum Valley Conservation Area in Sabah is a must. This pristine rainforest is home to one of the world’s most complex ecosystems and is a haven for wildlife. Trekking through the dense jungle, you may come across agile gibbons, elusive clouded leopards, and perhaps even a glimpse of the rare and endangered Borneo pygmy elephants. Be sure to spend a night or two at the Borneo Rainforest Lodge, where you can immerse yourself in the sights and sounds of the rainforest while enjoying comfortable accommodations.

Heading over to Kalimantan in Indonesia, Tanjung Puting National Park is a wildlife sanctuary that is home to the largest population of orangutans in the world. The park is famous for its unique orangutan rehabilitation program, pioneered by conservationist Dr. Biruté Galdikas. Visitors to Tanjung Puting can take a boat tour along the Sekonyer River, spotting orangutans swinging through the trees, as well as other wildlife such as gibbons, macaques, and crocodiles. Don’t miss the opportunity to visit the famous Camp Leakey, a research and rehabilitation center that has been at the forefront of orangutan conservation for over four decades.

As you embark on your wildlife safari in Borneo, it is essential to remember that responsible tourism is crucial for the conservation of these incredible animals. Supporting ethical wildlife sanctuaries, staying in eco-friendly accommodations, and following the guidance of local guides are all ways in which you can ensure that your visit has a positive impact on the wildlife and the communities of Borneo.

In conclusion, a wildlife safari in Borneo is an experience like no other. From witnessing the charming orangutans in their natural habitat to spotting other iconic species such as proboscis monkeys and pygmy elephants, Borneo offers a treasure trove of wildlife encounters. Whether you choose to explore the rainforests of Sabah or the wilderness of Kalimantan, a trip to Borneo is sure to leave you with memories to last a lifetime. So pack your binoculars, lace up your hiking boots, and get ready for an unforgettable adventure in the wilds of Borneo!
