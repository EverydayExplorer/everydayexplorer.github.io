The Ultimate Guide to Must-Read Books in 2021


Welcome, dear readers, to an exciting journey through the realm of literature in 2021! In this comprehensive blog, we will unravel the mysteries of the most captivating and thought-provoking books that have graced the shelves this year. Get ready to embark on an adventure that will transport you to different worlds, expose you to new ideas, and enrich your imagination.

1. “The Midnight Library” by Matt Haig: This enchanting novel takes us on an extraordinary journey with Nora Seed, a woman who finds herself in a library between life and death. Filled with moments of introspection and hope, this book will make you question the decisions that shape our lives.

2. “Klara and the Sun” by Kazuo Ishiguro: Immerse yourself in this poignant exploration of love and humanity. Follow Klara, an Artificial Friend designed to be a companion to a lonely teenager, as she navigates a world where technology and emotions intertwine.

3. “Project Hail Mary” by Andy Weir: From the author of “The Martian” comes another gripping science fiction adventure. Join Ryland Grace, the sole survivor on a desperate mission to save humanity, as he unravels the mysteries of an alien spacecraft hurtling towards Earth.

4. “Empire of Pain” by Patrick Radden Keefe: Dive into this meticulously researched and compelling exploration of the Sackler family, the owners of Purdue Pharma. Discover how their ruthless pursuit of profit contributed to the opioid crisis, leaving a devastating impact on countless lives.

5. “The Push” by Ashley Audrain: Brace yourself for a chilling psychological thriller that examines the complexities of motherhood. Follow Blythe as she questions her own sanity and struggles to protect her family from a dark and unsettling legacy.

6. “No One Is Talking About This” by Patricia Lockwood: This highly original novel delves into the chaotic world of social media and its impact on our lives. With a unique blend of humor and insight, Lockwood challenges us to question the nature of our digital existence.

7. “The Four Winds” by Kristin Hannah: Set against the backdrop of the Great Depression, this poignant historical fiction explores the indomitable spirit of a woman named Elsa and her journey to protect her family amidst the harsh realities of Dust Bowl America.

8. “Braiding Sweetgrass” by Robin Wall Kimmerer: Immerse yourself in this beautifully written blend of indigenous wisdom and scientific knowledge. Kimmerer invites us to rekindle our relationship with nature and understand the interconnectedness of all living beings.

9. “The Code Breaker” by Walter Isaacson: Venture into the world of genetics and the remarkable story of Jennifer Doudna, the co-developer of CRISPR technology. Isaacson takes us on a captivating journey, exploring the ethical dilemmas and potential of this groundbreaking scientific discovery.

10. “Concrete Rose” by Angie Thomas: Return to the vibrant world of Garden Heights with this powerful prequel to “The Hate U Give.” Thomas explores the life of Maverick Carter, the father of Starr, as he grapples with the challenges of fatherhood, loyalty, and finding his own path.

These ten exceptional books offer a glimpse into the diverse and captivating literary landscape of 2021. So, grab a cozy blanket, a steaming cup of your favorite beverage, and allow these must-read books to transport you to new worlds, expand your horizons, and ignite your imagination. Happy reading!
