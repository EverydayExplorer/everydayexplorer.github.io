Video Games as Art: Examining the Intersection of Creativity


In recent years, the debate surrounding video games as art has gained significant traction. While some may argue that video games are primarily a form of entertainment, many others believe that they possess artistic qualities that rival other traditional mediums such as literature, film, and visual art. This blog aims to dive deep into the question and explore the creative aspects of video games that lend themselves to being recognized as art.

One of the most crucial characteristics of video games as an art form is their ability to evoke emotions and tell compelling stories. Just like in a movie or a novel, video games have the power to transport players into immersive worlds, and engage them in narratives that can be thought-provoking and emotionally resonant. Games like ‘The Last of Us,’ ‘Journey,’ and ‘Life is Strange’ are remarkable examples of storytelling in video games, often eliciting profound emotions and leaving a lasting impact on players.

Moreover, video games offer a unique form of interactive storytelling. Unlike passive mediums like movies or books, video games allow players to actively participate in the narrative, making choices that affect the outcome of the story. This level of interactivity not only enhances the player’s sense of immersion but also gives them agency, allowing them to become an active participant in the creative process. This sort of co-creation between the player and the game developers adds a layer of complexity to the artistry of video games.

Another aspect that supports the argument for video games as art is their stunning visual design. From the breathtaking landscapes in games like ‘Horizon Zero Dawn’ to the intricate character models in ‘Final Fantasy,’ video games have come a long way in terms of visual fidelity. The attention to detail, the carefully crafted environments, and the artistic direction in video games often rival that of traditional art forms. Game developers invest significant time and effort into creating visually captivating worlds that evoke a sense of awe and wonderment.

Furthermore, video games possess a unique audio-visual aspect that contributes to their artistic value. The integration of music, sound effects, and voice acting creates a multisensory experience for players, enhancing the overall immersion and emotional impact. The synergy between these elements enables game designers to set the tone, create tension, and evoke specific emotions, similar to how a film score enhances the narrative of a movie.

Additionally, video games provide a platform for innovative and experimental game design. Independent game developers, in particular, have pushed the boundaries of what is possible in the gaming medium. Indie games like ‘Limbo,’ ‘Braid,’ and ‘Undertale’ have challenged conventional game design norms and explored unique mechanics, storytelling techniques, and art styles. This spirit of experimentation and creativity within the video game industry further cements its status as an art form.

Furthermore, video games have also been recognized by various institutions and museums as an art form. Exhibitions and retrospectives, such as the Museum of Modern Art’s ‘Applied Design’ collection, have featured video games alongside other traditional art forms. This acknowledgement from prestigious institutions gives video games a sense of legitimacy within the art world and further validates their artistic value.

Finally, video games have the potential to spark meaningful conversations and address important social and cultural issues. Games like ‘Papers, Please’ and ‘This War of Mine’ explore themes of morality, ethics, and the human condition, challenging players to confront difficult choices and dilemmas. By using the interactive nature of video games, developers can create experiences that allow players to gain insight into different perspectives and encourage empathy and understanding.

In conclusion, video games possess a wealth of creative attributes that unquestionably align them with traditional art forms. The ability to tell compelling stories, engage players in an interactive narrative, offer stunning visual design, utilize audio-visual elements, encourage innovative game design, and address important societal issues all contribute to their artistic value. As the video game industry continues to evolve and push boundaries, the recognition of video games as a legitimate art form will only continue to grow.
