Game Design Tips: Creating Memorable and Challenging Platforming Levels


Platforming games have been a staple of the gaming industry for decades. From classics like Super Mario Bros. to modern gems like Hollow Knight, platformers continue to captivate players with their unique blend of precise controls, engaging level design, and challenging obstacles. If you’re a game designer looking to create memorable and challenging platforming levels, you’ve come to the right place. In this article, we’ll explore some essential tips and tricks to help you craft levels that players will remember and enjoy.

  1. Understand the Basics of Platforming: Before you dive into level design, it’s crucial to have a deep understanding of the fundamentals of platforming. Play a wide variety of platforming games, both classic and contemporary, to analyze what makes them enjoyable. Pay attention to factors like player movement, jump mechanics, and how levels introduce and build upon challenges.

  2. Set Clear Objectives: Every level should have a clear objective or goal. Whether it’s reaching the end of the level, collecting a specific item, or defeating a boss, players need a purpose. Clearly communicate these objectives to players at the start of the level so they know what they’re working towards.

  3. Create a Learning Curve: Effective platforming level design involves a gradual increase in difficulty. Start with simple challenges to introduce players to new mechanics, and then gradually ramp up the complexity. This learning curve allows players to build their skills naturally, making the game more accessible and enjoyable.

  4. Master the Art of Flow: Flow is the sensation of being fully engaged and immersed in the game. To create flow in platforming levels, ensure that movement feels fluid and responsive. Design levels that encourage players to keep moving forward without interruptions. Avoid overly punishing checkpoints or overly long sections without save points.

  5. Vary Challenges and Obstacles: Repetition can make a game boring, so mix things up. Introduce a variety of obstacles, enemies, and puzzles throughout your levels. Use different combinations of elements to keep players on their toes. Surprise them with unexpected twists and challenges.

  6. Balance Difficulty: Striking the right balance between challenge and frustration is crucial. Levels should be challenging but fair. Avoid resorting to cheap tricks or unfair design. Playtesting is essential to finding the right difficulty level. Watch how players react and adjust accordingly.

  7. Reward Exploration: Encourage players to explore your levels by placing hidden secrets, collectibles, or shortcuts. Rewarding curiosity adds depth to your level design and can make replaying levels more enjoyable. It also keeps players engaged and invested in your game world.

  8. Tell a Story Through Design: Use level design to tell a story or convey a mood. Environmental storytelling can be incredibly effective in platformers. Consider how the visuals, music, and level layout work together to create a cohesive narrative experience.

  9. Iterate and Playtest: The process of designing platforming levels should involve plenty of iteration and playtesting. Playtesters can provide valuable feedback on level balance, difficulty spikes, and overall enjoyment. Don’t be afraid to make changes and refine your levels based on this feedback.

  10. Polish and Attention to Detail: The little details can make a big difference. Pay attention to the aesthetics of your levels, including the graphics, music, and sound effects. Polish can elevate an average level to an exceptional one.

  11. Embrace Innovation: While it’s essential to draw inspiration from classic platformers, don’t be afraid to innovate and try new things. Unique mechanics or level gimmicks can set your game apart and create memorable moments.

  12. Consider Accessibility: Keep in mind that not all players have the same skill level. Consider adding difficulty options or accessibility features to make your game more inclusive. Everyone should have the opportunity to enjoy your platforming masterpiece.

In conclusion, creating memorable and challenging platforming levels is both an art and a science. It requires a deep understanding of game design principles, a passion for the genre, and a willingness to iterate and refine your work. By following these tips and continuously playtesting and refining your levels, you can create platforming experiences that will leave a lasting impression on players and stand the test of time. Good luck, and happy designing!
