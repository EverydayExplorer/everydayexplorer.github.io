The Best Plants for Attracting Butterflies


When it comes to creating a welcoming and beautiful garden, attracting butterflies is a goal for many gardeners. Butterflies not only add a touch of vibrancy with their colorful wings, but they also play a crucial role in pollinating plants. To attract these delightful creatures to your garden, selecting the right plants is key. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best plants for attracting butterflies, helping you create a haven for these enchanting insects.

1. Milkweed (Asclepias): Milkweed is a top choice for attracting butterflies, particularly the iconic Monarch butterfly. Monarchs rely on milkweed plants as the sole host for their caterpillars. By planting milkweed in your garden, you provide a critical food source for Monarchs throughout their lifecycle. Additionally, milkweed flowers produce ample nectar that attracts adult butterflies of various species.

2. Coneflowers (Echinacea): Coneflowers are a classic choice for butterfly gardens. Their vibrant, daisy-like flowers are not only attractive to humans but also irresistible to butterflies. These colorful blooms provide a rich source of nectar, attracting a wide range of butterfly species including Painted Ladies, Swallowtails, and Fritillaries.

3. Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia): With their bright yellow petals and dark centers, Black-Eyed Susans are a popular choice for attracting butterflies. These hardy perennials bloom from summer to fall and provide a generous amount of nectar. They are particularly attractive to species such as the Silver-spotted Skipper and the Great Spangled Fritillary.

4. Butterfly Bush (Buddleia davidii): As the name suggests, the Butterfly Bush is a must-have for any butterfly garden. This shrub produces long clusters of flowers that attract butterflies from afar. With its wide range of colors, including purple, pink, white, and yellow, the Butterfly Bush is a beacon for many species, including the Painted Lady and the Eastern Tiger Swallowtail.

5. Lantana: Lantana is a heat-tolerant plant famous for its vibrant flowers that change color as they mature. These fragrant blooms produce a copious amount of nectar, attracting not only butterflies but also hummingbirds and bees. Lantana is particularly popular among species like the Cloudless Sulphur and the Gulf Fritillary.

6. Zinnias: Zinnias are annual flowers known for their bold and bright colors, making them a popular choice for gardens. These sun-loving plants are a butterfly magnet, attracting species such as the Eastern Tiger Swallowtail, the Painted Lady, and the Red Admiral. Planting a variety of zinnias in different colors and sizes will create a visually stunning and butterfly-friendly garden.

7. Verbena: Verbena is a versatile flowering plant that adds a pop of color to any garden. Its delicate clusters of small flowers are magnets for butterflies, attracting species like the American Lady, the Painted Lady, and the Buckeye. Verbena is also known for its long blooming season, ensuring a continuous food source for butterflies.

8. Marigolds (Tagetes): Marigolds are not only valued for their ability to repel pests, but they are also excellent for attracting butterflies. These hardy annuals produce vibrant blooms that come in various shades of orange and yellow, which are especially attractive to butterflies like the Cabbage White and the Clouded Sulphur.

9. Joe-Pye Weed (Eutrochium): Joe-Pye Weed is a tall, native perennial that not only adds vertical interest to your garden but also attracts butterflies with its large clusters of pink flowers. This plant is particularly favored by species like the Eastern Tiger Swallowtail and the Red Admiral. The abundant nectar and ample landing pads provided by Joe-Pye Weed make it a perfect choice for butterfly gardens.

10. Asters: Asters are a group of daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and fall, offering a late-season nectar source for butterflies. Their star-shaped blossoms come in a range of colors, from white and pink to purple and blue. Asters are highly attractive to species like the Monarch, the Painted Lady, and the Red Admiral.

To create a truly butterfly-friendly garden, incorporate a variety of plants that bloom at different times throughout the season. This ensures a continuous source of nectar and encourages butterflies to visit your garden regularly. Providing host plants for caterpillars, such as milkweed, is also essential for supporting the complete lifecycle of butterflies.

In conclusion, attracting butterflies to your garden can be a rewarding experience. By carefully selecting plants that provide a generous amount of nectar and serve as host plants for caterpillars, you can create a haven for these charming insects. From milkweed and coneflowers to butterfly bush and asters, the plants mentioned in this blog post are among the best choices for attracting butterflies to your garden. So get your gardening gloves on, get planting, and prepare to welcome an array of fluttering visitors to your outdoor oasis.
