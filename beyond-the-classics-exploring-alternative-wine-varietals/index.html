Beyond the Classics: Exploring Alternative Wine Varietals


When it comes to wine, many people often stick to the tried and true classics like Chardonnay, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Merlot. While these traditional varietals are undoubtedly delicious, they represent only a fraction of the vast world of wine. There is a whole universe of alternative wine varietals waiting to be uncovered and explored. In this blog, we will take a journey beyond the classics and delve into the exciting and diverse world of alternative wine varietals.

What are Alternative Wine Varietals?

Alternative wine varietals, also known as uncommon or lesser-known varietals, are grape varieties that are not as widely recognized or cultivated as the classics. They may have unique flavor profiles, lesser-known regions of origin, or simply have been overshadowed by more popular grapes.

The Benefits of Exploring Alternative Wine Varietals

Exploring alternative wine varietals offers numerous benefits for both wine enthusiasts and curious newcomers to the world of wine.

  1. Discovery of New Flavors: Alternative wine varietals often offer distinct and unique flavor profiles that can surprise and delight the palate. From the tropical fruit notes of Viognier to the earthy undertones of Aglianico, these varietals can expand your wine tasting horizons.

  2. Unique Expressions of Terroir: Alternative wine varietals often thrive in specific regions better than the classics. By exploring these varietals, you can gain a deeper understanding of the relationship between grape variety and terroir, as well as discover hidden gems from lesser-known wine regions.

  3. Supporting Small Producers: Many alternative wine varietals are grown by small, independent winemakers who are passionate about preserving and championing these underrated grapes. By exploring and purchasing wines made from alternative varietals, you can support these producers and help keep their ancient traditions alive.

  4. Conversation Starters: Breaking away from the classics and presenting guests with alternative wine varietals can spark intriguing conversations and create memorable experiences. These wines can be an excellent icebreaker at social events and a way to showcase your knowledge and adventurous taste.

Notable Alternative Wine Varietals to Explore

Now that we understand the benefits of exploring alternative wine varietals, let’s dive into some notable examples.

Chenin Blanc

Originating from the Loire Valley in France, Chenin Blanc is a versatile white grape that can produce a wide range of wine styles, from bone-dry to sweet. Its acidity, aromatics, and ability to age make it a favorite among wine enthusiasts.

Carmenere

Originally mistaken for Merlot in Chile, Carmenere is now recognized as a unique varietal. It offers flavors of dark fruit, spice, and herbal notes, making it an excellent alternative to Cabernet Sauvignon and other red varietals.

Assyrtiko

Hailing from the Greek island of Santorini, Assyrtiko is a white grape that produces crisp, mineral-driven wines with citrus and saline notes. It is an excellent choice for seafood pairings and a refreshing alternative to Sauvignon Blanc.

Malbec

While Malbec is gaining popularity in recent years, it is still considered an alternative varietal compared to the classics. Originally from Bordeaux, Malbec found its true home in Argentina, where it produces rich, full-bodied wines with dark fruit flavors and velvety tannins.

Tannat

Originating from Southwest France, Tannat is a robust red varietal known for its dark color, high tannins, and full-bodied structure. It has found success in Uruguay, producing wines with a bold, rustic character and aging potential.

Tips for Exploring Alternative Wine Varietals

  1. Research: Take the time to learn about the characteristics, origins, and suggested food pairings for each alternative varietal you want to explore. This will enhance your understanding and appreciation of these wines.

  2. Attend Tastings: Seek out wine tastings or events focused on alternative varietals. This can be an excellent opportunity to sample multiple wines and engage in discussions with experts and fellow wine enthusiasts.

  3. Stay Open-minded: Embrace the adventure of trying new wines and be open to unconventional flavors and aromas. Remember that alternative varietals can offer unique and unexpected experiences.

  4. Experiment with Food Pairings: As with the classics, alternative wine varietals can be wonderfully complemented by various dishes. Be adventurous and pair them with different cuisines to discover exciting flavor combinations.

  5. Keep a Wine Journal: Document your tasting experiences, including the varietal, region, producer, and your personal impressions. This can be a helpful reference for future wine purchases or for sharing recommendations with friends.

Conclusion

Beyond the classics lies a world of alternative wine varietals waiting to be explored. By stepping out of your comfort zone and embracing these lesser-known grapes, you can discover new flavors, support small producers, and engage in conversations that go beyond the usual. So why not embark on a tasting journey and broaden your wine horizons? Cheers to the endless possibilities of alternative wine varietals!
