Developing a Personal Board of Advisors: Seeking Guidance


In today’s fast-paced and highly competitive world, seeking guidance and support is crucial for personal and professional growth. We often ask ourselves, ‘How do I make better decisions? How do I navigate challenges? How do I stay motivated and focused?’ These are common dilemmas that many of us face on a daily basis. Fortunately, there is a valuable resource that can help us find answers to these questions - a personal board of advisors.

A personal board of advisors is a group of individuals who provide guidance, support, and insight based on their expertise and experience. They can be trusted family members, mentors, colleagues, or even industry experts. Developing such a board can be immensely beneficial, as it allows you to tap into a collective wisdom and gain diverse perspectives that can help you make informed decisions and navigate through various challenges.

So, how do you go about building your personal board of advisors? Here are some key steps to get you started:

1. Define your goals and needs: Before seeking out advisors, it is essential to clarify your personal and professional goals. What specific areas do you need guidance in? Are you looking to make a career transition or start a new business venture? Identifying your needs will help you find advisors who have the expertise and insights relevant to your goals.

2. Identify potential advisors: Once you have a clear sense of your needs, start looking for potential advisors who can fulfill those needs. Consider individuals who have achieved success in areas that align with your goals. Look for people who possess qualities you admire and who can offer the guidance and support you require.

3. Research and evaluate: Once you have identified potential advisors, conduct thorough research on each individual. Look into their backgrounds, achievements, and track records. Consider their experience, expertise, and values. It is important to evaluate whether their knowledge and perspectives align with your goals and values.

4. Approach advisors strategically: When reaching out to potential advisors, be specific about why you are seeking their guidance. Show them that you have done your research and are genuinely interested in their insights. Be clear about your goals and ask if they would be willing to be a part of your personal board of advisors.

5. Build strong relationships: Developing a personal board of advisors is not just about seeking advice; it is about building strong relationships based on trust and mutual respect. Invest time in getting to know your advisors on a personal level. Attend networking events, industry conferences, or join relevant online communities to foster connections and deepen relationships.

6. Maintain regular communication: Regular communication is key to nurturing the relationships with your advisors. Schedule periodic meetings or phone calls to discuss your progress, seek advice, and address any challenges you may be facing. Additionally, keep your advisors updated on your achievements and milestones. This will not only show your commitment but also help them feel invested in your success.

7. Be open to feedback and take action: One of the most important aspects of having a personal board of advisors is being open to feedback. Encourage your advisors to share their honest opinions and suggestions, even if they may challenge your existing ideas or beliefs. Actively consider their feedback and take appropriate action. Remember, the purpose of seeking guidance is to grow and improve.

8. Pay it forward: As you progress in your personal and professional journey, remember to pay it forward. Share your knowledge and experiences with others who may benefit from your guidance. Becoming an advisor yourself can be a rewarding experience and will help you solidify your own understanding and wisdom.

In conclusion, building a personal board of advisors can be a powerful strategy for seeking guidance and support. By leveraging the expertise and varied perspectives of trusted individuals, you can make better decisions, overcome challenges, and achieve your personal and professional goals. Remember to be intentional, proactive, and committed to nurturing these relationships in order to reap the full benefits of having a personal board of advisors.
