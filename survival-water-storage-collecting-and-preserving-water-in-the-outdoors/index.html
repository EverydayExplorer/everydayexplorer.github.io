Survival Water Storage: Collecting and Preserving Water in the Outdoors


Water is one of the most important elements for survival, especially in outdoor situations where access to clean water may be limited. Whether you are camping, hiking, or in a survival situation, having a reliable water storage plan is crucial. In this article, we will discuss the best practices for collecting and preserving water in the outdoors to ensure you stay hydrated and healthy.

  1. Collecting Water

When you find yourself in the outdoors without access to clean water, it is essential to know how to collect water from natural sources. Here are some methods you can use to collect water:

  • Rainwater: Collecting rainwater is one of the easiest ways to obtain clean water in the outdoors. Simply set up a tarp or improvised shelter to catch rainwater. Make sure to filter and purify the collected water before drinking.

  • Natural Springs and Streams: If you come across a natural spring or stream, you can collect water directly from these sources. However, it is important to filter and purify the water to remove any contaminants.

  • Plants: In a survival situation, you can also collect water from certain plants such as cacti or bamboo. Be sure to research which plants are safe to collect water from.

  • Digging a Solar Still: A solar still is a simple way to collect water using the sun’s heat. You can dig a hole in the ground, place a container in the center, cover the hole with a sheet of plastic, and weigh down the edges. The sun will evaporate the water from the ground, which will then condense on the plastic and drip into the container.

  • Storing Water

Once you have collected water, it is important to store it properly to keep it clean and safe to drink. Here are some tips for storing water in the outdoors:

  • Containers: Use clean containers to store water. Avoid using containers that previously held toxic substances, as they can contaminate the water. Look for containers made of food-grade plastic or stainless steel.

  • Treatment: To prevent the growth of bacteria and other contaminants, treat the stored water with purification tablets or a water filter. Boiling the water is also an effective way to kill harmful bacteria.

  • Shelter: Store your water containers in a cool, dark place to prevent the growth of algae and bacteria. Keep the containers off the ground to avoid contamination.

  • Rotation: Regularly rotate your water supply to ensure freshness. If stored properly, water can last for months or even longer.

  • Purifying Water

In addition to collecting and storing water, it is crucial to purify any water you collect from natural sources. Here are some methods for purifying water in the outdoors:

  • Boiling: Boiling water is the most effective way to kill harmful bacteria and parasites. Bring the water to a rolling boil for at least one minute, or three minutes at higher altitudes.

  • Water Filters: Water filters are portable devices that can remove bacteria, parasites, and other contaminants from water. Look for a water filter that is designed for outdoor use.

  • Chemical Treatment: Water purification tablets or drops can be used to kill bacteria and viruses in water. Follow the instructions on the packaging for the proper dosage.

  • UV Purification: UV purification pens or devices use ultraviolet light to kill microorganisms in water. This method is quick and effective but requires a power source.

In conclusion, collecting and preserving water in the outdoors is essential for survival. By knowing how to collect water from natural sources, store it properly, and purify it, you can ensure you have a safe and reliable water supply in any outdoor situation. Stay hydrated, stay safe, and enjoy your outdoor adventures!
