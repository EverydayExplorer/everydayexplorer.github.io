5 Easy Homemade Ice Cream Recipes


Who doesn’t love a scoop of creamy, indulgent ice cream on a hot summer day? Making your own ice cream at home can be not only fun but also rewarding, allowing you to customize flavors to your liking and avoid the artificial additives often found in store-bought varieties. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or a novice in the kitchen, these five easy homemade ice cream recipes are sure to delight your taste buds and impress your friends and family.

1. Classic Vanilla Bean Ice Cream

Nothing beats the simplicity and timeless appeal of a scoop of classic vanilla bean ice cream. To make your own, you’ll need:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 vanilla bean, split and seeds scraped (or 1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract)

In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a simmer. Remove from heat and add the vanilla bean seeds (or vanilla extract). Let the mixture cool to room temperature, then transfer it to an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Once churned, transfer the ice cream to a freezer-safe container and freeze until firm.

2. Decadent Chocolate Ice Cream

For all the chocolate lovers out there, this rich and indulgent chocolate ice cream is sure to satisfy your cravings. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 6 ounces semisweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a simmer. Remove from heat and add the chopped chocolate and vanilla extract, stirring until the chocolate is completely melted and the mixture is smooth. Let the mixture cool to room temperature, then transfer it to an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Once churned, transfer the ice cream to a freezer-safe container and freeze until firm.

3. Refreshing Mint Chocolate Chip Ice Cream

Cool and refreshing, mint chocolate chip ice cream is a classic favorite for a reason. Here’s how to make it at home:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 teaspoon pure peppermint extract
  • Green food coloring (optional)
  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips

In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a simmer. Remove from heat and stir in the peppermint extract and green food coloring (if using). Let the mixture cool to room temperature, then transfer it to an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. During the last few minutes of churning, add the chocolate chips. Once churned, transfer the ice cream to a freezer-safe container and freeze until firm.

4. Creamy Strawberry Ice Cream

Capture the taste of summer with this creamy strawberry ice cream recipe:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and chopped

In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a simmer. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Let the mixture cool to room temperature, then transfer it to a blender along with the chopped strawberries. Blend until smooth. Transfer the mixture to an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Once churned, transfer the ice cream to a freezer-safe container and freeze until firm.

5. Velvety Coconut Mango Ice Cream

Transport yourself to a tropical paradise with this exotic coconut mango ice cream:

  • 1 can (13.5 ounces) coconut milk
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced

In a medium saucepan, combine the coconut milk, whole milk, sugar, and salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a simmer. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Let the mixture cool to room temperature, then transfer it to a blender along with the diced mangoes. Blend until smooth. Transfer the mixture to an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Once churned, transfer the ice cream to a freezer-safe container and freeze until firm.

Conclusion

With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of time, you can whip up these delicious homemade ice cream recipes to enjoy all summer long. Whether you prefer classic flavors like vanilla and chocolate or more adventurous combinations like mint chocolate chip and coconut mango, there’s something for everyone to love. So why wait? Grab your ice cream maker and get churning!
