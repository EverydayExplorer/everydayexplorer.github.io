Turkey Hunting Tips: Mastering the Art of Calling


Turkey hunting is a thrilling outdoor pursuit that requires skill, patience, and a deep understanding of the wild turkey. One of the most critical aspects of turkey hunting is mastering the art of calling. Effective calling can make all the difference in your success as a turkey hunter, as it helps you lure the birds into range and ultimately leads to a successful harvest. In this blog post, we will explore some essential turkey hunting tips that will help you become a master caller and enhance your chances of bagging that elusive gobbler.

  1. Learn the Language of Turkeys

To become an effective caller, it is essential to learn the language of wild turkeys. Turkeys communicate with a variety of vocalizations, each carrying a different meaning. The most common calls hunters use include yelps, clucks, purrs, cuts, and gobbles. Spend time listening to real turkeys in the wild or watch videos that feature turkeys vocalizing. This will help you familiarize yourself with the distinct sounds and cadences of turkey calls, enabling you to replicate them accurately in the field.

  1. Invest in High-Quality Calls

Investing in high-quality turkey calls is a crucial step towards mastering the art of calling. The market offers a wide range of calls, including mouth calls, box calls, slate calls, and push-pull calls. Experiment with different types of calls and choose the ones that you feel most comfortable and confident using. Keep in mind that different calls produce different sounds, so having a variety of calls in your arsenal will increase your chances of mimicking various turkey vocalizations.

  1. Practice Different Calls

Once you have selected your preferred turkey calls, it is crucial to practice extensively. Turkey calling is a skill that requires practice and repetition to perfect. Start by mastering the basic yelp, which is the most common turkey vocalization and is used in a variety of situations. As you progress, practice other calls like clucks, purrs, and cuts to create a more comprehensive repertoire. Set aside time each day to practice your calls, mimicking the sounds of a turkey as accurately as possible.

  1. Understand Different Scenarios

Different turkey hunting scenarios call for different types of calls. Understanding these scenarios and when to use specific calls is crucial for success. For example, soft yelps are effective for locating distant turkeys and getting a response. Once you’ve located a responsive bird, transition to excited yelps and cuts to make the turkey believe there is a hot hen nearby. Clucks and purrs are more subtle and can be used when the turkeys are close to your location, adding realism to your setup.

  1. Be Patient and Realistic

Turkey hunting requires patience, and calling is no exception. When calling, it’s important to be patient and realistic, as turkeys have keen senses and can quickly detect any inconsistencies in your calls. Avoid overcalling or sounding too aggressive, as this can make the turkeys wary and hesitant to come closer. Remember that turkeys are creatures of habit, so if your initial calling does not receive a response, give it some time before making another calling sequence or adjusting your strategy.

  1. Use Natural Cover and Concealment

While mastering the art of turkey calling is crucial, it’s equally important to use natural cover and concealment to your advantage. Turkeys have excellent eyesight and can quickly detect any movement or odd shapes. Stay hidden by wearing camouflage clothing that blends well with the surroundings and setting up against a dense tree or bushes. This will allow you to call without being easily noticed by the turkeys, increasing your chances of a successful hunt.

  1. Hunt at the Right Time

Timing is everything in turkey hunting. Learning the peak breeding seasons and times in your hunting area will significantly enhance your chances of a successful hunt. During the breeding season, turkeys are more responsive to calls as they search for mates. Early mornings and late afternoons are typically the most active times for turkeys, so plan your hunts accordingly.

By following these turkey hunting tips and mastering the art of calling, you’ll greatly increase your chances of a successful hunt. Remember, practice makes perfect, so dedicate yourself to improving your calling skills and constantly honing your craft. Turkey hunting is a thrilling and rewarding pursuit, and becoming a skilled caller will take you one step closer to becoming a master turkey hunter. Happy hunting!
