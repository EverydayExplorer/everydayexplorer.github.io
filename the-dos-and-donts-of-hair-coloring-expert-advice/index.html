The Dos and Don'ts of Hair Coloring: Expert Advice


Are you considering changing up your hair color? Whether you want to cover up grays, add some dimension, or completely transform your look, hair coloring can be a fun and exciting way to express yourself. However, before you dive headfirst into a box of dye or book an appointment at the salon, there are a few important dos and don’ts that you should keep in mind. To help you achieve the best results and avoid any hair color mishaps, we’ve gathered expert advice on the dos and don’ts of hair coloring. So, let’s get started!

Dos:

1. Do Consider Your Skin Tone and Eye Color

When choosing a hair color, it’s crucial to consider your skin tone and eye color. Certain shades can complement your features and make them pop, while others may clash and create an unflattering look. If you have warm undertones, opt for hair colors like golden blonde, warm brown, or copper red. On the other hand, if you have cool undertones, shades like platinum blonde, ash brown, or burgundy can be more flattering. Similarly, your eye color can also play a role in determining the best hair color for you. For example, blue eyes can be enhanced by warm tones like caramel or honey blonde.

2. Do a Patch Test

Before applying hair color all over your head, it’s essential to do a patch test to check for any allergic reactions. Allergic reactions to hair dye can range from mild irritation to severe rashes, so it’s better to be safe than sorry. Apply a small amount of dye behind your ear or on your inner elbow, following the instructions on the packaging. Leave it on for the recommended time and observe any adverse reactions. If everything looks good, you’re good to go!

3. Do Deep Condition Your Hair Regularly

Hair coloring, especially if you opt for a lighter shade, can cause damage and dryness to your locks. To keep your hair healthy and moisturized, make deep conditioning a regular part of your hair care routine. Invest in a good quality deep conditioner or hair mask and use it at least once a week. This will help replenish moisture, repair any damage, and keep your colored hair looking vibrant and shiny.

4. Do Use Color-Safe Products

To maintain the longevity of your hair color, it’s important to use products specifically formulated for color-treated hair. Regular shampoos and conditioners can strip away the color and cause it to fade more quickly. Look for products that are labeled “color-safe” or “for color-treated hair.” These products are designed to be gentle on your hair and help preserve the vibrancy of your color.

5. Do Consult a Professional

While DIY hair coloring can be tempting, it’s always a good idea to consult a professional hairstylist, especially if you’re looking for a drastic change or have never colored your hair before. A hairstylist can assess your hair type, recommend the best color options for you, and ensure that the coloring process is done correctly to achieve the desired results. They have the expertise and knowledge to create a customized hair color that suits you perfectly.

Don’ts:

1. Don’t Overdo It with Bleaching

Bleaching can be a powerful tool for achieving lighter hair colors, but it should be used with caution. Over-bleaching can severely damage your hair, leaving it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. It’s important to follow the instructions on the bleach carefully and avoid leaving it on for longer than recommended. If you’re unsure about bleaching your hair at home, it’s best to leave it to the professionals.

2. Don’t Skip the Strand Test

Before applying hair color all over your head, it’s crucial to perform a strand test. This involves applying a small amount of dye to a hidden section of your hair and observing the results. The strand test will give you an idea of how the color will turn out and whether it’s the shade you desire. It’s better to discover any issues or unexpected results on a small section of hair rather than your entire head.

3. Don’t Wash Your Hair Immediately After Coloring

After coloring your hair, it’s important to give the color time to set and penetrate the hair shaft. Avoid washing your hair for at least 24 to 48 hours after coloring. This will help the color last longer and prevent it from fading prematurely. If you’re concerned about the smell of the hair dye, you can use a dry shampoo to freshen up your hair without washing it.

4. Don’t Forget to Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Just like your skin, your hair can also be damaged by the sun’s harmful UV rays. Exposure to sunlight can cause your hair color to fade and become dull. To protect your hair, wear a hat or use products that contain UV filters when you’re spending time outdoors. This will help preserve the vibrancy of your hair color and keep it looking fresh.

5. Don’t Be Afraid to Experiment

While there are some dos and don’ts when it comes to hair coloring, don’t be afraid to step out of your comfort zone and experiment with different shades and techniques. Hair color can be a fun and creative way to express your personality and style. If you’re unsure about a certain color, you can always start with temporary or semi-permanent dyes to test the waters. Remember, hair color is not permanent, and you can always change it if you’re not satisfied.

Final Thoughts

Hair coloring can be a transformative experience, but it’s important to approach it with caution and follow expert advice. By considering your skin tone and eye color, doing patch tests, deep conditioning regularly, using color-safe products, and consulting a professional when necessary, you can achieve beautiful and healthy-looking colored hair. On the other hand, avoiding over-bleaching, performing strand tests, waiting to wash your hair after coloring, protecting your hair from the sun, and embracing experimentation can help you avoid common hair color pitfalls. So go ahead, have fun, and enjoy your hair coloring journey!
