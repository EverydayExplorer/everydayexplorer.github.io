Top 10 Must-Read Books of the Year


As we approach the end of another year, it’s time to reflect on some of the most captivating and thought-provoking books that have graced our shelves in the past twelve months. From gripping novels to insightful non-fiction works, 2023 has been a remarkable year for literature. In this blog post, we’ll be exploring the top 10 must-read books of the year, offering a diverse selection that is sure to appeal to every type of reader.

1. ‘The Midnight Library’ by Matt Haig - This poignant and imaginative novel tells the story of Nora Seed, who finds herself in a mysterious library where each book represents a different life she could have lived. A powerful exploration of regret, choice, and the search for happiness.

2. ‘Klara and the Sun’ by Kazuo Ishiguro - In this thought-provoking dystopian novel, Ishiguro explores themes of artificial intelligence, love, and what it means to be human through the eyes of Klara, an AI girl who longs to understand the world around her.

3. ‘Project Hail Mary’ by Andy Weir - From the author of ‘The Martian,’ this thrilling sci-fi adventure follows astronaut Ryland Grace as he embarks on a mission to save humanity from extinction. With its blend of humor, science, and heart, ‘Project Hail Mary’ is a must-read for fans of hard sci-fi.

4. ‘The Push’ by Ashley Audrain - A chilling psychological thriller that delves into the complexities of motherhood and the bond between parent and child. Tautly written and emotionally resonant, ‘The Push’ is a gripping read that will stay with you long after you turn the final page.

5. ‘Crying in H Mart’ by Michelle Zauner - In this moving memoir, musician Michelle Zauner reflects on her Korean heritage, grief, and the healing power of food. With its lyrical prose and heartfelt storytelling, ‘Crying in H Mart’ is a touching tribute to family and culture.

6. ‘Matrix’ by Lauren Groff - Set in medieval France, ‘Matrix’ follows the life of Marie de France as she navigates power, politics, and her own desires within the confines of an abbey. A lush and immersive historical novel that reimagines the legend of the founding of an unconventional nunnery.

7. ‘The Prophets’ by Robert Jones Jr. - This stunning debut novel explores love, faith, and resistance on a Southern plantation in the antebellum era. Through the forbidden relationship between two enslaved men, Jones crafts a narrative that is both brutal and tender, shedding light on the untold stories of Black queer existence.

8. ‘Beautiful World, Where Are You’ by Sally Rooney - The latest novel from acclaimed author Sally Rooney follows the interconnected lives of four young people as they grapple with love, friendship, and the uncertainties of the modern world. With Rooney’s signature wit and incisive observations, ‘Beautiful World, Where Are You’ is a compelling exploration of millennial angst and desire.

9. ‘Assembly’ by Natasha Brown - Written in spare, razor-sharp prose, ‘Assembly’ offers a searing commentary on race, class, and identity in contemporary Britain. Through the eyes of an unnamed protagonist, Brown exposes the hidden tensions and microaggressions that shape our social interactions and sense of self.

10. ‘The Love Hypothesis’ by Ali Hazelwood - A charming and witty romantic comedy that follows PhD student Olive Smith as she enlists the help of a brooding professor to fake a relationship. Full of banter, chemistry, and heart, ‘The Love Hypothesis’ is a delightful read that will leave you smiling.

These are just a few of the standout books that have captured our attention and hearts in 2023. Whether you’re looking for a gripping thriller, a thought-provoking work of literary fiction, or a heartwarming romance, there’s something for everyone on this list. So grab a cozy blanket, brew a cup of tea, and lose yourself in the pages of these must-read books of the year. Happy reading!
