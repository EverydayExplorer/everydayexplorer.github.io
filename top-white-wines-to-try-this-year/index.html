Top White Wines to Try This Year


Are you ready to embark on a journey through the lush vineyards and sun-kissed hillsides of the world’s finest white wines? Whether you’re a seasoned sommelier or an enthusiastic novice, 2024 promises to be a year of delightful discoveries and tantalizing tastes in the world of white wine. From crisp Chardonnays to aromatic Rieslings, there’s a varietal to suit every palate and occasion. So, without further ado, let’s uncork the top white wines to try this year.

  1. Chardonnay: The Classic Crowd-Pleaser

  2. No list of top white wines would be complete without mentioning Chardonnay. This versatile varietal, hailing from the vineyards of Burgundy, France, continues to captivate wine enthusiasts worldwide. With its rich, buttery texture and notes of ripe fruit and oak, Chardonnay is the perfect companion for everything from seafood to poultry dishes.

  3. Sauvignon Blanc: Vibrant and Refreshing

  4. Sauvignon Blanc, known for its zesty acidity and vibrant citrus flavors, is a perennial favorite among white wine aficionados. Originating from the Loire Valley in France and now produced in wine regions across the globe, including New Zealand and California, this crisp and refreshing wine pairs beautifully with salads, seafood, and light pasta dishes.

  5. Riesling: A Symphony of Aromas

  6. Riesling, with its tantalizing aromas of peach, apricot, and honey, offers a sensory experience like no other. This aromatic white wine, produced predominantly in Germany but also thriving in regions such as Alsace, France, and the Finger Lakes in New York, strikes the perfect balance between sweetness and acidity. Whether enjoyed on its own or paired with spicy cuisine, Riesling is sure to leave a lasting impression.

  7. Pinot Grigio: Crisp and Approachable

  8. Pinot Grigio, beloved for its light body and crisp, clean finish, is a go-to choice for casual gatherings and sunny afternoons. Originating from the vineyards of Italy’s northern regions, particularly Friuli-Venezia Giulia and Veneto, Pinot Grigio delights the senses with its subtle notes of green apple, pear, and citrus. Pair it with light appetizers, salads, or seafood for a truly delightful experience.

  9. Gewürztraminer: Exotic and Spicy

  10. For those seeking something a little more exotic, Gewürztraminer is the perfect choice. Originating from the Alsace region of France and also thriving in Germany and the Pacific Northwest of the United States, Gewürztraminer is renowned for its heady aromas of lychee, rose petal, and spice. This full-bodied white wine pairs beautifully with bold, flavorful dishes such as Thai curry or aromatic cheeses.

  11. Viognier: Lush and Fragrant

  12. Viognier, with its lush texture and intoxicating floral aromas, is a white wine lover’s dream. Originating from the Rhône Valley in France and now gaining popularity in regions such as California and Australia, Viognier offers a sensory journey with every sip. Notes of ripe peach, apricot, and honeysuckle dance on the palate, making it the perfect accompaniment to rich, creamy dishes or aromatic Asian cuisine.

  13. Chenin Blanc: Versatile and Complex

  14. Last but certainly not least, Chenin Blanc deserves a spot on any list of top white wines to try. This versatile varietal, originating from the Loire Valley in France but also thriving in South Africa and California, offers a delightful range of flavors and styles. From crisp and dry to lusciously sweet, Chenin Blanc can be enjoyed in a variety of incarnations, making it the perfect choice for any occasion.

In conclusion, the world of white wine is as diverse and captivating as ever, with an array of varietals and styles to suit every taste and preference. Whether you’re a fan of the classics like Chardonnay and Sauvignon Blanc or eager to explore more exotic options like Gewürztraminer and Viognier, 2024 promises to be a year of exciting discoveries and memorable moments in the world of white wine. So, raise a glass and toast to a year filled with delicious adventures and new favorites waiting to be savored. Cheers!
