Attracting Hummingbirds: Flowers that Delight


Hummingbirds are fascinating creatures that captivate our attention with their stunning colors and incredible flying abilities. If you’re a bird enthusiast or simply want to bring more life and beauty to your garden, attracting hummingbirds is a wonderful way to do so. One of the most effective ways to attract these delightful birds is by planting flowers that they find irresistible. In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the top flowers that are sure to make your garden a hummingbird haven.

1. Red Columbine (Aquilegia canadensis): Hummingbirds are naturally drawn to red flowers, and the Red Columbine is no exception. This perennial plant features unique, bell-shaped flowers that provide nectar for hummingbirds. It’s a low-maintenance plant that thrives in partial shade, making it perfect for gardens with different light conditions.

2. Bee Balm (Monarda didyma): With its vibrant red, pink, or purple flowers, Bee Balm is a favorite among hummingbirds. This plant blooms throughout the summer, attracting not only hummingbirds but also butterflies and bees. Bee Balm prefers full sun and well-draining soil, making it an excellent choice for sunny areas in your garden.

3. Cardinal Flower (Lobelia cardinalis): Known for its striking scarlet-red flowers, the Cardinal Flower is a magnet for hummingbirds. This perennial plant thrives in moist soil and partial shade. Its tall, upright stems create a dramatic effect in any garden, while providing a reliable food source for hummingbirds.

4. Trumpet Vine (Campsis radicans): If you’re looking for a vine that will attract hummingbirds, look no further than the Trumpet Vine. This fast-growing plant produces large, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of orange and red. It’s important to note that the Trumpet Vine can be quite invasive, so it’s best to plant it in a controlled area where it won’t take over your entire garden.

5. Scarlet Sage (Salvia coccinea): The Scarlet Sage is a versatile plant that comes in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. Hummingbirds are particularly fond of the red-flowered varieties. This plant blooms from summer to fall, providing a long-lasting nectar source for hummingbirds. Scarlet Sage thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

6. Fuchsia (Fuchsia spp.): Fuchsia plants are loved for their stunning, pendulous flowers that come in various shades of red, pink, and purple. These vibrant flowers are a hummingbird’s delight, and they bloom from late spring to early fall. Fuchsia plants prefer partial shade and moist soil, making them perfect for hanging baskets or containers in shady areas.

7. Penstemon (Penstemon spp.): Penstemons are a diverse group of plants that come in many shapes, sizes, and colors. Hummingbirds are particularly attracted to the tubular flowers of Penstemon, which provide them with easy access to nectar. These plants are generally drought-tolerant and prefer full sun or partial shade.

When planting flowers to attract hummingbirds, it’s important to provide a variety of blooms that will attract them throughout the season. By incorporating a combination of these hummingbird-friendly flowers, you can create a garden that not only adds beauty to your surroundings but also provides a vital food source for these amazing birds.

Remember to maintain a pesticide-free environment, as hummingbirds are highly sensitive to chemicals. Additionally, keep your hummingbird feeders clean and filled with fresh nectar to supplement the natural food sources in your garden.

Attracting hummingbirds to your garden is a rewarding experience that allows you to observe these stunning creatures up close. By planting flowers that delight them, you’ll create a haven that not only attracts hummingbirds but also brings joy and beauty to your outdoor space. So, get out there, start planting, and prepare to be amazed by the delightful presence of hummingbirds in your garden.
