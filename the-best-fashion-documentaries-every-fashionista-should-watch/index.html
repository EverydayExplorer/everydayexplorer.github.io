The Best Fashion Documentaries Every Fashionista Should Watch


Fashion documentaries have become increasingly popular in recent years, providing a fascinating glimpse into the world of fashion, style, and the creative minds behind it all. Whether you consider yourself a fashion enthusiast or simply have an interest in the industry, there are certain documentaries that are a must-watch for every fashionista. In this blog, we will be exploring some of the best fashion documentaries that offer insight, inspiration, and entertainment for anyone with an interest in fashion.

1. ‘The September Issue’
‘The September Issue’ is a documentary that takes an in-depth look into the making of the iconic September issue of Vogue magazine. Directed by R.J. Cutler, the film follows the legendary editor-in-chief, Anna Wintour, and her team as they navigate the high-pressure world of fashion and work tirelessly to produce one of the most highly-anticipated issues of the year. This documentary gives viewers an inside look into the creative process behind a fashion magazine and the immense effort required to create a successful publication.

2. ‘Dior and I’
‘Dior and I’ offers an intimate portrayal of the iconic fashion house, Christian Dior, as it goes through a major transition with a new creative director, Raf Simons. Directed by Frédéric Tcheng, the film takes viewers behind the scenes of the prestigious fashion house as Simons prepares for his first haute couture collection. It not only provides a glimpse into the intricate craftsmanship and attention to detail that goes into each garment but also highlights the pressures faced by designers in the ever-evolving world of fashion.

3. ‘The First Monday in May’
Directed by Andrew Rossi, ‘The First Monday in May’ explores the intersection of fashion and art through the lens of the annual Met Gala. The documentary follows the preparations for the exhibition, ‘China: Through the Looking Glass,’ at the Metropolitan Museum of Art in 2015. The film showcases the collaborative efforts between the fashion industry and the world of art, demonstrating how fashion can be a form of artistic expression. It also delves into the theme of cultural appropriation in fashion, sparking important discussions about diversity and representation.

4. ‘McQueen’
‘McQueen’ is a documentary that pays tribute to the late British fashion designer, Alexander McQueen. Directed by Ian Bonhôte and Peter Ettedgui, the film tells the story of McQueen’s life, his incredible talent, and the impact he had on the fashion industry. Through interviews with friends, family, and collaborators, viewers gain a deeper understanding of McQueen’s creative process and the emotional turmoil that inspired his groundbreaking designs. This documentary showcases the raw and powerful nature of McQueen’s work while shedding light on the darker aspects of the fashion world.

5. ‘The True Cost’
‘The True Cost’ is an eye-opening documentary that explores the environmental and social impact of the fast fashion industry. Directed by Andrew Morgan, the film investigates the hidden costs behind cheap clothing, including the exploitation of garment workers and the pollution caused by mass production. It encourages viewers to consider the ethics and sustainability of their fashion choices, raising awareness about the importance of conscious consumerism in the fashion industry.

6. ‘IRIS’
‘Iris’ is a delightful documentary that celebrates the life and style of fashionista Iris Apfel. Directed by the iconic Albert Maysles, the film follows Apfel, known for her eccentric and bold fashion choices, as she navigates the world of fashion and design. ‘IRIS’ offers a refreshing perspective on fashion, emphasizing the importance of individuality, self-expression, and embracing personal style at any age.

These fashion documentaries provide invaluable insights into the fashion industry, its culture, and the creative minds that shape it. Whether you’re interested in the inner workings of a fashion magazine, the artistry behind couture design, or the social impact of fashion, these films offer something for everyone. So grab some popcorn, settle in, and get ready for a stylish and thought-provoking movie night.
