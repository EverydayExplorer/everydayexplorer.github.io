Comedy in Advertising: Clever Campaigns that Make Us Smile


In the world of advertising, where the competition for consumer attention is fierce, humor has proven to be a powerful tool. Cleverly crafted comedic campaigns have the ability to captivate audiences, leaving a lasting impression and even making us smile. In this blog post, we will explore the realm of comedy in advertising, showcasing some of the most memorable and successful campaigns that have tickled our funny bones.

One such campaign that immediately comes to mind is the iconic Old Spice ‘The Man Your Man Could Smell Like’ series. This series of commercials, featuring the charismatic and witty Isaiah Mustafa, took the advertising world by storm. Through a combination of clever writing, absurd scenarios, and Mustafa’s impeccable delivery, these ads became viral sensations. Whether it was Mustafa riding a horse on a beach or transforming from a bathroom to a boat, the humor in these commercials resonated with audiences, making them not only memorable but also highly shareable.

Another example of comedy in advertising is the widely popular Geico ‘Hump Day’ commercial. This commercial, set in an office environment, features a talking camel joyfully celebrating the middle of the workweek. The absurdity of a talking camel wandering around an office, asking everyone if they know what day it is, is what makes this commercial so memorable. The humor lies in the unexpectedness of the situation, creating a light-hearted and entertaining experience for viewers.

Moving on, we cannot overlook the hilarity brought to us by the Dollar Shave Club. Their debut commercial, simply titled ‘Our Blades Are F***ing Great,’ instantly caught the attention of viewers with its irreverent and cheeky approach. The founder of the company, Michael Dubin, stars in the commercial, delivering a witty and humorous script that pokes fun at the traditional shaving industry. The combination of Dubin’s comedic timing and the boldness of the script helped the Dollar Shave Club gain widespread recognition and a loyal customer base.

In addition to these examples, comedy in advertising has been used by various brands to address societal issues in a lighthearted manner. One such campaign is the ‘Real Beauty’ series by Dove. These commercials tackle the unrealistic beauty standards perpetuated by the media, using humor to shed light on the issue. By juxtaposing the comedic scenarios with an underlying message, Dove successfully engages viewers while making them reflect on the importance of self-acceptance and embracing their own unique beauty.

Comedy in advertising has the ability to break through the noise and leave a lasting impression on consumers. By utilizing clever writing, unexpected scenarios, and talented actors, brands can create campaigns that not only entertain but also connect with their target audience. Whether it’s through absurdity, wit, or satirical commentary, comedic campaigns have the power to make us smile and remember a brand long after the commercial ends.

In conclusion, the use of comedy in advertising has proven to be a successful strategy for brands looking to capture consumer attention. From the Old Spice ‘The Man Your Man Could Smell Like’ series to Geico’s ‘Hump Day’ commercial, and the Dollar Shave Club’s bold debut, these campaigns have demonstrated the power of humor in making a lasting impact. By incorporating comedy into their advertising, brands can create memorable and shareable experiences that not only entertain but also connect with their audience on a deeper level. So the next time you see a cleverly funny advertisement, take a moment to appreciate the creativity and wit behind it, and let it bring a smile to your face.
