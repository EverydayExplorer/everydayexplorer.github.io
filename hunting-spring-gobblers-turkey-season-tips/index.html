Hunting Spring Gobblers: Turkey Season Tips


Spring turkey hunting is an exhilarating and challenging experience for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you are an experienced hunter or a beginner, hunting spring gobblers requires skill, patience, and preparation. In this blog post, we will discuss some important tips to help you have a successful turkey hunting season.

1. Scout the Area

Before heading out to the hunting grounds, it is crucial to scout the area and familiarize yourself with the turkey’s habitat. Look for signs of turkey activity such as tracks, droppings, feathers, and scratch marks on the ground. Also, listen carefully for turkey calls and gobbling sounds. By understanding their patterns and behavior, you will have a better chance of locating them during the season.

2. Learn the Art of Calling

Mastering different turkey calls is essential for luring in gobblers. There are various types of calls, including box calls, slate calls, diaphragm calls, and locator calls. Practice using these calls before the season begins to perfect your technique. Learning to mimic different hen vocalizations, such as yelps, clucks, purrs, and cuts, will significantly increase your chances of attracting a gobbler.

3. Concealment is Key

Turkeys possess incredibly sharp eyesight, making concealment a critical aspect of turkey hunting. Invest in high-quality camouflage clothing that matches the surrounding environment. Consider wearing a facemask and gloves to cover any exposed skin. Additionally, setting up a blind or using natural cover like bushes or trees will help you blend into the surroundings, increasing your chances of a successful hunt.

4. Use Decoys Strategically

Decoys can be a valuable tool when hunting gobblers. Setting up a lifelike hen or jake decoy can attract curious gobblers and bring them within range. Ensure that you place your decoys in a way that allows you a clear shooting path. Also, be cautious when using full strut decoys as they may intimidate dominant gobblers and scare them away.

5. Time Your Hunts

Knowing the right time to hunt can greatly affect your success rate. Early mornings and late afternoons are typically the most active times for turkeys. Gobblers are active during the early morning hours as they search for hens. During midday, focus on scouting and listening for gobbles. Lastly, late afternoons can be productive as turkeys start to move towards roosting areas. By understanding their daily patterns, you can plan your hunts accordingly.

6. Be Patient

Patience is crucial while hunting spring gobblers. Gobblers can be wary and take their time approaching your calls. Avoid the temptation to call incessantly, as this may alert the turkey to your presence. Give each calling sequence ample time to work before changing your strategy. Remain as still as possible and keep your eyes and ears alert for any signs of approaching gobblers.

7. Safety First

Always prioritize safety during turkey hunting. Be aware of your surroundings and identify your target before taking a shot. Wear orange safety gear when moving to or from your hunting spot to ensure visibility to other hunters. Additionally, be cautious when using turkey calls, as their realistic sounds can attract other hunters. Clear communication with fellow hunters in your group is essential to avoid any accidents.

Conclusion

Turkey hunting is an exciting sport that requires skill, patience, and knowledge of turkey behavior. By scouting the area, mastering various turkey calls, using effective concealment, strategically placing decoys, hunting during the right times, being patient, and practicing safety measures, you increase your chances of a successful spring turkey hunting season. Remember, turkey hunting is not solely about harvesting a gobbler but also about appreciating the beauty and thrill of being in nature. Happy hunting!
