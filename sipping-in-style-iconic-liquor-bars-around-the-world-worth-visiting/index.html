Sipping in Style: Iconic Liquor Bars Around the World Worth Visiting


Are you a liquor connoisseur? Do you enjoy sipping unique cocktails in stylish and iconic bars? If so, then this blog post is for you! Today, we’ll take a journey around the world and explore some of the most famous liquor bars that are truly worth visiting. Get ready to sip in style and indulge in the exquisite flavors and ambiance offered by these establishments.

**1. Artesian, London, UK**

Located in the heart of London, Artesian at The Langham Hotel has been a hotspot for cocktail enthusiasts for many years. Renowned for its innovative and creative mixology, Artesian has consistently been ranked as one of the world’s best bars. With an extensive menu featuring both classic and contemporary cocktails, as well as a team of skilled mixologists, each visit to Artesian is guaranteed to be a memorable experience.

**2. The Dead Rabbit, New York City, USA**

Tucked away in the historic Financial District of New York City, The Dead Rabbit has become an iconic destination for whiskey lovers. Combining a traditional Irish pub atmosphere with a modern cocktail menu, The Dead Rabbit offers an extensive selection of over 130 types of Irish, Scotch, and American whiskies. Whether you’re a whiskey aficionado or a casual drinker, The Dead Rabbit is a must-visit for anyone seeking an authentic and enjoyable liquor experience.

**3. The American Bar, Savoy Hotel, London, UK**

Overlooking the River Thames, The American Bar at the Savoy Hotel has a rich history dating back to the late 19th century. This legendary bar has played host to famous personalities like Winston Churchill and Frank Sinatra, making it an iconic symbol of British luxury. The American Bar is known for its exquisite cocktails and impeccable service, setting the standard for classic bartending techniques. Sit back, relax, and enjoy a taste of elegance at this distinguished bar.

**4. Bar High Five, Tokyo, Japan**

Known for its meticulous attention to detail and exceptional craftsmanship, Bar High Five in Tokyo is a must-visit for cocktail enthusiasts. Led by legendary bartender Hidetsugu Ueno, the bar offers a wide selection of Japanese spirits and a unique experience in mixology. With a focus on simplicity and quality, Bar High Five guarantees a memorable evening of enjoying expertly crafted cocktails in a cozy and intimate setting.

**5. The Clumsies, Athens, Greece**

Nestled in the vibrant city of Athens, The Clumsies is an award-winning cocktail bar that combines elegance with a touch of playfulness. The bar’s unique concept focuses on presenting classic cocktails with a modern twist, using innovative techniques and ingredients. The Clumsies is not only known for its exceptional cocktails but also for its stunning interior design, which effortlessly blends the old with the new.

**6. Smuggler’s Cove, San Francisco, USA**

If you’re a rum enthusiast, then Smuggler’s Cove in San Francisco is the place to be. This iconic tiki bar boasts an impressive collection of over 550 types of rum from across the globe. With its lively and tropical atmosphere, you’ll feel like you’ve been transported to a Caribbean island. The experienced bartenders at Smuggler’s Cove excel in crafting rum-based cocktails that perfectly capture the spirit of the golden age of tiki cocktails.

**7. The Hemingway Bar, Prague, Czech Republic**

Stepping into The Hemingway Bar in Prague is like taking a trip back in time to the 1920s. Named after the famous American author Ernest Hemingway, this bar exudes an old-world charm and sophistication. The Hemingway Bar specializes in classic cocktails, with a particular emphasis on absinthe and rum. Sip on a meticulously crafted cocktail while soaking in the ambiance of this timeless establishment.

**8. Harry’s New York Bar, Paris, France**

Indulge in a piece of history at Harry’s New York Bar in Paris. Established in 1911, this iconic bar is known for inventing classic cocktails such as the Bloody Mary and the French 75. Over the years, it has attracted numerous famous patrons, including Ernest Hemingway and Coco Chanel. Stepping into Harry’s New York Bar feels like entering a time capsule, with its vintage decor and timeless charm. Don’t miss the opportunity to experience the magic of this legendary bar.

Whether you’re a cocktail enthusiast, a whiskey lover, or simply someone who appreciates luxurious experiences, these iconic liquor bars around the world are definitely worth visiting. Indulge in the art of mixology, savor unique flavors, and immerse yourself in the breathtaking ambiance offered by these establishments. Cheers to sipping in style!
