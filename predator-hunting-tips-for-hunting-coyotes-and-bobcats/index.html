Predator Hunting: Tips for Hunting Coyotes and Bobcats


Predator hunting is a thrilling and challenging sport that requires skill, knowledge, and patience. Among the most popular predators to hunt are coyotes and bobcats, known for their cunning nature and elusive behavior. If you’re eager to embark on a predator hunting adventure, here are some valuable tips to help you increase your chances of success.

1. Scout Your Hunting Area: Before heading out into the field, it’s crucial to scout the hunting area thoroughly. Look for signs of coyotes and bobcats such as tracks, scat, and scratch marks on trees. Identifying their preferred habitats, such as dense brush, open fields, or near water sources, will give you valuable insights into where to set up your hunting spot.

2. Use Appropriate Gear: The right gear can make all the difference in predator hunting. Invest in a high-quality rifle or shotgun that is accurate and reliable. Opt for a caliber suitable for coyotes and bobcats, such as .223 or .243. Additionally, equip yourself with quality optics, like a scope or binoculars, to spot predators from a distance. Don’t forget to wear appropriate camouflage clothing to blend in with the surroundings.

3. Master Predator Calls: One of the most effective techniques in predator hunting is using predator calls to lure in your targets. Coyotes and bobcats are highly responsive to distress calls of small prey animals. Invest in electronic or hand-operated predator calls and practice different distress sounds, such as rabbit, bird, or fawn distress. Experiment with various calls and volumes to find what works best in your hunting area.

4. Set up Strategic Decoys: To enhance the effectiveness of your predator calls, consider using decoys. Decoys can be anything that mimics the movement of prey animals, such as a motion decoy or a feathered lure. Strategically placing decoys near your hunting spot can attract coyotes and bobcats, increasing your chances of a successful hunt.

5. Hunt During Optimal Times: Coyotes and bobcats are primarily nocturnal animals but can be active during the day as well. The best time to hunt predators is during their most active periods, which are typically at dawn and dusk. Set up your hunting spot well before these times to allow yourself enough time to settle in silently and patiently wait for your targets.

6. Utilize Stealth and Concealment: Predators have exceptional senses, including acute hearing and a keen sense of smell. To avoid detection, practice stealthy movement and minimize noise. Additionally, use natural cover or blinds to conceal yourself while hunting. Pay attention to wind direction to prevent your scent from being carried towards the predators.

7. Practice Shot Placement: A clean and ethical kill is essential in predator hunting. Aim for vital organs, such as the heart or lungs, to ensure a quick and humane kill. Regular target practice and honing your shooting skills will improve your accuracy and increase the chances of a successful harvest.

8. Follow Hunting Regulations: Always familiarize yourself with local hunting regulations and obtain the necessary licenses and permits before embarking on a predator hunting excursion. Adhering to hunting laws not only ensures your safety but also contributes to the conservation and sustainability of predator populations.

Remember, predator hunting requires patience, persistence, and respect for the animals you pursue. It’s essential to approach this sport responsibly and ethically. By implementing these tips and continuously honing your skills, you’ll be well on your way to becoming a successful predator hunter. Happy hunting!
