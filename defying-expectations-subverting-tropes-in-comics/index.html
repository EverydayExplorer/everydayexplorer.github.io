Defying Expectations: Subverting Tropes in Comics


Comics have always been a medium that thrives on creativity and pushing boundaries. From the colorful pages of superhero adventures to the dark and gritty world of graphic novels, there is something for everyone in the world of comics. However, one of the most exciting aspects of comics is when creators decide to defy expectations and subvert tropes, creating fresh and original stories that keep readers on their toes.

One of the most common tropes in comics is the idea of the ‘chosen one.’ This trope typically revolves around a character who is destined for greatness, often possessing unique powers or abilities that set them apart from everyone else. While this type of story can be entertaining, it can also become predictable and stale if not handled correctly. That’s why it’s so refreshing when creators decide to take this trope and turn it on its head.

One example of this trope being subverted can be seen in the comic series ‘Saga’ by Brian K. Vaughan and Fiona Staples. In this series, the main characters, Alana and Marko, are far from the traditional ‘chosen ones.’ They are two ordinary people caught up in a galactic war, just trying to protect their family and stay alive. By defying the expectations of the chosen one trope, ‘Saga’ is able to tell a more nuanced and complex story that explores themes of family, love, and sacrifice in a way that feels fresh and original.

Another common trope in comics is the idea of the ‘damsel in distress.’ This trope typically involves a female character who is in need of rescue by a male hero. While this trope has been a mainstay in comics for decades, it is also one that has come under scrutiny for its outdated and sexist implications. That’s why it’s so empowering to see creators subvert this trope and create stories where female characters are strong, independent, and fully capable of saving themselves.

One such example can be found in the comic series ‘Ms. Marvel’ by G. Willow Wilson and Adrian Alphona. In this series, Kamala Khan takes on the mantle of Ms. Marvel and becomes a hero in her own right, facing challenges and villains without needing to be rescued by anyone else. By subverting the damsel in distress trope, ‘Ms. Marvel’ is able to present a more empowering and inclusive vision of heroism that resonates with readers of all ages.

In addition to defying expectations with character tropes, comics also have the power to subvert genre conventions and storytelling techniques. For example, the comic series ‘The Umbrella Academy’ by Gerard Way and Gabriel Bá takes the classic superhero team concept and injects it with a heavy dose of surrealism and dark humor. By blending genres and playing with narrative structure, ‘The Umbrella Academy’ is able to create a truly unique reading experience that keeps readers guessing with each turn of the page.

Ultimately, the beauty of comics lies in their ability to defy expectations and challenge the status quo. By subverting tropes and pushing boundaries, creators are able to breathe new life into the medium and tell stories that resonate with readers on a deeper level. Whether it’s reimagining classic character archetypes or blending genres in unexpected ways, comics have the power to surprise and delight in ways that few other storytelling mediums can. So the next time you pick up a comic, keep an eye out for those moments when creators decide to defy expectations and subvert tropes – you never know what kind of adventure awaits.
