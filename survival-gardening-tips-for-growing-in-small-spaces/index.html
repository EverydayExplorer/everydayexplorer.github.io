Survival Gardening: Tips for Growing in Small Spaces


In times of limited resources and uncertain futures, survival gardening has become a popular way for individuals to ensure a steady supply of fresh produce. However, for those living in small spaces, such as apartments or urban areas, the concept of growing your own food may seem daunting. But fear not! With a few smart strategies and some creativity, it is possible to grow your own food even in the tiniest of spaces. In this article, we will explore some valuable tips for survival gardening in small spaces.

1. Select the right plants: When space is a limitation, it is important to choose plants that are compact and well-suited for container gardening. Look for varieties that are specifically designed for small spaces, such as cherry tomatoes, lettuce, herbs, and peppers. These plants typically have shorter growth habits and can thrive in pots or hanging baskets.

2. Maximize vertical space: In small spaces, utilizing vertical space is key. Install wall-mounted planters or trellises to grow climbing vegetables like beans, peas, or cucumbers. These plants take up minimal ground space and can be trained to grow upwards, saving valuable floor space.

3. Take advantage of natural light: Small spaces often have limited access to natural light, so it is crucial to make the most of what you have. Place your plants near windows or balconies where they can receive adequate sunlight. If sunlight is scarce, consider investing in grow lights to provide the necessary light for your plants’ growth.

4. Use containers wisely: When gardening in small spaces, containers are your best friends. Opt for containers with good drainage and appropriate sizes for your plants. Hanging baskets, vertical planters, and window boxes are all excellent choices for maximizing space. Additionally, consider using unconventional containers like repurposed buckets, cans, or even old shoes, as long as they can hold soil and provide drainage.

5. Choose the right soil and fertilizer: Since you will be growing your plants in containers, it is essential to choose the right soil mixture to ensure optimal growth. Use a high-quality potting mix that is well-draining and nutrient-rich. Additionally, consider using organic fertilizers to provide the necessary nutrients for your plants’ health and productivity.

6. Practice companion planting: Companion planting involves growing different plants together to mutually benefit each other. When gardening in small spaces, companion planting can be particularly beneficial as it maximizes space utilization and enhances plant health. For example, planting herbs like basil or chives alongside tomatoes can help repel pests and improve overall plant growth.

7. Implement efficient watering techniques: In small spaces, watering can be a challenge due to limited access to water sources. To ensure efficient water usage, consider installing drip irrigation systems or self-watering containers. These systems can help deliver water directly to the roots, reducing water wastage and minimizing the frequency of watering.

8. Stay vigilant against pests and diseases: Even in small spaces, pests and diseases can pose a threat to your plants. Regularly inspect your plants for signs of infestation or disease, and take necessary preventive measures such as using organic pest controls or adjusting environmental conditions to discourage pest and disease development.

Survival gardening in small spaces may require some extra planning and creativity, but the rewards are well worth the effort. Not only will you have a fresh supply of nutritious produce, but you will also develop a deeper connection with nature and gain valuable survival skills. So, don’t let your limited space hold you back - start your own survival garden today and enjoy the fruits of your labor!
