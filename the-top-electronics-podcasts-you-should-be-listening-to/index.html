The Top Electronics Podcasts You Should Be Listening To


In today’s fast-paced world, staying up to date with the latest trends and advancements in the electronics industry can be quite a challenge. Thankfully, podcasts have emerged as an excellent way to stay informed and entertained while on the go. Whether you’re a tech enthusiast, a professional in the field, or simply curious about the latest gadgets, there’s a podcast out there for you. In this blog post, we’ll explore the top electronics podcasts that you should be listening to. From in-depth discussions on the latest smartphone releases to interviews with industry experts, these podcasts cover a wide range of topics that are sure to pique your interest.

1. ‘The Amp Hour Podcast’ - Hosted by Chris Gammell and Dave Jones, this podcast delves into the world of electronics design, engineering, and manufacturing. With a focus on practical tips and real-world experiences, Chris and Dave provide valuable insights into the electronics industry. From circuit design to component sourcing, this podcast is a must-listen for anyone interested in the nuts and bolts of electronics.

2. ‘The Spark Gap’ - If you’re looking for a podcast that explores the intersection of technology and creativity, then ‘The Spark Gap’ is for you. Hosted by Karl and Corey, this podcast features discussions on innovative electronic projects, DIY hacks, and the latest trends in the maker community. With a healthy dose of humor and a passion for all things electronics, Karl and Corey bring a unique perspective to the world of technology.

3. ‘Embedded’ - Hosted by Elicia White, ‘Embedded’ takes a deep dive into the world of embedded systems, IoT devices, and firmware development. With a focus on practical applications, Elicia explores the challenges and opportunities in building smart devices. Whether you’re a seasoned professional or a beginner in the field, this podcast offers valuable insights and expert advice.

4. ‘The Amped Up Podcast’ - Hosted by Judy Warner, this podcast focuses on the latest trends and innovations in the electronics industry. From interviews with industry leaders to discussions on emerging technologies, Judy covers a wide range of topics that are relevant to both professionals and enthusiasts. If you’re looking to stay ahead of the curve, ‘The Amped Up Podcast’ is a must-listen.

5. ‘MacroFab Engineering Podcast’ - Hosted by Parker Dillmann and Stephen Kraig, this podcast covers a wide range of topics in electronics manufacturing and engineering. From PCB design to manufacturing best practices, Parker and Stephen share their knowledge and experiences to help listeners navigate the complexities of the industry. With a mix of technical insights and practical advice, this podcast is a valuable resource for anyone involved in electronics manufacturing.

6. ‘The Engineering Commons Podcast’ - If you’re interested in a broader perspective on engineering and technology, ‘The Engineering Commons Podcast’ is worth a listen. Hosted by Jeff Shelton, Adam Higgins, and Brian Corey, this podcast explores various engineering disciplines, including electronics. From discussions on engineering ethics to interviews with industry experts, this podcast offers a well-rounded view of the engineering profession.

7. ‘The Amp Hour Electronics Podcast’ - Hosted by Chris Gammell and Dave Jones, this podcast focuses on the latest news, trends, and developments in the electronics industry. With a mix of technical discussions and entertaining banter, Chris and Dave keep listeners engaged and informed. From industry insights to DIY projects, this podcast offers something for everyone.

In conclusion, podcasts have become a valuable resource for staying informed and entertained in the world of electronics. Whether you’re a professional in the field or simply curious about the latest gadgets, these top electronics podcasts are sure to keep you engaged and inspired. From practical tips and real-world experiences to in-depth discussions and interviews with industry experts, these podcasts cover a wide range of topics that are relevant to both professionals and enthusiasts. So grab your headphones and start listening to these top electronics podcasts today!
