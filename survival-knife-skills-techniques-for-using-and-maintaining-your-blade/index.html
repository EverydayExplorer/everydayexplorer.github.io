Survival Knife Skills: Techniques for Using and Maintaining Your Blade


A survival knife is an essential tool for anyone venturing into the great outdoors. Whether you’re camping, hiking, or preparing for a disaster, knowing how to use and maintain your blade properly is crucial for your safety and success. In this blog post, we will explore the various techniques that will help you master your survival knife skills.

Choosing the Right Knife

Before we delve into the techniques, it’s important to choose the right knife for your needs. There are several factors to consider when selecting a survival knife:

  1. Blade Material: Look for a blade made of high-quality stainless steel or carbon steel. Stainless steel blades are more resistant to corrosion, while carbon steel blades are known for their durability and ability to hold a sharp edge.

  2. Blade Design: Consider the type of blade that suits your intended use. A drop-point blade, with a sharp, controllable tip, is suitable for general cutting tasks, while a tanto blade, with its reinforced point, is ideal for piercing and prying.

  3. Blade Length: Choose a blade length that suits your requirements. A blade length of 4-6 inches is typically recommended for general survival and bushcraft purposes.

  4. Handle Material: Look for a handle that provides a secure grip, even in wet conditions. G10, Micarta, and rubberized handles are popular choices due to their durability and grip.

Once you have chosen the right knife, it’s time to learn the essential survival knife skills.

Techniques for Using Your Survival Knife

1. Basic Cutting and Slicing

The most fundamental skill with a survival knife is basic cutting and slicing. Hold the knife in a firm grip, with your thumb on the spine of the blade for better control. Practice cutting through different materials, such as rope, branches, and food items, to develop your precision and efficiency.

2. Feathering and Batoning

Feathering is a technique used in fire building, where you make fine shavings on a stick to create tinder. With a survival knife, you can easily create feather sticks by holding the knife at a shallow angle against the wood and pulling downward. Batoning, on the other hand, involves using the spine of your knife to split wood by striking it with a baton.

3. Whittling and Carving

Whittling and carving are useful skills for crafting tools and utensils from wood. With your survival knife, practice carving notches, spatulas, or even simple figurines. Start with softwood and gradually move on to harder woods as you gain more experience.

4. Fire Building

A survival knife is an essential tool for fire building. Use your knife to create sparks by striking it against a ferro rod or a flint rock. Additionally, you can use your knife to prepare kindling, gather firewood, and even fashion a fire bow drill for friction fire starting.

Techniques for Maintaining Your Survival Knife

To ensure that your survival knife serves you well for years to come, proper maintenance is essential. Here are some techniques for maintaining your blade:

1. Regular Cleaning and Drying

After each use, clean your knife with mild soap and warm water. Avoid soaking the knife, as it can cause the handle material to deteriorate. Dry your knife thoroughly before storing it to prevent rust and corrosion.

2. Sharpening

Keep your blade sharp by regularly sharpening it with a whetstone or sharpening system. Maintain the original bevel angle of the blade while sharpening to preserve its cutting performance.

3. Lubrication

Apply a thin coat of oil to the blade periodically to prevent rust and corrosion. Food-grade mineral oil, gun oil, or WD-40 can be used for this purpose. Remember to wipe off any excess oil before storing the knife.

4. Storage

Store your knife in a dry and secure place. Consider using a sheath or a knife case to protect the blade from moisture and accidental damage.

By mastering both the techniques for using and maintaining your survival knife, you can ensure that it remains a reliable tool in any survival situation. Confidence and proficiency in handling your knife will increase your chances of success and keep you safe in the wilderness.

In conclusion, a survival knife is not just a tool but a lifeline in the great outdoors. By choosing the right knife, practicing the essential techniques, and maintaining your blade properly, you will be well-prepared to tackle any survival situation that comes your way. So, get out there, sharpen your skills, and embrace the adventure with your trusty survival knife by your side!
