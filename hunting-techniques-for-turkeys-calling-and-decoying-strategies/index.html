Hunting Techniques for Turkeys: Calling and Decoying Strategies


When it comes to hunting turkeys, mastering the art of calling and decoying is essential for a successful hunt. These techniques not only attract turkeys but also help hunters get closer to their prey. In this blog post, we will explore various calling and decoying strategies that will increase your chances of bagging that elusive tom turkey.

Calling Strategies:

1. Box Calls: Box calls are one of the most popular and effective turkey calls. They produce a wide range of turkey sounds, from soft purrs to loud yelps. Mastering the rhythm and cadence of box calls can help you imitate the natural calls of turkeys, luring them closer to your position.

2. Slate Calls: Slate calls, also known as pot calls, produce rich and realistic turkey sounds. They consist of a slate or glass surface that is rubbed with a striker to create the desired calls. Slate calls are versatile and can mimic a variety of turkey vocalizations, including clucks, purrs, and gobbles.

3. Mouth Calls: Mouth calls, also called diaphragm calls, are placed inside the hunter’s mouth and are operated by using tongue and airflow. These calls require practice to master, but once you get the hang of it, they can produce highly realistic turkey sounds. Mouth calls allow hunters to keep their hands free for aiming and shooting.

Decoying Strategies:

1. Decoy Placement: Proper decoy placement is crucial for attracting turkeys. Start by setting up realistic decoys, such as hen or jake decoys, in an open area where turkeys are likely to spot them. Position the decoys in a way that simulates a natural turkey grouping, with the dominant bird facing away from your hunting location. This will create a sense of security for incoming turkeys.

2. Motion Decoys: Incorporating motion decoys, such as a spinning fan or a bobbing hen decoy, can add an extra level of realism to your setup. Turkeys are naturally curious and will be drawn to movement. Experiment with different motion decoys to find what works best in your hunting area.

3. Calling and Decoying Combination: To maximize your chances of success, combine calling and decoying techniques. Start by using soft yelps and purrs to imitate a hen’s calls. This will grab the attention of nearby turkeys. As they approach, continue calling while using decoys to reinforce the illusion of a turkey group. This combination of sight and sound will often trigger a tom’s territorial instincts, bringing him within range.

Remember, mastering turkey hunting techniques takes time and practice. It’s important to familiarize yourself with the various calls and decoys available, as well as the behavior patterns of turkeys in your hunting area. Additionally, always follow local hunting regulations and safety guidelines to ensure a safe and ethical hunt.

So, whether you’re a seasoned turkey hunter or just starting out, honing your calling and decoying skills will greatly improve your chances of a successful turkey hunt. Happy hunting!
