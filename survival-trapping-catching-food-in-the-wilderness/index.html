Survival Trapping: Catching Food in the Wilderness


When you find yourself in the wilderness, far away from civilization and its convenient food sources, one of the most essential skills you can possess is the ability to catch your own food. Survival trapping is a time-tested method that has been used by humans for centuries to secure sustenance in challenging environments. In this blog post, we will explore the art of survival trapping, discussing various techniques and strategies that can help you catch food in the wilderness.

Understanding the Basics

Before delving into specific trapping techniques, it’s important to understand the basic principles behind survival trapping. The goal of trapping is to capture animals for food, and this requires a deep understanding of their behavior, habits, and natural habitats. By observing animal tracks, listening for their calls, and studying their patterns, you can increase your chances of success in trapping.

Setting Up Your Traps

One of the most popular and effective trapping methods is the snare trap. Snares are simple, lightweight, and easy to set up. To create a snare trap, you will need a sturdy piece of cordage or wire and a branch or sapling to act as the snare loop. Select a location where animals are likely to pass, such as game trails or near water sources. Attach the snare loop to the branch, making sure it is at the appropriate height for the target animal’s neck or body. Finally, secure the snare loop to a tree or stake, ensuring that it is taut and ready to catch any unsuspecting prey.

Camouflaging Your Traps

Animals in the wilderness are naturally cautious and wary of their surroundings. To increase the effectiveness of your traps, it’s crucial to camouflage them. By disguising your traps with natural materials such as leaves, twigs, and dirt, you can make them blend seamlessly into the environment. This will reduce the chances of animals detecting the traps and increase your chances of a successful catch.

Baiting Your Traps

Baiting is another vital aspect of survival trapping. By using bait that is attractive to the target animal, you can lure them into the trap. Different animals are attracted to different types of bait, so it’s important to research the preferences of the species you are targeting. Common bait options include small pieces of meat, fruits, and even insects. Place the bait near or inside the trap, making sure it is easily accessible to the animal.

Checking and Maintaining Traps

Once your traps are set, it’s important to regularly check and maintain them. Checking your traps frequently allows you to release any non-targeted animals and reset the traps if necessary. It also helps prevent animals from becoming injured or suffering unnecessarily. Additionally, regular maintenance ensures that your traps remain functional and effective. Replace worn-out components, adjust the tension of snares, and re-bait as needed.

The Ethics of Survival Trapping

While survival trapping can be a valuable skill, it’s essential to approach it with respect and ethical considerations. Only trap for food when necessary, and always prioritize humane treatment of animals. Release non-targeted animals unharmed, and strive to minimize suffering. Remember, survival trapping should be seen as a last resort, and efforts should be made to gather food through other means whenever possible.

Conclusion

Survival trapping is a valuable skill that can mean the difference between life and death in the wilderness. By understanding the basics, setting up effective traps, camouflaging them, baiting them appropriately, and regularly checking and maintaining them, you can increase your chances of catching food in the wild. However, it’s crucial to approach survival trapping ethically and responsibly, always prioritizing the well-being of animals. So, the next time you find yourself in the great outdoors, remember these techniques and embrace the art of survival trapping.
