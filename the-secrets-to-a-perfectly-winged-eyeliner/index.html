The Secrets to a Perfectly Winged Eyeliner


Achieving the perfect winged eyeliner can sometimes feel like an elusive goal. You’ve seen it on celebrities and influencers, and you want to recreate the look yourself. But no matter how many tutorials you watch or how many liners you try, it just never seems to turn out quite right. Well, fear not! In this blog post, we’re going to share with you the secrets to a perfectly winged eyeliner that will have you slaying the game in no time.

1. Choose the right eyeliner: The first step to achieving the perfect winged eyeliner is choosing the right eyeliner. While there are many different types of eyeliners available, we recommend using a liquid eyeliner with a fine tip. This will give you more control and precision when creating your wing.

2. Prep and prime your eyelids: Before applying your eyeliner, it’s important to prep and prime your eyelids. This will ensure that your eyeliner goes on smoothly and stays in place all day. Start by applying an eyeshadow primer to your lids, followed by a light dusting of translucent powder. This will help to absorb any excess oil and create a smooth canvas for your liner.

3. Use a guide for your wing: Creating a symmetrical wing can be a challenge, especially if you’re just starting out. To make it easier, use a guide for your wing. You can use a small piece of tape or a business card to create a straight line from the outer corner of your eye to the end of your eyebrow. This will serve as a guide when applying your liner and ensure that both wings are even.

4. Start with a thin line: When applying your eyeliner, start with a thin line along your upper lash line. This will serve as the base for your wing and help you to achieve a more seamless look. Use short, light strokes to create your line, and gradually build up the thickness as desired.

5. Create the wing: Once you have your base line, it’s time to create the wing. Using the guide you created earlier, extend the line outwards towards the end of your eyebrow. The angle and length of your wing will depend on your personal preference and eye shape. Experiment with different angles and lengths until you find the look that suits you best.

6. Clean up any mistakes: Don’t worry if your wing isn’t perfect on the first try. Even the most experienced makeup artists make mistakes. To clean up any smudges or uneven lines, use a small angled brush dipped in makeup remover or concealer. This will help to sharpen the edges of your wing and create a clean, polished look.

7. Set your eyeliner: To ensure that your winged eyeliner stays in place all day, it’s important to set it with a translucent powder or a matching eyeshadow. Use a small brush to gently press the powder or shadow over your liner, taking care not to smudge or disrupt the shape of your wing.

With these secrets in your beauty arsenal, you’ll be able to conquer the art of the perfect winged eyeliner. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don’t be discouraged if it takes a few tries to get it right. Keep experimenting, have fun, and soon enough, you’ll be rocking flawless wings like a pro. Happy winging!
