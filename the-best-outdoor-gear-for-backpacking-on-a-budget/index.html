The Best Outdoor Gear for Backpacking on a Budget


Are you an avid backpacker looking to explore the great outdoors without breaking the bank? Look no further! In this blog post, we have compiled a list of the best outdoor gear that will not only enhance your backpacking experience but also fit within your budget. So, let’s dive in and discover the must-have gear for your next adventure!

1. Backpack

A good quality backpack is the foundation of any successful backpacking trip. Look for a lightweight and durable backpack that offers ample storage space. Some popular budget-friendly options include the Teton Sports Scout 3400 and the Mountaintop 40L Hiking Backpack. These backpacks are not only affordable but also designed for comfort and durability.

2. Tent

When it comes to choosing a tent on a budget, look for one that offers a good balance between weight, durability, and price. The ALPS Mountaineering Lynx and the Coleman Sundome Tent are excellent choices for backpackers on a budget. These tents are easy to set up, provide sufficient protection from the elements, and won’t break the bank.

3. Sleeping Bag

A good night’s sleep is crucial after a long day of hiking. Opt for a lightweight and warm sleeping bag that fits your budget. The Kelty Cosmic 20 and the Teton Sports Tracker Ultralight Sleeping Bag are affordable options that offer excellent insulation and comfort. Ensure you choose a sleeping bag that is suitable for the temperature range you’ll be camping in.

4. Cooking Equipment

Fuel your adventures with delicious meals cooked on the go. Investing in a lightweight camping stove, such as the MSR PocketRocket 2, will allow you to prepare hot meals and beverages easily. Don’t forget to pack lightweight cookware, such as the GSI Outdoors Pinnacle Camper Cookset, to complete your camping kitchen setup.

5. Footwear

Choosing the right footwear is essential for a comfortable backpacking experience. Look for hiking boots or shoes that offer good traction, ankle support, and durability. The Merrell Moab 2 and the Columbia Newton Ridge Plus II are popular options that provide excellent value for money.

6. Clothing

When it comes to clothing, layering is key. Invest in moisture-wicking base layers, insulating mid-layers, and waterproof outer layers. Look for budget-friendly options from reputable outdoor brands like Columbia, REI, and Patagonia. Don’t forget to pack a lightweight and packable down jacket for those chilly evenings around the campfire.

7. Water Filtration

Staying hydrated is vital during your backpacking adventure. Instead of relying on expensive bottled water, invest in a reliable water filtration system. The Sawyer Mini Water Filtration System and the LifeStraw Personal Water Filter are compact, lightweight, and affordable options that ensure you have access to clean drinking water wherever you go.

8. Navigation

Ensure you have the right tools to navigate the wilderness safely. A compass and a detailed topographic map are essential for any backpacking trip. Additionally, consider using a GPS device or a smartphone app like Gaia GPS or AllTrails to assist you in navigating your chosen trail.

9. Lighting

Don’t get caught in the dark! Invest in a reliable headlamp or flashlight to light up your campsite and navigate after sunset. The Black Diamond Spot Headlamp and the Petzl Tikkina Headlamp are affordable options that offer excellent brightness and battery life.

10. First Aid Kit

Safety should always be a top priority. Pack a lightweight and compact first aid kit that includes essentials like bandages, antiseptic wipes, pain relievers, and blister treatment. The Adventure Medical Kits Ultralight/Watertight .7 is a comprehensive first aid kit that covers your basic medical needs while being budget-friendly.

With these budget-friendly outdoor gear recommendations, you can embark on your backpacking adventure without worrying about breaking the bank. Remember, it’s not about how much you spend on gear, but how you embrace the beauty of nature and the thrill of exploring the great outdoors. Happy backpacking!
