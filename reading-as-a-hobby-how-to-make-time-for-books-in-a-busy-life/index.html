Reading as a Hobby: How to Make Time for Books in a Busy Life


In today’s fast-paced world, finding time for hobbies can be a challenge. With work, family, and other commitments, it often feels like there are not enough hours in the day to do the things we love. However, one hobby that should not be overlooked is reading. Not only can it be a source of pleasure and escapism, but it also has numerous benefits for our mental and emotional well-being. In this blog post, we will explore some practical strategies for incorporating reading into a busy life.

1. Set aside dedicated reading time
One of the easiest ways to make time for reading is to schedule it into your daily routine. This could be as little as 15 minutes a day or as much as an hour, depending on your schedule. By designating a specific time for reading, you are more likely to stick to it and make progress on your books.

2. Make use of your commute or waiting time
For many people, commuting to and from work can take up a significant portion of their day. Instead of staring at your phone or listening to music, consider using this time to read. Whether you prefer physical books or e-readers, having a book handy can turn your commute into a productive reading session. Similarly, if you find yourself waiting for appointments or stuck in traffic, grab your book and make the most of the downtime.

3. Set realistic reading goals
It’s important to set realistic reading goals based on your schedule and commitments. Trying to read a book a week when you have limited free time might set you up for disappointment and frustration. Instead, aim for a goal that you can realistically achieve, such as reading two books a month. By setting achievable goals, you will feel a sense of accomplishment and be motivated to continue reading.

4. Prioritize reading over other activities
In our digital age, it can be easy to get caught up in social media, TV shows, or video games. While these activities can be enjoyable, they often consume a significant amount of our time. By consciously prioritizing reading over these activities, you can make room for books in your busy life. Consider reducing your TV or social media time by an hour each day and using that time to immerse yourself in a good book instead.

5. Join a book club or reading group
Sometimes, having a sense of accountability can motivate us to make time for our hobbies. Joining a book club or reading group can provide just that. Not only will you have the opportunity to discuss books with like-minded individuals, but you will also have a set schedule for reading and meeting. This can help you stay on track and committed to your reading goals.

6. Choose books that resonate with you
It is important to select books that genuinely interest you. If you find yourself forcing through books that you do not enjoy, reading will feel like a chore rather than a hobby. Explore different genres, authors, or topics that pique your curiosity and make reading an enjoyable experience. When you are genuinely engaged with the material, you will be more inclined to make time for it in your day.

7. Create a cozy reading space
Having a designated reading space can make the act of reading more appealing and comfortable. Find a quiet corner in your home, set up a comfortable chair or bean bag, and create a cozy reading nook. Limit distractions, such as noise or electronic devices, and focus on creating a peaceful environment that encourages relaxation and immersion in your book.

In conclusion, reading is a hobby that offers countless benefits and should be prioritized in our busy lives. By setting aside dedicated reading time, making use of idle moments, setting realistic goals, prioritizing reading over other activities, joining a book club, choosing books that resonate with us, and creating a cozy reading space, we can incorporate reading into our daily routines. So, grab a book, lose yourself in its pages, and unlock the joys and benefits of reading.
