How to Bake a Classic Apple Pie from Scratch


There’s something incredibly comforting about the aroma of a freshly baked apple pie wafting through the house. Perhaps it’s the combination of warm cinnamon and sweet apples that makes it such a beloved dessert. If you’ve ever wanted to try your hand at making a classic apple pie from scratch, look no further. In this blog post, we’ll guide you through the process step by step, ensuring delicious results every time.

Ingredients

To begin, gather all the necessary ingredients:

  • Crust:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed

  • 1 teaspoon salt

  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar

  • ¼ to ½ cup ice-cold water

  • Filling:

  • 6–8 medium-sized apples (such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)

  • ¾ cup granulated sugar

  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg

  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

Making a delicious apple pie starts with a flaky, buttery crust. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar. Cut the cold butter into small cubes and add it to the dry ingredients. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, mix the butter into the flour until the texture becomes crumbly.

Next, drizzle in the ice-cold water, a little at a time, while mixing the dough with a fork. Stop adding water when the dough starts to come together. Divide the dough in half and wrap each portion tightly in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

While the dough chills, it’s time to prepare the apple filling. Peel, core, and slice the apples into thin, even pieces. In a large bowl, combine the sugar, flour, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Add the sliced apples and toss them gently to coat them evenly with the sugar mixture. Finally, drizzle the lemon juice over the apples to prevent browning and enhance the flavor.

Step 3: Roll Out the Dough

Once the dough has chilled, remove one portion from the refrigerator. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough into a circle about 12 inches in diameter. Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch pie dish, pressing it gently into the bottom and sides. Trim any excess dough hanging over the edges of the dish.

Step 4: Assemble the Pie

Pour the apple filling into the prepared crust, ensuring it is evenly distributed. Roll out the second portion of dough to create the top crust. You can either position the dough as a whole or cut it into strips for a lattice design. If using a whole top crust, make sure to cut several slits to allow steam to escape while baking.

Step 5: Bake the Pie

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Place the assembled pie on a baking sheet to catch any spills. Bake for 15 minutes, then reduce the oven temperature to 375°F (190°C) and continue baking for 35-40 minutes until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

Step 6: Cool and Serve

Once the pie is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours. This will allow the filling to set. Serve the pie warm or at room temperature, optionally accompanied by a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.

Conclusion

Baking a classic apple pie from scratch may seem intimidating, but with the right ingredients and steps, it can easily become a cherished family tradition. With a flaky crust and a fragrant, sweet apple filling, this dessert is sure to delight everyone’s taste buds. So take the time to make this homemade apple pie and savor each and every bite. Your friends and family will thank you, and your house will be filled with warmth and love.
