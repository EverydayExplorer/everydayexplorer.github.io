Hunting Moose in the Rut: Strategies for Calling and Pursuing Bulls


The crisp autumn air fills your lungs as you trek through the rugged wilderness, your heart pounding with anticipation. You’re on the hunt for one of North America’s most majestic creatures: the moose. But this isn’t just any hunt—it’s the rut, the breeding season when bull moose are at their most active and vocal. To successfully bag a trophy bull during this time requires skill, patience, and a deep understanding of moose behavior. In this guide, we’ll explore effective strategies for calling and pursuing bulls during the rut.

Understanding the Rut

Before diving into hunting strategies, it’s crucial to understand the rut and its impact on moose behavior. The rut typically occurs in late September to early October, varying slightly depending on location and environmental factors. During this time, bull moose become hyper-focused on finding mates, leading them to exhibit increased aggression and vocalization.

Scouting

Like any successful hunt, preparation is key. Start by scouting potential hunting areas well in advance of the rut. Look for signs of moose activity such as fresh tracks, rubs on trees, and droppings. Pay attention to areas with dense vegetation near water sources, as moose prefer these areas for feeding and mating.

Choosing the Right Calls

Once you’ve identified a promising hunting spot, it’s time to choose your calls. There are several types of moose calls available, including cow calls, bull grunts, and antler rattling. Each call serves a different purpose and can be effective in different situations.

Cow calls mimic the sound of a female moose in estrus and can be highly effective at attracting curious bulls. Bull grunts, on the other hand, mimic the vocalizations of dominant bulls and can be used to challenge or provoke other bulls in the area. Antler rattling imitates the sound of two bulls clashing their antlers together, simulating a territorial dispute and drawing aggressive bulls into range.

Timing and Placement

Timing is critical when it comes to calling during the rut. Early mornings and late evenings are typically the most productive times to call, as moose are most active during these periods. Position yourself downwind of your intended calling area to prevent your scent from alerting nearby moose.

When placing calls, start with soft, subtle calls to gauge any nearby bull’s interest. If you receive a response, gradually increase the volume and intensity of your calls to draw the bull closer. Be patient and prepared to wait silently for extended periods between calls, as moose can take their time approaching.

Stalking and Pursuit

In some cases, calling may not immediately attract a bull within shooting range. If this happens, it may be necessary to switch tactics and pursue the bull on foot. Use the terrain to your advantage, moving quietly and staying low to avoid detection. Keep an eye out for fresh sign such as tracks or droppings, which can indicate the bull’s direction of travel.

When stalking a bull, always approach from downwind to minimize the risk of detection. Move slowly and deliberately, pausing frequently to listen for any sounds of movement. If the bull beds down, take advantage of the opportunity to close the distance quietly and carefully.

Conclusion

Hunting moose during the rut is a challenging yet rewarding endeavor that requires patience, skill, and a deep understanding of moose behavior. By scouting effectively, choosing the right calls, and employing sound stalking techniques, you can increase your chances of success and bag the trophy bull of your dreams. So gear up, hit the woods, and get ready for an unforgettable hunting experience amidst the sights and sounds of the rut.
