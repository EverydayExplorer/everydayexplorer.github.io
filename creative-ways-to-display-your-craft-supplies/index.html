Creative Ways to Display Your Craft Supplies


Are you a craft enthusiast looking for creative ways to display your craft supplies? Whether you are an avid sewer, knitter, or scrapbooker, having an organized and visually appealing workspace can make a world of difference. Not only will it inspire your creativity, but it will also make it easier for you to find and use your materials. In this blog post, we will explore some unique and innovative ways to display your craft supplies that will not only keep them organized but also double as eye-catching decor.

1. Mason jars: Mason jars have become quite popular in recent years, and for a good reason. They are not only versatile but also add a rustic charm to any space. Use mason jars to store and display your small craft supplies like buttons, beads, or even paintbrushes. You can attach them to a board or hang them from a wall-mounted organizer for easy access.

2. Pegboards: Pegboards are a classic choice when it comes to organizing craft supplies. Install a pegboard on your wall and use hooks or pegs to hang your tools, ribbons, and other supplies. You can customize the layout to fit your needs and even paint the pegboard to match your decor.

3. Shoe organizers: Don’t limit yourself to using shoe organizers solely for shoes. These versatile storage solutions can be repurposed to hold a variety of craft supplies. Hang a clear shoe organizer on the back of your craft room door and use the pockets to store yarn, fabric swatches, or colored pencils. The transparent pockets make it easy to see and grab what you need.

4. Spice racks: Spice racks are not just for the kitchen. Mount a spice rack on your wall and use it to store your small bottles of glitter, paint tubes, or spools of thread. The tiered design allows for easy access and adds a pop of color to your space.

5. Repurposed furniture: Give new life to old furniture by repurposing it as craft storage. A vintage dresser with drawers can be used to store your fabric stash, while an old bookshelf can hold your scrapbooking materials. Get creative with paint and finishes to match your craft room decor.

6. Wall-mounted baskets: Install a few wall-mounted wire or woven baskets in your craft room to hold larger items like knitting yarn, sewing patterns, or even books. This not only keeps everything off the floor but also adds visual interest to your walls.

7. Magnetic boards: If you have a collection of metal crafting tools like scissors or paper punches, consider using a magnetic board to display and organize them. Attach a metal sheet to the wall or repurpose an old baking sheet and use magnets to hold your tools in place. This not only keeps them within reach but also gives them a unique and decorative display.

8. Hanging rod: For fabric hoarders, a hanging rod can be a game-changer. Install a rod or even a curtain rod on your wall and hang your fabric rolls from it. This not only keeps them organized and wrinkle-free but also turns your fabric stash into a colorful art installation.

10. Glass jars and vases: Glass jars and vases can be both functional and decorative when used to store and display your craft supplies. Sort your buttons, beads, or colored pencils in glass jars and arrange them on a shelf or table. This not only keeps your supplies organized but also adds a pop of color to your space.

Remember, the key to displaying your craft supplies is to find a balance between functionality and aesthetics. Experiment with different storage solutions and get creative with how you arrange your materials. By incorporating these creative ideas into your craft room, you will have a visually appealing and organized space that will inspire your creativity and make crafting even more enjoyable. Happy organizing!
