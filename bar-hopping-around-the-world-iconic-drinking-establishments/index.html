Bar Hopping around the World: Iconic Drinking Establishments


Are you a lover of nightlife and seeking new experiences? If so, bar hopping around the world is an adventure that should be on your bucket list. From historic taverns to modern speakeasies, these iconic drinking establishments offer a taste of local culture, unique libations, and an unforgettable night out. In this blog post, we will take you on a virtual tour of some of the most renowned bars across the globe. So grab your passport and join us as we explore the world of bar hopping!

1. The Dead Rabbit – New York City, USA
Located in the heart of Manhattan, The Dead Rabbit is a must-visit for cocktail enthusiasts. This multi-award-winning establishment is known for its meticulously crafted drinks inspired by 19th-century recipes. The menu at The Dead Rabbit is divided into two floors – the Taproom and the Parlor – each offering a distinct ambiance and cocktail experience. Whether you’re looking for an expertly mixed classic cocktail or a specialty punch, The Dead Rabbit has you covered.

2. The American Bar – London, UK
Situated in the luxurious Savoy Hotel, The American Bar is one of the oldest cocktail bars in London. With a history dating back to the late 19th century, it has served as a favorite haunt for celebrities, royalty, and locals alike. The bar boasts an extensive menu that pays homage to classic cocktails while also introducing innovative concoctions. The elegant décor and live piano music create an atmosphere of sophistication, making it the perfect spot to unwind after a long day of exploring London.

3. Guinness Storehouse – Dublin, Ireland
No bar-hopping trip would be complete without a visit to the Guinness Storehouse in Dublin. This iconic establishment is not just a bar, but a cultural experience. Located in the St. James’s Gate Brewery, the Guinness Storehouse takes you on a journey through the history of Ireland’s most famous stout, Guinness. Enjoy a pint of the black stuff in the Gravity Bar, which offers panoramic views of Dublin’s skyline. Cheers to Irish hospitality and the world-renowned Guinness taste!

4. La Factoría – Old San Juan, Puerto Rico
Nestled in the vibrant streets of Old San Juan, La Factoría is a hidden gem known for its innovative craft cocktails. This speakeasy-style bar has gained international recognition for its dedication to mixology and atmosphere. As you explore the bar’s multiple rooms and hidden nooks, be prepared to have your taste buds delighted by unique tropical flavors infused with local ingredients. La Factoría is undoubtedly a place where history, culture, and divine cocktails seamlessly blend together.

5. The Baxter Inn – Sydney, Australia
Venture down an unassuming alley in Sydney, and you’ll discover The Baxter Inn, a whisky lover’s paradise. With over 800 varieties of whiskey lining the walls, this dimly lit bar exudes old-world charm. The knowledgeable bartenders will guide you through the extensive menu, ensuring you find the perfect dram to suit your taste. Don’t miss the opportunity to savor rare and limited-edition whiskies from around the world in this hidden gem of Sydney’s bar scene.

6. El Floridita – Havana, Cuba
If you’re a fan of daiquiris and the legendary Ernest Hemingway, a visit to El Floridita in Havana is a must. Known as the ‘Cradle of the Daiquiri,’ this historic bar has been a hotspot for locals and tourists alike since its opening in 1817. Picture yourself sitting at the same bar where Hemingway enjoyed his favorite cocktails. The lively atmosphere, live music, and masterful daiquiris make El Floridita a quintessential stop on your bar-hopping journey.

7. Sky Bar – Bangkok, Thailand
Perched on the 63rd floor of the State Tower, Sky Bar offers breathtaking panoramic views of Bangkok’s skyline. This open-air rooftop bar is famous for its innovative cocktails and extravagant atmosphere. Sip on a signature cocktail as you marvel at the city’s glittering lights from the rooftop’s famous ‘Hangovertini’ Sky Bridge. For a bar-hopping experience that blends dazzling views, world-class mixology, and a touch of glamour, Sky Bar is an absolute must-visit.

8. Oktoberfest – Munich, Germany
Bar hopping around the world would not be complete without experiencing Oktoberfest in Munich. This annual festival, celebrated every September, is the world’s largest beer festival. Join millions of beer enthusiasts from across the globe and indulge in traditional Bavarian beers, hearty food, and lively music. Raise your stein, embrace the joyous atmosphere, and join in the age-old tradition of prost-ing (toasting) with new friends in one of the many beer tents.

Conclusion

Embarking on a bar-hopping journey around the world allows you to experience the rich tapestry of cultures, tastes, and traditions that define each location. Whether you appreciate historic taverns, hidden speakeasies, or breathtaking rooftop bars, there is a plethora of iconic drinking establishments waiting to be discovered. From New York City to Bangkok, each bar offers a unique ambiance that reflects the local spirit and leaves visitors with lasting memories.

Remember to drink responsibly and savor the flavors and stories that each bar has to share. So, grab your friends, plan your itinerary, and get ready for an unforgettable adventure of bar hopping around the world. Cheers!
