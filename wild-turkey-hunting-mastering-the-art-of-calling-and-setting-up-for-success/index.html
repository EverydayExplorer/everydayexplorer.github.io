Wild Turkey Hunting: Mastering the Art of Calling and Setting Up for Success


Wild turkey hunting is a thrilling and challenging outdoor activity that requires a combination of skill, strategy, and patience. As one of the most popular forms of hunting in North America, mastering the art of calling and setting up for success is essential for any serious turkey hunter. In this blog post, we will explore the various techniques and strategies that can help you become a better turkey caller and increase your chances of a successful hunt. So gear up, grab your calls, and let’s dive into the world of wild turkey hunting!

Understanding Wild Turkey Behavior

Before we dive into the details of calling and setting up, it’s crucial to have a solid understanding of wild turkey behavior. Turkeys are highly social creatures, and their behavior revolves around establishing dominance, attracting mates, and avoiding predators. By understanding their natural tendencies, you can exploit their behavior to your advantage.

During the spring mating season, or the ‘spring strut,’ gobblers will actively seek out hens to breed. They will gobble, fluff their feathers, fan their tail, and strut around to attract females. This is an opportune time for hunters to use a combination of calls and decoys to lure them in.

The Art of Turkey Calling

Turkey calling is an essential skill that a hunter must master to successfully attract turkeys within shooting range. The primary calls used for wild turkey hunting are the yelp, cluck, purr, cackle, and gobble. Each call mimics a specific turkey sound and serves a different purpose.

  • Yelp: The yelp is the most basic and versatile call. It imitates a turkey’s general communication and is often used to locate birds or during casual encounters.
  • Cluck: The cluck is a short, sharp note that turkeys use to get the attention of others. It can be used to reassure turkeys or to provoke a response.
  • Purr: The purr is a soft, rolling call that indicates contentment. It is often used by hens to reassure young birds or when they are feeding.
  • Cackle: The cackle is a loud, excited call that turkeys make when they are excited or startled. It can be used to elicit a response from gobblers.
  • Gobble: The gobble is the classic call of a male turkey. It is a loud and distinctive call that is used to announce the gobbler’s presence and attract hens.

To master calling, practice using various calls and experiment with different pitches, rhythms, and durations. Using a quality turkey call, such as box calls, diaphragm calls, or slate calls, can greatly enhance your calling skills. Additionally, listening to recordings of real turkey sounds and observing live turkeys in their natural habitat can further refine your calling techniques.

Setting Up for Success

While calling is crucial, setting up in the right location is just as important. Here are some key factors to consider when selecting your hunting spot:

  1. Scouting: Before the hunting season begins, spend time scouting the area for turkey sign such as tracks, feathers, droppings, and scratchings. Look for roosting sites, feeding areas, and strutting zones. This will give you valuable insights into the turkeys’ movements and help you choose the best spot.

  2. Concealment: Turkeys have excellent eyesight, so it’s crucial to stay concealed. Wear full camouflage, including a facemask, gloves, and a hat. Use natural cover to break up your outline, such as bushes, trees, or ground blinds. Avoid making sudden movements that might alert the birds.

  3. Decoys: Deploying decoys can add a realistic touch to your setup and attract curious turkeys. Use hen decoys to mimic a potential mate for gobblers, and consider using a jake or tom decoy to provoke a competitive response. Place the decoys within shooting range and make sure they are visible to approaching turkeys.

  4. Calling Strategy: Depending on the situation, you can use a variety of calling strategies. For example, starting with soft yelps to locate turkeys and gradually increasing the intensity if there’s no response. Mimicking the sounds of a flock of turkeys can also create a sense of security, making gobblers more likely to approach.

  5. Patience and Persistence: Wild turkey hunting requires patience and persistence. It’s essential to be adaptable and try different setups and calling techniques if your initial attempts are unsuccessful. Remember, turkeys can be unpredictable, and persistence often pays off.

Conclusion

Wild turkey hunting is a challenging and exhilarating outdoor pursuit that demands skill, knowledge, and practice. By mastering the art of calling and setting up for success, you can increase your chances of a successful hunt and experience the thrill of interacting with these magnificent birds. Remember to practice your calls, study turkey behavior, scout your hunting area, and set up strategically. So get out there, embrace the challenge, and embark on your journey to becoming a successful wild turkey hunter. Happy hunting!
