The Dos and Don'ts of Heat Styling Your Hair


When it comes to heat styling your hair, there are certain dos and don’ts that you need to keep in mind to ensure that your locks stay healthy and fabulous. Heat styling tools like flat irons, curling wands, and blow dryers can be incredibly useful in achieving your desired hairstyle, but they can also wreak havoc on your hair if not used properly. In this blog post, we will discuss the dos and don’ts of heat styling your hair to help you achieve the perfect look without causing unnecessary damage.

DO: Use a heat protectant spray before styling your hair. Heat protectant sprays create a barrier between your hair and the heat, reducing the risk of damage. Make sure to apply the spray evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends where your hair is the most vulnerable.

DO: Invest in high-quality heat styling tools. Cheaper tools often lack proper temperature control, leading to uneven heat distribution and potential damage to your hair. Look for tools that have adjustable temperature settings and ceramic or tourmaline plates, as they provide more even heat and minimize damage.

DO: Start with clean, dry hair. Heat styling wet or damp hair can cause it to become frizzy and prone to breakage. Make sure your hair is completely dry before using any heat styling tools to avoid unnecessary damage.

DO: Use a lower heat setting. It’s tempting to crank up the heat to get quick results, but using excessive heat can cause severe damage to your hair. Start with a lower heat setting and gradually increase the temperature if needed. Remember, it’s better to be patient and take your time than to rush and risk damaging your hair.

DO: Use a heat protectant glove or tool. When using curling wands or flat irons, it’s essential to protect your hands from burns. Invest in a heat protectant glove or use a tool with a cool tip to avoid any accidents.

DON’T: Overuse heat styling tools. Heat styling every day can lead to dry, brittle hair. Try to limit the use of heat styling tools to a few times a week and give your hair a break in between to recover.

DON’T: Neglect your hair’s health. Heat styling can strip your hair of its natural moisture, making it dry and dull. Make sure to incorporate a deep conditioning treatment into your hair care routine to replenish moisture and keep your locks healthy.

DON’T: Use heat styling tools on damaged or chemically treated hair. If your hair is already damaged or treated, it is more susceptible to heat damage. Give your hair time to heal before subjecting it to heat styling.

DON’T: Leave heat styling tools on your hair for too long. Holding a heat styling tool in one place for an extended period can cause heat damage and even burn your hair. Keep the tool moving to distribute the heat evenly and minimize the risk of damage.

DON’T: Forget to use a heat protectant on a daily basis. Even if you’re not heat styling your hair, it’s a good idea to use a heat protectant spray before exposing your hair to other sources of heat, such as the sun or hot styling tools.

By following these dos and don’ts, you can enjoy the benefits of heat styling your hair while minimizing the risk of damage. Remember, healthy hair is beautiful hair, so take care of your locks and embrace your fabulous style with confidence!
