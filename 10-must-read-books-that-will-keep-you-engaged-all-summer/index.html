10 Must-Read Books That Will Keep You Engaged All Summer


Summer is here, which means it’s time to indulge in some good books! Whether you’re sitting by the pool or soaking up the sun on the beach, there’s nothing quite like getting lost in a great story. With so many books to choose from, it can be hard to decide where to start. That’s why we’ve compiled a list of ten must-read books that will keep you engaged all summer long.

1. ‘The Nightingale’ by Kristin Hannah
Set in France during World War II, ‘The Nightingale’ tells the story of two sisters who must navigate the harsh realities of war while also dealing with their own personal struggles. With its beautiful prose and compelling characters, this book is a real page-turner.

2. ‘The Silent Patient’ by Alex Michaelides
In this psychological thriller, a therapist becomes obsessed with uncovering the truth behind a patient’s mysterious silence. As he delves deeper into her past, he begins to uncover secrets that threaten to destroy them both.

3. ‘The Overstory’ by Richard Powers
Winner of the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction, ‘The Overstory’ is a sweeping epic that explores the complex relationship between humans and nature. From the ancient forests of the Pacific Northwest to the urban jungles of New York City, this book will leave you with a renewed appreciation for the natural world.

4. ‘Daisy Jones & The Six’ by Taylor Jenkins Reid
Set in the music scene of the 1970s, ‘Daisy Jones & The Six’ is a fictional oral history of a fictional band. With its vividly drawn characters and immersive storytelling style, this book will transport you back to a bygone era of rock and roll.

5. ‘The Immortalists’ by Chloe Benjamin
In this thought-provoking novel, four siblings visit a fortune teller who tells them the date of their deaths. As they each grapple with the knowledge of their own mortality, they must also confront the secrets and unspoken truths that have shaped their lives.

6. ‘Eleanor Oliphant is Completely Fine’ by Gail Honeyman
Quirky, funny, and heartwarming, ‘Eleanor Oliphant is Completely Fine’ tells the story of a socially awkward woman who learns to open up and connect with the world around her. This book will leave you with a smile on your face and a renewed appreciation for human connection.

7. ‘The Dutch House’ by Ann Patchett
Spanning five decades and two generations, ‘The Dutch House’ is a family saga that explores the bonds of brotherhood and the power of forgiveness. With its lush prose and richly drawn characters, this book is a true masterpiece.

8. ‘Educated’ by Tara Westover
A memoir of growing up in a strict and abusive household in rural Idaho, ‘Educated’ is a powerful testament to the human spirit and the power of education to transform lives. This book is a must-read for anyone who has ever felt like they don’t belong.

9. ‘The Water Dancer’ by Ta-Nehisi Coates
In this masterful work of historical fiction, Ta-Nehisi Coates imagines an alternate history of the Underground Railroad. With its lyrical prose and unforgettable characters, ‘The Water Dancer’ will transport you to a world of magic and wonder.

10. ‘Normal People’ by Sally Rooney
A captivating love story set in contemporary Ireland, ‘Normal People’ explores the complexities of modern relationships and the power dynamics that shape them. With its sharp wit and insightful observations, this book is a true must-read.

In conclusion, these ten books are sure to keep you engaged all summer long. From historical fiction to sweeping family sagas to psychological thrillers, there’s something for every reader on this list. So grab a book, find a comfortable spot, and prepare to get lost in a great story! Happy reading!
