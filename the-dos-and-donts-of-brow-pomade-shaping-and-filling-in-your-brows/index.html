The Dos and Don'ts of Brow Pomade: Shaping and Filling in Your Brows


When it comes to achieving flawless, well-defined eyebrows, brow pomade is a game-changer. This versatile product can help you shape, fill in, and enhance your brows for a stunning, polished look. However, using brow pomade requires finesse and attention to detail. To help you master the art of brow pomade application, we’ve put together a comprehensive guide to the dos and don’ts of shaping and filling in your brows.

The Dos:

  1. Choose the Right Shade: Selecting the perfect shade of brow pomade is crucial for achieving natural-looking results. Opt for a shade that matches your natural eyebrow color or is one shade lighter for a softer effect.

  2. Prep Your Brows: Before applying brow pomade, ensure that your eyebrows are clean and well-groomed. Use a spoolie brush to comb through your brows, removing any excess oils or product buildup.

  3. Use a Light Hand: Less is more when it comes to brow pomade. Start with a small amount of product on your angled brow brush and gradually build up the intensity as needed. A light hand will help you achieve a natural finish.

  4. Outline Your Brows: Begin by outlining the bottom edge of your eyebrows with the pomade, following the natural shape of your brows. Use light, feathery strokes for a soft, defined look.

  5. Fill in Sparce Areas: After outlining, use small, upward strokes to fill in any sparse areas in your brows. Focus on creating natural-looking hair-like strokes to mimic the appearance of real hair.

  6. Blend for Seamless Results: Once you’ve filled in your brows, use a clean spoolie brush to blend the pomade for a seamless finish. This step will help soften any harsh lines and create a polished, natural look.

  7. Set with Brow Gel: To lock your brows in place and add extra definition, finish off your look by applying a clear or tinted brow gel. This will help your brows stay put throughout the day.

The Don’ts:

  1. Avoid Overloading the Brush: When using brow pomade, resist the temptation to load up your brush with product. Excess pomade can result in overly harsh, unnatural-looking brows.

  2. Say No to Harsh Lines: Steer clear of creating harsh, blocky lines when outlining your eyebrows. Instead, opt for soft, precise strokes to maintain a natural appearance.

  3. Don’t Neglect Blending: Failing to blend your brow pomade can leave you with a harsh, unblended finish. Take the time to blend the product for a seamless, professional-looking result.

  4. Don’t Forget to Set Your Brows: Skipping the step of setting your brows with gel can lead to unruly, unkempt eyebrows. Setting gel helps hold your brows in place and adds the perfect finishing touch to your look.

  5. Avoid Using the Wrong Shade: Using a brow pomade that is too dark or too light for your natural brow color can result in an unnatural, unflattering appearance. Always opt for a shade that complements your natural eyebrows.

By following these dos and don’ts of using brow pomade to shape and fill in your brows, you can achieve stunning, well-defined eyebrows that enhance your overall look. With practice and attention to detail, you’ll be able to master the art of brow pomade application and enjoy beautifully sculpted brows every day.
