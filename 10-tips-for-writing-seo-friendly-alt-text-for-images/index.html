10 Tips for Writing SEO-Friendly Alt Text for Images


Alt text, also known as alternative text, is an important element of web design that often gets overlooked. However, when used effectively, alt text can enhance both the user experience and the search engine optimization (SEO) of a website. In this blog post, we’ll explore ten tips for writing SEO-friendly alt text for images, helping you to optimize your website and improve its visibility on search engine result pages (SERPs).

1. Be Descriptive: Alt text should accurately describe the content of the image. Use concise and specific language that conveys the image’s purpose or context. Avoid generic phrases or keywords that don’t add value to the description.

2. Include Relevant Keywords: Incorporating relevant keywords into your alt text can help search engines understand the context of the image and improve its ranking. However, avoid keyword stuffing, as it can be penalized by search engines.

3. Keep It Concise: Alt text should be brief and to the point. Ideally, aim for a description that is under 125 characters. This ensures that the alt text doesn’t overshadow the main content on the page.

4. Prioritize User Experience: While alt text is primarily used for SEO purposes, it’s essential to remember that it also serves visually impaired users who rely on screen readers. Ensure that your alt text provides a meaningful description that enhances the user experience.

5. Avoid Repetition: If multiple images on a page serve the same purpose, avoid duplicating alt text. Instead, differentiate the alt text for each image by providing unique and relevant descriptions.

6. Be Mindful of Context: Consider the context in which the image appears and provide alt text that complements the surrounding content. This helps search engines understand the relevance of the image to the overall page.

7. Don’t Rely Solely on File Names: While it’s tempting to use the image file name as alt text, this might not always provide a meaningful description. Instead, take the time to craft descriptive alt text that accurately reflects the image’s content.

8. Include Important Details: If the image contains important information, such as a chart or diagram, ensure that your alt text conveys this information accurately. This ensures that visually impaired users can comprehend the content.

9. Test Your Alt Text: Regularly review and test your alt text to ensure it is still relevant and effective. As your website evolves, so should your alt text to maintain its SEO value.

10. Consult Accessibility Guidelines: Familiarize yourself with accessibility guidelines, such as the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), to ensure that your alt text meets the requirements for accessibility.

By following these ten tips for writing SEO-friendly alt text for images, you’ll enhance the user experience and improve your website’s visibility on search engine result pages. Remember, alt text is not just about SEO; it’s about creating an inclusive and accessible web experience for all users. So, take the time to craft thoughtful and descriptive alt text that adds value to your website.
