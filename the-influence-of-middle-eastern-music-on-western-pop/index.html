The Influence of Middle Eastern Music on Western Pop


Middle Eastern music has a rich and diverse history that dates back thousands of years. It not only has had an impact on music within the Middle East but has also influenced Western pop music in significant ways. From the use of Middle Eastern instruments to the incorporation of Middle Eastern melodies and rhythms, the influence of Middle Eastern music on Western pop is undeniable.

One of the most notable ways in which Middle Eastern music has influenced Western pop is through the use of instruments. One of the most recognizable instruments from the Middle East is the oud, a short-necked lute that is commonly used in Arabic music. Many Western pop songs, such as ‘Kiss’ by Prince and ‘Black or White’ by Michael Jackson, have used the sound of the oud to create a Middle Eastern-inspired sound.

Other Middle Eastern instruments that have made an appearance in Western pop music include the qanun, a type of zither, and the darbuka, a goblet-shaped drum. The use of these instruments adds a unique flavor to Western pop music and helps to create a fusion between Middle Eastern and Western sounds.

Beyond the use of instruments, Middle Eastern music has also influenced Western pop through its use of melodies and rhythms. Middle Eastern melodies are characterized by their use of quarter tones, which are notes that sit between the notes of a traditional Western scale. These quarter tones give Middle Eastern music a distinct sound and have been used in Western pop songs to create a more exotic and foreign sound.

Similarly, Middle Eastern rhythms have also influenced Western pop music. The use of complex, polyrhythmic patterns in Middle Eastern music has been adopted by Western pop musicians to add a sense of depth and complexity to their music. For example, the opening of Madonna’s hit song ‘La Isla Bonita’ features a clave rhythm that is commonly used in Latin and Middle Eastern music.

Finally, Middle Eastern music has also influenced Western pop by inspiring musicians to incorporate themes and motifs from Middle Eastern culture into their music. The popularity of belly dancing and other Middle Eastern dance forms in Western society has led to a fascination with Middle Eastern culture and has resulted in many pop songs that feature lyrics and themes inspired by the region.

Ultimately, the influence of Middle Eastern music on Western pop has been a positive one. By incorporating Middle Eastern melodies, rhythms, and instruments into their music, pop musicians have been able to create new and exciting sounds that capture the imagination of listeners. The fusion of Middle Eastern and Western sounds has also helped to bridge the gap between different cultures and has brought a greater appreciation for Middle Eastern music to Western audiences.

In conclusion, the influence of Middle Eastern music on Western pop is an important part of the history of both genres. The use of Middle Eastern instruments, melodies, rhythms, and themes has helped to create a fusion between Middle Eastern and Western sounds that continues to captivate listeners to this day. As Western society becomes increasingly globalized, it is likely that this fusion will continue to evolve and inspire new generations of musicians.
