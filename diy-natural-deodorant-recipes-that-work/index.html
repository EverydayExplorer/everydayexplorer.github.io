DIY Natural Deodorant Recipes That Work


Are you tired of using commercial deodorants that are filled with chemicals and artificial fragrances? If so, it’s time to try making your own natural deodorant at home! DIY natural deodorants are not only effective at keeping you fresh and odor-free, but they are also better for your health and the environment. In this blog post, we will share some easy and effective DIY natural deodorant recipes that you can try. Say goodbye to harsh chemicals and hello to natural freshness!

Why Choose DIY Natural Deodorants?

Commercial deodorants often contain harmful ingredients such as aluminum, parabens, and synthetic fragrances. These ingredients have been linked to various health concerns, including breast cancer and hormone disruption By making your own natural deodorant, you have full control over the ingredients you use, ensuring that you are not exposing yourself to potentially harmful substances.

Recipe 1: Baking Soda and Coconut Oil Deodorant

Ingredients: - 1/4 cup baking soda - 1/4 cup arrowroot powder or cornstarch - 4 tablespoons coconut oil - 10-15 drops of essential oil (such as lavender or tea tree oil) for fragrance (optional)

Instructions: 1. In a bowl, mix the baking soda and arrowroot powder or cornstarch. 2. Add the coconut oil and essential oil (if using) to the dry mixture. 3. Stir well until all the ingredients are combined and form a smooth paste. 4. Transfer the mixture to a clean container or an empty deodorant stick. 5. Allow the deodorant to solidify at room temperature or in the refrigerator for a few hours. 6. Apply a small amount of the homemade deodorant to your underarms as needed.

Recipe 2: Shea Butter and Beeswax Deodorant

Ingredients: - 2 tablespoons shea butter - 2 tablespoons beeswax pellets - 2 tablespoons coconut oil - 2 tablespoons arrowroot powder or cornstarch - 10-15 drops of essential oil (such as lemon or eucalyptus) for fragrance (optional)

Instructions: 1. In a double boiler or a heat-safe bowl placed over a pot of simmering water, melt the shea butter, beeswax pellets, and coconut oil. 2. Once melted, remove from heat and stir in the arrowroot powder or cornstarch. 3. Add the essential oil (if using) and mix well. 4. Pour the mixture into a clean container or an empty deodorant stick. 5. Allow the deodorant to cool and solidify completely before use. 6. Apply a small amount of the homemade deodorant to your underarms as needed.

Recipe 3: Bentonite Clay and Witch Hazel Deodorant

Ingredients: - 1/4 cup bentonite clay - 1/4 cup witch hazel - 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar - 10-15 drops of essential oil (such as lavender or peppermint) for fragrance (optional)

Instructions: 1. In a bowl, mix the bentonite clay, witch hazel, and apple cider vinegar until well combined. 2. Add the essential oil (if using) and stir again. 3. Transfer the mixture to a clean container or an empty deodorant stick. 4. Allow the deodorant to thicken and set for a few hours. 5. Apply a small amount of the homemade deodorant to your underarms as needed.

Conclusion

Making your own DIY natural deodorant is a simple and rewarding process. Not only do these recipes provide effective odor protection, but they also allow you to customize the fragrance and avoid harmful chemicals found in commercial deodorants. Give these recipes a try and enjoy the benefits of natural freshness!

Remember, it’s important to note that everyone’s body chemistry is different, and what works for one person may not work for another. It may take some trial and error to find the perfect natural deodorant recipe that works best for you.
