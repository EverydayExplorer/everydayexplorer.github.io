Discovering the Hidden Gems of Street Food Around the Globe


As you travel around the world, you discover the local cuisine that is unique to each region. One of the best ways to experience the local flavors is through street food. Street food is not just a mode of sustenance for millions of people globally but also an essential part of their culture. Often, the best food can be found in small street food carts or pop-up stands that sell the most delicious and authentic dishes you won’t find anywhere else. If you’re passionate about food and enjoy discovering new culinary gems, you’ll know that street food is the way to go.

Many travelers who have explored the streets of cities worldwide know that hidden gems can easily be found when it comes to street food. Even though street food is becoming more mainstream, it still remains an off-the-beaten-path experience to be savored in many places. So, here are some hidden street food gems from around the globe that you must try on your food escapade.

1. Jhal Muri, India

Street food in India is a diverse array of flavors and aromas that can easily entice any foodie. However, one not-to-be-missed dish is Jhal Muri, which originated from Kolkata, West Bengal. It is a savoury street food snack made with crispy puffed rice, boiled potatoes, onions, peanuts, spices and a tangy tamarind sauce that’s mixed and tossed together. You’ll find street vendors selling this local delicacy in almost every corner of Kolkata.

2. Banh Mi, Vietnam

Banh Mi is a Vietnamese sandwich that has rapidly gained popularity worldwide. What started as a localized delicacy by street vendors in Vietnam has now become a global favorite. The Banh Mi comprises a crusty baguette with a crispy outer layer and a creamy inner texture that is stuffed with a delicious blend of grilled meat (pork or chicken), fresh vegetables, and a variety of sauces including sweet chilli, coriander, and Hoisin sauce. You’ll find beautiful street stalls selling this authentic dish throughout Vietnam.

3. Apam Balik, Malaysia

If you are a fan of sweet treats, then Apam Balik from Malaysia is the one for you. It is a local Malaysian dessert that is made from rice flour batter and cooked in a mini wok, then filled with sweet corn, peanuts, sugar, and butter before being folded over and served hot. This simple yet delicious dessert is a must-taste in Malaysia.

4. Gyro, Greece

Gyro is a hot sandwich that originates from Greece and is made with thinly sliced meat that is typically pork, chicken, or lamb. The meat is served on a warm pita bread and topped with tomatoes, onion, and a generous dollop of tangy tzatziki sauce. If you find yourself exploring the streets of Athens or any of the Greek islands, then be sure to make a stop at one of the street vendors who serve up the best Gyros in town.

5. Pirozhki, Russia

Pirozhki is a Russian savory pastry that is stuffed with a variety of fillings such as meat, mushroom, cabbage, potato, cheese, or even sweets like jam. It is perfect for a quick snack or a lazy winter lunch. Pirozhki puffs are a common street food in Russia and can be easily obtained from the street stands in Moscow or any other city.

Conclusion

Street food is a rich source of cultural heritage and unique flavors. As a traveler, it is the best way to experience a country’s local cuisine. Traveling and exploring street foods from around the world can reveal a whole new world of flavors and unique culinary experiences. From the bustling city of Mumbai to the vibrant streets of Vietnam, the street food culture is a must-explore for all foodie travelers. So, if you happen to be in any of these countries, be sure to discover the hidden gems of street food and indulge yourself in the unique flavors that these cuisines bring. Happy eating!
