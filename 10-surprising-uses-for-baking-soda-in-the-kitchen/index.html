10 Surprising Uses for Baking Soda in the Kitchen


Baking soda, also known as sodium bicarbonate, is a versatile ingredient that can be found in every kitchen. Often used as a leavening agent in baking, baking soda actually has numerous surprising uses beyond the oven. In this blog post, we will explore 10 surprising uses for baking soda in the kitchen that will not only save you time and money, but also help you maintain a clean and healthy cooking environment.

1. Natural Fruit and Vegetable Wash
Keeping our produce clean and free from harmful chemicals is a top priority when it comes to cooking healthy meals. Baking soda can be used as a natural fruit and vegetable wash to remove pesticides and other contaminants. Simply mix one tablespoon of baking soda with two cups of water, soak your fruits and vegetables for a few minutes, then rinse thoroughly. This simple and affordable solution will ensure that your produce is ready to be enjoyed.

2. Odor Neutralizer
One of the most well-known uses for baking soda is its ability to absorb odors. This makes it a great candidate for use in the kitchen, where strong and unpleasant smells can linger. To naturally eliminate odors from your refrigerator, sprinkle baking soda on a damp cloth and wipe down the surfaces. You can also leave an open box of baking soda in the back of your fridge to continuously absorb any unwanted odors.

3. Grease and Stain Remover
Dealing with stubborn grease stains on your pots, pans, or countertops? Baking soda can come to the rescue! Mix baking soda with a small amount of water to make a paste, apply it to the stained surface, and let it sit for a few minutes. Then, scrub the area with a sponge or a brush and rinse thoroughly. The powerful properties of baking soda will help break down the grease and lift the stains, leaving your kitchen sparkling clean.

4. Coffee and Tea Stain Remover
Are your mugs and teapots stained from years of daily use? Simply sprinkle baking soda onto a damp cloth or sponge and gently scrub the stains away. Baking soda’s mild abrasiveness will help remove the stubborn coffee or tea stains without scratching the surface.

5. Silverware Polish
If your silverware has lost its shine, baking soda can bring it back to life. Create a paste by mixing three parts baking soda with one part water, then apply it to your silverware using a cloth or sponge. Gently rub the paste onto the tarnished surfaces, rinse thoroughly, and dry with a soft cloth. Your silverware will regain its sparkle, and you’ll be ready to impress your guests with a beautifully set table.

6. Rust Remover
Is your kitchen plagued by rusty knives or other metal utensils? Baking soda is an excellent natural rust remover. Mix baking soda with a few drops of water to create a paste, apply it to the rusted areas, and let it sit for a few hours. Afterward, scrub the rust away with a brush or a sponge, rinse well, and pat dry. Your metal kitchenware will look as good as new!

7. Oven Cleaner
Cleaning the oven is often a dreaded chore, but baking soda can make it easier and more efficient. Sprinkle baking soda on the bottom of your oven, then spray a mixture of water and vinegar on top to create a paste. Let the paste sit overnight, then wipe it away with a damp cloth. The mixture will help loosen dirt and grease, allowing you to easily remove them the next day.

8. Disposal Cleaner
If your kitchen sink disposal has started to develop an unpleasant odor, baking soda can help freshen it up. Pour half a cup of baking soda down the drain, followed by a cup of vinegar. Let the mixture foam for a few minutes, then turn on the disposal and run hot water down the drain. The combination of baking soda and vinegar will remove any buildup and leave your disposal smelling clean.

9. Cutting Board Deodorizer
Wooden cutting boards can absorb odors from strong-smelling ingredients, such as garlic or onions. To eliminate these odors, sprinkle baking soda over your cutting board and let it sit for a few minutes. Afterwards, scrub the board with a sponge or a brush and rinse thoroughly. Your cutting board will be fresh and odor-free, ready for your next meal preparation.

10. Drain Cleaner
Clogged drains can be a nuisance, but baking soda can help keep them clear and flowing smoothly. Pour half a cup of baking soda down the drain, followed by half a cup of vinegar. Let the mixture sit for a few minutes, then flush it with hot water. This combination of baking soda and vinegar will help break down any blockages and keep your drains clean and odor-free.

In conclusion, baking soda is not just for baking delicious cakes and cookies. Its surprising uses in the kitchen make it a must-have ingredient for every household. From cleaning and deodorizing to freshening up drain, baking soda is a versatile and affordable solution to many common kitchen problems. So next time you’re in the kitchen, don’t forget to harness the power of baking soda and make your cooking experience even better.
