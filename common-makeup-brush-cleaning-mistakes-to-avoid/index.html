Common Makeup Brush Cleaning Mistakes to Avoid


When it comes to makeup application, the brushes you use can be just as important as the products themselves. A high-quality set of brushes can help you create smooth, flawless looks, but if they’re not cleaned properly, they can harbor bacteria, damage your skin, and even affect the quality of your makeup. Unfortunately, many people make common mistakes when cleaning their makeup brushes, leading to less-than-ideal results. Here are some of the most frequent errors and tips on how to avoid them.

Using the Wrong Cleaning Products

One of the most common mistakes is using products that aren’t designed for makeup brushes. Household cleaners, harsh soaps, and even some shampoos can be too abrasive and strip the brushes of their natural softness. Instead, opt for gentle brush cleaners or mild baby shampoos. These are formulated to clean without causing damage or leaving behind residues.

Not Cleaning Often Enough

Another typical error is not cleaning brushes frequently enough. If you’re a daily makeup user, your brushes can quickly accumulate product, oil, and bacteria. This buildup can lead to skin irritations and breakouts. Ideally, you should clean brushes at least once a week to keep them fresh and reduce the risk of skin problems.

Submerging Brushes in Water

It might seem logical to soak your brushes to get them thoroughly clean, but this can actually damage them. When you submerge brushes in water, especially hot water, it can weaken the glue that holds the bristles in place, causing them to fall out. Instead, focus on cleaning the bristles without soaking the base or ferrule (the metal part that holds the bristles together).

Applying Too Much Pressure

Scrubbing brushes too hard can lead to bristle breakage and a shorter lifespan for your tools. Gentle motions are key when cleaning makeup brushes. Use a light touch and circular motions to avoid damaging the bristles. If your brushes are particularly dirty, it’s better to clean them multiple times with gentle strokes rather than scrubbing them aggressively.

Not Drying Brushes Properly

Improper drying can lead to mold and mildew growth. After cleaning, never leave brushes lying flat on a surface to dry, as water can seep into the ferrule and cause damage. Instead, hang them upside down or at an angle to allow water to drain away from the bristles and ferrule. This helps prevent the glue from weakening and keeps the bristles in good condition.

Using Excessive Heat

While it might be tempting to use a hairdryer to speed up the drying process, excessive heat can be damaging. High heat can warp the bristles and damage the glue. Allow brushes to air dry naturally, ideally in a well-ventilated area. If you must use a hairdryer, set it to a cool or low heat setting and keep it at a safe distance from the brushes.

Ignoring the Brush Shape

Each makeup brush has a specific shape and design, and cleaning them improperly can cause them to lose their shape. Avoid excessive twisting or pulling on the bristles. After cleaning, gently reshape the brush heads to their original form before drying to ensure they retain their effectiveness during makeup application.

Neglecting the Handle

While the bristles are the most crucial part of a makeup brush, don’t forget about the handle. Handles can accumulate dirt, oil, and product residue, leading to a grimy feeling. Wipe down the handles with a damp cloth or antibacterial wipe to keep them clean and free from bacteria.

Using Dirty Hands During Cleaning

If your hands are dirty or oily, you’re not helping the situation by touching clean brushes. Always wash your hands thoroughly before cleaning your brushes to avoid transferring dirt and bacteria back onto them.

Not Letting Brushes Dry Completely

It can be tempting to use your brushes as soon as they’re slightly dry, but not letting them dry completely can lead to a damp, musty smell and potential mold growth. Make sure your brushes are completely dry before using or storing them to avoid these issues.

By avoiding these common makeup brush cleaning mistakes, you’ll ensure that your brushes last longer, your makeup looks better, and your skin stays healthy. Regular cleaning and proper care are key to maintaining high-quality makeup brushes and achieving the best results with your makeup application.
