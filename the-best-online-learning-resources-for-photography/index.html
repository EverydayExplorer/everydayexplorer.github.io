The Best Online Learning Resources for Photography


Are you ready to take your photography skills to the next level? Whether you’re a beginner looking to master the basics or an experienced photographer aiming to refine your craft, the world of online learning resources is here to guide you through the intricate art of photography. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best online platforms, courses, and resources that cater to photographers of all levels. From technical know-how to artistic composition, there’s something for everyone in the digital realm of photography education.

Online learning has revolutionized the way we acquire new skills, and photography is no exception. With a multitude of options available, it can be overwhelming to navigate the sea of resources. Fear not, as we’ve meticulously curated a list of the crème de la crème in the world of online photography education to ease your journey.

  1. Udemy: Known for its vast library of courses, Udemy offers a plethora of photography classes for beginners, enthusiasts, and professionals alike. From DSLR basics to advanced editing techniques, Udemy’s diverse range of courses caters to various skill levels and interests.

  2. CreativeLive: Renowned for its live workshops and tutorials, CreativeLive provides a unique learning experience for photographers. With courses taught by industry experts, CreativeLive offers in-depth insights into various aspects of photography, including lighting, composition, and post-processing.

  3. Lynda.com (now LinkedIn Learning): Boasting an extensive collection of photography tutorials, Lynda.com provides high-quality, professionally produced content. Whether you’re looking to master Adobe Photoshop or understand the principles of portrait photography, Lynda.com has you covered.

  4. KelbyOne: For those seeking a community-driven approach to learning, KelbyOne offers an array of photography classes, mentorship, and exclusive member benefits. With a focus on creativity and technical expertise, KelbyOne’s resources cater to photographers looking for a supportive and collaborative learning environment.

  5. Phlearn: Specializing in Photoshop and Lightroom tutorials, Phlearn is a go-to platform for photographers seeking to enhance their post-processing skills. With a mix of free and premium content, Phlearn offers comprehensive tutorials that cover everything from retouching to compositing.

  6. YouTube: A treasure trove of free content, YouTube hosts an abundance of photography tutorials, gear reviews, and behind-the-scenes insights from established photographers. Whether you’re exploring film photography or delving into the world of drone photography, YouTube offers a wealth of knowledge at your fingertips.

  7. Magnum Learn: For those interested in documentary and photojournalism, Magnum Learn provides unparalleled access to the expertise of Magnum Photos’ renowned photographers. From visual storytelling to documentary ethics, Magnum Learn offers masterclasses and workshops for aspiring photojournalists.

  8. Skillshare: With its focus on creativity and innovation, Skillshare offers a diverse range of photography classes taught by industry professionals. From smartphone photography to niche genres like food styling, Skillshare’s platform encourages experimentation and artistic exploration.

  9. The Art of Photography Podcast: Hosted by Ted Forbes, this podcast delves into the philosophical and artistic aspects of photography. Forbes’ insightful discussions on photography history, theory, and contemporary practices provide a thought-provoking perspective on the art form.

  10. National Geographic Photography Courses: For nature and wildlife enthusiasts, National Geographic offers photography courses led by professional photographers. With an emphasis on conservation and storytelling, these courses provide a unique opportunity to learn from the best in the field.

In conclusion, the digital landscape of photography education offers a wealth of opportunities for aspiring and seasoned photographers alike. Whether you’re seeking technical expertise, artistic inspiration, or community engagement, the best online learning resources for photography cater to a diverse range of interests and skill levels. So, embark on your photographic journey with confidence, armed with the knowledge that the world of online learning is at your fingertips, ready to elevate your craft to new heights.
