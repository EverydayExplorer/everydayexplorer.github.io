Mastering Topwater Fishing: Enticing Strikes from Below


Topwater fishing is known to be one of the most exhilarating forms of angling. Watching a fish explode on your lure can be nothing short of thrilling. However, mastering topwater fishing can take a lot of practice and patience. In this blog, we will discuss tips and techniques to help you become a topwater fishing pro and entice those strikes from below.

Choose the Right Lure

Choosing the right lure can make or break your topwater fishing experience. One of the most popular and effective topwater lures is the popper. Poppers create commotion on the surface that mimics a struggling baitfish, making them an irresistible target for predatory fish. Another popular topwater lure is a surface walker, which is designed to zig-zag across the surface, mimicking a fleeing baitfish. Whatever lure you choose, make sure it creates enough commotion to draw fish to the surface.

Match the Hatch

Matching the hatch is crucial to success in any form of fishing. Pay attention to the type of baitfish in the area you are fishing and choose a lure that mimics their size, shape, and color. If you are not sure what baitfish are present, observe the surface of the water for any activity or ask local anglers for advice.

Time of Day Matters

The time of day you choose to fish can make a difference in your topwater success. Early morning and late evening are prime times for topwater fishing. During these times, the sun is low, creating longer shadows and making it easier for predators to ambush prey from below. However, this doesn’t mean topwater fishing can’t be successful during the day. You just need to adjust your lure selection and presentation to the conditions at hand.

Master Your Presentation

Mastering your presentation is crucial to topwater fishing success. Vary your retrieve speed, twitch your lure, and pause it periodically to mimic the movements of a wounded baitfish. A common mistake anglers make is retrieving their lure too fast. Take your time and let your lure sit to entice any hesitant fish.

Slow and Steady Wins the Race

Patience is key in topwater fishing. Let your lure sit for a few seconds between retrieves to give the fish ample time to find and strike it. If you are not getting any bites, slow your retrieve and vary your presentation until you find what works.

Incorporate Sound

Adding sound to your lure can entice fish to strike. Many topwater lures come equipped with rattles, but you can also add a rattle or two to your lure to create more commotion.

If at First You Don’t Succeed, Try, Try Again

It’s important to remember that topwater fishing is not always easy. Don’t get discouraged if you don’t catch anything. Keep working on your technique, adjust your presentation and lur choice, and keep trying. The more you practice, the better you will become.

In conclusion, mastering topwater fishing takes practice, patience, and technique. Choosing the right lure, matching the hatch, recognizing the best time of day, mastering your presentation, and incorporating sound can all improve your chances of enticing strikes from below. But, remember, don’t give up if you don’t catch anything right away. Keep practicing and have fun with it!
