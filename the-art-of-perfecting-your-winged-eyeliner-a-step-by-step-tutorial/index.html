The Art of Perfecting Your Winged Eyeliner: A Step-by-Step Tutorial


If you’re someone who loves experimenting with makeup, but always finds yourself struggling with winged eyeliner, then this blog post is for you. The winged eyeliner look is a classic, timeless beauty trend that can instantly elevate your makeup game. However, many people find it challenging to achieve the perfect winged eyeliner look. In this step-by-step tutorial, we will break down the process and share some tips and tricks to help you master the art of winged eyeliner.

Step 1: Prepare Your Eyes

Before you start applying your winged eyeliner, it’s essential to prepare your eyes. Begin by cleansing your eyelids and removing any traces of makeup or oil. This step will ensure that your eyeliner glides on smoothly and lasts longer. If you have oily eyelids, you can also apply an eyeshadow primer to create a great base for your eyeliner.

Step 2: Choose the Right Eyeliner Product

There are various types of eyeliners available in the market, including liquid, gel, and pencil. Each type has its own advantages and disadvantages, so it’s essential to choose the one that you’re most comfortable using. For a precise and sharp winged eyeliner look, liquid eyeliners are usually the best choice. However, if you’re new to winged eyeliner or prefer a softer look, you can start with a pencil or gel eyeliner.

Step 3: Begin with a Fine Line

Start by creating a thin line along your upper lash line. This initial line will serve as a guide for your winged eyeliner shape. Take your time and use short, gentle strokes to ensure a smooth and even line. If you’re using a liquid eyeliner, it’s helpful to shake the bottle well before applying it to ensure consistent pigmentation.

Step 4: Create the Wing

Now, it’s time to create the wing. The wing is the most defining feature of the winged eyeliner look. To create the perfect wing, imagine an imaginary line extending from the corner of your eye towards the end of your eyebrow. Use a small angled brush or the tip of your eyeliner for precision, and flick it upwards to create the wing shape. Adjust the length and angle of the wing according to your preferences.

Step 5: Connect the Wing to the Eyelid

Once you have created the wing, connect it to the rest of your eyeliner by drawing a thin line from the tip of the wing towards the center of your lash line. Be careful while creating this line, as it needs to be thin and sharp to maintain the winged eyeliner effect.

Step 6: Fill in the Gaps

After you have connected the wing to your lash line, it’s time to fill in any gaps and create a seamless line. You can do this by carefully filling in the space between the wing and your lash line, using short strokes. Make sure to maintain the thickness and consistency of the line throughout.

Step 7: Clean-Up & Perfect

Once you have completed both eyes, take a clean cotton swab or a small angled brush dipped in makeup remover to clean up any mistakes or smudges. This step is crucial in achieving a clean and professional-looking winged eyeliner.

Bonus Tips & Tricks:

  • Practice makes perfect: Don’t be discouraged if you don’t achieve the perfect winged eyeliner look on your first try. Like any skill, mastering winged eyeliner takes practice. Keep trying, and you’ll get better each time.

  • Invest in good quality brushes: Having the right tools can make a significant difference in the application of your winged eyeliner. Invest in good quality brushes or applicators to ensure precise and effortless application.

  • Experiment with different styles: Winged eyeliner is a versatile look that can be customized to suit your preferences. Feel free to experiment with the length, thickness, and angle of your wing to find a style that flatters your eye shape best.

  • Use tape or a stencil: If you’re finding it challenging to create a straight wing, you can use tape or a stencil as a guide. Place the tape or stencil at the desired angle, apply your eyeliner, and then remove it for a clean winged eyeliner look.

  • Be patient and have a steady hand: Winged eyeliner requires patience and a steady hand. Take your time, relax, and try to keep your hand steady while applying the liner.

In conclusion, perfecting your winged eyeliner takes time, practice, and patience. With the step-by-step tutorial and tips and tricks mentioned above, you’ll be well on your way to achieving the flawless winged eyeliner look. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don’t be discouraged if it takes a few tries to get it right. Keep experimenting, have fun, and embrace your inner eyeliner artist!
