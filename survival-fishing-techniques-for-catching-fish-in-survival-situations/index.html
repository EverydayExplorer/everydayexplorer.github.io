Survival Fishing: Techniques for Catching Fish in Survival Situations


When faced with a survival situation in the wilderness, securing a reliable source of food is paramount to ensuring your survival. While foraging for edible plants and hunting small game are viable options, one often overlooked method of procuring sustenance is survival fishing. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore various techniques and strategies for catching fish in survival situations, equipping you with the knowledge and skills necessary to thrive in the wild.

Understanding Survival Fishing

Before diving into specific techniques, it’s essential to understand the principles behind survival fishing. Fish are a valuable source of protein and essential nutrients, making them an ideal target for sustenance in survival scenarios. However, successfully catching fish requires more than just luck – it demands patience, ingenuity, and a solid understanding of fish behavior and habitat.

Improvised Fishing Gear

In survival situations, you may not have access to conventional fishing equipment. However, with a bit of creativity and resourcefulness, you can fashion improvised fishing gear from materials found in your environment.

Handlines and Fishing Poles:

Handlines and makeshift fishing poles can be crafted from sturdy branches or saplings. Simply attach a length of fishing line or strong cord to the end of the pole, along with a hook and bait, and you’re ready to fish. Remember to fashion a simple reel using a stick or your hand to control the line’s release and retrieval.

Trotlines and Gorges:

Trotlines and gorges are passive fishing techniques that allow you to catch multiple fish simultaneously. To set up a trotline, tie a length of fishing line between two fixed points along a riverbank or across a stream, and attach multiple baited hooks at intervals along the line. Gorges, on the other hand, are sharpened stakes or sticks driven into the riverbed with bait attached. When a fish swallows the bait and pulls on the line, the gorge lodges in its throat, allowing you to retrieve it easily.

Bait and Lures

Choosing the right bait or lure is crucial to attracting fish in survival fishing. While live bait such as worms, insects, or small fish are highly effective, you can also use improvised lures made from feathers, cloth, or shiny objects to mimic the movement of prey.

Fishing Techniques

Once you’ve assembled your gear and bait, it’s time to employ various fishing techniques to increase your chances of success.

Still Fishing:

Still fishing involves casting your line into a likely fishing spot and allowing it to remain motionless until a fish takes the bait. This technique is effective in calm waters or near underwater structures where fish are likely to congregate.

Drift Fishing:

Drift fishing entails allowing your bait or lure to drift naturally with the current, enticing fish as it moves downstream. This technique is particularly effective in rivers or streams with a steady flow and can be performed from the shore or a drifting raft or boat.

Jigging:

Jigging involves imparting a jerky, up-and-down motion to your bait or lure to mimic injured prey, enticing fish to strike. This technique is effective in both still and moving waters and can be performed with handheld lines or fishing poles.

Conclusion

Survival fishing is a valuable skill that can mean the difference between hunger and satiety in the wilderness. By understanding the principles of survival fishing, improvising fishing gear from available materials, choosing the right bait and lures, and employing various fishing techniques, you can increase your chances of catching fish and securing a vital source of sustenance in survival situations. So the next time you find yourself in the wild, remember these techniques and fish with confidence knowing that you have the skills to thrive in even the most challenging environments.
