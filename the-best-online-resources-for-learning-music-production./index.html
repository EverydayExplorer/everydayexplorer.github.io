The Best Online Resources for Learning Music Production.


Are you an aspiring music producer looking to enhance your skills and knowledge in the field? With the rise of technology and the internet, there are now countless online resources available to help you learn music production from the comfort of your own home. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best online resources for learning music production, covering everything from tutorials and courses to forums and communities.

1. YouTube: YouTube is a goldmine of music production tutorials and educational content. There are numerous channels dedicated to teaching music production techniques, software tutorials, and even interviews with industry professionals. Some popular channels include “Produce Like A Pro,” “In The Mix,” and “Recording Revolution.” These channels offer a wide range of content suitable for beginners and advanced producers alike.

2. Udemy: Udemy is an online learning platform that offers a vast selection of music production courses. From beginner-level introductions to comprehensive courses on specific genres or software, Udemy has something for everyone. The courses are usually self-paced, allowing you to learn at your own convenience. Some popular music production courses on Udemy include “Music Production in Logic Pro X,” “Electronic Music Production with Ableton Live,” and “Mixing and Mastering in Pro Tools.”

3. Coursera: Coursera is another popular online learning platform that partners with top universities and institutions to offer high-quality courses. They have a range of music production courses, including “Introduction to Music Production” and “Music Production and Vocal Recording Technology.” Coursera courses often have a structured curriculum and assignments, providing a more formal learning experience.

4. Sound on Sound: Sound on Sound is a renowned music production magazine that has been around since the 1980s. Their website features a vast archive of articles, tutorials, and reviews covering all aspects of music production. Whether you’re looking for tips on recording techniques, gear reviews, or software tutorials, Sound on Sound has a wealth of valuable information.

5. Ableton Learning Music: If you’re specifically interested in learning music production using Ableton Live, Ableton’s official website offers a dedicated learning platform called “Learning Music.” This interactive website provides step-by-step lessons on music theory, production techniques, and using Ableton Live’s features. It’s a great resource for beginners looking to get started with Ableton Live.

6. Reddit: Reddit is a popular online community with various subreddits dedicated to music production. Subreddits like r/WeAreTheMusicMakers and r/edmproduction are excellent places to connect with fellow producers, ask questions, and share your work. These communities often have threads with valuable resources, tutorials, and feedback on your music.

7. Splice: Splice is an online platform that offers a vast library of samples, loops, and presets for music production. It’s not only a great resource for finding high-quality sounds but also provides educational content through their blog and YouTube channel. Splice also offers a subscription-based service called Splice Sounds, which gives you access to their extensive sample library for a monthly fee.

8. LinkedIn Learning: Formerly known as Lynda.com, LinkedIn Learning offers a wide range of music production courses taught by industry professionals. Their courses cover various topics, including music theory, mixing and mastering, and specific software tutorials. LinkedIn Learning is a great option if you prefer a more structured and professional learning experience.

9. KVR Audio: KVR Audio is a popular online community and marketplace for music production software, plugins, and virtual instruments. Their website features a forum where you can connect with other producers, ask questions, and find valuable resources. KVR Audio also has a comprehensive database of user reviews and ratings for various music production tools.

10. Online Forums and Communities: Apart from Reddit and KVR Audio, there are several other online forums and communities dedicated to music production. Websites like Gearslutz, Future Producers, and Soundtrap Community offer platforms for discussions, tutorials, and feedback.
