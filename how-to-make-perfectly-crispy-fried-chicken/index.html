How to Make Perfectly Crispy Fried Chicken


When it comes to comfort food, nothing beats a plate of perfectly crispy fried chicken. The golden, crunchy exterior combined with tender, juicy meat is a culinary delight that can satisfy any craving. If you’ve ever wondered how to achieve that coveted crispy texture, look no further. In this blog post, we’re going to share the secrets to making the best crispy fried chicken you’ve ever tasted.

1. Choose the right chicken: The key to achieving crispy fried chicken starts with selecting the right chicken pieces. Opt for bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs or drumsticks as they tend to have more flavor and moisture, resulting in juicier meat. Make sure to pat the chicken dry with paper towels to remove any excess moisture.

2. Marinate for flavor: A flavorful marinade not only adds taste but also helps tenderize the chicken. Create a marinade using buttermilk, salt, pepper, and your choice of herbs and spices. Let the chicken marinate in the fridge for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat.

3. Create a seasoned flour mixture: A well-seasoned flour mixture is essential for achieving a crispy crust. Combine all-purpose flour with a variety of spices such as paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Feel free to experiment with different spice combinations to suit your taste preferences.

4. Double-dredge for extra crunch: To ensure maximum crispiness, it’s important to double-dredge the marinated chicken. Dip each piece into the seasoned flour mixture, then into the buttermilk marinade once again, and finally back into the flour mixture. This double-dredging process creates a thick, crunchy coating that adheres to the chicken.

5. Use the right frying technique: To achieve that perfect crispy texture, frying the chicken at the right temperature is crucial. Heat vegetable oil in a heavy-bottomed skillet or Dutch oven to around 350°F (175°C). Gently place the chicken pieces skin-side down into the hot oil, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for about 10-12 minutes per side until the chicken is golden brown and cooked through.

6. Drain and rest: Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the oil and place it on a wire rack or paper towels to drain any excess oil. Allow the chicken to rest for a few minutes before serving. This resting period helps the crust to set and retain its crispiness.

7. Serve and enjoy: Finally, it’s time to dig in! Serve your perfectly crispy fried chicken with your favorite sides, such as mashed potatoes, coleslaw, or cornbread, for a complete and satisfying meal. Don’t forget to garnish with fresh herbs or a squeeze of lemon for an extra burst of flavor.

By following these steps, you’ll be able to create your own version of crispy fried chicken that will rival any restaurant. The combination of a flavorful marinade, a seasoned flour mixture, and the right frying technique will result in a mouthwatering dish that will impress your family and friends. So, put on your apron, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy the ultimate comfort food - perfectly crispy fried chicken!
