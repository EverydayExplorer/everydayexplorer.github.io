How to Calculate Cash-on-Cash Return in Real Estate Investments


Calculating the cash-on-cash return is a crucial step in evaluating the profitability of real estate investments. As an investor, understanding this metric can help you make informed decisions and maximize your returns. In this blog post, we will delve into the intricacies of calculating cash-on-cash return and provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to do it effectively.

Before we dive into the calculations, let’s first understand what cash-on-cash return is. Simply put, it is a measure of the annual return on the actual cash invested in a property. Unlike other metrics that consider the total investment (including debt), cash-on-cash return focuses solely on the cash invested by the investor.

To calculate the cash-on-cash return, you need two main pieces of information: the net operating income (NOI) and the total cash invested. The NOI represents the property’s annual income after deducting all operating expenses but before accounting for mortgage payments and income taxes. On the other hand, the total cash invested includes the down payment, closing costs, and any other upfront expenses.

Now, let’s break down the steps to calculate the cash-on-cash return:

Step 1: Calculate the Net Operating Income (NOI)
To determine the NOI, you need to gather all the income generated by the property and subtract all the operating expenses. This includes rental income, laundry revenue, parking fees, and any other sources of income. Operating expenses may include property management fees, property taxes, insurance, maintenance costs, and utilities. Subtracting the total operating expenses from the total income will give you the NOI.

Step 2: Determine the Total Cash Invested
To calculate the total cash invested, you need to consider the down payment, closing costs, and any other upfront expenses. This includes expenses such as appraisal fees, inspection fees, and legal fees. Add all these costs together to arrive at the total cash invested.

Step 3: Calculate Cash-on-Cash Return
To calculate the cash-on-cash return, divide the NOI by the total cash invested and multiply by 100 to get the percentage. The formula looks like this:

Cash-on-Cash Return = (NOI / Total Cash Invested) * 100

For example, let’s say the property generates an annual NOI of $30,000, and the total cash invested is $200,000. Using the formula, the cash-on-cash return would be:

Cash-on-Cash Return = ($30,000 / $200,000) * 100 = 15%

In this example, the cash-on-cash return is 15%, indicating that the investment generates a 15% return on the cash invested.

It’s important to note that cash-on-cash return is just one metric to consider when evaluating real estate investments. It provides a snapshot of the return on the cash invested, but it doesn’t take into account factors such as appreciation, tax benefits, and potential future income growth. Therefore, it should be used in conjunction with other investment analysis tools to make well-informed decisions.

In conclusion, calculating the cash-on-cash return is a valuable tool for real estate investors to assess the profitability of their investments. By understanding this metric and following the step-by-step guide provided in this blog post, you can make informed decisions and maximize your returns. Happy investing!
