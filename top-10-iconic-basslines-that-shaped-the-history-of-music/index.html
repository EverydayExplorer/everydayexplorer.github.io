Top 10 Iconic Basslines That Shaped the History of Music


When it comes to music, there are certain basslines that have become iconic and have left an indelible mark on the history of music. These basslines are not only memorable but have also played a pivotal role in shaping various genres and influencing countless musicians. In this article, we will explore the top 10 iconic basslines that have truly defined the course of music history.

1. ‘Billie Jean’ by Michael Jackson
One cannot talk about iconic basslines without mentioning ‘Billie Jean.’ The driving bassline, created by Louis Johnson, added a new dimension to pop music and became an instant classic.

2. ‘Another One Bites the Dust’ by Queen
John Deacon’s bassline in ‘Another One Bites the Dust’ is instantly recognizable. This bassline not only became one of Queen’s biggest hits but also became a staple in the world of rock and pop music.

3. ‘Money’ by Pink Floyd
Roger Waters’ bassline in ‘Money’ is a true masterpiece. The distinct rhythm and groove of this bassline helped define the sound of Pink Floyd and became a fan favorite.

4. ‘Stand by Me’ by Ben E. King
The bassline in ‘Stand by Me,’ played by Lloyd Trotman, is simple yet incredibly effective. It perfectly complements the soulful vocals of Ben E. King and has become one of the most recognizable basslines in music history.

5. ‘Under Pressure’ by Queen and David Bowie
‘Under Pressure’ features a bassline created by Queen’s John Deacon and David Bowie. This bassline, with its catchy rhythm and melodic hooks, has become synonymous with the song and has been sampled and referenced in various other tracks.

6. ‘Come Together’ by The Beatles
Paul McCartney’s bassline in ‘Come Together’ is both groovy and hypnotic. It showcases McCartney’s skills as a bass player and has become one of The Beatles’ most iconic basslines.

7. ‘Superstition’ by Stevie Wonder
‘Superstition’ features a funky bassline played by Stevie Wonder himself. This bassline, along with its infectious groove, has made the song a timeless classic and has influenced countless funk and R&B musicians.

8. ‘Good Times’ by Chic
Bernard Edwards’ bassline in ‘Good Times’ is a disco masterpiece. This bassline not only became a hit for Chic but also became one of the most sampled basslines in the history of music.

9. ‘Hysteria’ by Muse
Chris Wolstenholme’s bassline in ‘Hysteria’ is a true showcase of his technical prowess. This intricate and fast-paced bassline has become one of Muse’s signature songs and has inspired a new generation of bass players.

10. ‘Seven Nation Army’ by The White Stripes
‘Seven Nation Army’ is instantly recognizable due to its infectious bassline played on a guitar. This bassline, created by Jack White, has become a stadium anthem and has been chanted by crowds all over the world.

These are just a few examples of the iconic basslines that have shaped the history of music. Each of these basslines has its own unique character, and their influence can still be felt in contemporary music. Whether it’s the catchy hooks or the rhythmic grooves, these basslines have become an integral part of the songs they belong to and have left an indelible mark on the world of music.
