Top Educational Pinterest Accounts for Teachers


As the digital world continues to evolve, educators are constantly seeking innovative ways to enhance their teaching methods and engage their students. One powerful platform that has gained significant popularity in the educational community is Pinterest. Known for its visually appealing content and user-friendly interface, Pinterest has become a treasure trove of educational resources. In this blog post, we will explore the top educational Pinterest accounts that are making a difference in the lives of teachers worldwide.

1. WeAreTeachers:
With over a million followers, WeAreTeachers is a go-to resource for educators looking for fresh ideas, lesson plans, classroom organization tips, and much more. Their boards cover a wide range of topics, from STEM activities to social-emotional learning strategies. No matter what subject or grade level you teach, this account is sure to provide invaluable inspiration.

2. Edutopia:
From the George Lucas Educational Foundation, Edutopia offers a wealth of research-based articles, videos, and infographics related to education. Their Pinterest boards are well-organized and cover various aspects of teaching, including project-based learning, technology integration, and classroom management. Edutopia’s content is known for its practicality and effectiveness in promoting student engagement.

3. The Classroom Sparrow:
If you’re searching for creative teaching resources, The Classroom Sparrow is the Pinterest account to follow. This account focuses on secondary education and offers a plethora of visual aids, printables, and interactive activities. The Classroom Sparrow’s boards are curated with attention to detail, ensuring that teachers have access to high-quality materials that will captivate their students’ interest.

4. TeachThought:
TeachThought is an outstanding resource for teachers interested in promoting critical thinking skills, inquiry-based learning, and educational technology integration. Their Pinterest account features boards on topics such as Bloom’s Taxonomy, student-centered learning, and personalized learning. With a focus on 21st-century teaching strategies, TeachThought empowers educators to foster a dynamic and engaging classroom environment.

5. Cult of Pedagogy:
Jennifer Gonzalez, the face behind Cult of Pedagogy, is a former middle school teacher who has become a prominent figure in the education field. Her Pinterest account offers a multitude of resources, including articles, podcasts, and videos. Cult of Pedagogy prioritizes evidence-based practices and provides practical solutions for common challenges faced by teachers.

6. Reading Rockets:
Reading Rockets is a fantastic resource for educators looking to enhance their literacy instruction. Their Pinterest boards contain a wealth of resources for teaching reading, including phonics, fluency, comprehension, and vocabulary-building activities. With Reading Rockets’ support, teachers can foster a love of reading and help their students become lifelong learners.

7. The Art of Education University:
The Art of Education University’s Pinterest account is a haven for art teachers and enthusiasts alike. Their boards feature unique art projects, lesson plans, and techniques that aim to inspire creativity in the classroom. Whether you’re a seasoned art teacher or someone looking to incorporate more art into your curriculum, this account will provide you with endless inspiration.

8. Math Geek Mama:
For math teachers seeking innovative teaching strategies, Math Geek Mama is an essential Pinterest account to follow. This account offers a wide range of math-related resources, including games, worksheets, and hands-on activities. Math Geek Mama’s boards cover various math topics, from elementary concepts to advanced algebra, ensuring that teachers of all grade levels find valuable content.

9. TED-Ed:
TED-Ed’s Pinterest account brings the renowned TED Talks platform into the realm of education. Their boards feature TED-Ed lessons on a diverse range of subjects, making complex topics accessible to students of all ages. With TED-Ed’s engaging videos and thought-provoking questions, teachers can spark meaningful classroom discussions and inspire a thirst for knowledge.

10. National Geographic Education:
Finally, National Geographic Education’s Pinterest account is a treasure trove of resources for teachers looking to incorporate geography, science, and environmental education into their curriculum. Their boards offer stunning visuals, lesson plans, and educational articles that encourage students to explore the world around them. From animal habitats to climate change, National Geographic Education provides educators with the tools they need to foster global awareness.

In conclusion, Pinterest has emerged as a powerful tool for educators, offering a wealth of resources, inspiration, and practical teaching strategies. By following these top educational Pinterest accounts, teachers can tap into a vast network of like-minded professionals, enriching their teaching practices and enhancing student learning experiences. Whether you’re seeking art projects, math games, or innovative teaching techniques, these Pinterest accounts are sure to ignite your creativity and help you become an even more effective educator. Happy pinning!
