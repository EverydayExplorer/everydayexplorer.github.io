Discovering Greek Wines: From Assyrtiko to Xinomavro


Greece, a country known for its rich history, stunning landscapes, and vibrant culture, is also gaining recognition for its incredible wines. With a winemaking tradition that dates back thousands of years, Greece is home to a diverse range of grape varieties and unique terroirs that produce exceptional wines. In this blog post, we are going to explore the fascinating world of Greek wines, from the refreshing whites like Assyrtiko to the bold reds like Xinomavro.

1. Assyrtiko: The Pride of Santorini

When it comes to Greek white wines, Assyrtiko reigns supreme. This grape variety is predominantly grown on the island of Santorini, where its volcanic soils and proximity to the sea create the perfect conditions for producing high-quality wines. Assyrtiko wines are known for their crisp acidity, citrus flavors, and mineral character. They pair exceptionally well with seafood dishes and are a must-try for any wine enthusiast visiting Greece.

2. Moschofilero: A Fragrant Delight

Another Greek grape variety that deserves recognition is Moschofilero. Grown mainly in the Peloponnese region, Moschofilero produces aromatic and elegant white wines. With notes of rose petals, citrus fruits, and a hint of spice, Moschofilero wines are incredibly refreshing and can be enjoyed on their own or paired with a variety of Mediterranean dishes.

3. Agiorgitiko: The ‘Blood of Hercules’

Moving on to Greek red wines, Agiorgitiko is a name that stands out. Often referred to as the ‘Blood of Hercules’ due to its deep red color and powerful character, Agiorgitiko wines are produced primarily in the Nemea region of the Peloponnese. These wines are known for their intense fruit flavors, gentle tannins, and a touch of spice. Agiorgitiko pairs exceptionally well with grilled meats and hearty stews.

4. Xinomavro: The Greek Nebbiolo

Considered the king of Greek red grapes, Xinomavro is often compared to Italy’s Nebbiolo grape variety. Grown in northern Greece, mainly in the Naoussa region, Xinomavro produces complex and age-worthy red wines. With flavors of ripe red fruits, herbs, and earthy undertones, Xinomavro wines are known for their high acidity and firm tannins. These wines are perfect for aging and can develop incredible complexity and finesse over time.

5. Mavrodaphne: A Sweet Indulgence

Last but not least, we have Mavrodaphne, a Greek grape variety that produces luscious and sweet wines. Grown primarily in the Patras region, Mavrodaphne wines are known for their dark, rich flavors of dried fruits, chocolate, and spices. These dessert wines are often enjoyed on their own or paired with strong cheeses and chocolate-based desserts. Mavrodaphne’s unique character and sweetness make it a delightful indulgence for wine enthusiasts with a sweet tooth.

The Rise of Greek Wines

In recent years, Greek wines have been gaining international recognition and are now increasingly sought after by wine lovers around the world. With their unique grape varieties, diverse terroirs, and a growing number of talented winemakers embracing modern winemaking techniques, Greek wines are making their mark in the global wine scene.

If you’re a wine enthusiast looking to discover something new and exciting, Greek wines should definitely be on your radar. From the crisp and refreshing whites like Assyrtiko and Moschofilero to the bold and age-worthy reds like Agiorgitiko and Xinomavro, the wines of Greece offer a truly unique and unforgettable experience.

So, the next time you’re at a wine shop or restaurant, don’t hesitate to explore the Greek wine section. You might just uncover a hidden gem and embark on a delightful journey through the wines of Greece.

Note: The purpose of this blog post is to highlight the diversity and quality of Greek wines, as well as their rising popularity in the global wine market. It is not intended as a promotional piece for any specific brand or winery. Please enjoy Greek wines responsibly and remember to always drink in moderation.
