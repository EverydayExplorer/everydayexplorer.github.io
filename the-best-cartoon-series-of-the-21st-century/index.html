The Best Cartoon Series of the 21st Century


The 21st century has been a transformative era for television animation, leading to the creation of some of the most beloved and groundbreaking cartoon series in history. These shows have not only captured the hearts of children but have also resonated with adults, creating a nostalgic bond across generations. In this post, we’ll explore the best cartoon series of the 21st century, delving into what makes each one unique and impactful.

1. Avatar: The Last Airbender (2005-2008)

“Avatar: The Last Airbender” stands out as a monumental achievement in animated storytelling. Set in a richly crafted world where people can manipulate the four elements—water, earth, fire, and air—the series follows the journey of Aang, the last Airbender and the Avatar, who must master all four elements to restore balance to the world.

The show’s success can be attributed to its intricate character development, moral complexity, and stunning animation. Each character has a distinct personality and growth arc, making them relatable and compelling. Themes of friendship, responsibility, and the struggle between good and evil resonate deeply, ensuring that the show remains relevant years after its original airing.

2. Adventure Time (2010-2018)

“Adventure Time” is a surreal and whimsical series that follows the adventures of Finn the Human and Jake the Dog in the post-apocalyptic Land of Ooo. This show is notable for its blend of humor, creativity, and emotional depth. Each episode presents a new adventure, filled with eccentric characters and imaginative landscapes.

What sets “Adventure Time” apart is its ability to tackle complex themes, including loneliness, love, and mortality, all while maintaining a light-hearted and humorous tone. Its distinctive animation style and innovative storytelling have inspired countless other creators, solidifying its place as a cornerstone of modern animation.

3. BoJack Horseman (2014-2020)

“BoJack Horseman” takes a darker turn, presenting a satirical look at Hollywood through the lens of a washed-up anthropomorphic horse. This adult animated series explores themes of depression, addiction, and the quest for redemption. The show is lauded for its bold storytelling and willingness to tackle uncomfortable topics with honesty and nuance.

The character of BoJack serves as a complex protagonist whose flaws make him relatable. The series skillfully balances humor and tragedy, prompting viewers to reflect on their own lives and the nature of fame. With its unique animation style and thought-provoking narrative, “BoJack Horseman” has garnered critical acclaim and a dedicated fanbase.

4. Rick and Morty (2013-present)

“Rick and Morty” is a sci-fi animated series that follows the misadventures of an eccentric and alcoholic scientist, Rick Sanchez, and his good-hearted but easily influenced grandson, Morty. The show is known for its sharp wit, dark humor, and clever storytelling, often exploring existential themes and philosophical questions.

With its fast-paced narrative and unpredictable plot twists, “Rick and Morty” has attracted a cult following. It challenges traditional storytelling conventions and pushes the boundaries of what animated series can achieve. The show’s complex characters and intricate world-building make it a standout in contemporary animation.

5. Steven Universe (2013-2020)

“Steven Universe” is a groundbreaking series that centers around Steven, a young boy who is half-human and half-Gem, an alien race with magical abilities. The show is celebrated for its representation of LGBTQ+ themes, emotional storytelling, and diverse characters. It encourages acceptance, love, and understanding through its narrative.

The animation style is vibrant and expressive, complementing the show’s themes of self-discovery and personal growth. The music in “Steven Universe” is also noteworthy, with numerous songs that enhance the emotional depth of the story. This series not only entertains but also educates its audience on important social issues, making it a significant contribution to animated television.

6. Gravity Falls (2012-2016)

“Gravity Falls” is a mystery-comedy series that follows twins Dipper and Mabel Pines as they spend the summer with their great-uncle Stan in the mysterious town of Gravity Falls. The show combines humor, adventure, and supernatural elements, creating an engaging storyline that keeps viewers guessing.

The series is known for its clever writing, intricate plot, and rich character development. Each episode builds on the overarching mystery, drawing viewers deeper into the world of Gravity Falls. The dynamic between Dipper and Mabel adds a heartwarming layer to the narrative, making it a favorite among audiences of all ages.

7. The Amazing World of Gumball (2011-2019)

“The Amazing World of Gumball” combines various animation styles to create a unique and visually striking series. It follows the misadventures of Gumball Watterson, a blue cat, and his family and friends in the fictional city of Elmore. The show is known for its absurd humor, clever writing, and playful exploration of everyday life.

Its ability to blend different animation techniques—from traditional 2D to live-action elements—sets it apart from other cartoons. The series tackles relatable themes of family, friendship, and growing up while maintaining a fun and lighthearted tone.

8. Big Mouth (2017-present)

“Big Mouth” is an adult animated series that explores the awkwardness of adolescence and the challenges of growing up. The show follows a group of middle school friends as they navigate the trials of puberty, relationships, and self-acceptance. With its frank approach to topics like sexuality and mental health, “Big Mouth” offers a humorous yet insightful look at the teenage experience.

The animation style is colorful and exaggerated, mirroring the show’s comedic tone. Its willingness to tackle taboo subjects with honesty has resonated with audiences, making it a refreshing addition to the adult animation genre.

9. Futurama (1999-2013)

Though it originally premiered in the late 1990s, “Futurama” continued to impact the 21st century with its revival and new episodes. This sci-fi animated series follows the adventures of Fry, a pizza delivery boy who is cryogenically frozen and wakes up in the year 3000. The show is known for its clever satire, futuristic concepts, and memorable characters.

“Futurama” expertly blends humor with social commentary, often critiquing contemporary society through its futuristic lens. Its unique blend of science fiction and comedy has garnered a dedicated fanbase, showcasing the enduring appeal of the series.

10. Tuca & Bertie (2019-present)

“Tuca & Bertie” is a visually stunning animated series that follows the friendship between two birdwomen living in the same apartment building. The show explores themes of friendship, mental health, and the complexities of adulthood through a whimsical and surreal lens.

The animation style is vibrant and imaginative, reflecting the show’s unique storytelling approach. Its exploration of female friendship and the challenges of navigating life resonates with audiences, offering a fresh perspective in the world of animation.

Conclusion

The 21st century has ushered in a new era of animation, marked by diverse storytelling, innovative animation techniques, and a willingness to tackle complex themes. From the epic journey of “Avatar: The Last Airbender” to the surreal adventures of “Adventure Time,” these cartoon series have left an indelible mark on pop culture.

As we continue to embrace the world of animation, it’s clear that these shows will remain a significant part of our collective consciousness. The creativity and imagination displayed in these series not only entertain but also inspire future generations of animators and storytellers. Whether you’re revisiting old favorites or discovering new gems, the best cartoon series of the 21st century are sure to captivate and inspire.
