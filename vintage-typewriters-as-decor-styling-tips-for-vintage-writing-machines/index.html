Vintage Typewriters as Decor: Styling Tips for Vintage Writing Machines


In today’s digital age, it’s rare to come across a typewriter. These classic machines were once a staple in offices and homes, representing a time when writing was a deliberate and tangible process. However, vintage typewriters have found a new purpose as decor pieces, adding a touch of nostalgia and personality to any space.

If you’re considering incorporating a vintage typewriter into your home or office decor, there are a few styling tips you should keep in mind. Whether you’re a collector or simply appreciate the aesthetic appeal of these iconic machines, read on to discover how you can showcase them in your space.

1. Choose the Right Typewriter
When it comes to vintage typewriters, there are countless models and designs to choose from. From sleek, minimalist models to elaborate, decorative machines, the options are endless. Consider the overall theme and style of your space when selecting a typewriter. If you prefer a more modern and minimalist aesthetic, opt for a streamlined model in a neutral color. On the other hand, if you’re going for a vintage or bohemian look, a more ornate, colorful typewriter might be the perfect choice.

2. Create a Focal Point
Vintage typewriters are unique conversation starters that can serve as the focal point of a room. When styling your space, consider placing the typewriter on a prominent surface, such as a desk, console table, or bookshelf. Arrange other objects and decor around it to draw attention and create a cohesive look. For example, you could surround the typewriter with vintage books, antique letters, or vintage-inspired accessories.

3. Incorporate it into Existing Decor
To seamlessly integrate your vintage typewriter into your space, incorporate it into your existing decor. For instance, if your room has a rustic or industrial theme, consider placing the typewriter on a reclaimed wood desk or a vintage metal table. This will help tie the overall decor together and create a cohesive look.

4. Add a Touch of Greenery
Another way to elevate the look of your vintage typewriter is by adding a touch of greenery. Place a small potted plant or a vase of fresh flowers next to the typewriter to add life and freshness to the space. This will soften the overall look and create a more inviting atmosphere.

5. Use it Functionally
Vintage typewriters weren’t just decorative pieces; they were practical writing machines used for correspondence and creative pursuits. So why not bring back their original purpose? If you enjoy writing letters or journaling, use your vintage typewriter to add a personal touch to your correspondence. It’s a unique way to connect with the past and infuse a bit of nostalgia into your everyday life.

6. Embrace the Patina
Part of the charm of vintage typewriters is the patina and wear that comes with age. Instead of trying to restore your typewriter to its original condition, embrace its imperfections. These signs of use and age add character and authenticity to the piece. Showcase the typewriter as-is, and let its history shine through.

7. Display it with Care
Vintage typewriters are fragile and delicate, so it’s essential to handle them with care. Avoid placing them in direct sunlight, which can cause the materials to fade or deteriorate over time. Clean them gently using a soft, lint-free cloth to remove dust and debris.

In conclusion, vintage typewriters make for unique and visually appealing decor pieces that can elevate the overall aesthetic of a space. By choosing the right typewriter, creating a focal point, incorporating it into existing decor, adding a touch of greenery, using it functionally, embracing the patina, and displaying it with care, you can showcase your love for vintage machines while adding a touch of nostalgia to your home or office.

So, why not embrace the charm of these vintage writing machines and add a piece of history to your decor? Start exploring the world of vintage typewriters today and unlock a world of style and nostalgia.
