The Most Absurd Superstitions from Around the World


Superstitions are an intriguing aspect of human culture, often revealing the fears, hopes, and beliefs of different societies. While some superstitions have a basis in historical events or cultural practices, others seem downright absurd to the uninitiated. From avoiding black cats to knocking on wood, superstitions vary widely across the globe. Let’s take a journey to explore some of the most unusual and absurd superstitions from different parts of the world.

In Russia, it is believed that if you sit at the corner of a table, you won’t get married for 7 years. This superstition might seem bizarre to outsiders, but for many Russians, it’s a serious matter to consider when choosing a place to sit during a meal. The idea of being unmarried for seven years can be a daunting prospect for those who adhere to this belief.

Moving to Asia, in South Korea, it’s considered bad luck to write a person’s name in red ink. This superstition is rooted in the belief that writing someone’s name in red signifies that they are deceased. Therefore, using red ink for a living person’s name is considered extremely unlucky and disrespectful. It’s fascinating to see how cultural beliefs can shape such everyday practices.

In parts of Africa, there’s a superstition that if you sweep your house at night, you’ll sweep away your wealth. This belief reflects the idea that cleaning at night is not only a waste of time but also invites financial hardship. It’s a superstition that highlights the importance of timing and the fear of losing prosperity.

In some Latin American countries, it’s believed that putting a hat on a bed brings bad luck. This superstition is thought to have originated from the practice of placing a hat on the bed of a deceased person. As a result, placing a hat on a bed is seen as inviting death or misfortune into the household.

In India, there’s a superstition that cutting your nails after sunset brings financial problems. This belief is deeply ingrained in Indian culture, with many people ensuring that they trim their nails before dusk to avoid any potential financial hardships. The superstition highlights the significance of timing and the fear of attracting negative consequences.

These are just a few examples of the diverse and fascinating superstitions that exist around the world. While some may seem absurd to outsiders, they hold significant cultural and psychological meaning for those who believe in them. Superstitions often reflect the values, fears, and hopes of a society, offering a window into the human psyche.
