Unlocking Your Creativity: Books Every Artist Should Own


As an artist, unlocking your creativity is essential to producing your best work. Whether you’re a painter, writer, musician, or any other type of creative, having a foundation of knowledge and inspiration is crucial. One of the best ways to achieve this is by immersing yourself in books that can expand your artistic horizons and further develop your creative skills. In this blog post, we will explore some of the books that every artist should own, as they have the power to ignite your imagination and take your artistry to new heights.

1. ‘The Artist’s Way’ by Julia Cameron:
‘The Artist’s Way’ is a classic when it comes to unlocking your creativity. It’s a twelve-week program that guides artists through exercises and activities designed to unleash their creative potential. Cameron explores the concept of ‘morning pages,’ where individuals write three pages of stream-of-consciousness writing every morning. This practice helps clear the mind of clutter and allows for fresh ideas to flow freely. Additionally, the book offers valuable insight on overcoming creative blocks and finding inspiration in unexpected places.

2. ‘Big Magic’ by Elizabeth Gilbert:
In ‘Big Magic,’ Elizabeth Gilbert delves into the magical and mysterious process of creativity. Drawing from her own experiences as a writer, she explores the idea that creativity is not just for the select few but is accessible to all who seek it. Gilbert shares stories and anecdotes that inspire readers to fearlessly pursue their creative passions without the fear of judgment or failure. This book encourages artists to embrace curiosity and find joy in the creative process, ultimately unlocking their full potential.

3. ‘Bird by Bird’ by Anne Lamott:
Anne Lamott’s ‘Bird by Bird’ is a must-read for any aspiring writer or artist struggling with self-doubt and perfectionism. Through a collection of humorous and insightful anecdotes, Lamott provides invaluable advice on the craft of writing and the creative process in general. This book emphasizes the importance of taking one step at a time, embracing the messy nature of creativity, and seeing the beauty in imperfection. Lamott’s wisdom and wit will inevitably inspire artists to conquer their insecurities and unleash their creative voices.

4. ‘The War of Art’ by Steven Pressfield:
‘The War of Art’ by Steven Pressfield explores the inner battle every artist faces when trying to create. Pressfield introduces the concept of ‘resistance’ - the force that prevents us from realizing our creative dreams. He outlines practical strategies for overcoming resistance and breaking through creative blocks. This book serves as a powerful reminder that creativity requires discipline, perseverance, and a willingness to face and overcome our fears.

5. ‘Steal Like an Artist’ by Austin Kleon:
‘Steal Like an Artist’ offers a fresh perspective on creativity and the concept of originality. Kleon suggests that instead of trying to be completely original, artists should embrace the influence of others and borrow ideas to create something new. He provides practical tips and exercises to inspire artists to explore different mediums, seek inspiration outside of their comfort zones, and remix existing ideas to create unique works of art. This book encourages artists to embrace collaboration, curiosity, and the continuous quest for knowledge.

These five books are a great starting point for any artist looking to unlock their creativity and take their artistry to the next level. Each book offers unique insights, practical advice, and exercises that can help artists break through creative blocks and expand their perspectives. Remember, creativity is a journey, and these books provide valuable guidance along the way. So, grab a copy of these books, dive into their pages, and let your creativity soar!
