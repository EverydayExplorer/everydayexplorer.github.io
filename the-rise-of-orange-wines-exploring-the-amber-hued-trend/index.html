The Rise of Orange Wines: Exploring the Amber-Hued Trend


If you’re a wine enthusiast, chances are you’ve come across orange wines in recent years. These unique and intriguing wines have been making waves in the wine industry, captivating both sommeliers and casual wine drinkers alike. But what exactly are orange wines, and why are they gaining popularity? In this blog post, we will explore the rise of orange wines and delve into what makes them so special.

What are Orange Wines?

Orange wines, also known as amber wines or skin-contact wines, are made from white grape varieties but are produced using a winemaking technique borrowed from red wines. While traditional white wines are made by pressing the grapes and separating the juice from the skins, orange wines are fermented with the grape skins intact.

This extended contact between the grape juice and the skins gives the wines their distinctive orange-amber color, hence the name. The skins also impart tannins, texture, and complex flavors to the wine, resulting in a more robust and concentrated profile compared to many white wines.

A Rich History

Contrary to popular belief, orange wines are not a recent innovation. This winemaking technique dates back thousands of years to the traditional wine regions of Georgia, Slovenia, and Italy’s Friuli-Venezia Giulia. In fact, Georgia is often credited as the birthplace of orange wines, where the technique was originally used to preserve white grapes during fermentation in clay vessels known as qvevri.

Over time, orange wines fell out of favor, and winemakers shifted their focus to more modern and commercially successful winemaking methods. However, starting in the early 2000s, a group of natural winemakers in Italy and Slovenia rediscovered this ancient technique and brought orange wines back into the spotlight.

Aesthetic Appeal and Food Pairing

One of the reasons behind the rise of orange wines is their stunning visual appeal. The amber hue, ranging from deep gold to copper, adds an element of excitement to the wine-drinking experience. Additionally, the aesthetic qualities of the wine make it a popular choice for social media influencers and wine enthusiasts looking to capture the perfect Instagram picture.

Aside from their visual appeal, orange wines also offer a wide range of flavors and textures that make them versatile when it comes to pairing with food. Due to the tannins and complexity imparted by the grape skins, orange wines can stand up to bolder flavors, such as aged cheeses, charcuterie, spicy dishes, and even game meats. The tannins provide structure and balance to the wine when paired with such robust dishes, making it an ideal choice for adventurous food pairings.

Natural Winemaking and Sustainability

Another factor contributing to the rise of orange wines is the growing interest in natural winemaking and sustainability. Natural winemaking, which prioritizes minimal intervention and the use of organic and biodynamic practices, aligns well with the production of orange wines.

Many orange wines are made using grapes grown using sustainable farming practices and fermented with native yeasts. This hands-off approach allows the true expression of the grape and terroir to shine through, resulting in wines that are often described as unique and authentic.

The Future of Orange Wines

As the demand for unique and alternative wine options continues to grow, orange wines are likely to become more mainstream. Though still considered a niche, the trend is gaining traction in wine bars, restaurants, and specialty wine shops around the world.

Additionally, as winemakers experiment with different grape varieties and winemaking techniques, the diversity within the orange wine category is expanding. This means that wine lovers can expect to find a wide array of options to suit their taste preferences, from light and floral to bold and robust.

In conclusion, the rise of orange wines represents a shift towards more adventurous and eclectic wine choices. With their ancient winemaking technique, striking appearance, and distinctive flavors, it’s no wonder that orange wines are capturing the attention of wine enthusiasts everywhere. So next time you’re looking to try something new and exciting, don’t hesitate to explore the world of orange wines. Cheers!
