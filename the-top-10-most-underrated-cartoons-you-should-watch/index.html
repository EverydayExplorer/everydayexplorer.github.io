The Top 10 Most Underrated Cartoons You Should Watch


When it comes to cartoons, many of us have our go-to favorites that we love to re-watch. However, there are a myriad of underrated cartoons out there that often fly under the radar but are truly gems waiting to be discovered. Whether you’re a fan of classic animation or modern storytelling, there’s something for everyone in this list of the top 10 most underrated cartoons that you should definitely add to your watchlist.

1. “Gravity Falls”

“Gravity Falls” is a brilliant blend of mystery, adventure, and humor that appeals to both kids and adults. Created by Alex Hirsch, this animated series follows the adventures of twins Dipper and Mabel Pines as they spend their summer with their great-uncle Stan in the mysterious town of Gravity Falls. The show’s clever writing, intricate plotlines, and lovable characters make it a must-watch for any animation enthusiast.

2. “The Dragon Prince”

From the creators of “Avatar: The Last Airbender,” “The Dragon Prince” is a fantasy epic that boasts stunning animation and a rich, diverse world. With its compelling characters and intricate political intrigue, this series is a refreshing take on the classic fantasy genre, offering a unique blend of magic, adventure, and complex moral dilemmas.

3. “Over the Garden Wall”

This enchanting mini-series takes viewers on a whimsical journey through a mysterious and surreal forest, following two half-brothers as they try to find their way home. “Over the Garden Wall” combines beautiful animation with a hauntingly beautiful soundtrack, creating a captivating and atmospheric viewing experience that’s perfect for a cozy night in.

4. “The Spectacular Spider-Man”

While there have been numerous adaptations of the iconic web-slinger, “The Spectacular Spider-Man” often gets overlooked. This series captures the essence of Spider-Man’s story with a perfect balance of action, humor, and character development. It’s a well-crafted show that deserves more recognition for its faithful portrayal of the beloved superhero.

5. “Star vs. The Forces of Evil”

“Star vs. The Forces of Evil” is a delightful blend of magical girl tropes and interdimensional adventures. With its vibrant animation, quirky humor, and a strong emphasis on friendship and self-discovery, this series has a lot to offer for fans of lighthearted yet compelling storytelling.

6. “Gargoyles”

A classic from the ’90s, “Gargoyles” is a dark, action-packed animated series that weaves together mythology, history, and urban fantasy. The show’s complex narrative and morally ambiguous characters set it apart from typical children’s cartoons, making it a captivating watch for older audiences as well.

7. “The Legend of Korra”

As the sequel to “Avatar: The Last Airbender,” “The Legend of Korra” expands upon the beloved world of bending and elemental magic. With its mature themes, complex characters, and stunning fight choreography, this series offers a fresh perspective on the Avatar universe, exploring new conflicts and societal challenges.

8. “Young Justice”

“Young Justice” is a superhero series that follows the adventures of DC Comics’ younger heroes, offering a more serious and character-driven take on the superhero genre. With its intricate plotlines, diverse cast of characters, and compelling story arcs, this show is a hidden gem for fans of superhero fiction.

9. “Sym-Bionic Titan”

This action-packed series from the creator of “Samurai Jack” and “Dexter’s Laboratory” follows the adventures of three alien refugees as they navigate high school and defend Earth from extraterrestrial threats. With its stunning animation and dynamic action sequences, “Sym-Bionic Titan” is a thrilling and visually striking show that deserves more recognition.

10. “The Owl House”

“The Owl House” is a recent addition to the world of animation, offering a quirky and imaginative take on magic and fantasy. With its endearing characters, clever humor, and compelling world-building, this series has quickly gained a dedicated fanbase and is definitely worth a watch for anyone seeking a fresh and inventive animated experience.

Whether you’re a longtime animation enthusiast or looking for something new to watch, these underrated cartoons offer a diverse range of storytelling, animation styles, and genre-bending narratives that are sure to captivate audiences of all ages. So, why not take a break from the familiar and dive into the world of these hidden gems? You might just discover your new favorite cartoon series.
