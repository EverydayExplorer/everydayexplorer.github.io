10 Music Podcasts Every Music Enthusiast Should Subscribe To


In the vast world of music, there are countless ways to discover new songs, explore different genres, and dive deeper into the stories behind the music. One of the most captivating and immersive ways to do this is through music podcasts. These audio shows offer a unique platform for musicians, industry insiders, and enthusiasts to share their passion, knowledge, and experiences. Whether you’re a die-hard music lover or just looking to expand your musical horizons, here are 10 music podcasts that every music enthusiast should subscribe to.

1. ‘Song Exploder’: If you’ve ever wondered how your favorite songs come to life, this podcast is a must-listen. Hosted by Hrishikesh Hirway, ‘Song Exploder’ takes you behind the scenes of popular tracks, breaking them down to their musical and lyrical components. Artists like Lorde, The National, and Fleetwood Mac have all been featured, sharing their creative process and the stories behind their music.

2. ‘Switched on Pop’: Dive into the world of pop music with hosts Nate Sloan and Charlie Harding. ‘Switched on Pop’ analyzes the latest hits, dissecting their melodies, lyrics, and production techniques. This podcast offers a fresh perspective on the pop music landscape, exploring its cultural impact and artistic nuances.

3. ‘Dissect’: For those who crave in-depth musical analysis, ‘Dissect’ is a podcast that delves into albums, one episode at a time. Hosted by Cole Cuchna, this show examines the intricacies of albums like Kanye West’s ‘My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy’ and Frank Ocean’s ‘Blonde.’ Each episode dissects a song, revealing its hidden meanings, influences, and production techniques.

4. ‘The Trap Set’: Hosted by drummer Joe Wong, ‘The Trap Set’ brings you intimate conversations with renowned drummers from all genres. From rock legends like Chad Smith of the Red Hot Chili Peppers to jazz virtuosos like Roy Haynes, this podcast offers a glimpse into the lives and careers of some of the most influential drummers in the world.

5. ‘Pitchfork Review’: If you’re a fan of music journalism, ‘Pitchfork Review’ is a podcast that explores the latest trends, releases, and controversies in the music industry. Hosted by Puja Patel, this show features interviews with artists, critics, and industry insiders, providing a comprehensive look at the ever-evolving landscape of music.

6. ‘Cocaine & Rhinestones’: Dive into the rich history of country music with host Tyler Mahan Coe. ‘Cocaine & Rhinestones’ uncovers the stories behind iconic songs, artists, and moments in country music history. From the rise of legends like Johnny Cash to the impact of lesser-known figures, this podcast is a treasure trove for country music enthusiasts.

7. ‘The Soundtrack Show’: If film scores and soundtracks are your passion, ‘The Soundtrack Show’ is a podcast that explores the music behind the movies. Hosted by David W. Collins, this show analyzes iconic film scores, offering insights into their composition, influence, and impact on cinematic storytelling.

8. ‘Switched on Synths’: Take a journey into the world of synthesizers with this podcast hosted by synthesist Marc Doty. ‘Switched on Synths’ explores the history, techniques, and innovations of electronic music, featuring interviews with synth enthusiasts, manufacturers, and musicians. Whether you’re a synth geek or just curious about the fascinating world of electronic soundscapes, this podcast is a must-listen.

9. ‘The Vinyl Guide’: Vinyl records have made a resurgence in recent years, and ‘The Vinyl Guide’ celebrates the art of vinyl collecting and the stories behind rare records. Hosted by Nate Goyer, this podcast features interviews with collectors, musicians, and industry experts, offering a deep dive into the world of vinyl culture.

10. ‘The Joe Budden Podcast’: For a mix of music, culture, and insightful discussions, ‘The Joe Budden Podcast’ is a must-listen. Hosted by rapper Joe Budden, along with co-hosts Rory Farrell and Jamil ‘Mal’ Clay, this podcast covers everything from music industry news to personal anecdotes, providing a unique perspective on the hip-hop landscape.

These 10 music podcasts offer a diverse range of perspectives, insights, and stories for every music enthusiast to enjoy. Whether you’re looking to discover new music, explore the creative process, or learn more about the history of your favorite genres, these podcasts are sure to keep your ears entertained and your musical knowledge enriched. So, tune in, subscribe, and let the power of audio storytelling take you on an unforgettable musical journey.
