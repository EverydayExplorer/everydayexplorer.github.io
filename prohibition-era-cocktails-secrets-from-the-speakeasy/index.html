Prohibition Era Cocktails: Secrets from the Speakeasy


The Prohibition Era is widely known as a time of great social upheaval in America, with the banning of alcohol leading to widespread bootlegging and clandestine drinking establishments known as speakeasies. It was during this period that some of the most iconic cocktails in history were created, often utilizing homemade spirits and mixers to create unique and flavorful drinks.

Despite the illegality of alcohol, the demand for it remained high, and enterprising individuals across the country took advantage of this by setting up secret bars and speakeasies. These hidden establishments were often located in basements, back rooms or secret locations, and required a password or other form of code to gain entry. The atmosphere was exciting and illicit, providing a sense of rebellion and freedom for those who frequented them.

Of course, no speakeasy would be complete without a selection of cocktails to quench their patrons’ thirsts. Bartenders at these secret bars would use homemade spirits, syrups, and mixers to create unique and delicious drinks, often under the watchful eye of bouncers and bootleggers.

One of the most famous cocktails from this era is the classic martini, which was a favorite of many speakeasy-goers. Made using gin or vodka and vermouth, it was a simple but effective drink that packed a punch. Another popular choice was the Old Fashioned, which combined whiskey, bitters, sugar, and water to create a timeless classic.

But it wasn’t just the classics that were being concocted behind closed doors. Bartenders also experimented with new ideas, creating innovative drinks that would become staples in the world of mixology. Two such drinks were the Bee’s Knees and the French 75. The Bee’s Knees blended gin, lemon juice, and honey syrup, resulting in a sweet and refreshing drink that was perfect for sipping on a hot summer day. The French 75, on the other hand, combined gin, lemon juice, sugar, and champagne, creating a bubbly and effervescent cocktail that was perfect for celebrating special occasions.

Despite the fact that Prohibition has long since ended, the cocktails of the speakeasies live on. Cocktail bars around the world continue to serve up classics like the martini and the Old Fashioned, while also experimenting with new and exciting creations that pay homage to this exciting and rebellious era of American history.

If you’re interested in trying out some Prohibition-era cocktails for yourself, there are plenty of resources available online to help you get started. Websites like Liquor.com and Difford’s Guide offer recipes and tips for making these classic drinks at home, while books like ‘The Speakeasy Cocktail Book’ provide a deeper dive into the history and culture of this unique period in American history.

In conclusion, the Prohibition Era was a time of great social change and upheaval, but it was also a time of innovation and creativity. The cocktails that emerged from the speakeasies of the time continue to be enjoyed by people around the world today, serving as a reminder of the power of rebellion, freedom, and the pursuit of good times. Cheers!
