The Evolution of Dance in Music Videos: From the '80s to Today


Since the advent of music videos in the early 1980s, dance has played a prominent role in shaping the visual landscape of popular music. From iconic moves like Michael Jackson’s moonwalk to contemporary viral dance crazes on platforms like TikTok, the evolution of dance in music videos has been both reflective of and influential on broader cultural trends. In this blog post, we will delve into the history of dance in music videos, tracing its development from the ’80s to today.

The 1980s marked a turning point in the relationship between music and visual storytelling, with the rise of MTV and the prevalence of music videos as a promotional tool for artists. This era saw the emergence of iconic dance moves that have since become synonymous with the decade’s biggest hits. Michael Jackson’s ‘Thriller’ stands out as a prime example, featuring choreography that blended elements of pop, funk, and even horror movie tropes. The music video not only showcased Jackson’s incredible dancing skills but also set a new standard for storytelling through movement.

As we moved into the 1990s, dance in music videos continued to evolve, reflecting the changing sounds and aesthetics of the era. Hip-hop and R&B artists like Janet Jackson and TLC brought their own unique styles to the screen, incorporating street dance and innovative choreography into their visuals. The influence of music videos on dance culture was becoming increasingly apparent, with dancers and choreographers gaining recognition for their contributions to the medium.

The turn of the millennium brought with it a new digital age, where music videos could reach global audiences instantaneously through online platforms like YouTube. This shift allowed for greater diversity in dance styles and influences, as artists from around the world began to collaborate and share their moves with a wider audience. Choreographers like Brian Friedman and Parris Goebel rose to fame by creating viral dance routines for artists like Britney Spears and Justin Bieber, further blurring the lines between music and dance in the digital age.

Today, dance in music videos continues to push boundaries and challenge conventions, with artists using movement as a powerful form of self-expression and storytelling. Genres like K-pop have particularly embraced dance as a core component of their visual identity, with groups like BTS and BLACKPINK setting new standards for precision and innovation in choreography. Social media platforms like TikTok have also democratized dance, allowing users to create and share their own choreography to popular songs in real-time.

In conclusion, the evolution of dance in music videos has been a dynamic and ever-changing journey, reflecting the cultural shifts and technological advancements of the past four decades. From the iconic moves of the ’80s to the global dance trends of today, one thing remains clear: dance will always be an essential element of the music video experience, shaping our perceptions of art, identity, and community in profound ways.
