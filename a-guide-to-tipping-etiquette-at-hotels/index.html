A Guide to Tipping Etiquette at Hotels


Welcome to our ultimate guide on tipping etiquette at hotels! Whether you’re a frequent traveler or embarking on a well-deserved vacation, understanding the ins and outs of tipping can make your stay more enjoyable and help you navigate unfamiliar customs. In this comprehensive blog, we will explore various situations where tipping is customary, provide general guidelines, and shed light on regional differences. So, let’s dive in and uncover the secrets of proper tipping etiquette!

1. Arrival and Departure:
Upon arrival at a hotel, it’s common to tip the valet or bellhop who assists with your luggage. A general rule of thumb is to offer -2 per bag, but feel free to adjust based on the level of service provided. When departing, consider tipping housekeeping if they have gone above and beyond to ensure a pleasant stay.

2. Concierge Services:
If the concierge has helped you with restaurant reservations, tour bookings, or provided valuable local insights, it’s customary to show appreciation with a tip. Depending on the complexity of the request, -10 is a reasonable amount to consider.

3. Room Service and Dining:
When ordering room service, check if gratuity is already included. If not, a 15-20% tip is customary. In restaurants within the hotel, follow standard tipping practices based on quality of service and satisfaction.

4. Spa and Salon Services:
For spa and salon services, a 15-20% tip is customary. Keep in mind that some establishments may automatically include gratuity, so be sure to check the bill beforehand.

5. Shuttle Drivers and Transportation:
If your hotel provides shuttle services, consider tipping the driver -2 per person, or more for exceptional service. Similarly, taxi or rideshare drivers who go the extra mile can be rewarded with a 15-20% tip.

6. Hotel Staff:
While it may not be expected, tipping other hotel staff who have provided exceptional service can be a nice gesture. This includes front desk staff who have assisted with special requests, maintenance personnel who promptly address any concerns, and doormen who hail cabs or offer assistance.

7. Regional Differences:
It’s essential to be aware of regional differences in tipping customs, as they can vary greatly from country to country. For instance, in some European countries, service charges are often included in the bill, while in others, tipping is not as prevalent. Researching the tipping customs of your destination before you travel will ensure you’re prepared.

Remember, tipping is a way to acknowledge excellent service and show appreciation for the hard work of hotel staff. While the suggested amounts mentioned here serve as general guidelines, it’s crucial to consider individual circumstances and your personal budget. Furthermore, always assess the quality of service received before deciding on the appropriate tip.

By adhering to proper tipping etiquette, you’ll not only foster positive relationships with hotel staff but also contribute to a culture of appreciation in the hospitality industry. So, the next time you check into a hotel, confidently navigate the world of tipping, and make your stay a memorable one!

We hope this comprehensive guide to tipping etiquette at hotels has been helpful and informative. Happy travels and enjoy your hotel experiences with peace of mind knowing you’ve got the tipping game down pat!
