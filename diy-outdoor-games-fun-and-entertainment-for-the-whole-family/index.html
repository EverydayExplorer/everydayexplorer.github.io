DIY Outdoor Games: Fun and Entertainment for the Whole Family


The warm breeze of summer brings with it the promise of outdoor fun and games for the whole family. As the days grow longer and the sun shines brighter, there’s no better time to gather loved ones for some DIY outdoor entertainment. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, planning a picnic in the park, or simply looking to spice up your weekend, here are some exciting DIY outdoor games that are sure to delight everyone, from kids to adults.

1. Giant Jenga: Turn ordinary wooden blocks into a larger-than-life game of skill and strategy. Simply stack the blocks in rows of three, alternating the direction with each layer. Players take turns removing one block at a time from the tower and stacking it on top, without causing the tower to collapse. The anticipation builds with each move, making Giant Jenga a thrilling game for players of all ages.

2. Cornhole: A classic backyard favorite, cornhole is easy to set up and endlessly entertaining. All you need are two inclined boards with a hole at one end and bean bags to toss. Players take turns throwing the bean bags, aiming to land them on the board or, better yet, through the hole. Whether you’re a novice or a seasoned pro, cornhole is guaranteed to provide hours of fun for the whole family.

3. Water Balloon Dodgeball: Beat the heat with this refreshing twist on a traditional game. Fill up balloons with water and divide players into two teams. Set up boundaries and designate a safe zone for each team. The objective is simple: dodge incoming water balloons while trying to hit opponents with your own. With the added element of water, this game is sure to keep everyone cool and laughing all afternoon long.

4. DIY Obstacle Course: Get creative and design your own obstacle course using items found around the house. Set up stations for jumping, crawling, balancing, and more. Use pool noodles as hurdles, hula hoops as targets, and ropes for climbing challenges. Let each family member take turns navigating the course against the clock, or compete head-to-head for some friendly rivalry.

5. Lawn Twister: Turn your backyard into a colorful game board with this DIY twist on the classic game of Twister. Use spray paint to create circles in various colors on the grass, following the same pattern as the original game. Spin the wheel and call out instructions for players to place their hands or feet on different colored circles. The last player standing without falling over wins!

6. Giant Scrabble: Bring the beloved word game outdoors with this oversized version of Scrabble. Cut large squares of cardboard and write a letter on each one. Set up a grid on the ground and let players take turns spelling out words using the giant letter tiles. Keep score just like in the original game and see who can come up with the most creative words.

7. Ring Toss: Test your aim and coordination with this classic carnival game. Create your own ring toss setup using bottles or pegs and rings made from rope or plastic. Arrange the targets at varying distances and assign different point values to each one. Challenge family members to see who can score the most points in a set number of throws.

8. DIY Bowling: Transform empty plastic bottles into a makeshift bowling alley for hours of family fun. Set up the pins in a triangular formation at one end of the yard or driveway. Use a ball, whether it’s a soccer ball, basketball, or even a lightweight bowling ball, to knock down as many pins as possible with each roll. Keep score and see who can achieve the perfect strike.

9. Nature Scavenger Hunt: Explore the great outdoors and engage with nature through a scavenger hunt. Create a list of items for players to find, such as leaves, rocks, flowers, or animal tracks. Arm each participant with a bag or basket and set them loose to search for treasures in the backyard or local park. The first person to find everything on the list wins a prize!

10. DIY Water Slide: Beat the summer heat with a homemade water slide that promises hours of slippery, splashy fun. Use a large tarp or plastic sheeting as the base and set it up on a gentle slope. Secure the edges with stakes or weights to prevent slipping. Add a layer of soap or water for extra slipperiness, then let the kids (and kids at heart) take turns sliding down and cooling off in style.

With these DIY outdoor games, you can turn any gathering into a memorable event filled with laughter, friendly competition, and quality time spent with loved ones. So gather your family and friends, head outside, and let the games begin!
