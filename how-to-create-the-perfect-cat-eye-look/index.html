How to Create the Perfect Cat Eye Look


Are you tired of your everyday makeup routine and looking to add a touch of glamour to your look? The cat eye look is a classic and timeless makeup trend that can instantly elevate your style. Whether you’re heading to a special event or just want to feel a little extra fabulous, mastering the art of the cat eye can make all the difference. In this blog post, we will guide you through the step-by-step process of creating the perfect cat eye look, ensuring that you turn heads wherever you go.

Step 1: Gather Your Tools
Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary tools at hand. You will need an eyeliner pencil or gel liner, an angled brush, and a steady hand. Additionally, having a makeup remover or cotton swabs nearby can be helpful in case of any mistakes.

Step 2: Prep Your Eyes
Start by prepping your eyes with a primer or concealer to create a smooth canvas. This will help your eyeliner glide on effortlessly and stay in place throughout the day. Apply a thin layer of primer or concealer to your eyelids and blend it out using your fingertips or a brush.

Step 3: Choose Your Eyeliner
There are various types of eyeliners available, including pencil, liquid, and gel. Each has its own pros and cons, so choose the one that you feel most comfortable working with. Pencil eyeliners are great for beginners as they offer more control, while liquid and gel eyeliners provide a more precise and intense finish.

Step 4: Start with a Guide
Creating a cat eye can be tricky, especially if you’re new to the technique. To make it easier, start by drawing a small dot or line at the outer corner of your eye, where you want the wing to end. This will act as a guide and help you achieve symmetry on both eyes.

Step 5: Draw the Wing
Using your eyeliner, draw a diagonal line from the outer corner of your eye towards the dot or line you created in the previous step. The length and angle of the wing are up to you, depending on the desired intensity and shape. Take your time and make small strokes to ensure precision.

Step 6: Connect the Wing
Once you have drawn the wing, carefully connect it to your upper lash line. Start from the inner corner of your eye and work your way towards the wing, making sure the line is smooth and even. If you prefer a thicker line, you can go over it a second time to build up the intensity.

Step 7: Fill in the Gap
After connecting the wing to your upper lash line, check for any gaps or unevenness. Fill in any spaces by going over the line again, making sure to maintain a consistent thickness. This step will help create a seamless and polished cat eye look.

Step 8: Optional Lower Lash Line
If you want to intensify your cat eye look, you can also apply eyeliner to your lower lash line. Use a pencil or a small angled brush to draw a thin line, starting from the outer corner and stopping halfway across your eye. This will add extra definition and drama to your overall look.

Step 9: Clean Up and Perfect
Once you have completed both eyes, take a step back and assess the symmetry and overall appearance. If needed, use a cotton swab or a small brush dipped in makeup remover to clean up any smudges or mistakes. You can also use a concealer or highlighter to sharpen the edges and make your cat eye pop.

Step 10: Finish with Mascara
To complete your cat eye look, apply a coat or two of mascara to your upper and lower lashes. This will add volume, length, and definition, making your eyes appear even more captivating. Curl your lashes beforehand for an extra touch of glamour.
