Wine Trends to Watch in 2024


As we enter the exciting realm of 2024, the world of wine continues to evolve, presenting us with new trends and innovations that shape the way we enjoy this beloved beverage. From emerging varietals to sustainable practices, here are some wine trends to keep an eye on in the year ahead.

1. Rise of Indigenous and Lesser-Known Varietals: Wine enthusiasts are increasingly seeking out unique and lesser-known grape varietals that reflect a sense of place and tradition. In 2024, expect to see a surge in interest in indigenous varietals from regions around the world. From Assyrtiko in Greece to Tannat in Uruguay, these wines offer distinctive flavors and characteristics that set them apart from more mainstream varieties.

2. Sustainability Takes Center Stage: As environmental consciousness continues to grow, the wine industry is placing a greater emphasis on sustainability. Wineries are adopting eco-friendly practices, such as organic and biodynamic farming, water conservation, and renewable energy sources. Consumers are also seeking out wines that are certified organic or produced using sustainable methods, driving demand for environmentally responsible products.

3. Popularity of Low-ABV and Alcohol-Free Wines: With health and wellness top of mind for many consumers, there’s a growing interest in low-alcohol and alcohol-free wine options. These wines offer the same great taste as traditional wines but with reduced or no alcohol content, making them suitable for those looking to moderate their alcohol intake or abstain altogether. In 2024, expect to see an expansion of the low-ABV and alcohol-free wine market, with more options available to consumers.

4. Blending Tradition with Innovation: Winemakers are embracing both tradition and innovation, blending classic winemaking techniques with modern technology to produce wines that are both timeless and innovative. From natural fermentation methods to aging in unconventional vessels like clay amphorae, winemakers are experimenting with new approaches to winemaking while honoring centuries-old traditions.

5. Virtual Wine Experiences: The COVID-19 pandemic has accelerated the adoption of virtual wine experiences, allowing consumers to explore the world of wine from the comfort of their own homes. In 2024, expect to see continued growth in virtual wine tastings, online wine courses, and virtual winery tours. These virtual experiences offer a convenient and accessible way for wine lovers to learn, connect, and discover new wines.

6. Wine and Food Pairing Experiences: Wine and food pairing experiences are becoming increasingly popular, as consumers seek to enhance their culinary adventures with the perfect wine accompaniment. In 2024, expect to see more restaurants, wineries, and retailers offering curated wine and food pairing experiences, featuring unique and creative combinations that showcase the versatility of wine.

7. Embracing Diversity and Inclusion: The wine industry is making strides towards greater diversity and inclusion, with initiatives aimed at promoting representation and empowering underrepresented voices. In 2024, expect to see continued efforts to diversify the wine industry workforce, amplify diverse winemakers and wine regions, and create inclusive spaces for wine enthusiasts of all backgrounds.

In conclusion, the world of wine is as dynamic and diverse as ever, with new trends and innovations shaping the way we enjoy and experience this timeless beverage. From indigenous varietals to sustainable practices to virtual wine experiences, there’s never been a more exciting time to explore the world of wine. Cheers to a flavorful and fulfilling 2024!
