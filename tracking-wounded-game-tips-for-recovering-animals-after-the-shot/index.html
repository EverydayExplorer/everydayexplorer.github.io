Tracking Wounded Game: Tips for Recovering Animals After the Shot


Tracking and recovering wounded game is an essential part of ethical hunting. As responsible hunters, it is our duty to ensure a clean and humane shot, as well as follow up swiftly and efficiently to minimize suffering and make a clean kill. In this blog post, we will discuss some useful tips and techniques for tracking wounded game and increasing your chances of successful recovery.

Understanding Shot Placement:

Before we delve into the tracking aspect, it is crucial to understand shot placement and how it affects the animal’s ability to remain mobile after being hit. The lung/heart area is the ideal target for a quick and clean kill. A shot placed in this region will likely result in rapid blood loss, causing the animal to expire swiftly.

However, not all shots are perfect, and sometimes bullets may strike other areas, such as the liver, stomach, or intestines. Although these shots can still be lethal, wounded game can often run a considerable distance before eventually succumbing to their injuries. Understanding this will help you better predict the animal’s behavior and make tracking easier.

Reading Sign:

After taking the shot, give the animal some time to expire before approaching the area. This will prevent you from spooking the animal and potentially causing it to run further. When you start the tracking process, take note of any blood trails, hair, broken branches, or disturbed leaves. All of these signs can provide valuable information regarding the direction of travel and the severity of the wound.

Fresh blood indicates a recent injury, while darker blood suggests an older trail. Pay attention to the color, consistency, and quantity of blood. Bright red and frothy blood could indicate a lung shot, while darker and thicker blood may suggest a liver or muscle hit.

Using Technology:

In recent years, advancements in technology have made tracking wounded game much more efficient. One such tool is a blood tracking light. These lights enhance blood visibility in low light conditions, allowing you to identify even small droplets. Additionally, various smartphone applications can help you plot and track the wounded animal’s path, making the overall process more methodical.

Grid Search Technique:

When you lose the blood trail or have difficulty tracking the wounded animal, using a grid search technique can be highly effective. Divide the search area into smaller grids and search each section meticulously, marking off areas as they are cleared. This systematic approach reduces the chances of missing any signs and increases the probability of finding the animal.

Working with a Tracking Dog:

Another valuable resource for tracking wounded game is a well-trained tracking dog. Dogs have an incredible sense of smell and can follow a blood trail with ease, even if it is faint. If you do not have a dog of your own, consider connecting with local tracking dog handlers who can assist you in finding wounded game. This collaboration harnesses the expertise of both humans and canines, significantly improving the chances of recovery.

Conclusion:

Tracking and recovering wounded game is an essential skill for all hunters. By understanding shot placement, reading sign, leveraging technology, and utilizing systematic search techniques or the expertise of tracking dogs, you can increase your chances of recovering wounded game successfully.

Remember, the primary goal is to make ethical kills and minimize suffering. Respect the animal and the responsibility that comes with hunting. By following these tips and continuously honing your tracking skills, you can become a more proficient hunter while ensuring a humane and ethical approach to the sport.
