The Top 10 Dance Crazes That Took Over the Internet


Dance crazes have been a part of popular culture for decades, but with the rise of social media, they have the power to spread like wildfire and become global sensations in a matter of days. From catchy songs to viral videos, these dance crazes have taken over the internet and inspired millions of people to get up and dance. Here are the top 10 dance crazes that have captured the online world by storm:

  1. The Harlem Shake: Perhaps one of the earliest dance crazes to go viral on the internet, the Harlem Shake involves a group of people dancing wildly to the song ‘Harlem Shake’ by Baauer. The meme took over YouTube in 2013, with countless parody videos popping up left and right.

  2. The Whip/Nae Nae: Silento’s song ‘Watch Me (Whip/Nae Nae)’ sparked a dance craze that had people around the world whipping and nae-naeing in perfect sync. The dance moves were simple yet infectious, making it a hit on social media platforms like Vine and TikTok.

  3. The Dougie: Originating from rapper Doug E. Fresh, the Dougie dance craze became popular after Cali Swag District’s song ‘Teach Me How to Dougie’ hit the airwaves. The dance involves a combination of swaying, shoulder shimmies, and hand movements that had everyone trying to master the moves.

  4. The Renegade: Created by 14-year-old Jalaiah Harmon, the Renegade dance took TikTok by storm in 2020. The intricate choreography became a viral sensation and was performed by celebrities and influencers worldwide.

  5. The Floss: Popularized by the game Fortnite, the Floss dance became a global phenomenon with players and fans alike incorporating the quick arm movements into their own dance routines. The simple yet addictive dance move was seen everywhere from schools to stadiums.

  6. Gangnam Style: Psy’s ‘Gangnam Style’ became a worldwide phenomenon in 2012, with its catchy tune and quirky dance moves inspiring countless parodies and covers. The horse-riding dance move made its way into pop culture and remains one of the most-watched videos on YouTube.

  7. The Running Man Challenge: Set to the song ‘My Boo’ by Ghost Town DJs, the Running Man Challenge had people dancing in place while moving their arms back and forth in a running motion. The trend took off on social media, with athletes, celebrities, and fans joining in on the fun.

  8. The Dab: Popularized by hip-hop artists like Migos and Cam Newton, the Dab became a signature move for anyone looking to strike a pose. The dance move involves tucking your head into the crook of your arm while extending the other arm outwards, creating a sneezing-like motion.

  9. The Git Up: Blanco Brown’s song ‘The Git Up’ inspired a line dance craze that had people of all ages dancing along to the catchy beats. The dance incorporates moves like the two-step, cowboy boogie, and the butterfly, making it a fun and energetic routine to follow.

  10. Baby Shark Dance: While not technically a dance craze, the ‘Baby Shark Dance’ became a global sensation with its infectious tune and simple hand movements. The song’s popularity skyrocketed on YouTube, becoming one of the most-watched videos in internet history.

These 10 dance crazes have managed to captivate audiences around the world, bringing people together through music and movement. Whether you’re a seasoned dancer or just looking to have fun, these viral trends have proven that a good beat and some catchy choreography can unite us all in the joy of dancing.
