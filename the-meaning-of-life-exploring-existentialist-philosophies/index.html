The Meaning of Life: Exploring Existentialist Philosophies


Have you ever wondered about the meaning of life? It’s a question that has intrigued philosophers, thinkers, and individuals throughout the ages. From ancient times to modern-day, humans have been seeking answers to this profound question. In this blog post, we will delve into existentialist philosophies, which provide a unique perspective on the meaning of life.

Existentialism is a philosophical movement that emerged in the 19th and 20th centuries. It emphasizes individual existence, freedom, and the search for meaning in life. Existentialists believe that life has no inherent or predetermined meaning. Instead, they argue that we create our own meaning through our actions and choices.

One of the key figures in existentialist philosophy is Jean-Paul Sartre. In his famous work, ‘Being and Nothingness,’ Sartre delves into the concept of ’existential freedom.’ According to Sartre, human beings are condemned to be free. This means that we are responsible for our own lives and the choices we make. Our existence precedes our essence, and we are constantly in the process of creating ourselves.

Sartre argues that the search for meaning in life can be a source of anguish and despair. The absence of an objective meaning can lead to existential angst, as individuals grapple with the weight of their freedom. However, Sartre believes that this anguish is a necessary part of the human condition. It is through this struggle that we can find our own subjective meaning and authenticity.

Another key figure in existentialist philosophy is Albert Camus. In his influential book, ‘The Myth of Sisyphus,’ Camus explores the absurdity of human existence. He presents the myth of Sisyphus, a Greek mythological character who is condemned to roll a boulder up a hill for eternity, only for it to roll back down each time. Camus argues that life is inherently absurd, like Sisyphus’ futile task. However, he suggests that we can find meaning and happiness in embracing this absurdity.

Camus introduces the concept of ’the absurd hero’ who, like Sisyphus, confronts the absurdity of life with courage and rebellion. The absurd hero recognizes the lack of objective meaning but still chooses to live authentically and embrace life’s challenges. According to Camus, the struggle itself is what gives life meaning. It is through our actions and defiance of the absurd that we can find purpose and fulfillment.

Existentialist philosophies also recognize the importance of personal responsibility and authenticity. In a world without inherent meaning, existentialists argue that we have the freedom to define our own values and take responsibility for our actions. By living authentically, we align our choices with our true selves and create meaning in our lives. Authenticity requires self-awareness, introspection, and the courage to take ownership of our existence.

So, what is the meaning of life according to existentialist philosophies? It is a question that each individual must grapple with and answer for themselves. Existentialism encourages us to confront the existential angst and embrace the absurdity of life. Through our freedom, choices, and authentic living, we can find subjective meanings that resonate with our own values and aspirations.

While existentialism offers a thought-provoking perspective on the meaning of life, it is not without its criticisms. Some argue that existentialism portrays life as bleak and devoid of hope. They contend that this philosophy overlooks the potential for finding meaning in relationships, love, and the pursuit of knowledge. Others criticize existentialism for its individualistic focus, suggesting that it neglects the interconnectedness of human existence.

Ultimately, the search for meaning in life is a deeply personal and subjective endeavor. Existentialist philosophies provide a lens through which we can explore and contemplate our existence. They remind us of the importance of personal responsibility, freedom, and authenticity. Whether we find meaning in relationships, achievements, creativity, or simply in the act of living itself, existentialism prompts us to actively engage with the question and forge our own unique paths.

In conclusion, the meaning of life is a complex and multifaceted concept. Existentialist philosophies offer a unique perspective, emphasizing individual existence, freedom, and the creation of meaning through personal choices. Whether we agree with the existentialist viewpoint or not, it prompts us to reflect on our own lives and the values that guide us. Ultimately, the search for meaning is an ongoing journey, and it is up to each of us to define what gives our lives purpose and significance.
