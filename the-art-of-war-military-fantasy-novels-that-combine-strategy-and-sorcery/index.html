The Art of War: Military Fantasy Novels That Combine Strategy and Sorcery


Warfare has long been a source of fascination for storytellers, inspiring tales of heroism, sacrifice, and triumph against overwhelming odds. In the realm of fantasy literature, authors have taken this fascination to new heights, crafting stories that blend the art of war with the wonder of magic. These military fantasy novels combine strategy and sorcery in thrilling ways, weaving tales of epic battles, cunning tactics, and mystical powers that capture the imagination and transport readers to worlds where anything is possible.

At the heart of military fantasy lies the clash of armies and the struggle for supremacy, where kingdoms rise and fall on the battlefield and heroes are forged in the fires of war. From the sweeping battles of high fantasy epics to the gritty skirmishes of grimdark tales, military fantasy novels explore the intricacies of warfare in all its glory and horror.

One of the most beloved military fantasy series of all time is George R.R. Martin’s ‘A Song of Ice and Fire.’ Set in the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos, the series is renowned for its complex characters, intricate political intrigue, and epic battles. From the War of the Five Kings to the Battle of the Blackwater, Martin’s richly imagined world is a testament to the power of storytelling to capture the brutal realities of war and the enduring human spirit in the face of adversity.

Another standout example of military fantasy is Steven Erikson’s ‘Malazan Book of the Fallen’ series. Set in a sprawling world populated by gods, mages, and soldiers, the series follows the exploits of the Malazan Empire as it expands its influence across continents and clashes with ancient powers. With its intricate plotting, sprawling cast of characters, and epic scope, Erikson’s series is a masterclass in world-building and a gripping exploration of the human cost of war.

But military fantasy is not limited to just these iconic examples – it is a genre that encompasses a wide range of stories and styles. From the epic battles of Brandon Sanderson’s ‘Stormlight Archive’ to the gritty warfare of Joe Abercrombie’s ‘First Law’ trilogy, there is something for every reader to enjoy in the vast expanse of military fantasy literature.

So why are military fantasy novels so compelling? Perhaps it’s because they offer a glimpse into worlds where the stakes are higher, the conflicts more intense, and the heroes more courageous. Or perhaps it’s because they tap into our primal instincts for survival and conquest, inviting us to explore the depths of human nature in times of war.

Whatever the reason, one thing is clear – military fantasy novels have a unique ability to transport readers to worlds where the lines between good and evil are blurred, where heroes are forged in the crucible of battle, and where the fate of nations hangs in the balance. And as long as there are stories to be told of strategy and sorcery on the battlefield, readers will continue to be drawn to the genre for its thrilling escapism and its exploration of the timeless themes of war and redemption.
