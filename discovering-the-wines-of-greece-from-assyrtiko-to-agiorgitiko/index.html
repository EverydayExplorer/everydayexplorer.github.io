Discovering the Wines of Greece: From Assyrtiko to Agiorgitiko


Greece has long been known as the birthplace of elegant civilization, and its rich cultural heritage extends beyond its ancient ruins and breathtaking landscapes. In recent years, Greece has emerged as a vibrant wine-producing country, gaining recognition for its diverse and exceptional wine offerings. From the crisp and refreshing whites of Assyrtiko to the bold and luscious reds of Agiorgitiko, exploring the wines of Greece is like embarking on a journey through history, culture, and the incredible diversity of terroirs.

One of the most celebrated grape varieties in Greece is Assyrtiko. This indigenous white grape is primarily grown on the island of Santorini, where the volcanic soil imparts unique mineral characteristics to the wines. Assyrtiko is known for its high acidity, which gives it a crisp and refreshing character. Its citrus and stone fruit flavors, combined with a distinctive saline quality, make it a perfect match for seafood and Mediterranean cuisine. Assyrtiko can be enjoyed both as a young and vibrant wine or aged for a few years to develop more complex aromas and flavors.

Moving on to the red wines, Agiorgitiko takes center stage. This versatile grape is widely grown in the region of Nemea in the Peloponnese peninsula. Agiorgitiko wines offer a wide range of styles, from light-bodied and fruity to full-bodied and bold. Younger examples exhibit vibrant red fruit flavors, such as cherry and raspberry, with a touch of spice. As these wines age, they develop more complex aromas of dried fruit, leather, and earthiness. Agiorgitiko pairs beautifully with a variety of dishes, including grilled meats, stews, and aged cheeses.

While Assyrtiko and Agiorgitiko are undoubtedly two of the most prominent grape varieties, Greece is home to a wealth of other indigenous grapes that deserve recognition. Malagousia, for instance, is an aromatic white grape with notes of exotic fruits and vibrant acidity. Xinomavro, often referred to as Greece’s answer to Nebbiolo, produces elegant, age-worthy red wines with pronounced tannins and flavors of blackberries and spices. Moschofilero, another aromatic white grape, is known for its floral aromas, crisp acidity, and lively citrus flavors.

Exploring the wines of Greece not only introduces you to new and exciting flavors but also allows you to delve into the country’s fascinating wine history. The ancient Greeks were among the first to recognize the importance of winemaking, and their influence can still be felt in the wine culture of Greece today. The country has a long tradition of producing wine dating back thousands of years, and many winemakers today combine modern winemaking techniques with their rich heritage to create outstanding wines.

When it comes to wine regions, Greece offers an impressive array of microclimates, soil types, and altitudes. From the volcanic soils of Santorini to the mountainous vineyards of Naoussa, each region imparts its own distinct character to the wines. The diverse terroirs of Greece contribute to the complexity and uniqueness of the wines, making them truly special and worthy of exploration.

In recent years, Greek wines have been receiving well-deserved recognition on the international stage. Winemakers have embraced modern technologies and employed sustainable farming practices to craft high-quality wines that showcase the true potential of Greek grape varieties. The efforts of these passionate and dedicated winemakers have led to an increase in exports and a growing interest in Greek wines by wine enthusiasts around the world.

If you’re intrigued by the idea of discovering the wines of Greece, there are several ways to embark on this exciting journey. Many wine shops and online retailers now offer a wide selection of Greek wines, allowing you to explore the different regions and grape varieties from the comfort of your own home. Alternatively, you can visit wine bars and restaurants that specialize in Greek wines, where knowledgeable sommeliers can guide you through a tasting experience that showcases the best that Greece has to offer.

In conclusion, Greece is a country that is not only rich in history and culture but also has a vibrant and diverse wine scene. From the crisp and refreshing Assyrtiko to the bold and luscious Agiorgitiko, Greek wines offer a unique and exciting experience for wine lovers. Whether you’re a connoisseur or a curious beginner, exploring the wines of Greece is an opportunity to discover new flavors, learn about ancient winemaking traditions, and embark on a truly memorable wine journey. So why not raise a glass and toast to the wines of Greece? Cheers!
