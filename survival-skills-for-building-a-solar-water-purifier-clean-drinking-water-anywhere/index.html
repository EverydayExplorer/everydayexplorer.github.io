Survival Skills for Building a Solar Water Purifier: Clean Drinking Water Anywhere


Clean drinking water is essential for our survival, especially in emergency situations or when venturing into remote areas. While there are various methods to purify water, one effective and sustainable solution is a solar water purifier. In this blog post, we will explore the survival skills necessary to build a solar water purifier, allowing you to access clean drinking water anywhere.

Why Choose a Solar Water Purifier?

A solar water purifier harnesses the power of the sun to remove contaminants and make water safe to drink. This method is eco-friendly, cost-effective, and requires minimal equipment. By understanding the following survival skills, you will be able to construct a solar water purifier and ensure access to clean drinking water in challenging situations.

1. Location, Location, Location:
When building a solar water purifier, the first step is selecting an ideal location. Find an area where sunlight is abundant throughout the day, such as an open field or a rooftop. Ensure that the chosen location is away from sources of pollution, such as industrial sites or sewage systems, to avoid further contamination.

2. Gather the Necessary Materials:
To build a solar water purifier, you will need a few key materials. These include a large, clear plastic container, a smaller container for collecting purified water, a tube or hose, activated charcoal, fine mesh or cloth, and a heat-resistant adhesive or sealant.

3. Constructing the Solar Water Purifier:
Begin by cutting a hole in the lid of the larger plastic container, big enough to fit the smaller collection container snugly. Seal the edges of the hole with the heat-resistant adhesive to prevent any leaks. Next, attach the tube or hose to the smaller collection container, ensuring that it reaches the bottom.

4. Filtering Mechanism:
Place a layer of fine mesh or cloth over the opening of the smaller collection container. This will act as a filter, preventing large debris and contaminants from entering the purified water. On top of the mesh, add a layer of activated charcoal. The charcoal will help remove impurities and improve the taste of the water.

5. Solar Disinfection:
Fill the larger container with water from a questionable source, making sure not to overflow it. Place the lid on the container, ensuring that the smaller collection container is securely attached. Position the solar water purifier in the chosen location, where it will receive direct sunlight.

6. Wait and Collect:
Allow the sun’s heat to warm the water within the larger container. As the temperature rises, harmful microorganisms and contaminants will be killed off. The purified water will then condense on the lid and drip into the smaller collection container through the filter mechanism. Collect the clean drinking water from the smaller container as needed.

7. Maintenance and Precautions:
Regularly clean the filter mechanism to ensure optimal performance. Replace the activated charcoal periodically, as it can become saturated over time. Be cautious of extreme weather conditions, as intense heat or freezing temperatures may affect the efficiency of the solar water purifier.

Conclusion:

Building a solar water purifier is a valuable survival skill that provides access to clean drinking water anywhere. By harnessing the power of the sun, this method is eco-friendly, cost-effective, and relatively simple to construct. Remember to choose an ideal location, gather the necessary materials, and follow the steps outlined above to build your own solar water purifier. With this skill in your repertoire, you can ensure access to clean drinking water, even in the most challenging situations. Stay prepared, stay hydrated, and stay safe.
