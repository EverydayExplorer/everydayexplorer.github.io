The Best Fashion Documentaries for a Glimpse into Fashion History


Fashion has always been more than just clothing; it’s a reflection of culture, society, and the ever-evolving human spirit. Whether you’re a fashion enthusiast, a history buff, or simply curious about the world of haute couture, fashion documentaries offer a captivating way to explore the fascinating world of style and design. These documentaries not only delve into the glamour and glitz but also provide insight into the history and artistry that have shaped the fashion industry. So, if you’re ready for a stylish journey through time, here are some of the best fashion documentaries to consider.

1. ‘The September Issue’ (2009)

Directed by R.J. Cutler, ‘The September Issue’ offers an exclusive peek into the world of Vogue magazine as it prepares its iconic September issue. The film primarily follows Anna Wintour, the legendary editor-in-chief, and her team as they navigate the complex world of fashion. It’s a behind-the-scenes look at the meticulous process of creating a fashion bible and the larger-than-life personalities who shape its content.

2. ‘Dior and I’ (2014)

Get ready to step inside the prestigious House of Dior with this documentary directed by Frédéric Tcheng. ‘Dior and I’ chronicles the journey of Raf Simons, a Belgian designer, as he takes over the reins of the fashion house. The film beautifully captures the creative process, the stress, and the dedication required to create a Dior haute couture collection in just eight weeks.

3. ‘Bill Cunningham New York’ (2010)

Bill Cunningham was not just a fashion photographer; he was a true New York City institution. Directed by Richard Press, this documentary pays homage to the iconic street photographer who tirelessly documented fashion trends on the streets of the Big Apple. His candid snapshots of real people and their fashion choices provide a unique perspective on style and individuality.

4. ‘The True Cost’ (2015)

While most fashion documentaries celebrate the beauty of the industry, ‘The True Cost’ by Andrew Morgan takes a different approach. It sheds light on the darker side of fast fashion, exploring the environmental and social impact of our clothing choices. This eye-opening documentary challenges viewers to think more consciously about their fashion consumption.

5. ‘McQueen’ (2018)

Alexander McQueen was one of the most influential and innovative fashion designers of his generation. Directed by Ian Bonhôte and Peter Ettedgui, ‘McQueen’ delves into the life and work of this extraordinary talent. The film not only showcases his groundbreaking designs but also delves into the personal struggles that shaped his creative vision.
