Books for Young Adults: Must-Reads for Teens and Beyond


Books are an essential part of everyone’s life, and it is especially true for young adults. For teenagers, books not only provide an escape from reality but also help them grow and learn vital life lessons. With so many books out there, it can be challenging to know which ones to choose, so to help you out, we have compiled the must-reads for young adults.

1. The Hunger Games by Suzanne Collins

The Hunger Games trilogy is undoubtedly one of the most popular YA book series of all time. The story takes place in a dystopian future where one boy and one girl from each district are selected to compete in a televised fight for survival. The main character, Katniss Everdeen, becomes a hero for her bravery and strength in the face of the Capitol’s cruelty.

2. To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee

To Kill a Mockingbird is a classic and timeless novel that explores themes of racism, injustice, and moral courage. The book is set in the 1930s in Alabama and follows the story of a young girl, Scout Finch, and her brother Jem, as they witness their father, a lawyer, defend a black man in court.

3. The Fault in Our Stars by John Green

The Fault in Our Stars is a heart-wrenching novel about two teenagers, Hazel and Gus, who meet at a cancer support group. It’s a story of love, loss, and the strength of the human spirit. This book is a must-read for anyone who wants to be inspired and moved.

4. The Hate U Give by Angie Thomas

The Hate U Give is a powerful and timely novel that tackles issues of racism, police brutality, and injustice. The book follows the story of 16-year-old Starr Carter, who witnesses the shooting of her unarmed black friend by a white police officer. The book is an essential read for teens and adults alike, as it provides an important and necessary perspective on the Black Lives Matter movement.

5. Harry Potter series by J.K. Rowling

The Harry Potter series is undoubtedly one of the most beloved YA book series of all time. Set in a magical world, the story follows the life of a young wizard, Harry Potter, as he attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. The series is a delightful and enchanting read, filled with adventure, friendship, and lessons on bravery and loyalty.

6. The Perks of Being a Wallflower by Stephen Chbosky

The Perks of Being a Wallflower is a captivating and raw story about a high school freshman, Charlie, who struggles to make friends and fit in. The book is a poignant exploration of adolescence, mental health, and the challenges of growing up.

7. The Giver by Lois Lowry

The Giver is a thought-provoking and insightful novel that explores the importance of individuality, freedom, and memory. The book follows the story of Jonas, who lives in a utopian society where there is no pain, suffering, or emotion. It’s a powerful and moving story that will leave you thinking long after you finish reading it.

In conclusion, these are just a few of the must-reads for young adults and beyond. These books provide insight, inspiration, and perspective on the world we live in. So whether you’re a teen or an adult, pick up one of these books and take a journey into the minds and hearts of some of the most iconic YA characters of all time.
