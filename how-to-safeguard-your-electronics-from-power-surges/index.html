How to Safeguard Your Electronics from Power Surges


Power surges can happen anytime at any place, and they can cause damage to electronic equipment. A sudden power surge can cause a massive electric rush into your electronic equipment and possesses a significant threat to your expensive devices. Power surges can fry your computer, damage TVs, printers, refrigerators, and other appliances. Some of these power surges can be preventable; however, others cannot be avoided due to external factors. In this article, we will discuss how to safeguard your electronics from power surges.

What Is Power Surge?

A power surge is a sudden increase in electrical voltage that goes beyond the standard level and usually occurs within a fraction of a second. Power surges can occur due to various reasons such as lightning, power outage, or when turning on large appliances. When the surge of electricity enters your devices, it causes an overload of electricity, leading to damage to electronics. Power surges can also strike data centers, servers, and other essential devices that need protection from power surges.

Steps to Safeguard Your Electronics from Power Surges

1. Use a Surge Protector

A surge protector is the easiest and most effective way to safeguard your electronic devices from power surges. A surge protector is a power strip with a built-in surge protection mechanism that prevents damage to your electronic devices. You can place your computer, TV, printer, and other electronic devices into the surge protector, which will divert the power surge to the grounding wire instead of your devices, providing protection from power surges.

2. Unplug Electronic Devices

When a storm is approaching, it’s best to unplug your electronics from the power source. Unplugging your devices will prevent power surges from entering your appliances and causing damage. Although unplugging your electronics can be inconvenient, it’s better to be safe than to replace electronics every time a storm strikes.

3. Install A Whole-House Surge Protector

A whole-house surge protector is another way to safeguard your electronic devices from power surges. A whole-house surge protector is installed at the circuit breaker panel and can protect all electronic devices from power surges. It’s best to leave the installation of a whole-house surge protector to a professional electrician.

4. Keep Your Electrical System Updated

An outdated electrical system is more susceptible to power surges. Faulty wiring, worn-out switches, and old electrical equipment can cause surges to occur. Keeping your electrical systems updated with new wiring, switches, and equipment will reduce the risk of power surges from happening.

5. Use the Correct Wattage for Electronic Devices

Each electronic device in your home has a proper wattage limit. Overloading the wattage limit on your devices can cause power surges, which can lead to damage to your devices. Ensure that you use the correct wattage for your electronic devices to prevent power surges from happening.

Conclusion

In conclusion, power surges can cause damage to your electronic devices and appliances. Preventing power surges is an easy task that requires proper safeguards. The steps mentioned above will help make sure your electronic devices are protected from power surges and safe from damage. By using a surge protector, unplugging your electronics, installing a whole-house surge protector, keeping your electrical systems updated, and using the right wattage for your devices, you will avoid power surge damage and costly replacements.
