Getting Animated: Tips for Aspiring Cartoonists


Are you passionate about animation? Do you dream of bringing your characters to life on the screen? If so, you’re not alone. Many aspiring cartoonists share your love for this unique art form. However, breaking into the animation industry can be a daunting task. That’s why we’ve compiled a list of helpful tips to guide you in your journey to becoming an animated cartoonist.

## 1. Develop Your Artistic Skills

Before diving into the world of animation, it’s essential to have a strong foundation in drawing and illustration. Take the time to hone your artistic skills and learn the principles of design. Practice drawing different characters and experiment with various styles to discover your own unique voice as an artist. Building a solid artistic foundation will lay the groundwork for your future success as an animator.

## 2. Study the Fundamentals of Animation

Animation is much more than just drawing still images. It involves bringing those images to life through movement and timing. Take the time to study the fundamentals of animation, such as squash and stretch, timing, and anticipation. Understanding these principles will help you create more dynamic and engaging animations. There are plenty of online resources and tutorials available that can guide you through the intricacies of animation.

## 3. Learn Animation Software

In today’s digital age, many animators work with animation software to bring their creations to life. Familiarize yourself with industry-standard software such as Adobe Animate, Toon Boom Harmony, or Blender. These tools offer a wide range of features and functionalities that can make your animation process more efficient and professional-looking. Take the time to learn the ins and outs of the software and explore its capabilities.

## 4. Build a Strong Portfolio

A portfolio is a crucial component of showcasing your skills and attracting potential clients or employers. Gather your best artwork and animations and curate them into an impressive portfolio. Include a variety of styles and demonstrate your versatility as an artist. Your portfolio should highlight your strengths and show potential clients what you can bring to the table. Keep your portfolio up to date and constantly strive to improve and expand your body of work.

## 5. Network within the Animation Community

Building connections within the animation industry can open doors to opportunities you may have never considered. Attend industry events, join online communities, and participate in animation forums to connect with fellow animators, industry professionals, and enthusiasts. Sharing your work, receiving feedback, and collaborating with others can enhance your skills and expose you to new perspectives and ideas. Networking is crucial in any creative field, and animation is no exception.

## 6. Seek Feedback and Learn from Criticism

Constructive criticism can be your best friend when it comes to improving your craft. Don’t be afraid to seek feedback from experienced animators or industry professionals. They can provide valuable insights and help you identify areas for improvement. Embrace criticism as an opportunity to grow and refine your skills. Learning to accept feedback gracefully and incorporate it into your work will ultimately make you a better animator.

## 7. Never Stop Learning

The animation industry is constantly evolving, with new techniques and technologies emerging all the time. As an aspiring cartoonist, it’s essential to stay updated with the latest trends and advancements in your field. Continuously seek out learning opportunities, whether it’s attending workshops, taking online courses, or following industry blogs and podcasts. The more knowledgeable and adaptable you are, the better equipped you’ll be to succeed in the ever-changing world of animation.

## 8. Be Persistent and Passionate

Breaking into the animation industry can be a challenging and competitive endeavor, but don’t let that discourage you. Be persistent in pursuing your dreams and never lose sight of your passion for animation. Keep creating, learning, and evolving as an artist. Your dedication and enthusiasm will shine through in your work and set you apart from the crowd.

In conclusion, becoming an animated cartoonist requires dedication, hard work, and perseverance. Develop your skills, study the fundamentals, learn animation software, build a strong portfolio, network with others in the industry, seek feedback, and never stop learning. Remember to stay passionate and committed to your craft, and success will be within reach. So, go ahead and bring your animated dreams to life! Good luck!
