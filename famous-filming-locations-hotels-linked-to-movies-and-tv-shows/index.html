Famous Filming Locations: Hotels Linked to Movies and TV Shows


Lights, camera, action! When it comes to creating a memorable movie or TV show, the location plays a crucial role. And what better place to set the scene than in the luxurious and iconic hotels around the world? In this blog post, we’ll take you on a virtual tour of some of the most famous filming locations that are nestled within these remarkable hotels.

1. The Plaza Hotel, New York City
‘The Great Gatsby’ (2013), ‘Home Alone 2: Lost in New York’ (1992), and ‘The Way We Were’ (1973) all have one thing in common - they were filmed at The Plaza Hotel. This timeless landmark has been a favorite among filmmakers for its grandeur and elegance. With its stunning architecture and opulent interiors, it’s no wonder this hotel has become synonymous with luxury and glamour.

2. The Beverly Wilshire, Beverly Hills
If you’ve ever watched ‘Pretty Woman’ (1990) starring Julia Roberts and Richard Gere, then you’re already familiar with the Beverly Wilshire. Located right in the heart of Beverly Hills, this hotel has served as the backdrop for countless movies and TV shows. Its beautiful exterior and upscale interiors make it the perfect setting for stories that revolve around romance and sophistication.

3. The Taj Mahal Palace, Mumbai
When it comes to Indian cinema, the Taj Mahal Palace in Mumbai is an absolute icon. With its breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea and its rich history, this hotel has been featured in several Bollywood movies over the years. Notable films include ‘Race’ (2008), ‘Dhoom 3’ (2013), and ‘Wake Up Sid’ (2009). It’s not just the picturesque location that attracts filmmakers; it’s the sense of grandeur and cultural significance that this hotel emanates.

4. The Fairmont San Francisco, California
The Fairmont San Francisco has had its fair share of appearances on both the big and small screens. You might recognize it from movies like ‘Vertigo’ (1958), ‘The Rock’ (1996), and ‘Dirty Harry’ (1971). This iconic hotel offers stunning panoramic views of the city and serves as a testament to the rich history and architectural beauty of San Francisco.

5. The Park Hyatt Tokyo, Japan
Remember the critically acclaimed movie ‘Lost in Translation’ (2003) starring Bill Murray and Scarlett Johansson? Well, most of the film was shot at the Park Hyatt Tokyo. This luxurious hotel, situated in the bustling Shinjuku district, provides an exquisite backdrop for this tale of love and self-discovery. With its sleek design and breathtaking views of the Tokyo skyline, it’s no wonder that it captured the attention of director Sofia Coppola.

6. The Grand Hotel, Mackinac Island
If you’re a fan of classic movies, you’ll surely recognize The Grand Hotel from ‘Somewhere in Time’ (1980) starring Christopher Reeve and Jane Seymour. This Victorian-era hotel, located on Mackinac Island in Michigan, transports visitors back in time with its old-world charm and elegant ambiance. The picturesque setting and romantic atmosphere make it a favorite filming location for period dramas and romantic films.

7. The Ritz-Carlton, Half Moon Bay, California
Perched atop a cliff overlooking the Pacific Ocean, The Ritz-Carlton, Half Moon Bay has become a popular choice for filmmakers looking to capture the natural beauty of the California coastline. Movies such as ‘Chasing Mavericks’ (2012) and ‘Bandits’ (2001) have showcased the stunning vistas and dramatic cliffs that surround this luxurious resort. It’s a perfect blend of natural wonders and modern luxury.

These are just a few examples of famous filming locations that happen to be situated in some of the world’s most prestigious hotels. From Hollywood blockbusters to Bollywood extravaganzas, these hotels have witnessed the creation of cinematic magic. So the next time you find yourself checking into one of these iconic establishments, remember that you might just be walking in the footsteps of your favorite actors and actresses. Lights, camera, hotel!
