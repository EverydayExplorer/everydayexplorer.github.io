Bookish Resolutions: Setting Reading Goals for the New Year


New Year’s resolutions are a common tradition, often centered around personal growth and self-improvement. While many people focus on health and fitness goals, why not make reading a priority in the upcoming year? Setting bookish resolutions can help you expand your literary horizons, discover new authors, and find solace in the written word. In this blog post, we will explore the importance of setting reading goals, tips for creating achievable resolutions, and recommendations for diverse genres to explore.

Why Set Reading Goals?

Reading is not only a source of entertainment but also a gateway to knowledge and personal development. By setting reading goals, you create a structured approach to your reading habits and ensure that you dedicate time to indulge in your favorite pastime. Additionally, reading goals encourage you to explore different genres and authors, broadening your literary repertoire.

Tips for Creating Achievable Resolutions

When setting reading goals for the new year, it’s important to be realistic and consider your personal preferences and lifestyle. Here are some tips to help you create achievable bookish resolutions:

  1. Assess your reading habits: Take a moment to reflect on your reading habits from the past year. How many books did you read? What genres did you enjoy? Understanding your preferences will help you set goals that align with your interests.

  2. Set a target number of books: Determine how many books you would like to read in the upcoming year. Be mindful of your schedule and commitments, ensuring that your goal is attainable. Whether it’s 12 books, 24 books, or even 50 books, set a target that challenges you without overwhelming your reading schedule.

  3. Explore diverse genres: Make it a goal to step out of your comfort zone and explore genres you haven’t tried before. Whether it’s science fiction, historical fiction, or poetry, diversifying your reading choices can open up new worlds and perspectives.

  4. Join a book club or reading challenge: Engaging with a community of readers can enhance your reading experience and keep you motivated to achieve your goals. Join a local book club or participate in online reading challenges to connect with fellow bookworms and share recommendations.

  5. Create a reading schedule: Plan dedicated reading time in your daily or weekly routine. Whether it’s waking up 30 minutes earlier to read or setting aside an hour before bed, having a designated reading time will help you stay on track with your goals.

Recommended Genres for Bookish Resolutions

To help kickstart your reading goals, here are some diverse genres worth exploring in the new year:

  1. Historical fiction: Immerse yourself in different time periods and learn about history through captivating narratives.

  2. Science fiction: Venture into the realms of futuristic technology, alternate realities, and thought-provoking concepts.

  3. Mystery and thriller: Indulge in suspenseful page-turners that will keep you guessing until the very end.

  4. Non-fiction: Expand your knowledge and understanding of the world by delving into thought-provoking memoirs, biographies, or books on specific subjects of interest.

  5. Fantasy: Escape into magical worlds filled with mythical creatures, epic quests, and intricate world-building.

Remember, the purpose of bookish resolutions is to enhance your reading experience and encourage personal growth. Be flexible with your goals, allow yourself to deviate from your reading list, and most importantly, enjoy the journey.

So, as the new year approaches, take the opportunity to set bookish resolutions and embark on a literary adventure. With a clear vision and achievable goals, you’ll be well on your way to a year filled with captivating stories, newfound knowledge, and a deeper appreciation for the written word. Happy reading!
