Astronomical Events Calendar: Not-to-Miss Celestial Phenomena


As we look up at the night sky and marvel at the sparkling stars that we can see, it’s incredible to think about all of the astronomical events that are happening above us that we can’t see with the naked eye. However, there are some celestial phenomena that occur less frequently and with greater intensity, providing us with spectacular opportunities to witness these natural cosmic events.

Whether you’re an amateur astronomer or someone with a passing fascination with the mysteries of the universe, a good astronomical calendar will ensure you never miss an opportunity to witness some of the most awe-inspiring sights in the sky. Below are some of the most incredible astronomical events that you need to put in your calendar and not miss out on.

1. Total Solar Eclipse

The total solar eclipse occurs when the moon passes between the sun and the Earth, casting a shadow on the Earth’s surface. When the moon covers the sun, it creates a stunning moment of darkness, a moment which has been described as one of the most beautiful natural events in the world. The next total solar eclipse will occur on December 4, 2021, and will be visible from Antarctica, the Southern Ocean, and the coastal areas of South Africa, Australia, and South America.

2. Meteor Showers

Meteor showers occur when the Earth passes through a stream of debris left behind by a comet. When debris enters our atmosphere, it creates a beautiful display of light in the sky, which we call meteor showers. Some of the most popular meteor showers include Perseids, Geminids, and Leonids. The next Perseids meteor shower will occur on August 12, 2021, and will be visible from most areas in the Northern Hemisphere.

3. Super Moons

Super Moons occur when the moon is at its closest point to Earth and appears larger than usual. Due to the moon’s elliptical orbit, Super Moons can occur several times a year. The next Super Moon will occur on May 26, 2021, and will be the closest full moon of the year.

4. Planetary Alignments

Planetary alignments occur when two or more planets appear in close proximity to each other in the night sky. These events happen every few years and can be a spectacular sight for stargazers. The next planetary alignment that will occur will be on March 5, 2022, when Jupiter, Saturn, and Mercury appear in close proximity to each other.

5. Annular Solar Eclipse

An annular solar eclipse is a rare and spectacular sight that occurs when the moon passes between the sun and the Earth but does not cover the sun completely, leaving a “ring of fire” visible around the edges of the sun. The next annular solar eclipse will occur on October 14, 2023, and will be visible from parts of the United States, Central America, and South America.

6. Transit of Venus

The Transit of Venus is a rare event that occurs when Venus passes between the Earth and the sun, appearing as a small black disk moving across the sun’s surface. This event is much rarer than a solar eclipse and only occurs in pairs, separated by eight years and thousands of kilometers. The next Transit of Venus will not occur until December 2117.

Final Thoughts

There’s something endlessly fascinating about gazing up at the night sky and contemplating the vastness and beauty of the universe. Whether you are an avid astronomer or just appreciate the unbridled power of nature, there are some astronomical events you can’t afford to miss. From solar eclipses to meteor showers, these celestial phenomena offer a glimpse into the vastness of the cosmos and are sure to leave you feeling amazed and inspired. So mark your calendars and make sure to witness these incredible astronomical events when they occur.
