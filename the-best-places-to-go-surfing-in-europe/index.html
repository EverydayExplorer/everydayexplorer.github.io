The Best Places to Go Surfing in Europe


Welcome to our exciting blog where we dive into the captivating world of surfing in Europe! Whether you’re a seasoned surfer or just starting out, Europe offers a plethora of amazing surf spots that are bound to make your heart race and your feet itch to ride those waves. From the rugged coastlines of Portugal to the breathtaking beaches of France, let’s take a virtual journey through the best places to go surfing in Europe.

1. Peniche, Portugal:
Located on Portugal’s stunning Silver Coast, Peniche is known as the surfing capital of Portugal. With consistent waves and a variety of breaks suitable for all levels, this picturesque fishing town is a surfer’s paradise. The legendary Supertubos beach is a must-visit for experienced surfers, offering powerful and hollow waves that will challenge even the most skilled riders.

2. Biarritz, France:
Nestled in the heart of the French Basque Country, Biarritz is renowned for its rich surfing culture and incredible beach breaks. With its long sandy beaches and consistent swells, this vibrant town attracts surfers of all levels. The famous Côte des Basques beach is a favorite among beginners, providing gentle waves to practice and improve your skills.

3. Bundoran, Ireland:
If you’re looking for an off-the-beaten-path surfing destination, Bundoran on Ireland’s Wild Atlantic Way is the place for you. This small town boasts some of the best waves in Europe, thanks to its exposure to the powerful North Atlantic swells. The world-class reef breaks at The Peak and the beach breaks at Tullan Strand offer fantastic opportunities for intermediate and advanced surfers.

4. Zarautz, Spain:
Situated in the heart of the Basque Country, Zarautz is a laid-back coastal town offering an ideal mix of surf, sun, and culture. With its long sandy beach and consistent waves, Zarautz caters to surfers of all levels. The beach break offers accessible waves for beginners and intermediates, while the outer reefs provide more challenging conditions for advanced riders.

5. Newquay, United Kingdom:
Newquay in Cornwall, England, is a renowned surf destination with a vibrant surf scene and a variety of world-class breaks. Fistral Beach, one of Europe’s most famous surf spots, offers consistent waves all year round. With its sandy beach and reliable swells, it’s the perfect place for surfers of all levels to catch their first wave or improve their skills.

6. Ericeira, Portugal:
Just a short drive from Lisbon, Ericeira is a charming fishing village that is quickly gaining recognition as one of Europe’s top surfing destinations. This World Surfing Reserve is home to a variety of breaks suitable for surfers of all levels. From the powerful reef breaks at Ribeira d’Ilhas to the mellow beach breaks at Foz do Lizandro, Ericeira has something for everyone.

7. Hossegor, France:
Known as the surfing capital of Europe, Hossegor is a must-visit destination for surf enthusiasts. Located on the southwest coast of France, this lively town boasts some of the best beach breaks in the world. La Gravière, a powerful and hollow wave, attracts the most skilled surfers, while La Nord offers long and consistent rides suitable for all levels.

Europe truly has it all when it comes to surfing. Whether you prefer powerful reef breaks, mellow beach breaks, or anything in between, you’ll find a spot that suits your surfing style. So, grab your board, pack your wetsuit, and get ready for an unforgettable surf adventure in one of these incredible European destinations!
