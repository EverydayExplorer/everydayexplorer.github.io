The Influence of Mythology on Contemporary Literature: 10 Books that Draw on Ancient Tales


Mythology has always captivated our collective imagination. Whether it’s the ancient stories of Greek gods and heroes or the rich tales of Norse myth, these ancient tales continue to resonate with us today. Over the centuries, these mythological stories have found new life in contemporary literature, inspiring authors to explore and reimagine them in fresh and exciting ways. In this blog post, we will dive into the realm of mythology and explore 10 books that draw on these ancient tales, showcasing their enduring influence on contemporary literature.

1. ‘American Gods’ by Neil Gaiman:
Neil Gaiman takes readers on a tumultuous journey through the heart of America’s mythical landscape. In ‘American Gods,’ he weaves together a complex tapestry of gods from different mythologies, exploring how they adapt and evolve in the modern world.

2. ‘The Song of Achilles’ by Madeline Miller:
‘The Song of Achilles’ beautifully retells the epic love story between Achilles and Patroclus from Homer’s ‘The Iliad.’ Miller’s lyrical prose and rich character development make this retelling a modern masterpiece that brings ancient Greece to life.

3. ‘Circe’ by Madeline Miller:
Madeline Miller strikes gold once again with her enchanting retelling of the story of Circe, the witch from Homer’s ‘Odyssey.’ In this book, she gives a voice to a character often overlooked in the original myth and explores themes of power, identity, and womanhood.

4. ‘Percy Jackson & The Olympians’ series by Rick Riordan:
Rick Riordan’s bestselling series introduces readers to a modern-day world where the gods of Olympus are alive and well. Through the adventures of Percy Jackson, Riordan seamlessly blends ancient mythology with a contemporary setting, capturing the hearts of young readers worldwide.

5. ‘The Mists of Avalon’ by Marion Zimmer Bradley:
‘The Mists of Avalon’ breathes new life into the Arthurian legends by giving voice to the female characters. This novel explores the complex relationships between Morgaine, Guinevere, and Arthur, providing a fresh perspective on the ancient tales.

6. ‘The Penelopiad’ by Margaret Atwood:
In ‘The Penelopiad,’ Margaret Atwood gives a voice to Odysseus’ wife, Penelope. Through Penelope’s perspective, Atwood explores myth, storytelling, and the roles of women in ancient Greece, offering a compelling and thought-provoking read.

7. ‘The King Must Die’ by Mary Renault:
Mary Renault’s retelling of the myth of Theseus masterfully captures the hero’s journey and his transformation from a young boy to a legendary figure. Set against the backdrop of ancient Greece, ‘The King Must Die’ is a gripping and immersive exploration of myth and legend.

8. ‘Gods Behaving Badly’ by Marie Phillips:
Marie Phillips puts a comedic twist on mythology with ‘Gods Behaving Badly.’ In this satirical novel, the Greek gods are living in modern-day London, struggling to adapt to their diminished powers and navigating the challenges of contemporary life.

9. ‘The Alchemist’ by Paulo Coelho:
While not directly drawing on ancient mythology, ‘The Alchemist’ delves into the realms of symbolism and mysticism found in many myths. Coelho’s allegorical tale follows a young shepherd on a transformative journey across the desert as he pursues his personal legend.

10. ‘Norse Mythology’ by Neil Gaiman:
Neil Gaiman once again shines a spotlight on mythology, this time exploring the captivating world of Norse myth. Through skillful storytelling, Gaiman brings to life the ancient tales of Odin, Thor, Loki, and other Norse gods, reminding us of their enduring significance.

These ten books are just a glimpse into the vast and varied ways that mythology continues to inspire contemporary literature. Whether it’s through poignant retellings, imaginative reinterpretations, or clever reinventions, authors are continually drawn to the enduring power of these ancient tales. By tapping into the rich tapestry of mythology, these books bridge the gap between past and present, offering readers a deeper understanding of our shared human experience and the timeless themes that resonate through the ages. So the next time you pick up a book with mythological roots, remember the ancient tales that continue to shape our literary landscape.
