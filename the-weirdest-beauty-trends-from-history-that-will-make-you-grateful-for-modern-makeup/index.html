The Weirdest Beauty Trends from History That Will Make You Grateful for Modern Makeup


In today’s world, we have access to an incredible array of makeup products that help us enhance our natural beauty. From foundations and concealers to eyeshadows and lipsticks, the possibilities are endless. But have you ever wondered how people achieved beauty in the past, before the advent of modern makeup? Well, get ready to be amazed and grateful for the beauty trends we have today, as we explore some of the weirdest beauty trends from history.

1. Lead-Based Makeup: Back in ancient Rome, lead-based makeup was all the rage. Women would apply a mixture of lead and chalk to their faces to achieve a pale complexion. Little did they know that lead is highly toxic and can cause serious health issues, including lead poisoning. Thankfully, we now have safer alternatives to achieve a flawless complexion.

2. Eyebrow Shaving: In the 18th century, women in England followed a peculiar beauty trend of completely shaving off their eyebrows. They would then use mouse fur or even mouse skin to create false eyebrows. This bizarre trend was believed to give women a more youthful appearance. Today, we can shape and groom our eyebrows using various techniques without resorting to extreme measures.

3. Mercury for Skin Whitening: In ancient China, women would use mercury to achieve a fair complexion. They believed that mercury could lighten their skin tone and remove blemishes. However, prolonged exposure to mercury can cause severe damage to the nervous system and kidneys. We are fortunate to have a wide range of safe skin lightening products available today.

4. Blistering Agents for Rosy Cheeks: During the Victorian era, women desired rosy cheeks as a sign of good health and youthfulness. To achieve this, they would apply blistering agents such as Spanish fly or mustard to their faces. These substances caused skin irritation, blistering, and even burns. Thankfully, we now have blushes and bronzers that can give us a natural-looking flush without any harm.

5. Powdered Wigs: In the 17th and 18th centuries, both men and women in Europe wore powdered wigs as a symbol of status and fashion. These wigs were often made from horsehair or human hair and were heavily powdered with scented flour or starch. While they added a touch of elegance, they were also a breeding ground for lice and other pests. Today, we can achieve fabulous hairstyles without resorting to wigs.

6. Poisonous Lipsticks: In the early 20th century, some lipsticks contained harmful ingredients such as radium, which is highly radioactive. Women would apply these lipsticks, unaware of the potential health risks. Thankfully, modern lipsticks are rigorously tested and free from such dangerous substances.

7. Foot Binding: Although not directly related to makeup, foot binding was a beauty trend in ancient China that cannot go unnoticed. Women would bind their feet tightly to achieve small, delicate feet, which were considered attractive. This painful practice caused lifelong disabilities and health issues. Today, we embrace body positivity and appreciate our natural beauty without resorting to such extreme measures.

As we look back at these bizarre beauty trends from history, we can’t help but feel grateful for the advancements in modern makeup. We have a wide range of safe and effective products that allow us to express our individuality and enhance our features without compromising our health. So, let’s celebrate the wonders of modern makeup and embrace the beauty trends that make us feel confident and empowered.
