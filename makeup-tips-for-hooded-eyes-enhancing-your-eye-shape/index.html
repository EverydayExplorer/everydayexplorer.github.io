Makeup Tips for Hooded Eyes: Enhancing Your Eye Shape


Hooded eyes can sometimes pose a challenge when it comes to makeup application. The droopy or hooded shape of the eyelid can make it difficult to achieve certain eye makeup looks. But fear not, because with the right techniques and products, you can enhance your eye shape and create stunning eye makeup looks that will make your eyes pop. In this blog post, we will be sharing some makeup tips specifically tailored for hooded eyes.

1. Start with a good eye primer:
A good eye primer is key to making your eye makeup last all day and prevent it from creasing or smudging, especially if you have hooded eyes. The primer creates a smooth canvas for your eyeshadow and helps it adhere better. It also prevents your eyeshadow from transferring onto your upper lid. Apply a thin layer of eye primer all over your eyelids and allow it to dry before applying any eyeshadow.

2. Use matte or satin eyeshadows:
When it comes to eyeshadows for hooded eyes, it’s best to stick with matte or satin finishes. Shimmery or metallic eyeshadows tend to emphasize the hooded shape and can make your eyes look even more droopy. Opt for eyeshadows in neutral or warm tones that will help brighten your eyes and make them appear more lifted. Avoid applying dark eyeshadows all over your eyelid, as this can make your eyes look smaller. Instead, focus on applying lighter shades on the inner corner and on the center of the lid to create the illusion of bigger, more open eyes.

3. Apply a transition shade:
To create depth and dimension on hooded eyes, it is crucial to use a transition shade. This shade should be slightly darker than your skin tone and blended into the crease area. Applying a transition shade helps to define the crease and acts as a guide for the rest of your eyeshadow application. Use a fluffy blending brush to apply the transition shade and blend it outwards towards the brow bone.

4. Lift the eyes with a cut-crease technique:
The cut-crease technique is an excellent way to enhance the eyelid space for hooded eyes. This technique involves applying a contrasting color in the crease and creating a sharp line to make it appear lifted. Using a small angled brush, apply a darker eyeshadow shade along the hooded part of your lid, creating a defined line. Blend the color upwards towards the brow bone, making sure to soften any harsh edges. This technique creates the illusion of a higher crease and makes your eyes look more open.

5. Winged eyeliner:
Winged eyeliner is a classic makeup technique that works wonders for hooded eyes. Applying a winged eyeliner helps to elongate the eyes and draw attention away from the hooded part of the lid. Start by drawing a thin line along the lash line, gradually making it thicker towards the outer corner of the eye. Then, extend the line slightly upwards and outwards, creating a wing. Make sure to keep the wing subtle and not too dramatic to avoid overwhelming the eye shape.

6. Tightline the upper waterline:
Tightlining is a technique where you apply eyeliner to the upper waterline, which is the area between the lashes. This technique helps to define the eyes and make your lashes appear fuller. Use a waterproof eyeliner pencil in a dark shade and gently apply it to the upper waterline. Be careful not to poke your eye and take your time to ensure a precise application.

7. Curl your lashes and use a lengthening mascara:
Curling your lashes can instantly lift your hooded eyes and make them appear more awake. Invest in a good eyelash curler and use it before applying mascara. After curling your lashes, apply a lengthening mascara that will add volume and length to your lashes. Make sure to coat both the upper and lower lashes to create balance and prevent your eyes from looking top-heavy.

8. Define your brows:
Well-defined eyebrows can frame your eyes and make them stand out even more. Use an eyebrow pencil or powder to fill in any sparse areas in your brows and create a clean, defined shape. For hooded eyes, it’s best to opt for a slightly arched brow shape as it helps lift the eye area. Brush your brows upwards with a spoolie brush to create a natural, feathery look.

Remember, practice makes perfect when it comes to makeup application for hooded eyes. Experiment with different techniques and find what works best for your eye shape. With the right products, tools, and techniques, you can enhance your hooded eyes and create stunning eye makeup looks that will make you feel confident and beautiful.
