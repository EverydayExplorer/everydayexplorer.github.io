The Beauty of Plein Air Pastel: Capturing Softness and Light


When it comes to creating art, there are countless mediums to choose from. Each medium has its own unique qualities, allowing artists to express their creativity in different ways. One medium that stands out for its ability to capture softness and light is plein air pastel.

Plein air, a French term meaning ‘in the open air,’ refers to the practice of painting outdoors, directly from nature. This approach allows artists to immerse themselves in the beauty of their surroundings, capturing the essence and atmosphere of the scene before them. And when it comes to pastel, this medium truly shines in capturing the delicate interplay of softness and light.

Pastels are made from pure pigment mixed with a binder, resulting in vibrant and intense colors. This characteristic makes them perfect for capturing the nuanced variations of light and shadow found in outdoor scenes. The soft and velvety texture of pastel sticks allows artists to layer and blend colors seamlessly, creating a sense of depth and dimension.

One of the key aspects that makes plein air pastel so captivating is its ability to capture the softness of light. When painting outdoors, artists are exposed to the ever-changing qualities of natural light. Whether it’s the warm glow of sunrise or the soft hues of a sunset, pastel allows artists to recreate these fleeting moments with remarkable accuracy.

The opacity of pastel also lends itself to capturing the ethereal quality of softness. Whether it’s the gentle touch of a flower petal or the wispy clouds in the sky, pastel enables artists to convey these delicate details with precision. The medium’s ability to blend and layer colors creates a softness that is unparalleled, giving the artwork a dreamlike quality.

Another advantage of plein air pastel is its versatility. Artists can work with a wide range of pastel colors, from vibrant and bold to subtle and muted. This versatility allows artists to accurately depict the different moods and atmospheres of the outdoor scene they are capturing.

Furthermore, plein air pastel offers artists the opportunity to connect with nature on a deeper level. By painting outdoors, artists are able to experience the sights, sounds, and even the smells of their surroundings. This immersive experience not only enhances the creative process but also adds an authentic touch to the artwork.

In conclusion, plein air pastel is a medium that beautifully captures the softness and light found in nature. From the delicate strokes of a flower to the gentle hues of a sunset, pastel allows artists to recreate these fleeting moments with remarkable accuracy. The versatility of the medium, combined with its ability to blend and layer colors, creates a sense of depth and dimension that is truly captivating. So, the next time you find yourself inspired by the beauty of the outdoors, consider picking up a pastel stick and immersing yourself in the world of plein air pastel.
