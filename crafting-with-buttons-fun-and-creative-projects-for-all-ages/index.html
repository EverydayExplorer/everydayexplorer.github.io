Crafting with Buttons: Fun and Creative Projects for All Ages


Crafting is a great way to relax, tap into your creative side, and create something beautiful. If you’re looking for a simple and versatile craft supply, look no further than buttons. Buttons are not only functional, but they can also be used to add a touch of whimsy and charm to any project. In this blog post, we’ll explore various crafting ideas using buttons that are fun and suitable for all ages.

1. Button Bracelets:
Button bracelets are a trendy and fashionable accessory that can be customized to reflect your personal style. Simply thread colorful buttons onto elastic cord or thread and tie a knot at the ends to secure the buttons. You can mix and match different sizes, shapes, and colors of buttons to create a unique and eye-catching bracelet.

2. Button Flowers:
Button flowers are a delightful way to brighten up your home or to use as party decorations. Start by gluing a large button in the center of a cardboard circle. Then, arrange smaller buttons around the large button to resemble flower petals. You can use a variety of colors to create a vibrant bouquet or stick to one color scheme for a more elegant look. Attach a green pipe cleaner or wire to the back of the flower to create a stem.

3. Button Art:
Have an old canvas lying around? Why not transform it into a beautiful piece of button art? You can create a simple design, such as a heart or a star, or let your imagination run wild and create intricate mosaics using different sizes and shapes of buttons. Adhere the buttons to the canvas using a strong adhesive, and voila! You now have a stunning piece of art that can be displayed on your wall.

4. Button Magnets:
Personalize your fridge or magnetic board with button magnets. All you need are small magnets, preferably with adhesive backs, and some buttons. Simply attach the magnets to the back of the buttons and let them dry. You can mix and match colors or create themed sets, such as floral or animal-inspired magnets. These magnets not only serve a purpose but also add a touch of charm to your space.

5. Button Keychains:
Add a touch of whimsy to your keys with button keychains. To create a basic keychain, attach a small jump ring through the buttonholes and add a keyring or a small chain. You can also get creative and incorporate other elements, such as beads or charms, to make your keychain truly unique. These keychains make great gifts or can be used as bag charms as well.

6. Button Bouquets:
Instead of traditional flower bouquets, why not create a bouquet of button flowers? With a foam ball as the base, secure buttons to the ball using floral wire or toothpicks. Arrange the button flowers in a way that mimics a real flower bouquet. Not only will your button bouquet last forever, but it will also serve as a beautiful centerpiece for any occasion.

7. Button Embellishments:
Buttons can also be used as embellishments on various projects, such as cards, scrapbook pages, and fabric crafts. From adding a decorative touch to a handmade card to creating texture on a fabric bag, buttons can elevate any project. Get creative with your button placements, mix and match different sizes and colors, and let your imagination run wild.

Crafting with buttons is a fun and creative way to spend your time. The versatility of buttons allows you to create an endless array of projects suitable for all ages. Whether you’re a seasoned crafter or new to the crafting world, buttons are a must-have supply. So, gather your buttons, unleash your creativity, and start crafting!
