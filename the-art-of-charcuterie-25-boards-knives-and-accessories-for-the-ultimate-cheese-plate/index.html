The Art of Charcuterie: 25 Boards, Knives, and Accessories for the Ultimate Cheese Plate


When it comes to hosting a memorable gathering, a well-curated charcuterie board can steal the show. The art of charcuterie lies in the careful selection and arrangement of fine cheeses, cured meats, and complementary accompaniments. But to truly elevate your cheese plate game, you’ll need the right tools and accessories. In this article, we’ll explore 25 must-have items that will take your charcuterie presentation to the next level.

1. Cheese Boards: A good charcuterie board starts with a solid foundation. Invest in a variety of cheese boards in different shapes, sizes, and materials. From rustic wooden boards to sleek marble slabs, the options are endless. Choose boards that complement the overall theme and aesthetic of your gathering.

2. Cheese Knives: No charcuterie board is complete without the perfect set of cheese knives. Look for a set that includes a variety of blades for different types of cheese. A cheese plane for slicing, a soft cheese knife for spreading, and a sturdy cheese cleaver for hard cheeses are essential.

3. Cheese Markers: To help your guests navigate the variety of cheeses on your board, use cheese markers. These small, decorative tags can be placed next to each cheese, indicating its name, origin, and any other relevant information. Not only do they add an elegant touch, but they also make it easier for everyone to choose their favorites.

4. Cheese Paper: Preserve the quality and flavor of your cheese by wrapping leftovers in cheese paper. Unlike plastic wrap or aluminum foil, cheese paper allows the cheese to breathe while still protecting it from drying out. It’s a small investment that goes a long way in maintaining the taste and texture of your cheese.

5. Cheese Dome: If you want to add a touch of sophistication to your charcuterie presentation, consider investing in a cheese dome. These elegant glass or acrylic domes not only keep your cheese fresh but also create a visually appealing centerpiece for your board.

6. Cheese Wire: For those who appreciate the art of slicing cheese, a cheese wire is a must-have tool. It allows for precise and effortless cutting, ensuring that each slice is perfectly thin and uniform. Whether you’re serving a soft brie or a crumbly blue, a cheese wire will make the process a breeze.

7. Cheese Grater: Freshly grated cheese adds a delightful touch to any charcuterie board. Invest in a high-quality cheese grater with different grating options to cater to various cheese textures. From finely grated Parmesan to coarsely shredded cheddar, the possibilities are endless.

8. Cheese Curler: If you want to impress your guests with a unique cheese experience, a cheese curler is a great addition to your charcuterie arsenal. This tool allows you to transform blocks of cheese into beautiful, delicate curls, adding a touch of elegance and texture to your board.

9. Cheese Fondue Set: Take your charcuterie board to the next level by offering a cheese fondue experience. A cheese fondue set with a pot, forks, and a burner will allow your guests to dip their favorite bread, fruits, and vegetables into melted, gooey cheese. It’s an interactive and indulgent addition to any gathering.

10. Cheese Aging Cave: If you’re a cheese enthusiast who loves experimenting with aging cheese, consider investing in a cheese aging cave. This specialized fridge provides the perfect conditions for aging and maturing different types of cheese, allowing you to create unique flavors and textures.

11. Cheese Slicer: For those who prefer thinly sliced cheese, a cheese slicer is a handy tool to have. It ensures uniform slices and makes it easy to control portion sizes. Whether you’re making a sandwich or adding a delicate layer of cheese to your charcuterie board, a cheese slicer is a game-changer.

12. Cheese Melting Dome: If you want to add a touch of theater to your charcuterie presentation, a cheese melting dome is a fun accessory. This metal dome is placed over a melting cheese, creating a captivating visual as the cheese slowly melts and becomes oozy and delicious.

13. Cheese Tasting Wheel: Expand your cheese knowledge and impress your guests with a cheese tasting wheel. This educational tool provides a systematic approach to tasting and evaluating different cheeses. It helps you identify flavors, textures, and aromas, making you a cheese connoisseur in no time.

14. Cheese Plane: A cheese plane is a versatile tool that allows you to slice thin, even sheets of cheese. It’s perfect for creating delicate cheese shavings or adding a finishing touch to your charcuterie board. With a cheese plane, you can achieve professional-looking results with minimal effort.

15. Cheese Dome Stand: If you want to take your cheese dome presentation to new heights, invest in a cheese dome stand. These stands elevate your cheese dome, creating a dramatic centerpiece. Choose a stand that matches your overall aesthetic and complements the style of your gathering.

16. Cheese Wire Cutter: Similar to a cheese wire, a cheese wire cutter is designed specifically for slicing through hard cheeses. Its unique design allows for precise and effortless cutting, ensuring that each slice is perfect. Whether you’re serving a sharp cheddar or a nutty Gruyere, a cheese wire cutter will make the task effortless.

17. Cheese Board Utensils: Complete your charcuterie setup with a set of cheese board utensils. These small tools, including a mini fork, spatula, and pronged knife, make it easy for guests to serve themselves and create their own cheese combinations. They’re a practical and stylish addition to any cheese board.

18. Cheese Grilling Basket: Want to add a twist to your charcuterie board? Invest in a cheese grilling basket. This handy tool allows you to grill or melt cheese slices directly on the barbecue, creating a smoky and delicious addition to your cheese plate. It’s a unique way to elevate your charcuterie game.

19. Cheese Stencils: Impress your guests with intricately designed cheese shapes using cheese stencils. These decorative templates can be placed on top of soft or semi-soft cheeses, allowing you to create beautiful patterns or designs. It’s a simple yet effective way to add an artistic touch to your charcuterie board.

20. Cheese Accompaniments: To complement your cheese selections, don’t forget the accompaniments. From honey and fruit preserves to nuts and olives, there are countless options to enhance the flavors and textures of your cheese plate. Experiment with different combinations to create a truly memorable experience for your guests.

21. Cheese Cellar: For those who take cheese storage seriously, a cheese cellar is a game-changer. These temperature and humidity-controlled cabinets provide optimal conditions for storing and aging cheese. With a cheese cellar, you can ensure that your cheese is always at its best, ready to be enjoyed at any time.

22. Cheese Molds: If you’re feeling adventurous and want to try your hand at making your own cheese, cheese molds are essential. These molds come in various shapes and sizes, allowing you to create your own unique cheese creations. From fresh cheese balls to aged cheese wheels, the possibilities are endless.

23. Cheese Serving Platter: Present your charcuterie board in style with a cheese serving platter. These elegant platters are designed specifically for showcasing cheese, often featuring compartments for different cheese varieties. Choose a platter that matches your overall aesthetic and enhances the visual appeal of your cheese plate.

24. Cheese Fondue Forks: If you’re hosting a cheese fondue party, don’t forget to provide each guest with their own set of cheese fondue forks. These long forks are perfect for dipping and retrieving bite-sized pieces of bread, fruits, and vegetables from the cheese pot. They make the fondue experience more enjoyable and practical.

25. Cheese Storage Bags: To keep your cheese fresh and prevent it from absorbing unwanted flavors from the fridge, invest in cheese storage bags. These specially designed bags create a microclimate for your cheese, allowing it to breathe while protecting it from external odors. It’s a simple yet effective way to extend the shelf life of your cheese.

In conclusion, the art of charcuterie extends beyond the selection of cheeses and cured meats. With the right tools and accessories, you can create a visually stunning and highly enjoyable cheese plate experience. From cheese boards and knives to unique accessories like cheese curlers and melting domes, these items will elevate your charcuterie presentation to new heights. So, gather your favorite cheeses, gather your friends, and let the art of charcuterie take center stage at your next gathering.
