Calling All Turkey Hunters: How to Use Calls Effectively


Turkey hunting can be a thrilling and rewarding experience for those who know what they are doing. It requires patience, skill, and the right tools to lure these birds in. One of the most important tools in a turkey hunter’s arsenal is the call. Using calls effectively can make all the difference in attracting turkeys and having a successful hunt.

But with so many different types of calls available, it can be overwhelming for beginners to know where to start. In this blog, we’ll cover the basics of turkey calling and offer some tips on how to use calls effectively.

Types of Turkey Calls

There are several types of turkey calls available on the market. Here are some of the most common ones:

1. Box Calls - Box calls are the easiest to use and are great for beginners. They consist of a box and a lid that you slide back and forth to create a sound. Box calls are versatile and can mimic a variety of turkey sounds.

2. Slate Calls - Slate calls are made of a slate or glass surface and a striker. You use the striker to create vibration on the surface, which produces the turkey sound. Slate calls are popular for their realistic sound and are great for luring turkeys in close.

3. Diaphragm Calls - Diaphragm calls, also known as mouth calls, are placed in the hunter’s mouth and require air pressure to produce sound. They can mimic a variety of turkey sounds and are great for hands-free calling.

4. Locator Calls - Locator calls are used to locate turkeys in the area. These calls include owl hooters, crow calls, and coyote howlers.

Using Calls Effectively

Now that you know the types of calls available, it’s important to understand how to use calls effectively.

1. Practice - The key to using calls effectively is practice. Spend some time practicing different sounds with different calls. Try different pitches, tempos, and volumes to see what works best.

2. Know Your Surroundings - Before you start calling, take a look at your surroundings. Are there any natural obstacles or loud noises that could affect how the turkey hears your call? Adjust your calling volume and location accordingly.

3. Use Calls Sparingly - Don’t overdo it with the calls. Turkeys can become wary if they hear too many calls, and it can make them difficult to lure in. If you’re not getting any response, try changing up your call or waiting a while before trying again.

4. Be Patient - Turkey hunting requires patience. Sometimes it can take hours before you get a response, so be prepared to wait it out.

5. Get the Timing Right - Timing is everything when it comes to turkey calling. In the early morning, turkeys are more likely to respond to locator calls. Later in the morning, you may have more success with hen calls.

6. Learn the Language - Understanding the different turkey sounds and what they mean is essential to using calls effectively. Familiarize yourself with the sounds turkeys make and what they are communicating.

Conclusion

Turkey calling is a crucial aspect of turkey hunting, and using calls effectively can make all the difference in a successful hunt. Practice with different calls, be patient, and learn the language of turkeys to maximize your chances of attracting them. Remember to adjust your volume and location based on your surroundings and use calls sparingly to avoid scaring turkeys away. With these tips, you’ll be on your way to becoming a seasoned turkey hunter.
