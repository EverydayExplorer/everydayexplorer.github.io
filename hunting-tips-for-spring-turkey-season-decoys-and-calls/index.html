Hunting Tips for Spring Turkey Season: Decoys and Calls


Spring turkey season is just around the corner, and for hunting enthusiasts, it’s an exciting time to gear up and head out into the woods. One of the key strategies for a successful turkey hunt is using decoys and calls effectively. In this blog post, we will delve into some essential hunting tips to help you make the most of your spring turkey season.

Decoys play a crucial role in attracting turkeys and bringing them within your shooting range. When it comes to decoys, it’s important to consider the type, placement, and realism. There are various types of turkey decoys available, including hen, jake, and tom decoys. Each type serves a different purpose and can be used strategically to elicit certain responses from the turkeys.

When setting up your decoys, it’s important to position them in a natural and realistic manner. Placing them in an open area where they are easily visible to approaching turkeys is ideal. You can use stakes or lightweight decoy stands to keep them steady. It’s also a good idea to invest in high-quality, realistic decoys that mimic the appearance and posture of live turkeys. Remember, the more authentic your decoys look, the more likely they are to attract turkeys.

In addition to decoys, calls are another powerful tool in your hunting arsenal. Turkey calls can mimic the sounds of a hen, which can be used to grab the attention of toms or other nearby turkeys. There are various types of turkey calls, including box calls, slate calls, mouth calls, and diaphragm calls. Each call produces a distinct sound, so it’s important to practice and find the one that suits you best.

To effectively use turkey calls, it’s essential to understand the different vocalizations turkeys make. These include yelps, clucks, purrs, and gobbles. By mastering these calls, you can communicate with turkeys and create a realistic and convincing scenario. Practice your calling techniques before the season starts to ensure you sound like a confident and experienced hen.

When using calls, it’s crucial to be patient and observant. Turkeys have keen eyesight and can easily detect movement. Set up in a concealed location, such as a blind or natural cover, and use your calls sparingly. Overcalling can make turkeys suspicious, so it’s important to strike a balance and mimic the natural rhythm of turkey communication.

Lastly, always prioritize safety when hunting turkeys. Ensure you are familiar with local hunting regulations, wear appropriate camouflage clothing, and use a turkey hunting vest or orange hat to make yourself visible to other hunters. Safety should be the top priority for all hunters.

In conclusion, the effective use of decoys and calls can significantly improve your chances of a successful turkey hunt during the spring season. By investing in realistic decoys, mastering different turkey calls, and practicing patience and observation, you can increase your chances of bagging that elusive tom. Remember, turkey hunting is an art that requires practice, knowledge, and a deep understanding of turkey behavior. So get out there, embrace the challenge, and enjoy the excitement of spring turkey season!
