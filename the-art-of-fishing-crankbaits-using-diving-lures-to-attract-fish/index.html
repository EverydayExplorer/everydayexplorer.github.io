The Art of Fishing Crankbaits: Using Diving Lures to Attract Fish


Fishing is not just a hobby; it’s an art. And like any art form, it requires skill, knowledge, and the right tools. When it comes to attracting fish, crankbaits are a popular choice among anglers. These diving lures have a unique design that mimics the movements of prey, making them irresistible to fish. In this blog post, we will explore the art of fishing crankbaits and learn how to effectively use these diving lures to attract fish.

Understanding Crankbaits

Crankbaits are a type of fishing lure that imitates the appearance and movements of baitfish. They typically have a hard plastic body with a lip, or bill, on the front. This lip is what gives crankbaits their diving action. When retrieved, the lip causes the lure to dive down into the water, mimicking the movements of a wounded or fleeing baitfish. This action triggers a predatory response in fish, making them strike at the lure.

Choosing the Right Crankbait

To effectively use crankbaits, it’s important to choose the right lure for the conditions you’re fishing in. Factors such as water depth, water clarity, and the type of fish you’re targeting will all influence your choice of crankbait. Here are a few things to consider when selecting a crankbait:

  1. Diving Depth: Different crankbaits are designed to dive to different depths. Pay attention to the diving depth indicated on the packaging or product description and choose a crankbait that matches the depth you want to fish.

  2. Size and Color: Crankbaits come in a variety of sizes and colors. As a general rule, choose a size and color that closely resembles the baitfish in the area you’re fishing. If you’re unsure, opt for natural colors such as silver, gold, or shad patterns.

  3. Lip Shape: The shape of the lip on a crankbait determines its action in the water. Shallow diving crankbaits usually have a shorter, wider lip, while deep diving crankbaits have a longer, narrower lip. Experiment with different lip shapes to find the action that works best for your fishing style.

Techniques for Fishing Crankbaits

Now that you have the right crankbait, let’s dive into some techniques for effectively fishing with them:

  1. Casting and Retrieving: Start by casting your crankbait towards areas where fish are likely to be hiding, such as submerged rocks, fallen trees, or weed beds. Let the lure sink to the desired depth, then retrieve it with a steady, medium-paced retrieve. Vary your retrieve speed to imitate the movements of a wounded baitfish.

  2. Bumping and Deflecting: To trigger a strike, try bumping your crankbait into underwater structures or deflecting it off rocks. This action mimics the erratic movements of a fleeing prey, which can attract nearby fish.

  3. Pause and Twitch: Occasionally pause your retrieve and give the crankbait a twitch. This sudden change in movement can often entice fish to strike. Experiment with different pause lengths and twitching patterns to find what works best for the fish you’re targeting.

  4. Crankbait Trolling: If you’re fishing from a boat, trolling with crankbaits can be an effective technique. Use a depth finder to locate schools of fish, then troll your crankbait at the appropriate depth. Vary your trolling speed to find the sweet spot that triggers strikes.

Conclusion

Mastering the art of fishing crankbaits requires practice, patience, and a deep understanding of fish behavior. By choosing the right crankbait, employing the right techniques, and paying attention to the movements and actions of baitfish, you can increase your chances of attracting and catching fish. So, next time you head out to the water, remember the art of fishing crankbaits and enjoy the thrill of reeling in your next big catch!
