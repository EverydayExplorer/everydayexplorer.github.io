The Top 10 Craft Beers to Try in 2021


Craft beer is an industry that has been gaining popularity over the past few years, and with good reason. From complex flavor profiles to unique brewing techniques, craft beers offer a wide range of options for beer enthusiasts to explore. In 2021, there are several standout craft beers that are worth trying, whether you’re a seasoned beer connoisseur or just starting your craft beer journey. In this blog post, we will be exploring the top 10 craft beers to try in 2021. From hoppy IPAs to rich stouts, there’s something for everyone on this list. So, without further ado, let’s dive right in!

1. Pliny the Elder

Pliny the Elder is a classic American Double IPA that has been highly regarded in the craft beer community for years. Brewed by Russian River Brewing Company in California, this beer is known for its intense hoppy flavor and balanced malt backbone. With notes of citrus, pine, and a touch of sweetness, Pliny the Elder is a true beer lover’s delight.

2. Tree House Julius

If hazy IPAs are your thing, then Tree House Julius is a must-try in 2021. This Massachusetts-based brewery has gained a cult-like following for its juicy, hop-forward beers, and Julius is no exception. Bursting with tropical fruit flavors and a creamy mouthfeel, this beer is sure to satisfy any hophead’s cravings.

3. Trappist Westvleteren 12

For those looking for a more traditional craft beer experience, the Trappist Westvleteren 12 is a Belgian Quadrupel that shouldn’t be missed. Produced by the Saint Sixtus Abbey in Belgium, this limited-release beer is often considered one of the best in the world. With complex flavors of dark fruits, caramel, and spicy yeast, the Westvleteren 12 is a true masterpiece.

4. Founders CBS

If you’re a fan of barrel-aged beers, then Founders CBS (Canadian Breakfast Stout) is a must-try. This imperial stout is aged in bourbon barrels that were previously used to age maple syrup, giving it a unique and robust flavor profile. With notes of chocolate, coffee, maple, and bourbon, this beer is a decadent treat for any stout lover.

5. Hill Farmstead Edward

Hill Farmstead Brewery in Vermont is renowned for its exceptional beers, and Edward is one that shines bright. A hoppy American Pale Ale, Edward is brewed with a focus on balance and drinkability. With floral and citrusy hop flavors and a clean malt backbone, this beer is perfect for those seeking a refreshing and approachable craft beer experience.

6. WeldWerks Juicy Bits

Hazy IPAs continue to dominate the craft beer scene, and WeldWerks Juicy Bits is no exception. This Colorado-based brewery has gained a reputation for its exceptional hazy IPAs, and Juicy Bits is one of their flagship offerings. Bursting with tropical fruit flavors and a smooth, creamy mouthfeel, this beer is a prime example of what makes hazy IPAs so popular.

7. The Alchemist Heady Topper

Heady Topper from The Alchemist Brewery in Vermont holds a special place in craft beer lore. Considered one of the original hazy IPAs, Heady Topper helped kickstart the haze craze that we see in the craft beer world today. With its hop-forward flavors, balanced bitterness, and juicy character, Heady Topper is a must-try for any beer enthusiast.

8. Cantillon Gueuze

Sour beers have become increasingly popular in recent years, and Cantillon Brewery in Belgium has long been a pioneer in this style. Their Gueuze, a blend of Lambic beers of different ages, is a prime example of their expertise. With tart, funky flavors and refreshing acidity, Cantillon Gueuze is a delightful choice for those looking to explore the world of sour beers.

9. Three Floyds Zombie Dust

For hop enthusiasts, Three Floyds Zombie Dust is a beer that shouldn’t be missed. This American Pale Ale is heavily hopped with Citra hops, resulting in a burst of citrus and tropical fruit flavors. With its balanced bitterness and crisp finish, Zombie Dust is a favorite among hopheads.

10. Bells Two Hearted Ale

Last but not least, Bells Two Hearted Ale is a classic craft beer that continues to impress. Brewed by Bells Brewery in Michigan, this American IPA is known for its balanced hop flavor, showcasing both bitterness and citrus notes. With its drinkability and wide availability, Bells Two Hearted Ale is a great choice for those new to craft beer as well as seasoned beer enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Craft beer continues to evolve and push boundaries, providing beer enthusiasts with an ever-expanding range of flavors and styles to explore. The top 10 craft beers to try in 2021 offer a diverse selection that showcases the creativity and craftsmanship of brewers around the world. From hoppy IPAs to complex stouts, there’s something on this list for everyone to enjoy. So, why not embark on a craft beer adventure in 2021 and discover your new favorite brew? Cheers!
