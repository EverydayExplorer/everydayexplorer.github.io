Books That Have Revolutionized the Literary World: A Historical Perspective


Books have been at the forefront of human society for centuries, serving as tools for learning, entertainment, and societal transformation. The literary world has seen a myriad of books that have changed society and legacy, inspiring generations after generations. These books have challenged the norms and ideals of the era they were written in, molding a new future and leaving a mark on the world forever.

In this article, we will explore some of the books that have revolutionized the literary world, from the earliest times to the modern era. We will observe how these books changed society through their content, style and concept, and outline their influence on future literature.

1. The Epic of Gilgamesh:
The Epic of Gilgamesh is considered the first recorded epic poem in human history, dating back to 2000 BCE. The story talks about the adventures of the king of Uruk and his friend, Enkidu, covering themes of love, death, and immortality in a spiritual dimension. This classic piece of literature has inspired several literary works in epic poetry, mythology, and drama. It is an inspiration for classic writers such as Homer and Dante Alighieri, being a significant influence on the development of literary traditions in Mesopotamia and beyond.

2. The Bible:
The Bible is one of the most influential books in human history, with its texts spanning over millennia. It is a collection of religious texts considered sacred by Christians, Jews, and Muslims. The book tells the stories of God’s creation of the world, the history of humanity, and the life of Jesus Christ. The Bible has influenced literature, art, and politics, shaping the course of human history in several ways.

3. The Quran:
The Quran is the central religious text of Islam, consisting of the teachings of Prophet Muhammad in the seventh century. The book is regarded as the literal word of God and is considered the ultimate source of Islamic law and ethics. The Quran has played a crucial role in shaping Muslim culture, philosophy, and literature worldwide, with its verses widely recited and studied among the faithful.

4. The Iliad & The Odyssey:
The works of the Greek poet Homer marked a significant milestone in the development of Western literature. The Iliad and The Odyssey are considered two of the greatest epic poems of all time, telling the stories of the Trojan War and the adventures of Odysseus. These works have inspired and influenced Western literature, art, and culture for centuries, paving the way for future literary masters such as Shakespeare and Dante.

5. Don Quixote:
Written by Spanish author Miguel de Cervantes in the seventeenth century, Don Quixote is considered one of the most iconic works of literature in history. The book tells the story of a Spanish nobleman who proclaims himself a knight and embarks on various adventures with his loyal squire, Sancho Panza. Don Quixote is widely recognized as the first modern novel, merging elements of comedy, satire, and drama in a newly emerging literary style.

6. Pride and Prejudice:
Jane Austen’s Pride and Prejudice is a timeless masterpiece of literature, known for its charming wit and social critique. The novel tells the story of the Bennet family and their quest to find suitable husbands for their five daughters in nineteenth-century England. Austen’s work is notable for its realistic portrayal of women and their social status, offering an insightful commentary on the plight of women in society.

7. 1984:
George Orwell’s 1984 is a dystopian novel published in 1949. The book describes a future totalitarian society in which people are oppressed by an all-seeing, all-knowing government. The novel offers a stark warning about the dangers of unchecked state power, surveillance, and propaganda, elements that resonate with contemporary society.

In conclusion, books have been a catalyst for change in the literary world and society at large. The books discussed in this article have each left their unique mark, inspiring future generations of writers, artists, and thinkers. Their legacy continues to shape and influence the world as we know it today, offering a glimpse into the past and a blueprint for the future.
