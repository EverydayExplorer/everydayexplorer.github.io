DIY Pickles: Easy and Flavorful Pickling Recipes


Pickles are a beloved condiment that can add a tangy and refreshing twist to any meal. Whether you’re a pickle enthusiast or just looking to try something new, making your own pickles at home is a fun and rewarding experience. Not only do homemade pickles taste better than store-bought ones, but you also have the freedom to experiment with different flavors and ingredients. In this blog post, we will share some easy and flavorful pickling recipes that you can try at home.

Classic Dill Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 pickling cucumbers
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons pickling salt
  • 2 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon dill seeds

Instructions:

  1. Wash the cucumbers thoroughly and slice them into spears or rounds.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the water, vinegar, pickling salt, garlic, and dill seeds. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  3. Place the cucumber slices in a clean jar and pour the hot vinegar mixture over them, making sure the cucumbers are fully submerged.
  4. Let the pickles cool to room temperature, then cover the jar and refrigerate for at least 24 hours before enjoying. The pickles will continue to develop flavor over time.

Spicy Bread and Butter Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 medium-sized cucumbers
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 1/2 cups white vinegar
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon celery seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Slice the cucumbers into thin rounds and place them in a large bowl. Add the sliced onion.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the white vinegar, apple cider vinegar, sugar, mustard seeds, turmeric, celery seeds, red pepper flakes, black pepper, cloves, and cinnamon. Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring until the sugar has dissolved.
  3. Pour the hot vinegar mixture over the cucumbers and onion. Stir gently to combine.
  4. Let the pickles cool to room temperature, then transfer them to jars and refrigerate for at least 24 hours before enjoying. These pickles will have a sweet and spicy flavor that pairs well with sandwiches and burgers.

Asian-inspired Quick Pickled Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, radishes, cucumbers, etc.), thinly sliced or julienned
  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional for spice)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the rice vinegar, soy sauce, honey or maple syrup, grated ginger, minced garlic, sesame oil, and red pepper flakes.
  2. Add the sliced or julienned vegetables to the bowl and toss to coat them in the marinade.
  3. Let the vegetables sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes, or refrigerate for a few hours for a stronger flavor.
  4. Serve the pickled vegetables as a side dish or as a topping for salads, sandwiches, or rice bowls. These quick pickles add a burst of tangy and savory flavor to any meal.

Conclusion

Making your own pickles at home is a simple and enjoyable process that allows you to customize the flavors to your liking. Whether you prefer classic dill pickles, spicy bread and butter pickles, or Asian-inspired quick pickled vegetables, there’s a recipe for everyone. Experiment with different ingredients and spices to create your own unique pickling recipes. Enjoy the satisfaction of adding a homemade touch to your meals with these easy and flavorful pickling recipes.
