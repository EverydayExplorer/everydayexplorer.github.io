How to Mix and Match Patterns in Your Outfit: Clothing, Shoes, and Jewelry


Mixing and matching patterns in your outfit is a fun way to add interest and personality to your style. It can also be a daunting task, especially if you’re not used to playing with different patterns, colors, and textures. However, with a bit of guidance and creativity, you can confidently combine patterns in a way that looks stylish and put-together. This guide will walk you through the key elements of mixing patterns in clothing, shoes, and jewelry, providing you with tips, tricks, and examples to help you create outfits that turn heads.

Understanding the Basics of Pattern Mixing

Before diving into the specifics, it’s important to understand some basic principles of pattern mixing. Patterns come in a wide range of designs, from stripes and polka dots to florals and geometric prints. To mix patterns effectively, you’ll need to consider a few fundamental factors:

  1. Scale and Proportion: When mixing patterns, varying the scale is essential. Pairing large-scale patterns with smaller ones creates a balanced look. For example, a large floral print on a dress can be complemented by a smaller polka dot on a scarf or a geometric print on shoes.

  2. Color Coordination: The colors in your patterns should complement each other. Consider using a common color to tie different patterns together. For example, if you’re wearing a navy-striped shirt, try incorporating navy into other patterns in your outfit, like a floral skirt with navy accents.

  3. Neutral Base: Adding neutral elements can help ground your outfit and prevent it from becoming overwhelming. Items like denim, black or white tops, or beige shoes can serve as a neutral base, allowing your patterns to stand out without clashing.

Mixing Patterns in Clothing

Clothing is the most obvious place to start when mixing patterns. Here are some tips for creating harmonious outfits with multiple patterns:

  • Start with Stripes: Stripes are a versatile pattern that can be mixed with almost anything. Try pairing a striped top with a floral skirt, or wear a striped blazer over a polka-dot dress. Stripes can add a classic touch while allowing you to experiment with other patterns.

  • Mixing Florals: Florals are bold and eye-catching, but they can be challenging to mix. To keep it simple, choose one piece with a large floral pattern and pair it with a smaller-scale floral or a different pattern that has similar colors. For example, a large floral blouse can work well with a smaller polka dot skirt.

  • Combining Geometric Patterns: Geometric patterns like houndstooth, herringbone, and plaid are great for adding structure to an outfit. Try pairing a geometric skirt with a contrasting pattern, like stripes or polka dots. The key is to balance the scales and maintain a cohesive color palette.

  • Experiment with Animal Prints: Animal prints are bold but can be mixed effectively with other patterns. Consider using them as an accent rather than a primary pattern. For example, pair a leopard-print belt with a striped dress or wear animal-print shoes with a floral top.

Mixing Patterns with Shoes

Shoes are a fantastic way to add a pop of pattern to your outfit. Here’s how to mix patterns in footwear:

  • Patterned Sneakers: Sneakers with patterns are trendy and can add a playful touch to your outfit. Try pairing them with a solid-colored outfit to make the shoes the focal point, or wear them with a subtle pattern, like a striped dress.

  • Floral Heels: Floral heels are elegant and can elevate a simple outfit. Pair them with a solid-colored dress or a more subdued pattern, like pinstripes. This creates a chic and coordinated look.

  • Animal-Print Boots: Animal-print boots can be a statement piece. Wear them with neutral clothing to let the boots stand out, or pair them with another pattern that complements the print’s color scheme.

Mixing Patterns with Jewelry

Jewelry is an often-overlooked way to mix patterns into your outfit. It can add a touch of glamour and complete your look. Here’s how to incorporate patterned jewelry:

  • Layered Necklaces: Layered necklaces with different patterns can create a dynamic look. Try layering a simple chain with a necklace that has a geometric pendant or a floral design. This adds depth without overwhelming your outfit.

  • Statement Earrings: Statement earrings with bold patterns can be a fun addition to a patterned outfit. Consider wearing large earrings with a pattern that complements your clothing. For example, if you’re wearing a polka dot dress, opt for earrings with a similar pattern.

  • Patterned Bracelets: Bracelets with patterns, like bangles with geometric designs, can add texture to your outfit. Mix and match different bracelets to create a unique combination.

Final Tips for Mixing and Matching Patterns

Mixing patterns is all about experimenting and finding what works for you. Here are some final tips to keep in mind:

  • Start Small: If you’re new to pattern mixing, start with one patterned piece and add smaller patterns gradually. This helps you get used to the concept without feeling overwhelmed.

  • Use a Common Theme: Whether it’s a specific color or pattern type, having a common theme can make it easier to mix patterns effectively.

  • Experiment with Textures: Textures can add dimension to your outfit. Consider incorporating textured fabrics like knitwear or velvet to add depth to your patterns.

  • Balance Bold and Subtle: If you’re wearing a bold pattern, balance it with more subtle patterns or solid colors. This creates a harmonious look that isn’t too busy.

  • Trust Your Instincts: Ultimately, fashion is a form of self-expression. Trust your instincts and wear what makes you feel confident and comfortable.

By following these guidelines and embracing your creativity, you can mix and match patterns in your outfit with style and confidence. Whether you’re pairing clothing, shoes, or jewelry, the key is to have fun and enjoy the process of creating unique and eye-catching looks.
