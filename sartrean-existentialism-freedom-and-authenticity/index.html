Sartrean Existentialism: Freedom and Authenticity


Existentialism is a philosophical movement that emphasizes individual existence, freedom, and choice. One of the most prominent figures in existentialist philosophy is Jean-Paul Sartre. Sartre’s existentialism is characterized by the concepts of freedom and authenticity, which are central to understanding his philosophy.

Freedom is a fundamental aspect of human existence according to Sartre. He believed that human beings are condemned to be free, meaning that we are responsible for our actions and the choices we make. Unlike other philosophical systems that argue for predetermined destinies or external forces controlling our lives, Sartre believed that we have complete freedom to choose our paths.

However, with freedom comes a great burden. Sartre famously stated, ‘Man is condemned to be free because once thrown into the world, he is responsible for everything he does.’ This responsibility can be overwhelming, as it means that we are solely responsible for the consequences of our actions. We cannot blame external factors or circumstances for our choices; we must confront the weight of our decisions.

To truly embrace our freedom, Sartre argued that we must also strive for authenticity. Authenticity refers to living in accordance with our true selves, rather than conforming to societal expectations or external influences. Sartre believed that many people live in ‘bad faith,’ meaning they deny their freedom and responsibility by conforming to societal norms and expectations.

Living authentically requires self-reflection and introspection. It means being honest with ourselves about our desires, values, and beliefs, and actively choosing to live in alignment with them. It also means accepting the consequences of our choices, even if they go against societal norms or expectations.

Authenticity also involves accepting the inherent uncertainties and anxieties that come with freedom. Sartre acknowledged that making choices can be anxiety-inducing because of the infinite possibilities and potential outcomes. However, he argued that embracing this anxiety is essential for living an authentic life. By acknowledging and confronting our fears, we can fully embrace our freedom and make choices that align with our true selves.

Sartre’s philosophy of freedom and authenticity has profound implications for how we live our lives. It challenges us to question societal expectations and norms, encouraging us to think critically about the choices we make. It reminds us that we have the power to shape our own destinies and that our actions have consequences.

In conclusion, Sartrean existentialism places a strong emphasis on freedom and authenticity. It reminds us that we are responsible for our choices and that living authentically requires self-reflection and honesty. By embracing our freedom and making choices that align with our true selves, we can lead more fulfilling and meaningful lives. So, let us embrace our freedom, confront our anxieties, and strive for authenticity in everything we do.
