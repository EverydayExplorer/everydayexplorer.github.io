Mastering the Art of Contouring: A Step-by-Step Guide


Have you ever wondered how celebrities and beauty influencers achieve that perfectly sculpted look? The secret lies in the art of contouring. Contouring has taken the beauty world by storm, and it’s not difficult to see why. With just a few strategically placed shadows and highlights, you can transform your face, enhance your features, and achieve a flawless finish. In this step-by-step guide, we will walk you through the process of mastering the art of contouring.

Step 1: Prep your skin

Before you start contouring, it’s crucial to prep your skin properly. Start by cleansing your face and applying a moisturizer suitable for your skin type. This will ensure a smooth canvas for makeup application.

Step 2: Choose the right products and shades

Contouring requires the use of two shades: one darker shade for adding depth and dimension, and one lighter shade for highlighting and bringing forward specific areas. When selecting a contour shade, go for a color that is around two shades darker than your natural skin tone. For the highlight shade, choose a color that is two shades lighter than your skin tone.

Step 3: Identify your face shape

Understanding your face shape is vital when it comes to contouring. Different face shapes require distinct contouring techniques. The most common face shapes are oval, round, square, heart, and diamond. Take a moment to identify your face shape and research the best contouring techniques for it.

Step 4: Start with a base

Apply your foundation as you normally would, ensuring that it seamlessly blends with your natural skin tone. This will provide a base for your contouring and help create a more natural-looking finish.

Step 5: Contour the hollows of your cheeks

Using your darker contour shade and a contour brush, apply the product to the hollows of your cheeks to create depth and sculpted cheekbones. Start from your hairline near your ear and blend the product inwards towards the center of your cheek. Remember to blend well to avoid harsh lines.

Step 6: Define your jawline

To create a more defined jawline, apply the contour shade along your jawline. Blend it downwards towards your neck to create a seamless transition. This technique works effectively for round and square face shapes.

Step 7: Slim down your nose

If you wish to make your nose appear slimmer, use the contour shade on the sides of your nose. Apply it starting from the inner corner of your eyebrows and gently blend it towards the tip of your nose. Be sure to blend it well to avoid any harsh lines.

Step 8: Highlight your features

Using the lighter shade and a highlighter brush, apply the product to the areas you want to bring forward and enhance. This typically includes the top of your cheekbones, the bridge of your nose, the center of your forehead, and your cupid’s bow.

Step 9: Blend, blend, blend

The key to a natural-looking contour is seamless blending. Use a clean brush or a makeup sponge to blend your contour and highlight shades together. This will soften any harsh lines and create a beautiful gradient effect.

Step 10: Set your makeup

To ensure your contouring lasts all day long, set your makeup with a translucent powder. Gently dust it over your face using a large powder brush, focusing on the areas where you applied your contour and highlight shades.

Final Thoughts

Mastering the art of contouring can take time and practice, but with the right techniques and products, anyone can achieve a flawless, sculpted look. Remember, it’s essential to adapt the contouring techniques to suit your face shape, as this will provide the most flattering results.

So, go ahead and experiment with different shades and techniques until you find what works best for you. Contouring is all about enhancing your natural beauty and having fun with makeup. With this step-by-step guide, you’re well on your way to becoming a contouring pro!
