The Rise of Orange Wine: Embracing Skin-Contact Fermentation


In recent years, the wine world has seen a surge in popularity for a unique and intriguing style of wine known as orange wine. Unlike its red and white counterparts, orange wine is made using a process called skin-contact fermentation. This ancient winemaking technique has been around for thousands of years, but it is only now gaining recognition and appreciation from wine enthusiasts around the globe.

What is Orange Wine?

Orange wine is essentially a white wine that is made like a red wine. While white wine is typically made by fermenting the grape juice without the grape skin, orange wine is made by fermenting the grape juice along with the skins. This process gives the wine its characteristic amber or orange hue, hence the name.

The Origins of Orange Wine

Orange wine has its roots in the ancient winemaking traditions of Georgia, a country nestled between Europe and Asia. The Georgians have been making orange wine using the skin-contact fermentation technique for centuries. For them, this style of wine is not a trend or a fad but a way of life.

The resurgence of orange wine can be attributed to a handful of dedicated winemakers around the world who have embraced this traditional method and applied it to their own vineyards. These pioneers have helped to introduce this unique style of wine to a wider audience, sparking interest and curiosity among wine lovers.

The Making of Orange Wine

Making orange wine involves a slightly different winemaking process as compared to traditional white or red wine. After the grapes are harvested, they undergo a gentle crushing process, which allows the juice to come into contact with the skins. The grape skins contain natural yeasts, which kickstart the fermentation process. This extended contact with the skins gives the wine its distinct amber color, as well as its trademark tannins.

Orange wine is typically fermented in clay vessels known as qvevri, which are buried in the ground. These vessels provide a natural and stable environment for the wine to ferment in, allowing it to develop complex flavors and aromas. The use of qvevri is a nod to the traditional winemaking practices of Georgia, where this style of wine originated.

Characteristics of Orange Wine

Orange wine is often described as having a unique flavor profile that sets it apart from both red and white wines. It tends to have a more pronounced tannic structure, similar to red wine, which gives it a complexity and depth of flavor. Orange wine also exhibits aromas and flavors that are reminiscent of dried fruits, nuts, spices, and sometimes even a hint of honey.

The texture of orange wine is another distinguishing characteristic. The tannins derived from the grape skins can give the wine a slightly rough or grippy texture, which some wine enthusiasts find appealing. This textural element adds another layer of complexity to the overall drinking experience.

Food Pairing with Orange Wine

Orange wine’s versatility makes it an excellent choice for food pairing. Its unique flavor profile and structure make it a great match for a wide range of dishes. The tannins in orange wine can help to cut through the richness of fatty foods, making it a perfect pairing for dishes like roasted meats, game, and charcuterie.

Orange wine also pairs well with vegetarian dishes, such as roasted vegetables, herbaceous salads, and dishes with umami flavors. Its complex aromas and flavors can complement and enhance the flavors of these types of dishes.

The Growing Popularity of Orange Wine

Over the past decade, orange wine has gained a loyal following among wine enthusiasts and sommeliers. What was once a niche category has now become a sought-after style of wine in many top wine bars and restaurants around the world. The rising popularity of natural and artisanal wines has played a significant role in the increased interest in orange wine, as it embodies these qualities.

The distinctiveness of orange wine, with its vibrant color and unique flavor profile, has also contributed to its growing popularity. Wine enthusiasts are always on the lookout for something new and exciting, and orange wine offers a refreshing change from the usual white and red wines.

Embracing the Skin-Contact Fermentation

The rise of orange wine is not just a passing trend but a reflection of our changing wine preferences. As consumers become more curious and adventurous, they are seeking out wines that are made using traditional methods and express a sense of place. Orange wine, with its ancient winemaking technique and connection to a specific region, taps into this desire for authenticity and terroir-driven wines.

By embracing the skin-contact fermentation process and exploring the world of orange wine, we are broadening our wine horizons and experiencing a whole new world of flavors, textures, and aromas. Whether you are a seasoned wine enthusiast or a curious beginner, orange wine is certainly worth exploring and adding to your wine collection.

Conclusion

The rise of orange wine and the embracing of skin-contact fermentation is an exciting development in the world of wine. This unique style of wine offers a different experience and challenges our preconceptions about what wine should be. As it continues to gain popularity, orange wine is sure to find a permanent place on wine lists and in the hearts of wine lovers everywhere. So, don’t be afraid to dive into the world of orange wine and discover the treasures it has to offer. Cheers to the rise of orange wine!
