The Boulevardier: A Whiskey Twist on the Negroni


If you’re a fan of classic cocktails and enjoy the smooth, complex flavors of whiskey, then the Boulevardier is a drink that should definitely be on your radar. This delightful concoction is a whiskey twist on the Negroni, a classic Italian cocktail that combines gin, Campari, and sweet vermouth. The Boulevardier swaps out the gin for whiskey, creating a whole new level of depth and character.

Originating in the 1920s, the Boulevardier was first introduced by Erskine Gwynne, an American expatriate living in Paris. Gwynne, the editor of a monthly magazine called ‘The Boulevardier,’ sought to create a cocktail that would cater to the palates of American whiskey enthusiasts residing in Europe. The result was a remarkable blend of whiskey, Campari, and sweet vermouth that quickly gained popularity amongst the expat community.

Now, let’s dive into the ingredients and preparation of this exquisite libation. To make a Boulevardier, you’ll need the following:

- 1 ½ ounces of bourbon or rye whiskey (choose a high-quality whiskey to enhance the flavors)
- 1 ounce of Campari (a bitter Italian liqueur that adds a unique touch)
- 1 ounce of sweet vermouth (look for a smooth, aromatic vermouth for the best results)
- Orange peel (for garnish)

To start, fill a mixing glass or cocktail shaker with ice. Add the whiskey, Campari, and sweet vermouth to the glass. Stir the mixture gently for about 30 seconds to ensure all the flavors meld together. Strain the cocktail into a chilled rocks glass filled with ice cubes. Finally, garnish with a twist of orange peel to add a citrusy aroma to the drink.

The Boulevardier offers a perfect balance of sweet, bitter, and smoky flavors. The whiskey brings a rich and robust taste, while the Campari adds a bitter complexity that tantalizes the taste buds. The sweet vermouth adds a touch of sweetness and a nuanced herbal flavor that ties everything together.

One of the best things about the Boulevardier is its versatility. You can experiment with different types of whiskey to find your preferred flavor profile. Bourbon, with its notes of caramel and vanilla, adds a smooth sweetness to the cocktail, while rye whiskey brings a spicier, more robust character. You can also play around with the ratios of the ingredients to suit your personal taste.

When it comes to serving the Boulevardier, you can enjoy it on the rocks or straight up in a chilled cocktail glass. It’s a sophisticated and stylish drink that pairs well with a variety of cuisines, making it a versatile choice for any occasion.

In conclusion, the Boulevardier is a whiskey lover’s dream come true. Its harmonious blend of whiskey, Campari, and sweet vermouth creates a cocktail that is both bold and refined. Whether you’re a seasoned whiskey aficionado or just starting to explore the world of cocktails, the Boulevardier is a must-try. So, gather your ingredients, mix up a glass, and savor the complex flavors of this delightful whiskey twist on the Negroni. Cheers!
