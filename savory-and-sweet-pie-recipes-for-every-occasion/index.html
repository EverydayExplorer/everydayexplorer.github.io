Savory and Sweet Pie Recipes for Every Occasion


Pies are a classic dish that has been enjoyed for centuries. Whether you’re looking for a savory option to serve at a dinner party or a sweet treat to indulge in after a meal, pies are versatile and can be customized to suit any occasion. In this blog post, we will explore a variety of savory and sweet pie recipes that are sure to impress your guests and satisfy your cravings.

Savory Pies

1. Chicken Pot Pie

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pie crust (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, combine the chicken, mixed vegetables, chicken broth, and heavy cream. Cook over medium heat until heated through.
  3. Sprinkle the flour over the mixture and stir well to combine. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the sauce thickens.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a pie dish lined with pie crust.
  6. Top with another layer of pie crust and seal the edges. Cut a few slits on the top to allow steam to escape.
  7. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.

Serve this comforting chicken pot pie with a side salad for a hearty and satisfying meal.

2. Spinach and Feta Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 package frozen spinach, thawed and drained
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Phyllo dough sheets
  • Butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the spinach, feta cheese, Parmesan cheese, ricotta cheese, garlic, dill, parsley, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Layer the phyllo dough sheets in a greased pie dish, brushing each sheet with melted butter.
  4. Spoon the spinach and cheese mixture onto the phyllo dough.
  5. Fold the edges of the phyllo dough over the filling, creating a rustic-looking pie.
  6. Brush the top of the pie with melted butter.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the phyllo dough is golden brown and crispy.

This flavorful spinach and feta pie can be served as a main course or cut into smaller portions for appetizers.

Sweet Pies

1. Classic Apple Pie

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups thinly sliced apples (peeled and cored)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Pie crust (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Toss until the apples are coated evenly.
  3. Line a pie dish with one layer of pie crust.
  4. Pour the apple mixture into the pie crust, spreading it out evenly.
  5. Top with another layer of pie crust and seal the edges. Cut a few slits on the top to allow steam to escape.
  6. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the apples are tender.

Serve this classic apple pie warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a delicious dessert that never goes out of style.

2. Chocolate Pecan Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup light corn syrup
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 4 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • Pie crust (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sugar, corn syrup, melted butter, beaten eggs, and vanilla extract. Mix well.
  3. Stir in the chopped pecans and chocolate chips.
  4. Line a pie dish with one layer of pie crust.
  5. Pour the pecan and chocolate mixture into the pie crust, spreading it out evenly.
  6. Top with another layer of pie crust and seal the edges. Cut a few slits on the top to allow steam to escape.
  7. Bake for 50-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is set.

This indulgent chocolate pecan pie is perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth.

Conclusion

Pies are a versatile dish that can be enjoyed in both savory and sweet variations. With these delicious recipes, you can easily create pies for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to treat yourself to a homemade dessert, these savory and sweet pie recipes are sure to impress. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to bake some mouthwatering pies that will leave everyone wanting more. Enjoy!
