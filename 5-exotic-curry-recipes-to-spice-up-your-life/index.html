5 Exotic Curry Recipes to Spice Up Your Life


If you’re looking to add some excitement to your culinary repertoire, why not try your hand at preparing exotic curry dishes? Bursting with flavor and aromatic spices, curry is a beloved dish enjoyed by many around the world. In this guide, we’ll explore five exotic curry recipes that are sure to spice up your life and tantalize your taste buds.

1. Thai Green Curry with Shrimp:

Ingredients: - 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined - 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk - 2 tbsp green curry paste - 1 cup chicken broth - 1 red bell pepper, sliced - 1 cup sliced bamboo shoots - 1 tbsp fish sauce - 1 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar) - Fresh Thai basil leaves, for garnish - Cooked jasmine rice, for serving

Instructions: 1. In a large skillet or wok, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and cook for a minute until fragrant. 2. Pour in the coconut milk and chicken broth, stirring until well combined. Bring the mixture to a simmer. 3. Add the shrimp, bell pepper, and bamboo shoots to the skillet. Cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the shrimp are pink and cooked through. 4. Stir in the fish sauce and palm sugar, adjusting the seasoning to taste. 5. Serve the curry hot over cooked jasmine rice, garnished with fresh Thai basil leaves.

2. Indian Butter Chicken Curry:

Ingredients: - 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces - 1 cup plain yogurt - 2 tbsp garam masala - 2 tbsp ground cumin - 1 tbsp ground turmeric - 1 tbsp ground coriander - 1 tsp chili powder - 4 tbsp unsalted butter - 1 large onion, finely chopped - 4 garlic cloves, minced - 1-inch piece of ginger, grated - 1 can (14 oz) tomato sauce - 1 cup heavy cream - Salt and pepper, to taste - Fresh cilantro, for garnish - Cooked basmati rice, for serving

Instructions: 1. In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, garam masala, cumin, turmeric, coriander, and chili powder. Add the chicken pieces and toss to coat. Marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight in the refrigerator. 2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and ginger, cooking until softened and fragrant. 3. Add the marinated chicken to the skillet, cooking until browned on all sides. 4. Stir in the tomato sauce and heavy cream, simmering gently for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened. 5. Season the curry with salt and pepper, to taste. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot over cooked basmati rice.

3. Jamaican Goat Curry:

Ingredients: - 2 lbs goat meat, cut into bite-sized pieces - 2 tbsp curry powder - 1 tsp ground allspice - 1 tsp ground ginger - 1 tsp ground thyme - 1 onion, chopped - 4 garlic cloves, minced - 2 tomatoes, chopped - 2 cups coconut milk - 2 cups chicken broth - 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed - Salt and pepper, to taste - Cooked white rice, for serving

Instructions: 1. In a large bowl, combine the curry powder, allspice, ginger, and thyme. Add the goat meat and toss to coat evenly. 2. Heat a tablespoon of oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking until softened. 3. Add the seasoned goat meat to the Dutch oven, browning on all sides. 4. Stir in the chopped tomatoes, coconut milk, and chicken broth, bringing the mixture to a simmer. 5. Add the cubed potatoes to the pot, cooking until tender and the meat is cooked through. 6. Season the curry with salt and pepper, adjusting to taste. Serve hot over cooked white rice.

4. Indonesian Beef Rendang:

Ingredients: - 2 lbs beef chuck, cut into cubes - 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk - 4 kaffir lime leaves - 2 lemongrass stalks, bruised - 4 shallots, chopped - 4 garlic cloves, minced - 1-inch piece of ginger, grated - 4 red chilies, chopped (seeds removed for less heat) - 2 tbsp tamarind paste - 2 tbsp brown sugar - Salt, to taste - Cooked jasmine rice, for serving

Instructions: 1. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot, combine the coconut milk, kaffir lime leaves, and bruised lemongrass stalks. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat. 2. In a food processor or blender, combine the chopped shallots, garlic, ginger, and red chilies, blending into a smooth paste. 3. Add the spice paste to the pot, stirring until fragrant. 4. Add the beef cubes to the pot, stirring to coat in the aromatic sauce. 5. Stir in the tamarind paste and brown sugar, adjusting the seasoning to taste. 6. Simmer the beef rendang over low heat for 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened. 7. Serve hot over cooked jasmine rice.

5. Moroccan Vegetable Tagine:

Ingredients: - 1 eggplant, cubed - 2 zucchinis, sliced - 2 carrots, sliced - 1 onion, chopped - 4 garlic cloves, minced - 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes - 1 can (14 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed - 1 cup vegetable broth - 2 tsp ground cumin - 2 tsp ground coriander - 1 tsp ground cinnamon - 1 tsp ground ginger - 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro - Salt and pepper, to taste - Cooked couscous, for serving

Instructions: 1. In a large skillet or tagine pot, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking until softened. 2. Add the cubed eggplant, sliced zucchinis, and sliced carrots to the pot, stirring to combine. 3. Stir in the diced tomatoes, chickpeas, vegetable broth, ground cumin, ground coriander, ground cinnamon, and ground ginger. 4. Cover the pot and simmer gently for 30-40 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender. 5. Season the tagine with salt and pepper, to taste. Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro and serve hot over cooked couscous.

Conclusion:

Exploring exotic curry recipes is a fantastic way to elevate your culinary skills and expand your flavor palette. Whether you’re craving the bold spices of Thai green curry or the rich complexity of Indian butter chicken, there’s a curry dish to suit every taste. So, gather your ingredients, unleash your creativity in the kitchen, and prepare to embark on a flavorful journey around the globe with these five exotic curry recipes!
