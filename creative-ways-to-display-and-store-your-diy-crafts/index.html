Creative Ways to Display and Store Your DIY Crafts


Are you a DIY enthusiast who loves crafting? Do you find it challenging to keep your craft supplies organized and display your finished projects in an aesthetically pleasing way? If so, you’re in the right place! In this blog post, we will explore creative and practical ways to display and store your DIY crafts. By following these tips, you’ll not only keep your crafting space tidy but also showcase your handmade creations in style. So let’s dive in and unleash our creativity to tackle the challenge of craft organization and display!

1. Open Shelving

One of the easiest ways to display and store your DIY crafts is by using open shelving. This allows you to show off your creations while keeping them easily accessible. Install shelving units on the walls of your craft room or studio, and arrange your crafts in an organized manner. You can also divide the shelves into different sections based on the type of craft or color scheme for a visually appealing display.

2. Mason Jars and Glass Containers

Mason jars and glass containers can serve as both storage and display for various craft supplies. Fill them with buttons, beads, ribbons, or even paintbrushes, and arrange them on a shelf or countertop. Not only will they keep your supplies neatly organized, but they will also add a charming and rustic touch to your crafting space.

3. Pegboards

Pegboards are a versatile and functional solution for displaying your DIY crafts. Install a pegboard on the wall and hang your tools and supplies with the help of hooks and baskets. You can paint the board in vibrant colors to add a pop of personality to your craft room. Pegboards not only keep your crafting essentials within reach but also make for an eye-catching display.

4. Repurposed Furniture

Get creative with repurposed furniture to store and display your DIY crafts. Use an old ladder as a display rack for towels, fabric, or yarn. Convert an antique dresser into a craft storage unit by adding dividers and organizing your materials. Repurposing furniture not only adds character to your crafting space but also helps you make the most of your existing resources.

5. Rolling Carts

Investing in a rolling cart is a game-changer for craft organization. Choose a cart with multiple tiers and storage compartments to hold your supplies. You can customize it by attaching hooks, baskets, and containers to keep everything organized. The best part is that you can easily move the cart around, giving you flexibility and convenience while working on your projects.

6. Wall Grids and Wire Baskets

Wall grids and wire baskets are not only practical but also provide a stylish way to display your DIY crafts. Hang a wire grid on the wall and use S-hooks to hang your tools, scissors, and other supplies. Attach wire baskets to the grid to store yarn, fabric scraps, or embellishments. This modern and industrial look will add a touch of sophistication to your craft room.

7. Shadow Boxes and Display Cases

For showcasing your smaller DIY crafts, consider using shadow boxes or display cases. Arrange your miniature sculptures, jewelry, or handmade trinkets within these boxes to protect and display them simultaneously. Hang them on the wall or place them on a shelf to create an eye-catching focal point in your craft room.

8. Vintage Suitcases and Trunks

Vintage suitcases and trunks can add a touch of nostalgia to your craft space while serving as unique storage options. Stack them up to create a mini shelving unit or use them to store fabric, yarn, or scrapbooking supplies. The antique look of these suitcases will give your craft room a vintage vibe.

9. Wall-mounted Mason Jar Organizers

Another fun way to incorporate mason jars into your craft room is by creating wall-mounted organizers. Attach a wooden board to the wall and glue mason jar lids to it. Screw the jars onto the lids, and voila! You have a practical and visually appealing storage solution for small craft supplies like buttons, beads, or glitter.

10. Hanging Planters

Yes, you read that right! Hanging planters can be used to store and display your DIY crafts creatively. Secure hanging planters on the wall and store your paintbrushes, knitting needles, or pens in them. With their unique design and ability to free up space on your working table, hanging planters make for an unexpected and visually intriguing storage solution.

Conclusion

Organizing and displaying your DIY crafts doesn’t have to be a daunting task. By utilizing these creative ideas, you can transform your craft room into an organized and visually appealing space. From open shelving to repurposed furniture, from mason jars to hanging planters, there are endless possibilities to showcase your handmade creations while keeping your supplies within reach. So go ahead and let your creativity flow as you embark on this journey of craft organization and display! Happy crafting!
