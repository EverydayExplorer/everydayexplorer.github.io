The Top 10 Sports Documentaries You Need to Watch


The world of sports is filled with inspiring stories, intense competition, and unforgettable moments. These stories have been captured in numerous documentaries, providing viewers with a glimpse into the lives of athletes, coaches, and teams. In this blog post, we will explore the top 10 sports documentaries that you need to watch. These documentaries not only entertain but also educate and inspire, offering a unique perspective on the world of sports.

1. O.J.: Made in America (2016)
This critically acclaimed documentary series delves into the life of O.J. Simpson, a former NFL star turned infamous figure in American history. The film explores Simpson’s rise to fame, his involvement in the infamous murder trial, and his subsequent fall from grace. With a runtime of over four hours, O.J.: Made in America provides a comprehensive look at Simpson’s life and the cultural impact of his story.

2. The Last Dance (2020)
This 10-part documentary series chronicles the final season of Michael Jordan and the Chicago Bulls in 1997-1998. The film features never-before-seen footage and interviews with key players, coaches, and team members, providing a behind-the-scenes look at one of the most successful and iconic teams in NBA history. The Last Dance is a must-watch for basketball fans and anyone interested in the story of Michael Jordan’s legendary career.

3. Icarus (2017)
This gripping documentary follows filmmaker Bryan Fogel as he investigates the world of doping in sports. Initially set out to explore the effects of performance-enhancing drugs on his cycling performance, Fogel stumbles upon a much larger conspiracy involving Russia and the 2014 Sochi Winter Olympics. Icarus is a riveting exploration of the dark side of sports and the lengths athletes will go to gain an edge.

4. The Two Escobars (2010)
This documentary tells the story of two men named Escobar: Pablo Escobar, the notorious drug lord, and Andres Escobar, a Colombian soccer player. The film explores the intertwined lives of these two men and their impact on Colombian society during the 1990s. The Two Escobars is a powerful story of tragedy, corruption, and the power of sports to unite a nation.

5. The Ivory Game (2016)
This harrowing documentary exposes the illegal ivory trade and the devastating impact it has on African elephants. The film follows a group of activists, rangers, and undercover operatives as they work to expose and dismantle the criminal networks responsible for the poaching of thousands of elephants each year. The Ivory Game is a must-watch for anyone interested in animal rights and conservation.

6. Senna (2010)
This documentary tells the story of Ayrton Senna, one of the most successful and beloved Formula 1 drivers in history. The film features never-before-seen footage of Senna’s career, as well as interviews with those who knew him best. Senna is a thrilling and emotional tribute to a legendary driver and a powerful exploration of the dangers of Formula 1 racing.

7. The 16th Man (2016)
This documentary tells the story of the South African rugby team during the apartheid era. The film explores the role of rugby in South African society and the impact of the team’s integration on the country’s political landscape. The 16th Man is a powerful story of unity, resilience, and the transformative power of sports.

8. Hoop Dreams (1994)
This classic documentary follows two young men from the Chicago projects as they pursue their dreams of playing in the NBA. The film provides an intimate look at the challenges faced by these young athletes, both on and off the court, as they navigate the complex world of amateur basketball and the pressure to succeed. Hoop Dreams is a poignant and inspiring look at the power of perseverance and the American Dream.

9. The Rise and Fall of the Great American Sports Movie (2016)
This documentary explores the history of sports films in American cinema, from the early days of silent films to the modern blockbusters of today. The film features interviews with filmmakers, actors, and sports figures, providing a comprehensive look at the evolution of sports films and their impact on American culture.
