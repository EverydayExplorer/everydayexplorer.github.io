Animated Series That Are Actually for Adults


In recent years, the landscape of animated television has transformed dramatically. No longer confined to the realm of children’s programming, animated series now occupy a significant space within adult entertainment. These shows blend humor, social commentary, and complex storytelling, making them appealing to a mature audience. This article delves into some of the most popular animated series aimed at adults, highlighting their unique qualities, themes, and cultural impact.

The Emergence of Adult Animation

The shift toward adult-oriented animated series began in the late 20th century, with shows like The Simpsons pushing boundaries and exploring themes that resonated with older viewers. The success of such programs paved the way for a new wave of animated content that appeals to adults, often featuring darker humor, satirical elements, and intricate plots.

Today, adult animation encompasses a diverse array of genres, from comedy to drama, and even science fiction. This genre’s evolution reflects societal changes and the growing acceptance of animation as a valid medium for storytelling, capable of addressing complex themes and emotions.

1. BoJack Horseman

One of the standout series in adult animation is BoJack Horseman. Created by Raphael Bob-Waksberg, this show follows the life of a washed-up television star, BoJack, who happens to be an anthropomorphic horse. Set in a world filled with talking animals and humans, BoJack Horseman tackles serious subjects such as addiction, depression, and the struggle for personal redemption.

What sets this series apart is its ability to balance humor with poignant moments. The characters are deeply flawed yet relatable, allowing viewers to connect with their struggles. The show’s unique animation style and clever writing have garnered critical acclaim, making it a staple in the realm of adult animation.

2. Rick and Morty

Rick and Morty, created by Justin Roiland and Dan Harmon, is another prominent example of adult animation that has taken the world by storm. The series follows the misadventures of an eccentric scientist, Rick Sanchez, and his good-hearted but impressionable grandson, Morty. The show combines elements of science fiction with absurd humor, often delving into existential themes and the complexities of family dynamics.

The show’s rapid-fire wit, inventive storytelling, and intricate world-building have earned it a dedicated fanbase. Each episode presents a new adventure, filled with bizarre creatures, alternate realities, and thought-provoking questions about morality and the human condition. Rick and Morty has not only entertained audiences but also sparked discussions about its deeper themes, making it a landmark in adult animation.

3. Archer

Archer, created by Adam Reed, is an animated spy comedy that follows the exploits of Sterling Archer, a self-absorbed secret agent working for a dysfunctional intelligence agency. The show is known for its sharp dialogue, clever parodies, and distinctive animation style.

Archer stands out for its unique blend of humor and action, with each episode often resembling a mini-thriller. The characters are exaggerated versions of typical spy tropes, creating a satirical take on the espionage genre. As the series progresses, it explores the personal lives and relationships of its characters, adding depth to an otherwise lighthearted premise.

4. Futurama

From the mind of Matt Groening, the creator of The Simpsons, Futurama is a space-age comedy that follows the adventures of Philip J. Fry, a pizza delivery boy who is cryogenically frozen and wakes up 1,000 years in the future. Combining science fiction with humor, the show cleverly satirizes contemporary society and explores themes of identity, technology, and the human experience.

Futurama features a rich cast of characters, each with their quirks and story arcs. Its intelligent writing and cultural references have garnered a loyal following, making it a beloved classic among adult animated series. The show’s ability to blend comedy with thought-provoking themes sets it apart from typical animated fare.

5. Big Mouth

Created by Nick Kroll, Andrew Goldberg, Mark Levin, and Jennifer Flackett, Big Mouth is a coming-of-age animated series that tackles the awkwardness of adolescence. Through the lens of a group of middle schoolers, the show explores the various challenges of puberty, including body image, sexuality, and friendships.

The series is notable for its candid and humorous approach to sensitive topics, providing a relatable portrayal of the teenage experience. With its diverse cast of characters and unique animation style, Big Mouth has resonated with both young and adult audiences alike. The show emphasizes the importance of open conversations about growing up and navigating life’s challenges.

6. Solar Opposites

From one of the co-creators of Rick and Morty, Justin Roiland, Solar Opposites offers a fresh take on the alien sitcom genre. The series follows a family of aliens who crash-land on Earth and must navigate human society while grappling with their own cultural differences.

Solar Opposites combines humor with social commentary, exploring themes of identity, belonging, and the absurdity of human behavior. The show’s unique premise and engaging characters provide a humorous yet insightful look at the complexities of life on Earth from an outsider’s perspective.

7. Tuca & Bertie

Tuca & Bertie, created by Lisa Hanawalt, is an animated series that follows the friendship between two anthropomorphic birds living in the same apartment building. The show delves into themes of mental health, relationships, and the challenges of adulthood, all while maintaining a whimsical and visually stunning animation style.

With its unique storytelling and vibrant aesthetics, Tuca & Bertie has garnered a dedicated following. The show tackles issues such as anxiety, career struggles, and the pressures of adult life, making it relatable to many viewers. Its exploration of female friendship and empowerment adds depth to the narrative, setting it apart in the realm of adult animation.

8. The Boondocks

Based on the comic strip by Aaron McGruder, The Boondocks is an animated series that addresses race relations, social justice, and cultural issues in America. The show follows two young African American brothers, Huey and Riley Freeman, as they navigate life in a predominantly white suburb.

The Boondocks is known for its sharp social commentary and thought-provoking narratives. The series combines humor with serious themes, often sparking conversations about race, identity, and the complexities of modern society. Its bold approach to storytelling has made it a significant entry in the canon of adult animated series.

9. Daria

A spin-off of Beavis and Butt-Head, Daria centers around a cynical teenage girl navigating the trials and tribulations of high school. The show offers a satirical take on teenage life, tackling themes such as social conformity, identity, and the absurdity of teenage angst.

Daria stands out for its intelligent writing and relatable characters. The show’s protagonist, Daria Morgendorffer, has become an iconic figure, representing the struggles of adolescence with wit and humor. The series remains relevant today, resonating with new generations of viewers who appreciate its commentary on societal expectations.

10. Harley Quinn

Based on the DC Comics character, Harley Quinn is an animated series that reimagines the iconic anti-heroine’s story. The show follows Harley as she breaks free from the Joker’s influence and embarks on her own adventures in Gotham City.

With its irreverent humor and vibrant animation, Harley Quinn has garnered critical acclaim for its unique take on the superhero genre. The series explores themes of empowerment, friendship, and self-discovery, all while delivering a healthy dose of action and comedy. Its fresh perspective on beloved characters has made it a standout in the realm of adult animation.

Conclusion

The rise of adult animation has revolutionized the television landscape, providing viewers with diverse and thought-provoking content. These animated series not only entertain but also provoke discussions about important societal issues, making them relevant and impactful. From the existential musings of BoJack Horseman to the absurd adventures of Rick and Morty, these shows showcase the depth and creativity of adult animation, proving that this genre is more than just cartoons for kids.

As audiences continue to embrace these innovative series, it is clear that animated storytelling has found a permanent place in the realm of adult entertainment. Whether through humor, drama, or social commentary, these animated series challenge conventions and offer fresh perspectives on the human experience.
