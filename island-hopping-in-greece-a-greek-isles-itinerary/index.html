Island Hopping in Greece: A Greek Isles Itinerary


Greece is known for its stunning landscapes, rich history, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals. One of the best ways to experience all that Greece has to offer is through island hopping. With over 6,000 islands scattered across the Aegean and Ionian Seas, there is no shortage of options for a memorable island-hopping adventure.

In this blog post, we will guide you through a Greek Isles itinerary, highlighting some of the must-visit islands and the unique experiences they offer. Whether you are a history buff, a beach lover, or a food enthusiast, there is an island in Greece that will capture your heart.

Day 1-2: Athens

Your island-hopping adventure begins in Athens, the capital city of Greece. Spend a day or two exploring the ancient ruins of the Acropolis, including the iconic Parthenon. Immerse yourself in the history and culture of Greece at the Acropolis Museum, and take a stroll through the charming neighborhoods of Plaka and Monastiraki.

Day 3-5: Mykonos

From Athens, take a ferry or a short domestic flight to the cosmopolitan island of Mykonos. Known for its vibrant nightlife, luxurious resorts, and picturesque beaches, Mykonos offers a perfect blend of relaxation and excitement. Explore the narrow streets of Mykonos Town, visit the famous windmills, and soak up the sun at the stunning beaches of Paradise and Super Paradise.

Day 6-8: Santorini

Next stop, Santorini – the jewel of the Aegean Sea. Famous for its breathtaking sunsets, whitewashed houses, and blue-domed churches, Santorini is an island that will leave you in awe. Explore the charming villages of Oia, Fira, and Imerovigli, and take a dip in the crystal-clear waters of the volcanic beaches. Don’t forget to try the local delicacies, such as fava, tomato keftedes, and Assyrtiko wine.

Day 9-11: Crete

From Santorini, hop on a ferry to the largest and most diverse island in Greece – Crete. With its rich history, stunning landscapes, and delicious cuisine, Crete offers something for everyone. Explore the ancient ruins of Knossos, hike the breathtaking Samaria Gorge, and immerse yourself in the vibrant atmosphere of Chania’s old town. Indulge in traditional Cretan dishes like dakos, kalitsounia, and fresh seafood.

Day 12-14: Rhodes

Finish off your Greek Isles itinerary with a visit to the medieval island of Rhodes. Explore the UNESCO World Heritage-listed Old Town, wander through narrow cobbled streets, and marvel at the Palace of the Grand Master. Relax on the golden beaches of Faliraki and Lindos, and don’t miss the opportunity to taste some traditional Rhodian dishes, such as melekouni and soumada.

As you can see, island hopping in Greece offers an incredible variety of experiences. From the vibrant nightlife of Mykonos to the stunning sunsets of Santorini and the rich history of Crete and Rhodes, every island has its own unique charm. So grab your passport, pack your bags, and get ready for an unforgettable adventure through the Greek Isles.
