Funny Women Who Are Killing It in Stand-Up Comedy


Stand-up comedy has long been dominated by men, but that’s rapidly changing. Women are stepping onto stages worldwide, armed with wit, sarcasm, and razor-sharp humor, captivating audiences and challenging stereotypes. From classic comedy clubs to viral YouTube clips, these funny women are not just holding their own – they’re absolutely killing it!

  1. Ali Wong: If you haven’t watched Ali Wong’s stand-up specials on Netflix, you’re missing out. With her fearless and hilariously raunchy style, Wong fearlessly dives into taboo topics with an unapologetic flair. Her sharp observations about marriage, motherhood, and Asian-American identity resonate with audiences across the globe.

  2. Tig Notaro: Tig Notaro’s deadpan delivery and dry humor have earned her a dedicated following. She’s not afraid to tackle tough subjects, including her own battle with breast cancer, with an authenticity that’s both moving and side-splittingly funny. Her comedy special ‘Live’ remains a masterpiece of raw and honest storytelling.

  3. Phoebe Robinson: Known for her podcast ‘2 Dope Queens’ (with Jessica Williams) and her book ‘You Can’t Touch My Hair (And Other Things I Still Have to Explain),’ Phoebe Robinson is a force to be reckoned with in the comedy world. Her sharp wit and keen observations about race, gender, and pop culture make her stand out as a comedic powerhouse.

  4. Cristela Alonzo: Cristela Alonzo made history as the first Latina to create, produce, and star in her own network sitcom, ‘Cristela.’ Her stand-up comedy is equally groundbreaking, blending humor with social commentary about being a woman of color in America. Alonzo’s infectious energy and relatable storytelling make her a standout in the comedy scene.

  5. Hannah Gadsby: Australian comedian Hannah Gadsby burst onto the international scene with her groundbreaking Netflix special, ‘Nanette.’ Blending comedy with powerful storytelling, Gadsby challenges societal norms and confronts difficult topics with intelligence and vulnerability. Her unique approach to comedy has earned her critical acclaim and sparked important conversations about art, identity, and trauma.

  6. Iliza Shlesinger: With her razor-sharp wit and energetic stage presence, Iliza Shlesinger has become one of the most recognizable faces in stand-up comedy. Her comedy specials tackle everything from dating and relationships to the absurdities of modern life, resonating with audiences of all ages.

  7. Maria Bamford: Maria Bamford’s surreal and self-deprecating humor sets her apart in the comedy world. Known for her distinctive voices and characters, Bamford fearlessly explores her own struggles with mental illness and identity, turning personal pain into comedy gold. Her Netflix series ‘Lady Dynamite’ further showcases her unique comedic voice and storytelling prowess.

  8. Nikki Glaser: Nikki Glaser’s frank and fearless approach to comedy has earned her a loyal fanbase. Whether she’s discussing relationships, sex, or body image, Glaser’s unfiltered honesty and quick wit never fail to entertain. Her comedy specials and talk show appearances showcase her comedic talent and versatility.

  9. Aparna Nancherla: Aparna Nancherla’s offbeat humor and deadpan delivery have made her a standout in the comedy world. From her stand-up specials to her writing on shows like ‘Late Night with Seth Meyers’ and ‘BoJack Horseman,’ Nancherla’s unique perspective and quirky observations set her apart as a comedic force to be reckoned with.

  10. Michelle Wolf: Michelle Wolf made headlines with her controversial performance at the 2018 White House Correspondents’ Dinner, but her comedic talent extends far beyond politics. With her sharp wit and fearless commentary, Wolf tackles everything from feminism to pop culture with a refreshing and irreverent perspective.

These women are just a few examples of the incredible talent currently reshaping the landscape of stand-up comedy. With their fearless humor and unapologetic voices, they’re not just breaking barriers – they’re setting the stage for a new era of comedy where women reign supreme. So the next time you’re in need of a good laugh, look no further than these funny women who are absolutely killing it in stand-up comedy.
