15 Vegan Recipes That Will Impress Even Meat Eaters


When it comes to vegan cooking, many people assume that the dishes will be bland, flavorless, and lacking in variety. However, that couldn’t be further from the truth! In fact, vegan cuisine is incredibly diverse and can be just as impressive, if not more so, than dishes that include meat. In this blog post, we will be exploring 15 vegan recipes that are sure to impress even the most devout carnivores. So whether you’re a vegan looking to expand your recipe repertoire or someone looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, these recipes are for you!

1. Vegan Lentil Shepherd’s Pie

This hearty, savory dish is a vegan twist on a classic comfort food. Made with a flavorful lentil and vegetable filling, topped with a creamy layer of mashed potatoes, and baked to perfection, this shepherd’s pie is sure to be a crowd pleaser. The lentils provide a rich source of protein and fiber, making it a satisfying and nutritious meal.

2. Jackfruit ‘Pulled Pork’ Sandwiches

One of the most popular meat substitutes for vegans is jackfruit. When cooked and shredded, jackfruit has a texture similar to pulled pork, making it the perfect ingredient for vegan ‘pulled pork’ sandwiches. Top the shredded jackfruit with your favorite barbecue sauce, pile it high on a bun, and enjoy a delicious and satisfying sandwich that even meat eaters will love.

3. Vegan Buffalo Cauliflower Wings

Buffalo wings are a classic game day snack, but who says they have to be made with chicken? Cauliflower florets can be transformed into deliciously crispy wings that are coated in a spicy buffalo sauce. Serve them with vegan ranch or blue cheese dressing and celery sticks for the ultimate vegan appetizer that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

4. Vegan Eggplant Parmesan

Eggplant Parmesan is a staple in Italian cuisine, and this vegan version does not disappoint. Lightly breaded and baked eggplant slices are layered with marinara sauce, vegan cheese, and fresh basil, creating a dish that is rich in flavor and texture. Serve it with a side of pasta and a fresh salad for a complete and satisfying meal.

5. Vegan Portobello Mushroom ‘Steak’

Portobello mushrooms have a robust and meaty flavor that makes them a great substitute for steak. Marinate the mushrooms in a savory mixture of balsamic vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic, then grill or roast them to perfection. Serve the ‘steaks’ with roasted vegetables and a side of creamy mashed potatoes for a vegan version of a classic steak dinner.

6. Tempeh Tacos

Tacos are a versatile dish that can easily be made vegan. Instead of using ground beef or chicken, opt for tempeh, a fermented soy product that has a nutty flavor and a firm, meat-like texture. Crumble the tempeh and season it with taco seasoning, then sauté until browned. Serve the tempeh in corn tortillas with your favorite taco toppings for a delicious and satisfying vegan meal.

7. Vegan Chickpea Curry

Curry is a flavorful and aromatic dish that can easily be made vegan by substituting the meat with chickpeas. Simmer the chickpeas in a fragrant blend of spices, coconut milk, and tomato sauce for a creamy and hearty curry. Serve it over basmati rice or with naan bread for a complete and satisfying meal that will impress even the most skeptical meat eaters.

8. Vegan Stuffed Peppers

Stuffed peppers are a crowd-pleasing dish that can easily be made vegan. Fill bell peppers with a mixture of cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, and spices, then bake them until tender. Top the stuffed peppers with shredded vegan cheese and a dollop of vegan sour cream for a delicious and filling meal that is both healthy and satisfying.

9. Vegan Butternut Squash Risotto

Risotto is a creamy and comforting dish that can easily be made vegan by using vegetable broth and omitting the butter and cheese. Add roasted butternut squash and sautéed mushrooms for extra flavor and texture. The result is a rich and satisfying risotto that is both vegan and gluten-free.

10. Vegan Sushi Rolls

Sushi rolls are a popular dish that can easily be made vegan. Fill nori sheets with a variety of fresh vegetables, avocado, tofu, and vegan mayonnaise, then roll them up and slice into bite-sized pieces. Serve the sushi rolls with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger for a delicious and healthy vegan meal that will impress even the most discerning sushi lovers.

11. Vegan Mushroom and Lentil Bolognese

Bolognese sauce is traditionally made with ground beef, but this vegan version swaps the meat for mushrooms and lentils. The combination of sautéed mushrooms, lentils, onions, garlic, and tomatoes creates a rich and flavorful sauce that pairs perfectly with pasta. Serve the bolognese with a sprinkle of vegan Parmesan cheese and a side of garlic bread for a satisfying and comforting meal.

12. Vegan Sweet Potato and Black Bean Enchiladas

Enchiladas are a delicious and filling meal that can easily be made vegan. Fill tortillas with a mixture of mashed sweet potatoes, black beans, onions, and spices, then top with enchilada sauce and vegan cheese. Bake until the cheese is melted and bubbly, then serve with a side of guacamole and salsa for a flavorful and satisfying meal that will please vegans and meat eaters alike.

13. Vegan Vegetable Stir-Fry

Stir-fries are a quick and easy meal that can easily be made vegan. Cut a variety of vegetables into bite-sized pieces and sauté them in a flavorful sauce made from soy sauce, ginger, garlic, and agave nectar. Serve the stir-fry over a bed of steamed rice or noodles for a quick and delicious vegan meal that is low in calories and packed with nutrients.

14. Vegan Mediterranean Quinoa Salad

Quinoa salads are a great option for a light and refreshing vegan meal. Toss cooked quinoa with cucumbers, tomatoes, red onions, Kalamata olives, and a lemon-herb dressing for a Mediterranean-inspired salad that is bursting with flavor. Top it with a sprinkle of vegan feta cheese or toasted pine nuts for added texture and flavor.

15. Vegan Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Who says vegan desserts can’t be indulgent? This chocolate avocado mousse is rich, creamy, and decadent, yet it’s made with wholesome ingredients like ripe avocados, cocoa powder, and maple syrup. Blend all the ingredients together until smooth and refrigerate until set. Top the mousse with a dollop of coconut whipped cream and shaved dark chocolate for a dessert that will impress even the most dedicated chocolate lovers.

In conclusion, vegan cooking doesn’t have to be boring or devoid of flavor. With these 15 vegan recipes, you can impress even the most dedicated meat eaters with delicious and satisfying meals. Whether you’re serving lentil shepherd’s pie, jackfruit ‘pulled pork’ sandwiches, or vegan buffalo cauliflower wings, these recipes are sure to please vegans and non-vegans alike. So try incorporating more plant-based meals into your diet and discover the amazing flavors and variety that vegan cuisine has to offer.
