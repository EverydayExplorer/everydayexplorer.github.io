How to Properly Sight In Your Hunting Rifle


When it comes to hunting and shooting, proper sight alignment and sighting in your rifle is essential. Hunting demands precision, accuracy, and confidence in your shooting abilities. And to achieve that, you need to sight in your rifle properly.

Sighting in your hunting rifle may sound like a straightforward task, but it requires a bit of knowledge, patience, and time. With a properly sighted in rifle, you will have higher accuracy, better shot placement, and ultimately a more successful hunt.

Here are some essential tips on how to properly sight in your hunting rifle:

Choose the Right Scope

Your scope is a vital component in your rifle’s sighting system, and it should be chosen carefully. The type of hunting rifles you have will help you determine the magnification you’ll require. A variable power scope is an excellent choice for most hunting rifles. You should also consider the size of the objective lens, tube size, and reticle.

Mount the Scope Correctly

It’s essential to mount your scope correctly, ensuring that it’s level and straight. A scope not installed correctly can result in inaccurate shots, which is why it’s essential to get this step right. Tighten all bolts until firmly seated, but avoid over-tightening.

Find the Right Ammunition

The type of rifle ammunition you use impacts trajectory, speed, and ultimately how accurately your rifle shoots. The ammo you choose should match your hunting game to ensure that your shot placement is optimal. There are various types of ammunition to choose from, including hollow-point, ballistic-tip, and full-metal jacket rounds.

Prepare Your Hunting Rifle and Range

Before sighting in, ensure your hunting rifle is clean, and the bore is free of debris. Once cleaned, you should establish your shooting range. For long-range shooting, you should pick a range of at least 100 yards.

Understand Your Rifle’s Ballistic Trajectory

Once you have your rifle ready to go, it’s important to understand the ballistic trajectory. Firearms experts recommend finding a ballistic calculator, choosing your cartridge type, and zeroing in your rifle at 200 yards. This distance reduces bullet drop and increases accuracy.

Sight in the Rifle

Once you’ve prepared your rifle and shooting range, it’s time to sight in your rifle. You should do this by adjusting your scope’s windage and elevation until your shots hit your target’s bullseye. A rifle rest will help you stay steady during sighting in.

Fine-tune Your Sighting and Confirm Accuracy

After making adjustments and approaching your bullseye, it’s essential to confirm that your shots are accurate. To do this, adjust your scope and shoot a few more rounds to ensure that you have created a grouping of three or four shots within 1-2 inches of each other. After confirming accuracy, test the rifle at different ranges. Taking your rifle to another range and sighting in again ensures your accuracy at longer distances, thereby improving your hunting ability.

Conclusion

Sighting in your hunting rifle is crucial in getting the best possible shot. With a properly sighted-in rifle, you can ensure accurate shot placement that allows for quick and humane taking down of your prey. Remember to take your time, choose the right scope, ammunition, and confirm your accuracy by testing at various ranges. The above tips will help you properly sight in your hunting rifle and, ultimately, increase your chances of success in the field.
