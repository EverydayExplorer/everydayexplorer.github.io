Creating a Survival Library: Must-Read Books for Preppers


In today’s uncertain world, it’s becoming increasingly important to be prepared for any situation. Whether it’s a natural disaster, civil unrest, or a global pandemic, having the knowledge and skills to survive is crucial. One of the best ways to gain that knowledge is by building a survival library filled with must-read books for preppers. Not only will these books provide valuable information and guidance, but they will also serve as a reference in times of need.

Why Create a Survival Library?

When it comes to survival, knowledge is power. In an emergency situation, you may not have access to the internet or other resources. Having a well-stocked survival library ensures that you have access to vital information even when the world around you is in chaos. Additionally, books provide a sense of comfort and familiarity. They can be a source of inspiration and motivation during challenging times.

What to Look for in Prepper Books

When building your survival library, it’s important to choose books that cover a wide range of topics relevant to emergency preparedness. Here are a few key considerations when selecting must-read prepper books:

Comprehensive Coverage

Look for books that cover a wide range of topics such as food storage, water purification, self-defense, first aid, navigation, wilderness survival, and emergency shelter. A comprehensive library will equip you with the necessary knowledge to handle various survival scenarios.

Credible Authors

Choose books written by credible authors who have firsthand experience or extensive expertise in the field. Authors who have real-life experience in survival situations or are renowned experts in their respective fields will provide you with the most reliable and accurate information.

Up-to-Date Information

Ensure the books you choose are up-to-date and contain the latest information. Survival techniques and strategies can evolve over time, so it’s essential to have access to the most current and relevant information.

Practical Instruction

Look for books that provide practical instruction and step-by-step guides. Theory is important, but practical knowledge is what will help you when facing real-life survival situations. Books with clear instructions and illustrations are invaluable resources.

Diverse Perspectives

Consider books that provide diverse perspectives on survival and preparedness. Each author may approach the subject from a different point of view, offering unique insights and strategies. This diversity will broaden your knowledge and help you develop a well-rounded approach to preparedness.

Must-Read Books for Your Survival Library

Now that we understand what to look for in prepper books, let’s explore some must-read titles to include in your survival library:

1. ‘SAS Survival Handbook’ by John ‘Lofty’ Wiseman

Considered a classic in the field, this book provides comprehensive guidance on a wide array of survival skills. From basic first aid to building shelters and finding water, it covers it all. The author’s experience as a former SAS soldier lends credibility to the information shared in the book.

2. ‘The Prepper’s Blueprint’ by Tess Pennington

This book serves as a comprehensive guide to preparedness, covering everything from creating a survival plan to stockpiling supplies and building a community. It offers practical advice and actionable steps for preppers at all levels of experience.

3. ‘Emergency: This Book Will Save Your Life’ by Neil Strauss

Neil Strauss takes a unique approach to survival in this book by immersing himself in the world of disaster preparedness. He shares his personal experiences and lessons learned, providing a compelling and relatable narrative that resonates with readers.

4. ‘Bushcraft 101: A Field Guide to the Art of Wilderness Survival’ by Dave Canterbury

For those interested in wilderness survival, this book is a must-read. It covers a range of topics including building shelters, foraging for food, and starting fires. The author’s expertise and clear instructions make it an essential resource for outdoor enthusiasts and preppers alike.

5. ‘When All Hell Breaks Loose: Stuff You Need To Survive When Disaster Strikes’ by Cody Lundin

Cody Lundin, a renowned survival instructor, shares his practical advice on surviving various disaster scenarios. This book emphasizes the importance of psychological preparedness and provides valuable insights into the mindset required for survival.

6. ‘The Modern Survival Manual: Surviving the Economic Collapse’ by Fernando ‘FerFAL’ Aguirre

This book offers a unique perspective on survival in urban environments and focuses specifically on the challenges of an economic collapse. It provides practical guidance on topics such as home security, self-defense, and navigating through social unrest.

7. ‘The Pocket First-Aid Field Guide: Treatment and Prevention of Outdoor Emergencies’ by George E. Dallam

First aid skills are essential in any emergency situation, and this compact guide is a valuable resource to have on hand. It provides concise instructions for dealing with common outdoor injuries and illnesses, making it an excellent addition to your survival library.

8. ‘Prepper’s Long-Term Survival Guide’ by Jim Cobb

This book goes beyond short-term emergencies and focuses on long-term survival strategies. It covers topics such as sustainable food production, alternative energy sources, and securing your property. It’s a comprehensive guide for those looking to be self-reliant in the long run.

Building Your Survival Library

Building a survival library is an ongoing process. As you gain more knowledge and expertise, you may discover additional books that align with your specific interests and needs. Consider joining prepper communities and forums to get recommendations from like-minded individuals.

When organizing your library, categorize the books by topics such as food and water, shelter, self-defense, first aid, and wilderness survival. This will make it easier to locate the information you need quickly.

Remember, a survival library is only useful if you take the time to read and learn from the books within it. Regularly set aside time to study the material, practice the skills you learn, and stay updated on new techniques and developments in the field.

By creating a well-curated and diverse survival library, you’ll be better equipped to face any adversity that comes your way. Start building your collection today and invest in the knowledge that will enhance your preparedness. Stay safe, stay informed, and be ready for whatever may come.
