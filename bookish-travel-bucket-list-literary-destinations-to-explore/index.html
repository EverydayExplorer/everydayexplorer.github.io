Bookish Travel Bucket List: Literary Destinations to Explore


Are you an avid book lover looking to immerse yourself in the worlds of your favorite authors? If so, then a bookish travel bucket list is just what you need! There are countless literary destinations around the globe that offer an opportunity to step into the pages of beloved novels and experience the settings that inspired these stories. From famous landmarks to charming bookshops, these destinations will transport you to a world of imagination and storytelling.

1. London, England: The birthplace of numerous literary greats, London is a must-visit for any book lover. Explore the British Library, home to countless literary treasures including original manuscripts of famous authors like William Shakespeare and Jane Austen. Take a stroll along the Thames and visit iconic landmarks like the Globe Theatre, where Shakespeare’s plays were originally performed. Don’t forget to stop by famous bookstores like Foyles and Daunt Books, known for their vast collections and cozy reading corners.

2. Paris, France: Known as the ‘City of Love,’ Paris has also been a source of inspiration for many writers. Visit the Shakespeare and Company bookstore, a literary institution that has welcomed aspiring writers and book lovers for decades. Take a walk through the charming streets of the Latin Quarter, where Hemingway, Fitzgerald, and Joyce once roamed. And of course, don’t miss out on visiting the renowned cemetery, Père Lachaise, where you can pay homage to literary giants like Oscar Wilde and Marcel Proust.

3. Dublin, Ireland: For fans of Irish literature, Dublin is a treasure trove of literary history. Explore the Book of Kells at Trinity College, an illuminated manuscript dating back to the 9th century. Take a tour of the Dublin Writers Museum, showcasing the works of famous Irish authors like James Joyce, W.B. Yeats, and Samuel Beckett. And for a truly immersive experience, visit the pubs where literary greats such as Brendan Behan and Flann O’Brien used to gather and share their stories.

4. New York City, USA: The Big Apple has always been a hub for creativity and literary genius. Walk in the footsteps of the Beat Generation in Greenwich Village, visit the famous apartment building that inspired F. Scott Fitzgerald’s ‘The Great Gatsby,’ and explore the New York Public Library, one of the largest public library systems in the world. Don’t forget to stop by Strand Bookstore, a mecca for book lovers with its 18 miles of books on offer.

5. Edinburgh, Scotland: Steeped in history and literary tradition, Edinburgh is a dream destination for book lovers. Discover the inspiration behind J.K. Rowling’s Harry Potter series with a visit to the Elephant House café, where she first penned the famous novels. Take a tour of the Edinburgh Castle, where you can learn about the city’s rich literary history and the works of Scottish authors like Sir Walter Scott and Robert Louis Stevenson. And for a spooky adventure, explore the hidden closes and alleyways that inspired literary classics like ‘Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde.’

6. Tokyo, Japan: Japan has a rich literary tradition, with many celebrated authors hailing from this vibrant country. Visit the Yanaka neighborhood, known for its numerous old bookstores and antique shops. Explore the Morioka Shoten bookstore, which offers only one title at a time, allowing readers to fully immerse themselves in a single book. And don’t miss out on visiting the Museum of Modern Literature, which showcases the works of influential Japanese authors like Yukio Mishima and Natsume Soseki.

7. Buenos Aires, Argentina: For lovers of magical realism, Buenos Aires is a must-visit destination. Explore the charming neighborhood of San Telmo, where famous Argentine writer Jorge Luis Borges once lived. Visit the El Ateneo Grand Splendid bookstore, located in a former theater and considered one of the most beautiful bookstores in the world. And for a taste of local literature, dive into the works of writers like Julio Cortázar and Ernesto Sabato.

These are just a few of the countless literary destinations waiting to be explored. Whether you’re a fan of classic literature, contemporary novels, or poetry, there is a literary destination out there that will ignite your imagination and satisfy your bookish wanderlust. So, grab your favorite novel, pack your bags, and get ready to embark on a bookish adventure like no other!
