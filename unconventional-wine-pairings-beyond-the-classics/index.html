Unconventional Wine Pairings: Beyond the Classics


One of the great joys of wine is its ability to be paired with almost any type of food. While classics like red wine with steak or white wine with fish are well-known, there is a whole world of unconventional wine pairings waiting to be explored. In this blog post, we will dive into the realm of unconventional wine pairings and discover some unexpected and delightful combinations.

1. Pizza and Champagne:
Traditionally, pizza is associated with beer or soda, but a crisp and bubbly glass of Champagne can elevate your pizza dining experience to a whole new level. The acidity and effervescence of Champagne complement the flavors of tomato sauce, gooey cheese, and a variety of toppings. The contrast between the rich, savory pizza and the refreshing, crisp Champagne creates a unique and delicious combination that will impress your guests.

2. BBQ and Zinfandel:
Barbecue is all about bold and smoky flavors, and one wine that can stand up to the richness of BBQ is Zinfandel. The robust fruitiness and spice of Zinfandel pair wonderfully with the sweet, tangy, and savory flavors of barbecued meats. Whether you prefer ribs, pulled pork, or brisket, a glass of Zinfandel will enhance and balance the flavors of your BBQ feast.

3. Sushi and Riesling:
Sushi is often paired with sake, but Riesling can be an equally delightful and surprising choice. The natural sweetness and high acidity of Riesling complement the delicate flavors of raw fish and the soy-based dipping sauces. From light and dry to rich and sweet, Riesling offers a wide range of styles that can accommodate various types of sushi, making it a versatile and refreshing choice for sushi lovers.

4. Chocolate and Cabernet Sauvignon:
While dessert wines are commonly paired with chocolate, a full-bodied Cabernet Sauvignon can be an unexpected and wonderful match. The tannins in Cabernet Sauvignon help cut through the richness of chocolate and bring out the cocoa flavors. Opt for a dark chocolate with high cocoa content to create a harmonious combination of bitter and fruity notes that will leave you craving for more.

5. Spicy Thai Food and Gewürztraminer:
When it comes to pairing wine with spicy food, the general rule is to seek wines with a touch of sweetness. Gewürztraminer, with its floral aromas, tropical fruit flavors, and slight sweetness, is a perfect match for the complex and vibrant flavors of Thai cuisine. The sweetness of Gewürztraminer helps to tame the heat and bring out the subtle flavors of herbs and spices, resulting in a well-balanced and refreshing combination.

6. Cheeseburgers and Malbec:
The juicy and meaty flavors of a cheeseburger call for a wine with enough structure and richness to stand up to it. Enter Malbec – a bold and full-bodied red wine with dark fruit flavors and a hint of smokiness. The earthiness and robustness of Malbec complement the savory flavors of a cheeseburger, elevating this classic American dish to a whole new level of deliciousness.

7. Indian Curry and Grenache:
Pairing wine with the bold and complex flavors of Indian curry can be a challenge, but Grenache rises to the occasion. With its smooth texture, ripe fruit flavors, and gentle spice, Grenache can handle the richness and variety of spices found in Indian cuisine. Whether you’re enjoying a creamy butter chicken or a fiery lamb vindaloo, a glass of Grenache will complement the flavors and leave your taste buds satisfied.

As you can see, there are plenty of unconventional wine pairings that go beyond the classics. Exploring these unexpected combinations can elevate your dining experience and introduce you to a whole new world of flavors. So, next time you’re feeling adventurous, grab a bottle of wine and experiment with different pairings. Who knows, you might discover your own favorite unconventional wine pairing!

-–

This blog post explores unconventional wine pairings beyond the classics. Whether it’s pizza and Champagne or Indian curry and Grenache, there are numerous surprising combinations that can enhance your dining experience. By thinking outside the box and experimenting with different wine and food pairings, you can discover a whole new world of flavors. Don’t shy away from trying unconventional pairings and let your taste buds be your guide. Cheers to the endless possibilities of wine and food pairing!
