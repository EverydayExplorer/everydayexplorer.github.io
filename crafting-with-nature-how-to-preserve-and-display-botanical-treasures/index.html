Crafting with Nature: How to Preserve and Display Botanical Treasures


There is something magical about the beauty of nature and the intricate details found in the plant kingdom. From vibrant flowers to delicate leaves, the natural world offers a treasure trove of inspiration for crafters and enthusiasts. For those who want to capture and preserve the wonders of nature, crafting with botanical treasures is the perfect avenue for self-expression. In this blog post, we will explore various techniques and ideas for preserving and displaying botanical treasures to create stunning pieces that will last a lifetime.

1. Pressed Flower Art

One of the most popular techniques for preserving botanical treasures is pressing flowers. Pressed flower art has a long history and can be traced back to the Victorian era. To create pressed flower art, simply place flowers between the pages of a heavy book or use a flower press. After a few weeks, the flowers will have dried and flattened, ready to be used in various art projects. From framed artworks to greeting cards and bookmarks, the possibilities are endless.

2. Resin Jewelry

If you’re looking to create wearable art, resin jewelry is an excellent choice. By encasing small botanical elements in clear resin, you can preserve their beauty and create unique, one-of-a-kind pieces. Start by selecting small flowers, leaves, or even seeds that you want to immortalize. Arrange them in a mold and pour clear resin over them. As the resin cures, it will create a glossy, glass-like finish that showcases the botanical treasures within. Finish off with a chain or cord to create a stunning piece of jewelry.

3. Terrariums

Terrariums have become increasingly popular in recent years. These miniature indoor gardens are not only visually appealing but also a fantastic way to preserve and display botanical treasures. To create a terrarium, you will need a glass container, some pebbles for drainage, activated charcoal to keep the soil fresh, potting soil, and, of course, your chosen plants or moss. Arrange the plants in the container and add decorative elements like small figurines or rocks to create an enchanting scene. Terrariums are low-maintenance and make for beautiful home décor pieces.

4. Botanical Wall Art

Bring the beauty of nature indoors by creating botanical wall art. Start by collecting an assortment of dried leaves, flowers, and other plant materials. Arrange them in a visually pleasing composition on a canvas or a piece of wood. Secure the botanical elements using adhesive or glue, ensuring they are flat against the surface. Finally, you can finish the piece with a clear sealant to protect it and enhance the colors. Display your botanical wall art in your living room, bedroom, or even as a focal point in your home office.

5. Nature Journaling

For those who are inclined towards a more personal and introspective way of crafting, nature journaling is a wonderful option. A nature journal is a place to record observations, thoughts, and sketches inspired by the natural world. Each entry can include pressed flowers, leaf rubbings, or even watercolor paintings of your favorite plants. Nature journaling allows you to document your connection with nature and serves as a beautiful keepsake of your adventures.

These are just a few examples of how you can preserve and display botanical treasures through various crafting techniques. Let your creativity run wild and experiment with different materials and ideas. Nature offers us an endless supply of inspiration, so why not bring a piece of it into our everyday lives through our craft projects? Whether you’re a seasoned crafter or a beginner, crafting with nature is a fulfilling and sustainable way to create unique pieces that celebrate the beauty of the natural world. So go ahead, immerse yourself in the wonders of nature, and let your creativity bloom!
