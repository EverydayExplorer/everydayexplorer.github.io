The Rights of Persons with Albinism: Overcoming Discrimination


Albinism is a genetic disorder characterized by the lack of melanin production resulting in little or no skin pigmentation, hair color, and eye color. Persons with albinism (PWA) are often subject to discrimination, persecution, and stigmatization based on myths and beliefs in some African countries. The violation of their rights is a widespread phenomenon that requires immediate attention and action. In this blog, we will discuss the rights of persons with albinism and how they can overcome discrimination.

Right to Life: PWAs have the right to life and security of person, just like any other person. The violation of this right is often a result of beliefs that their body parts possess magical properties or bring good fortune. These beliefs have led to the killing and mutilation of PWAs across several African countries, especially in Tanzania, Malawi, and Burundi. Governments and civil society organizations must work together to end these atrocities by enforcing strict penalties, educating the public on albinism, and promoting positive perceptions of PWAs.

Right to Education: PWAs have the right to education, and this includes access to quality education without discrimination. However, PWAs often struggle to attend school due to a lack of understanding of albinism by teachers, inadequate facilities, and bullying by peers. Governments and stakeholders must work towards the integration of PWAs in classrooms, the provision of assistive devices, and training of teachers on albinism.

Right to Health: PWAs have the right to health and access to medical care without discrimination. However, in some African countries, the cost of sunscreen and other skin protection products are prohibitive, making it difficult for PWAs to protect themselves from the sun’s harmful rays. Governments should subsidize the cost of these products, and healthcare workers must be trained to provide better care for PWAs.

Right to Employment: PWAs have the right to work and be treated fairly in the workplace without discrimination. However, they often face challenges in securing employment due to negative perceptions of albinism and a lack of education and training opportunities. Employers should be encouraged to hire PWAs, and entrepreneurship opportunities should be created for PWAs to become self-reliant.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, PWAs deserve equal treatment and protection from discrimination, just like any other person. Governments and stakeholders must work together to end the violence, persecution, and stigma associated with albinism. PWAs also need access to quality education, healthcare, and employment opportunities to live fulfilling lives. Positive perceptions of albinism should be promoted through awareness campaigns and education, and strict penalties should be enforced against those who violate the rights of PWAs. By working together, we can end discrimination against PWAs and create a world that is inclusive and tolerant of all.
