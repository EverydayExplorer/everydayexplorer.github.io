The Art of Fermentation: 25 Fermenting Tools and Supplies for Home Fermentation


Fermentation is an ancient art that has been practiced for thousands of years, dating back to the time of the ancient Egyptians. It is a process that transforms food through the action of microorganisms, such as bacteria and yeast, which break down complex molecules and create new flavors and textures. Today, fermentation is enjoyed by people all around the world, from those who enjoy the tangy taste of sauerkraut to those who appreciate the fizzy bubbles of kombucha.

If you’re interested in exploring the world of fermentation, you’ll need the right tools and supplies to help you along the way. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a look at 25 essential fermenting tools and supplies for home fermentation enthusiasts.

1. Fermenting Jars

A good fermenting jar is a must-have for any home fermentation enthusiast. These jars are designed specifically for fermentation and come in various sizes, allowing you to ferment anything from small batches of sauerkraut to large quantities of kimchi. Look for jars with airlocks, which help to keep oxygen out and prevent mold growth.

2. Glass Weights

Glass weights are essential for keeping vegetables submerged in their brine during fermentation. This helps to prevent the growth of mold and ensures that your fermented foods are safe to eat.

3. Fermenting Lids

Fermenting lids are designed to fit on top of your fermenting jars and provide an airtight seal. This helps to keep oxygen out and prevent mold growth, ensuring that your fermented foods are safe to eat.

4. Stainless Steel Knives

A high-quality stainless steel knife is essential for chopping and slicing vegetables for fermentation. Look for a knife with a sharp edge and a comfortable handle.

5. Cutting Boards

A good cutting board is essential for preparing your fermented foods. Look for boards made from food-safe materials, such as bamboo or plastic, and consider investing in a separate board for fermentation to prevent cross-contamination.

6. Measuring Spoons and Cups

Accurate measuring is essential when it comes to fermentation, as even small variations in ingredients can affect the final result. Invest in a set of measuring spoons and cups to ensure that your fermented foods turn out just right.

7. Scale

A scale is an essential tool for any serious fermentation enthusiast. It allows you to measure ingredients accurately, ensuring that your fermented foods turn out just right.

8. Fermentation Crock

A fermentation crock is a large container designed specifically for fermenting large quantities of food. These crocks typically come with an airtight lid and a built-in spigot for easy access to your fermented foods.

9. Glass Measuring Jars

Glass measuring jars are perfect for measuring out your fermentation brine. They come in various sizes and are easy to clean and store.

10. Fermentation Airlocks

Fermentation airlocks are essential for keeping oxygen out of your fermenting jars. They fit on top of your jars and allow carbon dioxide to escape while preventing oxygen from entering.

11. Non-Reactive Pots and Pans

Non-reactive pots and pans are essential for preparing your fermentation brine. Look for pots and pans made from stainless steel, enameled cast iron, or glass.

12. Wooden Spoons

Wooden spoons are a great choice for stirring your fermentation brine, as they won’t react with the acidic environment.

13. Thermometer

A thermometer is essential for monitoring the temperature of your fermentation environment. Maintaining the right temperature is crucial for successful fermentation.

14. Fermentation Mat

A fermentation mat is designed to fit inside your fermentation crock, providing a barrier between your food and the crock itself. This helps to prevent any unwanted flavors from seeping into your fermented foods.
