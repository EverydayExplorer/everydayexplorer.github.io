The Top Educational Twitter Accounts for Teachers


In the digital age, social media platforms have become invaluable tools for educators seeking professional development, collaboration, and inspiration. Among the various social media platforms available, Twitter stands out as one of the most popular and influential when it comes to connecting with other educators and accessing valuable educational resources. Twitter’s fast-paced and concise nature makes it the perfect platform for teachers to exchange ideas, engage in discussions, and discover innovative teaching strategies. In this blog post, we will explore some of the top educational Twitter accounts that every teacher should follow for an enriching online experience.

1. @edutopia: Edutopia is a renowned nonprofit organization dedicated to transforming education through the use of technology, project-based learning, and innovative teaching practices. Their Twitter account regularly shares articles, videos, and resources that inspire educators to bring creativity and new methodologies into their classrooms.

2. @cultofpedagogy: Cult of Pedagogy, led by Jennifer Gonzalez, is a treasure trove of practical teaching strategies, classroom management tips, and insightful interviews with leading educators. The account tweets links to blog posts, podcasts, and videos that cover a wide range of relevant topics, making it a go-to resource for both new and experienced teachers.

3. @Larryferlazzo: Larry Ferlazzo, an accomplished teacher, author, and blogger, curates a Twitter feed filled with educational news, research, and lesson plans. His tweets often link to thought-provoking articles and resources from reputable sources, offering valuable insights into various aspects of education.

4. @TeachThought: TeachThought is an innovative education website that strives to provide teachers with the knowledge and tools they need to create engaging and student-centered classrooms. By following their Twitter account, educators can access a wealth of articles, infographics, and lesson plans centered on critical thinking, creativity, and educational technology.

5. @gcouros: George Couros, a celebrated author and speaker, shares his expertise on leadership, educational technology, and fostering a positive learning environment through his Twitter account. George’s tweets often emphasize the importance of empowering students, building relationships, and embracing innovation in education.

6. @AngelaMaiers: Angela Maiers, an internationally recognized educator and author, focuses on empowering educators and students to embrace their passions and make a difference in the world. Her Twitter account is a constant source of inspiration, with tweets that encourage teachers to cultivate a culture of compassion, creativity, and collaboration in their classrooms.

7. @CoolCatTeacher: Vicki Davis, known as the ‘Cool Cat Teacher,’ shares her expertise on educational technology, global collaboration, and digital literacy through her Twitter feed. Her account is a valuable resource for teachers seeking practical tips, advice, and inspiration on leveraging technology to enhance student learning.

8. @web20classroom: Steven W. Anderson, an educator and educational technology consultant, provides up-to-date information on cutting-edge technology tools, strategies, and trends through his Twitter account. By following his tweets, teachers can stay informed about the latest advancements in educational technology and how to effectively integrate them into their classrooms.

9. @alicekeeler: Alice Keeler, a Google-certified educator, offers a wealth of resources, tips, and tricks for using Google Apps for Education. Her Twitter account is a must-follow for teachers interested in maximizing the potential of Google tools to enhance collaboration, organization, and engagement among students.

10. @thomascmurray: Tom Murray, an influential voice in the field of educational leadership and technology integration, shares valuable insights and resources for educators on his Twitter account. His tweets often emphasize the importance of personalized learning, equity, and school transformation.

These are just a few examples of top educational Twitter accounts that provide a wealth of knowledge, inspiration, and resources for teachers. By following these accounts, educators can expand their professional networks, stay informed about the latest educational trends and research, and discover innovative teaching practices to enhance student learning. Twitter truly offers a vibrant online community for educators to connect, learn, and grow together.
