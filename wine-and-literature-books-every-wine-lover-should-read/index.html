Wine and Literature: Books Every Wine Lover Should Read


When it comes to wine, there is more to the experience than just the taste and aroma. Wine has a rich history and a deep connection to art and culture, including literature. For wine lovers who want to delve into the world of books and wine, there are some must-read titles that will enhance your appreciation for both.

1. ‘The Art of Wine Tasting’ by Isaac Baker: This comprehensive guide is perfect for beginners and seasoned wine enthusiasts alike. It covers everything from the basics of tasting wine to advanced techniques, making it a valuable resource for anyone looking to understand the intricacies of wine.

2. ‘The Wine Bible’ by Karen MacNeil: As the name suggests, this book is like a bible for wine lovers. It provides a comprehensive overview of wine regions around the world, including detailed information about different grape varieties, winemaking techniques, and the history of wine. It’s an excellent reference guide that will deepen your knowledge and appreciation for wine.

3. ‘To Cork or Not To Cork’ by George M. Taber: This book explores the fascinating world of wine closures, focusing on the debate between natural cork, synthetic cork, and screw caps. Taber delves into the history, science, and cultural significance of each closure, giving readers a deeper understanding of how this small detail can impact the wine-drinking experience.

4. ‘The Billionaire’s Vinegar’ by Benjamin Wallace: If you’re interested in the world of wine auctions and rare vintages, this book is a must-read. It tells the captivating story of a bottle of 1787 Château Lafite Bordeaux, purportedly owned by Thomas Jefferson, and the controversy surrounding its authenticity. This true story sheds light on the fascinating and sometimes murky world of high-end wine collecting.

5. ‘A Vineyard in My Glass’ by Gerald Asher: Asher, a renowned wine writer, shares his personal experiences and insights in this collection of essays. Through his stories, readers gain a glimpse into the world of winemaking and the people who dedicate their lives to producing exceptional wines. It’s a heartfelt and authentic exploration of the connection between wine, people, and place.

6. ‘Wine and War’ by Donald Kladstrup and Petie Kladstrup: This book takes a unique angle, examining the impact of World War II on the wine industry in France. It traces the stories of winemakers who risked their lives to protect their vineyards and preserve this cultural heritage. It’s a gripping tale that highlights the resilience and passion of those involved in the wine business.

7. ‘Reading Between the Wines’ by Terry Theise: This poetic and philosophical book explores the emotional and spiritual aspects of wine. Theise delves into the idea that wine is not just a beverage but a form of art that can evoke deeply felt experiences. It’s a thought-provoking read that will change the way you approach wine.

8. ‘Adventures on the Wine Route’ by Kermit Lynch: Lynch, an influential American wine importer, shares his experiences and insights in this travelogue-style book. He takes readers on a journey through the wine regions of France, introducing them to the people and wines that have shaped his career. It’s a delightful read that immerses you in the world of French wine.

9. ‘The Road to Burgundy’ by Ray Walker: This memoir tells the story of an American who leaves his job in finance to pursue his dream of becoming a winemaker in Burgundy, France. Walker’s journey is filled with challenges and triumphs, offering a firsthand account of the difficulties and rewards of making wine. It’s an inspiring read for anyone who has ever thought about following their passion.

10. ‘Wine and War: The French, the Nazis, and the Battle for France’s Greatest Treasure’ by Donald and Petie Kladstrup: This historical account explores the intersection of wine and war during World War II. The authors delve into the efforts of French winemakers to protect their vineyards and preserve their cultural heritage in the face of German occupation. It’s a fascinating read that sheds light on the resilience and determination of the French people.

So, if you’re a wine lover looking to deepen your knowledge and appreciation for wine, these books are a great place to start. They offer a diverse range of perspectives and insights into the world of wine, from the technical aspects of tasting to the cultural and historical significance of wine. Whether you’re a beginner or a seasoned oenophile, these books will enrich your wine journey and enhance your understanding of this ancient and complex beverage.
