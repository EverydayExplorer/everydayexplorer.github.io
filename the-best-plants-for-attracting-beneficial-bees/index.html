The Best Plants for Attracting Beneficial Bees


It’s no secret that bees play a vital role in our ecosystem. These buzzing creatures are not only responsible for pollinating a significant portion of our food crops, but they also contribute to the biodiversity of our planet. Unfortunately, bee populations have been declining in recent years due to habitat loss, pesticide use, and climate change. As gardeners and nature lovers, it’s important for us to help support and attract these beneficial bees by planting the right types of plants in our landscapes. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best plants for attracting beneficial bees.

1. Lavender (Lavandula): Known for its beautiful purple blooms and incredible fragrance, lavender is a favorite among bees. Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of bee species, including honeybees, bumblebees, and solitary bees. Planting lavender in your garden not only adds beauty and charm but also provides a valuable food source for these essential pollinators.

2. Sunflowers (Helianthus): Sunflowers are not only a delight to the human eye but also a magnet for bees. Bees are drawn to the large, vibrant flowers and the copious amounts of pollen and nectar they provide. Sunflowers are easy to grow and come in a variety of sizes, making them a versatile addition to any garden.

3. Bee Balm (Monarda): As the name suggests, bee balm is a bee’s best friend. With its colorful, tubular flowers, bee balm provides an abundant source of nectar for bees. It’s no wonder why this plant is so appealing to bees, with its sweet scent and vibrant hues. Bee balm is also known to attract butterflies and hummingbirds, making it a triple threat in the garden.

4. Salvia (Salvia officinalis): Salvia, commonly referred to as sage, is a perennial herb that not only adds flavor to your culinary creations but also attracts bees. Bees are particularly fond of the blue and purple varieties of salvia. Planting salvia in your garden will not only provide a feast for your taste buds but also benefit the local bee population.

5. Coneflowers (Echinacea): Coneflowers are a popular choice for attracting bees and other pollinators. These cheerful flowers come in a range of colors, including purple, pink, and white. Bees are naturally drawn to the abundant nectar and pollen-rich centers of coneflowers. Additionally, coneflowers produce seeds that attract finches and other seed-eating birds, adding another dimension of wildlife appeal to your garden.

6. Catmint (Nepeta): Catmint is not only loved by cats but also beloved by bees. With its fragrant foliage and lavender-blue flowers, catmint is a magnet for bees, butterflies, and other beneficial insects. This low-maintenance perennial is a great addition to any garden, providing beauty and support for our buzzing friends.

7. Russian Sage (Perovskia): Russian sage is a drought-tolerant perennial that bees find irresistible. Its blue-lavender flowers create a stunning display in the garden, while its aromatic leaves add a touch of elegance. Planting Russian sage in your garden will not only attract bees but also add a touch of sophistication to your landscape.

8. Wild Bergamot (Monarda fistulosa): Wild bergamot, also known as bee balm, is a native wildflower that bees find irresistible. With its lavender-pink flowers and distinct aromatic scent, wild bergamot is a must-have plant for attracting bees. It’s worth noting that this plant also has medicinal properties and can be used to make herbal teas and remedies.

9. Goldenrod (Solidago): Goldenrod is a late-blooming perennial that provides an essential food source for bees before winter sets in. Its bright yellow flowers offer a rich source of nectar and pollen, attracting bees and other pollinators. Goldenrod is often wrongly accused of causing allergies, whereas it’s actually ragweed that is responsible. By planting goldenrod, you can help support bees and dispel the myths surrounding this beautiful plant.

10. Purple Coneflower (Echinacea purpurea): Purple coneflower is a quintessential choice for attracting bees to your garden. Its vibrant purple flowers not only add a burst of color but also serve as a valuable food source for bees. Purple coneflower is also known for its medicinal properties and is commonly used in herbal remedies.

In conclusion, attracting beneficial bees to your garden is not only a wonderful way to support these essential pollinators but also to create a vibrant and thriving ecosystem in your own backyard. By planting a variety of nectar-rich flowers, such as lavender, sunflowers, bee balm, and salvia, you can provide bees with the fuel they need to thrive. Remember to choose plants that bloom at different times throughout the season to ensure a steady supply of food. By incorporating these bee-friendly plants into your garden, you can make a significant impact in supporting bee populations and protecting our environment.
