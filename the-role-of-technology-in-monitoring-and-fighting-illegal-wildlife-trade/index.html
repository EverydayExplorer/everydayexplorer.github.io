The Role of Technology in Monitoring and Fighting Illegal Wildlife Trade


Illegal wildlife trade has become one of the biggest threats to biodiversity and endangered species around the world. The trade is driven by demand for products made from animal parts, such as ivory, rhino horn, and pangolin scales, which are prized for their supposed medicinal or cultural value. Illegal wildlife trade not only threatens the survival of numerous species but also destroys ecosystems, fuels corruption and organized crime, and harms local communities dependent on wildlife resources.

The need to tackle illegal wildlife trade has led to the development of innovative technologies that can help monitor and fight this illicit activity. In this blog post, we will explore the role of technology in monitoring and fighting illegal wildlife trade.

Drones and Satellites

Drones and satellites are increasingly being used to monitor illegal wildlife trade activities. Drones with high-resolution cameras can be used to survey remote areas and track poachers, while satellites can provide detailed imagery of large areas to identify potential poaching hotspots.

In Tanzania’s Selous Game Reserve, a drone program was implemented to monitor the reserve and track poachers. The program saw significant success, with a 33% reduction in poaching incidents over two years. Similarly, satellites have been used to monitor forest cover and track illegal logging activity, leading to more effective enforcement and protection of forests.

DNA Analysis

DNA analysis has become a crucial tool in identifying the origin of seized wildlife products and tracing them back to their source. DNA samples can be taken from seized items such as ivory or rhino horn, and compared to a database of genetic samples from known populations of the species. This enables law enforcement agencies to identify the origin of the products and track down the poachers or traffickers responsible.

DNA analysis has been particularly successful in identifying the sources of elephant ivory seizures. By comparing the DNA of seized ivory to a database of DNA samples from elephants across Africa, researchers were able to trace most of the ivory to two main regions: West and Central Africa, and East Africa.

Blockchain Technology

Blockchain technology has the potential to revolutionize the way we track and trace wildlife products. By creating a transparent and tamper-proof ledger, blockchain technology can enable all parties involved in the supply chain, from producers to consumers, to track the origin and movement of products.

For example, the Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS) has partnered with IBM to develop a blockchain-based system to track and verify the movement of elephant ivory from source to destination. This system allows for greater accountability and transparency, making it easier to detect and prevent illegal trade.

Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence (AI) has the potential to greatly enhance the monitoring and detection of illegal wildlife trade activities. AI algorithms can analyze large amounts of data from cameras, drones, and other sources to detect suspicious activity and identify potential poaching hotspots.

For example, a team of researchers developed an AI algorithm that can analyze camera trap images to detect and identify individual animals, such as tigers or leopards. This technology can enable conservationists and law enforcement agencies to monitor and protect vulnerable animal populations more effectively.

Conclusion

The use of technology has become a crucial component in the fight against illegal wildlife trade. From drones and satellites to DNA analysis, blockchain technology, and AI, innovative technologies have the potential to greatly enhance our ability to monitor and combat this illicit activity. While technology alone cannot solve the problem of illegal wildlife trade, it can provide valuable tools and insights to support conservation efforts and protect vulnerable wildlife populations.
