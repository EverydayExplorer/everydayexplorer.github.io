The Science Behind the Addiction of Online Mobile Games and How to Cope


Online mobile games have become an indispensable part of our daily lives, providing us with ample entertainment and relaxation whenever we need to unwind. With the influx of various online gaming applications, it’s no surprise that people are getting addicted to them. While a little gaming can be therapeutic, excessive gaming can be detrimental to our health, leading to severe addiction.

The science behind the addiction of online mobile games is fascinating. Game developers and designers incorporate several elements that trigger our reward and pleasure centers in the brain. These stimulating factors keep us hooked to the screen for long periods, and we feel reluctant to leave the game once we start playing.

Here is an overview of the science behind the addiction of online mobile games:

1. Dopamine: This is a neurotransmitter that regulates our brain’s reward and pleasure centers. When we play games, dopamine is released in our brain, and we feel a sense of satisfaction and pleasure. Our brain craves these feelings, and we end up playing games for extended periods.

2. Social Interaction: Online mobile games have evolved into social platforms where players connect with other players from around the world. This social interaction releases oxytocin, which promotes trust and bonding. As human beings, we crave connections, and this keeps us engaged in the game.

3. FOMO: Fear of missing out, commonly referred to as FOMO, is another chronic condition that keeps gamers hooked. The constant need to level up, check leaderboards, and compete with others fuels this fear. People play games to avoid the feeling of missing out on exciting opportunities, and this leads to addiction.

Now that we’ve explained the science behind online mobile game addiction, it’s essential to understand how to cope with this condition.

1. Set Time Limits: Excessive gaming leads to addiction, and setting time limits is one way to cope with this condition. When gaming, set alarms to remind you when it’s time to stop playing. Discipline is key to breaking the cycle of addiction.

2. Engage in Other Activities: Addiction thrives in isolation, and engaging in other activities besides gaming is essential for recovery. Join a club, take a class, or engage in outdoor activities that don’t involve mobile gaming. This creates new experiences and makes life more fulfilling.

3. Find a Support System: Joining support groups or seeking help from a therapist is another way to cope with online mobile game addiction. A supportive community provides encouragement and accountability, and it’s always easier to overcome addiction with others walking the journey with you.

In conclusion, while online mobile gaming can be entertaining and relaxing, it can lead to addiction when not kept in check. Understanding the science behind this addiction and implementing coping strategies can help gamers break free from the cycle of addiction and live a fulfilling life. So, take control of your life and start implementing steps to cope with online mobile game addiction today.
