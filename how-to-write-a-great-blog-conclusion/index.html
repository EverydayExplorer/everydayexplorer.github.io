How to Write a Great Blog Conclusion


The conclusion of a blog post is often overlooked, but it plays a crucial role in solidifying the reader’s understanding of the topic and leaving a lasting impression. A well-written conclusion can reinforce the main points, inspire action, and encourage further engagement. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you craft a conclusion that will captivate your audience and leave them wanting more.

Summarize Key Points:

Begin your conclusion by succinctly summarizing the main points discussed throughout the post. This serves as a helpful reminder for readers, ensuring they retain the core message. Avoid restating everything verbatim; instead, focus on highlighting the most important takeaways.

Restate Your Thesis:

Reiterate your thesis statement in a slightly different way. This reminds readers of the purpose of the post and ties it all together. However, avoid simply repeating the same words; use this opportunity to reinforce your argument or offer a fresh perspective.

Answer the ‘So What?’ Question:

Address the reader’s natural curiosity by answering the question, ‘So what?’ Explain the significance of the topic, its relevance to their lives, or any potential implications. This helps readers understand why they should care and how the information can benefit them.

Inspire Action:

Encourage readers to take action based on the information provided in the post. This could involve subscribing to a newsletter, leaving a comment, sharing the post on social media, or exploring related resources. Use strong action verbs and make it easy for readers to follow through.

Ask Thought-Provoking Questions:

Pose open-ended questions that invite readers to reflect on the topic further. This encourages critical thinking and fosters ongoing engagement. Consider questions that relate to the main points or explore different perspectives.

Offer a Personal Anecdote:

Share a brief personal anecdote or experience that relates to the topic. This adds a human touch and makes the conclusion more relatable. It can also reinforce the main points or provide a different angle.

Provide a Call to Action:

End with a clear call to action that aligns with the purpose of the post. This could be a request for readers to leave a comment, sign up for a webinar, or visit another related resource. Make sure the call to action is specific and actionable.

Use Strong Language:

Employ powerful verbs, vivid imagery, and persuasive language to make your conclusion memorable. Avoid using clichés or generic phrases. Instead, opt for words that evoke emotions and leave a lasting impression.

Proofread Carefully:

Before publishing your blog post, proofread the conclusion carefully for any errors in grammar, spelling, or punctuation. A polished conclusion reflects well on your writing skills and enhances the overall quality of your post.

Additional Tips:

  • Keep it concise: Aim for a conclusion that is around 10-15% of the total post length.
  • Use transition words: Smoothly transition from the body of the post to the conclusion using phrases like ‘In conclusion,’ ‘To summarize,’ or ‘Ultimately.’
  • Avoid abrupt endings: End on a positive and forward-looking note, leaving readers with a sense of closure and satisfaction.
  • Consider using a quote: Incorporate a relevant quote from an expert or thought leader to support your main points and add credibility.

Conclusion:

Crafting a compelling blog conclusion is an essential aspect of effective writing. By summarizing key points, restating your thesis, answering the ‘So what?’ question, inspiring action, asking thought-provoking questions, offering a personal anecdote, providing a call to action, and using strong language, you can create a conclusion that leaves a lasting impression on your readers and encourages them to engage further with your content.
