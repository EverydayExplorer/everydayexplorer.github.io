Hidden Gems: Underrated Comic Book Series You Shouldn't Miss


Comic books have been a popular form of entertainment for decades, with iconic characters and storylines that have captivated audiences around the world. However, amidst the well-known superheroes and blockbuster franchises, there are hidden gems, underrated comic book series that deserve more recognition. In this blog post, we will explore some of these hidden gems that you shouldn’t miss. So, grab your cape and mask, and let’s dive into the world of underrated comic book series.

1. ‘Saga’ by Brian K. Vaughan and Fiona Staples: This epic space opera takes readers on a thrilling journey through a war-torn galaxy. With stunning artwork and a complex narrative, ‘Saga’ explores themes of love, family, and the consequences of war. Despite critical acclaim, this series remains relatively unknown to mainstream audiences.

2. ‘Locke & Key’ by Joe Hill and Gabriel Rodriguez: This supernatural horror series follows the Locke family as they discover a set of magical keys that unlock various doors, each with its own unique power. With its gripping storytelling and intricate world-building, ‘Locke & Key’ offers a fresh take on the horror genre.

3. ‘Chew’ by John Layman and Rob Guillory: Mixing crime noir with dark humor, ‘Chew’ introduces readers to Tony Chu, a detective with a unique ability - he can get psychic impressions from anything he eats. This quirky series combines elements of mystery, conspiracy, and culinary delight, creating a truly original reading experience.

4. ‘Black Hammer’ by Jeff Lemire and Dean Ormston: In this deconstructionist superhero series, a group of superheroes finds themselves trapped in a small town, unable to escape or remember their true identities. As the layers of their past are slowly revealed, ‘Black Hammer’ explores themes of identity, loss, and the cost of being a hero.

5. ‘The Wicked + The Divine’ by Kieron Gillen and Jamie McKelvie: This visually stunning series follows a group of gods who are reincarnated as pop stars every ninety years. With its compelling characters and exploration of fame, mortality, and the power of belief, ‘The Wicked + The Divine’ challenges the conventions of the superhero genre.

6. ‘Descender’ by Jeff Lemire and Dustin Nguyen: Set in a future where robots are outlawed, ‘Descender’ tells the story of a young robot boy named Tim-21, who may hold the key to the mystery of the robot genocide. With its breathtaking watercolor artwork and heartfelt storytelling, this series explores themes of humanity, artificial intelligence, and the nature of consciousness.

7. ‘Paper Girls’ by Brian K. Vaughan and Cliff Chiang: Set in the 1980s, this coming-of-age sci-fi series follows a group of newspaper delivery girls who stumble upon a time-traveling conspiracy. With its nostalgic setting and compelling characters, ‘Paper Girls’ combines elements of adventure, mystery, and friendship.

These underrated comic book series offer a refreshing alternative to the mainstream superhero fare, showcasing the incredible talent and creativity within the industry. Whether you’re a long-time fan or new to the world of comics, don’t miss the opportunity to explore these hidden gems. So, the next time you visit your local comic book store or browse online, keep an eye out for these extraordinary series that deserve a place on your bookshelf. Happy reading!
