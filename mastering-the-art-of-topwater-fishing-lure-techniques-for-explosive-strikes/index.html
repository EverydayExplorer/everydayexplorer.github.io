Mastering the Art of Topwater Fishing: Lure Techniques for Explosive Strikes


Topwater fishing is a common practice among anglers who love the thrill of catching fish that breach the surface and go for their lures in explosive strikes. However, many anglers find it difficult to master the art of topwater fishing, particularly using lure techniques that help encourage these explosive strikes.

If you are struggling with topwater fishing, you are not alone. In this blog, we’ll discuss how to improve your technique and master the art of topwater fishing.

1. Understand topwater fishing.

Topwater fishing is a specific type of fishing that involves using lures that float on the surface or just below it. This technique is popular for catching fish that prey on shallow swimming prey such as perch, panfish, bass, and trout. Targeting these species generally involves using lures, which look like the fish’s natural prey and have irresistible movement.

Topwater fishing is not as easy as it seems, but it is not impossible either. If you want to master the art of topwater fishing, you need to understand the strategies that work best while targeting different fish species.

2. Get the right equipment.

The first step to mastering the art of topwater fishing is to invest in the right equipment. This should start with a quality rod and reel combo, which should be of appropriate length and weight to suit your local waters. Additionally, you’ll need a specialized topwater lure, probably more than one that you can use interchangeably, and braided fishing line with a low test.

It is vitally important that you use the right equipment for the job and don’t use light gear when you should be using heavy gear. Having too light gear will make it difficult to reel in larger fish and can be a safety hazard.

3. Choose the right topwater lures.

When it comes to topwater lures, there are several different types to choose from. Each type is specifically designed to imitate different types of baitfish, and fish species respond differently to each kind.

Some of the most popular topwater lures available in the market include poppers, stick baits, buzzbaits, spook lures, and prop baits. Knowing which lures work best for the species you are targeting is essential for success. You might need different types of lures for different types of fish.

4. Master the art of presentation.

Presentation is key when it comes to topwater fishing. You need to present the lure exactly how it would look in natural, enticing action. So, take your time, study the water, and decide where to cast. Once you cast, allow the lure to rest for a few seconds and then retrieve it slowly with some pops and jerks. Sometimes, letting the lure sit for a bit will encourage a bite.

Retrieving the lure with slow, steady, jerky motions on the surface will create a disturbance in the water that will encourage fish to strike. If the fish isn’t biting, mix up the retrieve. You can vary the speed, change between short and long strokes, pause and let the lure settle, and experiment with different presentation angles.

5. Pay attention to the surroundings.

Finally, when mastering the art of topwater fishing, it is important to pay attention to your surroundings. Watch the water; look for signs of fish activity, or for signs of what they are feeding on. It will give you a great insight into what you should be doing, and what lures you should be using at that specific time.

In conclusion, mastering the art of topwater fishing is not an easy task. You need to start by understanding the technique and the different types of lures available, invest in the right equipment, and then focus on mastering the art of presentation. Paying attention to the surroundings is also key to becoming successful in this type of fishing. With time, patience, and practice, you too can master the art of topwater fishing and enjoy the thrill of explosive strikes.
