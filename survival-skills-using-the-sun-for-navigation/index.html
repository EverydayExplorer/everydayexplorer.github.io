Survival Skills: Using the Sun for Navigation


Navigating through the wilderness can be a daunting task, especially when you find yourself without a map or any electronic devices. In such situations, it is essential to rely on your survival skills to find your way back to civilization. One of the oldest and most reliable methods of navigation is by using the sun as your guide. In this article, we will explore the techniques and strategies for using the sun for navigation in survival situations.

Understanding the Sun’s Movement

Before we delve into the practical aspects of using the sun for navigation, let’s first understand how the sun moves across the sky. The sun rises in the east, reaches its highest point at noon, and sets in the west. This simple knowledge forms the foundation of utilizing the sun as a compass.

Finding Direction with Shadow Stick Method

To determine the cardinal directions using the sun, you can employ the shadow stick method. Here’s how it works:

  1. Find a straight stick and place it vertically into the ground.
  2. Mark the tip of the shadow cast by the stick with a stone or another object.
  3. Wait for about 15 minutes for the shadow to move.
  4. Mark the new position of the shadow tip.
  5. Draw a straight line connecting the two marks.
  6. This line represents the east-west axis, with the first mark indicating the west and the second mark indicating the east.
  7. Stand with your left foot on the first mark and your right foot on the second mark. You will be facing north, with south behind you.

Determining Time of Day with the Sun’s Position

Apart from finding direction, you can also determine the approximate time of day by observing the sun’s position in the sky. Here’s a simple method to estimate the time:

  1. Extend your arm fully and place your thumb on the horizon, aligning it with the sun.
  2. Count the number of finger widths between the horizon and the sun.
  3. Each finger width represents approximately 15 minutes. So, if the sun is three finger widths above the horizon, it is about 45 minutes past noon.

Using the Sun for Navigation on the Move

While the shadow stick method and estimating time of day are useful when you are stationary, what if you are on the move? Here are a few techniques that can help you navigate using the sun while walking:

  1. Determine your initial direction using the shadow stick method or by observing the sun’s position.
  2. Pick a distinctive landmark in the direction you want to go, such as a tree or a rock.
  3. Walk towards the landmark while keeping an eye on the sun. If the sun shifts to your right, you are heading east; if it moves to your left, you are heading west.
  4. Adjust your path accordingly to maintain a straight course towards your destination.

Additional Tips for Sun Navigation

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when using the sun for navigation:

  • Remember that the sun’s position changes throughout the day due to factors like daylight savings and your latitude. Adjust your calculations accordingly.
  • Cloudy or overcast days can make it challenging to determine the sun’s position. In such situations, try to find breaks in the clouds or wait for clearer moments to get a glimpse of the sun.
  • Practice using the sun for navigation in familiar areas before you find yourself in a survival situation. This will help you become more comfortable and proficient with the technique.

Conclusion

Mastering the art of using the sun for navigation can be a valuable skill in survival situations. By understanding the sun’s movement, employing the shadow stick method, estimating time of day, and adapting your navigation techniques while on the move, you can ensure that you stay on the right path towards safety. Remember, practice makes perfect, so hone your skills and be prepared for any wilderness adventure that comes your way.
