Wilderness Survival Skills for Summer: Heat and Sun Protection


Summer is a time when many people head out into the great outdoors to enjoy the beauty of nature. However, with the scorching heat and intense sun, it’s important to be well-prepared and equipped with the right wilderness survival skills. In this blog post, we will explore some essential tips for heat and sun protection during your summer wilderness adventures.

1. Stay hydrated: One of the most important aspects of surviving in the wilderness is to stay hydrated. The heat can quickly deplete your body’s water reserves, leading to dehydration. Always carry an ample supply of water and drink regularly to keep yourself hydrated. Additionally, consider packing electrolyte tablets or powdered drink mixes to replenish lost minerals.

2. Dress appropriately: Dressing for the heat is crucial when venturing into the wilderness. Opt for lightweight, breathable clothing that covers your skin to protect it from the sun’s harmful rays. Wearing a wide-brimmed hat and sunglasses can also provide additional protection for your face and eyes.

3. Use sunscreen: Sunscreen is an absolute must when spending extended periods outdoors. Apply a broad-spectrum sunscreen with a high SPF (Sun Protection Factor) to all exposed areas of your skin, including your face, neck, arms, and legs. Remember to reapply it every few hours, especially if you’re sweating or swimming.

4. Seek shade: Whenever possible, find shade to take breaks and rest. This can be under a tree, a rock formation, or even using a tarp or a lightweight tent. The shade will provide relief from the direct sun and help regulate your body temperature.

5. Time your activities: Plan your outdoor activities during the cooler parts of the day, such as early morning or late afternoon. This way, you can avoid the peak heat hours when the sun is at its strongest. It’s also a good idea to pace yourself and take regular breaks to rest and cool down.

6. Stay cool: There are several ways to stay cool in the wilderness. Wet a bandana or cloth and place it around your neck to help lower your body temperature. You can also soak your hat or shirt in water to keep yourself cool. Additionally, if you come across a natural water source, take the opportunity to splash some water on your face and body.

7. Be aware of heat-related illnesses: Heat exhaustion and heatstroke are serious conditions that can occur if you don’t take proper precautions. Familiarize yourself with the symptoms, such as dizziness, nausea, headache, rapid heartbeat, and confusion. If you or someone in your group experiences these symptoms, move to a cooler area, drink water, and seek medical assistance if necessary.

8. Know your limits: Lastly, it’s crucial to know your physical limits and listen to your body. If you start feeling excessively fatigued, dizzy, or unwell, it’s okay to cut short your outdoor activities and seek shelter. Pushing yourself beyond your limits can lead to dangerous situations.

By following these wilderness survival skills for summer heat and sun protection, you can fully enjoy your outdoor adventures while staying safe and comfortable. Remember, preparation and awareness are key when it comes to surviving in the wilderness. Stay hydrated, dress appropriately, use sunscreen, seek shade, time your activities, stay cool, and know your limits. Have a fantastic summer exploring the great outdoors!
