The Best Hotels for Artisanal Shopping: Support Local Crafts


When it comes to travel and exploration, one of the most rewarding experiences is immersing yourself in the local culture and supporting local artisans. And what better way to do that than by staying at hotels that not only provide a comfortable and luxurious experience but also celebrate and promote the unique crafts and traditions of their region? In this blog post, we will take you on a journey to discover the best hotels for artisanal shopping, where you can indulge in the beauty of handmade crafts while supporting local communities.

1. Amanbagh - Rajasthan, India: Nestled in the heart of Rajasthan, Amanbagh is a luxurious oasis that showcases the best of Indian craftsmanship. From ornate textiles and intricate jewelry to vibrant pottery and traditional paintings, this hotel offers an array of artisanal treasures. Take part in workshops led by local artists, where you can learn the ancient techniques behind these exquisite crafts.

2. Fogo Island Inn - Newfoundland, Canada: Located on a remote island off the coast of Newfoundland, Fogo Island Inn is not just a destination for breathtaking landscapes but also a hub for local artisans. The hotel collaborates with the community’s skilled craftspeople to create bespoke furniture, textiles, and artwork, all of which can be purchased during your stay. Every purchase directly supports the island’s economy and ensures the preservation of its rich cultural heritage.

3. Casa Gangotena - Quito, Ecuador: Situated in the heart of Quito’s historic center, Casa Gangotena combines luxury with a deep appreciation for Ecuadorian craftsmanship. This boutique hotel features stunning colonial architecture and is adorned with locally made textiles, ceramics, and woodwork. Explore the nearby markets and shops to find unique handmade souvenirs, such as alpaca wool blankets and hand-painted ceramics.

4. Riad Joya - Marrakech, Morocco: Immerse yourself in the vibrant colors and rich traditions of Morocco at Riad Joya. This elegant boutique hotel is a treasure trove of Moroccan craftsmanship, with every corner showcasing intricate tile work, carved plaster, and handwoven textiles. During your stay, explore the bustling souks of Marrakech, where you can find an array of artisanal goods, including leather bags, metal lanterns, and traditional Berber carpets.

5. Singita Lebombo Lodge - Kruger National Park, South Africa: For those seeking a safari experience intertwined with artisanal shopping, Singita Lebombo Lodge is the perfect choice. Located in the renowned Kruger National Park, this lodge celebrates the creativity of local artisans through its African-inspired decor. While on safari, visit the nearby communities and support local craftsmen who create unique beaded jewelry, hand-carved wooden sculptures, and handwoven baskets.

6. Hotel Unique - Sao Paulo, Brazil: In the bustling city of Sao Paulo, Hotel Unique stands out as a beacon of contemporary design and celebration of Brazilian craftsmanship. This architectural marvel overlooks Ibirapuera Park and features a stunning rooftop pool in the shape of a ship. Inside, you’ll find a curated collection of Brazilian art, design, and furniture. For a taste of local artisanal shopping, head to the trendy neighborhood of Jardins, where you can find everything from handmade leather goods to indigenous artwork.

7. Belmond Hotel Monasterio - Cusco, Peru: Situated in the historic city of Cusco, Belmond Hotel Monasterio is a luxurious retreat that showcases the beauty of Peruvian craftsmanship. The hotel itself is a former monastery dating back to the 16th century and is adorned with stunning religious art, handmade textiles, and intricate wood carvings. Explore the local markets and shops to find traditional Peruvian textiles, silver jewelry, and vibrant ceramics.

8. The Chedi Club Tanah Gajah - Bali, Indonesia: Surrounded by lush rice fields in Ubud, The Chedi Club Tanah Gajah is a haven for art enthusiasts. This boutique hotel features a private collection of Balinese art, including paintings, sculptures, and traditional masks. Take a tour of the nearby village of Ubud, where you can witness local artisans creating intricate batik textiles, silver jewelry, and wood carvings.

By choosing to stay at these hotels, you not only elevate your travel experience but also contribute to the preservation of local crafts and the empowerment of artisans. So, the next time you plan a trip, consider these destinations that allow you to indulge in the beauty of handmade treasures while supporting local communities. Happy travels and happy shopping!
