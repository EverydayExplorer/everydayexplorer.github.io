Exploring Different Approaches to Writing: Plot-driven vs. Character-driven Novels


When it comes to writing a novel, there are various approaches authors can take. Two popular approaches that often come up in discussions are plot-driven and character-driven novels. These approaches refer to the emphasis placed on either the plot or the characters in the development of the story. In this blog post, we will explore the differences between these two approaches and the advantages of each.

Plot-driven novels are often associated with fast-paced, action-packed stories. In these types of novels, the plot takes center stage, driving the narrative forward with its twists and turns. The plot is usually driven by a series of events that lead to a climactic resolution. The focus is on the external conflicts and obstacles that the characters must overcome, creating a sense of excitement and suspense for the reader.

One of the advantages of writing a plot-driven novel is that it keeps the reader engaged from start to finish. By focusing on the storyline and the events that unfold, authors can create a sense of urgency and suspense that drives the reader to keep turning the pages. Additionally, plot-driven novels tend to be popular in genres such as thrillers, mysteries, and action-adventure, where the readers are looking for a fast-paced and gripping experience.

On the other hand, character-driven novels prioritize the development and growth of the characters over the plot. These novels often delve deep into the internal struggles, emotions, and motivations of the characters, allowing readers to form a strong emotional connection with them. Character-driven stories explore the human experience, often focusing on themes such as personal growth, relationships, and identity.

One of the advantages of writing a character-driven novel is that it allows for a more nuanced exploration of complex human experiences. By focusing on the characters’ internal conflicts and emotions, authors can delve into the depths of the human psyche, creating relatable and memorable characters. Character-driven novels are often favored in literary fiction, where the focus is on the introspective journey of the characters rather than the external events.

While plot-driven and character-driven novels may seem like opposites, they are not mutually exclusive. Many successful novels strike a balance between the two approaches, creating a compelling storyline that is driven by well-rounded and relatable characters. In fact, some of the most enduring and beloved novels of all time, such as ‘To Kill a Mockingbird’ by Harper Lee or ‘Pride and Prejudice’ by Jane Austen, manage to combine both aspects seamlessly.

Ultimately, the choice between a plot-driven or character-driven approach depends on the type of story an author wants to tell. Some stories are best suited for a thrilling plot that keeps the reader on the edge of their seat, while others require a deeper exploration of the characters’ inner lives. As a writer, it is important to consider the desired impact on the reader and choose the approach that best serves the story being told.

In conclusion, the debate between plot-driven and character-driven novels is not one of right or wrong approaches, but rather a matter of personal preference and the type of story being told. Both approaches have their advantages and can result in compelling and engaging narratives. As authors, the key is to find the right balance between plot and character development, creating a story that resonates with readers and leaves a lasting impression. So whether you prefer a thrilling rollercoaster ride or a deep dive into the human psyche, there is a world of possibilities when it comes to exploring different approaches to writing a novel.
