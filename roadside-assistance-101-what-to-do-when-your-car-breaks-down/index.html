Roadside Assistance 101: What to Do When Your Car Breaks Down


When you’re out on the road, the last thing you want is for your car to break down unexpectedly. However, it’s a situation that many drivers will inevitably face at some point. Whether it’s a flat tire, dead battery, or engine trouble, knowing what to do when your car breaks down can help you stay safe and get back on the road as quickly as possible. In this comprehensive guide to roadside assistance, we’ll cover everything you need to know about handling a breakdown.

Stay Calm and Assess the Situation

The first thing to remember when your car breaks down is to stay calm. Panicking won’t solve anything and could make the situation worse. Take a deep breath and carefully assess your surroundings. Are you on a busy highway or a quiet side street? Are you able to safely pull over to the side of the road, or do you need to exit the vehicle and seek help?

Turn on Your Hazard Lights

Once you’ve safely pulled over to the side of the road, turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers that your vehicle is disabled. This will help prevent accidents and make it easier for roadside assistance professionals to locate you.

Move to a Safe Location

If possible, move your vehicle to a safe location away from traffic. This could be a nearby parking lot, gas station, or rest area. Avoid stopping on a curve or hill where other drivers may have difficulty seeing you.

Stay Inside Your Vehicle

Unless it’s absolutely necessary to exit the vehicle, such as in the case of a flat tire, it’s generally safer to stay inside your car while you wait for help to arrive. Lock your doors and roll up your windows to protect yourself from potential dangers.

Call for Assistance

Once you’re safely parked, call for roadside assistance. Most insurance policies offer some form of roadside assistance coverage, so check your policy or contact your insurance provider to see what services are available to you. Be prepared to provide your location, a description of the problem, and any other relevant details to the dispatcher.

Follow Instructions from the Dispatcher

Listen carefully to the instructions provided by the dispatcher and follow them closely. They may ask you to provide additional information or take certain steps to ensure your safety while you wait for assistance to arrive.

Wait for Help to Arrive

Once you’ve contacted roadside assistance, all you can do is wait for help to arrive. Use this time to stay calm and patient, and avoid attempting any repairs yourself unless you’re confident in your abilities and it’s safe to do so.

Be Prepared for Future Emergencies

While you can’t always prevent a breakdown from happening, there are steps you can take to be better prepared for future emergencies. Consider keeping a roadside emergency kit in your car with essentials like a flashlight, jumper cables, first aid supplies, and a reflective triangle or roadside flares. Familiarize yourself with basic car maintenance tasks like changing a tire or jump-starting a battery, so you’ll be better equipped to handle minor issues on your own.

Conclusion

Experiencing a breakdown can be stressful and inconvenient, but knowing what to do when it happens can help you stay safe and get back on the road as quickly as possible. By staying calm, calling for assistance, and following the guidance of roadside professionals, you can navigate the situation with confidence and minimize the impact on your day. And by being prepared for future emergencies, you can help ensure that you’re ready to handle whatever the road throws your way.
