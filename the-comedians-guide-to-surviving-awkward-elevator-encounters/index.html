The Comedian's Guide to Surviving Awkward Elevator Encounters


Elevator rides can be awkward. Standing in a confined space with strangers, surrounded by silence, can make even the most confident individuals feel uncomfortable. But fear not! As a comedian, you possess a unique set of skills that can turn any awkward elevator encounter into a hilarious moment. In this guide, we will explore some tips and techniques that will help you navigate those uncomfortable elevator rides with ease, leaving everyone laughing and wanting more.

1. Break the Ice with a Joke

One of the best ways to diffuse tension in an elevator is to break the ice with a well-timed joke. As a comedian, you have a natural talent for humor, so why not put it to good use? Start by observing your surroundings and finding something funny or peculiar to comment on. Whether it’s the elevator music, the decor, or even the awkward silence itself, a witty remark can instantly lighten the mood and put everyone at ease.

Example: “Is it just me, or does this elevator music make you want to break out into a spontaneous jazz routine? Anyone got some tap shoes handy?”

2. Embrace Awkwardness as Material

Awkward moments are a comedian’s bread and butter. Instead of trying to avoid or ignore the awkwardness in an elevator, use it as fuel for your comedic genius. Embrace the uncomfortable silence, the accidental brush of elbows, or the occasional accidental floor button press. Turn these moments into hilarious anecdotes, weaving them into your elevator routine. By acknowledging and embracing the awkwardness, you take control of the situation and transform it into a comedy goldmine.

Example: “So, I accidentally pressed the wrong floor button earlier. I guess we’ll all be taking an unexpected tour of the janitor’s closet. Buckle up, folks!”

3. Engage with Fellow Elevator Riders

Elevator rides are a perfect opportunity to engage with your fellow riders. Strike up a conversation, ask questions, or share a funny story. By involving others in your comedic banter, you create a sense of camaraderie and break down social barriers. Remember to keep it light and respectful, ensuring that everyone feels comfortable and included in the fun.

Example: “Hey, does anyone know the secret elevator handshake? I’ve been trying to figure it out for ages!”

4. Utilize Physical Comedy

Physical comedy can be a great tool to lighten the mood in an elevator. Use exaggerated movements, funny faces, or even a well-timed pratfall to elicit laughter from your fellow riders. Just be mindful of the limited space and ensure that your antics don’t invade anyone’s personal boundaries or make them feel uncomfortable.

Example: Trips over own feet, comically recovers “Whoa, I guess I should have skipped the ice skating lessons before hopping on this elevator!”

5. Leave Them Wanting More

As the elevator ride comes to an end, make sure to leave your fellow riders with a memorable impression. Deliver a final punchline, a witty farewell, or a playful gesture that will keep them smiling long after they’ve exited the elevator. Leave them wanting more of your comedic brilliance, and who knows, they might become your biggest fans!

Example: “Well, folks, it’s been a pleasure sharing this elevator ride with you. Remember, laughter is the best elevator music, so keep those smiles on your faces!”

In conclusion, elevators don’t have to be a breeding ground for awkwardness. As a comedian, you have the power to transform any uncomfortable elevator encounter into a hilarious and memorable experience. By utilizing jokes, embracing awkwardness, engaging with fellow riders, incorporating physical comedy, and leaving them wanting more, you can conquer any elevator ride with confidence and laughter. So, the next time you step into an elevator, remember these tips and turn that awkwardness into comedy gold! Happy elevating, fellow comedians!
