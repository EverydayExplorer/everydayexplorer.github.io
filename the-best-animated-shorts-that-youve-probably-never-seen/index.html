The Best Animated Shorts That You've Probably Never Seen


Animated shorts are an often overlooked but incredibly impactful form of storytelling. While animated feature films tend to steal the spotlight, there are many hidden gems in the world of animated shorts that deserve recognition. These bite-sized films can pack a big punch in terms of emotion, creativity, and artistry. In this post, we’ll explore some of the best animated shorts that you’ve probably never seen but definitely should.

  1. ‘La Maison en Petits Cubes’ - This beautiful Japanese animated short, directed by Kunio Kato, tells the story of an old man who lives in a world that is gradually being submerged underwater. To survive, he builds additional layers onto his house, each one representing a different stage of his life. The film is a poignant reflection on memories, loss, and the passage of time, all told without a single word of dialogue.

  2. ‘The Dam Keeper’ - A visually stunning piece of animation, ‘The Dam Keeper’ follows the story of a young pig who is responsible for maintaining a dam that protects his town from a constant stream of dark, harmful clouds. Directed by Robert Kondo and Daisuke Tsutsumi, this short explores themes of isolation, friendship, and the struggle to find one’s place in the world.

  3. ‘World of Tomorrow’ - Created by the brilliant mind of Don Hertzfeldt, ‘World of Tomorrow’ is a mind-bending sci-fi animated short that follows a young girl who is visited by a future version of herself. The future self takes the young girl on a journey through different stages of existence, touching on themes of love, technology, and the impermanence of life.

  4. ‘Father and Daughter’ - This Oscar-winning animated short, directed by Dutch filmmaker Michael Dudok de Wit, tells the story of a daughter who waits for her father to return from a boat trip. As the seasons change and the years pass, the daughter never gives up hope of being reunited with her father. The film beautifully captures the passage of time and the enduring power of love.

  5. ‘The Lost Thing’ - Based on the book by Shaun Tan, this animated short follows a boy who discovers a strange creature on the beach and sets out to find a new home for it. Directed by Shaun Tan and Andrew Ruhemann, ‘The Lost Thing’ is a whimsical and thought-provoking exploration of difference, acceptance, and the importance of kindness.

  6. ‘Borrowed Time’ - This stunning animated short, directed by Pixar animators Andrew Coats and Lou Hamou-Lhadj, tells the story of a sheriff who revisits a tragic moment from his past. As the sheriff grapples with his memories and regrets, the film delivers a powerful meditation on guilt, redemption, and the complexities of the human experience.

  7. ‘Pearl’ - Created entirely in virtual reality, ‘Pearl’ is a groundbreaking animated short directed by Patrick Osborne. The film follows a father and daughter as they travel the country in their car, chasing their dreams and building a life together. ‘Pearl’ is a deeply human story that celebrates the power of music, family, and the bond between parents and children.

These animated shorts may not have the same level of recognition as their feature-length counterparts, but they are no less deserving of praise and attention. Each of these films offers a unique and unforgettable experience, showcasing the incredible artistry and storytelling potential of the animated medium. Next time you’re looking for a new film to watch, consider diving into the world of animated shorts - you may just discover a hidden gem that touches your heart and stays with you long after the credits roll.
