Survival Food: What to Eat and How to Find It in the Wild


When venturing into the wild, it’s essential to be prepared for any situation that may arise. One crucial aspect of survival is knowing what to eat and how to find food in the untamed wilderness. In this blog post, we will explore the various types of survival food you can consume and the techniques you can employ to locate them.

1. Edible Plants:
Nature provides us with an abundance of edible plants that can sustain us in the wild. However, it’s vital to be knowledgeable about which plants are safe to consume. Some well-known edible plants include:

- Dandelion: The entire dandelion plant is edible, from its vibrant yellow flowers to its nutritious leaves. It can be eaten raw or cooked.
- Cattails: Found near water sources, cattails offer an array of edible parts, such as their young shoots, roots, and pollen.
- Wild Berries: Blueberries, blackberries, raspberries, and strawberries are just a few examples of the delicious and nutritious berries that can be foraged in the wild.

Remember, it’s crucial to correctly identify edible plants to avoid consuming anything toxic. Carry a field guide or take a survival course to enhance your knowledge in this area.

2. Insects and Bugs:
While not the most appetizing option, insects and bugs are an excellent source of protein and can be found abundantly in the wild. Some edible insects include:

- Crickets: High in protein, crickets can be roasted or ground into flour to be used in various survival recipes.
- Mealworms: These larvae of darkling beetles can be consumed raw or cooked and provide a good source of protein.
- Ants: Insects like ants can be foraged and consumed. They are a valuable source of nutrients and can be eaten raw or added to dishes for a tangy flavor.

3. Hunting and Fishing:
For those with the necessary skills, hunting and fishing can be effective methods of obtaining food in the wild. Some common survival hunting and fishing techniques include:

- Trapping: Setting up snares or traps is an efficient way to catch small game like rabbits or squirrels.
- Fishing: Utilize basic fishing techniques such as using a fishing line, hook, and bait to catch fish from nearby rivers, lakes, or streams.
- Bowhunting: If you have the proper equipment and skills, bowhunting can be a successful method of hunting larger game such as deer or wild boar.

Remember to check local regulations and obtain any required licenses before engaging in hunting or fishing activities.

4. Food Preservation:
In a survival situation, it’s crucial to know how to preserve food to ensure its longevity. Some methods of food preservation include:

- Smoking: Smoking meat or fish helps to preserve it by drying out the moisture and adding flavor.
- Drying: Dehydrating fruits, vegetables, or meat can help extend their shelf life and make them more portable.
- Canning: If you have access to canning equipment, preserving food in jars can maintain its freshness for an extended period.

In conclusion, knowing what to eat and how to find food in the wild is an essential skill for any survivalist. Familiarize yourself with edible plants, insects, and bugs, and learn basic hunting and fishing techniques. Additionally, understanding food preservation methods will ensure a steady supply of nourishment in challenging situations. Remember, preparation and knowledge are key when it comes to surviving in the wild.

Please note that while this blog provides valuable information on survival food, it is always recommended to consult with experts or take survival courses to enhance your skills and knowledge in this area. Stay safe and happy adventuring!
