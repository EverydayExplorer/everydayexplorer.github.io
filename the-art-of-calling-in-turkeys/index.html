The Art of Calling in Turkeys


When it comes to hunting, the art of calling in turkeys is a skill that can make or break a successful hunt. Whether you’re a seasoned hunter or just starting out, mastering the art of turkey calling can greatly enhance your hunting experience. In this blog, we’ll delve into the techniques, tips, and tools you need to become a master at calling in turkeys.

Understanding the Basics of Turkey Calling
Turkey calling is the act of imitating the sounds made by turkeys to attract them. Turkeys are highly vocal birds, and they use a variety of sounds to communicate with each other. As a hunter, your goal is to mimic these sounds to lure them within range.

The primary types of turkey calls include box calls, slate calls, diaphragm calls, and locator calls. Each type produces a different sound and requires a unique technique to use effectively. Understanding the intricacies of each call and when to use them is crucial for success in turkey hunting.

Turkey hunting is a thrilling and challenging outdoor pursuit that requires skill, patience, and a deep understanding of the art of calling in these majestic birds. Whether you are a seasoned hunter or a beginner looking to delve into the world of turkey hunting, mastering the art of calling is essential for a successful hunt. In this blog post, we will explore the techniques, tips, and tricks that will help you become a master caller and increase your chances of bagging that elusive gobbler.

1. Understanding Turkey Vocalizations:
Turkeys communicate through a variety of vocalizations, each carrying a specific meaning. The key to successful calling is to understand and replicate these sounds effectively. The three primary vocalizations you need to familiarize yourself with are the yelp, the cluck, and the purr. Each of these calls serves a distinct purpose and can be used strategically during different hunting scenarios.

2. Perfecting the Yelp:
The yelp is the most commonly used turkey call and serves as the foundation for other vocalizations. It is used by both hens and gobblers to communicate various messages such as location, contentment, or a call for companionship. Mastering the yelp requires practice, patience, and the ability to mimic the cadence and tone of a real turkey. Experiment with different types of yelps, including the basic yelp, the excited yelp, and the lost yelp, to increase your chances of luring in a tom.

3. Mastering the Cluck:
The cluck is a short, sharp call that turkeys use to get the attention of other birds or to express mild interest. It is a versatile call that can be used in combination with other calls to create a more realistic and compelling sequence. Practice variations of the cluck, such as the plain cluck, the double cluck, and the tree yelp, to add depth and realism to your calling repertoire.

4. Utilizing the Purr:
The purr is a soft, rolling call that turkeys use to convey contentment and relaxation. It is often heard when birds are feeding or roosting and can be an effective tool for calming down a skittish tom. Mastering the purr requires finesse and control, as it is a subtle call that requires a delicate touch. Practice using different purring techniques, such as the single note purr and the rolling purr, to add realism to your calling sequences.

5. The Importance of Realism:
To successfully call in turkeys, it is crucial to sound as realistic as possible. Invest in high-quality turkey calls that accurately replicate the sounds of a live bird. Additionally, take the time to study wild turkeys in their natural habitat, observing their behavior and vocalizations. This firsthand knowledge will help you fine-tune your calling techniques and increase your chances of success.

6. Patience and Persistence:
Turkey hunting is not for the faint of heart. It requires dedication, perseverance, and a willingness to learn from both successes and failures. Remember that calling in turkeys is an art, and like any art form, it takes time and practice to master. Be patient, persistent, and open to adapting your techniques based on the birds’ responses.

In conclusion, the art of calling in turkeys is a skill that every turkey hunter should strive to master. By understanding turkey vocalizations, perfecting the yelp, cluck, and purr, utilizing realism, and maintaining patience and persistence, you can increase your chances of a successful hunt. So, gear up, head out into the woods, and let the art of calling in turkeys lead you to an unforgettable hunting experience. Happy hunting!
