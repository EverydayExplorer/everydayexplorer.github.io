The 10 Best Non-Fiction Books You Should Read in 2021


In the fast-paced world of today, finding time to unwind with a good book can be both refreshing and informative. Non-fiction books provide a great way to engage with real-life stories, gain knowledge, and explore different perspectives. With 2021 well underway, there’s no shortage of outstanding non-fiction books to dive into. Whether you’re a history enthusiast, a science geek, or a self-help aficionado, there’s something for everyone. In this blog post, we will explore the 10 best non-fiction books you should read in 2021. From memoirs to investigative journalism, these books offer a wide range of topics to pique your interest and satisfy your intellectual cravings.

1. ‘Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind’ by Yuval Noah Harari

If you have ever wondered about the origin of our species and the forces that have shaped human civilization, ‘Sapiens’ is the book for you. Harari takes readers on a journey through time, exploring the development of Homo sapiens from our evolutionary predecessors to the modern-day. This gripping and thought-provoking book challenges conventional wisdom and invites readers to reflect on the nature of humanity.

2. ‘Educated’ by Tara Westover

‘Educated’ is a memoir that tells the remarkable story of Tara Westover’s journey from a childhood spent in a strict and isolating environment to her pursuit of education and independence. This powerful memoir explores the transformative power of education and the strength of the human spirit. Westover’s narrative is gripping and deeply moving, making it a must-read for memoir enthusiasts.

3. ‘Hidden Valley Road: Inside the Mind of an American Family’ by Robert Kolker

In ‘Hidden Valley Road,’ Robert Kolker takes readers inside the Galvin family, who became the subject of one of the largest studies on schizophrenia in America. This book offers an intimate portrayal of a family living with mental illness while shedding light on the scientific advances and challenges in the field of psychiatric research. Kolker’s meticulous research and sensitive storytelling make ‘Hidden Valley Road’ a truly compelling read.

4. ‘Braiding Sweetgrass: Indigenous Wisdom, Scientific Knowledge, and the Teachings of Plants’ by Robin Wall Kimmerer

In this beautifully written book, Robin Wall Kimmerer weaves together her experiences as a mother, scientist, and member of the Citizen Potawatomi Nation. ‘Braiding Sweetgrass’ combines indigenous wisdom with scientific knowledge, inviting readers to consider the reciprocal relationship between humans and the natural world. Through her storytelling, Kimmerer provides a unique perspective on ecological stewardship, sustainability, and the importance of preserving indigenous knowledge.

5. ‘The Splendid and the Vile: A Saga of Churchill, Family, and Defiance During the Blitz’ by Erik Larson

Erik Larson’s ‘The Splendid and the Vile’ offers a captivating account of Winston Churchill’s leadership during World War II. This meticulously researched book provides an intimate look at Churchill’s personal and political life during a time of immense challenge and uncertainty. Larson’s compelling narrative skillfully captures the tension and resilience of a nation while painting a vivid portrait of one of history’s most influential leaders.

6. ‘Caste: The Origins of Our Discontents’ by Isabel Wilkerson

Isabel Wilkerson’s ‘Caste’ delves into the deeply entrenched systems of social hierarchy and inequality that shape our societies. Drawing parallels between the caste system in India, the Jim Crow era in the United States, and modern-day dynamics, Wilkerson offers a thought-provoking analysis of power, privilege, and oppression. This eye-opening book challenges readers to examine their own biases and confront the structural inequalities that persist.

7. ‘The Code Breaker: Jennifer Doudna, Gene Editing, and the Future of the Human Race’ by Walter Isaacson

Walter Isaacson explores the groundbreaking work of biochemist Jennifer Doudna in ‘The Code Breaker.’ This gripping book takes readers on a journey through the world of gene editing, illuminating the possibilities and ethical dilemmas it presents. Isaacson skillfully combines scientific discovery, personal anecdotes, and the societal implications of gene editing, making ‘The Code Breaker’ both informative and exhilarating.

8. ‘How to Avoid a Climate Disaster: The Solutions We Have and the Breakthroughs We Need’ by Bill Gates

Bill Gates, the renowned entrepreneur and philanthropist, offers a compelling roadmap for tackling the climate crisis in ‘How to Avoid a Climate Disaster.’ Gates combines his expertise in technology with an understanding of the urgency and complexity of climate change. This book presents practical solutions and innovative ideas to shift our world toward a more sustainable future.

9. ‘Think Again: The Power of Knowing What You Don’t Know’ by Adam Grant

In ‘Think Again,’ organizational psychologist Adam Grant encourages readers to embrace intellectual humility and challenge their own assumptions. Grant explores the importance of resilience, curiosity, and open-mindedness in a rapidly changing world. By illustrating the power of rethinking our beliefs, ‘Think Again’ empowers readers to embrace a growth mindset and become lifelong learners.

10. ‘The Body Keeps the Score: Brain, Mind, and Body in the Healing of Trauma’ by Bessel van der Kolk

‘The Body Keeps the Score’ offers a profound exploration of the effects of trauma on the human body and mind. Bessel van der Kolk, a leading expert in the field of trauma, presents scientific research, personal anecdotes, and innovative therapeutic approaches. This book provides valuable insights into the healing process and offers hope for individuals and communities affected by trauma.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the caliber of non-fiction books released in 2021 is nothing short of remarkable. From historical narratives to thought-provoking memoirs and scientific discoveries, there is something for everyone on this list. Each of these books offers a unique perspective, inviting readers to broaden their horizons and engage with the world in new and enlightening ways. Whether you’re seeking personal growth, deepening your understanding of social issues, or simply looking for an entertaining read, these 10 best non-fiction books of 2021 will not disappoint. So grab a cup of coffee, find a cozy spot, and embark on a literary journey that will expand your knowledge and inspire your mind.
