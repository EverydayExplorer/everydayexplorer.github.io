Survival Knots for Climbing: Essential Techniques


Whether you’re a seasoned climber or just starting out, mastering survival knots is essential for ensuring your safety and success on the rock face. These knots aren’t just about securing yourself to the mountain; they’re about giving you the confidence to tackle even the most challenging routes. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover the essential survival knots every climber should know, along with step-by-step instructions on how to tie them.

1. Figure Eight Knot

The figure eight knot is the cornerstone of climbing knots. It’s used to tie into your harness, creating a secure loop that won’t come undone, even under pressure. To tie a figure eight knot, follow these steps:

  1. Pass the rope through the tie-in point on your harness.
  2. Form a loop by crossing the rope over itself.
  3. Wrap the end of the rope around the standing part.
  4. Pass the end of the rope through the loop you created.
  5. Tighten the knot by pulling both ends of the rope.

2. Double Fisherman’s Knot

The double fisherman’s knot is essential for joining two ropes together securely. Whether you’re rappelling or setting up a top-rope anchor, this knot will ensure your ropes stay connected. Here’s how to tie it:

  1. Lay the ends of the two ropes parallel to each other.
  2. Take one end and wrap it around both ropes twice.
  3. Pass the end through the two loops you created.
  4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 with the other end of the rope.
  5. Tighten the knot by pulling both ends of the ropes.

3. Clove Hitch

The clove hitch is perfect for attaching yourself to an anchor point or securing yourself to the rope while belaying. It’s easy to tie and untie, making it a versatile knot for various climbing situations. Follow these steps to tie a clove hitch:

  1. Wrap the rope around the anchor point.
  2. Cross the rope over itself to form an X.
  3. Wrap the rope around the anchor point again, crossing over the previous wrap.
  4. Pass the rope under the last wrap and tighten the knot.

4. Prusik Knot

The prusik knot is a friction hitch that allows you to ascend a rope safely. Whether you need to pass a knot or rescue a fellow climber, knowing how to tie a prusik knot is crucial. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Take a loop of smaller diameter rope and wrap it around the climbing rope.
  2. Pass the end of the loop through itself to create a girth hitch.
  3. Repeat the process with a second loop of rope, making sure it’s below the first loop.
  4. Tighten both loops against the climbing rope.

5. Bowline Knot

The bowline knot creates a secure loop at the end of a rope, making it ideal for creating anchors or attaching yourself to the climbing rope. Despite its simplicity, the bowline knot is incredibly strong and reliable. To tie a bowline knot, follow these steps:

  1. Form a small loop near the end of the rope.
  2. Pass the end of the rope through the loop.
  3. Wrap the end of the rope around the standing part.
  4. Pass the end of the rope back through the loop.
  5. Tighten the knot by pulling on the standing part and the end of the rope.

Conclusion

Mastering survival knots is a fundamental skill for climbers of all levels. By learning these essential techniques and practicing them regularly, you’ll be better prepared to tackle any route with confidence and safety. Remember, proper knot tying can mean the difference between a successful climb and a dangerous situation, so take the time to hone your skills and always prioritize safety above all else. Happy climbing!
