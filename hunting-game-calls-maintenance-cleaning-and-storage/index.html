Hunting Game Calls Maintenance: Cleaning and Storage


As hunting season comes to a close, it’s time to start thinking about properly maintaining and storing your hunting game calls. These essential tools are crucial for attracting game and ensuring a successful hunt, so it’s important to take good care of them to ensure they remain in top condition for the next hunting season. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know about cleaning and storing your hunting game calls to keep them in prime working condition.

Cleaning Your Hunting Game Calls

After a long hunting season, your game calls are likely to have accumulated dirt, debris, and possibly even moisture, which can affect their performance. Cleaning your game calls is a crucial step in maintaining their functionality and prolonging their lifespan. Here’s a step-by-step guide to cleaning your hunting game calls:

Step 1: Disassemble the Calls
Start by carefully disassembling your game calls to access all the individual components. This will make it easier to clean each part thoroughly.

Step 2: Remove Debris and Dirt
Use a soft brush or cloth to gently remove any visible debris and dirt from the calls. Pay special attention to the reeds and tone boards, as these are the most crucial parts of the call.

Step 3: Clean with Water and Mild Soap
For plastic or acrylic calls, you can use warm water and a mild soap to clean the individual parts. Avoid using harsh chemicals or solvents, as these can damage the materials.

Step 4: Dry Thoroughly
After cleaning, ensure that all the parts are completely dry before reassembling the calls. Moisture left in the calls can lead to mold or mildew growth, which can ruin the calls.

Storage Tips for Hunting Game Calls

Proper storage is just as important as cleaning when it comes to maintaining your hunting game calls. Here are some tips for storing your calls to ensure they remain in optimal condition:

Tip 1: Use Protective Cases
Invest in protective cases or pouches specifically designed for hunting game calls. These cases will help protect the calls from dust, moisture, and physical damage during storage.

Tip 2: Store in a Dry, Cool Place
Find a dry, cool storage area for your game calls. Avoid storing them in areas with high humidity or extreme temperatures, as these conditions can damage the calls.

Tip 3: Avoid Exposure to Sunlight
Sunlight can cause the materials of the calls to degrade over time. Store your calls in a place where they won’t be exposed to direct sunlight for prolonged periods.

Tip 4: Check Periodically
Even in storage, it’s important to periodically check your game calls for any signs of damage or wear. This will allow you to address any issues before they become more serious.

Conclusion

Proper maintenance and storage of your hunting game calls are essential for ensuring their longevity and performance. By following the cleaning and storage tips outlined in this guide, you can keep your game calls in top condition and ready for the next hunting season. Taking the time to care for your hunting game calls now will pay off when you’re back in the field, confident in the reliability of your essential hunting tools.
