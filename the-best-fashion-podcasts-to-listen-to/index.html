The Best Fashion Podcasts to Listen To


Are you a fashion enthusiast looking for a new way to stay up-to-date with the latest trends, industry news, and insightful conversations? Look no further than fashion podcasts. These audio shows have become a popular medium for fashion lovers to immerse themselves in the world of style, design, and creativity. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best fashion podcasts that are worth adding to your playlist.

1. “Fashion Unzipped” by The Telegraph

Hosted by Emily Cronin and Charlie Gowans-Eglinton, “Fashion Unzipped” is a podcast that dives deep into the fashion industry. With a mix of interviews, discussions, and insider insights, this podcast offers a comprehensive view of the fashion world. Whether you’re interested in runway shows, sustainability, or the business side of fashion, this podcast has something for everyone.

2. “The Business of Fashion Podcast” by The Business of Fashion

“The Business of Fashion Podcast” is a must-listen for anyone interested in the business side of the fashion industry. Hosted by Imran Amed, founder and CEO of The Business of Fashion, this podcast features interviews with industry leaders, entrepreneurs, and creative minds. Gain valuable insights into the ever-evolving fashion landscape and understand the challenges and opportunities that shape this dynamic industry.

3. “Dressed: The History of Fashion” by iHeartRadio

If you’re fascinated by the history of fashion and how it has evolved over the years, “Dressed: The History of Fashion” is the podcast for you. Hosted by fashion historians April Calahan and Cassidy Zachary, this podcast explores the stories behind iconic garments, the cultural significance of fashion, and the impact it has had on society. Each episode takes you on a journey through time, uncovering the fascinating narratives woven into our clothes.

4. “American Fashion Podcast” by American Fashion Podcast

For a closer look at the American fashion industry, tune in to the “American Fashion Podcast.” Hosted by Charles Beckwith and Cathy Schepis, this podcast features interviews with designers, entrepreneurs, and industry professionals. Gain valuable insights into the challenges and triumphs of building a fashion brand in the United States and learn about the diverse voices shaping American fashion.

5. “The Glossy Podcast” by Glossy

“The Glossy Podcast” offers a unique perspective on the fashion and beauty industries. Hosted by Glossy’s beauty and fashion editor-in-chief, Jill Manoff, this podcast features interviews with industry insiders, covering topics such as sustainability, influencer marketing, and the future of retail. Stay informed about the latest trends and developments that are shaping the fashion and beauty landscape.

6. “Fashion No Filter” by Camille Charriere and Monica Ainley

Hosted by fashion insiders Camille Charriere and Monica Ainley, “Fashion No Filter” provides an unfiltered glimpse into the fashion industry. From interviews with designers and models to discussions about body image and diversity, this podcast tackles important topics while offering an entertaining and insightful perspective.

7. “Wardrobe Crisis” by Clare Press

If you’re passionate about sustainable fashion and want to learn more about ethical practices within the industry, “Wardrobe Crisis” is the podcast for you. Hosted by sustainable fashion advocate Clare Press, this podcast explores the environmental and social impact of the fashion industry. Gain a deeper understanding of the importance of conscious consumption and discover how you can make a positive difference through your fashion choices.

Listening to fashion podcasts is not only an enjoyable way to stay informed but also an opportunity to gain valuable insights and expand your knowledge of the industry. Whether you’re a fashion student, a fashion professional, or simply someone with a love for style, these podcasts offer a wealth of information and inspiration.

So, grab your headphones and start exploring the world of fashion through these insightful and engaging podcasts. Happy listening!

Note: This blog post is not affiliated with or sponsored by any of the mentioned podcasts. The recommendations are based on personal opinion and popular reception within the fashion community.
