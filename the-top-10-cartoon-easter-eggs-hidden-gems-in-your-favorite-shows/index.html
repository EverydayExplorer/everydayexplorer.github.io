The Top 10 Cartoon Easter Eggs: Hidden Gems in Your Favorite Shows


In the world of animation, cartoon creators often hide little surprises and nods to their audience in the form of Easter eggs. These hidden gems can range from subtle references to other shows or movies, to clever in-jokes and even mistakes that ended up becoming iconic. In this blog post, we will explore the top 10 cartoon Easter eggs that you may have missed in your favorite shows.

1. SpongeBob SquarePants: The Krusty Krab’s secret menu
In the popular Nickelodeon series, SpongeBob SquarePants, there’s a secret menu hidden in the Krusty Krab’s sign. If you look closely, you can see that the sign reads ‘Krusty Krab: Home of the Best Burger in Town,’ with the word ‘Burger’ being the secret menu item.

2. The Simpsons: A tribute to a fallen writer
In the episode ‘Lisa’s Wedding,’ a chalkboard in the background reads ‘R.I.P. Jon,’ a tribute to Jon Bernstein, a writer who passed away during the production of the episode. This is just one of many Easter eggs in the long-running series.

3. Adventure Time: Marceline’s hidden tattoo
In the episode ‘Mystery Dungeon,’ Marceline’s character design reveals a tattoo on her upper arm. This tattoo is a reference to the creator of Adventure Time, Pendleton Ward, and his wife, Rebecca.

4. South Park: The Moleman’s secret identity
In the South Park episode ‘Moleman,’ the Moleman is revealed to be Stan Marsh’s father. This was a surprise to many fans and was kept a secret until the episode aired.

5. Rick and Morty: The Pickle Rick reference
In the episode ‘Pickle Rick,’ Rick turns himself into a pickle to avoid going to family therapy. This episode has inspired a popular internet meme, with people sharing their own ‘Pickle Rick’ moments.

6. The Powerpuff Girls: The missing letter
In the opening credits of The Powerpuff Girls, there is a sign that reads ‘Welcome to Townsville.’ However, the ‘S’ in ‘Townsville’ is intentionally missing, creating a visual pun on the word ’townsville.’

7. Futurama: The hidden Fry
In the episode ‘The Devil’s Hands Are Idle Playthings,’ Fry can be seen in the background of a scene, playing a small organ. This is a reference to the fact that Fry’s voice actor, Billy West, is also the voice of organist Peter Tork from the Monkees.

8. Family Guy: The hidden in-joke
In the episode ‘E. Peterbus Unum,’ Peter is shown wearing a shirt with the logo of the band Phish. This is a reference to the fact that the creator of Family Guy, Seth MacFarlane, is a huge Phish fan.

9. The Ren & Stimpy Show: The hidden message
In the episode ‘Man’s Best Friend,’ there is a scene where Ren and Stimpy are watching a dog show. In the background, you can see a sign that reads ‘P.U.,’ which is a reference to the show’s creator, John Kricfalusi, and his love for puns.

10. The Flintstones: The hidden logo
In the opening credits of The Flintstones, there is a sign that reads ‘Bedrock Cement Co.’ However, if you look closely, you can see that the ‘C’ in ‘Cement’ is actually a stylized image of a dinosaur. This is a reference to the fact that the show is set in the Stone Age, a time when dinosaurs still existed.

In conclusion, cartoon Easter eggs add a layer of depth and fun to our favorite shows. These hidden gems can range from subtle references to other shows or movies, to clever in-jokes and even mistakes that ended up becoming iconic. Keep an eye out for these Easter eggs the next time you watch your favorite cartoons, and maybe you’ll discover some hidden treasures of your own.
