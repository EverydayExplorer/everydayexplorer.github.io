Survival Knots: How to Tie and Use Knots for Various Situations


Knots are an essential skill for any outdoor enthusiast or survivalist. Whether you’re camping, hiking, boating, or faced with an emergency situation, knowing how to tie and use knots can be a lifesaver. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most commonly used survival knots and their applications in different situations.

1. The Bowline Knot:
The bowline knot is often referred to as the ‘king of knots’ due to its versatility and strength. It creates a fixed loop at the end of a rope that will not slip or come undone under load. This knot is perfect for securing a rope to a post, tree, or even used as a harness in emergency situations. It’s relatively easy to learn and can be tied quickly even in stressful situations.

2. The Clove Hitch:
The clove hitch is a simple knot that can be used to attach a rope to a cylindrical object, such as a tree branch or a tent pole. Its versatility makes it handy for setting up camping equipment, securing tarps, or building shelters. One of the key advantages of the clove hitch is that it can be easily adjusted and untied, making it ideal for quick setups and teardowns.

3. The Square Knot:
Also known as the reef knot, the square knot is used for joining two ropes of equal diameter. It provides a strong and secure connection that can be easily untied when required. This knot is often used for bundling items together, tying down gear, or creating makeshift tools. It’s important to note that the square knot is not suitable for joining ropes of different diameters, as it has a tendency to slip under uneven load.

4. The Taut-Line Hitch:
When it comes to securing and adjusting a tent guy line, the taut-line hitch is a go-to knot. This knot allows for easy tensioning and readjustment of the line, ensuring that your tent remains stable in windy conditions. Its ability to hold tension while being easy to loosen or tighten makes it a valuable knot for various situations where a secure but adjustable connection is required.

5. The Prusik Knot:
The Prusik knot is used for ascending or descending a rope. It’s a friction hitch that allows you to grip a rope tightly while still being able to easily slide it up or down. This knot is particularly useful in climbing and mountaineering, providing a reliable way to climb ropes or create makeshift pulley systems. Additionally, the Prusik knot can be used in rescue scenarios where a person needs to be hoisted or lowered with control.

6. The Figure Eight Knot:
The figure eight knot is a simple yet essential knot used to create a stopper at the end of a rope. It prevents the rope from slipping through a hole or a belay device, ensuring your safety when climbing, rappelling, or belaying. This knot is incredibly strong and reliable, making it a crucial skill for anyone involved in rock climbing or high rope activities.

7. The Fisherman’s Knot:
The fisherman’s knot, also known as the improved clinch knot, is commonly used in fishing to secure the fishing line to the hook, lure, or swivel. It is a reliable and easy-to-tie knot that maintains a high breaking strength. While fishing might not be a typical survival situation, knowing this knot can be helpful if you ever find yourself in a situation where fishing is necessary for survival.

In conclusion, the ability to tie and use knots is a fundamental skill for anyone venturing into the great outdoors or preparing for emergencies. The knots mentioned in this blog post are just a few examples of the countless variations out there, each serving a particular purpose. By learning these survival knots and practicing their applications, you’ll be well-prepared to handle a variety of situations that may arise during your outdoor adventures or unforeseen emergencies. Stay safe and keep practicing those knots!
