Top 10 Must-Read Books of the Decade


In a world driven by technology and instant gratification, books continue to hold a special place in our hearts. They transport us to different worlds, challenge our perspectives, and inspire us to explore the depths of our imagination. As we near the end of this decade, it’s the perfect time to reflect on the literary gems that have captivated readers over the past ten years. Here, we present to you the top 10 must-read books of the decade that have left an indelible mark on the literary landscape.

1. ‘The Underground Railroad’ by Colson Whitehead: This powerful novel takes us on a harrowing journey through the eyes of a young slave named Cora as she escapes from a cotton plantation using the Underground Railroad. It explores themes of freedom, resilience, and the enduring power of hope.

2. ‘The Goldfinch’ by Donna Tartt: Winner of the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction, this coming-of-age tale follows Theo Decker, a young boy who survives a tragic event and becomes entangled in the world of art forgery. Tartt’s masterful storytelling and rich character development make this a truly unforgettable read.

3. ‘Educated’ by Tara Westover: This memoir recounts the author’s upbringing in a strict and abusive household in rural Idaho, where education was forbidden. Westover’s journey towards self-discovery and empowerment through education is both inspiring and thought-provoking.

4. ‘Normal People’ by Sally Rooney: This contemporary novel explores the complex dynamics of a relationship between two Irish teenagers, Connell and Marianne, as they navigate through love, friendship, and personal growth. Rooney’s honest portrayal of young love and vulnerability strikes a chord with readers of all ages.

5. ‘The Girl on the Train’ by Paula Hawkins: A gripping psychological thriller that became an instant sensation, this novel follows Rachel Watson, an alcoholic who becomes entangled in a missing person investigation. Full of twists and turns, it keeps readers on the edge of their seats until the very end.

6. ‘The Hate U Give’ by Angie Thomas: Inspired by the Black Lives Matter movement, this young adult novel tells the story of Starr Carter, a sixteen-year-old girl who witnesses the fatal shooting of her unarmed friend by a police officer. Thomas confronts issues of racism, identity, and social justice in a powerful and timely manner.

7. ‘A Brief History of Seven Killings’ by Marlon James: Winner of the Man Booker Prize, this epic novel explores the aftermath of the attempted assassination of Bob Marley in Jamaica in the 1970s. James weaves together multiple perspectives and genres to create a vivid and immersive narrative.

8. ‘The Silent Patient’ by Alex Michaelides: This gripping psychological thriller follows Alicia Berenson, a famous painter who murders her husband and then stops speaking. The story unravels through the eyes of Theo Faber, a psychotherapist determined to uncover the truth behind Alicia’s silence.

9. ‘Little Fires Everywhere’ by Celeste Ng: Set in the seemingly idyllic town of Shaker Heights, Ohio, this novel delves into the complexities of motherhood, identity, and the weight of secrets. Ng’s exquisite storytelling and deeply flawed characters make this a compelling read.

10. ‘Becoming’ by Michelle Obama: In this highly anticipated memoir, former First Lady Michelle Obama shares her personal journey from the South Side of Chicago to the White House. With grace, candor, and wit, Obama’s memoir offers insights into her life, her role as a public figure, and her commitment to empowering others.

These ten books have not only entertained readers but have also challenged societal norms, sparked conversations, and left imprints on our collective consciousness. Whether you’re a bookworm or a casual reader, these must-read books of the decade are sure to captivate your imagination and leave a lasting impact. So, grab a cup of tea, find a cozy spot, and embark on a literary journey through the past ten years. Happy reading!
