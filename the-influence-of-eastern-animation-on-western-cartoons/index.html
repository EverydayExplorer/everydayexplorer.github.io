The Influence of Eastern Animation on Western Cartoons


Throughout the years, East Asian animation, commonly known as anime, has gained an incredible following around the world, including in Western countries. This growing popularity has not only resulted in the increased consumption of anime in the West, but it has also gone on to affect the number of Western cartoons that adopt eastern animation techniques in their style, storytelling, and animation. In this blog post, we will explore how and why eastern animation has influenced the Western animation industry.

Anime is an animation style that is characterized by its use of vibrant colors, exaggerated facial expressions, and big eyes. It is a style that originated from Japan and has since become a global phenomenon, with many anime shows and movies being dubbed or subtitled in English and enjoyed by Western audiences. As fans continue to embrace anime, many Western animators have turned towards the style to create their own cartoons.

One reason for this trend is the vast cultural difference between the East and West. By adopting an anime style, Western animators can incorporate unique and culturally diverse elements into their content. Eastern animation offers a range of themes and storytelling elements that are uncommon in Western-style cartoons. For instance, many anime shows feature storylines that span over several seasons, which allows the audience to interact with the characters over an extended period.

Another way in which Eastern animation has influenced Western cartoons is through the incorporation of various animation techniques. This has enhanced the visual appeal of Western cartoons, with many animators using traditional hand-drawn animation techniques, similar to what is used in Japanese anime series. A good example is the hit American animated television series, Avatar: The Last Airbender, which was influenced heavily by the anime style of animation. The show’s creators, Michael Dante DiMartino and Bryan Konietzko, confessed to being huge fans of anime and wanted to incorporate the style into their production.

Lastly, the rise of Eastern Animation has helped to stimulate the Western animation industry’s growth by expanding its horizons. The availability of anime in the West has allowed artists to draw inspiration from a wide range of styles, giving them the freedom to experiment with different animation techniques. This has led to the creation of unique and innovative cartoons, which have garnered large followings among Western audiences. The impact of Eastern animation has been seen in many popular Western animated series, such as Teen Titans, The Powerpuff Girls, and Samurai Jack, which have all adopted elements from the anime style of animation.

In conclusion, the influence of Eastern animation on Western cartoons is a trend that is here to stay. The cultural difference between the East and West has created an opportunity for Western animators to incorporate diverse themes, storytelling elements, and animation techniques. This has made Western cartoons more visually attractive and engaging for a global audience. While there are some concerns about appropriation, the positive aspects of the integration of Eastern animation on Western cartoons cannot be ignored. Western cartoons’ continued adoption and embrace of Eastern animation indicate that anime’s influence will continue to inspire the creation of original and innovative cartoons globally.
