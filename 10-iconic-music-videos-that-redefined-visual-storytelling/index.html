10 Iconic Music Videos That Redefined Visual Storytelling


Music videos have become an integral part of our culture, allowing artists to connect with their audience visually and leave a lasting impression. Over the years, there have been several music videos that have pushed the boundaries of visual storytelling, revolutionizing the medium and redefining what it means to create a captivating visual experience. In this article, we will take a look at 10 iconic music videos that have had a significant impact on the industry and continue to influence artists to this day.

1. Michael Jackson - ‘Thriller’ (1983)

No list of iconic music videos would be complete without mentioning Michael Jackson’s groundbreaking video for ‘Thriller.’ Directed by John Landis, this 14-minute short film set a new standard for music videos. With its cinematic quality, intricate dance routines, and a captivating storyline that incorporated horror elements, ‘Thriller’ remains one of the most iconic music videos of all time.

2. Madonna - ‘Like a Prayer’ (1989)

Madonna has always been known for pushing boundaries and ‘Like a Prayer’ was no exception. Directed by Mary Lambert, this controversial video combined religious imagery, racial tensions, and a powerful narrative to create a visually stunning and thought-provoking experience. Its boldness and willingness to address controversial subjects made ‘Like a Prayer’ a game-changer in the world of music videos.

3. Nirvana - ‘Smells Like Teen Spirit’ (1991)

Nirvana’s ‘Smells Like Teen Spirit’ music video, directed by Samuel Bayer, perfectly captured the grunge aesthetic of the 1990s. With its raw and energetic performance footage, distorted visuals, and rebellious spirit, this video became an anthem for a generation and solidified Nirvana’s place in music history.

4. Björk - ‘All is Full of Love’ (1999)

Directed by Chris Cunningham, Björk’s ‘All is Full of Love’ was a groundbreaking music video for its use of innovative CGI and robotics. The video depicted two humanoid robots slowly coming together and embracing, symbolizing the merging of technology and humanity. Its visually stunning visuals and poignant themes of love and technology made it one of the most memorable music videos of the late 90s.

5. Beyoncé - ‘Formation’ (2016)

Beyoncé’s ‘Formation’ music video, co-directed by Melina Matsoukas, was a powerful celebration of black culture and empowerment. With its stunning visuals, powerful choreography, and thought-provoking messages about racial identity and social issues, ‘Formation’ sparked conversations and solidified Beyoncé’s status as a visual storyteller.

6. OK Go - ‘Here It Goes Again’ (2006)

OK Go’s ‘Here It Goes Again’ music video, directed by Trish Sie, is known for its clever use of treadmills and synchronized choreography. This low-budget, one-take video became a viral sensation and showcased the power of creativity and simplicity in captivating an audience. It proved that a visually striking music video doesn’t require a huge budget but rather innovative ideas and execution.

7. Lady Gaga - ‘Bad Romance’ (2009)

Directed by Francis Lawrence, Lady Gaga’s ‘Bad Romance’ music video shocked and captivated audiences with its dark and visually stunning imagery. From the iconic ‘alien’ scenes to the elaborate fashion and intricate dance routines, ‘Bad Romance’ set a new standard for avant-garde visuals in music videos and solidified Lady Gaga as a visual and musical powerhouse.

8. Peter Gabriel - ‘Sledgehammer’ (1986)

Directed by Stephen R. Johnson, Peter Gabriel’s ‘Sledgehammer’ music video was an innovative combination of stop-motion animation and live-action sequences. The video showcased Gabriel’s quirky sense of humor and his willingness to embrace unconventional storytelling methods. ‘Sledgehammer’ remains one of the most visually unique and memorable music videos of the 80s.

9. Gorillaz - ‘Clint Eastwood’ (2001)

Gorillaz’s ‘Clint Eastwood’ music video, directed by Jamie Hewlett and Pete Candeland, introduced the world to the unique concept of a virtual band. The video combined animation and live-action elements to create a surreal and visually captivating experience. With its distinctive animation style and memorable characters, ‘Clint Eastwood’ paved the way for future animated music videos.

10. A-Ha - ‘Take on Me’ (1985)

Directed by Steve Barron, A-Ha’s ‘Take on Me’ music video combined live-action and animation in a groundbreaking way. The video tells the story of a girl who gets pulled into a comic book by a handsome, animated hero. The blend of reality and fantasy, along with its memorable rotoscoped animation, made ‘Take on Me’ one of the most visually innovative and enduring music videos of all time.

These 10 iconic music videos have not only redefined visual storytelling but have also left a long-lasting impact on the music industry as a whole. From their innovative use of technology to their willingness to address controversial subjects, these videos have pushed boundaries, sparked conversations, and inspired countless artists around the world. As music continues to evolve, one thing is certain – the power of visual storytelling in music videos will continue to captivate and engage audiences for years to come.
