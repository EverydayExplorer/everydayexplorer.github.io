10 Renovations That Can Increase Your Home's Value


In today’s competitive real estate market, it’s more important than ever to make your home stand out. One way to do this is to invest in renovations that can increase its value. Here are 10 renovations that are sure to deliver a high return on investment:

  1. Kitchen Remodel

The kitchen is often the heart of the home, so it’s no surprise that a kitchen remodel can significantly increase its value. According to the National Association of Realtors (NAR), a major kitchen remodel can recoup up to 70% of its cost when you sell your home.

When remodeling your kitchen, focus on updating the appliances, cabinets, and countertops. You should also consider adding an island or breakfast bar, if space allows.

  1. Bathroom Remodel

Bathrooms are another important room in the home, and a remodel can make a big difference in its value. NAR estimates that a bathroom remodel can recoup up to 60% of its cost when you sell your home.

When remodeling your bathroom, focus on updating the fixtures, tile, and vanity. You should also consider adding a walk-in shower or soaking tub, if space allows.

  1. Addition

Adding square footage to your home is a great way to increase its value. According to NAR, an addition can recoup up to 80% of its cost when you sell your home.

When planning an addition, consider your needs and the layout of your home. You may want to add a bedroom, bathroom, family room, or home office.

  1. Curb Appeal

The first impression of your home is important, so it’s worth investing in curb appeal. A few simple changes, such as painting the front door, adding new landscaping, or replacing the mailbox, can make a big difference.

When improving curb appeal, focus on creating a welcoming and inviting entrance. You should also make sure your home’s exterior is well-maintained and free of clutter.

  1. Energy Efficiency

Making your home more energy efficient is a smart investment that can save you money on your utility bills and increase its value. According to NAR, energy-efficient upgrades can recoup up to 50% of their cost when you sell your home.

When making energy-efficient upgrades, focus on improving insulation, replacing windows and doors, and installing energy-efficient appliances.

  1. Smart Home Features

Smart home features are becoming increasingly popular, and they can add value to your home. According to NAR, smart home features can recoup up to 30% of their cost when you sell your home.

When adding smart home features, focus on features that are practical and convenient, such as smart lighting, smart thermostats, and smart security systems.

  1. Outdoor Living Space

Outdoor living spaces are a great way to enjoy the outdoors and entertain guests. According to NAR, outdoor living spaces can recoup up to 70% of their cost when you sell your home.

When creating an outdoor living space, focus on creating a comfortable and inviting area. You may want to add a patio, deck, or fire pit.

  1. Finished Basement

A finished basement can add significant value to your home. According to NAR, a finished basement can recoup up to 70% of its cost when you sell your home.

When finishing your basement, focus on creating a space that is functional and inviting. You may want to add a bedroom, bathroom, family room, or home office.

  1. Garage Conversion

Converting your garage into a living space is a great way to add square footage to your home. According to NAR, a garage conversion can recoup up to 80% of its cost when you sell your home.

When converting your garage, focus on creating a space that is functional and inviting. You may want to add a bedroom, bathroom, family room, or home office.

  1. Pool

A pool is a great way to enjoy the outdoors and entertain guests. According to NAR, a pool can recoup up to 70% of its cost when you sell your home.

When installing a pool, focus on creating a space that is safe and inviting. You should also make sure the pool is properly maintained.

Conclusion

These are just a few of the many renovations that can increase the value of your home. By investing in the right renovations, you can make your home more attractive to buyers and get a higher return on your investment when you sell.
