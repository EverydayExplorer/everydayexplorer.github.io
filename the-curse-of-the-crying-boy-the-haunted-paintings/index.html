The Curse of the Crying Boy: The Haunted Paintings


When it comes to haunted objects, there are few as infamous and chilling as ‘The Crying Boy’ paintings. These seemingly innocent works of art have gained a notorious reputation for bringing misfortune and tragedy to those who possess them. With countless reports of bizarre occurrences and eerie events surrounding this cursed artwork, it’s no wonder that it has become the stuff of legends.

The story of the Crying Boy paintings begins in the 1950s in the United Kingdom. Italian artist Giovanni Bragolin, also known as Bruno Amadio, created a series of paintings featuring young boys in tears. The paintings quickly gained popularity and were mass-produced, leading to their widespread distribution across the country. Little did anyone know at the time that these paintings would soon become associated with supernatural phenomena.

Legend has it that a fireman’s house burned down, leaving only the Crying Boy painting unscathed. This incident sparked rumors that the painting was cursed, as similar stories of unexplained fires seemed to follow those who owned the artwork. The superstition gained even more traction when stories began to circulate of firefighters claiming that they would often find copies of the painting intact amidst the ruins of burned homes.

As the tales of the Crying Boy’s curse spread, people started to believe that the paintings harbored a malevolent spirit. Rumors suggested that the artist had made a deal with the devil, infusing the paintings with dark energy. Others believed that the souls of deceased orphans were trapped within the artwork, crying out for help and causing the tragedies that befell their owners.

The curse seemed to transcend time and place, as reports of inexplicable accidents, freak accidents, and unexplained deaths continued to surface. Owners of the Crying Boy paintings claimed that they experienced extreme bouts of bad luck, such as financial ruin, relationship breakdowns, and even untimely deaths of family members. It appeared that this cursed artwork had the power to bring nothing but sorrow and despair.

Despite the mounting evidence, skeptics argued that the curse of the Crying Boy paintings was merely a result of coincidence or urban legend. They dismissed the idea that a piece of artwork could possess supernatural powers and attributed the tragedies to simple bad luck. Nevertheless, the stories and alleged connections between the paintings and the misfortunes persisted.

Today, the Crying Boy paintings have become highly sought-after collector’s items. Some individuals claim to have intentionally sought out the artwork in hopes of experiencing the paranormal or profiting from its dark reputation. Others, however, refuse to own a Crying Boy painting, fearful of the potential consequences it may bring.

The mystery surrounding the curse of the Crying Boy paintings remains unsolved, leaving us to wonder if there is indeed something sinister lurking within these haunting works of art. Whether you believe in curses or dismiss them as mere superstition, there’s no denying the chilling allure that surrounds this haunting phenomenon. The legends and stories associated with the Crying Boy paintings continue to captivate and terrify those who dare to delve into their enigmatic realm.
