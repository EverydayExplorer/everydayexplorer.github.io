How to Score an Upgraded Room in a Hotel


When it comes to staying in a hotel, we all want the best experience possible. And one way to enhance your hotel stay is by scoring an upgraded room. Whether it’s a stunning view, a larger space, or access to exclusive amenities, an upgraded room can take your hotel experience to the next level. But how can you increase your chances of securing an upgraded room? In this blog post, we will explore some tips and tricks that can help you score an upgraded room in a hotel.

1. Time your stay strategically
Timing is everything when it comes to getting an upgraded room. Hotels are more likely to offer upgrades when they have plenty of availability. If you’re visiting during a slow season or on weekdays, you have a higher chance of getting a room upgrade. Avoid popular travel seasons and weekends when hotels tend to be fully booked.

2. Join loyalty programs
Many hotels offer loyalty programs that provide benefits to frequent guests, including room upgrades. Sign up for these programs and make sure to mention your membership during the check-in process. Loyalty members are often given priority when it comes to room upgrades and other perks.

3. Be polite and friendly
A positive attitude goes a long way. When checking in, be polite and friendly to the hotel staff. Show gratitude and appreciation for their assistance. Engage in casual conversation and build a friendly rapport. Being kind and respectful may increase your chances of being considered for an upgraded room.

4. Dress the part
While it may not be a crucial factor, dressing nicely can make a positive impression on hotel staff. When checking in, dress appropriately and avoid looking unkempt or casual. Although this tip might not guarantee an upgrade, it can certainly influence how the staff perceives you and can improve your chances.

5. Do your homework
Research the hotel’s upgrade policy before your stay. Some hotels have specific guidelines for room upgrades, such as offering upgrades to guests who book directly through their official website or stay for a minimum number of nights. By understanding a hotel’s policies, you can take advantage of any opportunities that may increase your chances of getting an upgraded room.

6. Ask politely for an upgrade
If you don’t ask, you don’t get. While it’s not guaranteed, it doesn’t hurt to politely inquire about the possibility of an upgrade at the time of check-in. Express your enthusiasm for the hotel and explain why an upgrade would enhance your stay. However, be prepared that this may not always work, as hotels have limited availability and may not be able to accommodate your request.

7. Consider booking directly with the hotel
When booking a hotel, consider making your reservation directly with the hotel rather than through third-party websites. Hotels often prioritize their direct bookings and may be more inclined to offer upgrades to guests who reserve directly with them. Additionally, booking directly with the hotel allows you to establish a direct line of communication, making it easier to discuss potential upgrades.

8. Celebrate a special occasion
If you’re celebrating a special occasion such as a birthday, anniversary, or honeymoon, don’t hesitate to mention it to the hotel staff. Hotels often go the extra mile to make celebrations memorable, which can include complimentary room upgrades. While this may not always be the case, it’s worth mentioning your special occasion in the hopes of receiving an upgrade.

9. Be flexible with your room choice
Hotels sometimes have last-minute cancellations or availability issues that could lead to an opportunity for an upgrade. If you’re flexible with your room choice, you may be more likely to receive an upgrade if it becomes available. Let the hotel staff know that you are open to different room options, and you may find yourself in a higher category room.

10. Leave reviews and feedback
After your stay, take the time to leave a positive review and provide feedback about your experience. Hotels value guest feedback, and being a proactive guest who leaves positive reviews can help you build a good rapport with the establishment. This can increase your chances of being remembered and potentially receiving an upgrade on future stays.

In conclusion, scoring an upgraded room in a hotel requires a combination of strategic planning, friendly demeanor, and a bit of luck. By following these tips, you can increase your chances of securing an upgraded room for your next hotel stay. Remember to be polite, flexible, and take advantage of loyalty programs and direct bookings. While an upgrade is never guaranteed, putting in the effort can certainly enhance your overall hotel experience.
