The Legends of Cornish Folklore: Knockers, Piskies, and the Lost Land of Lyonesse


Cornwall, a picturesque region in southwestern England, is not just known for its stunning coastline and rich history but also for its intriguing folklore. The legends of Cornish folklore are filled with enchanting tales of mystical creatures, such as Knockers, Piskies, and the Lost Land of Lyonesse. In this blog post, we will delve into the depths of these captivating legends and uncover the secrets they hold.

Let’s start with the Knockers. These mischievous creatures are said to be small, dwarf-like beings who dwell deep within the mines of Cornwall. They are known for their distinctive knocking sounds, which miners believed to be a sign of impending danger or valuable mineral deposits. Knockers were believed to be helpful beings, guiding miners to safety and pointing them towards hidden treasures. However, they were also known to play tricks on unsuspecting miners, leading them astray or stealing their tools. Despite their mischievous nature, the Knockers were seen as protectors of the mines and were respected by the mining community.

Next, we have the Piskies, diminutive magical beings that are deeply rooted in Cornish folklore. Piskies are often depicted as small, mischievous creatures, similar to fairies or pixies. They are believed to live in the countryside, particularly in the moors and woodlands of Cornwall. Piskies are known for their love of dancing, music, and pranks. They are said to be invisible to the human eye but can sometimes be heard playing their tiny musical instruments or leaving behind traces of their mischief. While Piskies are generally harmless, they are notorious for leading travelers astray, especially at night. It is believed that if you encounter a Piskie, you should treat them with respect, or else you might find yourself in a heap of trouble.

Lastly, we come to the Lost Land of Lyonesse, a mythical land that is said to have once existed off the coast of Cornwall. According to legend, Lyonesse was a prosperous kingdom, known for its beauty and wealth. However, due to its inhabitants’ sinful ways, the land was said to have sunk beneath the waves, leaving only a few islands visible during low tide. Many tales and poems have been written about Lyonesse, with some even suggesting that it was the birthplace of King Arthur himself. The lost land continues to captivate the imagination of locals and visitors alike, with some claiming to have seen the remnants of Lyonesse during exceptionally low tides.

The legends of Cornish folklore - Knockers, Piskies, and the Lost Land of Lyonesse - are an integral part of the region’s cultural heritage. They provide a glimpse into the rich tapestry of beliefs and traditions that have shaped Cornwall’s history. Whether you choose to believe in these mystical creatures or not, exploring the legends of Cornish folklore adds an air of magic and wonder to this beautiful corner of England. So, next time you find yourself in Cornwall, keep an eye out for Knockers, listen for the faint sounds of Piskie music, and let your imagination wander to the lost land of Lyonesse. Who knows what secrets you might uncover?
