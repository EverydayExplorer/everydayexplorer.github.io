The Best Animated Movies for Adults to Enjoy


Animated movies are often associated with children and family entertainment. However, there are numerous animated films that are created specifically for adults, offering sophisticated storytelling, complex characters, and thought-provoking themes. These movies provide a delightful experience for grown-ups who appreciate the artistry and creativity of the animation medium. In this article, we will explore some of the best animated movies for adults to enjoy.

1. ‘Spirited Away’ (2001)

Directed by Hayao Miyazaki, ‘Spirited Away’ is a Japanese animated film that has captivated audiences worldwide. The story follows a young girl named Chihiro who finds herself in a world of spirits after her parents turn into pigs. This visually stunning film explores themes of identity, courage, and the power of love. With its rich storytelling and beautiful animation, ‘Spirited Away’ is a must-watch for animation fans of all ages.

2. ‘Persepolis’ (2007)

‘Persepolis’ is a French animated film based on the graphic novel by Marjane Satrapi. It tells the story of a young girl growing up in Iran during the Islamic Revolution. The film touches on themes such as war, freedom, and the struggle for identity. ‘Persepolis’ offers a unique perspective on a critical period in history and showcases the power of animation to convey complex emotions and narratives.

3. ‘Waltz with Bashir’ (2008)

‘Waltz with Bashir’ is a powerful Israeli animated documentary that explores the director’s experiences as a soldier during the 1982 Lebanon War. Through a unique blend of animation and interviews, the film delves into the traumas of war and the human psyche. ‘Waltz with Bashir’ demonstrates the capability of animation to tackle heavy subjects and provoke deep reflection.

4. ‘Isle of Dogs’ (2018)

Directed by Wes Anderson, ‘Isle of Dogs’ is a stop-motion animated film that tells the story of a young boy named Atari who embarks on a quest to find his missing dog on a remote island inhabited by exiled canines. The film combines Anderson’s signature style with inventive animation techniques to deliver a visually stunning and emotionally resonant experience.

5. ‘Anomalisa’ (2015)

‘Anomalisa’ is a unique and introspective animated film created by Charlie Kaufman. The story follows a customer service expert who, while on a business trip, meets a remarkable woman who challenges his perceptions of the world. Through its use of puppets and meticulous attention to detail, ‘Anomalisa’ explores themes of loneliness, identity, and the search for connection in a beautifully crafted manner.

6. ‘Watership Down’ (1978)

Based on Richard Adams’ novel, ‘Watership Down’ is a British animated film that tells the story of a group of rabbits who embark on a journey to find a safe home. While marketed as a family film, ‘Watership Down’ tackles themes of mortality, power, and the human condition, making it a thought-provoking experience for adult viewers.

7. ‘Fantastic Mr. Fox’ (2009)

Directed by Wes Anderson, ‘Fantastic Mr. Fox’ is a stop-motion animated film adapted from Roald Dahl’s book. The movie follows the story of a clever fox and his adventures as he outwits three greedy farmers. With its charming characters, clever dialogue, and stunning animation, ‘Fantastic Mr. Fox’ is a delightful experience for animation enthusiasts of all ages.

In conclusion, animated movies are not solely for children. There is a vast array of animated films created specifically to entertain and engage adult viewers. The movies mentioned above are just a few examples of the best animated movies for adults to enjoy. These films demonstrate the power of animation as a medium for storytelling and offer unique perspectives on a wide range of themes. So, if you’re looking for a break from live-action films, why not give animated movies for adults a chance? You may be pleasantly surprised by the depth and creativity they have to offer.
