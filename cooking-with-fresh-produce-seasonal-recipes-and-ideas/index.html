Cooking with Fresh Produce: Seasonal Recipes and Ideas


As the temperatures start to rise and the days get longer, it’s time to start thinking about cooking with fresh produce. Eating seasonally not only ensures that you are getting the freshest, most flavorful ingredients possible, but it’s also better for the environment and your wallet. By choosing fruits and vegetables that are in season, you are reducing the carbon footprint associated with shipping produce long distances, and often, they are more affordable too. Plus, let’s be honest, nothing tastes better than a juicy tomato fresh-picked from the vine or a plump peach still warm from the sun. In this article, we will explore some seasonal recipes and ideas for cooking with fresh produce.

Spring:

Spring is a time of rebirth, and it’s evident in the plethora of produce that comes into season. Asparagus, peas, artichokes, ramps, rhubarb, strawberries, and more are all at their peak during this time of year.

One delicious recipe to try is an Asparagus and Pea Risotto. Begin by sautéing chopped onion in butter until translucent. Add arborio rice and stir until it becomes translucent. Then add in vegetable or chicken broth, one ladle at a time, stirring regularly. When the liquid has been absorbed, add another ladle, and continue until the rice is tender but still has some bite. Meanwhile, blanch asparagus and peas for a few minutes in boiling water, then add to the risotto along with grated Parmesan cheese, and finish with a squeeze of lemon juice. Serve garnished with fresh herbs, like chives or parsley.

Another fantastic spring recipe is a simple Strawberry Tart. Start with a pre-baked and cooled tart crust. Spread cream cheese or mascarpone cheese on top, then arrange fresh strawberries on top in a decorative pattern. Brush strawberries with melted apricot jam for a glaze, et voila, a simple and elegant dessert that showcases the sweetness of the season.

Summer:

Summer is the time of year for sunshine, backyard barbecues, and fresh fruit. Watermelon, cantaloupe, berries, cherries, tomatoes, zucchini, corn, and green beans are just a few of the many available options during this time.

One delicious summer recipe is a Grilled Peach Salad. Start by grilling peaches pits removed, cut into wedges (2-3 per peach). Grill until there are grill marks on each side of the peach. On a large platter or salad bowl, lay out a bed of lettuce greens. Arrange the grilled peaches on top of the lettuce, along with crumbled goat cheese, chopped toasted nuts, and a drizzle of balsamic vinaigrette.

Another simple summer recipe is Grilled Zucchini and Corn. Slice zucchini into rounds and cut corn into thirds. Brush both with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Grill over medium heat until zucchini is tender and corn has grill marks. Serve as a side dish, or make it into a salad with black beans, avocado, cilantro, and lime juice.

Fall:

Fall is a time for cozy sweaters, crunchy leaves, and an abundance of hearty vegetables. Butternut squash, sweet potatoes, Brussels sprouts, apples, pears, and pumpkins are just a few of the many ingredients to embrace during this time of year.

One comforting fall recipe is Butternut Squash Soup. Start by roasting cubed butternut squash in the oven until tender and slightly browned. Meanwhile, sauté diced onion and garlic in a large pot until translucent. Add in vegetable broth and roasted butternut squash, and blend until smooth using an immersion blender. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg. Serve hot, garnished with croutons and a drizzle of olive oil.

Another fall recipe to try is Apple Pecan Stuffing. Begin by sautéing diced onion and celery in butter until translucent. Add in chopped apples, pecans, and cubes of stale bread. Pour in enough chicken or vegetable broth to moisten the mixture and season with sage, thyme, salt, and pepper. Transfer to a baking dish and bake in the oven at 350F for 30-40 minutes until the top is golden brown.

Conclusion:

Cooking with fresh produce is an easy way to elevate any dish. By embracing what’s in season, you are not only getting the best-tasting ingredients, but you are also supporting local farms and reducing your carbon footprint. Whether you’re whipping up a spring risotto or a fall stuffing, there’s no wrong way to celebrate the bounty of nature’s harvest. So, let’s get cooking with fresh produce!
