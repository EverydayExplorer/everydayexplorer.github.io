The perception of time transcends the mere ticking of clocks and the passage of seconds, encompassing a multifaceted cognitive endeavor that spans the realms of attention, memory, anticipation, and subjective experience. The Neural Basis of Temporal Perception: Unraveling the Temporal Cortex

Central to the investigation of temporal perception lies the exploration of the neural substrates that instantiate our subjective experience of time, unveiling the intricate workings of the temporal cortex and the neural mechanisms involved in temporal processing. By delving into the neural basis of temporal perception, cognitive science unlocks the mesmerizing complexities of temporal cognition, enriching our comprehension of the neural circuitry that underlies our perception of time.

Temporal Experience and Culture: Exploring Temporal Diversity
