Solving Systems of Equations by Substitution: Methods and Examples


Solving systems of equations by substitution is a crucial concept in algebra. It allows us to find the values of variables that satisfy multiple equations simultaneously. This method offers a straightforward approach to solve complex systems, making it an essential tool for problem-solving in various fields such as physics, engineering, and economics. In this blog, we will explore the methods and examples of solving systems of equations by substitution, providing a comprehensive guide to help you master this fundamental algebraic technique.

Overview of Systems of Equations

Before diving into the specific method of substitution, let’s briefly review what a system of equations is. A system of equations is a set of two or more equations that share the same variables. Solving the system involves finding the values of these variables that satisfy all the equations simultaneously. Depending on the nature of the equations, systems can have unique solutions, infinite solutions, or no solutions at all.

The Method of Substitution

The method of substitution simplifies the process of solving systems of equations by isolating one variable in one equation and substituting it into the other equation(s). The general steps involved in solving a system of equations by substitution are as follows:

  1. Choose one equation and solve it for one variable in terms of the other variables.
  2. Substitute this expression into the other equation(s), replacing the variable you just solved for.
  3. Simplify the resulting equation(s) to obtain an equation with only one variable.
  4. Solve this simplified equation to find the value of the variable.
  5. Substitute this value back into either of the original equations to find the value(s) of the remaining variable(s).

Example 1: Solving a Linear System

Let’s consider a simple example to illustrate the process of solving a linear system of equations by substitution. Suppose we have the following system:

Equation 1: 2x + 3y = 8

Equation 2: 5x - y = 7

To start, let’s solve Equation 1 for x:

2x + 3y = 8

2x = 8 - 3y

x = (8 - 3y) / 2

Now, we substitute this expression for x into Equation 2:

5[(8 - 3y) / 2] - y = 7

Simplifying this equation gives us:

(40 - 15y - 2y) / 2 = 7

23 - 17y = 14

Next, we solve the simplified equation for y:

-17y = 14 - 23

-17y = -9

y = -9 / -17

y = 9/17

Finally, we substitute this value back into Equation 1 to find the value of x:

2x + 3(9/17) = 8

2x + 27/17 = 8

2x = 8 - 27/17

Simplifying further, we get:

2x = (136 - 27) / 17

2x = 109/17

x = (109/17) / 2

x = 109/34

Therefore, the solution to the given system of equations is x = 109/34 and y = 9/17.

Example 2: Solving a Nonlinear System

Now, let’s explore an example involving a nonlinear system of equations. Consider the following system:

Equation 1: x^2 + y^2 = 25

Equation 2: x - y = 3

To begin, we solve Equation 2 for x:

x = 3 + y

Next, we substitute this expression for x into Equation 1:

(3 + y)^2 + y^2 = 25

Expanding and simplifying this equation gives us:

9 + 6y + y^2 + y^2 = 25

2y^2 + 6y - 16 = 0

Simplifying further:

y^2 + 3y - 8 = 0

Now, we can solve this quadratic equation by factoring or using the quadratic formula. In this case, the equation factors as:

(y + 4)(y - 2) = 0

Setting each factor equal to zero, we find two possible values for y:

y + 4 = 0 or y - 2 = 0

y = -4 or y = 2

Substituting these values back into Equation 2, we get:

x - (-4) = 3 or x - 2 = 3

x + 4 = 3 or x - 2 = 3

x = 3 - 4 or x = 3 + 2

x = -1 or x = 5

Therefore, the solution to the given nonlinear system of equations is x = -1 and y = -4, or x = 5 and y = 2.

Conclusion

Solving systems of equations by substitution provides a powerful method for finding solutions to algebraic problems involving multiple equations. By isolating one variable and substituting it into other equations, we can simplify and reduce the system to a single variable equation. This method is applicable to both linear and nonlinear systems, allowing us to solve a wide range of problems.

Remember, practice is essential to become proficient in solving systems of equations by substitution. The more you work through examples and exercises, the better you will become at applying this technique. With dedication and practice, you will develop strong algebraic skills that will benefit you in various academic and real-life scenarios. So, embrace the method of substitution and conquer complex systems of equations with confidence!
