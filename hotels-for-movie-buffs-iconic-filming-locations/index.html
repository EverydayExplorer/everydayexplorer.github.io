Hotels for Movie Buffs: Iconic Filming Locations


Lights, camera, action! For movie buffs, there’s nothing quite like experiencing the magic of their favorite films in real life. And what better way to immerse yourself in the world of cinema than by staying at hotels that have been featured in iconic movies? In this blog post, we’ll take you on a journey through some of the most famous filming locations and the hotels where you can relive those cinematic moments.

1. The Plaza Hotel, New York City
Made famous by movies like ‘Home Alone 2: Lost in New York’ and ‘The Great Gatsby,’ The Plaza Hotel is a true icon of New York City. Located at the corner of Central Park, this luxurious hotel offers breathtaking views of the city skyline and a chance to step into the shoes of your favorite movie characters.

2. The Beverly Wilshire, Beverly Hills
If you’ve ever wanted to feel like Julia Roberts in ‘Pretty Woman,’ The Beverly Wilshire is the place to be. This elegant hotel has been a backdrop for numerous films, including the classic romantic comedy. With its prime location on Rodeo Drive, you can indulge in luxury shopping and soak up the glamorous atmosphere.

3. The Grand Budapest Hotel, Various Locations
Although the fictional Grand Budapest Hotel doesn’t actually exist, its stunning architecture and whimsical charm captured the hearts of moviegoers in Wes Anderson’s film of the same name. While you can’t stay at the Grand Budapest Hotel itself, you can find hotels around the world that pay homage to its distinctive style and eccentricity.

4. The Millennium Biltmore Hotel, Los Angeles
Step into the world of film noir at The Millennium Biltmore Hotel, a landmark in downtown Los Angeles. This elegant hotel has played a prominent role in movies like ‘Chinatown’ and ‘Ghostbusters.’ With its rich history and stunning architecture, it’s a must-visit for any movie enthusiast.

5. The Mount Rushmore Resort, South Dakota
Remember the iconic scene from ‘North by Northwest’ where Cary Grant is chased by a crop-dusting plane near Mount Rushmore? You can experience that thrill firsthand by staying at The Mount Rushmore Resort. Situated in the heart of the Black Hills, this hotel offers breathtaking views of the iconic monument and a chance to relive that classic Hitchcock moment.

6. The Marigold Hotel, Jaipur
For fans of ‘The Best Exotic Marigold Hotel,’ a visit to Jaipur, India is a must. While the actual hotel featured in the movie is fictional, you can find charming boutique hotels in Jaipur that capture the vibrant spirit and colorful culture depicted in the film. Immerse yourself in the sights, sounds, and flavors of India while living out your own cinematic adventure.

7. The Stanley Hotel, Colorado
If you’re a fan of horror movies, The Stanley Hotel in Colorado is a must-visit. This historic hotel inspired Stephen King’s ‘The Shining’ and has a reputation for being haunted. Take a ghost tour, explore the eerie hallways, and feel the spine-chilling atmosphere that inspired one of the greatest horror novels of all time.

These are just a few examples of hotels that offer movie buffs a chance to step into the world of cinema. Whether you’re a fan of classic films or contemporary blockbusters, there’s a hotel out there waiting to transport you to the magical world of the silver screen. So pack your bags, grab your popcorn, and get ready for a cinematic adventure like no other. Lights, camera, check-in!
