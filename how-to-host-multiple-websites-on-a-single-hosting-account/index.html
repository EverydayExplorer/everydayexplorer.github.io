How to Host Multiple Websites on a Single Hosting Account


Hosting multiple websites on a single hosting account can be an excellent way to save money and manage multiple sites easily. However, many people don’t know how to do this, which is why we’ll cover the basics in this blog post.

First, let’s define what we mean by hosting account. A hosting account is a subscription service offered by a web hosting provider that allows you to host your website(s) on their servers. Most hosting providers offer multiple plans, and each plan includes a certain amount of disk space and bandwidth.

Now, let’s look at the steps involved in hosting multiple websites on a single hosting account:

1. Choose a web hosting provider that allows multiple domains

One of the first things you need to do when looking for a hosting provider is to make sure they allow multiple domains. Most hosting providers offer this feature as a way to let you host multiple websites under a single account. Some of the popular hosting providers that offer this feature include Bluehost, HostGator, and SiteGround.

2. Decide on the number of websites you want to host

Before you start hosting multiple websites, it’s essential to decide on the number of sites you want to host. Depending on your needs, you might want to host two, three, or even more websites under a single hosting account. You also need to consider whether you’ll be hosting sites with high traffic or sites with low traffic.

3. Purchase a hosting plan that suits your needs

With the number of websites and traffic in mind, you can now purchase a hosting plan that suites your needs. Most hosting providers offer various plans, and you can choose the plan that provides the appropriate amount of disk space, bandwidth, and other features you need. Once you’ve purchased your hosting plan, your next step is to set up your hosting account.

4. Set up your hosting account

The process of setting up your hosting account may vary from one provider to another. However, most hosting providers will send you an email with a link to your control panel. Your control panel is where you’ll manage your hosting account, including setting up your domain name(s), installing WordPress, and other website management tasks.

5. Add your domain name(s)

After setting up your hosting account, the next step is to add your domain name(s) to your account. Most hosting providers offer step-by-step instructions on how to add a domain name to your account. You’ll need to enter the domain name(s) you want to add and configure the necessary DNS records.

6. Install WordPress on your hosting account

WordPress is the most popular platform for building websites, and it’s effortless to install on most hosting accounts. If your hosting provider offers a one-click WordPress installation tool, you can use that to install WordPress easily. If not, you can download WordPress from the official website, upload it to your hosting account, and follow the installation instructions.

7. Configure the hosting settings for your additional websites

Once you have installed WordPress, you can create new websites for your other domain names. Each website will need its own database and can be managed separately through your hosting account’s control panel. Make sure to configure the hosting settings for each website correctly, including setting up any necessary SSL certificates and backups.

Conclusion:

Hosting multiple websites on a single hosting account can be a cost-effective way to manage multiple sites. Follow the above steps to host your multiple websites on a single hosting account quickly. Remember to choose a hosting provider that allows multiple domains, purchase a plan that suits your needs and set up your hosting account. Add your domain names, install WordPress and configure the settings for each website. These steps will help you to host multiple websites on a single hosting account.
