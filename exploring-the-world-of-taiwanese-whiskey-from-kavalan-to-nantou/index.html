Exploring the World of Taiwanese Whiskey: From Kavalan to Nantou


In the vast world of whiskey, there are many hidden gems waiting to be discovered. One such gem that has been gaining recognition in recent years is Taiwanese whiskey. While Scotland and Ireland may be the first countries that come to mind when thinking about whiskey, Taiwan has quietly been making waves in the whiskey scene with its unique and flavorful offerings. In this blog post, we will delve into the world of Taiwanese whiskey, exploring two of the most prominent distilleries - Kavalan and Nantou.

Kavalan Distillery, located in the northeastern part of Taiwan, has quickly risen to prominence since its establishment in 2005. Named after the indigenous people who originally inhabited the area, Kavalan is renowned for its commitment to craftsmanship and innovation. The distillery takes advantage of Taiwan’s subtropical climate, which accelerates the aging process and imparts a distinct character to the whiskey. Kavalan’s whiskies have garnered numerous awards and accolades, solidifying their place among the world’s best.

One of Kavalan’s most popular expressions is the Kavalan Solist Vinho Barrique. This single malt whiskey is matured in American oak casks that were previously used to age red and white wines. The result is a rich and complex spirit with flavors of tropical fruits, vanilla, and dark chocolate. Each bottle is hand-bottled at cask strength, allowing whiskey enthusiasts to experience the full intensity of the flavors.

Moving on to Nantou Distillery, located in the central part of Taiwan, we encounter another player in the Taiwanese whiskey scene. Established in 2008, Nantou is owned by the state-run Taiwan Tobacco and Liquor Corporation. While Nantou may not have the same level of recognition as Kavalan, it is certainly worth exploring for whiskey enthusiasts seeking something different.

Nantou’s flagship whiskey is the Omar Bourbon Cask. This single malt is aged in ex-bourbon barrels, imparting a smooth and mellow character to the spirit. With notes of caramel, honey, and a hint of spice, the Omar Bourbon Cask offers a delightful sipping experience that showcases the craftsmanship of Nantou Distillery.

One of the intriguing aspects of Taiwanese whiskey is the influence of the local climate on the aging process. Taiwan’s warm and humid climate accelerates maturation, leading to whiskies that are often more mature than their age would suggest. This unique environment also imparts distinct flavors and aromas to the spirits, creating a truly unique tasting experience.

In recent years, Taiwanese whiskey has gained international recognition and has become a must-try for whiskey enthusiasts around the globe. The craftsmanship, innovation, and dedication to quality exhibited by distilleries like Kavalan and Nantou have put Taiwanese whiskey on the map.

Whether you’re a seasoned whiskey connoisseur or a curious beginner, exploring the world of Taiwanese whiskey is a journey that is sure to delight your senses. With its unique flavors, exceptional quality, and fascinating aging process, Taiwanese whiskey offers a fresh perspective on the age-old tradition of whiskey-making. So, gather your friends, pour a glass, and embark on a tasting adventure that will transport you to the enchanting world of Taiwanese whiskey. Cheers!
