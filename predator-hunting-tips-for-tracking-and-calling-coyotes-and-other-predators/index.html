Predator Hunting: Tips for Tracking and Calling Coyotes and Other Predators


Predator hunting is a thrilling and challenging outdoor pursuit that has gained significant popularity in recent years. Whether you are an experienced hunter looking to diversify your repertoire or a beginner seeking a new adventure, predator hunting provides a unique and exciting experience. One of the most sought-after predators to hunt is the elusive coyote, but other predators such as foxes, bobcats, and mountain lions can also be targeted. In this blog post, we will explore some essential tips for tracking and calling coyotes and other predators.

Tracking predators requires a combination of knowledge, skill, and patience. Understanding their behavior and habitat is crucial for successful hunting. Predators, like coyotes, often have large territories and are known to cover extensive distances in search of food. They are opportunistic hunters, capable of adapting to various environments. However, certain patterns can aid the predator tracker in predicting their movements.

Spending time in the field and observing signs of predator activity is a fundamental step in tracking. Look out for tracks, scat, hair, and kill sites. Predators tend to travel along established trails, so keeping an eye on game trails or clear travel corridors can increase your chances of success. Pay attention to areas with an abundant food source, such as dense thickets, water sources, and areas where prey animals congregate.

Once you have identified a potential hunting spot, it’s time to employ calling techniques to lure in predators. Calling can be an incredibly effective way to attract coyotes and other predators. There are several types of calls that hunters commonly use, including mouth calls, electronic calls, and hand calls. Each has its advantages and disadvantages, and the choice ultimately depends on personal preference and local hunting regulations.

Mouth calls, also known as diaphragm calls, require practice to master. These calls create a realistic animal sound that can fool predators into believing that there is prey in the area. Electronic calls, on the other hand, often come pre-loaded with a variety of sounds, making them user-friendly and versatile. Hand calls, such as the predator call, mimic the sounds of injured prey animals and can be an excellent choice when hunting predators in distress.

It’s important to note that using a combination of different calls can be more effective than relying on a single call. Predators can become wary of repetitive sounds, so varying your calls and imitating different prey species can increase your chances of attracting curious predators.

Timing and patience are crucial when predator hunting. Predators are most active during low-light periods, such as dawn and dusk. These are the best times to set up your hunting position and start calling. The cover of darkness provides predators with a sense of security, making them more likely to respond to calls. Additionally, hunting during the colder months can yield better results, as predators are more active in colder temperatures.

While tracking and calling are essential techniques for predator hunting, it’s also important to practice proper hunting ethics and safety precautions. Ensuring that you are compliant with local hunting laws and regulations is of utmost importance. Always obtain the necessary permits and licenses before heading out into the field.

Safety should also be a top priority. Predators can be dangerous animals, and it’s essential to handle firearms safely and responsibly. Be mindful of your surroundings, use appropriate safety gear, and never shoot at a target unless you are confident of what lies beyond it.

In conclusion, predator hunting is an exciting outdoor pursuit that requires a combination of tracking skills, calling techniques, and patience. By understanding predator behavior, identifying their habitats, and employing effective calling techniques, you can increase your chances of a successful hunt. However, it’s essential to remember proper hunting safety and ethics throughout the process. So, gear up and head into the field for an unforgettable predator hunting experience!
