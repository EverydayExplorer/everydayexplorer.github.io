Tasty and Creative Ways to Cook with Leftover Thanksgiving Turkey


The holiday season is upon us, and that means one thing - leftovers! After a hearty Thanksgiving feast, it’s not uncommon to find yourself with an abundance of leftover turkey. But fear not, because today we’re going to explore some tasty and creative ways to make the most of that leftover turkey. These mouthwatering recipes will not only help you use up every last morsel, but they’ll also bring some excitement and variety to your post-Thanksgiving meals. So let’s get cooking!

1. Turkey Pot Pie: Transform your leftover turkey into a comforting and delicious pot pie. Start by sautéing onions, carrots, and celery until tender. Add diced turkey, frozen peas, and corn to the mixture. Season with salt, pepper, and herbs like thyme and rosemary. Top it all off with a flaky pie crust and bake until golden brown. The result? A hearty and satisfying dish that is sure to please.

2. Turkey Enchiladas: Give your turkey a Mexican twist by making turkey enchiladas. Shred the leftover turkey and mix it with some sautéed onions, garlic, and spices like cumin and chili powder. Roll the mixture in tortillas and place them in a baking dish. Top with enchilada sauce and cheese, then bake until bubbly and golden. Serve with sour cream, guacamole, and salsa for a festive and flavorful meal.

3. Turkey and Cranberry Quesadillas: Combine the classic flavors of Thanksgiving in a unique and delicious way by making turkey and cranberry quesadillas. Spread cranberry sauce on a tortilla, then layer on sliced turkey and your favorite cheese. Fold the tortilla in half and cook in a skillet until the cheese is melted and the tortilla is crispy. Cut into wedges and serve as a fun and quick snack or appetizer.

4. Turkey Salad Sandwich: For a lighter option, whip up a turkey salad using your leftover turkey. In a bowl, combine diced turkey, chopped celery, dried cranberries, and toasted pecans. Mix in mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, and a squeeze of fresh lemon juice for some tanginess. Season with salt, pepper, and herbs like dill or parsley. Spread the turkey salad on your favorite bread, add some lettuce, and enjoy a refreshing and satisfying sandwich.

5. Turkey Fried Rice: Give your leftover turkey an Asian-inspired twist by making turkey fried rice. Heat some oil in a wok or large skillet and sauté diced onions, carrots, and peas until tender. Add cooked rice, soy sauce, and sesame oil to the pan and stir-fry until everything is well combined. Finally, add in the shredded turkey and cook until heated through. Top with chopped green onions and sesame seeds for added flavor and crunch.

So there you have it - a variety of tasty and creative ways to make the most of your leftover Thanksgiving turkey. These recipes will not only help reduce food waste but also add some excitement to your post-holiday meals. Whether you’re craving comfort food, international flavors, or something light and refreshing, there’s a recipe here for you. So put those leftovers to good use and enjoy the deliciousness that follows. Happy cooking!
