A Guide to Independent Bookstores Around the World


Welcome to our blog, where we embark on a literary adventure to explore independent bookstores around the world. Whether you’re an avid bookworm or simply someone who appreciates the charm and magic of these unique spaces, this guide will whisk you away to some of the most enchanting literary havens on the planet. So, grab your reading glasses, curl up with a cup of tea, and let’s begin this delightful journey!

1. Shakespeare and Company – Paris, France:
Our first stop takes us to the romantic city of Paris, where literary enthusiasts flock to the iconic Shakespeare and Company. This legendary bookstore, nestled in the vibrant Latin Quarter, has become a pilgrimage site for book lovers worldwide. Its rich history, dating back to the 1920s, makes it a must-visit destination for anyone seeking inspiration from the likes of Hemingway, Fitzgerald, and Joyce.

2. Atlantis Books – Santorini, Greece:
Prepare to be mesmerized by the breathtaking beauty of Santorini, and let Atlantis Books transport you to another realm. Tucked away in a cozy cave-like space overlooking the Aegean Sea, this whimsical bookstore is a treasure trove of literature. With a curated selection of books in multiple languages, it offers the perfect spot to soak up the sun and lose yourself in worlds created by extraordinary storytellers.

3. El Ateneo Grand Splendid – Buenos Aires, Argentina:
Next, we journey to Buenos Aires, where the grandeur of El Ateneo Grand Splendid awaits. Once a grand theater, this majestic bookstore retains its original architectural magnificence, complete with ornate frescoes, velvet curtains, and a stage-turned-café. Take a seat among the bookshelves or bask in the opulence of the theater boxes while indulging in your literary passions.

4. Powell’s City of Books – Portland, Oregon, USA:
Heading to the United States, we find ourselves in the quirky city of Portland, home to the world’s largest independent bookstore – Powell’s City of Books. This literary mecca spans an entire city block, housing over a million new and used books. Lose yourself in the labyrinthine aisles, and don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation with their knowledgeable staff. You’ll undoubtedly leave with a new favorite read.

5. Libreria Acqua Alta – Venice, Italy:
Our penultimate stop takes us to the magical city of Venice, where Libreria Acqua Alta beckons with its charm and eccentricity. This bookstore’s claim to fame lies in its unique display of books piled in bathtubs, gondolas, and even a full-sized boat. Navigating the maze of books is an adventure in itself, and don’t forget to climb the famous staircase made entirely of old encyclopedias for a breathtaking view of the canals.

6. The Last Bookstore – Los Angeles, California, USA:
Our final destination brings us to the vibrant city of Los Angeles, where The Last Bookstore stands as a testament to the endurance of independent bookshops. Housed in a former bank building, this bookstore blends the old with the new, featuring an exquisite collection of new and used books, vinyl records, and even a cozy labyrinth of book tunnels. Be sure to save time to explore the store’s art gallery and attend one of their literary events.

As we conclude our whirlwind tour of independent bookstores around the world, we hope this guide ignites your wanderlust and encourages you to support these literary sanctuaries. Remember, it’s not only about the books themselves but also the communities that thrive within these spaces. So, whether you find yourself strolling along the Seine or wandering the streets of Buenos Aires, take a moment to step into these havens of literary delight.

Be sure to share your experiences and recommendations in the comments below. Happy reading and adventuring!
