The Best Game Calls for Different Species


Game calls have been a popular tool among hunters for ages. Hunters use game calls extensively to attract a variety of animals while hunting. It is an essential part of hunting gear that can help you lure your prey successfully.

Different species of animals respond better to different game calls. Learning about the best game calls for different species can significantly improve your hunting game. Here is a comprehensive guide to help you understand the best game calls for different species.

Deer

Deer are sensitive animals, and a slight noise or movement can make them wary. Deer calls are designed to mimic the sounds that deer make, such as grunts, bleats, and snorts. The most popular deer calls are the grunt call and the bleat call.

The grunt call imitates the sound of a buck’s social call, which attracts deer and makes them curious. It is a versatile call that works well in most hunting situations. The bleat call mimics a deer’s distress call, which is useful when hunting does. It is effective during the rut (breeding season), when does are more responsive to the call.

Turkey

Turkey hunting requires different types of calls for different phases of the hunt. A hunter uses turkey calls to mimic the sounds of turkeys, such as yelps, clucks, and purrs, to attract them.

The yelp call is the most common and versatile turkey call that attracts both hens and gobblers. The cluck is a shorter, sharper call that turkeys use to communicate with each other. It is a calling technique that works well when a bird is nearby. The purr is a soft, rolling call that turkeys make when feeding. It is a low-frequency call that can help calm wary birds.

Duck

Duck hunting requires various types of calls that mimic the sounds of ducks, including quacks, chuckles, and whistles. Ducks are highly social animals that communicate with each other regularly, making calling an essential part of duck hunting.

The most commonly used duck call is a mallard hen call, which mimics the sound of a female mallard. The quack is a simple call that mimics the mallard hen’s quack. The greeting call mimics the mallard’s greeting call and can be loud or soft, depending on the birds’ distance.

Elk

Elk hunting requires calls that mimic the sounds of bulls and cows. Elk calls are generally divided into two categories: bugle calls and cow calls. Bugle calls mimic the sounds of a bull elk during the rut. Cow calls mimic the sounds of a cow elk and are used to lure bulls.

The most popular elk bugle call is the diaphragm bugle call, which is effective in imitating the sounds of a bull elk. The cow call imitates the natural sounds of a cow elk, which attract bull elk. Using a mix of cow calls and bugle calls can be highly effective when hunting elk.

Conclusion

Game calls play a crucial role in hunting, and using the right call for the right species can significantly improve your chances of success. By understanding the different types of game calls and when to use them, you can become a better hunter and enjoy more successful hunts. Remember to research and practice using the calls before heading out to the field, and you’ll be well on your way to your next trophy kill.
