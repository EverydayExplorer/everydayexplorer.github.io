Cartoons That Became Successful Movie Franchises


Cartoons have been a beloved form of entertainment for generations, captivating audiences with their colorful characters, imaginative worlds, and engaging storylines. While cartoons are often associated with the small screen, many beloved animated series have made the leap to the big screen with great success, spawning lucrative movie franchises that have delighted fans around the world. In this blog post, we’ll take a closer look at some of the most successful cartoons that have become blockbuster movie franchises, and explore what has made them so enduringly popular.

One of the most iconic examples of a cartoon that has successfully transitioned to the big screen is ‘The Simpsons.’ Originally created by Matt Groening as a series of animated shorts on ‘The Tracey Ullman Show,’ ‘The Simpsons’ quickly became a cultural phenomenon and one of the longest-running and most beloved animated series in television history. In 2007, ‘The Simpsons Movie’ was released to critical acclaim and commercial success, grossing over $527 million worldwide. The film retained the irreverent humor and sharp wit of the original series while also offering a larger-than-life cinematic experience that appealed to fans old and new.

Another cartoon-to-movie success story is ‘The LEGO Movie.’ Based on the popular line of construction toys, ‘The LEGO Movie’ brought the colorful world of LEGO bricks to life in spectacular fashion, combining dazzling animation with clever humor and a heartfelt message about creativity and imagination. The film was a surprise hit upon its release in 2014, grossing over $469 million worldwide and spawning a successful franchise that includes sequels, spin-offs, and even a theme park attraction. The success of ‘The LEGO Movie’ demonstrated the enduring appeal of the LEGO brand and its ability to resonate with audiences of all ages.

One of the most successful cartoon-to-movie franchises of all time is the ‘Shrek’ series. DreamWorks Animation’s irreverent take on fairy tale tropes struck a chord with audiences when the first film was released in 2001, grossing over $484 million worldwide and winning the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature. The film’s success spawned three sequels, as well as spin-off films and a successful franchise that has become a beloved cultural touchstone for audiences around the world. The ‘Shrek’ series is a testament to the power of clever storytelling, memorable characters, and universal themes to transcend the boundaries of animation and become enduring classics.

Another cartoon that has found success on the big screen is ‘SpongeBob SquarePants.’ Created by Stephen Hillenburg, ‘SpongeBob SquarePants’ has been entertaining audiences on television for over two decades with its wacky humor, colorful characters, and absurdly delightful adventures. In 2004, ‘The SpongeBob SquarePants Movie’ was released to critical acclaim and commercial success, grossing over $140 million worldwide and proving that the beloved sponge and his friends were just as entertaining on the big screen as they were on the small screen. The success of the film led to a sequel, ‘The SpongeBob Movie: Sponge Out of Water,’ as well as a third film, ‘The SpongeBob Movie: Sponge on the Run,’ demonstrating the enduring popularity of the franchise.

In conclusion, cartoons that have successfully transitioned to the big screen have demonstrated the enduring appeal of animated storytelling and the power of beloved characters to captivate audiences of all ages. Whether it’s the irreverent humor of ‘The Simpsons,’ the imaginative world-building of ‘The LEGO Movie,’ the fairy tale charm of ‘Shrek,’ or the wacky adventures of ‘SpongeBob SquarePants,’ these cartoons-turned-movie-franchises have left an indelible mark on popular culture and continue to entertain and inspire audiences around the world.
