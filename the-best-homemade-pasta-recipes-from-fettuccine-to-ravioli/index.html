The Best Homemade Pasta Recipes: From Fettuccine to Ravioli


Homemade pasta is a culinary delight, offering a unique texture and flavor that store-bought varieties simply cannot match. Whether you are a seasoned chef or a kitchen novice, making pasta from scratch can be a rewarding experience. This guide will take you through the best homemade pasta recipes, from the beloved fettuccine to the indulgent ravioli, ensuring you master these Italian classics.

Why Make Homemade Pasta?

Before diving into the recipes, let’s explore why homemade pasta is worth the effort:

  1. Freshness: Homemade pasta is incredibly fresh, resulting in superior taste and texture.
  2. Customization: You can experiment with different flours, add herbs, or even incorporate vegetables for unique flavors.
  3. Satisfaction: The process of making pasta from scratch is immensely satisfying and a great way to connect with culinary traditions.

Essential Tools for Making Pasta

To make pasta at home, you’ll need a few essential tools:

  • Mixing Bowls: For combining ingredients.
  • Rolling Pin or Pasta Machine: To roll out the dough to the desired thickness.
  • Chopping Board and Knife: For cutting the pasta into various shapes.
  • Kitchen Scale: To measure ingredients accurately.
  • Ravioli Mold or Cutter: If making ravioli.

Now, let’s roll up our sleeves and get started with some of the best homemade pasta recipes!

Classic Fettuccine

Fettuccine is a popular ribbon-shaped pasta often served with creamy sauces like Alfredo. Here’s how to make it from scratch.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    • Create a mound of flour on a clean surface and make a well in the center.
    • Crack the eggs into the well, add olive oil and salt.
    • Using a fork, gradually mix the flour into the eggs until a dough forms.
    • Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until it’s smooth and elastic.
    • Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.

  2. Roll Out the Dough:

    • Divide the dough into small portions.
    • Using a rolling pin or pasta machine, roll out each portion to about 1/8-inch thickness.

  3. Cut the Fettuccine:

    • Fold the rolled-out dough and cut it into 1/4-inch wide strips.
    • Unfold the strips and arrange them on a floured surface.

  4. Cook the Pasta:

    • Boil a large pot of salted water.
    • Cook the fettuccine for 2-3 minutes until al dente.
    • Drain and serve with your favorite sauce.

Spinach and Ricotta Ravioli

Ravioli are stuffed pasta pillows that offer endless filling possibilities. This spinach and ricotta version is a classic favorite.

Ingredients

  • For the Dough:

    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 3 large eggs
    • A pinch of salt

  • For the Filling:

    • 1 cup ricotta cheese
    • 1 cup cooked spinach, chopped
    • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • A pinch of nutmeg

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    • Follow the same steps as the fettuccine recipe to prepare the dough.

  2. Make the Filling:

    • In a bowl, mix the ricotta, spinach, Parmesan, salt, pepper, and nutmeg until well combined.

  3. Roll Out the Dough:

    • Roll the dough into thin sheets using a pasta machine or rolling pin.

  4. Assemble the Ravioli:

    • Place small spoonfuls of filling on one sheet of dough, spaced about 2 inches apart.
    • Brush the edges and spaces between the filling with water.
    • Lay another sheet of dough on top and press around the filling to seal.
    • Use a ravioli cutter or knife to cut out individual ravioli.

  5. Cook the Ravioli:

    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
    • Cook the ravioli in batches for 3-4 minutes until they float to the top.
    • Serve with your choice of sauce.

Tagliatelle with Pesto

Tagliatelle is another ribbon pasta, slightly thicker than fettuccine, and pairs wonderfully with pesto sauce.

Ingredients

  • For the Dough:

    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 3 large eggs
    • A pinch of salt

  • For the Pesto:

    • 2 cups fresh basil leaves
    • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    • 1/2 cup olive oil
    • 1/4 cup pine nuts
    • 2 cloves garlic
    • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    • Follow the basic dough preparation steps.

  2. Roll Out and Cut the Tagliatelle:

    • Roll the dough to about 1/8-inch thickness.
    • Cut into 1/4-inch wide strips.

  3. Make the Pesto:

    • In a food processor, combine basil, Parmesan, olive oil, pine nuts, and garlic.
    • Blend until smooth, and season with salt and pepper.

  4. Cook the Tagliatelle:

    • Boil a large pot of salted water.
    • Cook the tagliatelle for 2-3 minutes until al dente.
    • Toss with the fresh pesto and serve immediately.

Butternut Squash Tortellini

Tortellini are small, ring-shaped pasta filled with a variety of ingredients. This butternut squash version is perfect for autumn.

Ingredients

  • For the Dough:

    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 3 large eggs
    • A pinch of salt

  • For the Filling:

    • 1 cup roasted butternut squash, mashed
    • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
    • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    • 1 tsp sage, chopped
    • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    • Follow the basic dough preparation steps.

  2. Make the Filling:

    • Combine the roasted butternut squash, ricotta, Parmesan, sage, salt, and pepper in a bowl and mix until smooth.

  3. Roll Out the Dough:

    • Roll the dough into thin sheets.

  4. Assemble the Tortellini:

    • Cut the dough into 3-inch squares.
    • Place a small spoonful of filling in the center of each square.
    • Fold the squares into triangles, sealing the edges with water.
    • Bring the two corners of the triangle together to form a ring shape.

  5. Cook the Tortellini:

    • Boil a large pot of salted water.
    • Cook the tortellini for 3-4 minutes until they float to the top.
    • Serve with a sage butter sauce or your favorite accompaniment.

Spaghetti alla Chitarra with Tomato Sauce

Spaghetti alla chitarra is a traditional pasta from Abruzzo, Italy, known for its square cross-section.

Ingredients

  • For the Dough:

    • 2 cups semolina flour
    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • 4 large eggs
    • A pinch of salt

  • For the Tomato Sauce:

    • 2 cups canned tomatoes, crushed
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1/4 cup olive oil
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Fresh basil leaves

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    • Mix the semolina flour, all-purpose flour, eggs, and salt.
    • Knead until smooth and let rest for 30 minutes.

  2. Roll Out and Cut the Spaghetti:

    • Roll the dough into thin sheets.
    • Using a chitarra (or a knife), cut into thin, square-shaped strands.

  3. Make the Sauce:

    • Heat olive oil in a pan and sauté garlic until fragrant.
    • Add crushed tomatoes, salt, and pepper.
    • Simmer for 20 minutes, then add fresh basil.

  4. Cook the Spaghetti:

    • Boil a large pot of salted water.
    • Cook the spaghetti for 2-3 minutes until al dente.
    • Toss with the tomato sauce and serve.

Conclusion

Making homemade pasta is a rewarding endeavor that brings the authentic flavors of Italy into your kitchen. With these recipes for fettuccine, ravioli, tagliatelle, tortellini, and spaghetti alla chitarra, you can create delicious meals that will impress family and friends. Enjoy the process, experiment with different fillings and sauces, and most importantly, savor every bite of your homemade pasta creations!
