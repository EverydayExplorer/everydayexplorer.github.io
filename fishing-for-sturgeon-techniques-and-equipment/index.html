Fishing for Sturgeon: Techniques and Equipment


Fishing for sturgeon can be an incredible experience for any angler. These prehistoric fish are known for their size, strength, and the challenge they pose when caught. Whether you’re an experienced angler or a beginner looking for a thrilling adventure, fishing for sturgeon is an excellent choice. In this blog post, we will discuss the techniques and equipment needed to successfully fish for sturgeon.

Understanding Sturgeon

Before diving into the techniques and equipment, it’s essential to have a basic understanding of sturgeon. Sturgeon are large, freshwater fish that can grow to impressive sizes. They have a unique body shape, with elongated bodies and bony plates known as scutes. Sturgeon are bottom-dwelling fish, and they primarily feed on crustaceans, worms, and small fish. In terms of behavior, sturgeon tend to swim close to the bottom of rivers and lakes, making them a challenging species to catch.

Techniques for Fishing Sturgeon

  1. Choosing the Right Location: Sturgeon are commonly found in large rivers, lakes, and tidal estuaries. Research and identify the best fishing spots in your area. Sturgeon often gather near deep holes, submerged rocks, and narrow channels where they can find food. Look for areas with strong currents and structures that provide shelter.

  2. Timing: Sturgeon are most actively feeding during the spring and fall months. This is when they migrate to spawning grounds or search for food. Early mornings and late evenings are generally the best times to catch sturgeon, as they tend to be more active during low light conditions.

  3. Using Heavy Gear: Given the size and strength of sturgeon, it’s crucial to use appropriate gear. A medium-heavy to heavy action fishing rod, preferably 7 to 9 feet in length, paired with a sturdy reel is recommended. Opt for a line with a high breaking strength, such as a braided line with a test strength of 50 to 80 pounds. Additionally, sturgeon are known for their aerial acrobatics when hooked, so having a quality drag system on your reel is essential.

  4. Bottom Fishing Techniques: The most effective technique for targeting sturgeon is bottom fishing. Start by anchoring your boat or fishing from the shore near a promising spot. Use a heavy sinker to ensure your bait reaches the bottom. Sturgeon have a keen sense of smell, so using a bait with a strong scent like salmon eggs, shrimp, or nightcrawlers can significantly increase your chances of success. Cast your baited hook upstream from your fishing spot and allow it to drift naturally with the current. Keep your line taut and be prepared for a strong bite.

  5. Patience and Persistence: Fishing for sturgeon can demand patience and persistence. These fish are known to be cautious and can take their time before committing to biting. It’s not uncommon to wait for several hours without a bite, but once you hook into a sturgeon, the wait will be well worth it.

Safety Considerations

When fishing for sturgeon, it’s essential to prioritize safety. Sturgeon are powerful fish, and their size alone poses potential risks. Here are a few safety considerations to keep in mind:

  1. Handle with Care: Never attempt to lift a sturgeon by its gill plates or mouth. Instead, use a net or grippers designed for handling fish. Sturgeon can thrash and cause injury, so always exercise caution when handling them.

  2. Use Strong Equipment: Ensure your equipment is capable of handling the weight and strength of a sturgeon. Using weak gear can lead to line breakages or rod failure, increasing the risk of injury.

  3. Boating Safety: If fishing from a boat, be aware of the sturgeon’s size. Ensure your boat is stable enough to support the weight of a potentially large fish. Additionally, always wear a life jacket and follow boating safety guidelines.

In Conclusion

Fishing for sturgeon is an exciting and challenging experience that can provide lifelong memories. By understanding the behavior of these unique fish and using the right techniques and equipment, you’ll significantly increase your chances of success. Remember to always prioritize safety and respect the fish and the environment. So, grab your fishing gear, find a suitable location, and get ready for an unforgettable sturgeon fishing adventure!
