The Art of Cellaring: How to Properly Store and Age Wine


As wine enthusiasts, we often come across bottles that are meant to be aged. Cellaring wine is a practice that has been cherished for centuries, as it allows the flavors of the wine to develop and mature over time, resulting in a truly sublime drinking experience. However, proper storage and aging techniques are crucial to ensure that the wine reaches its full potential. In this blog post, we will explore the art of cellaring and provide you with tips on how to properly store and age wine.

Why age wine?

Before we delve into the specifics of cellaring, let’s first understand why we age wine in the first place. While most wines are meant to be consumed shortly after purchase, certain wines possess the potential to improve with age. Aging allows the wine to undergo complex chemical reactions that soften the tannins, enhance the aromas, and create a more harmonious and integrated flavor profile. It is important to note that not all wines are suitable for aging, and it is crucial to research and select wines that have a proven track record for improving with age.

Ideal storage conditions

To properly age wine, it is essential to provide the right storage conditions. Temperature, humidity, light, and vibration are the key factors that must be carefully controlled.

Temperature: Wine should be stored at a consistent temperature between 45째F to 65째F (7째C to 18째C). Fluctuations in temperature can cause the wine to age prematurely or develop faulty flavors. It is advisable to avoid extreme temperatures, as both heat and cold can damage the wine.

Humidity: Wine bottles should be stored in an environment with humidity levels between 50% to 80%. Low humidity can cause corks to dry out, leading to oxidation and spoilage, while high humidity can promote the growth of mold and mildew. A wine cellar or a dedicated wine refrigerator can help maintain the ideal humidity levels.

Light: Direct sunlight or excessive artificial light can negatively impact the aging process and lead to the degradation of the wine. UV rays can break down the complex organic compounds in wine and result in off-flavors and aromas. Wine should be stored in a dark or dimly lit area to prevent UV exposure.

Vibration: Wine is a delicate elixir that can be negatively affected by excessive vibrations. Vibrations can disrupt the sediments in the wine and disturb its aging process. To minimize vibrations, it is best to store wine bottles away from heavy traffic areas or mechanical devices.

Choosing the right storage method

Now that we have covered the ideal storage conditions, let’s discuss the different methods of wine storage.

Wine cellar: A traditional wine cellar is a dedicated space designed to store wines at optimal conditions. Ideally, a wine cellar should be underground or in a cool, insulated room with temperature and humidity controls. The constant temperature and low light exposure create the perfect environment for long-term wine storage.

Wine refrigerator: For wine enthusiasts with limited space or those living in apartments, a wine refrigerator is a popular choice. Wine refrigerators are designed to replicate the conditions found in a wine cellar and provide a compact and controlled environment for aging wine. They come in various sizes to accommodate small to large wine collections.

Wine cabinets: Wine cabinets are another option for storing and aging wine. These cabinets are typically standalone and provide temperature and humidity controls. However, they are generally smaller in capacity compared to wine refrigerators and might not be suitable for long-term storage of large wine collections.

Aging potential of wines

Not all wines are created equal when it comes to aging. Certain grape varieties, regions, and winemaking techniques lend themselves better to cellaring. Red wines generally have a higher aging potential compared to white wines, as they typically have higher tannin levels and more robust flavor profiles. The tannins in red wines soften over time, resulting in a smoother and more balanced wine.

Old World wines, particularly those from Bordeaux, Burgundy, and Rioja, are renowned for their aging potential. These wines often have a higher acidity and structure, making them suitable for long-term cellaring. However, there are also New World wines, such as those from Napa Valley, Australia’s Barossa Valley, and Argentina’s Mendoza, that can age beautifully.

It is important to note that not every bottle of wine will improve with age. It is essential to research the specific wine and vintage before deciding to age it. Wine experts, sommeliers, and reputable wine publications can provide valuable insights into the aging potential of a particular wine.

Tips for cellaring wine

Here are some additional tips to help you properly store and age your wine:

1. Lay bottles horizontally: Storing wine bottles on their side keeps the cork moist and prevents it from drying out. A dry cork can lead to oxidation and spoilage of the wine. This is particularly important for wines sealed with natural cork closures.

2. Keep an inventory: Maintaining a record of your wine collection helps you keep track of the age, vintage, and storage conditions of each bottle. A wine inventory can also help you plan for future wine consumption.

3. Handle with care: When handling wine bottles, it is important to avoid excessive shaking or disturbance. Gentle movements are essential to prevent sediment disruption and damage to the wine.

4. Practice patience: Aging wine requires time and patience. Not all wines will reach their peak immediately, and some may take several years to develop their full potential. It is important to resist the temptation and enjoy the wine at its optimal age.

5. Learn from experience: Cellaring wine is a continuous learning process. Each bottle opened provides valuable insights into the aging potential of a particular wine. Take note of your observations and use them to refine your cellaring techniques.

Conclusion

The art of cellaring wine is a captivating endeavor that requires both knowledge and passion. By providing the right storage conditions and selecting suitable wines, you can embark on a journey of experiencing the magical transformations that aging brings to wine. Remember, each bottle of well-aged wine tells a story and provides a sensory adventure that few experiences can rival. So, go ahead, start cellaring your favorite bottles, and delight in the pleasure of savoring finely aged wine. Cheers!
