Hunting Techniques for Big Game: Glassing and Spotting


When it comes to hunting big game, one of the most effective techniques is glassing and spotting. This tried and true method allows hunters to scout out their prey from a distance, increasing their chances of success. In this blog post, we will explore the ins and outs of glassing and spotting, providing you with valuable tips and tricks to enhance your hunting experience.

Glassing refers to the act of using binoculars or spotting scopes to scan a wide area for signs of game. This technique is particularly useful when hunting in open terrain, where animals can be difficult to spot with the naked eye. By using optics, hunters can cover vast distances and identify potential targets without alerting them to their presence.

To effectively glass, it’s important to choose the right optics. Binoculars with a magnification range of 8x to 10x are generally recommended for big game hunting. Spotting scopes, with their higher magnification power, are ideal for observing animals from a greater distance. Additionally, investing in quality optics with good light transmission and image clarity will greatly enhance your glassing experience.

When glassing, it’s crucial to have a systematic approach. Start by scanning the area in a grid-like pattern, moving from left to right or right to left. Take your time to thoroughly scan each area before moving on. Look for any movement, unnatural shapes, or signs of wildlife such as tracks, droppings, or feeding areas. Pay attention to areas with good cover, as animals often seek shelter in vegetation or near water sources.

Spotting, on the other hand, involves using your optics to identify specific details or characteristics of an animal. This can include determining the species, sex, or age of the game. Look for antlers, body size, coloration, or any other distinctive features that can help you make an accurate assessment. Remember to be patient and observant, as animals may be camouflaged or partially hidden.

To make the most of your glassing and spotting efforts, consider the following tips:

1. Choose the right time: Animals are most active during the early morning and late afternoon hours. Plan your glassing sessions accordingly to increase your chances of spotting game.

2. Use a stable platform: Invest in a tripod or steady rest to minimize hand shake and ensure a steady view. This will greatly improve your ability to spot and identify game.

3. Be patient: Successful glassing requires patience and persistence. Animals may be hidden or in motion, so take the time to thoroughly scan the area.

4. Use natural cover: When glassing, position yourself behind natural cover such as rocks, trees, or bushes. This will help conceal your presence and prevent animals from being spooked.

5. Practice field judging: Developing the ability to estimate the size and age of an animal will greatly improve your hunting success. Study field guides, attend workshops, and practice your skills to become a proficient spotter.

By incorporating glassing and spotting techniques into your hunting repertoire, you can significantly enhance your chances of success. Remember to always prioritize safety, follow local hunting regulations, and respect the environment and wildlife. Happy hunting!
