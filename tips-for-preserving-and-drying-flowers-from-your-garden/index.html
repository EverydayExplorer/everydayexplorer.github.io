Tips for Preserving and Drying Flowers from Your Garden


Flowers are not only beautiful when they’re in full bloom, but they can also bring joy and a touch of nature to our homes even when they’re dried and preserved. Drying flowers from your garden is a wonderful way to extend their lifespan and create stunning arrangements that can be enjoyed year-round. In this post, we will explore some helpful tips and techniques for preserving and drying flowers from your own garden.

Choosing the Right Flowers

Not all flowers are suitable for drying, so it’s important to choose the right ones from your garden. Generally, flowers with sturdy petals, such as roses, lavender, and hydrangeas, dry well. Delicate flowers like tulips and daisies may not retain their shape and color as effectively. It’s also best to choose flowers that are not fully bloomed, as they will continue to open during the drying process.

Harvesting the Flowers

The timing of when you harvest your flowers is crucial for successful drying. Pick them early in the morning, after the dew has evaporated but before the heat of the day sets in. This ensures that the flowers are at their freshest and have the highest moisture content. Use sharp pruning shears or scissors to cut the stems at an angle, about 6-8 inches long, just above a leaf node. Remove any leaves or foliage that would be submerged in water during the drying process.

Air Drying

Air drying is the most common and easiest method of preserving flowers. To air dry your flowers, gather them into small bunches and tie the stems together with a rubber band or twine. Hang the bunches upside down in a well-ventilated area, away from direct sunlight. This allows the flowers to dry gradually and retain their shape and color. Make sure there is enough space between the bunches to prevent them from touching each other.

Silica Gel Method

If you’re looking for a quicker method that preserves the flowers’ original shape and color, silica gel is your best bet. Silica gel is a desiccant that absorbs moisture from the flowers, leaving them perfectly preserved. Fill a container with silica gel and gently place the flowers on top, making sure they are not touching each other or the sides of the container. Carefully pour more silica gel over the flowers until they are completely covered. Seal the container with a lid and leave it undisturbed for several days. Once the flowers are dry, carefully remove them from the silica gel and brush off any excess.

Pressing Flowers

Pressing flowers is another popular method of preserving their beauty. This technique is particularly suitable for delicate flowers such as pansies and violets. To press flowers, place them between two sheets of absorbent paper, such as blotting paper or parchment paper. Put the paper with the flowers inside a heavy book or between the pages of a flower press. Stack additional books or apply pressure to the flower press to flatten the flowers. Leave them undisturbed for a few weeks until they are completely dry and flat.

Displaying Dried Flowers

Once your flowers are dried and preserved, it’s time to display them in a way that showcases their beauty. You can create stunning arrangements by arranging them in vases, shadow boxes, or even glued onto canvas for a unique piece of art. Consider adding other dried elements such as leaves, grasses, or seed pods to enhance the visual appeal. Remember to keep your dried flowers away from direct sunlight and areas with high humidity to prevent them from fading or molding.

Conclusion

Preserving and drying flowers from your garden is a rewarding and creative way to enjoy their beauty long after they’ve bloomed. Whether you choose to air dry, use silica gel, or press them, these tips will help you achieve the best results. Experiment with different flowers and techniques to find what works best for you. So, go ahead and bring the beauty of your garden indoors by preserving and drying your favorite flowers!
