Hunting for Upland Birds with Spaniels: Training and Handling


When it comes to upland bird hunting, few hunting partners are as capable and enthusiastic as spaniels. These versatile and energetic dogs have a long history of being excellent companions in the field, and their natural instincts make them ideal for flushing out birds in dense cover. In this blog post, we will explore the training and handling techniques that will help you maximize your success when hunting upland birds with spaniels.

1. Choosing the Right Spaniel Breed:
There are several spaniel breeds that excel in upland bird hunting, including the English Springer Spaniel, Cocker Spaniel, and Brittany Spaniel. Each breed has its own unique characteristics and abilities, so it’s important to choose a breed that suits your hunting style and preferences. Consider factors such as size, temperament, and energy level when selecting your spaniel hunting companion.

2. Early Socialization and Obedience Training:
Proper socialization and obedience training are crucial for any hunting dog, and spaniels are no exception. Start early by exposing your spaniel puppy to different sights, sounds, and environments to build their confidence and prevent fear or anxiety. Basic obedience commands like ‘sit,’ ‘stay,’ and ‘come’ are essential for maintaining control in the field.

3. Introduction to Bird Scent and Retrieving:
Spaniels have a natural instinct for finding and flushing birds, but they still require proper training to develop their skills. Introduce your spaniel to bird scent by using bird wings or scent dummies during training sessions. Teach them to locate and flush birds, and gradually introduce retrieving exercises to hone their retrieving abilities.

4. Steadiness and Steady to Shot:
One of the key aspects of training spaniels for upland bird hunting is teaching them to remain steady in the field. This means they should stay in place when a bird is flushed and not break until given the command. Steady to shot training involves teaching your spaniel to sit or stay when a shot is fired, preventing them from chasing after the bird prematurely and potentially interfering with the hunt.

5. Field Training and Handling Techniques:
Field training is where all the pieces of the puzzle come together. Use live birds or bird launchers to simulate real hunting scenarios and reinforce your spaniel’s training. Practice handling techniques such as quartering, where your spaniel systematically covers the ground in a zigzag pattern, and signaling them to flush birds on command.

6. Safety Considerations:
Safety should always be a top priority when hunting with spaniels or any other hunting dog. Be familiar with local hunting regulations, wear blaze orange clothing to make yourself visible to other hunters, and ensure your spaniel is wearing a brightly colored vest. Keep your spaniel under control at all times and avoid shooting over their heads. Always prioritize the well-being of your spaniel and those around you.

In conclusion, hunting for upland birds with spaniels can be an incredibly rewarding experience. With proper training and handling techniques, your spaniel can become a skilled and reliable hunting companion. Remember to start early, be consistent, and prioritize safety throughout the training process. Happy hunting!
