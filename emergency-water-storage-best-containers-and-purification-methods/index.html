Emergency Water Storage: Best Containers and Purification Methods


In emergencies, such as natural disasters or pandemics, access to clean water may become limited or even unavailable. It is crucial to have a plan in place for emergency water storage to ensure the safety and well-being of you and your family. In this blog post, we will discuss the best containers for water storage and various purification methods to ensure you have access to clean drinking water during challenging times.

Water Storage Containers

When it comes to storing water for emergencies, choosing the right containers is vital. Here are some of the best options available:

  1. Food-Grade Plastic Containers: High-density polyethylene (HDPE) containers are considered the safest option for water storage. Look for containers that are specifically labeled as ‘food-grade’ and have a tight-fitting lid. These containers are resistant to chemicals and have a long shelf life.

  2. Glass Containers: Glass containers are an excellent choice for long-term water storage. They do not leach chemicals into the water and are easy to clean. However, glass containers can be heavy and prone to breakage, so proper handling and storage are essential.

  3. Stainless Steel Containers: Stainless steel containers are durable, lightweight, and resistant to corrosion. They are a popular choice for outdoor enthusiasts and emergency preparedness. Look for containers that have a silicone gasket seal to prevent leakage.

  4. BPA-Free Water Bottles: BPA (bisphenol A) is a chemical commonly found in plastic containers that can leach into the water and be harmful to our health. Opt for BPA-free water bottles made from materials like Tritan or polypropylene to store smaller amounts of water for short-term emergencies.

Regardless of the container you choose, it is crucial to clean and sanitize them before filling them with water for storage. Use mild dish soap and warm water to clean the containers, and rinse them thoroughly to remove any traces of soap.

Water Purification Methods

While storing water is essential, it is equally important to have a reliable method to purify water in case the stored water becomes contaminated. Here are some effective water purification methods:

  1. Boiling: Boiling water is one of the simplest and most reliable methods of purifying water. Bringing water to a rolling boil for at least one minute will kill most types of bacteria, viruses, and parasites. Allow the water to cool before consuming.

  2. Water Filters: Water filters, such as activated carbon or ceramic filters, are capable of removing bacteria, protozoa, and some viruses from water. Look for filters that meet the NSF International standards for water filtration.

  3. Water Purification Tablets: Water purification tablets are small, portable, and easy to use. These tablets release a disinfectant, such as chlorine or iodine, into the water, killing most pathogens. However, they may leave an unpleasant smell or taste in the water.

  4. UV Water Purifiers: UV water purifiers use ultraviolet light to destroy bacteria, viruses, and other microorganisms in the water. These portable devices are generally battery-operated and can purify water quickly.

  5. Chemical Disinfection: Chlorine bleach or iodine can be used to disinfect water. Add measured amounts of these chemicals to the water, mix thoroughly, and let it sit for the recommended contact time before consuming. However, be cautious with the amount of chemicals used, as they can be harmful if not properly dosed.

It is essential to be familiar with the water purification method you choose and carefully follow the instructions provided to ensure its effectiveness.

Conclusion

Emergency water storage and purification are vital components of any emergency preparedness plan. Having a sufficient supply of clean drinking water can help you and your family stay hydrated and healthy during challenging times. Consider investing in the best water storage containers and familiarize yourself with reliable water purification methods.

Remember to regularly inspect and rotate your water storage to ensure its freshness and viability. Stay prepared, stay safe!
