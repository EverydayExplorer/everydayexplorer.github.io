The Top 5 Bestselling Mystery Novels of All Time


Are you a fan of mysteries? Do you enjoy the thrill of untangling complex plots and uncovering hidden truths? If so, then this blog post is for you! In this article, we will delve into the world of mystery novels and discuss the top 5 bestselling mystery novels of all time. These books have captivated readers around the globe with their clever narratives, intriguing characters, and unexpected twists. So, without further ado, let’s begin our journey through the captivating world of mystery!

1. Agatha Christie’s ‘Murder on the Orient Express’

Agatha Christie is a name that is synonymous with mystery novels. Her book ‘Murder on the Orient Express’ is an absolute classic in the genre and continues to captivate readers even decades after its publication. Set aboard the luxurious Orient Express, the story follows renowned detective Hercule Poirot as he investigates a murder that occurs on the train during a snowstorm. With a cast of diverse characters and a mind-boggling plot, this novel has rightfully earned its place as one of the bestselling mysteries of all time.

2. Arthur Conan Doyle’s ‘The Hound of the Baskervilles’

No list of bestselling mystery novels would be complete without mentioning Arthur Conan Doyle’s famous detective, Sherlock Holmes. ‘The Hound of the Baskervilles’ is perhaps one of the most well-known stories featuring Holmes, and it is a chilling tale of myth, legend, and murder. Holmes and his loyal sidekick Dr. Watson are called to investigate the mysterious death of Sir Charles Baskerville, and the story takes a turn for the supernatural when a legendary hound is believed to be responsible. With twists and turns at every corner, this novel has become a beloved classic in the mystery genre.

3. Dan Brown’s ‘The Da Vinci Code’

Moving into more contemporary times, ‘The Da Vinci Code’ by Dan Brown took the world by storm when it was published in 2003. Blending historical facts with a gripping fictional narrative, this novel follows symbologist Robert Langdon as he unravels a complex web of clues and conspiracy theories. With its fast-paced storytelling and provocative themes, ‘The Da Vinci Code’ became an instant bestseller and has since spawned a series of sequels and a blockbuster movie adaptation.

4. Gillian Flynn’s ‘Gone Girl’

For those who enjoy psychological thrillers, ‘Gone Girl’ by Gillian Flynn is sure to leave you on the edge of your seat. This gripping novel revolves around the disappearance of Amy Dunne on her fifth wedding anniversary, and as the plot unfolds, nothing is as it seems. Full of unexpected twists and unreliable narrators, ‘Gone Girl’ keeps readers guessing until the very end. With its dark themes and shocking revelations, it is no wonder that this book quickly became a bestseller and gained a cult following.

5. Raymond Chandler’s ‘The Big Sleep’

Lastly, we have Raymond Chandler’s ‘The Big Sleep,’ a hard-boiled detective novel that defined the noir genre. Set in 1930s Los Angeles, the story follows private detective Philip Marlowe as he investigates a case involving blackmail, corruption, and murder. Chandler’s gritty writing style, complex characters, and atmospheric descriptions have made this novel a classic in the mystery genre. Its influence can still be felt in contemporary crime fiction, making it a must-read for any mystery aficionado.

In conclusion, these are just five of the bestselling mystery novels of all time, each with its own unique blend of suspense, intrigue, and captivating storytelling. Whether you prefer the classic whodunit style of Agatha Christie or the modern thrillers of Gillian Flynn, there is a mystery novel out there to suit every taste. So why not dive into one of these bestselling titles and experience the excitement of unraveling a mystery for yourself? Happy reading!
