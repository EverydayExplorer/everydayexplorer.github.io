The Philosophy of Courage: What Makes a Person Brave?


As humans, we face numerous challenges throughout our lives, some of which can be overwhelming and daunting. These trials come in different shapes and sizes, ranging from minor everyday scenarios to life-altering situations that require immense courage to persevere. But what is courage, and what makes a person brave? These are some of the questions that have puzzled philosophers, psychologists, and scientists for centuries.

Courage, simply put, is the ability to face fear, danger, or adversity with confidence and bravery. It is the willingness to stand up for what one believes in and to take risks when necessary. However, courage is not the absence of fear, but rather the ability to overcome it. It involves pushing beyond one’s comfort zone, stepping out of the familiar, and embracing uncertainty.

One of the central tenets of philosophical thought on courage is that it is a virtue. In Ancient Greece, Aristotle identified courage as one of the four cardinal virtues that are necessary for living a fulfilling life. He believed that courage was not only vital for facing external challenges but also for dealing with internal struggles. For him, courage was about finding the right balance between fear and confidence, recklessness and cowardice, and having the moral fortitude to act in accordance with one’s values.

Similarly, in his book ‘Meditations,’ the Roman Emperor Marcus Aurelius viewed courage as an essential aspect of virtue. He believed that courage was the foundation of all other virtues because it required the individual to confront their fears, doubts, and weaknesses. According to him, courage was not only about facing physical danger but also about having the moral courage to do what is right, even when it is difficult.

Throughout history, many individuals have demonstrated extraordinary courage in the face of adversity. People like Mahatma Gandhi, Martin Luther King Jr., Nelson Mandela, and Malala Yousafzai are examples of individuals who displayed enormous bravery and resolve in the face of opposition. These people, like others, had something special that made them stand out- a well-defined purpose, a strong sense of conviction, and the ability to overcome their fears and doubts.

When it comes to defining what makes a person brave, psychologists and scientists have taken a different approach. Researchers in the field of neuroscience have discovered that courage is linked to our brain’s chemical and physical makeup. In a study conducted in 2017, researchers found that courage is associated with the release of the hormone oxytocin in the brain. Oxytocin is responsible for feelings of trust, bonding, and empathy, which are essential for building strong relationships with others.

Moreover, scientists have also discovered that the amygdala, a part of the brain responsible for processing emotions, plays a critical role in how we respond to fear. Individuals with larger amygdalas tend to have a heightened sensitivity to danger, which can lead to increased anxiety and fear. However, these individuals can also display greater courage when faced with adversity.

In conclusion, courage is an essential aspect of human life that is necessary for personal growth, social interaction, and societal progress. It is a virtue that requires both moral and physical courage, the ability to confront one’s fears, and the willingness to take calculated risks when necessary. While courage is often associated with extraordinary feats, it is also evident in everyday life, where individuals face various challenges that require them to step out of their comfort zones. Ultimately, to be brave is to be human, to confront the unknown with confidence and to trust in oneself to overcome any obstacle that comes our way.
