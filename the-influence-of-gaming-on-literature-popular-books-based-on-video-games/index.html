The Influence of Gaming on Literature: Popular Books Based on Video Games


In the ever-evolving landscape of popular culture, the realms of literature and video games have often found themselves intertwined in unexpected and exciting ways. While the traditional forms of storytelling in books and the interactive experiences offered by video games might seem like distant cousins, they share a unique synergy. This synergy has given rise to a fascinating phenomenon: books based on video games.

As video games continue to capture the hearts and minds of millions worldwide, it’s no surprise that this burgeoning medium has begun to inspire literary works that delve into the rich narratives, complex characters, and immersive worlds found within the gaming universe. In this blog, we’ll explore the influence of gaming on literature and showcase some popular books that have emerged from this unique intersection of media.

The Fusion of Two Worlds: Gaming and Literature

At first glance, it may seem paradoxical to connect the world of gaming, a visual and interactive medium, with the written word. However, upon closer examination, it becomes clear that these two forms of storytelling share fundamental elements. Both are vessels for storytelling, capable of conveying emotions, ideas, and complex narratives. They draw the reader/player into new worlds, offering escapism and immersion.

Authors and game developers alike have recognized the potential in bridging these two mediums. The result? An array of novels that either expand upon the lore and stories found in video games or provide new perspectives on well-known characters and settings. These books offer a deeper dive into the narratives that gamers have come to love.

The Appeal of Video Game Novels

Why have novels based on video games gained such popularity? There are several factors at play:

  1. Expanded Universes: Video games often feature expansive, meticulously crafted worlds with detailed lore and history. Books provide a medium to explore these universes in greater depth, offering readers a chance to uncover hidden stories and connect with characters on a more profound level.

  2. Character Exploration: Video game protagonists and antagonists can be incredibly complex, with rich backstories and motivations. Novels allow for a more intimate look into the minds and hearts of these characters, making them even more relatable and engaging.

  3. Nostalgia and Fandom: Many video game novels appeal to die-hard fans who have invested countless hours into a particular game series. These books offer an opportunity to relive and extend the gaming experience, fostering a sense of nostalgia and attachment.

  4. Storytelling Potential: Great storytelling is not limited by the medium. Books offer authors the chance to weave intricate narratives and explore the psychological and emotional depths of characters in ways that may not be fully realized in a game’s interactive format.

Now, let’s dive into some popular books based on video games that have left their mark on the literary world:

1. The Witcher Series by Andrzej Sapkowski

Long before Geralt of Rivia graced our screens in the critically acclaimed video game adaptation, he was the protagonist of a series of Polish novels by Andrzej Sapkowski. These novels served as the source material for the video games and offer an expansive look into the world of The Witcher, filled with monsters, magic, and morally ambiguous choices.

2. Halo: The Fall of Reach by Eric Nylund

Eric Nylund’s novel takes fans of the Halo franchise back to the origins of Master Chief and the Spartan program. This book delves into the military science fiction universe with compelling characters and gripping storytelling that expands upon the rich lore established in the games.

3. Assassin’s Creed Series by Oliver Bowden

The Assassin’s Creed series is known for its intricate historical settings and compelling narrative. Author Oliver Bowden has penned numerous novels based on the game series, offering readers a chance to explore the hidden war between Assassins and Templars throughout various time periods.

4. Bioshock: Rapture by John Shirley

Bioshock is renowned for its thought-provoking narrative and dystopian setting. John Shirley’s novel ‘Bioshock: Rapture’ takes readers into the depths of the underwater city of Rapture, offering a chilling look at its descent into chaos.

5. Resident Evil: City of the Dead by S.D. Perry

S.D. Perry’s Resident Evil novel series is a beloved addition to the franchise. ‘City of the Dead’ is just one of many books that explores the horror and suspense of the Resident Evil universe, providing a deeper look into the T-Virus outbreak.

6. Dragon Age: The Stolen Throne by David Gaider

For fans of the Dragon Age series, ‘The Stolen Throne’ is a must-read. Written by David Gaider, one of the key creative minds behind the games, this novel provides essential backstory and context for the game’s world, characters, and conflicts.

The Future of Video Game Literature

As video games continue to evolve and captivate audiences, it’s likely that the synergy between gaming and literature will only grow stronger. Authors will find new opportunities to explore the uncharted territories of these virtual worlds, providing fans with even more ways to engage with their favorite gaming franchises.

In conclusion, the influence of gaming on literature is a testament to the power of storytelling in all its forms. Books based on video games offer a unique lens through which fans can delve deeper into the worlds they love, providing new dimensions to cherished characters and narratives. As the lines between media continue to blur, we can only look forward to more exciting literary adventures born from the boundless creativity of both authors and game developers. So, whether you’re an avid gamer or a bookworm, there’s no better time to explore the captivating intersection of gaming and literature.
