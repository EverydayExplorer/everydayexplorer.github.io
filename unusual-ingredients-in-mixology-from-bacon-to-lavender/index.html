Unusual Ingredients in Mixology: From Bacon to Lavender


Mixology is an art form that continues to evolve with each passing year. From classic cocktails to trendy concoctions, there is always something new to discover in the world of beverages. One of the most exciting aspects of mixology is the use of unusual ingredients. These ingredients add a unique twist to drinks, creating unforgettable flavor profiles that surprise and delight the palate. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most unusual ingredients in mixology, from bacon to lavender.

1. Bacon: Yes, you read that right. Bacon is not just for breakfast anymore. Mixologists have found creative ways to incorporate the smoky, salty flavor of bacon into drinks. Whether it’s a bacon-infused vodka Bloody Mary or a maple bacon Old Fashioned, the addition of this savory ingredient adds a whole new level of depth to cocktails.

2. Lavender: This delicate purple flower is not just for sachets and bath salts. Lavender adds a floral and slightly sweet flavor to drinks. Its aromatic qualities make it perfect for infusions, syrups, and garnishes. A lavender martini or lavender lemonade are refreshing and aromatic options that are sure to impress.

3. Cucumber: While cucumber is a common ingredient in salads and sandwiches, it’s not often associated with cocktails. However, its crisp and refreshing taste makes it a great addition to many drinks. Cucumber-infused vodka, cucumber mules, and cucumber margaritas are just a few examples of how this versatile ingredient can elevate a cocktail.

4. Saffron: Known as the world’s most expensive spice, saffron adds a distinct earthy and floral flavor to drinks. It pairs well with citrus and herbal flavors, making it a popular choice for cocktails. Saffron-infused gin and saffron-infused simple syrup are great starting points for experimenting with this luxurious ingredient.

5. Black Pepper: Black pepper may seem like an unlikely ingredient in mixology, but its spicy and slightly floral notes can enhance the flavor of cocktails. A black pepper-infused simple syrup can add a subtle kick to drinks like the classic Old Fashioned or a spicy margarita.

6. Rosemary: Best known for its use in savory dishes, rosemary can also lend its distinctive herbal flavor to cocktails. Its woody and fragrant aroma complements a wide range of spirits, making it an excellent addition to drinks like gin fizz or rosemary-infused vodka lemonade.

7. Beetroot: Known for their vibrant color and earthy flavor, beets have been making their way into the mixology scene. Beet juice or beet-infused spirits like vodka or tequila can add a unique twist to drinks. Try a beetroot gin and tonic or a beet martini for a cocktail that is as beautiful as it is delicious.

8. Elderflower: Elderflower liqueur has gained popularity in recent years due to its delicate floral flavor. A splash of elderflower liqueur can transform a simple glass of sparkling wine or add a hint of sweetness to a gin cocktail. Elderflower adds a touch of elegance and sophistication to any drink.

9. Chamomile: Known for its calming properties, chamomile can also add a soothing and delicate flavor to drinks. Chamomile-infused vodka or chamomile tea syrup can be used in a variety of cocktails, from martinis to mules. The subtle floral notes of chamomile create a drink that is both relaxing and refreshing.

10. Cardamom: A spice often used in Middle Eastern and Indian cuisine, cardamom adds a warm and aromatic flavor to cocktails. It pairs well with citrus, berries, and even chocolate. Try a cardamom-infused rum or a cardamom-spiced mule for a unique and exotic drink experience.

These unusual ingredients in mixology create a world of flavors waiting to be explored. Whether you’re a professional bartender or an amateur mixologist, don’t be afraid to get creative and experiment with these unique ingredients. From bacon-infused vodka to lavender lemonade, there is a whole world of unexpected flavors waiting to be discovered in the world of mixology. So raise your glass and toast to the adventurous spirit of mixology!
