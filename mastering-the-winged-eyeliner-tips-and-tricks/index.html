Mastering the Winged Eyeliner: Tips and Tricks


The winged eyeliner, also known as the cat eye, is a classic makeup look that adds instant glamour and sophistication to any eye shape. However, mastering the art of winged eyeliner can be a daunting task, especially for beginners. From achieving the perfect flick to ensuring symmetry between both eyes, there are several challenges that makeup enthusiasts face when attempting this iconic look. But fear not! With the right tips and tricks, you can become a pro at winged eyeliner in no time. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll share everything you need to know to master the winged eyeliner look like a makeup artist.

1. Choose the Right Eyeliner:

The first step to mastering winged eyeliner is selecting the right eyeliner product. While there are several options available, including liquid, gel, and pencil eyeliners, liquid liners with fine, tapered applicator tips are typically the best choice for creating precise lines and sharp wings.

2. Start with a Clean Canvas:

Before applying eyeliner, ensure that your eyelids are clean and free of any oil or makeup residue. This will help the eyeliner adhere smoothly and prevent smudging throughout the day. You can also apply a primer or eyeshadow base to prolong the wear of your eyeliner and enhance its intensity.

3. Map Out Your Wing:

One of the most challenging aspects of winged eyeliner is achieving symmetry between both eyes. To make this process easier, start by mapping out the shape of your wing before applying any product. Use a small angled brush or a piece of tape to create a guideline for your wing, extending it from the outer corner of your eye towards the end of your eyebrow.

4. Use Short, Precise Strokes:

When applying eyeliner, use short, precise strokes rather than attempting to draw one continuous line. This will give you better control over the thickness and shape of your wing, resulting in a more polished finish. Start by drawing a thin line along your upper lash line, gradually increasing the thickness as you reach the outer corner of your eye.

5. Angle Your Wing Correctly:

The angle of your wing can dramatically impact the overall look of your eyeliner. For a classic cat-eye look, angle your wing upwards towards the end of your eyebrow. Alternatively, for a more subtle and elongated wing, angle it slightly downwards towards the outer corner of your eye.

6. Clean Up Any Mistakes:

Don’t worry if your winged eyeliner isn’t perfect on the first try – it takes practice to master this technique! If you make any mistakes or your lines aren’t as sharp as you’d like, simply dip a small angled brush in makeup remover or concealer and use it to clean up any smudges or uneven edges.

7. Set Your Eyeliner with Eyeshadow:

To prevent your winged eyeliner from smudging or fading throughout the day, set it with a matching eyeshadow. Use a small angled brush to gently press a matte black or brown eyeshadow over the top of your eyeliner, locking it in place and intensifying the color.

8. Practice, Practice, Practice:

As with any makeup technique, practice makes perfect when it comes to winged eyeliner. Don’t be discouraged if your first few attempts don’t turn out exactly as you’d hoped – keep practicing, experimenting with different angles and techniques until you find what works best for you.

With these tips and tricks in mind, you’ll be well on your way to mastering the art of winged eyeliner like a pro. So grab your favorite eyeliner and get ready to elevate your makeup game with this timeless and iconic look. Whether you’re heading to a special event or simply want to add a touch of glamour to your everyday makeup routine, winged eyeliner is sure to make a statement and turn heads wherever you go.
