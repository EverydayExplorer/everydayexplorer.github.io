Famous Filming Locations: Hotels Featured in Movies and TV Shows


When it comes to creating iconic movie scenes, filmmakers often rely on the perfect backdrop to set the stage. And what better backdrop than a luxurious hotel? Throughout the years, many hotels around the world have played starring roles in movies and TV shows, cementing their status as famous filming locations. In this article, we’ll explore some of these iconic hotels and the memorable productions they have been a part of.

1. The Plaza Hotel, New York City, USA
The Plaza Hotel is perhaps one of the most famous hotels in New York City, and it has made numerous appearances in movies and TV shows. Fans of ‘Home Alone 2: Lost in New York’ will recognize it as the hotel where Kevin McCallister stayed and had memorable encounters with the concierge. With its grandeur and elegance, The Plaza has become synonymous with luxury and class, making it an ideal location for filmmakers.

2. The Ritz Hotel, Paris, France
The Ritz Hotel in Paris is not only known for its exquisite accommodations but also for its appearances in films. Audrey Hepburn’s iconic character, Coco Chanel, was seen residing in the Ritz Hotel in the movie ‘Coco Before Chanel.’ The hotel’s timeless charm and romantic atmosphere have attracted filmmakers seeking to capture the essence of Parisian luxury.

3. The Grand Budapest Hotel, Various Locations
While The Grand Budapest Hotel itself doesn’t exist in reality, the film directed by Wes Anderson showcased various stunning hotels across Germany and Poland. These hotels served as the inspiration for the fictional establishment in the movie. The whimsical and colorful design of these real-life hotels added to the charm of the film and made it a visual delight for viewers.

4. The Fontainebleau Miami Beach, Miami, USA
The Fontainebleau Miami Beach has been featured in multiple films and TV shows, showcasing its iconic architecture and glamorous atmosphere. In the James Bond film ‘Goldfinger,’ the hotel served as the backdrop for key scenes, becoming synonymous with the sophistication and allure of Miami Beach. The hotel’s rich history and celebrity clientele have made it a popular choice for filmmakers looking for a touch of elegance.

5. The Fairmont Hotel Vancouver, Vancouver, Canada
The Fairmont Hotel Vancouver has appeared in several movies, including the classic film ‘Fantastic Four.’ Its distinctive stone exterior and opulent interior make it a sought-after filming location for producers. The hotel’s timeless beauty and proximity to Vancouver’s vibrant cityscape have made it a favorite among filmmakers.

6. The Millennium Biltmore Hotel, Los Angeles, USA
The Millennium Biltmore Hotel in Los Angeles has a long history with the entertainment industry, having appeared in numerous movies and TV shows. Its grand architecture and glamorous ballrooms have made it a popular choice for period dramas, such as the Oscar-winning film ‘Chinatown’ and the TV series ‘American Horror Story.’

These are just a few examples of the many hotels around the world that have become famous filming locations. From New York City to Paris to Miami, these hotels have provided the perfect settings for unforgettable movie and TV moments. The next time you find yourself staying in one of these hotels, remember that you may be stepping into a piece of cinematic history.

In conclusion, hotels have played a vital role in bringing movies and TV shows to life. Their unique designs, luxurious amenities, and rich histories make them an ideal backdrop for filmmakers. So, if you ever come across one of these famous filming locations, take a moment to appreciate the magic that has been captured within their walls.
