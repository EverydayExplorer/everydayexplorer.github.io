The Evolution of Awards Shows: From Honoring Excellence to Making Statements


In recent years, awards shows have undergone a significant transformation. What was once a platform solely dedicated to honoring excellence in the entertainment industry has now become a stage for making bold statements. From political activism to social justice, awards shows have become a powerful tool for celebrities to voice their opinions and shed light on important issues.

The evolution of awards shows can be traced back to the late 1960s and early 1970s when the civil rights movement and the feminist movement were at their peak. During this time, artists and activists started using awards shows as a platform to raise awareness about social injustices and advocate for change. It was a time when the concept of ‘art for art’s sake’ was challenged, and artists realized the power they held in shaping public opinion.

One of the most iconic moments in awards show history occurred in 1973 at the Academy Awards. Marlon Brando, who won the Best Actor award for his role in ‘The Godfather,’ refused to accept the award and instead sent Native American activist Sacheen Littlefeather to deliver a speech on his behalf. Brando’s gesture was a powerful statement against the mistreatment of Native Americans in Hollywood and drew attention to the lack of diversity in the industry.

Fast forward to the present day, and awards shows have become even more politically charged. The #MeToo movement, which gained momentum in 2017, sparked a wave of activism in the entertainment industry. The 2018 Golden Globe Awards saw celebrities wearing black in solidarity with victims of sexual harassment and assault. The red carpet became a platform for actresses to share their stories and demand change in the industry.

Another notable example is the 2019 Oscars, where Spike Lee won his first competitive Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay for ‘BlacKkKlansman.’ Lee used his acceptance speech to highlight the importance of voting in the upcoming presidential election and urged viewers to make a difference.

This shift from honoring excellence to making statements has not been without controversy. Critics argue that awards shows have become too politicized and that the focus should be on celebrating artistic achievement rather than promoting personal agendas. However, supporters argue that artists have a responsibility to use their platform to bring attention to important issues and effect change.
