10 DIY Crafts for a Scandinavian-Inspired Home


Scandinavian-inspired decor has gained tremendous popularity over the years for its minimalism, functionality, and nature-centric aesthetics. This design style focuses on simplicity and a soothing color palette, making it perfect for creating a peaceful and inviting atmosphere within your home. If you’re looking to incorporate this timeless trend into your space, why not indulge in some DIY crafts? Not only will these projects add a personal touch to your Scandinavian-inspired home, but they’ll also be a great way to unleash your creativity. In this blog post, we’ll explore 10 DIY crafts that are simple, affordable, and will elevate your Scandinavian decor game!

1. Wooden Picture Frames

One of the key elements of Scandinavian design is the use of natural materials, particularly wood. Give your family photos and artwork a Scandinavian twist by creating your own wooden picture frames. All you’ll need are some wooden boards, a saw, sandpaper, and wood glue. Measure and cut the boards to your desired size, sand them down to achieve a smooth finish, and then glue the pieces together to form a frame. You can leave the wood in its natural state or apply a light coat of white paint for a more minimalist look.

2. Macrame Plant Hangers

Bringing nature indoors is a fundamental aspect of Scandinavian design. Incorporate greenery into your Scandinavian-inspired home by making macrame plant hangers. Macrame is a technique that involves knotting cords to create intricate patterns. You can find numerous tutorials online that guide you through the process of making macrame plant hangers. Hang these hangers near a window or from a ceiling hook, and fill them with various houseplants to add a touch of freshness to your space.

3. Knitted Blankets

Stay cozy and warm during the colder months by making your own knitted blankets. Knitted textures and chunky knit blankets are iconic elements of Scandinavian design. Choose a neutral-colored yarn such as cream, gray, or beige, and follow a simple knitting pattern. Even if you’re a beginner, there are plenty of beginner-friendly patterns available online. Drape these blankets over your sofa or layer them on your bed for an instant Scandinavian-inspired look.

4. Painted Wooden Bead Garland

Wooden bead garlands are a versatile decor piece that can be used in various ways. Create your own Scandinavian-inspired wooden bead garland by getting a string of wooden beads and painting them in soft pastel hues, such as pale pink, mint green, and baby blue. String them together, leaving spaces between the beads, and you’ll have a beautiful garland that can be hung on walls, draped over mirrors, or used as a decorative touch on shelves or mantels.

5. Concrete Candle Holders

Concrete accents add an industrial edge to the clean and minimalist aesthetics of Scandinavian design. Create your own concrete candle holders by mixing concrete mix with water and pouring it into molds. You can use any molds you have on hand, such as plastic containers or even old yogurt pots. Insert a tea light candle into the mold while the concrete is still wet, and let it dry overnight. Once dry, remove the mold, and you’ll have unique and stylish candle holders that perfectly complement your Scandinavian-inspired home.

6. Woven Wall Hangings

Bring texture and warmth to your walls with woven wall hangings. These simple yet beautiful decorations are easy to make and can be customized to fit your style and color palette. Purchase a wooden or metal weaving loom, some yarn in various shades, and get creative with different weaving techniques. Hang these woven wall hangings on a blank wall or above a bed or sofa to instantly transform your space into a cozy Scandinavian haven.

7. Minimalist Wall Art

Scandinavian design embraces simplicity, and that extends to wall art as well. Create your own minimalist wall art by framing botanical prints, black and white photographs, or abstract art. Keep the frames simple and sleek, using materials like light wood or metal. Arrange these pieces on a gallery wall or stagger them throughout your space to add a touch of elegance and personality.

8. DIY Clay Plant Pots

Add a personal touch to your Scandinavian-inspired home by making your own clay plant pots. Air-dry clay is an easy-to-use and affordable material for this project. Roll out the clay to your desired thickness and shape, and then press textured fabrics or stamps onto the surface to add intricate designs. Allow the clay to dry completely, and then paint it in soft pastels or leave it in its natural state. These handmade clay plant pots will add a unique touch to your space while showcasing your creativity.

9. Scandinavian-Inspired Cushion Covers

Cushions are key elements in Scandinavian design, as they provide comfort and warmth to spaces. Transform plain cushion covers into Scandinavian-inspired masterpieces by using stencils, fabric paint, or even textile markers. Opt for geometric patterns, nature-inspired motifs, or simple stripes in neutral colors. These unique cushion covers will instantly update your sofa or chairs, and tie the entire Scandinavian look together.

10. Wooden Wall Shelves

Maximize your storage and display space by creating your own wooden wall shelves. Scandinavian design is all about functionality and clean lines, and these DIY shelves offer both. Measure and cut wooden boards to your desired shelf length, and then attach them to the wall using brackets. You can leave the wood in its natural state, paint it in a white or pastel shade, or even stain it for a more rustic look. Use these shelves to display your favorite books, decor pieces, or plants, keeping with the minimalistic Scandinavian theme.

Conclusion

Incorporating Scandinavian-inspired decor into your home doesn’t have to break the bank. With these 10 DIY crafts, you can easily add a touch of Scandinavian elegance and creativity to your space. From handmade wooden picture frames to macrame plant hangers, each project offers an opportunity to infuse your personal style while staying true to the principles of Scandinavian design. So, grab your toolset, unleash your inner DIY enthusiast, and watch your Scandinavian-inspired home come to life!
