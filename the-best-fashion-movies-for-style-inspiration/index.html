The Best Fashion Movies for Style Inspiration


Fashion is not just about the clothes we wear. It is a form of self-expression, a way to communicate our personality and sense of style to the world. And what better way to find inspiration for our own fashion choices than through movies? From iconic costumes to stylish characters, fashion movies have long been a source of inspiration for fashion enthusiasts and trendsetters alike. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best fashion movies of all time, and how they can inspire your own style.

1. ‘Breakfast at Tiffany’s’ (1961):
Audrey Hepburn’s portrayal of Holly Golightly in this classic film is a fashion enthusiast’s dream. From her little black dress to her oversized sunglasses, her effortlessly chic style continues to inspire fashion trends even today. The movie perfectly captures the elegance and timeless appeal of Audrey Hepburn’s style, making it a must-watch for anyone looking for sophisticated fashion inspiration.

2. ‘The Devil Wears Prada’ (2006):
This comedy-drama film is a must-watch for fashion lovers and aspiring fashionistas. Meryl Streep’s portrayal of Miranda Priestly, the powerful editor-in-chief of a fashion magazine, is both captivating and inspiring. The movie showcases the fast-paced and cutthroat world of fashion, and the importance of personal style in the industry. With its stunning outfits and behind-the-scenes look at the fashion world, ‘The Devil Wears Prada’ is a great source of style inspiration.

3. ‘Coco Before Chanel’ (2009):
This biographical drama traces the early life of iconic fashion designer Coco Chanel. The movie provides insights into Coco Chanel’s journey towards creating her signature brand and revolutionizing women’s fashion. From her simple yet elegant designs to her penchant for menswear-inspired fashion, Coco Chanel’s style remains influential to this day. ‘Coco Before Chanel’ is a must-watch for anyone interested in the history of fashion and the story behind one of its most influential figures.

4. ‘Clueless’ (1995):
‘Clueless’ is a cult classic that defined ’90s fashion. The movie follows Cher Horowitz, a popular high school student with a penchant for fashion. With its plaid skirts, knee-high socks, and matching sets, ‘Clueless’ has left an indelible mark on the fashion world. The movie’s playful and colorful outfits continue to inspire modern-day fashion trends, making it a must-watch for anyone looking for fun and youthful style inspiration.

5. ‘Sex and the City’ (2008):
Based on the popular TV series, this movie brings back the beloved characters of Carrie Bradshaw, Samantha, Charlotte, and Miranda. Known for its extravagant fashion, ‘Sex and the City’ offers a treasure trove of style inspiration. From Carrie’s iconic tutu skirt to Samantha’s bold and daring choices, the movie showcases a wide range of fashion styles that cater to different personalities. Whether you’re into boho chic or sleek and modern looks, ‘Sex and the City’ has something for everyone.

6. ‘The Great Gatsby’ (2013):
This cinematic adaptation of F. Scott Fitzgerald’s iconic novel is a visual feast for fashion enthusiasts. Set in the 1920s, ‘The Great Gatsby’ showcases the glamorous and opulent fashion of the Jazz Age. With its glittering dresses, Art Deco-inspired accessories, and dapper suits, the movie provides a glimpse into the extravagant fashion of the era. Watching ‘The Great Gatsby’ is sure to inspire you to add a touch of vintage glamour to your own wardrobe.

7. ‘Funny Face’ (1957):
Starring Audrey Hepburn and Fred Astaire, ‘Funny Face’ is a romantic musical comedy that offers a delightful blend of fashion and music. Audrey Hepburn’s character, Jo Stockton, goes from a bookstore employee to a high fashion model, showcasing a range of breathtaking outfits along the way. The movie’s iconic fashion shoot scenes and elegant Parisian backdrop make it a must-watch for anyone seeking style inspiration.

8. ‘A Single Man’ (2009):
Directed by Tom Ford, this film is a visual masterpiece in terms of both cinematography and fashion. Set in the 1960s, ‘A Single Man’ follows the story of George Falconer, a gay college professor grappling with loss. The movie features impeccably tailored suits, classic silhouettes, and a muted color palette that exudes elegance and sophistication. Tom Ford’s signature style permeates every frame, making ‘A Single Man’ a must-watch for anyone looking for refined and timeless style inspiration.

In conclusion, fashion movies can be a great source of style inspiration. Whether you’re looking for classic elegance, ’90s nostalgia, or a glimpse into the glamorous world of fashion, these movies offer a wealth of fashion inspiration. From Audrey Hepburn’s iconic style in ‘Breakfast at Tiffany’s’ to the vibrant outfits of ‘Clueless,’ there is something for everyone. So grab some popcorn, sit back, and let these movies inspire your own fashion choices.
