Tips for Selling Your Home with Pets


Selling a home can be a stressful process, and it becomes even more challenging when you have pets. As a pet owner, you may face additional obstacles and considerations when trying to sell your home. However, with a little planning and preparation, you can ensure a smooth and successful selling process. In this blog post, we will discuss some useful tips that will help you sell your home while keeping your pets happy and stress-free.

1. Declutter and Clean

Before listing your home, it’s essential to declutter and clean every room thoroughly. This is especially important if you have pets, as they can often leave behind evidence of their presence, such as fur, odors, and scratched surfaces. Potential buyers may be turned off by these signs, so make sure to remove any pet toys, litter boxes, and visible pet hair or stains.

2. Repair any pet-related damage

Pets are part of our families, but they can sometimes cause damage to our homes. Take the time to identify and repair any pet-related damage to your property. This could include scratched doors or furniture, chewed baseboards, or stained carpets. Addressing these issues before putting your home on the market will help present a clean and well-maintained space to potential buyers.

3. Neutralize pet odors

Pets can bring the joy, but they can also leave behind unwanted odors. Make sure you tackle any pet-related odors by thoroughly cleaning carpets, curtains, and furniture. Consider using pet-friendly odor eliminators or air fresheners before showings or open houses. Additionally, ensure that you clean litter boxes and pet cages regularly to prevent any off-putting smells.

4. Displaying Pet-Friendly Features

Highlighting pet-friendly features can attract potential buyers who have pets themselves. For example, showcase any built-in pet doors or dedicated pet areas, such as a mudroom or a fenced backyard. These features can differentiate your home from other properties and make it more appealing to pet owners. Consider adding photos or mentioning these features in your listing description.

5. Staging for Success

When staging your home, keep in mind the needs of both potential buyers and your pets. Stage your home to create a warm and inviting atmosphere while ensuring a safe and comfortable space for your furry friends. Avoid displaying valuable items that could be knocked over or damaged by pets. Additionally, consider temporarily removing or replacing any delicate or highly scratchable furniture.

6. Temporary Relocation during Showings

Having pets around during showings can be distracting and may not be ideal for potential buyers. Consider temporarily relocating your pets during showings or open houses. This could mean boarding them at a local kennel, leaving them with a trusted friend or family member, or simply confining them to a secure and quiet area of the house. By doing this, you can create a peaceful and distraction-free environment for potential buyers.

7. Update Your Pet Information

Make sure to update your pet’s information with your real estate agent and include any relevant details in your property listing. Potential buyers with allergies or specific pet preferences will appreciate this transparency. Mentioning the number and type of pets you have, as well as any restrictions or regulations in your neighborhood regarding pets, can help avoid any surprises or conflicts later on.

8. Plan for Showings and Open Houses

When scheduling showings or open houses, make sure to plan ahead for your pets. Consider their routine and needs, such as feeding, potty breaks, and exercise. If possible, try to schedule such events when your pets are typically calm or relaxed, such as during nap times. This will minimize any stress or disruption to their daily routines.

9. Make Necessary Repairs

During the home selling process, you may come across issues related to your pets, such as damaged landscaping or fencing. Make sure to address these issues promptly and make any necessary repairs. A well-maintained property will inspire confidence in potential buyers, and they will appreciate your attention to detail.

10. Disclose Pet-Related Information

Last but not least, it’s crucial to disclose any pet-related information to potential buyers. This includes detailing any past incidents, damages, or repairs caused by your pets. Being upfront and transparent will build trust between you and potential buyers and prevent any misunderstandings or issues during negotiations or after closing.

In conclusion, selling a home with pets doesn’t have to be a daunting task. By following these tips, you can ensure that your pet-friendly home appeals to a wide range of buyers while keeping your pets safe and comfortable during the selling process. Remember, a well-prepared and well-maintained home will increase your chances of selling quickly and at a favorable price. Good luck with your home sale!
