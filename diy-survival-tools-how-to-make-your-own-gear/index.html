DIY Survival Tools: How to Make Your Own Gear


When it comes to survival situations, being prepared can make all the difference. One of the best ways to ensure you’re ready for anything life throws at you is to create your own survival tools. Not only is making your gear a rewarding and educational experience, but it also allows you to customize your tools according to your specific needs and preferences. This comprehensive guide will take you through various DIY survival tools you can create, the materials you’ll need, and step-by-step instructions to help you craft your own reliable gear.

Why Make Your Own Survival Tools?

Creating your own survival tools has several advantages:

  1. Customization: You can tailor your tools to suit your specific circumstances, preferences, and environment.
  2. Cost-effective: DIY projects can often be done on a budget, using materials you already have or can acquire inexpensively.
  3. Skill development: Crafting your own gear helps develop practical skills, enhancing your overall preparedness for survival situations.
  4. Satisfaction: There’s a certain pride that comes with knowing you created something with your own hands that could potentially save your life or help in an emergency.

Essential DIY Survival Tools

Before diving into the specifics of making your own gear, let’s take a look at some essential survival tools you can create.

1. Survival Knife

A reliable knife is one of the most important tools you can have in a survival situation. While you can purchase a high-quality knife, making your own can be an excellent exercise in resourcefulness.

Materials Needed:

  • A piece of high-carbon steel (old files or saw blades work well)
  • A sturdy handle (wood, paracord, or micarta)
  • Sandpaper
  • A file or grinder
  • Drill and drill bits
  • Epoxy or strong adhesive

Instructions:

  1. Shape the Blade: Using a file or grinder, start shaping your blade. A drop point or tanto style is ideal for versatility. Be sure to leave enough metal for the tang (the part of the blade that extends into the handle).
  2. Harden the Steel: Heat your blade with a propane torch until it’s glowing red, then quench it in oil. This hardens the steel, making it durable.
  3. Polish the Blade: Use sandpaper to smooth the edges and polish the blade to prevent rust.
  4. Create the Handle: Cut your handle material to size and shape it to fit your grip comfortably. Drill holes for the tang and attach the handle using epoxy or adhesive.
  5. Final Touches: Once the adhesive has cured, give the knife one last polish and ensure the blade is sharp.

2. Fire Starter

Fire is crucial for warmth, cooking, and signaling for help in survival scenarios. A DIY fire starter can be easily made using common materials.

Materials Needed:

  • A small tin can
  • Cotton balls or dryer lint
  • Petroleum jelly
  • A lighter or matches
  • Steel wool (optional for added fire-starting capabilities)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Cotton Balls: Soak cotton balls in petroleum jelly until saturated. This will help them burn longer.
  2. Pack the Tin Can: Place the soaked cotton balls into the tin can, packing them tightly.
  3. Seal and Store: Seal the tin can with a lid to keep it waterproof. Store it in your survival kit for easy access.
  4. Using Steel Wool: For an alternative method, you can use steel wool with a 9-volt battery to create sparks, which can ignite your fire starter.

3. Paracord Survival Bracelet

A paracord bracelet is not only a fashion statement but also a practical tool that can be unraveled and used in various survival situations.

Materials Needed:

  • Paracord (550 paracord is recommended)
  • A buckle
  • Scissors
  • Lighter

Instructions:

  1. Measure and Cut: Measure your wrist and cut a length of paracord about 10-12 feet long.
  2. Attach the Buckle: Thread one end of the paracord through one side of the buckle, and fold it back on itself, securing with a knot.
  3. Weave the Bracelet: Use the remaining length of paracord to create a cobra weave around the buckle. Continue until you reach the desired width.
  4. Finish Off: Once complete, thread the other end through the second buckle side, and secure with a knot. Use a lighter to melt the ends of the paracord to prevent fraying.

4. Fishing Kit

A fishing kit can be a lifesaver in a survival scenario, providing a reliable food source. You can easily assemble one using everyday items.

Materials Needed:

  • A small container (like a film canister or pill bottle)
  • Fishing line
  • Hooks (can be made from bent metal or purchased)
  • Sinkers (small rocks or metal)
  • Bobbers (can be made from cork or plastic)
  • Bait (dried food, or you can catch worms)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Container: Place the fishing line inside the container, leaving enough length to spool out when needed.
  2. Attach Hooks: Tie hooks to the line, spacing them out appropriately. You can bend metal into a hook shape as an alternative.
  3. Add Sinkers and Bobbers: Include sinkers (small rocks or metal) and bobbers (cork pieces) to your kit.
  4. Store Bait: If possible, store bait in a separate section of the container to keep it fresh.

5. Water Filtration System

Access to clean drinking water is essential for survival. A DIY water filtration system can help make contaminated water safe to drink.

Materials Needed:

  • A plastic bottle
  • Sand
  • Charcoal (activated charcoal is best)
  • Gravel
  • Coffee filter or cloth

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Bottle: Cut the bottom off a plastic bottle to create a funnel shape.
  2. Layer the Filtration Materials: Start with a coffee filter or cloth at the neck of the bottle. Next, add a layer of sand, followed by a layer of charcoal, and finally, a layer of gravel.
  3. Collect Water: Pour contaminated water into the top of the bottle, allowing it to filter through the layers. Collect the filtered water from the bottom.

6. Survival Shelter

Creating a shelter is an essential skill in survival situations. You can make an effective temporary shelter using natural materials found in the environment.

Materials Needed:

  • Branches and sticks
  • Leaves, grass, or pine needles
  • Rope or paracord (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Select a Location: Choose a flat, dry area away from hazards like falling branches or flooding.
  2. Build a Frame: Use sturdy branches to create a frame in a triangular or lean-to shape. Secure with rope or paracord if available.
  3. Add Insulation: Layer leaves, grass, or pine needles over the frame to provide insulation and protection from the elements.
  4. Create an Entrance: Leave one side open for entry and cover it when you enter for warmth.

Final Thoughts

In a survival situation, your creativity and resourcefulness can be your best assets. By making your own survival tools, you not only prepare yourself for the unexpected but also gain a deeper understanding of your environment and the skills necessary to thrive in it.

The tools and techniques outlined above are just the beginning. As you gain experience, consider exploring more advanced gear, refining your skills, and sharing your knowledge with others. Remember, the best survival tool you can have is the knowledge and confidence to use what you create effectively. So gather your materials, roll up your sleeves, and get crafting—your survival gear awaits!
