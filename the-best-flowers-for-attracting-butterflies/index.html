The Best Flowers for Attracting Butterflies


Lush gardens adorned with vibrant flowers are a sight to behold. But what if you could take it a step further and attract some delicate visitors to your garden? Yes, we’re talking about butterflies! These beautiful creatures add a touch of enchantment to any outdoor space. If you’re wondering which flowers are the best for attracting butterflies, look no further. In this blog post, we will explore a variety of floral options that will have these winged wonders flocking to your garden.

1. Butterfly Bush (Buddleja davidii): As the name suggests, this shrub is a magnet for butterflies. It produces clusters of nectar-rich blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white. The fragrance of the flowers is irresistible to butterflies, making it a must-have in any butterfly garden.

2. Milkweed (Asclepias): Milkweed is a favorite among monarch butterflies. It serves as a host plant for their caterpillars and provides a reliable source of nectar for adult butterflies. With its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, milkweed is not only a practical choice but also adds a pop of color to your garden.

3. Purple Coneflower (Echinacea purpurea): This perennial flower not only attracts butterflies but also provides a feast for bees and other pollinators. Its daisy-like petals in shades of pink and purple are a visual treat, while its sweet nectar acts as a magnet for butterflies.

4. Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta): With its bright yellow petals and dark centers, the black-eyed Susan is a cheerful addition to any garden. Butterflies are particularly fond of this flower, drawn to its nectar-filled blooms. Planting a patch of black-eyed Susans is a surefire way to invite a flutter of butterflies into your garden.

5. Liatris (Blazing Star): If you’re looking to add some vertical interest to your garden, liatris is a great choice. Its tall, spiky blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white are adored by butterflies. Liatris also serves as a valuable source of nectar late into the summer, ensuring a steady flow of butterfly visitors.

6. Zinnia: Zinnias are a popular choice for butterfly gardens due to their vibrant colors and long blooming period. These annual flowers come in a wide array of hues, from fiery oranges to soft pinks. Their nectar-rich flowers are a feast for butterflies, ensuring they keep coming back for more.

7. Verbena: Verbena is a low-growing perennial that forms sprawling mats of colorful flowers. Its clusters of small blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white are irresistible to butterflies. Planting verbena in your garden will not only attract butterflies but also add a burst of color to your landscape.

Creating a butterfly-friendly garden is a rewarding endeavor that brings joy and beauty to your outdoor space. By incorporating these butterfly-attracting flowers into your garden, you can create a haven for these delicate creatures. Remember to provide a water source, such as a shallow dish with pebbles, to quench their thirst. With a little patience and a touch of nature’s magic, you’ll soon find your garden abuzz with the graceful dance of butterflies.
