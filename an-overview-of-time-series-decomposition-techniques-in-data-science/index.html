An Overview of Time Series Decomposition Techniques in Data Science


Time series data is a common type of data that involves observations taken at different points in time. It is characterized by its sequential nature and the dependence of each observation on previous observations. Time series data can be found in various domains such as finance, economics, weather forecasting, and many others.

In data science, understanding and analyzing time series data is crucial for making accurate predictions and gaining valuable insights. Time series decomposition techniques play a key role in unraveling the underlying components of time series data, providing a better understanding of its structure and behavior.

Time series decomposition involves breaking down a time series into its individual components, typically trend, seasonal, and residual components. By decomposing a time series, analysts can better understand the patterns and variations within the data, which can then be used for forecasting, anomaly detection, and other data-driven tasks.

In this blog post, we will explore some common time series decomposition techniques used in data science. Let’s dive in!

1. Moving Averages

Moving averages are a simple yet effective technique for time series decomposition. It involves computing the average of a window of consecutive observations and using it as the value for the center of the window. This smoothed value helps in identifying the trend component of the time series.

Moving averages can be of various types, such as simple moving average (SMA) and exponential moving average (EMA). SMA computes the arithmetic mean of the observations within the window, whereas EMA assigns more weight to recent observations. Moving averages can be applied to a time series multiple times to smoothen out the data and highlight the underlying trend.

2. Seasonal Decomposition of Time Series (STL)

Seasonal decomposition of time series (STL) is a widely used technique for time series decomposition. It decomposes a time series into its trend, seasonal, and residual components using a robust and flexible approach. STL is robust to outliers and can handle irregular and non-constant seasonal patterns.

The STL decomposition algorithm applies a smoothing technique to estimate a non-parametric and non-stationary trend component. It then estimates the seasonal component by averaging the observations within each seasonal period. Finally, it computes the residual component by subtracting the trend and seasonal components from the original time series. STL is highly customizable, allowing users to specify the window size for the seasonal component and the degree of smoothing for the trend component.

3. Seasonal and Trend decomposition using Loess (STL-LOESS)

STL-LOESS is an extension of the STL decomposition technique in which it uses the Locally Estimated Scatterplot Smoothing (LOESS) algorithm for estimating the trend component. LOESS is a non-parametric regression method that fits a smooth curve through the points of a scatterplot. It adapts to local variations in the data, making it suitable for time series analysis.

By incorporating LOESS into the STL framework, STL-LOESS provides a more robust and flexible decomposition of time series. It can handle complex trend patterns and is less sensitive to the choice of window size for the seasonal component. STL-LOESS is particularly useful when there are abrupt changes and outliers in the time series data.

4. Seasonal and Trend decomposition using Regression (STL-REG)

STL-REG is another variant of the STL decomposition technique that uses regression models for estimating the trend component. Instead of applying a smoothing technique like LOESS, STL-REG employs regression analysis to capture the trend component of the time series.

STL-REG fits a linear or non-linear regression model to the time series data by incorporating the seasonal and residual components as predictors. It estimates the trend component by removing the effects of the seasonal and residual components. STL-REG provides a flexible modeling framework and can accommodate various regression models, such as polynomial regression, exponential regression, or even machine learning models.

Conclusion

Time series decomposition is an essential technique for understanding and analyzing time series data in data science. By decomposing a time series into its constituent components, analysts can gain insights into the underlying trends, seasonal patterns, and residual variations. Moving averages, STL, STL-LOESS, and STL-REG are just a few of the many time series decomposition techniques available.

When working with time series data, it is essential to choose the most appropriate decomposition technique based on the specific characteristics and goals of your analysis. Experimentation and evaluation of different techniques are often necessary to find the best fit for your data.

In conclusion, mastering time series decomposition techniques can empower data scientists to unlock valuable insights and make accurate predictions in various domains. With the growing availability of time series data, the demand for skilled practitioners who can effectively analyze and interpret it will continue to rise.
