The Most Underrated Liquors You’ve Never Tried


Are you ready to embark on an exciting journey into the world of underrated liquors? If you’re a fan of discovering hidden gems and expanding your palate, then you’re in for a treat! In this blog, we’ll be exploring some of the most underrated liquors that you may never have tried before. So, grab a glass, sit back, and join us on this flavorful adventure!

1. Pisco: Originating from Peru and Chile, Pisco is a grape brandy that often flies under the radar. Its versatility makes it a great addition to cocktails, and its smooth, fruity flavors are a delight to the senses. Whether you prefer a classic Pisco Sour or want to explore new mixology creations, Pisco is a must-try.

2. Aquavit: Hailing from Scandinavia, Aquavit is a spirit infused with a variety of herbs and spices like caraway, dill, and fennel. It possesses a unique flavor profile that is perfect for those who enjoy exploring different taste sensations. Served chilled or in cocktails, Aquavit brings a refreshing and earthy twist to the table.

3. Mezcal: While tequila tends to take the spotlight, Mezcal is a smoky, agave-based spirit that deserves recognition. Produced in Mexico, Mezcal offers a wide range of flavors depending on the type of agave used and the production techniques. Its complexity and depth make it an intriguing choice for adventurous drinkers.

4. Cachaça: If you’re a fan of rum, you’ll love Cachaça. This Brazilian spirit is made from sugarcane juice and adds an exotic flair to your cocktails. From the famous Caipirinha to other tropical concoctions, Cachaça infuses a vibrant and lively vibe into every sip.

5. Shochu: Originating from Japan, Shochu is a distilled spirit made from various ingredients such as sweet potatoes, barley, or rice. It offers a unique alternative to sake and has gained popularity for its lower alcohol content and delicate flavor profile. Shochu can be enjoyed neat, on the rocks, or in cocktails, making it a versatile addition to any liquor cabinet.

6. Armagnac: Often overshadowed by its cousin, Cognac, Armagnac is a French brandy that deserves its moment in the spotlight. Produced in the Gascony region, Armagnac boasts rich and robust flavors with hints of dried fruits, spices, and oak. Sip on this exquisite spirit to truly appreciate its depth and complexity.

7. Genever: Considered the predecessor to gin, Genever is a traditional Dutch spirit that offers a unique tasting experience. It possesses a malted grain base with a blend of botanicals, resulting in a smooth and aromatic spirit. Whether enjoyed neat, on the rocks, or in a classic cocktail, Genever is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered.

Now that we’ve explored some of the most underrated liquors, it’s time to expand your drinking horizons and try something new. These spirits may not be as well-known as their popular counterparts, but they certainly bring a whole new level of excitement to your glass.

Remember, when it comes to discovering underrated liquors, it’s all about embracing the adventure and being open to new experiences. So, go ahead, embark on this flavor-filled journey, and let your taste buds be your guide. Cheers to the underrated and the undiscovered!
