Dealing with Back-to-School Anxiety: Parenting Solutions


As summer winds down and the days grow shorter, it’s time to start thinking about getting the kids ready to head back to school. For many families, this transition can be filled with excitement and anticipation. But for others, it can also bring about feelings of anxiety and stress, both for children and their parents.

Back-to-school anxiety is a common issue that many families face. Whether it’s worries about making new friends, academic pressures, or simply the fear of the unknown, these feelings can manifest in a variety of ways and impact the entire household. But fear not, there are plenty of strategies that parents can employ to help ease their children’s anxiety and make the back-to-school transition as smooth as possible.

  1. Start the Conversation Early: One of the best ways to tackle back-to-school anxiety is by addressing it head-on. Start talking to your child about their feelings well before the first day of school. Encourage them to share their worries and concerns, and let them know that it’s okay to feel nervous. By opening up the lines of communication early, you can help your child feel more comfortable expressing their emotions and seeking support when they need it.

  2. Create a Routine: Children thrive on routine, and having a predictable schedule can help alleviate anxiety. Work with your child to establish a daily routine that includes set times for waking up, getting ready for school, homework, and bedtime. Knowing what to expect can provide a sense of stability and security, which can be especially helpful during times of transition.

  3. Practice Relaxation Techniques: Teach your child simple relaxation techniques that they can use to manage their anxiety when it arises. Deep breathing exercises, mindfulness meditation, and progressive muscle relaxation are all effective tools for calming the mind and body. Encourage your child to practice these techniques regularly, both at home and in school, so they become second nature when they’re feeling stressed.

  4. Focus on the Positive: Help your child shift their focus from what could go wrong to what could go right. Encourage them to think about the things they’re looking forward to about the new school year, whether it’s seeing friends, participating in extracurricular activities, or learning new subjects. By reframing their perspective in a positive light, you can help your child approach the new school year with optimism and excitement.

  5. Stay Connected: Stay connected with your child’s teachers and school staff to ensure that you’re aware of any changes or challenges that may arise. Attend parent-teacher meetings, participate in school events, and volunteer in the classroom when possible. By staying involved in your child’s education, you can help them feel supported and empowered to succeed.

  6. Seek Professional Help if Needed: If your child’s anxiety persists or significantly interferes with their daily functioning, don’t hesitate to seek professional help. A mental health professional, such as a therapist or counselor, can work with your child to develop coping strategies and provide additional support as needed.

  7. Take Care of Yourself: Finally, don’t forget to take care of yourself as well. Parenting a child with anxiety can be challenging, and it’s important to prioritize your own well-being. Make time for self-care activities that help you relax and recharge, whether it’s exercising, reading, or spending time with friends. Remember, you can’t pour from an empty cup, so be sure to prioritize your own needs as you support your child through their back-to-school journey.

In conclusion, back-to-school anxiety is a common experience for many children and families, but it doesn’t have to overshadow the excitement of a new school year. By employing these parenting solutions, you can help your child manage their anxiety and approach the new school year with confidence and resilience. Remember to start the conversation early, create a routine, practice relaxation techniques, focus on the positive, stay connected, seek professional help if needed, and most importantly, take care of yourself. With patience, understanding, and support, you and your child can navigate the back-to-school transition together successfully.
