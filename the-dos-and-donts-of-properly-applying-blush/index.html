The Dos and Don'ts of Properly Applying Blush


Blush is a makeup essential that can instantly enhance your complexion and give your face a healthy, radiant glow. However, applying blush can be a tricky task. Many people make common mistakes that can result in a less-than-desirable outcome. To help you achieve a flawless blush application every time, we have compiled a list of dos and don’ts that you should keep in mind. Read on to discover the secrets to properly applying blush.

DO: Choose the Right Shade
One of the most important aspects of applying blush is selecting the right shade for your skin tone. The goal is to find a color that mimics a natural flush. For fair skin, go for light pinks or peaches. Medium skin tones can opt for rose or coral shades, while deeper skin tones can rock deeper pinks or berries. Remember, the key is to enhance your complexion, not overpower it.

DO: Prep Your Skin
Before applying blush, it’s essential to prep your skin properly. Start by cleansing and moisturizing your face to create a smooth canvas. Apply a primer to help your blush last longer. If you have oily skin, consider using a mattifying primer to control shine. Additionally, make sure your foundation is well-blended and set with powder before applying blush.

DO: Use the Right Tools
Investing in the right tools can make a world of difference in your blush application. Opt for a fluffy, dome-shaped brush that allows for precise and even distribution of color. Avoid using small or dense brushes as they can deposit too much product, resulting in a heavy-handed look. Remember, blending is key to achieving a natural and seamless finish.

DO: Apply Blush to the Apples of Your Cheeks
The apples of your cheeks are the roundest part when you smile. This is where blush should be applied to create a youthful and fresh look. Smile in the mirror and use a light hand to apply blush to the center of your cheeks, blending it gently towards your temples. Avoid applying blush too close to your nose or too low on your face, as it can make you look tired or older.

DO: Build Up the Color Gradually
It’s always better to start with a light application and build up the color gradually. Tap off any excess product before applying blush to avoid depositing too much pigment at once. Remember, it’s easier to add more blush than to take it away, so start with a light touch and build up to your desired intensity.

DON’T: Forget to Blend
Blending is the key to achieving a natural-looking blush. After applying blush, take a clean brush and blend out any harsh lines or edges. This will ensure a seamless transition between your blush and the rest of your makeup. Avoid leaving any visible streaks or patches of color on your cheeks.

DON’T: Apply Blush Too Far Below Your Cheekbones
While contouring has become a popular makeup technique, applying blush too far below your cheekbones can create an unflattering effect. Blush is meant to add color and life to your face, not to mimic the shadows created by contouring. Stick to applying blush on the apples of your cheeks for a youthful and radiant look.

DON’T: Overdo It
Less is more when it comes to blush. Applying too much blush can make you look clownish or overly flushed. Remember, the goal is to enhance your natural complexion, not to create an unnatural, rosy appearance. Start with a light hand and build up the color gradually until you achieve your desired look.

DON’T: Neglect Your Skin Type
Consider your skin type when selecting the right blush formula. If you have oily skin, opt for a powder blush that can absorb excess oil and provide a matte finish. Dry skin types can benefit from cream or liquid blush formulas that add a dewy and hydrated look. Don’t forget to set your blush with a light dusting of translucent powder to make it last longer.

In conclusion, properly applying blush can make a significant difference in your overall makeup look. By following these dos and don’ts, you’ll be able to achieve a natural and flattering blush application every time. Remember to choose the right shade, prep your skin, use the right tools, apply blush to the apples of your cheeks, and blend for a seamless finish. Avoid applying blush too far below your cheekbones, overdoing it, and neglecting your skin type. With these tips in mind, you’ll be able to rock a healthy and radiant blush that enhances your natural beauty.
