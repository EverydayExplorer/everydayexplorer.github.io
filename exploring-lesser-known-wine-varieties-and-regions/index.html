Exploring Lesser-Known Wine Varieties and Regions


When it comes to wine, most people are familiar with the popular varieties like Chardonnay, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Merlot. These wines have gained widespread recognition and are produced in many regions around the world. However, there is a whole world of lesser-known wine varieties and regions waiting to be explored. In this blog post, we will take a closer look at some of these hidden gems and why they are worth discovering.

1. Assyrtiko - Greece:
Assyrtiko is a white grape variety that originates from the island of Santorini in Greece. Known for its vibrant acidity and mineral-driven flavors, Assyrtiko produces crisp and refreshing wines. These wines often exhibit citrus and stone fruit notes, making them a perfect choice for seafood dishes.

2. Aglianico - Italy:
Aglianico is a red grape variety that is native to southern Italy. This bold and robust wine is often compared to Barolo and Barbaresco from the Piedmont region. Aglianico wines are known for their intense dark fruit flavors, high tannins, and a hint of smokiness. They pair well with hearty dishes like roasted meats and stews.

3. Xinomavro - Greece:
Xinomavro is a red grape variety that is widely grown in the northern regions of Greece. It produces wines with complex flavors of red fruits, dried herbs, and earthy undertones. Xinomavro wines are often compared to Nebbiolo from Italy due to their similar characteristics. These wines can age beautifully and are a great choice for wine enthusiasts looking for something unique.

4. Mencia - Spain:
Mencia is a red grape variety that is mostly found in the northwestern part of Spain, particularly in the Bierzo region. Mencia wines are medium-bodied with flavors of red berries, floral notes, and a touch of spice. They offer a great balance between fruitiness and earthiness, making them versatile when it comes to food pairing.

5. Rkatsiteli - Georgia:
Rkatsiteli is an ancient white grape variety that is native to Georgia. This versatile grape produces wines with a range of styles, from dry to sweet and even orange wines. Rkatsiteli wines are known for their vibrant acidity, flavors of tropical fruits, and a distinct floral character. They pair well with a variety of dishes, from grilled seafood to spicy Asian cuisine.

6. Frappato - Italy:
Frappato is a red grape variety that is primarily grown in Sicily, Italy. This light-bodied wine is known for its bright red fruit flavors, floral aromas, and a hint of spice. Frappato wines are incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed on their own or paired with a wide range of dishes, from pasta to grilled vegetables.

7. Furmint - Hungary:
Furmint is a white grape variety that is mostly grown in Hungary, particularly in the Tokaj region. This grape is famous for producing sweet wines, including the renowned Tokaji Aszú. Furmint wines offer flavors of honey, apricot, and orange peel, with a refreshing acidity that balances the sweetness. They are a perfect choice for dessert or as an aperitif.

These are just a few examples of the many lesser-known wine varieties and regions waiting to be discovered. Exploring these wines can be an exciting journey, allowing you to expand your palate and experience new flavors. So, the next time you’re in search of a unique wine experience, don’t hesitate to venture off the beaten path and try one of these hidden gems. Cheers to the exploration of lesser-known wine varieties and regions!
