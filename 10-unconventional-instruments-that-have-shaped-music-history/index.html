10 Unconventional Instruments That Have Shaped Music History


Music has a rich and diverse history, with countless instruments playing a vital role in shaping the sounds we know and love today. While most people are familiar with conventional instruments like the guitar, piano, or drums, there exists a fascinating world of unconventional instruments that have made their mark on music history. In this blog post, we will explore ten of these unique and often overlooked instruments that have left an indelible impact on the musical landscape.

1. Theremin: Invented in the early 20th century by Leon Theremin, this electronic instrument is played without physical contact. Using two antennas, the player controls pitch and volume by moving their hands around them. The eerie and otherworldly sound of the theremin has been featured in various genres, from classical to sci-fi movie soundtracks.

2. Ondes Martenot: Developed in the 1920s by Maurice Martenot, this early electronic instrument produces ethereal, wavering tones. With its keyboard and a ribbon controller, the ondes Martenot has been used in classical compositions and film scores, adding a haunting and mysterious quality to the music.

3. Glass Harmonica: Invented by Benjamin Franklin in 1761, this instrument consists of glass bowls of varying sizes, mounted horizontally on a spindle. By rubbing the rims of the glasses with wet fingers, a musician can produce hauntingly beautiful melodies. Composers like Mozart and Beethoven were fascinated by the glass harmonica’s unique sound and incorporated it into their compositions.

4. Hang Drum: Created in the year 2000 by Felix Rohner and Sabina Scharer, the Hang Drum is a percussive instrument that resembles a UFO. With its mesmerizing and meditative sound, the Hang Drum has gained popularity in the world of new-age and ambient music.

5. Octobass: Standing at a towering 3.5 meters tall, the Octobass is the largest bowed string instrument ever constructed. Invented in the 19th century, this colossal instrument produces incredibly low, rumbling tones that add a unique depth to orchestral compositions.

6. Musical Saw: Despite its humble origins as a carpentry tool, the musical saw has been embraced by musicians around the world. By bending and vibrating a saw blade, a musician can create haunting and melancholic melodies. The musical saw has found its way into various genres, including folk, blues, and even rock.

7. Conch Shell: Native to coastal regions, the conch shell has been used as a musical instrument for thousands of years. By blowing into the conch’s opening, a musician can produce resonant and powerful tones. The conch shell’s distinctive sound has been featured in traditional music from cultures across the globe.

8. Waterphone: Invented by Richard Waters in the late 1960s, the waterphone is a unique percussion instrument that creates haunting and ethereal sounds. By bowing its rods and resonators and adding water, the musician can produce a wide range of eerie tones, making it a favorite in horror movie soundtracks.

9. Musical Tesla Coil: Combining science and music, the musical Tesla coil produces electrically generated tones by creating high-voltage electrical discharges. With its crackling and buzzing sounds, this instrument adds a futuristic and electrifying element to experimental music.

10. Hurdy-Gurdy: Dating back to the medieval period, the hurdy-gurdy is a string instrument played by cranking a wheel that rubs against the strings, producing a droning sound. This unique instrument has been used in folk music across Europe for centuries, adding a distinctive and rustic quality to traditional tunes.

These ten unconventional instruments have not only shaped music history but have also expanded the boundaries of what we consider musical expression. From the eerie tones of the theremin to the haunting melodies of the glass harmonica, these instruments have left an indelible mark on the world of music. Exploring their unique sounds can open up a whole new world of sonic possibilities and inspire musicians to think outside the box. So, the next time you listen to your favorite song, take a moment to appreciate the unconventional instruments that have played a part in shaping the music we know and love today.
