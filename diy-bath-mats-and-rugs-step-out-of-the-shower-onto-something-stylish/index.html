DIY Bath Mats and Rugs: Step Out of the Shower onto Something Stylish


When it comes to creating a tranquil and stylish bathroom, one often overlooked accessory is the bath mat or rug. Not only do these functional pieces serve the purpose of keeping your feet warm and dry as you step out of the shower, but they can also add a touch of personality and style to your bathroom decor. Instead of settling for generic options from the store, why not try your hand at creating your own DIY bath mats and rugs? In this blog post, we will share a variety of ideas and step-by-step instructions to help you create stunning bath mats and rugs that are truly one-of-a-kind.

Materials You Will Need

Before diving into the different DIY bath mat and rug ideas, let’s start by listing the materials you will need:

  1. Base material (fabric, rope, or memory foam)
  2. Non-slip rug pad (optional)
  3. Scissors
  4. Measuring tape
  5. Hot glue gun or sewing machine (depending on the project)
  6. Fabric dye or paint (optional, for adding patterns or colors)
  7. Embellishments (buttons, beads, or appliques)

Once you have gathered all the materials, it’s time to move on to the fun part - creating your DIY bath mats and rugs!

1. Braided Fabric Bath Mat

For a cozy and rustic look, consider making a braided fabric bath mat. This project is great for repurposing old t-shirts or other fabric scraps. Here’s how you can do it:

  1. Cut the fabric into long strips, around 2 inches wide.
  2. Knot three strips together at one end.
  3. Start braiding the strips, adding new strips as needed to maintain the desired length.
  4. Continue braiding until you reach the desired size for your bath mat.
  5. Trim any excess fabric and secure the ends with a knot or by sewing them together.
  6. Optionally, attach a non-slip rug pad to the bottom of the mat using a hot glue gun or stitching it in place for added stability.

2. Rope Bath Rug

If you prefer a more minimalist and contemporary look, a rope bath rug may be just what you need. This project requires a thick and durable rope, such as nylon or cotton. Here’s how to create it:

  1. Coil the rope into a spiral shape, starting from the center.
  2. Secure each coil with hot glue or by stitching it in place.
  3. Continue coiling the rope, making sure to keep the same shape and size.
  4. Once you have reached the desired size, trim any excess rope and secure the end.
  5. If desired, attach a non-slip rug pad to the bottom for added safety.

3. Memory Foam Bath Mat

For those who prioritize comfort, a memory foam bath mat is an excellent choice. This project requires a memory foam mattress topper or cushion as the base material. Here’s how to make it:

  1. Measure and cut the memory foam to the desired size and shape of your bath mat.
  2. Using a non-slip rug pad or fabric, cut a piece slightly larger than the memory foam.
  3. Place the memory foam on top of the fabric.
  4. Fold the edges of the fabric over the memory foam and secure them using a hot glue gun or by sewing them together.
  5. Trim any excess fabric. Optionally, you can dye the fabric or add patterns using fabric paint.
  6. Attach a non-slip rug pad to the bottom of the mat for added stability.

4. Fabric Painted Bath Rug

If you want to add a personal touch to a store-bought bath mat, fabric painting is a great option. Here’s how you can transform a plain bath mat with some fabric paint:

  1. Choose a plain, light-colored bath mat made of a fabric suitable for painting (cotton or microfiber work well).
  2. Select your desired design or pattern for the bath mat.
  3. Place a piece of cardboard or plastic under the area you will be painting to prevent bleed-through.
  4. Use fabric paint or fabric markers to apply the design onto the bath mat.
  5. Allow the paint to dry completely before using the mat.

5. Embellished Bath Mat

For a touch of luxury and elegance, consider embellishing a bath mat with buttons, beads, or appliques. Here’s how to create an embellished bath mat:

  1. Start with a plain bath mat, preferably in a neutral color.
  2. Choose your desired embellishments, such as buttons, beads, or appliques.
  3. Arrange the embellishments on the bath mat to create a design that you like.
  4. Use a hot glue gun or needle and thread to attach the embellishments to the bath mat securely.
  5. Allow the glue to dry completely before using the mat.

Conclusion

Creating your own DIY bath mats and rugs allows you to add a personal touch to your bathroom while also showcasing your creativity. Whether you prefer a cozy braided fabric mat, a minimalist rope rug, a comfortable memory foam bath mat, a fabric painted mat, or an embellished design, there is a DIY option for every style and preference. So, why settle for generic when you can step out of the shower onto something stylish and uniquely yours?
