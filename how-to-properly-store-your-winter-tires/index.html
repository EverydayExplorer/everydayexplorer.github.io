How to Properly Store Your Winter Tires


Winter is upon us, and for many of us, that means it’s time to switch out our regular tires for winter tires. Winter tires are specifically designed to provide better traction on snowy and icy roads, making them essential for safe winter driving. However, it’s important to store your winter tires properly when they’re not in use to ensure their longevity and performance. In this blog post, we’ll discuss some tips on how to properly store your winter tires.

1. Clean and Inspect: Before storing your winter tires, it’s crucial to clean them thoroughly. Remove any dirt, debris, or salt from the tires using a mild soap and water solution. Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners as they can damage the rubber. Once the tires are clean, inspect them for any signs of damage or wear. If you notice any issues, such as cuts or bulges, it may be time to replace the tires.

2. Choose a Suitable Storage Location: When it comes to storing your winter tires, finding the right location is key. Ideally, you should store them in a cool, dry, and dark place. Extreme temperatures, direct sunlight, and moisture can all have a negative impact on the tires’ condition. Avoid storing them near sources of heat or chemicals as well.

3. Tire Storage Bags: Investing in tire storage bags can help protect your winter tires from dust, moisture, and other elements. These bags are specifically designed to keep the tires in good condition during storage. Make sure to label each bag with the position of the tire (front left, front right, rear left, rear right) to ensure they’re properly installed when it’s time to put them back on your vehicle.

4. Tire Stacking: If you’re storing multiple sets of winter tires, it’s important to stack them properly to prevent any damage. Ideally, you should stack them vertically, one on top of the other. Avoid stacking them too high, as this can cause the lower tires to deform under the weight. If space is limited, consider investing in a tire rack to keep them organized and prevent any unnecessary pressure on the tires.

5. Tire Pressure: Before storing your winter tires, make sure to check and adjust the tire pressure to the manufacturer’s recommended levels. Proper tire pressure helps maintain their shape and prevents any flat spots from forming during storage. Remember to check the tire pressure again before reinstalling them on your vehicle.

6. Regularly Rotate: If you’re storing your winter tires for an extended period, it’s a good idea to rotate them every few months. This helps prevent uneven wear and ensures that all tires are exposed to similar conditions.

By following these tips, you can ensure that your winter tires stay in great shape during their period of storage, ready to provide you with the best performance when the colder months arrive. Properly storing your winter tires not only extends their lifespan but also ensures your safety on the roads. So, take the time to give your winter tires the care they deserve, and they’ll reward you with excellent performance season after season. Stay safe and enjoy the winter driving experience!
