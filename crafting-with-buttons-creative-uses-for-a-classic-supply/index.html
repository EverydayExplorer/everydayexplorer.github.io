Crafting with Buttons: Creative Uses for a Classic Supply


Buttons may seem like a small and insignificant craft supply, but they hold immense potential for creativity and artistic expression. From adding a pop of color to clothing to creating stunning button art, there are countless ways to incorporate buttons into your crafting projects. In this blog post, we will explore some unique and innovative uses for buttons that will inspire you to think outside the box and take your crafting skills to the next level.

1. Button Jewelry: Who says buttons are only meant for clothing? Turn them into beautiful pieces of jewelry by stringing them together to create necklaces, bracelets, or even earrings. Mix and match different sizes, colors, and shapes to design one-of-a-kind accessories that are sure to make a statement.

2. Button Art: Unleash your inner artist by using buttons as a medium for creating unique and eye-catching artwork. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced artist, buttons can be arranged in various patterns and designs to create stunning visual masterpieces. Consider creating a button mosaic, a button tree, or even a button portrait for a truly captivating art piece.

3. Button Embellishments: Add a touch of whimsy and personality to your clothing, accessories, or home decor items by embellishing them with buttons. Sew buttons onto a plain tote bag, a throw pillow, or a denim jacket to instantly transform them into stylish and personalized creations. Mix and match different button sizes and colors to create visually appealing designs that reflect your individual style.

4. Button Crafts for Kids: Buttons can be a fantastic craft supply for children as well. Encourage their creativity by providing them with buttons of various sizes and colors and let their imagination run wild. They can glue buttons onto paper to create vibrant button collages, make button magnets, or even use them in sensory play activities like button sorting or threading.

5. Button Cards and Gift Wrapping: Make your cards and gift wrapping stand out by incorporating buttons into the design. Glue a few buttons onto the front of a handmade card to add a touch of charm and dimension. You can also use buttons as decorative accents on gift tags or as closures for gift boxes, giving your presents a unique and personalized touch.

6. Button Home Decor: Buttons can be a great addition to your home decor, adding a playful and whimsical touch to any space. Create button garlands to drape across a mantel or hang on a wall. Use buttons to embellish picture frames, mirrors, or even lampshades. The possibilities are endless when it comes to using buttons to spruce up your living space.

Remember to source buttons from various places like thrift stores, flea markets, or even repurposing old clothing. This way, you can find unique and vintage buttons that will add character to your craft projects.

In conclusion, buttons are not just a practical fastening tool; they are a versatile craft supply that can be used in countless creative ways. Whether you’re a seasoned crafter or just starting out, incorporating buttons into your projects will add a unique and personal touch. So, grab your buttons, unleash your creativity, and get ready to craft something truly extraordinary.
