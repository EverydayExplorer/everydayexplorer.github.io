The Best Non-Fiction Books of the Decade


In the fast-paced world of fiction, non-fiction often takes a backseat. However, over the past decade, there have been some exceptional non-fiction books that have captivated readers and left a lasting impact. From memoirs to investigative journalism, these books have provided insights into different aspects of the world we live in. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best non-fiction books of the decade that are not to be missed.

1. ‘Educated’ by Tara Westover:
Tara Westover’s memoir is a powerful exploration of the author’s journey from a secluded rural life to the halls of academia. With her remarkable storytelling, Westover invites readers into her world, where she grapples with her family’s strict beliefs and pursues education against all odds. ‘Educated’ is a testament to the transformative power of knowledge and the resilience of the human spirit.

2. ‘Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind’ by Yuval Noah Harari:
In this thought-provoking book, Harari takes readers on a journey through the history of our species, from the emergence of Homo sapiens to the present day. With a blend of anthropology, history, and philosophy, ‘Sapiens’ challenges our perceptions of what it means to be human and offers insights into the forces that have shaped our societies.

3. ‘The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks’ by Rebecca Skloot:
This groundbreaking work of investigative journalism tells the remarkable story of Henrietta Lacks, a poor African-American woman whose cells were taken without her consent and became the foundation of medical breakthroughs. Skloot delves into the ethical implications of this story while also shedding light on the systemic racism and exploitation that Lacks and her family experienced.

4. ‘Bad Blood: Secrets and Lies in a Silicon Valley Startup’ by John Carreyrou:
The rise and fall of Theranos, a biotech company once valued at billions of dollars, is chronicled in this gripping exposé by Carreyrou. With meticulous research and interviews, the author uncovers the deceit and manipulation that surrounded the company’s founder, Elizabeth Holmes. ‘Bad Blood’ serves as a cautionary tale about the dark side of ambition and the consequences of unchecked power.

5. ‘Just Mercy: A Story of Justice and Redemption’ by Bryan Stevenson:
Bryan Stevenson, a lawyer and founder of the Equal Justice Initiative, shares his experiences defending wrongly convicted individuals on death row. Through his storytelling, Stevenson exposes the flaws and biases within the criminal justice system, highlighting the urgent need for reform. ‘Just Mercy’ is a moving and compelling call for justice and compassion.

These five non-fiction books have made a significant impact on readers and have stood out among the vast array of non-fiction literature in the past decade. Each book offers a unique perspective on the human experience, challenging readers to question their beliefs and understand the world from a different lens.

Whether you’re a fan of memoirs, investigative journalism, or historical narratives, these books are sure to leave a lasting impression. So, grab a cup of coffee, find a cozy spot, and dive into the world of non-fiction with these remarkable literary works.
