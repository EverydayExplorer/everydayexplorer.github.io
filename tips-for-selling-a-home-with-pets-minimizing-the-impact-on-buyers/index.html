Tips for Selling a Home with Pets: Minimizing the Impact on Buyers


When it comes to selling a home, there are a lot of factors to consider in order to maximize its appeal to potential buyers. One often overlooked factor is the presence of pets in the home. While many people adore animals, some buyers may have concerns or allergies that could be a hindrance when it comes to making a decision about purchasing a property. As a seller, it is crucial to minimize the impact that pets may have on potential buyers. In this blog post, we will provide you with some valuable tips to help you navigate this aspect of selling your home.

1. Deep Clean Your Home

Before putting your home on the market, it is essential to thoroughly deep clean your home, paying special attention to areas where pets spend a lot of time. This includes carpets, furniture, and any designated pet areas. Consider hiring professional carpet cleaners to eliminate any lingering odors and remove pet hair. By starting with a clean slate, you will create a more appealing environment for potential buyers, regardless of whether or not they have pets themselves.

2. Remove Traces of Pets

While your furry friends are an important part of your family, it is important to minimize their presence during showings. Ideally, it would be best to relocate your pets temporarily during the selling process, especially during open houses and private showings. This will help potential buyers focus on the property itself, rather than being distracted by the presence of animals or potential allergens. If it is not feasible to remove your pets from the property entirely, at least make sure they are secured in a designated area that buyers can easily avoid.

3. Neutralize Odors

Pets can often leave behind lingering odors in a home, especially if accidents have occurred. While you might not notice these odors, potential buyers may be put off by them. To neutralize pet odors, invest in an effective air purifier and use pet-friendly odor eliminators to ensure that your home smells fresh and inviting. Additionally, consider opening windows before showings to allow fresh air to circulate throughout the space.

4. Repair Any Pet-Related Damage

Pets, especially energetic dogs, can sometimes cause damage to your home. Before listing your property, take the time to repair any pet-related damage, such as scratched doors, chewed furniture, or stained carpets. Buyers do not want to feel like they will be inheriting a project or facing additional costs because of past pet-related damage. By addressing these issues ahead of time, you can ensure that your home appears well-maintained and appealing to potential buyers.

5. Stage Your Home Strategically

When it comes to staging your home, be mindful of your pet’s belongings and accessories. While your pets’ toys or bedding may be an integral part of their daily life, it is best to reduce their presence during showings. Consider storing pet-related items discreetly or temporarily, showcasing a clean and clutter-free space instead. This will help potential buyers visualize themselves in the home without the distraction of pet-related items.

6. Market the Pet-Friendly Aspects

While it’s important to minimize the visible impact of pets, it’s worth highlighting the pet-friendly aspects of your home during the selling process. Mention any pet-friendly features, such as a fenced backyard, a pet grooming station, or easy access to walking paths. This will attract buyers who have pets or who are considering getting one in the future.

7. Consider Professional Photography

High-quality, professional photographs are crucial when marketing your home online. When it comes to showcasing your property, it’s best to use pictures that exclude pets and showcase the space in its most appealing condition. This will help attract potential buyers who may be sensitive or indifferent to pets. By presenting a clean and pet-free home in your listing photos, you increase the chances of capturing the attention of a broader range of potential buyers.

8. Communicate with Your Real Estate Agent

Lastly, ensure clear communication with your real estate agent about the presence of pets and any concerns buyers may have. They can help strategize ways to minimize the impact of pets during showings, schedule viewings strategically, and provide guidance on effectively marketing your home to buyers who may have pets.

Selling a home with pets doesn’t have to be a challenge. By following these tips and putting in a little extra effort, you can minimize the impact that pets may have on potential buyers, making your home more appealing and increasing your chances of a successful sale. Remember, the goal is to create a neutral, clean, and inviting environment that will make buyers feel at ease and excited about the possibility of calling your property their new home.
