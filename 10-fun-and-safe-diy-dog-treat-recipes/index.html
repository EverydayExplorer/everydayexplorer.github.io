10 Fun and Safe DIY Dog Treat Recipes


If you’re a dog owner, you know how important it is to keep your furry friend happy and healthy. One way to do that is by making your own dog treats! Not only is it a fun and rewarding activity, but it also ensures that you know exactly what ingredients are going into your pup’s snacks. In this blog post, we’ll share 10 fun and safe DIY dog treat recipes that are sure to make your pup’s tail wag with joy!

1. Peanut Butter Banana Bites
Ingredients:
- 1 ripe banana
- 1/4 cup peanut butter (make sure it doesn’t contain xylitol, as it’s toxic to dogs)
- 1 cup oat flour

Instructions:
- Mash the banana in a bowl until smooth.
- Add the peanut butter and oat flour to the mashed banana and mix until well combined.
- Roll the dough into bite-sized balls and place them on a baking sheet.
- Bake at 350°F for 15 minutes or until golden brown.
- Let them cool completely before serving.

2. Apple Carrot Cookies
Ingredients:
- 1 apple, grated
- 1 carrot, grated
- 1/2 cup oat flour
- 1/2 cup rolled oats
- 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce

Instructions:
- Preheat your oven to 350°F.
- In a large bowl, mix the grated apple, grated carrot, oat flour, rolled oats, and applesauce.
- Form the dough into small cookies and place them on a baking sheet.
- Bake for 20 minutes or until golden brown.
- Allow them to cool before giving them to your pup.

3. Sweet Potato Chews
Ingredients:
- 1 large sweet potato

Instructions:
- Preheat your oven to 250°F.
- Slice the sweet potato into thin strips.
- Place the slices on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
- Bake for 2-3 hours or until the chews are dry and crispy.
- Let them cool completely before serving.

4. Pumpkin Peanut Butter Balls
Ingredients:
- 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
- 1/2 cup peanut butter (make sure it doesn’t contain xylitol)
- 2 cups rolled oats

Instructions:
- In a large bowl, mix the pumpkin puree, peanut butter, and rolled oats until well combined.
- Roll the mixture into small balls and place them on a baking sheet.
- Freeze for at least 2 hours or until firm.
- Serve the frozen balls as a refreshing treat for your pup.

5. Chicken and Rice Biscuits
Ingredients:
- 1 cup cooked chicken, shredded
- 1 cup cooked brown rice
- 1/4 cup chicken broth

Instructions:
- Preheat your oven to 350°F.
- In a bowl, combine the shredded chicken, cooked brown rice, and chicken broth.
- Mix until well combined.
- Roll out the dough and cut it into biscuit shapes.
- Place the biscuits on a baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown.
- Allow them to cool completely before giving them to your dog.

6. Blueberry Yogurt Drops
Ingredients:
- 1 cup plain yogurt
- 1/2 cup fresh blueberries

Instructions:
- Blend the plain yogurt and fresh blueberries until smooth.
- Pour the mixture into a silicone mold or drop small spoonfuls onto a parchment-lined baking sheet.
- Freeze for at least 2 hours or until firm.
- Serve the frozen drops as a tasty and refreshing treat for your pup.

7. Cheese and Bacon Biscuits
Ingredients:
- 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
- 1/4 cup bacon bits
- 1 cup oat flour

Instructions:
- Preheat your oven to 350°F.
- In a bowl, mix the shredded cheddar cheese, bacon bits, and oat flour until well combined.
- Roll out the dough and cut it into biscuit shapes.
- Place the biscuits on a baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.
- Let them cool before serving.

8. Carob Peanut Butter Balls
Ingredients:
- 1/2 cup carob powder
- 1/2 cup peanut butter (make sure it doesn’t contain xylitol)
- 1/2 cup coconut flour

Instructions:
- In a bowl, mix the carob powder, peanut butter, and coconut flour until well combined.
- Roll the dough into small balls and place them on a baking sheet.
- Freeze for at least 2 hours or until firm.
- Serve the frozen balls as a delicious and healthy treat for your pup.

9. Spinach and Chicken Jerky
Ingredients:
- 1 cup cooked chicken, shredded
- 1 cup fresh spinach

Instructions:
- Preheat your oven to 200°F.
- Blend the cooked chicken and fresh spinach until smooth.
- Spread the mixture thinly on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
- Bake for 2-3 hours or until the jerky is dry and crispy.
- Allow it to cool before giving it to your dog.

10. Banana Pumpkin Biscuits
Ingredients:
- 1 ripe banana
- 1/2 cup canned pumpkin puree
- 2 cups oat flour

Instructions:
- Preheat your oven to 350°F.
- In a bowl, mash the ripe banana until smooth.
- Add the canned pumpkin puree and oat flour to the mashed banana and mix until well combined.
- Roll out the dough and cut it into biscuit shapes.
- Place the biscuits on a baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.
- Let them cool completely before serving.

Conclusion:
Making your own dog treats is a great way to bond with your furry friend while ensuring they’re getting the best ingredients. These 10 fun and safe DIY dog treat recipes are not only delicious but also packed with wholesome ingredients that your pup will love. So grab your apron, gather the ingredients, and start baking these tasty treats for your four-legged companion!
