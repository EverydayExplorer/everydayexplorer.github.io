The Curse of the Omen: Tragic Events on Set


The Curse of the Omen refers to a series of unfortunate incidents that occurred during the production of the classic horror film ‘The Omen’ in 1976. Directed by Richard Donner, this iconic movie tells the story of a young boy who is believed to be the Antichrist.

From the very beginning of the production, strange occurrences plagued the set. One of the first incidents happened when the lead actor, Gregory Peck, narrowly escaped a plane crash. Peck had initially decided to take the ill-fated flight, but at the last minute changed his plans. The plane he was originally scheduled to be on was struck by lightning and crashed, claiming the lives of everyone on board. This near-death experience was just the beginning.

Another eerie coincidence took place when the crew traveled to a location in the United Kingdom. A member of the production team, who was responsible for organizing the transportation, was killed in a car accident. This incident sent shockwaves through the set, as it was the first major casualty associated with the movie.

Perhaps the most haunting event occurred when principal photographer John Richardson and his assistant Liz Moore were involved in a horrific car crash. The accident took place on Friday the 13th, 1976 - a date that is notorious for its association with bad luck. Unfortunately, Moore did not survive the accident, and Richardson was left severely injured. What makes this incident even more chilling is the fact that the accident occurred near a road sign that read ‘Ommen,’ a town in the Netherlands.

Numerous other strange incidents continued to occur throughout the production of ‘The Omen.’ Lightning struck a plane that was scheduled to transport the cast and crew, just as it had done in one of the movie’s most famous scenes. Animal trainer Gerry Crampton was mauled to death by a tiger, and special effects artist John Richardson, who had survived the car accident, lost his assistant to a fall during the filming of a scene.

While these incidents may be dismissed as mere coincidence, they certainly give rise to thoughts of a lurking curse. The unusual chain of events even prompted some crew members to consult with psychic experts and perform rituals in an attempt to ward off the curse. However, their efforts seemed to be in vain, as the tragic occurrences continued.

In an attempt to dispel the alleged curse, the producers decided to hold a special screening of the movie inside the prestigious Carlton Theatre in London. However, fate had other plans. Just hours before the screening, a cinema projectionist named Keith Moon was killed in a freak accident at the exact same location. Moon was the drummer for the legendary rock band The Who, adding another layer of notoriety to the series of unfortunate events associated with ‘The Omen.’

Despite the curse and the tragic events that unfolded during its production, ‘The Omen’ went on to become a critically acclaimed and commercially successful film. It received positive reviews from critics and garnered numerous awards, including an Academy Award for Best Original Score. The success of the movie solidified its place as a horror classic, but the shadow of the curse still looms over it.

To this day, fans and film enthusiasts speculate about the existence of the curse and its connection to the supernatural. Some believe that the movie’s subject matter, which delves into the realm of occult and devilish powers, somehow attracted negative energy. Others see the curse as nothing more than a series of tragic coincidences.

Whether you believe in curses or not, the tragic events that occurred during the production of ‘The Omen’ are indeed captivating. They serve as a reminder that the line between fiction and reality can sometimes blur in mysterious and unsettling ways. ‘The Curse of the Omen: Tragic Events on Set’ will forever be etched in the annals of film history, leaving us to ponder whether there are unseen forces at work in our lives.
