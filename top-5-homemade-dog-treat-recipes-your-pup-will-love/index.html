Top 5 Homemade Dog Treat Recipes Your Pup Will Love


As a dog owner, you want nothing but the best for your furry companion. From long walks in the park to cozy naps on the couch, our dogs bring us joy and unconditional love. And what better way to spoil them than with homemade dog treats? Not only are these treats much healthier than store-bought options, but they also allow you to control the ingredients, ensuring your pup gets the best nutrition. In this blog post, we will explore the top five homemade dog treat recipes that your pup will love.

1. Peanut Butter and Banana Biscuits:

Ingredients:
- 1 cup whole wheat flour
- 1/2 cup rolled oats
- 1/2 cup mashed ripe banana
- 1/4 cup peanut butter (unsalted)
- 1/4 cup water

Instructions:
- Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
- In a large bowl, mix together the flour and oats.
- Add the mashed banana, peanut butter, and water. Stir well until the ingredients are fully combined.
- Roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface and cut out shapes using a cookie cutter.
- Place the shapes on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for about 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.
- Allow the treats to cool completely before serving them to your dog.

2. Sweet Potato Chews:

Ingredients:
- 2 large sweet potatoes

Instructions:
- Preheat your oven to 250°F (120°C).
- Wash and peel the sweet potatoes.
- Cut them into thin, uniform slices. You can use a mandolin slicer for better consistency.
- Place the slices on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, ensuring they don’t overlap.
- Bake for approximately 2 to 2.5 hours, flipping the slices halfway through.
- Once the chews are dried and crispy, remove them from the oven and let them cool before giving them to your dog.

3. Chicken and Vegetable Slices:

Ingredients:
- 2 cups boneless chicken breast (cooked and shredded)
- 1/2 cup peas (cooked and mashed)
- 1/2 cup carrots (cooked and mashed)
- 1 cup brown rice (cooked)

Instructions:
- Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
- In a large bowl, combine all the ingredients and mix well.
- Spread the mixture onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, aiming for a thickness of about half an inch.
- Bake for approximately 25-30 minutes, or until the mixture is set and slightly browned.
- Allow the slices to cool completely before cutting them into bite-sized pieces.

4. Pumpkin and Oat Balls:

Ingredients:
- 1 cup canned pumpkin (unsweetened)
- 1/2 cup oats
- 1/4 cup honey
- 1/4 cup peanut butter (unsalted)

Instructions:
- In a mixing bowl, combine all the ingredients and mix well until fully blended.
- Roll the mixture into small balls, about the size of a nickel.
- Place the balls on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and freeze for at least 1 hour to set.
- Once the balls are firm, transfer them to an airtight container and store them in the refrigerator.

5. Apple and Cheddar Biscuits:

Ingredients:
- 1 cup whole wheat flour
- 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
- 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
- 1/2 cup apple (peeled and grated)
- 1/4 cup water

Instructions:
- Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
- In a large bowl, mix together the flour and cheese.
- Add the applesauce, grated apple, and water. Stir well until the ingredients are fully combined.
- Roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface and cut out shapes using a cookie cutter.
- Place the shapes on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for about 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.
- Allow the treats to cool completely before serving them to your dog.

Remember to always consult with your veterinarian before introducing any new treats or ingredients into your dog’s diet. These homemade dog treats should be given in moderation and as part of a balanced diet. Treats are meant to enhance the bond between you and your furry friend, so have fun making them and enjoy seeing the happiness in your dog’s eyes when you reward them with these tasty homemade treats!
