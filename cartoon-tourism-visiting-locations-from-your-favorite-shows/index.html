Cartoon Tourism: Visiting Locations from Your Favorite Shows


Are you a fan of cartoons? Do you find yourself getting lost in the colorful worlds of your favorite animated shows? If so, then get ready to embark on a journey like no other – cartoon tourism! In this blog post, we will explore the fascinating concept of visiting real-life locations that have inspired some of the most beloved cartoons of all time. So grab your backpack, put on your adventure hat, and let’s dive into the world of cartoon tourism!

1. Springfield, USA (The Simpsons):
Let’s start our cartoon tourism adventure in the iconic town of Springfield, the home of everyone’s favorite dysfunctional family, The Simpsons. While Springfield may be a fictional place, its inspiration can be found in various locations across the United States. One such location is Matt Groening’s hometown of Portland, Oregon, which served as the basis for the show’s setting. Take a stroll through the streets, visit local landmarks, and experience the charm that inspired the animated world of The Simpsons.

2. Bikini Bottom (SpongeBob SquarePants):
Next on our cartoon tourism itinerary is the underwater city of Bikini Bottom, where SpongeBob SquarePants and his friends reside. While you can’t exactly dive into the ocean and explore the real Bikini Bottom, you can visit the charming city of Avalon on Santa Catalina Island, California. With its vibrant marine life, crystal-clear waters, and a touch of whimsy, Avalon captures the essence of SpongeBob’s beloved hometown.

3. Gravity Falls, Oregon (Gravity Falls):
For fans of the supernatural and mysterious, a visit to the real-life inspiration behind Gravity Falls, Oregon, is a must. While the town itself is fictional, the show draws inspiration from the beautiful and enigmatic region of the Pacific Northwest. Explore the lush forests, unravel hidden secrets, and immerse yourself in the eerie atmosphere that made Gravity Falls a beloved cartoon destination.

4. Adventure Time Land (Adventure Time):
Get ready for a truly mathematical adventure as we explore the vibrant and imaginative world of Adventure Time. While there may not be a specific real-life location that inspired the Land of Ooo, you can experience the whimsy and wonder of the show in various theme parks and attractions around the world. From Adventure Time-themed rides to interactive experiences, you can immerse yourself in the colorful world of Finn, Jake, and their friends.

5. Zootopia (Zootopia):
Last but certainly not least, we venture into the animal kingdom of Zootopia. While the city itself may be a work of animation, you can find inspiration for this bustling metropolis in the diverse cities around the world. From New York City’s vibrant energy to Tokyo’s technological advancements, Zootopia draws inspiration from various real-life locations to create a world where animals live in harmony.

As you can see, cartoon tourism offers a unique and exciting way to explore the world around us. From fictional towns to real-life inspirations, these locations allow us to step into the shoes of our favorite animated characters and experience their world firsthand. So why not add a touch of whimsy to your next travel itinerary and embark on your own cartoon tourism adventure? Let your imagination run wild and let the magic of cartoons guide you on an unforgettable journey. Happy travels!
