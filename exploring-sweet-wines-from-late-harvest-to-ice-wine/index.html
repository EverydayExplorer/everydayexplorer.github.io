Exploring Sweet Wines: From Late Harvest to Ice Wine


When it comes to wine, sweetness is often associated with dessert wines. These delightful concoctions are made from grapes that have been left on the vine longer to accumulate natural sugars, resulting in a unique and indulgent taste. From Late Harvest to Ice Wine, the world of sweet wines is diverse and captivating. In this blog post, we will take a closer look at the different types of sweet wines, how they are made, and why they are worth exploring.

Late Harvest Wines

Late Harvest wines are made from grapes that have been left on the vine well past their regular harvest time. As the grapes continue to ripen, they develop higher sugar levels, which translates into a sweeter wine. This extended hang time also allows the flavors of the grapes to intensify, resulting in a more complex and luscious taste.

Late Harvest wines can be found in both red and white varieties. Riesling, Gewürztraminer, and Chenin Blanc are popular grape varietals used for white Late Harvest wines, while Cabernet Sauvignon and Zinfandel are commonly used for the red counterparts. These wines are often characterized by their honeyed aromas, balanced acidity, and rich, fruity flavors.

Ice Wine

Ice Wine, also known as Eiswein in German, is a luxurious and highly sought-after sweet wine. It is made from grapes that have been left on the vine until freezing temperatures arrive, usually in late winter. The grapes are harvested and pressed while still frozen, resulting in a concentrated juice with high sugar levels.

The freezing process allows the water in the juice to crystallize, leaving behind a more concentrated and intensely flavored liquid. This results in a wine with a unique balance of sweetness and acidity that is highly prized by wine enthusiasts. Ice Wines are often made from white grape varietals such as Riesling, Vidal Blanc, and Gewürztraminer.

Canada and Germany are renowned for their production of quality Ice Wines, with the perfect climatic conditions for freezing grapes naturally. In recent years, other regions such as the United States, Austria, and even Australia have also begun producing exceptional Ice Wines.

Noble Rot Wines

Noble Rot, or Botrytis cinerea, is a type of fungus that can be beneficial for sweet wine production. Under specific conditions, such as foggy mornings and warm afternoons, this ‘good’ mold can affect grapes by dehydrating them and concentrating their sugars and flavors. The resulting wines are known as Noble Rot wines or, more commonly, Botrytized wines.

The most famous Noble Rot wine is Sauternes from the Bordeaux region of France. This wine is made predominantly from Semillon grapes, with some Sauvignon Blanc and Muscadelle blended in. The Noble Rot gives Sauternes its signature sweet, honeyed taste with notes of apricot, peach, and tropical fruit.

Aside from Sauternes, other regions such as Tokaj in Hungary and the Rheingau in Germany also produce outstanding Botrytized wines.

Pairing Sweet Wines

Sweet wines can be incredibly versatile when it comes to food pairings. They are not only meant to be enjoyed with desserts but can also enhance a range of savory dishes. Here are a few popular pairing suggestions:

  • Late Harvest Riesling: Pair with spicy Asian cuisine, like Thai or Indian dishes, as the sweetness of the wine helps to balance the heat of the spices.
  • Ice Wine: Enjoy on its own as a luxurious after-dinner treat or pair with creamy desserts like cheesecake or crème brûlée. It also complements blue cheeses and foie gras.
  • Noble Rot Wines: Pair with strong cheeses such as Roquefort or aged Gouda. They also pair well with pâté or roasted duck.

Conclusion

Exploring sweet wines can open up a whole new world of flavors and experiences. Late Harvest wines, Ice Wines, and Noble Rot wines all offer unique and indulgent tasting experiences that deserve to be savored.

Whether you enjoy them on their own, paired with desserts, or even matched with savory dishes, sweet wines have a place at the table for every occasion.

So next time you find yourself craving something sweet, consider indulging in a bottle of Late Harvest wine, Ice Wine, or Noble Rot wine. Discover the complex aromas, rich flavors, and velvety textures that these delightful sweet wines have to offer. Cheers to the sweet life!
