The Art of Achieving the Perfect Winged Eyeliner


Are you tired of spending hours perfecting your winged eyeliner only for it to end up looking wonky or uneven? Don’t worry, you’re not alone. Many of us struggle with achieving that flawless cat-eye look. But fear not, because today we’ll be sharing some expert tips and tricks on how to achieve the perfect winged eyeliner.

Why is the Winged Eyeliner So Popular?

Winged eyeliner is a classic makeup look that has been around for decades and continues to be a favorite among beauty enthusiasts. It’s a timeless and versatile technique that can instantly elevate your makeup game. Whether you’re going for a natural everyday look or a glamorous evening look, winged eyeliner adds that extra oomph to your eyes and enhances their shape.

Tools and Products You’ll Need

Before we dive into the step-by-step process of achieving the perfect winged eyeliner, let’s talk about the tools and products you’ll need:

  1. Eyeliner: Liquid or gel eyeliners with a precise tip are recommended for creating sharp and clean lines.
  2. Eye primer: Applying an eye primer helps to create a smooth canvas and ensures longevity.
  3. Eyeshadow: Optional but recommended for a more polished look. Choose neutral shades that complement your skin tone.
  4. Angled brush: Useful for cleaning up any mistakes or sharpening the wing.
  5. Makeup remover: Essential for fixing mistakes or removing the eyeliner at the end of the day.

Step-by-Step Guide to the Perfect Winged Eyeliner

Step 1: Prep the eyes

Before applying any makeup, it’s crucial to clean your eyelids and apply a small amount of eye primer. This will help create a smooth base, prevent creasing, and make the eyeliner last longer.

Step 2: Trace the outline

Starting from the inner corner of your eye, draw a thin line along your upper lash line using a liquid or gel eyeliner. Gradually thicken the line as you reach the outer corner.

Step 3: Create the wing

To create the wing, imagine an invisible line extending from the outer corner of your eye towards the tail end of your eyebrow. This will serve as a guide for the wing’s angle and length. From the outer corner of your eye, draw a slightly diagonal line upwards to create the flick. Connect the flick to the initial line you traced along your upper lash line.

Step 4: Fill in the wing

Once you have the outline of the wing, fill it in with the eyeliner. Use short, precise strokes to ensure an even and opaque application. If needed, go back and thicken the line along the upper lash line to achieve your desired intensity.

Step 5: Clean up and sharpen the wing

To create a sharp and clean wing, take an angled brush with a small amount of makeup remover or concealer. Carefully trace along the outer edge of the wing to clean up any imperfections and sharpen the edges.

Step 6: Optional: Apply eyeshadow

For a more polished look, apply a neutral eyeshadow all over your eyelid. This will help blend the winged eyeliner with the rest of your eye makeup and provide a seamless finish.

Tips for the Perfect Winged Eyeliner

  • Practice makes perfect: Don’t get discouraged if your first few attempts don’t turn out perfectly. Like any skill, mastering the art of winged eyeliner requires practice and patience.
  • Use tape or a spoon: If you’re struggling with creating symmetrical wings, you can use tape or the rounded edge of a spoon as a guide. Simply place it against the outer corner of your eye and trace along the edge.
  • Experiment with different eyeliners: Not all eyeliners are created equal. Try different formulas and find one that suits your preferences. Some people find that liquid eyeliners provide better precision, while others prefer the flexibility of gel liners.
  • Don’t tug or pull on the eyelids: Applying eyeliner shouldn’t be a painful process. Avoid tugging or pulling on your eyelids, as this can lead to wrinkles and sagging over time. Instead, gently rest your elbow on a steady surface for better control and stability.
  • Clean your makeup brushes regularly: It’s important to clean your eyeliner brush regularly to prevent bacteria buildup and ensure smooth application. Use a mild brush cleanser or gentle soap to keep your brushes in top condition.

Conclusion

Achieving the perfect winged eyeliner may seem like an uphill battle, but with the right tools, techniques, and a little bit of practice, you can master this classic beauty look. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun experimenting with different wing shapes and styles. So go ahead, grab your favorite eyeliner, and take your eye makeup game to the next level with the perfect winged eyeliner!
