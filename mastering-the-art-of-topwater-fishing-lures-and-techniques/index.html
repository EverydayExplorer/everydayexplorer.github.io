Mastering the Art of Topwater Fishing: Lures and Techniques


Are you an angler looking to take your fishing experiences to the next level? If so, mastering the art of topwater fishing should be at the top of your list. Topwater fishing is an incredibly exciting and rewarding technique that can produce some of the most heart-stopping strikes you’ll ever experience. In this blog post, we’ll discuss the best topwater lures and techniques to help you become a master of this exhilarating style of fishing. So grab your fishing gear and let’s dive in!

First things first - what exactly is topwater fishing? As the name suggests, topwater fishing involves targeting fish that are feeding on or near the surface of the water. This technique mimics the action of natural prey that typically reside in the upper layers of the water column. The idea is to entice fish to strike by creating a commotion on the water’s surface that simulates a wounded or vulnerable baitfish.

Now that we understand the basics, let’s talk about the three main types of topwater lures that every angler should have in their tackle box:

1. Poppers: Poppers are one of the most popular topwater lures for a reason. These lures are designed to create a loud popping or splashing noise when jerked or twitched on the surface. This action mimics a wounded baitfish struggling to survive and can be irresistible to predatory fish. When using poppers, remember to vary your retrieves by pausing or speeding up your retrieval to trigger strikes.

2. Walk-the-dog baits: Walk-the-dog baits are another must-have for topwater fishing enthusiasts. These lures feature a slim profile with a front-facing weighted nose and a tail that wiggles from side to side when twitched. The key to successfully using walk-the-dog baits is mastering the technique of smoothly twitching your rod tip to generate the ‘walk’ action. This side-to-side wiggling motion mimics a wounded or fleeing baitfish and can drive fish wild.

3. Buzzbaits: Buzzbaits are a favorite among anglers targeting bass and other predatory species. These lures feature a spinning blade that creates a buzzing or whirring sound as it spins through the water. The combination of noise and surface disturbance can trigger an aggressive strike from feeding fish. When fishing buzzbaits, focus on areas with vegetation or cover, as these lures are designed to be retrieved over the top of the water’s surface.

Now that we have covered the essential topwater lures let’s discuss some techniques that will help you increase your success rate:

1. Time of day: Topwater fishing is most effective during low light conditions such as early morning, late evening, or even on cloudy days. These conditions provide cover for fish and make them more likely to strike at surface lures.

2. Stealth: While topwater fishing can be exhilarating, it’s essential to approach your fishing spot with caution. Fish near the surface are more skittish than those deeper down, so avoid making unnecessary noise and sudden movements that could spook them.

3. Experiment with retrieves: Varying your retrieve techniques can be the key to triggering strikes from finicky fish. Try using a steady retrieve, a stop-and-go retrieve, or even a ‘pop and drop’ technique to see what works best on any given day.

4. Match the hatch: Pay attention to the size and color of the baitfish present in the area you are fishing. Choose lures that closely resemble the natural prey to increase your chances of success.

5. Practice patience: Topwater fishing can require a bit more patience than other techniques. It may sometimes take several casts before you get a strike, but when it happens, it will be worth the wait.

In conclusion, mastering the art of topwater fishing is a journey that requires practice, observation, and experimentation. By using the right lures, techniques, and understanding the behavior of fish, you can increase your success rate and have an unforgettable fishing experience. So go out there, give these tips a try, and get ready to witness the explosive strikes that topwater fishing has to offer. Happy fishing!
