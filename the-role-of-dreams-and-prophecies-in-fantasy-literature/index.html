The Role of Dreams and Prophecies in Fantasy Literature


Fantasy literature has long captivated readers with its ability to transport them to magical realms, where anything is possible. One of the enduring elements of this genre is the use of dreams and prophecies to drive the narrative forward. Dreams and prophecies introduce an air of mystery and intrigue, and create a sense of destiny and inevitability. In this blog post, we will explore the role of dreams and prophecies in fantasy literature, and why they continue to be such a popular and important element in the genre.

Dream as a Window to the Unconscious

Dreams have always been a fascinating subject for exploration. They are a mysterious realm where the unconscious mind takes over, and anything can happen. In fantasy literature, dreams often serve as a means for characters to receive important information or forewarning about future events. They can act as a window to the future or provide insight into the past. Dreams are a way for characters to tap into their subconscious and receive guidance or direction.

For example, in J.R.R. Tolkien’s ‘The Lord of the Rings,’ the character of Frodo frequently has dreams that give him insight into upcoming dangers and the course of his journey. These dreams not only provide important information for Frodo, but they also heighten the sense of suspense and anticipation for the reader.

Dreams also allow authors to delve into the psyche of their characters and explore their fears, desires, and motivations. By presenting the inner thoughts and emotions of the characters through dreams, authors can create a deeper connection between readers and their fictional creations.

Prophecies as a Catalyst for Action

Prophecies, on the other hand, serve a different purpose in fantasy literature. Unlike dreams, prophecies are often delivered by a higher power or an oracle and are seen as a foretelling of future events. Prophecies act as a catalyst for action, setting the plot in motion and driving the characters toward their destiny.

One of the most famous examples of prophecies in fantasy literature is found in J.K. Rowling’s ‘Harry Potter’ series. The prophecy that a ‘child with the power to vanquish the Dark Lord’ will be born sets the stage for the entire series. This prophecy sets Harry Potter on his path to confront Voldemort, and drives much of the action and conflict throughout the books.

Prophecies create a sense of inevitability and destiny, encouraging characters to fulfill their roles and face their ultimate challenges. They add an element of tension and suspense, as characters are constantly striving to interpret and fulfill these prophecies.

The Power of Foreknowledge

Both dreams and prophecies provide characters with foreknowledge of future events, allowing them to make decisions and take action based on this knowledge. This foreknowledge can be a powerful tool, as it gives characters an advantage over their adversaries.

In George R.R. Martin’s ‘A Song of Ice and Fire’ series, the character of Bran Stark has the ability to see into the past and the future through his dreams. This foreknowledge not only helps him navigate the complex politics of Westeros, but also gives him the ability to influence events and change the course of history.

However, foreknowledge can also be a double-edged sword. Characters who are aware of their fate may find themselves trapped in a predetermined path, unable to escape their destiny. This creates a sense of fatalism and raises questions about free will and the nature of choice.

Conclusion

Dreams and prophecies play a vital role in fantasy literature, adding an element of mystery, suspense, and foreboding to the narrative. They provide characters with insight into future events and serve as a catalyst for action. Dreams allow for exploration of the characters’ subconscious minds, while prophecies create a sense of destiny and inevitability. Together, dreams and prophecies contribute to the allure and enchantment of the fantasy genre, captivating readers with their ability to transport them to worlds where dreams come true and destinies are revealed.
