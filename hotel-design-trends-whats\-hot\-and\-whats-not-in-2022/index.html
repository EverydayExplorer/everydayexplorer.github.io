Hotel Design Trends: What’s Hot and What’s Not in 2022

The design of a hotel plays a crucial role in attracting guests and enhancing their overall experience. In today’s ever-evolving hospitality industry, it is important for hotel owners and designers to stay updated with the latest trends and incorporate them into their properties. As we head into 2022, let’s explore the design trends that are expected to dominate the hotel industry and those that are slowly fading away.

Hot Design Trends for 2022

1. Sustainability and Eco-Friendliness: Environmental consciousness has become a global concern, and guests are increasingly looking for eco-friendly accommodation options. In 2022, we will see more hotels adopting sustainable practices, such as using energy-efficient appliances, implementing water-saving measures, and incorporating renewable materials into their design.

2. Biophilic Design: Biophilic design focuses on incorporating natural elements, such as plants, wood, and natural light, into interior spaces. This trend is expected to gain even more popularity in 2022 as hotels aim to create a calming and soothing atmosphere for their guests.

3. Touchless Technology: The COVID-19 pandemic has accelerated the adoption of touchless technology in hotels. From contactless check-in and payment systems to voice-activated room controls, guests will appreciate the convenience and safety provided by these features.

4. Flexible Spaces: With the rise of remote work and digital nomadism, hotels are reimagining their spaces to cater to this new trend. We will see the incorporation of flexible workspaces within the hotel premises, allowing guests to combine leisure and work seamlessly.

5. Personalized Experiences: Guests are seeking unique and personalized experiences during their hotel stay. In 2022, we can expect to see hotels leveraging technology and data to tailor their services to individual preferences, providing a more personalized and memorable stay.

Design Trends that are Fading Away

1. Traditional Key Cards: Key cards have long been a staple in the hotel industry, but they are slowly becoming outdated. In 2022, we will see a significant shift towards smartphone-based room access, eliminating the need for physical cards.

2. Overuse of Technology: While technology has its place in enhancing guest experiences, an overreliance on it can sometimes feel impersonal. Hotels will strike a balance between utilizing technology and maintaining a genuine human touch to create a warm and welcoming environment.

3. Cookie-Cutter Designs: Generic hotel designs that lack character and uniqueness are gradually becoming less appealing. In 2022, hotels will focus on creating distinct and memorable spaces that reflect the local culture and heritage.

4. Single-Use Plastics: As part of the sustainability trend, hotels are actively working towards reducing their reliance on single-use plastics. In 2022, we will see a decrease in the use of plastic water bottles, straws, and other disposable items, with hotels opting for more eco-friendly alternatives.

5. Standardized Room Layouts: Guests are increasingly seeking variety and choice when it comes to hotel room layouts. In 2022, hotels will move away from the one-size-fits-all approach and offer a range of room configurations to cater to different preferences and group sizes.

In conclusion, 2022 promises to be an exciting year for hotel design. From embracing sustainability and biophilic elements to leveraging technology for personalized experiences, hotels will continue to evolve to meet the changing needs and desires of the modern traveler. By staying informed about these trends, hotel owners and designers can create unique and memorable spaces that attract and delight guests for years to come.
