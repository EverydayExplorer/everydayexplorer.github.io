What to Know About Insurance for Home Renovations


Home renovations can be an exciting and transformative experience for homeowners. Whether you’re upgrading your kitchen, adding a new bathroom, or expanding your living space, it’s important to consider the impact these renovations can have on your home insurance. In this blog post, we will delve into the key aspects of insurance for home renovations, providing you with the information you need to navigate this often-overlooked aspect of the renovation process.

1. Notify Your Insurance Provider: Before you embark on any home renovation project, it’s crucial to inform your insurance provider. Failing to do so can lead to potential coverage gaps or even the denial of claims in the future. By notifying your insurance provider, you can ensure that your policy is updated to reflect the changes being made to your home.

2. Understand Coverage Limits: Home renovations can significantly increase the value of your property. As a result, it’s important to review and potentially adjust your coverage limits to adequately protect your investment. Failing to do so may leave you underinsured in the event of damage or loss during the renovation process.

3. Builder’s Risk Insurance: Depending on the scope of your renovation project, you may need to consider obtaining builder’s risk insurance. This specialized policy provides coverage for the structure and materials during construction. Builder’s risk insurance is typically taken out by the contractor, but it’s important for homeowners to understand the details of this coverage and ensure it is in place.

4. Liability Coverage: Home renovations often involve the presence of contractors and subcontractors on your property. In the event of an accident or injury during the renovation, you may be held liable. To protect yourself from potential lawsuits or claims, it’s crucial to review your liability coverage and consider increasing the limits if necessary.

5. Additional Living Expenses: Depending on the scale of your renovation, you may need to temporarily move out of your home. In such cases, it’s important to review your policy to understand what coverage is provided for additional living expenses. This coverage can help cover costs associated with accommodations, meals, and other necessary expenses while your home is undergoing renovations.

6. Home Security: During the renovation process, your home may be more vulnerable to theft or vandalism. Take steps to enhance your home security, such as installing security cameras, alarms, or smart home systems. Not only will this help protect your property, but it may also qualify you for insurance discounts.

7. Keep Documentation: Throughout the renovation process, it’s essential to keep detailed records, including contracts, invoices, and photographs of the work being done. This documentation can be invaluable in the event of a claim or dispute with your insurance provider.

8. Review Policy Exclusions: Some home insurance policies may have specific exclusions for certain types of renovations or construction methods. Before starting any renovation project, carefully review your policy to understand any exclusions that may apply. If necessary, discuss these exclusions with your insurance provider to explore potential solutions.

In conclusion, home renovations can have a significant impact on your home insurance coverage. By notifying your insurance provider, understanding your coverage limits, and considering specialized policies like builder’s risk insurance, you can ensure that you are adequately protected throughout the renovation process. Don’t forget to review your liability coverage, consider additional living expenses, enhance home security, keep thorough documentation, and be aware of any policy exclusions. By taking these steps, you can enjoy the benefits of your home renovation with peace of mind.
