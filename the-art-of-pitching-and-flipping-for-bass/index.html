The Art of Pitching and Flipping for Bass


Bass fishing is one of the most popular sports among anglers worldwide. It requires skill, patience, and knowledge of various techniques in order to successfully catch these elusive fish. One technique that stands out among the rest is pitching and flipping.

Pitching and flipping are two similar yet distinct methods of presenting lures to bass in tight cover. These techniques are effective in getting your bait into hard-to-reach areas like under docks, overhanging trees, and brush piles where bass are known to hide.

So, what exactly is pitching and flipping? Pitching involves making short underhand casts with minimal noise and disturbance to the water. This technique is great for targeting bass around shallow cover, such as vegetation and stumps. On the other hand, flipping involves making longer and controlled casts with your baitcaster reel and a heavier lure. Flipping is ideal for targeting bass in deeper cover, such as bushes, laydowns, and boat docks.

To master the art of pitching and flipping, you need to have the right equipment. Here’s a list of the essentials you’ll need:

1. Rod: A medium-heavy to heavy action casting rod is recommended for both pitching and flipping. The length of the rod should be around 7-8 feet. This will provide you with the necessary leverage to control your bait and handle big bass.

2. Reel: Pair your rod with a high-quality low-profile baitcasting reel. Look for a reel that has a smooth drag system and is capable of handling heavier lines.

3. Line: When it comes to pitching and flipping, many anglers prefer using braided line. Braided line has low stretch and high strength, which is crucial for pulling bass out of heavy cover. Start with a line weight of around 30-50 pounds.

4. Terminal Tackle: Use heavy-duty hooks like flipping hooks or straight shank hooks. These hooks are specifically designed for pitching and flipping and have a wide gap, which increases your hook-up ratio. Pair your hooks with tungsten weights for better sensitivity and a compact profile.

Now that you have the right equipment, let’s dive into the techniques of pitching and flipping.

Pitching Technique:
1. Stand at the front of your boat, facing the target area.
2. Hold the rod in your dominant hand with your thumb resting on the spool of the reel.
3. Cradle the line with your other hand, creating a small loop between your index finger and the reel.
4. Hold the lure with your free hand and extend your arm towards the target area.
5. Snap your wrist forward, releasing the line from your hand, and allowing the lure to glide towards the target area.
6. Use your thumb to control the descent of the lure, keeping it within the desired strike zone.
7. Once the lure has landed, engage the reel and begin retrieving your bait.

Flipping Technique:
1. Stand at the front of your boat and hold the rod between your thumb and forefinger with the reel facing upward.
2. Use your opposite hand to hold the line against the rod handle, creating tension.
3. Pull a sufficient length of line from the reel so that when you let go, the bait will reach the target area.
4. Point your rod towards the target area and release the line by opening your opposite hand.
5. Allow the bait to fall freely to the bottom while maintaining control with your thumb.
6. Once the bait reaches the bottom, engage the reel and start reeling in the slack line.
7. Lift the rod tip and repeat the process in different areas of the cover.

Remember, practice makes perfect. Spend some time on the water honing your pitching and flipping skills. Pay attention to your surroundings and target high-percentage areas such as points, docks, and fallen trees. Experiment with different lures and retrieve speeds to find what works best for you.

Pitching and flipping can be challenging techniques to master, but they can greatly increase your chances of catching trophy bass. So, gear up, practice your technique, and get ready to hook some big ones!
