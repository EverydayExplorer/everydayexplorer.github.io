How to Make the Most of Your Health Savings Account (HSA)


Healthcare costs can be a pain point for many Americans. According to the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services, national health expenditures in the United States reached .8 trillion in 2019. It’s no surprise then that many people are looking for ways to save on healthcare costs. One way to do this is by opening and maximizing a Health Savings Account (HSA).

What is an HSA?

An HSA is a tax-advantaged savings account that is used to pay for medical expenses. It is available to individuals who are enrolled in a high-deductible health plan (HDHP). The funds in an HSA can be used to pay for qualified medical expenses such as deductibles, copayments, and coinsurance. The money in an HSA is tax-deductible, which means that it can be used to reduce taxable income.

How to make the most of an HSA

1. Contribute the maximum amount

The IRS sets limits on how much money can be contributed to an HSA each year. In 2021, individuals can contribute up to ,600 and families can contribute up to ,200. By contributing the maximum amount to your HSA, you can take advantage of the tax benefits and save even more money on healthcare expenses.

2. Plan for future medical expenses

Your HSA can be used to pay for medical expenses that you incur today, but it can also be used to pay for future medical expenses. This means that you can use your HSA to save for healthcare expenses that you may incur in the future, such as retirement healthcare costs or long-term care expenses.

3. Shop around for healthcare services

Another way to make the most of your HSA is by shopping around for healthcare services. Just because you have a high-deductible health plan doesn’t mean that you have to pay top dollar for healthcare services. By shopping around for healthcare services, you can find providers that offer high-quality care at a more affordable price.

4. Keep your receipts

It’s important to keep track of your medical expenses and save all of your receipts. This will make it easier to claim expenses from your HSA and provide documentation for tax purposes. By keeping your receipts, you can also ensure that you are not paying for expenses that are not covered by your HSA.

5. Invest your HSA funds

If you have funds in your HSA that you don’t plan on using anytime soon, you may want to consider investing them. Many HSA providers offer investment options that can help your money grow over time. By investing your HSA funds, you can potentially earn more money to pay for healthcare expenses in the future.

In conclusion, an HSA can be a valuable tool for saving money on healthcare expenses. By contributing the maximum amount, planning for future expenses, shopping around for healthcare services, keeping your receipts, and investing your funds, you can make the most of your HSA and potentially save thousands of dollars on healthcare costs.
