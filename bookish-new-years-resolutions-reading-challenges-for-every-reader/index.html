Bookish New Year's Resolutions: Reading Challenges for Every Reader


As the New Year rolls around, it’s time to dust off those bookshelves, crack open some fresh pages, and set our sights on literary adventures. Whether you’re a seasoned bookworm or someone looking to reignite their love for reading, setting bookish resolutions can add a fun and fulfilling dimension to your year ahead. From tackling towering tomes to exploring new genres, there’s a reading challenge out there for every type of reader. So, let’s dive into some exciting reading challenges to inspire your literary journey in the coming year.

  1. The Classics Conquest: Have you always wanted to delve into the world of classic literature but never quite got around to it? Make this the year you conquer the classics! Create a list of timeless masterpieces from authors like Jane Austen, Charles Dickens, and Leo Tolstoy, and challenge yourself to read a set number by the year’s end. Not only will you broaden your literary horizons, but you’ll also gain insight into the foundation of modern storytelling.

  2. The Genre Explorer: Are you stuck in a reading rut, always gravitating towards the same type of book? Break out of your comfort zone and become a genre explorer! Make a list of genres you’ve never tried before, whether it’s science fiction, fantasy, mystery, or romance, and commit to reading at least one book from each category. You might just discover a new favorite genre you never knew existed.

  3. The Epic Series Quest: Dive into the immersive worlds of epic book series and embark on a quest to conquer them all. Whether it’s George R.R. Martin’s ‘A Song of Ice and Fire,’ J.K. Rowling’s ‘Harry Potter’ series, or Brandon Sanderson’s ‘Stormlight Archive,’ choose a series that intrigues you and set a goal to complete it within the year. Get lost in sprawling narratives, complex characters, and rich world-building as you journey through each installment.

  4. The Diverse Voices Challenge: Expand your reading horizons by seeking out books written by authors from diverse backgrounds and cultures. Make a conscious effort to include voices that are often underrepresented in mainstream literature, whether it’s authors of color, LGBTQ+ writers, or authors from different regions of the world. Not only will you gain new perspectives and insights, but you’ll also contribute to a more inclusive literary landscape.

  5. The Literary Bucket List: Create a literary bucket list filled with books you’ve always wanted to read but never had the chance to. Whether it’s a classic you’ve been meaning to tackle or a contemporary bestseller that piqued your interest, jot down titles that have been languishing on your TBR pile and make it your mission to finally check them off. You’ll feel a sense of accomplishment with each book you cross off your list.

  6. The Reading Challenge Roulette: Add an element of surprise to your reading journey with a reading challenge roulette. Write down different reading prompts on slips of paper, such as ‘a book with a one-word title,’ ‘a book set in a different country,’ or ‘a book recommended by a friend,’ and place them in a jar. Each month, randomly select a prompt from the jar and choose a book that fits the criteria. It’s a fun and unpredictable way to keep your reading experience fresh and exciting.

  7. The Page Count Marathon: Challenge yourself to read a certain number of pages or books within the year, pushing your reading stamina to new heights. Whether it’s aiming to read 100 books in a year or tackling a hefty tome that’s been collecting dust on your shelf, set a page count goal that stretches your reading muscles. Keep track of your progress and celebrate each milestone along the way.

Whatever reading challenge you decide to take on, remember that the goal is to enjoy the journey and expand your literary horizons. Don’t be afraid to mix and match challenges or tailor them to suit your reading preferences. The most important thing is to keep turning those pages and savoring the magic of storytelling with each book you read. Here’s to a year filled with literary adventures and bookish delights!
