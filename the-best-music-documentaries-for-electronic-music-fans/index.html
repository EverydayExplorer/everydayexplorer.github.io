The Best Music Documentaries for Electronic Music Fans


Music has the incredible power to transport us to another realm, evoking emotions and memories like nothing else can. For electronic music fans, the connection between the beats, the synths, and the dance floor is a sacred bond. If you’re an avid lover of electronic music, then you know that the genre’s history is rich and complex. And what better way to dive deep into the world of electronic music than through the lens of a captivating documentary? In this blog post, we will explore some of the best music documentaries that every electronic music fan should watch. From the pioneers who shaped the genre to the underground scenes that continue to push boundaries, these films offer a fascinating glimpse into the world of electronic music.

1. ‘808’ (2015) - Directed by Alex Dunn, ‘808’ is a documentary that delves into the origins of the iconic Roland TR-808 drum machine. This film explores how this simple piece of equipment revolutionized music production, becoming an integral part of electronic music history. Through interviews with legendary musicians like Pharrell Williams, Beastie Boys, and David Guetta, ‘808’ takes us on a journey through the evolution of electronic sound.

2. ‘Daft Punk Unchained’ (2015) - Directed by Hervé Martin Delpierre, ‘Daft Punk Unchained’ is a riveting documentary that chronicles the rise of the enigmatic French duo, Daft Punk. From their humble beginnings in the underground Parisian scene to their global domination, this film offers an intimate look into the creative minds behind some of electronic music’s most iconic tracks. With interviews from collaborators and industry insiders, ‘Daft Punk Unchained’ is a must-watch for any fan of the genre.

3. ‘The Chemical Brothers: Don’t Think’ (2012) - Directed by Adam Smith, ‘The Chemical Brothers: Don’t Think’ is a concert film that captures the energy and spectacle of a live performance by the legendary electronic duo. Shot at Japan’s Fuji Rock Festival, this visually stunning documentary immerses viewers in the electrifying world of The Chemical Brothers. With mind-bending visuals and an eclectic mix of their greatest hits, ‘Don’t Think’ is a treat for both the eyes and the ears.

4. ‘Kraftwerk and the Electronic Revolution’ (2008) - Directed by Rob Johnstone, ‘Kraftwerk and the Electronic Revolution’ is a comprehensive documentary that explores the impact and influence of the German electronic music pioneers, Kraftwerk. From their early experimental days to their lasting legacy, this film showcases how Kraftwerk’s innovative sound and futuristic aesthetics shaped the entire electronic music landscape. Featuring interviews with musicians, journalists, and fans, ‘Kraftwerk and the Electronic Revolution’ is a must-see for anyone interested in the roots of electronic music.

5. ‘I Dream of Wires’ (2014) - Directed by Robert Fantinatto, ‘I Dream of Wires’ is a documentary that delves into the world of modular synthesizers. This film explores the resurgence of interest in analog synthesizers and the passionate community that surrounds them. With interviews from renowned musicians and modular enthusiasts, ‘I Dream of Wires’ takes us on a journey through the evolution of electronic sound and the enduring allure of hardware synthesis.

These are just a few of the best music documentaries that electronic music fans should check out. Whether you’re a seasoned raver or a curious newcomer, these films offer a captivating and insightful look into the world of electronic music. So grab some popcorn, turn up the volume, and let these documentaries transport you to the pulsating heart of the electronic music scene.
