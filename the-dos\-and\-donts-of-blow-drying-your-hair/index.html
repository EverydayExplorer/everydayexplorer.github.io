The Dos\ and\ Donts of Blow-drying Your Hair


When it comes to blow-drying your hair, there is a right way and many wrong ways to do it. Blow-drying can be a great way to achieve a smooth and polished look, but if done incorrectly, it can lead to damage and frizz. In this blog post, we will discuss the do’s and don’ts of blow-drying your hair, providing you with the knowledge necessary to achieve beautiful, healthy-looking hair.

The Do’s

Do Use a Heat Protectant

Before blow-drying your hair, it is essential to apply a heat protectant. Heat protectants create a barrier between your hair and the high temperatures of the blow dryer, shielding your hair from potential damage. Look for a heat protectant spray or serum that is suitable for your hair type, and apply it evenly throughout your damp hair before blow-drying.

Do Start with Damp Hair

To achieve the best blow-drying results, it is important to begin with damp hair. Towel-dry your hair after washing it, removing excess moisture, and then proceed to blow-dry. Blotting your hair rather than rubbing it with a towel can help prevent unnecessary frizz and breakage.

Do Use a Wide-toothed Comb

Using a wide-toothed comb while blow-drying can help detangle your hair gently and prevent unnecessary pulling or breakage. Start combing from the ends, working your way up to the roots, as this can minimize damage and help distribute heat evenly.

Do Use the Right Tools

Investing in a high-quality blow dryer and diffuser can make a significant difference in the final outcome of your blowout. Look for blow dryers that offer multiple heat and speed settings, as well as a cool shot button to seal the hair cuticles after blow-drying. A diffuser attachment can also be beneficial for those with curly or wavy hair, as it helps distribute heat evenly and reduces frizz.

Do Section Your Hair

When blow-drying, it is essential to section your hair. This technique allows each section to receive equal heat distribution and ensures that you don’t miss any spots. Divide your hair into manageable sections using hair clips or hair ties, and work on one section at a time until your entire head of hair is dry.

The Don’ts

Don’t Use High Heat

Using high heat directly on your hair can cause damage and dryness. Opt for the lowest heat setting possible that is still effective for drying your hair. Remember to keep the blow dryer moving continuously to avoid excessively drying out any particular area.

Don’t Blow-dry on Wet Hair

Blow-drying soaking wet hair is a recipe for disaster. Excessive heat on wet hair can cause the hair shaft to swell and lead to damage. Make sure to towel dry your hair and remove the excess moisture before beginning the blow-drying process.

Don’t Hold the Blow Dryer Too Close

Holding the blow dryer too close to your hair can cause it to become dry and brittle. Maintain a distance of at least six inches between your hair and the nozzle. By doing so, you will minimize the risk of overheating your hair and causing unnecessary damage.

Don’t Forget to Use the Cool Shot

The cool shot function on your blow dryer is there for a reason – to seal the hair cuticles and lock in the style. In the final stage of blow-drying, switch to the cool shot setting and direct the airflow downwards along the hair shaft. This will help to set your style, reduce frizz, and give your hair a polished finish.

Don’t Overdo It

Blow-drying your hair every day can lead to damage and dryness over time. Allow your hair to air-dry whenever possible, and limit blow-drying to a few times a week. It’s also essential to give your hair regular breaks from heat styling to maintain its overall health and integrity.

Conclusion

Blow-drying your hair can be a quick and efficient way to achieve a salon-worthy look right at home. By following these do’s and don’ts, you can ensure that you are protecting your hair from unnecessary damage while achieving the desired results. Remember to use a heat protectant, start with damp hair, and section your hair. Be mindful of the heat setting, distance, and use the cool shot function. Lastly, give your hair regular breaks from heat styling to maintain its overall health and vitality. With these tips in mind, you can blow-dry your hair like a pro and enjoy fabulous-looking hair every day!
