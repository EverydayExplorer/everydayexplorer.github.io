Kitchen Design Trends: Whats\ Hot\ and\ Whats Not


When it comes to designing a kitchen, there are many trends to consider. With advancements in technology, materials, and design overall, homeowners have more options than ever before. However, not all trends are created equal. Some stand the test of time while others fade away quickly. Here are some kitchen design trends that are currently hot, and some that are not.

What’s Hot:

1. Open Shelving

Open shelving has been trending over the last few years and it’s not going anywhere. This trend brings a more relaxed and casual feel to the kitchen. It also allows for easy access to items that are frequently used. In addition, it provides the opportunity to display beautiful dishes, glassware, and other decorative pieces.

2. Smart Appliances

Smart technology has taken over our lives and it’s now making its way into our kitchens. Smart appliances offer convenience, efficiency, and automation of everyday cooking tasks. They can be controlled with our smartphones, making it easier to manage our busy lives.

3. Bold Colors

Bold colors are making a comeback in kitchens. Homeowners are ditching the traditional white and gray and opting for bolder, brighter hues. Vibrant colors like navy blue, dark green, and even pink are being used on cabinetry, walls, and backsplashes.

4. Mixed Metals

Mixed metals have been popular in other areas of the home, and now they are making their way into the kitchen. Combining metals like brass, copper, and stainless steel brings depth and warmth to the space. It also allows for more flexibility when it comes to fixtures and hardware.

5. Natural Elements

Bringing natural elements into the kitchen is another trend that is gaining popularity. Incorporating materials like wood, stone, and marble creates a warm and inviting atmosphere. It also brings a sense of timeless elegance to the space.

What’s Not:

1. Granite Countertops

While granite countertops were once the standard in kitchen design, they are now becoming a thing of the past. Homeowners are opting for more unique and eco-friendly materials like concrete, quartz, and soapstone.

2. Matching Cabinets

Matching cabinets have been a trend for years, but it’s now time to say goodbye. Homeowners are mixing and matching different materials and styles to create a more customized look. This adds personality and interest to the space.

3. Subway Tile Backsplash

Subway tile backsplashes have been a staple in kitchen design for years. While they are still a classic choice, homeowners are looking for something more unique and eye-catching. Alternative options like graphic tile, mosaic, and even wallpaper are becoming more popular.

4. Minimalism

Minimalism has been a trend in many areas of home design, but it’s not for everyone. Homeowners are opting for more personalized and eclectic spaces. Adding collections, artwork, and other decorative elements adds warmth and personality to the kitchen.

5. Closed-Off Spaces

Closed-off kitchen spaces are a thing of the past. Homeowners are looking for open and airy spaces that flow into other areas of the home. Removing walls and creating open concept floor plans allow for more natural light and better traffic flow.

In conclusion, while kitchen design trends come and go, some stand the test of time. Incorporating elements like natural materials, bold colors, and mixed metals add personality and interest to the space. Opting for more unique and eco-friendly materials for countertops, mixing and matching different cabinet styles, and removing walls create a more personalized and open kitchen. On the other hand, trends like closed-off spaces, minimalism, and subway tile backsplashes are becoming less popular. As you design your kitchen, it’s important to consider what trends appeal to you and what will stand the test of time.
