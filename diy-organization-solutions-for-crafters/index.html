DIY Organization Solutions for Crafters


Crafting is a beloved hobby for many people. Whether you enjoy sewing, painting, scrapbooking, or any other form of crafting, one thing is for certain – organization is key. Without proper organization, your crafting materials can quickly become a chaotic mess, making it difficult to find what you need when you need it. Thankfully, there are plenty of DIY organization solutions for crafters that can help you keep your crafting supplies neat, tidy, and easily accessible. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best DIY organization solutions for crafters that will not only help you stay organized but also save you time and make your crafting experience more enjoyable.

1. Mason Jar Storage

Mason jars are versatile and inexpensive storage solutions that can be used for a variety of craft supplies. You can use them to store buttons, beads, paintbrushes, pens, markers, and more. To take your mason jar storage to the next level, consider attaching the jars to a pegboard or a wall-mounted shelf. This will not only keep your supplies organized but also add a decorative touch to your craft room.

2. Drawer Dividers

If you have a desk or a table with drawers in your craft room, drawer dividers are a must-have. They are inexpensive and easy to install, and they can turn messy, cluttered drawers into well-organized ones. Use drawer dividers to separate your various crafting supplies such as papers, ribbons, scissors, and glue sticks. This will make it much easier to find what you’re looking for and prevent items from getting lost or damaged.

3. Pegboards and Hooks

Pegboards are a fantastic organization solution for crafters because they allow you to hang your supplies right where you can see them. Install a pegboard on a wall in your craft room and use hooks to hang scissors, rulers, yarn, washi tape, and other items. You can also attach small baskets or shelves to the pegboard to hold smaller items like buttons or embroidery floss. The best part about pegboards is that they are customizable, meaning you can rearrange the hooks and baskets as your needs change over time.

4. Magnetic Strips

Magnetic strips, typically used for storing knives in the kitchen, can also be used to keep your craft tools organized. Attach a magnetic strip to the back of your craft table or on the inside of a cabinet door, then use it to hold metal tools like scissors, needles, and tweezers. This way, your tools will be easily accessible and won’t get lost in the chaos of your craft supplies.

5. Repurposed Furniture

Don’t overlook the potential of repurposed furniture for craft storage. Look for old dressers, bookshelves, or cabinets that can be transformed into functional storage solutions. For example, you can use an old dresser with drawers to store fabric or paper, or a bookshelf to display and organize your finished projects. With a fresh coat of paint and some creative thinking, you can turn old furniture into beautiful and practical storage for your crafting supplies.

6. Hanging Shoe Organizer

Hanging shoe organizers are a popular and inexpensive organization solution for small items. Hang one on the back of your craft room door or on a wall, and use the individual pockets to store scissors, paintbrushes, pens, glue sticks, and other small crafting supplies. This allows you to easily see and access your supplies, without taking up valuable desk or drawer space.

7. Labeling Systems

No organization system is complete without proper labeling. Invest in a label maker or use printable labels to clearly identify the contents of your storage containers, drawers, and shelves. This will save you time and frustration when searching for specific items and will help you maintain an organized space over the long term.

Conclusion

Staying organized as a crafter doesn’t have to be a daunting task. With these DIY organization solutions, you can transform your chaotic craft room into a well-organized space that inspires creativity. From mason jar storage to pegboards and hooks, there are plenty of options to choose from depending on your needs and preferences. Remember to label your storage containers and drawers for easy identification, and don’t be afraid to repurpose furniture to suit your organizational needs. By implementing these DIY organization solutions, you’ll spend less time searching for supplies and more time doing what you love – crafting!
