The World of Bitters: Adding Depth and Complexity to Your Cocktails


Bitters have been around for centuries, but it wasn’t until the early 1800s that they became a staple in cocktail culture. These concentrated elixirs, made from herbs, roots, and barks, add depth and complexity to any drink, from a simple gin and tonic to a complex whiskey sour. Bitters are not just a flavor, but a powerful tool for any bartender looking to elevate their craft. In this blog post, we’ll explore the world of bitters and how they can be used to add depth and complexity to your cocktails.

What are Bitters?

Bitters are highly-concentrated extracts made from a blend of herbs, roots, and barks. They are used to add a bitter or sour taste to cocktails and can be found in a variety of flavors, from orange to chocolate. Bitters are usually added in small amounts and are a key ingredient in many classic cocktails, such as the Manhattan, Old Fashioned, and Negroni. They are also used to create new and exciting flavor combinations.

Different Types of Bitters

There are many different types of bitters, each with its own unique flavor profile. Here are some of the most popular types of bitters:

Angostura Bitters - The most famous bitters in the world, Angostura bitters are made from a secret blend of herbs and spices and have a sharp, bitter taste with notes of cinnamon, clove, and ginger.

Orange Bitters - Made from the peels of bitter oranges, orange bitters add a bright, citrusy flavor to cocktails and are often used in Old Fashioneds and Manhattans.

Aromatic Bitters - A classic cocktail ingredient, aromatic bitters are made from a blend of herbs and spices and have a strong, bitter taste with notes of cinnamon, clove, and nutmeg.

Peychaud’s Bitters - This New Orleans-style bitters is known for its bright pink color and sweet, floral taste. It adds a unique twist to classic cocktails like the Sazerac.

How to Use Bitters

Bitters are a versatile ingredient that can be used in a variety of ways to add depth and complexity to cocktails. Here are a few tips on how to use bitters:

Add a few drops of bitters to your favorite cocktail for a quick and easy flavor boost.

Experiment with different types of bitters to create new and exciting flavor combinations.

Use bitters to balance out the sweetness of a cocktail. A few drops of bitters can add a bitter or sour note that can cut through the sweetness.

Create your own bitters by infusing herbs, roots, and barks in alcohol. This is a fun and creative way to experiment with different flavors and create your own unique bitters.

In Conclusion

Bitters are a must-have ingredient in any bartender’s toolkit. They add depth and complexity to cocktails and can be used to create new and exciting flavor combinations. Experiment with different types of bitters and use them to create your own unique cocktails. With bitters, the possibilities are endless.
