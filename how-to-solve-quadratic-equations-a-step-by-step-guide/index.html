How to Solve Quadratic Equations: A Step-by-Step Guide


Quadratic equations may seem like a complicated subject, but in reality, they are not difficult to solve if you follow a few simple rules. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to solve quadratic equations and help you gain a better understanding of the topic.

What Are Quadratic Equations?

Quadratic equations are polynomial equations in which the highest power of the variable is two. They are usually expressed in the following form: ax²+bx+c=0. In this form, a, b, and c are coefficients, with a being the coefficient of the squared term, b being the coefficient of the linear term, and c being the constant term.

The term ‘quadratic’ comes from the Latin root ‘quadra,’ which means ‘square.’ As the name suggests, quadratic equations often have solutions that are perfect squares. The solutions of a quadratic equation can be found using a variety of methods, but the most common method is by using the quadratic formula.

Step-by-Step Guide on Solving Quadratic Equations

Step 1: Determine the Coefficients

The first step in solving a quadratic equation is to determine the three coefficients, a, b, and c, in the quadratic equation ax²+bx+c=0. These coefficients can be obtained from the quadratic equation itself.

Step 2: Apply the Quadratic Formula

Once you have determined the coefficients, you can apply the quadratic formula to solve the equation. The quadratic formula is as follows:

x = (-b ± √(b²-4ac))/2a

In this formula, the ‘±’ symbol represents both the positive and negative solutions of the equation. The term under the square root, b²-4ac, is known as the discriminant.

Step 3: Simplify and Solve

Once you have applied the formula, simplify the expression under the square root and solve for x. You can use basic algebraic techniques to simplify the expression and solve for x.

Step 4: Check the Solution

After you have found the solutions for x, it is important to check if they are valid solutions. To do this, substitute the solutions back into the original equation and verify that they make it true. If the solutions do not make the equation true, you may need to go back and repeat the steps.

Conclusion

Solving quadratic equations is not as difficult as it may seem at first glance. With the help of the quadratic formula and a few basic algebraic techniques, you can solve any quadratic equation. Remember to check your solutions to ensure that they make the equation true and do not forget to practice solving quadratic equations regularly to stay proficient in the subject. We hope this article has been helpful to you and that you now have a better understanding of how to solve quadratic equations.
