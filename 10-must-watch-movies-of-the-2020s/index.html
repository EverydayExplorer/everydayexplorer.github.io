10 Must-Watch Movies of the 2020s


The 2020s have already seen some remarkable films that have captivated audiences with their storytelling, cinematography, and performances. Whether you’re a fan of drama, action, or comedy, there’s something for everyone. Let’s dive into 10 must-watch movies from the 2020s that have made a significant impact on the film industry and captured the hearts of moviegoers around the world.

1. ‘Parasite’ (2019)

Directed by Bong Joon-ho, ‘Parasite’ is a South Korean dark comedy thriller that took the world by storm. The film follows the Kim family as they cunningly infiltrate the wealthy Park family’s household, leading to unexpected and thrilling consequences. With its thought-provoking social commentary and masterful storytelling, ‘Parasite’ rightfully earned the Academy Award for Best Picture, becoming the first non-English language film to do so.

2. ‘Nomadland’ (2020)

Directed by Chloé Zhao, ‘Nomadland’ tells the story of a woman who embarks on a journey through the American West after losing everything in the Great Recession. The film beautifully captures the essence of human resilience and the beauty of the American landscape, earning critical acclaim and numerous accolades, including the Academy Award for Best Picture.

3. ‘Dune’ (2021)

Denis Villeneuve’s adaptation of Frank Herbert’s science fiction novel ‘Dune’ has been hailed as a visual masterpiece. With its stunning visuals, epic storytelling, and stellar cast, including Timothée Chalamet and Zendaya, ‘Dune’ has redefined the science fiction genre for a new generation of moviegoers.

4. ‘Soul’ (2020)

Pixar’s ‘Soul’ takes audiences on a heartwarming and introspective journey as it explores life’s purpose and the beauty of the human experience. With its vibrant animation, compelling characters, and soul-stirring music, ‘Soul’ has resonated with audiences of all ages, earning praise for its emotional depth and thought-provoking themes.

5. ‘Minari’ (2020)

Directed by Lee Isaac Chung, ‘Minari’ is a poignant and tender film that follows a Korean-American family as they pursue the American dream in rural Arkansas. The film’s heartfelt storytelling and authentic performances have made it a standout in the 2020s, earning acclaim for its portrayal of immigrant experiences and family dynamics.

6. ‘Judas and the Black Messiah’ (2021)

Shaka King’s ‘Judas and the Black Messiah’ is a powerful and timely film that delves into the story of Fred Hampton, chairman of the Illinois Black Panther Party, and the FBI informant who infiltrated the organization. With its gripping narrative and standout performances, including Daniel Kaluuya’s portrayal of Fred Hampton, the film has sparked important conversations about social justice and systemic oppression.

7. ‘The Power of the Dog’ (2021)

Directed by Jane Campion, ‘The Power of the Dog’ is a haunting and atmospheric drama that explores themes of masculinity, power, and repressed emotions. With its stunning cinematography and compelling performances from Benedict Cumberbatch and Kirsten Dunst, the film has garnered critical acclaim and awards recognition for its bold storytelling.

8. ‘The French Dispatch’ (2021)

Wes Anderson’s ‘The French Dispatch’ is a visually enchanting and whimsical film that brings to life the stories published in a fictional American magazine based in a French city. With its signature quirky style, star-studded cast, and intricate storytelling, ‘The French Dispatch’ has charmed audiences with its unique blend of humor and heart.

9. ‘A Quiet Place Part II’ (2020)

John Krasinski’s ‘A Quiet Place Part II’ is a nerve-wracking and intense thriller that continues the story of a family living in a post-apocalyptic world inhabited by blind monsters with acute hearing. With its suspenseful sequences and emotional depth, the film has solidified its place as a must-watch sequel in the horror genre.

10. ‘The Mitchells vs. the Machines’ (2021)

From the creative minds at Sony Pictures Animation, ‘The Mitchells vs. the Machines’ is a delightful and visually inventive animated film that follows the quirky Mitchell family as they navigate a robot apocalypse. With its humor, heart, and dazzling animation, the film has charmed audiences and critics alike, making it a standout animated feature of the 2020s.

These 10 must-watch movies from the 2020s offer a diverse range of genres, themes, and storytelling styles, showcasing the creativity and innovation of contemporary cinema. Whether you’re a cinephile or simply looking for captivating entertainment, these films are sure to leave a lasting impression and provide a memorable viewing experience.
