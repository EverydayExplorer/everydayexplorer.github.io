Tips for Designing a Kitchen with an Industrial Edge


Industrial design has become increasingly popular in recent years, as it brings a unique and edgy feel to any space. When it comes to designing a kitchen with an industrial edge, there are several key tips to keep in mind. From choosing the right materials to incorporating raw elements, here are some essential guidelines to consider when designing an industrial-style kitchen.

1. Expose Raw Materials One of the hallmarks of industrial design is the use of raw and exposed materials. When designing a kitchen with an industrial edge, consider leaving brick or concrete walls exposed, or incorporating metal elements such as pipes and beams. These raw materials add character and give the space an authentic industrial feel.

2. Utilize Stainless Steel Stainless steel is a staple in industrial design and is commonly used in kitchens. Not only does it give a sleek and modern look to the space, but it is also durable and easy to clean. Consider incorporating stainless steel appliances, countertops, or even a stainless steel backsplash to enhance the industrial aesthetic.

3. Incorporate Reclaimed Wood Another key element of industrial design is the use of reclaimed wood. This material adds warmth and character to the space, balancing out the coolness of other industrial materials. Consider using reclaimed wood for kitchen cabinetry, open shelves, or even a kitchen island. The natural texture and imperfections of reclaimed wood will add a rustic and authentic feel to the space.

4. Opt for Open Shelving Industrial design often embraces the concept of ‘openness.’ When designing an industrial-style kitchen, consider incorporating open shelving instead of traditional upper cabinets. This not only creates a more visible and accessible storage solution but also adds to the overall industrial aesthetic. Displaying rustic dishes, glassware, or cookware on open shelves adds a touch of personality to the space.

5. Embrace Minimalism Industrial design is often associated with minimalism and simplicity. Avoid clutter and opt for clean lines and a minimalist color palette. Neutral colors such as gray, white, and black work well in industrial kitchens, allowing the raw materials and textures to take center stage. Keep the focus on the chosen industrial elements and avoid unnecessary decorations or excessive accessories.

6. Focus on Lighting Lighting plays a crucial role in any kitchen design, but specifically in an industrial-style kitchen. Aim to incorporate industrial-inspired lighting fixtures such as exposed bulbs, pendant lights, or metal sconces. These fixtures not only provide necessary task lighting but also contribute to the overall industrial aesthetic. Consider installing pendant lights above a kitchen island or hanging exposed bulb fixtures above open shelving for an authentic industrial look.

7. Mix Industrial Elements with Modern Touches While industrial design is characterized by its raw and rustic elements, it doesn’t mean the space has to feel outdated. Mix industrial elements with modern touches to create a contemporary industrial look. For example, pairing stainless steel appliances with sleek, minimalist cabinetry adds a modern twist to the industrial aesthetic. Striking the right balance between industrial and modern elements will give your kitchen a fresh and stylish feel.

8. Incorporate Concrete Countertops Concrete countertops are a popular choice for industrial-style kitchens. Not only are they durable and heat-resistant but they also contribute to the overall industrial aesthetic. The raw and rugged appearance of concrete adds an authentic touch to the space. Pairing concrete countertops with stainless steel appliances and reclaimed wood elements creates a cohesive and visually appealing industrial design.

9. Don’t Forget About Flooring Flooring choice can significantly impact the overall look and feel of a kitchen. Opt for materials that align with the industrial aesthetic, such as concrete, polished concrete, or even reclaimed wood. Patterned tiles can also be a great option, adding visual interest and texture to the space. Ensure that the flooring material is durable and easy to clean, as kitchens are high-traffic areas.

10. Add Industrial Accessories Lastly, don’t forget to incorporate industrial-inspired accessories to complete the look. Vintage-inspired kitchen tools, metal barstools, or industrial-style pendant lights can enhance the industrial vibe. Mix and match different textures and materials to create a visually interesting and cohesive space.

In conclusion, designing a kitchen with an industrial edge requires careful consideration of materials, colors, and overall aesthetics. By following these tips, you can create a unique and stylish industrial-style kitchen that is both functional and visually appealing. Remember to embrace raw and exposed materials, incorporate stainless steel, reclaimed wood, and open shelving, and focus on minimalism and neutral color palettes. With the right combination of industrial elements and modern touches, your kitchen can become a stunning industrial space that you’ll enjoy for years to come.
