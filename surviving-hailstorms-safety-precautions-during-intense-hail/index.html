Surviving Hailstorms: Safety Precautions during Intense Hail


When it comes to severe weather, hailstorms are one of the most destructive forces of nature. Hail can vary in size from small pebbles to large golf balls, and the impact of these frozen projectiles can cause extensive damage to property and pose a serious threat to personal safety.

In order to protect yourself and your belongings during intense hailstorms, it is important to be prepared and take necessary safety precautions. In this blog post, we will discuss effective measures to survive hailstorms and minimize potential risks.

1. Stay Informed: Keep a close eye on weather forecasts and listen to local news for updates on severe weather warnings. Nowadays, with smartphones and weather apps, it’s easier than ever to stay informed. Being aware of the impending hailstorm will give you time to take necessary precautionary measures.

2. Find Shelter: The best way to avoid hail injuries is to find shelter indoors. If you are at home, take cover in the basement or the lowest level of your house. If you don’t have a basement, move to an interior room on the ground floor, such as a bathroom or closet. Stay away from windows and skylights, as they can shatter due to the impact of hailstones. If you are outside, seek refuge in a sturdy building or a vehicle with a solid roof.

3. Protect Your Vehicle: If you are unable to find immediate shelter, it is important to protect your vehicle from hail damage. In case you are caught in a hailstorm while driving, pull over to a safe location away from trees, power lines, and other potential hazards. If possible, park under a covered area or use a car cover or thick blankets to protect your vehicle from hailstones. Avoid parking under bridges or overpasses, as they can become congested and dangerous during severe weather events.

4. Use Protective Gear: If you are outside and unable to find shelter, use protective gear to minimize the risk of injury. Wear a helmet to protect your head from the impact of hailstones. Cover your body with thick clothing, such as a jacket or heavy blanket, to reduce the force of hail hitting your skin. Additionally, it is advisable to wear safety goggles to protect your eyes from flying debris.

5. Secure Outdoor Items: Before a hailstorm hits, secure or bring inside any outdoor objects that can be easily picked up and become hazardous projectiles. This includes patio furniture, garbage cans, plant pots, and children’s toys. These items can be quickly swept up by strong winds during a hailstorm, causing damage to property or posing a risk to people in the vicinity.

6. Document Damage: After the hailstorm has passed, it is important to document any damage to your property or vehicles. Take photographs or videos of the damage as evidence for insurance claims. It is also recommended to contact your insurance provider as soon as possible to report the damage.

7. Seek Professional Assistance: In case of significant damage to your property or vehicles, it is advisable to seek professional help. Contact your insurance company or a reputable restoration service for assistance in repairing the damage caused by the hailstorm. They can guide you through the claim process and ensure that repairs are done correctly and safely.

Remember, safety should always be your top priority during severe weather events like hailstorms. By following these safety precautions and staying informed, you can significantly reduce the risks associated with intense hailstorms and protect yourself and your belongings.

In conclusion, surviving hailstorms requires preparedness and quick action. By staying informed, finding shelter, protecting your vehicle, using protective gear, securing outdoor items, documenting damage, and seeking professional assistance when needed, you can increase your chances of remaining safe during intense hailstorms.
