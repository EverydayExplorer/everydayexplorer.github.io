The Exoplanet Zoo: Strange and Bizarre Discoveries


The fascination with the universe and its mysteries has been an eternal one. Humans have always been curious and have tried to understand the vastness of space and the unknown. One of the most intriguing explorations in space is the search for exoplanets or planets beyond our solar system. Over the years, astronomers have discovered some strange and bizarre exoplanets which have raised many questions and piqued our interest.

Welcome to the Exoplanet Zoo, where we explore some of the most fascinating and unusual exoplanets discovered until now.

It’s interesting to note that we have discovered more than 4,000 exoplanets, and every day, new ones are being found. Some of these exoplanets are so bizarre that they challenge our understanding of the universe and planetary systems. Let’s take a look at some of the strangest and most unusual exoplanets we have come across.

KELT-9b

KELT-9b is the hottest exoplanet discovered yet. It’s a gas giant about twice the size of Jupiter and orbits very close to its host star, KELT-9. KELT-9b’s surface temperature is a scorching 4,300 degrees Celsius, hotter than some stars. It’s so hot that its atmosphere is blasted away from the planet by the star’s radiation, forming a comet-like tail behind it.

TrES-2b

TrES-2b is another strange exoplanet. It is sometimes referred to as the ‘Dark Planet’ because it is less reflective than coal. TrES-2b reflects just 1% of the light that falls on it, making it darker than any other planet discovered in our galaxy. Scientists believe that TrES-2b absorbs almost all of the light that hits it, giving it its unique dark appearance.

HD 189733b

HD 189733b is a blue-colored gas giant exoplanet located 63 light-years from Earth. Its blue color comes from the silicate particles present in its atmosphere. Apart from its color, HD 189733b is known for its strong winds. Its wind speeds can reach up to 8,000 kilometers per hour, which is seven times the speed of sound.

2M1207b

2M1207b is a planetary system that includes a young star and a brown dwarf, which is a failed star that never ignited. The brown dwarf has a diameter of around 60,000 kilometers and is over five times the size of Jupiter. 2M1207b orbits around the brown dwarf and is believed to be a gas giant planet. The most surprising thing about this system is that astronomers believe it has its own moon. This is an extremely rare occurrence as most moons in our solar system orbit planets, not brown dwarfs.

WASP-12b

WASP-12b is another ‘hot Jupiter’ exoplanet that orbits very close to its host star. Its extreme proximity to its host star has caused it to be pulled out of shape, making it more of an egg shape than a sphere. WASP-12b is also known for its strange ‘disappearing’ atmosphere. Scientists believe that the planet’s atmosphere is evaporating as the star’s heat blasts it away. The atmosphere is so large that it creates a huge, glowing, comet-like tail behind the planet.

These are just a few examples of some of the strange and bizarre exoplanets out there. The universe is vast and full of mysteries, and the discovery of these unusual planets only adds to our fascination with the unknown.

The search for exoplanets is an ongoing process, and scientists are constantly developing new methods to detect them. With new technology and discoveries, we can only imagine what else lies out there waiting to be explored.

In conclusion, the exoplanet zoo is a strange yet fascinating place. These exoplanets challenge our understanding of the universe and its planetary systems. Each discovery brings us one step closer to unraveling the secrets of the universe and the possibility of life beyond our solar system.
