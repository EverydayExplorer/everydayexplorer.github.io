The saga of the Flannan Isles Lighthouse unfolds like a chilling maritime fable, fraught with ominous foreboding and perplexing enigmas. In December 1900, three intrepid lighthouse keepers—Thomas Marshall, James Ducat, and Donald MacArthur—were dispatched to man the remote outpost perched upon the craggy cliffs of Eilean Mor, one of the Flannan Isles. Days turned into weeks, and weeks gave way to months as the resolute keepers maintained their vigil atop the desolate isle.
