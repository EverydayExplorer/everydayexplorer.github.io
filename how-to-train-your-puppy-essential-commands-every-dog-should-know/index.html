How to Train Your Puppy: Essential Commands Every Dog Should Know


Bringing a new puppy into your home is an exciting experience, but it also comes with the responsibility of training them to become well-behaved and obedient companions. Training your puppy not only strengthens your bond with them but also ensures their safety and the safety of those around them. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover the essential commands every dog should know to set them up for a lifetime of good behavior.

  1. Sit

  2. Teaching your puppy to sit is one of the first commands you should introduce. It’s a basic obedience command that can be useful in various situations, such as when greeting guests or waiting for their food.

  3. Method: Hold a treat above your puppy’s nose and slowly move it back over their head. As their head follows the treat, their bottom will naturally lower into a sitting position. Once they are sitting, say the word ‘sit’ and reward them with the treat.

  4. Stay

  5. Teaching your puppy to stay is essential for their safety and control in potentially dangerous situations. It teaches them self-control and patience.

  6. Method: Start with your puppy in a sitting position. Hold your hand up in front of them and say ‘stay’ in a firm but gentle voice. Take a step back and reward them if they remain in place. Gradually increase the distance and duration as they improve.

  7. Come

  8. The ‘come’ command is crucial for calling your puppy back to you, whether they’re off-leash in the park or just wandering around the house.

  9. Method: Start in a low-distraction environment and call your puppy’s name followed by the command ‘come.’ When they come to you, reward them with praise and treats. Gradually increase the distance and practice in different environments.

  10. Down

  11. Teaching your puppy to lie down on command can help with impulse control and calming them in stressful situations.

  12. Method: Start with your puppy in a sitting position. Hold a treat in your hand and lower it to the ground in front of them. As they follow the treat, say the command ‘down’ and reward them when they lie down completely.

  13. Leave It

  14. The ’leave it’ command is essential for preventing your puppy from picking up and potentially eating harmful objects or substances.

  15. Method: Hold a treat in one hand and a different, less appealing object in the other hand. Show your puppy the less appealing object and say ’leave it.’ When they look away or ignore the object, reward them with the treat from your other hand.

  16. Drop It

  17. Teaching your puppy to ‘drop it’ is crucial for getting them to release items they shouldn’t have in their mouth, such as shoes or household items.

  18. Method: Offer your puppy a toy or object to play with. When they have it in their mouth, show them a treat and say ‘drop it.’ When they release the object, reward them with the treat and praise.

  19. Heel

  20. The ‘heel’ command teaches your puppy to walk politely beside you without pulling on the leash.

  21. Method: Start walking with your puppy on a leash by your side. Use treats to lure them into the desired position and reward them for walking calmly beside you. Gradually decrease the frequency of treats as they learn to walk nicely on a leash.

  22. Wait

  23. The ‘wait’ command teaches your puppy to pause and wait for your signal before proceeding, whether it’s crossing a road or going through a door.

  24. Method: Start with your puppy on a leash and give the command ‘wait.’ Hold the leash firmly and step in front of them. If they try to move, gently pull them back and repeat the command until they understand to wait for your signal.

Consistency, patience, and positive reinforcement are key when training your puppy. Remember to keep training sessions short and fun to maintain your puppy’s attention and motivation. With time and practice, your puppy will master these essential commands and become a well-behaved and obedient companion for life.
