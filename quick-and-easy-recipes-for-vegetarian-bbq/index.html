Quick and Easy Recipes for Vegetarian BBQ


When you think of a barbecue, the sizzle of meat on the grill might be the first thing that comes to mind. But who says that vegetarians can’t enjoy the joys of a BBQ? In fact, vegetarian BBQ can be just as delicious and satisfying as its carnivorous counterpart. Whether you’re a dedicated vegetarian or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, we’ve got you covered with some quick and easy recipes that will make your next BBQ a hit with vegetarians and meat-eaters alike.

1. Grilled Veggie Skewers

Grilled veggie skewers are a classic and versatile choice for any vegetarian BBQ. You can use a variety of vegetables like bell peppers, zucchini, mushrooms, cherry tomatoes, and red onions. To prepare these skewers, simply chop the veggies into bite-sized pieces, marinate them in your favorite BBQ sauce or a mixture of olive oil, garlic, and herbs, then thread them onto skewers. Grill until they’re tender and slightly charred, and you’ve got a colorful and tasty BBQ side dish.

2. Portobello Mushroom Burgers

Mushroom caps make an excellent meat substitute on the grill. Portobello mushroom burgers are hearty and satisfying, with a meaty texture and a rich umami flavor. To make them, remove the stems, brush the caps with olive oil and your choice of seasonings (garlic, balsamic vinegar, and soy sauce work well), and grill them until they’re tender. Serve them on whole-grain buns with your favorite burger toppings like lettuce, tomato, and avocado.

3. Grilled Corn on the Cob

Corn on the cob is a BBQ staple, and it’s naturally vegetarian. Simply peel back the husks, remove the silk, and brush the corn with melted butter or olive oil. Season with salt, pepper, and your favorite herbs or spices. Then, grill the corn until it’s slightly charred, turning it occasionally. You’ll have a sweet and smoky side dish that everyone will love.

4. Stuffed Bell Peppers

Stuffed bell peppers are a unique and delicious addition to your vegetarian BBQ menu. Hollow out bell peppers and fill them with a mixture of cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, diced tomatoes, and your choice of seasonings. Top with cheese if desired, and grill until the peppers are tender and the filling is heated through. It’s a satisfying main course that’s both flavorful and nutritious.

5. BBQ Tofu Skewers

Tofu is a versatile protein that takes on the flavors of whatever it’s marinated in, making it a perfect candidate for the BBQ. Cut extra-firm tofu into cubes, marinate in your favorite BBQ sauce, and thread onto skewers. Grill until the tofu is crispy on the outside and flavorful on the inside. These skewers are protein-packed and bursting with BBQ goodness.

6. Grilled Portobello Mushroom Steaks

If you’re looking for something a bit more substantial than a burger, try grilling portobello mushroom steaks. Marinate the mushrooms in a mixture of olive oil, balsamic vinegar, garlic, and rosemary, then grill until they’re tender and caramelized. These ‘steaks’ are a mouthwatering centerpiece for your vegetarian BBQ feast.
