Hunting Cottontail Rabbits: Tips and Tactics


Hunting cottontail rabbits can be a thrilling and rewarding outdoor pursuit. These small, elusive creatures are known for their speed and agility, making them a challenging target for hunters. If you’re interested in pursuing cottontail rabbits, this comprehensive guide will provide you with essential tips and tactics to improve your chances of a successful hunt.

Understanding Cottontail Rabbits

Before embarking on a cottontail rabbit hunting adventure, it’s essential to understand your quarry. Cottontail rabbits are small mammals that inhabit various regions across North America. They are named for their distinctive white cotton-like tail, which they flash as a warning signal when they feel threatened.

These creatures are crepuscular, which means they are most active during dawn and dusk. They prefer dense cover for protection and forage on a diet of grasses, clover, and various plants. Cottontail rabbits are known for their agility and evasiveness, which can make them challenging targets for hunters.

Essential Gear for Cottontail Rabbit Hunting

Before you head out into the field, you’ll need to ensure you have the right gear for cottontail rabbit hunting. Here’s a list of essential items:

  1. Shotgun: A 20 or 12-gauge shotgun is a popular choice for cottontail rabbit hunting. Choose a shotgun with a modified choke to give you a balance of spread and range.

  2. Ammunition: Use No. 6 or No. 7½ shot for cottontail rabbits. This shot size provides the necessary pellet density to effectively take down these small game animals.

  3. Hunting Clothing: Wear appropriate camouflage clothing to blend into the environment. Ensure you have warm and waterproof clothing during inclement weather.

  4. Boots: Invest in comfortable and waterproof boots to keep your feet dry and comfortable while tracking rabbits.

  5. Blind or Decoys: These can be useful for creating a concealed shooting position or attracting rabbits.

  6. Calls: While not necessary, predator calls can sometimes be used to attract cottontail rabbits.

  7. Hunting License: Always ensure you have the required hunting permits and licenses for your area.

  8. Game Bag: A game bag or vest will help you carry your harvested rabbits without damaging the meat.

  9. Knife: A sharp knife is essential for field dressing your rabbits.
