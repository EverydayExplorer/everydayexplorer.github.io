10 Iconic Fantasy Series You Should Read


Fantasy literature has the unique ability to transport readers to extraordinary worlds filled with magic, mythical creatures, and epic adventures. The genre has evolved over the decades, producing a plethora of iconic series that have captured the imaginations of millions. In this post, we’ll explore ten of the most iconic fantasy series that every reader should consider diving into. Whether you’re a seasoned fantasy aficionado or a newcomer to the genre, these series promise to enchant and entertain.

1. The Lord of the Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien

Perhaps the most iconic fantasy series of all time, The Lord of the Rings is a must-read for any fan of the genre. Originally published in three volumes between 1954 and 1955, this epic tale follows the journey of Frodo Baggins as he seeks to destroy the One Ring, a powerful artifact created by the Dark Lord Sauron to control Middle-earth.

Tolkien’s richly detailed world-building, intricate languages, and deep lore have set the standard for modern fantasy. The struggle between good and evil, the importance of friendship, and the allure of power are central themes that resonate throughout the series. This trilogy not only introduced readers to unforgettable characters like Gandalf, Aragorn, and Gollum but also inspired countless adaptations, including Peter Jackson’s award-winning film trilogy.

2. A Song of Ice and Fire by George R.R. Martin

The A Song of Ice and Fire series, which inspired the immensely popular television series Game of Thrones, is renowned for its complex characters, intricate political plots, and unpredictable twists. The series, which began with A Game of Thrones in 1996, is set in the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos, where noble families vie for power and the Iron Throne.

Martin’s writing is characterized by its moral ambiguity, where heroes and villains are often indistinguishable. Themes of betrayal, loyalty, and the harsh realities of power are deeply explored, making the series both compelling and thought-provoking. While fans eagerly await the release of the final books, the existing volumes offer a rich tapestry of storytelling that is impossible to ignore.

3. The Wheel of Time by Robert Jordan

Spanning fourteen books, The Wheel of Time is a monumental fantasy series that has captivated readers since its debut in 1990. The story follows Rand al’Thor and his friends as they navigate a world where time is cyclical, and the struggle between Light and Shadow is perpetual.

Jordan’s meticulous world-building, intricate magic system, and expansive cast of characters create a vivid and immersive experience. The series is celebrated for its strong female characters and its exploration of themes such as destiny, sacrifice, and the nature of power. With its recent television adaptation, The Wheel of Time has gained a new audience, making it a prime time for both new and returning readers to delve into this epic saga.

4. His Dark Materials by Philip Pullman

Philip Pullman’s His Dark Materials trilogy, beginning with Northern Lights (published as The Golden Compass in the U.S.), offers a captivating blend of fantasy, philosophy, and adventure. The series follows Lyra Belacqua and her journey through parallel universes, where she encounters daemons, armored bears, and the mysterious substance known as Dust.

Pullman’s work is rich in allegory and explores themes of innocence, knowledge, and the conflict between science and religion. The trilogy challenges readers to ponder profound questions about existence and morality, making it a thought-provoking read for both young adults and adults alike. The recent television adaptation has reignited interest in this beloved series, inviting new readers to discover its wonders.

5. The Chronicles of Narnia by C.S. Lewis

C.S. Lewis’s The Chronicles of Narnia is a classic series that has enchanted readers for generations. Starting with The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe, published in 1950, the series takes readers on a journey to the magical land of Narnia, where children discover talking animals, epic battles, and the eternal struggle between good and evil.

Lewis weaves Christian allegory throughout the narratives, particularly through the character of Aslan, the great lion, symbolizing sacrifice and redemption. The whimsical yet profound nature of Lewis’s storytelling makes this series an ideal read for both children and adults. The enduring popularity of Narnia has led to numerous adaptations, ensuring its place in the hearts of fantasy lovers everywhere.

6. The Earthsea Cycle by Ursula K. Le Guin

Ursula K. Le Guin’s Earthsea Cycle is a beautifully written series that delves into themes of balance, self-discovery, and the consequences of one’s actions. Beginning with A Wizard of Earthsea in 1968, the series follows the journey of Ged, a young wizard, as he learns about magic, power, and his own identity.

Le Guin’s prose is poetic, and her world-building is both imaginative and thought-provoking. The series challenges traditional fantasy tropes, focusing on the importance of understanding and harmony rather than conflict and conquest. Le Guin’s exploration of gender, culture, and spirituality adds depth to her storytelling, making the Earthsea Cycle a timeless classic in the fantasy genre.

7. Mistborn Trilogy by Brandon Sanderson

Brandon Sanderson’s Mistborn Trilogy begins with Mistborn: The Final Empire and introduces readers to a unique world where a dark lord known as the Lord Ruler reigns supreme. The series follows Vin, a street urchin who discovers her powers as a Mistborn, capable of ingesting and using metals to perform magic.

Sanderson’s intricate magic system, combined with his skillful storytelling, creates a gripping narrative filled with twists and turns. Themes of rebellion, trust, and the nature of power are explored throughout the series, making it a thrilling read. With Sanderson’s growing popularity, the Mistborn series has become a cornerstone of modern fantasy literature.

8. The First Law Trilogy by Joe Abercrombie

Joe Abercrombie’s First Law Trilogy, which begins with The Blade Itself, is a gritty and darkly humorous take on the fantasy genre. The series is set in a world where moral ambiguity reigns, and characters are flawed, making for a refreshing departure from traditional hero narratives.

Abercrombie’s writing is sharp and engaging, filled with witty dialogue and complex characters. The series explores themes of power, revenge, and the consequences of violence, all while maintaining a sense of realism in a fantastical setting. With its blend of action, humor, and deep character development, the First Law Trilogy has carved out a unique niche within the fantasy world.

9. The Dark Tower Series by Stephen King

Stephen King’s The Dark Tower Series is a genre-blending epic that combines elements of fantasy, horror, and Westerns. Beginning with The Gunslinger, the series follows Roland Deschain, the last Gunslinger, on his quest to reach the Dark Tower, a mythical structure that serves as the nexus of all universes.

King’s intricate storytelling weaves together multiple narratives and characters from his other works, creating a vast and interconnected universe. Themes of fate, sacrifice, and the nature of reality are central to the series, making it a thought-provoking and compelling read. The Dark Tower series is a testament to King’s versatility as an author and his ability to transcend genre boundaries.

10. The Broken Earth Trilogy by N.K. Jemisin

N.K. Jemisin’s Broken Earth Trilogy begins with The Fifth Season and has garnered critical acclaim for its innovative storytelling and exploration of social themes. Set in a world plagued by catastrophic geological events, the series follows a marginalized group of people known as orogenes, who possess the ability to control seismic activity.

Jemisin’s writing is both lyrical and powerful, addressing issues of race, oppression, and identity through the lens of speculative fiction. The trilogy’s unique narrative structure and complex characters push the boundaries of traditional fantasy storytelling, making it a groundbreaking work in the genre. Jemisin’s exploration of societal issues within a fantastical setting sets a new standard for what fantasy can achieve.

Each of these iconic fantasy series has left a significant mark on the genre, influencing countless authors and captivating readers across generations. Whether you seek epic adventures, intricate worlds, or thought-provoking themes, these ten series offer something for everyone. So grab a book, lose yourself in a fantastical realm, and let your imagination soar!
