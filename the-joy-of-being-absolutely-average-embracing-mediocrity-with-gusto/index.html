The Joy of Being Absolutely Average: Embracing Mediocrity with Gusto


In a world that often celebrates extraordinary achievements and exceptionalism, the idea of embracing mediocrity might seem counterintuitive. We’re bombarded with messages that encourage us to strive for greatness, to stand out from the crowd, and to pursue excellence at all costs. But what if I told you that there’s beauty in being absolutely average? What if I told you that embracing mediocrity with gusto could lead to a life filled with contentment, peace, and fulfillment? Welcome to the joy of being absolutely average.

Act 1: The Myth of Extraordinary

Let’s start by debunking a common myth: the belief that we must constantly strive for greatness in order to find happiness and fulfillment. While there’s nothing wrong with setting ambitious goals and pushing ourselves to excel, the relentless pursuit of perfection can often lead to stress, anxiety, and a sense of never feeling ‘good enough.’ In our quest to be extraordinary, we may overlook the simple joys of everyday life and fail to appreciate the beauty in our own ordinariness.

Act 2: The Power of Acceptance

Enter the joy of being absolutely average, where the pressure to perform is replaced by a sense of acceptance and self-compassion. Instead of constantly comparing ourselves to others and striving to be the best, we can embrace the fact that it’s okay to be average. In fact, being average allows us to focus on what truly matters: cultivating meaningful relationships, pursuing hobbies that bring us joy, and finding contentment in the present moment. As author Brené Brown writes, ‘Authenticity is the daily practice of letting go of who we think we’re supposed to be and embracing who we are.’

Act 3: The Freedom of Unburdening

In a society that often glorifies success and achievement, embracing mediocrity can be a radical act of self-liberation. Rather than constantly chasing after external validation and recognition, we can find freedom in simply being ourselves, flaws and all. When we release the burden of constantly striving for perfection, we create space for authenticity, vulnerability, and true connection with others. As the saying goes, ‘Comparison is the thief of joy.’ By letting go of the need to measure up to impossible standards, we can reclaim our joy and find fulfillment in the simple act of being.

Act 4: The Joy of Enoughness

And so, as we embrace the joy of being absolutely average, we learn to celebrate the beauty in our own ordinariness. We discover that true happiness isn’t found in accolades or achievements, but rather in the small moments of everyday life: a shared laugh with a friend, a quiet moment of reflection, or the warmth of a loved one’s embrace. In a world that often tells us we need to do more, be more, and achieve more, embracing mediocrity becomes a radical act of self-love and self-acceptance. After all, isn’t it liberating to know that we are enough, just as we are?

In conclusion, the joy of being absolutely average invites us to let go of the pressure to perform and instead embrace the beauty in our own ordinariness. So here’s to celebrating our flaws, embracing our imperfections, and finding fulfillment in the simple act of being. After all, being average has never felt so extraordinary.
