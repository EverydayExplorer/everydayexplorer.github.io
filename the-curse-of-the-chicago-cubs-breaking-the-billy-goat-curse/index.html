The Curse of the Chicago Cubs: Breaking the Billy Goat Curse


In the world of sports, curses and superstitions have always fascinated fans and spectators alike. From the Curse of the Bambino that haunted the Boston Red Sox for 86 years to the Curse of the Billy Goat that plagued the Chicago Cubs for over a century, these stories have become woven into the fabric of baseball folklore. Today, we delve into the infamous curse that haunted the Chicago Cubs, and ultimately explore how the curse was finally broken.

The story of the Billy Goat Curse originates back to the 1945 World Series. The Cubs, who had been a dominant force in Major League Baseball in the early 20th century, were vying for their first championship since 1908. As the story goes, a tavern owner named William Sianis purchased two tickets to Game 4 of the World Series between the Cubs and the Detroit Tigers, planning to attend the game with his pet goat named Murphy.

However, when Sianis and Murphy arrived at Wrigley Field, they were told that the goat would not be allowed into the stadium. Outraged, Sianis declared, ‘The Cubs ain’t gonna win no more!’ This was the birth of the so-called ‘Billy Goat Curse.’

Despite Sianis’ curse, the Cubs initially seemed unfazed. They went on to win Game 4, tying the series at 2-2. However, the curse seemed to take hold in Game 5 when the Cubs lost in a heartbreaking 9th inning collapse. From that point forward, the Cubs could not muster a single victory in the series, losing to the Tigers in seven games.

After the World Series defeat, the Cubs’ fortunes took a drastic downturn. They endured decades of disappointment, often coming close but ultimately falling short of a championship. The team became synonymous with curses and the futility that seemed to hang over their heads.

Generations of Cubs fans would grow up hearing stories about the curse, passing down the belief that a billy goat had the power to prevent their beloved team from winning. But as the saying goes, ‘Every curse eventually cracks.’

Fast forward to 2016, the year that would finally break the curse. With a talented roster and the guidance of manager Joe Maddon, the Cubs roared through the regular season, finishing with a league-best record of 103-58. The team’s young core, including Kris Bryant, Anthony Rizzo, and Kyle Schwarber, took the league by storm, capturing the hearts of Cubs fans everywhere.

In the postseason, the Cubs faced formidable opponents but managed to battle their way to the World Series. It was here that the curse would be put to the ultimate test. In a thrilling seven-game series against the Cleveland Indians, the Cubs found themselves down 3-1, facing the prospect of yet another heartbreak.

However, the Cubs never wavered. Led by a resolute and determined group of players, they fought their way back, winning Game 5 and Game 6 to force a decisive Game 7. This was the moment Cubs fans had longed for, a chance to finally break free from the clutches of the curse.

Game 7 proved to be an instant classic. The tension was palpable as both teams battled relentlessly, trading blows in a back-and-forth affair. In the 10th inning, with the score tied at 6-6, the Cubs rallied and scored two runs, taking an 8-6 lead. Closer Aroldis Chapman struck out the final batter, and the Chicago Cubs were crowned World Series champions for the first time in 108 years.

The scenes of jubilation that followed were truly remarkable. Cubs fans, young and old, wept tears of joy as the curse was finally broken. Celebrations erupted across the city of Chicago and beyond, as an entire nation reveled in the historic moment.

The breaking of the Billy Goat Curse was a testament to the resilience and perseverance of the Chicago Cubs organization. They defied decades of disappointment and anguish, rising above the curse to claim their rightful place as champions. It was a triumph that will forever be etched in the annals of baseball history.

In the aftermath of the curse, the Cubs continued to thrive. They made the playoffs in subsequent seasons, showcasing the strength and depth of their roster. While they did not repeat their World Series triumph, the Cubs have firmly established themselves as perennial contenders and have created a new era of success in Chicago.

The Curse of the Billy Goat will forever remain a captivating and beloved part of Cubs folklore. It serves as a reminder that in the realm of sports, curses can be broken, and dreams can come true. The Chicago Cubs’ journey stands as a testament to the unwavering spirit of their fans and the enduring power of hope.
