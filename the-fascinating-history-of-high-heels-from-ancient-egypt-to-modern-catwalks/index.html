The Fascinating History of High Heels: From Ancient Egypt to Modern Catwalks


Over the centuries, high heels have become more than just a fashion statement; they have played a significant role in history, reflecting societal norms, power dynamics, and even revolutions. From their humble beginnings in ancient civilizations to gracing the runways of today, the history of high heels is truly fascinating. Join us on a journey through time as we explore how these stylish yet controversial shoes have evolved.

Our journey begins in ancient Egypt, around 3500 BCE, where high heels first made their appearance. Back then, both men and women wore heeled shoes mainly for practical purposes, such as elevating their feet above dirty and sandy surfaces. The higher the heels, the higher the social status, as it indicated wealth and prominence.

Fast forward to ancient Rome, and we find that high heels took on a different meaning. Roman actors wore elevated shoes on stage to distinguish themselves from the lower-class characters. These early forms of high heels were known as “kothorni” and were not only a symbol of status but also served to make the actors more visible to the audience.

As we move into the Middle Ages, high heels began to take a backseat, with flat shoes becoming the norm. However, in the 15th century, things changed dramatically with the birth of the chopine. This towering shoe, with its platform sole, was worn by noblewomen throughout Europe. The height of the chopine ranged from a few inches to a staggering 18 inches, serving as a clear indicator of wealth and nobility. Walking in these shoes was no easy feat, often requiring the assistance of servants or even walls for support.

The Renaissance period saw a shift in high heel fashion, with men joining the trend. The “Louis” heel, named after King Louis XIV of France, became popular among men in the 17th century. These heels, often red and adorned with intricate designs, symbolized power and authority. The King himself wore heels to appear taller and more imposing, setting a trend that would endure for centuries.

In the 18th century, high heels became more refined and delicate. The iconic red-soled heels, synonymous with luxury and elegance, were introduced by shoemaker Nicholas Lestage. This period also saw the emergence of the stiletto heel, boosting femininity and sensuality.

The 20th century brought significant changes to high heel fashion. The invention of the sewing machine allowed for mass production, making heels more accessible to the general public. The 1920s marked the rise of the flapper era and the introduction of the T-strap heel, a comfortable yet stylish option for women. As the world entered the post-war era, high heels became a symbol of femininity and glamour, epitomized by movie stars like Marilyn Monroe and Audrey Hepburn.

Fast forward to the present day, and high heels continue to dominate the fashion landscape. From the runway to the red carpet, designers and celebrities alike push the boundaries of heel designs. High heels have become a tool for self-expression, with avant-garde designs and unconventional materials taking center stage.

The history of high heels is a testament to the ever-evolving nature of fashion and its deep-rooted connection to culture and society. From their practical beginnings in ancient Egypt to their symbolic power throughout history, high heels have left an indelible mark on the world of fashion. Whether you wear them for their empowering effect or simply to add a touch of glamour, high heels will forever be an iconic and fascinating part of our collective style heritage.
