Kicking It Right: Fundamental Techniques in Soccer Kicking


Soccer, also known as football in many parts of the world, is a sport loved and played by millions of people globally. It’s a beautiful game that requires a combination of skill, strategy, and teamwork. While every aspect of soccer is crucial, one fundamental technique that stands out is kicking. Mastering the art of kicking is essential for players to excel on the field and contribute effectively to their team’s success.

Kicking in soccer involves striking the ball with precision and power using different parts of the foot. Each kick serves a specific purpose, whether it’s for passing, shooting, or controlling the ball. In this blog, we will delve into the fundamental techniques of soccer kicking and explore how players can kick it right!

1. The Instep Kick:
The instep kick, also known as the laces kick, is one of the most common kicks used in soccer. This technique involves striking the ball with the top of the foot, specifically the shoelaces area. The instep kick provides accuracy, power, and control. To execute this kick correctly, players should plant their non-kicking foot next to the ball, keep their head down, and swing their kicking leg through, making contact with the middle of the ball. This kick is perfect for long passes, shooting on goal, or delivering powerful crosses.

2. The Inside of the Foot Pass:
The inside of the foot pass is a versatile and accurate technique used for short to medium-range passes. It involves using the inside of the foot to strike the ball. To perform this pass effectively, players should approach the ball at a slight angle, plant their non-kicking foot next to the ball, and use the inside of their kicking foot to make contact with the middle or lower half of the ball. This pass is ideal for maintaining possession, playing through tight spaces, or setting up teammates in close proximity.

3. The Outside of the Foot Pass:
The outside of the foot pass is a skillful technique that allows players to surprise opponents and deliver accurate passes in various situations. To execute this pass, players should angle their body slightly, open up their hips, and use the outside of their foot to strike the ball. This technique is useful when players need to quickly change the direction of the ball or make a pass around an opponent.

4. The Chip Shot:
The chip shot is an elegant technique often used when players need to lift the ball over an onrushing goalkeeper or defenders. It involves striking the bottom center of the ball with the toes pointed down, creating a lofted trajectory. To execute this shot effectively, players should approach the ball at a slight angle, take a short backswing, and strike the ball with finesse. The chip shot is perfect for scoring goals from tight angles or surprising goalkeepers from a distance.

5. The Volley:
The volley is a spectacular technique that requires excellent timing and coordination. It involves striking the ball while it is in mid-air, without letting it hit the ground. To perform a successful volley, players should position themselves correctly, keep their eyes on the ball, and use the desired kicking technique (instep, inside or outside of the foot) to make contact with the ball at the right moment. The volley is often used for shooting on goal or delivering powerful crosses.

Mastering these fundamental kicking techniques requires practice, patience, and dedication. Players should focus on developing their technique, accuracy, and power through regular training sessions. Additionally, it is important to work on improving foot-eye coordination, balance, and agility to enhance overall kicking proficiency.

In conclusion, kicking is a fundamental skill in soccer that greatly influences a player’s performance on the field. By mastering the instep kick, inside and outside of the foot passes, chip shots, and volleys, players can contribute effectively to their team’s success. So, whether you’re a beginner or an experienced player, make sure to kick it right and elevate your game to new heights!
