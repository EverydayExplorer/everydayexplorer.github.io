Food Plot Planning: Attracting Game to Your Hunting Property


As hunting season approaches, many hunters turn their attention to preparing their hunting properties to attract game. One effective strategy for increasing the presence of game animals on your property is through food plot planning. Food plots can provide a consistent and reliable food source for wildlife, making your property more attractive to deer, turkey, and other game species. In this guide, we’ll explore the key steps involved in planning and establishing successful food plots on your hunting property.

1. Assess Your Property

The first step in food plot planning is to assess your property to determine the best locations for planting. Look for areas with good soil quality, adequate sunlight, and natural cover nearby. Consider the layout of your property and identify potential travel corridors and bedding areas where game animals are likely to frequent.

2. Choose the Right Plants

Selecting the right plants for your food plots is crucial for attracting and sustaining game animals. Choose a variety of plants that provide both food and cover throughout the year. Popular options include clover, chicory, soybeans, and brassicas. Consider the nutritional needs of the target species on your property and choose plants that will meet those needs.

3. Plan Your Planting Strategy

Once you’ve selected the plants for your food plots, it’s time to plan your planting strategy. Determine the size and shape of each plot based on the available space and the specific needs of the plants you’ve chosen. Consider creating a mix of small, secluded plots and larger, open areas to provide diversity and attract a variety of game species.

4. Prepare the Soil

Proper soil preparation is essential for the success of your food plots. Test the soil to assess its pH level and nutrient content, and make any necessary amendments to ensure optimal growing conditions for your chosen plants. Clear the planting area of weeds and debris, and consider using a seed drill or broadcast spreader to ensure even seed distribution.

5. Plant and Maintain Your Food Plots

Once the soil is prepared, it’s time to plant your food plots. Follow the recommended planting depths and spacing for each type of seed, and be sure to water the plots regularly until the seeds germinate. Once the plants begin to grow, monitor the plots regularly for signs of pests or disease, and take appropriate action to protect your investment.

6. Monitor and Adapt

Finally, continue to monitor your food plots throughout the hunting season and beyond. Keep track of which plants are thriving and which may need to be replaced or supplemented. Pay attention to the behavior of game animals on your property and make adjustments to your food plot strategy as needed to maximize their effectiveness.

In conclusion, food plot planning is a valuable tool for attracting game to your hunting property. By carefully selecting and planting the right mix of plants, and by maintaining and adapting your food plots over time, you can create an attractive and sustainable habitat that will draw game animals to your property year after year. With proper planning and care, you can significantly increase your chances of a successful hunting season while also promoting wildlife conservation and habitat enhancement.
