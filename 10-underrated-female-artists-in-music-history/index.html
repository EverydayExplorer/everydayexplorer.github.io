10 Underrated Female Artists in Music History


Throughout history, the music industry has been predominantly male-dominated, with only a few women breaking through the barriers and achieving recognition for their talents. While we often hear about iconic female artists like Madonna, Beyoncé, and Adele, there are countless underrated female musicians whose contributions are often overlooked. In this blog post, we will explore the lives and careers of 10 underrated female artists in music history. These women have defied societal norms, pushed boundaries, and left an indelible mark on the music industry.

1. Sister Rosetta Tharpe

Sister Rosetta Tharpe, often referred to as the ‘Godmother of Rock and Roll,’ was an African-American singer, songwriter, and guitarist who gained popularity in the 1930s and 1940s. Known for her soulful vocals and virtuosic guitar playing, Tharpe was a pioneer in blending gospel music with blues, paving the way for future rock and roll musicians. Despite her immense talent and influence, Sister Rosetta Tharpe remains relatively unknown to many music enthusiasts.

2. Elizabeth Cotten

Elizabeth Cotten was an American folk and blues musician who gained recognition quite late in life. After working as a domestic helper for several years, Cotten’s talent as a guitarist and songwriter was discovered by folk music enthusiasts in the 1950s. Her signature style of fingerpicking, known as ‘Cotten picking,’ revolutionized acoustic guitar playing. Despite her talent and unique musical style, Cotten’s contributions to music are often overlooked.

3. Sister Gertrude Morgan

Sister Gertrude Morgan was an American folk artist and gospel singer who fused her artistic expression with her faith. Morgan created vibrant artwork and composed and performed gospel songs, often using homemade instruments. Her unique blend of music and visual art painted a picture of her spirituality and creativity. Despite her innovative approach and contributions to both the music and art worlds, Sister Gertrude Morgan remains relatively unknown.

4. Lhasa de Sela

Lhasa de Sela was a Mexican-American singer-songwriter known for her mesmerizing vocals and introspective lyrics. Singing in Spanish, English, and French, Lhasa’s music blended elements of folk, Latin, and world music. Her hauntingly beautiful voice and poetic songwriting captivated audiences around the world. Despite critical acclaim and a loyal fan base, Lhasa de Sela’s music often goes unnoticed by mainstream audiences.

5. Laura Nyro

Laura Nyro was an American singer-songwriter who rose to prominence in the 1960s and 1970s. Known for her soulful voice and introspective lyrics, Nyro’s music encompassed a wide range of genres, from pop to jazz to R&B. Her songs have been covered by numerous artists, including Barbra Streisand and The 5th Dimension, but her own recordings remain relatively obscure.

6. Buffy Sainte-Marie

Buffy Sainte-Marie is a Canadian-American singer-songwriter known for her powerful voice and politically charged songwriting. Throughout her career, she has used her platform to advocate for Indigenous rights and bring attention to social issues. Despite her activism and musical talent, Buffy Sainte-Marie’s contributions to the music industry are often overshadowed.

7. Wanda Jackson

Wanda Jackson, often referred to as the ‘Queen of Rockabilly,’ was an American singer-songwriter who paved the way for future female musicians in the male-dominated rock and roll genre. Known for her energetic performances and rebellious attitude, Jackson’s music blended rockabilly, country, and rock and roll. Despite her significant contributions to music history, Wanda Jackson’s name is often not mentioned alongside her male counterparts.

8. Shuggie Otis

Shuggie Otis is an American singer, songwriter, and multi-instrumentalist known for his soulful voice and eclectic musical style. His blend of funk, R&B, and psychedelic rock influenced many musicians in the 1970s. Despite critical acclaim and collaborations with renowned artists like Frank Zappa and Al Kooper, Shuggie Otis remains relatively unknown to the general public.

9. Meiko Kaji

Meiko Kaji is a Japanese singer and actress who gained fame in the 1970s for her roles in several cult films. Her hauntingly beautiful voice and emotionally charged performances made her a beloved figure in Japanese cinema. Despite her popularity in Japan, Meiko Kaji’s music is often overlooked by international audiences.

10. Édith Piaf

Édith Piaf was a French singer and actress who rose to fame in the mid-20th century. Known for her emotional and passionate performances, Piaf’s music resonated with audiences worldwide. Her iconic song ‘La Vie en Rose’ remains one of the most recognizable French songs to this day. However, Piaf’s influence and contributions to music history often go underappreciated.

Conclusion

These 10 underrated female artists in music history have each made significant contributions to the industry. Their unique voices, innovative styles, and powerful performances have left an indelible mark on the music world. Despite facing societal and industry challenges, these artists have persevered and created timeless music that deserves recognition. It’s time to celebrate their talent and ensure that their legacies are not forgotten.
