Artisanal Cheese Tasting 101: From Brie to Gouda


Welcome to the world of artisanal cheese tasting, where every bite takes you on a journey through flavors, textures, and aromas. From the creamy decadence of Brie to the nutty richness of Gouda, there’s a wide variety of cheeses waiting to be explored. In this blog, we will delve into the basics of artisanal cheese tasting, helping you develop a deeper appreciation for these culinary delights.

1. Understanding the Basics:
Artisanal cheese refers to cheese that is handcrafted in small batches, using traditional methods and high-quality ingredients. This distinction sets artisanal cheeses apart from mass-produced ones, resulting in unique and complex flavors. To begin your cheese tasting journey, it is essential to understand the basics of cheese categories.

2. The Categories of Artisanal Cheese:
There are several categories of artisanal cheese, each with its own distinct characteristics. Let’s explore a few popular ones:

- Soft Cheeses: Brie and Camembert fall into this category. They have a creamy texture and a bloomy rind. Pair them with crusty bread or fresh fruits for a delightful experience.

- Hard Cheeses: Examples include Gouda, Cheddar, and Parmesan. These cheeses are aged for extended periods, resulting in a firm texture and intense flavors. Grate them over pasta or enjoy them on a charcuterie board.

- Blue Cheeses: Known for their distinctive blue veins, these cheeses are sharp and tangy. Roquefort and Stilton are famous examples. Pair them with honey or dried fruits to balance their flavors.

- Washed-Rind Cheeses: These cheeses have a pungent aroma and a soft texture. Taleggio and Epoisses belong to this category. Enjoy them with crusty bread and a glass of wine for a truly indulgent experience.

3. The Art of Tasting:
Now that you’re familiar with the various cheese categories, let’s dive into the art of cheese tasting itself. Here are some steps to help you fully appreciate the flavors:

- Appearance: Start by observing the cheese’s appearance. Note the color, texture, and any visible mold or rind.

- Aroma: Gently inhale the cheese’s aroma. Take notice of any earthy, nutty, or fruity notes that waft towards your senses.

- Texture: Take a small piece of cheese and let it rest on your tongue. Pay attention to the texture - is it creamy, crumbly, or firm? Does it melt in your mouth?

- Flavor: Chew the cheese slowly, allowing its flavors to develop on your palate. Is it sweet, salty, or savory? Do you detect any hints of herbs or spices?

- Aftertaste: Finally, pay attention to the cheese’s aftertaste. Does it leave a lingering flavor on your tongue? Is it pleasant and balanced, or does it leave a bitter or metallic taste?

4. Pairing Artisanal Cheese with Accompaniments:
To truly elevate your cheese tasting experience, experiment with pairing different cheeses with complementary accompaniments:

- Fruits: Fresh and dried fruits like grapes, figs, and apricots add a touch of sweetness and provide a refreshing contrast to the richness of the cheese.

- Nuts: Walnuts, almonds, and hazelnuts bring a delightful crunch and nutty flavor that pairs wonderfully with a variety of cheeses.

- Bread and Crackers: Crusty baguette slices, artisanal bread, or buttery crackers act as a neutral base, allowing the cheese to shine.

- Honey and Jams: Drizzle a touch of honey or spread some fruity jams on your cheese to create a harmonious blend of flavors.

- Wine and Beer: Don’t forget to pair your artisanal cheeses with suitable beverages. White wines, red wines, and craft beers can enhance the overall tasting experience.

In conclusion, artisanal cheese tasting is an adventure for your taste buds. By understanding the basics, honing your tasting skills, and exploring various cheese categories, you can unlock a world of flavors and textures. So gather your cheese board, invite your friends, and embark on a delicious journey through the world of artisanal cheese. Enjoy!
