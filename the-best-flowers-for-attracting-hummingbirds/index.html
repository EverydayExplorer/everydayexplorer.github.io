The Best Flowers for Attracting Hummingbirds


Flowers have long been admired for their beauty and their ability to bring joy to our lives. But did you know that certain flowers can also attract these tiny wonders of nature known as hummingbirds? With their iridescent feathers and incredible flight capabilities, hummingbirds are among the most fascinating creatures on our planet. And if you’re a fan of these little acrobats, attracting them to your garden can be a rewarding experience.

Hummingbirds are often drawn to flowers that are brightly colored and have a tubular shape. These characteristics not only catch their attention but also provide a convenient source of nectar. While hummingbirds primarily feed on nectar from flowers, they also rely on insects and spiders for protein. Therefore, it’s essential to provide a diverse range of blooms that cater to both their nectar and insect needs.

One of the best flowers for attracting hummingbirds is the trumpet vine (Campsis radicans). This native North American plant produces large, trumpet-shaped flowers in vibrant shades of red, orange, or yellow. The tubular shape of the blooms is perfect for hummingbirds to feed from, and the vibrant colors are irresistible to these energetic birds. Trumpet vine is a vigorous climber, so it’s ideal for planting along a fence or trellis, where hummingbirds can easily access the flowers.

Another favorite of hummingbirds is the cardinal flower (Lobelia cardinalis). With its intense red blooms, this perennial plant will surely catch the eye of these feathered visitors. The tubular flowers are designed specifically for hummingbirds, allowing them to dip their long bills deep into the flower to access the sweet nectar. Cardinal flowers prefer moist soil and partial shade, making them an excellent choice for planting near a water feature or in a woodland garden.

Salvia is another flower that hummingbirds find irresistible. There are many different varieties of salvia to choose from, but the most popular among hummingbirds is the blue salvia (Salvia guaranitica). This perennial plant produces long, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple, which perfectly complement the vivid green and red feathers of hummingbirds. Blue salvia blooms continuously throughout the summer, providing a reliable source of nectar for these tiny birds.

If you’re looking for a low-maintenance option, consider planting bee balm (Monarda) in your garden. With its unique shape and bold colors, bee balm is not only attractive to hummingbirds but also to bees and butterflies. The tubular flowers of bee balm come in shades of red, pink, purple, and white, and they bloom for an extended period, attracting hummingbirds throughout the summer. Bee balm is also resistant to deer and rabbits, making it an excellent choice for gardens in areas with wildlife.

Honeysuckle (Lonicera) is another flower that hummingbirds find irresistible. This vining plant produces tubular blooms in a range of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and pink. The sweet scent of honeysuckle is often a dead giveaway that there are hummingbirds nearby. Honeysuckle is a fast-growing plant, so it’s best to provide it with a trellis or arbor for support.

For a tropical touch in your garden, try planting the hummingbird flower (Zauschneria californica). This perennial plant features trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red or orange that bloom from late summer to fall, providing a late-season source of nectar for hummingbirds. Hummingbird flower is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for gardens in arid regions.

When designing your garden, remember to provide a mix of annuals and perennials to ensure a continuous supply of nectar throughout the seasons. Some popular annuals that attract hummingbirds include petunias, fuchsias, and impatiens. Perennials like columbine, penstemon, and phlox are also excellent choices.

To attract hummingbirds, it’s crucial to create a garden that provides food, shelter, and fresh water. In addition to planting nectar-rich flowers, consider adding a hummingbird feeder to supplement their diet. These feeders should be placed in a quiet area away from predators and should be cleaned regularly to prevent the spread of diseases.

Creating a hummingbird-friendly garden not only benefits these beautiful creatures but also adds color and vitality to your outdoor space. So why not transform your garden into a haven for hummingbirds by planting a variety of flowers that will attract these tiny marvels? With the right selection of flowers and a little bit of patience, you’ll be rewarded with the sight of these enchanting birds gracing your garden with their presence.
