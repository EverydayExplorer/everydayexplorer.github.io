The Ghosts of the Stanley Hotel: Inspiring Stephen King's 'The Shining'


The Stanley Hotel, located in Estes Park, Colorado, is infamous for its rich history and haunting tales. These ghostly legends have inspired countless writers and filmmakers, including the renowned horror author Stephen King. It is believed that the spirits that reside within the Stanley Hotel played a significant role in inspiring King’s iconic novel, ‘The Shining.’

The Stanley Hotel was built in 1909 by Freelan Oscar Stanley, an inventor and entrepreneur. The grand architecture and scenic surroundings of the hotel quickly made it a popular destination for wealthy vacationers. However, over the years, guests and staff members have reported strange occurrences and eerie encounters within the hotel’s walls.

One of the most famous ghostly inhabitants of the Stanley Hotel is said to be Flora Stanley, Freelan’s wife. She is often seen roaming the hallways of the hotel, dressed in a formal gown. Many guests have reported feeling a cold breeze and hearing the sound of a woman’s laughter echoing through the corridors.

Another ghostly figure frequently spotted at the Stanley Hotel is that of a young boy. It is believed that he is the spirit of a child who tragically died on the premises. Guests have reported seeing him playing in the hallways or even appearing at the foot of their beds.

Perhaps the most well-known paranormal hotspot at the Stanley Hotel is Room 217. This room is said to be occupied by the spirit of Elizabeth Wilson, a former housekeeper. Legend has it that she was injured in an explosion while lighting a lantern in this very room. Guests who dare to stay in Room 217 have reported seeing her ghostly figure, feeling her presence, and even witnessing objects move on their own.

It was during a stay at the Stanley Hotel in 1974 that Stephen King’s fascination with the supernatural was sparked. King and his wife checked into the hotel during its off-season, as it was on the verge of closing for the winter. They were the only guests in the entire building, which created a spooky atmosphere. King’s room, ironically numbered 217, became the inspiration for the infamous Room 237 in ‘The Shining.’

The eerie experiences and ghostly encounters King had at the Stanley Hotel formed the foundation for his chilling novel. The isolated setting, the haunted hallways, and the presence of supernatural beings all found their way into the pages of ‘The Shining.’ The book went on to become a bestseller and later a critically acclaimed film directed by Stanley Kubrick.

The Stanley Hotel still stands today, drawing in visitors from around the world who are intrigued by its haunted reputation. Guests can take ghost tours, stay in the reportedly haunted rooms, or simply soak in the history and atmosphere of this iconic establishment.

In conclusion, the ghostly tales and mysterious happenings at the Stanley Hotel have captivated the imagination of many, including Stephen King. The spirits that reside within its walls continue to inspire and intrigue, leaving visitors and readers alike with a sense of awe and wonder. So, if you’re a fan of the paranormal or simply enjoy a good ghost story, make sure to add the Stanley Hotel to your list of must-visit destinations.
