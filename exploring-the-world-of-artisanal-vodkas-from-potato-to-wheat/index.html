Exploring the World of Artisanal Vodkas: From Potato to Wheat


Artisanal vodkas have been gaining popularity in recent years, with connoisseurs seeking out unique and handcrafted spirits that offer a distinctive taste and character. While traditional vodkas made from grain or potatoes have long been the norm, artisanal vodka distillers are pushing boundaries and experimenting with different ingredients and techniques to create truly exceptional spirits. In this blog post, we will explore the world of artisanal vodkas, specifically focusing on the production of vodkas made from potato and wheat.

Potato vodka is known for its smooth and creamy texture, as well as its rich and earthy flavor profile. This style of vodka has a long history, particularly in Eastern European countries where potatoes are abundant. In order to make potato vodka, distillers start by selecting the best quality potatoes, preferably those that are high in starch content. These potatoes are then crushed, fermented, and distilled, often through a process called rectification. This process involves multiple distillations to ensure a clean and pure final product.

One of the most famous potato vodkas in the world is made in Poland, where it is known as ‘Zubrowka.’ This vodka is infused with a grass called bison grass, giving it a unique and slightly herbal taste. Zubrowka is often served with apple juice or in cocktails like the classic ‘White Bison,’ which combines Zubrowka with apple juice and lime.

Moving on to wheat vodka, this style offers a lighter and more delicate flavor compared to potato vodka. Wheat is a common ingredient in vodka production, particularly in Western countries. Just like with potato vodka, the first step in making wheat vodka is to choose high-quality wheat grains. After the grains are milled, they are mixed with purified water to create a mash. This mash is then fermented and distilled to remove impurities and produce a clear and smooth vodka.

One notable example of wheat vodka is Grey Goose, a French vodka known for its refined and elegant taste. Grey Goose is produced using French winter wheat, which is sown in October and harvested in August. This particular wheat variety is chosen for its soft and rich character, resulting in a vodka with subtle floral and citrus notes.

Whether you prefer the robustness of potato vodka or the refined elegance of wheat vodka, exploring the world of artisanal vodkas can be a thrilling and enriching experience. Artisanal distillers are constantly innovating, introducing new ingredients and techniques to create vodkas that satisfy the discerning palates of spirits enthusiasts.

When it comes to selecting an artisanal vodka, it’s essential to consider factors such as the quality of ingredients, the distillation process, and the reputation of the distillery. Many artisanal vodka producers prioritize using locally sourced ingredients and sustainable practices, making their spirits not only delicious but also environmentally conscious.

In conclusion, artisanal vodkas made from potato and wheat offer a wide range of flavors and experiences for those looking to expand their vodka repertoire. From the rich and earthy notes of potato vodka to the delicate elegance of wheat vodka, there is something for everyone in the world of artisanal spirits. So why not elevate your next cocktail or sipping experience by delving into the fascinating world of artisanal vodkas? Cheers to exploration and the pursuit of extraordinary taste!
