Building a Trap for Small Game: Survival Hunting Techniques


When it comes to survival hunting, building a trap for small game can be a crucial skill to have. Whether you find yourself in a survival situation or simply want to hone your bushcraft skills, knowing how to construct an effective trap can mean the difference between going hungry and enjoying a meal. In this guide, we’ll explore various techniques for building traps to catch small game, providing you with the knowledge to increase your chances of success in the wild.

Understanding Small Game Trapping

Before we delve into the specifics of trap construction, it’s essential to understand the principles behind small game trapping. Small game, such as rabbits, squirrels, and birds, are often skittish and quick, making them challenging to catch through traditional hunting methods. Traps offer a passive and efficient means of securing a meal without expending excessive energy.

Types of Traps

Snare Traps

Snare traps are simple yet effective devices for catching small game. To construct a snare trap, you’ll need a flexible wire or cord and a noose. Locate an area frequented by your target game, such as a game trail or feeding ground. Set up the snare by fashioning a small loop in the cord and securing it to a stationary object, then positioning the noose in the path of the animal. When the animal moves through the noose, the loop tightens around its neck or body, restraining it.

Deadfall Traps

Deadfall traps are designed to crush the small game beneath a heavy object, typically a large rock or log. To build a deadfall trap, prop up the heavy object with a trigger mechanism, such as a stick or bait. When the game disturbs the trigger, the weight falls, trapping the animal underneath. Deadfall traps require careful construction to ensure they are both effective and humane.

Pitfall Traps

Pitfall traps involve creating a concealed pit in the ground, often with spikes or sharpened stakes at the bottom. Cover the pit with a thin layer of natural material to disguise it, then entice the game to step onto the covering, causing it to fall into the pit. Pitfall traps can be labor-intensive to construct but can yield successful results when executed correctly.

Constructing a Trap

When building a trap for small game, it’s essential to consider the local environment, the behavior of your target game, and the available resources. Take note of animal tracks, droppings, and other signs of activity to identify suitable trap locations. Additionally, camouflage the trap with natural materials to blend it into the surroundings, increasing its chances of success.

Legal and Ethical Considerations

Before setting up traps for small game, it’s crucial to familiarize yourself with local hunting and trapping regulations. Ensure that you have the necessary permits and understand the legalities surrounding trapping in your area. Furthermore, always prioritize ethical trapping practices, aiming to minimize the suffering of the game and avoid catching non-target species.

Conclusion

Building a trap for small game is a valuable skill for survival hunting and bushcraft enthusiasts. By understanding the principles of small game trapping and mastering various trap construction techniques, you can enhance your ability to secure food in a wilderness setting. Remember to approach trapping with respect for the environment and the animals you seek to catch, and always prioritize safety and ethical considerations in your hunting endeavors. With practice and perseverance, you can develop the expertise to build effective traps for small game, increasing your self-reliance and survival capabilities in the great outdoors.
