The Best Destinations to Visit for Lunar New Year and other Cultural Festivals


As we enter a new year, it’s time for us to start planning our travels for the next 12 months. One of the best ways to experience different cultures and traditions is by attending their annual festivals. Whether it’s the Lunar New Year, Diwali, or Hanami, each festival offers a unique insight into the history and customs of the country. In this blog, we’ll explore some of the best destinations to visit for Lunar New Year and other cultural festivals.

1. China for Lunar New Year

The Lunar New Year, also known as the Spring Festival, is the biggest festival in China and is celebrated over a 15-day period. It is a time where families gather together, exchange gifts and enjoy festive food. During the celebrations, you’ll see beautiful lanterns and hear the roar of fireworks. Red is the prominent color for the Lunar New Year, as it is believed to bring good luck and ward off evil spirits. Make sure to visit Tiananmen Square, where you’ll see a stunning display of fireworks and performances.

2. India for Diwali

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is India’s most significant festival, celebrated by Hindus, Sikhs, and Jains worldwide. It signifies the triumph of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance. During Diwali, you’ll see buildings, homes, and streets lit up with decorative lights and candles. People exchange sweets and gifts, wear new clothes, and decorate their homes with Rangoli (colorful geometric designs made from rice or sand). Make sure to visit Jaipur during Diwali for its grand display of fireworks, and the beautiful Amber Fort also lights up.

3. Japan for Hanami

In Japan, Hanami translates to ‘flower viewing.’ It is celebrated every year in early April when cherry blossom trees are in full bloom. Japan’s Hanami tradition dates back over a thousand years and is a time for people to celebrate the beauty of nature with picnics under the trees. Cherry blossoms, called sakura in Japanese, are symbolic of the fleeting and fragile nature of life. Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka all have beautiful parks and gardens for you to enjoy Hanami.

4. Thailand for Songkran

Songkran is the traditional New Year in Thailand, celebrated from the 13th to the 15th of April. During the festival, the streets come alive with water fights as people spray each other with water guns and buckets. It is a symbol of purification and the washing away of old negativity. Make sure to visit Chiang Mai for its grand celebrations, including parades, traditional dances, elephant shows, and temple offerings.

5. Mexico for Dia de los Muertos

Dia de los Muertos, or the Day of the Dead, is a Mexican holiday celebrated from October 31st to November 2nd. It is a time for people to remember their loved ones who have passed away. The celebration is filled with colorful decorations, candles, and marigold flowers, believed to guide the souls of the dead back to the land of the living. The festivities include traditional foods, music, and dancing. Make sure to visit Mexico City for its grand festival that includes parades, live music, and dancing.

In conclusion, attending cultural festivals gives you a unique opportunity to experience the traditions, art, craft, and food of different communities. Travel to the destinations mentioned above during the festivals to soak in the vibrant atmosphere, meet new people and learn about the culture. Remember to stay respectful of local customs and traditions when participating in each event. Pack your bags and travel to these destinations for a once-in-a-lifetime experience!
