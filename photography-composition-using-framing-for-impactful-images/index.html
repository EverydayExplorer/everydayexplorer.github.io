Photography Composition: Using Framing for Impactful Images


Have you ever looked at a photograph and just felt blown away by its composition? Maybe the way the subject is positioned just so, or the interesting angles and lines that draw your eye in. What you’re experiencing is the impact of good composition. A photograph’s composition refers to the way the different elements of an image are arranged within the frame, and it’s a key ingredient in making a great photo.

One of the most important aspects of composition is framing. Framing is the way you use the elements in the scene to guide the viewer’s eye towards the subject of the photo. It’s like creating a window into the world that directs the viewer’s attention just where you want it.

There are a few different techniques you can use when it comes to framing. Here are a few good ones to keep in mind:

1. Using natural lines: This technique is all about looking for the natural lines in a scene and using them to guide the viewer’s eye towards the subject. For example, if you’re taking a photo of a road, the lines of the road can be used to draw the viewer in.

2. Using contrasting colors: If your subject stands out against its background because of a bright or contrasting color, you can use that to your advantage by positioning the subject in a way that makes it stand out even more. This can be especially effective in a landscape image.

3. Using foreground elements: Adding a foreground element to your image creates a sense of depth and also helps guide the viewer’s eye towards the subject. For example, if you’re taking a landscape photo, you might find a rock or a tree to include in the foreground.

4. Using framing devices: Another option is to use a physical object in the scene to create a frame around the subject. For example, you might take a photo of a person through a window or a doorway.

Regardless of which technique you use, the key is to think about how you want to guide the viewer’s eye towards the subject of the photo, and then use the elements in the scene to achieve that objective.

One thing to keep in mind as you experiment with framing is that sometimes less is more. You don’t want to overwhelm the viewer with too many competing elements. Instead, try and keep it simple and focused on your subject. A well-framed image can have a big impact on the viewer, and can even make an otherwise ordinary subject look extraordinary.

If you’re just getting started with photography and composition, framing is definitely a technique you’ll want to explore. Remember that composition is all about experimentation, so don’t be afraid to try new things and see what works for you. With practice, you’ll start to develop an eye for what makes a great photo, and framing will be just one tool in your toolkit.
