How to Help Students Who Struggle with Reading


Reading is a fundamental skill that forms the basis for academic success and lifelong learning. However, some students may find reading challenging and struggle to keep up with their peers. As an educator or parent, it’s crucial to recognize the signs of reading difficulties and provide the necessary support to help these students overcome their challenges. In this blog post, we will explore effective strategies for helping students who struggle with reading.

1. Identify Reading Difficulties Early

Early identification of reading difficulties is essential for effective intervention. Look out for signs such as:

  • Difficulty recognizing letters and sounds.
  • Poor phonemic awareness (ability to manipulate individual sounds).
  • Struggles with decoding and word recognition.
  • Limited vocabulary.
  • Difficulty comprehending and retaining what is read.

If you notice any of these signs, it’s important to intervene promptly to prevent reading difficulties from escalating.

2. Collaborate with a Reading Specialist

Working closely with a reading specialist or tutor can make a significant difference in helping struggling readers. These professionals are trained to assess reading abilities, diagnose specific difficulties, and develop tailored interventions. Collaborate with them to create an individualized learning plan that addresses the specific needs of the struggling reader.

3. Foster a Love for Reading

Creating a positive reading environment is crucial in motivating struggling readers. Offer a variety of reading materials that match their interests and reading levels. Encourage them to explore different genres and formats, such as graphic novels, magazines, and audiobooks. Make reading enjoyable by incorporating interactive activities, such as acting out a story or creating illustrations. By fostering a love for reading, students are more likely to engage in independent reading and improve their skills over time.

4. Implement Multisensory Instruction

Multisensory instruction is a research-based approach that engages multiple senses (visual, auditory, kinesthetic) to enhance learning. This method is particularly effective for students with reading difficulties, as it helps them make stronger connections between letters, sounds, and words. Consider incorporating activities that involve tracing letters in sand, manipulating letter tiles, or using interactive computer programs that provide feedback. These multisensory strategies can improve phonics skills and boost reading fluency.

5. Break Down Tasks into Smaller Steps

Reading can be overwhelming for struggling readers, so breaking down tasks into smaller, manageable steps can help build their confidence. For instance, break a complex reading passage into smaller paragraphs or sentences and gradually work on comprehension skills. Provide visual aids, such as graphic organizers or charts, to help students organize their thoughts and make connections between ideas. Breaking down tasks into smaller steps makes reading more approachable and allows struggling readers to experience success along the way.

6. Provide Targeted Intervention

In addition to the general reading activities, students who struggle with reading may benefit from targeted intervention programs. These programs, such as Orton-Gillingham, Wilson Reading System, or Barton Reading, are designed specifically for students with reading difficulties. These structured programs provide explicit instruction in phonics, decoding, spelling, and reading comprehension. Consult with a reading specialist to determine the most appropriate intervention program for the struggling reader’s needs.

7. Encourage Regular Practice

Consistent practice is key to improving reading skills. Encourage struggling readers to engage in regular reading activities, both in school and at home. Set aside dedicated time for independent reading and provide opportunities for guided practice. Use technology tools, such as online reading programs or apps, to make reading practice more engaging and interactive.

8. Offer Supportive Feedback and Encouragement

Struggling readers need a supportive and positive learning environment. Provide feedback that focuses on their effort, progress, and specific areas of improvement. Celebrate their accomplishments, no matter how small, to boost their confidence and motivation. Encourage them to take risks and persevere, even when faced with challenges. By creating a supportive atmosphere, you can help struggling readers build resilience and develop a growth mindset.

9. Involve Parents and Guardians

Collaboration between teachers, parents, and guardians is crucial in supporting struggling readers. Communicate regularly with parents, informing them about their child’s progress, areas of improvement, and strategies that can be implemented at home. Provide resources and suggestions for reading activities that parents can do with their child. Encourage parents to read aloud to their child and discuss books together to further develop their reading skills.

10. Monitor Progress and Adjust Instruction

Closely monitor the progress of struggling readers and adjust instruction as needed. Regular assessments, such as running records or fluency checks, can help track growth and identify areas that require further support. Modify instruction based on the student’s progress, providing additional practice or introducing more challenging reading materials once they have developed foundational skills.

In conclusion, helping students who struggle with reading requires a targeted and individualized approach. By identifying difficulties early, collaborating with specialists, fostering a love for reading, implementing multisensory instruction, breaking down tasks, providing targeted intervention, encouraging regular practice, offering supportive feedback, involving parents, and monitoring progress, we can empower struggling readers to become proficient and confident readers.
