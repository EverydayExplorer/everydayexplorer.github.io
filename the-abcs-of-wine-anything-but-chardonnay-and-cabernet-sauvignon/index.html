The ABCs of Wine: Anything But Chardonnay and Cabernet Sauvignon


When it comes to exploring the world of wine, most people tend to gravitate towards the familiar and well-known varietals like Chardonnay and Cabernet Sauvignon. While these wines certainly have their merits, there is a whole alphabet of other exciting and unique wines waiting to be discovered. In this blog post, we will dive into the ABCs of wine, focusing on Anything But Chardonnay and Cabernet Sauvignon.

A - Albariño: Originating from Spain, Albariño is a white wine with refreshing acidity and notes of citrus and tropical fruits. It pairs perfectly with seafood and is a great alternative to Chardonnay for those seeking a lighter and crisper wine.

B - Barbera: Hailing from Italy’s Piedmont region, Barbera is a red wine known for its juicy acidity and bright cherry flavors. It is a versatile wine that can be enjoyed with a variety of dishes, from pasta to grilled meats.

C - Chenin Blanc: Chenin Blanc is a white wine that thrives in regions such as the Loire Valley in France and South Africa. It showcases flavors ranging from crisp apple and pear to honey and floral notes. Chenin Blanc can be enjoyed as a dry, off-dry, or even a sweet wine, making it incredibly versatile.

D - Dolcetto: Another red wine from Italy, Dolcetto is known for its soft tannins and flavors of blackberry and plum. It is a great everyday wine that pairs well with a range of Italian dishes, such as pizza and pasta.

E - Encruzado: This white wine varietal is native to Portugal and produces wines with a rich texture, vibrant acidity, and flavors of peach, apricot, and citrus. Encruzado is a delightful alternative to Chardonnay for those looking for something a bit different.

F - Falanghina: Originating from Italy’s Campania region, Falanghina is a white wine with bright acidity and flavors of citrus, peach, and pear. It pairs well with seafood and light pasta dishes.

G - Grenache: Grenache is a red wine varietal that is widely planted around the world. It produces wines with ripe red fruit flavors, medium body, and soft tannins. Grenache is often used in blends but can also shine on its own.

H - Hárslevelű: This Hungarian white wine is known for its aromatic qualities and flavors of apricot, honey, and spice. It is a fantastic alternative to Chardonnay and pairs well with Asian cuisine.

I - Inzolia: Inzolia is a white wine grape that is primarily grown in Sicily, Italy. It produces wines with a medium body and flavors of citrus, apple, and almond. Inzolia is a great choice for those seeking a refreshing and vibrant white wine.

J - Jacquère: Jacquère is a white wine grape native to the Savoie region in France. It produces wines with crisp acidity, delicate floral aromas, and flavors of green apple and lemon. It is a fantastic choice for those who enjoy light and refreshing wines.

K - Kékfrankos: Kékfrankos, also known as Blaufränkisch, is a red wine grape that is widely grown in Central Europe. It produces wines with flavors of blackberry, cherry, and spice. Kékfrankos is a great alternative to Cabernet Sauvignon for those seeking a medium-bodied, fruit-forward red wine.

L - Lambrusco: Lambrusco is a sparkling red wine from Italy. It is known for its vibrant red fruit flavors, lively bubbles, and refreshing acidity. Lambrusco is a fun and versatile wine that pairs well with a variety of foods.

M - Marsanne: Marsanne is a white wine grape that is often blended with Roussanne in the Rhône Valley of France. It produces wines with flavors of ripe pear, honey, and nuts. Marsanne is a great alternative to Chardonnay for those seeking a fuller-bodied white wine.

N - Nero d’Avola: Nero d’Avola is a red wine grape that is indigenous to Sicily, Italy. It produces wines with flavors of black cherry, plum, and spice. Nero d’Avola is a fantastic choice for those who enjoy bold and fruit-forward red wines.

O - Orvieto: Orvieto is a white wine from the Umbria region of Italy. It is a blend of several grape varieties, including Trebbiano and Grechetto. Orvieto wines are typically crisp, with flavors of citrus, apple, and floral notes.

P - Pinotage: Pinotage is a red wine grape that is unique to South Africa. It produces wines with flavors of ripe red and black fruit, smoky undertones, and a hint of earthiness. Pinotage is a bold and robust wine that pairs well with grilled meats.

Q - Quarts de Chaume: Quarts de Chaume is a sweet white wine from the Loire Valley in France. It is made from Chenin Blanc grapes that have been affected by noble rot. Quarts de Chaume wines are lusciously sweet, with flavors of honey, apricot, and marmalade.

R - Riesling: Riesling is a versatile white wine grape that is grown in many wine regions around the world. It produces wines with a range of styles, from bone-dry to lusciously sweet. Riesling wines are known for their vibrant acidity, floral aromas, and flavors of citrus, stone fruits, and honey.

S - Syrah: Syrah, also known as Shiraz in some regions, is a red wine grape that produces bold and full-bodied wines. Syrah wines are known for their flavors of blackberry, black pepper, and spice. They pair well with grilled meats and hearty stews.

T - Tempranillo: Tempranillo is a red wine grape that is primarily grown in Spain. It produces wines with flavors of cherry, plum, and leather, along with a hint of earthiness. Tempranillo is a fantastic alternative to Cabernet Sauvignon for those seeking a medium-bodied and food-friendly red wine.

U - Ugni Blanc: Ugni Blanc, also known as Trebbiano, is a white wine grape that is widely planted in France and Italy. It produces wines with crisp acidity, flavors of citrus and green apple, and a light-bodied profile. Ugni Blanc is often used for blending but can also be enjoyed on its own.

V - Verdejo: Verdejo is a white wine grape that is native to Spain. It produces wines with bright acidity, flavors of citrus, peach, and tropical fruits, and a herbal note. Verdejo is a fantastic alternative to Chardonnay for those seeking a zesty and aromatic white wine.

W - Welschriesling: Welschriesling is a white wine grape that is grown in several countries, including Austria and Hungary. It produces wines with flavors of green apple, pear, and white flowers. Welschriesling is a refreshing and easy-drinking wine.

X - Xarel·lo: Xarel·lo is a white wine grape that is primarily grown in the Catalonia region of Spain. It produces wines with vibrant acidity, flavors of citrus and green apple, and a hint of minerality. Xarel·lo is often used in the production of Cava, Spain’s sparkling wine.

Y - Yarra Valley: The Yarra Valley is a wine region in Victoria, Australia, known for producing high-quality cool-climate wines. The region is particularly renowned for its Chardonnay and Pinot Noir, but it also produces excellent examples of other varietals such as Sauvignon Blanc and Syrah.

Z - Zinfandel: Zinfandel is a red wine grape that is primarily grown in California. It produces wines with bold flavors of blackberry, raspberry, and spice. Zinfandel can be enjoyed in a range of styles, from lighter-bodied and fruit-forward to full-bodied and robust.

By exploring the ABCs of wine, we can expand our horizons and discover a world of exciting and unique flavors. Whether you’re a fan of white wines or prefer the boldness of reds, there is an alternative to Chardonnay and Cabernet Sauvignon that is sure to delight your palate. So next time you’re browsing the wine aisle, don’t be afraid to try something different and embrace the diversity that the world of wine has to offer. Cheers!
