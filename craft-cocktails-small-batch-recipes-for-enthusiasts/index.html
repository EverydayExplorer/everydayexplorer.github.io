Craft Cocktails: Small Batch Recipes for Enthusiasts


*Disclosure: This blog post contains affiliate links. If you click through and make a purchase, we may earn a commission at no additional cost to you.*

Craft cocktails have become increasingly popular in recent years, as enthusiasts rediscover the art of mixing drinks with high-quality, small-batch ingredients. Gone are the days of basic cocktails made with generic liquors and artificial additives. Today, cocktail enthusiasts are embracing the creativity and complexity that comes with crafting their own delicious concoctions at home.

In this blog post, we will explore the world of craft cocktails and share some small-batch recipes that will surely impress your guests. Whether you’re a seasoned mixologist or just getting started, these recipes are bound to elevate your cocktail game to new heights. So grab your shaker, prepare your ingredients, and let’s get mixing!

1. Classic Manhattan
- 2 ounces of bourbon or rye whiskey
- 1 ounce of sweet vermouth
- 2-3 dashes of Angostura bitters
- Maraschino cherry, for garnish (optional)
- Orange twist, for garnish (optional)

To make a Classic Manhattan, simply combine the bourbon or rye whiskey, sweet vermouth, and bitters in a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir gently until well-chilled and strain into a chilled cocktail glass. Garnish with a maraschino cherry or an orange twist, if desired.

2. Negroni
- 1 ounce of gin
- 1 ounce of Campari
- 1 ounce of sweet vermouth
- Orange twist, for garnish

To make a Negroni, mix equal parts of gin, Campari, and sweet vermouth in a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir until well-combined and strain into a chilled rocks glass filled with ice. Garnish with an orange twist and enjoy the perfect balance of bitterness and sweetness.

3. Moscow Mule
- 2 ounces of vodka
- 1 tablespoon of freshly squeezed lime juice
- 4-6 ounces of ginger beer
- Lime wheel, for garnish
- Mint sprig, for garnish (optional)

To make a refreshing Moscow Mule, combine vodka and lime juice in a copper mug filled with ice. Top it off with ginger beer, give it a gentle stir, and garnish with a lime wheel. For an extra touch of freshness, add a mint sprig as a garnish.

4. Old Fashioned
- 2 ounces of bourbon or rye whiskey
- 1 sugar cube
- 2-3 dashes of Angostura bitters
- Orange twist, for garnish
- Maraschino cherry, for garnish (optional)

For an Old Fashioned, muddle the sugar cube and bitters in an old-fashioned glass. Add a splash of water and stir until the sugar has dissolved. Fill the glass with ice, add the bourbon or rye whiskey, and give it a gentle stir. Garnish with an orange twist and a maraschino cherry, if desired.

5. Mojito
- 2 ounces of rum
- 1 tablespoon of freshly squeezed lime juice
- 2 teaspoons of sugar
- 4-6 fresh mint leaves
- Soda water, to top
- Lime wheel, for garnish
- Mint sprig, for garnish (optional)

To create a refreshing Mojito, muddle the sugar, lime juice, and mint leaves in a highball glass. Add rum and crushed ice, then top it off with soda water. Give it a gentle stir and garnish with a lime wheel and a mint sprig if desired. Sip on this delightful cocktail on warm summer days!

Craft cocktails are not only about the drinks themselves but also the quality and uniqueness of the ingredients. Experiment with different spirits, bitters, fruits, and garnishes to create your own signature cocktails. By using small-batch ingredients, you can ensure the highest quality and flavor in every sip.

So whether you’re entertaining guests or simply indulging in a solo happy hour, these craft cocktail recipes are sure to impress. Remember to drink responsibly and always enjoy in moderation. Cheers!
