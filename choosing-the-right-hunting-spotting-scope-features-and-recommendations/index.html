Choosing the Right Hunting Spotting Scope: Features and Recommendations


When it comes to hunting, having the right equipment is crucial. One piece of gear that can greatly improve your hunting experience is a spotting scope. Spotting scopes are designed to provide hunters with a magnified view of distant objects, making it easier to spot game and evaluate their size and behavior. However, with so many different options available on the market, choosing the right spotting scope can be a daunting task. In this blog post, we will discuss the features you should consider when selecting a spotting scope and provide some recommendations to help you make an informed decision.

1. Magnification:
One of the most important features to consider when choosing a spotting scope is the magnification power. The magnification determines how much closer the object will appear when viewed through the scope. While higher magnification may seem desirable, it can also make the image shakier and darker, especially in low light conditions. For general hunting purposes, a spotting scope with a magnification range of 20-60x is typically sufficient.

2. Objective Lens Diameter:
The objective lens diameter is another important factor to consider. The size of the objective lens determines the amount of light that enters the spotting scope, which directly affects the image brightness and clarity. A larger objective lens diameter allows for more light transmission and is ideal for low light conditions. A spotting scope with an objective lens diameter of 60-80mm is recommended for hunting.

3. Field of View:
The field of view refers to the width of the observable area when looking through the spotting scope. A wider field of view is beneficial for locating and tracking moving targets. However, keep in mind that a wider field of view often comes at the expense of magnification power. It’s important to strike a balance between a wide field of view and adequate magnification for your hunting needs.

4. Lens Coating:
Lens coatings are applied to spotting scopes to minimize glare, increase light transmission, and enhance image quality. Look for spotting scopes that feature multi-coated or fully multi-coated lenses. These coatings help reduce reflections and improve the overall clarity and contrast of the image. Additionally, some spotting scopes may have special coatings like anti-reflective coatings or hydrophobic coatings, which can further enhance performance in different environmental conditions.

5. Durability:
Hunting can be physically demanding, and your spotting scope will likely be exposed to rough handling and harsh weather conditions. Therefore, it’s essential to choose a spotting scope that is rugged and durable. Look for spotting scopes that are made of high-quality materials, preferably with a rubber armor coating to provide a secure grip and protect against impacts. Additionally, spotting scopes that are waterproof and fog-proof are highly recommended, as they can withstand moisture and temperature changes without compromising the image quality.

With these features in mind, here are a few recommendations for spotting scopes that are widely regarded by hunters:

- Vortex Optics Diamondback HD Spotting Scope: This spotting scope offers a range of magnification options, excellent image quality, and a durable construction. It is known for its exceptional value for money.

- Leupold SX-5 Santium HD Spotting Scope: Leupold is a renowned optics manufacturer, and this spotting scope lives up to the brand’s reputation. It provides crystal-clear images, a wide field of view, and superior low-light performance.

- Nikon Prostaff 5 Fieldscope: Nikon is known for its quality optics, and the Prostaff 5 spotting scope is no exception. It offers a compact design, great image quality, and is budget-friendly, making it an excellent choice for beginner hunters.

- Swarovski STR 80 Spotting Scope: If budget is not a concern, the Swarovski STR 80 is considered one of the best spotting scopes on the market. It provides unparalleled image quality, superb light transmission, and exceptional durability. However, it comes with a higher price tag.

In conclusion, choosing the right hunting spotting scope is essential for improving your hunting experience. Consider factors such as magnification, objective lens diameter, field of view, lens coating, and durability when making your decision. The recommended spotting scopes mentioned in this blog post are highly regarded by hunters, but always remember to choose a spotting scope that suits your specific needs and budget. So, invest in a good spotting scope and take your hunting game to the next level!
