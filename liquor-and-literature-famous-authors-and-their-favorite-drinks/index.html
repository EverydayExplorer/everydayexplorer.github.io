Liquor and Literature: Famous Authors and Their Favorite Drinks


As readers, we often find ourselves immersed in the worlds created by our favorite authors, but have you ever wondered what fueled their creativity? It’s no secret that many famous writers had a penchant for a good drink, and their favorite tipples often played a significant role in their lives and work. In this blog post, we’ll take a journey through the drinks that fueled the creativity of some of the most celebrated literary figures in history.

Ernest Hemingway, known for his larger-than-life persona, was particularly fond of mojitos. This classic Cuban cocktail made with rum, lime juice, sugar, soda water, and mint was a favorite of Hemingway, who famously enjoyed them at La Bodeguita del Medio in Havana. It’s said that he once even wrote a recipe for the perfect mojito, which can still be found framed on the wall of the bar.

Moving across the Atlantic, we encounter the iconic figure of F. Scott Fitzgerald, whose love for gin was well-documented. The author of ‘The Great Gatsby’ was known to enjoy a good gin rickey, a refreshing concoction of gin, lime juice, and soda water. This classic cocktail perfectly embodies the glitz and glamour of the Jazz Age, which Fitzgerald so vividly captured in his writing.

On the other side of the spectrum, we have the enigmatic and reclusive Emily Dickinson, whose drink of choice was a simple glass of sherry. Dickinson was known for her introspective and contemplative poetry, and it’s easy to imagine her sitting down with a glass of sherry, lost in thought as she penned her profound verses.

Moving into the realm of fantasy, we come to J.R.R. Tolkien, the mastermind behind ‘The Lord of the Rings’ series. Unsurprisingly, given his love for creating richly detailed worlds, Tolkien was known to enjoy a good pint of beer. In fact, he even created his own Elvish-inspired language for the word ‘beer’ – ‘barley wine’ in Quenya.

Not to be left out, the legendary Agatha Christie, queen of mystery, had a taste for a good dry martini. With her sharp wit and keen observational skills, it’s easy to imagine her sipping on a martini as she plotted the intricate twists and turns of her famous whodunits.

It’s fascinating to see how these iconic authors’ drink choices often reflected their personalities and writing styles. Whether it was the boldness of a mojito, the sophistication of a gin rickey, the contemplative nature of sherry, the earthiness of beer, or the elegance of a martini, these drinks undoubtedly left their mark on the literary world.

So, the next time you pick up a novel by one of these literary giants, take a moment to raise a glass in their honor, and perhaps even enjoy a sip of their favorite drink. After all, there’s something undeniably charming about the idea of sharing a drink with the authors who have shaped our literary landscape. Cheers to the writers who found inspiration in a glass, and to the stories they left behind for us to savor.
