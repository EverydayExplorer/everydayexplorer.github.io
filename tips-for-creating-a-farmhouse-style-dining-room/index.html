Tips for Creating a Farmhouse Style Dining Room


Are you looking to create a warm and inviting dining space with a touch of country charm? Look no further than the farmhouse style. This popular interior design trend embraces rusticity and simplicity, making it perfect for creating a cozy dining room that encourages family gatherings and memorable meals. In this blog post, we will share some valuable tips and insights on how to create a farmhouse style dining room that will become the heart of your home.

1. Choose the Right Dining Table

The centerpiece of any dining room is undoubtedly the dining table. When it comes to farmhouse style, opt for a solid wood table with a distressed or weathered finish. The more rustic the table looks, the better! Consider a farmhouse-style trestle table or a rectangular table with turned legs for an authentic look.

2. Incorporate Reclaimed Wood

To achieve an authentic farmhouse look, consider incorporating reclaimed wood into your dining room. This could be in the form of a farmhouse-style sideboard, floating shelves, or even a reclaimed wood accent wall. Reclaimed wood adds character, warmth, and a touch of history to your dining space.

3. Embrace Neutral Colors

Farmhouse style is all about embracing neutral colors. Think whites, creams, beiges, and light grays for your walls, furniture, and accessories. These colors create a calming and relaxed atmosphere while allowing other design elements to shine.

4. Mix and Match Seating

Gone are the days of having a matching set of dining chairs. In a farmhouse-style dining room, mixing and matching seating is encouraged. Consider using a combination of wooden chairs, benches, and even upholstered options to create an eclectic and inviting look. You can unify the seating by using coordinating fabrics or paint colors on different pieces.

5. Invest in a Farmhouse Chandelier

Lighting plays a crucial role in setting the ambiance in any dining room. A statement chandelier with rustic or industrial elements is a must-have for a farmhouse-style dining room. Look for fixtures made of distressed metal, wrought iron, or reclaimed wood to add an instant farmhouse charm to your space.

6. Layer with Textures and Patterns

To make your farmhouse dining room visually interesting, layer different textures and patterns. Consider using a chunky knit throw draped over the back of a chair, a braided jute rug under the table, and patterned curtains on the windows. Mixing textures like wood, metal, linen, and burlap will add depth and visual interest to your space.

7. Display Farmhouse Accessories

Accessorizing your dining room is an opportunity to add personality and a touch of whimsy to your farmhouse-style design. Display vintage-inspired accessories such as antique signs, galvanized metal buckets, mason jars, and farmhouse-inspired artwork. These small touches will further enhance the rustic charm of your dining space.

8. Incorporate Natural Elements

Farmhouse style embraces nature and the outdoors. Incorporate natural elements like fresh flowers, potted herbs, or even a small indoor herb garden into your dining room decor. These elements not only add beauty but also invigorate your space with a fresh and invigorating aroma.

9. Don’t Forget the Table Settings

No farmhouse-style dining room is complete without thoughtfully set table settings. Opt for vintage-inspired china, simple white linens, and mismatched silverware to add a touch of nostalgia and charm. Experiment with different table arrangements, such as a burlap runner, decorative chargers, or seasonal centerpieces, to create a personalized and inviting table.

10. Keep it Cozy

Last but not least, a farmhouse-style dining room should be cozy and welcoming. Add plush seat cushions, oversized pillows, and soft throws to the seating area to create a comfortable space for lingering conversations and memorable meals.

In conclusion, creating a farmhouse-style dining room is all about embracing rusticity, simplicity, and warmth. By following these tips and incorporating elements like reclaimed wood, neutral colors, mixed seating, farmhouse accessories, and natural elements, you can transform your dining space into a charming farmhouse retreat. Remember, the key is to create a cozy, inviting atmosphere that encourages family and friends to gather and enjoy each other’s company. Now, go ahead and create the farmhouse dining room of your dreams!
