The Art of Winged Eyeliner: Mastering the Cat Eye Look


If there’s one makeup trend that never goes out of style, it’s the classic winged eyeliner. Also known as the cat eye, this iconic look has been loved and mastered by beauty enthusiasts for decades. With its timeless appeal and ability to instantly elevate any makeup look, it’s no wonder that the art of winged eyeliner continues to captivate and inspire.

But mastering the perfect cat eye can be a challenge, especially for those who are new to the world of makeup. The precision required to create a flawless wing can be intimidating, but fear not! With a few key tips and tricks, you’ll be rocking the cat eye look like a pro in no time.

First things first, let’s talk about the tools you’ll need. Investing in a good quality liquid eyeliner is essential for achieving a clean and precise line. The liquid formula glides smoothly across the lid, allowing for greater control and ease of application. Additionally, having a small angled brush on hand can help you perfect the winged flick at the outer corner of the eye.

Before diving into the application process, it’s important to prep your eyelids. Start by applying a primer to create a smooth canvas and ensure that your eyeliner stays in place throughout the day. If you have oily eyelids, consider setting the primer with a translucent powder to prevent any smudging or creasing.

Now, let’s get down to business. Begin by drawing a thin line along your upper lash line, starting from the inner corner and working your way outwards. Take your time and keep the line as close to the lashes as possible. This will create the illusion of fuller lashes and provide a solid base for the wing.

Next, it’s time to create the wing. Position the angled brush or the tip of your liquid eyeliner at the outer corner of your eye, angled towards the tail end of your eyebrow. Using small, gentle strokes, gradually extend the line upwards, creating a flick that follows the natural shape of your eye. The length and angle of the wing is entirely up to you, so feel free to experiment and find a style that suits your eye shape and personal preference.

Once you’ve created the wing, it’s time to connect it to the main eyeliner. Starting from the tip of the wing, draw a line towards the center of your eyelid, following the curve of your lash line. Take your time and make sure the line is smooth and even. If you find it challenging to create a straight line, you can use small dots as guides and then connect them.

To complete the look, fill in any gaps and ensure that the line is bold and defined. If desired, you can also add a touch of eyeliner to your lower lash line, starting from the outer corner and stopping halfway. This will help balance the overall look and add a hint of drama.

Remember, practice makes perfect. Don’t be discouraged if your first few attempts don’t turn out exactly as planned. Winged eyeliner takes time and patience to master, but with enough practice, you’ll become a pro in no time.

To make your cat eye look even more dramatic, consider adding a coat of mascara to your lashes or applying false lashes for added volume and length. And don’t forget to pair your perfectly winged eyeliner with a bold lip or a neutral eyeshadow look to let your eyes take center stage.

In conclusion, mastering the cat eye look is all about practice, precision, and a steady hand. With the right tools, techniques, and a little bit of confidence, you’ll soon be creating flawless winged eyeliner looks that will turn heads wherever you go. So grab your eyeliner, take a deep breath, and let your creativity soar as you master the art of winged eyeliner.
