Crime Solving 101: Mystery Books for Aspiring Detectives


Are you a fan of mysteries? Do you dream of becoming a detective and solving crimes? If so, you’re in luck! In this blog post, we’ll dive into the world of mystery books and explore some must-read titles for aspiring detectives like yourself. Whether you’re just starting your journey or have been a fan of the genre for years, these books are sure to keep you on the edge of your seat and sharpen your crime-solving skills.

1. ‘The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo’ by Stieg Larsson: This international bestseller introduces us to Lisbeth Salander, a brilliant hacker with a troubled past. When a wealthy businessman hires investigative journalist Mikael Blomkvist to solve the mystery of his niece’s disappearance, the duo finds themselves entangled in a web of dark secrets and corruption. With its intricate plot and complex characters, this book is a must-read for any aspiring detective.

2. ‘Gone Girl’ by Gillian Flynn: This psychological thriller takes us on a twisted journey through the crumbling marriage of Nick and Amy Dunne. When Amy goes missing on their fifth wedding anniversary, Nick becomes the prime suspect. As the police investigation unfolds, the reader is taken on a rollercoaster ride of deception, manipulation, and unexpected twists. Flynn’s brilliant storytelling and unreliable narrators make ‘Gone Girl’ a gripping read for any aspiring detective.

3. ‘The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes’ by Arthur Conan Doyle: No list of mystery books would be complete without the iconic detective Sherlock Holmes. This collection of short stories introduces us to the brilliant mind of Holmes and his loyal companion, Dr. John Watson. From solving baffling cases like ‘A Scandal in Bohemia’ to unraveling the mysterious ‘Hound of the Baskervilles,’ Holmes’s deductive reasoning and keen observation skills make him a legendary figure in the world of crime-solving.

4. ‘The Da Vinci Code’ by Dan Brown: In this fast-paced thriller, Harvard symbologist Robert Langdon finds himself on a quest to unravel a centuries-old secret. Teaming up with cryptologist Sophie Neveu, Langdon must decipher hidden codes and symbols to uncover the truth behind a murder in the Louvre Museum. With its blend of art, history, and religious conspiracy, ‘The Da Vinci Code’ is a gripping read that will put your detective skills to the test.

5. ‘Big Little Lies’ by Liane Moriarty: While not strictly a traditional mystery novel, ‘Big Little Lies’ is a captivating story that delves into the dark secrets and lies lurking beneath the surface of a seemingly perfect community. When a murder occurs at a school trivia night, the reader is left guessing who the victim and the perpetrator are until the very end. Moriarty’s skillful storytelling and well-developed characters make this book a page-turner for any aspiring detective.

As you dive into these mystery books, take note of the techniques used by the authors to keep readers engaged and guessing. Pay attention to the pacing, plot twists, and character development. Challenge yourself to solve the mysteries alongside the detectives and see if you can uncover the truth before they do.

Remember, reading mystery books is not only a thrilling form of entertainment, but it can also help develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills. So grab a cup of tea, curl up with one of these books, and embark on your own crime-solving adventure. Happy reading, aspiring detectives!
