The Best Game Calls for Hunting: How to Attract Your Target


Hunting is a popular outdoor activity that requires skill, patience, and the right equipment. One important tool in a hunter’s arsenal is a game call. Game calls are devices used to mimic the sounds made by animals in order to attract them to a particular area. Whether you are a seasoned hunter or a beginner, having the best game calls can greatly improve your chances of success in the field. In this article, we will explore some of the top game calls on the market and discuss how to effectively use them to attract your target.

1. Duck Calls:
Duck hunting is a popular pursuit for many outdoor enthusiasts. To attract ducks, you need a high-quality duck call that accurately replicates the sounds made by these birds. One excellent option is the Zink Calls Power Hen PH-2 Double Reed Duck Call. This call offers a wide range of tones and pitches, allowing you to create realistic duck sounds that will catch the attention of passing flocks.

When using a duck call, it’s important to master different calling techniques. Start with soft, subtle quacks to imitate a contented duck. As ducks get closer, increase the volume and intensity of your calls to create a sense of urgency. Incorporating feeding chuckles and greeting calls into your repertoire will also make your setup more convincing.

2. Turkey Calls:
Turkey hunting requires specific calls to attract gobblers during the mating season. One highly recommended turkey call is the Primos Hunting Hook Hunter Turkey Mouth Call. This diaphragm call produces realistic yelps, purrs, and clucks that will make even the most cautious turkeys come running.

When using a turkey call, it’s important to practice different calls to portray the right scenario. Start with soft yelps to imitate a lonely hen. As you attract the attention of a tom, simulate excited clucks and purrs to convey interest. Mixing in gobbles can also trigger a response from dominant toms looking for a fight.

3. Deer Calls:
Deer hunting is a popular activity among hunters, and using the right game call can greatly enhance your chances of success. The Illusion Systems Extinguisher Deer Call is a top choice for many hunters. With an adjustable reed system, it allows you to produce a wide range of deer vocalizations, from doe bleats to aggressive grunts.

When using a deer call, it’s important to understand the vocalizations that deer make and what they mean. A series of doe bleats can be used to attract a curious buck, while aggressive grunts can be used to simulate a territorial challenge. By understanding the behavior and vocalizations of deer, you can effectively use a deer call to bring game within shooting range.

4. Predator Calls:
If you enjoy predator hunting, a high-quality predator call is essential. The FOXPRO Shockwave Electronic Predator Call is a versatile option that offers a wide range of sounds, from distressed prey to coyote vocalizations. With its remote control and loudspeaker system, it allows you to effectively control and manipulate your calling setup.

When using a predator call, it’s crucial to imitate the sounds of distressed prey or rival predators. Start with soft distress calls to pique the interest of predators in the area. As you attract them closer, switch to more aggressive vocalizations such as coyote howls or territorial challenges. Implementing pauses and changes in volume can also add realism to your calling setup.

In conclusion, having the best game calls for hunting is crucial for attracting your target. Whether you are hunting ducks, turkeys, deer, or predators, there are specific calls available that can help you effectively replicate the sounds of these animals. By mastering different calling techniques and understanding the behavior of your target species, you can greatly increase your chances of success in the field. Choose the right game calls for your hunting style and practice using them to improve your skills. Happy hunting!
