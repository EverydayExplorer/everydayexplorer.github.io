Survival Fishing Techniques: Catching Food in the Wilderness


If you find yourself stranded in the wilderness without any access to food, one of the most essential survival skills you can have is the ability to catch food through fishing. Fishing not only provides you with a reliable source of sustenance, but it can also be a therapeutic and enjoyable activity in stressful situations. In this blog post, we will discuss some essential survival fishing techniques that can help you catch food in the wilderness.

Before diving into the techniques, it’s crucial to choose the right fishing gear for survival situations. A compact fishing kit is ideal for survival scenarios as it is lightweight and easy to carry. The essentials of a survival fishing kit include fishing line, hooks of various sizes, swivels, split shots, and small lures or bait. It’s also advisable to include an emergency fishing kit, which often contains more robust gear like a collapsible fishing rod or pocket-sized fishing reel.

Now, let’s discuss some effective survival fishing techniques that can increase your chances of catching food in the wilderness:

1. Handline Fishing: This is perhaps the simplest yet effective survival fishing method. It involves using a fishing line, hook, and bait without the need for a fishing rod. Simply tie the hook to the end of the fishing line and attach the bait. Cast the line into the water and wait for a bite. Once you feel a tug on the line, slowly reel in the fish and secure your meal.

2. Improvised Fishing Rod: In the absence of a fishing rod, you can make an improvised one using the resources available in the wilderness. Find a long and flexible tree branch, preferably one with a slight curve. Attach your fishing line to one end of the branch and tie a hook with bait to the other end. Make sure to reinforce the connection with some cordage or vine. Use the improvised fishing rod to cast your line into the water and wait for a fish to bite.

3. Trapping: Another effective survival fishing technique is setting up fish traps. This method allows you to catch fish without actively monitoring the trap. You can construct a simple fish trap using found materials like rocks, sticks, and a net made from clothing or parachute cord. Find a shallow area in a river or stream and set up the trap in a way that directs fish into the net. Leave the trap overnight or for several hours and check it later to see if you’ve caught any fish.

4. Spear Fishing: Spear fishing requires some skill and patience, but it can yield great results in survival situations. Look for shallow pools or slow-flowing sections of the water where fish might congregate. Craft a sturdy spear with a sharp pointy tip, either by sharpening a long stick or attaching a makeshift spearhead. Stand or crouch in the water, and when you spot a fish, swiftly thrust the spear towards it. Aim for a quick and accurate strike to increase your chances of success.

5. Improvised Nets: If you can find suitable materials like vines, clothing, or parachute cord, you can make an improvised net to catch fish in the wilderness. Construct a frame using sturdy sticks or branches and weave the netting material around it. When setting up, be sure to position the net in an area where fish are likely to swim through. Leave the net in the water for some time and periodically check it for any caught fish.

Remember, in survival situations, it’s crucial to be patient and persistent when fishing for food. Different environments and conditions may require experimentation with various techniques and locations. It’s also important to research and understand the local fish species and their habits to increase your chances of success.

In conclusion, survival fishing techniques are essential skills for anyone venturing into the wilderness. Handline fishing, improvised fishing rods, fish traps, spear fishing, and improvised nets are all effective methods to catch food in survival scenarios. Carrying a compact fishing kit and knowing how to utilize natural resources around you can significantly improve your chances of securing a meal in the wild. So, familiarize yourself with these techniques, practice them, and be prepared for any survival situation that may arise. Stay safe and happy fishing!
