Comic Book Tropes We Hate to Love: From Retcons to Deus Ex Machina


Comic books have always been a beloved medium, captivating readers with their colorful characters and thrilling storylines. However, within this vibrant world, there are certain tropes that have become a double-edged sword for fans. These tropes, while often frustrating, have a unique ability to keep us hooked and coming back for more. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most infamous comic book tropes that we hate to love.

1. Retcons: Retcons, short for retroactive continuity, are a common tool used by comic book writers to alter or rewrite previously established events in a character’s history. While retcons can sometimes be necessary for plot development or to fix inconsistencies, they can also be a source of frustration for fans who have grown attached to a character’s established backstory. The constant shifting and reshaping of a character’s history can feel disorienting and undermine the emotional investment fans have made in their favorite heroes or villains.

2. Deus Ex Machina: Deus Ex Machina, Latin for ‘god from the machine,’ refers to a plot device where an unexpected, contrived event or character suddenly resolves a seemingly impossible situation. While this trope can create exciting and unexpected plot twists, it can also leave readers feeling cheated. When a seemingly insurmountable obstacle is resolved by a convenient and often unexplained intervention, it can undermine the tension and believability of the story. It’s like having a superhero show up out of nowhere to save the day without any foreshadowing or logical explanation.

3. Damsels in Distress: The trope of the damsel in distress has long been a staple of comic books. Despite the progress made in portraying strong and independent female characters, there are still instances where female characters are reduced to helpless victims in need of rescue. While this trope may have its roots in the classic hero’s journey, it can perpetuate harmful stereotypes and undermine the agency of female characters. It’s time for comic books to break free from this tired narrative and give female characters the chance to shine as heroes in their own right.

4. Clichéd Origin Stories: Origin stories are an essential part of comic book lore, providing readers with the background and motivations of their favorite characters. However, there are times when these stories become overly formulaic and predictable. The ’tragic accident’ or ‘alien from another planet’ origins can feel repetitive and uninspired. Comic book writers should strive to find new and inventive ways to introduce their characters, giving readers fresh and engaging narratives that defy expectations.

Despite our frustrations with these tropes, they continue to persist in the comic book world because, deep down, we can’t help but be drawn to them. They have become an integral part of the medium, shaping the stories and characters we love. While we may roll our eyes at the predictable plot twists or the clichéd origin stories, we keep turning the pages, eager to see what comes next.

In conclusion, comic book tropes like retcons, deus ex machina, damsels in distress, and clichéd origin stories may frustrate us at times, but they also hold a strange allure. They keep us invested in the world of comics, fueling our fascination with these larger-than-life characters and their extraordinary adventures. So, as much as we hate to love them, we can’t deny their enduring appeal.
