The Best Fashion Documentaries to Watch in 2024


As we step into 2024, the world of fashion continues to evolve, showcasing a dynamic blend of creativity, culture, and innovation. For fashion enthusiasts, there’s no better way to dive deep into this vibrant industry than through documentaries that reveal the stories behind the designs, the designers, and their impact on society. Here’s a curated list of the best fashion documentaries to watch this year, each offering a unique perspective on the multifaceted world of style.

1. “The First Monday in May”

This documentary takes viewers behind the scenes of the Met Gala, one of the most anticipated events in the fashion calendar. Following the 2015 exhibit “China: Through the Looking Glass,” the film explores the interplay between fashion and celebrity, art and commerce. It highlights the meticulous planning, the artistic vision, and the careful consideration that goes into creating a spectacle that captures the public’s imagination. The film is a testament to how fashion can serve as a powerful form of cultural expression and commentary.

2. “McQueen”

“McQueen” is a poignant exploration of the life and legacy of the late Alexander McQueen, one of the most influential designers of our time. The documentary chronicles his journey from a working-class boy in London to a global fashion icon. Through interviews, archival footage, and stunning visuals of his iconic runway shows, the film captures McQueen’s genius and the emotional struggles he faced throughout his career. It’s a must-watch for anyone interested in the darker side of fashion and the pressures that come with fame.

3. “Franca: Chaos and Creation”

This documentary pays tribute to Franca Sozzani, the legendary editor-in-chief of Vogue Italia. Through intimate interviews with Sozzani and those who knew her best, the film delves into her innovative approach to fashion journalism and her commitment to using fashion as a platform for addressing social issues. From her groundbreaking editorials to her visionary collaborations, “Franca: Chaos and Creation” showcases how one woman can change the narrative of an entire industry.

4. “The September Issue”

A classic in the realm of fashion documentaries, “The September Issue” offers a rare glimpse into the processes behind the production of Vogue’s most important issue of the year. Following Anna Wintour, the formidable editor-in-chief, and her team, the film highlights the challenges and triumphs of putting together the September issue. It’s a fascinating look at the power dynamics in fashion and the creative decisions that shape trends.

5. “Dior and I”

“Dior and I” takes viewers into the world of haute couture as it chronicles the debut collection of Raf Simons for the iconic fashion house, Christian Dior. The documentary captures the intense pressures of the fashion calendar, as Simons races against the clock to create a collection that pays homage to Dior’s legacy while also establishing his own vision. The film beautifully contrasts the artistry of fashion design with the logistical challenges of the industry.

6. “The True Cost”

This documentary tackles the often-overlooked aspects of the fashion industry: its environmental impact and the ethical concerns surrounding fast fashion. “The True Cost” takes viewers on a journey through various countries, revealing the human and environmental costs of producing cheap clothing. It raises important questions about consumerism and encourages viewers to rethink their purchasing habits. This documentary is a wake-up call for anyone who loves fashion and wants to make more informed choices.

7. “House of Z”

“House of Z” follows the rise, fall, and resurgence of designer Zac Posen. The film chronicles his journey from a prodigy in the fashion world to facing the harsh realities of the industry, including financial struggles and personal challenges. With candid interviews and behind-the-scenes footage, it’s an inspiring story of resilience and creativity. Posen’s ability to bounce back and reinvent himself serves as a powerful reminder of the ever-changing nature of fashion.

8. “The Gospel According to André”

This documentary explores the life of André Leon Talley, a trailblazer in the fashion industry known for his flamboyant style and deep knowledge of fashion history. The film chronicles his journey from the segregated South to becoming the first African American creative director at Vogue. Through personal anecdotes and reflections on his career, Talley shares insights about diversity in fashion and the importance of representation. His story is both inspiring and enlightening, making this documentary a must-watch.

9. “Fashion’s Last Tycoon”

“Fashion’s Last Tycoon” is a deep dive into the life and career of the legendary designer Karl Lagerfeld. The documentary provides an intimate look at Lagerfeld’s creative process, his influence on fashion, and his larger-than-life personality. Through interviews with friends, colleagues, and fashion insiders, the film paints a portrait of a man who was not just a designer but a cultural icon. It’s a fascinating exploration of the impact one individual can have on an entire industry.

10. “Killing Eve: Fashion as Art”

While not a traditional fashion documentary, “Killing Eve” has impacted the fashion world with its unique style and costume design. This documentary examines how the show’s costumes have influenced fashion trends and showcased the artistry of costume designer Phoebe de Gaye. By highlighting the intersection of television and fashion, it offers a fresh perspective on how clothing can tell stories and shape characters.

11. “In Vogue: The 90s”

This documentary takes a nostalgic look at the fashion scene in the 1990s, exploring how the decade shaped contemporary fashion. Featuring interviews with models, designers, and editors, it showcases the iconic looks and trends that defined the era. From grunge to minimalism, “In Vogue: The 90s” celebrates the creativity and innovation that emerged during this pivotal time in fashion history.

12. “Rising Fashion”

“Rising Fashion” focuses on the next generation of designers who are changing the landscape of the fashion industry. This documentary highlights emerging talents from diverse backgrounds, showcasing their unique perspectives and innovative designs. By shedding light on these rising stars, the film emphasizes the importance of inclusivity and sustainability in the future of fashion. It’s an inspiring reminder that the industry is constantly evolving, and new voices are always emerging.

13. “The Fashion Fund”

This documentary series follows the CFDA/Vogue Fashion Fund, which aims to support emerging designers in the fashion industry. The film chronicles the challenges and triumphs of the finalists as they navigate the competitive landscape of fashion. With insights from industry experts and established designers, “The Fashion Fund” provides a behind-the-scenes look at the support system helping to nurture the next generation of talent.

14. “Halston”

“Halston” chronicles the life and career of the iconic American designer Roy Halston Frowick, who was known for his glamorous and innovative designs in the 1970s. This documentary explores how Halston became a leading figure in fashion, blending art, culture, and celebrity. It delves into his rise to fame, his collaborations with artists like Andy Warhol, and the eventual decline of his empire. Halston’s story is a captivating exploration of creativity, fame, and the impact of personal choices on one’s career.

15. “The Wardrobe: A Love Story”

This touching documentary explores the emotional connection many people have with their clothing. It features interviews with various individuals who share their stories about significant pieces in their wardrobes, revealing how fashion can serve as a form of self-expression and memory. “The Wardrobe: A Love Story” goes beyond the surface of fashion, illustrating the profound impact clothing can have on our identities and personal history.

Conclusion

As we embrace 2024, these fashion documentaries provide a compelling window into the diverse narratives that shape the industry. Whether you’re a seasoned fashionista or a curious newcomer, these films will inspire, educate, and challenge your perceptions of fashion. From the intricate artistry of haute couture to the pressing ethical concerns within the industry, each documentary offers a unique perspective that contributes to the ongoing conversation about fashion’s role in society. So grab your popcorn, settle in, and prepare to be both entertained and enlightened by the captivating world of fashion through these must-watch documentaries.
