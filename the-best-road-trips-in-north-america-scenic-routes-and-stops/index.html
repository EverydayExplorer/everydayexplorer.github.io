The Best Road Trips in North America: Scenic Routes and Stops


When it comes to epic road trips, North America has some of the most scenic routes and breathtaking stops. From rugged coastlines to towering mountains, there is something for everyone to enjoy. Whether you are a nature lover, an adventure seeker, or simply looking to explore the great outdoors, these road trips are sure to have you in awe. In this blog post, we will be delving into the best road trips in North America, highlighting the most scenic routes and must-see stops along the way.

1. Pacific Coast Highway, California
Stretching for approximately 655 miles along the coast of California, the Pacific Coast Highway is renowned for its spectacular ocean views and iconic stops. Beginning in the picturesque town of Monterey and ending in sunny San Diego, this road trip offers a diverse range of landscapes. Highlights include Big Sur, where towering cliffs meet the crashing waves, and the stunning beach towns of Santa Barbara and Malibu. Don’t forget to stop at the world-famous Bixby Creek Bridge for a photo opportunity you won’t want to miss.

2. Icefields Parkway, Alberta, Canada
Considered one of the most scenic drives in the world, the Icefields Parkway runs for 144 miles through the Canadian Rockies, connecting Jasper and Banff National Parks. Along the way, you’ll be treated to jaw-dropping views of snow-capped mountains, sparkling glaciers, and turquoise lakes. Make sure to stop at Athabasca Falls, Peyto Lake, and the Columbia Icefield for an unforgettable experience. Wildlife sightings are also common, with chances to see bears, elk, and mountain goats in their natural habitat.

3. Blue Ridge Parkway, North Carolina and Virginia
The Blue Ridge Parkway is a stunning road trip that meanders for 469 miles through the Appalachian Mountains. Known for its vibrant fall foliage, this route offers a breathtaking display of colors in autumn. Traveling along the parkway, you’ll encounter charming mountain towns, scenic overlooks, and countless hiking trails. Make sure to visit the iconic Grandfather Mountain, take a detour to the picturesque town of Asheville, and explore the Great Smoky Mountains National Park.

4. Cabot Trail, Nova Scotia, Canada
Situated on the rugged coast of Nova Scotia, the Cabot Trail is a 185-mile loop that offers breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean and the Cape Breton Highlands. This road trip combines stunning coastal scenery, lush forests, and charming fishing villages. Make sure to stop at Cape Breton Highlands National Park, where you can hike along the Skyline Trail and catch a glimpse of migrating whales. Don’t forget to try some fresh seafood while exploring the charming towns of Baddeck and Ingonish.

5. Going-to-the-Sun Road, Montana
Located in Glacier National Park, Going-to-the-Sun Road is a marvel of engineering that spans 50 miles through the heart of the park. The road offers stunning views of glaciers, alpine meadows, and pristine lakes. Along the way, make sure to stop at Logan Pass to take in the panoramic views, and keep an eye out for wildlife, including mountain goats and bighorn sheep. This road trip is truly a once-in-a-lifetime experience and is best enjoyed during the summer months when the road is open.

6. Highway 101, Oregon and Washington
For those looking to explore the beautiful coastline of the Pacific Northwest, Highway 101 is the perfect road trip. Stretching from the redwood forests of Northern California to the Olympic National Park in Washington, this route offers stunning views of rugged cliffs, sandy beaches, and picturesque lighthouses. Along the way, don’t miss Cannon Beach, home to the iconic Haystack Rock, and the charming coastal towns of Astoria and Port Townsend.

7. The Loneliest Road, Nevada
If you’re looking for solitude and wide-open spaces, then the Loneliest Road in America is the perfect choice. Stretching across the vast expanse of Nevada, this road trip offers a unique opportunity to experience the desolation and beauty of the desert. Highlights include the stunning Great Basin National Park, the quirky town of Ely, and the breathtaking vistas of Sand Mountain. Make sure to bring plenty of water, food, and gas, as amenities along the way are sparse.

8. The Overseas Highway, Florida
Connecting the Florida Keys to the mainland, the Overseas Highway is a road trip like no other. Stretching for 113 miles, this scenic route offers stunning views of the turquoise waters of the Gulf of Mexico and the Atlantic Ocean. Along the way, make sure to stop at Bahia Honda State Park for its beautiful beaches, and take a snorkeling or diving excursion in the famous coral reefs of the area. The highlight of the journey is reaching Key West, where you can enjoy the vibrant nightlife and explore Ernest Hemingway’s former residence.

These road trips are just a few examples of the countless scenic routes and stops that North America has to offer. Whether you’re a nature lover, an adventure seeker, or simply looking to experience the beauty of the open road, these road trips are sure to provide an unforgettable journey. So, pack your bags, hit the road, and get ready to create memories that will last a lifetime.
