Survival Trapping: Setting and Checking Traps for Food


Survival situations can be challenging, and one of the most crucial skills to have is the ability to procure food. While hunting and fishing are effective methods, setting up traps can also provide a reliable source of sustenance. In this blog post, we will explore the art of survival trapping, focusing on the proper techniques for setting and checking traps to maximize your chances of success.

1. Understanding the Basics of Trapping
Before diving into the specifics, it’s essential to have a basic understanding of trapping. Traps are designed to capture animals by either snaring, crushing, or restraining them. Survival trapping involves setting up these traps strategically to catch game for food. It is crucial to choose the right type of trap based on the target animal and the environment you are in.

2. Choosing the Right Trap
There are various types of traps available, each with its own advantages and best use cases. Here are a few commonly used survival traps:

a. Snare Traps: Snare traps are simple and effective for catching small to medium-sized animals. They work by using a looped wire or cord that tightens around the animal’s neck or body when triggered.

b. Deadfall Traps: Deadfall traps are designed to crush the prey with a heavy object, such as a rock or log. They are effective for catching small game like rodents or rabbits.

c. Spring Snaps: Spring snap traps are commonly used for catching larger game like beavers or raccoons. These traps use a spring mechanism to catch the animal when triggered.

d. Fish Traps: Fish traps are specifically designed to catch fish in rivers or streams. They can be constructed using baskets, nets, or funnels to guide the fish into a confined space.

3. Setting Traps
Once you have chosen the right trap for your target animal, it’s time to set it up correctly. Here are some essential tips to keep in mind:

a. Location: Choose a location where the animal is likely to pass through, such as game trails, near water sources, or areas with signs of animal activity like tracks or droppings.

b. Concealment: Camouflage the trap by using natural materials like leaves, sticks, and dirt. This helps to make it blend in with the surroundings and increases the chances of success.

c. Trigger Mechanism: Ensure that the trigger mechanism is set correctly and sensitive enough to capture the animal. Test the trap to make sure it is functioning correctly before leaving it unattended.

d. Bait: Use appropriate bait to attract the target animal. This could be food scraps, scent lures, or even specific animal calls, depending on the trap and the animal you are targeting.

4. Checking Traps
Regularly checking your traps is crucial to increase your chances of catching game and to prevent unnecessary suffering. Here are some guidelines for checking traps:

a. Frequency: Check your traps at least once every 24 hours. Animals caught in traps can suffer from stress, dehydration, or exposure. Checking traps regularly allows you to deal with the captured animals quickly.

b. Safety Measures: Always approach the trap cautiously, as some animals may become aggressive when captured. Use protective gear like gloves and be prepared to handle the situation appropriately.

c. Ethical Considerations: If you catch a non-target animal or a protected species, release it immediately and reset the trap. Respect wildlife and follow local regulations and ethical guidelines.

d. Trap Maintenance: While checking the traps, inspect them for any damage or wear and tear. Replace or repair any faulty parts to ensure the trap remains effective.

5. Additional Tips for Success
To improve your trapping skills and increase your chances of success, consider the following tips:

a. Learn from Experts: Take the time to study and learn from experienced trappers. Books, online resources, and local survival courses can provide valuable insights and techniques.

b. Practice Makes Perfect: Set up mock traps in your backyard or practice area to familiarize yourself with the process. This will help you become more efficient when setting traps in a real survival situation.

c. Diversify Your Traps: Setting up multiple traps of different types increases your chances of success. Different animals have different preferences and behaviors, so having a variety of traps will help you catch a wider range of game.

d. Respect Nature: While trapping for survival is a necessary skill, it’s essential to respect wildlife and the environment. Use trapping as a last resort and always prioritize conservation and ethical considerations.

In conclusion, survival trapping is a valuable skill to have in a survival situation. By understanding the basics, choosing the right traps, setting them correctly, and regularly checking them, you can increase your chances of procuring food when other methods may not be available. Remember to practice ethical trapping, respect wildlife, and always prioritize conservation efforts.
