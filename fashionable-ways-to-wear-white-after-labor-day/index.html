Fashionable Ways to Wear White after Labor Day


Labor Day has come and gone, and so has the age-old fashion rule of not wearing white after this holiday. Traditionally, white has been associated with warm weather and summer fashion, but in recent years, fashion enthusiasts have been breaking this rule and embracing white as a year-round color choice. So, if you’re wondering how to wear white after Labor Day and remain stylish, we have got you covered! In this blog post, we will explore some fashionable ways to incorporate white into your fall and winter wardrobe.

1. White Knitwear:

One of the easiest ways to incorporate white into your fall/winter wardrobe is through knitwear. A chunky white sweater paired with jeans and ankle boots creates a cozy and chic look for those colder days. You can also layer a white cardigan or a white turtleneck under a blazer for a more sophisticated outfit. The key is to choose knitwear in heavier fabrics like wool or cashmere to match the season.

2. White Outerwear:

Another trendy way to wear white after Labor Day is by incorporating white outerwear into your outfits. A white coat or jacket instantly transforms any look, adding a touch of elegance and sophistication. For a classic look, opt for a tailored white blazer or a sleek white trench coat. If you’re feeling more daring, try a white faux fur coat or a puffer jacket to stay warm and stylish during the colder months.

3. Monochrome White Look:

If you want to make a bold fashion statement, consider going for an all-white or monochrome white outfit. Pair white trousers with a white blouse or a white sweater for a clean and polished look. You can also experiment with different textures and fabrics to add depth and interest to your outfit. Don’t be afraid to accessorize with white shoes and a white handbag to complete the monochrome look.

4. White Dresses and Skirts:

Dresses and skirts are not just limited to the spring and summer seasons. A white dress or skirt can be a great addition to your fall and winter wardrobe. Pair a white midi dress with a leather jacket and ankle boots for a stylish and edgy ensemble. For a more feminine look, opt for a white lace dress paired with a chunky cardigan and knee-high boots. The key is to balance the delicate white garment with heavier fall/winter accessories.

5. White Accessories:

If you’re not quite ready to dive into head-to-toe white outfits, you can still incorporate white into your fall and winter wardrobe through accessories. White accessories like scarves, hats, gloves, and handbags can instantly elevate any outfit and add a touch of sophistication. Pair a white scarf with a camel coat or a white handbag with a black ensemble for a chic and modern look.

Final Thoughts:

Gone are the days when white was only reserved for the summer months. Nowadays, fashion enthusiasts are embracing white as a year-round color choice. By incorporating white knitwear, outerwear, monochrome looks, dresses and skirts, and accessories into your fall and winter wardrobe, you can create stylish and fashionable outfits that defy the old fashion rule of not wearing white after Labor Day. So, go ahead and embrace white in all its glory this season!
