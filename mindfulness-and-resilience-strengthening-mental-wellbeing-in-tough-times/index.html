Mindfulness and Resilience: Strengthening Mental Wellbeing in Tough Times


In today’s world, we are facing some of the toughest times in recent history. The COVID-19 pandemic has brought with it a sense of uncertainty, fear, and stress that has taken a toll on our mental wellbeing. It is in moments like these when we need to focus on our resilience and mindfulness. These two practices will help us strengthen our mental wellbeing and navigate the tough times with greater ease.

Let’s begin by defining mindfulness. Mindfulness is the practice of being present in the moment, without judgment. It is the act of bringing your attention to your breath, sensations, and thoughts without getting caught up in them. Mindfulness encourages us to pay attention to the present moment, and not dwell on past or future events. When we practice mindfulness, it reduces stress, anxiety, and depression. It has become popular in recent years and has been adopted by many people as a way to improve their mental wellbeing.

Now, let’s look at resilience. Resilience is the ability to bounce back from adversity. It is our ability to adapt to challenging situations, overcome obstacles, and handle stress. Resilience is not a characteristic that we are born with, it is a skill that we can practice and develop over time. When we practice resilience, we become better equipped to handle tough times and manage stress. It helps us to maintain our mental wellbeing in difficult circumstances.

So, how can we combine mindfulness and resilience to strengthen our mental wellbeing during tough times?

1. Practice mindfulness meditation daily

One of the best ways to cultivate mindfulness is through meditation. Meditation is the act of bringing our attention to a specific object, such as the breath, and keeping it there. Regular meditation practice can help us strengthen our mindfulness, which in turn helps us develop our resilience. By meditating daily, we also learn to observe our thoughts and emotions rather than getting caught up in them. This helps us to respond to challenging situations, rather than react impulsively.

2. Focus on your strengths

When times are tough, it’s easy to get caught up in our weaknesses and shortcomings. But, focusing on our strengths is important for building resilience. By focusing on our strengths, we develop a sense of self-confidence that allows us to tackle challenges with greater ease. When we acknowledge our strengths, it also helps us to maintain a more positive outlook, which further supports our mental wellbeing.

3. Cultivate gratitude

Gratitude is the practice of being thankful for what we have, rather than focusing on what we lack. Cultivating gratitude is important for strengthening our resilience because it helps us maintain a positive attitude, even in difficult circumstances. When we focus on the good things in our lives, it creates a sense of perspective that helps us to manage stress more effectively.

4. Seek support from others

No one is an island, and we all need support from others during tough times. Seeking support from friends, family, or mental health professionals can help us to build our resilience and maintain our mental wellbeing. It’s important to remember that asking for help is not a sign of weakness, but rather a sign of strength.

In conclusion, cultivating mindfulness and resilience are two of the most effective ways to strengthen our mental wellbeing during tough times. By practicing mindfulness meditation, focusing on our strengths, cultivating gratitude, and seeking support from others, we can develop our resilience and maintain a positive attitude in the face of adversity. It’s important to remember that building resilience is a skill that takes time and practice, but with dedication and commitment, anyone can do it.
