The Importance of Music in Advertising: How Jingles Stick in Your Head


The use of music in advertising has been a powerful tool for decades. From catchy jingles to memorable tunes, music has the ability to stick in our minds long after the ad has ended. This phenomenon is no accident; it is a result of careful strategizing and understanding of human psychology.

Why does music have such a profound impact on our memory and emotions? The answer lies in the way our brains process music. Music stimulates various parts of our brain simultaneously, including the auditory cortex, limbic system, and prefrontal cortex. This multisensory experience creates a strong connection between the music and the associated emotions and memories.

The use of music in advertising dates back to the early 20th century, when jingles became popular. Jingles are short, catchy tunes designed to promote a product or brand. Their purpose is to create a memorable association between the brand and the tune. The repetition and simplicity of jingles make them easy to remember, ensuring that the brand stays top-of-mind for consumers.

One of the most iconic jingles of all time is the ‘I’m Lovin’ It’ tune by McDonald’s. This jingle, created by Pharrell Williams, has become synonymous with the brand and is instantly recognizable worldwide. It conveys a sense of happiness, joy, and satisfaction, which aligns perfectly with McDonald’s brand image.

But jingles are not the only way music can enhance advertising campaigns. The right song can evoke specific emotions and create a deeper connection with the audience. Many successful ads have used popular songs to elicit an emotional response and convey the message effectively. Take for example the famous Apple iPod commercial featuring the song ‘1984’ by Eurythmics. The combination of the visually striking imagery, powerful lyrics, and memorable melody created a lasting impact on viewers, selling not just a product but an entire cultural movement.

In addition to creating emotional connections, music can also enhance the storytelling aspect of an ad. By carefully selecting and integrating the right music, advertisers can create a narrative, build tension, and convey a message more effectively. Think of the suspenseful music in horror movie trailers or the uplifting tunes in commercials that promote a feel-good message. The music acts as a tool to guide the emotions of the audience and enhance the overall impact of the ad.

Furthermore, music can help in brand recognition and recall. When a certain tune is consistently used across different ads and platforms, it becomes instantly recognizable and associated with the brand. This is known as sonic branding, and it has become an essential part of many successful advertising campaigns. Some examples include the Intel chime, the Nokia ringtone, and the Netflix ’ta-dum’ sound. These sonic logos have become a powerful asset for the respective brands, ensuring instant recognition and recall in the minds of the consumers.

From a neuroscientific perspective, music has been found to stimulate the release of dopamine, a neurotransmitter associated with pleasure and reward. When we hear a song we enjoy, our brains reward us with feelings of happiness and satisfaction. Advertising campaigns that leverage this reward system can create a positive association between the brand and the pleasurable experience of hearing the music. This can lead to increased brand loyalty and sales.

In conclusion, music plays a vital role in advertising. From jingles to popular songs, music has the power to stick in our heads and create lasting memories. By strategically selecting and integrating the right music, advertisers can evoke emotions, enhance storytelling, and improve brand recognition and recall. When used effectively, music can be a game-changer in advertising campaigns, leaving a lasting impact on the target audience. So the next time you find yourself humming a catchy tune from a commercial, remember the power of music in advertising and how it sticks in your head.
