Creative Ways to Use Fresh Herbs: From Pesto to Infusions


Fresh herbs are like a secret weapon in the kitchen. Not only do they add incredible flavor to dishes, but they also come with a host of health benefits. If you’re tired of using herbs in the same old ways, it’s time to get creative! In this blog post, we’ll explore some unique and exciting ways to use fresh herbs, from making pesto to infusions. Get ready to take your culinary skills to the next level!

1. Herb-infused oils and vinegars: Infusing oils and vinegars with fresh herbs is a fantastic way to add a burst of flavor to your dishes. Simply fill a bottle with your favorite herbs, such as basil, rosemary, or thyme, and pour in your chosen oil or vinegar. Let it sit for a couple of weeks, and you’ll have a delicious homemade infusion that can be used in salad dressings, marinades, or drizzled over roasted vegetables.

2. Herb butter: Elevate your butter by infusing it with fresh herbs. Start by softening unsalted butter and mixing in finely chopped herbs like parsley, chives, or dill. Roll the flavored butter into a log using plastic wrap and chill until firm. This herb-infused butter can be sliced and added to grilled steaks, tossed with pasta, or spread on warm bread.

3. Herbal teas and cocktails: It’s not just culinary dishes that can benefit from fresh herbs; your beverages can too! Make your own herbal teas by steeping fresh herbs like mint, chamomile, or lemon verbena in hot water. For a refreshing twist, add a few sprigs of fresh herbs to your favorite cocktails. A mojito with fresh basil or a gin and tonic with rosemary will take your happy hour to new heights.

4. Herb-infused honey: For a sweet and herbaceous treat, try infusing honey with fresh herbs. Fill a jar with your chosen herbs, such as lavender, thyme, or sage, and pour in raw honey. Allow the herbs to steep in the honey for a week or two, then strain out the solids. The infused honey can be drizzled over desserts, added to teas, or used to glaze roasted meats.

5. Herb salts and sugars: Take your seasonings to the next level by making herb-infused salts and sugars. Blend fresh herbs like cilantro, thyme, or rosemary with coarse sea salt or granulated sugar. Spread the mixture on a baking sheet and let it dry out for a day or two. Once dried, transfer to a jar and use these flavorful salts and sugars to add a unique twist to your recipes.

6. Herb pestos: Pesto is a classic way to use fresh herbs, but don’t limit yourself to just basil! Experiment with different combinations like cilantro and lime, parsley and walnut, or mint and pistachio. These herbaceous sauces can be tossed with pasta, spread on sandwiches, or used as a marinade for grilled meats and vegetables.

With these creative ideas, you’ll never run out of ways to use fresh herbs in your cooking. From infused oils to herb salts, the possibilities are endless. So, go ahead and raid your herb garden or pick up some fresh bunches from the store. Your taste buds will thank you for the explosion of flavor, and your dishes will never be the same again!
