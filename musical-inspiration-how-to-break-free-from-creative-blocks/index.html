Musical Inspiration: How to Break Free from Creative Blocks


Every musician or artist faces creative blocks, periods when inspiration seems to have vanished. Be it an inability to write a new song or come up with a fresh melody, these are dispiriting times. However, it is important to understand that creativity works in mysterious ways, and it takes just one trigger to unleash a wave of new ideas.

Here are some simple yet effective ways musicians can break free from creative blocks and find their musical inspiration.

1. Draw inspiration from other artists

As the old saying goes, no idea is entirely original. Most great artists in history have been influenced by someone else’s work, and the same should apply to you. Whether you need help finding a new chord progression or a new melody, exposing yourself to the work of other musicians can provide you with a new sense of inspiration. Listen to new music, go to concerts or watch live performances. This can inspire you to pick up your instrument and start playing, or take the pen and start writing.

2. Change your environment

Most musicians have a specific space where they play, practice, or write songs. While these spaces may be a source of comfort and familiarity, they can also become stifling over time. Changing your environment can help to provide a fresh perspective and could lead to new ideas and inspiration. Go out and find different creative spaces like parks or coffee shops that inspire you to write and play.

3. Collaborate with other musicians

Collaboration can be a great way to break free from a creative block. It’s an opportunity to share ideas and opinions, giving you access to a new perspective. This, in turn, can inspire you to create something you never would have thought of on your own. Working with others provides an opportunity to feed off of each other’s creativity, as well as offer instant feedback and direction.

4. Learn a new skill or take on a new challenge

Sometimes all it takes to rekindle creativity is to do something entirely different and challenging. Learning a new instrument or taking a new approach to creating music can help break out of a creative rut. This allows you to open your mind and focus on the fundamentals. It’s an opportunity to study something new, and the knowledge you gain could provide a source of creative inspiration.

5. Try freestyling

Sometimes starting with an empty slate can be intimidating, where there is nothing to build upon. In such cases, freestyling is perfect as it allows you to sketch out ideas spontaneously. You can come up with a chord progression, melody or lyrics, and what you come up with might be the inspiration you need to break free.

6. Take a break

Most importantly, don’t forget that creativity requires energy. Taking a break and giving yourself some time to rest and recharge is essential. This could be a few days or even weeks away from your instrument, allowing you to come back to it with a renewed sense of energy and clarity.

Breaking free from creative blocks takes time and patience. It’s important to keep pushing forward and trust that inspiration will come. Keep searching for novel experiences and environments, expose yourself to other artists’ work and collaborate as much as possible. Even when you think you’ve run out of ideas, know that there is still plenty of musical inspiration to be found.
