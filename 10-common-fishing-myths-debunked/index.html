10 Common Fishing Myths Debunked


Fishing is one of the most popular outdoor activities enjoyed by millions of people around the world. Whether it’s freshwater, saltwater, fly fishing, or ice fishing, anglers are always eager to catch the biggest and best fish. However, over time, several fishing myths have emerged, leading many anglers astray. In this blog post, we will debunk 10 common fishing myths so you can improve your chances of success on your next fishing trip.

1. Myth: Fish can’t see fishing line

One common myth among anglers is that fish cannot see fishing line, and therefore, the color and thickness don’t matter. In reality, fish have excellent eyesight and can easily detect fishing lines, especially in clear water. It’s best to use a fishing line that matches the water conditions and is as invisible as possible.

2. Myth: Fish don’t bite in the rain

Many fishermen believe that fish don’t bite when it’s raining. While it’s true that fish may become less active during heavy rain, they can still be caught. In fact, rainwater can wash insects and small prey into the water, making it an opportune time for fish to feed.

3. Myth: Brightly colored lures attract more fish

Anglers often assume that using brightly colored lures will attract more fish. However, fish are not necessarily attracted to color but rather to the movement and vibration of the lure. In some situations, using natural or subtle colored lures can be just as effective, if not more so.

4. Myth: Fish prefer loud noises

There is a common belief that creating loud noises, such as banging the boat or using a noisy lure, will attract fish. While some fish may be curious about the commotion, it can also scare off the more cautious ones. It’s best to be stealthy and make minimal noise to avoid spooking the fish.

5. Myth: Fish always feed at dawn and dusk

It is often said that fish are most active during the early morning and evening hours. While it’s true that many fish species are more active during these times, they can still be caught throughout the day. Factors such as weather conditions, water temperature, and the fish’s natural feeding habits play a significant role in their feeding patterns.

6. Myth: Bigger bait equals bigger fish

Many anglers believe that using larger bait will attract bigger fish. While this can be true in some cases, it’s not always the case. Fish have different feeding habits and preferences, and using the right size of bait that matches the fish’s natural prey is more important.

7. Myth: Fish don’t bite in cold water

Some fishermen believe that fish don’t bite in cold water, especially during the winter months. While fish do become less active in colder water, they are still capable of feeding. Adjusting your tactics and using appropriate baits and lures for cold water fishing can increase your chances of success.

8. Myth: Fish always stay in the same spot

Many anglers assume that once they find a good fishing spot, they can return to it time and time again for guaranteed success. However, fish are constantly on the move, following their food sources and seeking shelter. It’s crucial to understand fish behavior and adapt your fishing strategy accordingly.

9. Myth: You need expensive gear to catch fish

While having high-quality fishing gear can enhance your overall fishing experience, it is not a prerequisite for catching fish. Many beginner anglers have caught impressive fish using basic equipment. Having the proper knowledge, technique, and understanding of fish behavior is more important than the brand of gear you use.

10. Myth: Catch and release always saves fish

Catch and release has become a popular practice within the fishing community to ensure sustainable fishing. However, it’s important to handle fish with care and follow proper catch and release techniques to ensure their survival. Mishandling fish or fishing in certain conditions may cause unnecessary stress or harm, reducing the chances of survival.

In conclusion, debunking fishing myths is essential for anglers to improve their skills and increase their chances of success. Understanding fish behavior, using appropriate equipment and techniques, and debunking common misconceptions will undoubtedly lead to a more enjoyable and productive fishing experience. So, the next time you hit the water, keep these debunked myths in mind and have a successful fishing trip!
