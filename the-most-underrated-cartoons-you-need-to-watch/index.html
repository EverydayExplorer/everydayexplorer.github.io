The Most Underrated Cartoons You Need to Watch


In an era where animation has evolved into a powerful storytelling medium, countless cartoons have captured the hearts of audiences. While some shows have soared to fame and garnered massive fanbases, others have remained hidden gems, waiting quietly for their moment in the spotlight. This blog post takes you on a journey through the most underrated cartoons that deserve your attention. We’ll explore unique plots, compelling characters, and the artistry behind these shows that might have slipped under your radar.

1. Over the Garden Wall

A Journey of Discovery

Over the Garden Wall is a miniseries that combines whimsy with a touch of darkness. Created by Patrick McHale, this enchanting cartoon follows two half-brothers, Wirt and Greg, as they navigate a mysterious forest known as the Unknown. The series is notable for its gorgeous hand-drawn animation, reminiscent of classic illustrations. Each episode is a standalone story that weaves together themes of brotherhood, fear, and the search for belonging.

Why You Should Watch It

What makes Over the Garden Wall particularly underrated is its ability to appeal to both children and adults. The humor is clever, the music is hauntingly beautiful, and the storytelling is rich with folklore elements. Each character they encounter is unique and adds depth to the narrative, making it a visually stunning and emotionally resonant experience.

2. The Owl House

A Magical Escape

The Owl House is a Disney Channel series created by Dana Terrace that follows a teenage girl named Luz who stumbles into a magical realm. There, she befriends a rebellious witch and a tiny warrior. The show’s combination of humor, heart, and LGBTQ+ representation makes it a standout in modern animation.

Why You Should Watch It

This show is often overshadowed by more mainstream Disney properties, but its narrative depth and character development are exceptional. With its rich lore, vibrant animation, and progressive themes, The Owl House offers a fresh perspective on friendship and self-discovery.

3. Freakazoid!

A Comedic Adventure

Freakazoid! is a cult classic that aired in the mid-1990s, created by Steven Spielberg and Paul Rugg. The series follows a teenage boy named Dexter Douglas who becomes the superhero Freakazoid after a computer accident. It is known for its fast-paced humor and absurdist comedy.

Why You Should Watch It

Though it struggled to find a mainstream audience during its original run, Freakazoid! deserves recognition for its clever writing and pop culture references. The show breaks the fourth wall and delivers a unique brand of humor that appeals to viewers of all ages. Its eccentric characters and unpredictable storylines make it a delightful watch.

4. Hilda

An Adventure in a Magical World

Based on the graphic novels by Luke Pearson, Hilda is a Netflix animated series that follows a blue-haired girl who explores a fantastical world filled with trolls, giants, and other creatures. The show is beautifully animated and features a calming soundtrack that immerses viewers in Hilda’s adventures.

Why You Should Watch It

Hilda is often praised for its artistry and storytelling. The series cleverly balances lighthearted moments with deeper themes, such as friendship and the importance of nature. It’s a perfect choice for those who appreciate a gentle yet captivating narrative.

5. Tuca & Bertie

A Unique Perspective

Created by Lisa Hanawalt, Tuca & Bertie is an adult animated series that explores the friendship between two bird-women living in the same apartment building. The show tackles themes of mental health, relationships, and the struggles of adulthood through a colorful and surreal lens.

Why You Should Watch It

Despite its critical acclaim, Tuca & Bertie did not receive the attention it deserved upon release. The animation style is vibrant and distinct, making it visually appealing. Its raw and honest portrayal of female friendship is both refreshing and relatable, resonating with viewers who seek depth in their animated content.

6. Beware the Batman

A New Take on a Classic Hero

Beware the Batman is an animated series that offers a fresh spin on the Batman mythos. The show features a darker tone and introduces lesser-known villains like Anarky and Professor Pyg. It was notable for its use of CGI animation, setting it apart from traditional superhero cartoons.

Why You Should Watch It

While it faced criticism for its animation style, Beware the Batman has a unique storytelling approach that delves into Batman’s detective skills rather than focusing solely on action. The series offers a more nuanced portrayal of the characters and their motivations, making it a must-watch for any Batman fan.

7. The Amazing World of Gumball

A Whimsical Universe

The Amazing World of Gumball is a surreal comedy that follows the misadventures of a young cat named Gumball Watterson and his family. The show’s unique blend of animation styles, including live-action backgrounds and traditional animation, creates a visually striking experience.

Why You Should Watch It

This series is often overlooked despite its clever humor and inventive storytelling. The absurd situations Gumball and his friends find themselves in are both hilarious and thought-provoking, making it suitable for viewers of all ages. Its sharp social commentary adds an extra layer of depth to the humor.

8. DuckTales (2017)

A Modern Classic

The 2017 reboot of DuckTales brought new life to the beloved franchise originally created by Carl Barks. This modern adaptation follows Scrooge McDuck and his grandnephews as they embark on treasure hunts and adventures around the globe.

Why You Should Watch It

While the original DuckTales is iconic, this reboot features modern animation techniques and storytelling that resonates with today’s audience. The character development is rich, and the humor is sharp, making it an enjoyable watch for both longtime fans and newcomers alike.

9. Infinity Train

A Journey Through the Unknown

Infinity Train is an anthology series that follows different characters as they board a mysterious train with infinite cars, each containing its own unique world. Each season focuses on a different protagonist and explores themes of personal growth and self-discovery.

Why You Should Watch It

Despite its compelling storytelling and innovative premise, Infinity Train remains underappreciated. The show’s ability to tackle complex issues like trauma and identity in a way that’s accessible to younger audiences is what sets it apart. Its artistic animation style and emotional depth make it a standout series.

10. Gravity Falls

A Mysterious Adventure

Gravity Falls is a Disney Channel series that follows twins Dipper and Mabel Pines as they spend the summer with their great-uncle in a mysterious town filled with supernatural occurrences. The show is celebrated for its engaging plot and clever writing.

Why You Should Watch It

Though it gained a loyal fanbase, Gravity Falls is often overshadowed by other Disney properties. Its intricate plotlines and well-developed characters create a richly layered narrative that keeps viewers engaged. The blend of humor and mystery makes it a staple for anyone who enjoys a good adventure.

Conclusion

In a landscape filled with animated shows, these underrated cartoons offer fresh narratives, unique art styles, and relatable themes. They deserve recognition for their ability to entertain and provoke thought, making them essential viewing for fans of animation. Whether you’re looking for humor, adventure, or heartwarming stories, these hidden gems are waiting to be discovered. So grab your popcorn and dive into these remarkable series that promise to leave you entertained and inspired. Happy watching!
