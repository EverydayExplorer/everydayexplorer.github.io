A Brief History of Japanese Manga and its Influence on Cartoons


Manga and anime are two forms of entertainment that have taken the world by storm. These art mediums have captivated audiences with their unique art styles, compelling stories, and vibrant characters. While anime refers to animated shows or movies, manga specifically refers to the comic books or graphic novels of Japanese origin. In this blog post, we will delve into the history of Japanese manga and explore its influence on cartoons.

The roots of manga can be traced back to the 12th century, where illustrated scrolls known as ’emakimono’ were popular in Japan. These scrolls depicted various stories and were often used to entertain and educate. However, it wasn’t until the 18th and 19th centuries that manga as we know it today started to emerge.

During this time, woodblock prints known as ‘ukiyo-e’ became popular. These prints showcased various subjects, including historical events, landscapes, and even scenes from plays and literature. Artists like Hokusai and Hiroshige played a significant role in popularizing ukiyo-e, and their influence can still be seen in manga today.

The modern form of manga started to gain traction in the late 19th century, with publications like ‘Eshinbun Nipponchi’ and ‘Jiji Manga’ becoming popular. These publications featured satirical cartoons and political commentary, giving birth to the concept of manga as a form of social commentary.

In the early 20th century, manga continued to evolve with the introduction of serialized manga magazines. These magazines, such as ‘Shonen Club’ and ‘Shojo Club,’ catered to specific demographics and showcased a wide range of stories and genres. The serialized format allowed for longer and more complex narratives, which further contributed to the growth of manga as a medium.

The influence of manga on cartoons can be seen as early as the 1960s, with the introduction of shows like ‘Astro Boy’ and ‘Speed Racer.’ These shows showcased the distinct visual style of manga and brought Japanese animation to a global audience. The success of these shows paved the way for future collaborations between Japanese and international animation studios, further spreading the influence of manga.

One of the most significant contributions of manga to cartoons is the concept of storytelling. Manga often features deep and immersive narratives that explore various themes and emotions. The serialized nature of manga allows for long-form storytelling, giving creators the opportunity to develop complex characters and story arcs. This approach to storytelling has influenced cartoons around the world, with many shows adopting similar narrative structures and character development.

The visual style of manga has also had a profound impact on cartoons. The use of bold lines, exaggerated expressions, and dynamic action scenes are all elements that can be traced back to manga. This distinctive art style has been embraced by various animation studios, not only in Japan but globally as well. Shows like ‘Avatar: The Last Airbender’ and ‘Steven Universe’ incorporate manga-inspired visuals, further blurring the lines between manga and cartoons.

The influence of manga on cartoons is not limited to visual aesthetics and storytelling. The themes explored in manga, such as friendship, love, and coming of age, resonate with audiences worldwide. Cartoons that draw inspiration from manga often explore these universal themes, making them relatable to viewers of different cultures and backgrounds.

In recent years, the popularity of manga and anime has only grown, with franchises like ‘One Piece’ and ‘Attack on Titan’ becoming global sensations. This growing popularity has led to collaborations between Japanese and international animation studios, resulting in shows like ‘RWBY’ and ‘Voltron: Legendary Defender’ that embrace a fusion of anime and cartoon aesthetics.

In conclusion, the history of manga is a testament to the creativity and ingenuity of Japanese artists. From its humble origins in illustrated scrolls to its global influence on cartoons, manga has left an indelible mark on the world of entertainment. Its unique visual style, compelling storytelling, and universal themes continue to captivate audiences around the world. Whether you’re a fan of manga, anime, or cartoons, the impact of manga on animation is undeniable.
