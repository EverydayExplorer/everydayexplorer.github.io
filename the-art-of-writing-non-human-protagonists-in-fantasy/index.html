The Art of Writing Non-Human Protagonists in Fantasy


In the vast realm of fantasy writing, the art of crafting non-human protagonists is a skill that can transport readers to worlds beyond imagination. While human characters often take center stage, there is a unique allure to characters that transcend the boundaries of humanity. Non-human protagonists add depth, intrigue, and a fresh perspective to fantasy narratives, captivating readers with their otherworldly essence. In this blog post, we will explore the intricacies of writing non-human protagonists in fantasy and uncover the secrets to creating truly memorable characters.

1. Embrace Diversity:
One of the most exciting aspects of writing non-human protagonists is the endless possibilities for diversity. From mythical creatures like dragons and elves to sentient plants and celestial beings, the realm of fantasy offers an inexhaustible range of non-human characters. Embrace this diversity and explore the unique traits, customs, and cultures of your non-human protagonist. Give them a rich backstory and motivations that are distinct from their human counterparts, allowing readers to immerse themselves in a world unlike any they have experienced before.

2. Challenge Stereotypes:
Non-human protagonists provide an opportunity to challenge stereotypes and push the boundaries of traditional storytelling. Break free from the clichés often associated with non-human characters and explore their complexities. For example, a typically fearsome creature like a werewolf can be portrayed as compassionate and empathetic, defying the expectations of readers. By subverting stereotypes, you can create multidimensional characters that resonate with your audience on a deeper level.

3. Develop a Unique Voice:
Writing non-human protagonists requires a keen understanding of their distinct perspective and voice. Consider how their physical attributes, senses, and experiences shape their perception of the world. Do they perceive time differently? Do they communicate through telepathy or other means? These unique traits influence their voice, allowing you to create a character with a truly alien or fantastical perspective. By developing a unique voice, you can immerse readers in the mind of your non-human protagonist, fostering a deep connection between character and audience.

4. Explore Themes of Otherness:
Non-human protagonists often embody the theme of otherness, highlighting the experiences of those who exist outside the realm of normalcy. Use this theme to explore issues of identity, acceptance, and the human condition. By delving into the struggles and triumphs of non-human characters, you can shed light on the complexities of our own society and challenge readers to question their preconceived notions.

5. Balance Humanity and Non-Humanity:
While non-human protagonists possess fantastical qualities, it is essential to strike a balance between their non-human attributes and relatable humanity. Infuse your characters with emotions, flaws, and desires that resonate with readers. By grounding your non-human protagonists in relatable experiences, you create a powerful connection that transcends the boundaries of species.

In conclusion, the art of writing non-human protagonists in fantasy is an opportunity to stretch the limits of imagination and create truly captivating stories. Embrace diversity, challenge stereotypes, develop unique voices, explore themes of otherness, and strike a balance between humanity and non-humanity. By following these guidelines, you can craft non-human characters that leave a lasting impression on readers, transporting them to realms beyond their wildest dreams.
