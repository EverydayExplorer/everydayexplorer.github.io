Comedy in Advertising: Brands That Make You Laugh


In the world of advertising, brands are constantly looking for ways to grab attention and make a lasting impression on consumers. One of the most effective ways to do this is through humor. Comedy in advertising not only entertains audiences but also helps to establish a memorable and positive association with the brand.

The power of laughter should not be underestimated. It has the ability to create a connection with consumers and make a brand more relatable. When people laugh, they feel happy and positive, which can lead to increased brand affinity and ultimately drive sales.

By incorporating comedy into their advertisements, brands can stand out in a cluttered advertising landscape. A funny ad has a higher chance of going viral, as people are more likely to share and talk about something that made them laugh. This can lead to increased brand visibility and reach, without the need for a big advertising budget.

So, which brands are the masters of comedy in advertising? Let’s take a look at some examples:

  1. Old Spice: Known for their humorous and offbeat commercials, Old Spice has successfully used comedy to rebrand their products and appeal to a younger audience. Their iconic ‘The Man Your Man Could Smell Like’ campaign featuring a suave and confident spokesperson has become a cultural phenomenon.

  2. Geico: Geico’s commercials are consistently funny and memorable. From their talking gecko to their ‘Hump Day’ camel, Geico knows how to use humor to capture attention and reinforce their brand message.

  3. Doritos: Doritos Super Bowl ads have become highly anticipated each year for their comedic value. The brand’s use of humor and user-generated content has helped them engage with their audience and build a strong brand identity.

  4. Skittles: Skittles’ quirky and absurd commercials have become a trademark of the brand. They often take a humorous approach that appeals to a young and lighthearted demographic.

  5. Budweiser: Budweiser’s Super Bowl ads are some of the most anticipated each year, and they often incorporate humor to create memorable moments. From their famous talking frogs to their Clydesdale horses, Budweiser knows how to make people laugh while still conveying their brand message.

These are just a few examples of brands that have successfully used comedy in advertising to create a lasting impact. However, it’s important to note that not all humor works in every situation. Brands must be careful to strike the right balance between humor and maintaining their brand image. Comedy should enhance the brand’s message, not distract from it.

In addition, brands must be mindful of cultural sensitivities and avoid offensive or controversial humor. While edgy humor can be effective, it’s important to know the audience and ensure that the comedy lands in a positive way.

In conclusion, comedy in advertising is a powerful tool that can help brands cut through the noise and establish a lasting connection with consumers. By making people laugh and creating a positive association, brands can increase their visibility, reach, and ultimately drive sales. So, the next time you see a funny ad, take a moment to appreciate the creativity and strategy behind it.
