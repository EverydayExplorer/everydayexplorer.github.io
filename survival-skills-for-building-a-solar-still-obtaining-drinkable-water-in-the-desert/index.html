Survival Skills for Building a Solar Still: Obtaining Drinkable Water in the Desert


In the scorching heat of the desert, finding water can be a matter of life and death. The lack of water sources can leave you dehydrated and vulnerable. However, with the right survival skills, you can learn how to build a solar still to obtain drinkable water and increase your chances of survival. In this blog post, we will explore the step-by-step process of constructing a solar still and provide tips for maximizing its efficiency.

1. Choosing the Right Location:
Selecting the right location for your solar still is crucial for maximizing its effectiveness. Look for a spot that receives ample sunlight throughout the day and has access to a water source, such as a muddy area or a small pond. This will ensure a higher water yield.

2. Gathering Materials:
To build a solar still, you will need the following materials:
- A digging tool (shovel or trowel)
- A clear plastic sheet (preferably a large one)
- Rocks or heavy objects

3. Digging a Hole:
Start by digging a hole in the ground. The size of the hole will depend on the amount of water you need. A depth of 3 to 4 feet is generally sufficient. Make sure the hole is wider at the top and narrows down towards the bottom.

4. Placing the Collection Container:
Place a clean container at the bottom of the hole to collect the condensed water. Make sure it is stable and won’t tip over.

5. Creating the Moisture Source:
Next, pour water into the hole around the collection container. This will create a moist environment and allow water to evaporate.

6. Covering the Hole:
Carefully place the clear plastic sheet over the hole, ensuring that it completely covers the hole and extends beyond its edges. Use rocks or heavy objects to secure the edges of the plastic sheet, preventing any air from entering.

7. Maximizing Efficiency:
To maximize the efficiency of your solar still, follow these tips:
- Check the solar still regularly to ensure that the plastic sheet remains tightly sealed and there are no leaks.
- If necessary, add more water to the hole to maintain a moist environment.
- Position the solar still in direct sunlight to increase the rate of evaporation.

8. Collecting Drinkable Water:
As the sun’s heat causes the water to evaporate, it will condense on the inner surface of the plastic sheet. The condensed water will then drip down and collect in the container at the bottom of the hole. Carefully remove the container and drink the collected water. Remember to filter or purify it further if needed.

9. Staying Hydrated:
Building a solar still is an effective way to obtain drinkable water in the desert, but it’s important to remember that it may not provide a sufficient water supply for long-term survival. It is crucial to continue searching for additional water sources and conserve the water obtained from the solar still.

Conclusion:
Having the knowledge of building a solar still can be a lifesaver in the desert. By following these survival skills, you can increase your chances of obtaining drinkable water and enhance your overall chances of survival. Remember, always prioritize staying hydrated and be prepared for any challenges that may arise. Stay safe and stay hydrated!
