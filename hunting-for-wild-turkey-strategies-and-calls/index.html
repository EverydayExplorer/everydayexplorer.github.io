Hunting for Wild Turkey: Strategies and Calls


As outdoor enthusiasts, many of us are drawn to the thrill and challenge of hunting. While there are various game animals to pursue, hunting wild turkey offers a unique and exciting experience. The cunning nature of these birds and the thrill of the chase make turkey hunting a popular choice among hunters.

In this blog, we will delve into the strategies and calls that can help you become a successful wild turkey hunter. Whether you are a seasoned hunter or just starting out, these tips will give you an edge in the pursuit of this elusive game.

1. Scouting: Before heading out into the field, it is essential to scout the area where you plan to hunt. Look for signs of turkey activity such as tracks, feathers, droppings, and scratching. By identifying their roosting, feeding, and breeding areas, you can increase your chances of encountering a wild turkey.

2. Concealment: Turkeys have excellent eyesight, so it is crucial to blend into your surroundings. Wear camouflage clothing that matches the terrain and use a full face mask and gloves to avoid giving away your position. Setting up in natural cover, such as a brush or tree line, will further enhance your concealment.

3. Decoys: Using decoys can be an effective strategy to attract turkeys. Lifelike decoys, such as hen or jake decoys, can create a visual stimulus and draw turkeys into range. Place them strategically, considering the wind direction and the line of sight from approaching turkeys.

4. Calls: Mastering turkey calls is essential for any turkey hunter. There are several types of calls, including box calls, slate calls, diaphragm calls, and locator calls. Each call produces different turkey sounds, such as clucks, purrs, yelps, and gobbles. Practice these calls to mimic the natural sounds of turkeys and lure them closer.

5. Patience and Persistence: Turkey hunting requires patience and persistence. It is common for turkeys to be hesitant or call-shy, especially in areas with high hunting pressure. Be prepared to sit for hours, wait for the perfect shot opportunity, and be persistent in your efforts. Remember, a successful hunt often comes to those who can outlast their prey.

6. Safety: Safety should always be a top priority when hunting. Familiarize yourself with local hunting regulations, wear blaze orange clothing to make yourself visible to other hunters, and always practice safe firearm handling. Additionally, be aware of your surroundings and avoid stalking or shooting at sounds or movements that you cannot positively identify as a turkey.

By following these strategies and mastering turkey calls, you can increase your chances of a successful wild turkey hunt. Remember, hunting is not just about the harvest; it’s about connecting with nature, honing your skills, and appreciating the beauty of the great outdoors.

So, gear up, head out, and embrace the challenge of hunting for wild turkey. With the right knowledge and a bit of luck, you might just bag that trophy gobbler you’ve been dreaming of. Happy hunting!
