Fun and Festive Holiday Crafts for the Whole Family


Are you looking for some fun and festive holiday crafts to do with your family? Look no further! In this blog post, we will be sharing a variety of crafts that are perfect for the holiday season. Whether you celebrate Christmas, Hanukkah, Kwanzaa, or any other holiday, these crafts will bring joy and creativity to your home.

1. DIY Ornament Decorating:
One classic holiday craft that the whole family can enjoy is decorating ornaments. Head to your local craft store and pick up a set of plain ceramic ornaments. Then, gather an assortment of colorful paints, glitter, and other decorative materials. Set up a painting station and let everyone’s creativity soar! You can also personalize the ornaments with each family member’s initials or favorite designs. These ornaments will make beautiful additions to your tree or can be given as heartfelt gifts.

2. Festive Wreath Making:
Another great holiday craft is making your own wreaths. Start by purchasing a plain wreath form from a craft store or gather materials such as twigs or evergreen branches. Then, use wire or hot glue to attach an assortment of festive decorations, such as pinecones, berries, ribbons, or ornaments. Get creative with your designs and let each family member add their personal touch. Hang these wreaths on your front door or use them as festive decorations throughout your home.

3. Holiday-themed Snow Globe:
Snow globes are enchanting decorations that can be made with just a few simple materials. Start by collecting small glass jars or containers with lids. Next, gather small figurines, fake snow or glitter, and glycerin (to keep the snowflakes falling slowly). Attach the figurine to the inside of the lid using waterproof glue, and fill the jar with water, glitter, and a few drops of glycerin. Carefully seal the jar, making sure it is watertight, and then turn it upside down and watch the snowflakes fall. These homemade snow globes will make magical decorations or thoughtful gifts.

4. Handmade Holiday Cards:
Nothing says ‘Happy Holidays’ better than a handmade card. Set up a dedicated crafting area with colored paper, markers, pens, glitter, and other embellishments. Let each family member create their own unique card design, incorporating holiday-themed drawings or heartfelt messages. These handmade cards will be cherished by family and friends who receive them. Plus, it’s a great opportunity for children to practice their writing skills and express their creativity.

5. Scented Sachets:
Add a touch of holiday cheer to your home with scented sachets. Begin by selecting festive fabric scraps, such as holiday-themed prints or classic tartans. Cut two pieces of fabric into your desired shape and size, leaving a small opening for filling. Mix together a combination of dried herbs, spices, and essential oils. Some popular options include cinnamon sticks, cloves, dried oranges, and peppermint oil. Fill the sachet with the scented mixture and sew the opening closed. Place these fragrant sachets in your drawers, hang them in your closet, or give them as gifts.

6. Gingerbread House Decorating:
Gingerbread houses are a holiday tradition loved by many. Start by baking or purchasing a gingerbread house kit. Set up a decorating station with an assortment of candies, icing, and sprinkles. Let each family member decorate their own gingerbread house, using their imagination to create colorful and whimsical designs. This activity is not only fun but also encourages teamwork and creativity.

7. Holiday-themed Popsicle Stick Ornaments:
Popsicle stick ornaments are an easy and budget-friendly craft. Start by collecting colored popsicle sticks and gluing them together in various shapes, such as snowflakes, stars, or Christmas trees. Once the glue is dry, let the kids decorate the ornaments with paint, glitter, ribbons, and other craft materials. These ornaments can be hung on the tree or used as colorful decorations around the house.

8. DIY Advent Calendar:
Countdown the days until Christmas with a DIY advent calendar. Start by creating 24 small envelopes or labeled paper bags. Fill each envelope with a small treat or activity idea, such as watching a holiday movie, baking cookies, or going on a winter walk. Hang the envelopes on a string or display them in a decorative box. Each day, open one envelope as a family to reveal the surprise inside. This craft not only adds excitement to the holiday season but also encourages quality family time.

9. Holiday-themed Handprint Art:
Capture memories and create beautiful artwork at the same time with handprint art. Use non-toxic paint to apply handprints onto canvas or plain paper. Let the paint dry and then transform the handprints into holiday-themed images, such as reindeer, Santa Claus, or Christmas trees. This craft is perfect for younger children as it allows them to participate in creating festive decorations.

10. DIY Holiday Stockings:
Why not create personalized holiday stockings for each family member? Start by selecting fabric that matches your home decor or choose festive prints. Trace and cut out two stocking shapes from the fabric. Sew the edges together, leaving the top open, and create a loop for hanging. Allow each family member to decorate their own stocking using fabric paint, ribbons, appliques, or personal touches. Hang these beautiful stockings by the fireplace or staircase and enjoy the anticipation of filling them with treats and small gifts.

In conclusion, the holiday season is the perfect time to engage in fun and festive crafts with your family. These crafts not only bring joy and creativity into your home but also create lasting memories. From DIY ornament decorating to creating personalized holiday stockings, there are endless opportunities to get crafty and celebrate the holiday spirit together. So gather your supplies, unleash your creativity, and have a merry and crafty holiday season with your loved ones!
