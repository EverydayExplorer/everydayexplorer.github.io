Photographing Architecture: Finding Patterns and Symmetry


Architecture is something that surrounds us everyday. We walk past countless buildings on our daily commutes, but rarely stop to appreciate them. However, when we do take the time to observe the architectural details of a building, patterns and symmetry often become evident. These details can make for incredibly compelling and visually stunning photographs.

When photographing architecture, it is important to pay attention to the patterns and symmetry that exist within the building’s design. These elements are often what make a building interesting and unique. They can be found in a variety of forms, such as the repetition of shapes or colors, the placement of windows or doors, or even the overall structure of the building itself.

One way to find patterns and symmetry in architecture is by looking for lines. Buildings are often designed using a variety of different lines, such as horizontal, vertical, and diagonal. These lines can be found in the overall structure of the building, as well as in the details, such as columns, arches, or staircases. By photographing these lines and emphasizing them in composition, an interesting photograph can be created.

Another way to find patterns and symmetry in architecture is to look for reflections. Many modern buildings incorporate reflective surfaces, such as glass, metal, or water, which can create interesting symmetrical patterns. By photographing these reflections and incorporating them into the composition, a visually stunning photograph can be created.

When photographing architecture, it’s important to pay attention to the lighting. Different lighting conditions can dramatically affect the appearance of a building, and can either emphasize or detract from the patterns and symmetry within the design. Shooting during the golden hours of sunrise or sunset can create warm, soft lighting that can beautifully accentuate the patterns and symmetry within the building.

Finally, when photographing architecture, it’s important to pay attention to the details. Details such as intricate patterns or ornate decorations can create interesting compositions that highlight the patterns and symmetry within the building. By paying attention to the details, a photograph can be created that tells a story and captures the essence of the building’s design.

In conclusion, photographing architecture can be an excellent way to find and capture patterns and symmetry in our built environment. By paying attention to lines, reflections, lighting, and details, stunning and visually compelling photographs can be created. The next time you’re walking past a building, take a closer look and see if you can find the patterns and symmetry within the architecture – it may just lead to your next great photograph.
