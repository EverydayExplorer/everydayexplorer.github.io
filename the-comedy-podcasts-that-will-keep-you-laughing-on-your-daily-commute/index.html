The Comedy Podcasts that Will Keep You Laughing on Your Daily Commute


Podcasts have become the go-to entertainment option for many people during their daily commute. Whether you’re stuck in traffic or waiting for public transportation, podcasts can make the journey more enjoyable. And if you’re in need of a good laugh, comedy podcasts are the way to go. In this blog post, we’ll be exploring some of the best comedy podcasts that will keep you laughing on your daily commute. So buckle up, get ready for some hilarious moments, and let’s dive in!

1. ‘The Joe Rogan Experience’: Hosted by comedian Joe Rogan, this podcast features long-form conversations with a wide range of guests, including comedians, actors, and experts in various fields. With its laid-back atmosphere and humorous discussions, ‘The Joe Rogan Experience’ offers a perfect blend of comedy and insights.

2. ‘Conan O’Brien Needs a Friend’: Late-night talk show host Conan O’Brien brings his signature wit and charm to this podcast as he interviews celebrities and friends. With a mix of hilarious banter and heartfelt conversations, ‘Conan O’Brien Needs a Friend’ will keep you laughing while adding a personal touch.

3. ‘My Dad Wrote A Porno’: If you’re looking for something out of the ordinary, this podcast is for you. Hosted by Jamie Morton, James Cooper, and Alice Levine, ‘My Dad Wrote A Porno’ takes listeners on a hilarious journey through Jamie’s father’s self-published erotic novel. The hosts’ witty commentary and infectious laughter make this podcast a must-listen for comedy fans.

4. ‘Comedy Bang! Bang!’: Hosted by Scott Aukerman, ‘Comedy Bang! Bang!’ is a podcast that features interviews with both established and up-and-coming comedians. The show’s format is a mix of improv games, character sketches, and hilarious conversations that never fail to entertain. You’ll find yourself laughing out loud and becoming a fan of the guests’ comedic talents.

5. ‘The Dollop’: This podcast, hosted by comedians Dave Anthony and Gareth Reynolds, combines history and comedy in a unique and captivating way. Each episode explores a bizarre or lesser-known story from American history, with Dave narrating and Gareth adding comedic commentary. ‘The Dollop’ is both educational and hilarious, making it a perfect choice for your daily commute.

6. ‘How Did This Get Made?’: If you’re a fan of movies, particularly the not-so-great ones, you’ll love ‘How Did This Get Made?’ Hosted by Paul Scheer, Jason Mantzoukas, and June Diane Raphael, this podcast dissects and hilariously critiques some of the worst movies ever made. The hosts’ chemistry and comedic timing will surely have you laughing in your seat.

7. ‘The Hilarious World of Depression’: While not a traditional comedy podcast, ‘The Hilarious World of Depression’ offers a unique blend of humor and vulnerability. Hosted by John Moe, this podcast features interviews with comedians who have experienced mental health challenges. By combining personal stories with laughter, this podcast offers a refreshingly honest look at mental health and comedy.

8. ‘No Such Thing As A Fish’: Hosted by the researchers behind the hit BBC quiz show ‘QI,’ ‘No Such Thing As A Fish’ explores bizarre and interesting facts from around the world. With its clever wit and playful banter, this podcast will keep you entertained and laughing while expanding your knowledge.

9. ‘The Ricky Gervais Show’: Fans of Ricky Gervais will enjoy this podcast, which features Ricky, Stephen Merchant, and Karl Pilkington discussing a wide range of topics. The hosts’ humorous conversations and Karl’s unique take on the world make ‘The Ricky Gervais Show’ a comedy gem.

10. ‘Call Chelsea Peretti’: If you’re a fan of Chelsea Peretti’s stand-up comedy and acting, you’ll love her podcast. In ‘Call Chelsea Peretti,’ Chelsea takes calls from listeners and responds to their questions and comments in her signature comedic style. This podcast offers a mix of funny anecdotes, hilarious rants, and insightful advice.

These are just a few of the many comedy podcasts that will keep you laughing on your daily commute. Whether you’re a fan of stand-up comedy, improv, or conversations with funny people, there’s a podcast out there for you. So next time you’re stuck in traffic or waiting for your train, give one of these comedy podcasts a try. You won’t be disappointed!
