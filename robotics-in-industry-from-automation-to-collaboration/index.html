Robotics in Industry: From Automation to Collaboration


Robotics has become an integral part of the industrial sector in recent years. Industrial automation has been implemented for decades, with the primary goal being to automate repetitive tasks. With the advancement of technology, it is no longer just about saving time and increasing productivity; robotics in industry has shifted to collaborative robots, also known as cobots, that can work alongside humans.

Automation of Industrial Processes

Industrial robots have been in use for decades for automation and controlling of industrial processes. These types of robots have been used to improve productivity, reduce costs, increase efficiency, and minimize human errors. Automation in industries lead to significant productivity gains and results in increased profitability. These robots are programmed to perform repetitive tasks that have a high precision level.

In the automotive industry, robots perform welding, assembly, and painting without any human intervention. In the food processing industry, they can perform sorting, packaging, and even slicing. These machines allow workers to shift to more complex tasks, thereby enhancing productivity and reducing repetitive strain injuries.

Some Benefits of Automation

There are quite a few benefits to the automation of industrial processes, which include:

• Improved Quality Control – Automation helps to maintain production standards and uniformity of products.

• Increased Productivity – Industrial robots work 24/7 and do not need breaks or rest days. This improves the production capacity of the industry.

• Consistency and Speed – Robots can work faster and more consistently than humans, thus increasing speed of production.

• Higher Safety Standards – Automation significantly reduces the possibility of worker injuries, leading to a safer workplace.

Collaborative Robots

The emergence of cobots is a game-changer for industry. Cobots have been designed to collaborate with humans to perform complex tasks and are designed to work safely alongside humans. These robots are safe to work with, efficient, and flexible due to their modular design. Collaborative robots are lightweight and can easily be moved from one workstation to another.

The increased use of collaborative robots in industry means humans, and robots are now working more closely than ever before increasing the productivity level in the industry. This shift in the use of robots also calls for a more skilled workforce, with workers needing to be trained on how to operate and program the robots. The use of cobots in the industry has also led to the creation of new jobs such as robot supervisors or robot trainers.

Some Advantages of Collaborative Robots

Below are some advantages of collaborative robots:

• Increased Accuracy – Cobots are designed to work precisely alongside humans, ensuring that tasks are carried out with high precision levels.

• Improved Flexibility – Collaborative robots can be programmed quickly to perform a wide range of tasks.

• Improved Efficiency – Cobots support human operators by automating repetitive or dangerous tasks.

• Increased Safety – Collaborative robots are designed to work alongside humans, thus reducing the possibility of accidents in the workplace.

Conclusion

The use of robots in industry is set to increase even more as the need for increased accuracy, improved efficiency, and safety becomes a priority. Automation has reduced the amount of repetitive work done by humans, resulting in safer workplaces and a more skilled workforce. Cobots have opened new collaboration opportunities between humans and robots, further increasing the efficiency and productivity of the industry. With continued technological development and investment, the future of robotics in industry is set to be exciting and transformative.
