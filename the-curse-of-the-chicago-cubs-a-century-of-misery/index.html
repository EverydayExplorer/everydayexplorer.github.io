The Curse of the Chicago Cubs: A Century of Misery


In the storied history of Major League Baseball, few teams evoke as much emotion and fascination as the Chicago Cubs. The Cubs’ century-long championship drought has been the subject of countless articles, books, documentaries, and conversations. For Cubs fans, the elusive taste of victory has become synonymous with their beloved team.

It all began in 1908 when the Cubs clinched their last World Series title, defeating the Detroit Tigers in five games. Little did they know that it would be the pinnacle of success for the next century. Since that fateful year, the Cubs have come agonizingly close to championship glory on several occasions, only to fall short in heartbreaking fashion.

One cannot discuss the Curse of the Chicago Cubs without mentioning the infamous curse of the billy goat. During the 1945 World Series, a tavern owner named William Sianis attempted to bring his pet goat, Murphy, into Wrigley Field. However, he was refused entry, and in a fit of frustration, Sianis declared, ‘Them Cubs, they ain’t gonna win no more.’ The Cubs lost the series to the Detroit Tigers, and the curse was born.

As time went on, the curse gained traction in popular culture and Cubs lore. It seemed that no matter the talent on the field, the Cubs were destined to fall short. They became known as the ‘Lovable Losers,’ a term used to describe their endearing but perennial lack of success. This reputation further cemented the idea of a curse hanging over the franchise.

Throughout the decades, the Cubs had their fair share of near-misses. The 1969 season stands out as a particularly painful memory for Cubs fans. With a commanding lead in the National League East, the team famously collapsed, allowing the New York Mets to surge past them and win the division. This collapse became known as the ‘Miracle Mets’ and left Cubs fans devastated once again.

Even with glimpses of hope, such as playoff appearances in 1984 and 1989, the Cubs couldn’t overcome the weight of history. The Steve Bartman incident in 2003 further added to the team’s misery. With a chance to advance to the World Series, a foul ball down the left-field line turned into a disaster when a fan, Steve Bartman, reached for the ball, interfering with the play. The Cubs went on to lose that game and the series, further fueling the belief in the curse.

However, the turning point came in 2016. Against all odds, the Cubs finally broke the curse and won their first World Series title in 108 years. Led by a talented roster, including Kris Bryant, Anthony Rizzo, and Javier Baez, the team defeated the Cleveland Indians in an epic seven-game series. The curse was finally lifted, and Cubs fans rejoiced in the culmination of a century of heartache.

The Curse of the Chicago Cubs serves as a reminder of the power of belief and perseverance. Despite the countless disappointments and setbacks, the Cubs and their fans never lost hope. This enduring loyalty became a defining characteristic of the franchise, and the ultimate redemption in 2016 only amplified the joy felt throughout Chicago and among Cubs fans worldwide.

In conclusion, the Curse of the Chicago Cubs shaped the narrative of the team for over a century. It became a symbol of perseverance, loyalty, and unwavering hope. While the curse may be a thing of the past, its legacy lives on in the hearts of Cubs fans. With the curse broken, the Cubs look to the future with renewed optimism, knowing that they have overcome the seemingly insurmountable and emerged triumphant.
