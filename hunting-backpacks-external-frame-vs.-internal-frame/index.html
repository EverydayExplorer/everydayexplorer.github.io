Hunting Backpacks: External Frame vs. Internal Frame


When it comes to hunting, having a reliable backpack is essential. It serves as your lifeline, carrying all the necessary gear and supplies for a successful outing. While there are various types of hunting backpacks available, the debate between external frame and internal frame backpacks continues to divide hunters. In this blog post, we will explore the differences between these two types of hunting backpacks and help you make an informed decision on which one suits your needs best.

External Frame Backpacks

External frame backpacks have been around for decades and were the go-to choice for hunters in the past. These backpacks feature a sturdy metal or plastic frame that is attached to the outside of the pack. The frame provides structure and support, allowing for better weight distribution. This design is particularly beneficial when carrying heavy loads or hauling out game.

One of the advantages of an external frame backpack is its excellent ventilation. The frame keeps the pack away from your back, allowing air to circulate, which helps prevent excessive sweating during long treks. Additionally, the external frame provides ample space to attach extra gear or bulky items, such as a tent or sleeping bag, making it ideal for extended hunting trips.

Internal Frame Backpacks

In recent years, internal frame backpacks have gained popularity among hunters due to their versatility and improved design. Unlike external frame backpacks, internal frame packs have a built-in frame made of lightweight materials such as aluminum or carbon fiber. The frame is hidden within the pack, providing a sleek and streamlined appearance.

One of the significant advantages of internal frame backpacks is their ability to hug your body closely. This feature allows for increased stability and maneuverability, making it easier to navigate through rough terrains and dense vegetation. The compact design also reduces the risk of snagging on branches or getting caught in tight spaces.

Another advantage of internal frame backpacks is their weight distribution. The frame conforms to your body, ensuring that the load stays centered and close to your center of gravity. This feature is particularly useful when climbing or trekking on uneven terrain, as it minimizes the risk of losing balance or straining your back.

Choosing the Right Backpack for You

When deciding between an external frame and internal frame backpack, it’s essential to consider your hunting style and specific needs. If you frequently embark on long hunting trips or need to carry heavy gear, an external frame backpack might be the best choice for you. The additional storage space and weight distribution capabilities make it a reliable companion for extended adventures.

On the other hand, if you prefer a more compact and agile backpack that allows for better maneuverability, an internal frame backpack should be your top pick. Its streamlined design and close fit to your body ensure maximum comfort and stability during challenging hunts.

In conclusion, both external frame and internal frame backpacks have their unique advantages. It ultimately boils down to personal preference and the requirements of your hunting expeditions. Regardless of your choice, investing in a high-quality hunting backpack is crucial to ensure a successful and enjoyable hunting experience. So, choose wisely and embark on your next hunting adventure with confidence!

Remember, your backpack is your trusted companion in the wilderness, carrying not only your gear but also your hopes and aspirations for a fruitful hunt. Make sure to choose a backpack that aligns with your needs and preferences, and let it be the backbone of your hunting endeavors. Happy hunting!
