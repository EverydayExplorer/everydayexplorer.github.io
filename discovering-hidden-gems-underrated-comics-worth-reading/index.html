Discovering Hidden Gems: Underrated Comics Worth Reading


In the bustling world of comic books, where caped crusaders and iconic heroes dominate the shelves, there exists a realm of hidden gems waiting to be unearthed. These underrated comics, often overshadowed by the mainstream titles, possess a charm and allure all their own. From indie darlings to overlooked series from major publishers, these hidden treasures offer readers a fresh perspective and a unique storytelling experience. Join me as we delve into the world of underrated comics worth discovering.

  1. ‘Saga’ by Brian K. Vaughan and Fiona Staples Topping many lists of underrated comics is ‘Saga,’ a space opera epic that defies genre conventions. Written by Brian K. Vaughan and beautifully illustrated by Fiona Staples, ‘Saga’ follows the journey of star-crossed lovers Alana and Marko as they navigate a war-torn galaxy with their newborn daughter in tow. Filled with imaginative world-building, complex characters, and poignant themes, ‘Saga’ is a must-read for any comic book enthusiast seeking a captivating narrative.

  2. ‘The Umbrella Academy’ by Gerard Way and Gabriel Bá Created by My Chemical Romance frontman Gerard Way and artist Gabriel Bá, ‘The Umbrella Academy’ offers a refreshing take on the superhero genre. Set in an alternate universe where a dysfunctional family of super-powered individuals must band together to save the world, this series blends humor, heart, and action in equal measure. With its quirky characters and inventive plot twists, ‘The Umbrella Academy’ has earned its place as a beloved cult classic.

  3. ‘Monstress’ by Marjorie Liu and Sana Takeda A dark fantasy masterpiece, ‘Monstress’ transports readers to a world ravaged by war and political intrigue. Written by Marjorie Liu and lavishly illustrated by Sana Takeda, this Eisner Award-winning series follows the journey of Maika Halfwolf, a young woman with a mysterious past and a powerful connection to a deadly supernatural force. With its lush artwork and richly layered storytelling, ‘Monstress’ offers a captivating blend of horror, magic, and feminist themes.

  4. ‘Bone’ by Jeff Smith A modern classic of the graphic novel medium, ‘Bone’ is a sprawling epic that defies categorization. Written and illustrated by Jeff Smith, this whimsical tale follows the adventures of three cousins—Fone Bone, Phoney Bone, and Smiley Bone—as they journey through a fantastical land filled with talking animals, dragons, and ancient mysteries. With its endearing characters and timeless appeal, ‘Bone’ has enchanted readers of all ages for decades.

  5. ‘Paper Girls’ by Brian K. Vaughan and Cliff Chiang Set in the 1980s, ‘Paper Girls’ follows a group of teenage newspaper delivery girls who find themselves caught in a time-traveling adventure of epic proportions. Written by Brian K. Vaughan and illustrated by Cliff Chiang, this series blends nostalgia, humor, and sci-fi thrills into a compulsively readable saga. With its engaging premise and dynamic artwork, ‘Paper Girls’ is a hidden gem that deserves a spot on every comic book aficionado’s shelf.

In a medium dominated by blockbuster franchises and iconic superheroes, it’s easy for lesser-known titles to get lost in the shuffle. However, by seeking out these underrated comics, readers can discover a wealth of untapped creativity and imagination. Whether you’re a fan of sci-fi epics, dark fantasies, or quirky comedies, there’s a hidden gem waiting to capture your imagination and expand your horizons. So don’t be afraid to stray off the beaten path and explore the diverse and vibrant world of underrated comics. Who knows what hidden treasures you might uncover?
