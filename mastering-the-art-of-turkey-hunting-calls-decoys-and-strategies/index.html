Mastering the Art of Turkey Hunting: Calls, Decoys, and Strategies


Turkey hunting is an exhilarating outdoor activity that requires skill, patience, and a deep understanding of turkey behavior. Whether you are a seasoned hunter or a beginner, mastering the art of turkey hunting can greatly enhance your chances of success in the field. In this blog post, we will explore the critical elements of turkey hunting, including calls, decoys, and strategies, and provide you with valuable tips to improve your skills.

Understanding Turkey Behavior

Before delving into turkey hunting techniques, it is crucial to gain a fundamental understanding of turkey behavior. Turkeys are highly intelligent creatures with keen senses, including exceptional eyesight and hearing. They have the ability to quickly detect any unusual movements or sounds in their surroundings, making stealth a vital component of a successful hunt.

Turkeys are social animals and communicate with each other through a variety of calls and body language. By familiarizing yourself with the different turkey vocalizations, you can effectively mimic their sounds, increasing your chances of attracting them within range.

The Art of Turkey Calling

Turkey calling is an essential skill for any aspiring turkey hunter. There are various types of calls that you can use, each serving a unique purpose. Some of the most common turkey calls include:

  1. Box Calls: A box call consists of a hollow wooden box and a lid. By sliding the lid across the top of the box, you can produce realistic turkey sounds. Box calls are versatile and produce both high-pitched yelps and low-pitched purrs, making them an excellent choice for beginners.

  2. Slate Calls: Slate calls, also known as friction calls, produce realistic turkey sounds by rubbing a striker against a slate surface. These calls are extremely versatile and can imitate a wide range of turkey vocalizations, including purrs, clucks, and yelps.

  3. Mouth Calls: Mouth calls, or diaphragm calls, are placed inside the hunter’s mouth, allowing for hands-free operation. With practice, hunters can produce various turkey sounds, such as yelps, cuts, and gobbles, using different tongue and mouth movements. Mouth calls require practice to master, but they offer the advantage of leaving your hands free to hold your firearm or bow.

  4. Locator Calls: Locator calls are not meant to imitate turkey sounds but rather to elicit a response from turkeys in the area. These calls mimic the vocalizations of other animals, such as owls or crows, and can help locate the roosting or feeding areas of turkeys.

To become proficient in turkey calling, it is essential to practice regularly. Listening to real turkey vocalizations and instructional videos can provide valuable insights into the nuances of different calls. Additionally, using a call with the correct cadence, pitch, and volume can significantly increase your chances of attracting a turkey within range.

The Role of Decoys in Turkey Hunting

Decoys play a vital role in turkey hunting, as they provide a visual attractant to lure turkeys within shooting range. When strategically placed, decoys can create an illusion of safety and encourage approaching turkeys to display dominant or courtship behavior.

There are various types of turkey decoys available on the market, including jake (juvenile male), hen, and tom (mature male) decoys. Each decoy serves a distinct purpose:

  • Hen Decoys: Hen decoys are lifelike replicas of female turkeys. They are effective in attracting toms during the breeding season, as they signify the presence of receptive hens.

  • Jake Decoys: Jake decoys replicate young male turkeys and can be used in conjunction with hen decoys. They can either elicit a response from a dominant tom, seeking to establish its dominance, or attract curious subordinate toms.

  • Tom Decoys: Tom decoys resemble mature male turkeys and can be used to challenge dominant toms. They are highly effective during the early breeding season when toms are actively seeking out rivals.

Proper decoy placement is crucial to maximize their effectiveness. Positioning a hen decoy in an open area, such as a field or clearing, can make it visible from a distance, attracting toms. Placing a jake decoy near the hen decoy signifies competition and can provoke aggressive responses from dominant toms. Ensuring that the decoys face your hunting location can encourage turkeys to come within shooting range.

Effective Strategies for Turkey Hunting

In addition to mastering turkey calling and utilizing decoys, employing effective hunting strategies is key to a successful turkey hunt. Here are some valuable tips to improve your chances of harvesting a turkey:

  1. Scouting: Spend time scouting your hunting area before the season begins. Look for signs of turkey activity, such as scratch marks, feathers, or droppings. Knowing where the turkeys roost, feed, and travel can give you a significant advantage on your hunt.

  2. Camouflage: Turkeys have excellent eyesight and can easily detect any unnatural movements or colors. Wearing a camouflage suit that matches your surroundings, including a face mask and gloves, is essential for blending in with your environment.

  3. Concealment: Setting up in a concealed location, such as a ground blind or natural cover, can provide you with the necessary cover to remain undetected while waiting for turkeys to approach.

  4. Patience: Turkey hunting requires patience and perseverance. Don’t be discouraged if your initial attempts are unsuccessful. Stay focused, remain still, and wait for the perfect shot opportunity.

  5. Plan Your Shot: Before taking a shot, make sure you have a clear view of the turkey’s vitals. Aim for the head and neck or the vital area of the turkey’s body for a clean and ethical kill.

  6. Safety: Always prioritize safety while turkey hunting. Be aware of your surroundings and never shoot unless you have positively identified your target and what lies beyond it.

By honing your turkey calling skills, utilizing decoys effectively, and employing sound hunting strategies, you can become a skilled turkey hunter. Remember to practice regularly and be open to learning from experienced hunters. Turkey hunting is a challenging and rewarding pursuit, and with the right combination of skills and knowledge, you can master the art of turkey hunting. Happy hunting!
