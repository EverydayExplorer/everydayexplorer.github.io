The Best Places to Go Camping in New Zealand


Welcome to our adventurous journey through the breathtaking landscapes of New Zealand, where nature lovers and camping enthusiasts can find an abundance of stunning locations to pitch their tents and connect with the great outdoors. From snow-capped mountains to pristine beaches, New Zealand offers a diverse range of camping experiences that are sure to leave you in awe. So, grab your camping gear and join us as we explore the best places to go camping in this magnificent country!

1. Milford Sound:
Tucked away in the heart of Fiordland National Park, Milford Sound is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that features towering cliffs, cascading waterfalls, and picturesque valleys. Camping here allows you to immerse yourself in the tranquility of this stunning fiord, with options for both beachside and forest camping.

2. Abel Tasman National Park:
If you’re a fan of golden sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lush green forests, Abel Tasman National Park is the perfect camping destination. Located on the northern tip of the South Island, this coastal paradise boasts a variety of camping options, including beachfront campsites and scenic forest spots.

3. Tongariro National Park:
Known for its dramatic volcanic landscapes, Tongariro National Park offers a unique camping experience. Home to the famed Tongariro Alpine Crossing, this park allows you to wake up to breathtaking views of volcanic peaks and emerald lakes. Camping here provides an opportunity to explore some of New Zealand’s most iconic hiking trails.

4. Lake Tekapo:
Situated in the heart of the South Island, Lake Tekapo offers mesmerizing views of the Southern Alps and its stunning turquoise waters. Camping here provides a front-row seat to witness the celestial beauty of the night sky, as Lake Tekapo is part of the Aoraki Mackenzie International Dark Sky Reserve.

5. Mount Cook National Park:
Speaking of the night sky, Mount Cook National Park is another remarkable destination for camping and stargazing. Nestled beneath the majestic peaks of the Southern Alps, this park offers a range of camping options amidst the alpine beauty. Be prepared to be awe-struck by the grandeur of New Zealand’s highest mountain, Aoraki/Mount Cook.

6. Coromandel Peninsula:
For those seeking a coastal camping adventure, the Coromandel Peninsula is a must-visit destination. With its stunning white-sand beaches, lush rainforests, and hidden coves, this region offers a range of campsites that cater to all preferences. Don’t miss out on exploring the famous Cathedral Cove and Hot Water Beach while you’re here!

7. Fiordland National Park:
We cannot forget to mention Fiordland National Park, home to the world-famous Milford Sound. While camping in the park, you’ll be surrounded by towering peaks, pristine lakes, and dense rainforests. Take a boat cruise through the fiords or embark on a multi-day hiking adventure on one of the park’s renowned tracks, such as the Milford Track or the Kepler Track.

8. Mount Aspiring National Park:
Located in the Southern Alps, Mount Aspiring National Park is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. With its glacier-carved valleys, pristine rivers, and majestic peaks, this park offers a range of camping opportunities for nature lovers. Whether you prefer riverside camping or alpine adventures, Mount Aspiring has it all.

9. Stewart Island:
For those seeking a truly remote and untouched camping experience, Stewart Island is a hidden gem. Located off the southern coast of the South Island, this island is a haven for nature lovers and offers a unique opportunity to spot rare wildlife, including the elusive kiwi bird. Camping here allows you to disconnect from the world and immerse yourself in the island’s serene beauty.

10. Rotorua:
Known for its geothermal wonders, Rotorua offers a unique camping experience amidst bubbling mud pools, geysers, and hot springs. Enjoy the therapeutic benefits of soaking in natural hot pools and indulge in the region’s rich Maori culture. Rotorua’s camping grounds provide an excellent base to explore the surrounding geothermal attractions.

New Zealand truly offers an incredible array of camping destinations, each with its unique charm and natural wonders. Whether you’re seeking coastal beauty, alpine adventures, or immersive wildlife encounters, this country has it all. So, pack your camping gear, embrace the spirit of adventure, and get ready to create memories that will last a lifetime in the best places to go camping in New Zealand!
