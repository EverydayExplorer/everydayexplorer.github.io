Creating a Personal Board of Advisors for Career Guidance


In today’s fast-paced and competitive job market, it’s essential to have a strong support system to guide you throughout your career. While mentors and coaches can provide valuable advice and insights, creating a personal board of advisors takes career guidance to the next level. This group of trusted individuals can offer diverse perspectives, share expertise, and provide valuable connections that can propel your career forward. In this blog post, we will explore the importance of creating a personal board of advisors and how to build one effectively.

Why do you need a personal board of advisors?

  1. Diverse perspectives: One of the most significant advantages of having a personal board of advisors is gaining access to a range of viewpoints. Each advisor brings a unique set of experiences, skills, and expertise, which can help you see things from different angles and broaden your understanding of various career opportunities.

  2. Supplementing existing knowledge and skills: No matter how talented and knowledgeable you are, there will always be areas where you lack expertise. By surrounding yourself with advisors who specialize in different fields, you can tap into their knowledge and skills to supplement your own. This allows you to learn from the best and continuously improve your abilities.

  3. Access to valuable connections: Your personal board of advisors can provide you with access to their professional networks, opening doors to new opportunities and connections within your industry. These connections can be instrumental in advancing your career, securing new clients, or even landing your dream job.

  4. Accountability and support: Career decisions can be tough, and having a group of trusted advisors to bounce ideas off can provide you with valuable feedback and support. They can hold you accountable for your goals, challenge your assumptions, and provide guidance during critical decision-making moments.

How to build a personal board of advisors

  1. Identify your needs and goals: Before recruiting advisors, take some time to reflect on your career aspirations and the areas where you need support the most. Determine the specific skills, expertise, and perspectives you would like your advisors to possess, so you can create a well-rounded board that addresses your specific needs.

  2. Seek diverse perspectives: Look for individuals who possess different backgrounds, experiences, and skill sets. This diversity will ensure that you have a wide range of perspectives to draw from when seeking advice. Consider people from different industries, seniority levels, and even different geographical locations.

  3. Consider trusted contacts: Start by reaching out to your existing network of contacts, including previous colleagues, mentors, former professors, and industry leaders. These individuals already know you and can vouch for your skills and potential. They will be more likely to accept your invitation to be part of your board of advisors.

  4. Engage with industry thought leaders: Attend industry conferences, seminars, and networking events to connect with influencers and thought leaders. Engaging with these individuals can help expand your network and allow you to establish relationships with industry experts who can provide valuable guidance.

  5. Formalize the relationship: Once you’ve identified potential advisors, reach out to them and explain your interest in forming a personal board of advisors. Clearly articulate your goals and expectations, and be prepared to articulate what value you would like to provide in return. Establish a regular cadence for meeting or communicating, and be respectful of their time and schedule.

  6. Maintain regular communication: Regular check-ins with your personal board of advisors are essential for building and maintaining these relationships. Be diligent about scheduling meetings or calls, and come prepared with specific questions or topics to discuss. Additionally, keep them updated on your progress and any major developments in your career.

  7. Provide value: Remember that relationships are a two-way street. Offer your skills, knowledge, and expertise to your advisors whenever possible. Share articles or industry insights that might be relevant to their work, extend professional introductions, or even offer to lend a helping hand on projects they are working on. By being proactive and providing value, you will strengthen these relationships over time.

In conclusion, building a personal board of advisors is a strategic move that can greatly enhance your career trajectory. Through the diverse perspectives, expertise, and connections of your advisors, you can gain valuable insights, make more informed decisions, and expand your professional network. Invest time and effort in cultivating these relationships, and you will reap the rewards throughout your career journey. Remember, success is not achieved alone, and surrounding yourself with a personal board of advisors can be the key to unlocking your full potential.
