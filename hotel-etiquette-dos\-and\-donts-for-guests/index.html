Hotel Etiquette: Dos\ and\ Donts for Guests


When it comes to staying at a hotel, it’s important to remember that you are a guest and should act accordingly. Having good hotel etiquette not only ensures a pleasant and smooth experience for yourself, but also demonstrates respect for the staff and other guests. In this blog post, we will explore some of the do’s and don’ts for hotel guests, helping you make the most of your stay while being mindful of others.

Do’s

  1. Do greet the hotel staff: A warm smile and a friendly ‘hello’ go a long way in setting a positive tone for your stay. Remember that hotel staff work hard to ensure your comfort, and acknowledging their efforts can make a big difference.

  2. Do respect the hotel policies: Familiarize yourself with the hotel’s rules and regulations upon check-in. These policies are in place to maintain a pleasant environment for all guests, so make sure to adhere to them.

  3. Do keep your noise level down: Remember that hotels are shared spaces, and excessive noise can disturb other guests. Keep your volume to a reasonable level, especially during quiet hours.

  4. Do communicate your needs: If you have any specific requirements or preferences, such as extra pillows or a change in room temperature, don’t hesitate to inform the hotel staff. They are there to ensure your comfort and will do their best to meet your needs.

  5. Do maintain cleanliness: Treat your hotel room as you would your own home. Keep it clean and tidy, disposing of trash properly. Remember to report any maintenance issues promptly so that they may be addressed.

  6. Do tip appropriately: While tipping customs can vary by country, it is generally considered polite to tip hotel staff for exceptional service. This includes housekeeping, bellhops, and concierge services.

  7. Do control your children: If you are traveling with kids, ensure they are well-behaved and mindful of other guests. It is your responsibility to ensure they do not disrupt the tranquility of the hotel.

  8. Do be courteous in shared spaces: Respect other guests in common areas such as the lobby, elevators, and dining areas. Allow others to pass, keep conversations at a moderate volume, and be mindful of personal space.

Don’ts

  1. Don’t smoke in non-smoking areas: Most hotels have designated smoking areas, so make sure to follow these guidelines. Smoking in non-smoking areas not only goes against hotel policy but can also disturb other guests.

  2. Don’t misuse hotel property: Hotel amenities, such as towels, bathrobes, or room furnishings, are provided for your convenience during your stay. Misusing or removing these items can result in additional charges and is considered disrespectful.

  3. Don’t disturb other guests late at night: Be mindful of the time when returning to your room or engaging in activities that may create noise. Avoid slamming doors, loud conversations, or playing loud music during late hours.

  4. Don’t ignore safety precautions: Familiarize yourself with the hotel’s emergency exits and safety procedures. In the event of an emergency, stay calm and follow instructions provided by the staff.

  5. Don’t be demanding or rude: Remember that the hotel staff is there to assist you, but being overly demanding or rude will not yield positive results. Treat the staff with respect and address any concerns in a polite and constructive manner.

  6. Don’t overcrowd the elevator: During peak hours, elevators can become crowded. Be considerate of other guests and allow enough space for everyone. If possible, opt for the stairs to ease congestion.

  7. Don’t leave your belongings unattended: Keep your valuables secure by using the hotel safe or keeping them with you. It is better to be safe than sorry when it comes to your personal belongings.

  8. Don’t overstay your welcome in common areas: While it’s fine to spend time in common areas, remember that others may also want to utilize these spaces. Avoid hogging seats or tables for an extended period, especially during busy times.

By following these do’s and don’ts of hotel etiquette, you can ensure a pleasant and respectful stay for yourself and those around you. Remember that being a good hotel guest goes a long way in creating a positive and enjoyable experience for everyone involved. So, whether you’re on a business trip, a family vacation, or simply enjoying a getaway, make sure to practice good hotel etiquette and leave a positive impression wherever you go!
