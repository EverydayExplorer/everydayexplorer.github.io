Puppy Training Basics: From Potty Training to Basic Commands


Bringing home a new puppy is an exciting and joyous experience. However, along with all the fun and cuddles, comes the responsibility of training your furry friend. Ensuring your puppy is well-behaved and understands basic commands is crucial for their safety and your peace of mind. In this blog post, we will guide you through the puppy training basics, including potty training and teaching them basic commands.

1. Potty Training

Potty training is often the first challenge new puppy owners face. It can be a frustrating and messy process, but with consistency and patience, you can successfully train your puppy to relieve themselves in the appropriate area.

  • Establish a routine: Take your puppy outside first thing in the morning, after meals, and before bedtime. Creating a consistent schedule will help them understand when and where they should go.
  • Positive reinforcement: Reward your puppy with praise and treats when they eliminate in the designated area. This positive reinforcement will help them associate going potty outside with positive experiences.
  • Supervision and confinement: When you can’t keep a close eye on your puppy, use a crate or a confined space to prevent accidents. Dogs naturally avoid soiling their sleeping area, so they will try to hold it until you let them outside.
  • Clean accidents properly: Accidents happen, especially during the early stages of training. Clean up accidents promptly using enzyme-based cleaners to remove the scent completely. If the scent remains, it may encourage your puppy to go in the same spot again.

2. Basic Commands

Teaching your puppy basic commands lays the foundation for more advanced training and ensures their safety in various situations. Here are some essential commands to focus on:

  • Sit: Hold a treat close to your puppy’s nose and then move it up, causing their head to follow the treat and their bottom to lower. Once they are in the sitting position, say ‘sit’ and reward them with the treat and praise. Repeat this command several times until they understand.
  • Stay: Begin with your puppy in a sitting position. Open your palm and say ‘stay’ while taking a step back. Reward them with praise and treats for staying put. Gradually increase the distance and duration of the stay over time.
  • Come: Start by getting down to your puppy’s level and opening your arms. Excitedly say ‘come’ and encourage them to run to you. When they reach you, reward them with praise and treats. Practicing this command in a safe and controlled environment will help your puppy understand the importance of coming when called.
  • Lie Down: Begin with your puppy in the sitting position. Hold a treat close to their nose and slowly move it towards the ground, causing them to lie down. Once they are lying down, say ‘down’ and reward them with the treat and praise. Practice this command until your puppy can lie down on command.

3. Consistency and Positive Reinforcement

Consistency is key when it comes to puppy training. Set clear rules and enforce them every day. Use the same commands, gestures, and rewards consistently so that your puppy can understand what you expect from them. Additionally, make sure to provide positive reinforcement, such as treats, praise, and affection, whenever your puppy behaves correctly. This will motivate them to repeat the desired behavior.

4. Avoid Harsh Training Methods

It’s important to note that using harsh training methods or punishments can have negative effects on your puppy’s behavior and overall well-being. Physical punishment or yelling can cause fear and anxiety, hindering the training process. Instead, focus on positive reinforcement and gentle guidance to help your puppy become a well-adjusted and obedient companion.

Final Thoughts

Puppy training requires time, patience, and consistency. By following the basics of potty training and teaching your puppy essential commands using positive reinforcement, you are setting them up for a lifetime of good behavior. Remember to be patient and understanding, as each puppy learns at their own pace. Enjoy the journey of training your puppy and building a strong bond that will last for years to come.
