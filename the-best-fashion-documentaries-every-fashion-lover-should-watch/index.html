The Best Fashion Documentaries Every Fashion Lover Should Watch


Fashion documentaries have become increasingly popular in recent years, offering a behind-the-scenes look into the fashion industry, its influential figures, and the creative process behind some of the most iconic designs. Whether you’re a fashion lover or simply interested in the world of style, these documentaries are a must-watch. From stories of legendary designers to explorations of fashion’s impact on society, here are some of the best fashion documentaries that will both educate and inspire.

1. The September Issue (2009)

Directed by R.J. Cutler, The September Issue takes you on a journey into the making of the September 2007 issue of Vogue magazine, which at the time was the largest issue ever published. This documentary offers a captivating insight into the world of fashion journalism, featuring Vogue’s influential editor-in-chief, Anna Wintour, and her team as they navigate the high-pressure process of creating a fashion bible. With stunning visuals and an intimate portrayal of the fashion industry, this documentary is a must-watch for any fashion enthusiast.

2. Dior and I (2014)

Directed by Frédéric Tcheng, Dior and I follows the creative director of Christian Dior, Raf Simons, as he prepares his first haute couture collection for the iconic fashion house. The documentary offers viewers a rare glimpse into the meticulous craftsmanship of haute couture, showcasing the intense pressure and dedication required to create a collection in just eight weeks. With its beautiful cinematography and insider access to the prestigious House of Dior, this documentary is a feast for the eyes and a testament to the artistry of fashion.

3. The First Monday in May (2016)

Directed by Andrew Rossi, The First Monday in May explores the intersection of fashion and art through the lens of the Metropolitan Museum of Art’s Costume Institute and its annual gala. The documentary highlights the planning and execution of the highly anticipated event, providing an inside look at the collaboration between the worlds of fashion, celebrity, and museum culture. With appearances by industry icons such as Anna Wintour and designers like Karl Lagerfeld and Andrew Bolton, this documentary shows how fashion can be seen as a form of artistic expression.

4. McQueen (2018)

Directed by Ian Bonhôte and Peter Ettedgui, McQueen tells the extraordinary story of fashion designer Alexander McQueen, known for his shocking and boundary-pushing creations. The documentary delves into McQueen’s rebellious spirit, his tumultuous personal life, and his groundbreaking collections that challenged the norms of the fashion industry. Through archival footage and interviews with friends, family, and collaborators, this documentary celebrates McQueen’s creativity while shedding light on the dark side of fame and the pressures of the fashion world.

5. The True Cost (2015)

Directed by Andrew Morgan, The True Cost is a thought-provoking documentary that examines the social and environmental impact of fast fashion. As consumers, we often don’t see the behind-the-scenes of the industry, and this film sheds light on the hidden costs of our clothing obsession. From devastating environmental pollution to the exploitation of garment workers, The True Cost urges viewers to rethink their buying habits and consider the ethical implications of the fashion choices they make.

6. Bill Cunningham New York (2010)

Directed by Richard Press, Bill Cunningham New York pays tribute to legendary street fashion photographer Bill Cunningham, who was known for his candid snapshots of everyday New Yorkers’ style. The documentary offers an intimate look into Cunningham’s life and his incredible dedication to his craft. His passion for fashion and his unique viewpoint on style are celebrated throughout the film, making it a must-watch for anyone interested in the intersection between fashion and street culture.

7. Valentino: The Last Emperor (2008)

Directed by Matt Tyrnauer, Valentino: The Last Emperor follows fashion icon Valentino Garavani during the last two years of his career. The documentary captures Valentino’s final couture show and gives viewers a glimpse into his extravagant lifestyle and the empire he built. With appearances by industry luminaries such as Anna Wintour and Giorgio Armani, this film showcases Valentino’s undeniable influence on the fashion world and his unwavering commitment to beauty and elegance.

Conclusion

Fashion documentaries offer a unique and informative way to explore the diverse aspects of the fashion industry. From the high-pressure world of Vogue magazine to the creative process behind iconic fashion houses, these documentaries provide viewers with rare insights into the fashion world’s inner workings. Whether you’re interested in the history of fashion, the impact of fast fashion, or the lives of legendary designers, there is a documentary out there that will cater to your interests. So grab some popcorn, sit back, and prepare to be enthralled by the captivating world of fashion.
