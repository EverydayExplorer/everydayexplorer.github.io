Hidden Gems: Underrated Books You Need to Add to Your TBR


In a world where bestseller lists dominate the literary landscape, it’s easy for lesser-known gems to slip through the cracks. But if you’re tired of reading the same titles everyone else is talking about, it might be time to dive into the world of underrated books. These hidden gems might not have the marketing muscle of their blockbuster counterparts, but they pack just as much punch when it comes to storytelling. So, if you’re looking to add some fresh titles to your TBR pile, here are some underrated books that deserve a spot on your shelf.

1. ‘The Shadow of the Wind’ by Carlos Ruiz Zafón
Set in post-war Barcelona, this atmospheric novel follows young Daniel as he discovers a mysterious book in the Cemetery of Forgotten Books. As he delves into the story behind the book and its author, Daniel finds himself drawn into a web of secrets and intrigue. With its lush prose and intricate plot, ‘The Shadow of the Wind’ is a literary masterpiece that deserves a wider audience.

2. ‘Station Eleven’ by Emily St. John Mandel
In this hauntingly beautiful novel, Mandel imagines a world devastated by a flu pandemic, where civilization as we know it has collapsed. As a traveling troupe of actors and musicians roams the barren landscape, they strive to preserve art and culture in a world that has lost so much. ‘Station Eleven’ is a powerful meditation on survival, resilience, and the enduring power of human connection.

3. ‘The Night Circus’ by Erin Morgenstern
Step into the enchanting world of Le Cirque des Rêves, a magical circus that appears without warning and disappears just as mysteriously. Within its black-and-white striped tents, visitors encounter wonders beyond their wildest imaginations. But behind the scenes, two young illusionists are locked in a fierce competition, unaware that they are bound together by a destiny they cannot escape. With its evocative prose and spellbinding storytelling, ‘The Night Circus’ is a feast for the senses.

4. ‘The Golem and the Jinni’ by Helene Wecker
In the melting pot of early 20th-century New York City, a chance encounter between a golem and a jinni sets off a chain of events that will change both of their lives forever. As they navigate the unfamiliar terrain of human society, they form an unlikely bond that transcends their differences. Blending elements of fantasy, historical fiction, and mythology, ‘The Golem and the Jinni’ is a captivating tale of friendship, identity, and the search for belonging.

5. ‘The Sparrow’ by Mary Doria Russell
When a group of Jesuit missionaries discovers evidence of extraterrestrial life on a distant planet, they embark on a journey of discovery that will challenge their faith and test the limits of human understanding. Told through a series of interwoven narratives, ‘The Sparrow’ explores profound questions about faith, morality, and the nature of the universe. With its thought-provoking premise and richly drawn characters, this novel will linger in your mind long after you turn the final page.

In a literary landscape dominated by bestsellers and blockbusters, it’s easy for underrated gems to get lost in the shuffle. But these books prove that great storytelling can be found in unexpected places. So, the next time you’re looking for something new to read, consider giving one of these hidden gems a chance. Who knows? You might just discover your new favorite book.
