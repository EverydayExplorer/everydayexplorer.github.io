The Best Plants for Late Summer Color in Your Garden


When the late summer season rolls around, many gardens can start to feel dull and lackluster. However, with the right selection of plants, you can infuse vibrant and eye-catching color back into your outdoor space. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best plants for late summer color in your garden.

1. Black-eyed Susan (Rudbeckia): This perennial plant is known for its bright yellow or orange daisy-like flowers with prominent black centers. They bloom from mid-summer to late fall, making them perfect for injecting a burst of color into your late summer garden. Black-eyed Susans are also attractive to butterflies and bees, adding to the overall beauty of your garden.

2. Coneflower (Echinacea): Coneflowers are another perennial favorite that can add a pop of color to your late summer garden. With their large, daisy-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, they create a stunning display. Coneflowers are also low-maintenance and drought-tolerant, making them ideal for those hot summer months.

3. Mexican Sunflower (Tithonia): If you’re looking for a plant that can create an instant impact, the Mexican Sunflower is the way to go. This annual plant produces fiery orange or red flowers that can reach heights of up to six feet. Not only do they provide a striking appearance, but Mexican Sunflowers also attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.

4. Sedum: Sedums are drought-tolerant succulent plants that thrive in late summer. They come in a variety of colors, including shades of pink, red, yellow, and white. Sedums are a great choice for garden borders or rock gardens due to their ability to tolerate various soil conditions.

5. Autumn Joy (Sedum ‘Herbstfreude’): This specific variety of sedum deserves a special mention due to its exceptional late summer color. Autumn Joy produces large flower heads in shades of pink that eventually turn a stunning copper color as the season progresses. This plant requires minimal care and can tolerate heat and drought well.

6. Verbena: Verbena is an annual plant that blooms from summer to fall, making it perfect for late summer color. With their clusters of small, vibrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, they add a touch of elegance to any garden. Verbena is also a favorite of butterflies, so you can expect some fluttering visitors to your garden.

7. Joe-Pye Weed (Eutrochium): This tall perennial plant is a great option for adding height and color to your late summer garden. Joe-Pye Weed produces large, dome-shaped flower heads in shades of pink and purple. These flowers not only attract butterflies but also act as a magnet for bees and other pollinators.

8. Japanese Anemone (Anemone x hybrida): Japanese Anemones are late-flowering perennials known for their delicate and elegant flowers. They come in shades of white, pink, and purple, and their blooms can last well into autumn. Japanese Anemones are great for adding a touch of grace and sophistication to your garden.

9. Butterfly Bush (Buddleia): As the name suggests, the Butterfly Bush is a magnet for butterflies and other pollinators. This shrub produces elongated clusters of flowers in shades of purple, pink, white, and yellow. With their sweet fragrance and vibrant colors, Butterfly Bushes make a perfect addition to any late summer garden.

10. Salvia: Salvia is a diverse group of plants that offer a wide range of colors and sizes. Whether you choose to go for the striking blue Salvia ‘Black and Blue’ or the vibrant red Salvia ‘Hot Lips,’ these plants add depth and richness to your garden. Salvia flowers attract hummingbirds, bees, and butterflies, making them a dynamic choice for late summer color.

Now that you know about the best plants for late summer color, it’s time to start planning your garden. By incorporating these stunning varieties into your outdoor space, you can enjoy a continuous display of vibrant hues throughout the late summer season. Whether you opt for perennial favorites like Black-eyed Susans and Coneflowers or annual showstoppers like Mexican Sunflowers and Verbena, your garden will become a visual delight for both you and any visitors or pollinators that happen to pass by. So, get your gardening gloves on and transform your garden into a late summer paradise.
