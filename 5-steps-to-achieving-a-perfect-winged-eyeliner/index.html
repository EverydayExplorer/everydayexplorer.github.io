5 Steps to Achieving a Perfect Winged Eyeliner


Achieving the perfect winged eyeliner can be a daunting task, but with the right technique and a little bit of practice, anyone can do it! Whether you’re a makeup enthusiast or just looking to enhance your everyday look, winged eyeliner is a classic and timeless trend that can instantly make your eyes pop and add a touch of glamour to your overall appearance. In this blog post, we will guide you through the 5 steps to achieving a flawless winged eyeliner look that will surely turn heads.

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before we begin, it’s important to have the right tools at your disposal. For this particular look, you will need an eyeliner pen or a gel eyeliner, an angled eyeliner brush, and some cotton swabs for touch-ups. Make sure your eyeliner is fresh and not dried out to ensure smooth and precise application.

Step 2: Prep Your Eyes

To ensure a long-lasting winged eyeliner look, it’s crucial to start with a clean canvas. Begin by removing any excess oil or makeup residue from your eyelids using a gentle cleanser or makeup remover. Afterward, apply a primer specifically designed for your eyelids to create a smooth surface for the eyeliner application. This step will also help prevent smudging throughout the day.

Step 3: Mapping Out Your Wing

The next step is to map out the shape and angle of your winged eyeliner. Using a light, feathery hand, draw a thin line following the natural curvature of your upper lash line. This line will act as the base for your wing. Next, envision the desired length and angle of your wing. To help achieve symmetry, you can use a small piece of tape or a business card as a guide. Align it with the outer corner of your eye, angling it towards the tail end of your eyebrow. This will serve as a guide to create the perfect flick.

Step 4: Filling In the Wing

Once you’ve mapped out your wing, it’s time to fill it in. Using short, precise strokes, gradually fill in the space between the base line and the tip of your wing. If you’re using an eyeliner pen, slightly tilt it at an angle to create a sharp, defined line. If you’re using a gel eyeliner, dip your angled brush into the product, wiping off any excess, and then apply it with a steady hand. Take your time during this step, as it’s essential to achieve a smooth and even line.

Step 5: Connecting the Wing to the Lash Line

Finally, it’s time to connect the wing to your lash line. Starting from the inner corner of your eye, thicken the line and gradually make it thinner as you move towards the wing. Be sure to fill in any gaps and ensure a seamless connection between the wing and the rest of your eyeliner. Take a step back and assess the symmetry and sharpness of your wing. If needed, use a cotton swab dipped in makeup remover to clean up any imperfections or to sharpen the edges.

Bonus Tips:

- Practice makes perfect: Don’t be discouraged if your first attempts at winged eyeliner are not as precise as you hoped. Like any skill, mastering the winged eyeliner technique takes practice. Keep trying, and soon enough, you will achieve the desired result.

- Experiment with different eyeliners: Different eyeliner formulas offer varying levels of control and precision. Experiment with different types, such as pen liners, gel liners, or liquid liners, to find the one that works best for you.

- Adjust the wing angle to suit your eye shape: Winged eyeliner is not a one-size-fits-all look. You can customize the angle and length of your wing to complement your unique eye shape. Play around with different orientations to see what accentuates your eyes the most.

In conclusion, mastering the art of winged eyeliner can seem intimidating at first, but with these 5 steps and a bit of patience, achieving a flawless winged eyeliner is within your reach. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don’t be afraid to experiment and find the technique that works best for you. With time, you’ll be able to create stunning winged eyeliner looks that will leave everyone in awe. So go ahead, grab your eyeliner, and start practicing today!
