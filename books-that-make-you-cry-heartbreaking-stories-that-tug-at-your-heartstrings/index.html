Books That Make You Cry: Heartbreaking Stories That Tug at Your Heartstrings


Books have the power to take us on emotional journeys that leave us feeling heartbroken, yet grateful for the experience. These stories tug at our heartstrings and make us feel deeply connected to the characters, their struggles, and their triumphs. Whether it’s a tearjerker romance or a gut-wrenching memoir, there’s something incredibly cathartic about shedding tears over a well-written book. In this post, we’ll explore some of the most heartbreaking books that are sure to make you cry.

  1. The Fault in Our Stars by John Green: This young adult novel tells the story of two teenagers with cancer who fall in love. Despite their illness, Hazel and Gus embark on an unforgettable journey that will leave you reaching for the tissues. Green’s beautiful prose and poignant insights into life and death make this a must-read for anyone who wants to experience the beauty of love in the face of adversity.

  2. A Little Life by Hanya Yanagihara: This novel is not for the faint of heart. Yanagihara’s epic masterpiece follows the lives of four college friends as they navigate the ups and downs of adulthood. At its heart is Jude, a man with a traumatic past that continues to haunt him throughout his life. This sweeping tale of friendship, trauma, and the human spirit will leave you feeling both emotionally drained and strangely uplifted.

  3. The Book Thief by Markus Zusak: Set during World War II, this novel tells the story of a young girl named Liesel who steals books to find solace in a world torn apart by war. Zusak’s lyrical writing and unique narrative voice make this a poignant and unforgettable read that will leave you feeling both heartbroken and inspired.

  4. Me Before You by Jojo Moyes: This bestselling novel tells the story of Louisa Clark, a young woman who takes a job caring for a quadriplegic man named Will Traynor. As their relationship deepens, Louisa begins to realize that Will has given up on life. Moyes’ heart-wrenching tale of love and sacrifice will leave you sobbing long after you’ve turned the final page.

  5. The Kite Runner by Khaled Hosseini: This novel tells the story of Amir, a young boy from Kabul whose life is forever changed by a traumatic event. Hosseini’s powerful tale of redemption and forgiveness is both heartbreaking and ultimately uplifting, reminding us that even in the darkest of times, there is still hope.

  6. The Art of Racing in the Rain by Garth Stein: This novel is told from the perspective of Enzo, a dog who belongs to race car driver Denny Swift. Enzo’s love for his owner and his unwavering loyalty will have you reaching for the tissues as you follow their journey through life’s ups and downs. Stein’s beautiful writing and unique narrative voice make this a must-read for any animal lover.

  7. The Time Traveler’s Wife by Audrey Niffenegger: This novel tells the story of Henry, a man with a genetic disorder that causes him to time travel involuntarily, and his wife Clare, who must navigate the challenges of their unconventional relationship. Niffenegger’s imaginative storytelling and tender portrayal of love in the face of extraordinary circumstances will leave you both heartbroken and in awe.

In conclusion, these books are not for the faint of heart. They will make you cry, but they will also leave you feeling deeply moved and grateful for the experience. Whether it’s the beauty of love in the face of adversity or the power of forgiveness, these stories remind us of the importance of human connection and the resilience of the human spirit. So, grab your tissues and dive into one of these heartbreaking tales – you won’t be disappointed.
