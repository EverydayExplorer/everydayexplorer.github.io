The Rights of Internally Displaced Persons: Mobilizing Support


Internally displaced persons (IDPs) are individuals or groups who have been forced to flee their homes due to conflict, violence, natural disasters, or other circumstances but have not crossed an international border. They remain within their own country, often seeking shelter in makeshift camps or with host families. The rights and well-being of IDPs are crucial, as they face numerous challenges in rebuilding their lives and are often marginalized and forgotten.

Mobilizing support for IDPs is essential to ensure their protection and to address their needs effectively. This blog aims to shed light on the rights of IDPs and provide insights into how we can collectively mobilize support to improve their situation.

1. Understanding the Rights of IDPs

The rights of IDPs are protected under international human rights law and humanitarian law. The United Nations Guiding Principles on Internal Displacement outlines the fundamental rights and guarantees that should be upheld for IDPs. These include the rights to life, liberty, and security of the person, access to humanitarian assistance, protection from arbitrary displacement, and the right to return voluntarily to their homes when the conditions allow.

2. Meeting Basic Needs

The immediate needs of IDPs include access to clean water, food, shelter, healthcare, and education. These basic necessities are often compromised during displacement, putting IDPs at risk of malnutrition, disease, and limited access to education. Mobilizing support means ensuring that IDPs have access to these essential services.

Local and international organizations, governments, and communities should work together to establish temporary shelters, set up food distribution centers, provide healthcare facilities or mobile clinics, and ensure that children have access to continuous education. Such efforts can go a long way in easing the suffering of IDPs and supporting their well-being.

3. Protection and Security

IDPs are particularly vulnerable to human rights violations and exploitation. Women and children are at higher risk of gender-based violence, sexual exploitation, and human trafficking. Therefore, mobilizing support for IDPs should prioritize their protection and security.

This can be achieved by establishing safe spaces, ensuring the presence of security forces or peacekeepers in IDP camps, and providing psychosocial support for survivors of trauma. Additionally, promoting awareness about the rights and available legal remedies can empower IDPs to assert and protect themselves.

4. Advocacy and Empowerment

Raising awareness about the situation of IDPs and advocating for their rights is crucial. This can be done through various means, such as media campaigns, public events, and engaging with policymakers and influencers. By mobilizing support and generating empathy, we can put pressure on governments and international bodies to take action and allocate resources to protect and assist IDPs.

Furthermore, empowering IDPs through capacity-building programs, vocational training, and income-generating activities can contribute to their long-term resilience and self-reliance. When IDPs are given the opportunity to participate in decision-making processes and when their voices are heard, it promotes a sense of ownership and agency in rebuilding their lives.

5. Collaboration and Partnerships

Addressing the needs of IDPs requires a multi-stakeholder approach. Governments, NGOs, international organizations, local communities, and IDPs themselves must collaborate and form partnerships to mobilize support effectively.

Engaging with governments to create policies that protect and promote the rights of IDPs is essential. Governments should work to integrate IDPs into national development plans, provide legal recognition of their rights, and allocate sufficient resources to address their specific needs. Collaboration with NGOs and international organizations can help ensure the effective delivery of humanitarian aid, support advocacy efforts, and provide expertise and technical assistance.

Local communities play a crucial role as well. By fostering inclusivity, tolerance, and empathy, they can provide support and assistance to IDPs and contribute to their successful integration into new communities.

In conclusion, mobilizing support for internally displaced persons is an urgent and necessary endeavor. By recognizing and respecting their rights, addressing their basic needs, ensuring their protection and security, empowering them, and fostering collaboration, we can make a positive difference in the lives of IDPs. Let us raise our voices, raise awareness, and work together to mobilize support for these vulnerable individuals and give them the dignity, respect, and opportunities they deserve.
