The Best Books to Read in 2021


Are you looking for some captivating reads to dive into this year? Look no further! In this blog post, we will explore a carefully curated list of the best books to read in 2021. From gripping novels to informative non-fiction, this list has something for everyone.

Reading is not only a great way to escape reality and immerse yourself in unique worlds, but it also offers numerous mental benefits. It can expand your horizons, boost your creativity, and improve your cognitive abilities. So why not make reading a priority this year and embark on a literary adventure?

Without further ado, let’s jump into the best books to read in 2021:

1. ‘The Invisible Life of Addie LaRue’ by V.E. Schwab

This beautifully written novel tells the story of Addie LaRue, a young woman who makes a Faustian bargain to live forever but is cursed to be forgotten by everyone she meets. Set in various time periods and exploring themes of love, yearning, and the importance of human connections, this book will leave you contemplating the power of memory and what it means to truly be seen.

2. ‘Project Hail Mary’ by Andy Weir

From the acclaimed author of ‘The Martian,’ comes another thrilling science fiction adventure. In ‘Project Hail Mary,’ Weir takes us on a journey through space alongside Ryland Grace, a solitary astronaut fighting to save humanity from an imminent threat. Packed with scientific ingenuity, wit, and heart, this novel is a must-read for sci-fi enthusiasts.

3. ‘Klara and the Sun’ by Kazuo Ishiguro

Nobel laureate Kazuo Ishiguro presents us with a thought-provoking tale set in a near-future world. The book follows Klara, an Artificial Friend with artificial intelligence, as she navigates her existence and develops an understanding of human emotions. Ishiguro delivers a poignant exploration of love, morality, and what it means to be human.

4. ‘The Push’ by Ashley Audrain

‘The Push’ is a haunting psychological thriller that delves into the complex world of motherhood. As Blythe navigates the challenges of raising her daughter, she is overwhelmed by a growing sense of unease and fear. Audrain skillfully examines the dark side of maternal instinct, leaving readers questioning their own perceptions of motherhood.

5. ‘Braiding Sweetgrass’ by Robin Wall Kimmerer

For those seeking a book that combines scientific knowledge with Native American wisdom, ‘Braiding Sweetgrass’ is the perfect choice. Robin Wall Kimmerer, an accomplished botanist and member of the Citizen Potawatomi Nation, shares her profound reflections on the reciprocal relationship between humans and the natural world. This enlightening and poetic read will inspire a deeper connection to the Earth.

6. ‘The Sanatorium’ by Sarah Pearse

If you enjoy atmospheric thrillers with a touch of mystery, ‘The Sanatorium’ is a must-read. Set in an isolated hotel in the Swiss Alps, this chilling tale follows Elin Warner as she investigates a series of strange events and unearths dark secrets about the hotel’s past. Pearse keeps readers on the edge of their seats with atmospheric descriptions and an eerie sense of foreboding.

7. ‘Empire of Pain: The Secret History of the Sackler Dynasty’ by Patrick Radden Keefe

In this investigative piece of non-fiction, Keefe exposes the controversial history of the Sackler family, who built their fortune through the opioid crisis. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, the author sheds light on the devastating consequences of the family’s actions and raises essential questions about corporate ethics and accountability.

8. ‘The Code Breaker’ by Walter Isaacson

Walter Isaacson, renowned for his biographies of creative geniuses, turns his attention to the groundbreaking science of gene editing in ‘The Code Breaker.’ This engrossing book tells the story of Jennifer Doudna, the co-creator of CRISPR, a revolutionary tool that allows scientists to edit DNA. Isaacson skillfully navigates complex scientific concepts, making them accessible to both science enthusiasts and general readers.

9. ‘The Four Winds’ by Kristin Hannah

Set during the Great Depression, ‘The Four Winds’ transports readers to the Dust Bowl era and follows the journey of Elsa Martinelli as she fights to protect her family and find a better life. Kristin Hannah’s powerful storytelling captures the resilience of the human spirit in the face of adversity, making this historical fiction novel an emotional and captivating read.

10. ‘Hamnet’ by Maggie O’Farrell

Winner of the Women’s Prize for Fiction, ‘Hamnet’ explores the tragic story behind William Shakespeare’s son and the impact of his death on the famous playwright. O’Farrell’s lyrical prose and meticulous historical research bring the world of 16th-century England to life, delivering a moving tale of love, loss, and the enduring power of art.

These ten books represent a diverse range of genres and themes, ensuring there is something to pique the interest of every reader. Whether you prefer heart-pounding suspense, thought-provoking literary fiction, or mind-bending science fiction, these titles are worth adding to your reading list for 2021.

Remember, the best way to fully experience a book is to allow yourself to be completely absorbed in its pages, so find a cozy spot, grab a cup of tea, and lose yourself in the magic of reading. Happy reading!
