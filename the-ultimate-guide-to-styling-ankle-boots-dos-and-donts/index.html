The Ultimate Guide to Styling Ankle Boots: Dos and Don'ts


Ankle boots - a versatile and stylish footwear choice that can elevate any outfit. From casual to formal, these little wonders have the power to add that extra oomph to your look. But styling ankle boots can sometimes be a bit tricky, as there are a few dos and don’ts to keep in mind. In this ultimate guide, we will explore the art of styling ankle boots and provide you with some valuable tips to rock this fashion staple like a pro.

Dos:

1. Play with proportions: Ankle boots look best when paired with outfits that create a balanced silhouette. If you’re wearing skinny jeans or leggings, tuck them into your boots to elongate your legs. For dresses or skirts, opt for a length that hits just above or below the knee to maintain a flattering proportion.

2. Experiment with different heel heights: Ankle boots come in various heel heights, and each can create a different vibe. For a casual daytime look, go for flat or block-heeled ankle boots. If you’re aiming for a more glamorous and sophisticated look, opt for stiletto-heeled ankle boots. Remember to consider your comfort level and the occasion when choosing the right heel height.

3. Layer with socks or tights: Ankle boots can be worn year-round by adding a pair of stylish socks or tights. During colder months, opt for cozy knit socks or opaque tights to keep your feet warm and add a pop of color or texture to your outfit. In warmer months, go for sheer or patterned tights to add a touch of elegance to your look.

4. Mix and match with different styles: Ankle boots come in various styles, from classic leather to edgy combat boots. Don’t be afraid to mix and match different styles to create unique and interesting outfits. Pair your leather ankle boots with a feminine dress or your combat boots with a flirty skirt for a fashion-forward look.

Don’ts:

1. Avoid ankle straps: When it comes to styling ankle boots, it’s best to avoid ankle straps. Ankle straps can visually shorten your legs and disrupt the sleek silhouette that ankle boots create. Instead, opt for ankle boots without straps or choose styles with minimal and discreet straps.

2. Say no to bulky socks: While adding socks to ankle boots can be a great styling choice, avoid bulky socks that can make your feet look chunky. Opt for thin or sheer socks that won’t add unnecessary bulk and will create a more streamlined look.

3. Skip the wrong hemline: When wearing ankle boots, be mindful of the hemline of your pants, skirts, or dresses. Avoid hemlines that hit right at the top of your boots, as this can create an unflattering and awkward visual break. Instead, choose hemlines that hit above or below the ankle boots for a more polished and cohesive look.

4. Avoid the wrong color combinations: Mixing and matching colors is a fun way to create interesting outfits, but be cautious when it comes to pairing ankle boots with certain colors. Avoid pairing black ankle boots with black leggings or pants, as this can create a ‘booted’ look that visually shortens your legs. Instead, opt for contrasting colors or lighter shades to create a more balanced and elongating effect.

Styling ankle boots can be a delightful fashion adventure. By following these dos and don’ts, you’ll be well on your way to mastering the art of ankle boot styling. Remember, confidence is key, so wear your ankle boots with pride and let your personal style shine through. Happy styling!
