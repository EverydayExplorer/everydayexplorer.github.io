Building a Solar Still: Collecting Water in Survival Situations


In survival situations, finding clean drinking water can be a major challenge. Whether you’re lost in the wilderness or facing a natural disaster, having access to safe drinking water is crucial for your survival. One effective method of collecting water in such situations is by building a solar still. In this blog post, we will explore what a solar still is, how it works, and the steps to build one.

What is a Solar Still?

A solar still is a simple yet ingenious device that uses the power of the sun to collect and purify water. It works on the principle of evaporation and condensation. By harnessing the energy from the sun, a solar still can extract pure, drinkable water from various sources, including saltwater, contaminated water, and even moisture in the air.

How does a Solar Still work?

The working principle of a solar still is relatively simple. It consists of three main components: a collection container, a condensation surface, and a cover.

  1. Collection Container: This is the part of the solar still that holds the water to be purified. It can be any waterproof container, such as a plastic sheet, a bucket, or even a hole in the ground. The container should be able to collect the condensed water.

  2. Condensation Surface: The condensation surface is positioned above the collection container. It is usually a clear plastic sheet or a piece of glass. This surface allows sunlight to penetrate and heat the water, causing it to evaporate.

  3. Cover: The cover is placed over the condensation surface to create a closed environment. It prevents the evaporated water from escaping and directs it towards the condensation surface. The cover should be sloped to allow the condensed water to flow towards the collection container.

Building a Solar Still: Step-by-Step Guide

Now that we understand the basic components and working principle of a solar still, let’s dive into the steps to build one:

  1. Choose a Location: Find a sunny spot where you can set up your solar still. Make sure it receives ample sunlight throughout the day.

  2. Dig a Hole: Dig a hole in the ground, deep enough to accommodate your collection container. The hole should be wider at the top and narrower at the bottom.

  3. Place the Collection Container: Position your collection container at the bottom of the hole. Ensure that it is stable and won’t collapse under the weight of the soil.

  4. Create a Condensation Surface: Place a clear plastic sheet or a piece of glass over the hole, covering it completely. Secure the edges of the sheet by burying them in the surrounding soil.

  5. Slope the Cover: Angle the cover slightly towards the collection container, so that the condensed water will flow into it.

  6. Weight the Center: Place a small weight, such as a rock or a stone, at the center of the cover. This will create a depression, allowing the condensed water to collect and flow towards the container.

  7. Wait and Collect: Leave your solar still undisturbed for several hours or even a day. As the sun heats the water, it will evaporate, condense on the condensation surface, and flow into the collection container. Check the container periodically and collect the purified water.

Conclusion

Building a solar still can be a lifesaving skill in survival situations. By harnessing the power of the sun, you can collect and purify water from various sources. Remember to choose a sunny location, dig a hole, place a collection container, create a condensation surface, slope the cover, and wait patiently for the water to collect. Stay hydrated and stay safe!
