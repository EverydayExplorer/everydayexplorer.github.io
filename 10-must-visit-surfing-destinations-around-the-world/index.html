10 Must-Visit Surfing Destinations Around the World


Surfing has become one of the most popular water sports worldwide, attracting enthusiasts from all walks of life. Whether you’re a beginner or a seasoned pro, there’s always a thrill in riding the perfect wave. If you’re in search of the ultimate surfing experience, look no further than these 10 must-visit surfing destinations around the world. From iconic breaks to hidden gems, these locations promise an unforgettable ride.

1. Pipeline, Hawaii
Known as the Mecca of surfing, Pipeline in Oahu, Hawaii, is a dream destination for avid surfers. This reef break is notorious for its powerful and hollow waves, attracting professional surfers from around the globe. The adrenaline rush and the beauty of Pipeline make it a must-visit for any surfer looking for an unforgettable experience.

2. Jeffreys Bay, South Africa
Jeffreys Bay, or ‘J-Bay’ as locals lovingly call it, is one of the best right-hand point breaks in the world. Located in the Eastern Cape of South Africa, this destination offers consistent and long rides that make it a surfer’s paradise. With its diverse breaks, J-Bay caters to surfers of all levels, providing an unforgettable experience no matter your skill level.

3. Uluwatu, Bali
Bali has long been synonymous with sun, sea, and surf. Uluwatu, located on the southern tip of the island, offers a world-class surfing experience. With its consistent swells and perfect left-hand barrels, Uluwatu is a favorite among experienced surfers. The stunning cliffs and breathtaking sunsets make it an enchanting destination for any traveler.

4. Tamarindo, Costa Rica
If you’re looking for a surfing destination that combines outstanding breaks with a laid-back vibe, Tamarindo in Costa Rica is the place to be. This small town on the Pacific coast boasts warm water, consistent waves, and a lively surf scene. Whether you’re a beginner or an expert, Tamarindo offers waves for all levels, making it an ideal destination for surfers of all abilities.

5. Hossegor, France
Surfing in Europe? Hossegor in France is a must-visit destination. Located on the southwest coast, this surf town is renowned for its world-class beach breaks. With consistent swells year-round, Hossegor attracts surfers from all over the world. It’s not just the waves that make this place special – the vibrant surf culture and delicious French cuisine add to the overall experience.

6. Gold Coast, Australia
Australia is known for its incredible surf culture, and the Gold Coast is at the heart of it all. With its warm climate, golden beaches, and impressive point breaks, the Gold Coast offers an unforgettable surfing experience. From Snapper Rocks to Kirra, this stretch of coastline is home to some of the best waves in the world, attracting surfers of all levels.

7. Puerto Escondido, Mexico
If you’re seeking a thrilling surfing experience, look no further than Puerto Escondido in Mexico. This picturesque town on the Oaxacan coast is home to the infamous Zicatela Beach, known as the Mexican Pipeline. Zicatela produces massive waves that can reach up to 30 feet, attracting experienced surfers looking for an adrenaline rush. With its warm water and vibrant atmosphere, Puerto Escondido is a must-visit for any adventurous surfer.

8. Taghazout, Morocco
Morocco may not be the first place that comes to mind when you think of surfing, but Taghazout has quickly become a favorite destination for surfers seeking diverse breaks and a vibrant surf culture. Located on the Atlantic coast, this charming fishing village offers everything from long point breaks to powerful beach breaks. With its warm weather, friendly locals, and delicious cuisine, Taghazout is a hidden gem for wave riders.

9. Santa Cruz, California
Known as the birthplace of mainland surfing, Santa Cruz in California offers a mix of world-class breaks and a rich surfing heritage. Steamer Lane, Pleasure Point, and The Hook are just a few of the famous breaks that attract surfers year-round. The cool climate, consistent swells, and laid-back beach vibes make Santa Cruz a must-visit destination for any surfer exploring the West Coast.

10. Raglan, New Zealand
Last but certainly not least, Raglan in New Zealand is a surfing destination that should be on every wave rider’s bucket list. With its long left-hand break at Manu Bay, Raglan offers surfers the chance to ride one of the longest waves in the world. Surrounded by stunning landscapes and a friendly local community, Raglan promises a unique and memorable surfing experience.

In conclusion, these 10 must-visit surfing destinations around the world offer a range of experiences for surfers of all levels. From iconic breaks to hidden gems, these locations provide the perfect combination of waves, beauty, and adventure. So, grab your board, wax it up, and get ready for the ride of a lifetime in one of these spectacular surfing destinations.
