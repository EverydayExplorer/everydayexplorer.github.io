Avoiding Online Banking Scams: Tips to Protect Yourself


Online banking scams have been on the rise in recent years. While online banking has made banking more convenient and accessible, it has also opened doors for scammers to take advantage of unsuspecting victims. According to a report by the FBI, there was a 50% increase in losses due to cybercrime in 2019, with losses totaling over .5 billion. This highlights the need to be vigilant and take proactive measures to protect ourselves from online banking scams. Here are some tips to help you avoid falling victim to online banking scams.

1. Review Your Accounts Regularly

One of the most important things you can do to protect yourself from online banking scams is to review your accounts regularly. This means checking your bank statements and transactions on a regular basis, whether it’s daily, weekly, or monthly. This way, you can quickly identify any unauthorized transactions and report them to your bank. The faster you spot unauthorized transactions, the quicker you can take action to prevent further damage.

2. Use Strong Passwords

Using strong passwords is another key defense against online banking scams. A strong password should be at least 12 characters long and include a mix of upper and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using common dictionary words or easily guessable passwords such as your birthdate or pet’s name. Also, avoid using the same password across multiple accounts.

3. Be Wary of Suspicious Emails

Phishing emails are a common tactic used by scammers to trick people into giving up their personal information, such as usernames, passwords, and banking details. Phishing emails often contain links that lead to fake login pages that look like the real thing. Be wary of unsolicited emails, especially those that appear to be from your bank. Always check the sender’s email address, as scammers can create fake email addresses that look similar to your bank’s.

4. Keep Your Software Updated

Keeping your software up to date is another important step in protecting yourself from online banking scams. Software updates often contain security patches that address vulnerabilities that could be exploited by scammers. This includes not just your operating system, but also your web browser and anti-virus software.

5. Use Two-Factor Authentication

Two-factor authentication is another effective way to protect yourself from online banking scams. This involves using an additional layer of security beyond your password, such as a one-time code sent to your phone. This reduces the risk of someone accessing your account even if they have your password.

6. Only Access Your Accounts on Secure Networks

Finally, only access your online banking accounts on secure networks. Avoid using public Wi-Fi networks, as they are often not secure and can be easily hacked. If you must use public Wi-Fi, use a virtual private network (VPN) to encrypt your data and protect your privacy.

In conclusion, online banking scams are on the rise, but there are steps you can take to protect yourself. Remember to review your accounts regularly, use strong passwords, be wary of suspicious emails, keep your software updated, use two-factor authentication, and only access your accounts on secure networks. By following these tips, you can minimize your risk of falling victim to online banking scams and keep your hard-earned money safe.
