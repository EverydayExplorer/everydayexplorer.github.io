10 Fishing Myths Busted by Science


Fishing is a popular outdoor activity enjoyed by millions of people around the world. Despite its popularity, fishing is surrounded by many myths and misconceptions that have been passed down from one generation to another. Unfortunately, these myths can prevent anglers from having a successful and enjoyable fishing experience. In this article, we will be busting 10 of the most common fishing myths using scientific evidence.

1. Fish only bite at dawn and dusk

One of the most commonly held beliefs among anglers is that fish are only active during the early morning and late evening hours. However, this is not entirely true. While it is true that some species of fish are more active during these times, many species can be caught throughout the day. In fact, some species like bluegill and catfish are more active during the middle of the day.

2. Fish only bite on sunny days

Many anglers believe that fish only bite on sunny days when the water is clear and calm. However, this is not entirely true. The truth is that fish can be caught on cloudy days, windy days, and even on rainy days. The key is to understand the behavior of the fish you are targeting and adjust your fishing technique accordingly.

3. The bigger the bait, the bigger the fish

Another common myth is that the bigger the bait, the bigger the fish you will catch. However, this is not always true. While some species of fish prefer larger baits, many fish can be caught using smaller baits. In fact, using a smaller bait can sometimes be more effective as it appears more natural to the fish.

4. Fish can’t see in color

Many anglers believe that fish are color blind and cannot see colors. However, this is not entirely true. While fish do not see colors the same way humans do, they can still see colors to some degree. Research has shown that fish can see some colors such as red, green, and blue.

5. Fish always swim in schools

Another common myth is that fish always swim in schools. While many species of fish do swim in schools, not all fish do. Many species such as bass and pike are solitary fish and are not found in schools.

6. Fish only eat live bait

Many anglers believe that fish will only eat live bait and will not take artificial lures. However, this is not entirely true. While some species of fish prefer live bait, many fish can be caught using artificial lures. In fact, using an artificial lure can sometimes be more effective as it allows you to cover more ground and target a wider range of fish.

7. Fish will always return to the same spot

Another common myth is that fish will always return to the same spot. While fish do have a tendency to return to areas where they have found food in the past, they are not locked into a specific location. Fish are constantly on the move, which means that they may not be in the same spot every time you fish.

8. The colder the water, the slower the fish

Many anglers believe that fish become lethargic in cold water and swim at a slower pace. However, this is not entirely true. While some species of fish do become less active in cold water, others become more active. Understanding the behavior of the fish you are targeting is key to catching them in any condition.

9. It’s impossible to catch fish in clear water

Many anglers believe that it’s impossible to catch fish in clear and shallow water. However, this is not entirely true. While fish in clear water may be more cautious, they can still be caught using the right technique and bait. In fact, fishing in clear water can sometimes be more effective as it allows you to see the fish and adjust your technique accordingly.

10. Fishing is all about luck

Finally, a common myth is that fishing is all about luck. While luck can play a role in fishing, it’s not the only factor. Being successful at fishing requires knowledge, skill, and patience. Understanding the behavior of the fish you are targeting and adjusting your technique accordingly can greatly increase your chances of a successful fishing trip.

In conclusion, there are many fishing myths that have been passed down from one generation to another. However, by using scientific evidence, we can bust these myths and set the record straight. By understanding the behavior of the fish you are targeting and adjusting your technique accordingly, you can greatly increase your chances of a successful fishing trip.
