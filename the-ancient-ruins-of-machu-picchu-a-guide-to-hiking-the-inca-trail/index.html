The Ancient Ruins of Machu Picchu: A Guide to Hiking the Inca Trail


Machu Picchu is a breathtaking archaeological wonder nestled high in the Andes mountains of Peru. This ancient Incan city, shrouded in mystery and steeped in history, continues to captivate the imagination of adventurers and history buffs alike. The ruins of Machu Picchu, often referred to as the ‘Lost City of the Incas,’ are a testament to the ingenuity and engineering prowess of the Incan civilization. In this blog post, we’ll explore the magnificent ancient ruins of Machu Picchu and provide you with a comprehensive guide on hiking the famous Inca Trail to reach this stunning UNESCO World Heritage site.

## The History of Machu Picchu

Built in the 15th century, Machu Picchu served as the royal estate of the Incan emperor, Pachacuti. The city was constructed at an altitude of 2,430 meters (7,970 feet) and remained hidden for centuries, escaping the clutches of Spanish conquistadors. It wasn’t until 1911 when the American explorer Hiram Bingham rediscovered Machu Picchu, bringing it to the attention of the world.

## Why Hike the Inca Trail?

Hiking the Inca Trail to Machu Picchu is an experience like no other. This ancient trail, which stretches for approximately 26 miles (42 kilometers), winds its way through stunning Andean landscapes, lush cloud forests, and passes numerous ancient archaeological sites. The trail offers a unique opportunity to trace the footsteps of the Incas and immerse yourself in the rich history and natural beauty of the region.

## Planning Your Inca Trail Hike

### Permits and Restrictions

Due to the popularity of the Inca Trail, Peru’s Ministry of Culture has implemented restrictions on the number of hikers allowed on the trail each day. Only around 500 people, including guides and porters, are permitted to start the Inca Trail each day. It is essential to obtain permits well in advance to secure your spot.

### Best Time to Hike

The dry season from May to September is considered the best time to hike the Inca Trail. During these months, rainfall is minimal, and you can enjoy clear skies and warmer temperatures. It’s worth noting that the Inca Trail is closed during February for annual maintenance.

### Physical Fitness and Acclimatization

Hiking the Inca Trail is a physically demanding endeavor. It’s crucial to ensure that you are in good physical shape and have allowed enough time for acclimatization to the high altitude. Give yourself a couple of days to adjust in Cusco or the Sacred Valley before starting the trek.

### Choosing a Tour Operator

While it’s possible to hike the Inca Trail independently, most hikers opt to go with a licensed tour operator. They provide experienced guides, meals, camping equipment, and porters to carry much of the gear. Do thorough research and read reviews before selecting a tour operator to ensure you have a safe and enjoyable experience.

## The Inca Trail Experience

### Day 1: Cusco to Wayllabamba

After an early morning pick-up from your hotel in Cusco, you’ll be driven to the start of the Inca Trail. The first day involves a relatively moderate hike, allowing you to acclimate to the altitude and absorb the stunning scenery. You’ll pass through lush valleys, traverse ancient Incan terraces, and finally reach the campsite at Wayllabamba.

### Day 2: Wayllabamba to Pacaymayo

Day two is the most challenging part of the Inca Trail. You’ll ascend steep slopes, cross the infamous ‘Dead Woman’s Pass’ at an altitude of 4,215 meters (13,828 feet), and descend into the Pacaymayo Valley. The spectacular views and a sense of accomplishment make it all worthwhile.

### Day 3: Pacaymayo to Wiñay Wayna

On day three, you’ll hike through diverse landscapes, including cloud forests filled with exotic flora and fauna. Along the way, you’ll encounter several archaeological sites, including the impressive ruins of Runkuracay and Sayacmarca. The day ends at Wiñay Wayna, an ancient Incan site perched on a mountainside, where you’ll camp for the night.

### Day 4: Wiñay Wayna to Machu Picchu

The final day of the trek starts before dawn to reach Inti Punku, or the Sun Gate, and watch the sunrise over Machu Picchu. As the first rays of light illuminate the ancient city, you’ll be filled with awe and wonder. After a short trek down to the site, you’ll explore the ruins and learn about its history with a knowledgeable guide.

## Exploring Machu Picchu

Once you reach Machu Picchu, take your time to explore this extraordinary wonder. Marvel at the precision of Incan stonework, walk along ancient terraces, and enter sacred temples. Don’t miss iconic spots like the Intihuatana Stone, the Temple of the Sun, and the Temple of the Three Windows.

## Additional Tips for a Memorable Experience

- Pack light: Your porters will do the heavy lifting, but it’s essential to bring only the essentials to make the trek more comfortable.
- Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water to combat the effects of high altitude and prevent altitude sickness.
- Layer your clothing: Be prepared for changing weather conditions by dressing in layers that can easily be added or removed.
- Protect yourself from the sun: Apply sunscreen, wear a hat, and use sunglasses to shield yourself from the strong Andean sun.
- Respect the environment: Keep the trail clean and respect the ancient ruins by following the rules and regulations set by the authorities.

## In Conclusion

Hiking the Inca Trail to the ancient ruins of Machu Picchu is an experience of a lifetime. The combination of awe-inspiring landscapes, Incan history, and breathtaking ruins make it a journey unlike any other. By following the planning tips and recommendations in this guide, you’ll be well-prepared to embark on an unforgettable adventure that will leave you with lasting memories of this incredible wonder of the world.
