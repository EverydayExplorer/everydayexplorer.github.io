DIY Traps and Snares for Wilderness Survival


Do you love spending time in the great outdoors? Are you an avid camper, hiker, or adventurer? If so, then learning how to build DIY traps and snares can be an important skill for your wilderness survival toolkit. Whether you find yourself in a dire situation or want to augment your camping experience with some fresh game, knowing how to effectively set up traps and snares can make all the difference. Not only can these tools help you catch food, but they are also an excellent way to test your survival skills and resourcefulness. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore various DIY traps and snares that you can easily assemble and use in the wilderness.

Understanding the Basics

Before diving into the world of DIY traps and snares, it’s essential to familiarize yourself with the basic principles. First and foremost, you must understand that trapping animals for survival or sustenance purposes should always be done within legal and ethical boundaries. Check local regulations and seek guidance regarding which animals are legal to trap in your area.

The goal of setting up a trap or snare is to immobilize or capture an animal without causing unnecessary suffering. Always prioritize the safety and well-being of the animal. Remember, traps and snares are only tools, and how you use them determines their ethical implications.

Simple Snare Traps

Snare traps are designed to catch animals by their neck or body, securing them until you arrive to dispatch them in a humane manner. Here are a few simple snare trap setups you can rely on in the wilderness:

  1. Basic Snare: One of the simplest snares to construct is the basic snare. It requires a looped cord or wire, and a set of sticks or branches to support the snare. Position the snare loop in an area frequented by your target animal, making sure it is at the right height when the animal attempts to walk through it. Attach the snare to a sturdy supporting structure and ensure it is tight enough to secure the animal when it is caught.

  2. Pole Snare: The pole snare is another effective method that uses a pole to create tension. After attaching the snare to the pole, carve a trigger stick that will hold the snare in place. When an animal takes the bait, it will release the trigger stick and tighten the snare, trapping the creature.

  3. Spring Snare: As the name suggests, this type of snare utilizes a spring mechanism to capture animals. It requires some basic engineering skills. The spring snare can be made using branches, cordage, and a flexible sapling. When an animal tugs on the bait, the sapling is released, and the snare tightens around the animal.

Effective Trap Systems

In addition to snare traps, there are various other types of traps that can be highly effective in the wilderness. Here are a few DIY trap systems that you can consider:

  1. Deadfall Trap: This trap is built by positioning a heavy rock or log that is prepped to fall when triggered by the animal. Place bait in a specific location, where the animal’s movement will trigger the trap, causing the heavy object to fall and crush the animal. This is one of the most versatile and reliable traps, suitable for catching a variety of game.

  2. Pitfall Trap: The pitfall trap is designed to capture animals by luring them into a hole in the ground. Dig a hole deep enough to ensure the trapped animal cannot escape and camouflage it as much as possible. Place bait or attractive objects near or above the hole to entice the animal into falling in.

  3. Fish Traps: When in a wilderness area with a water source, fish traps can come in handy. These traps are designed to allow fish to enter easily but make it difficult for them to exit. Constructing a fish trap typically involves creating a funnel-like entrance that funnels fish into the main holding area where they can be easily caught.

Improvisation and Adaptation

Keep in mind that the key to successful trapping in the wilderness lies in your ability to improvise and adapt. Nature is unpredictable, and animals can be cunning, so being flexible with your trap placement and design can greatly increase your chances of success. Always monitor your traps regularly to increase their effectiveness and minimize any suffering caused to animals.

Conclusion

Knowing how to build DIY traps and snares for wilderness survival is an invaluable skill that can enhance your outdoor experiences and serve as a lifeline in dire situations. However, it is essential to approach trapping with respect for the environment and the welfare of animals. By understanding the principles behind traps and snares and incorporating ethical considerations, you can safely and effectively harness these tools while exploring the great outdoors.

Remember, trapping should always be carried out responsibly, legally, and ethically. Prioritize your own safety, respect nature, and seek proper training and guidance before implementing any trapping techniques. With the right knowledge and skills, DIY traps and snares can be an empowering addition to your wilderness survival arsenal.
