Escape to the Countryside: Books Set in Small Towns and Rural Landscapes


In an increasingly fast-paced and urbanized world, there is something undeniably appealing about escaping to the countryside. The tranquility, the close-knit communities, and the beauty of nature have long been a source of inspiration for writers around the world. Many authors have set their works in small towns and rural landscapes, capturing the essence of rural life and exploring the themes of love, self-discovery, and the search for meaning. In this blog post, we will take a closer look at some of the best books that transport readers to the countryside and provide a much-needed escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

1. ‘Pride and Prejudice’ by Jane Austen: This timeless classic is set in the English countryside and follows the story of Elizabeth Bennet as she navigates the complexities of love, class, and societal expectations. The picturesque setting of rural England serves as a backdrop for the unfolding drama, adding a sense of quaintness and charm to the narrative.

2. ‘To Kill a Mockingbird’ by Harper Lee: While this Pulitzer Prize-winning novel is primarily known for its exploration of racial injustice in the American South, it also offers a vivid portrayal of life in a small, rural town. The setting of Maycomb, Alabama, captures the essence of a tight-knit community and highlights the beauty and struggles of living in a close community.

3. ‘Cannery Row’ by John Steinbeck: Set in Monterey, California during the Great Depression, ‘Cannery Row’ depicts the lives of characters residing in a small coastal town. Through Steinbeck’s masterful storytelling, readers are transported to a simpler time and place, where the sense of community and interconnectedness plays a pivotal role.

4. ‘Cold Mountain’ by Charles Frazier: This epic novel takes readers on a journey through the rural landscapes of the American Civil War era. Set in North Carolina, the story follows the experiences of Inman, a wounded Confederate soldier, as he embarks on a treacherous journey home to Cold Mountain. Frazier’s vivid descriptions of the natural beauty of the rural South provide a stark contrast to the horrors of war.

5. ‘The Guernsey Literary and Potato Peel Pie Society’ by Mary Ann Shaffer and Annie Barrows: Set on the island of Guernsey in the aftermath of World War II, this heartwarming novel is composed of a series of letters exchanged between writer Juliet Ashton and the members of the Guernsey Literary and Potato Peel Pie Society. Through the letters, readers are transported to the idyllic island and gain insight into the lives of its residents.

6. ‘The Little House on the Prairie’ series by Laura Ingalls Wilder: This beloved children’s series chronicles the life of a pioneer family in the American Midwest. Set in the 19th century, these books paint a vivid picture of rural life, from building a home in the wilderness to the challenges of farming and surviving harsh winters. The series offers a glimpse into a bygone era and the importance of community and resilience.

7. ‘Gilead’ by Marilynne Robinson: This Pulitzer Prize-winning novel is set in the fictional town of Gilead, Iowa, and is narrated by Reverend John Ames, an elderly minister. Through his contemplative reflections and recollections, readers are immersed in the quiet beauty of the rural Midwest and gain a deeper understanding of faith, forgiveness, and the passage of time.

These books, among many others, invite readers to escape to small towns and rural landscapes, offering a respite from the frantic pace of modern life. They provide an opportunity to slow down, connect with nature, and explore the universal themes that transcend time and place. Whether it’s Austen’s enchanting English countryside or Wilder’s pioneering spirit, these books offer a glimpse into the simplicity, beauty, and challenges of rural life.

As more people yearn for a simpler, quieter existence, books set in small towns and rural landscapes continue to provide a welcome escape. From the heartwarming tales of community and resilience to the introspective reflections on life’s deeper meaning, these books offer a window into a world that is closer to the heart and nature. So next time you find yourself craving a break from the city, pick up one of these books and embark on a literary journey to the countryside. You never know what truths you may uncover, or how a story set in a small town may resonate with your own experiences. Happy reading and happy escaping!
