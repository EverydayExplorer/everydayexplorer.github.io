Comedy in Advertising: Brands That Know How to Make Us Laugh


In the world of advertising, there are countless strategies that brands use to capture the attention of their audience. One of the most effective and memorable tactics is humor. When done right, comedy in advertising has the power to make a lasting impression, create a strong emotional connection, and boost brand recall. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of comedy in advertising and highlight some of the brands that have mastered the craft of making us laugh.

Why Comedy Works in Advertising

Before we dive into specific examples, it’s important to understand why comedy is such a powerful tool in advertising. Humor has a universal appeal and the ability to break down barriers, making it an effective way for brands to connect with consumers on a personal level. When people laugh, they feel good, and that positive emotion becomes associated with the brand that made them laugh. As a result, consumers are more likely to remember the brand and develop a favorable attitude towards it.

Moreover, humor can also help cut through the noise in today’s oversaturated advertising landscape. With so many messages bombarding consumers every day, a funny ad has a much better chance of standing out and grabbing attention. This is particularly true in the age of social media, where humorous ads are more likely to be shared, liked, and commented on, further amplifying the brand’s message.

Brands That Excel in Comedy Advertising

Now let’s take a look at some examples of brands that have leveraged comedy to great effect in their advertising campaigns.

1. Old Spice: The ‘The Man Your Man Could Smell Like’ campaign is a prime example of how humor can transform a brand’s image. The over-the-top, absurdly humorous commercials not only went viral but also redefined the perception of Old Spice, making it cool and relevant to a younger audience.

2. Geico: Geico has become synonymous with clever and funny advertising. The brand’s use of quirky characters like the gecko, the caveman, and the ‘Hump Day’ camel has made its commercials highly memorable and shareable, leading to a significant boost in brand recognition and sales.

3. Dollar Shave Club: This subscription-based razor and grooming products company burst onto the scene with a hilarious, low-budget YouTube video that quickly went viral. The irreverent humor and straight-talking CEO made Dollar Shave Club stand out in a crowded market, ultimately leading to its acquisition by Unilever for $1 billion.

4. Snickers: ‘You’re Not You When You’re Hungry’ is a campaign that has consistently delivered humor and memorable storytelling. By depicting people as cranky versions of themselves due to hunger, Snickers has not only entertained but also effectively communicated its brand message.

5. Doritos: The ‘Crash the Super Bowl’ campaign invited consumers to create their own Doritos commercials, resulting in a series of hilarious and highly shareable ads. This user-generated content approach not only engaged the audience but also provided Doritos with a wealth of creative, funny ads to choose from.

The Impact of Comedy on Brand Success

The success stories of these brands illustrate the significant impact that comedy can have on advertising. By using humor strategically, these brands have managed to cut through the clutter, engage their audience, and create a strong, positive association with their products. The result? Increased brand awareness, improved brand perception, and ultimately, a positive impact on sales.

In conclusion, humor has the power to captivate and connect with audiences in ways that other advertising strategies can’t. When executed effectively, comedy in advertising can drive brand recognition, foster customer loyalty, and ultimately lead to business success. As brands continue to compete for consumer attention, those that can make us laugh are likely to have the last laugh in the world of advertising.
