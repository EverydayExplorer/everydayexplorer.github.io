The Best Plants for Attracting Hummingbirds


Hummingbirds are fascinating creatures that bring joy and excitement to any garden. Their vibrant colors, speedy movements, and ability to hover in mid-air make them a delight to watch. If you want to attract these beautiful birds to your garden, one of the most effective ways is by planting the right flowers and plants. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best plants for attracting hummingbirds and creating a haven for these tiny creatures.

  1. Bee Balm (Monarda) - Bee Balm is a popular choice for attracting hummingbirds. Its tubular flowers, available in various shades of red, pink, and purple, are perfect for the long beaks of these birds. Be sure to plant them in a sunny spot with well-draining soil.

  2. Salvia - Salvia is another excellent option for attracting hummingbirds. With its bright red, tube-shaped flowers, it provides nectar that is irresistible to these birds. Salvias are low-maintenance and can be grown in containers or in flower beds.

  3. Trumpet Vine (Campsis radicans) - If you’re looking for a vine to cover an arbor or trellis, the Trumpet Vine is a great choice. Its large, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow are a hummingbird favorite. Make sure to trim the vine regularly to keep it under control.

  4. Coral Bells (Heuchera) - Coral Bells are not only known for their attractive foliage but also for their petite, bell-shaped flowers that are adored by hummingbirds. These plants do well in both sun and shade, making them versatile additions to any garden.

  5. Columbine (Aquilegia) - Columbines are delicate, elegant flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, yellow, and purple. Their unique shape and spur-like petals make them irresistible to hummingbirds. Plant them in partial shade to full sun for best results.

  6. Lupine (Lupinus) - Lupines are tall, showy flowers that produce vibrant spikes of blooms in shades of pink, purple, blue, and white. The tubular shape of their flowers is perfect for hummingbirds, and the plants also add height and drama to any garden.

  7. Bleeding Heart (Dicentra) – Bleeding Hearts are known for their heart-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. These pendulous flowers are not only eye-catching but also attract hummingbirds with their nectar. Plant them in shade or partial shade for best results.

  8. Bottlebrush (Callistemon) – Bottlebrush plants produce unique, cylindrical, brush-like spikes of flowers that closely resemble a bottle brush. The bright red flowers are a favorite of hummingbirds and will add an exotic touch to your garden.

  9. Trumpet Honeysuckle (Lonicera sempervirens) – The vibrant red, trumpet-shaped flowers of the Trumpet Honeysuckle are a hummingbird magnet. It’s a fast-growing vine that can be trained on a trellis or fence to provide a natural perch for these delightful creatures.

  10. Penstemon – Penstemons are native wildflowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, purple, and blue. Their tubular flowers are perfect for hummingbirds, and they are a low-maintenance plant that can thrive in various soil types.

When creating a hummingbird-friendly garden, it’s important to provide a variety of plants with different flowering times to ensure a continuous nectar supply throughout the season. Remember to avoid using pesticides or chemicals that may be harmful to hummingbirds and other pollinators.

In conclusion, attracting hummingbirds to your garden is a delightful and rewarding experience. By incorporating these top 10 plants for attracting hummingbirds, you can create an inviting habitat that will entice these colorful birds to visit and provide endless joy for you and your guests. Happy gardening!
