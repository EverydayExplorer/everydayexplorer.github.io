Exploring the World of Amphorae Wines: Ancient Techniques Revived


In the world of winemaking, there is a trend that is captivating the attention of wine enthusiasts and connoisseurs alike. It is a trend that takes us back in time, to the ancient techniques of winemaking. We are talking about amphorae wines – a unique and intriguing category of wines that is gaining popularity in the modern wine industry.

Amphorae wines are made using a method that dates back thousands of years. The technique involves fermenting and aging the wine in clay vessels called amphorae. These vessels, which resemble large, earthenware jars, have been used for winemaking since ancient times. They offer a natural and traditional approach to winemaking, allowing the wine to develop its character in a way that is distinct from modern methods.

One of the key characteristics of amphorae wines is their ability to showcase the terroir – the unique combination of soil, climate, and geography that gives a wine its distinct flavors and aromas. The clay vessels used in the winemaking process are porous, allowing the wine to interact with the clay and extract subtle nuances from it. This results in wines that are often described as earthy, mineral, and full of complexity.

Another intriguing aspect of amphorae wines is the use of natural fermentation. Unlike many modern winemaking techniques that rely on commercial yeast strains, amphorae wines are fermented using the wild yeast present on the grape skins. This spontaneous fermentation process adds another layer of complexity and uniqueness to the wines, resulting in flavors and aromas that are truly one-of-a-kind.

Amphorae wines are not limited to a specific region or grape variety. Winemakers all over the world are embracing this ancient technique and experimenting with different grape varieties and winemaking styles. From Italy to Georgia, from Spain to California, winemakers are rediscovering the magic of amphorae wines and reviving a piece of winemaking history.

One of the pioneers in the revival of amphorae wines is Josko Gravner, an Italian winemaker who made headlines in the early 2000s with his orange wines. Gravner’s wines are made using an extended maceration technique, where the grape skins and juice are left in contact for a prolonged period of time. The result is a range of wines that are rich in texture, amber in color, and brimming with flavors of dried fruits, nuts, and spice.

Amphorae wines are not just a passing trend in the wine world. They represent a return to the roots of winemaking, a celebration of tradition and craftsmanship. These wines are not for everyone, as their unique flavors and textures can be an acquired taste. But for those who appreciate the artistry and history behind winemaking, amphorae wines offer a thrilling journey into the past.

So next time you’re looking for a bottle of wine that tells a story, consider exploring the world of amphorae wines. From the ancient techniques revived by modern winemakers to the rich, complex flavors that they offer, these wines are a testament to the enduring allure of the past in the ever-evolving world of wine. Whether you’re a seasoned wine enthusiast or just starting to discover the world of wine, amphorae wines are sure to captivate your taste buds and transport you to a time long gone.
