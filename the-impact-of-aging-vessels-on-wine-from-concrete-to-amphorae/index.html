The Impact of Aging Vessels on Wine: From Concrete to Amphorae


When it comes to making wine, one of the most important factors that contribute to its quality and flavor profile is how it is aged. Aging vessels play a crucial role in the development of wine, as they provide a controlled environment that allows the wine to mature and evolve over time. In recent years, there has been a growing trend in the use of alternative aging vessels, such as concrete tanks and amphorae, which offer unique characteristics and are believed to have a significant impact on the final product.

Traditionally, oak barrels have been the go-to aging vessel for winemakers worldwide. The use of oak imparts distinctive flavors and aromas to the wine, such as vanilla, spice, and toast. However, some winemakers have been exploring alternative options, seeking vessels that offer a more neutral environment and allow the true expression of the grape and terroir to shine through.

Concrete tanks have gained popularity in recent years due to their ability to regulate temperature and provide a consistent aging environment. Unlike oak, concrete is non-porous, which means it does not add any flavors or tannins to the wine. This allows the wine to develop more slowly and naturally, resulting in a more focused and precise expression of the grape varietal. Concrete also allows a slight amount of oxygen exchange, which can help soften tannins and round out the wine.

Amphorae, on the other hand, are ancient vessels that have been used in winemaking for thousands of years. They are typically made of clay and have a unique shape and design that allows for the micro-oxygenation of wine. This gentle exposure to oxygen can lead to the development of complex flavors and textures in the wine. Amphorae are also known for their ability to retain temperature stability, which can be beneficial during the aging process. The use of amphorae has seen a resurgence in recent years, as winemakers seek to reconnect with ancient winemaking techniques and produce wines with a distinct sense of place.

The impact of aging vessels on wine is not limited to flavor and aroma development; it also has a significant influence on the overall structure and aging potential of the wine. For example, oak barrels can impart tannins to the wine, which gives it structure and the potential for aging. On the other hand, concrete tanks and amphorae offer a more gentle aging environment, allowing the wine to develop slowly and subtly over time. This can result in wines that are more approachable in their youth but still have the potential to age gracefully.

While the use of alternative aging vessels is gaining traction in the wine industry, it is important to note that they are not suitable for all styles of wine. Certain grape varietals and winemaking techniques may benefit more from traditional oak aging, while others may benefit from the unique characteristics offered by concrete tanks or amphorae. It ultimately comes down to the winemaker’s vision and the desired style of the wine.

In conclusion, the impact of aging vessels on wine cannot be underestimated. Whether it’s the nuanced flavors and aromas imparted by oak barrels, the precision and focus offered by concrete tanks, or the complex textures and structures achieved with amphorae, the choice of aging vessel plays a crucial role in shaping the final product. As winemakers continue to explore alternative methods and techniques, the world of wine is becoming even more diverse and exciting. So, the next time you enjoy a glass of wine, take a moment to appreciate the vessel it was aged in, as it is a crucial factor in the wine’s journey from vineyard to glass.
