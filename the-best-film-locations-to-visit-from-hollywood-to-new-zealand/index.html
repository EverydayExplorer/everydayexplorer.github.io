The Best Film Locations to Visit: From Hollywood to New Zealand


When it comes to film locations, there are few places in the world that can compete with Hollywood. The glitz and glamour of this iconic city have been immortalized in countless films, making it a must-visit destination for movie lovers. However, Hollywood isn’t the only place where you can experience the magic of the silver screen. From New York City to New Zealand, there are plenty of other film locations that are worth adding to your travel bucket list.

First up, let’s talk about Hollywood. Home to the world-famous Hollywood sign, Walk of Fame, and iconic studio lots, this city is a film lover’s paradise. Take a tour of Universal Studios to get a behind-the-scenes look at how movies are made or visit the Hollywood Museum to see memorabilia from your favorite films. And of course, no visit to Hollywood would be complete without catching a movie at the historic TCL Chinese Theatre or the El Capitan Theatre.

Next on our list is New York City. Known as the ‘Big Apple,’ this bustling metropolis has served as the backdrop for countless films. From the iconic Empire State Building to Central Park, there are plenty of recognizable locations to visit. Take a stroll through Times Square, where you’ll feel like you’re right in the middle of a movie set. For a taste of classic New York, make sure to visit the Brooklyn Bridge or take a ferry to the Statue of Liberty.

Moving on to New Zealand, this stunning country has become a popular destination for filmmakers in recent years. The breathtaking landscapes, from lush green forests to rugged mountains, have been featured in blockbuster movies like ‘The Lord of the Rings’ and ‘The Hobbit’ trilogy. Take a tour of the film locations in the North Island, such as Hobbiton and Mount Doom, or explore the majestic landscapes of the South Island, including Milford Sound and Lake Wakatipu.

If you’re a fan of the ‘Harry Potter’ series, then a visit to London should be on your film location itinerary. Explore the magical world of Hogwarts by visiting the Warner Bros. Studio Tour London - The Making of Harry Potter. Get a behind-the-scenes look at the sets, costumes, and props used in the films, and even take a stroll through Diagon Alley. And don’t forget to visit Platform 9 ¾ at King’s Cross Station, where you can take a photo with the famous trolley disappearing into the wall.

For fans of classic films, a trip to Los Angeles is a must. Visit the iconic Griffith Observatory, which was featured in ‘Rebel Without a Cause,’ or take a drive down Mulholland Drive, made famous by the film of the same name. And of course, you can’t miss a visit to the Beverly Hills Hotel, where many Hollywood legends have stayed over the years.

Last but not least, let’s talk about Paris. Known for its romantic charm and picturesque streets, this city has been the setting for many iconic films. Take a walk along the Seine River or visit the Eiffel Tower, both of which have been featured in numerous movies. And for fans of ‘Midnight in Paris,’ you can even take a literary-themed tour of the city, visiting the spots that were frequented by famous writers like Hemingway and Fitzgerald.

These are just a few of the best film locations to visit around the world. Whether you’re a die-hard movie buff or just looking to explore some incredible destinations, these places offer a unique blend of cinematic history and real-world beauty. So pack your bags, grab your camera, and get ready to experience the magic of the movies in these unforgettable locations.
