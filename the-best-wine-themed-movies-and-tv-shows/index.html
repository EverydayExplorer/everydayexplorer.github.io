The Best Wine-Themed Movies and TV Shows


Wine, with its complex flavors and rich history, has long been a muse for filmmakers and television creators alike. From romantic comedies set in lush vineyards to intense dramas unfolding in prestigious wineries, the world of wine-themed entertainment offers a delightful escape for aficionados and novices alike. So, pour yourself a glass of your favorite vintage and settle in as we explore some of the best wine-themed movies and TV shows that are sure to tantalize your senses.

  1. Sideways (2004): Director Alexander Payne’s ‘Sideways’ follows two friends, Miles and Jack, on a wine-tasting road trip through California’s Santa Ynez Valley. As they navigate through the picturesque landscapes and indulge in copious amounts of wine, the duo’s journey becomes a poignant exploration of friendship, love, and the complexities of life. With its witty dialogue and stellar performances, ‘Sideways’ is a must-watch for any wine enthusiast.

  2. A Good Year (2006): Directed by Ridley Scott and based on Peter Mayle’s novel of the same name, ‘A Good Year’ stars Russell Crowe as a London banker who inherits a vineyard in Provence, France. As he grapples with the decision to sell the estate, he rediscovers his passion for life, love, and, of course, wine. The film’s stunning cinematography captures the beauty of the French countryside, making it a feast for the eyes as well as the soul.

  3. Bottle Shock (2008): Set in California’s Napa Valley in the 1970s, ‘Bottle Shock’ chronicles the true story of the Judgment of Paris, a blind wine tasting that shocked the wine world by pitting French wines against upstart California wines. With an ensemble cast including Alan Rickman, Chris Pine, and Bill Pullman, the film captures the essence of the American dream and the transformative power of passion and perseverance.

  4. Somm (2012): For those fascinated by the intricacies of wine tasting and the journey to becoming a master sommelier, ‘Somm’ offers a captivating glimpse into this elite world. The documentary follows four sommeliers as they prepare for the notoriously difficult Master Sommelier exam, pushing themselves to the limits of their knowledge and endurance. ‘Somm’ is a testament to the dedication and obsession required to achieve greatness in the world of wine.

  5. Uncorked (2020): Directed by Prentice Penny, ‘Uncorked’ tells the story of a young man torn between his passion for wine and his family’s expectations. As he pursues his dream of becoming a master sommelier, he must confront his father’s disapproval and navigate the complexities of race and class in the wine industry. With its heartfelt performances and nuanced exploration of identity and ambition, ‘Uncorked’ offers a fresh take on the traditional wine-themed narrative.

  6. Wine Country (2019): Directed by Amy Poehler, ‘Wine Country’ follows a group of longtime friends as they reunite for a weekend getaway in California’s Napa Valley to celebrate a milestone birthday. As they laugh, reminisce, and, of course, drink copious amounts of wine, they confront past grievances and reevaluate their relationships. With its stellar ensemble cast and relatable humor, ‘Wine Country’ is a delightful ode to the bonds of friendship and the joys of indulgence.

  7. Red Obsession (2013): This documentary delves into the world of fine wine from an unexpected angle—China’s burgeoning obsession with Bordeaux wines. Narrated by Russell Crowe, ‘Red Obsession’ explores the intersection of wealth, power, and taste in China’s rapidly expanding economy, shedding light on the global impact of the country’s newfound passion for luxury wines.

  8. The Wine Show (2016-present): For those who prefer their wine-themed entertainment in episodic form, ‘The Wine Show’ offers a delightful blend of travelogue, cooking show, and, of course, wine appreciation. Hosted by actors Matthew Goode and Matthew Rhys, the series follows the duo as they embark on wine-related adventures around the world, meeting winemakers, chefs, and fellow enthusiasts along the way.

Whether you’re a seasoned oenophile or simply enjoy a good glass of wine now and then, these movies and TV shows are sure to satisfy your cravings for both entertainment and vinicultural delights. So, grab a corkscrew, uncork your favorite bottle, and let these wine-themed tales transport you to vineyards near and far. Cheers!
