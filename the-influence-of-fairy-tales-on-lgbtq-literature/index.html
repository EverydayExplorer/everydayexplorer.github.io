The Influence of Fairy Tales on LGBTQ+ Literature


Once upon a time, in a world filled with imagination and wonder, fairy tales captivated the hearts and minds of readers young and old. These enchanting stories, with their magical creatures, brave heroes, and epic quests, have been a source of inspiration for countless authors throughout history. But did you know that fairy tales have also played a significant role in shaping LGBTQ+ literature?

Fairy tales, with their themes of transformation, self-discovery, and the triumph of love over adversity, have provided a rich tapestry for LGBTQ+ authors to explore and express their experiences. By subverting traditional gender roles, challenging societal norms, and embracing the power of storytelling, LGBTQ+ writers have found a unique platform to share their voices and narratives.

One of the earliest examples of the influence of fairy tales on LGBTQ+ literature can be found in ‘Ash’ by Malinda Lo. Lo’s reimagining of the Cinderella story introduces readers to a young woman named Ash, who finds solace and love in the arms of the mysterious Huntress. Through this retelling, Lo explores themes of forbidden love, self-acceptance, and the quest for personal freedom.

Another notable work that draws upon fairy tale elements is ‘The Miseducation of Cameron Post’ by Emily M. Danforth. Set in rural Montana, the novel follows Cameron Post as she navigates her journey of self-discovery and coming to terms with her sexuality. Drawing inspiration from the classic tale of Snow White, Danforth weaves a powerful narrative that challenges societal expectations and explores the complexities of identity.

Fairy tales have also provided a backdrop for LGBTQ+ authors to explore themes of transformation and self-acceptance. In ‘Boy, Snow, Bird’ by Helen Oyeyemi, the author reimagines the Snow White story to delve into themes of gender, race, and identity. The novel centers around Boy Novak, who escapes an abusive father and finds herself in the midst of a family secret that challenges her perception of herself and those around her.

It is not just in novels that the influence of fairy tales can be seen in LGBTQ+ literature. Short stories and poetry have also embraced the magical allure of these timeless tales. In ‘The Rose and The Beast: Fairy Tales Retold’ by Francesca Lia Block, readers are treated to a collection of reimagined fairy tales that explore themes of love, desire, and identity. From a queer retelling of ‘Beauty and the Beast’ to a lesbian love story inspired by ‘The Little Mermaid,’ Block’s collection showcases the power of fairy tales to transcend boundaries and embrace diversity.

The influence of fairy tales on LGBTQ+ literature extends beyond individual works and authors. These stories have given rise to a genre known as ‘queer fairy tales,’ where authors and storytellers reimagine traditional tales through an LGBTQ+ lens. Through these retellings, LGBTQ+ authors have reclaimed narratives, challenged heteronormative constructs, and created spaces for marginalized voices to be heard.

In conclusion, fairy tales have had a profound impact on LGBTQ+ literature. By providing a platform for self-expression, exploring themes of identity and acceptance, and challenging societal norms, these enchanting stories have paved the way for LGBTQ+ authors to share their experiences with the world. As we continue to celebrate diversity and inclusivity, let us embrace the magic of fairy tales and the powerful influence they have had on LGBTQ+ literature.
