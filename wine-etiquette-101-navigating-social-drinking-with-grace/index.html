Wine Etiquette 101: Navigating Social Drinking with Grace


When attending social events or dining out, it’s essential to choose the right wine that complements your meal or the occasion. Understanding basic wine pairing principles, such as white wine with seafood and red wine with red meat, can help you make an informed decision. Don’t hesitate to ask the waitstaff or sommelier for recommendations if you’re unsure.

2. Wine Tasting Protocol
Attending a wine tasting event? Remember to follow the proper protocol. Hold your wine glass by the stem, not the bowl, to prevent heating the wine with your hands. Swirl the wine gently to release its aromas and take small sips to fully appreciate its flavors. When discussing the wine, focus on its characteristics rather than personal preferences.

3. Cheers, Glasses, and Toasts
When it’s time to raise your glass for a toast, maintain eye contact with your fellow drinkers and say ‘Cheers!’ Avoid clinking glasses too forcefully, as it can spill the wine or break the glass. If you’re part of a larger group, make sure to clink glasses with everyone at your table. And remember, it’s customary to take a sip after the toast.

4. Wine Pouring Etiquette
If you’re hosting a dinner party or gathering, mastering the art of wine pouring will impress your guests. Pour wine in a clockwise direction around the table, starting with the person closest to you. Avoid filling the glass to the brim; instead, aim for a third of the glass, allowing room for swirling and appreciation of the wine’s aromas.

5. Handling Wine Accoutrements
When presented with wine accoutrements, such as corkscrews and decanters, it’s important to use them correctly. Familiarize yourself with different types of corkscrews and practice opening a wine bottle smoothly and confidently. If a wine needs decanting, pour it slowly into the decanter, allowing for proper aeration and separation from any sediment.

6. Proper Wine Storage
Are you a wine collector or planning to keep a few bottles at home? Ensure proper wine storage to maintain its quality. Store wine bottles horizontally to keep the cork moist and prevent it from drying out, which could lead to oxidation. Maintain a consistent temperature and avoid exposure to direct sunlight or strong odors.

7. Engaging in Wine Conversations
Engaging in wine conversations can be enjoyable and educational. When discussing wine, be respectful of other people’s opinions and avoid making condescending remarks. Share your knowledge, but also be open to learning from others. This will create a positive and inclusive environment for everyone involved.

Remember, wine etiquette is all about enjoying the experience and respecting those around you. By following these tips, you’ll navigate social drinking with grace, whether you’re attending a formal wine tasting or simply enjoying a glass with friends. So, raise your glass, savor the flavors, and toast to a delightful journey into the world of wine!

We hope you found this guide to wine etiquette helpful! Cheers to your next wine adventure, and may it be filled with joy, laughter, and delightful conversations.
