Fashion in Literature: Iconic Book Characters


Fashion has always played a significant role in literature, not only as a way to describe characters but also as a means of expressing their personalities and defining their roles within a story. From the flamboyant attire of the dandy to the timeless elegance of the femme fatale, fashion in literature has the power to create unforgettable characters that stay with us long after we finish reading the book.

One of the most iconic book characters known for their fashion sense is Jay Gatsby from F. Scott Fitzgerald’s classic novel, ‘The Great Gatsby.’ Gatsby’s extravagant parties and lavish lifestyle are mirrored in his impeccable wardrobe, consisting of perfectly tailored suits, colorful silk shirts, and elegant bow ties. Through his stylish attire, Gatsby embodies the spirit of the Roaring Twenties and the pursuit of the American Dream.

Another literary character who is synonymous with fashion is the enigmatic Holly Golightly from Truman Capote’s ‘Breakfast at Tiffany’s.’ As a quintessential fashionista, Holly is known for her elegant little black dresses, oversized sunglasses, and glamorous accessories. Her impeccable taste in fashion reflects her desire to create an aura of sophistication and glamour, masking her vulnerable and complex nature.

Moving on to the world of fantasy, we encounter the iconic character of Dumbledore from J.K. Rowling’s ‘Harry Potter’ series. Despite his magical wizarding robes, Dumbledore’s eccentric sense of fashion sets him apart. With his long, flowing robes, embroidered with intricate symbols and patterns, Dumbledore exudes wisdom, power, and a touch of mystery. His fashion choices serve as a visual representation of his larger-than-life personality and his role as the wise and powerful headmaster of Hogwarts.

Literature also boasts strong female characters who use fashion to make a statement. Scarlett O’Hara from Margaret Mitchell’s ‘Gone with the Wind’ is a perfect example. Set against the backdrop of the Civil War, Scarlett’s resilient spirit is reflected in her determination to dress elegantly, despite the harsh realities of the war. From her iconic green velvet dress made from her mother’s curtains to her stylish bonnets, Scarlett’s fashion choices symbolize her strength, adaptability, and determination to survive in challenging times.

Another memorable female character known for her fashion-forward choices is Lisbeth Salander from Stieg Larsson’s ‘The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo.’ Lisbeth’s punk-inspired style, complete with her signature black leather jacket, piercings, and tattoos, represents her non-conformist attitude and rebellious nature. Her unconventional fashion sense becomes a visual representation of her complex personality, highlighting her strength, intelligence, and refusal to adhere to societal norms.

Fashion in literature is not limited to fictional characters; real-life authors have also made their mark with their distinct fashion choices. Take Virginia Woolf, for example. Known for her modernist writing style, Woolf’s fashion sense was equally forward-thinking. She was often seen in loose, flowing garments, defying the restrictive fashion trends of her time. With her unconventional fashion choices, Woolf embodied the spirit of rebellion and freedom that resonated in her groundbreaking works of literature.

In conclusion, fashion in literature has the power to create unforgettable characters and bring stories to life. From the dapper gentlemen to the fierce femmes fatales, fashion serves as a visual representation of a character’s personality, status, and inner world. Whether it’s the vivid descriptions of Gatsby’s opulent attire or the rebellious style of Lisbeth Salander, fashion in literature adds depth and richness to the narrative, making the characters more relatable and memorable. So, the next time you delve into a great book, don’t forget to pay attention to the fashion choices of the characters. You might just discover a whole new layer of their personality waiting to be unveiled.
