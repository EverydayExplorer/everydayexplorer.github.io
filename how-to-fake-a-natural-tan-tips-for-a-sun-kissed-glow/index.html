How to Fake a Natural Tan: Tips for a Sun-Kissed Glow


Who doesn’t love the look of a sun-kissed glow? A natural tan can make you feel confident and radiant, but spending hours under the scorching sun isn’t always the best idea. Luckily, there are ways to fake a natural tan without exposing your skin to harmful UV rays. In this blog post, we will share some tips and tricks to help you achieve a beautiful, sun-kissed glow without the sunburn.

1. Exfoliate before you tan: To ensure a smooth and even tan, it’s important to exfoliate your skin before applying any self-tanning products. Use a gentle scrub or exfoliating mitt to remove dead skin cells and create a smooth canvas for your tan. Focus on rough areas like elbows, knees, and ankles to avoid any patchy spots.

2. Choose the right self-tanning product: There are countless self-tanning products available on the market, from lotions to sprays to mousses. Finding the right one for your skin type and desired result is crucial. If you’re a beginner, opt for a gradual tanner or a tinted moisturizer to build up your tan gradually. For a deeper tan, choose a product with a higher concentration of DHA (dihydroxyacetone), the active ingredient that gives you that golden glow.

3. Prep your skin: Before applying any self-tanner, it’s important to prep your skin properly. Start by moisturizing dry areas, such as your elbows, knees, and ankles, to prevent them from absorbing too much product. You can also apply a thin layer of petroleum jelly to your cuticles and eyebrows to avoid any staining. It’s also a good idea to wear gloves or use a tanning mitt to protect your hands from getting too dark.

4. Apply your self-tanner evenly: When applying your self-tanner, start with a small amount and work your way up. Begin at your feet and work your way up your body, using circular motions to ensure an even application. Take your time and blend the product well, paying extra attention to areas that tend to be lighter, such as your knees and elbows.

5. Don’t forget your face: To fake a natural tan, it’s important to extend the color to your face as well. You can use a self-tanning lotion specifically formulated for the face or mix a small amount of your regular self-tanner with your moisturizer. Apply the product evenly, blending it down your neck and onto your décolletage for a seamless finish.

6. Maintain your tan: Once you’ve achieved your desired tan, it’s important to maintain it properly. Hydrate your skin daily with a moisturizer to prolong the life of your tan and prevent it from fading unevenly. Avoid exfoliating your skin too vigorously, as this can strip away your tan. Instead, opt for gentle exfoliation every few days to keep your skin smooth and radiant.

7. Touch up as needed: Over time, your tan will naturally fade. To keep it looking fresh and natural, touch up any areas that may have faded or become patchy. Use a gradual tanner or a tinted moisturizer to even out your tan and maintain that sun-kissed glow.

Faking a natural tan is a great way to achieve a radiant and healthy-looking complexion without the harmful effects of the sun. By following these tips and tricks, you can enjoy a beautiful tan all year round. Remember, confidence is key, and with a little help from self-tanning products, you can rock that sun-kissed glow no matter the season.
