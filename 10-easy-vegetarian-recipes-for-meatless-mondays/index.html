10 Easy Vegetarian Recipes for Meatless Mondays


Embracing Meatless Mondays is a fantastic way to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, while also reaping the benefits of a healthier lifestyle. Whether you’re a seasoned vegetarian or just looking to cut back on meat, these ten easy vegetarian recipes are not only delicious but also simple enough to whip up on a busy weekday. Get ready to explore vibrant flavors, wholesome ingredients, and a variety of cuisines that will keep your taste buds tingling all day long!

1. Chickpea Salad Sandwich

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise (vegan if desired)
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Whole grain bread or your choice of sandwich bread
  • Fresh lettuce leaves

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mash the chickpeas with a fork until flaky but not completely smooth.
  2. Stir in the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, celery, and red onion. Mix until well combined.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Spread the chickpea mixture onto slices of bread and top with fresh lettuce leaves. Serve immediately.

This protein-packed sandwich is perfect for a quick lunch or a light dinner, providing all the satisfaction of a traditional chicken salad without any meat.

2. Spinach and Ricotta Stuffed Shells

Ingredients:

  • 12 jumbo pasta shells
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 1/2 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the jumbo pasta shells according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the chopped spinach, ricotta cheese, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper.
  4. Fill each pasta shell with the spinach and ricotta mixture, then place them in a baking dish.
  5. Pour marinara sauce over the stuffed shells and sprinkle with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden. Serve warm.

These stuffed shells are a comforting dish that will make you forget you’re skipping meat!

3. Quinoa and Black Bean Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Lime wedges for serving
  • Olive oil, salt, and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring vegetable broth to a boil. Add quinoa, cover, and reduce heat to low. Cook for about 15 minutes or until quinoa is fluffy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, and diced bell pepper. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Serve the mixture in bowls, topped with avocado slices and fresh cilantro. Squeeze lime juice over the top before serving.

This bowl is not only a feast for the eyes but also loaded with nutrients, making it a perfect Meatless Monday meal.

4. Vegetable Stir-Fry with Tofu

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (broccoli, bell peppers, carrots, etc.)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add cubed tofu and cook until golden brown on all sides. Remove tofu and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add ginger and garlic, sautéing until fragrant. Add mixed vegetables and stir-fry for 5-7 minutes until tender-crisp.
  3. Return the tofu to the skillet and add soy sauce. Toss everything together and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  4. Serve over cooked rice or noodles.

This vibrant stir-fry is quick, easy, and packed with flavor, perfect for a weeknight dinner.

5. Lentil Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté onions, carrots, and celery until softened. Add garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  2. Stir in lentils, vegetable broth, diced tomatoes, cumin, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30-35 minutes until lentils are tender.
  3. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

This hearty lentil soup is perfect for meal prep and is sure to warm you up on a chilly evening.

6. Caprese Salad with Balsamic Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe tomatoes, sliced
  • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup balsamic glaze
  • Olive oil, salt, and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. On a platter, alternate layers of tomato and mozzarella slices.
  2. Tuck fresh basil leaves between the layers.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic glaze, then season with salt and pepper.

This classic Italian salad is refreshing, simple, and full of flavor, making it a perfect appetizer or side dish.

7. Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn or flour tortillas
  • Toppings: avocado, cilantro, lime juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss diced sweet potatoes with olive oil, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes until tender.
  2. Warm tortillas in a skillet or microwave. Fill each tortilla with roasted sweet potatoes and black beans.
  3. Top with avocado slices, fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice.

These tacos are a delightful fusion of flavors and textures that will leave you wanting more.

8. Vegetable Curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, cauliflower, etc.)
  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooked rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat coconut oil over medium heat. Add onions, garlic, and ginger, sautéing until fragrant.
  2. Stir in mixed vegetables and cook for a few minutes. Add curry powder and salt, stirring to coat.
  3. Pour in coconut milk and simmer for 15-20 minutes until vegetables are tender.
  4. Serve hot over cooked rice.

This creamy vegetable curry is a comforting and aromatic dish that is perfect for any weeknight dinner.

9. Zucchini Noodles with Pesto

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium zucchinis
  • 1 cup basil pesto (store-bought or homemade)
  • Cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Olive oil, salt, and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Using a spiralizer or vegetable peeler, create zucchini noodles.
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add zucchini noodles and sauté for 2-3 minutes until just tender.
  3. Toss with pesto and cherry tomatoes, seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve immediately.

This fresh and vibrant dish is a low-carb alternative to pasta and is bursting with flavor!

10. Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup or honey
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine avocados, cocoa powder, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  2. Spoon the mousse into serving dishes and chill in the fridge for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Indulge in this rich and creamy dessert that is as healthy as it is delicious, perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth without any guilt.

Incorporating these easy vegetarian recipes into your Meatless Monday routine can lead to new culinary adventures while supporting a sustainable lifestyle. Each dish is not only simple to prepare but also packed with nutrients, flavors, and creativity. Remember, going meatless doesn’t mean sacrificing taste—rather, it opens the door to a world of exciting plant-based meals. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and enjoy a delicious Meatless Monday feast!
