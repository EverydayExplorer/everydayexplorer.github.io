Liquor and Literature: Classic Drinks from Famous Authors


As literature enthusiasts, we often find ourselves captivated by the words of famous authors. Their works transport us to different worlds, evoke deep emotions, and inspire us in countless ways. But did you know that some of these literary geniuses also had a penchant for liquor? That’s right! In this blog post, we’ll explore the intriguing connection between liquor and literature by delving into classic drinks favored by famous authors.

1. Ernest Hemingway and the Daiquiri:
Ernest Hemingway, known for his bold and adventurous writing style, was also quite the connoisseur of cocktails. One drink that he particularly enjoyed was the Daiquiri. Hemingway would frequent La Floridita, a famous bar in Havana, Cuba, where he would savor his favorite concoction. The Hemingway Daiquiri, also known as the Papa Doble, is a variation of the classic Daiquiri, made with white rum, lime juice, grapefruit juice, maraschino liqueur, and a splash of sugar syrup. Its vibrant flavors perfectly complement Hemingway’s lively prose.

2. F. Scott Fitzgerald and the Gin Rickey:
F. Scott Fitzgerald, the author behind ‘The Great Gatsby,’ had an affinity for elegant parties and glamorous lifestyles. One of his preferred drinks was the Gin Rickey. This refreshing cocktail is a simple blend of gin, lime juice, and club soda. Its effervescence and tangy citrus notes mirror the sparkling atmosphere and vivacious characters found in Fitzgerald’s novels. So, next time you’re looking to capture a bit of the roaring ’20s, mix yourself a Gin Rickey and let the words of Fitzgerald transport you to the lavish world of Jay Gatsby.

3. Charles Bukowski and the Whiskey Sour:
Known for his gritty and unapologetic writing, Charles Bukowski was no stranger to the allure of whiskey. Bukowski’s preferred drink was the Whiskey Sour. This classic cocktail, made with whiskey, lemon juice, and sugar, perfectly encapsulates Bukowski’s raw and no-frills style. The combination of sweet and sour flavors mirrors the bitter and sweet truths portrayed in his works. So, kick back, pour yourself a Whiskey Sour, and let Bukowski’s unfiltered words wash over you.

4. Edgar Allan Poe and the Champagne Cocktail:
Edgar Allan Poe, the master of macabre literature, had an affinity for the finer things in life, including champagne. One cocktail that Poe enjoyed was the Champagne Cocktail. This elegant drink combines champagne, sugar, bitters, and a twist of lemon or orange peel. Its effervescence and delicate flavors create an air of mystery and sophistication, much like Poe’s haunting tales. So, raise a glass of Champagne Cocktail to the literary genius and delve into the enigmatic world of Poe.

5. Dorothy Parker and the Martini:
Dorothy Parker, revered for her sharp wit and biting humor, found solace in the classic Martini. Known for her membership in the Algonquin Round Table, a group of literary and cultural luminaries, Parker would often exchange quips and brilliant repartee over a well-made Martini. This iconic cocktail, made with gin and vermouth, perfectly embodies Parker’s razor-sharp intellect and sophisticated taste. So, channel your inner wit and pour yourself a Martini while enjoying Parker’s acerbic writings.

In conclusion, liquor and literature share a fascinating connection, as witnessed through the favorite drinks of famous authors. From the vibrant Daiquiri loved by Hemingway to the elegant Champagne Cocktail favored by Poe, each drink reflects the essence of its creator’s writing style and personality. So, the next time you pick up a classic book, consider sipping on one of these iconic cocktails to truly immerse yourself in the spirit of the author and their work. Cheers to the literary adventures that await!
