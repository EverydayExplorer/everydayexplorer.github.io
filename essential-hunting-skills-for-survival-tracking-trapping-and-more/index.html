Essential Hunting Skills for Survival: Tracking, Trapping, and more


Hunting has been an essential skill for survival since the beginning of human history. In today’s modern world, where we can easily buy food from the supermarket, hunting may seem like a forgotten skill. However, in times of crisis or emergency situations, having the ability to hunt for food can be a life-saving skill. In this blog post, we will discuss some essential hunting skills for survival, including tracking, trapping, and more.

Tracking is the art of following the trails left by animals. Knowing how to track can greatly increase your chances of successfully hunting for food. When tracking animals, it is important to look for signs such as footprints, scat, and broken foliage. By studying these signs, you can determine the direction the animal is moving, its size, and even its behavior.

There are several techniques and tips for successful tracking. Firstly, it is important to move quietly and slowly, making sure to avoid making unnecessary noises that could scare away the game. Secondly, it is crucial to stay downwind of the animal you are tracking, as animals have a keen sense of smell and can easily detect the scent of a human. Additionally, it is important to be observant and pay attention to your surroundings, as the animal may leave subtle clues that can lead you to its location.

Trapping is another important hunting skill that can be highly effective in securing food in a survival situation. Traps are typically used to catch small game such as rabbits, squirrels, and birds. There are various types of traps, including snares, deadfalls, and pit traps. Snares work by capturing the animal by the neck, while deadfalls are designed to crush the animal. Pit traps, on the other hand, are holes dug into the ground with stakes or spikes at the bottom, causing the animal to fall in and become trapped.

When setting traps, it is important to choose an area where there is a high likelihood of animal activity, such as near a game trail or near a water source. It is also crucial to camouflage the trap to make it blend in with the surroundings and increase its chances of success. Furthermore, regular maintenance and checking of traps are essential to ensure that they are functioning properly and to prevent the unnecessary suffering of animals.

In addition to tracking and trapping, there are other hunting skills that are important for survival. One such skill is shooting. Being skilled with a firearm can greatly increase your chances of successfully hunting for food. It is crucial to practice regularly to improve your accuracy and aim. Additionally, it is important to always handle firearms safely and responsibly.

Another essential hunting skill is butchering and field dressing game. Once you have successfully hunted an animal, it is important to know how to properly dress and butcher it. This involves removing the skin, internal organs, and other inedible parts of the animal. Proper field dressing ensures that the meat is clean and safe to consume.

Lastly, it is important to have knowledge of the local flora and fauna. Understanding the various plants and animals in your area can help you identify potential sources of food as well as potential dangers. Knowing which plants are edible and can provide sustenance can greatly enhance your survival skills.

In conclusion, hunting skills are crucial for survival in emergency situations or during times of crisis. Tracking, trapping, shooting, butchering, and knowledge of local flora and fauna are all important skills that can greatly enhance your chances of successfully hunting for food. While hunting may seem like a forgotten skill in today’s modern world, being able to provide food for yourself and your loved ones is an invaluable skill to have. So, take the time to learn and practice these essential hunting skills for survival. Your life may depend on it.
