The Best Books You Should Read in 2021: A Comprehensive List


Are you an avid bookworm? Do you love getting lost in the pages of a captivating story or immersing yourself in the wisdom and knowledge that books have to offer? Well, you’re in luck! In this comprehensive blog post, we will be sharing with you a list of the best books that you should read in 2021.

2021 has been an eventful and challenging year for many of us. With the ongoing pandemic and the changing world around us, a good book has the power to transport us to another world, provide solace, and help us gain new perspectives. Whether you enjoy fiction, non-fiction, self-help, or poetry, there’s something on this list for everyone.

1. ‘The Four Winds’ by Kristin Hannah: Set during the Great Depression, this historical fiction novel follows the journey of a Texas woman who must make difficult choices to ensure the survival of her family. With beautiful storytelling and well-developed characters, ‘The Four Winds’ is a must-read for fans of emotional and powerful historical novels.

2. ‘Project Hail Mary’ by Andy Weir: From the author of ‘The Martian,’ this science fiction thriller takes us on an adventure in space with Ryland Grace, the sole survivor on a desperate mission to save humanity. With its blend of suspense, humor, and scientific accuracy, ‘Project Hail Mary’ will keep you hooked until the very last page.

3. ‘Untamed’ by Glennon Doyle: In this empowering memoir, Glennon Doyle shares her journey of self-discovery and encourages readers to embrace their true selves. With its powerful messages of authenticity and resilience, ‘Untamed’ is a book that will inspire and empower you to live life on your own terms.

4. ‘The Push’ by Ashley Audrain: If you’re a fan of psychological thrillers, this gripping debut novel is a must-read. ‘The Push’ tells the story of a mother grappling with the reality that her young daughter may be a danger to herself and others. With its haunting narrative and suspenseful twists, this book will keep you on the edge of your seat.

5. ‘The Code Breaker’ by Walter Isaacson: Dive into the world of genetics and the groundbreaking discoveries of Jennifer Doudna, the co-creator of CRISPR technology. Isaacson’s engaging storytelling and in-depth research make ‘The Code Breaker’ a captivating and enlightening read for anyone interested in the potential of genetic engineering.

6. ‘Klara and the Sun’ by Kazuo Ishiguro: This poignant and thought-provoking novel by the Nobel Prize-winning author tells the story of Klara, an Artificial Friend who serves as a companion to a lonely teenager. Exploring themes of love, human connection, and the implications of AI, ‘Klara and the Sun’ is a beautifully written and timely novel.

7. ‘Caste: The Origins of Our Discontents’ by Isabel Wilkerson: In this powerful non-fiction book, Wilkerson explores the hidden hierarchies that shape our lives and examines how the caste system has influenced social dynamics in America. With its profound insights and meticulous research, ‘Caste’ provides a compelling examination of the root causes of inequality.

8. ‘Concrete Rose’ by Angie Thomas: Set in the same universe as Angie Thomas’s debut novel ‘The Hate U Give,’ this prequel takes us back to the world of Garden Heights and follows the story of Maverick Carter, the father of the protagonist in the first book. With its exploration of love, family, and resilience, ‘Concrete Rose’ is a gripping coming-of-age story that will resonate with readers of all ages.

9. ‘The Sanatorium’ by Sarah Pearse: If you’re a fan of atmospheric thrillers, this gripping novel is perfect for you. Set in a remote and eerie hotel in the Swiss Alps, ‘The Sanatorium’ follows the story of a detective investigating a series of mysterious deaths. With its chilling setting and spine-tingling suspense, this book will keep you guessing until the very end.

10. ‘Anxious People’ by Fredrik Backman: Known for his heartwarming and insightful novels, Fredrik Backman presents a story that blends comedy and drama seamlessly. ‘Anxious People’ takes us on a captivating journey as a failed bank robber takes hostages in an apartment viewing and an unlikely group of people bond over their shared experiences. With its relatable characters and poignant exploration of human emotions, this book will leave you feeling uplifted and hopeful.

There you have it, a comprehensive list of the best books you should read in 2021. Whether you’re looking for captivating stories, thought-provoking non-fiction, or empowering memoirs, these books are sure to keep you entertained and inspired. So grab a cup of tea, cozy up in your favorite reading nook, and let these books take you on unforgettable journeys. Happy reading!
