Wine and Literature: Iconic Wines in Famous Books


Wine and literature have a long and storied history, with wine often being used as a symbol of sophistication, pleasure, and even transcendence in many famous novels. Throughout the years, authors have skillfully woven wine into their narratives, using it to enhance the atmosphere, reveal character traits, and add depth to their stories. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most iconic wines mentioned in famous books, showcasing their significance and how they contribute to the overall themes of the narratives.

1. Château Lafite Rothschild - ‘The Great Gatsby’ by F. Scott Fitzgerald:
In F. Scott Fitzgerald’s classic novel, ‘The Great Gatsby,’ the glamorous and enigmatic Jay Gatsby is often seen toasting and sipping the renowned Château Lafite Rothschild. This wine represents wealth, luxury, and the allure of the American Dream. The parties at Gatsby’s mansion are awash with extravagance, and the presence of this prestigious wine further accentuates the opulence of the era.

2. Château Margaux - ‘Jane Eyre’ by Charlotte Brontë:
In Charlotte Brontë’s Gothic masterpiece, ‘Jane Eyre,’ the protagonist, Jane, encounters the brooding and mysterious Mr. Rochester. Their conversations are often accompanied by the sharing of Château Margaux, a powerful Bordeaux red wine. The wine symbolizes the complex and nuanced nature of their relationship, as Jane finds herself captivated by Mr. Rochester’s charm and haunted past.

3. Madeira wine - ‘Moby-Dick’ by Herman Melville:
In Herman Melville’s epic novel, ‘Moby-Dick,’ the character of Ishmael delightfully describes enjoying a glass of Madeira wine while contemplating the wonders of the sea. Madeira wine, a fortified wine from the Portuguese island of Madeira, embodies the strong and adventurous spirit of the whaling men in the story. Its rich flavor and historical significance make it the perfect companion for the sailors’ tales and their pursuit of the great white whale.

4. Veuve Clicquot - ‘Great Expectations’ by Charles Dickens:
In Charles Dickens’ beloved novel, ‘Great Expectations,’ the eccentric Miss Havisham, jilted on her wedding day, sits in her crumbling mansion, surrounded by decaying remnants of her wedding feast. Among the dusty remains is a single bottle of Veuve Clicquot champagne. The wine, known for its elegance and effervescence, mirrors Miss Havisham’s past grandeur and present desolation, underscoring themes of time, loss, and decay.

5. Montepulciano d’Abruzzo - ‘Under the Tuscan Sun’ by Frances Mayes:
In Frances Mayes’s memoir, ‘Under the Tuscan Sun,’ she takes readers on her journey of renovating an old villa in Tuscany. As Mayes delves into the idyllic Italian way of life, the wine that consistently accompanies her meals is Montepulciano d’Abruzzo. This red wine from the Abruzzo region symbolizes the rustic charm and simple pleasures of Italy, serving as a backdrop to Mayes’s personal transformation and her deepening connection to the land.

6. Amarone - ‘The Shadow of the Wind’ by Carlos Ruiz Zafón:
Carlos Ruiz Zafón’s captivating novel, ‘The Shadow of the Wind,’ unfolds in the labyrinthine streets of post-war Barcelona. Among the shadows and secrets lies the Cemetery of Forgotten books, where a young protagonist ventures to uncover the mysteries of an elusive author. In this atmospheric novel, the intense and complex Amarone wine acts as an emblem of the hidden layers and intoxicating allure of the city and its stories.

Throughout literature, wines are used to convey a wide range of emotions, from joy and celebration to despair and longing. These iconic wines become more than just beverages; they become symbolic elements that help shape the overall narrative and deepen our understanding of the characters and their experiences.

Whether it’s the opulence of the Roaring Twenties captured in a glass of Château Lafite Rothschild or the timeless allure of Tuscany in a sip of Montepulciano d’Abruzzo, wine has a way of transporting us to different times and places. So, the next time you crack open a bottle of your favorite wine, remember the integral role it has played in literature and how it continues to inspire readers and writers alike.

Savor the stories, savor the wines. Cheers!
