The Best Fishing Books Every Angler Should Read


When it comes to fishing, there’s no better way to broaden your knowledge and enhance your angling skills than by delving into the world of fishing books. Whether you’re a novice angler looking to learn the basics or an experienced fisherman seeking advanced techniques, the right fishing book can provide you with valuable insights, tips, and inspiration. In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the best fishing books every angler should read, covering a range of topics from fly fishing to bass fishing and everything in between!

1. ‘The Complete Angler’ by Izaak Walton: Considered a classic in the field of fishing literature, this book has stood the test of time since its publication in 1653. Walton combines practical advice on angling techniques with philosophical musings, making it a delightful read for any angler.

2. ‘A River Runs Through It’ by Norman Maclean: This semi-autobiographical novella beautifully captures the author’s love for fly fishing and the natural world. It’s a must-read for those who appreciate the artistry and spirituality of fly fishing.

3. ‘The Orvis Fly-Fishing Guide’ by Tom Rosenbauer: Orvis is a renowned name in the fly fishing community, and this guidebook reflects the brand’s expertise. From equipment selection to casting techniques, this comprehensive guide covers it all.

4. ‘In the Company of Rivers’ by Robert DeMott: A collection of essays about trout fishing and the joy of being in nature, this book is a lyrical exploration of the author’s experiences on various rivers. It’s a great read for those who want to immerse themselves in the beauty of the sport.

5. ‘Bass Fishing for Beginners’ by Peter Kaminsky: If you’re new to bass fishing and want to get started on the right foot, this book is an excellent resource. Kaminsky breaks down the fundamentals of bass fishing, including different lures, tackle, and strategies to help you land that trophy bass.

6. ‘The Total Fishing Manual’ by Joe Cermele and the Editors of Field & Stream: As the name suggests, this comprehensive guide covers a wide range of fishing techniques and species. From freshwater to saltwater, from basic to advanced, this book has it all, making it a must-have addition to any angler’s library.

7. ‘Sylvester Nemes’ Soft Hackled Fly Addict’ by Sylvester Nemes: This book focuses on the art of soft-hackled fly fishing and provides detailed instructions for tying and fishing these versatile flies. If you’re an avid fly fisherman looking to expand your arsenal, this book is a valuable resource.

8. ‘The Fish That Changed America’ by Steve L. Robbins: This book delves into the historical impact of the introduction of non-native fish species in American waters. It’s a fascinating read that sheds light on the environmental and ecological consequences of human intervention in natural habitats.

9. ‘The Four Season Angler’ by Ray Bergman: This book provides a comprehensive overview of the different fishing techniques and strategies to be employed in each season. With detailed information on fishing during winter, spring, summer, and fall, this book is an invaluable resource for anglers who enjoy fishing year-round.

10. ‘Trout Bum’ by John Gierach: This collection of essays is not just about fishing; it’s about the lifestyle and camaraderie that comes with being a passionate angler. Gierach’s witty and insightful observations will resonate with anyone who has experienced the joy of being a ’trout bum.’

In conclusion, these top ten fishing books cover a range of topics and interests, catering to anglers of all levels of experience. Whether you’re looking for practical advice, inspiration, or simply a good read that transports you to the water’s edge, these books are sure to satisfy your angling cravings. So grab a book, find a cozy spot, and immerse yourself in the wonderful world of fishing literature!
