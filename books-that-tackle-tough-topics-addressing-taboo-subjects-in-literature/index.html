Books That Tackle Tough Topics: Addressing Taboo Subjects in Literature


In today’s world, literature has the power to challenge societal norms and shed light on topics that are often considered taboo. Books that tackle tough subjects have the ability to spark important conversations, promote empathy, and encourage understanding. In this blog post, we will explore a selection of thought-provoking books that fearlessly delve into taboo subjects, pushing the boundaries of what is considered acceptable in literature.

1. ‘Lolita’ by Vladimir Nabokov:
Considered one of the most controversial novels of all time, ‘Lolita’ explores the disturbing relationship between Humbert Humbert, a middle-aged literature professor, and Dolores Haze, a twelve-year-old girl. Nabokov’s masterful prose and intricate storytelling challenge readers to confront the uncomfortable reality of pedophilia. This book raises important questions about power dynamics, morality, and the nature of desire.

2. ‘American Psycho’ by Bret Easton Ellis:
‘American Psycho’ takes readers on a dark and disturbing journey into the mind of Patrick Bateman, a wealthy investment banker and serial killer. Ellis’s unflinching portrayal of violence, explicit sexuality, and materialism forces readers to confront the dark underbelly of American society. This book explores themes of consumerism, identity, and the masks we wear in the pursuit of success.

3. ‘The Color Purple’ by Alice Walker:
Alice Walker’s Pulitzer Prize-winning novel, ‘The Color Purple,’ tackles several taboo subjects, including racism, sexism, and incest. Set in the early 20th century, the book follows the life of Celie, a young African-American woman who endures years of abuse and oppression. Through Celie’s journey of self-discovery and resilience, Walker explores themes of love, forgiveness, and the power of female relationships.

4. ‘Beloved’ by Toni Morrison:
Toni Morrison’s haunting novel, ‘Beloved,’ confronts the horrors of slavery and its lasting impact on African-American communities. The story revolves around Sethe, a former slave who escaped to Ohio but is haunted by the memories of her past. Morrison’s lyrical prose and supernatural elements create a powerful narrative that explores the psychological trauma of slavery and the search for freedom and identity.

5. ‘The Handmaid’s Tale’ by Margaret Atwood:
‘The Handmaid’s Tale’ presents a dystopian society in which women are subjugated and reduced to reproductive vessels. Margaret Atwood’s chilling portrayal of a future where religious fundamentalism and patriarchy reign supreme forces readers to confront issues of gender inequality, reproductive rights, and the erosion of individual freedoms. This book serves as a stark warning against complacency and the dangers of oppressive regimes.

6. ‘Speak’ by Laurie Halse Anderson:
Laurie Halse Anderson’s ‘Speak’ tackles the difficult subject of sexual assault and its impact on a teenager’s life. The story follows Melinda, a high school freshman who becomes isolated and withdrawn after being raped at a party. Through Melinda’s journey of self-expression and healing, Anderson sheds light on the importance of speaking up, breaking the silence, and supporting survivors of sexual assault.

These books represent just a fraction of the literature that fearlessly addresses taboo subjects. They challenge our preconceived notions, encourage empathy, and provide a platform for important discussions. By reading and engaging with these thought-provoking works, we can gain a deeper understanding of the complexities of the human experience and work towards a more inclusive and compassionate society.
