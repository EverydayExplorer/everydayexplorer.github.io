Tips for Finding a Hotel with Accessible Facilities


For individuals with disabilities, finding a hotel with accessible facilities can be a challenge. While the travel industry has made great strides in recent years to accommodate the needs of disabled travelers, there is still work to be done. In this blog post, we will provide you with some tips to help you find a hotel that meets your accessibility requirements.

1. Research, research, research: Before booking a hotel, it is crucial to do your homework. Look for hotels with detailed information about their accessible facilities on their website. This should include information about accessible rooms, bathrooms, entrances, parking, and common areas. Additionally, check for online reviews or forums where other disabled travelers share their experiences and recommendations.

2. Contact the hotel directly: Once you have identified a potential hotel, contact them directly to inquire about their accessible facilities. This will allow you to get a clear understanding of what is available and if it meets your specific needs. Ask about the width of doorways, the height of beds, the presence of grab bars in the bathroom, and any other specific requirements you may have.

3. Consult accessibility rating websites: There are several websites that specialize in rating hotels based on their accessibility. These websites provide detailed information about the accessibility features available in various hotels, including photos and user reviews. Some popular accessibility rating websites include AccessibleGO and DisabledGo.

4. Look for hotels with industry certifications: Many hotels strive to meet accessibility standards set by industry organizations. Look for hotels that have received certifications such as the ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act) compliance or accessibility ratings provided by organizations like the International Association of Accessibility Professionals (IAAP). These certifications can give you peace of mind that the hotel has made efforts to be inclusive and accommodating.

5. Consider location: When searching for a hotel, consider its location in relation to the activities and attractions you plan to visit. Ensuring the hotel is located near accessible transportation options, restaurants, and other amenities is essential for a comfortable and convenient stay. Additionally, check if the hotel offers accessible transportation services or is in close proximity to accessible public transportation.

6. Inquire about additional services: Some hotels offer additional services to meet the needs of disabled guests. For instance, they may provide wheelchair rentals, offer assistance with luggage, or have staff trained in sign language. Inquire about these services when contacting the hotel to ensure they can accommodate your specific needs.

7. Read the fine print: Before making a reservation, carefully review the hotel’s cancellation and refund policies. Unexpected changes in plans can occur, and it’s important to know that you have flexibility in case you need to modify or cancel your booking.

8. Be an advocate: If you encounter any accessibility issues during your stay, don’t hesitate to speak up. Provide feedback to the hotel management, whether it be positive or constructive, to help them improve their accessibility services for future guests.

Finding a hotel with accessible facilities may require some extra effort, but it is crucial for a comfortable and enjoyable travel experience. By following these tips, you can increase your chances of finding a hotel that meets your accessibility requirements. Remember to conduct thorough research, contact the hotel directly, consult accessibility rating websites, look for certifications, consider the location, inquire about additional services, read the fine print, and be an advocate for yourself and others. Happy travels!
