DIY Storage Solutions for Crafters: Organize Your Supplies


Are you tired of digging through a pile of craft supplies every time you want to start a project? Do you find yourself struggling to find the right materials when you need them? If so, it’s time to get your craft supplies organized. Having an organized storage solution for your craft supplies not only makes it easier to find what you need but also inspires creativity by displaying your materials in a visually appealing way.

In this blog post, we will explore various DIY storage solutions that will help you organize your craft supplies efficiently. Whether you have a dedicated craft room or a small corner in your home, these ideas will work for any space.

1. Pegboard Wall

A pegboard wall is an excellent storage solution for crafters. Not only does it help keep your supplies organized, but it also adds a decorative touch to your craft room. Simply mount a pegboard on the wall and use hooks to hang your tools, scissors, and spools of thread. You can also attach small baskets or shelves to hold your paints, brushes, and other supplies.

2. Mason Jar Storage

If you have small craft supplies like beads, buttons, or sequins, storing them in mason jars is a great idea. Not only are they affordable and easily accessible, but they also give your craft space a stylish, rustic look. Label each jar with the content inside for easy identification.

3. Drawer Dividers

Utilizing drawer dividers is a simple and effective way to organize your craft supplies. You can use them to separate different types of materials, such as ribbons, tapes, or papers. This not only keeps things tidy but also makes it easier to find what you need. Customize the dividers according to the size of your drawers to maximize space.

4. Wall-Mounted Shelves

Another great storage solution for crafters is wall-mounted shelves. They provide easy access to your supplies while keeping your workspace clutter-free. You can use these shelves to store books, magazines, fabric, or any other items. Consider adding decorative boxes or baskets to store smaller items and keep everything organized.

5. PVC Pipe Storage

If you have a collection of wrapping papers or rolls of fabric, consider using PVC pipes for storage. Cut the pipes into shorter sections, attach them to a pegboard or wall-mounted brackets, and insert the rolls. This not only keeps your materials organized but also prevents them from getting damaged or wrinkled.

6. Hanging Shoe Organizers

Hanging shoe organizers are not just for shoes! They can be a fantastic storage solution for crafters, especially for smaller items like yarn, embroidery floss, or paintbrushes. Hang the organizer on the back of a door or on a wall and utilize the pockets to neatly store your supplies. This allows for easy visibility and accessibility while keeping everything in its place.

7. Recycled Jars and Containers

Don’t throw away empty jars or containers; repurpose them to store your craft supplies. From peanut butter jars to coffee canisters, glass or plastic containers can be labeled and used to hold buttons, beads, or other small items. This is a cost-effective and environmentally friendly storage solution that adds a unique touch to your craft space.

8. Tackle Box Organizer

A tackle box is not just for fishing! Consider using a tackle box to store your smaller craft supplies like needles, threads, or beads. The multiple compartments make it easy to separate and categorize your materials, ensuring they are readily accessible whenever you need them.

9. Magnetic Strip

If you have a collection of metal tools like scissors or rulers, a magnetic strip is a must-have in your craft space. Mount it on the wall and attach your metal tools to it. This keeps them within reach and eliminates the need to rummage through drawers or containers to find what you need.

10. Paper Towel Holder Ribbon Organizer

Do you have a collection of ribbons that always seem to get tangled? Consider using a paper towel holder to keep them neatly organized. Simply slide the rolls onto the holder and pull out the desired ribbon when needed. This ensures your ribbons are easily accessible and free from tangles.

With these DIY storage solutions, you can now enjoy a clutter-free craft space and easily find your supplies whenever inspiration strikes. Remember, organizing your craft supplies not only saves you time but also enhances your crafting experience. So, start organizing today and unleash your creativity like never before!
