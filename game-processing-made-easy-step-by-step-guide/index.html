Game Processing Made Easy: Step-by-Step Guide


There’s nothing quite like the thrill of hunting down your own game and enjoying the fruits of your labor. Whether it’s a deer, elk, or even a wild turkey, the feeling of providing for yourself and your family is hard to beat. However, for many beginners or even seasoned hunters, the process of game processing can be quite intimidating. But fear not, because we have put together a detailed step-by-step guide to make game processing easy for you.

Step 1: Field Dressing
Once you’ve successfully hunted your game, the first step is field dressing. Field dressing is the process of removing the internal organs of the animal to prevent spoilage and make further processing easier. Here are the basic steps to perform field dressing:

  1. Make a shallow incision around the anus and continue it forward toward the sternum.

  2. Cut through the skin and underlying tissues along the belly, exposing the intestines.

  3. Insert two fingers beneath the diaphragm and cut all the way to the sternum, being careful not to puncture any organs.

  4. Remove the entrails and save the heart, liver, and kidneys if desired.

  5. Rinse the cavity with clean water and pat it dry.

Step 2: Skinning
Now that the animal is field dressed, it’s time to skin it. Skinning is the process of removing the hide or fur from the carcass. Here’s how you can do it effectively:

  1. Find a sturdy branch or use a skinning pole to hang the animal.

  2. Starting at the hind legs, use a sharp knife to make an incision from the knee to the anus.

  3. Carefully peel the hide away from the body using your hands, working your way up to the front legs and neck.

  4. Use your knife to separate the hide from the carcass, being cautious not to cut into the meat.

  5. Once the hide is completely removed, trim away any excess fat or connective tissue.

Step 3: Quartering and Deboning
Quartering and deboning the animal is an essential step in game processing as it allows for easier transportation and more manageable cuts of meat. Follow these steps to effectively quarter and debone your game:

  1. Begin by removing the animal’s front legs at the shoulder joints and hind legs at the hip joints.

  2. Separate the spine from the body by cutting along each side of the backbone from the neck to the tail.

  3. Divide the carcass into manageable sections by cutting between the ribs.

  4. Once quartered, begin deboning each piece by carefully removing the meat from the bones using a sharp knife.

  5. Trim away any excess fat or silver skin, and cut the meat into desired cuts such as steaks, roasts, or ground meat.

Step 4: Packaging and Freezing
Now that you have your game processed into manageable cuts of meat, it’s important to properly package and freeze them to maintain their freshness and flavor. Follow these guidelines for packaging and freezing:

  1. Use freezer-safe, airtight packaging such as vacuum-sealed bags or heavy-duty freezer paper.

  2. Label each package with the type of meat, cut, and date of processing.

  3. Remove as much air as possible before sealing to prevent freezer burn.

  4. Store the packaged meat in a freezer set to 0°F or below for optimal preservation.

By following these step-by-step instructions, game processing can be made easier and less daunting. However, keep in mind that proper game processing requires some practice and patience. Don’t get discouraged if you make mistakes along the way – learning from each experience will only make you better.

Remember, it’s always important to follow local regulations and hunting guidelines when processing game. Additionally, it’s recommended to seek guidance from experienced hunters or professionals to ensure you’re handling and processing your game in the safest and most efficient manner.

So, whether you’re a seasoned hunter looking to refine your skills or a beginner embarking on your first hunting expedition, this step-by-step guide will help make game processing a rewarding and enjoyable experience. Happy hunting and bon appétit!
