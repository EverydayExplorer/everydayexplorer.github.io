10 Metrics to Track in Google Search Console for Blog SEO


If you are a blogger, you know how important it is to have your content discovered by the right audience. This is where search engine optimization (SEO) comes into play. Google Search Console, a free tool offered by Google, provides valuable insights into your website’s performance in search results. By tracking key metrics in Google Search Console, you can optimize your blog and improve its visibility. In this article, we will discuss the top 10 metrics you should track in Google Search Console for effective blog SEO.

1. Impressions

Impressions refer to the number of times your blog post appeared in search results. Tracking impressions can help you measure your blog’s visibility and understand the reach of your content.

2. Click-through Rate (CTR)

CTR is the percentage of users who clicked on your blog’s link after it appeared in search results. A high CTR indicates that your title and meta description are compelling and relevant to users’ search queries. By monitoring CTR, you can optimize your blog’s title tags and meta descriptions to attract more clicks.

3. Average Position

Average position shows where your blog post ranks in search results for specific keywords. Monitoring this metric allows you to track how well your blog is performing in terms of search ranking. Lower average positions indicate higher visibility and better SEO performance.

4. Queries

Google Search Console provides insight into the specific search queries that are driving traffic to your blog. By analyzing these queries, you can identify keywords that are performing well and optimize your content accordingly. Additionally, you can discover new keyword opportunities that can help you target a wider audience.

5. Clicks

Clicks represent the number of times users clicked on your blog’s link in search results. By tracking clicks, you can measure the effectiveness of your content in generating organic traffic. Increasing the number of clicks can lead to higher conversion rates and more engaged users.

6. Pages Indexed

This metric shows the number of blog pages that are indexed by Google. Monitoring this metric ensures that your content is being crawled and included in search results. If you notice a discrepancy between the number of indexed pages and the number of pages on your blog, it could indicate potential indexing issues that you need to address.

7. Mobile Usability

In today’s mobile-first world, ensuring that your blog is mobile-friendly is essential. Google Search Console provides a mobile usability report that highlights any issues that may affect the user experience on mobile devices. By addressing these issues, you can improve your blog’s SEO performance on mobile devices.

8. Internal Links

Internal linking plays a crucial role in SEO. Monitoring the internal links metric in Google Search Console can help you track the number of internal links leading to each blog post. By optimizing internal linking, you can distribute link equity throughout your blog and improve its overall SEO performance.

9. External Links

External links are links from other websites pointing to your blog. Tracking this metric allows you to monitor the number of external backlinks your blog receives. High-quality backlinks can boost your blog’s credibility and organic search visibility. By analyzing this metric, you can identify opportunities to build relationships with other websites and attract more external links.

10. Index Coverage

The index coverage report in Google Search Console provides information about indexing issues encountered by Google when crawling your blog. By monitoring index coverage, you can identify any pages that are not being indexed or are experiencing indexing errors. Addressing these issues can ensure that your content is fully indexed and visible in search results.

In conclusion, Google Search Console is a powerful tool for monitoring and improving your blog’s SEO performance. By tracking these 10 metrics, you can gain valuable insights into your blog’s visibility, ranking, and organic traffic. By optimizing your blog according to these metrics, you can attract more engaged users, improve conversions, and ultimately grow your blog’s success.
