Hunting Optics Buying Guide: What to Consider


When it comes to hunting, having the right optics is essential. Whether you’re a beginner or a seasoned hunter, having a clear view of your target can make all the difference. With so many options available on the market, choosing the right hunting optics can be overwhelming. In this comprehensive buying guide, we’ll walk you through the key factors to consider when purchasing hunting optics.

1. Magnification and Objective Lens Size:
One of the first things to consider when buying hunting optics is the magnification and objective lens size. The magnification determines how close you can see your target, while the objective lens size influences the amount of light that enters the scope. It’s important to strike a balance between magnification and lens size to ensure a clear and bright image.

2. Reticle Type:
The reticle, also known as the crosshair, plays a crucial role in aiming and targeting. There are various reticle types available, including duplex, mil-dot, and BDC (bullet drop compensator). Each reticle type has its advantages, so it’s important to choose one that suits your hunting style and preferences.

3. Optic Coating:
Optic coatings are designed to enhance light transmission and reduce glare. When selecting hunting optics, look for lenses with multiple coatings, such as fully multi-coated or multi-coated. These coatings improve image clarity, brightness, and color fidelity, allowing you to spot your target even in low-light conditions.

4. Durability and Weather Resistance:
Hunting often takes place in rugged environments and adverse weather conditions. Therefore, it’s crucial to choose hunting optics that are built to withstand these challenges. Look for optics that are shockproof, fog-proof, and waterproof. Optics with a sturdy construction and nitrogen or argon purging will ensure reliable performance in any hunting scenario.

5. Eye Relief and Field of View:
Eye relief refers to the distance between your eye and the scope. It’s important to choose optics with sufficient eye relief to prevent eye strain and ensure a comfortable shooting experience. Additionally, consider the field of view, which determines how much you can see through the optics at a given distance. A wider field of view allows for better target acquisition and tracking.

6. Price Range:
Hunting optics come in a wide range of prices, from budget-friendly options to high-end models. Set a budget based on your needs and stick to it. Remember that while high-end optics may offer superior features, there are also excellent mid-range options that provide great performance without breaking the bank.

7. User Reviews and Recommendations:
Before making a final decision, it’s always a good idea to read user reviews and seek recommendations from fellow hunters or experts. Their experiences and insights can provide valuable information about the performance, durability, and reliability of different hunting optics.

In conclusion, choosing the right hunting optics is crucial for a successful hunting experience. Consider factors such as magnification, objective lens size, reticle type, optic coating, durability, eye relief, field of view, and price range. By carefully evaluating these factors and conducting thorough research, you’ll be able to make an informed decision and find hunting optics that meet your specific needs. Happy hunting!
