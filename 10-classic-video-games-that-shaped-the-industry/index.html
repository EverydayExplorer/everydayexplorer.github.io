10 Classic Video Games That Shaped the Industry


Video games have come a long way since their inception in the 1970s. They have evolved from simple, pixelated graphics and limited gameplay mechanics to stunning, immersive experiences with rich narratives and lifelike visuals. As the industry continues to grow and captivate millions of players worldwide, it’s important to reflect on the games that have helped shape it into what it is today. In this blog post, we will explore 10 classic video games that have had a significant impact on the industry.

1. Super Mario Bros. (1985)
Released for the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES), Super Mario Bros. is widely regarded as one of the most influential video games of all time. It introduced players to Mario, the mustachioed plumber, and his quest to rescue Princess Peach from the villainous Bowser. The game’s tight controls, clever level design, and memorable soundtrack set the standard for platformers and laid the foundation for future Mario games.

2. The Legend of Zelda (1986)
Another iconic NES game, The Legend of Zelda revolutionized the action-adventure genre. Players took on the role of Link, a hero tasked with saving the mystical land of Hyrule and rescuing Princess Zelda. The game’s sprawling world, non-linear gameplay, and innovative dungeon design introduced players to a new level of exploration and puzzle-solving.

3. Super Metroid (1994)
Considered a masterpiece by many, Super Metroid for the Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES) pushed the boundaries of what a 2D platformer could be. It introduced players to the open-ended exploration of the alien planet Zebes as bounty hunter Samus Aran, with its atmospheric environments, tight gameplay, and memorable boss battles leaving a lasting impact on the industry.

4. Doom (1993)
Doom changed the face of first-person shooters forever. Developed by id Software, this game popularized the genre and introduced the concept of multiplayer deathmatches. With its fast-paced action, intense gunfights, and innovative level design, Doom set the stage for the countless first-person shooters that followed.

5. Final Fantasy VII (1997)
Final Fantasy VII, released for the PlayStation, brought the role-playing genre to new heights. With its immersive storytelling, complex characters, and stunning visuals, this game captured the hearts of millions of players worldwide. It showcased the potential for storytelling in video games and solidified Final Fantasy as one of the most beloved game franchises.

6. Half-Life (1998)
Half-Life, developed by Valve Corporation, revolutionized narrative-driven first-person shooters. It introduced players to the silent protagonist Gordon Freeman, who must battle through an alien-infested research facility. Half-Life’s immersive storytelling, realistic physics, and innovative gameplay mechanics set a new standard for the genre and paved the way for subsequent narrative-driven games.

7. The Sims (2000)
The Sims, created by game designer Will Wright, brought the concept of virtual life simulation to the mainstream. This game allowed players to create and control virtual characters, known as Sims, and guide them through everyday activities. The Sims’ open-ended gameplay, along with its focus on creativity and player agency, made it a groundbreaking title that captivated a broad audience.

8. World of Warcraft (2004)
World of Warcraft (WoW) is widely regarded as one of the most successful MMORPGs (Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Games) of all time. Developed by Blizzard Entertainment, WoW introduced millions of players to the vast, immersive world of Azeroth. Its addictive gameplay, social interactions, and constant content updates made it a cultural phenomenon and set the bar for future MMOs.

9. Minecraft (2011)
Minecraft, created by Markus Persson, is a sandbox game that allows players to build and explore virtual worlds made up of blocks. With its limitless creativity and player-driven gameplay, Minecraft became a worldwide sensation. It inspired countless players to create their own virtual masterpieces and laid the foundation for the popularity of open-world survival games.

10. The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt (2015)
The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, developed by CD Projekt Red, is often hailed as one of the greatest role-playing games ever made. With its sprawling open world, engaging storyline, and complex characters, this game showcased the potential for mature storytelling in video games. The Witcher 3’s critical and commercial success solidified CD Projekt Red’s reputation as a top-tier game developer.

These ten classic video games have had a profound impact on the industry, pushing boundaries, setting new standards, and captivating players for decades. From defining genres to showcasing the potential for storytelling and player agency, they have left an indelible mark and continue to inspire game developers and players alike. As the industry evolves, it’s important to acknowledge and celebrate the games that have shaped it into the thriving, diverse medium that it is today.
