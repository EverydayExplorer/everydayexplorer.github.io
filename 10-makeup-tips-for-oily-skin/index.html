10 Makeup Tips for Oily Skin


If you have oily skin, you know the struggle of trying to keep your makeup in place all day. Dealing with excess shine, makeup melting off, and constant touch-ups can be frustrating. But fear not, because we’ve got you covered! In this blog post, we will share 10 makeup tips specifically tailored for those with oily skin. So, let’s dive right in!

1. Prep your skin

Before applying any makeup, it’s crucial to prepare your skin properly. Start by cleansing your face with a gentle cleanser that is specifically formulated for oily skin. This will help remove excess oil and dirt, creating a clean canvas for your makeup.

2. Use a mattifying primer

A mattifying primer is your best friend when it comes to combating oily skin. Look for a primer that is oil-free and specifically designed to control shine. Applying a mattifying primer before your foundation will create a smooth base and help your makeup last longer.

3. Choose an oil-free foundation

When selecting a foundation, opt for oil-free formulas that are labeled as “matte” or “oil-control.” These foundations are specially formulated to absorb excess oil and provide a long-lasting matte finish. Apply the foundation with a brush or sponge for an even application.

4. Set your foundation with powder

To lock in your foundation and prevent it from sliding off your face, set it with a translucent powder. Use a large fluffy brush to apply the powder lightly all over your face, paying extra attention to your T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin), where oil tends to accumulate.

5. Blot, don’t wipe

Instead of reaching for a tissue or wipe to remove excess oil throughout the day, use blotting papers. Blotting papers are designed to absorb oil without disturbing your makeup. Press the blotting paper gently onto your skin, focusing on areas where shine is most prominent.

6. Opt for powder-based products

When it comes to choosing makeup products, go for powder-based options rather than cream or liquid formulas. Powder blushes, eyeshadows, and bronzers will help absorb excess oil and keep your makeup intact for longer periods.

7. Avoid heavy moisturizers

While moisturizing is essential for all skin types, those with oily skin should opt for lightweight, oil-free moisturizers. Heavy moisturizers can clog your pores and lead to more oil production. Look for gel-based or water-based moisturizers that provide hydration without adding extra shine.

8. Use a setting spray

After you’ve completed your makeup application, set it with a setting spray. Setting sprays are designed to lock in your makeup and prevent it from smudging or melting off. Look for a setting spray that is oil-free and has mattifying properties for the best results.

9. Don’t forget to touch up

Throughout the day, you may still experience some shine, especially in your T-zone. Instead of adding more layers of makeup, use a translucent powder or blotting paper to touch up and absorb excess oil. This will help keep your makeup looking fresh and matte.

10. Practice good skincare habits

Lastly, taking care of your skin is essential for managing oily skin. Cleanse your face twice a day, exfoliate regularly to remove dead skin cells, and use oil-free products in your skincare routine. By maintaining a consistent skincare regimen, you can improve the overall condition of your skin and reduce excess oil production.

In conclusion, having oily skin doesn’t mean you can’t achieve a flawless makeup look. By following these 10 makeup tips, you can keep shine at bay and ensure your makeup stays put all day long. Remember, preparation, choosing the right products, and regular touch-ups are key to managing oily skin and achieving a matte finish that lasts. Now go ahead and rock that flawless makeup look with confidence!
