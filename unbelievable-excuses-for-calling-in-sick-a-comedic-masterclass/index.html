Unbelievable Excuses for Calling in Sick: A Comedic Masterclass


Calling in sick to work can sometimes feel like an art form, especially when the reasons behind it stretch the limits of believability and veer into comedic territory. Everyone appreciates a day off, but the excuses some people concoct are truly masterful in their creativity and audacity. Whether you’re in need of a good laugh or some wildly imaginative reasons to stay home (not that we endorse such behavior), here’s a collection of unbelievable excuses that take calling in sick to a whole new level.

1. Alien Abduction: Believe it or not, someone actually used the excuse of being abducted by aliens to avoid going to work. They claimed that they were unexpectedly taken during the night and didn’t return until early morning. They even went so far as to provide a detailed account of their interstellar journey, describing the aliens and their spacecraft. Perhaps the only thing more outlandish than the excuse was the fact that they expected their boss to believe it!

2. The Stubborn, Psychic Octopus: One employee called in sick claiming that a psychic octopus told them to stay at home because they saw a vision of a terrible traffic accident on the employee’s route to work. The octopus, allegedly a resident of the local aquarium visited the day before, apparently had a track record of predicting sports results accurately. While creative, this excuse might work better in a fantasy novel than in the real world.

3. Their Fake Dog Ran Away: In a twist on the classic “my dog ate my homework,” one individual told their employer that they couldn’t come to work because their dog had run away. However, the plot thickened when the employer remembered the employee had previously mentioned they didn’t own a dog. The next day, the employee miraculously found their non-existent dog and brought in photos of a random dog from the internet to prove it.

4. The 24-Hour Breakup Recovery Program: Breaking up is hard to do, but calling in sick because you’ve scheduled a 24-hour breakup recovery program might be pushing it. This inventive employee claimed they had enrolled in a one-day intensive course designed to heal broken hearts through meditation, ice cream, and sappy movies. Emotional health is important, but the employer was left wondering if such a program even exists.

5. They Were Too Smart to Work: One person called in sick claiming that their incredible intelligence had suddenly spiked overnight, leaving them so intellectually enhanced that they found it impossible to perform mundane work tasks without diminishing their mental capacity. They insisted they needed the day to explore their new intellectual gifts and ponder the mysteries of the universe.

6. Lottery Winner Woes: An employee once claimed they couldn’t come to work because they had won the lottery and were too overwhelmed with financial planning and potential investment strategies. The twist? They hadn’t actually won yet, but felt so lucky that day that they were certain they would win and needed to prepare themselves mentally and financially.

7. Possessed by Spirits: A particularly bold excuse came from an employee who claimed they were possessed by spirits and had to find an exorcist before they could return to work. Not only did this take the cake for creativity, but it also left the employer baffled and unsure how to respond.

8. Accidental Trip to a Different City: One employee phoned in sick after they ‘accidentally’ boarded a plane to a different city. According to them, they were supposed to go to the airport to pick up a friend, but they got mixed up and boarded a flight instead. The day was spent trying to navigate their way back home.

While these excuses are hilarious and imaginative, they are also a reminder of the importance of honesty in the workplace. Regularly calling in sick with outlandish excuses can lead to a loss of trust and even job termination. It’s always best to keep it real with your employer, even if the truth is as simple as needing a mental health day. After all, everyone needs a genuine break now and then, and a straightforward approach will always be more effective than claiming an alien abduction or a psychic octopus intervention.
