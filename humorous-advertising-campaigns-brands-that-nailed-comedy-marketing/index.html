Humorous Advertising Campaigns: Brands That Nailed Comedy Marketing


In the highly competitive world of advertising, it can be challenging for brands to stand out and grab the attention of consumers. However, some brands have successfully used humor as a powerful tool in their marketing campaigns to create memorable and engaging content. These brands not only entertain their audience but also create a strong brand association. In this blog, we will explore some of the brands that have effectively used comedy marketing to captivate their customers and boost their sales.

  1. Old Spice - The Man Your Man Could Smell Like Old Spice, the iconic men’s grooming brand, took a bold and hilarious approach to their advertising campaign in 2010. They introduced ‘The Man Your Man Could Smell Like’ - a character played by the charismatic actor Isaiah Mustafa. The campaign consisted of a series of short, fast-paced commercials that showcased the benefits of Old Spice products in a humorous and outlandish way. The ads went viral, generating millions of views on YouTube and creating a massive buzz on social media. The success of this campaign helped Old Spice transform its image and reach a younger, more diverse audience.

  2. Dollar Shave Club - Our Blades Are F**ing Great! Dollar Shave Club, a subscription-based razor blade company, made a splash in the industry with their hilarious and irreverent ad campaign in 2012. The brand’s founder, Michael Dubin, starred in the company’s first video, titled ‘Our Blades Are F**ing Great!’ The video quickly went viral, showcasing Dubin’s comedic talent and effectively communicating the company’s value proposition. The witty script, combined with Dubin’s deadpan delivery, resonated with consumers and helped Dollar Shave Club grow from a small start-up to a major competitor in the shaving industry.

  3. Geico - 15 Minutes Could Save You 15% or More on Car Insurance Geico, an auto insurance company, is widely known for its clever and humorous advertising campaigns. One of their most memorable slogans, ‘15 minutes could save you 15% or more on car insurance,’ has become a cultural phenomenon. Geico consistently uses humor to convey its message, featuring memorable characters like the Gecko, the Caveman, and the Hump Day camel. The brand’s witty and relatable commercials have helped establish Geico as a leading player in the insurance industry.

  4. Bud Light - Dilly Dilly Bud Light, the popular beer brand, created a humorous and highly successful advertising campaign centered around the catchphrase ‘Dilly Dilly.’ The campaign featured a medieval-themed world with characters like the King and the Bud Knight. The commercials were filled with witty banter and comedic situations, capturing the attention of consumers and making ‘Dilly Dilly’ a cultural phenomenon. Bud Light effectively used humor to position itself as a fun and light-hearted choice for beer drinkers.

  5. Snickers - You’re Not You When You’re Hungry Snickers, the popular chocolate bar, launched a clever advertising campaign called ‘You’re Not You When You’re Hungry’ in 2010. The campaign featured a series of commercials where people exhibited strange behavior due to hunger and transformed back to their normal selves after eating a Snickers bar. The ads featured celebrities like Betty White and Danny Trejo, adding to their comedic appeal. Snickers’ humorous approach not only entertained viewers but also effectively communicated the brand’s message of satisfying hunger.

These are just a few examples of brands that have successfully used humor in their advertising campaigns to connect with consumers and drive sales. By injecting comedy into their marketing strategies, these brands were able to create memorable content that resonated with their target audience. Humor has the power to engage, entertain, and leave a lasting impression on consumers. If done right, it can be a game-changer for any brand looking to stand out in a crowded marketplace. So, the next time you’re working on a marketing campaign, don’t be afraid to add a dash of humor and see how it can transform your brand’s presence and success!
