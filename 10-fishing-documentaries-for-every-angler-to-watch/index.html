10 Fishing Documentaries for Every Angler to Watch


Fishing is not just a hobby; it’s a passion for many. Whether you’re an experienced angler or just starting with the sport, there’s always something new to learn. And what better way to gain inspiration, knowledge, and immerse yourself in the world of fishing than watching a fishing documentary?

Documentaries have the power to transport us to different corners of the earth, showcasing the beauty of nature and the art of angling. They capture the thrill of the catch, the challenges faced by fishermen, and the stories that come with each adventure. If you’re looking for some fishing documentaries to add to your watchlist, you’ve come to the right place. Here are ten must-watch documentaries for every angler:

1. ‘The Endless Summer’ (1966)
‘The Endless Summer’ is a classic in the genre of fishing documentaries. Directed by Bruce Brown, it follows the journey of surfers-turned-anglers chasing the perfect wave and the ultimate fishing spot around the world. This film captures the essence of fishing as a way of life and will leave you longing for your own endless summer.

2. ‘Rivers of a Lost Coast’ (2008)
This documentary takes you to the wild and rugged coastline of California, where salmon and steelhead fishing was once legendary. ‘Rivers of a Lost Coast’ explores the rise and fall of these fisheries and the impact it had on the local communities. It’s a poignant and beautiful documentary that will make you appreciate the importance of conservation.

3. ‘Chasing Silver’ (2004)
If you’re into fly fishing, ‘Chasing Silver’ is a must-watch. This documentary follows two friends as they embark on an epic journey to catch the elusive tarpon, also known as the ‘silver king.’ Shot in stunning locations like the Florida Keys and the Bahamas, this film showcases the passion and dedication required to chase after this iconic game fish.

4. ‘The Fisherman’s Son’ (2015)
‘The Fisherman’s Son’ is an inspiring documentary that tells the story of Ramón Navarro, a Chilean surfer and fisherman who dedicated his life to protecting his local wave and the ocean. This film combines breathtaking cinematography with an important message about the importance of environmental stewardship.

5. ‘Mako: The Jaws of Death’ (1975)
For those who enjoy the thrill of big game fishing, ‘Mako: The Jaws of Death’ is a thrilling documentary. It follows the adventures of a fisherman who forms an unlikely bond with a mako shark he names ‘Emma.’ This film challenges our perception of sharks and raises important questions about the ethics of sport fishing.

6. ‘Blue Planet II’ (2017)
While not solely focused on fishing, ‘Blue Planet II’ is a groundbreaking documentary series that explores the wonders of the ocean. From the mesmerizing behavior of marine creatures to the delicate balance of marine ecosystems, this series will leave you in awe of the beauty and complexity of our planet’s underwater world.

7. ‘Alaska: Silence & Solitude’ (2009)
This documentary highlights the beauty and solitude of fly fishing in remote locations of Alaska. It follows two anglers as they embark on a unique journey, navigating treacherous waters and battling extreme weather conditions. ‘Alaska: Silence & Solitude’ is a visual feast for the eyes and a reminder of the rewards that await those who are willing to explore the unknown.

8. ‘Leviathan’ (2012)
‘Leviathan’ is not your typical fishing documentary. This experimental film takes a unique approach to capturing the essence of commercial fishing in the North Atlantic. It uses immersive visuals and sound design to convey the harsh realities of life at sea and the interconnectedness of humans and the natural world.

9. ‘Artifishal’ (2019)
‘Artifishal’ is a thought-provoking documentary that explores the impact of hatcheries and fish farms on wild fish populations. It delves into the complexities of salmon farming and the unintended consequences it can have on the environment. This film will make you question the sustainability of the fishing industry and the choices we make as consumers.

10. ‘River Monsters’ (2009-2017)
No list of fishing documentaries would be complete without mentioning ‘River Monsters.’ Hosted by angler and biologist Jeremy Wade, this popular series takes viewers on a thrilling journey to uncover the truth behind legendary freshwater fish and the stories that surround them. Each episode is filled with suspense, education, and jaw-dropping encounters with some of the world’s most fearsome creatures.

So there you have it, ten fishing documentaries that every angler should watch. Whether you’re interested in the pursuit of trophy catches, the beauty of nature, or the conservation of our oceans, these documentaries will entertain, inspire, and educate. So grab your fishing gear, settle into your favorite chair, and prepare to be transported to a world of fishing adventures. Happy watching!
