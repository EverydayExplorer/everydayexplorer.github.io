The Best Games for Developing Strategy Skills


Games have always been a great source of entertainment and fun. But did you know that they can also be incredibly beneficial for developing strategy skills? Whether you’re a student looking to enhance your critical thinking abilities or a professional aiming to improve your decision-making prowess, strategy games offer a stimulating and enjoyable way to sharpen those skills. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best games for developing strategy skills and how they can help you become a strategic mastermind.

1. Chess: Let’s start with the classic game of chess. Known as the ‘king of board games,’ chess is a timeless strategy game that has been played for centuries. It requires players to think several moves ahead, anticipate their opponent’s moves, and develop a cohesive plan to outmaneuver their rival. Chess is not only an excellent game to enhance logical thinking and problem-solving abilities but also teaches patience, concentration, and foresight.

2. Civilization VI: If you’re interested in world history and want to test your strategic abilities at a grand scale, Civilization VI is the game for you. As the leader of a civilization, you are tasked with building and expanding your empire from scratch. You must make decisions on diplomacy, trade, research, and war, while considering factors like resources, infrastructure, and cultural development. Civilization VI challenges your ability to manage limited resources, adapt to changing circumstances, and optimize your strategic planning.

3. XCOM 2: XCOM 2 is a turn-based tactical game that puts you in command of an elite multinational military organization fighting against an alien invasion. The game requires careful planning, precise execution, and resource management. Each mission presents unique challenges, forcing you to think strategically about squad composition, positioning, and risk assessment. XCOM 2 improves your decision-making skills under pressure and enhances your ability to adapt to unexpected situations.

4. Go: Originating from ancient China, Go is one of the oldest strategy games in existence. Despite its deceptively simple rules, Go offers an incredibly complex and deep gameplay experience. Players take turns placing stones on a grid, aiming to surround and capture their opponent’s territory. Go teaches patience, spatial awareness, and the ability to balance offense and defense. It challenges you to think strategically about long-term implications and develop a flexible approach to achieve victory.

5. Starcraft II: Regarded as one of the most competitive real-time strategy games, Starcraft II demands fast thinking, multitasking, and efficient decision-making. In this futuristic war game, players control one of three unique factions and must build bases, gather resources, research technologies, and deploy armies to outmaneuver and defeat their opponents. Starcraft II trains your ability to manage limited resources, make split-second decisions, and adapt your strategy on the fly.

6. Dota 2: Dota 2 is a popular multiplayer online battle arena game that not only requires strategic thinking but also emphasizes teamwork and coordination. You join a team of five players, each controlling a powerful hero with unique abilities, and work together to destroy the enemy’s base. Dota 2 hones your ability to analyze complex situations, communicate effectively, and make strategic decisions within a team dynamic.

By immersing yourself in these strategy games, you can enhance various skills that are crucial in both personal and professional settings. From critical thinking and problem-solving to adaptation and teamwork, these games provide a challenging yet enjoyable platform for you to exercise your strategic muscles.

Remember, gaming should be approached with a healthy balance of fun and learning. So, whether you’re playing solo or with friends, make sure to enjoy the process and embrace the valuable lessons these games have to offer. Sharpen your strategy skills, and who knows, you might become the next mastermind in your field!
