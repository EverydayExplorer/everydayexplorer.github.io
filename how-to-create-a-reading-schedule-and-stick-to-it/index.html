How to Create a Reading Schedule and Stick to It


In today’s fast-paced world, finding time to read can be a challenge. With so many distractions, it’s easy to let our reading habits fall by the wayside. However, reading is not only a great way to relax and unwind, but it’s also an excellent way to expand our knowledge and stimulate our minds. If you’re struggling to find time to read, creating a reading schedule can help you prioritize this important activity. In this blog post, we will discuss how to create a reading schedule and, more importantly, how to stick to it.

Why Do You Need a Reading Schedule?

Before we dive into the specifics, let’s talk about why having a reading schedule is beneficial. First and foremost, a reading schedule helps you prioritize reading in your daily routine. By allocating specific time slots for reading, you are more likely to follow through and make reading a consistent habit.

Additionally, a reading schedule allows you to set goals and track your progress. Whether you aim to read a certain number of books per month or want to finish a specific book by a particular deadline, having a schedule helps you stay on track and measure your success.

Step 1: Assess Your Current Reading Habits

To create a realistic reading schedule, start by evaluating your current reading habits. Ask yourself questions such as:

  • How often do I read?
  • How long do I typically spend reading in one sitting?
  • What time of day am I most likely to read?
  • Do I have any existing responsibilities or commitments that may impact my reading time?

Understanding your current habits and constraints will help you tailor your reading schedule to your specific needs and lifestyle.

Step 2: Set Realistic Goals

Once you have assessed your current reading habits, it’s time to set realistic goals. Begin by determining how much time you can realistically commit to reading each day or each week. It’s crucial to be honest with yourself and choose a time frame that you can realistically stick to, even if it’s just 15 minutes a day.

Next, set a goal for how many books you want to read within a specified time frame. This could be a monthly or yearly goal, depending on your preference. Setting specific goals helps you stay motivated and gives you something to strive for.

Step 3: Create a Reading Schedule

Now that you have determined your reading goals, it’s time to create a reading schedule. Start by identifying the best time of day for you to read. Some people prefer mornings, while others find evenings to be the most conducive to reading. Choose a time slot when you are generally free from distractions and can fully immerse yourself in a book.

Next, allocate a specific duration for your reading sessions. Depending on your schedule and preferences, this could be anywhere from 15 minutes to an hour or more. Consider dividing your reading time into smaller chunks throughout the day if you find it difficult to allocate a large block of time.

Remember to be flexible when creating your schedule. Life often throws unexpected surprises your way, and it’s important to adapt your routine as needed. If you miss a reading session on a particular day, try to catch up by adding extra time on another day.

Step 4: Make Reading a Priority

Creating a reading schedule is one thing, but sticking to it is another challenge altogether. To make reading a priority, it’s essential to eliminate or minimize distractions during your designated reading time. Turn off your phone or put it on silent mode, find a quiet and comfortable space, and let others around you know that you are not to be disturbed.

Building a habit takes time, consistency, and discipline. To help make reading a consistent part of your daily routine, consider setting reminders or alarms for your reading sessions. Treat your reading schedule as you would any other important appointment or commitment.

Step 5: Find Accountability and Support

Sometimes, it can be difficult to stay motivated on your own. Find an accountability partner or join a book club to keep yourself accountable and motivated. Share your reading goals and progress with others and engage in discussions about the books you’re reading. Not only will this provide external motivation, but it can also enhance your overall reading experience.

Final Thoughts

Creating a reading schedule and sticking to it requires planning, consistency, and commitment. However, the rewards are well worth the effort. Reading not only broadens your knowledge and imagination but also helps reduce stress and improve your overall well-being. So, take a step towards building a reading habit that will enrich your life and create your personalized reading schedule today!
