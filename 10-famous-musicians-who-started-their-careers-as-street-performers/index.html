10 Famous Musicians Who Started Their Careers as Street Performers


Street performance or busking has long been a traditional way for musicians to showcase their talent and connect with audiences. Some of the most successful musicians today actually began their careers as street performers, honing their skills and building a fan base from the ground up. In this blog post, we will take a closer look at 10 famous musicians who got their start as street performers, highlighting their inspiring stories of determination and perseverance.

1. Ed Sheeran:

With hit songs like ‘Shape of You’ and ‘Thinking Out Loud,’ Ed Sheeran is now an international superstar. But before he rose to fame, Sheeran spent his early years as a street performer in London. Armed with just his guitar, he would perform on the streets, parks, and subway stations, eventually attracting attention and signing his first record deal.

2. Tracy Chapman:

Tracy Chapman’s breakthrough came in the late 1980s with her hit song ‘Fast Car.’ However, before her big break, Chapman was a regular on the streets of Boston, captivating passersby with her soulful voice and poignant lyrics. Her time as a street performer helped shape her unique sound and laid the foundation for her successful music career.

3. Passenger:

Known for his heartfelt ballads and storytelling, Passenger, whose real name is Mike Rosenberg, began his musical journey as a busker in the streets of England and Australia. He gained international recognition with his hit song ‘Let Her Go,’ but his roots as a street performer are still evident in his intimate and personal performances.

4. Rod Stewart:

Rod Stewart is a rock icon, known for his raspy voice and energetic stage presence. However, before achieving fame as a solo artist, Stewart made a living as a busker in London during the 1960s. His street performances allowed him to develop his distinctive style and eventually led to his successful career in the music industry.

5. BB King:

Considered one of the greatest guitarists of all time, BB King started his journey as a street performer in the 1940s, playing on street corners in Mississippi. His soulful blues music and captivating stage presence eventually propelled him to legendary status, leaving an indelible mark on the world of music.

6. Jewel:

Before becoming a multi-platinum recording artist, Jewel spent her teenage years living in her car and performing on the streets of San Diego. Her unique folk-pop style and heartfelt lyrics caught the attention of record labels, leading to the release of her debut album, ‘Pieces of You,’ which became a massive success.

7. Hozier:

Best known for his breakout hit ‘Take Me to Church,’ Hozier began his music career by performing on the streets of Dublin, Ireland. His soulful voice and poetic songwriting attracted a devoted following and eventually led to him signing with a major record label.

8. Ben Harper:

From his early days as a street performer in his hometown of Claremont, California, Ben Harper’s soulful blend of folk, blues, and rock music resonated with audiences. His unique sound and passionate performances helped him build a dedicated fan base, leading to a successful music career.

9. Damien Rice:

With his hauntingly beautiful songs, Damien Rice captured the hearts of listeners worldwide. Before achieving commercial success, Rice spent years as a street performer in Dublin, captivating audiences with his emotional performances. His time as a busker shaped his raw and introspective songwriting style.

10. KT Tunstall:

KT Tunstall’s catchy songs like ‘Black Horse and the Cherry Tree’ and ‘Suddenly I See’ have made her a household name. However, prior to her success, Tunstall took to the streets of London to showcase her talent, busking outside busy tube stations. Her street performances provided her with the confidence and experience to pursue a career in music.

Conclusion:

These famous musicians prove that starting your music career as a street performer can be a stepping stone to success. Their determination, talent, and connection with audiences on the streets have paved the way for their remarkable journeys in the music industry. Busking not only allows musicians to perfect their craft but also gives them a unique platform to showcase their creativity directly to the public. So, the next time you come across a talented street performer, remember that they could be the next big music sensation.
