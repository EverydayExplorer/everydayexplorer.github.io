How to Cook Perfect Pasta: Tips and Tricks from Italian Nonnas


When it comes to cooking pasta, nobody does it better than the Italian nonnas. With their years of experience and time-tested techniques, they have perfected the art of creating delicious and flavorful pasta dishes. If you want to learn how to cook perfect pasta like an Italian nonna, then you’re in the right place. In this blog post, we will share some tips and tricks that will take your pasta game to the next level.

1. Start with High-Quality Ingredients

The first step in cooking perfect pasta is to use high-quality ingredients. Italians take their pasta seriously, and they believe that using the best ingredients will result in the best-tasting dish. Look for imported Italian pasta brands that are made from durum wheat semolina. Make sure to also use fresh and locally sourced ingredients for your sauce, such as tomatoes, garlic, and basil.

2. Choose the Right Pasta Shape

Italians have a wide variety of pasta shapes to choose from, and each shape lends itself to different types of sauces and flavors. For creamy sauces, opt for long and flat noodles like fettuccine or linguine. For thick and chunky sauces, choose a pasta shape with ridges like rigatoni or penne. The shape of the pasta can greatly impact the overall taste and texture of the dish, so choose wisely.

3. Salt the Water

This is one of the most important steps in cooking perfect pasta. Italians believe that pasta should be cooked in well-salted water, as it imparts flavor to the pasta itself. Don’t be afraid to use a generous amount of salt in the cooking water. A good rule of thumb is to use 1 tablespoon of salt for every 4 cups of water.

4. Cook Pasta Al Dente

Al dente, which means ’to the tooth’ in Italian, is the perfect texture for pasta. It refers to pasta that is cooked until it is tender, but still has a slight bite to it. To achieve al dente pasta, follow the cooking time on the package, but start testing for doneness a minute before the recommended time. The pasta should be cooked, but not mushy.

5. Reserve Pasta Water

Before you drain the cooked pasta, make sure to reserve a cup of the cooking water. This starchy water is a secret ingredient that Italian nonnas swear by. It is perfect for thinning out your sauce and helping it adhere to the pasta. The pasta water contains natural starches that can help create a velvety and flavorful sauce.

6. Don’t Rinse the Pasta

Contrary to popular belief, rinsing your cooked pasta is not necessary. This is especially true if you plan on adding sauce to the pasta. Rinsing the pasta removes the starchy surface that helps the sauce cling to the noodles. Instead, simply drain the pasta and toss it immediately with your desired sauce.

7. Finish Cooking the Pasta in the Sauce

Once your pasta is cooked, it’s time to bring it all together. Transfer the drained pasta directly into the pan with your sauce and toss it well to coat every strand with flavor. Allow the pasta to cook for a minute or two in the sauce to further absorb the flavors. This step ensures that every bite of pasta is infused with the delicious sauce.

8. Add a Drizzle of Olive Oil

To add an extra layer of flavor to your pasta dish, drizzle some good-quality extra virgin olive oil over the cooked pasta before serving. The oil will not only enhance the taste but also give the dish a beautiful glossy finish. You can also add a sprinkle of freshly grated Parmesan cheese or some fresh herbs like parsley or basil.

9. Serve Immediately

Pasta is best enjoyed when it is served immediately after cooking. It should be hot and steaming when it reaches the table. Overcooked pasta can become mushy and lose its texture, so make sure to time your cooking process accordingly. Serve the pasta with a side of crusty bread and a simple green salad for a complete and satisfying meal.

10. Experiment and Have Fun!

While these tips and tricks will help you cook perfect pasta, don’t be afraid to get creative and experiment with different flavors and combinations. Italian nonnas are known for their love of traditional recipes, but they also know how to add their own personal touch to a dish. So, don’t be afraid to put your own spin on classic pasta recipes and have fun with the process.

In conclusion, cooking perfect pasta is all about using high-quality ingredients, choosing the right pasta shape, salting the water, cooking al dente, reserving pasta water, finishing in the sauce, adding olive oil, serving immediately, and letting your creativity shine. With these tips and tricks from Italian nonnas, you’ll be well on your way to creating delicious and authentic Italian pasta dishes that will impress family and friends. Buon appetito!
