Debunking Common Myths about Credit


Credit is an essential part of our financial lives. From buying a home to leasing a car, we rely on credit to make significant purchases. However, despite its importance, credit is still a topic that is widely misunderstood. There are many myths about credit that have been perpetuated over the years, making it challenging to separate fact from fiction. In this blog, we’ll take a closer look at the most common credit myths and debunk them.

Myth #1: Checking your credit will lower your score

One of the most common myths about credit is that checking your credit score will lower it. This is simply not true. When you check your credit score, it is considered a “soft inquiry,” which means it does not affect your score. In fact, checking your credit score regularly is a wise move because it can help you identify errors and take steps to improve your score.

Myth #2: Closing a credit card will improve your credit score

Another prevailing myth about credit is that closing a credit card will improve your credit score. In reality, closing a credit card can hurt your score, especially if the card has a high credit limit or you have had the card for a long time. This is because closing a credit card reduces your available credit, which can increase your credit utilization ratio and lower your score. It’s generally best to keep your credit cards open, even if you’re not using them.

Myth #3: Paying off a debt will remove it from your credit report

Many people believe that paying off a debt will automatically remove it from your credit report. Unfortunately, this is not true. When you pay off a debt, it will still remain on your credit report for up to seven years. However, paying off a debt can have a positive impact on your credit score, as it shows lenders that you are capable of responsibly managing your debt.

Myth #4: You have only one credit score

Many people assume that there is only one credit score, but this is not true. There are many credit scoring models, and each lender may use a different one to evaluate your creditworthiness. Additionally, the credit bureaus - TransUnion, Experian, and Equifax - may calculate your score differently based on the information they have on file. This means that you may have multiple credit scores, and they may not all be the same.

Myth #5: You need to carry a balance on your credit card to build credit

Finally, one of the most pervasive myths about credit is that you need to carry a balance on your credit card to build credit. This is not true. In fact, carrying a balance can hurt your credit score because it increases your credit utilization ratio. Instead, use your credit card to make small purchases and pay off the balance in full each month. This will demonstrate to lenders that you are capable of responsibly managing your credit.

In conclusion, credit is a complex topic, but it’s essential to understand the facts about credit so you can make informed decisions about your finances. By debunking these common credit myths, you can be better equipped to manage your credit and improve your financial well-being. Remember to regularly check your credit score, keep your credit cards open, pay off your debt, be aware of your credit scores and don’t carry balances on your credit cards, and you’ll be on your way to building a strong credit profile.
