10 Must-Read Books of 2021 - The Ultimate Booklist for Bookworms


Are you a bookworm looking for new additions to your reading list? Look no further! We have compiled the ultimate booklist of 2021 for all the book lovers out there. From thrilling mysteries to thought-provoking non-fiction, this list has something for everyone. So grab a cup of coffee, cozy up in your favorite reading nook, and get ready to dive into these must-read books!

1. The Silent Patient by Alex Michaelides

If you’re a fan of psychological thrillers, this gripping novel is a must-read. The Silent Patient follows Alicia Berenson, a successful painter who murders her husband and then refuses to speak. Enter Theo Faber, a psychotherapist who is determined to unravel the mystery behind her silence. This page-turner will keep you guessing until the very end.

2. Normal People by Sally Rooney

This critically acclaimed novel explores the complicated relationship between Connell and Marianne from their school days in a small town in Ireland to their university years in Dublin. Normal People delves deep into themes of love, class, and the complexities of human connection. Rooney’s engaging writing style and compelling characters make this a must-read for any literary enthusiast.

3. The Vanishing Half by Brit Bennett

Set in the United States during the 1950s to the 1990s, The Vanishing Half tells the story of twin sisters who choose different paths in life: one remains in their small, black community, while the other passes as white. This thought-provoking novel explores themes of identity, race, and family bonds, and will leave readers reflecting on the choices we make and the impact they have on our lives.

4. The Midnight Library by Matt Haig

In this enchanting novel, Nora Seed finds herself in a mystical library between life and death, where each book represents a different version of her own life. The Midnight Library takes readers on a journey of self-discovery as Nora explores the infinite possibilities of what could have been. Haig’s whimsical writing style combined with profound philosophical questions make this a must-read for anyone searching for meaning in their own lives.

5. Project Hail Mary by Andy Weir

From the author of The Martian comes another science-fiction masterpiece. Project Hail Mary follows Ryland Grace, a lonely astronaut who wakes up on a spaceship with no memory of how he got there or what his mission is. As he unravels the mystery of his circumstances, he discovers that the fate of humanity rests in his hands. This thrilling and scientifically accurate adventure will keep you on the edge of your seat until the very last page.

6. Klara and the Sun by Kazuo Ishiguro

Nobel laureate Kazuo Ishiguro takes readers on a journey through the mind of an Artificial Friend named Klara. In a world where AFs are companions for lonely teenagers, Klara develops a unique perspective and yearns to understand the complexities of human emotions. Klara and the Sun is a profound exploration of love, consciousness, and what it means to be human in an increasingly technological world.

7. Empire of Pain by Patrick Radden Keefe

For non-fiction enthusiasts, this investigative book is a must-read. Empire of Pain provides an in-depth exploration of the Sackler family, founders of Purdue Pharma, and their role in the opioid crisis. Keefe meticulously unravels the web of deception, exploring the corporate greed and ethical implications behind one of the most devastating public health crises in modern times. This book is a powerful and timely account that sheds light on a pressing issue affecting communities worldwide.

8. A Promised Land by Barack Obama

In the first volume of his presidential memoirs, former President Barack Obama offers a candid and introspective account of his early political career and the first years of his presidency. A Promised Land provides a behind-the-scenes look at the challenges and triumphs Obama faced as he worked to navigate a rapidly changing political landscape. This memoir is a must-read for anyone interested in politics or contemporary history.

9. The Code Breaker by Walter Isaacson

Have you ever wondered about the implications of advances in genetic engineering? The Code Breaker dives into the world of CRISPR gene-editing technology and its potential for both scientific breakthroughs and ethical dilemmas. Walter Isaacson, acclaimed biographer, explores the life and work of Jennifer Doudna, one of the pioneers of CRISPR, and raises thought-provoking questions about the future of genetic engineering.

10. Mexican Gothic by Silvia Moreno-Garcia

For lovers of gothic horror, this atmospheric novel is a must-read. Mexican Gothic follows socialite Noemí Taboada as she investigates her cousin’s mysterious illness in the creepy and isolated High Place mansion. As Noemí delves deeper into the secrets of the house, she unravels a terrifying truth. This book is a perfect blend of chilling suspense, beautifully crafted prose, and rich cultural heritage.

In conclusion, the year 2021 has blessed us with a wide array of captivating books across various genres. Whether you’re a fan of thrillers, thought-provoking literature, or gripping non-fiction, this ultimate booklist has something for everyone. So, pick up one of these must-read books, and get ready for a literary adventure that will keep you captivated till the very last page. Happy reading, fellow bookworms!
