How to Properly Store Winter Tires: Tips for Maintaining Performance


Winter is a beautiful season that brings with it breathtaking snowy landscapes and cozy moments by the fireplace. But as we embrace the winter wonderland, it’s important not to overlook the maintenance and storage of our winter tires. After all, these trusty companions are crucial for ensuring our safety on icy and snowy roads. In this blog post, we will walk you through some helpful tips on how to properly store winter tires, so that they maintain their performance and keep you safe on the road.

1. Clean Your Tires:
Before storing your winter tires, it’s important to give them a good clean. Use a mild soap, water, and a soft brush to remove any dirt, grime, and salt that may have accumulated during the winter season. This step is crucial to prevent any corrosion or damage to the tires during storage.

2. Check for Damage:
While cleaning your winter tires, take the opportunity to inspect them for any signs of damage. Look for cuts, punctures, or uneven wear. If you notice any issues, it’s best to have them addressed by a professional before storing them away. This will not only extend the life of your tires but also ensure your safety on the road when you use them again.

3. Properly Inflate Your Tires:
Before storing your winter tires, make sure they are properly inflated. This is important because overinflated or underinflated tires can lead to damage over time. Refer to your vehicle’s manual or the tire manufacturer’s recommendations for the correct tire pressure. By storing them with the right amount of air pressure, you’ll help maintain their shape and performance.

4. Store Tires in a Cool, Dry Location:
Finding the ideal place to store your winter tires is crucial. Ideally, you should look for a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight and sources of heat. Extreme temperatures can cause the rubber to deteriorate, so it’s important to avoid storing them in areas like garages that experience frequent temperature fluctuations. A basement or climate-controlled storage area would be perfect for ensuring the longevity of your tires.

5. Invest in Tire Storage Bags or Tire Racks:
To further protect your winter tires during storage, consider investing in tire storage bags or tire racks. These accessories help to keep your tires clean and protected from dust, moisture, and any potential damage. Tire racks are also a great option if you have limited storage space, allowing you to stack your tires neatly and efficiently.

6. Rotate Your Tires:
Rotating your winter tires during storage is a smart move to prevent any flat spots from developing. This is especially important if you plan to store them for an extended period. Every few weeks, rotate the tires by moving them to a different position to distribute the weight evenly. This simple step will help maintain their roundness and ensure a smooth ride when you use them again.

7. Avoid Storing Tires Near Chemicals:
When storing your winter tires, it’s crucial to keep them away from any chemicals, solvents, or petroleum-based products. These substances can cause the rubber to deteriorate or become damaged. Make sure to store your tires separately from any such materials to ensure their longevity and performance.

Remember, properly storing winter tires is not only important for their performance but also for your safety on the roads. By following these tips, you can ensure that your winter tires are in top condition when you need them again. With a little care and attention, you’ll be ready to conquer the next winter season with confidence and peace of mind. Stay safe and enjoy the snowy adventures that lie ahead!
