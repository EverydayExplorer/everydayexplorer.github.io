Exploring Different Types of Personal Injury Damages: Economic and Non-Economic


When it comes to personal injury cases, understanding the different types of damages that can be awarded is crucial. Personal injury damages are designed to compensate the victim for their losses and provide the financial means to recover from the incident. In this blog post, we will explore the two main categories of personal injury damages: economic and non-economic.

Economic Damages

Economic damages, also referred to as special damages, are the tangible losses that can be easily quantified with a specific dollar value. These damages aim to compensate the victim for the financial hardship they have endured as a result of the injury. Here are some common types of economic damages:

1. Medical Expenses: Personal injuries often require medical treatment, including hospital stays, surgeries, medication, physical therapy, and rehabilitation. All these costs can quickly add up, and economic damages are meant to cover these expenses incurred by the victim.

2. Lost Income: If the injury prevents the victim from working or results in a decrease in their earning capacity, they may be entitled to compensation for the wages lost. Economic damages will take into account the income that the victim would have earned had the accident not occurred.

3. Property Damage: In cases where personal property is damaged or destroyed due to the negligence of another party, the victim may be eligible to receive compensation for the repair or replacement costs.

4. Other Financial Losses: Economic damages may also cover additional financial losses such as transportation expenses related to medical appointments, costs of hiring household help during recovery, and any other out-of-pocket expenses incurred as a result of the injury.

Non-Economic Damages

Non-economic damages, also known as general damages, are more subjective and aim to compensate the victim for intangible losses that are not easily quantifiable. These damages are meant to provide compensation for the pain and suffering endured by the victim as a result of the injury. Here are some common types of non-economic damages:

1. Pain and Suffering: Personal injuries can cause physical pain, discomfort, and emotional distress. Non-economic damages take into account the physical and emotional suffering endured by the victim.

2. Emotional Distress: The emotional impact of an accident or injury can be substantial and may include anxiety, depression, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), and other psychological disorders. Non-economic damages can compensate for the emotional distress experienced by the victim.

3. Loss of Enjoyment of Life: If the injury significantly impacts the victim’s ability to participate in activities they once enjoyed, non-economic damages may be awarded to compensate for the loss of enjoyment of life.

4. Loss of Consortium: In some cases, when the injury affects the relationship between the victim and their spouse or family members, compensation for the loss of consortium may be awarded. This includes the loss of companionship, emotional support, and intimacy.

Understanding the difference between economic and non-economic damages is essential when pursuing a personal injury claim. It is important to gather all relevant evidence, including medical records, bills, and expert opinions, to accurately quantify your damages and present a strong case.

In conclusion, personal injury damages can be categorized into economic and non-economic damages. The economic damages compensate for tangible losses, such as medical expenses and lost income, while the non-economic damages aim to compensate for intangible losses, such as pain and suffering and emotional distress. If you or someone you know has suffered a personal injury, consulting with an experienced personal injury attorney can help ensure that you receive fair compensation for your damages.
