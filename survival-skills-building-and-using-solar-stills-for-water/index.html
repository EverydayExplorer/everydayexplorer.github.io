Survival Skills: Building and Using Solar Stills for Water


In a survival situation, access to clean drinking water is essential. While there are various methods to obtain water in the wilderness, one technique that stands out is building and using solar stills. Solar stills are simple yet effective devices that harness the power of the sun to purify water, making it safe to drink. In this blog post, we will explore the process of building and using solar stills, providing you with a valuable survival skill that could potentially save your life.

What is a Solar Still?
A solar still is a device designed to extract pure water from contaminated sources using the sun’s energy. It works on the principle of evaporation and condensation, effectively separating impurities from the water. Solar stills are particularly useful in arid environments or situations where access to freshwater is limited.

Building a Solar Still
To build a solar still, you will need a few basic materials:

1. Dig a hole: Start by digging a hole in the ground, ensuring that it is deep enough to accommodate your water source and the collection container. The hole should be at least two feet deep.

2. Place a container: Place a clean container, such as a plastic sheet or a waterproof material, in the center of the hole. Make sure it is large enough to collect a sufficient amount of water.

3. Cover the hole: Cover the hole with a transparent plastic sheet, ensuring that it is tightly secured around the edges. You can use rocks or soil to hold it in place.

4. Create a slope: Create a slight slope by placing a small rock or stick at the center of the plastic sheet. This will allow condensation to flow towards the collection container.

Using a Solar Still
Once your solar still is set up, the process of extracting water begins:

1. Add water: Pour contaminated or muddy water around the sides of the hole, being careful not to disturb the plastic sheet covering the hole. The water should seep into the ground and collect at the bottom.

2. Wait for condensation: As the sun heats up the ground and the water evaporates, moisture will condense on the underside of the plastic sheet. This condensed water will then flow towards the collection container.

3. Collect the water: After a few hours, check the collection container. You should find clean, drinkable water that has been purified through the process of evaporation and condensation.

4. Repeat the process: If you require more water, simply add additional contaminated water to the solar still and let the process continue. Solar stills can be used repeatedly, providing a continuous supply of clean water.

Benefits of Using Solar Stills
There are several advantages to using solar stills for water purification:

1. Accessibility: Solar stills can be constructed using readily available materials, making them a practical option in emergency situations.

2. Reliability: Solar stills rely on the sun’s energy, which is abundant in most environments. As long as there is sunlight, you can rely on a solar still to produce clean water.

3. Portability: Solar stills can be easily dismantled and transported to different locations if necessary. This flexibility allows you to adapt to changing survival scenarios.

4. Cost-effective: Compared to other water purification methods, solar stills are cost-effective and require minimal maintenance.

In conclusion, building and using solar stills for water purification is a valuable survival skill to have. By harnessing the power of the sun, you can obtain clean drinking water in even the harshest of environments. Remember to always prioritize safety and proper hygiene practices when using solar stills or any other survival techniques. Stay prepared, stay safe, and stay hydrated in the great outdoors.
