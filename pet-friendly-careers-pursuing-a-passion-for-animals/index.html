Pet-Friendly Careers: Pursuing a Passion for Animals


As pet ownership continues to rise in the US, so does the demand for pet-related jobs. While some individuals may view working with animals as just a hobby or side gig, there are actually many pet-friendly careers that can lead to successful and fulfilling careers. Pursuing a passion for animals can be incredibly rewarding, not just emotionally, but financially as well. In this blog, we will explore some of the best pet-friendly careers for those who love to work with animals and how to pursue them.

1. Veterinary Medicine

Veterinary medicine is a career field that is typically associated with animal care. Veterinarians diagnose and treat medical issues in animals of all sizes, from cats and dogs to horses and cows. However, veterinary medicine also includes other pet-related professions such as veterinary technicians and veterinary assistants. These individuals work alongside veterinarians to provide vital support in animal care.

To pursue this career, one must undergo extensive training and education. A four-year bachelor’s degree in a pre-veterinary program is typically required, followed by a four-year Doctor of Veterinary Medicine (DVM) program. While the training can be rigorous, the reward is a career that allows you to work closely with animals every day.

2. Pet Grooming

Pet grooming is another popular pet-friendly career field. As a pet groomer, you will work with animals to keep their coats clean, trimmed, and well-maintained. Pet groomers may also provide additional services such as nail clipping, teeth cleaning, and ear cleaning. This career is great for people who enjoy working hands-on with animals and do not mind getting a little dirty.

To become a pet groomer, you will need to complete a pet grooming training program. Generally, these programs take anywhere from three to six months to complete. Some states require pet groomers to obtain a license, so be sure to research your state’s requirements before pursuing this career.

3. Pet Sitting and Dog Walking

Pet sitting and dog walking are becoming increasingly popular pet-friendly careers. These careers are ideal for animal lovers who enjoy spending time with pets but may not necessarily want to work in an office or clinical setting. Pet sitters and dog walkers are responsible for taking care of pets while their owners are away. This can include feeding, watering, walking, playing, and administering any medication needed.

To become a pet sitter or dog walker, you don’t typically need any formal education or certification. However, obtaining a certification from a reputable organization such as the National Association of Professional Pet Sitters can help set you apart from the competition.

4. Animal Training

Animal training is a career field that encompasses many different roles, including dog trainers, horse trainers, and wildlife trainers. Animal trainers work with animals to teach them specific behaviors and skills. They may work in zoos, theme parks, or private businesses.

To pursue a career in animal training, you will typically need a bachelor’s degree in animal behavior or a related field. Experience working with animals is also important in this field.

5. Pet Photography

Pet photography is a growing industry. As a pet photographer, you will capture images of pets and their owners for everything from social media to professional portraits. Pet photography requires patience, creativity, and a love of animals.

While there are no formal education requirements for this career, a degree in photography or a related field is helpful. Building a strong portfolio and networking with others in the industry can also help you succeed as a pet photographer.

Conclusion

Pursuing a career working with animals can be incredibly rewarding both emotionally and financially. There are many pet-friendly career options available that allow you to spend your days working with animals. Whether you are interested in veterinary medicine, pet grooming, animal training, pet sitting or dog walking, or pet photography, there is a career that can help you turn your passion for animals into a successful career. Now that you’ve learned about some pet-friendly careers, you can start exploring ways to pursue them. With dedication, persistence, and some hard work, you can land the perfect job working with animals.
