Inspiring Lives: 12 Biographies That Celebrate Extraordinary Individuals


When we think about influential people who have shaped our world throughout history, a few names come to mind like Gandhi, Mother Teresa, and Nelson Mandela. However, there are many lesser-known yet equally inspiring individuals whose endeavors have left an indelible mark on our world. Their biographies are just as, if not more, compelling as those of the better-known figures. Here, we provide a list of 12 biographies that celebrate extraordinary individuals whose lives will inspire and motivate you.

1. Claudia Rankine: Citizen
Claudia Rankine is an award-winning poet, essayist, and playwright who has authored books that aim to start conversations about race and systemic racism in America. Her book Citizen: An American Lyric describes everyday acts of racism experienced by people of color all over the country.

2. Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie: We Should All Be Feminists
Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie is a Nigerian writer and activist for women’s rights. Her book We Should All Be Feminists is a call to arms for the feminist movement and a reminder of the need for women’s empowerment.

3. Ray Dalio: Principles: Life and Work
Ray Dalio is the founder of Bridgewater Associates, one of the largest hedge funds in the world. In his book Principles: Life and Work, he shares the principles he has used throughout his life to achieve personal and professional success.

4. Alice Waters: Coming to My Senses: The Making of a Counterculture Cook
Alice Waters is an American chef and restaurateur who is famous for her efforts at popularizing the farm-to-table movement. In her book Coming to My Senses, she shares her journey and how she became a pioneer in the world of food and sustainability.

5. Barack Obama: A Promised Land
Barack Obama is a former President of the United States whose book A Promised Land gives readers an inside look into his life and presidency and the difficult decisions he had to make.

6. Ruth Bader Ginsburg: My Own Words
Ruth Bader Ginsburg was a Supreme Court justice and a women’s rights advocate. Her book My Own Words contains a collection of her speeches, writings, and opinions that offer insight into her legal career and the legacy she leaves behind.

7. Rachel Carson: Silent Spring
Rachel Carson was a marine biologist, conservationist, and writer who used her book Silent Spring to bring awareness to the dangers of pesticides and their detrimental effects on the environment.

8. Malala Yousafzai: I Am Malala
Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani activist for women’s education, and her autobiography I Am Malala recounts her journey from being shot by the Taliban to becoming one of the world’s most notable advocates for girls’ education.

9. Temple Grandin: Thinking in Pictures: My Life with Autism
Temple Grandin is an animal science expert and autism advocate. Her book Thinking in Pictures offers an autobiographical account of her experiences growing up with autism.

10. Michelle Obama: Becoming
Michelle Obama is the former First Lady of the United States, and in her autobiography Becoming, she shares her journey from growing up on the South Side of Chicago to becoming a successful lawyer, businesswoman, and advocate.

11. J.K. Rowling: The Casual Vacancy
J.K. Rowling is the author of the Harry Potter series, but her book The Casual Vacancy is not a fantasy story. It is a story about a small town in England and its politics, dealing with issues such as class, poverty, and prejudice.

12. Tara Westover: Educated
Tara Westover was born into a family of survivalists in Idaho and was not allowed to go to school or see a doctor. In her memoir Educated, she recounts how she overcame these obstacles and eventually received a PhD from Cambridge University.

In conclusion, these 12 biographies celebrate individuals who have achieved excellence in various fields and left an impact on the world. Through their unique stories of triumph and adversity, they inspire us to be better versions of ourselves and make a meaningful difference in the world. Whether you are interested in politics, the environment, social justice, or personal growth, these individuals’ lives and experiences offer valuable insights that can benefit everyone.
