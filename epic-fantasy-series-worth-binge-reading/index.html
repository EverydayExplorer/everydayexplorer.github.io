Epic Fantasy Series Worth Binge-Reading


If you’re a fan of epic fantasy, you know the feeling of getting lost in a captivating world, surrounded by fascinating characters and embarking on thrilling adventures. Whether you’re an avid reader or new to the genre, binge-reading a well-crafted epic fantasy series can be a truly immersive experience.

In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the most remarkable epic fantasy series that are definitely worth binge-reading. From well-established classics to newer additions to the genre, these series will keep you turning pages and craving for more.

1. The Wheel of Time by Robert Jordan

‘The Wheel of Time’ is an epic fantasy series that spans a whopping 14 books. Written by Robert Jordan and completed by Brandon Sanderson after Jordan’s passing, this series is a must-read for any fan of the genre. Set in a sprawling world where the Wheel of Time weaves the lives of many characters, this series is known for its complex plot, intricate world-building, and rich character development.

2. A Song of Ice and Fire by George R.R. Martin

No list of epic fantasy series would be complete without mentioning ‘A Song of Ice and Fire.’ This series, famously adapted into the television show ‘Game of Thrones,’ has captivated readers with its political intrigue, complex characters, and unpredictable plot twists. Set in the fictional world of Westeros, George R.R. Martin’s series is known for its gritty realism and intricate web of alliances and conflicts.

3. The Stormlight Archive by Brandon Sanderson

Brandon Sanderson is widely regarded as one of the best authors in the fantasy genre, and ‘The Stormlight Archive’ is a prime example of his skill. Set in the world of Roshar, this series follows a diverse cast of characters as they navigate a world plagued by war and supernatural forces. Known for its detailed world-building, intricate magic system, and compelling characters, this series is a must-read for any fantasy enthusiast.

4. The Kingkiller Chronicle by Patrick Rothfuss

‘The Kingkiller Chronicle’ by Patrick Rothfuss is a beautifully written epic fantasy series that follows the story of Kvothe, a musician and magician with a mysterious past. Rothfuss’ prose and attention to detail make this series a joy to read, and the intricate plot will keep you guessing until the very end. With two books currently released and a third eagerly anticipated, now is the perfect time to dive into this enchanting series.

5. The Malazan Book of the Fallen by Steven Erikson

‘The Malazan Book of the Fallen’ is a ten-book series written by Steven Erikson that has gained immense popularity among fantasy readers. Set in a sprawling world with a vast array of characters, this series is known for its complex plot, philosophical themes, and epic battles. While it can be a challenging read at times, the payoff is immensely satisfying for fans of grand, epic storytelling.

6. The Broken Empire Trilogy by Mark Lawrence

For readers who enjoy darker, grittier fantasy, ‘The Broken Empire Trilogy’ by Mark Lawrence is a must-read. This series follows the story of Jorg Ancrath, a ruthless prince who seeks revenge in a post-apocalyptic world. Lawrence’s writing is atmospheric and thought-provoking, and he masterfully blurs the lines between hero and villain. If you’re looking for a series that delves into the complexities of human nature, this is the one for you.

7. The Lightbringer series by Brent Weeks

Brent Weeks’ ‘The Lightbringer’ series is a refreshing take on the epic fantasy genre, featuring a unique magic system based on light manipulation. This series follows the story of Gavin Guile, a powerful sorcerer, and his role in a world on the brink of war. With compelling characters, brilliant world-building, and an intricate plot, this series will keep you hooked from start to finish.

8. The Inheritance Trilogy by N.K. Jemisin

N.K. Jemisin’s ‘The Inheritance Trilogy’ is a groundbreaking series that explores themes of power, oppression, and redemption in a meticulously crafted world. Featuring a diverse cast of characters and a narrative that spans generations, this series challenges traditional fantasy tropes and pushes the boundaries of the genre. Jemisin’s writing is powerful and thought-provoking, making this series a must-read for any fan of epic fantasy.

Conclusion

There’s no shortage of epic fantasy series out there, but these eight are definitely worth binge-reading. From the classic world of ‘Wheel of Time’ to the gritty realism of ‘A Song of Ice and Fire,’ each series offers its own unique take on the genre. So, grab a mug of your favorite beverage, find a cozy spot, and dive into one of these epic adventures. Happy binge-reading!
