Hunting for Wild Turkey: Tactics and Calls


Welcome to our comprehensive guide on hunting for wild turkeys! Whether you are an experienced turkey hunter or just starting out, this blog post is here to provide you with valuable tactics and tips to enhance your hunting success. From understanding turkey behavior to mastering different calls, we will cover it all. So, let’s dive in and discover the exciting world of turkey hunting!

Understanding Wild Turkey Behavior:

Before we get into the specific tactics and calls, it’s important to understand the behavior of wild turkeys. Turkeys are highly intelligent and have exceptional eyesight, making them a challenging quarry. They have an acute sense of hearing and can spot the slightest movement from afar. To outsmart these wily birds, you must learn their patterns and adapt your hunting strategies accordingly.

1. Scouting: Start by scouting the area you plan to hunt in. Look for signs of turkey activity such as droppings, scratch marks, feathers, and tracks. Observe their roosting and feeding areas, as well as their travel routes. This information will help you determine the best spots to set up your hunting blind or tree stand.

2. Roosting: Turkeys roost in trees overnight, so knowing their preferred roosting spots is crucial. Listen for their distinct gobbling calls at dawn and dusk to locate their roosting areas. Set up near these spots early in the morning, before the turkeys fly down from their roosts.

3. Strutting Zones: During the breeding season, male turkeys, or toms, seek out open areas known as strutting zones to display their feathers and attract hens. These areas are ideal for setting up decoys and calling in turkeys.

Mastering Turkey Calls:

Turkey calls are essential tools for luring in wild turkeys. There are different types of calls that mimic various turkey sounds, each serving a specific purpose. Here are the main types of turkey calls and how to effectively use them:

1. Box Calls: Box calls produce realistic hen yelps, clucks, and purrs. Hold the box against the palm of your hand and run the lid across the surface to create sounds. Experiment with different rhythms and volumes to imitate a hen’s natural cadence.

2. Slate Calls: Slate calls, also known as pot calls, consist of a slate or glass surface and a striker. By dragging the striker across the surface, you can produce hen yelps, purrs, and soft clucks. Vary the pressure and speed to create different tones and pitches.

3. Mouth Calls: Mouth calls, or diaphragm calls, offer hands-free operation once you master the technique. Place the call against the roof of your mouth, using your tongue and airflow to create sounds. Practice different pitches and volumes to imitate multiple turkeys.

Tactical Approaches:

Now that you have a good understanding of turkey behavior and can effectively replicate their vocalizations, let’s explore some effective tactical approaches to help you bag that elusive gobbler:

1. Ambush Hunting: Set up your blind or tree stand in areas where turkeys frequently travel, such as along their feeding routes or near strutting zones. Use decoys to make your setup more convincing and call sparingly to draw them in close.

2. Stalking: If you prefer a more mobile approach, practice your stealthy skills and utilize the terrain to your advantage. Move slowly, using available cover to get closer to your target. Use soft clucks and purrs intermittently to lure a curious turkey within range.

3. Fall Hunting: While turkey hunting is traditionally associated with the spring mating season, fall hunting can be equally rewarding. During the autumn, turkeys gather in flocks and are more likely to respond to flock calls. Scattering the flock and then using assembly calls can bring them back together, increasing your chances of success.

Conclusion:

Hunting for wild turkeys can be an exhilarating and challenging experience. By understanding turkey behavior, mastering different calls, and employing effective tactics, you can greatly increase your chances of a successful hunt. Remember, patience and persistence are key. Stay stealthy, practice your calling techniques, and enjoy the beauty of nature while chasing these magnificent birds. Good luck and happy hunting!
