Crafting with Shells: DIY Projects for Beachy and Coastal Decor


Are you looking to infuse your home with some beachy and coastal vibes? Look no further than crafting with shells! Shells are not only beautiful and abundant, but they also carry the essence of the ocean within them, making them the perfect material for creating stunning decor pieces. In this blog, we’ll explore some DIY projects that will help transform your space into a coastal paradise.

1. Shell Wreaths: A shell wreath is a fantastic way to welcome guests into your home with a touch of the beach. To create your own shell wreath, start by gathering a variety of shells in different shapes and sizes. You can either purchase shells from your local craft store or collect them yourself during your beach walks. Arrange the shells in a circular pattern on a wreath base, using glue to secure them. Feel free to add in other beachy elements like starfish or coral to enhance the overall aesthetic. Hang your shell wreath on your front door or in any room to instantly add a coastal feel.

2. Shell Candles: Another fun and easy DIY project is creating shell candles. Not only do these candles look beautiful, but they also exude a calming oceanic scent when lit. To make your own shell candles, you’ll need some seashells, candle wicks, and wax (you can use old candles or purchase wax specifically for candle-making). Start by cleaning the shells thoroughly and securing the wicks to the bottom of each shell with a small amount of hot glue. Melt the wax and pour it into each shell, ensuring that the wicks stay in the center. Allow the wax to cool and solidify before trimming the wicks. Your shell candles are now ready to be displayed and used as a centerpiece or accent piece in your coastal-themed space.

3. Shell Terrariums: For those with a green thumb, shell terrariums are a great way to incorporate shells into your indoor garden. Begin by finding a clear glass container or vase and filling it with layers of sand, gravel, and activated charcoal to create a proper drainage system. Add potting soil and select small succulents or air plants that are well-suited for terrariums. Arrange the plants in the container, and then add in your shells for an extra touch of coastal charm. Place your shell terrarium in a well-lit area, but away from direct sunlight. Remember to water your plants as instructed for their specific needs. With some care, your shell terrarium will thrive and become a beautiful addition to your coastal-themed decor.

4. Shell Art: If you’re feeling a bit more adventurous, why not try your hand at creating shell art? Get a canvas or a piece of wood and paint it with a beachy color like seafoam green or sandy beige. Once the base coat is dry, gather your shells and arrange them in a design of your choosing on the canvas or wood. You can create a beautiful mosaic effect or spell out words like ‘beach’ or ‘sea’ using smaller shells. Secure the shells to the surface using strong adhesive glue. Once the glue is dry, seal the artwork with a clear varnish to protect the shells and give the piece a finished look. Display your shell art on a wall or shelf to add a focal point to your coastal-inspired space.

5. Shell Wind Chimes: Let the soothing sound of the ocean grace your backyard or patio with a DIY shell wind chime. Start by collecting different-sized shells with natural holes or carefully drill a small hole near the top of each shell. Next, gather some driftwood or a stick from your beach adventures to serve as the base of your wind chime. Attach fishing line or twine to each shell and then tie them to the driftwood at varying heights. Hang the wind chime in a spot where it can catch the wind and create a beautiful melody. Not only will it add a decorative touch to your outdoor space, but it will also transport you to the beach with its enchanting sound.

Crafting with shells is not only a great way to express your creativity but also a way to bring the relaxing and serene atmosphere of the beach into your home. Whether you’re an experienced crafter or just starting out, these DIY projects are simple yet effective in adding coastal charm. So get those shells out, and let your imagination run wild as you create stunning pieces that will transform your space into a seaside haven.
