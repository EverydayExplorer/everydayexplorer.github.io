The Funny Side of History: Little-known Stories That Will Make You Smile


In the vast tapestry of human history, there are countless tales that have been passed down through generations. From epic battles to groundbreaking inventions, history provides us with a wealth of knowledge and insight into the triumphs and struggles of our ancestors. However, amidst the grand narratives, there lies a hidden treasure trove of little-known stories that are sure to bring a smile to your face. Join us on a journey through time as we explore the funny side of history.

One such tale takes us back to ancient Egypt, where Pharaoh Sneferu, known for his ambitious building projects, set his sights on constructing the world’s first true pyramid. As the story goes, Sneferu was determined to create a monument that would stand the test of time. However, his engineers faced a peculiar problem - how to transport massive stone blocks across the desert to the construction site.

In a stroke of comedic genius, one of the engineers proposed using a fleet of specially trained geese to drag the heavy stones. Yes, you heard that right, geese! These feathered creatures, renowned for their honking and waddling, were harnessed to the stones, much to the amusement of onlookers. The sight of geese strutting across the desert, pulling monumental blocks, surely brought smiles and laughter to the faces of the ancient Egyptians.

Moving forward in time, we find ourselves in medieval Europe, where tales of chivalry and courtly love abound. But amidst the knights and damsels, there existed a peculiar tradition known as the ‘fool’s errand.’ This mischievous practice involved tricking unsuspecting knights into embarking on absurd quests, often leading them on wild goose chases.

One famous example involves a noble knight named Sir Lancelot, who was known for his bravery and gallantry. However, his fellow knights decided to play a practical joke on him. They convinced Lancelot that he needed to retrieve the mythical ‘Snipe’ bird from a distant forest, claiming that it possessed magical powers. Little did Lancelot know that the Snipe was merely a figment of their imagination.

Undeterred, Lancelot set off on his quest, armed with nothing but a bag and a sense of determination. After hours of fruitless searching, he finally realized the prank and returned to the castle, much to the delight of his comrades. The tale of Sir Lancelot’s pursuit of the elusive Snipe became a favorite among the knights, reminding them to always find humor in the most unexpected places.

As we fast forward to more recent times, we encounter the story of a group of mischievous college students who decided to pull off the ultimate prank. In 1959, a group of students at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) managed to place a police car on top of the university’s iconic dome. How did they achieve this feat? With creativity and a touch of humor, of course!

The students meticulously disassembled the vehicle and reassembled it atop the dome, leaving campus security scratching their heads in disbelief. The sight of a police car perched precariously on the dome became a legendary tale, forever etched in the annals of MIT’s history. The students’ audacity and wit brought laughter to the entire campus, proving that even the most serious institutions can embrace a little lightheartedness.

These are just a few examples of the funny side of history, showcasing the creativity and humor that have permeated human existence throughout the ages. These lesser-known stories may not have made it into the history books, but they remind us that laughter has always been an integral part of our shared human experience.

So, the next time you delve into the depths of history, take a moment to seek out these hidden gems, and let them bring a smile to your face. For in the laughter of the past, we find a connection to our ancestors, a reminder that even in the face of adversity, humor has the power to uplift, unite, and make us smile.
