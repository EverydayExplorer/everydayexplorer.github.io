How to Fish with Crankbaits Like a Pro


When it comes to fishing, crankbaits are a popular and effective lure choice for many anglers. These versatile lures can mimic a variety of baitfish and are designed to create a realistic wobbling action in the water. Learning how to fish with crankbaits like a pro can greatly increase your chances of landing a big catch. In this guide, we’ll explore the ins and outs of crankbait fishing and provide you with some expert tips and techniques to help you become a more successful angler.

Choosing the Right Crankbait

Before you hit the water, it’s important to choose the right crankbait for the job. There are many different types and styles of crankbaits available, each designed for specific fishing conditions and target species. Here are a few things to consider when selecting a crankbait:

1. Size: Crankbaits come in a range of sizes, from small mini-crankbaits to larger deep-diving models. The size you choose will depend on the size of the baitfish in your fishing area and the depth at which you plan to fish.

2. Depth: Crankbaits are designed to run at specific depths in the water. Some are shallow runners, designed to stay near the surface, while others are deep-divers, capable of reaching depths of 20 feet or more. Consider the depth of the water you’ll be fishing in and choose a crankbait that runs at the appropriate depth.

3. Color and Pattern: The color and pattern of your crankbait can also make a difference in your success. It’s generally best to choose a crankbait that closely resembles the local baitfish or matches the water conditions. Experiment with different colors and patterns until you find what works best in your fishing area.

Crankbait Fishing Techniques

Now that you have the right crankbait, let’s dive into some techniques to help you fish like a pro:

1. Cast and Retrieve: One of the most common techniques for fishing with crankbaits is the cast and retrieve method. Simply cast your crankbait out into the desired fishing area, let it sink to the desired depth, and then retrieve it back to you with a steady, alternating retrieve. This technique is effective for covering large areas of water and enticing fish to strike.

2. Bump and Pause: Another effective technique is to cast your crankbait out, let it sink to the desired depth, and then give it a few sharp jerks or twitches before pausing. This imitates a wounded or struggling baitfish, which can trigger a predatory response from nearby fish. After the pause, continue with a steady retrieve. Experiment with the length of the pause and the intensity of the jerks to find what works best in your fishing area.

3. Slow and Steady: In some situations, a slow and steady retrieve can be extremely effective when fishing with crankbaits. This technique works well when fish are less active or the water temperature is cooler. By reeling your crankbait in slowly and steadily, you can create a subtle wobbling action that entices even the most finicky fish to strike.

4. Bounce off Structure: Crankbaits are excellent lures for fishing around structure, such as rocks, logs, or submerged vegetation. When fishing in these areas, try to bounce your crankbait off the structure to trigger a reaction from nearby fish. This sudden change in direction can often elicit a strike, as it simulates an injured baitfish.

5. Vary Your Retrieve Speed: Don’t be afraid to experiment with the speed of your retrieve. Sometimes a fast retrieve can trigger aggressive strikes, while other times a slow retrieve is more effective. Vary your retrieve speed until you find what works best in your fishing area.

6. Pay Attention to Water Temperature: Water temperature plays a significant role in fish behavior. As the water warms up, fish become more active and willing to chase down faster-moving crankbaits. Conversely, in cooler water, fish may be lethargic and prefer a slower presentation. Pay attention to the water temperature and adjust your crankbait fishing techniques accordingly.

Final Tips and Considerations

Here are a few additional tips and considerations to keep in mind when fishing with crankbaits:

1. Use the Right Gear: To fish with crankbaits effectively, it’s important to use the right gear. A medium to medium-heavy rod with a fast or extra-fast action is ideal for crankbait fishing. Pair it with a reel that has a smooth drag system and a high gear ratio for quick line retrieval.

2. Pay Attention to the Season: Fish behavior can vary depending on the season. In colder water, fish may be deeper and less active, requiring you to fish with deeper-diving crankbaits. In warmer water, fish may be more shallow, allowing you to use shallower-running crankbaits.

3. Practice Patience: Crankbait fishing can require patience, especially when targeting finicky fish. Experiment with different techniques, retrieve speeds, and colors until you find what works best. Don’t be afraid to adjust your approach if you’re not getting any bites.

4. Follow Local Regulations: Always check and follow local fishing regulations, including catch limits and size restrictions. It’s important to practice ethical and sustainable angling.

In conclusion, fishing with crankbaits can be a rewarding and productive way to target a variety of fish species. By choosing the right crankbait, using the proper techniques, and paying attention to the details, you can increase your chances of success on the water. Remember to experiment with different approaches and be adaptable to the conditions and the behavior of the fish. With practice and persistence, you’ll be fishing with crankbaits like a pro in no time. Happy fishing!
