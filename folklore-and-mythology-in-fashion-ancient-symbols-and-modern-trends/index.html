Folklore and Mythology in Fashion: Ancient Symbols and Modern Trends


Fashion has always been a reflection of society’s values, beliefs, and cultural heritage. From the colors we wear to the patterns we choose, every aspect of fashion tells a story. In recent years, there has been a resurgence of interest in folklore and mythology, with designers incorporating ancient symbols into their creations. In this blog post, we will explore the fascinating intersection of folklore, mythology, and fashion, and how designers are embracing these ancient symbols in modern trends.

One of the most intriguing aspects of folklore and mythology is the rich symbolism associated with different cultures and civilizations. These symbols often carry deep meanings and are steeped in history. By incorporating them into fashion, designers are not only paying homage to the past, but also infusing their creations with a sense of mystery and intrigue.

Ancient symbols such as the Celtic knot, the Eye of Horus, and the Greek key pattern have made their way onto runways and into fashion collections. These symbols have a timeless appeal and add a touch of mysticism to any outfit. Whether it’s a delicate necklace adorned with the Tree of Life or a bold dress featuring the Egyptian scarab, these symbols make a statement and invite conversation.

One designer who has embraced folklore and mythology in her fashion line is Stella McCartney. In her Spring/Summer collection, she drew inspiration from Norse mythology, incorporating intricate Viking-inspired patterns and symbols into her designs. The result was a collection that evoked a sense of strength and power, while also highlighting the beauty and intricacy of ancient art forms.

Another designer who has explored the realm of mythology is Alexander McQueen. Known for his avant-garde and theatrical designs, McQueen has often used ancient symbols to create a sense of drama and storytelling in his collections. From the iconic skull motif to the use of Greek mythology in his Spring/Summer 2019 collection, McQueen’s designs transcend fashion and become wearable works of art.

But it’s not just high-end designers who are embracing folklore and mythology in fashion. In recent years, there has been a rise in the popularity of ‘witchy’ and bohemian aesthetics, with brands like Free People and Spell & The Gypsy Collective incorporating symbols such as moons, stars, and mystical creatures into their designs. These brands tap into the allure of the unknown and the natural world, creating a sense of connection to something greater than ourselves.

So why is folklore and mythology in fashion gaining traction now? In a world that is increasingly digital and disconnected, there is a longing for a deeper connection to our roots and a desire to infuse meaning into our everyday lives. By wearing symbols and patterns that have been passed down through generations, we are not only celebrating our heritage but also creating a bridge between the past and the present.

In conclusion, folklore and mythology in fashion offer a unique and captivating way to express our individuality and connect with the world around us. Whether it’s a subtle nod to ancient symbols or a bold statement piece, these designs invite us to explore the stories and traditions that have shaped our collective consciousness. So next time you’re getting dressed, consider adding a touch of folklore and mythology to your outfit and let your fashion become a powerful storyteller.
