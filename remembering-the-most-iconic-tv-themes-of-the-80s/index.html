Remembering the Most Iconic TV Themes of the 80s


The 80s was a decade that left an indelible mark on the world of television. From iconic shows that are still beloved today to unforgettable characters that became household names, the 80s was a time of innovation and creativity in television. But one aspect of 80s television that often gets overlooked is the memorable theme songs that accompanied these shows. In this blog post, we will take a nostalgic trip down memory lane and remember some of the most iconic TV themes of the 80s.

The Power of a Good Theme Song

Before we dive into specific TV themes, let’s take a moment to appreciate the power of a good theme song. A catchy theme song has the ability to transport us back in time, evoke emotions, and create a sense of nostalgia. It becomes intricately tied to the show itself, forever engrained in our memories. In the 80s, theme songs were not just background music, but an integral part of the viewing experience.

1. ‘The A-Team’

Who can forget the thrilling theme song of ‘The A-Team’? Composed by Mike Post, this TV theme is instantly recognizable with its lively horns and pulsating beat. It perfectly captures the essence of the show, which revolved around a team of former Special Forces soldiers on a mission to help those in need, all while evading the military. This theme song will forever be associated with the image of the A-Team van speeding through the streets, guns blazing.

2. ‘The Golden Girls’

‘The Golden Girls’ was a beloved sitcom about four older women living together in Miami. But what truly made this show stand out was its infectious theme song, ‘Thank You for Being a Friend.’ Written and performed by Andrew Gold, this heartwarming tune became an anthem for friendship and solidarity. Even today, when we hear the opening notes, we can’t help but sing along and remember the golden memories of this iconic show.

3. ‘Miami Vice’

‘Miami Vice’ was a groundbreaking police drama that took viewers into the glamorous and dangerous world of Miami’s vice squad. But it was its unforgettable theme song composed by Jan Hammer that truly set the tone for the series. The combination of synthesizers, drums, and electric guitars captured the essence of the show’s sleek and stylish atmosphere. This instrumental theme song still resonates with audiences today and instantly transports us back to the neon-lit streets of Miami.

4. ‘Cheers’

‘Cheers’ was a sitcom set in the friendly neighborhood bar where everybody knows your name. Its theme song, ‘Where Everybody Knows Your Name,’ sang by Gary Portnoy, perfectly captured the warm and welcoming atmosphere of the show. The lyrics spoke to the universal desire to be part of a place where you belong and feel accepted. For many viewers, hearing this theme song was like being transported to the familiar and comforting setting of the Cheers bar.

5. ‘Knight Rider’

‘Knight Rider’ was a beloved action series that introduced us to the iconic character of Michael Knight and his talking car, KITT. But it was the show’s theme song, composed by Stu Phillips and Glen A. Larson, that got hearts racing. Combining high-energy synthesizers, driving drums, and an unforgettable melody, this theme song perfectly captured the excitement and adventure of the show. Even today, hearing those opening notes elicits a sense of nostalgia and a rush of adrenaline.

Conclusion

The 80s was truly a golden age for television, and the theme songs of that era played a significant role in shaping our memories of those shows. Whether it was the high-octane action of ‘The A-Team,’ the heartwarming friendship of ‘The Golden Girls,’ or the electrifying sounds of ‘Miami Vice’ and ‘Knight Rider,’ these TV themes became ingrained in our collective consciousness. As we remember the most iconic TV themes of the 80s, let’s cherish the melodies that still evoke a sense of nostalgia and transport us back to a time when television was filled with magic and wonder.
