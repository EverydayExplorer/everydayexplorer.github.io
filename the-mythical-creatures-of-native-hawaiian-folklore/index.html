The Mythical Creatures of Native Hawaiian Folklore


When it comes to mythological creatures, many people think of Greek gods and monsters or fantastical beasts from other ancient civilizations. However, Native Hawaiian folklore is home to a fascinating array of mythical creatures that are just as captivating. These creatures, known as ‘aumakua’, have been an integral part of Hawaiian culture for centuries.

The ‘aumakua’ are guardian spirits that take the form of animals and watch over their family or clan. They are believed to possess the spirit and knowledge of their ancestors and are revered as protectors and guides. These mythical creatures are deeply respected and play an important role in Native Hawaiian traditions.

One of the most well-known ‘aumakua’ is the ‘mo’o’. These are dragon-like creatures that are said to dwell in bodies of water, such as rivers, ponds, and even the ocean. They can take on various forms, from small lizards to large serpents, and are believed to be connected to water and rain. The ‘mo’o’ are both feared and respected, as they are known to have great power and can bring both blessings and destruction.

Another mythical creature in Hawaiian folklore is the ‘menehune’. These are said to be small, dwarf-like beings that inhabit the forests and mountains of Hawaii. The ‘menehune’ are known for their exceptional craftsmanship and are said to be responsible for building various structures, such as heiau (temples) and fishponds. According to legend, they work mostly at night and are highly skilled and mischievous. They are seen as protectors of nature and are believed to have a strong connection with the land.

One of the most beloved ‘aumakua’ in Hawaiian mythology is the ‘honu’. These are sea turtles that are considered sacred and have a special place in Hawaiian culture. The ‘honu’ are believed to be the symbol of longevity, peace, and good luck. They are often associated with the goddess Kauila, who is said to watch over all sea turtles. The ‘honu’ are highly respected and protected, and harming them is believed to bring bad luck.

In addition to these well-known creatures, there are numerous other ‘aumakua’ in Hawaiian folklore, each with its own unique characteristics and significance. For example, the ‘io’ is a majestic hawk that is considered a symbol of royalty and is believed to be the manifestation of the god Lono. The ’nioi’ is a ghostly spirit that is said to haunt certain places and is often associated with misfortune. The ‘pueo’ is an owl that is considered a guardian and protector of the land.

The stories and legends surrounding these mythical creatures have been passed down through generations, and they continue to be an important part of Hawaiian culture today. These creatures not only provide insight into the rich history and traditions of Native Hawaiians but also connect people with the natural world and the spiritual realm.

From the majestic ‘mo’o’ to the mischievous ‘menehune’ and the sacred ‘honu’, the mythical creatures of Native Hawaiian folklore are an enchanting and integral part of the cultural fabric of the islands. They embody the deep connection between the land, sea, and people, and serve as a reminder of the importance of respecting and preserving nature.

Whether viewed as protectors, guides, or representations of ancient spirits, these mythical creatures continue to capture the imagination of locals and visitors alike. Exploring their stories and significance offers a glimpse into the unique and vibrant mythology of Native Hawaiian culture.

So, next time you find yourself in the islands, take a moment to learn more about the mythical creatures of Native Hawaiian folklore. Embrace the magic and wonder that surrounds these creatures and let them guide you into the captivating world of Hawaiian mythology.
