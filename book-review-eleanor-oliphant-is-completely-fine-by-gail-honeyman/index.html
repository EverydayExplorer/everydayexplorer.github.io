Book Review: 'Eleanor Oliphant Is Completely Fine' by Gail Honeyman


In a world where we often strive for perfection, ‘Eleanor Oliphant Is Completely Fine’ by Gail Honeyman takes us on a journey of self-discovery, reminding us that it’s okay to be imperfect. This captivating novel explores the complexities of human connection, mental health, and the healing power of friendship.

Set in Glasgow, Scotland, the story revolves around Eleanor Oliphant, a socially awkward and isolated woman in her thirties. Eleanor’s life is filled with routines and solitude, as she goes through the motions of her mundane existence. However, everything changes when she meets Raymond, an IT guy from her office.

What unfolds is a heartwarming and often heartbreaking tale as Eleanor and Raymond navigate the challenges of life together. Gail Honeyman beautifully delves into Eleanor’s past, gradually unraveling the layers of her troubled upbringing and the impact it has had on her present. Through her unique voice and witty observations, Eleanor’s character comes alive, capturing the readers’ hearts from the very first page.

Honeyman’s writing is a masterclass in storytelling, effortlessly blending humor and poignancy. She tackles sensitive subjects such as trauma, loneliness, and mental health with sensitivity and empathy. Eleanor’s journey towards self-acceptance is both relatable and inspiring, reminding us of the resilience of the human spirit.

One of the standout aspects of ‘Eleanor Oliphant Is Completely Fine’ is the well-crafted supporting characters. From Raymond, the lovable and kind-hearted colleague, to Sammy, an elderly man Eleanor meets during a chance encounter, each character adds depth and richness to the story. Through these relationships, Honeyman explores the power of human connection and the healing that can come from unexpected friendships.

While ‘Eleanor Oliphant Is Completely Fine’ deals with heavy themes, it also celebrates the small joys of life. Honeyman’s vivid descriptions and attention to detail bring Glasgow to life, making it almost a character in itself. From the bustling streets to the cozy pubs, the city becomes the backdrop for Eleanor’s transformation.

This novel is a testament to the resilience of the human spirit and the importance of compassion. It reminds us that no one is completely fine, and that’s okay. We all have our scars and struggles, but it’s through connection and understanding that we can find healing and hope.

In conclusion, ‘Eleanor Oliphant Is Completely Fine’ is a powerful and thought-provoking novel that will stay with you long after you’ve turned the last page. Gail Honeyman’s compelling storytelling and unforgettable characters make this book a must-read. It challenges societal norms and prompts us to question our own perceptions of what it means to be ‘completely fine.’ So, grab a cup of tea, settle into your favorite reading spot, and allow yourself to be captivated by Eleanor’s extraordinary journey of self-discovery.
