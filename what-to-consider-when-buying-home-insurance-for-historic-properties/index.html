What to Consider When Buying Home Insurance for Historic Properties


Owning a historic property is a dream for many homeowners. The charm, character, and rich history that these homes possess can make them truly special places to live. However, along with the unique beauty of historic homes comes a unique set of challenges, particularly when it comes to insuring them.

Buying home insurance for a historic property requires careful consideration and attention to detail. Standard home insurance policies may not provide adequate coverage for the specific risks associated with older homes. Here are some important factors to keep in mind when purchasing insurance for your historic property:

  1. Replacement Cost vs. Actual Cash Value: One of the first decisions you’ll need to make when purchasing home insurance for a historic property is whether to opt for replacement cost coverage or actual cash value coverage. Replacement cost coverage will reimburse you for the full cost of repairing or rebuilding your home, while actual cash value coverage takes depreciation into account, meaning you’ll receive less money for older items. Given the potentially high cost of restoring historic features, replacement cost coverage is often the better option for historic properties.

  2. Specialized Coverage: Historic homes often have unique features and materials that may not be covered by standard insurance policies. Look for insurers that offer specialized coverage for historic properties, including coverage for things like antique fixtures, custom millwork, and historical landscaping. Additionally, consider purchasing coverage for specific risks that are common in older homes, such as coverage for damage from termite infestations or coverage for the cost of complying with local historic preservation regulations.

  3. Appraisal and Documentation: Before purchasing insurance for your historic property, it’s essential to have it properly appraised and documented. This will ensure that you have an accurate understanding of the value of your home and its unique features, which will in turn help you purchase the right amount of insurance coverage. Be sure to work with appraisers and insurance agents who have experience with historic properties and who understand the specific factors that can affect their value.

  4. Local Regulations and Historic Designations: Historic properties are often subject to special regulations and restrictions imposed by local historic preservation boards or other governing bodies. These regulations may dictate what types of modifications you can make to your home, what materials you can use, and even what color you can paint your house. When purchasing insurance for your historic property, be sure to consider how these regulations may impact your coverage needs. For example, you may need to purchase additional coverage to pay for the cost of complying with historic preservation regulations if your home is damaged.

  5. Risk Assessment and Mitigation: Finally, before purchasing insurance for your historic property, it’s important to assess the risks it faces and take steps to mitigate them. For example, older homes may be more susceptible to fire damage due to outdated wiring or heating systems, so investing in modernizing these systems can help reduce your risk and lower your insurance premiums. Similarly, taking steps to protect your home from common risks like water damage or theft can help you qualify for discounts on your insurance premiums.

In conclusion, buying home insurance for a historic property requires careful consideration and attention to detail. By understanding the unique risks associated with older homes and working with experienced professionals, you can ensure that you have the right coverage in place to protect your investment for years to come.
