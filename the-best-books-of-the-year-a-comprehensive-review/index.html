The Best Books of the Year: A Comprehensive Review


As the year comes to an end, it’s time to take a look back at the best books that were published in the last 365 days. From gripping page-turners to insightful non-fiction works, literary fiction to hard-hitting social commentary, the range of titles that have been released this year is truly impressive. So, without further ado, here is a comprehensive review of the best books of 2021.

Fiction

This year has witnessed some extraordinary novels from established and debut authors alike. From esteemed literary authors like Kazuo Ishiguro, Richard Powers and Colson Whitehead to breakout sensations like Maggie Shipstead and Lauren Groff, there’s something for everyone in 2021’s fiction offerings.

One of the most talked-about novels of the year was Maggie Shipstead’s Great Circle. The novel tells the story of Marian Graves, a daring aviator who disappears while attempting to circumnavigate the globe in the early 20th century. Shipstead seamlessly weaves together multiple timelines spanning several decades and continents to create a truly epic tale.

Another standout novel was Richard Powers’ Bewilderment, which explores the bond between a widowed astrobiologist and his troubled son. Powers’ writing is both lyrical and thought-provoking, and he tackles complex themes such as artificial intelligence, extinction and the nature of consciousness with deftness and grace.

And of course, no list of the best fiction of the year would be complete without mentioning Colson Whitehead’s The Harlem Shuffle. This electric crime novel set in 1960s New York was highly anticipated and well worth the wait. Whitehead’s sharp prose and intricate plot kept readers hooked from the first page to the last.

Non-fiction

2021’s non-fiction books were just as impressive as its fiction titles. From investigative journalism to memoirs, there are plenty of works that are both informative and engaging.

One standout title was Four Hundred Souls: A Community History of African America, edited by Ibram X. Kendi and Keisha N. Blain. Kendi and Blain enlisted 90 Black writers to each contribute a short essay about a different year in African American history, covering the 400 years since the arrival of the first enslaved Africans in Virginia. The result is a captivating and comprehensive history of a community that has long been underrepresented.

Another important work of non-fiction this year was Caste: The Origins of Our Discontents by Isabel Wilkerson. This groundbreaking book examines the pervasive effects of caste in American society and invites readers to examine their own biases, shedding light on the deep-rooted systemic issues that continue to plague us.

Finally, readers were treated to a fascinating memoir from a true national treasure: Cicely Tyson’s Just As I Am. In this candid and inspiring book, Tyson reflects on her eight-decade career as an actress, detailing the challenges she faced as a Black woman in Hollywood and sharing intimate stories of her personal life and the influential people she encountered along the way.

Conclusion

2021 has been a truly remarkable year in the world of publishing. From thought-provoking non-fiction to thrilling page-turners, there was no shortage of outstanding books to choose from. Whether you’re looking to expand your knowledge or escape into a captivating story, there’s no doubt that the best books of the year have something for everyone. So why not pick up a few of these titles and see what all the fuss is about? You won’t be disappointed.
