Exploring Lesser-Known Wine Varietals


When it comes to wine, many people are familiar with popular varietals such as Chardonnay, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Merlot. However, there is a whole world of lesser-known wine varietals waiting to be discovered. These underappreciated grapes may not be as famous, but they offer a unique and exciting wine-tasting experience. In this blog post, we will take a closer look at some of these hidden gems and explore why they deserve a spot on your wine rack.

1. Assyrtiko

Originating from the island of Santorini in Greece, Assyrtiko is a white grape variety that produces crisp, refreshing wines. With its high acidity, this grape is excellent for making dry white wines that are known for their citrusy and mineral-driven flavor profile. Assyrtiko can be enjoyed as a standalone wine or paired beautifully with seafood dishes due to its bright acidity and saline notes.

2. Tannat

Originally from Southwest France, Tannat is a red grape variety that has gained popularity in recent years. It is known for its bold, tannic nature, which gives the wine structure and aging potential. Tannat wines often exhibit dark fruit flavors like blackberry and plum, with additional notes of spice and leather. Uruguay has also embraced Tannat, and the country has become a renowned producer of this unique varietal.

3. Verdelho

Originally from Portugal, Verdelho is a white grape varietal that has found a new home in Australia, particularly in the Hunter Valley region. Verdelho wines are vibrant and aromatic, exhibiting tropical fruit flavors like pineapple and passionfruit. They often have a zesty acidity, making them a perfect match for light and fresh seafood dishes or enjoyed on their own on a sunny day.

4. Aglianico

Aglianico is a red grape variety that hails from Southern Italy, specifically the regions of Campania and Basilicata. Often referred to as the ‘Barolo of the South,’ Aglianico produces robust and full-bodied wines with intense dark fruit flavors, such as black cherry, blackberry, and plum. These wines are characterized by their firm tannins and high acidity, which makes them ideal for aging and pairing with hearty meat dishes.

5. Mencia

Mencia is a red grape variety that is native to the northwest region of Spain, particularly the Bierzo region. Mencia wines are known for their vibrant fruit flavors, such as raspberry and red cherry, along with floral and herbal undertones. They usually have a medium body and smooth tannins, making them incredibly approachable and versatile when it comes to food pairing.

By exploring these lesser-known wine varietals, you can expand your palate and discover new and exciting flavors. Whether you enjoy a crisp and refreshing white wine or a bold and robust red, there is a hidden gem waiting for you to uncork. So why not step outside of your comfort zone and give these underappreciated varietals a chance? Your taste buds will thank you!

In conclusion, while it’s always enjoyable to indulge in familiar and popular wine varietals, there is something truly thrilling about diving into the world of lesser-known grapes. From the citrusy Assyrtiko to the robust Aglianico, these varietals offer a range of flavors and experiences that are worth exploring. So the next time you’re at a wine store or ordering at a restaurant, don’t be afraid to step outside your comfort zone and discover the beauty of these hidden gems. Cheers to new horizons and the joy of exploring lesser-known wine varietals!
