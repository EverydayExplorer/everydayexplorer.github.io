Road Less Traveled: Exploring the Best Underrated Destinations


Ah, the joy of traveling - it’s the perfect way to explore new cultures, learn new things, and broaden our perspectives. But while some people prefer the well-beaten path as they traverse the globe, others are interested in exploring the road less traveled - to discover the hidden gems that aren’t yet overrun by tourists. If you’re among the latter group, you’re in for a treat. Here at AAA, we’ve put together a list of the best underrated destinations for you to explore on your next adventure.

1. Tallinn, Estonia
The tiny Baltic country of Estonia is home to Tallinn, a beautiful city with a rich history and culture. This city has a medieval old town with narrow streets, cobbled lanes, and Gothic architecture, making it one of the best-preserved medieval cities in Europe. The city is also famous for its vibrant nightlife and delicious food, where local cafes and restaurants serve traditional Estonian dishes.

2. Luang Prabang, Laos
Nestled on the banks of the Mekong River, Luang Prabang is a small town in Laos that is known for its serene atmosphere and beautiful landscapes. Ancient Buddhist temples, French colonial architecture, and traditional markets make it a place of diverse cultural experiences that give you a glimpse into the past. People in Luang Prabang are friendly and welcoming, and the pace of life is slow, making it the perfect place for a relaxing getaway.

3. Ljubljana, Slovenia
Slovenia is a country that is small in size but large in character, and Ljubljana, the capital city, is no exception. The city has been named the European Green Capital for its eco-friendly policies and practices, making it a unique destination to explore. This vibrant city has a compact historic centre, a picturesque riverfront, and trendy cafes, all of which contribute to its charm. The city’s proximity to nature also allows you to enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and cycling.

4. Gdansk, Poland
Located on the northern coast of Poland, Gdansk is a city that is famous for its historical and cultural significance. This city was once the centre of Baltic trade and stands as a testament to its past with historic monuments and landmarks. Visitors can explore the beautiful old town that is filled with colourful buildings and visit the famous St. Mary’s Church with its intricate architecture.

5. Krabi, Thailand
Krabi is a paradise for beach lovers with its turquoise waters and white sandy beaches. This town in southern Thailand is known for its tranquil beauty, lush greenery, and diverse marine life. Its unique rock formations such as the Railay Beach, Tiger Cave Temple, and Phi Phi Islands make it one of the best destinations for adventure seekers.

6. Cuenca, Ecuador
Located in the Andes Mountains, Cuenca is a city that is known for its colonial architecture and natural beauty. This city is home to some of the best-preserved colonial architecture in South America, which earned it a UNESCO World Heritage site designation. The Tomebamba River, which runs through the city, is also a beautiful sight, and visitors can enjoy the charming cafes and artisan markets dotted around the city.

7. Darjeeling, India
Tucked away in the Himalayan foothills, Darjeeling is a picturesque town that is known for its beautiful tea gardens, panoramic views, and old-world charm. Its pleasant climate all year round makes it the perfect destination for nature lovers and those looking for a peaceful retreat. Darjeeling is also home to the UNESCO World Heritage site, Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, also known as the ‘Toy Train,’ a narrow-gauge train that offers stunning views of the mountains.

8. Colonia del Sacramento, Uruguay
Colonia del Sacramento is a charming port town in Uruguay that is known for its cobbled streets, colonial architecture, and beautiful waterfront. Visitors can walk along the streets and enjoy the colourful buildings and visit the charming cafes and art galleries. The town is also home to a famous lighthouse that provides stunning views of the waterfront.

There you have it, eight of the best underrated destinations to explore on your next adventure. Each of these cities has its unique attractions that make them stand out from the rest. Whether you’re looking for history, culture, nature, or relaxation, these destinations offer all that and much more. So go ahead, explore the road less traveled, and make your next vacation a memorable one.
