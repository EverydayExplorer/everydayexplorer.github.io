Hotels with Unique Architectural Designs: Stay in Style


Are you tired of staying in the same old generic hotels with cookie-cutter designs? If you’re someone who appreciates architecture and loves to immerse yourself in stylish and unique surroundings, then this blog post is for you. In this article, we will take you on a tour of some of the most breathtaking hotels around the world, each with its own distinct architectural design that will leave you in awe. So pack your bags and get ready to stay in style!

1. The Marina Bay Sands, Singapore - When it comes to unique architectural designs, this hotel takes the cake. The most iconic feature of the Marina Bay Sands is its rooftop infinity pool, which stretches across three towers, creating a breathtaking view of the city skyline. Designed by renowned architect Moshe Safdie, this hotel is a masterpiece of modern architecture and offers luxurious accommodations with a touch of elegance.

2. ICEHOTEL, Sweden - If you’re looking for a truly one-of-a-kind experience, the ICEHOTEL in Sweden is the place to be. As the name suggests, this hotel is made entirely out of ice and snow, and each year it is rebuilt to have a completely new design. From ice carvings to ice sculptures, this hotel is a true winter wonderland. Don’t worry about freezing though, as you’ll be provided with warm reindeer skins to keep you cozy during your stay.

3. The Manta Resort, Tanzania - Nestled in the crystal-clear waters of the Indian Ocean, The Manta Resort boasts an underwater hotel room that will make all your mermaid dreams come true. The room is located four meters below the surface and offers a 360-degree view of the marine life surrounding it. Falling asleep to the gentle sway of the ocean waves and waking up to the sight of colorful fish swimming by your window is an experience you won’t forget.

4. Radisson Blu Aqua Hotel, Chicago - If you’re a fan of contemporary architecture, then the Radisson Blu Aqua Hotel in Chicago will be right up your alley. Designed by the award-winning architect Jeanne Gang, this hotel is known for its unique undulating facade. With its distinctive blue color and wave-like shape, the hotel stands out among the city skyline. It offers modern and stylish accommodations, making it the perfect choice for design enthusiasts.

5. The Library, Thailand - Located on the beautiful island of Koh Samui, The Library is a minimalist’s dream. This boutique hotel features a striking red pool that stands out against the white sand beach and turquoise waters. The color scheme continues throughout the hotel, with white and red accents creating a modern and sleek design. If you’re looking for a place to relax and rejuvenate, The Library’s serene atmosphere and unique design will do the trick.

6. Palacio de Sal, Bolivia - Imagine staying in a hotel made entirely out of salt. That’s exactly what you’ll get at Palacio de Sal in Bolivia. From the walls to the furniture, everything in this hotel is made of salt. Located on the Uyuni Salt Flats, this hotel offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and allows you to experience a truly unique and surreal environment.

7. Explora Patagonia Hotel Salto Chico, Chile - Situated in the heart of Torres del Paine National Park, Explora Patagonia Hotel Salto Chico offers guests a chance to immerse themselves in the breathtaking beauty of Patagonia while enjoying luxurious accommodations. The hotel’s design blends seamlessly with the natural surroundings, with large windows that offer panoramic views of mountains, glaciers, and lakes. If you’re an adventure seeker, this hotel is the perfect base for exploring the rugged Patagonian wilderness.

In conclusion, if you’re looking to take your hotel stay to the next level and experience truly unique architectural designs, these hotels around the world are sure to leave a lasting impression. From swimming in an infinity pool high above the city to sleeping underwater or staying in a hotel made entirely of ice, these accommodations offer more than just a place to sleep – they offer an experience that will stay with you long after you check out. So why settle for ordinary when you can stay in style in hotels that are architectural masterpieces?
