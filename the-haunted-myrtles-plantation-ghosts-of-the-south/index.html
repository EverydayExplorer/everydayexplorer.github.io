The Haunted Myrtles Plantation: Ghosts of the South


Have you ever wanted to experience the thrill of the supernatural? Look no further than the infamous Myrtles Plantation, dubbed ‘one of America’s most haunted homes.’ Located in St. Francisville, Louisiana, this stunning plantation is said to be haunted by no less than 12 restless spirits, making it a must-visit for thrill-seekers and paranormal enthusiasts alike.

A Rich History

The Myrtles Plantation has a long and storied history that dates back to the late 18th century. Built in 1796 by General David Bradford, this stunning antebellum home has seen its fair share of tragedies and mysteries. One of the most famous stories revolves around a former slave named Chloe, who was reportedly killed after poisoning members of the family. Chloe’s ghost is said to wander the property, still seeking forgiveness for her actions.

Over the years, the Myrtles Plantation has changed hands numerous times, with each owner leaving their own unique mark on the property. Today, it serves as a bed and breakfast, allowing visitors to not only experience the haunted history but also to spend the night in the very rooms where these ghostly encounters are said to take place.

The Ghostly Encounters

Visitors to the Myrtles Plantation have reported a wide range of paranormal activities. From eerie footsteps and mysterious knocking sounds to unexplained apparitions, there is no shortage of ghostly encounters at this infamous location. Some guests claim to have seen the ghost of a young girl playing on the veranda, while others have heard the sounds of children laughing and running in the dead of night.

One of the most chilling experiences that guests have reported is the sensation of being touched by unseen hands. Many attribute this to the spirits that are said to reside within the haunted walls of the Myrtles Plantation. Phantom smells, cold spots, and objects mysteriously moving on their own are just a few of the other supernatural experiences that visitors have claimed to witness during their stay.

The Haunted Mirror

Perhaps one of the most intriguing aspects of the Myrtles Plantation is the haunted mirror located in the home. Known as the ‘Haunted Mirror of the Myrtles,’ this antique piece is said to harbor the spirits of Sara Woodruff and her children, who died tragically within the home. Visitors who dare to look into the mirror claim to see the apparitions of the deceased family staring back at them.

The haunted mirror has been the subject of much speculation and study, with paranormal enthusiasts from all over the world flocking to the Myrtles Plantation to witness its supernatural effects firsthand. Many believe that the mirror acts as a portal, allowing the spirits to communicate with the living.

Ghostly Gatherings

The Myrtles Plantation has gained a reputation as one of the most haunted locations in the United States. As a result, it has become a popular destination for ghost hunters and paranormal investigators. Regular ghost tours and overnight stays are available for those brave enough to explore the supernatural side of this historic property.

Throughout the year, special events are organized to celebrate the haunted history of the Myrtles Plantation. From Halloween ghost hunts to séances and psychic readings, these events offer an immersive and spine-tingling experience for those seeking a closer encounter with the otherworldly inhabitants of the plantation.

In Conclusion

Whether you’re a believer in the supernatural or simply curious about the ghostly legends that surround the Myrtles Plantation, one thing is for certain – this historic home holds a lot more than just architectural beauty. With its tragic history and documented paranormal activities, it’s no wonder that so many people are drawn to this Southern gem.

So, if you’re ready to embark on a ghostly adventure, consider a visit to the Myrtles Plantation. Be warned, however, that you may leave with much more than just a spine-tingling memory. The spirits that call this place home may just decide to make their presence known in ways you never thought possible.
