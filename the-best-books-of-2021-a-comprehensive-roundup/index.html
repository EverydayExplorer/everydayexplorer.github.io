The Best Books of 2021: A Comprehensive Roundup


With each passing year, numerous books are published, offering readers a chance to delve into new worlds, gain knowledge, and experience different perspectives. As 2021 comes to a close, it’s time to reflect on the best books that have graced our shelves. From gripping novels to thought-provoking non-fiction, there has been no shortage of literary gems in the past year. In this comprehensive roundup, we will highlight some of the standout books that have captured the attention and hearts of readers worldwide.

1. Fiction:

a. ‘Klara and the Sun’ by Kazuo Ishiguro - Nobel laureate Kazuo Ishiguro returned with a mesmerizing tale of an artificial friend named Klara who longs to understand the human world. This thought-provoking narrative explores themes of human connection and the nature of consciousness.

b. ‘Project Hail Mary’ by Andy Weir - From the author of ‘The Martian’ comes another exhilarating science fiction adventure. ‘Project Hail Mary’ follows an astronaut who wakes up alone on a spaceship, with no memory of who he is or how he got there. Weir’s ability to weave science into his storytelling is second to none.

c. ‘The Push’ by Ashley Audrain - Exploring the complexities of motherhood, Audrain’s debut novel grips readers from the first page. It tells the haunting story of a mother who questions the bond she shares with her daughter, raising thought-provoking questions about the nature vs. nurture debate.

2. Non-Fiction:

a. ‘Empire of Pain: The Secret History of the Sackler Dynasty’ by Patrick Radden Keefe - In this meticulously researched investigative work, Keefe delves into the dark history of the Sackler family and their role in the opioid crisis. ‘Empire of Pain’ exposes the greed and deception that fueled the rise of a pharmaceutical empire.

b. ‘Group: How One Therapist and a Circle of Strangers Saved My Life’ by Christie Tate - This memoir chronicles the author’s experience with group therapy and the transformative power of vulnerability. Tate’s candid storytelling resonates deeply, shedding light on the importance of connection and support in our lives.

c. ‘The Code Breaker: Jennifer Doudna, Gene Editing, and the Future of the Human Race’ by Walter Isaacson - Isaacson’s gripping biography explores the life and groundbreaking work of Jennifer Doudna, the scientist behind CRISPR gene-editing technology. This book not only dives into the scientific advancements but also raises ethical questions about the future of gene editing.

3. Mystery/Thriller:

a. ‘The Sanatorium’ by Sarah Pearse - This atmospheric crime thriller takes place in a secluded hotel in the Swiss Alps, where a murder investigation unfolds amidst a snowstorm. Pearse’s ability to create tension and suspense makes this debut novel a must-read for mystery lovers.

b. ‘The Maidens’ by Alex Michaelides - Following the success of ‘The Silent Patient,’ Michaelides delivers another gripping psychological thriller. Set at Cambridge University, the story follows a therapist who becomes entangled in a series of murders, exploring themes of obsession and deception.

c. ‘The Plot’ by Jean Hanff Korelitz - A cleverly plotted tale that blurs the line between reality and fiction, ‘The Plot’ revolves around an aspiring writer who steals the plot for his novel from a deceased student. Korelitz crafts a gripping story that keeps readers guessing until the very end.

4. Young Adult:

a. ‘Concrete Rose’ by Angie Thomas - A prequel to the bestselling ‘The Hate U Give,’ Thomas explores the life of Maverick Carter, the father of the protagonist in her previous novel. Set in the same neighborhood, ‘Concrete Rose’ addresses themes of fatherhood, community, and identity.

b. ‘Firekeeper’s Daughter’ by Angeline Boulley - This powerful debut novel follows a Native American teenager who becomes involved in a murder investigation on her reservation. Boulley weaves together elements of mystery, cultural identity, and social issues, creating a captivating story that resonates with readers of all ages.

c. ‘Wings of Ebony’ by J. Elle - In this electrifying fantasy novel, Elle introduces readers to a young Black girl who discovers her own magic and fights to save her community from injustice. ‘Wings of Ebony’ offers a fresh take on the fantasy genre, blending social commentary with a thrilling narrative.

Conclusion:

The year 2021 has offered an abundance of exceptional books across various genres. From thought-provoking fiction to enlightening non-fiction, these books have captivated readers with their compelling stories and unique perspectives. Whether you enjoy exploring futuristic worlds, unraveling mysteries, or diving into the depths of human emotions, there is a book from 2021 that is sure to entice and engage you. So, grab a cup of tea, cozy up in your favorite reading spot, and immerse yourself in the best books of the year.
