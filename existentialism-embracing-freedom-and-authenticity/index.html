Existentialism: Embracing Freedom and Authenticity

4 min · 743 words

Existentialism is a philosophical movement that arose in the 19th and 20th centuries, and it continues to influence our thinking to this day. At its core, existentialism is concerned with the individual’s experience of existence and the choices they make in a seemingly meaningless or absurd world. In this blog post, we will explore the central themes of existentialism, highlighting the significance of embracing freedom and authenticity in our lives.

The Absurdity of Existence

Existentialism begins with the premise that the world is fundamentally absurd. We are born into a world without inherent meaning or purpose. This absurdity can be overwhelming, as it throws us into a state of existential crisis, questioning the very essence of our existence. However, existentialism doesn’t view this absurdity as a negative aspect of life. Instead, it urges us to confront and embrace this absurdity head-on.

Freedom and Responsibility

Existentialism places great emphasis on the concept of freedom. According to existentialist philosophers like Jean-Paul Sartre, our freedom is absolute. We are not bound by external forces or predetermined destinies. This freedom can be both liberating and terrifying. With freedom comes responsibility; we are accountable for the choices we make and the actions we take.

Embracing our freedom means accepting the burden of responsibility and taking ownership of our lives. We must recognize that we have the power to shape our destiny, and that every decision we make has the potential to shape our future. This acknowledgement is a crucial first step towards living an authentic life.

Authenticity: The Search for Meaning

In the face of life’s inherent absurdity, existentialism invites us to seek our own meaning and purpose. This search for meaning is deeply personal and unique to each individual. Existentialism rejects any external authority or predefined meaning; instead, it encourages us to create our own meaning through our choices and actions.

Living an authentic life means aligning our actions with our core values and beliefs. It involves being true to ourselves, even in the face of social expectations and pressures. Authenticity requires self-reflection and self-awareness, as we strive to understand our true desires and motivations. It is only by living authentically that we can find a sense of fulfillment and purpose in the midst of life’s absurdity.

Existentialist Anxiety

Existentialism acknowledges that the freedom to choose comes with anxiety. The weight of responsibility and the fear of making wrong decisions can be paralyzing. This existential anxiety is inevitable and can be seen as a necessary part of the human condition.

Existentialists argue that instead of avoiding or suppressing anxiety, we should embrace it. Anxiety is an essential part of our existence, and by facing it head-on, we can learn to navigate the complexities of life with greater clarity and authenticity. Embracing anxiety can push us towards growth and self-discovery, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and meaningful existence.

Conclusion

Existentialism provides us with a framework for understanding our existence in a world without intrinsic meaning. By embracing our freedom and taking responsibility for our choices, we can live more authentically. This means creating our own meanings and values, aligning our actions with our core beliefs, and embracing the anxiety that comes with freedom. Existentialism invites us to confront life’s absurdity and find purpose in the midst of it. While the journey may be challenging, the rewards of living an authentic life make it all worthwhile.
