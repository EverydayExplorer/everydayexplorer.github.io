How to Make Natural Cordage for Survival


In survival situations, having the ability to create your own resources is crucial. One essential skill to learn is how to make natural cordage. Cordage is a versatile material that can be used for various purposes such as building shelters, creating traps, crafting tools, and even making clothing. Natural cordage made from plant fibers is not only readily available in most environments, but it is also sustainable and eco-friendly. In this blog post, we will explore different methods of creating natural cordage for survival.

Selecting the Right Plants

When making natural cordage, it is important to select the right type of plant material. Not all plants are suitable for cordage-making, as some may be too brittle or lack sufficient strength. Here are a few plants commonly used for making natural cordage:

1. Stinging Nettle (Urtica Dioica)

Stinging nettle is a versatile plant that can be found in many parts of the world. Its stem fibers are long, strong, and durable, making it an excellent choice for cordage. The fibers of stinging nettle are easily harvested by breaking off the stem and stripping away the outer bark.

2. Common Dogbane (Apocynum Cannabinum)

Also known as Indian Hemp, common dogbane is another plant that produces excellent cordage fiber. The inner bark of the plant is what you’ll be utilizing to create your natural cordage. To harvest, remove the outer bark and then separate the inner fibers.

3. Yucca Plant (Yucca spp.)

Yucca plants are often found in desert regions and have long, fibrous leaves that can be used for cordage-making. To extract the fibers, peel the leaves and scrape away the pulpy material, leaving only the long, white fibers.

Processing the Plant Fibers

Once you have selected the appropriate plants and harvested the necessary fibers, you need to process them to make them suitable for cordage. Here’s a step-by-step guide to processing plant fibers for natural cordage:

  1. Stripping the Bark: Using a sharp knife or your fingernails, remove the outer bark of the plant material. This will expose the inner fibers.

  2. Separating the Fibers: After removing the outer bark, carefully separate the individual fibers. Depending on the plant material, you may need to scrape or peel off excess pulp to reveal the clean fibers.

  3. Twisting and Drying: Take a handful of fibers and twist them together tightly. This will enhance the strength of the cordage. Once twisted, hang the cordage in a cool, dry place to allow it to dry completely. Drying will improve the durability of the cordage and prevent it from rotting.

Techniques for Creating Natural Cordage

Now that we have processed the plant fibers, we can move on to the actual creation of the cordage. There are various techniques for making natural cordage, but two of the most common ones are the reverse wrap technique and the two-ply twist technique.

1. Reverse Wrap Technique

The reverse wrap technique involves twisting the fibers in the opposite direction of their natural twist. This creates a stronger cordage that is less likely to untwist. Here’s how to create cordage using the reverse wrap technique:

  1. Take two strands of twisted fibers and hold them parallel to each other.

  2. Cross the two strands so that they form an ‘X’ shape.

  3. Twist one strand clockwise and the other counterclockwise.

  4. As you twist, start crossing the strands over each other in a clockwise direction.

  5. Continue the twisting and crossing until you reach the desired length of cordage.

2. Two-Ply Twist Technique

The two-ply twist technique involves twisting two strands of fiber in the same direction, creating a stronger cordage. Here’s how to create cordage using the two-ply twist technique:

  1. Take two strands of twisted fibers and hold them parallel to each other.

  2. Twist both strands in the same direction (clockwise or counterclockwise).

  3. Hold one end of the twisted strands and start twisting them together in the opposite direction.

  4. Continue twisting until the two strands of cordage are tightly twisted together.

Conclusion

Being able to make natural cordage is a valuable skill to have in a survival situation. By knowing how to identify plants suitable for cordage-making, processing the fibers correctly, and using various techniques to create the cordage, you can ensure that you always have a reliable source of strong and durable material for your survival needs. So, the next time you find yourself in the wilderness, remember these tips and techniques for making natural cordage.
