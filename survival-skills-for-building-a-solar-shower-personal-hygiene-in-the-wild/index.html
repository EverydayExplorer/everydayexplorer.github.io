Survival Skills for Building a Solar Shower: Personal Hygiene in the Wild


Imagine yourself in the middle of the wilderness, surrounded by towering trees and the symphony of chirping birds. You’ve embarked on an adventure, leaving behind the comforts of modern civilization. As you navigate through nature, one thing becomes increasingly apparent - personal hygiene. While it may not be the first thing that comes to mind when you think of survival skills, maintaining cleanliness is crucial for your overall well-being. In this blog post, we will delve into the art of building a solar shower and explore the importance of personal hygiene in the wild.

The wilderness can be a harsh and unforgiving environment, but with the right knowledge and skills, you can adapt and thrive. One of the most ingenious ways to maintain personal hygiene in the wild is by building a solar shower. A solar shower is a portable device that uses the sun’s energy to heat water, providing you with a refreshing and cleansing experience.

To build a solar shower, you’ll need a few basic materials. Start by finding a sturdy container, such as a large plastic bag or a collapsible water jug. Make sure it has a tight seal to prevent leakage. Next, gather some black plastic or a dark-colored material that can absorb heat effectively. This will serve as the outer layer of your solar shower. Attach the black material to your container, ensuring that it covers the majority of its surface area.

Now, it’s time to find a suitable spot to set up your solar shower. Look for an area that receives ample sunlight throughout the day, such as an open clearing or a sunny patch near your campsite. Hang your solar shower from a tree branch or any other elevated structure, ensuring that it is secure and stable.

Fill your container with water from a nearby source, such as a river or a stream. It’s essential to remember that water in the wilderness may contain harmful bacteria, so be sure to filter or purify it before use. Once your container is filled, seal it tightly to prevent any water from escaping.

Now, it’s time to let the sun work its magic. Place your solar shower in direct sunlight, allowing the dark-colored material to absorb and retain heat. As the sun’s rays penetrate the black material, the water within the container will gradually warm up. Depending on the intensity of the sun, it may take a few hours for the water to reach a comfortable temperature. Patience is key!

While you wait for your solar shower to heat up, take this opportunity to gather other essentials for personal hygiene in the wild. Pack biodegradable soap, shampoo, and a washcloth or sponge. These lightweight and eco-friendly items will help you stay clean without leaving a negative impact on the environment.

Once your water has reached a desirable temperature, it’s time to indulge in a wilderness spa experience. Hang your solar shower at a height that allows water to flow freely. Stand beneath it, and let the warm water cascade over your body, washing away dirt, sweat, and fatigue. Take your time, savoring the rejuvenating feeling of cleanliness in the wild.

Aside from the obvious benefits of personal hygiene, practicing cleanliness in the wild also has psychological and emotional advantages. It can boost your morale and mental well-being, helping you stay positive and focused on your survival journey. Additionally, maintaining personal hygiene reduces the risk of infections, skin irritations, and other health issues that can arise from neglecting cleanliness.

In conclusion, personal hygiene is an essential aspect of survival skills in the wild. Building a solar shower allows you to enjoy the luxury of cleanliness, even in the remotest corners of nature. By harnessing the power of the sun, you can create a simple yet effective method to maintain personal hygiene and promote overall well-being. So, embrace the art of building a solar shower, and let the wild become your personal oasis of cleanliness and comfort.
