The Dos and Don'ts of Applying False Lashes


False lashes can instantly elevate your makeup look, adding drama and intensity to your eyes. But applying false lashes can be a daunting task, especially if you’re a beginner. Don’t worry, though! With the right techniques and a little practice, you’ll become a pro in no time. In this article, we’ll go over the dos and don’ts of applying false lashes, so you can achieve a flawless and natural look every time.

Dos:

1. Choose the Right Lashes

When it comes to false lashes, one size does not fit all. Consider the shape of your eyes and the look you want to achieve. If you have round eyes, opt for lashes that are longer in the center to create a more elongated effect. If you have hooded eyes, choose lashes that are more voluminous to open up your eyes. Experiment with different styles until you find the perfect fit for your eye shape and desired look.

2. Trim the Lashes

Before applying your false lashes, trim them to fit your eye shape. Measure the lash against your natural lash line and trim from the outer corner if necessary. This will ensure that the lashes blend seamlessly with your natural lashes and prevent any discomfort.

3. Use a High-Quality Adhesive

Invest in a good lash adhesive to ensure that your lashes stay in place all day. Look for latex-free adhesives that dry clear for a more natural finish. Apply a thin strip of adhesive along the lash band and wait a few seconds for it to become tacky before applying the lashes. This will help the lashes adhere better and prevent them from sliding around.

4. Apply Lashes Close to the Natural Lash Line

To achieve a natural look, it’s important to apply the lashes as close to your natural lash line as possible. Use tweezers or a lash applicator to carefully place the lashes on your lash line, starting from the outer corner and working your way in. Press gently to secure the lashes in place, making sure they are evenly spaced.

5. Blend with Mascara

After applying your false lashes, blend them with your natural lashes using mascara. This will help create a seamless look and make your lashes appear fuller and more voluminous. Use a mascara wand to coat both your natural lashes and the false lashes, making sure to comb through any clumps for a polished finish.

Don’ts:

1. Apply Too Much Glue

One of the biggest mistakes when applying false lashes is using too much glue. Excess glue can weigh down the lashes, making them uncomfortable to wear and more likely to come off. Apply a thin, even layer of adhesive along the lash band for a secure hold without any excess.

2. Rush the Application Process

Applying false lashes requires patience and precision. Take your time to ensure that the lashes are properly aligned and evenly placed. Rushing the application process can lead to crooked lashes or uneven placement, resulting in an unnatural look. Set aside enough time to carefully apply your lashes, especially if you’re a beginner.

3. Neglect Your Natural Lashes

While false lashes can enhance your look, it’s important not to neglect your natural lashes. Remove your false lashes gently at the end of the day using an oil-free makeup remover. Avoid pulling or tugging on the lashes, as this can damage your natural lashes. Give your natural lashes a break from false lashes occasionally to allow them to breathe and regenerate.

4. Forget to Clean and Store Your Lashes

To extend the lifespan of your false lashes, it’s essential to clean and store them properly. After removing your lashes, gently remove any excess glue using tweezers or your fingers. Place the lashes back into the original packaging or a clean, dry container to prevent them from getting damaged or losing their shape. Cleaning and storing your lashes correctly will ensure that they can be reused multiple times.

5. Overdo the Length or Volume

When choosing false lashes, it’s important to keep in mind that less is often more. Overdoing the length or volume of your lashes can make them look unnatural and overpowering. Opt for lashes that enhance your natural beauty rather than overpowering it. Remember, the goal is to achieve a natural and seamless blend between your natural lashes and the false lashes.

Applying false lashes can be a fun and creative way to enhance your eye makeup. By following these dos and don’ts, you’ll be well on your way to achieving a flawless and natural look every time. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don’t be discouraged if it takes a few tries to master the application technique. With time and patience, you’ll become a false lash pro in no time!
