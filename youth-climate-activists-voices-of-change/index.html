Youth Climate Activists: Voices of Change


In recent years, youth climate activists have taken center stage in the fight against climate change. Young people around the world are mobilizing to demand action from their governments and individuals alike to address the urgent threat of climate change.

The voices of these young activists are powerful and urgent, and they are amplifying the calls of scientists and environmentalists who have been warning about the dangers of climate change for decades. Here are some of the voices and stories of youth climate activists who are leading the charge for change.

Greta Thunberg

One of the most well-known and influential youth climate activists is Greta Thunberg, a 17-year-old from Sweden. Thunberg first gained international attention in August 2018 when she began skipping school to sit outside the Swedish parliament to demand action on climate change. Her protests spread, and in 2019 she traveled to the United States to speak at the United Nations Climate Action Summit and meet with lawmakers.

Thunberg’s powerful speeches have inspired millions of young people around the world to join the fight against climate change, and she continues to be a prominent voice in the movement for climate action.

Xiuhtezcatl Martinez

Xiuhtezcatl Martinez is a 20-year-old indigenous environmental activist from Colorado. He has been working on environmental issues since he was six years old and became a youth director of the environmental group Earth Guardians at age 15.

Martinez has been involved in lobbying lawmakers and organizing protests to demand action on climate change. He also delivered a speech at the United Nations in 2015 when he was just 15 years old, and his activism has been recognized with numerous awards and accolades.

Isra Hirsi

Isra Hirsi is a 17-year-old from Minnesota who co-founded the U.S. Youth Climate Strike in 2019. Hirsi’s passion for climate activism comes from her concern for her future and the futures of her peers.

As a daughter of Ilhan Omar, a Somali-born American politician, she experienced first hand the challenges that marginalized communities face when it comes to environmental justice. She believes that young people have a responsibility to speak out on environmental issues.

Vanessa Nakate

Vanessa Nakate is a 23-year-old from Uganda who is leading the fight against climate change in Africa. Nakate founded the Rise Up Movement in 2019 to demand climate action from the governments of Africa. She has been involved in organizing protests and lobbying lawmakers across the continent.

Nakate’s activism has put her in the global spotlight, and she has been featured in numerous media outlets and received accolades for her leadership and dedication to the cause.

Jamie Margolin

Jamie Margolin is an 18-year-old climate activist from Seattle who founded the Zero Hour movement in 2017. Zero Hour is a youth-led organization that is working to center the voices of young people in the fight for climate action.

Margolin has been involved in organizing protests and lobbying lawmakers, and she has also written a book, ‘Youth to Power: Your Voice and How to Use It,’ which encourages young people to get involved in activism and make their voices heard.

These are just a few of the inspiring young people who are leading the fight against climate change. Their voices are powerful and impactful, and they are making an enormous difference in the global effort to address this urgent issue.

By elevating the voices of these young people and supporting their activism, we can create a better future for ourselves and for generations to come. We must listen to their calls for change and take action to protect our planet and our future.
