Survival Water Sources: How to Find and Purify Water in the Wild


When it comes to surviving in the wild, one of the most important skills to have is the ability to find and purify water. Water is essential for our survival, and being able to locate and make it safe to drink can mean the difference between life and death in a survival situation. In this blog post, we will explore various sources of water in the wild and the methods to purify it.

1. Natural Water Sources
First and foremost, knowing where to find water sources in the wild is crucial. These sources may include rivers, streams, lakes, and ponds. Additionally, keep an eye out for any signs of animal tracks or vegetation, as they can often lead to water sources. In desert environments, look for low-lying areas or depressions where water may collect after rainfall.

2. Rainwater
Rainwater is a valuable resource that can be collected and used for drinking. If you have a plastic sheet or tarp, you can fashion a makeshift rain catchment system. Simply secure the sheet between two trees, creating a slope, and place a container at the lowest point to collect the water. Remember to drink the water only after purifying it.

3. Groundwater
In certain situations, finding groundwater may be your best option. To locate groundwater, look for areas with lush vegetation or dig a hole in a sandy area. Wait for water to fill up the hole and then use a cloth or piece of clothing to filter out any debris before consuming.

4. Natural Springs
Natural springs are areas where groundwater reaches the surface. They are typically clean sources of water, but it is important to purify the water before drinking to eliminate any potential bacteria. Springs can often be found at the base of hills or mountains, and signs of vegetation can indicate their presence.

5. Plants and Trees
Believe it or not, some plants and trees can yield water that can be harvested for survival purposes. For example, certain vines can be cut and used as straws to access the moisture inside. However, it is crucial to learn about the local plant life before attempting to extract water in this manner, as some plants can be toxic.

Now that we have covered various survival water sources, let’s discuss methods to purify water in the wild.

1. Boiling
Boiling water is one of the most effective ways to kill harmful bacteria and parasites. Bring the water to a rolling boil for at least one minute (or three minutes at higher altitudes) to ensure it is safe to drink. Be aware that boiling water does not remove chemical contaminants, so this method is best used in conjunction with other purification techniques.

2. Filtration
Water filtration is another method that can be employed to remove impurities. Different types of water filters are available, ranging from simple portable filters to more advanced systems. These filters work by physically trapping impurities and can effectively remove bacteria, parasites, and sediment. However, they may not be able to eliminate viruses, so it is important to check the specifications of the filter you choose.

3. Chemical Treatment
Chemical treatments such as iodine or chlorine tablets are portable and easy to use in situations where boiling or filtration isn’t possible. These chemicals kill most bacteria, viruses, and parasites, making the water safe to consume. However, it is crucial to follow the instructions provided with the chemical treatment to ensure proper dosing and wait time.

4. Solar Disinfection
Solar disinfection, also known as SODIS, is a method that uses the power of the sun to kill harmful microorganisms. It involves filling a clear plastic bottle with water and placing it in direct sunlight for several hours. This method is effective against bacteria, viruses, and parasites, but it may not remove chemical contaminants.

In conclusion, knowing how to find and purify water in the wild is vital for survival. Remember to always prioritize finding a water source, purify it using the methods outlined above, and use your judgment when assessing the safety of the water. Being prepared and knowledgeable about water sources and purification techniques will greatly increase your chances of staying hydrated and healthy while in the wilderness. Stay safe and always prioritize your water needs in any survival situation.
