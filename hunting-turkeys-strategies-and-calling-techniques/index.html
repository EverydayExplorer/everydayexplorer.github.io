Hunting Turkeys: Strategies and Calling Techniques


Turkey hunting is a thrilling and challenging sport that requires skill, patience, and the right techniques. Whether you’re a seasoned hunter or new to the game, understanding the strategies and calling techniques can significantly increase your chances of a successful hunt. In this blog post, we will delve into the world of turkey hunting, sharing valuable insights and tips to help you bag that elusive gobbler.

1. Understanding Turkey Behavior:

Before you embark on your turkey hunting adventure, it’s crucial to have a good understanding of turkey behavior. Turkeys have keen eyesight and exceptional hearing, making them highly aware of their surroundings. They are social creatures that communicate through a variety of calls, ranging from gobbles to yelps. By learning to interpret these calls and understanding their behavior patterns, you can increase your chances of getting within range.

2. Scout Your Hunting Area:

Successful turkey hunting starts with scouting. Begin by identifying areas with high turkey populations, such as open fields, roosting sites, or feeding areas. Look for signs of turkey activity, such as tracks, droppings, and scratching in the ground. By scouting your hunting area ahead of time, you can pinpoint the best spots to set up your blinds or tree stands.

3. Master the Art of Turkey Calling:

Calling is an essential aspect of turkey hunting, as it allows you to mimic the sounds of a hen and attract gobblers. There are various types of turkey calls, including box calls, slate calls, mouth calls, and diaphragm calls. Each call requires practice and finesse to produce realistic hen sounds. Experiment with different calls and find the one that suits your style and produces the most authentic sounds.

4. Know When to Call:

Timing is everything when it comes to turkey hunting. Understanding when to call and how frequently can make a significant difference in your success rate. Start your hunt by using soft, subtle calls to locate and elicit a response from gobblers. Once you’ve established their presence, switch to more aggressive calls to entice them closer. Remember, turkeys have exceptional hearing, so use calls sparingly and be patient.

5. Concealment and Setup:

Turkeys have exceptional eyesight, so concealing yourself and your hunting blind or tree stand is crucial. Use natural cover such as bushes, trees, or terrain features to break up your outline and blend into the surroundings. Set up your decoys strategically to create a realistic scene that will attract curious gobblers. Consider using a combination of hen and jake decoys to spark competition among gobblers.

6. Practice Safe Hunting:

Safety should always be a top priority when engaging in any form of hunting. Always wear blaze orange or other highly visible clothing to ensure you’re visible to other hunters in the area. Be aware of your surroundings, and never stalk a turkey or shoot at sounds or movement. Proper identification of your target is crucial to avoid accidents and ensure a safe hunting experience.

In conclusion, turkey hunting is an exhilarating pursuit that requires a combination of skill, knowledge, and patience. By understanding turkey behavior, mastering the art of calling, scouting your hunting area, and practicing safe hunting techniques, you can increase your chances of a successful hunt. Remember, each hunting experience is unique, and learning from each outing will only enhance your skills as a turkey hunter. So grab your gear, head out into the woods, and may the gobblers be in your favor!
