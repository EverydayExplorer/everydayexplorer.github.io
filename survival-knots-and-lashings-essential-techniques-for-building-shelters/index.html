Survival Knots and Lashings: Essential Techniques for Building Shelters


When it comes to surviving in the great outdoors, one of the most essential skills to have is knowing how to tie survival knots and lashings. These techniques are not only important for securing gear and equipment, but they are also crucial for building shelters in emergency situations. In this post, we will cover some of the most essential survival knots and lashings that you should know to effectively build a shelter and increase your chances of survival in the wilderness.

  1. Square Knot: The square knot, also known as a reef knot, is a versatile and widely used knot for joining two ropes together. It is easy to tie and untie, making it a valuable knot for a variety of applications. To tie a square knot, simply cross the two ends of the rope, with one end going over the other, then pass one end under and through the loop created. Repeat the process with the other end going in the opposite direction.

  2. Clove Hitch: The clove hitch is a quick and easy knot for securing a rope to a pole or tree. It is often used in conjunction with other knots to create a stable structure. To tie a clove hitch, simply wrap the rope around the pole or tree, cross the rope over itself, then tuck it under the first loop created.

  3. Sheet Bend: The sheet bend is a knot used for joining two ropes of different thicknesses together. It is a secure knot that is easy to tie and untie, making it a valuable skill in survival situations where you may need to combine ropes for building a shelter. To tie a sheet bend, create a loop with one rope and pass the other rope through the loop, then wrap it around both ropes and tuck it under itself.

  4. Taut Line Hitch: The taut line hitch is a knot used for securing a rope to a pole or tree while allowing for easy adjustment and tightening. It is a valuable knot for setting up tarps or shelters that need to be taut and secure. To tie a taut line hitch, wrap the rope around the pole, then create a loop and pass the rope through it three times before tightening.

Now that you know some essential survival knots, let’s discuss lashings. Lashings are used to secure poles or branches together to create a framework for shelters. There are several types of lashings that are commonly used in survival situations, including:

  • Square Lashing: The square lashing is used to secure two poles at a right angle to each other. It is a strong and stable lashing that is essential for building the foundation of a shelter. To tie a square lashing, cross the two poles at a right angle, then wrap the rope around both poles in a figure-eight pattern before finishing with a clove hitch.

  • Diagonal Lashing: The diagonal lashing is used to secure two poles at a diagonal angle to each other. It is a versatile lashing that can be used to create a variety of shapes and structures for shelters. To tie a diagonal lashing, cross the two poles at a diagonal angle, then wrap the rope around both poles in a figure-eight pattern before finishing with a clove hitch.

  • Tripod Lashing: The tripod lashing is used to secure three poles together at one end to create a tripod structure. It is a simple and effective lashing that is commonly used for building shelters and cooking structures. To tie a tripod lashing, gather three poles together at one end, then wrap the rope around all three poles in a figure-eight pattern before finishing with a clove hitch.

By mastering these essential survival knots and lashings, you will be better equipped to build secure and stable shelters in emergency situations. Remember to practice these skills regularly so that you can confidently rely on them when facing challenges in the wilderness. Stay safe and prepared, and happy adventuring!
