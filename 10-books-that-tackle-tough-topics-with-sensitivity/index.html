10 Books That Tackle Tough Topics with Sensitivity


In today’s world, it is important to explore and discuss tough topics that affect individuals and society as a whole. Books have the power to tackle these issues head-on, offering insights and perspectives that can foster empathy and understanding. This blog post will highlight 10 books that handle tough topics with sensitivity, allowing readers to explore and engage in meaningful conversations.

  1. ‘The Hate U Give’ by Angie Thomas: This powerful novel tackles the issue of police violence and systemic racism through the eyes of Starr Carter, a young black girl who witnesses the shooting of her unarmed friend by a police officer. Thomas’s heartfelt storytelling and authentic portrayal of the aftermath make this book a must-read for anyone interested in understanding the Black Lives Matter movement.

  2. ‘Speak’ by Laurie Halse Anderson: Addressing the difficult topic of sexual assault, ‘Speak’ follows the journey of Melinda, a high school student who becomes a social outcast after being raped at a summer party. Anderson’s storytelling skillfully portrays the emotional turmoil and stigma surrounding this issue, emphasizing the importance of finding one’s voice and seeking justice.

  3. ‘Thirteen Reasons Why’ by Jay Asher: Dealing with themes of teen suicide and bullying, this controversial novel explores the reasons behind a young girl’s decision to end her life through a series of cassette tapes. Asher’s narrative forces readers to confront the impact of their actions and highlights the urgent need for empathy and kinder interactions.

  4. ‘The Fault in Our Stars’ by John Green: This heart-rending story delves into the world of two teenagers, Hazel and Gus, who are battling cancer. Green’s portrayal of their love story juxtaposes the fragility of life with the strength of the human spirit, reminding readers of the value of cherishing every moment.

  5. ‘Educated: A Memoir’ by Tara Westover: In this memoir, Westover recounts her journey from an isolated, abusive family living in rural Idaho to obtaining a Ph.D. from Cambridge University. ‘Educated’ tackles the theme of educational opportunity and the potential consequences of being raised in a closed-off environment. Westover’s resilience and determination to rise above her circumstances inspire readers to challenge their own limitations.

  6. ‘The Kite Runner’ by Khaled Hosseini: Set against the backdrop of war-torn Afghanistan, this novel explores the themes of guilt, redemption, and betrayal. Hosseini’s gripping storytelling exposes the complex nature of friendship, loyalty, and the effects of violence on individuals and communities. ‘The Kite Runner’ offers a profound understanding of cultural divides and the power of forgiveness.

  7. ‘Small Great Things’ by Jodi Picoult: Tackling the sensitive issue of racial prejudice and white supremacy, Picoult’s novel follows the story of Ruth, a black labor and delivery nurse, who is accused of causing the death of a white supremacist couple’s newborn baby. Through multiple perspectives, Picoult prompts readers to question their own biases and to confront the institutional racism deeply ingrained in society.

  8. ‘The Perks of Being a Wallflower’ by Stephen Chbosky: This coming-of-age novel explores the often-taboo topics of mental health, trauma, and the struggles of adolescence. Through the eyes of the introverted protagonist, Charlie, readers gain insight into the challenges of finding one’s place in the world and the importance of genuine connections.

  9. ‘The Color Purple’ by Alice Walker: Walker’s Pulitzer Prize-winning novel addresses themes of racism, sexism, and abuse through the eyes of Celie, an African American woman in the early 20th century. By highlighting the resilience and strength of the human spirit, ‘The Color Purple’ emphasizes the power of self-acceptance and overcoming adversity.

  10. ‘Between the World and Me’ by Ta-Nehisi Coates: In this powerful memoir written as a letter to his son, Coates reflects on the experience of being a black man in America and confronts the country’s history of violence and systemic racism. This book prompts readers to question their own privilege and to engage in conversations surrounding racial justice.

These 10 books offer a diverse range of perspectives and voices that tackle tough topics with sensitivity. By delving into these narratives, readers can gain a deeper understanding of the complexities surrounding issues such as racism, mental health, sexual assault, and more. Through empathy and awareness, literature has the power to foster change and create a more compassionate society.
