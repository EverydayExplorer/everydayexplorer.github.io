The Evolution of Dance in Music Videos: From Thriller to Twerk


Music videos have long been avenues for artists to showcase not just their musical talent but also their dance moves. Over the years, we have seen dance evolve in music videos, reflecting the changing trends and cultural norms of society. From Michael Jackson’s iconic ‘Thriller’ to the recent rise of twerking, this article takes a closer look at the fascinating evolution of dance in music videos.

1. The Birth of Dance in Music Videos

The introduction of dance in music videos can be traced back to the 1980s when MTV became a prominent platform for showcasing music videos. Artists started incorporating dance routines into their videos to engage viewers visually and enhance the overall performance. One of the most significant milestones during this time was Michael Jackson’s ‘Thriller’ released in 1983, which set a new standard for dance in music videos.

2. The Pop and Lock Era

Following the success of ‘Thriller,’ the 1980s witnessed the rise of pop and lock dance moves. Artists like MC Hammer popularized the infectious dance style with his hit single ‘U Can’t Touch This’ in 1990, influencing a generation of dancers. The pop and lock style involved sharp, intricate movements that captivated audiences and became a defining characteristic of music videos during this period.

3. The Emergence of Hip-Hop Dance

As the 1990s rolled in, hip-hop music and dance began to dominate the music scene. Artists such as Puff Daddy (now known as Diddy) and Missy Elliott brought hip-hop dance to the forefront of music videos. In the video for ‘Mo Money Mo Problems’ released in 1997, Puff Daddy showcased his dancing skills alongside fellow artists Biggie Smalls and Mase, solidifying hip-hop dance as a vital element of music videos.

4. Dance as Expression

The early 2000s saw dance in music videos evolve from being just a routine to a form of artistic expression. Artists like Beyoncé, Justin Timberlake, and Usher began incorporating elaborate choreographies into their videos, elevating dance to a new level. Beyoncé’s ‘Single Ladies (Put a Ring on It)’ video released in 2008 became a global phenomenon, showcasing synchronized dance moves that fans all over the world tried to emulate.

5. The Rise of Social Media Dance Challenges

With the advent of social media platforms like TikTok and Instagram, dance challenges have become a significant part of the music video landscape. Artists now release videos with specific dance moves, encouraging fans to participate and create their versions using hashtags. This interactive approach has enabled fans to feel more connected to the artist, resulting in greater exposure for both the song and the dance.

6. Twerking and its Impact

In recent years, one particular dance move has become synonymous with music videos – twerking. Originating from African dance styles, twerking gained widespread attention after Miley Cyrus’s controversial performance at the 2013 MTV Video Music Awards. Since then, artists like Nicki Minaj, Cardi B, and Megan Thee Stallion have popularized twerking in their music videos, pushing the boundaries of artistic expression and provoking discussions about sexuality and feminism.

In conclusion, dance in music videos has come a long way since Michael Jackson’s groundbreaking ‘Thriller’ video. From the birth of dance in music videos in the 1980s to the emergence of hip-hop dance in the 1990s and the recent rise of twerking, we have witnessed dance evolve as a powerful form of expression and cultural commentary. As artists continue to innovate and push boundaries, we can only anticipate what new dance trends will emerge in future music videos.

-–

By exploring the evolution of dance in music videos, we have witnessed how influential these videos have been in shaping popular culture. From Thriller to twerking, each dance trend has left its mark on the music industry and society as a whole. As artists and dancers continue to innovate and push boundaries, we can look forward to witnessing further evolutions in dance and music videos in the years to come.
