Before hitting the trails, remember to take a few precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable hiking experience for both you and your pet:

- Check if the trail has any specific rules or regulations regarding pets. Some trails may require dogs to be on a leash or have restrictions during certain seasons.

- Pack essentials such as water, snacks, and a first aid kit for both you and your pet. It’s also a good idea to bring along some waste bags to clean up after your furry companion.

- Keep an eye on the weather forecast and be prepared for any changes in conditions. Extreme heat or cold can be dangerous for your pet, so plan your hike accordingly.

- Take breaks during your hike to allow your pet to rest and rehydrate. Remember, they may not have the same endurance as you, so it’s important to consider their well-being.
