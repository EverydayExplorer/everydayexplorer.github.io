Wine and Literature: Iconic References and Pairings


When it comes to pairing wine and literature, we are taken on a delightful journey that combines the pleasures of the written word with the sensory experience of a well-crafted wine. Both wine and literature possess the power to transport us to different times, places, and emotions. Through the ages, many literary works have made references to wine, and in turn, wine has been associated with certain iconic literary moments. In this blog post, we will explore some of these references and discover the perfect wine pairings for these literary classics.

1. Ernest Hemingway’s ‘A Moveable Feast’ and Champagne:
Ernest Hemingway, known for his love of adventure and fine beverages, famously wrote about his experiences in Paris during the 1920s in his memoir, ‘A Moveable Feast.’ In this book, Hemingway captures the essence of the Parisian bohemian lifestyle, often accompanied by the clinking of Champagne glasses. To honor Hemingway’s love for Champagne and the vibrant literary scene of 1920s Paris, we recommend pairing this memoir with a crisp and elegant Champagne. The effervescence of the bubbles perfectly complements the lively spirit of Hemingway’s writing.

2. F. Scott Fitzgerald’s ‘The Great Gatsby’ and Bordeaux:
‘The Great Gatsby,’ F. Scott Fitzgerald’s masterpiece set during the Jazz Age, is a tale of decadence, wealth, and unfulfilled dreams. The characters in the novel frequently indulge in extravagant parties, featuring a never-ending flow of wines and spirits. To capture the essence of this iconic novel, we suggest pairing it with a rich and sophisticated Bordeaux. The complex flavors of a Bordeaux blend echo the depth and complexity of the characters in Fitzgerald’s tale.

3. Jane Austen’s ‘Pride and Prejudice’ and English Sparkling Wine:
Jane Austen’s novels are beloved for their wit, social commentary, and romantic plotlines. In ‘Pride and Prejudice,’ the characters often gather for elegant social events, where sparkling wine plays a prominent role. To enhance your reading experience, we recommend sipping on a glass of English sparkling wine while immerse yourself in the world of Mr. Darcy and Elizabeth Bennet. English sparkling wines, crafted with the traditional method used in Champagne production, offer a delightful effervescence that perfectly complements the charm and elegance of Austen’s writing.

4. Charles Baudelaire’s ‘Les Fleurs du Mal’ and Vin de Sauternes:
‘Les Fleurs du Mal’ is a seminal collection of poetry by Charles Baudelaire, infused with themes of eroticism, decadence, and beauty. To fully appreciate the dark and sensual atmosphere that Baudelaire creates, pair this literary masterpiece with a glass of Vin de Sauternes. This luscious and golden dessert wine from the Bordeaux region of France brings a touch of sweetness and intrigue to the reading experience, just like the intoxicating verses of Baudelaire.

5. William Shakespeare’s Sonnets and Port:
Shakespeare’s sonnets, known for their lyrical beauty and exploration of love and mortality, are a treasure trove of poetic brilliance. To accompany the sonnets’ emotional depth, we suggest sipping on a glass of Port wine. This fortified wine from the Douro Valley of Portugal offers a rich and robust flavor profile that pairs harmoniously with the introspective and passionate nature of Shakespeare’s verses.

In conclusion, the combination of wine and literature creates a sensory experience that delights both the mind and the palate. By exploring the iconic references to wine in literature, we can enhance our reading experience and indulge in the flavors that mirror the emotions and themes of each literary work. So, whether you’re enjoying a glass of Champagne while delving into the literary world of Hemingway or savoring a rich Bordeaux blend while immersing yourself in the decadence of ‘The Great Gatsby,’ remember to raise your glass to the timeless connection between wine and literature. Cheers!
