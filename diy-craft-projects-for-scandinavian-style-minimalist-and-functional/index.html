DIY Craft Projects for Scandinavian Style: Minimalist and Functional


Scandinavian design is renowned for its minimalist aesthetic, clean lines, and functional approach to living spaces. Embracing simplicity, natural elements, and a sense of coziness, Scandinavian style has become increasingly popular in home decor. If you’re looking to infuse your home with the timeless charm of Scandinavian design, why not try your hand at some DIY craft projects? In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore a variety of DIY ideas to help you achieve the minimalist and functional look of Scandinavian style in your home.

1. Wooden Wall Hooks

Create stylish and functional wooden wall hooks to organize your entryway or hallway. Start by selecting a piece of untreated or lightly stained wood. Cut it into equal-sized pieces, sanding the edges for a smooth finish. Then, drill holes for mounting and attach metal hooks. Arrange the hooks in a row or cluster on your wall for a practical yet visually appealing storage solution.

2. Concrete Planters

Bring a touch of Scandinavian minimalism to your indoor garden with DIY concrete planters. Mix concrete according to package instructions and pour it into silicone molds of various shapes and sizes. Insert small plastic containers or cups into the wet concrete to create space for plants. Once dry, remove the molds and containers, and sand the edges for a smooth finish. Plant your favorite greenery or succulents for a contemporary and natural accent to your home decor.

3. Linen Pillow Covers

Elevate your living room or bedroom with handmade linen pillow covers inspired by Scandinavian simplicity. Cut linen fabric to the desired size for your pillows, leaving extra fabric for seams. Sew the fabric together, leaving one side open for inserting the pillow form. Turn the cover inside out, insert the pillow form, and hand-stitch the opening closed for a clean finish. Opt for neutral colors like white, beige, or light gray to maintain the minimalist aesthetic.

4. Geometric Wall Art

Create striking geometric wall art pieces to add visual interest to your walls. Start by cutting plywood or MDF boards into geometric shapes such as triangles, hexagons, or squares. Paint each shape in a different shade of white, gray, or pastel to create a cohesive yet dynamic look. Arrange the shapes on your wall in a geometric pattern, using adhesive strips or nails to secure them in place. This DIY project will instantly elevate the style of any room with its modern and minimalist appeal.

5. Scandi-Inspired Macrame Wall Hanging

Channel the cozy vibes of Scandinavian hygge with a handmade macrame wall hanging. Learn basic macrame knots such as the square knot, half knot, and lark’s head knot to create a textured and visually appealing design. Use natural cotton or linen cord in neutral tones to complement the Scandinavian aesthetic. Hang your macrame creation above a bed, sofa, or reading nook for a touch of warmth and texture.

6. Leather Magazine Holder

Keep your reading materials organized in style with a DIY leather magazine holder. Cut pieces of leather or faux leather into rectangles, then fold and glue them into pockets of equal size. Attach the pockets to a wooden or metal frame using adhesive or rivets. Hang the magazine holder on your wall or place it next to your favorite reading chair for easy access to magazines, books, and newspapers.

7. Birch Bark Candle Holders

Add a rustic Scandinavian touch to your tabletops with DIY birch bark candle holders. Collect pieces of birch bark from fallen branches or purchase them from a craft store. Cut the bark into strips and wrap them around glass votive holders, securing them in place with twine or glue. Place tea light candles inside the holders and arrange them as a centerpiece on your dining table or mantel for a cozy and natural ambiance.

8. Knit Throw Blanket

Cozy up on chilly evenings with a handmade knit throw blanket in a classic Scandinavian pattern. Choose soft and chunky yarn in neutral tones like cream, beige, or light gray. Follow a simple knitting pattern to create a textured design such as stripes, chevrons, or diamonds. Once complete, drape your knit throw over a sofa or armchair to add warmth and comfort to your living space.

9. Wooden Coasters

Protect your furniture in style with DIY wooden coasters inspired by Scandinavian design. Cut slices of wood from fallen branches or purchase pre-cut wooden discs from a craft store. Sand the edges and surface of each disc to remove any roughness. Paint or stain the wood in natural tones or Scandinavian-inspired patterns such as stripes or geometric shapes. Seal the coasters with a clear varnish for durability and use them to set drinks on your coffee table or dining table.

10. Hanging Herb Garden

Bring the freshness of nature indoors with a hanging herb garden crafted from wooden crates or metal baskets. Line the bottom of each crate or basket with plastic liners to prevent water damage. Fill them with potting soil and plant your favorite herbs such as basil, mint, and rosemary. Hang the herb gardens near a sunny window in your kitchen for easy access to fresh herbs while cooking, adding both functionality and beauty to your space.

In conclusion, DIY craft projects offer a fun and creative way to incorporate the minimalist and functional elements of Scandinavian style into your home decor. From handmade textiles to natural accents, these DIY ideas will help you create a warm and inviting atmosphere inspired by the timeless appeal of Scandinavian design. So roll up your sleeves, gather your materials, and get ready to infuse your home with the understated elegance of Scandinavian style through these delightful DIY projects.
