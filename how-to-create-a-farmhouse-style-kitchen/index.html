How to Create a Farmhouse Style Kitchen


Are you looking to revamp your kitchen into a warm and inviting space? Look no further than the charming and timeless appeal of a farmhouse style kitchen. With its rustic elements, vintage accents, and cozy atmosphere, a farmhouse style kitchen can transform your cooking space into a welcoming retreat.

In this blog post, we will delve into the key elements and design tips to help you create a farmhouse style kitchen that exudes comfort and character. Whether you live in a country cottage or a modern urban apartment, these tips can be easily adapted to suit your space and personal style.

1. Choose Warm and Natural Colors

Farmhouse style kitchens are known for their warm and earthy color palettes. Opt for neutral shades like creams, beiges, and soft greys for your kitchen walls. These colors evoke a cozy and lived-in feel, creating the perfect backdrop for the rustic elements that will be incorporated later on.

2. Utilize Reclaimed and Distressed Materials

To achieve an authentic farmhouse look, consider incorporating reclaimed and distressed materials into your kitchen design. For example, opt for reclaimed wood for your kitchen cabinets or kitchen island. The natural patina and imperfections of reclaimed wood add character and charm to the space.

3. Install Open Shelves

Open shelves are a hallmark of farmhouse style kitchens. They not only create a visually appealing display of your kitchenware but also provide easy access to your frequently-used items. Choose rustic wooden shelves or metal brackets to add to the farmhouse aesthetic.

4. Mix Different Textures

To create visual interest, mix different textures in your farmhouse kitchen. Consider adding a subway tile backsplash, a vintage farmhouse sink, or a distressed wooden dining table. By incorporating various textures, you can add depth and character to your space.

5. Opt for a Farmhouse Sink

A farmhouse sink, also known as an apron sink, is a staple in farmhouse style kitchens. These deep and wide sinks not only provide practicality but also act as a focal point in the kitchen. Choose a white or porcelain sink to maintain the classic farmhouse look.

6. Use vintage accessories

Accessorizing is the key to creating an authentic farmhouse style kitchen. Look for antique pieces such as mason jars, metal baskets, and vintage signs to add a touch of nostalgia to your space. These accessories not only add character but also serve a functional purpose, such as storing kitchen utensils or displaying fresh flowers.

7. Incorporate Open Shelving

Farmhouse kitchens often feature open shelving, which can showcase your collection of vintage dishware, glassware, and cookbooks. Opt for floating wooden shelves or wire mesh shelves to add a rustic touch while keeping your essentials within easy reach.

8. Choose the right lighting

Lighting plays a crucial role in setting the mood in any space, and a farmhouse kitchen is no exception. Opt for pendant lights with industrial or vintage-inspired designs to complement the rustic aesthetic. Additionally, consider installing a statement chandelier above your dining table to add a touch of elegance to the space.

9. Add a touch of greenery

Adding a touch of greenery can breathe life into your farmhouse style kitchen. Consider placing potted herbs or small plants on your windowsill, or hang a macrame plant hanger from the ceiling. Not only will this add a pop of color, but it will also create a fresh and inviting atmosphere.

10. Keep it cozy

Lastly, don’t forget to add cozy elements to your farmhouse kitchen. Opt for a farmhouse-style kitchen table with mismatched chairs, soft cushions, and a tablecloth. Add a plush area rug in front of the sink or beneath the dining table to keep your feet warm and add extra comfort to the space.

In conclusion, creating a farmhouse style kitchen is all about combining warm, natural colors, reclaimed materials, vintage accessories, and cozy elements. By carefully curating these elements, you can transform your kitchen into a charming and inviting space that exudes rustic elegance. Whether you live in the countryside or the city, a farmhouse style kitchen is sure to add timeless appeal and make your kitchen the heart of your home.
