The Best Hiking Trails to Explore around the World


Hiking, an outdoor activity that brings you up close with nature, is one of the best ways to rejuvenate and unwind. From massive mountains to picturesque valleys, the world has some of the most breathtaking hiking trails. These trails not only offer stunning views but also test your endurance, agility, and strength.

Here are some of the best hiking trails around the world that you should explore:

1. Inca Trail, Peru

The Inca Trail, a popular trek in South America, remains the most popular hiking trail in Peru. It is a 26-mile trail that leads you to Machu Picchu, one of the seven wonders of the world. This trail offers you a glimpse of the Incan civilization, as you walk past ruins and explore the breathtaking Andes Mountains.

2. Mount Kilimanjaro, Tanzania

Walking up Mount Kilimanjaro, the highest mountain in Africa, is an experience like no other. The hike takes you on a journey through different ecological zones, from dense rainforests to alpine deserts. At the pinnacle, you’ll be rewarded with an unparalleled view of the savannah below.

3. Glacier National Park, USA

Located in Montana, Glacier National Park is another popular hiking destination. It offers a variety of trails that traverse past pristine lakes, glaciers, alpine meadows, and forests. The park has over 700 miles of trails, ranging from leisurely strolls to arduous hikes.

4. Milford Track, New Zealand

The Milford Track, one of the most famous trails in New Zealand, is a spectacular journey that takes you through Fiordland National Park. It offers you the chance to explore New Zealand’s rugged terrain, as you hike past verdant valleys, alpine tracks, and cascading waterfalls. The trek is only open during the summer months and requires prior booking.

5. Torres del Paine National Park, Chile

Chile’s Torres del Paine National Park is recognized for its breathtaking scenery, clear blue lakes, and granite towers. There are numerous trails to explore here, from day hikes to overnight treks, each offering unique views of the park’s natural wonders.

6. Santa Cruz Trek, Peru

Located in the Andes Mountains, the Santa Cruz Trek offers unparalleled views of the Cordillera Blanca mountain range. This four-day hike takes you through picturesque valleys, past turquoise lakes, and towering peaks. It’s a perfect trek if you’re looking to escape the crowds and be immersed in nature.

7. Everest Base Camp Trek, Nepal

The Everest Base Camp Trek, considered one of the most challenging hikes in the world, takes you through the Himalayas to the base camp of the tallest mountain in the world. The trek takes you through remote villages and stunning mountain passes, and it provides an intimate view of Nepalese culture and traditions.

8. The West Highland Way, Scotland

Scotland’s West Highland Way is a 96-mile trek that takes you through the Scottish Highlands. The trail is perfect for hikers who want to enjoy the lush beauty of the country without climbing mountains. It meanders through picturesque villages, tranquil lakes, and lush green pastures.

9. The Pacific Crest Trail, USA

The Pacific Crest Trail is an epic trek that runs from Mexico to Canada. It stretches across the western United States through deserts, forests, and mountains. This 2,650-mile trail is for serious hikers who are looking for a challenge and want to reconnect with nature.

10. The Laugavegur Trail, Iceland

The Laugavegur Trail, in Iceland, is a four-day hike that takes you through hot springs, lava fields, and glaciers. The trek offers endless views of scenic beauty, as you walk through moss-covered lava fields and past bubbling mud pools.

The Takeaway

Hiking is an exciting way to explore some of the world’s most stunning landscapes. Whether it’s a day’s hike or an arduous journey through rugged terrain, these trails offer a unique opportunity to connect with nature. Try out one of these hikes for your next adventure, and you’re sure to create unforgettable memories.
