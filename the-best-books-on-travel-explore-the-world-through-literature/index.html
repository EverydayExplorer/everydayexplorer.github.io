The Best Books on Travel: Explore the World Through Literature


Traveling is a truly transformative experience. It allows us to explore different cultures, learn about history, and broaden our perspectives. But what if you can’t hop on a plane and backpack your way around the world? Don’t worry, because with the power of literature, you can still embark on amazing adventures and discover new places without leaving the comfort of your home.

In this blog post, we will explore some of the best books on travel that will transport you to different corners of the world. These books are not just travel guides, but immersive tales that will inspire your wanderlust and ignite your imagination. So, let’s dive into the literary world and explore the world through literature.

1. ‘The Alchemist’ by Paulo Coelho
‘The Alchemist’ takes readers on a journey through the exotic landscapes of Egypt and Spain. It follows the story of a young shepherd named Santiago who sets out to find a hidden treasure. But along the way, he discovers much more than material wealth. This book is a captivating blend of adventure and self-discovery, and it reminds us that sometimes the greatest treasure lies within us.

2. ‘Wild’ by Cheryl Strayed
If you’re looking for a book that will inspire you to get out of your comfort zone, look no further than ‘Wild.’ This memoir documents Cheryl Strayed’s solo hike along the Pacific Crest Trail, spanning over 1,000 miles. As she faces physical and emotional challenges, Strayed reflects on her past and finds healing and strength in nature. ‘Wild’ is a powerful reminder of the transformative power of travel and the resilience of the human spirit.

3. ‘Eat, Pray, Love’ by Elizabeth Gilbert
In ‘Eat, Pray, Love,’ Elizabeth Gilbert chronicles her year-long journey through Italy, India, and Indonesia. After a painful divorce, Gilbert sets out to find herself and experiences the pleasures of eating in Italy, spiritual enlightenment in India, and love in Bali. This memoir is a heartfelt exploration of self-discovery and the pursuit of happiness, and it will make you want to plan your own soul-searching adventure.

4. ‘In a Sunburned Country’ by Bill Bryson
Bill Bryson is known for his humorous and insightful travel writing, and ‘In a Sunburned Country’ is no exception. In this book, Bryson takes readers on a hilarious journey through Australia, a country teeming with unique wildlife, breathtaking landscapes, and quirky locals. With Bryson’s witty observations and vivid descriptions, you’ll feel like you’re experiencing the wonders of Down Under firsthand.

5. ‘The Beach’ by Alex Garland
‘The Beach’ is set in Thailand and tells the story of Richard, a young backpacker in search of an unspoiled paradise. When he stumbles upon a hidden beach, he is drawn into a community of fellow travelers seeking an escape from the realities of the world. But as tensions rise, Richard realizes that even the most idyllic places can hide darkness. This gripping novel explores the allure and dangers of wanderlust, and it will make you question the true meaning of paradise.

6. ‘Into the Wild’ by Jon Krakauer
‘Into the Wild’ is the true story of Christopher McCandless, an idealistic young man who abandons his possessions and hitchhikes to Alaska to live in the wilderness. This book delves into McCandless’s journey of self-discovery, his encounters with fellow travelers, and ultimately, his tragic fate. ‘Into the Wild’ is a haunting exploration of the thirst for adventure and the consequences of pursuing it relentlessly.

7. ‘The Lost City of Z’ by David Grann
In ‘The Lost City of Z,’ David Grann delves into the true story of Percy Fawcett, a British explorer who ventured deep into the Amazon in search of a mythical city. Fawcett’s quest for the Lost City of Z becomes an obsession, leading him into the heart of the jungle on multiple expeditions. Grann skillfully weaves together history, adventure, and mystery, offering readers a captivating tale of exploration and the allure of the unknown.

8. ‘A Year in Provence’ by Peter Mayle
If you’re dreaming of the French countryside, ‘A Year in Provence’ is the perfect book for you. Peter Mayle recounts his experiences and adventures as an Englishman who moves to Provence with his wife. From navigating the complexities of French bureaucracy to enjoying the simple pleasures of the regional cuisine, Mayle’s witty and charming observations will transport you to the sun-drenched villages of Provence, leaving you craving a glass of ros√© and a slice of baguette.

9. ‘The New York Times: 36 Hours World’ by Barbara Ireland
For those with a passion for city exploration, ‘The New York Times: 36 Hours World’ is an essential travel companion. This book features a collection of itineraries for 150 destinations across six continents, carefully curated to maximize your time in each city. Whether you prefer iconic landmarks or hidden gems, this book will guide you through the highlights of each destination, helping you make the most of your limited time.

10. ‘The Old Man and The Sea’ by Ernest Hemingway
While not a traditional travel book, ‘The Old Man and The Sea’ takes readers on a journey through the vast expanse of the sea. Set in Cuba, this timeless classic tells the story of an aging fisherman and his battle with a giant marlin. Hemingway’s evocative prose immerses readers in the beauty and power of the ocean, capturing the sense of adventure and the indomitable spirit of the human quest.

In conclusion, these books on travel offer us a way to escape and explore the world through the pages of a book. Through the vivid descriptions, deep insights, and captivating stories, they transport us to different corners of the globe, sparking our imagination and quenching our thirst for adventure. So, whether you’re a seasoned traveler or an armchair explorer, make sure to add these books to your reading list and embark on a literary journey to explore the world.
