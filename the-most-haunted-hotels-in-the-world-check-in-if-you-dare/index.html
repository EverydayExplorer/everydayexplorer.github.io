The Most Haunted Hotels in the World: Check-In If You Dare


Are you a fan of paranormal activity? Do you crave spine-chilling thrills during your travels? If so, then this blog post is for you. We’re going to take a spine-tingling journey through some of the most haunted hotels in the world. So, grab a cup of hot cocoa and get ready to be spooked!

1. The Stanley Hotel, Colorado, USA
Made famous by Stephen King’s novel ‘The Shining,’ The Stanley Hotel is notorious for its ghostly inhabitants. Guests have reported seeing apparitions, hearing strange noises, and experiencing unexplained temperature drops. If you’re feeling brave, you can even take a ghost tour and explore the hotel’s eerie history.

2. The Queen Mary, California, USA
Once a luxurious ocean liner, The Queen Mary is now permanently docked in Long Beach and serves as a hotel. But be warned, it’s also home to several restless spirits. Visitors have reported encounters with a lady in white, a sailor who perished during World War II, and even children playing in the hallways late at night.

3. The Langham Hotel, London, England
With a history dating back to 1865, The Langham Hotel has had its fair share of ghostly encounters. One of its most famous apparitions is that of a Victorian-era doctor, who is often seen wandering the hallways. The hotel offers a ‘Ghosts and Ghouls’ tour, where you can learn about the hotel’s haunted past.

4. The Crescent Hotel, Arkansas, USA
Nestled in the picturesque Ozark Mountains, The Crescent Hotel is known as ‘America’s Most Haunted Hotel.’ It has a dark history, including being a cancer hospital in the 1930s. Guests have reported seeing ghostly figures, hearing disembodied voices, and feeling a chilling presence in their rooms.

5. The Ballygally Castle Hotel, Northern Ireland
If you’re looking for a haunted castle experience, then The Ballygally Castle Hotel is the place to be. Legend has it that the ghost of Lady Isabella Shaw haunts the tower room, knocking on doors and mysteriously disappearing. The hotel even offers a ‘Ghost Room’ package for those brave enough to spend the night in the haunted tower.

6. The Fairmont Banff Springs Hotel, Alberta, Canada
Nestled in the heart of the Canadian Rockies, The Fairmont Banff Springs Hotel is not only known for its stunning views but also for its ghostly residents. Guests have reported encounters with a bride who tragically died on her wedding day and a bellman who assists guests but vanishes into thin air.

7. The Russell Hotel, Sydney, Australia
Located in the historic Rocks district, The Russell Hotel is said to be haunted by a sailor who tragically died in one of its rooms. Guests have reported hearing footsteps, feeling a presence in their rooms, and even witnessing objects move on their own. If you’re feeling brave, you can stay in the haunted room and experience the paranormal for yourself.

These are just a few examples of the most haunted hotels in the world. If you’re a thrill-seeker or a believer in the supernatural, these hotels offer a unique and spine-chilling experience. Just remember to bring your courage and a trusty camera to capture any ghostly encounters. Happy ghost hunting!
