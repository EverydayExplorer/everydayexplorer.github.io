How to Properly Store Winter Tires: Tips for Prolonging Their Lifespan


As winter approaches, it’s important to start thinking about the proper storage of your winter tires. Your winter tires play a crucial role in ensuring your safety on icy and snowy roads, but improper storage can lead to premature wear and reduce their overall lifespan. In this blog post, we will provide you with essential tips on how to properly store winter tires, prolonging their lifespan and saving you money in the long run.

Why is proper storage important?

Before we delve into the tips for storing winter tires, let’s understand why proper storage is essential. Winter tires are made with a specific tread pattern and rubber compound designed to provide optimal traction in cold weather conditions. However, when stored improperly, the rubber can deteriorate, causing a loss in their grip and performance.

Storing your winter tires correctly during the off-season will prevent the rubber from hardening, cracking, and deformation, ensuring they are in optimal condition and ready to provide the best traction when winter arrives.

1. Clean and inspect before storage

Before storing your winter tires, it’s important to clean them thoroughly. Use a mild soap and water solution and a brush to remove any dirt, debris, and road grime. Avoid using aggressive cleaning agents or brushes that could damage the rubber.

After cleaning, inspect the tires for any signs of damage, such as cuts, punctures, or bulges. Any tires with significant damage should be replaced before storing them. It’s also a good idea to measure the tread depth to ensure they can still provide proper traction in the next winter season.

2. Choose the right storage location

Finding the proper storage location is crucial for maintaining your winter tires’ quality. Look for a cool, dry, and well-ventilated area, preferably indoors. Extreme temperature fluctuations, sunlight exposure, and moisture can all damage the rubber, so avoid storing them in spaces such as garages with excessive heat or direct sunlight.

Additionally, keep the tires away from any chemicals, solvents, or sources of ozone, such as electric motors or generators. These compounds can break down the rubber and cause premature aging.

3. Store them properly

Once you have cleaned and inspected your tires and have found the right storage location, it’s time to store them properly. Start by deflating the tires to the recommended pressure specified by the manufacturer. This step prevents the tires from becoming misshapen over time and maintains their structural integrity.

Next, place each tire in a tire storage bag or wrap them individually with plastic. This step helps to protect the tires from dust, sunlight, and any potential moisture. If you don’t have access to tire bags, consider using large, heavy-duty garbage bags.

4. Store them upright or stacked

When it comes to storing winter tires, you have two options: storing them upright or stacked. Storing them upright, such as on a tire rack, is the preferred method as it prevents any unnecessary stress on the sidewalls. This approach also ensures that the weight of the tires is evenly distributed, preventing deformation.

If you opt to stack the tires, make sure to place a piece of cardboard or a tire protector between each tire to prevent them from sticking together or deforming.

5. Regularly inspect your stored winter tires

While your winter tires are in storage, it’s important to periodically inspect them for any signs of damage or deterioration. Check for cracks, bulges, dryness, or any other changes in the rubber’s appearance. Also, ensure that rodents or pests have not made a home out of your stored tires.

By regularly inspecting your stored winter tires, you can catch and address any issues early before they worsen and potentially ruin the tires.

Conclusion

Properly storing your winter tires is crucial for maintaining their longevity and ensuring optimal safety when you need them most. By following these simple tips, you can protect your investment and ensure that your winter tires are in top condition for the next winter season.

Remember to clean and inspect the tires before storage, choose the right storage location, store them properly either upright or stacked, and regularly inspect them during the off-season. By taking these steps, you’ll have peace of mind knowing that your winter tires are well-preserved and ready to tackle the harsh winter conditions ahead.
