Creative and Delicious Ways to Cook with Fresh Herbs


Fresh herbs are a game-changer in the kitchen. Not only do they add vibrant flavors to your dishes, but they also bring a touch of freshness and aroma that can elevate any meal. If you’re looking for creative and delicious ways to cook with fresh herbs, you’ve come to the right place. In this blog post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering recipes that will make the most of your herb garden. So, roll up your sleeves and let’s get cooking!

1. Herb-infused oils and vinegars:
One of the easiest ways to use fresh herbs is by infusing them into oils or vinegars. Not only does this add a burst of flavor, but it also prolongs the shelf life of your herbs. Choose your favorite herbs like basil, rosemary, or thyme, and combine them with high-quality olive oil or vinegar. Let the herbs steep for a few weeks, strain, and voila! You have a versatile ingredient that can be used in dressings, marinades, or simply drizzled over grilled vegetables.

2. Herb butter:
Who doesn’t love a pat of butter melting over a hot-off-the-grill steak or a slice of warm bread? Take your butter game to the next level by incorporating fresh herbs into it. Mix softened butter with finely chopped herbs like parsley, chives, or dill. Add a pinch of salt and store it in the fridge until it solidifies. Use herb butter to add a burst of flavor to your grilled meats, roasted vegetables, or even slathered on a crusty baguette.

3. Herb-infused salts:
If you’re a fan of flavored salts, then herb-infused salts are a must-try. Simply mix dried herbs like thyme, oregano, or lavender with coarse sea salt. Let the flavors meld together for a couple of days before using. Sprinkle herb-infused salts over grilled meats, roasted potatoes, or even popcorn for a unique and savory twist.

4. Herb pesto:
Pesto is a classic Italian sauce made with fresh herbs, garlic, nuts, cheese, and olive oil. While basil is the traditional herb used in pesto, you can get creative and experiment with different combinations. Try using cilantro and cashews for a vibrant and nutty pesto, or mix parsley and walnuts for a fresh and earthy version. The possibilities are endless! Use herb pesto as a sauce for pasta, a spread on sandwiches, or a dip for veggies.

5. Herb-infused cocktails:
Why limit the use of herbs to just cooking? Fresh herbs can also add a delightful twist to your favorite cocktails. Muddle herbs like mint, basil, or thyme with sugar or simple syrup to release their flavors. Mix them with your choice of spirits like gin, vodka, or rum, add some citrus juice, and shake it up. The result? A refreshing and herbaceous cocktail that will impress your guests.

Fresh herbs have the power to transform your cooking and take it to new heights. Whether you’re infusing them into oils, making herb-infused butters or salts, creating herb pesto, or adding them to your cocktails, the possibilities are endless. So, get creative, experiment with different herb combinations, and let your taste buds be your guide. Trust us, once you start cooking with fresh herbs, you’ll never go back to dried ones again. Happy herb-infused cooking!
