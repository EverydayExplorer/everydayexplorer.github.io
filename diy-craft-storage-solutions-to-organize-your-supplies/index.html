DIY Craft Storage Solutions to Organize Your Supplies


Crafting is a wonderful hobby that allows us to express our creativity and make beautiful things with our own hands. However, one challenge that many crafters face is finding efficient ways to store and organize their supplies. From yarn and fabric to beads and paintbrushes, craft supplies can quickly become cluttered and disorganized if not properly stored. Fortunately, there are plenty of DIY craft storage solutions that can help you keep your supplies tidy and easily accessible. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of creative and practical storage ideas to help you organize your craft supplies like a pro.

1. Pegboard Wall Organizer

A pegboard wall organizer is a versatile and customizable storage solution that can help you keep your craft supplies neat and organized. Install a pegboard on a wall in your craft room or studio, and use hooks, baskets, and shelves to hang and store your supplies. You can easily rearrange the pegboard to accommodate different supplies and keep frequently used items within reach. Use labeled containers or jars to store smaller items like buttons, beads, and sewing notions, and hang larger items like scissors, rulers, and cutting mats on hooks.

2. Mason Jar Storage

Mason jars are not just for canning – they also make excellent storage containers for craft supplies. Use mason jars to store items like paintbrushes, markers, pens, and pencils. You can customize the jars with paint, fabric, or ribbon to match your craft room decor. To save space, mount a wooden board on the wall and attach hose clamps to hold the mason jars securely in place. This DIY mason jar storage solution is not only functional but also adds a rustic and charming touch to your craft room.

3. Shoe Organizer Craft Caddy

Repurpose a hanging shoe organizer into a convenient craft caddy to store and organize your supplies. Hang the shoe organizer on the back of a door or on a wall, and use the pockets to hold items like yarn, ribbon, fabric scraps, and knitting needles. Label the pockets with the contents for easy identification, and use clear plastic pockets so you can see what’s inside at a glance. This space-saving storage solution is perfect for small craft rooms or for crafters who need to maximize vertical space.

4. PVC Pipe Storage

PVC pipes are inexpensive and readily available at home improvement stores, making them a great material for DIY craft storage. Cut PVC pipes into short lengths and mount them on a wall or inside a cabinet to create storage tubes for items like knitting needles, crochet hooks, and dowels. You can also use larger diameter PVC pipes to store rolls of wrapping paper, fabric bolts, or vinyl rolls. Customize the PVC pipe storage by painting it or adding decorative washi tape to match your craft room decor.

5. Rolling Cart Organizer

A rolling cart organizer is a versatile storage solution that can be easily moved around your craft room or studio as needed. Use a utility cart with multiple shelves or drawers to store and organize your supplies. Sort your supplies into categories like paper, fabric, paint, and embellishments, and use bins, baskets, or trays to keep everything organized. Label the drawers or shelves with the contents for easy identification, and use the top of the cart as a workspace for crafting projects. When not in use, simply roll the cart out of the way to free up floor space.

6. Magnetic Strip Tool Holder

Keep your scissors, rulers, and other metal tools organized and easily accessible with a magnetic strip tool holder. Mount a magnetic strip on a wall or inside a cabinet, and attach metal tools to the strip to keep them neatly organized and within reach. You can also use magnetic spice tins or small containers to hold small metal items like needles, pins, and paper clips. This DIY storage solution is perfect for crafters who want to keep their tools organized without taking up valuable workspace.

7. Drawer Divider Inserts

Maximize the storage space in your craft room drawers with custom drawer divider inserts. Use foam board, cardboard, or wood to create dividers that fit snugly inside your drawers, and divide the space into smaller compartments for storing items like buttons, beads, thread spools, and sewing notions. You can customize the size and shape of the compartments to fit your specific storage needs, and use labels or drawer liners to add a decorative touch. Drawer divider inserts are an affordable and easy-to-make storage solution that can help you keep your craft supplies organized and accessible.

In conclusion, DIY craft storage solutions are a practical and affordable way to keep your supplies organized and accessible. Whether you have a dedicated craft room or a small crafting corner, these creative storage ideas can help you make the most of your space and keep your supplies tidy. Get inspired and start organizing your craft supplies today!
