Wilderness Navigation Tools: Compass, Map, and Sun Techniques


As outdoor enthusiasts, we often find ourselves in need of reliable navigation tools when exploring the wilderness. Whether we are hiking, backpacking, or engaging in any other outdoor activity, having the proper tools can be crucial to our safety and enjoyment of the experience. In this blog, we will explore three essential wilderness navigation tools: the compass, map, and sun techniques.

The compass is perhaps the most iconic navigation tool, and for good reason. It has been used for centuries by explorers, adventurers, and even everyday travelers. A compass works by indicating magnetic north, helping us determine our direction even in unfamiliar terrain. It consists of a needle that aligns itself with the Earth’s magnetic field, pointing towards the magnetic North Pole.

When using a compass, it is important to understand how to read the various markings on the housing. The outer ring, for example, displays the degrees in a circle, ranging from 0 to 360. By aligning the compass with the needle pointing towards north, we can then determine our intended direction. This simple yet effective tool is a must-have for anyone venturing into the wilderness.

In conjunction with a compass, a good map is essential for wilderness navigation. A map provides a visual representation of the land, including topographical features, trails, rivers, and landmarks. By studying a map, we can gain a better understanding of our surroundings and plan our route accordingly.

Topographic maps, in particular, are invaluable for outdoor navigation. They provide details about elevation, contours, and terrain, allowing us to make informed decisions about the difficulty of a particular route. In addition, many maps now include GPS coordinates, which can be used in conjunction with handheld GPS devices to pinpoint our exact location.

However, relying solely on a compass and a map can sometimes be challenging, especially in situations where landmarks are not visible or when we need to quickly orient ourselves. In such cases, the sun can serve as a powerful natural compass. By observing the position of the sun throughout the day, we can determine our direction of travel.

The most basic technique is to use the sun’s position at different times of the day as a visual reference. For example, when the sun is rising, it is generally in the east, while it sets in the west. In the northern hemisphere, the sun will be closer to the southern part of the sky during midday. Conversely, in the southern hemisphere, the opposite is true. This technique, combined with a basic understanding of the time of day, can provide a good approximation of our direction.

Another technique is to use an analog watch as a makeshift compass. By pointing the hour hand towards the sun and bisecting the angle between the hour hand and the 12 o’clock mark, we can find a rough approximation of the north-south line. This method takes into account the fact that the hour hand moves relative to the stationary sun, allowing for rough navigation in the absence of a compass.

In conclusion, when venturing into the wilderness, it is imperative to have a proper set of navigation tools at our disposal. The compass, map, and sun techniques all play important roles in ensuring our safety and helping us find our way in unfamiliar territory. By mastering these tools and techniques, we can enhance our outdoor experiences and navigate with confidence. So, before embarking on your next wilderness adventure, make sure to pack these essential navigation tools and gain the knowledge needed to use them effectively.
