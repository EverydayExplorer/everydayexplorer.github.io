Analyzing the Symbolism in Classic Literature


Symbolism is a powerful literary device that authors use to convey deeper meanings and enhance the overall experience of reading a novel. Classic literature is abundant with symbolism, as authors often use it to explore themes, emotions, and social commentary. In this blog post, we will delve into the world of analyzing symbolism in classic literature and explore the impact it has on the reader’s understanding and interpretation of the text.

What is Symbolism?

Symbolism refers to the use of symbols or objects to represent ideas, emotions, or concepts beyond their literal meaning. These symbols can be tangible objects, such as a red rose symbolizing love, or they can be more abstract, such as the color green symbolizing jealousy or envy. Symbolism allows authors to express complex ideas and evoke emotions in a subtle and profound way.

The Role of Symbolism in Classic Literature

Classic literature is renowned for its depth and complexity, and symbolism plays a crucial role in enhancing these literary works. Symbolism helps authors create layers of meaning, enabling readers to explore various interpretations and engage in thought-provoking discussions. Through the use of symbols, classic authors can convey themes and motifs that resonate with readers long after they have finished reading the book.

Examples of Symbolism in Classic Literature

  1. The Apple in ‘Frankenstein’ - In Mary Shelley’s iconic novel, the apple is a recurring symbol that represents knowledge, specifically the forbidden knowledge pursued by both Victor Frankenstein and his creation. The apple symbolizes the pursuit of knowledge beyond one’s limits and the destructive consequences of playing god.

  2. The Green Light in ‘The Great Gatsby’ - F. Scott Fitzgerald uses the green light across the water from Jay Gatsby’s mansion to symbolize Gatsby’s unrestrainable desire for the unattainable. It represents Gatsby’s hope, dreams, and ultimately his inability to fully grasp the American Dream.

  3. The Conch Shell in ‘Lord of the Flies’ - William Golding’s novel utilizes the conch shell as a symbol of civilization and democracy. It represents order and control on the island, but as the boys descend into savagery, the power of the conch diminishes, symbolizing the disintegration of their society.

These examples illustrate how symbolism can enrich classic literature by adding depth and complexity to the themes explored in the novels. Symbolism allows authors to create memorable and impactful stories that resonate with readers on a profound level.

Analyzing Symbolism in Classic Literature

When analyzing symbolism in classic literature, it is essential to consider various factors such as context, authorial intent, and recurring symbols throughout the text. Here are some steps to follow when analyzing symbolism:

  1. Identify symbols - Begin by identifying the recurring symbols or objects throughout the novel. Look for objects, colors, or actions that seem to have deeper meaning beyond their literal purpose.

  2. Consider context - Pay attention to the context in which symbols are used. Analyze how they relate to specific characters, settings, or events within the story. Symbols can change their meaning based on the context in which they are presented.

  3. Research cultural and historical context - Understanding the cultural and historical context in which a novel was written can provide valuable insights into the symbolism used. Research the time period, societal norms, and author’s background to gain a deeper understanding of the intended meaning behind the symbols.

  4. Interpret and analyze - Once you have identified the symbols and considered the context, it’s time to interpret and analyze their meaning. Look for patterns, recurring motifs, and thematic connections. Pay attention to how the symbols evolve throughout the story and their impact on the characters and plot.

  5. Engage in literary discussions - Lastly, engage in discussions with fellow readers, book clubs, or online communities to gain different perspectives and interpretations. Classic literature is often open to interpretation, and discussing symbolism with others can provide valuable insights and new ways of understanding the text.

The Impact of Symbolism in Classic Literature

The use of symbolism in classic literature has a profound impact on readers. It adds depth and complexity to the text, evoking emotions and sparking introspection. Symbolism invites readers to explore different layers of meaning and encourages critical thinking. It allows readers to connect with the characters and themes on a deeper level, making the reading experience more enriching and thought-provoking.

Moreover, symbolism in classic literature facilitates discussions and analysis, both within academic settings and casual reading communities. It encourages readers to reflect on their own experiences, beliefs, and values, fostering a better understanding of the human condition and the world around us.

In conclusion, analyzing symbolism in classic literature is a rewarding endeavor that allows readers to uncover the hidden meanings, themes, and motifs within these timeless works. Symbolism adds depth and complexity to the storytelling, encouraging readers to engage in thoughtful discussions and explore different interpretations. By delving into the world of symbolism in classic literature, readers gain a deeper appreciation for the craft of these authors and the enduring relevance of these literary masterpieces.
