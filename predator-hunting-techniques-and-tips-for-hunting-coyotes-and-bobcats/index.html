Predator Hunting: Techniques and Tips for Hunting Coyotes and Bobcats


For those who crave the thrill of the hunt, predator hunting offers an exciting and challenging experience. Among the most cunning and elusive predators are coyotes and bobcats. In this blog, we will explore effective techniques and valuable tips to help you become a successful predator hunter.

1. Scout the Territory:
Before heading out into the field, it is crucial to thoroughly scout the hunting area. Look for signs of predator activity, such as tracks, scat, or scratch marks on trees. Additionally, study the landscape to identify potential ambush points, natural funnels, and high-traffic areas.

2. Calls and Decoys:
Predators are highly responsive to calls, making them an indispensable tool for luring them out into the open. Electronic predator calls can mimic the sounds of distressed prey, making them irresistible to coyotes and bobcats. Experiment with different calls, including rabbit distress, fawn bleats, or bird distress, to see which ones are most effective in your area.

To enhance the effectiveness of your calls, consider using decoys. Decoys, such as a distressed rabbit or a wounded bird, can create a visual attraction that complements the auditory allure of your calls.

3. Concealment and Camouflage:
Predators have keen senses, including exceptional eyesight and a remarkable sense of smell. To outsmart them, it is essential to blend into your surroundings. Wear camouflage clothing that matches the environment you’ll be hunting in, and consider using a portable blind or natural cover to further conceal your presence. Additionally, pay attention to wind direction to avoid alerting predators with your scent.

4. Night Hunting:
Predators are predominantly nocturnal creatures, making night hunting an effective strategy. Equip yourself with a reliable night vision scope or thermal imaging device to give yourself the advantage of seeing in the dark. Be sure to check local hunting regulations regarding night hunting and obtain any necessary permits.

5. Shot Placement:
When it comes to predator hunting, shot placement is crucial. Aim for vital areas such as the heart and lungs to ensure a clean and ethical kill. Familiarize yourself with the anatomy of coyotes and bobcats to make accurate shots. Consider practicing at a shooting range to improve your marksmanship skills.

6. Safety Precautions:
As with any form of hunting, safety should always be a top priority. Familiarize yourself with local hunting regulations, wear appropriate protective gear, and always inform someone of your hunting plans. Additionally, consider hunting with a partner for added safety and camaraderie.

7. Responsible Hunting:
Predator hunting plays a vital role in wildlife management, but it should always be conducted responsibly. Practice ethical hunting practices, respect private property rights, and always follow the laws and regulations set forth by your state or country.

In conclusion, predator hunting offers a thrilling and challenging experience for hunters. By employing effective techniques such as scouting, using calls and decoys, mastering concealment, considering night hunting, practicing shot placement, prioritizing safety, and hunting responsibly, you can increase your chances of success in hunting coyotes and bobcats. Happy hunting!

Note: The information provided in this article is for educational and informational purposes only. Hunting regulations may vary by location, so be sure to check local laws and regulations before engaging in predator hunting activities.
