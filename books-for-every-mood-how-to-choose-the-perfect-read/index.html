Books for Every Mood: How to Choose the Perfect Read


As humans, we often experience a wide range of emotions - happiness, sadness, excitement, relaxation, and everything in between. When we find ourselves in these different emotional states, we may turn to various sources of entertainment and distraction to help us navigate our feelings. One such source is books.

Books have the power to transport us to different worlds, to make us laugh, cry, and think deeply about life. But with so many books out there, how do we know which ones to choose for each mood? In this blog post, we’ll explore some tips and recommendations for choosing the perfect read for every mood.

If you’re feeling happy and lighthearted, you might want to pick up a book that will make you laugh or feel good. Rom-coms and lighthearted fiction are great choices for this mood. Some popular books in this category include ‘Eleanor Oliphant is Completely Fine’ by Gail Honeyman, ‘The Rosie Project’ by Graeme Simsion, and ‘The Flatshare’ by Beth O’Leary.

On the other hand, if you’re feeling introspective or contemplative, you might want to delve into more serious literature or non-fiction. Memoirs, biographies, and philosophical works can all be great choices for this mood. Some examples include ‘The Year of Magical Thinking’ by Joan Didion, ‘The Autobiography of Malcolm X’ as told to Alex Haley, and ‘Man’s Search for Meaning’ by Viktor E. Frankl.

When you’re feeling sad or going through a tough time, it’s important to choose books that offer comfort and hope. Inspirational stories or books that deal with grief and loss can be helpful in these situations. Some popular choices include ‘The Alchemist’ by Paulo Coelho, ‘The Five People You Meet in Heaven’ by Mitch Albom, and ‘A Monster Calls’ by Patrick Ness.

On days when you’re feeling stressed or overwhelmed, it can be beneficial to choose books that help you relax and unwind. Cozy mysteries, light romance novels, and feel-good stories are all great choices for this mood. Some examples include ‘The No. 1 Ladies’ Detective Agency’ by Alexander McCall Smith, ‘The Guernsey Literary and Potato Peel Pie Society’ by Mary Ann Shaffer and Annie Barrows, and ‘The Secret Life of Bees’ by Sue Monk Kidd.

If you’re feeling adventurous and want to explore new worlds, science fiction and fantasy novels can be great choices. These genres offer endless possibilities for imagination and exploration. Some popular books in this category include ‘The Hunger Games’ by Suzanne Collins, ‘Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone’ by J.K. Rowling, and ‘The Lord of the Rings’ trilogy by J.R.R. Tolkien.

Regardless of your mood, it’s important to remember that the most important factor in choosing a book is whether or not it speaks to you personally. These recommendations are simply starting points - you may find that you prefer different genres or authors altogether. The key is to experiment and find what works best for you.

In conclusion, books have the power to offer comfort, joy, and inspiration in all of life’s different moods. Whether you’re feeling happy, sad, stressed, or adventurous, there’s a book out there that’s perfect for you. So why not pick up a new read today and see where it takes you?
