Cartoon Careers: Behind-the-Scenes Jobs in Animation


Are you a fan of animated cartoons? Have you ever wondered about the talented individuals who bring your favorite characters to life? While the spotlight often shines on animators and voice actors, there’s a whole world of behind-the-scenes jobs in animation that are equally essential to the creation of your beloved shows and movies. In this blog post, we’ll take a peek behind the curtain and explore some of the lesser-known but crucial roles in the world of cartoon careers.

1. Storyboard Artist

Before any animation can begin, a storyboard artist lays the groundwork by creating a visual blueprint of the entire project. Think of them as the architects of animation. They work closely with the director to translate the script into a series of drawings that outline the key scenes, camera angles, and character movements. This crucial step helps ensure that everyone involved in the production is on the same page before the animation process begins.

2. Character Designer

Every iconic cartoon character, from Mickey Mouse to SpongeBob SquarePants, started as a concept in the mind of a character designer. These talented artists are responsible for bringing characters to life by designing their appearance, personality, and distinctive traits. Whether it’s crafting the sleek lines of a superhero or the exaggerated features of a comedic sidekick, character designers play a pivotal role in creating memorable animated personalities.

3. Background Artist

While the characters take center stage, the world they inhabit is just as important. Background artists are tasked with creating the vibrant and immersive settings that bring animation to life. Whether it’s a bustling cityscape, a fantastical kingdom, or a whimsical forest, these artists use their creativity and attention to detail to establish the atmosphere and mood of each scene.

4. Animator

Perhaps the most well-known role in animation, animators are the ones who breathe life into the characters through movement. Using a combination of traditional hand-drawn techniques and digital software, animators create the illusion of motion by meticulously crafting each frame. Whether it’s a subtle facial expression or an elaborate action sequence, animators possess a unique blend of artistic talent and technical skill.

5. Voice Actor

While voice acting may be more recognizable to the average viewer, it’s still worth mentioning here. Voice actors are the ones who give characters their distinct voices and personalities. Through their vocal performances, they bring emotion, humor, and depth to the animated world, forging a connection with audiences that transcends the screen.

6. Sound Designer

Last but certainly not least, sound designers are responsible for the auditory elements of animation. From dialogue and music to sound effects and ambient noise, these unsung heroes enhance the viewing experience by creating a rich sonic landscape that complements the visuals. Whether it’s the roar of a dinosaur or the subtle rustle of leaves, sound designers play a crucial role in immersing audiences in the world of animation.

In conclusion, the world of animation is a collaborative effort that relies on the talent and expertise of a diverse array of individuals. From storyboard artists and character designers to animators and voice actors, each role contributes to the magic of bringing cartoons to life. So the next time you sit down to watch your favorite animated show or movie, take a moment to appreciate the hard work and creativity that went into making it a reality.
