The Best Fashion Documentaries of All Time


Fashion documentaries offer a mesmerizing glimpse into the world of style, creativity, and innovation. From showcasing the behind-the-scenes drama of the fashion industry to exploring the artistic vision of designers, these films provide a rich tapestry of inspiration and insight for fashion enthusiasts and creatives alike. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best fashion documentaries of all time that have left an indelible mark on the industry and captivated audiences around the globe.

1. ‘The September Issue’ (2009)
Directed by R.J. Cutler, ‘The September Issue’ takes viewers inside the hallowed halls of Vogue magazine as legendary editor-in-chief Anna Wintour and her team prepare the iconic September issue. Offering a rare peek into the meticulous process of creating a fashion bible, this documentary is a must-watch for anyone curious about the inner workings of the fashion publishing world.

2. ‘Dior and I’ (2014)
This captivating documentary follows designer Raf Simons as he takes the helm at the prestigious fashion house of Dior. Directed by Frédéric Tcheng, ‘Dior and I’ chronicles the intense pressure and creative challenges faced by Simons as he races against time to create his debut haute couture collection. The film offers a poignant look at the intersection of artistry and commerce in the fast-paced world of high fashion.

3. ‘Advanced Style’ (2014)
A celebration of aging with style and grace, ‘Advanced Style’ shines a spotlight on the vibrant fashion scene of New York City’s most fashionable seniors. Directed by Lina Plioplyte, this documentary showcases the eclectic and fearless personal style of seven stylish older women who prove that age is no barrier to self-expression and creativity. With its empowering message of individuality and self-confidence, ‘Advanced Style’ is a refreshing departure from the youth-centric focus of mainstream fashion media.

4. ‘McQueen’ (2018)
A haunting portrait of the late designer Alexander McQueen, this documentary directed by Ian Bonhôte and Peter Ettedgui delves into the tumultuous life and groundbreaking work of one of fashion’s most visionary talents. From his early days as a rebellious young designer to his meteoric rise to fame and tragic death, ‘McQueen’ offers a raw and intimate look at the man behind the myth. With its stunning visuals and candid interviews, this film is a testament to McQueen’s enduring legacy and revolutionary impact on the fashion world.

5. ‘Bill Cunningham New York’ (2010)
Directed by Richard Press, ‘Bill Cunningham New York’ pays tribute to the legendary street style photographer Bill Cunningham, whose keen eye for fashion and unassuming demeanor made him a beloved figure in the industry. The documentary follows Cunningham as he tirelessly documents the sartorial trends of New York City, capturing the essence of the city’s vibrant street culture with his camera. With its heartfelt portrayal of Cunningham’s passion for his craft and his unwavering dedication to his art, this film is a touching ode to a true fashion icon.

In conclusion, fashion documentaries offer a unique and compelling perspective on the world of style and design, shedding light on the creative process, the personalities behind the scenes, and the cultural significance of fashion. Whether you’re a seasoned fashion enthusiast or simply curious about the magic of the runway, these documentaries are sure to inspire, entertain, and enlighten. So grab some popcorn, settle in, and let these cinematic gems transport you to the fascinating and glamorous world of fashion.
