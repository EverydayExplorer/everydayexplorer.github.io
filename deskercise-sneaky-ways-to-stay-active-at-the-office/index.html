Deskercise: Sneaky Ways to Stay Active at the Office


In today’s fast-paced world, spending long hours hunched over a desk has become the norm for many of us. As we juggle deadlines, meetings, and endless emails, it’s easy for physical activity to take a back seat. But what if I told you that you could stay active without ever leaving your desk? Enter deskercise - the art of sneaking in exercise while you work. In this blog post, we’ll explore some clever and effective ways to stay active at the office without disrupting your workflow.

  1. Take the Stairs: It may seem like a small change, but opting for the stairs instead of the elevator can make a big difference. Challenge yourself to take the stairs whenever possible, whether it’s heading to a meeting on a different floor or just taking a quick break. Not only will you get your heart pumping, but you’ll also strengthen your leg muscles in the process.

  2. Desk Stretches: Sitting at a desk all day can lead to stiffness and discomfort. Combat this by incorporating desk stretches into your routine. Simple stretches like neck rolls, shoulder stretches, and hamstring stretches can be done right at your desk. Aim to stretch for a few minutes every hour to keep your muscles limber and prevent tightness.

  3. Chair Yoga: Who says you need a yoga mat to practice yoga? Chair yoga is a great way to sneak in some exercise while you work. From seated twists to forward folds, there are plenty of yoga poses that can be adapted for the office environment. Not only will chair yoga help improve your flexibility and posture, but it can also help reduce stress and increase focus.

  4. Desk Squats: Squats are a fantastic way to work multiple muscle groups at once, but you don’t need to hit the gym to reap the benefits. Try incorporating desk squats into your daily routine by standing up from your chair and lowering yourself down as if you were going to sit back down. Aim for three sets of 10-15 reps throughout the day to strengthen your legs and glutes.

  5. Desk Push-Ups: Push-ups are a classic exercise for building upper body strength, and you can do them right at your desk. Place your hands on the edge of your desk shoulder-width apart and step back until your body is at an angle. Lower yourself down until your chest nearly touches the desk, then push yourself back up. Desk push-ups are a great way to work your chest, shoulders, and arms without ever leaving your workspace.

  6. Walking Meetings: Instead of conducting meetings in a stuffy conference room, why not take them outside? Walking meetings not only get you out of the office and into the fresh air, but they also provide an opportunity to sneak in some physical activity. Whether you’re brainstorming ideas or discussing project updates, walking meetings can help boost creativity and productivity.

  7. Desk Pedaling: If you’re lucky enough to have a desk bike or under-desk elliptical, take advantage of it! Pedaling while you work is a great way to keep your legs moving and burn some extra calories throughout the day. Even if you don’t have access to a desk bike, you can still simulate the motion by tapping your feet or doing leg lifts under your desk.

  8. Water Bottle Workouts: Keep a filled water bottle at your desk and use it as makeshift weights for quick workouts. You can do bicep curls, shoulder raises, and tricep extensions using your water bottle as resistance. It’s a simple yet effective way to squeeze in some strength training during your busy day.

Incorporating deskercise into your daily routine is a simple and effective way to stay active at the office. By making small changes to your workspace and daily habits, you can improve your health, boost your mood, and increase your productivity. So why wait? Start deskercising today and feel the difference for yourself!
