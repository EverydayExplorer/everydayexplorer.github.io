Survival Food: What to Eat (and Avoid) in a Crisis


When faced with a crisis or emergency situation, ensuring access to proper nutrition becomes crucial. Having a stockpile of survival food that can provide essential nutrients and sustain us during challenging times is a smart and responsible approach. In this blog post, we will explore what to eat and what to avoid when it comes to survival food, helping you make informed choices that can make a difference when the going gets tough.

1. Prioritize Nutrient-Dense Foods:
In a crisis, it is important to get the most bang for your buck when it comes to nutrition. Choosing foods that are nutrient-dense will ensure that you are getting all the essential vitamins, minerals, and macronutrients your body needs. Opt for foods that are high in protein, healthy fats, and complex carbohydrates. Some examples include canned fish, beans, nuts, whole grains, and dried fruits.

2. Long Shelf-Life:
When stocking up on survival food, it is crucial to consider the shelf life of the products. Look for foods that have a long shelf life and can be stored for an extended period without spoiling. Canned goods, dehydrated foods, and freeze-dried meals are excellent options as they can last for months or even years, providing you with a reliable food source during a crisis.

3. Hydration is Key:
While it’s essential to have food supplies, don’t forget about the importance of staying hydrated. In a crisis, access to clean water may be limited, so consider storing water purification tablets or investing in a portable water filter. Additionally, include electrolyte-rich drinks or powdered mixes to replenish vital minerals lost through sweating and exertion.

4. Avoid Excessive Sodium and Sugar:
When selecting survival food, be cautious of products that are high in sodium and sugar. While these may taste good in the short term, excessive consumption can lead to dehydration and other health issues. Opt for low-sodium canned goods and dried fruits with no added sugars to ensure a healthier and more balanced diet during a crisis.

5. Allergies and Dietary Restrictions:
Consider the dietary needs and restrictions of everyone in your household when planning your survival food stockpile. If you or someone in your family has food allergies or follows a specific diet, such as vegan or gluten-free, ensure that you include suitable options in your supplies. This will help maintain proper nutrition and avoid any potential health issues that may arise from consuming allergens or incompatible foods.

6. Variety and Comfort:
In times of crisis, it’s important to maintain morale and comfort. Including a variety of foods in your survival stockpile can help alleviate boredom and provide a sense of normalcy. Consider including comfort foods like chocolate, coffee, or other non-perishable treats that can provide a much-needed boost during challenging times.

In conclusion, when it comes to survival food, prioritize nutrient-dense options with a long shelf life. Avoid excessive sodium and sugar, consider dietary restrictions, and include a variety of foods to maintain morale. By making informed choices and planning ahead, you can ensure that you and your loved ones have access to nourishing meals in times of crisis. Stay prepared, stay safe, and stay nourished.
