Choosing the Right Broadheads: Tips for Accurate Arrow Placement


When it comes to bowhunting, accurate arrow placement is crucial for a successful and ethical harvest. And one of the key factors that contribute to accurate arrow placement is choosing the right broadheads. Broadheads are the business end of your arrows and they play a critical role in determining the outcome of your shot. In this blog post, we will discuss some important tips to help you choose the right broadheads for accurate arrow placement.

1. Consider Your Hunting Style

Before diving into the various types of broadheads, it’s essential to consider your hunting style. Are you hunting from a tree stand or ground blind? Are you planning to take shots at long distances or are you more of a close-range hunter? Answering these questions will help you determine the specific features you need in your broadheads.

2. Fixed Blade Broadheads

Fixed blade broadheads are the traditional choice for bowhunters. They feature a solid design with two or three blades that are either chisel or cut-on-contact. Fixed blade broadheads are known for their strength and durability. They are typically more accurate than mechanical broadheads but can be harder to tune. If you are a seasoned hunter with greater bow tuning skills, fixed blade broadheads might be a suitable choice for you.

3. Mechanical Broadheads

Mechanical broadheads, also known as expandable broadheads, have gained popularity in recent years. These broadheads have blades that remain closed during flight and open upon impact. The advantage of using mechanical broadheads is their increased flight accuracy due to their streamlined design. They also create larger wound channels and tend to leave a bigger blood trail, improving the chances of recovery. However, they may be less durable than fixed blade broadheads and can be more prone to failure, especially when shot at heavy bone.

4. Hybrid Broadheads

As the name suggests, hybrid broadheads combine the best features of both fixed blade and mechanical broadheads. They have a fixed blade in the front section and mechanical blades in the rear section. This design provides the durability and penetration of a fixed blade while allowing for improved flight accuracy and larger wound channels. Hybrid broadheads can be a great option for hunters who want the best of both worlds.

5. Consider the Cutting Diameter

Another important factor to consider when choosing broadheads is the cutting diameter. This refers to the size of the wound channel that the broadhead creates upon impact. A larger cutting diameter can increase the chances of a quick and ethical harvest, as it causes more damage to vital organs and arteries. However, it’s important to note that broadheads with larger cutting diameters may require higher energy to penetrate effectively.

6. Weight and Arrow Spine

The weight of your broadheads, along with the arrow spine, can significantly impact your accuracy. It’s crucial to choose broadheads that match the weight specifications recommended by the manufacturer of your bow and arrows. Using broadheads that are too heavy or too light can affect the flight trajectory of your arrow and lead to inaccurate shot placement.

7. Research and Testing

Before making a final decision, do your research on different broadhead brands and models. Read reviews, watch videos, and seek advice from experienced bowhunters. Additionally, consider testing multiple broadheads by shooting them into a target to evaluate their flight accuracy, penetration, and durability. This way, you can make an informed decision based on your personal preferences and hunting requirements.

In conclusion, choosing the right broadheads is essential for accurate arrow placement in bowhunting. Consider your hunting style, evaluate the pros and cons of different types of broadheads, and take into account factors like cutting diameter, weight, and arrow spine. With the right broadheads in your quiver, you’ll increase your chances of achieving accurate arrow placement and a successful harvest. Happy hunting!
