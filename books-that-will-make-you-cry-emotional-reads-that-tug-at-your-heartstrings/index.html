Books That Will Make You Cry: Emotional Reads That Tug at Your Heartstrings


In the world of literature, there exists a unique power to evoke emotions and stir the deepest parts of our souls. Books have an uncanny ability to transport us to different realms, allowing us to experience a myriad of emotions along the way. While some books make us laugh or fill us with joy, there is a special category of literature that holds the power to make us shed tears and leave an indelible mark on our hearts. These are the books that will make you cry - emotional reads that tug at your heartstrings.

1. ‘The Kite Runner’ by Khaled Hosseini
Set against the backdrop of war-torn Afghanistan, ‘The Kite Runner’ tells the story of Amir and his tumultuous journey of redemption. This heart-wrenching tale explores themes of friendship, guilt, and sacrifice, leaving readers emotionally shattered yet profoundly moved.

2. ‘The Fault in Our Stars’ by John Green
Prepare to have your heartstrings mercilessly tugged as you delve into the lives of Hazel and Gus, two teenagers battling cancer. John Green’s poignant writing captures the rawness of their emotions and takes readers on a rollercoaster ride of love, loss, and the quest for meaning in the face of mortality.

3. ‘A Little Life’ by Hanya Yanagihara
This epic novel follows the lives of four college friends as they navigate the complexities of adulthood and friendship. Hanya Yanagihara masterfully explores themes of trauma, love, and the enduring power of friendship, creating a narrative that is equal parts devastating and beautiful.

4. ‘The Book Thief’ by Markus Zusak
Narrated by Death himself, ‘The Book Thief’ is an unforgettable tale set in Nazi Germany. Through the eyes of Liesel, a young girl with an insatiable love for books, readers are transported into a world of unimaginable cruelty and extraordinary resilience. Prepare to be moved to tears as the power of words and human connection triumphs over darkness.

5. ‘Me Before You’ by Jojo Moyes
In this heartrending love story, Louisa Clark finds herself in the employ of Will Traynor, a once-vibrant man now confined to a wheelchair. Jojo Moyes explores themes of life, love, and the complexities of choice, leaving readers grappling with profound questions about the value of life and the power of human connection.

6. ‘The Lovely Bones’ by Alice Sebold
Alice Sebold takes readers on a haunting journey through the afterlife of Susie Salmon, a young girl who was brutally murdered. As Susie watches over her family from her ethereal vantage point, readers are confronted with themes of grief, healing, and the enduring power of love.

7. ‘The Diary of a Young Girl’ by Anne Frank
One of the most powerful memoirs ever written, ‘The Diary of a Young Girl’ gives readers an intimate glimpse into the life of Anne Frank, a Jewish girl hiding from the Nazis during World War II. Anne’s voice resonates with authenticity, capturing the fear, hope, and resilience of a young girl grappling with the horrors of war.

8. ‘A Thousand Splendid Suns’ by Khaled Hosseini
In this heartbreaking tale set in Afghanistan, Khaled Hosseini weaves together the lives of two women, Mariam and Laila, against the backdrop of political turmoil. This emotional saga explores themes of love, sacrifice, and the indomitable spirit of women in the face of adversity.

9. ‘The Time Traveler’s Wife’ by Audrey Niffenegger
Audrey Niffenegger’s unconventional love story follows the lives of Henry and Clare, whose relationship is complicated by Henry’s involuntary time-traveling. As their lives intersect at different points in time, readers are taken on an emotional journey that explores the complexities of love, fate, and the inevitability of loss.

10. ‘The Color Purple’ by Alice Walker
This Pulitzer Prize-winning novel tells the story of Celie, a young African-American girl living in the early 20th century. Through Celie’s letters, readers bear witness to her journey of self-discovery, resilience, and the power of female friendship, all against a backdrop of racism and oppression.

These books, with their ability to evoke tears and stir deep emotions, serve as a testament to the power of literature. Through their narratives, we are reminded of the shared human experience and the profound impact that stories can have on our lives. So, if you’re looking for a good cry or a book that will leave an indelible mark on your heart, look no further than these emotional reads that will tug at your heartstrings.
