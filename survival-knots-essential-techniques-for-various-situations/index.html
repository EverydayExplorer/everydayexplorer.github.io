Survival Knots: Essential Techniques for Various Situations


When it comes to survival situations, having the right knowledge and skills can mean the difference between life and death. One skill that is often overlooked but is incredibly useful is the ability to tie different types of knots. Whether you find yourself in the wilderness, on a camping trip, or even in an emergency situation, knowing how to tie survival knots can help you secure shelter, build traps, create improvised tools, and more. In this blog post, we will explore some essential survival knots and their applications in various situations.

What makes a good survival knot?

Before we dive into the specific knots, it is important to understand what makes a good survival knot. A good knot should be strong, secure, easy to tie and untie, and it should hold up well under tension. You want a knot that won’t slip or come undone when you need it the most. Additionally, it is helpful if the knot is versatile and can be used for multiple purposes.

The Square Knot

Also known as the reef knot, the square knot is a basic knot that every survivalist should know how to tie. It is commonly used for tying two ends of a rope together to secure items or create a lashing. To tie a square knot, follow these steps:

  1. Cross the two ends of the rope over each other.
  2. Take the end that is now on top and pass it over and under the other end of the rope.
  3. Take the same end and pass it over the other end once more.
  4. Pull both ends tightly to secure the knot.

The square knot is great for tying together two ropes of equal thickness, as well as wrapping bundles of items together.

The Bowline Knot

Next on our list of essential survival knots is the bowline knot. This knot forms a fixed loop at the end of a rope, which makes it perfect for creating a temporary handle, securing a rope to a post, or even constructing a makeshift harness. To tie a bowline knot, follow these steps:

  1. Form a small loop near the end of the rope, leaving a long enough tail to create the desired loop size.
  2. Pass the free end of the rope through the loop from underneath.
  3. Bring the free end back over the main part of the rope, then pass it through the small loop.
  4. Tighten the knot by pulling on the free end and the standing part of the rope.

The bowline knot is considered one of the strongest and most reliable knots, making it an excellent choice in survival situations.

The Taut-line Hitch

When it comes to setting up tents or tarps, the taut-line hitch is a knot you won’t want to be without. This adjustable knot allows you to tighten or loosen a line as needed, making it ideal for securing tents and shelters in various conditions. To tie a taut-line hitch, follow these steps:

  1. Wrap the rope around a post or anchor point, then pass the free end of the rope underneath the standing part.
  2. Bring the free end of the rope back over the standing part and pass it through the loop you just created.
  3. Make one more loop around the standing part of the rope, bringing the free end back through the loop.
  4. To adjust the tension of the knot, slide it up or down the standing part of the rope.

The taut-line hitch is a crucial knot for securing tents in windy conditions, as it allows for quick adjustments to maintain a tight pitch.

The Clove Hitch

The clove hitch is a useful knot that can be used for a variety of applications such as securing a rope to a tree or post or even creating a makeshift clothesline. To tie a clove hitch, follow these steps:

  1. Wrap the rope around the object you want to secure.
  2. Cross the working end of the rope over the standing part.
  3. Bring the working end under the standing part and then over the working end.
  4. Pull both ends of the rope to tighten the knot.

The clove hitch is easy to tie and untie, yet it holds firmly under tension. It is an excellent knot for temporary fastening purposes.

The Prusik Knot

Last but not least, the Prusik knot is a versatile knot that is commonly used for ascending and descending ropes. This knot can be used to create a friction hitch, allowing you to move up and down a rope with ease. To tie a Prusik knot, follow these steps:

  1. Take a smaller diameter rope and pass it around the main rope, creating a loop.
  2. Twist the loop three times, forming a helical shape.
  3. Pass the end of the loop through the beginning of the loop, forming another loop within the helical loop.
  4. Tighten the knot by sliding the loops up towards the main rope.

The Prusik knot is an excellent knot for emergency situations when you need to climb or descend a rope quickly and securely.

Conclusion

Survival knots are an essential skill for anyone venturing into the great outdoors or preparing for emergency situations. With a solid understanding of these essential knots, you will have the confidence and ability to secure shelter, create tools, build traps, and more. Practice these knots regularly to ensure that you can tie them quickly and efficiently when you need them the most. Remember, knowledge and preparation are key to survival, and mastering survival knots is just one step towards being well-prepared for any situation that comes your way.
