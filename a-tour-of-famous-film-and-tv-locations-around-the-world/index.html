A Tour of Famous Film and TV Locations around the World


If you’re a fan of movies or TV shows, then you’ll know just how atmospheric a great location can be. Whether it’s a stunning natural landscape, a fascinating cityscape, or an iconic building, the setting can be just as important to a TV show or movie as the characters and the dialogue. That’s why there’s an entire industry devoted to finding the perfect shooting locations, and why so many fans of popular TV shows and movies love to go on location tours.

If you’re looking for some inspiration for your next trip, here are some famous film and TV locations around the world that you might want to consider.

1. Iceland

Iceland has become a popular location for filmmakers in recent years, thanks to its stunning landscapes and dramatic scenery. Fans of ‘Game of Thrones’ will recognize many of the locations used in the show, including the Thingvellir National Park, which served as the stunning backdrop for the Wildling camp. Iceland also featured in ‘Interstellar,’ ‘Star Wars: The Force Awakens,’ and ‘Prometheus.’

2. New Zealand

New Zealand is another popular destination for filmmakers, thanks to its dramatic and varied landscapes. The ‘Lord of the Rings’ trilogy was filmed here, and fans of the movies can visit many of the locations, including Hobbiton, the village of the hobbits. Other popular locations include Queenstown, which served as the backdrop for Isengard, and Mount Doom, which was filmed on the slopes of Mount Ngauruhoe.

3. New York City

New York City has played host to countless movies and TV shows over the years, and it’s almost impossible to visit the city without encountering a location from your favorite show or movie. From Central Park (seen in ‘When Harry Met Sally’ and ‘Elf’) to the Empire State Building (featured in ‘Sleepless in Seattle’ and ‘King Kong’), New York is a treasure trove of filming locations. The city’s streets and buildings have also provided the backdrop for countless iconic movie scenes, from the opening sequence of ‘Breakfast at Tiffany’s’ to the final showdown in ‘The Godfather.’

4. England

England is another country that has provided a wealth of filming locations over the years. Fans of ‘Harry Potter’ can visit many of the locations used in the movies, including the Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, which was filmed at Alnwick Castle in Northumberland. Other popular locations include Oxford, where many of the scenes from ‘The Golden Compass’ were shot, and the city of Bath, which featured in ‘Les Miserables.’

5. Australia

Australia has been the setting for some iconic movies over the years, and visitors can visit many of the locations used in these movies. Fans of ‘Priscilla, Queen of the Desert’ might want to take a trip to Broken Hill, where much of the movie was filmed, while those who loved ‘The Great Gatsby’ should visit the stunning Art Deco buildings of Sydney. Other popular filming locations in Australia include the Kakadu National Park, which featured in ‘Crocodile Dundee.’

These are just a few of the many famous film and TV locations around the world that you can visit on a tour. Whether you’re a fan of Hollywood blockbusters or indie films, there’s sure to be a location that will capture your imagination and provide the perfect backdrop for your next adventure. So why not start planning your tour today, and discover some of the world’s most iconic film and TV locations for yourself?
