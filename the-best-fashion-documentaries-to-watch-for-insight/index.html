The Best Fashion Documentaries to Watch for Insight


Fashion documentaries offer a unique glimpse into the glamorous and sometimes cutthroat world of the fashion industry. They provide valuable insights into the creative process, the business side of fashion, and the personal stories of influential designers and industry insiders. Whether you’re a fashion enthusiast, a budding designer, or simply curious about the fashion industry, these documentaries are a must-watch. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best fashion documentaries that provide fascinating insight into the world of fashion.

1. ‘The September Issue’ (2009)
Directed by R.J. Cutler, ‘The September Issue’ takes viewers behind the scenes of Vogue magazine’s iconic September issue. This documentary offers a rare glimpse into the editorial process and the intense collaboration between Vogue editor-in-chief Anna Wintour and creative director Grace Coddington. The film showcases the high-pressure environment of the fashion industry and the meticulous attention to detail that goes into creating a fashion magazine.

2. ‘Dior and I’ (2014)
Directed by Frédéric Tcheng, ‘Dior and I’ chronicles the journey of Raf Simons, the newly appointed creative director of the House of Dior. The film provides an intimate look at the creation of Simons’ debut haute couture collection for Dior. Viewers witness the challenges and triumphs of bringing a collection to life, as well as the legacy of the iconic fashion house. ‘Dior and I’ offers a rare glimpse into the creative process of one of the most revered fashion designers of our time.

3. ‘The First Monday in May’ (2016)
Directed by Andrew Rossi, ‘The First Monday in May’ explores the intersection of fashion and art through the lens of the annual Met Gala. The film follows the meticulous planning and execution of the Metropolitan Museum of Art’s Costume Institute exhibition, combining fashion and art. This documentary offers a captivating look at the collision of creativity, celebrity, and high fashion, showcasing the influence of fashion on the global cultural landscape.

4. ‘McQueen’ (2018)
Directed by Ian Bonhôte and Peter Ettedgui, ‘McQueen’ delves into the extraordinary life and career of the late British fashion designer Alexander McQueen. Through interviews with family, friends, and colleagues, as well as archival footage, the film captures the rebellious spirit and dark genius of McQueen. This documentary explores the intersection of fashion, art, and identity, shedding light on the immense talent and tragic loss of one of fashion’s most influential figures.

5. ‘The True Cost’ (2015)
Directed by Andrew Morgan, ‘The True Cost’ takes a critical look at the social and environmental impact of the fast fashion industry. This eye-opening documentary exposes the hidden costs of cheap clothing, from the exploitation of garment workers to the environmental degradation caused by mass production. ‘The True Cost’ serves as a wake-up call, urging viewers to reconsider their consumption habits and embrace a more sustainable approach to fashion.

These fashion documentaries offer a captivating and informative look into the world of fashion, providing valuable insights and inspiration. Whether you’re interested in the creative process, the business side of fashion, or the social impact of the industry, these films are sure to leave you with a deeper understanding and appreciation for the fashion world. So grab some popcorn, get cozy, and prepare to be immersed in the captivating world of fashion through these compelling documentaries.
