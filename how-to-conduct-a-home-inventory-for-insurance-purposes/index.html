How to Conduct a Home Inventory for Insurance Purposes


Have you ever considered the possibility of a natural disaster destroying your home or losing your valuable possessions due to theft? Have you thought about how you can probably be under-insured or over-insured on your homeowners’ insurance policy? It’s understandable if these thoughts have never crossed your mind. However, it’s important to take precautions, plan, and prepare a home inventory for insurance purposes.

What is a home inventory?

A home inventory is an extensive list of all your possessions, both inside and outside of your home. Think of it as something similar to a ‘master list’ of your belongings, including their descriptions, purchase price, and photographs. You can use this list to streamline your annual tax returns, create an estate plan, estimate the value of your belongings, and most importantly, ensure that you are properly insured.

Why is a home inventory necessary?

An up-to-date home inventory allows you to:

1. Accurately estimate the value of your possessions - Without a proper inventory, calculating the value of your valuables can be difficult. This hinders you, in case of loss or damage, to receive a fair payout from your insurance.

2. Ensure that you have not under-insured or over-insured your possessions - Over-insuring your belongings means that you are paying extra premiums for coverage that you don’t really need. While on the other hand, under-insuring your property means you would not receive enough compensation to cover the costs of replacing lost or damaged items.

3. Save time and reduce your stress levels following a loss - Natural disasters or robbery could be traumatic and stressful. In such a situation, having a well-prepared inventory reduces the amount of time you spend cataloging your losses, hence, giving yourself more time to recover from the situation.

How to conduct a home inventory?

Conducting an inventory may be time-consuming but later turns out to be a great relief if the need arises. The following steps can be taken to complete your home inventory.

1. Make a list of every part of your home you want to document - every room in your house, attic, basement, and even your garage.

2. Categorize your items - Choose the method that works best for you, depending on how many items you own. You can categorize your inventory by rooms, by type, by brand, or value. An example of categories to use include; electronics, furniture, antiques, clothing, jewelry, recreational equipment, etc.

3. Start documenting your belongings - Note the brand, make, model, date of purchase, price, and location of each item. Don’t forget to take photographs of your valuables. You should also jot down any specific details like serial numbers, any flaws or defects of your items, and any receipts you may have. For valuable items like jewelry or fine arts, consider hiring an appraiser to give you a professional evaluation.

4. Safely move your inventory out of your home - Consider saving this inventory both in hard and soft copies. You can choose to have your list on paper or electronically. Storing a soft copy in the cloud or external storage can give you added protection.

5. Update your inventory often - Keep updating your inventory as you purchase new items or dispose of older ones.

In summary, conducting a home inventory is an essential step in ensuring you have adequate homeowner’s insurance coverage and complete peace of mind. It’s a practical reminder of valuable items in your possession and shows that you took all steps to prepare for the unexpected. Start your inventory today.
