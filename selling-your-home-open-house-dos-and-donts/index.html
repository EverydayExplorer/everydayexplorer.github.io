Selling Your Home: Open House Do's and Don'ts


Selling your home can be an exciting yet daunting process. One of the key elements in the selling journey is the open house. This is your opportunity to showcase your home to potential buyers, make a lasting impression, and hopefully secure a sale. To ensure that your open house is a success, there are certain do’s and don’ts that you should keep in mind. Let’s delve into the dos and don’ts that can make or break your open house experience.

Do: Declutter and Depersonalize
One of the most important things you can do before an open house is to declutter and depersonalize your home. Potential buyers want to be able to envision themselves living in the space, and having personal items and clutter lying around can make it difficult for them to do so. Clearing out personal items and excess clutter can make your home feel more spacious and inviting.

Don’t: Leave Pets at Home
While your furry friends may be beloved members of your family, it’s best to find alternative arrangements for them during the open house. Not all visitors may be comfortable around pets, and some may have allergies. Additionally, having pets present can be distracting and may take the focus away from your home. Consider asking a friend or family member to look after your pets during the open house.

Do: Highlight Key Features
Showcase the best features of your home during the open house. Whether it’s a stunning fireplace, a renovated kitchen, or a spacious backyard, make sure these key features are highlighted. Consider adding fresh flowers, setting the dining table, or creating inviting seating areas to help potential buyers imagine themselves living in the space.

Don’t: Neglect Curb Appeal
The exterior of your home is the first thing that potential buyers will see, so it’s important to make a good first impression. Ensure that the lawn is well-maintained, the exterior of the house is clean, and the entrance is inviting. Adding some potted plants or flowers can also enhance the curb appeal of your home.

Do: Provide Information and Takeaways
Prepare informational packets or brochures that highlight key details about your home, such as its age, any recent renovations, and unique features. Providing this information can help potential buyers remember your home and distinguish it from others they may have seen. Consider including takeaways such as branded pens or notepads for visitors to remember your home by.

Don’t: Stay at Home During the Open House
While it may be tempting to stick around during the open house to answer questions or provide insights, it’s generally best for sellers to make themselves scarce. Potential buyers may feel uncomfortable discussing their thoughts about the home in front of the current owner. Instead, let your real estate agent handle the open house and be available to answer any questions they may have.

Do: Clean and Stage
Prior to the open house, ensure that your home is sparkling clean. From floors to windows, every surface should shine. Additionally, staging your home with tasteful decor and furniture can help potential buyers envision themselves living in the space. Consider hiring a professional stager if you feel that it will enhance the appeal of your home.

Don’t: Neglect Maintenance
Take care of any necessary maintenance tasks before the open house. Fix leaky faucets, replace burnt-out light bulbs, and touch up any scuff marks on the walls. These small details can make a big difference and demonstrate to potential buyers that your home has been well-maintained.

In conclusion, hosting an open house is a crucial aspect of selling your home. By following these do’s and don’ts, you can ensure that your open house is a success and leaves a positive impression on potential buyers. Remember, the goal is to make your home as inviting and appealing as possible, and these tips can help you achieve that. Good luck with your open house, and may it lead to a successful sale of your home!
