The Best Fantasy Anthologies to Read Now


Fantasy literature has the incredible ability to transport readers to realms beyond imagination, filled with enchantment, adventure, and thrilling characters. While full-length novels often get most of the attention, fantasy anthologies offer a unique treasure trove of stories that showcase the breadth of creativity in the genre. These collections provide a chance to discover new voices, revisit beloved authors, and explore a variety of themes and styles, all within a single binding. In this post, we will delve into some of the best fantasy anthologies you can read right now, each offering a unique glimpse into the vast universe of fantasy writing.

1. The Sandman: Endless Nights by Neil Gaiman

Neil Gaiman’s The Sandman series is a cornerstone of modern fantasy, and Endless Nights is a magnificent exploration of its themes. This anthology consists of eight interconnected stories, each focusing on one of the Endless—Dream, Death, Desire, Despair, Delirium, Destiny, and Destruction. Gaiman’s eloquent prose combines elements of mythology, horror, and romance, making it a must-read for fans of the genre. The illustrations by various artists further enhance the immersive experience, inviting readers to lose themselves in a world where dreams and reality intertwine.

2. The Big Book of Science Fiction edited by Ann and Jeff VanderMeer

While not strictly limited to fantasy, The Big Book of Science Fiction boasts a diverse collection that includes fantasy elements throughout its pages. Compiled by the renowned editors Ann and Jeff VanderMeer, this anthology covers a broad spectrum of speculative fiction, featuring works from classic authors such as H.G. Wells and Isaac Asimov, alongside contemporary writers like Nnedi Okorafor and Ted Chiang. The anthology’s rich variety showcases the evolution of speculative fiction, making it an essential read for anyone interested in the genre’s roots and its future.

3. The Dark Tower: The Gunslinger Born by Stephen King

Stephen King’s The Dark Tower series is a monumental achievement in fantasy literature, weaving together elements of horror, western, and science fiction. The Gunslinger Born is a graphic novel anthology that serves as a prequel to the series, exploring the origins of Roland Deschain, the last gunslinger. With beautifully illustrated panels that capture the gritty, desolate world of Mid-World, this anthology provides a thrilling entry point for readers new to King’s epic saga while delighting long-time fans with deeper insights into Roland’s character and journey.

4. The Year’s Best Fantasy and Horror edited by Ellen Datlow and Terri Windling

This anthology series is a staple in the fantasy and horror community, showcasing the best stories from each year. Edited by the talented duo of Ellen Datlow and Terri Windling, it features a diverse range of voices, ensuring representation from both established and emerging authors. Each edition highlights the most noteworthy tales, from spine-chilling horror to whimsical fantasy, making it a perfect way to stay current with the genre. The anthology captures the zeitgeist of its time, reflecting societal changes and the evolution of storytelling.

5. The Apex Book of World SF edited by Lavie Tidhar

The Apex Book of World SF is a groundbreaking anthology that highlights the best speculative fiction from around the globe. Edited by Lavie Tidhar, this collection features stories from diverse cultures, showcasing the richness of fantasy and science fiction beyond Western borders. Readers will encounter unique mythologies, innovative narratives, and culturally-specific themes that challenge the conventions of traditional fantasy. This anthology is an essential read for those looking to broaden their understanding of the genre and discover new voices from around the world.

6. The Starlit Wood: New Fairy Tales edited by Alex Shvartsman

In The Starlit Wood, editor Alex Shvartsman gathers a collection of modern fairy tales that reimagine classic stories while introducing new narratives. Featuring contributions from some of today’s most exciting fantasy authors, this anthology offers fresh perspectives on traditional themes such as love, betrayal, and transformation. Each story stands alone, yet collectively they create a tapestry of enchantment that celebrates the timeless allure of fairy tales. Readers will find themselves captivated by the blending of contemporary issues with age-old motifs, making this a delightful read for fantasy lovers of all ages.

7. The Best American Fantasy and Science Fiction edited by John Joseph Adams and Heidi Pitlor

This anthology brings together some of the finest short stories from the past year, chosen through a careful selection process overseen by editors John Joseph Adams and Heidi Pitlor. Each volume features a curated collection of stories that represent the pinnacle of fantasy and science fiction writing, showcasing both established authors and rising stars. The anthology is a fantastic way to discover new works that push the boundaries of the genre while celebrating the creativity and imagination that define it.

8. The Improbable Adventures of Sherlock Holmes edited by John Joseph Adams

For fans of both fantasy and mystery, The Improbable Adventures of Sherlock Holmes offers a delightful fusion of the two genres. This anthology features stories that place the iconic detective in fantastical scenarios, from battling mythical creatures to solving supernatural mysteries. With contributions from various authors, including Neil Gaiman and Kim Newman, this collection reimagines Arthur Conan Doyle’s beloved character in imaginative and unexpected ways. The stories pay homage to the detective’s legacy while expanding the boundaries of his universe, making it a fascinating read for fans of both genres.

9. The Best of the Best American Science Fiction and Fantasy edited by Gardner Dozois

This anthology is a compilation of the finest stories from the Best American Science Fiction and Fantasy series over the years. Curated by Gardner Dozois, this collection features a range of stories that exemplify the best of the genre, showcasing both established giants and new voices. The anthology’s strength lies in its variety, as it spans different styles, themes, and perspectives, providing readers with a comprehensive overview of contemporary fantasy and science fiction. It’s a perfect pick for anyone looking to discover the highlights of the genre in one convenient volume.

10. Rogues edited by George R.R. Martin and Gardner Dozois

Rogues is an anthology that explores the morally ambiguous characters who inhabit fantasy worlds. Edited by the renowned George R.R. Martin and Gardner Dozois, this collection features stories by top authors, including Patrick Rothfuss, Scott Lynch, and Garth Nix. Each tale delves into the lives of antiheroes, thieves, and tricksters, offering readers a thrilling exploration of the darker side of fantasy. The anthology showcases the complexity of character development, inviting readers to empathize with characters who may not always follow the rules.

Conclusion

Fantasy anthologies are an excellent way to immerse oneself in the genre, offering a diverse array of stories that capture the imagination. From traditional fairy tales to modern reinterpretations, these collections provide readers with the opportunity to experience a wide range of narratives and styles. Whether you are a long-time fantasy lover or new to the genre, the anthologies listed above offer something for everyone. Dive into these enchanting worlds, and let your imagination soar!
