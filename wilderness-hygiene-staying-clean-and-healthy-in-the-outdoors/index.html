Wilderness Hygiene: Staying Clean and Healthy in the Outdoors


When venturing into the wilderness, whether it’s a day hike or a multi-day backpacking trip, maintaining proper hygiene is essential for your overall well-being. While it may seem challenging to stay clean and healthy in the outdoors, there are simple practices you can incorporate into your routine to ensure you have a comfortable and enjoyable experience. In this blog post, we will explore some tips and tricks for wilderness hygiene that will help you stay fresh and avoid common outdoor ailments.

1. Pack the Essentials

Before heading out into the wild, make sure you have the necessary hygiene essentials in your backpack. These include biodegradable soap, a small towel, toilet paper or wet wipes, and a toothbrush and toothpaste. Remember to pack these items in sealed bags to prevent any leaks or contamination.

2. Practice Leave No Trace Principles

Maintaining proper wilderness hygiene goes hand in hand with practicing Leave No Trace principles. Always dispose of waste properly by burying human waste in a cathole at least 200 feet away from water sources. Use biodegradable soap and make sure to rinse off at least 200 feet away from any water bodies. Pack out all your trash and leave the environment as pristine as you found it.

3. Stay Hydrated

Staying hydrated is crucial for overall health, especially when spending time outdoors. Drinking plenty of water not only keeps you hydrated but also helps flush out toxins from your body. Carry a water filter or water purification tablets to ensure a safe drinking supply throughout your adventure.

4. Personal Hygiene

Maintaining personal hygiene in the wilderness doesn’t have to be complicated. Start by washing your hands regularly with biodegradable soap, especially before meals and after using the restroom. A small portable hand sanitizer can also come in handy when water is scarce. Keep your nails trimmed short to prevent dirt and bacteria from accumulating.

5. Oral Hygiene

Taking care of your oral hygiene is just as important in the outdoors as it is at home. Brush your teeth at least twice a day using a small amount of toothpaste and clean water. Spit out the toothpaste away from water sources to prevent contamination. Flossing is also recommended to remove food particles and maintain healthy gums.

6. Foot Care

Your feet are your most valuable asset when exploring the wilderness, so taking care of them is crucial. Ensure your feet stay clean and dry by changing into clean socks regularly and using foot powder to prevent blisters and fungal infections. If you’re crossing water bodies, make sure to dry your feet thoroughly to avoid developing foot rot.

7. Menstrual Hygiene

For women, managing menstrual hygiene in the wilderness requires some extra planning. Pack an adequate supply of sanitary products in sealed bags and consider using menstrual cups or reusable pads to minimize waste. Always follow Leave No Trace principles when disposing of menstrual waste.

8. Clothing Hygiene

Keeping your clothing clean in the wilderness is important not only for comfort but also for preventing bacterial growth and odors. Pack lightweight, quick-drying clothes that are easy to wash and dry. If possible, wash your clothes in a stream or lake using biodegradable soap, and hang them to dry in the sun.

9. Insect and Tick Prevention

Insects and ticks can carry diseases, so it’s crucial to protect yourself from bites. Use insect repellent containing DEET or natural alternatives like citronella or lemon eucalyptus oil. Wear long sleeves, pants, and socks to minimize exposed skin. After spending time outdoors, thoroughly check your body for ticks and remove them promptly.

10. Mental Hygiene

Lastly, don’t overlook the importance of mental hygiene in the outdoors. Take time to relax, meditate, and enjoy the tranquility of nature. Disconnect from technology and immerse yourself in the beauty of your surroundings. A healthy mind contributes to a healthy body.

By incorporating these wilderness hygiene practices into your outdoor adventures, you can ensure a clean, healthy, and enjoyable experience. Remember, hygiene is not only about personal comfort but also about respecting and preserving the environment. Embrace the beauty of the wilderness while taking care of yourself and leaving no trace behind.

Now, get out there and explore the great outdoors with confidence and cleanliness!
