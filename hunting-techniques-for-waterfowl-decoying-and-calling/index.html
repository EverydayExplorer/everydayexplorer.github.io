Hunting Techniques for Waterfowl: Decoying and Calling


When it comes to hunting waterfowl, there are two techniques that have proven to be highly effective: decoying and calling. These methods not only require skill and patience but also a deep understanding of the behavior and habits of waterfowl. In this blog post, we will explore these two techniques in detail, providing valuable insights and tips for successful waterfowl hunting.

Decoying is the art of using decoys to attract waterfowl to a specific location. The key to successful decoying is to create a realistic and enticing setup that mimics the natural environment of the waterfowl. This involves carefully selecting and positioning decoys, taking into consideration factors such as species, wind direction, and water depth. Decoys come in various shapes, sizes, and colors, so it’s important to choose ones that accurately represent the types of waterfowl you are targeting.

When setting up your decoy spread, it’s crucial to create a sense of realism. This can be achieved by placing decoys in small groups, imitating the natural behavior of waterfowl. Additionally, incorporating motion decoys, such as spinning wings or flapping decoys, can add an extra level of attraction and enticement. Remember to regularly adjust and reposition your decoys to keep them looking natural and enticing.

Now let’s talk about calling, which is the technique of imitating the sounds made by waterfowl to attract them closer. There are several types of calls that hunters use, including duck calls, goose calls, and whistle calls. Each call is designed to imitate specific vocalizations made by waterfowl, such as quacks, honks, or feeding sounds.

To effectively use calling, it’s important to practice and master different calls, as well as understand the appropriate times to use them. For example, using a feeding call when waterfowl are actively feeding can elicit a response. On the other hand, using a hail call to grab their attention from a distance can be effective when scouting for waterfowl. It’s also crucial to pay attention to the behavior of the waterfowl and adjust your calling accordingly. For instance, if they are responding positively to your calls, you can continue with the same call or even mimic their responses.

Combining decoying and calling techniques can greatly increase your chances of a successful waterfowl hunt. By creating a realistic and enticing decoy spread and effectively imitating the vocalizations of waterfowl, you can attract them closer and increase the likelihood of a successful shot.

In conclusion, decoying and calling are two essential techniques for waterfowl hunting. Mastering these techniques requires practice, knowledge of waterfowl behavior, and an understanding of the different calls and decoys available. By honing your skills in decoying and calling, you can enhance your hunting experience and increase your chances of a successful waterfowl hunt.

Remember, successful waterfowl hunting requires not only skill but also respect for the environment and the animals. Always abide by local hunting regulations and practice ethical hunting practices. Happy hunting!
