Firearm Safety for Survivalists: Rules and Best Practices


As a survivalist, being prepared for any situation is of utmost importance. One key aspect of preparedness is firearm safety. Whether you are a beginner or an experienced shooter, understanding and following the rules and best practices of firearm safety is crucial to ensure the safety of yourself and those around you. In this article, we will explore the essential rules and best practices every survivalist should be familiar with.

1. Treat Every Firearm as Loaded

The first and most fundamental rule of firearm safety is to treat every firearm as if it is loaded, even if you have personally checked it and know it is unloaded. Never assume that a firearm is safe. By adhering to this rule, you develop a mindset of caution and avoid accidental discharge.

Always keep your firearm pointed in a safe direction, away from yourself and others. Avoid pointing the firearm at anything you don’t intend to shoot. This rule must be followed at all times, whether you are handling a firearm, cleaning it, or storing it.

2. Keep Your Finger off the Trigger

Another critical rule of firearm safety is to keep your finger off the trigger until you are ready to shoot. This is especially important during handling, loading, and unloading firearms. By keeping your finger outside the trigger guard, you minimize the risk of an unintentional discharge.

3. Know Your Target and What’s Beyond It

Before firing any shot, always identify your target and what lies beyond it. Knowing your target is essential to ensure you are shooting at what you intend to and not at something unintended. By being aware of what’s beyond your target, you can avoid potential harm to others or property.

4. Use Proper Ammunition and Correctly Maintain the Firearm

Using the correct ammunition for your firearm is crucial for safety and optimal performance. Using the wrong ammunition can cause a malfunction or catastrophic failure, resulting in severe injury or damage to the firearm. Always consult the firearm’s manual and follow the manufacturer’s recommendations regarding ammunition.

Proper maintenance is equally important. Regularly inspect your firearm for any signs of wear or damage. Clean and lubricate it as per the manufacturer’s instructions to ensure its reliable function.

5. Store Firearms Securely

When not in use, firearms should be stored securely to prevent unauthorized access. Invest in a sturdy gun safe, lockbox, or storage cabinet to ensure your firearms are kept away from children, potential thieves, or anyone who should not have access to them. Additionally, store ammunition separately from firearms to ensure an added layer of safety.

6. Seek Proper Training and Practice Regularly

No matter how experienced or skilled you are with firearms, ongoing training and practice are essential for firearm safety. Seek professional training from qualified instructors who can help improve your skills and reinforce safety practices.

Regular practice at a shooting range will help you become more proficient in handling firearms while adhering to safety protocols. Additionally, staying up to date with any changes in firearm laws and regulations is essential to ensure you remain compliant and informed.

Conclusion

Firearm safety for survivalists is not a matter to be taken lightly. By following these rules and best practices, you will create a safer environment for yourself and those around you. Remember, responsible firearm ownership requires ongoing education, training, and a commitment to safety. By developing good habits and instilling the importance of firearm safety, you can enjoy the benefits of a valuable survival tool while ensuring the well-being of everyone involved. Stay safe and be prepared!
