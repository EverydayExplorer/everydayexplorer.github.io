Beyond the Bestsellers: 12 Indie Books Worth Checking Out


Welcome to our blog, where we dive into the fascinating world of literature! Today, we want to introduce you to a curated selection of indie books that deserve your attention. We all love bestselling books, but there is something special about discovering hidden gems within the independent publishing community. So, put on your reading glasses, grab a cup of your favorite beverage, and let’s explore “Beyond the Bestsellers: 12 Indie Books Worth Checking Out.”

1. “The Enchanted Hour” by Megan Cox Gurdon: Dive into the world of reading aloud with this heartwarming book. Gurdon explores the magical connection between reading and human connection, highlighting the power of stories to bridge gaps and create lasting bonds.

2. “The Beekeeper’s Daughter” by Jane Jordan: This captivating historical fiction takes us on a journey to 19th-century Cornwall. With a mix of romance, mystery, and secrets, Jordan weaves a tale that will keep you turning the pages late into the night.

3. “The Salt Path” by Raynor Winn: Embark on a transformative journey along the South West Coast Path with Winn and her husband. This memoir is a testament to the resilience of the human spirit, as the couple navigates challenges while rediscovering the beauty of nature and the power of love.

4. “The Invisible Life of Addie LaRue” by V.E. Schwab: Schwab delivers a captivating blend of historical fiction and fantasy in this mesmerizing tale. Follow the immortal Addie LaRue as she navigates the world, leaving an indelible mark on those she encounters.

5. “The Golem and the Jinni” by Helene Wecker: Set in late 19th-century New York, this enchanting novel brings together two mythical creatures from different worlds. Wecker’s storytelling prowess shines as she explores themes of identity, friendship, and the power of cultural heritage.

6. “The Seven Deaths of Evelyn Hardcastle” by Stuart Turton: This mind-bending murder mystery will keep you guessing until the very end. Turton masterfully combines elements of Agatha Christie with a touch of supernatural, creating a thrilling and unique reading experience.

7. “The Bookman’s Tale” by Charlie Lovett: For all the book lovers out there, this is a must-read. Lovett takes us on a literary adventure as we follow a rare book dealer in his quest to uncover the truth behind a centuries-old mystery.

8. “The House in the Cerulean Sea” by T.J. Klune: Prepare to be enchanted by this delightful and heartwarming fantasy. Klune introduces us to a magical orphanage and its quirky inhabitants, teaching us the importance of acceptance and love.

9. “The Night Watchman” by Louise Erdrich: Set in 1950s rural North Dakota, Erdrich’s novel brings to life a vibrant cast of characters amidst the fight for Native American rights. This evocative and beautifully written story is a testament to the strength of community and the power of hope.

10. “The Keeper of Lost Things” by Ruth Hogan: Hogan weaves a whimsical tale centered around lost objects and the stories they hold. This heartwarming novel reminds us of the intricacies and connections that make up our lives.

11. “The Housekeeper and the Professor” by Yoko Ogawa: Immerse yourself in this tender and poignant story of an unlikely friendship between a brilliant mathematician and his housekeeper. Ogawa’s lyrical prose creates a compelling narrative that explores the beauty of memory and human connection.

12. “The Vanishing Half” by Brit Bennett: This thought-provoking novel delves into themes of identity and race through the lives of twin sisters who choose different paths. Bennett’s storytelling is both compelling and profound, making this a must-read for anyone seeking a deeper understanding of the human experience.

These twelve indie books offer a treasure trove of compelling stories, unique perspectives, and unforgettable characters. So, why not step outside the realm of bestsellers and explore the rich and diverse world of independent literature? Happy reading!
