The Story of Ice Wine: How Freezing Temperatures Create Liquid Gold


Ice wine, also known as ’liquid gold,’ is a precious and luxurious treat for wine enthusiasts around the world. This delightful dessert wine is made from grapes that have been frozen while still on the vine, resulting in a unique and concentrated flavor profile. In this blog post, we will dive into the fascinating story of ice wine and explore how freezing temperatures contribute to its production.

The origins of ice wine can be traced back to Germany in the late 18th century. Legend has it that a harsh winter froze the grapes on the vine, and winemakers decided to press the frozen grapes to see what would happen. To their surprise, the resulting juice was exceptionally sweet and flavorful. This accidental discovery marked the beginning of ice wine production, which eventually spread to other cold-climate regions around the world.

To understand why ice wine is so special, we need to examine the process behind its creation. The key factor is freezing temperatures, which play a crucial role in concentrating the sugars, acidity, and flavors in the grapes. Ice wine can only be made when the grapes are frozen naturally on the vine, usually at temperatures below -8°C (17.6°F).

In order to preserve the grapes’ integrity, winemakers carefully select the ideal conditions for ice wine production. The grapes used for ice wine are typically late-harvest varieties, such as Riesling, Vidal Blanc, and Cabernet Franc, which have a high sugar content. These grapes are left on the vine until they reach their peak ripeness, often well into the winter months.

Once the grapes are frozen, they are hand-picked, usually during the early morning hours when temperatures are at their coldest. This manual harvesting process ensures that only the highest quality grapes are selected for ice wine production.

After harvesting, the frozen grapes are immediately pressed before they have a chance to thaw. Unlike traditional winemaking, where the juice of the grape is the primary focus, ice wine production relies on the concentrated sugars that have crystallized within the frozen grapes. This highly concentrated juice, known as must, is collected and fermented at a much slower pace than regular wines.

One of the challenges winemakers face during ice wine production is the low yield. Frozen grapes produce much less juice than their unfrozen counterparts, resulting in limited quantities of ice wine. This scarcity contributes to the high price tag associated with ice wine, making it a truly luxurious beverage for special occasions.

The fermentation process for ice wine can take several months, as the cold temperature significantly slows down the yeast’s activity. This extended fermentation period ensures that the wine retains its delicate flavors and aromas, while also developing additional complexity.

Once fermentation is complete, the ice wine is aged in stainless steel or oak barrels, depending on the winemaker’s preference. This aging process further enhances the wine’s flavor profile, allowing it to mellow and develop unique characteristics over time.

When it comes to tasting ice wine, one can expect a lusciously sweet and rich experience. The high sugar content, balanced by the wine’s acidity, creates a perfectly harmonious and indulgent dessert wine. Ice wine often exhibits flavors of honey, tropical fruits, apricot, and peach, with a long and lingering finish.

While Canada and Germany are renowned for their production of ice wine, other cold-climate regions, such as the United States (particularly New York and Michigan), Austria, and Hungary, have also joined the ranks of esteemed ice wine producers.

In conclusion, ice wine is a truly remarkable creation that showcases the artistry and ingenuity of winemaking. The freezing temperatures that transform grapes into liquid gold result in a wine that is decadently sweet, intensely flavorful, and undeniably luxurious. Whether enjoyed on its own or paired with a delectable dessert, ice wine is an experience that is sure to captivate and delight the palate. Cheers to the enchanting story of ice wine!
