The Aviation Cocktail: Soaring to New Heights


When it comes to classic cocktails, the Aviation is an unsung hero that deserves more recognition. With its beautiful pale blue color, smooth taste, and a touch of floral notes, this drink is bound to take your taste buds on a journey. Whether you’re an aviation enthusiast or a cocktail connoisseur, the Aviation Cocktail is sure to leave you flying high.

Origins of the Aviation Cocktail

The Aviation Cocktail was first introduced in the early 20th century and gained popularity during the Prohibition era. Its exact origins are a bit fuzzy, but it is widely believed to have been created by Hugo Ensslin, a bartender at the Hotel Wallick in New York City. Ensslin’s recipe was featured in his book, ‘Recipes for Mixed Drinks,’ published in 1916.

Components of the Aviation Cocktail

The Aviation Cocktail is a gin-based drink that consists of a few key ingredients. These include:

1. Gin: The backbone of the Aviation, gin brings the juniper flavors that make this cocktail unique. London dry gin is often preferred for its crisp and clean taste.

2. Maraschino Liqueur: This sweet cherry liqueur adds a subtle complexity and a touch of sweetness to the cocktail. A little goes a long way, as too much can overpower the other flavors.

3. Fresh Lemon Juice: The tartness of fresh lemon juice balances out the sweetness of the liqueur, providing a refreshing and zesty character.

4. Creme de Violette: This distinctive ingredient gives the Aviation its signature blue hue and imparts a delicate floral flavor. Creme de Violette can be difficult to find, but it can be replaced with Crème Yvette, a similar liqueur with a hint of berry flavors.

Preparing the Aviation Cocktail

To prepare an Aviation Cocktail, follow these simple steps:

1. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice cubes.

2. Add 2 ounces of gin, ½ ounce of maraschino liqueur, ¾ ounce of fresh lemon juice, and ¼ ounce of Creme de Violette.

3. Shake vigorously for about 10-15 seconds to chill the ingredients.

4. Strain the mixture into a coupe glass.

5. Garnish with a lemon twist for an extra touch of elegance.

Pairings and Variations

The Aviation Cocktail can be enjoyed on its own or paired with various appetizers or light dishes. The floral and citrus notes of the drink make it a perfect accompaniment for seafood, such as oysters or shrimp cocktail. It also pairs well with creamy goat cheese or delicate desserts like lemon tart or lavender-infused pastries.

For those who love experimenting with flavors, there are a few variations of the Aviation Cocktail worth trying:

1. Blue Moon: This variation replaces the Creme de Violette with Blue Curacao, resulting in a brighter blue hue and a slightly different flavor profile.

2. Gin Blossom: In this twist, St-Germain elderflower liqueur is substituted for maraschino liqueur, adding a touch of floral sweetness.

3. Paper Plane: This modern cocktail swaps gin for bourbon and replaces the Creme de Violette with Aperol, creating a bold and bitter-sweet concoction.

Conclusion

The Aviation Cocktail may have had a period of obscurity, but it is now gaining the attention it deserves. It perfectly blends the flavors of gin, maraschino liqueur, lemon juice, and Creme de Violette to create a sophisticated and visually stunning cocktail. So take a sip, close your eyes, and let the Aviation Cocktail transport you to new heights, where the sky is the limit.
