The Dos and Don'ts of Writing a Will


Writing a will may not be the most exciting task, but it is an essential one. A will ensures that your wishes are carried out after you’re gone, providing clarity and peace of mind to your loved ones. However, it’s important to approach this process with caution and attention to detail. In this blog post, we will explore the dos and don’ts of writing a will, guiding you through the process to ensure your wishes are properly documented.

The Dos

1. Do Seek Legal Advice

It’s always a good idea to consult with a lawyer when writing your will. They have the expertise and knowledge to guide you through the process, ensuring that your will is legally valid and covers all necessary aspects. A lawyer can also help you navigate complex family dynamics and ensure that your will accurately reflects your wishes.

2. Do Be Clear and Specific

When drafting your will, clarity is key. Be specific about your intentions and clearly identify your beneficiaries. Ambiguity can lead to confusion and potential disputes among your loved ones. Take the time to think through your decisions and clearly outline your wishes to avoid any misunderstandings.

3. Do Update Your Will Regularly

Life is constantly changing, and your will should reflect those changes. Review your will periodically, especially after major life events such as marriage, divorce, the birth of a child, or the acquisition of significant assets. Updating your will ensures that it remains accurate and up to date with your current circumstances and wishes.

4. Do Appoint an Executor

An executor is responsible for carrying out the instructions in your will. Choose someone you trust, who is organized and capable of handling the responsibilities involved. Discuss your decision with the person beforehand and ensure they are willing to take on the role. It’s also a good idea to name an alternate executor in case your first choice is unable or unwilling to fulfill the role.

5. Do Consider a Guardian for Minor Children

If you have minor children, it’s crucial to name a guardian who will care for them in the event of your passing. Choose someone who shares your values, parenting style, and who is willing to take on the responsibility. Discuss your decision with the potential guardian and ensure they are prepared for the role.

The Don’ts

1. Don’t Procrastinate

Writing a will is a task that many people put off, often assuming they have plenty of time. However, life is unpredictable, and it’s important not to wait until it’s too late. Start the process as soon as possible to ensure your wishes are properly documented and legally valid.

2. Don’t Rely on DIY Templates

While there are DIY will templates available online, it’s generally not recommended to rely solely on these. Laws regarding wills can vary by jurisdiction, and a generic template may not cover all necessary aspects or comply with local requirements. Seek professional advice to ensure your will is legally sound and tailored to your specific needs.

3. Don’t Forget Digital Assets

In today’s digital age, it’s important to consider your digital assets when writing your will. These can include online accounts, cryptocurrencies, or digital media. Clearly outline your wishes regarding these assets and ensure your executor has the necessary information to access and manage them.

4. Don’t Overlook Contingency Planning

While we hope for the best, it’s essential to plan for the worst-case scenario. Consider including provisions for unforeseen circumstances, such as the simultaneous death of you and your beneficiaries. This ensures that your assets are distributed according to your wishes, even in the face of unexpected events.

5. Don’t Keep Your Will a Secret

Once you’ve written your will, it’s important to communicate its existence and location to your loved ones. Inform your executor and trusted family members or friends about the location of your will and any relevant details. This ensures that your wishes can be carried out efficiently and minimizes the risk of your will being lost or overlooked.

In conclusion, writing a will is a crucial step in ensuring your wishes are carried out after you’re gone. By following these dos and don’ts, you can create a will that accurately reflects your intentions, provides peace of mind, and minimizes the potential for disputes among your loved ones. Remember, consulting with a legal professional is always advisable to ensure your will is legally valid and tailored to your specific circumstances.
