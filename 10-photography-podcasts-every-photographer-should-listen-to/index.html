10 Photography Podcasts Every Photographer Should Listen To


In today’s digital age, the photography industry is constantly evolving. As a photographer, it’s essential to stay updated with the latest trends, techniques, and inspiration. While there are numerous ways to enhance your skills, one of the most convenient and accessible options is listening to photography podcasts. These audio shows provide a wealth of knowledge, tips, and insights from experienced photographers and industry experts. In this blog post, we have compiled a list of 10 photography podcasts that every photographer should listen to. So grab your headphones and get ready to be inspired!

1. ‘The Candid Frame’ hosted by Ibarionex Perello:
‘The Candid Frame’ is a go-to podcast for photographers seeking inspiration and in-depth conversations with renowned photographers. Ibarionex Perello interviews a wide range of photographers, discussing their creative process, challenges, and career journeys. This podcast offers valuable insights and practical advice for photographers of all levels.

2. ‘The Six Figure Photography Podcast’ hosted by Ben Hartley:
If you’re looking to take your photography business to the next level, ‘The Six Figure Photography Podcast’ is a must-listen. Hosted by business strategist Ben Hartley, this podcast focuses on the business side of photography, offering tips on marketing, pricing, and building a successful brand.

3. ‘The Art of Photography’ hosted by Ted Forbes:
Ted Forbes, a photographer and filmmaker, dives deep into the art and philosophy of photography in his podcast, ‘The Art of Photography.’ From exploring the works of renowned photographers to discussing various artistic techniques, this podcast will broaden your understanding and appreciation of photography as an art form.

4. ‘Master Photography Podcast’ hosted by Jeff Harmon and Brent Bergherm:
For technical tips, gear reviews, and post-processing tutorials, look no further than the ‘Master Photography Podcast.’ Hosted by Jeff Harmon and Brent Bergherm, this podcast covers a wide range of photography-related topics, including landscape, portrait, and wildlife photography.

5. ‘The Sprouting Photographer Podcast’ hosted by Bryan Caporicci:
Bryan Caporicci, a wedding and portrait photographer, shares his expertise and interviews industry leaders in ‘The Sprouting Photographer Podcast.’ This podcast focuses on the business and marketing aspects of photography, helping photographers build successful and sustainable careers.

6. ‘The Beginner Photography Podcast’ hosted by Raymond Hatfield:
If you’re new to photography or looking to brush up on the basics, ‘The Beginner Photography Podcast’ is a valuable resource. Hosted by Raymond Hatfield, this podcast covers essential photography techniques, gear recommendations, and tips for capturing stunning images.

7. ‘The Grid’ hosted by Scott Kelby and Erik Kuna:
‘The Grid’ is a live weekly talk show hosted by Scott Kelby and Erik Kuna that covers a broad range of photography topics. This podcast features guest appearances from industry experts, gear reviews, and discussions on the latest photography news, making it a well-rounded resource for photographers.

8. ‘The Photo Taco Podcast’ hosted by Jeff Harmon:
Hosted by Jeff Harmon, ‘The Photo Taco Podcast’ focuses on answering photography-related questions in a concise and informative manner. From demystifying technical concepts to providing quick tips for better images, this podcast is perfect for photographers seeking practical advice.

9. ‘The Togcast’ hosted by Sam Gregory and Paul Sanders:
‘The Togcast’ is a podcast dedicated to interviewing photographers from various genres and backgrounds. Hosted by Sam Gregory and Paul Sanders, this podcast offers insights into the creative process, personal projects, and the stories behind captivating images.

10. ‘The Martin Bailey Photography Podcast’ hosted by Martin Bailey:
Hosted by Martin Bailey, a nature and wildlife photographer, ‘The Martin Bailey Photography Podcast’ covers a wide range of photography-related topics. From gear reviews to travel photography tips, this podcast provides valuable insights for photographers looking to expand their skills.

Conclusion:

Photography podcasts offer a convenient way to learn, be inspired, and stay connected to the photography community. Whether you’re seeking business advice, technical tips, or artistic inspiration, the 10 podcasts mentioned in this blog post are a valuable resource for photographers of all levels. So why not add them to your playlist and embark on a journey of growth and creativity? Happy listening and happy shooting!
