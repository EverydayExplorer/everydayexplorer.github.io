Top Kitchen Trends: Whats\ In\ and\ Whats Out


When it comes to home design, the heart of the home is undoubtedly the kitchen. This is the space where families gather to cook, eat, and create memories. As such, it’s no surprise that the kitchen tends to be the focal point of many home renovations. If you’re considering updating your kitchen or simply want to stay on top of the latest trends, this blog post will guide you through the top kitchen trends that are in and out.

Let’s start with what’s in.

1. Open Shelving: Say goodbye to traditional upper cabinetry and hello to open shelving. Open shelving not only opens up the space visually but also allows you to display your most beautiful kitchenware and accessories.

2. Statement Backsplashes: A trend that has been gaining popularity in recent years is the statement backsplash. Whether it’s a gorgeous mosaic, patterned tiles, or a bold color, a statement backsplash can instantly transform the look of your kitchen.

3. Smart Kitchens: With technology becoming an integral part of our lives, it’s no surprise that smart kitchens are on the rise. From smart appliances that can be controlled from your smartphone to voice-activated faucets, incorporating technology into your kitchen can make your daily tasks more convenient and efficient.

4. Mixed Metals: Gone are the days when you had to choose just one metal finish for your kitchen. The trend now is to mix metals for a more eclectic and personalized look. Combining brushed brass with stainless steel or copper accents can add depth and visual interest to your kitchen space.

5. Sustainable Materials: As more people become conscious of their environmental impact, sustainable materials are becoming increasingly popular in kitchen design. From recycled glass countertops to reclaimed wood cabinets, using sustainable materials not only looks great but also helps reduce your carbon footprint.

Now, let’s move on to what’s out.

1. All-White Kitchens: While white kitchens have been popular for many years, the trend is shifting towards more color and texture. All-white kitchens can sometimes create a stark and sterile look, so consider adding pops of color or incorporating natural materials to add warmth and personality to your space.

2. Granite Countertops: Granite countertops, once considered the epitome of luxury, are slowly falling out of favor. Homeowners are now opting for more unique and modern materials such as quartz, marble, and concrete, which offer a wider range of colors and patterns.

3. Closed Floor Plans: Open-concept living has been all the rage for years, and this includes open kitchens. Closed-off kitchens are now considered outdated, as they don’t allow for easy flow and interaction between the kitchen and the rest of the living spaces. If you have a closed floor plan, consider removing walls or adding a pass-through to create a more open and connected space.

4. Matchy-Matchy Decor: Gone are the days of perfectly coordinated kitchen decor sets. Mixing different styles, colors, and textures is now seen as more interesting and individualistic. Don’t be afraid to mix and match different materials and finishes to create a unique and personalized look in your kitchen.

5. Standard Lighting: Basic, recessed can lights are no longer the go-to choice for kitchen lighting. Instead, consider incorporating a mix of different lighting types, such as pendant lights over the island, under-cabinet lighting for task areas, and statement chandeliers for a touch of elegance. Lighting is not only functional but also a great way to add a decorative element to your kitchen.

In conclusion, keeping up with the latest kitchen trends can help you create a modern and stylish space that reflects your personal taste. From open shelving and statement backsplashes to sustainable materials and smart appliances, there are plenty of options to choose from. Just remember that trends come and go, so it’s essential to choose elements that speak to you and will stand the test of time. Happy kitchen designing!
