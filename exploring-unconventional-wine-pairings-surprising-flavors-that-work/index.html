Exploring Unconventional Wine Pairings: Surprising Flavors That Work


When it comes to wine pairings, most people tend to stick to the classics. A nice red with a juicy steak, or a crisp white with some fresh seafood. However, there are so many other flavors out there that can be paired with wine in surprising and delicious ways. In this blog post, we’ll explore some unconventional wine pairings that you may not have considered before.

1. Spicy Foods + Sparkling Wine:
Spicy foods can be a challenge when it comes to wine pairings. However, the effervescence of sparkling wine can help cool down the heat and balance out the flavors. Whether you’re enjoying a spicy Thai curry or a plate of hot wings, try pairing it with a chilled glass of sparkling wine. The bubbles will cleanse your palate and enhance the complex flavors of the spicy dish.

2. Barbecue + Zinfandel:
Barbecue is known for its bold and smoky flavors, and a Zinfandel wine can hold its own against the intensity of grilled meats. The fruity and jammy notes of the Zinfandel complement the rich flavors of the barbecue sauce, creating a mouthwatering combination. Whether you’re enjoying ribs, pulled pork, or even grilled vegetables, reach for a bottle of Zinfandel to elevate your barbecue experience.

3. Chocolate + Cabernet Sauvignon:
Chocolate is often associated with desserts, but it can also be paired with wine for a decadent treat. The tannins in Cabernet Sauvignon help balance out the sweetness of chocolate, creating a harmonious pairing. Whether you’re enjoying a bar of dark chocolate or a rich chocolate cake, a glass of Cabernet Sauvignon will enhance the flavors and create a luxurious experience.

4. Sushi + Rosé:
Sushi is usually paired with either sake or a light white wine, but a refreshing rosé can also be a fantastic choice. The delicate flavors of the sushi, combined with the acidity and fruitiness of the rosé, create a delightful contrast. Whether you’re enjoying sashimi, nigiri, or rolls, try pairing it with a chilled glass of dry rosé for a unique and delicious experience.

5. Pizza + Chianti:
Pizza is a classic comfort food, and it deserves a wine pairing that can match its bold flavors. Chianti, a red wine from Tuscany, is a great choice. The acidity and earthy flavors of Chianti complement the tomato sauce, while the medium-bodied nature of the wine doesn’t overwhelm the pizza flavors. Whether you’re enjoying a classic Margherita or a meat lover’s pizza, a bottle of Chianti will elevate your pizza night.

6. Fried Chicken + Champagne:
Fried chicken may not be the first thing that comes to mind when thinking about wine pairings, but it can actually be a match made in heaven. The crispiness and richness of fried chicken are complemented by the acidity and effervescence of Champagne. The bubbles help cleanse the palate and cut through the fatty flavors, creating a delightful combination. Whether you’re enjoying homemade fried chicken or grabbing a bucket from your favorite fast-food joint, pop open a bottle of Champagne for a truly indulgent experience.

7. Indian Curry + Gewürztraminer:
Indian curry is known for its bold and aromatic flavors, which can sometimes be a challenge to pair with wine. However, Gewürztraminer, a white wine with its own aromatic character, can stand up to the spices and enhance the flavors of the curry. The floral and fruity notes of Gewürztraminer complement the complex flavors of Indian cuisine, creating a unique and enjoyable pairing. Whether you’re enjoying a spicy chicken tikka masala or a fragrant vegetable curry, a glass of Gewürztraminer will enhance the experience.

In conclusion, there are countless unconventional wine pairings that can surprise and delight your taste buds. From spicy foods and sparkling wine to chocolate and Cabernet Sauvignon, don’t be afraid to think outside the box and experiment with different flavors. The key to a successful pairing is finding the right balance and complementing the flavors of both the food and the wine. So next time you’re planning a special meal, consider these unusual pairings and prepare for a culinary adventure. Cheers!
