Exploring the Wines of New York: From Finger Lakes Rieslings to Long Island Merlots


When it comes to wine, many people immediately think of regions like Napa Valley or Bordeaux. However, there is a hidden gem in the northeastern United States that should not be overlooked – New York. The state of New York may not be as famous when it comes to wine, but it offers a diverse range of exceptional wines that are worth exploring. From the refreshing Rieslings of the Finger Lake region to the elegant Merlots of Long Island, there is something for every wine lover in New York.

The Finger Lakes Region: Home to World Class Rieslings

Situated in upstate New York, the Finger Lakes region is known for its stunning natural beauty and, of course, its exceptional Rieslings. The region is named after the 11 narrow, finger-shaped lakes that were formed by ancient glaciers. These lakes, along with the unique microclimates they create, play a crucial role in the production of high-quality wines.

The cooler climate of the Finger Lakes region is ideal for growing aromatic white grape varieties such as Riesling. The lakes act as natural heat sinks, protecting the vineyards from harsh winters and providing a longer growing season. As a result, the Rieslings produced here are known for their vibrant acidity, intense aromatics, and distinct mineral character.

Visitors to the Finger Lakes can embark on a wine trail journey, exploring the different wineries and experiencing the beauty of the region. Some of the most notable wineries include Dr. Konstantin Frank Wine Cellars, Wagner Vineyards, and Hermann J. Wiemer Vineyard. These wineries proudly showcase the unique terroir of the Finger Lakes, producing world-class Rieslings that have garnered international recognition.

The Hudson Valley: Where Wine Meets History

Moving closer to New York City, we find the Hudson Valley, a region known for its rich history and breathtaking landscapes. While the area is primarily known for its apples and cider production, there is a growing presence of wineries that deserve attention.

The Hudson Valley benefits from a moderate climate, influenced by the Hudson River and the surrounding mountains. This makes it suitable for a variety of grape varieties, including Chardonnay, Cabernet Franc, and Pinot Noir. The wineries in this region are characterized by their small-scale and family-owned operations, resulting in wines that are often unique and reflective of the winemaker’s passion and dedication.

One of the prominent wineries in the Hudson Valley is Benmarl Winery, located in Marlboro. Known for its picturesque vineyard, nestled between the mountains and the river, Benmarl offers a range of wines that showcase the region’s diversity. Millbrook Vineyards & Winery, situated in the Hudson River Valley AVA, is another must-visit destination. This estate produces award-winning wines that truly embody the character of the region.

Long Island: A Haven for Merlot Lovers

Heading further south, we find Long Island, an area renowned for its beautiful beaches and, surprisingly, its exceptional Merlots. While Long Island may not be the first place that comes to mind when thinking about quality Merlot, it has rapidly gained recognition for producing wines of elegance and finesse.

The maritime climate of Long Island, coupled with the gravelly soil, creates optimal conditions for growing Merlot grapes. The cooling breezes from the Atlantic Ocean help to moderate temperatures, allowing the grapes to ripen slowly and develop complex flavors. As a result, the Merlots of Long Island are often characterized by their fruit-forwardness, supple tannins, and remarkable aging potential.

No exploration of Long Island’s wineries would be complete without a visit to Wölffer Estate Vineyard in Sagaponack. This iconic winery not only produces exceptional Merlots but also offers a variety of other wines. Wölffer Estate Vineyard boasts a beautiful tasting room and vineyard, providing visitors with a glimpse into the world of Long Island wine.

The Rise of New York Wines

Over the past few decades, New York wines have gained recognition and respect from wine enthusiasts around the world. The state has made significant investments in research and development, which has led to improved vineyard practices and winemaking techniques. This, combined with the unique microclimates and terroir, has allowed New York to craft wines that showcase both quality and diversity.

Besides Riesling, Merlot, and Chardonnay, New York is also producing outstanding Cabernet Franc, Gewürztraminer, and sparkling wines. The state has several designated American Viticultural Areas (AVAs), including the Finger Lakes, Hudson River Region, and North Fork of Long Island. Each region offers a distinct wine experience and a glimpse into the creative spirit of New York winemakers.

Conclusion

While New York may not be the first place that comes to mind when thinking about wine regions, it has certainly established itself as a force to be reckoned with. From the refreshing Rieslings of the Finger Lakes to the elegant Merlots of Long Island, the wines of New York offer a delightful journey of discovery for any wine lover. So, don’t overlook this hidden gem – venture into New York wine country and experience the unique flavors and diverse terroir that make New York wines truly exceptional.
