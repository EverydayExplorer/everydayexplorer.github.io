River Fishing: Tactics for Landing Big Fish


When it comes to river fishing, the thrill of hooking and landing a big fish is unmatched. Whether you’re an experienced angler or just starting out, employing the right tactics can significantly increase your chances of success. In this blog post, we will explore some tried and tested tactics that will help you land those elusive big fish in rivers.

1. Choose the Right Location:
Finding the right fishing spot is crucial when targeting big fish in rivers. Look for areas with deep pools, submerged structures like fallen trees or rocks, and areas with strong currents. These spots provide cover and are ideal feeding grounds for larger fish.

2. Timing is Everything:
Timing your fishing trip can make a world of difference. Big fish are more active during certain times of the day. Early mornings and late afternoons are prime feeding times, as the water is cooler and fish are more likely to be actively searching for food. Plan your fishing trips accordingly to maximize your chances of success.

3. Use Live Bait:
Big fish are often opportunistic feeders and prefer live bait. Opt for larger baitfish like shiners, chubs, or crawfish to attract those trophy-sized fish. Make sure to rig your live bait properly to mimic natural movement and entice big fish to strike.

4. Master the Art of Casting:
Casting accuracy plays a vital role in river fishing. Practice your casting technique to ensure precise placement of your bait or lure. Aim for areas with cover or where the current changes, as big fish often hide in these spots waiting for their next meal.

5. Experiment with Different Lures:
While live bait is effective, don’t underestimate the power of lures. Experiment with various types of lures, such as crankbaits, spinnerbaits, or soft plastics, to find what works best in your fishing location. Pay attention to the color, size, and action of the lure to match the prey fish and attract big fish.

6. Work with the Current:
Understanding the river’s current is crucial for successful fishing. Big fish often position themselves strategically to take advantage of the current flow. Cast your bait or lure upstream and let it drift naturally with the current. This technique presents a more natural presentation and increases your chances of enticing big fish to strike.

7. Patience and Persistence:
Landing big fish in rivers requires patience and persistence. Don’t get discouraged if you don’t get immediate results. Keep trying different tactics, adjusting your presentation, and exploring new areas until you find success. Remember, perseverance pays off when it comes to landing those trophy-sized fish.

8. Practice Catch and Release:
Lastly, it’s essential to practice responsible fishing by practicing catch and release. Big fish are often the breeding stock and play a vital role in maintaining healthy fish populations. Take a quick photo, measure the fish, and release it back into the water promptly. This ensures future generations can experience the thrill of catching big fish in rivers.

In conclusion, river fishing for big fish requires a combination of skill, knowledge, and patience. By following these tactics, you’ll be well on your way to landing those trophy-sized fish. Remember to respect the environment, practice catch and release, and enjoy the thrill of the chase. Happy fishing!
