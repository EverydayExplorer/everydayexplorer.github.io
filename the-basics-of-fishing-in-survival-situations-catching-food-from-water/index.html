The Basics of Fishing in Survival Situations: Catching Food from Water


Fishing is a valuable survival skill that can provide a consistent source of food in emergency situations. Whether you find yourself lost in the wilderness or stranded on a deserted island, knowing how to catch fish from water can mean the difference between going hungry and having sustenance to keep you going. In this blog post, we will explore the basics of fishing in survival situations, including the different methods and techniques you can use to catch fish.

1. Assessing the Environment:
Before you start fishing, it’s important to assess the environment around you. Take note of the bodies of water nearby, such as rivers, lakes, or the ocean. Look for signs of fish activity, such as jumping fish or ripples on the water’s surface. If possible, try to identify the types of fish that are native to the area. This will help you determine what fishing methods and bait to use.

2. Choosing the Right Fishing Gear:
When it comes to fishing in survival situations, keeping your gear simple and lightweight is key. A basic fishing kit should include a fishing line, hooks, and bait. It’s also useful to have a small container to store your catches and a knife for cleaning the fish. Remember to pack a length of strong cord or paracord for creating makeshift fishing lines if needed.

3. Fishing Methods:
There are several effective fishing methods that can be employed in survival situations. The most common methods include:

- Handline Fishing: This method involves using a fishing line with a single hook attached to one end and holding the other end in your hand. Simply cast the line into the water and wait for a fish to bite. This method is simple and requires minimal equipment.

- Spearfishing: Spearfishing is a primitive but effective way of catching fish. You can fashion a spear from a long, sturdy stick and sharpen one end to create a point. Sneak up on fish in shallow waters and thrust the spear downwards to impale them. This method requires patience, skill, and the ability to hold your breath underwater.

- Improvised Nets: If you have access to suitable materials, you can create improvised nets to catch fish. Using paracord or vines, create a net by tying a series of knots to form a grid pattern. Set the net in a strategic location, such as a river or a tidal area, and wait for fish to swim into it. Once trapped, carefully lift the net, gather the fish, and secure them in your container.

4. Bait Selection:
Choosing the right bait is crucial for successful fishing. In survival situations, you may not have access to specialized fishing bait, but there are several alternatives you can use:

- Natural Bait: Look for insects, worms, or other small creatures in the environment that fish naturally feed on. These can be used as live bait to attract fish to your hook.

- Lures: In the absence of natural bait, you can create improvised lures using materials such as bottle caps, shiny metal pieces, or brightly colored fabric tied to your fishing line. These makeshift lures can mimic the movements of prey and attract fish.

5. Patience and Persistence:
Fishing in survival situations requires patience and persistence. Fish may not always bite right away, so it’s important to remain calm and persistent. Be prepared to spend time observing the water and experimenting with different techniques and bait. Remember, the more time and effort you put into fishing, the higher your chances of success.

6. Safety Precautions:
While fishing in survival situations, it’s crucial to prioritize safety. Always be aware of your surroundings, especially if you are near fast-flowing rivers or the ocean. Keep a safe distance from the water’s edge and wear appropriate footwear to avoid slipping and falling. If you decide to wade into water, make sure to assess the depth and current strength before venturing further.

In conclusion, knowing the basics of fishing in survival situations can greatly increase your chances of catching food from water. Assessing the environment, choosing the right gear and bait, employing various fishing methods, and practicing patience and persistence are crucial elements of successful fishing. Remember to always prioritize safety and adapt your techniques to suit your surroundings. With these skills in your arsenal, you’ll have a reliable source of food that can sustain you during times of emergency.
