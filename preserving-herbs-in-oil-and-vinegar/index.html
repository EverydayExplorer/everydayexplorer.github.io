Preserving Herbs in Oil and Vinegar


Today, I want to share with you a time-honored method for preserving herbs - using oil and vinegar. Preserving herbs in oil and vinegar not only helps to extend their shelf life but also allows you to enjoy the flavors and aromas of your favorite herbs throughout the year. Plus, it’s a super simple process that anyone can master!

Preserving herbs in oil and vinegar is a great way to capture the essence of fresh herbs, making them a versatile addition to your culinary endeavors. By infusing the herbs into oil or vinegar, you create a flavorful concoction that can be used in a variety of dishes, from salads and marinades to soups and sauces. Not only do these preserved herbs add a burst of flavor, but they also bring a touch of elegance to your recipes.

To get started, you’ll need some fresh herbs of your choice, such as basil, rosemary, thyme, or oregano. It’s important to use fresh, high-quality herbs for optimal results. You’ll also need a clean jar with a tight-fitting lid, as well as some oil and vinegar. For the oil, you can use olive oil, which is a popular choice, or experiment with other oils like sunflower or grapeseed. When it comes to vinegar, white wine vinegar or apple cider vinegar work well.

The first step is to thoroughly wash and dry the herbs. Removing any dirt or debris is crucial to ensure the best quality preserved herbs. Once the herbs are clean and dry, you can start packing them into the jar. You can leave the herbs whole, or roughly chop them if you prefer smaller pieces.

Next, it’s time to infuse the herbs with the oil or vinegar. Simply pour your chosen liquid into the jar, making sure it covers the herbs completely. You may need to use a spoon or a chopstick to release any air bubbles trapped among the herbs. Remember to leave some space at the top of the jar to allow room for expansion.

After sealing the jar tightly, place it in a cool, dark spot for at least two weeks. This allows the flavors of the herbs to infuse into the oil or vinegar fully. The longer you let it sit, the stronger the flavors will become. However, it’s important to note that herbs preserved in vinegar tend to have a shorter shelf life compared to those preserved in oil.

Once your herbs have infused to your liking, you can strain the oil or vinegar to remove any leftover herb particles. You can use a sieve, cheesecloth, or even a coffee filter for this step. If using oil, transfer the infused oil into a clean, airtight container. It’s important to store the oil in a cool, dark place to maintain its quality and flavor.

Preserved herbs in oil and vinegar can be used in a multitude of ways. For example, the herb-infused oil can be used as a flavorful base for dressings, marinades, or as a finishing oil for roasted or grilled vegetables. The herb-infused vinegar, on the other hand, can be used to create delicious salad dressings or to brighten up sauces and soups. The possibilities are endless!

By preserving herbs in oil and vinegar, you not only save money by not having to buy fresh herbs every time you need them, but you also reduce food waste. This method allows you to enjoy your favorite herbs all year round, even after they may no longer be readily available at the grocery store or farmer’s market. It’s a sustainable way to savor the flavors of summer throughout the year.

In conclusion, preserving herbs in oil and vinegar is a simple yet effective way to capture the flavors and aromas of fresh herbs. It’s a fantastic method for extending the shelf life of herbs and infusing oil and vinegar with their vibrant essence. Whether you’re an avid home cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this preservation technique is definitely worth a try. So go ahead, experiment with different herb combinations, and let your creativity shine through in your culinary creations!
