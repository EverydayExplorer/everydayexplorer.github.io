Wilderness Survival Books: Must-Reads for Outdoor Enthusiasts


In a world increasingly dominated by screens and concrete jungles, there’s something undeniably primal and alluring about the wilderness. Whether you’re an avid outdoor adventurer or just someone who appreciates the raw beauty of nature, there’s a special kind of magic in exploring the great outdoors. But with that sense of adventure comes a need for knowledge and preparation. That’s where wilderness survival books come into play.

These books aren’t just about learning how to start a fire or build a shelter (although those skills are undoubtedly important). They’re about cultivating a mindset of resilience, resourcefulness, and respect for the natural world. From timeless classics to modern guides, the world of wilderness survival literature is vast and varied. So, whether you’re a seasoned woodsman or a complete novice, here are some must-read wilderness survival books to add to your reading list:

  1. ‘Bushcraft 101: A Field Guide to the Art of Wilderness Survival’ by Dave Canterbury: Considered by many as a modern classic, this book covers everything from basic survival skills to more advanced techniques like tracking and trapping. Canterbury’s no-nonsense approach and wealth of experience make this a must-have for anyone venturing into the wild.

  2. ‘Wilderness Survival’ by Gregory Davenport: This comprehensive guide covers a wide range of survival scenarios, from desert to arctic environments. With detailed illustrations and practical advice, Davenport equips readers with the knowledge they need to thrive in the toughest of conditions.

  3. ‘The SAS Survival Handbook’ by John ‘Lofty’ Wiseman: Written by a former SAS survival instructor, this book is packed with essential tips and techniques for staying alive in any situation. From navigation to first aid, Wiseman’s expertise shines through on every page.

  4. ‘Deep Survival: Who Lives, Who Dies, and Why’ by Laurence Gonzales: More than just a how-to guide, this book delves into the psychology of survival. Drawing on real-life stories and scientific research, Gonzales explores what separates those who thrive in adversity from those who perish.

  5. ‘98.6 Degrees: The Art of Keeping Your Ass Alive’ by Cody Lundin: With a focus on practicality and humor, Lundin’s book is both informative and entertaining. From urban disasters to wilderness emergencies, he covers it all with his trademark wit and wisdom.

  6. ‘A Walk in the Woods’ by Bill Bryson: While not a traditional survival manual, this memoir of Bryson’s attempt to hike the Appalachian Trail offers valuable insights into the joys and challenges of outdoor exploration. Plus, it’s guaranteed to make you laugh out loud.

  7. ‘The Ultimate Wilderness Survival Handbook’ by Outdoor Life: Compiled by the experts at Outdoor Life magazine, this book covers everything from basic survival skills to advanced bushcraft techniques. With contributions from a variety of outdoor experts, it’s a comprehensive resource for adventurers of all skill levels.

Whether you’re planning a weekend camping trip or embarking on a solo expedition into the unknown, these wilderness survival books are sure to serve as invaluable companions. So, before you venture out into the wild, take the time to arm yourself with knowledge. After all, in the wilderness, the most important tool you have is your mind.
