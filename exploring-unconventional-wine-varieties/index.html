Exploring Unconventional Wine Varieties


When it comes to wine, most people are familiar with the classic varieties like Chardonnay, Merlot, and Cabernet Sauvignon. But did you know that there is a whole world of unconventional wine varieties waiting to be discovered? These lesser-known grapes can offer unique flavors and experiences that will delight even the most seasoned wine connoisseur. In this blog post, we’ll take a journey through some of these unconventional wine varieties, exploring their origins, characteristics, and the regions where they thrive.

Orange Wine: A Unique Fermentation Process

Let’s start our exploration with orange wine. No, it’s not made from oranges, but rather from white grapes that are left in contact with their skins during fermentation. This process gives the wine a vibrant orange hue and a distinctive flavor profile. With notes of dried fruit, honey, and a slightly tannic finish, orange wine is a great choice for those looking for something different.

Blaufränkisch: The Hidden Gem of Austria

Moving on to red wine, we have Blaufränkisch, a hidden gem from Austria. This grape variety produces wines that are dark, spicy, and full-bodied. With flavors of blackberries, cherries, and hints of pepper, Blaufränkisch is a versatile wine that pairs well with a variety of dishes. If you’re a fan of Cabernet Sauvignon or Syrah, give Blaufränkisch a try and prepare to be pleasantly surprised.

Tannat: A Bold and Powerful Red

For those who prefer bold and powerful red wines, Tannat is the perfect choice. Originally from the Madiran region of France, this grape variety has found a new home in Uruguay, where it thrives in the country’s warm climate. Tannat wines are characterized by their deep color, intense tannins, and flavors of dark berries, chocolate, and tobacco. If you’re looking for a wine that can stand up to hearty dishes like steak or game meats, Tannat is the way to go.

Assyrtiko: A Taste of Greece

If you’re a fan of white wine, don’t miss out on Assyrtiko from Greece. This grape variety is grown primarily on the island of Santorini, where the volcanic soil and sea breeze contribute to its unique character. Assyrtiko wines are crisp, dry, and refreshing, with vibrant acidity and flavors of citrus, green apple, and minerality. Pair Assyrtiko with fresh seafood or enjoy it on its own as a refreshing aperitif.

Mencia: Spain’s Best-Kept Secret

Last but not least, we have Mencia, a red grape variety from Spain that is gaining recognition for its high-quality wines. Mencia wines are medium-bodied with flavors of red berries, floral notes, and a touch of spice. This versatile wine pairs well with a range of dishes, from grilled meats to vegetarian fare. If you’re a fan of Pinot Noir or Gamay, give Mencia a try and discover Spain’s best-kept secret.

Conclusion: Expand Your Wine Horizons

Exploring unconventional wine varieties is a fantastic way to expand your wine horizons and discover new flavors and experiences. From orange wine to Blaufränkisch, Tannat, Assyrtiko, and Mencia, there are countless options waiting to be explored. So next time you’re in the mood for a glass of wine, why not step outside your comfort zone and try something new? Cheers to the wonderful world of unconventional wines!
