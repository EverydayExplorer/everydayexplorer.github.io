Treestand Safety: Precautions and Best Practices for Elevated Hunts


Elevated hunts, such as hunting from treestands, are popular among hunters for several reasons. They provide an advantage by offering a broader field of view and keeping the hunter’s scent elevated, making it less likely for game to detect their presence. However, hunting from treestands also carries inherent risks that hunters must be aware of and take precautions to ensure their safety. In this blog post, we will explore treestand safety and outline the best practices that every hunter should follow when engaging in elevated hunts.

The Importance of Treestand Safety

Treestand accidents can result in serious injuries and even fatalities. According to a study published in the Journal of Trauma and Acute Care Surgery, treestand accidents are responsible for approximately 50,000 injuries and 20 deaths each year in the United States alone. These numbers highlight the need for hunters to prioritize safety when using treestands.

Precautions to Take Before Hunting from a Treestand

Before you head out for your next elevated hunt, there are several precautions you should take to ensure your safety:

  1. Choose a quality treestand: Invest in a treestand that is sturdy, reliable, and meets industry safety standards. Inspect the treestand regularly for any signs of wear and tear. Replace worn out or damaged parts promptly.

  2. Be familiar with your equipment: Before using a treestand, thoroughly read and understand the manufacturer’s instructions. Practice setting up and taking down the treestand in a safe environment until you are comfortable doing it correctly.

  3. Survey the area: Prior to setting up your treestand, survey the area for potential hazards such as dead branches, uneven ground, or obstacles that may impede your descent. Clear away any debris that could compromise your safety.

  4. Always use a safety harness: When using a treestand, it is crucial to wear a safety harness that is properly fitted and connected to the tree. Ensure that your harness is rated for your weight and that it is in good condition.

Best Practices for Treestand Hunts

In addition to the precautions mentioned above, following these best practices will further enhance your safety when hunting from a treestand:

  1. Practice safe climbing: Use a climbing system or climbing sticks with boot-friendly steps to ascend and descend from your treestand. Avoid making sudden movements or carrying unnecessary gear while climbing.

  2. Maintain three points of contact: While ascending or descending from your treestand, always maintain three points of contact with the ladder or climbing sticks. This could be two hands and one foot, or two feet and one hand. By doing so, you’ll minimize the risk of slipping or falling.

  3. Use a lifeline or a haul line: To ensure safe transportation of your gear, use a lifeline or a haul line to raise and lower your equipment. This avoids the need to carry unnecessary weight while climbing and eliminates the risk of dropping gear from the treestand.

  4. Be cautious of your shot selection: When aiming for a shot from your treestand, be mindful of your surroundings. Ensure there are no obstacles between you and the game, and always be aware of your shooting lane. Take ethical shots that reduce the risk of wounding an animal and follow local hunting regulations.

  5. Stay alert and focused: Hunting from an elevated position can provide an exhilarating experience, but it’s crucial to remain alert and focused at all times. Avoid alcohol or substances that may impair your judgment, and be aware of any changes in weather conditions.

Conclusion

Elevated hunts offer hunters a unique advantage, but treestand safety should always be a top priority. By taking the necessary precautions and following the best practices outlined in this blog post, you can minimize the risks associated with hunting from treestands. Remember, accidents can happen to even the most experienced hunters, so always prioritize safety and enjoy your hunt responsibly.
