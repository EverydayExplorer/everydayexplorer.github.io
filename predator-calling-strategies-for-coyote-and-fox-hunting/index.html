Predator Calling: Strategies for Coyote and Fox Hunting


For many hunters, there’s nothing quite like the thrill of predator calling. The excitement of mimicking prey distress calls and coaxing in coyotes or foxes is a true test of skill. But to be successful in predator calling, you need more than just a predator call - you need a strategy. In this blog post, we will explore some effective strategies for coyote and fox hunting, allowing you to increase your success rate and make the most out of your hunting experiences.

Understanding Predator Behavior:
Before diving into the strategies, it’s crucial to have an understanding of predator behavior. Coyotes and foxes are intelligent creatures that rely on their senses, particularly their acute hearing, to locate prey. They are naturally cautious and wary, making them challenging to hunt. However, this cautious nature can work to your advantage if you understand how to use it to lure them in.

Choosing the Right Predator Call:
One of the most critical factors when it comes to predator calling is selecting the right call. There are two main types of calls: hand calls and electronic calls. Hand calls are versatile and allow you to produce a wide range of sounds, including distress calls and prey sounds. Electronic calls, on the other hand, offer a more realistic sound and can be more effective in certain situations. Whichever type of call you choose, make sure to practice with it extensively to mimic the sounds accurately.

Scouting and Location:
Another crucial aspect of successful predator calling is scouting and locating the right spots. Coyotes and foxes are territorial, and they have their preferred hunting areas and travel routes. Look for signs of predator activity such as tracks, scat, or feeding sites. You might also consider using trail cameras to monitor the area and identify patterns of movement. By understanding the behavior and habitat preferences of your targets, you can increase your chances of success.

Timing and Season:
Timing is everything in predator calling. In general, the best times to hunt coyotes and foxes are during early morning or late afternoon when they are most active. Avoid hunting during midday when these predators are more likely to be resting. Additionally, target the periods when the prey they feed on, such as rabbits or small rodents, are most active. This will increase the likelihood of attracting predators.

Camouflage and Concealment:
Predators have sharp eyesight, so proper concealment is crucial to avoid detection. Wear appropriate camouflage clothing that matches the environment you are hunting in. Use natural cover to hide yourself, such as bushes, trees, or rocks. It’s also a good idea to set up your hunting spot downwind from the expected approach routes to prevent being detected by the keen sense of smell predators possess.

Decoy and Attraction Techniques:
To enhance your predator calling success, consider using decoys or attraction techniques. These can be visual or scent-based. Visual decoys resemble wounded prey animals or other coyotes/foxes to provoke curiosity or aggressive responses. Scent-based attractants, such as using predator gland lures or bait, can also be effective in drawing in predators. Experiment with different techniques and observe their results to fine-tune your strategy.

Calling Sequences and Cadences:
The way you use your predator call can significantly impact your success. It’s essential to know when and how long to call. Start with a series of soft, subtle calls to mimic distressed prey. After a short pause, increase the volume and intensity to grab the predator’s attention. Vary the duration and cadence, mimicking the unpredictability of real-life prey sounds. Remember, don’t overcall, as predators can become suspicious if they hear continuous calls for an extended period.

Patience and Persistence:
Predator calling requires patience and persistence. It’s not uncommon to spend hours waiting for a predator to show up. Often, it’s the last few minutes of daylight that yield the most success. Stick to your strategy, stay focused, and maintain a positive mindset. Even if you don’t see any action on a particular hunt, the experience gained and the knowledge of the area will prove valuable in future outings.

Conclusion:
Predator calling for coyotes and foxes is an exhilarating hunting experience that challenges the skills of even the most seasoned hunters. By understanding predator behavior, choosing the right call, scouting your hunting areas, using proper camouflage, and employing effective calling and attraction techniques, you can significantly increase your chances of success. Remember to stay patient, persistent, and continuously improve your skills through practice and observation. Happy hunting!
