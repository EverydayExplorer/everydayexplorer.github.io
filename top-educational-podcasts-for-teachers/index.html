Top Educational Podcasts for Teachers


The world of education is constantly evolving, and teachers are always on the lookout for new and innovative ways to engage their students and enhance their teaching techniques. One popular medium that has gained significant traction in recent years is the podcast. Podcasts have become a go-to resource for educators seeking professional development, inspiration, and practical tips. In this blog post, we will explore the top educational podcasts for teachers that are worth adding to your playlist.

1. ‘The Cult of Pedagogy Podcast’ hosted by Jennifer Gonzalez: If you’re looking for practical strategies, expert interviews, and insightful discussions on a wide range of educational topics, this podcast is a must-listen. Jennifer Gonzalez, an experienced educator herself, offers valuable advice and resources for teachers at all levels.

2. ‘The Ed Mylett Show’ hosted by Ed Mylett: While not exclusively focused on education, this podcast offers invaluable inspiration and lessons from some of the most successful individuals across various fields. Ed Mylett has conversations with thought leaders, entrepreneurs, and influencers, discussing topics like mindset, leadership, and personal growth. These insights can be applied to the educational setting, helping teachers cultivate a positive environment in their classrooms.

3. ‘The Google Teacher Tribe Podcast’ hosted by Kasey Bell and Matt Miller: Technology has become an integral part of education, and this podcast is all about leveraging Google tools and other educational technology in the classroom. Kasey Bell and Matt Miller share practical tips, innovative ideas, and best practices for using technology to enhance student learning and engagement.

4. ‘Culturally Responsive Teaching and Learning’ hosted by Zaretta Hammond: As educators, it is crucial to adopt culturally responsive practices to meet the needs of every student in the classroom. Zaretta Hammond’s podcast dives deep into the topic of culturally responsive teaching and learning, providing insight into creating an inclusive learning environment that values diversity and promotes equity.

5. ‘Teaching Keating’ hosted by John Keating: John Keating, a passionate educator, shares his experiences, insights, and strategies for enhancing teaching practices. This podcast offers practical advice on topics like classroom management, differentiated instruction, and fostering student engagement.

6. ‘The We Teach So Hard Podcast’ hosted by Jennifer Gonzalez, Vicki Davis, and Lisa Durff: This podcast is a candid and relatable exploration of the challenges and joys of teaching. The hosts, all experienced teachers, share their stories, insights, and practical tips for overcoming obstacles and staying motivated in the field of education.

7. ‘Talks with Teachers’ hosted by Brian Sztabnik: Brian Sztabnik interviews accomplished educators and thought leaders, uncovering their secrets to success in the classroom. This podcast offers a wealth of ideas and inspiration for teachers looking to improve their teaching practice and connect with a supportive educational community.

8. ‘The TeachThought Podcast’ hosted by Drew Perkins: This podcast focuses on critical thinking, teaching strategies, and educational innovation. Drew Perkins engages in thought-provoking conversations with educators, authors, and experts, exploring topics like project-based learning, inquiry-based instruction, and curriculum design.

9. ‘The EdTech Take Out’ hosted by Jon Samuelson and Mindy Cairney: For educators interested in exploring the latest educational technology tools and trends, this podcast is an excellent resource. Jon Samuelson and Mindy Cairney discuss practical ways to integrate technology into the classroom, providing valuable insights and recommendations for edtech tools and strategies.

10. ‘The Educators’ Spin On It’ hosted by Kim Vij and Amanda Boyarshinov: This podcast focuses on early education, providing resources, tips, and activities for parents and teachers of young learners. Kim Vij and Amanda Boyarshinov share their expertise on topics like literacy development, sensory play, and STEAM education.

By including these top educational podcasts for teachers in your playlist, you can stay updated on the latest trends, gain inspiration, and discover innovative teaching strategies. Whether you’re a novice teacher or a seasoned educator, there is something for everyone in the world of educational podcasts. So, grab your headphones and start listening to these insightful podcasts that will undoubtedly enhance your teaching practice and ultimately benefit your students.
