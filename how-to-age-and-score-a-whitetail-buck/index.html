How to Age and Score a Whitetail Buck


When it comes to hunting whitetail bucks, one of the most important aspects to consider is the age and score of the deer. Being able to properly determine the age and score of a buck not only helps in making ethical hunting decisions, but it also adds to the excitement and satisfaction of the hunt. In this blog post, we will discuss the methods and techniques that can be used to accurately age and score a whitetail buck.

Age Determination

Age determination is a crucial step in hunting whitetail bucks because it allows hunters to target the mature animals that are considered a trophy. There are several methods that can be used to estimate the age of a buck, including physical characteristics, tooth wear, and body characteristics.

Physical Characteristics

Physical characteristics such as body size and antler development can provide valuable clues about a buck’s age. Young bucks tend to have smaller bodies and less antler mass compared to older bucks. Antler development can also be an indicator of age, as young bucks typically have thinner antlers with less branching, while older bucks tend to have thicker and more massive antlers.

Tooth Wear

Tooth wear is another method that can be used to estimate the age of a whitetail buck. As deer age, their teeth wear down, which can be observed by examining the teeth. Young bucks typically have sharp and pointed teeth, while older bucks have more rounded and flattened teeth due to the wear and tear of chewing on vegetation.

Body Characteristics

Observing the body characteristics of a buck can also provide insights into its age. Older bucks tend to have a more muscular and saggy appearance compared to younger bucks. They may also exhibit more scars and battle wounds on their bodies, which can indicate their dominance and experience in territorial disputes.

Scoring System

Scoring a whitetail buck allows hunters to compare the size and trophy quality of different bucks. The most commonly used scoring system for whitetail bucks is the Boone and Crockett scoring system, which measures antler size and symmetry.

Antler Size

Antler size is determined by several factors, including the number of points, beam length, beam diameter, and mass measurements. Each of these measurements contributes to the overall score of the buck. For example, bucks with more points and longer beams typically score higher than bucks with fewer points and shorter beams.

Antler Symmetry

Antler symmetry refers to the balance and proportionality of the antlers. Bucks with antlers that are symmetrical and evenly matched in size and shape tend to score higher than bucks with asymmetrical antlers. Symmetry is an important factor in determining the overall trophy quality of a buck.

Conclusion

Being able to accurately age and score a whitetail buck is an essential skill for any serious deer hunter. By understanding the physical characteristics, tooth wear, and body characteristics of a buck, hunters can estimate its age and target the mature animals they are looking for. Additionally, using a scoring system like the Boone and Crockett system allows hunters to compare the trophy quality of different bucks. So, if you are a whitetail deer hunter, make sure to hone your skills in age determination and scoring to enhance your hunting experience and increase your chances of harvesting a trophy buck.
