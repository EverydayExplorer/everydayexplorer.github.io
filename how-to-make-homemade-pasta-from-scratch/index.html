How to Make Homemade Pasta from Scratch


There’s something undeniably satisfying about homemade pasta. The tender, toothsome strands, cooked just right and coated in your favorite sauce, can elevate a simple meal into a culinary masterpiece. While store-bought pasta is convenient, nothing beats the taste and texture of pasta made from scratch. In this blog post, we’ll guide you through the process of making homemade pasta, step by step.

To start, you’ll need just a few basic ingredients: all-purpose flour, eggs, and a pinch of salt. The ratio is simple - one cup of flour for every two eggs. Begin by placing the flour on a clean work surface, forming a well in the center. Crack the eggs into the well, and add the salt. Gently whisk the eggs with a fork, gradually incorporating the flour from the edges until a shaggy dough forms.

Next, it’s time to knead the dough. Using the heel of your hand, push the dough away from you, then fold it back over itself. Continue this motion for about 10 minutes, until the dough becomes smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky.

Once the dough is properly kneaded, let it rest for about 30 minutes. This allows the gluten to relax and makes the dough easier to roll out. While the dough is resting, you can prepare your pasta-making equipment. If you have a pasta machine, set it up according to the manufacturer’s instructions. If not, a rolling pin and a sharp knife will do the job just fine.

After the dough has rested, divide it into smaller portions, about the size of an orange. Flatten one portion with your hand, then dust it with flour. Using a rolling pin or a pasta machine, roll out the dough into a thin sheet, approximately 1/8 inch thick. Make sure to keep dusting with flour as needed to prevent sticking.

Now comes the fun part - shaping the pasta. There are countless pasta shapes to choose from, but let’s start with the classic fettuccine. Fold the rolled-out dough into a loose cylinder, then use a sharp knife to cut it into thin ribbons, about 1/4 inch wide. Unravel the ribbons and gently toss them with flour to prevent sticking.

At this point, your homemade pasta is ready to be cooked. Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil, then carefully add the pasta. Fresh pasta cooks much faster than dried pasta, so keep a close eye on it. It should be ready in just a couple of minutes, once it floats to the surface and is al dente.

Drain the pasta, reserving a small amount of cooking water. Toss the pasta with your favorite sauce, adding a little bit of the reserved water to create a silky, cohesive dish. Whether it’s a simple tomato sauce, a creamy Alfredo, or a vibrant pesto, homemade pasta is the perfect canvas for your culinary creativity.

Making homemade pasta from scratch may seem intimidating at first, but with a little practice, it becomes a satisfying and rewarding culinary endeavor. The taste and texture of freshly made pasta are unparalleled, and the process itself can be a therapeutic and enjoyable experience. So why not give it a try? Roll up your sleeves, get your hands dirty, and embark on a pasta-making adventure in the comfort of your own kitchen. Your taste buds will thank you!
