Creative Ways to Organize Your Craft Supplies


Are you tired of rummaging through a messy craft room, searching for that one specific colored thread or a particular paintbrush? If so, it’s time to get organized! Having a well-organized craft space not only makes your creative process smoother but also allows you to fully enjoy your crafting hobby. In this blog post, we will explore some creative ways to organize your craft supplies, helping you create a functional and inspiring workspace.

1. Mason Jar Madness

Mason jars are versatile and perfect for storing small craft supplies. Use them to house buttons, beads, sequins, and even paintbrushes. To make them even more visually appealing, you can paint the lids in coordinating colors or wrap them with colorful washi tape. Stack the jars on a shelf or attach them to a pegboard for easy access.

2. Pegboard Power

Speaking of pegboards, they are a game-changer when it comes to organizing craft supplies. Mount a pegboard on your wall and hang hooks, baskets, and shelves to hold your tools, ribbons, and other supplies. Not only does this keep everything within reach, but it also adds a unique and visually interesting element to your craft room.

3. Shoe Organizer Solution

Don’t let your shoe organizer gather dust in the closet. Repurpose it as a craft supply organizer! Hang it on the back of your craft room door and use the pockets to store scissors, glue sticks, markers, and other small items. This way, you’ll have everything neatly organized and easily accessible.

4. Magnetic Magic

Magnets can be your best friend when it comes to organizing metal craft supplies. Attach a magnetic strip to the inside of a cabinet door or on the wall, and use it to store your metal scissors, paper clips, and even your collection of sewing needles. This keeps them visible and prevents them from getting lost in the depths of your craft room.

5. Label Love

Labels are a simple yet effective way to keep your craft supplies organized. Invest in a label maker or use printable labels to tag your storage bins, drawers, and containers. This not only helps you find what you need quickly but also adds a professional touch to your craft room.

6. Repurposing Delight

Instead of buying expensive storage solutions, look around your house for items that can be repurposed. For example, an old spice rack can become a paint rack, a cutlery tray can hold your knitting needles, or a wine rack can store your rolls of ribbon. Get creative with repurposing and save money while organizing your craft supplies!

7. Color Coding Brilliance

Color coding is not only visually pleasing but also a practical way to organize your craft supplies. Sort your materials by color, whether it’s yarn, fabric, or paper. This not only makes finding what you need easier but also inspires you to create beautiful color-coordinated projects.

8. Roll It Up

Maximize your storage space by rolling up your fabric and storing it in clear plastic bins. This not only keeps your fabric wrinkle-free but also allows you to see what you have at a glance. Stack the bins on shelves for easy access and a tidy craft room.

9. Tackle Box Tidiness

Tackle boxes are not just for fishing! They are excellent for organizing small craft supplies like beads, buttons, and embroidery floss. The divided sections keep everything sorted, and the handle makes it easy to transport your supplies to your crafting table.

10. Think Vertical

When space is limited, think vertical! Install floating shelves on your walls to store your craft supplies. Utilize the space above your craft table by attaching hanging baskets or hooks to hold frequently used tools. By going vertical, you’ll make the most of your available space and keep your craft supplies organized.

Now that you have these creative and practical ideas for organizing your craft supplies, it’s time to transform your craft room into a beautifully organized space. Choose the methods that work best for you and your crafting style. With everything in its place, you’ll be able to focus more on your creative projects and enjoy the process even more. Happy crafting!
