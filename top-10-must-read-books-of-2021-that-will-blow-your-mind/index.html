Top 10 Must-Read Books of 2021 That Will Blow Your Mind


Are you an avid reader looking for some mind-blowing books to dive into this year? Look no further! In this blog post, we’ve compiled a list of the top 10 must-read books of 2021 that will captivate your imagination, challenge your perspective, and leave you thinking long after you’ve turned the last page. Whether you’re a fan of fiction, non-fiction, or even memoirs, we’ve got a diverse selection to suit every taste. So grab a cup of tea, find a cozy spot, and get ready to add these gems to your reading list!

1. ‘The Invisible Life of Addie LaRue’ by V.E. Schwab

This enchanting novel takes readers on a journey through time with its captivating protagonist, Addie LaRue. The story follows Addie as she makes a Faustian bargain to live forever but is cursed to be forgotten by everyone she meets. With poetic prose and an exploration of themes like identity, freedom, and love, this book is an absolute must-read for fantasy lovers.

2. ‘The Four Winds’ by Kristin Hannah

Set during the Great Depression era, ‘The Four Winds’ tells the powerful story of a courageous woman, Elsa, and her struggle to protect her family during one of America’s darkest periods. This historical fiction novel beautifully captures the resilience of the human spirit and the unbreakable bonds of love and family.

3. ‘Project Hail Mary’ by Andy Weir

From the author of ‘The Martian’ comes another gripping science fiction adventure that will keep you on the edge of your seat. ‘Project Hail Mary’ follows Ryland Grace, a lone astronaut on a desperate mission to save humanity from an imminent threat. With clever twists and mind-boggling scientific concepts, this book is perfect for science fiction enthusiasts craving a thrilling read.

4. ‘Klara and the Sun’ by Kazuo Ishiguro

Nobel laureate Kazuo Ishiguro takes readers on a thought-provoking journey in ‘Klara and the Sun.’ This dystopian novel explores the intricate relationship between humans and artificial intelligence, following the story of Klara, an Artificial Friend designed to be a companion for children. With Ishiguro’s signature introspection and philosophical ponderings, this novel is sure to make you question the nature of humanity.

5. ‘Hamnet’ by Maggie O’Farrell

Winner of the Women’s Prize for Fiction, ‘Hamnet’ is a historical fiction novel that delves into the heartbreaking story behind Shakespeare’s play ‘Hamlet.’ With beautiful prose and a profound exploration of grief, love, and loss, Maggie O’Farrell breathes life into the untold story of Shakespeare’s family, making it a must-read for literature enthusiasts.

6. ‘No One Is Talking About This’ by Patricia Lockwood

This debut novel by Patricia Lockwood is a unique blend of humor, wit, and existential musings. ‘No One Is Talking About This’ explores the impact of the internet on our lives through the lens of a social media influencer. With sharp observations and poetic language, Lockwood offers a poignant commentary on the state of our digital world.

7. ‘The Sanatorium’ by Sarah Pearse

If you’re a fan of psychological thrillers, ‘The Sanatorium’ will keep you hooked from beginning to end. Set in a converted sanatorium in the Swiss Alps, this chilling novel follows Elin Warner, a detective investigating a series of mysterious deaths. With an eerie atmosphere and a web of secrets waiting to be uncovered, Sarah Pearse delivers a spine-tingling read that’s hard to put down.

8. ‘Empire of Pain: The Secret History of the Sackler Dynasty’ by Patrick Radden Keefe

In this gripping non-fiction book, Patrick Radden Keefe exposes the dark history behind the Sackler family, the owners of Purdue Pharma, the maker of OxyContin. ‘Empire of Pain’ is a meticulously researched and compelling account of the Sackler’s rise to power and the devastating consequences of their ruthless pursuit of profit. This book offers important insights into the opioid crisis and the influence of the pharmaceutical industry.

9. ‘Braiding Sweetgrass: Indigenous Wisdom, Scientific Knowledge, and the Teachings of Plants’ by Robin Wall Kimmerer

‘Braiding Sweetgrass’ is a profound and lyrical exploration of the relationship between humans and the natural world. In this blend of indigenous wisdom and scientific insight, Robin Wall Kimmerer invites readers to reexamine their connection to the Earth and to recognize the inherent value of the land and its resources. This book is a powerful testament to the importance of ecological stewardship and the wisdom of indigenous cultures.

10. ‘Caste: The Origins of Our Discontents’ by Isabel Wilkerson

In ‘Caste,’ Isabel Wilkerson presents a groundbreaking analysis of the hidden social hierarchy that underlies American society. Drawing parallels between the caste system in India, the Jim Crow era in America, and the present-day systemic injustices, Wilkerson challenges readers to confront the uncomfortable truths of our society. This thought-provoking and timely book is essential reading for anyone interested in understanding systemic racism and working towards a more just future.

Conclusion

There you have it: the top 10 must-read books of 2021 that will blow your mind! From mesmerizing fiction to thought-provoking non-fiction, these books offer something for every reader. Whether you’re looking to escape into a fantastical world, explore historical events from a fresh perspective, or challenge your understanding of the world, these books will keep you engaged and leave a lasting impact. So make sure to add these titles to your reading list and prepare to be transported to new literary realms. Happy reading!
