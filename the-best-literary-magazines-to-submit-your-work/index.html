The Best Literary Magazines to Submit Your Work


As a writer or poet, one of the most important steps in getting your work noticed is finding the right literary magazine to submit to. Literary magazines offer a platform for emerging writers to showcase their talent and gain recognition in the literary community. With so many options out there, it can be overwhelming to choose the best literary magazine to submit your work to. But fear not, because in this blog post, we will discuss some of the top literary magazines that are known for publishing high-quality work and have a strong following.

1. The New Yorker
The New Yorker is widely regarded as one of the most prestigious literary magazines in the world. Known for its fiction and poetry sections, getting published in The New Yorker is a significant accomplishment. They publish work from both established and emerging writers and have a dedicated readership that spans across different demographics.

2. Granta
Granta is a British literary magazine that has been publishing exceptional writing for over one hundred years. They accept fiction, poetry, non-fiction, and even photography submissions. Granta is known for showcasing fresh and innovative voices, making it an excellent platform for emerging writers to gain exposure.

3. Tin House
Tin House is a biannual literary magazine that strives to publish the best contemporary fiction, non-fiction, and poetry. They have a reputation for publishing work that pushes boundaries and challenges conventional storytelling. Tin House also organizes writing workshops and hosts an annual summer writers’ conference, making it a hub for writers to connect and learn from each other.

4. Glimmer Train
Glimmer Train is a highly respected literary magazine that is dedicated to publishing the best short fiction. They have been around for over 30 years and have helped launch the careers of many successful writers. Glimmer Train publishes work from both established and emerging writers and has a loyal following of readers and writers alike.

5. The Paris Review
The Paris Review is a literary magazine known for publishing high-quality fiction, poetry, and interviews with notable authors. Founded in Paris in 1953, The Paris Review has been a staple in the literary world for decades. Getting published in The Paris Review is considered a significant achievement for any writer.

6. AGNI
AGNI is a prestigious literary magazine published by Boston University. They accept submissions in multiple genres, including fiction, poetry, essays, and translations. AGNI is known for its commitment to publishing work that is both aesthetically engaging and intellectually stimulating.

7. One Story
As the name suggests, One Story focuses on publishing one story at a time. Each issue features a single work of fiction, making it a unique platform for writers to showcase their short stories. One Story pays its contributors generously and has gained a reputation for publishing top-notch fiction.

8. Electric Literature
Electric Literature is an online literary magazine that publishes a wide range of fiction, poetry, and essays. They are known for their commitment to promoting diverse voices and experimental writing. Electric Literature also features book recommendations, interviews, and essays on the craft of writing, making it a valuable resource for writers.

9. The Missouri Review
The Missouri Review is a highly regarded literary magazine that has been publishing exceptional work for over 40 years. They accept submissions in multiple genres and pay their contributors generously. The Missouri Review also hosts an annual writing contest, offering writers a chance to win cash prizes and publication.

10. Ploughshares
Ploughshares is a prestigious literary magazine published by Emerson College. They publish a wide range of work, including fiction, poetry, and non-fiction. Ploughshares has been around for over 40 years and is known for publishing work that is both emotionally resonant and beautifully crafted.

When submitting your work to literary magazines, it’s important to research each publication’s submission guidelines and read previous issues to get a sense of the type of work they publish. While these ten literary magazines are well-known for their high-quality publications, there are many other reputable publications out there. It’s essential to find the magazines that align with your writing style and aesthetic. Remember, getting published in a literary magazine is not only a great way to gain exposure but also an acknowledgment of your literary talent. So, polish your work, follow the submission guidelines, and keep the faith. Your words deserve to be heard.
