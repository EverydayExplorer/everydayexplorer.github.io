Wine in Literature: Famous References and Quotes


When it comes to literature, wine has always played a significant role, adding depth and flavor to the written word. From ancient epics to modern novels, wine has been portrayed as a symbol of celebration, passion, and even tragedy. In this blog post, we will delve into the world of wine in literature, exploring famous references and quotes that have stood the test of time.

1. ‘Age appears best in four things: old wood to burn, old wine to drink, old friends to trust, and old authors to read.’ - Francis Bacon

This quote by Francis Bacon captures the essence of wine’s connection to time and aging. It suggests that like old wine, literature and friendships also improve with age, gaining richness and complexity.

2. ‘Wine is bottled poetry.’ - Robert Louis Stevenson

Robert Louis Stevenson’s comparison between wine and poetry highlights the artistic and sensory experience that both offer. Just as poetry can transport us to different emotions and landscapes, wine can transport us to different flavors and sensations.

3. ‘In victory, you deserve Champagne. In defeat, you need it.’ - Napoleon Bonaparte

Napoleon Bonaparte’s famous quote underlines the association of wine, specifically Champagne, with celebration and solace. It suggests that wine, especially Champagne, is a drink that should be enjoyed in moments of triumph and moments of despair.

4. ‘Wine is the only artwork you can drink.’ - Luis Fernando Olaverri

Luis Fernando Olaverri’s quote emphasizes the transient nature of wine as an art form. Unlike other forms of art, wine is meant to be consumed, allowing the drinker to fully experience and appreciate its nuances and complexities.

5. ‘One should always be drunk. That’s all that matters…But with what? With wine, with poetry, or with virtue, as you choose. But get drunk.’ - Charles Baudelaire

Charles Baudelaire’s quote takes a more philosophical approach, suggesting that intoxication, whether through wine, poetry, or virtue, is essential to fully embrace life. It implies that wine can be a catalyst for self-expression and exploration.

6. ‘It is well to remember that there are five reasons for drinking: the arrival of a friend, one’s present or future thirst, the excellence of the wine, or any other reason.’ - Latin Proverb

This Latin proverb highlights the social aspect of wine, emphasizing that it can be enjoyed for various reasons. Whether it be the company of friends, a desire to quench one’s thirst, or simply the quality of the wine itself, wine has the power to enhance any occasion.

7. ‘Wine is the most healthful and most hygienic of beverages.’ - Louis Pasteur

Louis Pasteur’s quote sheds light on the health benefits associated with wine. While moderation is key, wine has long been believed to have positive effects on cardiovascular health and overall well-being.

In conclusion, wine has woven its way through the tapestry of literature, offering a multi-dimensional symbol of celebration, passion, and reflection. From ancient philosophers to modern-day authors, the references to wine in literature serve as a reminder of its timeless allure. So, the next time you uncork a bottle of your favorite vintage, remember that you are not just indulging in a beverage, but also immersing yourself in a literary tradition that has spanned centuries. Cheers!
