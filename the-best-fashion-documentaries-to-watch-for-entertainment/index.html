The Best Fashion Documentaries to Watch for Entertainment


Fashion documentaries offer a fascinating glimpse into the world of style, design, and the creative minds behind the scenes. Whether you’re a fashion enthusiast or simply looking for some entertainment, these documentaries provide a captivating blend of art, history, and personal stories. In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the best fashion documentaries that are not only entertaining but also educational and inspiring. So grab some popcorn, sit back, and get ready to immerse yourself in the glamorous world of fashion.

1. ‘The September Issue’ (2009)
Directed by R.J. Cutler, ‘The September Issue’ takes you behind the scenes of Vogue magazine’s iconic September issue, which is known to be the most important and influential edition of the year. Follow the legendary editor-in-chief Anna Wintour and her team as they navigate the high-stakes world of fashion, showcasing the challenges, creativity, and meticulous attention to detail that goes into creating a fashion bible.

2. ‘Dior and I’ (2014)
Directed by Frédéric Tcheng, ‘Dior and I’ offers an intimate look at the creation of Raf Simons’ first haute couture collection as the creative director of the iconic fashion house, Christian Dior. Witness the intense pressure, the artistic process, and the intricate craftsmanship that goes into producing one of the most prestigious fashion collections in the world.

3. ‘The First Monday in May’ (2016)
Directed by Andrew Rossi, ‘The First Monday in May’ takes you inside the world of the Metropolitan Museum of Art’s Costume Institute and their annual Met Gala. Explore the intersection of fashion and art as curator Andrew Bolton and fashion icon, Vogue’s Anna Wintour, collaborate to create a groundbreaking exhibition, showcasing the influence of Chinese fashion on Western designers.

4. ‘McQueen’ (2018)
Directed by Ian Bonhôte and Peter Ettedgui, ‘McQueen’ delves into the extraordinary life and career of British fashion designer Alexander McQueen. Witness his rise to fame, his rebellious nature, and his innovative approach to fashion. This thought-provoking documentary uncovers the dark side of the fashion industry while celebrating McQueen’s exceptional talent and creative genius.

5. ‘Bill Cunningham New York’ (2010)
Directed by Richard Press, ‘Bill Cunningham New York’ is a heartwarming portrait of the late street fashion photographer, Bill Cunningham. Follow his tireless dedication to capturing the essence of New York City’s street style, his unique eye for fashion, and his unwavering commitment to staying true to himself and his art.

6. ‘Valentino: The Last Emperor’ (2008)
Directed by Matt Tyrnauer, ‘Valentino: The Last Emperor’ offers an intimate and behind-the-scenes look at the final years of renowned fashion designer Valentino Garavani’s career. Witness his creative process, his relationship with his long-time partner Giancarlo Giammetti, and the emotional journey of saying goodbye to the fashion world.

These fashion documentaries not only entertain but also provide valuable insights into the fashion industry’s inner workings, the dedication required to succeed, and the impact that fashion has on our culture. So, whether you’re a fashion aficionado or simply looking for some engaging entertainment, these documentaries are a must-watch. Prepare to be inspired, educated, and captivated by the fascinating world of fashion!
