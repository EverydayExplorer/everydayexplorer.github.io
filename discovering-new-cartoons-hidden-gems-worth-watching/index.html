Discovering New Cartoons: Hidden Gems Worth Watching


Cartoons have always been loved by people of all ages. From the time we were kids, we eagerly awaited Saturday mornings to catch our favorite animated shows. These beloved childhood memories still bring a smile to our faces. But beyond the classics we all know, there is an array of hidden gems in the world of cartoons that are worth discovering.

In this blog post, we will explore some lesser-known cartoons that deserve recognition for their exceptional storytelling, unique animation styles, and thought-provoking themes. These hidden gems will not only pique your interest but also provide you with fresh entertainment options.

1. “Gravity Falls”: This critically acclaimed animated series was created by Alex Hirsch and became an instant hit when it aired on Disney Channel. The show centers around twin siblings Dipper and Mabel Pines, who spend their summer in the mysterious town of Gravity Falls. With its intriguing mysteries, complex character development, and a perfect blend of humor and darkness, “Gravity Falls” captures the hearts of viewers of all ages.

2. “Over the Garden Wall”: A mini-series with only ten episodes, “Over the Garden Wall” is a beautifully animated show that feels like a dark fairy tale. It follows two brothers, Wirt and Greg, who find themselves lost in a strange forest known as the Unknown. The series beautifully explores themes of family, self-discovery, and the importance of perseverance. With its enchanting visuals and hauntingly melodic soundtrack, “Over the Garden Wall” guarantees a captivating viewing experience.

3. “The Dragon Prince”: Created by Aaron Ehasz, the head writer of “Avatar: The Last Airbender,” “The Dragon Prince” is a fantasy adventure set in a world where humans and magical creatures coexist. The show focuses on the unlikely friendship between a human prince, an elven assassin, and a young mage, as they embark on a dangerous journey to prevent war between their kingdoms. With its compelling plot, diverse characters, and stunning animation, “The Dragon Prince” holds its own in the realm of animated series.

4. “Kipo and the Age of Wonderbeasts”: Set in a post-apocalyptic world, “Kipo and the Age of Wonderbeasts” showcases the adventures of a young girl named Kipo, who finds herself on a quest to search for her missing father. This Netflix original series boasts a unique mix of vibrant animation, catchy music, and a diverse cast of characters. With its underlying messages of acceptance and friendship, “Kipo and the Age of Wonderbeasts” is a hidden gem that deserves more recognition.

5. “Hilda”: Based on the graphic novels by Luke Pearson, “Hilda” follows the adventurous young girl of the same name as she explores a mystical world filled with magical creatures and encounters. With its charming art style, heartwarming storytelling, and theme of embracing change, “Hilda” has gained a loyal following. The show beautifully captures the essence of childhood wonder and reminds us of the importance of curiosity and imagination.

6. “Craig of the Creek”: This Cartoon Network series revolves around Craig, a young boy who, along with his two best friends, explores the wilderness of their neighborhood creek, turning it into a world of imaginative adventures and camaraderie. With its relatable characters, relatable themes of friendship and the power of imagination, and diverse representation, “Craig of the Creek” is an absolute gem for both kids and adults alike.

7. “The Owl House”: Created by Dana Terrace, the mastermind behind “Gravity Falls,” “The Owl House” is a fantasy-comedy series that follows Luz, a teenage girl who accidentally stumbles upon a portal to a magical realm. There, she befriends a rebellious witch named Eda and an adorably sarcastic demon named King. With its vibrant animation, relatable characters, and underlying themes of self-acceptance and pursuing one’s passions, “The Owl House” has quickly become a fan favorite.

These hidden gems in the world of cartoons offer something extraordinary for viewers seeking new animated content. Whether you’re nostalgic for the golden age of Saturday morning cartoons or simply in search of captivating storytelling and beautiful animation, these lesser-known shows are an absolute delight. So, grab some popcorn, cozy up, and prepare to be enchanted by these hidden treasures. Happy watching!
