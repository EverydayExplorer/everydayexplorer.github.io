Surviving a Bear Attack: Dos and Don'ts


If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, hiker, or camper, you probably have a healthy respect for the wildlife that shares the great outdoors with you. While the chances of encountering a bear are relatively low, it’s essential to be prepared and know how to react if you ever find yourself face-to-face with one of these formidable creatures. Here are some crucial dos and don’ts to keep in mind when it comes to surviving a bear attack.

Dos:

1. Do Stay Calm

In the event of a bear encounter, it’s crucial to remain as calm as possible. Sudden movements or loud noises can startle the bear and provoke an attack. Take deep breaths and try to maintain a composed demeanor.

2. Do Assess the Situation

Before taking any action, assess the bear’s behavior. Is it a grizzly bear or a black bear? Is it a sow with cubs? Understanding the type of bear and its current state can help you make informed decisions about how to proceed.

3. Do Back Away Slowly

If the bear hasn’t noticed you, quietly and slowly back away, maintaining eye contact with the bear. Be sure not to turn your back on the bear, as this can trigger a predatory response.

4. Do Make Yourself Big

If the bear has noticed you and is approaching, make yourself appear as large as possible. Wave your arms slowly and speak to the bear in a calm, firm voice. This can intimidate the bear and discourage it from coming closer.

5. Do Use Bear Spray

If the bear continues to approach and shows signs of aggression, use bear spray if you have it. Aim for the bear’s face and discharge the spray in short bursts. Bear spray has been proven to be an effective deterrent in bear encounters.

6. Do Play Dead (For Grizzly Bears)

In the event of a grizzly bear attack, and the bear makes contact, playing dead may be your best chance of survival. Lie flat on your stomach, clasp your hands behind your neck, and spread your legs to prevent the bear from flipping you over.

Don’ts:

1. Don’t Run

Running away from a bear can trigger its predatory instincts and provoke a chase. Bears are incredibly fast and can easily outrun humans, so fleeing is not a viable option.

2. Don’t Climb a Tree

Contrary to popular belief, climbing a tree to escape a bear is not a safe strategy. Both grizzly and black bears are adept climbers, and attempting to flee up a tree may only provoke the bear further.

3. Don’t Make Direct Eye Contact

While maintaining eye contact with the bear is essential, staring directly into its eyes can be perceived as a threat or a challenge. Instead, keep your gaze averted or averted.

4. Don’t Approach Bear Cubs

If you encounter a bear with cubs, avoid approaching them at all costs. Mother bears are fiercely protective of their offspring, and any perceived threat to their cubs can lead to an aggressive response.

5. Don’t Scream or Make Sudden Movements

Loud noises and sudden movements can startle a bear and trigger an attack. Stay as quiet and calm as possible, avoiding any actions that may be interpreted as aggressive.

6. Don’t Play Dead (For Black Bears)

Unlike grizzly bears, playing dead is not an effective strategy for surviving a black bear attack. Instead, fight back vigorously using any available means, such as rocks, sticks, or your bare hands.

In conclusion, while the prospect of a bear encounter can be daunting, being equipped with the knowledge of how to respond can significantly increase your chances of surviving such an incident. Remember, prevention is the best defense, so making noise while hiking, storing food properly, and being aware of your surroundings can help avoid bear encounters altogether. However, if you do find yourself in a bear’s presence, staying calm, assessing the situation, and following the dos and don’ts outlined here can make all the difference. Stay safe and respect the wildlife – for both your sake and theirs.
