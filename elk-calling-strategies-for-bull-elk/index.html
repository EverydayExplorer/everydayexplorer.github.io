Elk Calling: Strategies for Bull Elk


There’s nothing quite as thrilling as the sound of a bull elk bugling in the crisp mountain air. Elk calling, the art of imitating the sounds made by elk, is not only an exciting way to interact with these majestic creatures but also a highly effective hunting strategy. Whether you’re a seasoned elk hunter or just starting out, mastering elk calling techniques can greatly increase your chances of success in the field.

Why is Elk Calling Important?

Elk calling is important for a number of reasons. Firstly, it allows hunters to effectively communicate with elk. Bulls use bugling and other vocalizations to communicate with other elk in their herd, and by mimicking these sounds, hunters can attract them and lure them in closer. Additionally, elk calling can help to mask the hunter’s presence and position. By sounding like a fellow elk, hunters can blend into the surroundings and increase their chances of getting within bow or rifle range.

Elk Calling Techniques

There are various elk calling techniques that hunters can employ, depending on the situation and the desired outcome. The most common techniques include bugling, cow calling, and using estrus sounds.

Bugling: Bugling is the signature sound of a bull elk, and it is used to establish dominance and attract cows during the rut. To bugle effectively, hunters need to mimic the deep, resonant sound of a bull elk. This can be achieved with a variety of different bugle calls available on the market. It’s important to note that bugling can be most effective during the peak of the rut when bull elk are actively seeking out mates.

Cow Calling: Cow calling is a technique used to imitate the sound of a cow elk. This can be done using diaphragm calls, external calls, or even by mouth. Cow calling can be effective throughout the season, as it mimics the sounds of a receptive cow elk, attracting both bulls and other cows. By using soft mews, chirps, and estrus calls, hunters can subtly imitate the sounds of a cow in heat, which can be particularly appealing to bull elk.

Estrus Sounds: During the rut, cow elk emit special vocalizations known as estrus sounds. These calls indicate that a cow is in heat, and they drive bull elk into a frenzy. By using estrus sounds, such as estrus whines or estrus screams, hunters can effectively imitate a cow in the height of her reproductive cycle. This can bring in aggressive bull elk looking for a receptive mate.

Choosing the Right Call

When it comes to elk calling, choosing the right call is crucial. With a wide variety of elk calls on the market, it’s important to find one that suits your calling style and preferences. Some hunters prefer diaphragm calls, while others prefer external calls or even bugling tubes. Experimenting with different calls and sounds is key to finding the one that works best for you.

Practicing Your Calling

To master elk calling, practice is essential. Start by listening to actual elk vocalizations, either in the wild or through recordings, to familiarize yourself with the sounds elk make. Then, practice mimicking those sounds using your chosen call. Start with basic cow calls, then move on to bugling and estrus sounds. Remember to start softly and gradually increase the volume and intensity of your calls, mimicking the natural progression of elk vocalizations.

Timing and Location

Timing and location are two crucial factors when it comes to elk calling. Understanding the rutting cycle of elk in your area can greatly improve your chances of success. Typically, the rut occurs in September and October, but it can vary depending on geographical location. During this time, bull elk are more responsive to calls and more actively looking for mates.

Location is also important when elk calling. It’s essential to be in an area where elk are active and likely to respond to your calls. Look for fresh elk sign such as tracks, droppings, and rubs, and pay attention to the terrain and bedding areas. A good understanding of elk behavior and habitat can help you identify the best spots to set up and call.

Conclusion

Elk calling is a highly effective strategy for bull elk hunting, offering hunters the opportunity to communicate with these magnificent animals and increase their chances of success in the field. By mastering elk calling techniques, choosing the right call, and practicing regularly, hunters can enhance their hunting experience and bring themselves closer to the ultimate prize - a bull elk. Remember, patience, persistence, and understanding elk behavior are key to becoming a proficient elk caller. So get out there, hone your skills, and enjoy the thrilling experience of elk calling. Happy hunting!
