The Best Photography Tutorials on YouTube


Photography is an art that combines creativity and technical skills, allowing individuals to capture moments and express their unique perspectives of the world. With the advent of digital cameras and smartphones, photography has become more accessible than ever, enabling enthusiasts to explore their passion without the constraints of expensive equipment. One of the most effective ways to enhance your photography skills is through online tutorials, and YouTube has emerged as a treasure trove of valuable content. In this post, we will explore some of the best photography tutorials available on YouTube that cater to various skill levels and interests.

1. Understanding the Basics of Photography

Before diving into advanced techniques, it is essential to grasp the fundamentals of photography. Numerous YouTube channels offer comprehensive tutorials that cover the basics, helping beginners develop a solid foundation.

a. Jared Polin - FroKnowsPhoto

Jared Polin, known for his engaging and informative style, runs the FroKnowsPhoto channel. His “Photography Basics” series is an excellent starting point for novices. Polin breaks down complex concepts like exposure, aperture, shutter speed, and ISO in a way that is easy to understand. The use of real-life examples and demonstrations helps reinforce these concepts, making them accessible to anyone eager to learn.

2. Mastering Composition Techniques

Once you have a grasp of the basics, the next step is to understand composition. Good composition can elevate an ordinary photograph into a stunning piece of art.

a. Peter McKinnon

Peter McKinnon is a well-known figure in the photography community, and his YouTube channel is filled with creative inspiration. His video on “Photography Composition Techniques” explores the rule of thirds, leading lines, framing, and other essential composition strategies. McKinnon’s charismatic personality and storytelling ability make his tutorials enjoyable to watch, while the practical tips provided can be easily implemented in your photography.

b. The Art of Photography

Hosted by Ted Forbes, The Art of Photography channel dives deep into various aspects of photography, including composition. Forbes’ video on “Understanding Composition” delves into the principles that can help you create visually compelling images. His approach encourages photographers to think critically about their work, fostering creativity and originality.

3. Exploring Advanced Techniques

For those looking to take their photography skills to the next level, advanced techniques can open up new possibilities. YouTube is filled with channels that focus on specialized skills, such as long exposure, macro photography, and more.

a. SLR Lounge

SLR Lounge is a fantastic resource for photographers interested in advanced techniques. Their tutorials cover a wide range of topics, including lighting, post-processing, and advanced camera settings. The channel’s “Mastering Exposure” series provides in-depth insights into how to control exposure in challenging lighting conditions, making it an invaluable resource for intermediate and advanced photographers.

b. B&H Photo Video

B&H Photo Video is a hub for photography tutorials, featuring renowned professionals who share their expertise. Their series on “Advanced Photography Techniques” covers various genres, from portrait photography to landscape and product photography. The tutorials often include behind-the-scenes footage, allowing viewers to see how professionals approach their craft.

4. Enhancing Your Editing Skills

Post-processing is an integral part of modern photography. Learning how to edit your photos effectively can dramatically improve your final images. YouTube offers a plethora of editing tutorials that cater to different software, including Adobe Lightroom and Photoshop.

a. Phlearn

Phlearn is a popular channel dedicated to photography and editing tutorials. Their series on Lightroom and Photoshop is particularly noteworthy. The videos are well-structured, guiding viewers through various editing techniques, from basic adjustments to advanced retouching. The clear explanations and visual demonstrations make it easy for viewers to follow along and apply the techniques to their own work.

b. Piximperfect

For those looking to master Photoshop, the Piximperfect channel is a goldmine of information. Run by Unmesh Dinda, Piximperfect focuses on Photoshop tutorials that cover essential tools and techniques. Whether you want to learn about skin retouching, color grading, or creating composites, Dinda’s tutorials provide step-by-step guidance that can help you achieve professional-looking results.

Photography encompasses a wide variety of genres, each with its unique challenges and techniques. YouTube has channels dedicated to specific types of photography, allowing you to deepen your knowledge in your area of interest.
