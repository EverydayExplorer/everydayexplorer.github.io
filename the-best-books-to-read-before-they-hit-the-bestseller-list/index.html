The Best Books to Read Before They Hit the Bestseller List


In today’s fast-paced world, keeping up with the latest trends can be a challenge. This is especially true when it comes to literature. With so many books being published every day, it’s easy to miss out on the next big hit. That’s why it’s important to stay one step ahead and discover the best books before they become bestsellers. In this blog post, we’ll explore some underrated gems that deserve your attention.

  1. The Silent Patient by Alex Michaelides: Topping the charts in recent years, this psychological thriller has taken the literary world by storm. With its atmospheric setting and twisty plot, this novel will leave you on the edge of your seat until the very last page. If you enjoy suspenseful reads that will keep you guessing, don’t miss out on this one.

  2. Eleanor Oliphant Is Completely Fine by Gail Honeyman: This debut novel has quickly gained a cult following and for good reason. Its quirky protagonist, Eleanor, captivates readers with her unique perspective on life. As the story unfolds, we delve into her journey of self-discovery and find ourselves laughing and crying along the way. Join Eleanor as she learns to navigate the complexities of human connection and find joy in the everyday.

  3. Where the Crawdads Sing by Delia Owens: A New York Times bestseller, this moving tale is set in the marshes of North Carolina. With its breathtaking descriptions of nature and unforgettable characters, this literary masterpiece will transport you to another world. Part coming-of-age story, part mystery, this book will grab hold of your heartstrings and never let go.

  4. The Ten Thousand Doors of January by Alix E. Harrow: If you love magical realism and enchanting storytelling, this book is for you. In this adventure-filled tale, January Scaller discovers a whole new world of possibilities through doors that lead to other realms. With beautiful prose and intricate world-building, this book will ignite your imagination and remind you of the transformative power of storytelling.

  5. Educated by Tara Westover: A memoir that reads like fiction, Educated is a powerful testament to the resilience of the human spirit. Tara Westover recounts her journey from growing up in a strict and isolated household to gaining an education against all odds. This remarkable story will challenge your perspective on education, family, and the pursuit of one’s dreams.

  6. The Giver of Stars by Jojo Moyes: Set in Depression-era America, this historical fiction novel follows the lives of a group of women who become horseback librarians, delivering books to people in remote areas. With its strong female characters, themes of friendship and empowerment, and a touch of romance, The Giver of Stars is a heartwarming and engaging read that will leave you feeling inspired.

  7. Before the Coffee Gets Cold by Toshikazu Kawaguchi: If you’re a fan of magical realism mixed with heartwarming stories, this Japanese novel is a must-read. Set in a small café, where visitors have the chance to travel back in time, this book explores themes of regret, forgiveness, and the human desire for second chances. With its thought-provoking narrative, this book will make you contemplate the choices we make and the paths we take.

Discovering lesser-known books that have the potential to become bestsellers can be a rewarding experience. It allows you to engage with literature in a unique way, becoming an early advocate for talented authors who may not have reached mainstream recognition yet. So, challenge yourself to step outside of popular reading lists and explore these hidden literary gems. Who knows, you might just discover your next favorite book before everyone else does!
