The Revival of Pre-Prohibition Cocktails


When it comes to the world of cocktails, there is no denying the influence of the Prohibition era on the way we drink today. From speakeasies to bootleggers, this period in American history brought about a shift in how people enjoyed their alcoholic beverages. However, with the end of Prohibition in 1933, many classic cocktails fell out of favor, making way for more modern and simplistic drinks.

But in recent years, there has been a noticeable revival of pre-Prohibition cocktails. Bartenders and mixologists are embracing the rich and complex flavors of these vintage drinks and reintroducing them to a new generation of cocktail enthusiasts. And the popularity of these classic cocktails is not limited to just the United States. Around the world, bars and restaurants are paying homage to the era of speakeasies and jazz by serving up these iconic drinks.

So, what exactly are pre-Prohibition cocktails, and why are they making a comeback? Pre-Prohibition cocktails, as the name suggests, refer to those cocktails that were popular before the enactment of the 18th amendment in 1920, which banned the production, sale, and transportation of alcoholic beverages in the United States. These cocktails were crafted with great care and attention to detail, using a variety of spirits, liqueurs, bitters, and fresh ingredients.

One of the most famous pre-Prohibition cocktails is the Old Fashioned. Made with whiskey, sugar, bitters, and a twist of citrus, it is a true classic that has stood the test of time. Another popular choice is the Sazerac, a New Orleans original that combines rye whiskey, absinthe, and Peychaud’s Bitters. And let’s not forget about the iconic Manhattan, made with whiskey, sweet vermouth, and bitters.

What sets these pre-Prohibition cocktails apart from their modern counterparts is the level of complexity and depth of flavor. Each ingredient is carefully chosen to create a balanced and harmonious drink that is a true work of art. The use of bitters, for example, adds a unique and complex flavor profile that is often missing in modern cocktails. And unlike many of today’s drinks, pre-Prohibition cocktails are not overly sweet, allowing the flavors of the spirits to shine through.

But what exactly is driving the revival of pre-Prohibition cocktails? One reason is the growing appreciation for craft and artisanal cocktails. As consumers become more discerning in their drinking preferences, they are seeking out cocktails that are made with quality ingredients and attention to detail. Pre-Prohibition cocktails fit the bill perfectly, as they are crafted with care and precision.

Another reason for the revival of these vintage drinks is nostalgia. The Prohibition era has long held a romanticized and glamorous image, with images of smoky jazz clubs, flapper dresses, and secret speakeasies. By sipping on a pre-Prohibition cocktail, people are transported back in time, experiencing a taste of the past.

Furthermore, the revival of pre-Prohibition cocktails goes hand in hand with the modern craft cocktail movement. Bartenders and mixologists are constantly pushing the boundaries of creativity, experimenting with unique flavor combinations and techniques. And what better way to showcase their skills than by reviving classic cocktails that have withstood the test of time?

In conclusion, the revival of pre-Prohibition cocktails is not just a passing trend. It is a testament to the enduring appeal of these classic drinks and the craftsmanship that goes into making them. Whether you are a seasoned cocktail enthusiast or just starting to explore the world of mixology, I encourage you to step back in time and experience the magic of pre-Prohibition cocktails. Cheers!
