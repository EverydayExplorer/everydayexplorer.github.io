Behind the Voices: Meet the Talented Actors of Your Favorite Cartoons


For many of us, cartoons were a big part of our childhood. From the misadventures of SpongeBob SquarePants to the epic battles of Dragon Ball Z, we’ve come to love and revere our beloved animated characters. But have you ever stopped to think about the talented actors who bring these characters to life? While the drawn animations may be the stars, the voice actors who give them their voice and personality are just as important.

In this blog, we want to celebrate the incredible actors who have given us some of the most unforgettable animated characters in pop culture history.

Tara Strong

Arguably one of the most prolific voice actors of our time, Tara Strong has lent her voice to countless iconic characters. Perhaps most notably, she voiced the titular character in The Fairly OddParents and Twilight Sparkle in My Little Pony: Friendship is Magic. But her vocal range extends far beyond those two shows. From playing tragic villain Harley Quinn in Batman to voicing Dil Pickles in Rugrats, Tara Strong has proven her versatility time and time again.

Tom Kenny

Most of us know Tom Kenny as the voice of SpongeBob SquarePants, but his voiceover career dates back to the early 1990s. Alongside his iconic yellow sponge alter-ego, Kenny has lent his voice to characters like Ice King in Adventure Time, Eduardo in Foster’s Home for Imaginary Friends, and The Mayor in Powerpuff Girls. He’s even voiced characters in video games like the insane Captain Qwark in the popular Ratchet & Clank series.

Grey Griffin

Grey Griffin may not be a household name, but you’ve definitely heard her voice. Starting her career in the early 1990s, Griffin has gone on to voice characters like Daphne Blake in the recent Scooby-Doo movies, Azula in Avatar: The Last Airbender, and Frankie Foster in Foster’s Home for Imaginary Friends. She’s even lent her voice to the world of video games, playing characters in games like Call of Duty and Fallout.

Billy West

You may not recognize the name, but you certainly know the voice. Billy West has been a staple of animation for over 30 years. Some of his most recognizable roles include Fry, Zoidberg, and the Professor in Futurama, and Stimpy in Ren & Stimpy. In addition to his work in cartoons, West has lent his voice to video games like Ratchet & Clank and Disenchantment.

John DiMaggio

If you’ve seen an adult animated show in the last decade, chances are you’ve heard John DiMaggio’s voice. The New Jersey-native has voiced some of the most memorable characters in cartoons in recent memory. From Bender in Futurama to Jake the Dog in Adventure Time and Marcus Fenix in the Gears of War games, DiMaggio’s gruff voice and impeccable comedic timing have made him a fan-favorite.

Conclusion

The world of voice acting has an incredible array of talent, from veterans like Billy West to relatively newcomers like Grey Griffin. They’re the unsung heroes of animation, putting in hours of work to make our favorite characters come to life. So the next time you’re watching an animated show or movie, take a moment to appreciate the immense work and talent that goes into giving those characters their voice.
