The Best Running Trails for Scenic and Challenging Routes


As outdoor enthusiasts, runners are constantly seeking new and exciting routes to conquer. And what better way to elevate your running game than by exploring some of the most scenic and challenging trails? Whether you are a beginner looking to test your endurance or an experienced runner craving a new running adventure, we have compiled a list of the best running trails that offer breathtaking views and a good dose of challenge.

1. The Grand Canyon Rim Trail - Arizona, USA:
Aptly named, the Grand Canyon Rim Trail offers unparalleled beauty and an adrenaline rush like no other. Stretching for 13 miles along the South Rim of the Grand Canyon, this trail takes you through awe-inspiring vistas that will leave you breathless. With its varied terrain and steep drops, this trail is not for the faint-hearted but is definitely worth the effort for the unforgettable panoramic views.

2. The Inca Trail - Peru:
For those who seek a cultural and physical experience, the Inca Trail in Peru is a perfect choice. This iconic trek takes you through the ancient ruins of Machu Picchu, weaving amidst lush greenery, striking mountains, and fascinating archaeological sites. With its combination of elevation gain, stunning vistas, and historical significance, the Inca Trail offers a challenging yet rewarding adventure for trail-running enthusiasts.

3. The Dolomites Alta Via 2 - Italy:
If you crave a trail with jaw-dropping views and a touch of European charm, look no further than the Dolomites Alta Via 2 in Italy. This trail winds its way through the spectacular Dolomite mountain range, offering stunning views of jagged peaks, alpine meadows, and picturesque villages. Along the 109-mile route, you’ll encounter rugged terrain, steep ascents, and descents, making it a thrilling challenge for seasoned runners.

4. The Laugavegur Trail - Iceland:
Iceland’s Laugavegur Trail is a must-visit for trail-runners who desire a unique and otherworldly experience. This 35-mile trail takes you through a diverse landscape featuring geothermal hot springs, colorful mountains, glaciers, and vast lava fields. With rustic huts along the route, this trail provides an opportunity to immerse yourself in the raw beauty of Iceland’s natural wonders while pushing your physical limits.

5. The Annapurna Circuit - Nepal:
For the ultimate trail-running adventure, the Annapurna Circuit in Nepal delivers grandeur on a grand scale. This challenging route encircles the mighty Annapurna mountain range, featuring breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks, cascading waterfalls, and ancient monasteries. With its varying terrain, high altitude, and challenging passes, the Annapurna Circuit is a bucket-list trail that promises both physical and mental growth.

6. The Kalalau Trail - Hawaii, USA:
If tropical paradise and rugged beauty are what you seek, the Kalalau Trail in Hawaii is sure to surpass your expectations. This 22-mile trail along the Na Pali Coast takes you through lush green valleys, towering cliffs, and pristine beaches. With its narrow paths, steep inclines, and stunning coastal vistas, the Kalalau Trail offers a one-of-a-kind running experience that combines the beauty of nature with a challenging workout.

7. The Torres del Paine Circuit - Chile:
Located in the southernmost reaches of Chile, the Torres del Paine Circuit offers pristine wilderness and awe-inspiring landscapes. This 52-mile trail takes you through Patagonia’s rugged terrain, passing glaciers, crystal-clear lakes, and granite peaks. With its unpredictable weather conditions and demanding stretches, this trail is suitable for experienced trail runners looking for a true adventure.

8. The Zion National Park - Utah, USA:
Utah’s Zion National Park is a trail runner’s paradise, offering a multitude of trails that boast stunning red rock formations and sweeping canyon views. From the iconic Angel’s Landing trail, known for its steep switchbacks and dizzying heights, to the challenging West Rim trail, the park provides a variety of options for all skill levels. With its mesmerizing scenery and rigorous ascents, Zion National Park is a true gem for trail runners.

9. The Tour du Mont Blanc - France, Italy, Switzerland:
For a trail-running experience that takes you through three different countries, the Tour du Mont Blanc is a dream come true. This iconic route covers roughly 105 miles around the Mont Blanc massif, weaving through alpine meadows, charming villages, and rugged mountain passes. With its challenging climbs and descents, as well as breathtaking vistas of glaciers and towering peaks, the Tour du Mont Blanc is an adventure that will test your physical limits and leave you with lasting memories.

10. The Waihe’e Ridge Trail - Hawaii, USA:
Another Hawaiian gem, the Waihe’e Ridge Trail on the island of Maui offers runners a combination of lush rainforest, panoramic ocean views, and a challenging ascent. This 4.5-mile trail takes you through an enchanting forest, ending at a ridgeline overlooking the coastline. With its steep inclines, rocky terrain, and verdant surroundings, the Waihe’e Ridge Trail offers an invigorating workout amidst the beauty of Hawaii’s natural landscape.

In conclusion, these running trails offer a perfect balance of scenic beauty and physical challenge for trail-running enthusiasts. From awe-inspiring canyons to rugged mountain ranges and lush rainforests, each trail provides a unique opportunity to push your limits while immersing yourself in the splendor of nature. So lace up your running shoes, pack your sense of adventure, and embark on a journey that will test your endurance, reward your efforts, and leave you with memories to last a lifetime.
