How to Properly Store Your Car: Tips for Long-Term Parking


When it comes to long-term parking, properly storing your car is crucial to ensure its performance and longevity. Whether you’re going on a vacation, relocating for a while, or simply won’t be using your vehicle for an extended period, taking the necessary steps to store your car correctly is essential. In this article, we’ll provide you with some tips on how to properly store your car for long-term parking, ensuring that it stays in optimal condition while you’re away.

1. Find a Suitable Storage Location

The first step in properly storing your car is finding a suitable storage location. Ideally, you should park your car in a covered area such as a garage or carport to protect it from the elements, especially if you live in an area with extreme weather conditions. If a covered area is not available, consider using a car cover. Additionally, ensure that the storage location is secure to minimize the risk of theft or damage.

2. Clean and Wash Your Car Thoroughly

Before storing your car, it’s essential to clean both the interior and exterior thoroughly. Start by removing any trash or debris in the interior and vacuuming the carpets and seats. Wipe down all surfaces, including the dashboard, center console, and door panels, using a mild cleaner. Additionally, clean the exterior of your car, including the windows, wheels, and tires. Applying a coat of wax will help protect the paintwork during the storage period.

3. Change the Oil and Fluids

Another important step in preparing your car for long-term parking is changing the oil and fluids. Old oil and dirty fluids can result in corrosion and engine damage, especially if left sitting for an extended period. Change the oil and filter, and top up other fluids such as coolant, brake fluid, and windshield washer fluid. It’s also a good idea to fill up the gas tank to prevent moisture and condensation from accumulating.

4. Maintain the Battery

Car batteries tend to drain over time, especially if the car is not used for an extended period. To prevent your battery from dying, it’s recommended to either disconnect it or use a battery maintainer. Disconnecting the battery can help conserve its charge, but keep in mind that doing so may reset some of your car’s settings. A battery maintainer, on the other hand, will keep your battery charged without any risk of losing settings.

5. Protect the Tires

Long-term parking can cause flat spots on your tires, which can lead to vibrations and discomfort when driving. To prevent this, inflate your tires to the recommended pressure and consider using wheel chocks to prevent your car from moving. If you have a jack, raising your car and placing it on jack stands can also help alleviate pressure on the tires.

6. Cover or Shield Vulnerable Areas

When storing your car, consider covering or shielding vulnerable areas to offer extra protection. Dust, sunlight, and pests can all cause damage to your vehicle. Use a breathable car cover to protect the paintwork and prevent dust from settling. You can also place mothballs or cotton balls soaked in peppermint oil near the car to deter pests.

7. Visit or Start the Car Periodically

If possible, try to visit or start your car periodically during the storage period. This will help in maintaining the battery charge and ensure that the car’s components remain lubricated. Driving the car for a short distance can also help distribute the oil and fluids throughout the engine, which is beneficial for your car’s overall health.

By following these tips, you can ensure that your car remains in optimal condition when stored for long periods. Properly storing your car will not only protect it from damage but also save you from potential costly repairs down the line. So, the next time you plan to leave your car unused for an extended period, make sure to give it the care it deserves and implement these tips for long-term parking.
