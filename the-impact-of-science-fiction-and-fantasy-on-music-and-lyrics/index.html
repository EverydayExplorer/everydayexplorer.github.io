The Impact of Science Fiction and Fantasy on Music and Lyrics


Science fiction and fantasy have long captivated the imagination of people all over the world. From otherworldly creatures to futuristic technologies, these genres have created whole new worlds for us to explore and escape to. But did you know that science fiction and fantasy have also made a significant impact on the world of music and lyrics?

Throughout history, musicians and songwriters have drawn inspiration from the themes, characters, and settings found in science fiction and fantasy. Perhaps one of the most prominent examples is the legendary rock band Queen. Their iconic song ‘Bohemian Rhapsody’ is often regarded as one of the greatest rock songs of all time, and its lyrics are filled with fantastical imagery and storytelling elements. The song’s lyrics, which tell the story of a person’s journey into a mysterious and otherworldly realm, have captured the hearts and imaginations of millions of listeners worldwide.

Another example of science fiction and fantasy’s influence on music can be found in the work of progressive rock band Pink Floyd. Their album ‘The Wall,’ released in 1979, tells the story of a rock star named Pink who builds a metaphorical wall around himself as a defense mechanism against the outside world. The album’s lyrics and concept draw heavily from themes of isolation and alienation, creating a dark and atmospheric world that resonates with listeners to this day.

In more recent times, the influence of science fiction and fantasy can be heard in the music of electronic music producer and DJ, Deadmau5. Drawing inspiration from science fiction books and movies, Deadmau5 has created a distinct sound that takes listeners on a futuristic journey through space and time. His track ‘Strobe’ is often praised for its dreamlike quality and ethereal atmosphere, capturing the essence of science fiction and fantasy in musical form.

But it’s not just the lyrics and themes of songs that have been shaped by science fiction and fantasy. The very sound and production techniques used in music have also been influenced by these genres. Take, for example, the use of synthesizers and electronic instruments in modern music. These futuristic-sounding instruments have become synonymous with science fiction and have been used to add an otherworldly element to songs and soundtracks. From the iconic ‘Blade Runner’ score to the synth-driven sound of ’80s pop music, the influence of science fiction and fantasy can be heard throughout the musical landscape.

Furthermore, science fiction and fantasy have also influenced the aesthetics and visual aspects of music. Many artists and bands have embraced the fantastical and futuristic elements of these genres in their stage shows, music videos, and album artwork. The costumes, set designs, and visuals used in these performances often evoke a sense of awe and wonder, transporting audiences to imaginary worlds and creating an immersive experience.

In conclusion, it is clear that science fiction and fantasy have had a profound impact on the world of music and lyrics. From the storytelling elements and imagery found in iconic songs to the futuristic soundscapes and visual aesthetics of modern music, these genres have provided a fertile ground for musicians and songwriters to explore and experiment. Whether it’s through lyrics that tell fantastical tales or through the sonic textures and atmospheric productions, science fiction and fantasy continue to inspire and captivate artists and listeners alike. So next time you listen to your favorite song or attend a live performance, take a moment to appreciate the influence of science fiction and fantasy on the music that moves you.
