10 Kitchen Hacks Every Home Cook Should Know

4 min · 687 words

In today’s fast-paced world, home cooks are constantly looking for ways to streamline their cooking process while still creating delicious meals. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or just starting out in the kitchen, having a few handy kitchen hacks up your sleeve can make a world of difference. From saving time to enhancing flavors, here are 10 kitchen hacks every home cook should know.

1. Revive Stale Bread with Water: Don’t let that loaf of bread go to waste! If you find yourself with stale bread, simply run it under water and pop it in the oven for a few minutes. The steam will revive the bread, leaving it fresh and ready to use.

2. Freeze Excess Herbs in Olive Oil: Fresh herbs can add a burst of flavor to any dish, but they often go bad before we can use them all. To preserve your herbs, chop them up and freeze them in ice cube trays filled with olive oil. These herb-infused oil cubes can be easily added to your cooking whenever you need a pop of flavor.

3. Prevent Pot Boil-overs with a Wooden Spoon: Tired of your pots boiling over and creating a mess on your stovetop? Simply place a wooden spoon across the top of the pot. The wooden spoon will break the surface tension of the boiling water, preventing it from spilling over.

4. Use Citrus Peels to Freshen Garbage Disposal: Instead of throwing away your citrus peels, toss them into your garbage disposal. The oils in the peels will freshen up your disposal and leave a pleasant scent in your kitchen.

5. Store Cut Avocados with Onion: Avocados can quickly turn brown once they’re cut open, making them less appealing to eat. To keep your avocados fresh for longer, store them with a slice of onion in an airtight container. The onion will release gases that help slow down the browning process.

6. Freeze Wine in Ice Cube Trays for Cooking: If you find yourself with leftover wine, don’t let it go to waste. Pour the wine into ice cube trays and freeze them. These wine cubes can be easily added to your cooking, enhancing the flavor of sauces and stews.

7. Keep Brown Sugar Soft with Marshmallows: Brown sugar often hardens when exposed to air, making it difficult to work with. To keep your brown sugar soft and easy to use, place a couple of marshmallows in the container. The marshmallows will absorb moisture, preventing the sugar from clumping together.

8. Use Baking Soda to Clean Burnt Pans: Burnt pans can be tough to clean, but baking soda is here to save the day. Sprinkle some baking soda onto the burnt areas, then add a little water to form a paste. Let it sit for a few hours, then scrub away the burnt residue easily.

9. Preserve Cheese with Wax Paper: Cheese often dries out in the refrigerator, losing its flavor and texture. To keep your cheese fresh, wrap it in wax paper before storing it in the fridge. The wax paper will help maintain the cheese’s moisture, keeping it delicious for longer.

10. Use an Ice Cream Scoop for Perfect Cookie Dough: Want to make perfectly round and evenly sized cookies? Use an ice cream scoop to portion out the dough. This ensures that each cookie is the same size, resulting in a batch of beautifully baked treats.

These kitchen hacks are sure to make your time in the kitchen more enjoyable and efficient. Incorporate them into your cooking routine and watch as your culinary skills soar to new heights. Happy cooking!
