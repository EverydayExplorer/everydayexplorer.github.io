Hunting Coyotes at Night: Tactics and Techniques


Welcome to our comprehensive guide on hunting coyotes at night! If you’re an avid hunter looking to test your skills under the moonlit sky, or if you’re simply intrigued by the art of nocturnal hunting, you’ve come to the right place. In this blog post, we will explore various tactics and techniques that will help you become a successful hunter of coyotes after sundown. So, grab your gear and let’s dive in!

Before we delve into the specifics of hunting coyotes at night, it’s important to note that regulations regarding nocturnal hunting may vary from state to state. Make sure to familiarize yourself with local laws and obtain the necessary permits before embarking on your adventure.

Equipment and Preparation:  
When it comes to night hunting, having the right equipment is essential. Here are a few items you may want to consider:

Night Vision and/or Thermal Scopes: These devices amplify available light or detect heat signatures, allowing you to spot coyotes in the dark.

  1. Decoys and Calls: Coyotes are curious creatures, and the use of decoys and various calls can help attract them within range.

Quality Firearms and Ammunition: Choose a firearm suitable for coyote hunting, ensuring it is accurate and reliable. Opt for ammunition optimized for coyote hunting as well.

Scout the Area:  
Before your hunting excursion, it's crucial to scout the hunting location during daylight hours. Look for signs of coyote activity, such as tracks, droppings, or dens. Familiarizing yourself with the terrain will help you plan your approach and set up accordingly.

Set Up Your Hunting Area:  
Choose a strategic location to set up your hunting spot. Coyotes are known to frequent open fields, meadows, and areas near water sources. Make sure you have a clear line of sight and a concealed position that minimizes the risk of being detected.

Use Calls and Decoys:  
Coyotes are highly vocal animals, and using calls can be an effective way to lure them in. Electronic calls or mouth-blown calls that mimic the sounds of prey or distressed coyotes can be particularly successful. Consider using decoys, such as a motionless rabbit or an injured animal, to make your setup even more enticing.

Employ Stealth and Patience:  
Hunting at night requires a heightened level of stealth and patience. Move slowly and as quietly as possible, avoiding sudden movements or loud noises. Coyotes have excellent hearing and keen senses, so staying undetected is crucial. Patience is key; be prepared to wait for extended periods until your prey comes into range.

Coordinate with a Partner:  
Having a hunting partner can greatly enhance your chances of success. One person can focus on calling and decoy manipulation, while the other focuses on taking the shot. Effective communication and synchronization between partners are essential for a successful night hunting experience.

Safety First:  
As with any hunting activity, safety should be your top priority. Always be aware of your surroundings, ensuring you have a safe backstop for your shots. Use proper firearm handling techniques and wear appropriate protective gear, such as ear and eye protection.

Remember, the goal of hunting coyotes at night is not only to harvest these elusive creatures but also to engage in ethical and responsible hunting practices. Be respectful of the environment and wildlife, and follow all local laws and regulations.

So, there you have it – a comprehensive guide to hunting coyotes at night. By employing these tactics and techniques, you’ll be well on your way to an exhilarating and successful nocturnal hunting experience. Stay safe, have fun, and may your nights be filled with exciting coyote encounters!
