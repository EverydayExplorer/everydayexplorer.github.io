Pet-Friendly DIY Thanksgiving Decorations: Giving Thanks with Your Furry Companion


With Thanksgiving just around the corner, it’s time to start thinking about how to decorate your home and show your gratitude. But why not include your furry companion in the festivities? In this blog post, we’ll explore some pet-friendly DIY Thanksgiving decorations that will not only add a touch of warmth and charm to your home but also make your four-legged friend feel loved and included.

1. Paw Print Turkey Place Cards:
Start by creating adorable place cards for your Thanksgiving table. Using non-toxic paint, gently coat your pet’s paw with a fall-inspired color, such as orange or brown. Then, carefully press their paw onto a small piece of cardstock or craft paper. Once dry, use a pen or marker to add a turkey body and write your guest’s name. These personalized place cards will surely bring a smile to everyone’s face.

2. Leaf Garland:
Bring the beauty of the outdoors inside with a pet-friendly leaf garland. Collect a variety of colorful leaves from your backyard or local park (ensure they are non-toxic to pets). Using a hole puncher, make a small hole near the stem of each leaf. Thread a piece of twine or ribbon through the holes, alternating the leaves. Hang the garland across doorways or along the fireplace mantel to create a festive and pet-safe atmosphere.

3. Gratitude Jar:
Thanksgiving is a time for expressing gratitude, so why not involve your furry companion in this tradition? Find a clear jar or container and decorate it with stickers or ribbons. Each day leading up to Thanksgiving, write down something you and your pet are grateful for on a small piece of paper and place it in the jar. On Thanksgiving Day, read aloud all the notes of gratitude and reflect on the blessings your pet brings to your life.

4. Pumpkin Treat Dispenser:
Thanksgiving is synonymous with pumpkins, so why not turn one into a fun treat dispenser for your pet? Start by selecting a small-sized pumpkin and cut a hole near the top large enough for your pet’s treats to fit through. Scoop out the seeds and flesh, leaving a hollow space inside. Decorate the outside of the pumpkin with pet-safe paint or markers. Fill it with your pet’s favorite treats and watch them enjoy the challenge of retrieving their tasty rewards.

5. Pet-friendly Wreath:
Welcome your guests with a pet-friendly Thanksgiving wreath. Using a wreath form made of natural materials, such as grapevine or straw, attach pet-safe decorations like small fabric pumpkins, leaves, and acorns. Avoid using any toxic materials that could harm your pet if they decide to give the wreath a sniff or nibble. Hang the wreath on your front door or above your pet’s favorite resting spot to add a festive touch to your home.

By incorporating these pet-friendly DIY Thanksgiving decorations, you’ll create a warm and inviting atmosphere for both your human and furry guests. Remember to prioritize your pet’s safety by avoiding toxic materials and ensuring they are supervised while enjoying these decorations. This Thanksgiving, give thanks with your furry companion and make lasting memories together.
