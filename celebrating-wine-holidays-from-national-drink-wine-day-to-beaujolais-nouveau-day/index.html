Celebrating Wine Holidays: From National Drink Wine Day to Beaujolais Nouveau Day


Wine lovers around the world rejoice as they mark their calendars for a series of exciting wine holidays throughout the year. From National Drink Wine Day to Beaujolais Nouveau Day, these celebrations offer an opportunity to indulge in the rich history and diverse flavors of this beloved beverage. Join us as we embark on a journey through the wine calendar, uncovering the stories behind these festive occasions that bring wine enthusiasts together.

National Drink Wine Day (February 18th)

What better way to start our wine holiday extravaganza than with National Drink Wine Day? On this special day, oenophiles raise their glasses to celebrate the joy and sophistication that wine brings to our lives. Whether you prefer a full-bodied red, a crisp white, or a sparkling rosé, this is the perfect occasion to explore new varietals and share your passion with fellow wine enthusiasts. So grab a bottle, gather your friends, and toast to the beauty of wine!

World Malbec Day (April 17th)

Originating in France but finding its true home in Argentina, Malbec has become a beloved varietal that has captured the hearts of wine lovers worldwide. World Malbec Day pays homage to this robust and fruity red wine, inviting enthusiasts to savor its unique flavors and indulge in its vibrant character. From the vineyards of Mendoza to the wine bars of Buenos Aires, this day is a celebration of Argentina’s winemaking prowess and the remarkable journey of Malbec.

International Chardonnay Day (May 24th)

Chardonnay, the queen of white wines, takes center stage on International Chardonnay Day. This versatile grape varietal has captivated wine enthusiasts with its buttery texture, tropical fruit flavors, and subtle oakiness. From Burgundy to California, Chardonnay offers a wide range of expressions influenced by different regions and winemaking techniques. On this day, raise a glass of Chardonnay and appreciate the elegance and complexity this wine brings to the table.

Beaujolais Nouveau Day (Third Thursday of November)

Beaujolais Nouveau Day marks the highly anticipated release of the youngest French wine. Produced in the Beaujolais region of France, this light and fruity red wine is made from Gamay grapes and undergoes a quick fermentation process. Its release is celebrated with great fanfare, with wine lovers eagerly awaiting the moment to taste the freshly pressed juice. On this day, wine enthusiasts come together to revel in the vibrant flavors of Beaujolais Nouveau, accompanied by traditional French cuisine and lively festivities.

National Zinfandel Day (Third Wednesday of November)

The bold and robust Zinfandel takes the spotlight on National Zinfandel Day. With its rich dark fruit flavors and spicy undertones, Zinfandel has solidified its place as a quintessential American wine. From the sun-drenched vineyards of California to the historic wineries of the East Coast, Zinfandel showcases the diversity and innovation of American winemaking. On this day, raise a glass of Zinfandel and celebrate the unique expression of the American wine industry.

As wine enthusiasts, we are fortunate to have a calendar filled with wine holidays that allow us to explore the world of wine, discover new flavors, and connect with fellow enthusiasts. So mark your calendars, gather your friends, and embark on a wine-filled journey that will delight your senses and deepen your appreciation for this timeless beverage. Cheers to the joy of celebrating wine holidays!
