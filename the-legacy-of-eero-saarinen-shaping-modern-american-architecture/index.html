The Legacy of Eero Saarinen: Shaping Modern American Architecture


Eero Saarinen was a Finnish-American architect who played a significant role in shaping modern American architecture. His innovative designs and iconic structures have left a lasting legacy that continues to inspire architects and captivate the public to this day.

Born in 1910 in Kirkkonummi, Finland, Saarinen immigrated to the United States with his family in 1923. His father, Eliel Saarinen, was also an architect and served as a great influence on his son’s career. Eero studied sculpture and furniture design at the Cranbrook Academy of Art in Michigan, where his father was the president.

Saarinen’s architectural career took off in the 1940s, when he collaborated with his father on the design of the influential Cranbrook campus buildings. This collaboration set the stage for Saarinen to establish his own firm and embark on a remarkable journey that would forever transform American architecture.

One of Saarinen’s most renowned works is the Gateway Arch in St. Louis, Missouri. Completed in 1965, this iconic stainless steel structure stands at a staggering height of 630 feet, symbolizing the westward expansion of the United States. The arch’s sleek, curved design is a testament to Saarinen’s ability to merge modernist principles with a sense of elegance and grandeur.

Another notable Saarinen creation is the TWA Flight Center at John F. Kennedy International Airport in New York. Completed in 1962, this futuristic terminal building showcases Saarinen’s mastery of organic forms and his ability to create spaces that evoke a sense of movement and fluidity. Although the TWA Flight Center ceased its operations in 2001, it remains an architectural marvel and has been designated a New York City landmark.

Saarinen’s impact on the architectural landscape extends beyond individual buildings. He was a pioneer of the ’total design’ approach, which involved carefully considering every aspect of a project, from the building’s exterior and interior to its furnishings and landscaping. This holistic approach created harmonious environments that seamlessly integrated architecture, art, and design.

In addition to his iconic structures, Saarinen also designed a wide range of furniture pieces that have become timeless classics. His Tulip Chair, Womb Chair, and Pedestal Table are still highly sought after today, embodying his belief in creating functional yet aesthetically pleasing objects.

Tragically, Saarinen’s career was cut short when he passed away at the age of 51 in 1961. However, his influence continues to be felt in the work of countless architects who have been inspired by his bold vision and innovative approach. His legacy lives on through the buildings he created and the ideas he championed.

Eero Saarinen’s impact on modern American architecture cannot be overstated. His groundbreaking designs and visionary approach have left an indelible mark on the architectural landscape. From the Gateway Arch to the TWA Flight Center, his structures continue to captivate and inspire, reminding us of the power of architecture to shape our world. Saarinen’s legacy serves as a testament to the enduring influence of a visionary architect who pushed the boundaries of design and forever changed the face of American architecture.
