Top 10 Music Documentaries Every Fan Should Watch


Are you a music enthusiast looking for some captivating documentaries to delve into? Well, you’re in luck because we’ve curated a list of the top 10 music documentaries that every fan should watch. From iconic rock bands to legendary solo artists, these documentaries offer an intimate look into the lives and careers of some of the most influential figures in the music industry. So grab your popcorn, turn up the volume, and get ready to be entertained and inspired by these incredible films.

1. ‘Amy’ (2015)
‘Amy’ is a poignant documentary that chronicles the life and tragic death of the immensely talented singer-songwriter Amy Winehouse. Through rare footage and interviews, the film provides a candid portrayal of Winehouse’s meteoric rise to fame and the personal struggles that ultimately led to her untimely passing.

2. ‘20 Feet from Stardom’ (2013)
This Oscar-winning documentary shines a spotlight on the unsung heroes of the music world - the backup singers. ‘20 Feet from Stardom’ celebrates the remarkable voices behind some of the biggest hits in music history and explores the challenges and triumphs of these talented artists.

3. ‘Searching for Sugar Man’ (2012)
‘Searching for Sugar Man’ is a captivating documentary that follows the mysterious journey of musician Rodriguez, who was virtually unknown in the United States but achieved cult status in South Africa. The film unravels the enigma surrounding Rodriguez’s disappearance from the music scene and the surprising turn of events that followed.

4. ‘The Beatles: Eight Days a Week’ (2016)
Directed by Ron Howard, this documentary delves into the exhilarating journey of The Beatles, from their early days performing in Liverpool to their unprecedented global stardom. Featuring rare concert footage and in-depth interviews, ‘Eight Days a Week’ offers a compelling look at the phenomenon that was The Beatles.

5. ‘What Happened, Miss Simone?’ (2015)
Nina Simone’s indelible mark on the music industry is explored in this powerful documentary that delves into the highs and lows of her extraordinary career. ‘What Happened, Miss Simone?’ paints an intimate portrait of the iconic singer, pianist, and civil rights activist, showcasing her unparalleled talent and unwavering activism.

6. ‘Gimme Shelter’ (1970)
This iconic documentary captures the tumultuous events surrounding The Rolling Stones’ 1969 American tour, culminating in the ill-fated Altamont Free Concert. ‘Gimme Shelter’ provides a raw and unflinching portrayal of the era’s counterculture and the darker side of rock ’n’ roll, making it a must-watch for music history enthusiasts.

7. ‘Whitney: Can I Be Me’ (2017)
‘Whitney: Can I Be Me’ offers an intimate look at the life and career of the incomparable Whitney Houston. Through candid interviews and archival footage, the documentary explores the complexities of Houston’s personal and professional life, shedding light on the pressures of fame and the pursuit of authenticity.

8. ‘The Last Waltz’ (1978)
Directed by Martin Scorsese, ‘The Last Waltz’ captures the legendary farewell concert of The Band, featuring iconic performances by music luminaries such as Bob Dylan, Eric Clapton, Joni Mitchell, and more. The film is a testament to the enduring legacy of The Band and the magic of their final performance.

9. ‘Marley’ (2012)
‘Marley’ is a definitive documentary that pays homage to the reggae legend Bob Marley, tracing his extraordinary journey from humble beginnings in Jamaica to global superstardom. With unprecedented access to Marley’s family and archival footage, the film provides a comprehensive portrait of the music icon and his enduring impact.

10. ‘Muscle Shoals’ (2013)
This mesmerizing documentary delves into the rich musical history of Muscle Shoals, Alabama, a small town that became a powerhouse of soulful and influential music recordings. ‘Muscle Shoals’ explores the studio and the remarkable musicians who made it a mecca for artists like Aretha Franklin, Wilson Pickett, and many others.

These remarkable music documentaries offer a captivating glimpse into the lives, legacies, and the cultural impact of some of the most iconic figures in the music industry. Whether you’re a die-hard fan or simply appreciate compelling storytelling, these films are sure to strike a chord and leave you with a newfound appreciation for the artistry and resilience of these musical legends. So, grab your remote, press play, and let the magic of music unfold before your eyes.
