The Role of Music in Cartoons: From Jingles to Soundtracks


Cartoons have been a favorite pastime for generations, and music has played a significant role in making them the multi-billion dollar industry they are today. From the catchy jingles that play during catchy commercials to the epic soundtracks in modern-day cartoons, music is an essential component that sets the tone and mood for the characters and their stories.

The earliest animated shows, such as Felix the Cat and Mickey Mouse, had basic musical scores that only served to provide a background ambience. However, as the art of animation progressed and color was introduced, music began to take on a more significant role. During the 1950s and 1960s, the golden age of animation, cartoons had a unique sound: light-hearted and cheerful music that suited the cartoon characters’ actions and personality. These shows had memorable intros and catchy jingles that are still recognizable even after all these years.

In the 1970s, cartoons began to change, and so did the music. The introduction of the limited animation style and the need to cater to a broader audience required music that could appeal to both children and adults. This meant introducing elements of rock, pop, and funk music into the scores. The music had to be both fun and contemporary, and bands like The Archies and The Jackson 5 were created to symbolize this change.

The 1980s saw a dramatic shift in the way music was used in cartoons, with the development of the music video and its influence on storytelling. Cartoons had more elaborate scores featuring diverse genres such as pop, rock, and jazz. Cartoons like ‘Transformers,’ ‘Thundercats,’ and ‘He-Man’ had epic musical themes that added to their larger-than-life storylines.

Fast forward to the present day, and the music used in cartoons has shifted once again. Technological advancements have made it easier to incorporate complex soundscapes into shows. The use of electronic music and synthwave has become popular in shows like ‘Stranger Things’ and ‘Over the Garden Wall.’ The scores in modern-day cartoons are no longer meant to be mere background music. They are complex pieces of music that set the mood, drive the story, and provide insight into the characters’ emotions.

In conclusion, music has played a significant role in the evolution of cartoons. From the early days of basic musical scores to today’s intricate soundscapes, music has had an enormous impact on the storyline and animation. The catchy jingles of the past and the epic soundtracks of today have helped make cartoons timeless and memorable. The music serves to bring the characters to life and create a sense of nostalgia for generations of viewers. As technology advances, who knows how music will be used in cartoons in the future, but one thing is for sure, it will continue to play a vital role.
