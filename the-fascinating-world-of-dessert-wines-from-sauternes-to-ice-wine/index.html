The Fascinating World of Dessert Wines: From Sauternes to Ice Wine


When it comes to wine, most people think about the typical categories of red, white, and rosé. But there is a whole other world of wine that often gets overlooked - dessert wines. These sweet and luscious wines are truly a treat for the senses, and they deserve a place at the table alongside other fine wines.

One of the most well-known and revered dessert wines is Sauternes. This French wine hails from the Bordeaux region and is made from Semillon, Sauvignon Blanc, and Muscadelle grapes. What sets Sauternes apart is the process by which it is made. The grapes are left on the vine until they are affected by Botrytis cinerea, also known as noble rot. This fungus causes the grapes to shrivel and concentrates the sugars, resulting in a wine that is rich, honeyed, and incredibly complex.

Sauternes is often enjoyed as a pairing with foie gras or blue cheese due to its intense sweetness and balancing acidity. It’s a decadent wine that demands attention, and its golden color and velvety texture are a feast for the eyes and palate.

Moving across the Atlantic, we come to another legendary dessert wine - the Ice Wine. Ice Wine, also known as Eiswein in Germany, is made from grapes that are left on the vine until they freeze. This freezing process concentrates the sugars and flavors, resulting in a wine that is both intensely sweet and refreshing.

Canada is known for its exceptional Ice Wines, particularly those produced in the Niagara Peninsula region. Here, the cold winters and well-drained soils create the perfect conditions for the grapes to freeze and harvest. The resulting wines are often described as having flavors of apricots, peaches, and honey, with a refreshing acidity that balances out the sweetness.

Ice Wine is best enjoyed on its own or paired with light desserts like fruit tarts or crème brûlée. Its sweetness and vibrant acidity make for a delightful combination that can elevate any dessert experience.

While Sauternes and Ice Wine may be the most famous and widely recognized dessert wines, there are many other varieties to explore. From Hungary’s Tokaji Aszú, which is made from grapes affected by noble rot like Sauternes, to Italy’s Vin Santo, a sweet wine often enjoyed with cantucci biscuits, every region has its own unique take on dessert wines.

In recent years, there has also been a surge of interest in late harvest wines. These wines are made from grapes that are left on the vine longer than usual, allowing them to become riper and sweeter. Late harvest wines can be found in various regions around the world, including California, South Africa, and New Zealand.

When it comes to pairing dessert wines, the possibilities are endless. From creamy cheesecakes to rich chocolate desserts, dessert wines have the versatility to enhance a wide range of flavors. Their sweetness can help to balance out the richness of desserts, while their acidity can provide a refreshing contrast.

Now that you have been introduced to the fascinating world of dessert wines, it’s time to embark on your own tasting journey. Whether you prefer the honeyed complexity of Sauternes, the refreshing sweetness of Ice Wine, or the unique flavors of Tokaji Aszú, there is a dessert wine out there waiting to be discovered. So indulge your senses and raise a glass to the sweet side of wine!
