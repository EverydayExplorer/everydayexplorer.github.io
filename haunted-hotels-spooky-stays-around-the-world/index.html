Haunted Hotels: Spooky Stays Around the World


Have you ever wondered what it would be like to spend a night in a haunted hotel? For those who crave an adrenaline rush and have a fascination with the supernatural, haunted hotels offer a unique and spine-chilling experience. From mysterious footsteps echoing down empty hallways to unexplained flickering lights, these spooky stays around the world are not for the faint of heart.

One such haunted hotel that has gained notoriety is the Stanley Hotel in Estes Park, Colorado. Known as the inspiration for Stephen King’s famous novel, ‘The Shining,’ this historic hotel is rumored to be haunted by several restless spirits. Guests have reported seeing apparitions in their rooms, hearing children’s laughter when no children are present, and feeling an eerie presence in the hotel’s infamous Room 217. For those brave enough to stay, the Stanley Hotel offers ghost tours and paranormal investigations, providing a truly immersive and hair-raising experience.

If you’re looking for a haunted hotel with a touch of luxury, the Langham Hotel in London is the perfect choice. With a history dating back to 1865, this opulent hotel has welcomed numerous famous guests over the years, including Mark Twain and Oscar Wilde. However, it is also believed to be haunted by several spirits, most notably a Victorian-era doctor who is said to roam the hallways. Despite its ghostly reputation, the Langham Hotel remains a top choice for travelers seeking a blend of elegance and the supernatural.

For those seeking a haunted hotel experience on the other side of the world, the Ballygally Castle Hotel in Northern Ireland offers a truly chilling stay. Built in the 17th century, this castle-turned-hotel has a haunted room known as the ‘Ghost Room.’ Legend has it that a former resident, Lady Isabella Shaw, fell to her death from the tower window while trying to escape her husband. Guests have reported feeling a ghostly presence in the room, hearing mysterious knocks on the door, and witnessing objects move on their own. With its rich history and ghostly tales, the Ballygally Castle Hotel is a must-visit for thrill-seekers.

Moving on to the United States, the Crescent Hotel in Eureka Springs, Arkansas, is a haunted hotel that has captivated visitors for decades. Built in 1886, this grand Victorian-style hotel has a dark past, having served as a hospital, a college, and even a morgue at one point. It is believed to be haunted by various spirits, including a nurse pushing a gurney, a young girl playing in the halls, and even a ghostly cat. The Crescent Hotel offers ghost tours and overnight paranormal investigations, allowing guests to delve into the hotel’s haunted history.

Lastly, we have the Hotel del Coronado in San Diego, California, which is not only a beachfront paradise but also a hotspot for paranormal activity. This iconic hotel is said to be haunted by the ghost of Kate Morgan, a young woman who died under mysterious circumstances in 1892. Guests have reported seeing her apparition on the beach, feeling cold spots in their rooms, and witnessing objects move on their own. Despite its ghostly reputation, the Hotel del Coronado remains a popular destination for those seeking a luxurious and spooky getaway.

Whether you’re a believer in the supernatural or simply seeking an unforgettable adventure, haunted hotels offer a unique and thrilling experience. From the Stanley Hotel in Colorado to the Hotel del Coronado in California, these spooky stays around the world provide a blend of history, luxury, and the paranormal. So, if you’re brave enough, book a room and prepare for a night you’ll never forget. Just be sure to keep the lights on and your wits about you, as you never know what ghostly encounters await you in the darkness.
