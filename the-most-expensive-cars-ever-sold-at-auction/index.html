The most expensive cars ever sold at auction


When it comes to the world of automobiles, there are many things to marvel at. From the fastest cars to the most luxurious brands, there is always something to admire. However, there is one category in which some of the most expensive cars in the world can be found – auction sales.

Over the past few decades, a number of high-profile auctions have taken place, in which some of the rarest and most sought-after cars in existence have been sold. In this blog post, we will take a closer look at some of the most expensive cars ever sold at auction and what makes them so unique.

1. 1963 Ferrari 250 GTO – $70 Million

The 1963 Ferrari 250 GTO is without a doubt one of the most iconic cars in the world. Considered by many to be the Holy Grail of automobiles, only 39 were ever produced. In 2018, the 1963 Ferrari 250 GTO became the most expensive car ever sold at auction when it was purchased for a staggering $70 million.

What makes this car so special? For one, it is a racing legend, having won numerous races in its heyday. Its aerodynamic design and powerful engine made it an undeniable force on the track. Additionally, it is incredibly rare and has a storied history, having been owned by some of the most prominent figures in the automotive world.

2. 1957 Ferrari 335 Sport Scaglietti – $35.7 Million

Not far behind the 1963 Ferrari 250 GTO in terms of price is the 1957 Ferrari 335 Sport Scaglietti. This car was sold in 2016 at the Artcurial Motorcars auction in Paris for an astonishing $35.7 million.

The Ferrari 335 Sport Scaglietti is prized for its sleek and aerodynamic design, as well as its storied racing history. It was originally driven by racing legend Mike Hawthorn, who won the 1957 Buenos Aires 1000KM race in it. With its powerful V12 engine and advanced suspension system, this car is a true masterpiece of automotive engineering.

3. 1956 Aston Martin DBR1 – $22.5 Million

The 1956 Aston Martin DBR1 is another iconic racing car that has achieved legendary status over the years. With only five ever produced, this car is incredibly rare and highly sought after by collectors.

In 2017, a 1956 Aston Martin DBR1 became the most expensive British car ever sold at auction when it was purchased for $22.5 million. This car is notable for having won the 1959 24 Hours of Le Mans, as well as numerous other races throughout its career.

4. 1961 Ferrari 250 GT SWB California Spider – $18.5 Million

The 1961 Ferrari 250 GT SWB California Spider is one of the most elegant and luxurious cars ever produced. Its sleek lines and open-air design make it a timeless classic that is highly desired by collectors.

In 2015, a 1961 Ferrari 250 GT SWB California Spider sold for $18.5 million, making it one of the most expensive Ferraris ever sold at auction. This car is particularly unique because it was once owned by legendary actor James Coburn, who was himself a car enthusiast.

5. 1962 Shelby Cobra CSX2000 – $13.75 Million

Last but not least is the 1962 Shelby Cobra CSX2000. This car, which was the first ever built by Carroll Shelby, is a true American icon. Its powerful V8 engine and lightweight design made it a force to be reckoned with on the racetrack.

In 2016, the 1962 Shelby Cobra CSX2000 was sold at auction for $13.75 million. This car is particularly special because it started the entire Cobra craze that would later sweep the automotive world. Today, the Shelby Cobra remains one of the most beloved and sought-after cars ever produced.

In conclusion, the world of auction sales has produced some truly remarkable and expensive cars over the years. From classic Ferraris to legendary racing cars, these vehicles are prized for their rarity, performance, and historical significance. While most of us may never have the opportunity to own one of these cars, they will always be admired and appreciated as symbols of automotive excellence.
