DIY Macram Plant Hangers: Bring Greenery into Your Home


Are you looking to spruce up your home with some greenery? Look no further than DIY macramé plant hangers! These trendy and versatile hangers are the perfect way to bring a touch of nature into your living space. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of creating your own macramé plant hangers, step by step. Get ready to unleash your creativity and add a unique touch to your home decor.

What is macramé, you may ask? Macramé is a form of textile art that involves knotting cords together to create decorative patterns. It originated in the 13th century and has since gained popularity in the world of interior design. Macramé plant hangers are not only aesthetic but also functional, as they allow you to hang your planters from the ceiling or walls, saving precious floor space.

Now, let’s dive into the DIY process. Here’s what you’ll need:

1. Macramé cord: You can find this at your local craft store or order it online. Opt for a sturdy and durable cord that can hold the weight of your planters.

2. Wooden or metal rings: These will serve as the base for your plant hangers. Choose a size that suits your preference and the size of your planters.

3. Scissors: A good pair of scissors is essential for cutting and trimming the cord.

4. Measuring tape: This will help you determine the length of the cord needed for each knot.

Once you have gathered all the materials, follow these steps:

1. Cut the macramé cord into equal lengths, depending on how long you want your plant hangers to be. Remember to leave extra length for knotting.

2. Fold a cord in half and pass it through the ring, creating a loop.

3. Bring the ends of the cord through the loop and pull tight, creating a lark’s head knot.

4. Repeat this process with the remaining cords, spacing them evenly around the ring.

5. Now, it’s time to start knotting. You can choose from a variety of macramé knots, such as the square knot, spiral knot, or half hitch knot. Experiment with different knots to create unique patterns and textures.

6. Continue knotting until you reach your desired length. Remember to leave enough space at the bottom for your planter.

7. Once you’re satisfied with the length, gather all the cords together and tie a secure knot at the bottom.

8. Trim any excess cord and comb out the fringes for a neat finish.

Congratulations! You have successfully created your own macramé plant hanger. Now, all that’s left to do is find the perfect spot to hang it and add your favorite plant.

Not only do macramé plant hangers add a touch of nature to your home, but they also provide numerous benefits. Plants have been proven to improve air quality, reduce stress, and increase productivity. With macramé plant hangers, you can create a green oasis right in your own living space.

In conclusion, DIY macramé plant hangers are a fantastic way to bring greenery into your home. They are not only visually appealing but also allow you to showcase your creativity and personal style. So why wait? Grab your supplies and get started on your macramé journey. Happy knotting!
