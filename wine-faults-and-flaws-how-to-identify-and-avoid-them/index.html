Wine Faults and Flaws: How to Identify and Avoid Them


Wine is a complex and diverse beverage that can bring joy and pleasure to many. However, like any agricultural product, it is not immune to faults and flaws. Understanding and being able to identify these issues can greatly enhance your appreciation and enjoyment of wine. In this blog post, we will explore some common wine faults and flaws, and provide tips on how to avoid them.

1. Cork Taint (TCA)
One of the most well-known wine faults is cork taint, also known as TCA (2,4,6-trichloroanisole). This compound can impart a musty, wet cardboard-like odor to wines. It is typically caused by the presence of mold or fungi on cork closures. To identify corked wine, trust your nose - if the wine smells damp, moldy, or like wet newspaper, it may be affected by TCA. To avoid this fault, opt for screw caps or synthetic corks, as they offer a more reliable seal.

2. Oxidation
Oxidation is another common wine flaw that occurs when the wine is exposed to too much air. This can lead to a loss of freshness, color, and fruit flavors. Oxidized wine may taste flat, dull, or even vinegary. To prevent oxidation, store your wine bottles in a cool, dark place with consistent temperature and humidity. Once opened, make sure to properly reseal the bottle and consume the wine within a few days.

3. Acetic Acid (Volatile Acidity)
Acetic acid, commonly known as volatile acidity, is a wine flaw that can give wines a sharp, vinegary smell. It is often associated with microbial spoilage, particularly the presence of acetic acid bacteria. While a small amount of volatile acidity can add complexity to certain styles of wine, excessive levels can be unpleasant. To avoid this, ensure that your wines are stored at the correct temperature, and that any winemaking equipment is properly sanitized.

4. Reduction
The opposite of oxidation, reduction occurs when wine lacks oxygen exposure. It can result in off-putting aromas, such as rotten eggs or burnt rubber. Reduction can be caused by various factors, including excessive sulfur dioxide, lack of oxygen during winemaking, or poor wine closure. To mitigate the risk of reduction, ensure that you aerate your wine properly before consuming it and store it in a clean environment.

5. Brettanomyces
Brettanomyces, often referred to as ‘Brett,’ is a yeast that can cause wine faults. While some people appreciate the characteristic ‘Brett’ aromas, such as barnyard or medicinal notes, excessive levels can overpower the wine’s fruit flavors. To identify ‘Brett’ affected wine, look for off flavors such as sweaty leather or band-aid-like aromas. Winemaking techniques, such as meticulous hygiene and monitoring of sulfur dioxide levels, can help prevent this yeast from flourishing.

6. Heat Damage
Exposure to high temperatures can lead to heat damage in wine. This can result in cooked or stewed flavors, loss of fruitiness, and increased alcohol perception. To protect your wine from heat damage, avoid storing it in hot areas or exposing it to direct sunlight for extended periods. During transportation, consider using insulated packaging or temperature-controlled shipping to minimize the risk.

7. Maderization
Maderization refers to the process whereby wine takes on characteristics similar to that of Madeira, such as nutty, caramel, or oxidized notes. This flaw is usually caused by prolonged exposure to heat and air. To prevent maderization, store your wines away from direct sunlight and excessive temperature fluctuations. Additionally, once a bottle is opened, it is best to consume it within a reasonable timeframe to minimize the impact of oxidation.

Conclusion
By familiarizing yourself with common wine faults and flaws, you can avoid disappointment and ensure a pleasurable experience when enjoying a bottle of wine. Remember to trust your senses - sight, smell, and taste - when assessing a wine’s condition. Proper storage, handling, and serving practices are crucial in preserving the integrity of the wine. With this knowledge, you can confidently navigate the world of wine and maximize your enjoyment. Cheers!
