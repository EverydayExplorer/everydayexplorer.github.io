Survival Hygiene: Staying Clean in the Great Outdoors


When you’re out in the great outdoors, whether it’s camping, hiking, or surviving in a wilderness setting, staying clean is not only essential for your physical well-being but also for your mental and emotional health. Maintaining good hygiene practices can help prevent illness, infections, and discomfort, allowing you to fully enjoy your outdoor adventures. In this guide, we’ll explore practical tips and tricks for staying clean and fresh in the wilderness.

Water, Your Greatest Ally

Water is your best friend when it comes to staying clean in the great outdoors. Not only is it essential for hydration, but it’s also crucial for maintaining good hygiene. Whether you’re near a natural water source or carrying your own supply, here’s how you can make the most of it:

Bathing Basics

  • Quick Dip: If you have access to a clean water source, take a quick dip to rinse off sweat and dirt. Use biodegradable soap sparingly, and make sure to rinse well to minimize environmental impact.
  • DIY Shower: For a makeshift shower, fill a clean plastic bag with water, poke a few holes in it, and hang it from a tree. Voila! You have a gravity-fed outdoor shower.

Hand Hygiene

  • Hand Washing: Proper hand hygiene is crucial, especially before handling food. Use a small amount of biodegradable soap and water to wash your hands thoroughly.

Personal Care Essentials

While you may not have your entire bathroom cabinet with you in the wilderness, a few essential items can go a long way in keeping you clean and fresh:

Dental Hygiene

  • Toothbrush and Toothpaste: Don’t compromise on oral hygiene. Brush your teeth at least twice a day to keep your mouth feeling clean and prevent dental issues.

Body Care

  • Biodegradable Soap: Opt for environmentally friendly, biodegradable soap for washing yourself and your clothes. It’s gentle on the skin and less harmful to the environment.
  • Wet Wipes: These are a convenient option for freshening up when a full bath or shower isn’t possible.

Feminine Hygiene

  • Menstrual Products: For those who menstruate, it’s essential to be prepared with the appropriate products. Consider environmentally friendly options like menstrual cups or biodegradable pads.

Waste Disposal

Proper waste disposal is an integral part of maintaining hygiene in the outdoors. Follow Leave No Trace principles and ensure that all waste, including human waste, is disposed of responsibly. Use designated facilities when available, and if not, follow guidelines for digging cat holes and packing out toilet paper.

Clothing Care

Clean clothes can make a world of difference in how you feel during outdoor activities. Here are some tips for maintaining clean clothing:

  • Air-Drying: Whenever possible, air-dry your clothes to keep them fresh and reduce odors.
  • Spot Cleaning: Address stains and small areas of dirt promptly to prevent them from setting in.

Final Thoughts

Staying clean in the great outdoors is not only about physical comfort but also about respecting the environment and minimizing your impact. By following these hygiene practices, you can enjoy your outdoor adventures while preserving the natural beauty around you. Remember, a little effort in maintaining hygiene goes a long way in ensuring a pleasant and healthy outdoor experience.

Whether you’re a seasoned outdoor enthusiast or a beginner venturing into the wilderness, prioritizing survival hygiene will allow you to make the most of your time in nature. So, pack your hygiene essentials, respect the environment, and embrace the freshness of the great outdoors!
