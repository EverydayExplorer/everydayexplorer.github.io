Survival Skills for Building a Fish Trap: Catching Dinner in Waterways


When it comes to survival in the wild, having the skills to catch your own food is essential. While fishing with a rod and reel is a common method, there are situations where building a fish trap can be a more efficient and effective way to catch dinner in waterways. In this blog post, we will explore the art of building a fish trap, along with some important survival skills to help you become a successful fisherman in the wild.

1. Understanding the Basics:
Before diving into the construction of a fish trap, it is important to have a good understanding of the target species and their behavior. Different types of fish have different preferences when it comes to habitat and food. Researching the local fish species in the area you are in will give you an upper hand in designing a trap that will attract and catch the most fish.

2. Choosing the Right Location:
Selecting the right location for your fish trap is crucial. Look for areas in the waterway where fish are likely to congregate, such as near rocks, fallen logs, or underwater vegetation. These spots provide cover and are prime feeding grounds for fish. Additionally, pay attention to the water current. Fish tend to swim with the current, so placing your trap in a strategic location will increase your chances of a successful catch.

3. Building the Fish Trap:
Now that you have scouted the area and understand the fish behavior, it’s time to build your fish trap. One of the simplest and most effective types of fish traps is the funnel trap. It consists of two cone-shaped funnels connected by a cylindrical chamber. The wide end of the funnel faces the water, while the narrow end leads to the chamber where the fish will be trapped.

To construct a funnel trap, gather materials such as flexible branches, sturdy vines, and nylon fishing line. Start by creating the frame of the trap using the branches and secure them with the vines. Use the fishing line to weave a net-like structure around the frame, leaving small gaps for the fish to swim through. Make sure the funnel entrance is wide enough for the fish to enter but narrows down towards the chamber, preventing their escape.

4. Baiting the Trap:
To increase the effectiveness of your fish trap, baiting is essential. Fish are attracted to certain smells and tastes, so using the right bait will entice them to enter the trap. Common baits include small pieces of fish, insects, or even homemade concoctions like a mixture of bread crumbs and fish oil. Place the bait strategically near the entrance of the trap to lure the fish inside.

5. Checking and Collecting the Catch:
Once your fish trap is set up and baited, it’s important to periodically check on it to see if you’ve caught any fish. Depending on the location and fish behavior, checking every few hours or even overnight might be necessary. When collecting your catch, be cautious of any potential hazards such as sharp fins or teeth. Use gloves and handle the fish with care.

In conclusion, building a fish trap can be a valuable survival skill for catching dinner in waterways. By understanding the basics, choosing the right location, constructing the trap, baiting it effectively, and regularly checking on it, you increase your chances of a successful catch. Remember, practice makes perfect, so keep honing your survival skills and embrace the adventure of catching your own food in the wild. Happy fishing!
