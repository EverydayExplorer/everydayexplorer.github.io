The Forgotten Generation: Human Rights and Older Persons


As we progress into the future, it’s essential to shine a light on the often overlooked and underrepresented generation - the older population. The older generation, often referred to as the ‘Forgotten Generation,’ faces unique challenges and deserves attention in the context of human rights. In this blog post, we will explore the human rights issues facing older persons and why it’s crucial to advocate for their rights in today’s society.

Understanding the Forgotten Generation

The demographic landscape of the world is rapidly changing, with the older population becoming increasingly significant. According to the World Health Organization, the number of people aged 60 and older is expected to double by 2050, reaching nearly 2.1 billion. Despite this significant demographic shift, older persons often find themselves marginalized and their human rights disregarded.

Challenges Faced by Older Persons

Older persons encounter a myriad of challenges that directly impact their human rights. Ageism, which refers to discrimination and prejudice against individuals based on their age, is a pervasive issue that affects the older generation. This can manifest in various forms, including employment discrimination, lack of access to healthcare, and social exclusion.

Financial insecurity is another pressing concern for many older individuals. Some older persons face economic hardship due to inadequate retirement savings, limited job opportunities, or lack of access to social security benefits. This financial instability can impede their ability to access basic necessities and lead a dignified life.

Healthcare and Age-related Discrimination

Access to quality healthcare is a fundamental human right, yet older persons often encounter age-related discrimination within healthcare systems. They may face barriers to receiving appropriate medical treatment, preventive care, or access to essential medications. This discrimination not only infringes on their right to health but also contributes to health disparities among older individuals.

Furthermore, elder abuse is a significant human rights issue that deserves greater attention. Physical, emotional, or financial abuse of older persons is a violation of their basic human rights and contributes to their vulnerability. Addressing elder abuse requires a multifaceted approach that encompasses legal protections, social support systems, and community awareness.

Advocating for Change

In order to uphold the human rights of older persons, it’s imperative to advocate for policy changes and societal attitudes that promote inclusion, dignity, and respect for the older generation. This includes initiatives to combat ageism, ensure access to affordable healthcare, and provide social safety nets that protect older individuals from economic hardship.

Empowering older persons to actively participate in decision-making processes that affect their lives is crucial for promoting their rights. This can involve creating opportunities for intergenerational dialogue, promoting lifelong learning, and fostering age-friendly environments that support the diverse needs of older individuals.

The Role of Technology and Innovation

Technology and innovation have the potential to positively impact the lives of older persons by promoting independence, social connectivity, and access to vital services. From telemedicine and digital literacy programs to age-friendly smart cities, technological advancements can play a pivotal role in addressing the unique needs of the older generation and safeguarding their human rights.

In conclusion, the Forgotten Generation, comprising older persons, deserves recognition and advocacy to ensure their human rights are upheld. By addressing ageism, advocating for equitable healthcare, combating elder abuse, and embracing innovation, we can create a more inclusive and supportive society for older individuals. It is our collective responsibility to champion the rights of the older generation and create a world where aging is synonymous with dignity, respect, and empowerment.
