Real Estate Market Trends: Whats Hot and Whats Not

4 min · 735 words

As we approach the close of 2021, there is no doubt that the real estate market has had its own fair share of ups and downs. The COVID-19 pandemic greatly impacted the global economy, and the real estate industry was not left unaffected. However, the real estate market has experienced impressive growth and transformation amid the pandemic, with hot trends emerging as some markets surged, while others seemed to fall flat. In this blog, we’ll be taking a closer look at what’s hot and what’s not in the real estate market trends.

What’s Hot

1. Suburban and Rural Properties

As the pandemic made it quite challenging for people to live in crowded cities, there has been a steady increase in demand for properties in suburban areas and rural locations. People who run businesses that are no longer restricted to physical space have had more freedom to work anywhere, and many have taken advantage of this by moving to less populated areas. This resulted in a surge in demand for spacious homes with ample outdoor space.

2. Smart Homes

The use of smart-home technology has become increasingly popular and convenient for homeowners in recent years. Smart home devices such as voice assistants, thermostats, and security cameras provide convenience that simplifies life and enhances the home’s security. As people spend more time at home, the demand for smart home technology has increased.

3. Remote Work Spaces

With most people working from home, some buyers are now more interested in properties with designated workspaces. Many homeowners have transformed their extra rooms or basements into home offices, which now require quiet and private spaces. As such, property buyers with remote work requirements tend to look for extra rooms that can double as a workspace.

What’s Not

1. Homes Without Outdoor Space

With outdoor gatherings being the safest way to socialize during the pandemic, properties that lack outdoor space have struggled to find buyers. Many house hunters are on the lookout for spacious backyards, patios, or balconies that provide outdoor living spaces.

2. Urban Apartments

Urban apartments, especially those located in densely populated metropolitan areas, have seen a decline in demand with the pandemic. People who once enjoyed the vibrancy of city life are now more inclined to move away from the crowds due to the dangers of close proximity and reduced foot traffic for businesses.

3. Large Multi-Family Dwellings

Large multi-family dwellings, such as multi-unit properties and apartments, have experienced reduced demand due to stricter social distancing protocols that limit the use of shared spaces. Many people are also now looking for more private homes with outdoor spaces to avoid close contact with other people.

Conclusion

These are just a few of the several trends that define the current real estate market landscape. There’s no doubt that the pandemic has changed our lives and, subsequently, our preferences in real estate. As the future remains uncertain, it is vital to stay current on these trends and stay attuned to changing buyer behavior. If you are in the market for a new property, working with a real estate agent with a keen understanding of these trends can prove immensely beneficial. By keeping tabs on what’s hot and what’s not, you can position yourself to make an informed decision that can be both financially and emotionally rewarding.
