Survival Knots Every Outdoor Enthusiast Should Master


As an outdoor enthusiast, mastering a variety of survival knots is crucial for your safety and success in the wilderness. Whether you’re setting up camp, securing gear, or navigating challenging terrain, knowing the right knots can make all the difference. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore some essential survival knots that every outdoor enthusiast should master, along with step-by-step instructions on how to tie them effectively.

  1. The Bowline Knot: The bowline knot is a versatile and reliable knot that forms a secure loop at the end of a rope. It’s ideal for tasks such as securing a tarp or tent, creating a loop for a makeshift harness, or attaching a line to an anchor point. To tie a bowline knot, follow these steps:

  2. Form a small loop near the end of the rope, with the free end passing over the standing end.

  3. Pass the free end through the loop from underneath, then bring it back down and around the standing end.

  4. Finally, thread the free end back through the loop and tighten the knot by pulling both ends firmly.

  5. The Clove Hitch: The clove hitch is a simple yet effective knot for securing a rope to a pole or stake. It’s commonly used for setting up tarps, building shelters, or tying down equipment. To tie a clove hitch, follow these steps:

  6. Wrap the rope around the pole or stake, crossing over itself to form an ‘X.’

  7. Bring the free end of the rope under the standing end, then wrap it around the pole again, crossing over the previous turn.

  8. Pass the free end under the last wrap and tuck it under the crossed section of rope to secure the knot.

  9. The Square Knot (Reef Knot): The square knot is a simple and versatile knot that is used for joining two ropes of equal diameter. It’s commonly used for bundling items together, tying off a bandage, or joining ropes during emergency repairs. To tie a square knot, follow these steps:

  10. Cross the two ropes over each other to form a simple overhand knot.

  11. Tie another overhand knot by crossing the ropes in the opposite direction.

  12. Tighten the knot by pulling both ends simultaneously, ensuring that the two knots are snugly aligned.

  13. The Taut-Line Hitch: The taut-line hitch is an adjustable knot that is perfect for securing tent guy lines or other lines that need to be tightened or loosened easily. It’s designed to hold firm under tension while still allowing for adjustments as needed. To tie a taut-line hitch, follow these steps:

  14. Wrap the rope around the anchor point, then bring the free end parallel to the standing end.

  15. Wrap the free end around the standing end, crossing over itself to form a loop.

  16. Pass the free end through the loop from underneath, then wrap it around the standing end once more.

  17. Finally, thread the free end back through the loop and tighten the knot by pulling the standing end.

  18. The Figure-Eight Knot: The figure-eight knot is a simple and secure stopper knot that prevents the rope from slipping through an anchor point or belay device. It’s commonly used in rock climbing, rappelling, and sailing to secure the end of a rope. To tie a figure-eight knot, follow these steps:

  19. Form a loop with the end of the rope, crossing the free end over the standing end to create a simple loop.

  20. Pass the free end around behind the standing end, then thread it back through the loop from underneath.

  21. Tighten the knot by pulling both ends firmly, ensuring that the loop remains closed.

By mastering these essential survival knots, you’ll be better prepared to handle a variety of situations and challenges in the outdoors. Practice tying each knot until you can do so confidently and quickly, and remember to inspect your knots regularly to ensure they are secure. With the right skills and knowledge, you can navigate the wilderness with confidence and safety.
