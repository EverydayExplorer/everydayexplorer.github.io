Haunted Hotels: Checking into the Paranormal


Have you ever wondered what it would be like to stay in a hotel that is rumored to be haunted? For those with a taste for the supernatural, haunted hotels offer a unique and spine-tingling experience. From mysterious footsteps in empty hallways to eerie apparitions, these hotels have stories that will leave you both fascinated and spooked. In this blog post, we’ll take a deep dive into some of the world’s most famous haunted hotels, exploring their chilling histories and the paranormal encounters reported by guests and staff.

The Stanley Hotel - Estes Park, Colorado

Made famous by Stephen King’s novel “The Shining,” The Stanley Hotel is an iconic haunted destination. Nestled in the picturesque Rocky Mountains, this grand hotel has a rich history dating back to 1909. Many guests and staff claim to have experienced unexplained phenomena, such as ghostly children playing in the hallways and pianos playing by themselves in the dead of night. The hotel offers guided ghost tours, allowing visitors to explore the eerie corridors and hear hair-raising tales of the supernatural.

The Queen Mary - Long Beach, California

Once a luxurious ocean liner, The Queen Mary is now a stationary hotel that harbors a reputation for being one of the most haunted places in the United States. With a history that includes serving as a troopship during World War II, the Queen Mary has seen its fair share of tragedy and is said to be home to several spirits. Guests have reported encounters with ghostly figures, phantom footsteps, and doors slamming shut on their own. The hotel even offers a “Ghosts and Legends” tour, delving into the ship’s paranormal past.

The Langham Hotel - London, England

Steeped in elegance and history, The Langham Hotel has welcomed distinguished guests for over 150 years. However, beneath its opulent facade lies a darker side. The hotel is believed to be haunted by several spirits, including a German prince who jumped out of a window to his death, and a doctor who murdered his wife and then committed suicide. Guests have described encounters with these restless souls, as well as inexplicable cold spots and unexplained noises. The Langham embraces its haunted reputation, offering guests the chance to stay in its infamous Room 333, known for its paranormal activity.

The Crescent Hotel - Eureka Springs, Arkansas

Perched on a hill overlooking the picturesque town of Eureka Springs, The Crescent Hotel has a storied past filled with tragedy and mystery. Once a hospital run by a fraudulent doctor, the hotel is said to be haunted by the spirits of patients who suffered under his care. Visitors have reported seeing ghostly nurses, hearing cries of pain from empty rooms, and feeling an overwhelming sense of unease in certain areas of the building. Guided ghost tours are available, inviting guests to explore the hotel’s haunted history and perhaps even have their own otherworldly encounter.

The Myrtles Plantation - St. Francisville, Louisiana

Listed on the National Register of Historic Places, The Myrtles Plantation is renowned for its beauty and its ghostly inhabitants. Built in 1796, the plantation is said to be haunted by the spirits of former slaves and the ghost of a young girl who tragically met her end on the property. Guests have reported seeing apparitions, hearing phantom footsteps, and witnessing objects moving on their own. The Myrtles offers guided history tours that delve into the plantation’s past, including its haunted reputation.

Checking into the Paranormal

Whether you’re a skeptic or a believer, there’s no denying the allure of haunted hotels. These historic properties offer a glimpse into the past and a chance to experience the unexplained. From chilling ghost tours to the thrill of potentially encountering something otherworldly, a stay at a haunted hotel is an unforgettable experience for those intrigued by the paranormal.

If you’re considering a stay at one of these haunted hotels, be prepared for a stay unlike any other. With their rich histories, ghostly tales, and spine-tingling encounters, these hotels are sure to leave a lasting impression. Just remember, when checking into the paranormal, you might just find yourself face to face with the unknown.
