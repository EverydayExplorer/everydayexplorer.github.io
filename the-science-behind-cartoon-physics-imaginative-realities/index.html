The Science Behind Cartoon Physics: Imaginative Realities


When we watch cartoons, we know that what we see on the screen is not meant to be taken literally. After all, it is hard to imagine a world where anvils fall from the sky and characters can run off the edge of a cliff and still stay suspended in mid-air. Yet, at the same time, we still enjoy these moments.

This is where cartoon physics come into play. Cartoon physics is a concept that refers to the imaginary physics that create a sense of gravitation defying events we typically see in cartoons. Things like characters who can run on water, tunnels that stretch and bend, and characters turning into various objects are what we are talking about.

In today’s blog post, we will explore how the science of cartoon physics works, and why it is so important to the success of cartoons.

Gravity Is Not Obeyed

In the real world, gravity is one of the fundamental forces that govern our daily lives. It dictates how heavy things fall, how high we can jump, and how objects move through space. However, in the world of cartoons, gravity tends to take a back seat to imagination.

While gravity is still a force in the world of cartoons, it is often ignored or defied. This can lead to some wild and wacky moments in cartoons that would never be possible in real life.

Materials Behave Differently in Cartoons

In the real world, materials have certain properties that make them behave in specific ways. For example, wood is sturdy and will maintain its shape while cloth is flexible and can be shaped into various forms.

However, in the world of cartoons, materials can behave in very different ways. For example, characters can become elastic and stretchy, or objects can be altered to become soft and malleable.

In cartoons, the rules of materials are different; they can be used as a tool to tell the story rather than being beholden to physics.

Cartoon Realities Are Fantastical

Cartoons allow us to escape from real life and enter into a fantastical world where anything is possible. This is a significant reason why cartoons are so popular among children and adults alike. The imagination runs wild when we are not bound by the laws of physics.

Characters can transform into anything they want, and objects can be modified in all kinds of ways, giving us a sense of freedom and making us believe that anything is possible.

Using These Concepts to Create Believable Stories

While cartoon physics may seem far-fetched, these concepts are what make cartoon stories so relatable to audiences. By taking the fundamental laws of physics and altering them, storytellers can create a world that is believable within its own confines.

When creating cartoon stories, it is important to pay attention to cartoon physics to ensure that the story still makes sense while retaining its fantastical elements. It’s a delicate balance that can make or break the story.

Conclusion

Cartoon physics is an important aspect of cartoons that helps create a world that is believable yet imaginative. By altering the fundamental laws of physics, storytellers can create a world where anything is possible, and characters can achieve the impossible.

So the next time you find yourself watching a cartoon, take a moment to appreciate the imagination and creativity that goes into it and the use of cartoon physics to create a world that is truly unique.
