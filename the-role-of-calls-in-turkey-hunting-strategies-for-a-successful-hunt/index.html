The Role of Calls in Turkey Hunting: Strategies for a Successful Hunt


Turkey hunting is a popular and thrilling outdoor activity that attracts countless hunters each year. Part of what makes turkey hunting so exciting is the strategic use of calls. Calls are essential tools that enable hunters to communicate with turkeys and entice them within shooting range. In this blog post, we will explore the role of calls in turkey hunting and provide strategies for a successful hunt. So, grab your gear and let’s get started!

## Understanding Calls

Calls are devices or vocalizations used by hunters to mimic the sounds made by turkeys. These sounds include yelps, clucks, purrs, and gobbles. Turkey call devices can be divided into two main categories: friction and air-operated calls.

Friction calls, such as box calls, slate calls, and push-button calls, produce sound when you create friction between different materials by stroking, tapping, or pushing them together. On the other hand, air-operated calls, like diaphragm calls and tube calls, rely on the movement of air through various reeds and chambers to create turkey sounds.

## The Importance of Calls in Turkey Hunting

Calls play a crucial role in turkey hunting as they serve several purposes:

1. **Locating Turkeys**: Using locator calls, such as owl hooters or crow calls, hunters can elicit a response from nearby turkeys. These calls replicate the sounds made by natural predators, which often lead turkeys to gobble in return, revealing their location.

2. **Attracting Turkeys**: Once you have located turkeys, it’s time to start using calls that mimic the sounds made by female turkeys. These calls, known as hen calls, can attract gobblers that are interested in mating. Through skilled use of calls, hunters can create a conversation between themselves and the turkeys, enticing them to move closer.

3. **Creating Distraction**: Sometimes, even if you have located a turkey, it may be reluctant to approach your position. In such cases, a technique called ‘shock gobbling’ can be employed. Hunters use loud and sudden noise, often produced by a crow call or coyote howler, to startle the turkey and provoke a gobble. This allows the hunter to gauge the bird’s location and adjust their strategy accordingly.

## Strategies for Using Calls Effectively

To ensure a successful turkey hunt, it is essential to employ the right strategies when using calls. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. **Practice Makes Perfect**: Mastery of turkey calls takes practice. Spend time familiarizing yourself with different call types and perfecting your technique. To improve your skills, invest in quality calls and consider using instructional videos or audio recordings to learn from experienced hunters.

2. **Know When to Call**: Timing is crucial when using turkey calls. In the early morning, turkeys are more vocal and actively searching for mates. This is an excellent time to use calls to locate and attract turkeys. However, as the day progresses and turkeys become more cautious, it’s important to use calls strategically and sparingly to avoid spooking them.

3. **Match Your Calls to the Situation**: Turkeys have various vocalizations for different situations. Match your calls to replicate the specific sounds of turkeys in your hunting area. For example, if you’re hunting in an area with a lot of hens, using soft yelps and purrs can be more effective at attracting gobblers.

4. **Use Decoys**: Combine the use of calls with turkey decoys for maximum effectiveness. Decoys enhance the realism of your setup and can draw in turkeys that are hesitant to come closer. Place decoys strategically, paying attention to the direction of the prevailing winds and any natural cover that can provide a realistic hiding spot for the hunter.

5. **Be Patient and Persistent**: Turkey hunting requires patience and persistence. It’s essential to stay in one location for an extended period, even if your initial calls do not yield immediate results. Turkeys may take time to respond or approach, so be prepared to wait it out.

## Conclusion

Calls are an integral part of turkey hunting, allowing hunters to communicate with turkeys and increase their chances of a successful hunt. Understanding the different types of calls and their purposes, and employing effective strategies, can greatly enhance your turkey hunting experience. Remember to practice your calling techniques, use calls strategically, and be patient and persistent. So, get out there and enjoy the thrill of turkey hunting, knowing that you have the skills and knowledge to make your hunt a success!
