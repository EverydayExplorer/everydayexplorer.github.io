The Symbolism of Mythological Colors: Red, Blue, and Gold


Colors have always played a significant role in human culture and mythology. Throughout history, colors have been used to represent various emotions, ideas, and concepts. In mythology, colors are often assigned symbolic meanings that help give depth and significance to the stories and characters they are associated with. In this blog post, we will explore the symbolism of three mythological colors: red, blue, and gold.

Red is a color that is commonly associated with power, passion, and intensity. In mythology, red often represents fire, blood, and vitality. It is the color of the god of war, Mars, in Roman mythology. Mars is known for his ferocity and aggression, and the color red is a reflection of his powerful and passionate nature. In many mythological tales, red is also used to symbolize love and desire, such as in the story of Cupid and Psyche. In this story, Cupid’s arrows are said to be tipped with red, representing the passionate and transformative power of love.

Blue, on the other hand, is a color that is often associated with calmness, depth, and wisdom. In mythology, blue is often connected to water and the sea. It represents the vastness and mystery of the ocean, as well as the serenity and tranquility that can be found in its depths. In Greek mythology, Poseidon, the god of the sea, is often depicted with blue skin and hair, emphasizing his connection to the waters. Similarly, in Hindu mythology, Lord Krishna is often portrayed with blue skin, symbolizing his divine wisdom and transcendental nature.

Gold, with its radiant and luminous hue, is a color that has long been associated with wealth, power, and divinity. In mythology, gold represents the sun and its life-giving properties. The golden sun symbolizes illumination, enlightenment, and spiritual awakening. In many mythologies, gods and goddesses are depicted wearing golden crowns and robes, symbolizing their elevated status and divine nature. Gold is also often associated with abundance and prosperity, as it represents the material wealth and luxury that can be achieved.

The symbolic meanings of these mythological colors extend beyond their individual representations. When combined, they can take on even more profound significance. For example, the combination of red and gold can represent both power and divinity. The color red symbolizes the strength and passion that comes with power, while gold represents the divine and elevated nature of gods and goddesses. On the other hand, the combination of blue and gold can represent the union of wisdom and divinity. Blue signifies wisdom and depth, while gold represents the divine and transcendent.

Understanding the symbolism behind these mythological colors can enhance our appreciation of ancient stories and their underlying meanings. By recognizing the deeper meaning attributed to colors, we gain a greater understanding of the characters, themes, and messages conveyed in mythological tales. Whether it’s the fiery intensity of red, the tranquil wisdom of blue, or the radiant divinity of gold, colors have the power to bring mythological stories to life and leave a lasting impact on our imagination.

In conclusion, the symbolism of mythological colors holds great importance in the world of mythology. Red, blue, and gold each represent different aspects of human nature and the divine. The power and passion of red, the wisdom and depth of blue, and the divinity and wealth of gold all contribute to the rich tapestry of mythological storytelling. By understanding the symbolic meanings of these colors, we can unlock deeper insights into the characters and themes of ancient myths, allowing us to appreciate their beauty and significance even more.
