Adventure Seekers' Haven: Hotels near National Parks


Immersing yourself in the wonders of nature is a dream for many adventure seekers, and what better way to do so than by staying at hotels near national parks? Whether you’re a hiking enthusiast, wildlife lover, or simply seeking a retreat into the wilderness, these accommodations offer the perfect blend of comfort and proximity to some of the most stunning landscapes on the planet. Join us as we explore a selection of hotels that serve as gateways to nature’s grandeur:

1. The Majestic Yosemite Hotel (formerly The Ahwahnee) - Yosemite National Park, California
Nestled in the heart of Yosemite Valley, The Majestic Yosemite Hotel exudes old-world charm and elegance. Surrounded by towering granite cliffs and majestic waterfalls, this historic hotel offers easy access to iconic landmarks like Half Dome and Yosemite Falls. After a day of exploring, unwind in the hotel’s cozy lounge or indulge in a gourmet meal at the acclaimed dining room.

2. Many Glacier Hotel - Glacier National Park, Montana
Perched on the shores of Swiftcurrent Lake, Many Glacier Hotel is a rustic retreat in one of America’s most breathtaking national parks. Wake up to panoramic views of snow-capped peaks and pristine alpine lakes before embarking on a day of hiking along the park’s extensive trail system. Don’t miss the chance to join a boat tour or wildlife watching excursion right from the hotel’s doorstep.

3. Old Faithful Inn - Yellowstone National Park, Wyoming
An architectural marvel, Old Faithful Inn is a beloved landmark within Yellowstone National Park. Situated near the iconic Old Faithful geyser, this historic lodge offers rustic accommodations and unparalleled views of the park’s geothermal wonders. Attend a ranger-led program, marvel at the park’s diverse wildlife, and witness the awe-inspiring eruptions of Old Faithful just steps away from your room.

4. The Oasis at Death Valley - Death Valley National Park, California
Escape to the desert oasis of Death Valley and stay at The Oasis at Death Valley (formerly Furnace Creek Resort). Set against the backdrop of the rugged Panamint Mountains, this resort provides a cool refuge after a day of exploring the park’s otherworldly landscapes. Relax by the spring-fed pool, play a round of golf on the world’s lowest course, and stargaze under the clear desert skies.

5. Jenny Lake Lodge - Grand Teton National Park, Wyoming
Experience luxury in the heart of Grand Teton National Park at Jenny Lake Lodge. Surrounded by towering mountains and pristine forests, this secluded retreat offers elegant cabins and exceptional dining experiences. Explore the park’s hiking trails, take a scenic boat ride on Jenny Lake, and keep your eyes peeled for moose, elk, and grizzly bears.

6. El Tovar Hotel - Grand Canyon National Park, Arizona
Perched on the rim of the Grand Canyon, El Tovar Hotel is a historic gem that has welcomed guests since 1905. Enjoy breathtaking views of one of the world’s most iconic landscapes from your room or the hotel’s veranda. Embark on a guided hike into the canyon, marvel at sunset over the rim, and savor a meal at the renowned El Tovar Dining Room.

7. Paradise Inn - Mount Rainier National Park, Washington
Surrounded by wildflower meadows and ancient forests, Paradise Inn offers a cozy retreat in the shadow of Mount Rainier. Explore the park’s network of trails, including the famous Skyline Trail, which offers unparalleled views of the mountain’s icy glaciers. After a day of adventure, relax by the fireplace in the inn’s historic lobby.

8. The Lodge at Bryce Canyon - Bryce Canyon National Park, Utah
Situated just steps from the rim of Bryce Canyon, The Lodge at Bryce Canyon provides a front-row seat to the park’s otherworldly rock formations known as hoodoos. Wake up to sunrise over the canyon, hike along scenic trails, and join a ranger program to learn about the park’s unique geology and wildlife.

From the towering peaks of Yosemite to the colorful canyons of Bryce, these hotels near national parks offer unparalleled access to some of America’s most cherished landscapes. Whether you’re seeking adventure or simply yearning for a peaceful escape into nature, these accommodations serve as ideal bases for exploring the wonders of our national parks. So pack your hiking boots, grab your camera, and get ready for an unforgettable journey into the wild. Your next adventure awaits just beyond the doorstep of these extraordinary hotels.
