How to Create a Subtle and Natural Contour


Contouring has become a popular makeup technique in recent years, allowing individuals to enhance their facial features and create a more sculpted look. However, many people are hesitant to try contouring because they fear it will look heavy, unnatural, or overdone. In this blog post, we will explore how to create a subtle and natural contour that enhances your features without looking too dramatic.

1. Start with a Clean Canvas
Before you begin contouring, it’s essential to start with a clean and moisturized face. Cleanse your skin thoroughly, and apply a lightweight moisturizer to ensure a smooth base for your makeup.

2. Choose the Right Shade of Contour
One of the keys to achieving a subtle and natural contour is selecting the right shade of contour product. Opt for a contour shade that is one to two shades darker than your natural skin tone. Avoid shades that are too warm or too cool, as they can appear unnatural on the skin.

3. Use a Light Hand
When applying contour, it’s important to use a light hand and build the product gradually. Begin by lightly tapping your brush or sponge into the contour product, then tap off any excess to avoid applying too much product at once. Remember, it’s easier to add more product than to remove excess.

4. Focus on the Hollows of Your Face
To create a subtle contour, focus on applying the product to the hollows of your face. These are the areas that naturally have shadows, such as the hollows of your cheeks, temples, and jawline. Use a small, fluffy brush to gently blend the product into the skin, using circular motions for a seamless finish.

5. Blend, Blend, Blend
Blending is the key to achieving a natural-looking contour. Take your time to blend the product thoroughly, ensuring there are no harsh lines or obvious color differences. You can use a clean brush or a damp beauty sponge to blend the contour seamlessly into your foundation.

6. Avoid Applying Contour to the Entire Face
To maintain a subtle and natural contour, it’s important to avoid applying the product to the entire face. Contouring should be used strategically to enhance specific features, rather than as an all-over face product. Stick to contouring the areas that naturally have shadows, as mentioned earlier.

7. Set Your Contour
To ensure your contour lasts all day, set it with a powder. Choose a translucent powder or a powder that matches your skin tone to set the contour in place. Gently sweep the powder over the contoured areas with a fluffy brush to lock in the product and prevent it from moving throughout the day.

8. Highlight with Care
To complete your subtle and natural contour, add a touch of highlighter to the high points of your face. Apply a small amount of highlighter to the tops of your cheekbones, the bridge of your nose, and the cupid’s bow. Use a light hand and blend well for a soft, glowing effect.

Remember, the goal of a subtle and natural contour is to enhance your features, not to drastically change your appearance. By following these steps and practicing, you’ll be able to create a contour that looks effortless and enhances your natural beauty. Happy contouring!
