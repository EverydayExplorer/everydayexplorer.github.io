Survival Skills: Setting Traps for Small Game


Are you ready to take your survival skills to the next level? In a survival situation, knowing how to set traps for small game can be a game-changer. By mastering the art of trapping, you can secure a steady source of food and increase your chances of survival. In this blog post, we will explore the essential techniques and strategies for setting traps that will help you thrive in the wilderness.

Choosing the Right Location

When it comes to setting traps for small game, location is key. Look for signs of animal activity, such as tracks, droppings, or chewed vegetation. These indicators can lead you to areas where small game is likely to frequent. Additionally, consider setting traps near water sources, as animals often visit these areas for hydration.

Understanding Animal Behavior

To effectively set traps, it is essential to understand the behavior of the target species. Different animals have different habits and preferences, so tailoring your trap to their behavior will greatly increase your chances of success. For example, some animals are more likely to be caught in ground-level snares, while others may be better suited for tree-based traps.

Snare Traps

Snare traps are one of the most common and effective methods for catching small game. To set a snare, choose a location where animals are likely to pass through, such as a game trail or near a food source. Use a sturdy cord or wire to create a loop, making sure it is large enough to fit around the animal’s neck or body. Secure the snare to a tree or stake, ensuring it is positioned at the appropriate height. Place bait near the snare to entice the animal to approach.

Deadfall Traps

Another effective trap for small game is the deadfall trap. This trap relies on the weight of a falling object to crush the animal beneath it. To set a deadfall trap, find a large, stable rock or log and prop it up with sticks or other supports. Create a trigger mechanism that will release the weight when the animal disturbs it, using natural materials like twigs or vines. Bait the area around the trap to attract the animal’s attention and increase the chances of success.

Pitfall Traps

Pitfall traps are particularly effective for catching small mammals or reptiles. To set a pitfall trap, dig a deep hole in the ground and place sharpened sticks or spikes at the bottom. Cover the hole with leaves or other natural materials to camouflage it. Animals will unsuspectingly fall into the pit, unable to escape the sharp stakes at the bottom. Be cautious when using this trap, as it may not be suitable for catching larger game.

Camouflage and Concealment

To increase the effectiveness of your traps, it is crucial to camouflage them effectively. Use natural materials like leaves, branches, and dirt to blend your traps into the surrounding environment. This will make them less noticeable to wary small game and increase the likelihood of a successful catch. Additionally, minimize human scent by wearing gloves when handling traps and bait.

Patience and Maintenance

Setting traps for small game requires patience and persistence. Check your traps regularly to ensure they are still in working order and to prevent animals from suffering unnecessarily. Remember to release any non-target animals that may have been caught accidentally. By maintaining your traps and practicing responsible trapping techniques, you can maintain a sustainable food supply while respecting the natural ecosystem.

Now that you have learned the essential techniques for setting traps for small game, it’s time to put your newfound knowledge into practice. Remember to always prioritize safety and ethical considerations when trapping animals. With the right skills and mindset, you can become a master of survival in the wilderness. Happy trapping!
