Reimagining Fairy Tales: Modern Retellings Worth Exploring


Once upon a time, in a world not too dissimilar from our own, fairy tales captivated the hearts and imaginations of both young and old. These timeless stories featured princesses locked away in towers, brave knights rescuing damsels in distress, and magical creatures roaming enchanted forests. However, in this day and age, it’s time to reimagine these beloved tales and give them a modern twist. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most captivating modern retellings of fairy tales that are definitely worth exploring.

1. ‘Cinder’ by Marissa Meyer:
Imagine a world where cyborgs, androids, and a wicked Lunar queen rule the land. In this futuristic reimagining of the classic Cinderella tale, Marissa Meyer takes readers on a thrilling journey where Cinder, a talented mechanic and cyborg, finds herself at the center of an intergalactic struggle. With action, romance, and a dash of mystery, ‘Cinder’ breathes new life into the age-old story.

2. ‘A Curse So Dark and Lonely’ by Brigid Kemmerer:
In this Beauty and the Beast retelling, Brigid Kemmerer introduces us to Harper, a modern-day heroine with cerebral palsy. When Harper is transported to the magical world of Emberfall, she becomes tangled in a curse that forces her to relive the same season over and over again. With its gripping plot and diverse characters, ‘A Curse So Dark and Lonely’ challenges traditional notions of beauty and explores the power of love and inner strength.

3. ‘Wicked’ by Gregory Maguire:
We’ve all heard the story of the Wicked Witch of the West from ‘The Wizard of Oz,’ but have you ever wondered what made her so wicked? In ‘Wicked,’ Gregory Maguire delves into the origins of Elphaba, the misunderstood green-skinned protagonist. Set in a politically charged land of Oz, this novel explores themes of discrimination, friendship, and the complexities of good and evil. Prepare to have your preconceptions shattered as you discover the untold story of the Wicked Witch.

4. ‘The Lunar Chronicles’ by Marissa Meyer:
Marissa Meyer strikes again with her imaginative retelling of classic fairy tales set in a futuristic world. In this epic series, each book focuses on a different character, weaving their stories together in a thrilling and unexpected way. From a cyborg Cinderella to a Little Red Riding Hood who is a skilled spaceship pilot, ‘The Lunar Chronicles’ is a fantastical rollercoaster ride that combines science fiction, fantasy, and romance with a modern twist.

5. ‘A Court of Thorns and Roses’ by Sarah J. Maas:
Sarah J. Maas brings a fresh and mature take on ‘Beauty and the Beast’ in this captivating fantasy series. Feyre, a young human huntress, inadvertently crosses into a dangerous faerie realm and finds herself caught in a web of political intrigue, forbidden love, and ancient curses. As she navigates the treacherous world of the fae, Feyre discovers her own strength and fights for a love that transcends societal expectations.

These modern retellings of fairy tales not only breathe new life into timeless stories but also offer social commentary, diverse representation, and thought-provoking themes. They remind us that fairy tales are not just meant for children, but have the power to captivate and inspire readers of all ages. So, grab a cup of tea, curl up in your favorite reading nook, and let these modern retellings transport you to a world where the boundaries of imagination know no limits.
