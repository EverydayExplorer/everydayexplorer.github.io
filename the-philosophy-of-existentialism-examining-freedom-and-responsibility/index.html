The Philosophy of Existentialism: Examining Freedom and Responsibility


Existentialism is a philosophical movement that emerged in the 19th and 20th centuries, and it continues to influence our understanding of freedom, responsibility, and the meaning of life. In this blog post, we will explore the core principles of existentialism and delve into the profound questions it raises about our existence.

At its core, existentialism argues that individuals have the utmost freedom and responsibility in shaping their own lives. Existentialists believe that each person is responsible for defining their own meaning and purpose in life, as opposed to relying on external sources of authority or predetermined destinies. This philosophy encourages individuals to embrace their freedom and make choices that align with their authentic selves.

One of the key figures in the development of existentialism is Jean-Paul Sartre. Sartre famously proclaimed that ’existence precedes essence,’ meaning that our existence precedes any inherent meaning or purpose in life. According to Sartre, we are born into the world as blank slates, and it is through our actions and choices that we create our own essence or identity. This idea places the responsibility on individuals to actively engage with their existence and actively create meaning in their lives.

Existentialism also highlights the inherent anxiety and angst that comes with the pursuit of freedom and responsibility. When we fully grasp the weight of our freedom, we become acutely aware of the potential consequences of our choices. This awareness can lead to a sense of existential dread, as we realize the tremendous power we hold in shaping our own lives. However, existentialists argue that it is precisely through confronting this anxiety that we can live authentically and embrace our freedom.

Furthermore, existentialism explores the concept of authenticity and the quest to live in accordance with one’s true self. According to existentialists, society often imposes expectations and norms that can hinder individuals from fully expressing their true nature. In order to live an authentic life, one must break free from societal pressures and external influences, and instead, listen to their own inner voice. This pursuit of authenticity requires individuals to engage in deep self-reflection and strive for self-awareness.

Existentialism also deeply examines the concept of choice and its relationship to freedom and responsibility. Existentialists argue that every choice we make has consequences, and we are thus responsible for the outcomes of our choices. This perspective emphasizes personal accountability and rejects the notion of blaming external factors or circumstances for our actions. By acknowledging our responsibility, we can reclaim our agency and actively shape our lives in line with our values.

In conclusion, existentialism offers a powerful philosophical framework for understanding freedom and responsibility. It challenges us to confront the inherent ambiguity and uncertainty of existence, and it encourages us to embrace our freedom and actively shape our lives. By taking responsibility for our choices and living authentically, we can find meaning and purpose in our lives. The philosophy of existentialism invites us to explore the depths of our own existence and navigate the complexities of freedom and responsibility with courage and authenticity.
