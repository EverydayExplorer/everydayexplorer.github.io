Fishing with Artificial Baits: Tips for Choosing and Using Lures


Fishing with artificial baits, specifically lures, is a popular and effective technique that many anglers swear by. Whether you’re a seasoned angler or a beginner, using artificial baits can greatly increase your chances of landing that big catch. In this blog post, we will cover some important tips for choosing and using lures, so you can make the most out of your fishing experience.

Why use artificial baits?

The use of artificial baits, such as lures, has several advantages over traditional live bait. For one, artificial baits can be reused multiple times, making them more cost-effective in the long run. Additionally, lures can mimic the movements and appearance of real baitfish or prey, making them highly attractive to fish. They also give you more control over your fishing experience, as you can choose a lure that targets a specific species or fishing condition.

Choosing the right lure

Choosing the right lure is crucial for the success of your fishing trip. With so many options available on the market, it can be overwhelming to decide which one to use. Here are some key factors to consider when selecting a lure:

  1. Type of lure: There are various types of lures, including crankbaits, spinnerbaits, jigs, soft plastics, and topwater baits. Each lure type has its own unique action and purpose. For example, crankbaits are great for covering a large area of water quickly, while topwater baits are effective for targeting fish near the surface.

  2. Color: Fish are attracted to different colors based on their natural prey. In clear water, choose natural and realistic colors, while in murky or stained water, opt for brighter, more visible colors. It’s also a good idea to have a variety of colors in your tackle box to adapt to different fishing conditions.

  3. Size: The size of the lure should match the size of the fish you are targeting. Larger lures are more suitable for bigger game fish, while smaller lures are ideal for targeting panfish or finesse fishing.

  4. Action: The action of a lure refers to how it moves and behaves in the water. Some lures have a tight wobble, while others have a wide side-to-side motion. Pay attention to the action of the lure and choose one that matches the behavior of the fish you’re targeting.

Using lures effectively

Once you’ve chosen the right lure, it’s important to understand how to use it effectively. Here are some tips to make the most out of your artificial bait:

  1. Vary your retrieve: Experiment with different retrieve speeds and techniques to see what entices the fish. Sometimes a slow and steady retrieve works, while other times a stop-and-go action can trigger strikes.

  2. Imitate natural movements: Try to mimic the movements of real baitfish or prey with your lure. For example, jerk your rod tip to create a darting action or twitch your bait to simulate a wounded baitfish. The more natural your lure appears in the water, the more likely it is to attract fish.

  3. Pay attention to depth: Adjust the depth at which your lure runs to match the location of the fish. Use techniques like adding weights or adjusting the length of your line to control the depth at which your lure swims.

  4. Be patient and observant: Fishing with lures requires patience and observation. Take note of any patterns or signs of fish activity, such as surface swirls or baitfish activity. Stay focused and pay attention to subtle cues that might lead you to the fish.

  5. Keep learning and experimenting: Fishing is a continuous learning process. Be open to trying new lures, techniques, and locations. Keep track of what works and what doesn’t, and apply your knowledge to improve your fishing skills.

In conclusion, fishing with artificial baits, specifically lures, can be a rewarding and productive experience. By choosing the right lure and using it effectively, you can increase your chances of catching more fish. Remember to consider the type, color, size, and action of the lure when making your selection. Vary your retrieve, imitate natural movements, pay attention to depth, and stay patient and observant. Finally, never stop learning and experimenting with new techniques. Good luck and tight lines!
