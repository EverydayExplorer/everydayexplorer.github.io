Increasing Website Accessibility: Design and Development Strategies


In today’s digital landscape, having a user-friendly and accessible website is no longer a luxury, but a necessity. With the growing importance of inclusivity and the need to cater to all types of users, increasing website accessibility should be a top priority for businesses. Not only does it help create a more user-centric experience, but it also boosts search engine optimization (SEO) and ensures compliance with accessibility regulations.

Designing and developing an accessible website requires a thoughtful approach and adherence to best practices. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Use Clear and Consistent Design Elements: A clean and organized design not only improves user experience but also makes it easier for individuals with disabilities to navigate your website. Maintain a consistent layout, typography, and color scheme throughout the site. Ensure sufficient color contrast between the foreground and background to aid users with visual impairments.

2. Incorporate Descriptive Alt Texts: Including descriptive alternative texts (alt texts) for your images is essential. Alt texts provide textual descriptions of images, enabling individuals who are visually impaired to understand the context. Be sure to accurately describe the image’s content or function in a concise manner.

3. Optimize Website Navigation: Navigation is key to helping users find the information they’re looking for quickly and easily. Implement a logical and intuitive website structure, employing clear headings, subheadings, and menus. Use breadcrumbs and sitemaps to enhance navigation and facilitate screen reader usage.

4. Create Transcripts and Captions for Multimedia Content: Videos and audio elements should be accompanied by transcripts or captions. This accommodation benefits deaf or hard-of-hearing users, as well as individuals who may prefer reading instead of watching/listening. Including these transcripts also helps search engines index the content, improving the visibility of your website.

5. Ensure Responsive Design: With the ever-increasing use of mobile devices, it’s crucial to make your website responsive. Responsive design ensures that your website adapts to different screen sizes and resolutions, making it accessible to users across various devices. Responsive websites also rank higher in search engine results pages (SERPs), as mobile-friendliness is an SEO ranking factor.

6. Enhance Text Readability: Choose fonts and font sizes that are easy to read, especially for users with dyslexia or visual impairments. Avoid using overly decorative or complex fonts, and ensure sufficient contrast between text and the background. Provide options to adjust the font size to accommodate individual preferences.

7. Test and Validate Accessibility: Regularly test your website for accessibility using tools like WebAIM’s WAVE or W3C’s WCAG 2.1 guidelines. These tools scan your website for accessibility issues, allowing you to identify and fix any problems. Regular testing ensures that your website remains accessible and compliant over time.

By implementing these design and development strategies, you can significantly enhance your website’s accessibility and cater to a broader audience. Not only will you improve the overall user experience, but you will also boost your website’s SEO rankings and comply with accessibility standards. Remember, creating an inclusive digital experience is not just the right thing to do, but it also makes good business sense.
