Hidden Gems: Underrated Movies and TV Shows to Watch


In a world dominated by blockbuster films and popular TV shows, it’s easy for hidden gems to go unnoticed. These underrated movies and TV shows often fail to receive the recognition they deserve, despite their exceptional storytelling, brilliant performances, and unique perspectives. Today, I am going to share with you some of these hidden gems that are definitely worth checking out.

Movies

  1. ‘Moon’ (2009): Directed by Duncan Jones, this science fiction film is a hidden gem that takes viewers on a thought-provoking journey. It stars Sam Rockwell, who delivers an outstanding performance as an astronaut nearing the end of his three-year shift on a lunar base. The movie explores themes of isolation, identity, and the nature of humanity.

  2. ‘The Fall’ (2006): Directed by Tarsem Singh, this visually stunning film tells the story of a young girl who befriends a stuntman while recovering in a hospital. As the stuntman weaves an epic tale for the girl, we are drawn into a world of fantasy and adventure. With breathtaking cinematography and a mesmerizing narrative, ‘The Fall’ is a hidden gem that captivates from start to finish.

  3. ‘Brick’ (2005): Directed by Rian Johnson, this neo-noir film is a gritty and stylish homage to classic detective stories. Starring Joseph Gordon-Levitt, ‘Brick’ follows a high school loner delving into the seedy underbelly of his community to solve the murder of his ex-girlfriend. With its unique blend of genres and intelligent writing, ‘Brick’ is a compelling hidden gem that keeps you guessing until the very end.

  4. ‘Short Term 12’ (2013): Directed by Destin Daniel Cretton, this emotionally powerful film takes place in a group home for troubled teenagers. With stellar performances by Brie Larson and a talented ensemble cast, ‘Short Term 12’ tackles themes of love, trauma, and redemption. It is an authentic and heart-wrenching hidden gem that will leave a lasting impact on anyone who watches it.

  5. ‘The Fall of the American Empire’ (2018): Directed by Denys Arcand, this Canadian crime comedy-drama explores the moral dilemma of a deliveryman who stumbles upon two bags of money. As he finds himself entangled with criminals, lawyers, and sex workers, the film delves into thought-provoking social commentary. With its clever writing and brilliant performances, ‘The Fall of the American Empire’ is an underrated gem that provides both entertainment and food for thought.

TV Shows

  1. ‘Hannibal’ (2013-2015): This psychological horror series, based on characters from Thomas Harris’ novels, is a visually stunning and intellectually challenging masterpiece. With Mads Mikkelsen’s captivating performance as the infamous Hannibal Lecter and the intricate cat-and-mouse game between him and FBI profiler Will Graham, ‘Hannibal’ is a hidden gem that keeps you on the edge of your seat.

  2. ‘Fringe’ (2008-2013): Created by J.J. Abrams, Alex Kurtzman, and Roberto Orci, this science fiction series follows a team of FBI agents investigating mysterious and often unexplained phenomena known as ‘The Pattern.’ With its blend of procedural storytelling, complex mythology, and unforgettable characters, ‘Fringe’ is an underrated gem that will satisfy both sci-fi enthusiasts and those who crave compelling character-driven narratives.

  3. ‘Penny Dreadful’ (2014-2016): Set in Victorian London, this horror-drama series weaves together iconic literary characters such as Dracula, Frankenstein, and Dorian Gray into a dark and captivating narrative. With its stunning production design, impeccable acting, and rich storytelling, ‘Penny Dreadful’ is an underrated gem that breathes new life into classic Gothic literature.

  4. ‘The Expanse’ (2015-present): Based on James S.A. Corey’s bestselling book series, this science fiction series has gained a dedicated following for its meticulously crafted world-building, complex characters, and politically charged storyline set in a future where humanity has colonized the solar system. With its compelling mix of hard science fiction and political intrigue, ‘The Expanse’ is a hidden gem that deserves more recognition.

  5. ‘Orphan Black’ (2013-2017): This gripping sci-fi mystery series revolves around a woman who discovers she is one of many clones, each with their own distinct personalities and secrets. Tatiana Maslany’s tour-de-force performances as multiple characters make ‘Orphan Black’ a standout hidden gem that explores themes of identity, ethics, and the profound impact of nature versus nurture.

These hidden gems, both in the world of movies and TV shows, offer unique and captivating storytelling experiences that deserve to be discovered. So, the next time you’re looking for something new to watch, don’t overlook these underrated gems. They may just become your new favorites!
