Trolling for Walleye: Techniques and Gear


When it comes to angling for walleye, one of the most effective and exciting methods is trolling. Trolling involves dragging bait or lures behind a moving boat to entice these elusive fish. Not only does it provide an opportunity to cover a large area of water, but it also allows for a variety of techniques and gear to be used. In this article, we will explore the techniques and gear necessary to make your trolling for walleye adventure a successful one.

Trolling Techniques

  1. Speed Control: One of the key factors in successful walleye trolling is maintaining the right speed. Walleye are known to be finicky eaters, and the speed at which your bait or lure is moving can make all the difference. Typically, a speed of 1 to 2.5 miles per hour is optimal for walleye trolling, but it’s important to experiment and adjust based on the specific conditions you encounter.

  2. Depth Control: Understanding the depth at which walleye are feeding is crucial. Using a depth finder or fish finder can help you locate the desired depth. Once you have determined the depth, you can adjust your trolling setup accordingly. This may involve using different types of lures or adding weight to your line to achieve the desired depth.

  3. Lure Selection: Choosing the right lure is key when it comes to trolling for walleye. Some popular choices include crankbaits, spinnerbaits, and soft plastics. It’s important to consider the color, size, and action of the lure to match the feeding patterns and preferences of walleye in your fishing area. Additionally, using lures that imitate baitfish or other natural prey can increase your chances of success.

  4. Presentation: Varying your lure presentation can often trigger a walleye strike. Experiment with different trolling techniques such as zigzagging, figure eights, or changing the speed and depth of your trolling setup. Walleye can be unpredictable, so it’s essential to keep them guessing and adjust your presentation accordingly.

Trolling Gear

  1. Rods and Reels: When it comes to trolling, it’s recommended to use medium to medium-heavy spinning or baitcasting rods paired with a sturdy reel. The fishing rod should have enough backbone to handle the weight of the trolling setup and provide enough sensitivity to feel the subtle walleye bites. Consider using a line counter reel to accurately measure the amount of line you have out.

  2. Trolling Lines: Using specific trolling lines is crucial for success. Monofilament, fluorocarbon, or braided lines can all be suitable, depending on the conditions and personal preference. Look for lines with low visibility and high strength to withstand the pressure of trolling. It’s also essential to maintain proper line tension to prevent tangling and increase your chances of hooking walleye.

  3. Planer Boards: Planer boards are a valuable tool for trolling as they allow you to cover a wider area of water. These boards are attached to your fishing line and set out to the side of the boat. They help carry your bait or lure away from the boat, reducing spooking and increasing your chances of catching walleye. Make sure to invest in quality planer boards that are easy to use and durable.

  4. Downriggers: Downriggers are another gear option for trolling that allows you to fish at specific depths. These devices use a weight and release mechanism to control the depth at which your bait or lure is presented. Downriggers are especially useful when targeting walleye in deeper waters. They can be attached to your boat and adjusted easily to reach the desired depth.

  5. Trolling Accessories: Additional accessories such as snap weights, inline weights, and trolling spoons can enhance your trolling setup. Snap weights and inline weights can be easily added or removed to adjust the depth at which your bait or lure is running. Trolling spoons are effective in imitating baitfish and can attract walleye from a distance. These accessories can be used in combination with other trolling techniques to increase your chances of success.

In conclusion, trolling for walleye is an exciting and rewarding fishing technique. By mastering the right techniques and using the appropriate gear, you can significantly increase your chances of landing these prized fish. Experimenting with lure selection, presentation, speed, and depth control, along with investing in quality trolling gear, can elevate your trolling experience to the next level. Remember to stay adaptable and embrace the challenges of fishing for walleye, as they are known for keeping anglers on their toes. Tight lines and happy trolling!
