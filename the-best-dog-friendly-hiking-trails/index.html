The Best Dog-Friendly Hiking Trails


Are you tired of leaving your furry friend behind when you embark on a thrilling hiking adventure? Well, fret no more! We have compiled a list of the best dog-friendly hiking trails that will allow you and your beloved canine companion to explore the great outdoors together. So, grab your hiking boots, leash up your four-legged friend, and get ready for an unforgettable adventure!

1. Runyon Canyon Park, Los Angeles, California
Nestled in the heart of Los Angeles, Runyon Canyon Park offers breathtaking views of the city skyline while providing ample space for your pup to roam and play. With multiple trails ranging in difficulty, you and your furry friend can choose the perfect path for your hiking abilities. Don’t forget to bring water for both you and your dog to stay hydrated during your hike!

2. Mount Tamalpais State Park, Mill Valley, California
If you’re looking for a challenging hike with stunning panoramic views, Mount Tamalpais State Park is the place to be. This dog-friendly hiking trail offers a variety of routes, including the popular Dipsea Trail, which winds through lush forests and opens up to breathtaking vistas. Keep in mind that some trails may require your dog to be on a leash, so be sure to check the park’s regulations before planning your visit.

3. Acadia National Park, Maine
For those seeking an adventure on the East Coast, Acadia National Park is a must-visit destination. With over 100 miles of dog-friendly hiking trails, this park offers a diverse range of landscapes, including rocky coastlines, lush forests, and granite mountaintops. Be sure to explore the iconic Jordan Pond Path, which provides stunning views of the park’s pristine lakes and mountains.

4. Griffith Park, Los Angeles, California
Another gem in the City of Angels, Griffith Park offers a plethora of dog-friendly hiking trails suitable for all skill levels. From leisurely walks around the iconic Griffith Observatory to more challenging treks up to the Hollywood Sign, there’s something for everyone. Don’t forget to bring your camera to capture the breathtaking views of Los Angeles and the surrounding area.

5. Mount Rainier National Park, Washington
If you and your furry friend are up for a more challenging adventure, Mount Rainier National Park is the perfect destination. With its towering peaks, glaciers, and alpine meadows, this park offers a truly breathtaking hiking experience. While not all trails are dog-friendly, there are several designated trails where your four-legged companion is welcome to join you.

Remember, when hiking with your dog, it’s crucial to be prepared. Pack enough water for both you and your pup, bring a leash, and always clean up after your furry friend. Additionally, be mindful of your dog’s limitations and choose trails that are suitable for their breed and fitness level.

So, what are you waiting for? Grab your hiking gear, leash up your pup, and embark on an exciting adventure on one of the best dog-friendly hiking trails. The great outdoors is calling, and your furry friend is ready to answer!
