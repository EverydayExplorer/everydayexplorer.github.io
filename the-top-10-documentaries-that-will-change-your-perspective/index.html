The Top 10 Documentaries That Will Change Your Perspective


Documentaries have the power to captivate, educate, and challenge our perspectives on the world. Whether they shed light on social issues, explore untold stories, or delve into the mysteries of the universe, documentaries have the ability to leave a lasting impact on viewers. In this blog post, we will explore the top 10 documentaries that have the potential to change your perspective and broaden your horizons.

1. ‘Blackfish’ (2013)
Directed by Gabriela Cowperthwaite, ‘Blackfish’ is a powerful documentary that exposes the dark side of the captive whale industry. Through interviews with former SeaWorld trainers and heartbreaking footage, the film highlights the ethical concerns surrounding the treatment of these intelligent creatures. ‘Blackfish’ will make you question the morality of keeping animals in captivity for entertainment purposes.

2. ‘The Cove’ (2009)
Directed by Louie Psihoyos, ‘The Cove’ is an eye-opening documentary that uncovers the brutal dolphin hunting practices in Taiji, Japan. The film follows a team of activists as they expose the mass slaughter of dolphins and the connection to the captivity industry. ‘The Cove’ reveals the devastating consequences of human greed and the lengths people will go to hide the truth.

3. ‘Citizenfour’ (2014)
Directed by Laura Poitras, ‘Citizenfour’ is a gripping documentary that chronicles the events surrounding Edward Snowden’s decision to leak classified documents about government surveillance. The film offers a unique insight into the world of whistleblowers and raises important questions about privacy, security, and individual liberties in the digital age.

4. ‘Exit Through the Gift Shop’ (2010)
Directed by the mysterious street artist Banksy, ‘Exit Through the Gift Shop’ is a thought-provoking documentary that blurs the line between art and commercialism. The film follows the journey of Thierry Guetta, a French immigrant in Los Angeles, as he attempts to document the street art scene. ‘Exit Through the Gift Shop’ challenges our perception of what is considered art and raises questions about authenticity and the commercialization of creativity.

5. ‘The Act of Killing’ (2012)
Directed by Joshua Oppenheimer, ‘The Act of Killing’ is a haunting documentary that revisits the Indonesian genocide of the 1960s. The film explores the perspective of the perpetrators, who are given the opportunity to recreate their acts of violence through reenactments. ‘The Act of Killing’ forces viewers to confront the dark depths of human nature and reflects on the consequences of impunity and collective denial.

6. ‘Grizzly Man’ (2005)
Directed by Werner Herzog, ‘Grizzly Man’ tells the story of Timothy Treadwell, an environmentalist and bear enthusiast who spent thirteen summers living among grizzly bears in Alaska. Through Treadwell’s own footage and interviews with those who knew him, the documentary explores the complex relationship between humans and nature, as well as the boundaries of human-animal interaction.

7. ‘Food, Inc.’ (2008)
Directed by Robert Kenner, ‘Food, Inc.’ unveils the hidden truths of the industrial food system in the United States. The documentary exposes the unethical practices of factory farming, the corporate control of our food supply, and the impact on our health and the environment. ‘Food, Inc.’ encourages viewers to become more conscious consumers and advocates for sustainable and ethical food production.

8. ‘The Corporation’ (2003)
Directed by Mark Achbar and Jennifer Abbott, ‘The Corporation’ examines the rise and influence of corporations in modern society. Through interviews with CEOs, activists, and scholars, the film analyzes the nature and behavior of corporations and their impact on social and environmental issues. ‘The Corporation’ challenges our perception of these entities and prompts us to question their role in shaping our world.

9. ‘Man on Wire’ (2008)
Directed by James Marsh, ‘Man on Wire’ tells the extraordinary story of Philippe Petit, a French high-wire artist who illegally walked between the Twin Towers of the World Trade Center in 1974. The documentary combines archival footage, interviews, and reenactments to capture the audacity and thrill of Petit’s daring feat. ‘Man on Wire’ celebrates the human spirit and the pursuit of seemingly impossible dreams.

10. ‘Jiro Dreams of Sushi’ (2011)
Directed by David Gelb, ‘Jiro Dreams of Sushi’ offers a glimpse into the life of Jiro Ono, an 85-year-old sushi master in Tokyo. The film explores Jiro’s relentless pursuit of perfection and his unwavering dedication to his craft. ‘Jiro Dreams of Sushi’ is a meditative and inspiring documentary that encourages viewers to reflect on the value of passion, commitment, and the pursuit of excellence.

These ten documentaries represent a diverse range of subjects and storytelling styles, but they all share the common goal of challenging our perspectives and encouraging us to see the world in a different light. Whether it’s through exposing hidden truths, exploring the depths of human nature, or celebrating the triumph of the human spirit, these documentaries have the power to provoke thought, spark conversation, and inspire change. Open your mind, grab some popcorn, and embark on a journey of discovery with these transformative films.
