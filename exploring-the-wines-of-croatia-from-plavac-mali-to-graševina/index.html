Exploring the Wines of Croatia: From Plavac Mali to Graševina


Croatia may not be the first country that comes to mind when thinking about wine, but it is certainly gaining recognition as a hidden gem in the wine world. With a rich winemaking history that spans over 2,500 years, the country offers a diverse selection of grape varieties and unique winemaking techniques. In this blog post, we will explore the wines of Croatia, from Plavac Mali to Graševina, and discover why they are worth exploring.

One of Croatia’s most famous indigenous grape varieties is Plavac Mali. This robust red grape is primarily grown in the Dalmatia region along the Adriatic coast. It is a cross between two well-known grapes, Crljenak Kaštelanski (the ancestor of Zinfandel) and Dobričić. Plavac Mali typically produces full-bodied wines with high alcohol content and bold flavors of dark fruits, Mediterranean herbs, and hints of earthiness. These wines pair exceptionally well with rich meat dishes, such as lamb or game, and are a perfect match for the local cuisine.

Another notable grape variety in Croatia is Graševina, also known as Welschriesling. This white grape is grown throughout the country, but it shines in the northeastern region of Slavonia. Graševina wines are known for their fresh acidity, aromatic profile, and versatility. They range from dry to semi-sweet, offering a wide range of options for every palate. The dry versions of Graševina often exhibit citrus and green apple notes, while the sweeter styles showcase honey and floral aromas. These wines are fantastic pairings for seafood, light salads, or simply sipping on a warm summer day.

While Plavac Mali and Graševina are the most well-known grapes in Croatia, the country boasts numerous other indigenous varieties that are worth exploring. Babić, another red grape from Dalmatia, is known for its elegance, with flavors of ripe red fruits and smooth tannins. Teran is a red grape grown in the Istria region, producing wines with high acidity, intense fruitiness, and vibrant color. Malvazija Istriana is a white grape also from Istria, characterized by its citrus and floral notes, with a touch of minerality.

What sets Croatian wines apart is the diversity of microclimates and terroirs found throughout the country. From the coastal regions with their hot Mediterranean climate to the cooler continental climates inland, each area offers a unique expression of the grape varieties grown there. Additionally, the traditional winemaking techniques, such as aging wines in large wooden barrels called ‘barrique,’ contribute to the distinct character of Croatian wines.

Visiting the wineries and vineyards of Croatia is an experience not to be missed. The country’s wine regions are not only picturesque, with rolling hills and beautiful landscapes, but they provide an opportunity to meet passionate winemakers and taste their exceptional wines. Many wineries offer guided tours and tastings, providing visitors with an insight into the winemaking process and a chance to learn about the history and traditions of winemaking in Croatia.

Despite being lesser-known on the global wine stage, Croatian wines have been receiving international recognition and awards. The country’s winemakers are dedicated to producing high-quality wines and are constantly innovating while staying true to their traditional winemaking practices. So, whether you are a wine enthusiast looking to expand your palate or a curious traveler seeking new adventures, exploring the wines of Croatia will undoubtedly be a rewarding experience.

In conclusion, Croatia’s winemaking heritage, diverse grape varieties, and unique terroirs make it an exciting destination for wine lovers. From the robust Plavac Mali to the versatile Graševina, Croatian wines offer a wide range of flavors and styles to explore. With their exceptional quality and a touch of Mediterranean charm, these wines have the potential to become the next big thing in the world of wine. So, raise a glass and discover the wines of Croatia – a true hidden gem waiting to be explored!
