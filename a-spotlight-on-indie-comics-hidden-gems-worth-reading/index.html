A Spotlight on Indie Comics: Hidden Gems Worth Reading


Comic books have come a long way since their inception in the mid-20th century. While mainstream superheroes like Superman and Batman dominate the comic book industry, there is an entire world of indie comics waiting to be explored. These hidden gems offer unique storytelling, diverse characters, and captivating artwork that can rival even the most popular superhero titles. In this blog post, we will shine a spotlight on some of the best indie comics worth reading.

1. ‘Saga’ by Brian K. Vaughan and Fiona Staples

This critically acclaimed series combines elements of science fiction and fantasy to tell the epic tale of a young family trying to survive in a war-torn galaxy. The character-driven narrative, coupled with stunning artwork by Staples, has earned ‘Saga’ a devoted fanbase and numerous awards.

2. ‘Monstress’ by Marjorie Liu and Sana Takeda

Set in a post-apocalyptic world inspired by Asian mythology, ‘Monstress’ follows the journey of a young girl with a mysterious past who must navigate a world filled with magic, monsters, and political intrigue. The breathtaking artwork and intricate world-building make this series a must-read for any fan of dark fantasy.

3. ‘Sex Criminals’ by Matt Fraction and Chip Zdarsky

In this irreverent and hilarious series, a couple discovers that they can freeze time when they have sex. They decide to use their unique abilities to rob banks and save a struggling library. ‘Sex Criminals’ deconstructs societal taboos surrounding sex while delivering a clever and entertaining story.

4. ‘Lumberjanes’ by Shannon Watters, Grace Ellis, Brooke A. Allen, and Noelle Stevenson

’ Lumberjanes’ follows a group of friends attending a summer camp filled with supernatural mysteries and adventures. With its diverse cast of characters, positive representation, and emphasis on friendship and teamwork, this series has gained a devoted following and has been praised for its inclusivity and positive messages.

5. ‘Paper Girls’ by Brian K. Vaughan and Cliff Chiang

Set in the 1980s, ‘Paper Girls’ follows a group of paper delivery girls who find themselves caught in a time-traveling adventure. This nostalgic series blends sci-fi, mystery, and coming-of-age themes into a captivating story that keeps readers hooked from start to finish.

6. ‘The Wicked + The Divine’ by Kieron Gillen and Jamie McKelvie

‘The Wicked + The Divine’ explores the lives of gods who are reincarnated as pop stars every 90 years. This visually stunning series combines mythology, pop culture, and music into a unique and thought-provoking narrative. With its compelling characters and intricate plot, this comic has gained a dedicated fanbase and critical acclaim.

7. ‘Bitch Planet’ by Kelly Sue DeConnick and Valentine De Landro

‘Bitch Planet’ takes place in a dystopian future where non-compliant women are sent to a penal planet. This feminist and socially conscious series tackles themes of sexism, power, and societal expectations. With its bold artwork and unapologetic storytelling, ‘Bitch Planet’ challenges the norms of the comic book industry.

8. ‘Descender’ by Jeff Lemire and Dustin Nguyen

‘Descender’ tells the story of a young robot named Tim-21 who may hold the key to the survival of the galaxy. This space opera explores themes of humanity, artificial intelligence, and the consequences of technology. The beautiful watercolor artwork by Nguyen adds depth and emotion to the story, making ‘Descender’ a must-read for sci-fi fans.

9. ‘Harrow County’ by Cullen Bunn and Tyler Crook

Set in a rural Southern town, ‘Harrow County’ follows a young woman named Emmy who discovers that she is connected to the supernatural world. This chilling horror series combines folklore, suspense, and stunning artwork to deliver a haunting and atmospheric reading experience.

10. ‘Black Hammer’ by Jeff Lemire and Dean Ormston

‘Black Hammer’ tells the story of a group of superheroes who are trapped in a small town after defeating a powerful villain. This deconstruction of the superhero genre explores themes of identity, loss, and the consequences of heroism. With its rich storytelling and compelling characters, ‘Black Hammer’ has become a modern classic in the world of indie comics.

These are just a few examples of the many hidden gems in the world of indie comics. Whether you’re a longtime comic book reader or new to the medium, these titles offer something unique and refreshing. From epic space operas to thought-provoking social commentary, indie comics continue to push the boundaries of storytelling and provide a platform for diverse voices to be heard. So, the next time you’re looking for something new to read, don’t forget to give these indie comics a chance.
