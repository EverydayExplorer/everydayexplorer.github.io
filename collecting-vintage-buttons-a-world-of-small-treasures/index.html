Collecting Vintage Buttons: A World of Small Treasures

3 min · 627 words

Have you ever looked closely at a button on your clothing and wondered about its history? Buttons may seem like small, insignificant objects, but in the world of vintage collecting, they hold a wealth of interesting stories and unique designs. Whether you are a seasoned collector or new to the hobby, exploring the world of vintage buttons is sure to open up a whole new realm of creativity and appreciation for intricate craftsmanship. In this blog post, we will dive into the fascinating world of collecting vintage buttons and discuss their history, types, and valuable tips for building your own collection.

The History of Vintage Buttons

Buttons have been used by humans for thousands of years. Dating back to ancient civilizations, buttons were initially made of materials such as bone, seashells, and even wood. It wasn’t until the 14th century that buttons made from precious metals and gemstones became popular amongst the nobility. As manufacturing techniques advanced, buttons made from metals like brass and steel became more accessible to the general population.

The 18th and 19th centuries are considered the golden age of button manufacturing, with many factories producing exquisite buttons. The rise of the garment industry during the Industrial Revolution further increased the demand for buttons, both as functional closures and decorative accents. Notable button manufacturers from this era include Minton, Wedgwood, Boulton, and J. G. Graves.

Types of Vintage Buttons

Vintage buttons come in a wide variety of styles, shapes, and materials. Each type of button tells a unique story about the era it was produced in. Some popular types of vintage buttons include:

  1. Shell Buttons: Made from seashells, these buttons were commonly used in the late 18th and early 19th centuries. They often have intricate carvings and delicate detailing.

  2. Glass Buttons: Glass buttons gained popularity in the late 18th century and remained fashionable throughout the 19th century. They can be plain or hand-painted with intricate designs, adding a touch of elegance to any garment.

  3. Metal Buttons: Metal buttons are highly durable and can be made from various metals such as brass, steel, and copper. They come in a wide range of designs, from simple to ornate, and were prevalent during the early 20th century.

  4. Celluloid Buttons: First introduced in the late 19th century, celluloid buttons became the first successful plastic buttons. They are lightweight, colorful, and often feature intricate molded designs.

  5. Bakelite Buttons: Popular in the 1920s and ’30s, bakelite buttons are made from a type of early plastic. These buttons often have unique shapes and vibrant colors, making them highly sought after by collectors.

Building Your Vintage Button Collection

Starting a vintage button collection can be an exciting journey. Here are some valuable tips to get started:

1. Research and Learn

Take the time to study and familiarize yourself with the different styles and materials used in vintage buttons. Join online communities, read books, and attend button collector gatherings or events to expand your knowledge. Understanding the history and value of buttons will help you make informed purchasing decisions.

2. Begin with a Theme

Consider starting your collection with a specific theme or focus. It could be based on a particular era, material, or design. Having a theme will help narrow down your choices and make your collection more cohesive.

3. Explore Various Sources

Vintage buttons can be found in a variety of places, including antique shops, flea markets, online auctions, and even in your own family’s heirlooms. Don’t limit yourself to one source; explore different avenues to find unique and rare buttons.

4. Quality Over Quantity

Collecting vintage buttons is not about accumulating as many as possible, but rather about finding high-quality, unique pieces. Focus on acquiring buttons that are in good condition, have interesting designs, and are made from quality materials.

5. Create Displays and Documentations

Display your vintage button collection proudly! Consider creating shadow boxes, framing individual buttons, or organizing them in a visually appealing way. Additionally, ensure that you document information about each button, including its origin, materials, and any historical significance.

The Value of Vintage Buttons

Apart from their aesthetic appeal, vintage buttons can hold significant value. Rare and highly sought-after buttons can appreciate in worth over time. Moreover, vintage buttons are not just limited to clothing; they are often used in jewelry-making, crafting, and even as decorative elements on home décor items. So, even if you’re not an avid collector, investing in a few vintage buttons can add a touch of history and uniqueness to your personal style.

Conclusion

Collecting vintage buttons is a hobby that offers endless possibilities for exploration, creativity, and learning. Each button tells a story and represents a piece of history that goes beyond its practical use. Whether you appreciate the intricate designs, historical significance, or simply enjoy the thrill of the hunt, vintage buttons truly are a world of small treasures waiting to be discovered. So, start your collection today and embark on a journey that will bring both joy and appreciation for these fascinating objects of the past.
