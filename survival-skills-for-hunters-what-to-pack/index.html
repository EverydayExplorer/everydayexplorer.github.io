Survival Skills for Hunters: What to Pack


Hunting can be an exciting and rewarding experience, but it also comes with its own set of challenges. There are several essential items that every hunter should pack to ensure their safety and well-being in the great outdoors. In this blog post, we will discuss the top survival skills for hunters and what to pack for a successful hunt.

1. Navigation Tools

When venturing into unfamiliar territory, it is crucial to have reliable navigation tools. A GPS device or compass can help you find your way and prevent you from getting lost. Additionally, a topographic map of the area can provide valuable information about the terrain and potential landmarks. Make sure to familiarize yourself with these tools before heading out on your hunting trip.

2. First Aid Kit

Accidents can happen, even to the most experienced hunters. Having a first aid kit on hand can make a difference in a medical emergency. The kit should include essentials such as bandages, antiseptic wipes, pain relievers, a thermometer, and any necessary prescription medication. Familiarize yourself with basic first aid procedures, such as how to treat cuts, sprains, and insect bites.

3. Water and Food

Staying hydrated is vital when spending extended periods in the wilderness. Pack enough water to last the duration of your hunt, and consider carrying water purification tablets or a portable water filter in case you run out. As for food, opt for lightweight, easily packable options such as energy bars, trail mix, and dehydrated meals. Ensure that your food supply is sufficient for the duration of your trip.

4. Fire-Making Tools

Fire is essential for staying warm, cooking food, and signaling for help if needed. It is crucial to pack fire-making tools such as waterproof matches or a lighter. Additionally, having some dry kindling and fire starters can greatly assist you in starting a fire even in wet conditions. Make sure to practice fire-building skills before your hunting trip to ensure you can successfully start a fire when needed.

5. Shelter and Sleeping Equipment

If your hunting trip involves overnight stays, having suitable shelter and sleeping equipment is imperative. A lightweight tent or tarp can protect you from the elements and provide a comfortable place to rest. Remember to pack a sleeping bag, sleeping pad, and a pillow for a good night’s sleep. Consider the weather conditions and terrain of your hunting location and choose appropriate gear accordingly.

6. Emergency Signaling Devices

In case of an emergency, it’s important to have a way to signal for help. Pack emergency signaling devices such as a whistle, a signal mirror, or a flare gun. These tools can attract attention from rescuers and increase your chances of being found. Familiarize yourself with the correct usage of these devices before your hunting trip to ensure you can effectively use them if needed.

7. Extra Clothing and Protective Gear

Weather conditions can change rapidly in the wilderness, so it is crucial to pack extra clothing and protective gear. Ensure that you have a durable, waterproof jacket, warm layers, and appropriate footwear for the terrain. Packing a hat, gloves, and sunglasses can provide added protection from the elements. It’s always better to have more options for clothing than to risk exposure to harsh weather conditions.

8. Hunting Equipment and Supplies

Of course, no hunting trip is complete without essential hunting equipment and supplies. This includes your preferred firearm or bow, ammunition, hunting knives, game bags, and a field dressing kit for processing the game. Make sure that all your equipment is in working order and properly maintained before your trip. Taking care of your hunting gear can prevent potential malfunctions in the field.

9. Insect Repellent and Sun Protection

Don’t forget to protect yourself from bugs and the sun’s harmful rays. Pack insect repellent to ward off mosquitoes and ticks, which can carry diseases. Additionally, bring along sunscreen with a high SPF to protect your skin from sunburn and long-term damage. Apply these products regularly to ensure maximum effectiveness throughout your hunting trip.

10. Personal Items and Documents

Lastly, don’t forget to pack personal items and important documents. This includes your hunting license, identification, and any necessary permits. It’s also a good idea to bring a small amount of cash, a durable multi-tool, a reliable flashlight with spare batteries, and a fully charged cell phone for emergency communication. These items can prove invaluable in various situations during your hunting adventure.

By packing these essential items, you will be well-prepared for any situation that may arise during your hunting trip. Remember to thoroughly plan your trip, inform someone of your itinerary, and always prioritize safety. Happy hunting and stay safe in the great outdoors!
