The ABCs of Wine: Anything But Chardonnay/Cabernet


When it comes to wine, most people often associate it with Chardonnay and Cabernet Sauvignon. While these two varietals are undeniably popular, there is a whole world of wine out there waiting to be explored. Today, we’re going to dive into the ABCs of Wine, focusing on anything but Chardonnay and Cabernet.

A - Albariño: Let’s start with an exciting white grape varietal - Albariño. Hailing from the Rías Baixas region in Spain, Albariño wines are known for their crisp acidity, vibrant flavors of citrus, and stunning floral aromas. Perfect for seafood lovers, Albariño pairs beautifully with fresh oysters and grilled shrimp.

B - Barbera: Moving on to the reds, Barbera is an Italian grape variety that produces wines with bold flavors and robust acidity. Hailing primarily from the Piedmont region, Barbera wines often boast rich cherry and plum flavors, balanced by earthy undertones. With its versatility, Barbera can be enjoyed both on its own or paired with a variety of dishes, such as pasta, pizza, or grilled meats.

C - Chenin Blanc: Originating from the Loire Valley in France, Chenin Blanc is a white grape varietal that offers an incredible range of styles. From dry to sweet, still to sparkling, Chenin Blanc showcases flavors of crisp green apple, honey, and vibrant acidity. Whether it’s a bone-dry Vouvray or a luscious dessert wine, Chenin Blanc is a versatile choice for any occasion.

D - Dolcetto: Another Italian gem, Dolcetto is a red grape varietal hailing from Piedmont. Known for producing approachable and fruity wines, Dolcetto showcases flavors of blackberry, black cherry, and hints of spice. With its soft tannins and lively acidity, Dolcetto pairs excellently with a variety of foods, from pizza to charcuterie.

E - Assyrtiko: Let’s travel to Greece now and discover Assyrtiko, an indigenous white grape variety from Santorini. Assyrtiko wines are famed for their mineral-driven profiles, vibrant citrus flavors, and a refreshing saline finish. This grape can handle heat and produces wines that pair superbly with seafood and Mediterranean cuisine.

F - Gamay: Gamay, originating from the Beaujolais region in France, is a red grape varietal that produces light and fruity wines. With its vibrant acidity and flavors of red fruits like strawberry and raspberry, Gamay is a great alternative to heavier red wines. Chilled slightly, it is a delightful wine to enjoy on warm summer evenings.

G - Grüner Veltliner: Grüner Veltliner is the signature white grape of Austria, offering a range of styles from light and simple to complex and age-worthy. Flavors of green apple, white pepper, and refreshing acidity make Grüner Veltliner a versatile and food-friendly choice. It pairs exceptionally well with Austrian cuisine, as well as Asian dishes.

H - Grenache: Grenache, sometimes referred to as Garnacha, is a red grape varietal that is grown in various regions around the world. Spain, France, and Australia are known for producing exceptional Grenache wines. With its bold red fruit flavors, hints of spice, and silky tannins, Grenache is a fantastic choice to enjoy with grilled meats, stews, or even chocolate desserts.

I - Ice Wine: While not a specific grape varietal, ice wine deserves a place on this list due to its uniqueness. It is made from grapes that are left on the vine until they freeze, resulting in concentrated flavors and high levels of sweetness. Ice wines are a perfect indulgence on their own or paired with fruit-based desserts.

J - Jacquère: Found primarily in the Savoie region of France, Jacquère is a white grape varietal that produces light and refreshing wines. With its delicate flavors of green apple, lemon, and a hint of minerality, Jacquère wines are perfect for warm summer afternoons on the patio or paired with light seafood dishes.

K - Kerner: Kerner is a white grape varietal that originated in Germany and is a cross between Riesling and Trollinger. This aromatic grape produces wines with bright citrus flavors, floral aromas, and crisp acidity. Kerner can be enjoyed on its own or paired with dishes like roasted chicken or spicy Asian cuisine.

L - Malbec: While much less known than its cousin Cabernet Sauvignon, Malbec is a red grape varietal that has gained popularity in recent years, especially from Argentina. With its dark fruit flavors, velvety tannins, and hints of chocolate and spice, Malbec is a versatile wine that pairs exceptionally well with grilled meats and hearty dishes.

M - Moscato: Closing our list with something sweet, Moscato is a white grape varietal known for producing aromatic and light-bodied wines with a refreshing sweetness. Flavors of orange blossom, peaches, and a slight effervescence make Moscato a delightful dessert wine or a refreshing choice for a brunch gathering.

N - Nerello Mascalese: From the volcanic soils of Mount Etna in Sicily, Nerello Mascalese is a red grape varietal that showcases flavors of red berries, Mediterranean herbs, and a distinctive mineral character. With its medium body and fine tannins, Nerello Mascalese pairs well with grilled meats, roasted vegetables, and traditional Italian cuisine.

O - Orvieto: Orvieto is a white wine from the Umbria region of Italy. It is made primarily from Grechetto and Trebbiano grapes, offering crisp acidity, flavors of citrus and pear, and a hint of minerality. Orvieto is a versatile wine that can be enjoyed on its own or paired with a variety of dishes, from seafood to creamy pasta.

P - Pinotage: Originating from South Africa, Pinotage is a red grape varietal that is a cross between Pinot Noir and Cinsault. Known for its bold flavors of dark berries, plums, and hints of smoke, Pinotage is often aged in oak barrels, adding layers of complexity to the wine. It pairs well with grilled meats, spicy dishes, and even chocolate desserts.

Q - Queen of the Rosés: Rosé wines have become increasingly popular over the years, and rightfully so. With their vibrant pink hues, refreshing acidity, and fruity flavors, rosés are perfect for warm summer days or as an aperitif. From Provence to California, there is a stunning array of rosé wines to explore and enjoy.

R - Rioja: Rioja is a Spanish wine region renowned for its red wines made primarily from Tempranillo grapes. Rioja wines often exhibit flavors of ripe red fruits, vanilla, and a hint of spice. With its medium body and velvety tannins, Rioja wines are a fantastic choice for pairing with a variety of dishes, from roasted lamb to aged cheeses.

S - Sauvignon Blanc: While we said we would focus on anything but Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc deserves a mention for its vibrant and zesty character. With its crisp acidity, flavors of citrus, tropical fruits, and distinctive grassy notes, Sauvignon Blanc is a refreshing choice for warm weather and pairs beautifully with seafood, salads, and fresh goat cheese.

T - Tempranillo: Tempranillo is the queen of red grape varietals in Spain, and it is primarily cultivated in the Rioja and Ribera del Duero regions. With its flavors of black fruits, tobacco, and vanilla, Tempranillo wines are often aged in oak barrels, resulting in added complexity and a smooth mouthfeel. This versatile wine pairs well with grilled meats, Spanish tapas, and even paella.

U - Ugni Blanc: Also known as Trebbiano, Ugni Blanc is a white grape varietal that is widely grown in France and Italy. It is known for its high yields and crisp acidity, making it a popular choice for making brandy and white wine blends. Ugni Blanc wines offer flavors of green apple, lemon zest, and a refreshing finish.

V - Vermentino: Hailing from Italy and other Mediterranean regions, Vermentino is a white grape varietal that thrives in warm climates. Vermentino wines exhibit flavors of citrus, green apple, and a touch of minerality. With its bright acidity and refreshing nature, Vermentino is an excellent choice for pairing with seafood, salads, and light vegetarian dishes.

W - Gewürztraminer: Gewürztraminer is a white grape varietal that produces aromatic wines with intense floral aromas, lychee, and spicy flavors. Originally from the Alsace region in France, Gewürztraminer is now cultivated in various parts of the world. This full-bodied and slightly sweet wine pairs beautifully with spicy dishes, Asian cuisine, and fruit-based desserts.

X - Xarel·lo: Xarel·lo is a white grape varietal native to Catalonia, Spain, primarily used in the production of Cava, the Spanish sparkling wine. With its flavors of green apple, citrus, and delicate floral notes, Xarel·lo adds freshness and complexity to the blend. In addition to Cava, Xarel·lo is also used to produce still white wines that pair well with seafood and lighter dishes.

Y - Yellow Muscat: Yellow Muscat, also known as Muscat Blanc à Petits Grains, is one of the oldest grape varietals in the world. This white grape is famous for its pronounced floral aromas, lychee, and orange blossom flavors. With its vibrant acidity and sweetness, Yellow Muscat is an indulgent choice for dessert or as an aperitif wine.

Z - Zinfandel: Last but not least, Zinfandel is a red grape varietal that is widely grown in California. Zinfandel wines are known for their ripe berry flavors, hints of spice, and often have higher alcohol content. This bold and fruity wine pairs well with grilled meats, hearty pizzas, or cheese platters.

In conclusion, the world of wine is vast and full of treasures beyond Chardonnay and Cabernet Sauvignon. From Albariño to Zinfandel, there are countless grape varieties to explore and enjoy. So, the next time you’re in the wine aisle, be adventurous and try something new. Who knows, you might just discover your new favorite wine. Cheers to the ABCs of Wine, Anything But Chardonnay/Cabernet!
