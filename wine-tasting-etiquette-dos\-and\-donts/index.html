Wine Tasting Etiquette: Dos\ and\ Donts


Wine tasting is a sensory journey that allows us to appreciate the complexities of different wines. Whether you are a novice or a seasoned wine connoisseur, it is important to understand the etiquette that accompanies this refined activity. In this blog post, we will explore the do’s and don’ts of wine tasting etiquette to help you make the most of your wine tasting experience.

Do: Dress Appropriately

When attending a wine tasting event, it is essential to dress appropriately. While you don’t have to be in formal attire, it is advisable to choose smart-casual clothing. Avoid strong fragrances, as they can interfere with both your own sensory experience and that of others around you.

Don’t: Be Late

Punctuality is key when attending a wine tasting event. Arriving late disrupts the flow of the event and may cause you to miss out on important information or tastings. Be considerate of others and make sure to arrive on time or even a few minutes early.

Do: Pace Yourself

Wine tasting is all about savoring and appreciating the flavors and characteristics of each wine. To fully enjoy the experience, it is crucial to pace yourself. Take small sips and allow the wine to linger on your palate, identifying the various flavors and aromas. Drinking too quickly can dull your senses and prevent you from fully appreciating the intricacies of each wine.

Don’t: Over-Indulge

While wine tasting is an enjoyable activity, it is important to remember that the focus should be on tasting, not consuming excessive amounts of wine. Over-indulging not only clouds your judgment but can also be disrespectful to the hosts and other participants. Limit your consumption to small sips and make sure to drink water and eat some food to cleanse your palate between tastings.

Do: Use Proper Glassware

Using the correct glassware can enhance your wine tasting experience. Opt for a tulip-shaped glass with a stem, as this allows for swirling without warming the wine with your hands. The shape of the glass helps concentrate the aromas, enabling you to better appreciate the wine’s bouquet.

Don’t: Swirl Excessively

Swirling the wine gently in your glass is a common technique used to release its aromas. However, excessive swirling can be considered rude, as it may splash the wine and potentially disturb others nearby. Use a moderate swirling motion to capture the wine’s essence without creating a spectacle.

Do: Ask Questions

Wine tasting events often provide an opportunity to learn from experts in the field. Don’t hesitate to ask questions about the wines being tasted, the regions they come from, or any other relevant information. Winemakers and sommeliers are generally enthusiastic about sharing their knowledge, so take advantage of the opportunity to expand your wine education.

Don’t: Dominate the Conversation

While it’s good to ask questions and engage in conversation during a wine tasting, it is important to be mindful of others. Avoid monopolizing the conversation or interrupting others when they are speaking. Allow everyone to share their opinions and experiences, fostering a respectful and inclusive environment.

Do: Spit or Dump Wine (if available)

In professional wine tastings, it is common to have a spit bucket or dump bucket available. These are provided to allow participants to taste multiple wines without becoming intoxicated. If you feel the need to spit or discard wine, it is perfectly acceptable to do so. Simply hold the wine in your mouth for a moment to sense its flavors and then discreetly spit it into the provided vessel.

Don’t: Be Afraid to Express Your Opinion

Wine tasting is subjective, and everyone’s palate is different. Embrace your own taste preferences and don’t be afraid to express your opinion. Whether you love a particular wine or find it less appealing, share your thoughts respectfully and constructively. Remember that taste is personal, and diversity of opinions makes for interesting discussions.

Final Thoughts

When it comes to wine tasting, knowing the do’s and don’ts of etiquette can enhance your experience and ensure that you are respectful to other participants and hosts. By dressing appropriately, being punctual, pacing yourself, asking questions, and expressing your opinion, you contribute to a positive and enjoyable wine tasting event. So, the next time you attend a wine tasting, keep these tips in mind and savor the journey of discovering new wines. Cheers!
