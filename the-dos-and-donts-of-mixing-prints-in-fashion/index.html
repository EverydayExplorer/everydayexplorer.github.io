The Dos and Don'ts of Mixing Prints in Fashion


In the ever-evolving world of fashion, one trend that continues to captivate and inspire is the art of mixing prints. This bold and daring style choice allows individuals to express their unique personalities and create eye-catching outfits. However, mastering the art of print mixing can be a daunting task. To help you navigate this fashion frontier, we have compiled a comprehensive list of dos and don’ts that will guide you towards print mixing success.

DO: Start with Similar Colors

One of the key principles of print mixing is to begin with a cohesive color palette. By selecting prints that share similar hues, you create a harmonious foundation for your outfit. For instance, you can pair a floral top with a striped skirt if they both feature shades of blue and green. This approach ensures that your prints complement each other rather than clash.

DON’T: Overwhelm with Busy Patterns

While print mixing is all about bold choices, it’s important not to go overboard. Avoid combining multiple busy patterns that compete for attention. Instead, opt for one statement piece and balance it with more subtle prints. For example, pair a vibrant leopard print blouse with solid-colored pants and accessorize with a striped scarf. This way, you create visual interest without overwhelming the eye.

DO: Experiment with Scale

Playing with the scale of prints is a fantastic way to add depth and dimension to your outfit. Pair a larger, bolder print with a smaller, more intricate one to create a visually appealing contrast. For instance, a polka dot blouse can be effortlessly paired with a floral skirt, allowing the different scales to complement each other.

DON’T: Ignore Proportions

When it comes to mixing prints, it’s crucial to consider the proportions of each print. If you’re on the petite side, opt for smaller prints to avoid overwhelming your frame. Conversely, if you have a taller or curvier figure, you can experiment with larger prints that can balance your proportions. Remember, the goal is to create a harmonious and flattering silhouette.

DO: Use Neutral Anchors

Neutral prints, such as stripes or animal prints, can act as a bridge between contrasting patterns. Incorporating these versatile prints into your outfit can help anchor the different prints together, creating a cohesive look. For example, pair a leopard print jacket with a floral dress, and add a striped belt to tie everything together.

DON’T: Forget about Texture

In addition to prints, texture can also play a significant role in creating a visually stunning outfit. Mixing different textures, such as lace, velvet, or sequins, can add an extra layer of interest to your ensemble. Combine a textured top with a printed skirt to create a dynamic and captivating look.

DO: Accessorize Wisely

Accessories can make or break a print mixing outfit. Opt for simple and understated accessories that won’t overpower your prints. A solid-colored handbag or a pair of neutral shoes can complement your ensemble without taking away from the statement you’ve created with your prints.

In conclusion, mastering the art of mixing prints in fashion requires a delicate balance of creativity and restraint. By following these dos and don’ts, you can confidently embrace this trend and create fashion-forward outfits that showcase your personal style. Remember, fashion is all about self-expression, so don’t be afraid to push the boundaries and have fun with your print mixing adventures!
