The Safest Cars of 2021: Top Picks for Crash Tests


When it comes to buying a new car, safety is always a top priority. We all want to feel secure and protected while on the road, especially considering the increasing number of car accidents each year. Fortunately, automobile manufacturers have made significant advancements in safety technology, resulting in the production of safer cars than ever before.

In this article, we will take a closer look at the safest cars of 2021 based on crash tests conducted by reputable organizations such as the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) and the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety (IIHS). These organizations rigorously test new car models to assess their crashworthiness and safety features.

The Importance of Crash Tests

Crash tests are essential for evaluating a car’s ability to protect its occupants in the event of a collision. They provide valuable information about the structural integrity of the vehicle, the effectiveness of safety features, and their ability to mitigate injuries during a crash.

The NHTSA and IIHS both conduct crash tests, but they use different methodologies. The NHTSA’s tests include frontal crash, side crash, and rollover tests, whereas the IIHS conducts frontal offset, side, roof strength, and head restraint tests.

Now, let’s delve into some of the top picks for crash tests in 2021, based on the safety ratings provided by these organizations.

1. Volvo XC90

Volvo has been at the forefront of automotive safety for many years, and the XC90 is no exception. It has consistently impressed both the NHTSA and IIHS with its exceptional crash test ratings. The XC90 boasts a sturdy construction, a comprehensive suite of safety features, and advanced driver assistance systems that make it one of the safest SUVs on the market. Its impressive crash avoidance and mitigation technologies, such as forward collision warning and automatic emergency braking, contribute to its top safety ratings.

2. Honda Accord

The Honda Accord has earned top marks from both the NHTSA and IIHS. Known for its reliability and practicality, the Accord also excels in terms of safety. It received high scores in various crash tests, including the rigorous IIHS small overlap front crash test. The Accord comes equipped with Honda Sensing, a suite of safety technologies that includes features such as collision mitigation braking, lane keeping assist, adaptive cruise control, and road departure mitigation.

3. Toyota Camry

The Toyota Camry is a perennial best-seller and for good reason. In addition to its reputation for reliability and fuel efficiency, the Camry also boasts excellent safety ratings. It has consistently performed well in crash tests conducted by the NHTSA and IIHS. The Camry comes equipped with Toyota Safety Sense, a suite of advanced safety features that includes pre-collision warning, automatic emergency braking, lane departure alert, and dynamic radar cruise control.

4. Subaru Outback

Subaru has a longstanding commitment to safety, and the Outback is a shining example of that commitment. This rugged and versatile SUV has garnered top safety ratings from both the NHTSA and IIHS. The Outback features Subaru’s EyeSight driver assistance system, which includes features like pre-collision braking, lane keep assist, and adaptive cruise control. Its strong structural design, advanced airbag system, and available driver assistance features make it an excellent choice for safety-conscious buyers.

5. Mazda CX-5

Mazda is known for producing cars that are not only stylish and fun to drive but also safe. The Mazda CX-5 is a compact SUV that has consistently achieved high safety ratings. It received a Top Safety Pick+ designation from the IIHS, the highest rating possible. The CX-5 offers a range of safety features, including advanced front airbags, blind-spot monitoring, rear cross-traffic alert, and lane departure warning. Its robust construction and innovative safety technologies contribute to its impressive crash test performance.

Conclusion

When shopping for a new car, it’s crucial to prioritize safety. The safest cars of 2021, as determined by crash tests conducted by the NHTSA and IIHS, offer a combination of strong structural design, advanced safety features, and crash avoidance technologies. The Volvo XC90, Honda Accord, Toyota Camry, Subaru Outback, and Mazda CX-5 are just a few examples of the safest cars available in the market today. Remember to consider these safety ratings and features when making your next car purchase, as they can provide you with peace of mind and protect you and your loved ones on the road.
