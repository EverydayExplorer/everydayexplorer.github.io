Top 7 Survival Knots Every Adventurer Should Know


In the world of outdoor adventure, survival skills are just as crucial as your ability to navigate through the wilderness or build a fire. Among these skills, knowing how to tie the right knots can make a significant difference in your safety and success. Whether you’re camping, hiking, climbing, or engaging in any outdoor activity, mastering these essential knots will equip you to handle various situations that may arise. Here’s a comprehensive guide to the top seven survival knots every adventurer should know.

1. Bowline Knot

The bowline knot is often referred to as the “king of knots” due to its versatility and strength. This knot creates a fixed loop at the end of a rope, making it ideal for rescue operations, mooring boats, or securing gear. One of the standout features of the bowline is that it does not slip or bind under load, allowing for easy untie even after bearing weight.

How to Tie a Bowline Knot:

  1. Create a small loop in the rope, leaving a long tail.
  2. Pass the working end of the rope through the loop from underneath.
  3. Bring the working end behind the standing part.
  4. Thread the working end back down through the loop.
  5. Pull both the standing part and the tail to tighten.

Uses:

  • Securing a boat to a dock
  • Creating a loop for rescue
  • Attaching gear to a harness

2. Double Fisherman’s Knot

The double fisherman’s knot is essential for joining two lengths of rope securely. It’s particularly useful in climbing and fishing, where you often need to connect two ropes to make a longer line. This knot is known for its reliability and strength, making it a trusted choice among outdoor enthusiasts.

How to Tie a Double Fisherman’s Knot:

  1. Lay the two ropes parallel to each other.
  2. Take the working end of one rope and wrap it around the other rope twice.
  3. Pass the working end through the loops created.
  4. Pull tight to secure the first half of the knot.
  5. Repeat the process with the second rope.
  6. Slide the two knots together to form a secure connection.

Uses:

  • Joining two climbing ropes
  • Connecting fishing lines
  • Creating a longer paracord

3. Clove Hitch

The clove hitch is a quick and easy knot used for securing a rope to a pole or post. It’s often used in camping for securing tents or tarps. While it’s simple to tie, the clove hitch can slip if not under constant tension, making it best for temporary applications.

How to Tie a Clove Hitch:

  1. Wrap the rope around the object (like a pole) once.
  2. Wrap the rope around the object a second time in the opposite direction.
  3. Tuck the working end under the last wrap.
  4. Pull tight to secure.

Uses:

  • Securing tents
  • Attaching a rope to a tree
  • Creating a temporary anchor point

4. Figure Eight Knot

The figure eight knot is a fundamental knot for climbers and adventurers alike. It creates a strong and secure loop that can be used for various applications, including belaying and rappelling. The figure eight is also easy to inspect, making it a favorite among safety-conscious climbers.

How to Tie a Figure Eight Knot:

  1. Make a loop in the rope, leaving a long tail.
  2. Pass the tail end through the loop.
  3. Wrap the tail around the standing part.
  4. Bring the tail back down through the loop.
  5. Pull both ends to tighten.

Uses:

  • Creating a loop for climbing
  • Belaying and rappelling
  • Connecting two ropes

5. Taut-Line Hitch

The taut-line hitch is a fantastic knot for creating adjustable loops in a rope. This knot is particularly useful for tensioning a tarpaulin or a tent. It can slide along the rope to adjust the tension but holds firm under load, making it essential for any adventurer.

How to Tie a Taut-Line Hitch:

  1. Wrap the rope around the standing part.
  2. Pass the working end through the loop you just created.
  3. Wrap the working end around the standing part a second time.
  4. Pass the working end back through the loop.
  5. Pull the standing part to slide the knot, and pull the working end to tighten.

Uses:

  • Securing tents
  • Tensioning tarps
  • Creating adjustable lines

6. Prusik Knot

The prusik knot is a crucial knot for climbers and those who work in high places. It’s a type of friction hitch that allows you to ascend or descend a rope safely. The prusik knot grips the rope when weight is applied but slides freely when not under load, making it highly useful in rescue situations.

How to Tie a Prusik Knot:

  1. Take a loop of rope (the prusik) and place it under the main line.
  2. Wrap it around the main line three times.
  3. Pass the prusik loop through itself.
  4. Pull to tighten and adjust as needed.

Uses:

  • Ascending and descending climbing ropes
  • Creating a safety backup during rappelling
  • Rescue operations

7. Sheet Bend

The sheet bend is a reliable knot for joining two ropes of different diameters. It’s particularly useful in sailing and fishing, where you may need to connect ropes of varying thicknesses. The sheet bend is easy to tie and untie, making it a practical choice for quick adjustments.

How to Tie a Sheet Bend:

  1. Take the thicker rope and form a U-shape with it.
  2. Pass the thinner rope behind the thicker rope.
  3. Bring the end of the thinner rope over the thicker rope and tuck it under the U.
  4. Pull the thinner rope to tighten.

Uses:

  • Joining ropes of different sizes
  • Fishing line connections
  • Securing tarps

Conclusion

Mastering these seven survival knots can significantly enhance your outdoor experience and ensure your safety in various situations. Whether you’re securing gear, setting up camp, or engaging in adventurous activities, having these knots in your skill set is invaluable. Take the time to practice each knot until you can tie them confidently and quickly. Remember, in the wilderness, the ability to secure your equipment and create safe connections can be the difference between a successful adventure and a dangerous situation. So grab some rope and start practicing these essential survival knots today!
