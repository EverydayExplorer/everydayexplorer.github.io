Upcycled Garden Planters: Turn Everyday Items into Green Oasis


Have you ever looked at your old household items and wondered if there was a way to give them a new lease on life? Well, look no further because I have the perfect solution for you—upcycled garden planters. With a little bit of creativity and some DIY skills, you can transform everyday items into beautiful green oases in your garden.

Upcycling is all about repurposing items that would otherwise end up in the landfill, and what better way to do that than by turning them into planters? Not only does this help reduce waste, but it also adds a unique touch to your garden, making it a true reflection of your personality and style. Plus, upcycled planters are a great conversation starter when guests visit your outdoor space.

So, let’s dive into some creative ideas for upcycled garden planters:

1. Vintage Suitcase Planter: Dust off that old suitcase hiding in your attic and give it a new purpose as a planter. Simply line it with plastic or add drainage holes, fill it with soil, and plant your favorite flowers or herbs. The worn-out look of the suitcase will add a rustic charm to your garden.

2. Tire Planter: Instead of disposing of old tires, turn them into eye-catching planters. Paint them in vibrant colors or patterns, stack them on top of each other, and fill them with soil. You can create a tiered planter by using different sizes of tires, adding depth and visual interest to your garden.

3. Teapot Planters: If you have mismatched teapots lying around, put them to good use as quirky planters. Remove the lid, drill drainage holes at the bottom, and fill them with small succulents or colorful flowers. These whimsical planters will surely be a focal point in your garden.

4. Wooden Pallet Planters: Don’t throw away those wooden pallets from your recent renovation project. Instead, transform them into vertical planters. Attach small pots or mason jars to the pallet, fill them with soil, and hang it on a garden wall. This allows you to maximize space while creating a stunning green display.

5. Mason Jar Herb Garden: Got a collection of mason jars cluttering your kitchen? Repurpose them as herb gardens. Paint the lids with chalkboard paint for labeling, drill drainage holes at the bottom, and plant herbs like basil, rosemary, or mint. Not only will you have a convenient herb garden, but it will also add a touch of rustic charm to your outdoor space.

6. Colander Planter: That old colander that’s been replaced by a shiny new one can find new life as a planter. Line it with moss or burlap to prevent soil from falling through, fill it with potting mix, and plant colorful flowers. Hang it from a tree branch or place it on a table for a unique centerpiece.

7. Rain Boot Planters: Don’t toss out your kids’ outgrown rain boots just yet. Clean them up, drill some drainage holes, and fill them with soil. Plant cheerful annuals or trailing vines, and arrange them near the entrance of your garden or along a pathway.

These are just a few ideas to get you started on your upcycled garden planter journey. Remember, the possibilities are endless when it comes to repurposing everyday items into green oases. So, let your imagination run wild, experiment with different materials, and create a garden that is not only eco-friendly but also uniquely yours.

By incorporating upcycled garden planters, you are not only doing your part for the environment but also adding a personal touch to your outdoor space. So, take a look around your home, gather those forgotten items, and let the transformation begin. Happy upcycling!
