Wilderness Medicine: Basic Techniques for Treating Injuries and Illnesses


When venturing into the great outdoors, it’s essential to be prepared for any unforeseen circumstances. Accidents happen, and being knowledgeable in wilderness medicine can make all the difference in providing timely and effective care in remote areas. In this blog, we’ll explore some basic techniques for treating injuries and illnesses in the wilderness.

1. Assessing the Situation

The first step in any medical emergency is to assess the situation. Take a moment to ensure the scene is safe for you and the patient. Look for any hazards that may put you at risk, such as unstable terrain, wild animals, or poisonous plants.

Once you’ve determined the scene is safe, approach the patient and assess their condition. Is the patient conscious? Are they breathing? Check for severe bleeding or any obvious injuries. This initial assessment will help you prioritize your treatment.

2. Stabilizing the Patient

In wilderness medicine, stabilizing the patient is crucial to prevent further injury or illness. If the patient is conscious and breathing, reassure them and keep them calm. Focus on keeping their airway open and their spine aligned, if there’s a possibility of a neck or spinal injury.

For patients with suspected fractures or sprains, immobilize the injured area using splints or improvised materials like branches or trekking poles. This will help prevent further damage until proper medical care can be reached.

3. Managing Wounds

Wound management is an important aspect of wilderness medicine. Start by cleaning the wound with clean water or antiseptic solution if available. Apply gentle pressure to control bleeding. For deep wounds, consider using butterfly closures to close the edges of the wound and promote healing.

It’s essential to pack a well-stocked first aid kit that includes various sizes of bandages, sterile gauze, adhesive tape, antiseptic ointment, and disposable gloves. These supplies will come in handy when dealing with minor cuts, abrasions, or puncture wounds.

4. Dealing with Infections

In a wilderness setting, infections can quickly become a serious concern. If a wound shows signs of infection—such as redness, swelling, warmth, or pus—clean the area thoroughly and apply antiseptic ointment. It’s advisable to have oral antibiotics in your first aid kit, as they can be effective in treating and preventing infections, especially in more remote areas.

5. Treating Common Illnesses

Knowing how to manage common illnesses in the wilderness can save lives. Diarrhea is a prevalent problem due to contaminated water or food. Make sure to stay hydrated and carry oral rehydration salts to replace lost electrolytes. In case of severe dehydration or persistent symptoms, it’s essential to seek medical help.

Another common wilderness illness is heat stroke or heat exhaustion. To treat these conditions, move the patient to a shaded area, remove excess clothing, and cool the body with cold water or wet towels. Hydrating the patient with plenty of fluids is crucial.

6. Communication and Evacuation

In a wilderness emergency, communication is key. Always carry a fully charged mobile phone or a satellite communication device like a personal locator beacon (PLB) or satellite phone. These devices can be essential for calling for help and obtaining medical advice if available.

In more remote areas with little to no signal, it’s advisable to have a well-practiced plan for evacuating the patient safely. This could involve using signaling devices like whistle, mirror, or flares to attract attention or building a signal fire.

Conclusion

Wilderness medicine is a vital skillset for anyone who spends time in the great outdoors. Remember to always assess the situation, stabilize the patient, and manage wounds appropriately. Stay aware of common illnesses and have the necessary supplies and knowledge to treat them. Lastly, ensure you have a reliable means of communication and a well-thought-out plan for evacuation if necessary.

By being equipped with these basic techniques for treating injuries and illnesses, you can enjoy the wilderness with confidence, knowing that you’re prepared for any medical emergency that may arise. Stay safe, and happy exploring!
