How to Leave a Review and Give Feedback for Hotels


Are you a frequent traveler who loves to explore new places and stay in hotels? If so, you may understand the importance of leaving a review and giving feedback for hotels. Not only does it help other travelers make informed decisions, but it also provides valuable feedback to the hotel staff, enabling them to improve their services. In this blog post, we will discuss the best practices for leaving a review and giving feedback for hotels.

1. Be specific and detailed: When writing a review, it’s essential to be specific and provide as much detail as possible. Mention specific aspects of your stay, such as the cleanliness of the room, the friendliness of the staff, the quality of amenities, and the overall atmosphere of the hotel. By providing specific details, you can help future travelers understand what to expect from their stay.

2. Be honest and fair: Honesty is crucial when leaving a hotel review. Share your genuine experience, both positive and negative, to provide an accurate representation of your stay. However, it’s equally important to be fair and objective in your assessment. Sometimes, a particular aspect may not have met your personal preferences, but that doesn’t mean it’s a flaw in the hotel’s services.

3. Highlight exceptional service: If you encountered exceptional service during your stay, make sure to highlight it in your review. Recognizing the efforts of hotel staff who went above and beyond can not only make their day but also encourage other travelers to choose that hotel. Be specific in mentioning the names of staff members who provided outstanding service, as it adds credibility to your review.

4. Offer constructive criticism: While it’s important to highlight the positive aspects of your stay, constructive criticism can also be valuable for the hotel. If you encountered any issues during your stay, such as slow Wi-Fi, noisy rooms, or a lack of amenities, mention them in your review. However, be sure to provide suggestions on how the hotel can improve these areas. Constructive criticism helps hotels identify areas for improvement and enhances the overall guest experience.

5. Use a star rating system: Many review platforms offer a star rating system, allowing you to rate different aspects of your stay, such as cleanliness, comfort, service, and value for money. Utilize this system to provide a quick and easy summary of your overall experience. Be sure to explain the factors behind your rating in the review itself, as star ratings alone may not convey the full story.

6. Be mindful of your tone: It’s important to maintain a respectful and professional tone when leaving a review. Remember that your review will be read by others, including the hotel staff. Instead of resorting to negative or hostile language, focus on providing constructive feedback. A well-articulated and polite review is more likely to be taken seriously and considered for improvements.

7. Give credit where it’s due: If you booked your hotel through an online travel agency or a booking platform, consider leaving a review on that platform as well. These reviews help other travelers in their decision-making process and provide valuable feedback to the booking platform. By taking the time to write a review, you are contributing to a vibrant and transparent community of travelers.

By following these tips, you can leave a thoughtful and informative review that helps both future travelers and hotel management. Remember, your feedback can make a difference and contribute to the overall improvement of the hospitality industry. So the next time you have a hotel stay, take a few moments to share your experience and provide valuable feedback. Happy reviewing!
