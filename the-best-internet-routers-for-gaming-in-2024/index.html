The Best Internet Routers for Gaming in 2024


As gaming continues to evolve at a rapid pace, the importance of a robust internet connection has never been clearer. In 2024, gamers require routers that not only provide speed but also ensure reliable connectivity with minimal lag. With a plethora of options available in the market, selecting the right router can be daunting. This guide aims to simplify your decision-making process by highlighting the best internet routers for gaming in 2024.

Why a Good Router Matters for Gaming

Before diving into the top routers, itâ€™s essential to understand why having a good router is critical for gaming. A quality router can enhance your gaming experience by providing:

  • Low Latency: Reduces the time it takes for data to travel between your device and the gaming server, ensuring a smoother gaming experience.
  • High Speed: Allows for faster downloads and updates, which is crucial for online gaming.
  • Reliable Connections: Minimizes disconnections during crucial moments in gaming.
  • Better Range: Ensures that all areas of your home can enjoy strong internet connectivity, crucial for multi-player setups.

Key Features to Consider

When choosing a gaming router, focus on the following features:

  1. Speed Standards: Look for routers that support Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax) or the upcoming Wi-Fi 7 (802.11be) standards for optimal speed.
  2. Dual-Band or Tri-Band Capability: Dual-band routers operate on both the 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands, while tri-band routers add an additional 5 GHz band for even better performance.
  3. Quality of Service (QoS): This feature prioritizes gaming traffic over other types of internet usage, ensuring your gaming sessions are uninterrupted.
  4. Security Features: Built-in security protocols protect your network from potential threats.
  5. Ethernet Ports: For those who prefer wired connections, having multiple Ethernet ports is essential.

1. Position Your Router Correctly

The placement of your router can significantly impact its performance. Place it in a central location, away from walls and obstructions, to maximize coverage and strength.

2. Use Wired Connections When Possible

For the best performance, connect your gaming console or PC directly to the router using an Ethernet cable. This minimizes latency and ensures a stable connection.

3. Enable QoS Settings

Most modern routers come with QoS settings that allow you to prioritize gaming traffic. Ensure this feature is enabled to enjoy a smoother gaming experience.

4. Regularly Update Firmware
