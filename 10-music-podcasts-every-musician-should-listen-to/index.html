10 Music Podcasts Every Musician Should Listen To


As a musician, finding inspiration, learning new techniques, and staying updated with the latest trends in the music industry are essential for your growth. One of the best ways to achieve this is by listening to music podcasts. With an abundance of music podcasts available, it can be overwhelming to choose the ones that best suit your interests and needs. To make your selection easier, we’ve curated a list of 10 music podcasts every musician should listen to. Whether you’re a songwriter, producer, or performer, these podcasts offer valuable insights, tips, and entertainment to fuel your musical journey.

1. Song Exploder
Hosted by musician and composer Hrishikesh Hirway, Song Exploder delves into the creative process behind some of the most iconic songs. With in-depth interviews with artists, Song Exploder provides a unique behind-the-scenes look at the making of music, offering valuable insights for songwriters and music producers.

2. The Working Songwriter
Hosted by Joe Pug, The Working Songwriter is a podcast that takes a deep dive into the world of songwriting. Featuring interviews with established and emerging songwriters, this podcast explores the craft, business, and lifestyle of being a working musician, making it a must-listen for aspiring songwriters.

3. Dissect
For musicians who appreciate the art of album-making, Dissect is a podcast that rigorously analyzes a different album each season. Hosted by Cole Cuchna, this podcast provides an in-depth, educational, and entertaining exploration of the musical and lyrical themes within albums, making it a valuable resource for music enthusiasts.

4. The Music Business Hacks
Navigating the music industry can be challenging, and The Music Business Hacks podcast, hosted by Simon Tam, offers practical advice, tips, and strategies for musicians looking to build a sustainable career in music. From marketing to legal matters, this podcast covers a wide range of topics relevant to musicians.

5. The Soundtrack Show
For composers and music producers, The Soundtrack Show is a treasure trove of knowledge. Hosted by David W. Collins, this podcast delves into the world of film, TV, and video game music, offering deep insights into the craft of composing for visual media.

6. Switched on Pop
Hosted by musicologist Nate Sloan and songwriter Charlie Harding, Switched on Pop explores the making and meaning of popular music. Analyzing hit songs and their cultural impact, this podcast is a fascinating resource for musicians seeking to understand the intricacies of contemporary music.

7. The DIY Musician Podcast
Created by CD Baby, The DIY Musician Podcast is a valuable resource for independent musicians. Covering topics such as music promotion, distribution, and fan engagement, this podcast equips musicians with the knowledge and tools to navigate the music industry on their own terms.

8. The Working Class Audio Podcast
For music producers and audio engineers, The Working Class Audio Podcast, hosted by Matt Boudreau, provides candid conversations with industry professionals. From technical insights to career advice, this podcast offers a comprehensive look into the world of audio production.

9. The Music Production Podcast
Hosted by Brian Funk, The Music Production Podcast offers practical tips, techniques, and inspiration for music producers. From workflow strategies to sound design, this podcast is a valuable resource for producers looking to refine their craft and expand their creative horizons.

10. The Musician’s Guide to Hustling
Hosted by Ari Herstand, The Musician’s Guide to Hustling is a podcast that offers actionable advice for independent musicians. Covering topics such as touring, live performance, and music entrepreneurship, this podcast empowers musicians to take control of their careers and achieve success on their own terms.

In conclusion, these 10 music podcasts cater to a wide range of musical interests and are invaluable resources for musicians looking to expand their knowledge, refine their skills, and navigate the complexities of the music industry. By incorporating these podcasts into your listening routine, you can gain valuable insights, stay inspired, and take significant strides in your musical journey. Whether you’re a songwriter, producer, performer, or music enthusiast, these podcasts offer a wealth of information and entertainment to enrich your musical experience.
