How to Make Homemade Sourdough Bread from Scratch


Are you tired of store-bought bread that lacks flavor and texture? Do you want to try something different and impress your family and friends with your baking skills? Then it’s time to make homemade sourdough bread from scratch!

Sourdough bread is known for its tangy flavor and chewy texture. Making it at home may seem intimidating, but with a little patience and practice, you can become a master bread baker in no time. Not only is homemade sourdough bread delicious, but it’s also a healthier alternative to store-bought bread as it undergoes a natural fermentation process.

In this blog post, we will guide you through the step-by-step process of making homemade sourdough bread from scratch. Let’s get started!

Step 1: Create and Feed Your Sourdough Starter

To make sourdough bread, you’ll need to create and maintain a sourdough starter. A sourdough starter is a mixture of flour and water that becomes naturally fermented by wild yeast and beneficial bacteria.

To create a sourdough starter, mix equal parts of flour and water in a jar. For example, you can start with 100 grams of flour and 100 grams of water. Stir well until the mixture is thoroughly blended. Leave the jar loosely covered with a clean cloth and let it sit at room temperature for 24 hours. This allows wild yeast and bacteria from the environment to colonize the mixture.

The next day, you’ll need to ‘feed’ your sourdough starter. Discard about half of the mixture and add equal parts flour and water. For instance, if you have 100 grams of starter, remove 50 grams and add 50 grams of flour and water. Stir well and let it sit for another 24 hours. Repeat this feeding process every day until your starter becomes active and doubles in size within a few hours of feeding.

Step 2: Mixing the Dough

Once your sourdough starter is active, it’s time to mix the dough. In a large mixing bowl, combine 500 grams of flour, 300 grams of water, and 100 grams of sourdough starter. Mix well until all the ingredients are incorporated.

Cover the bowl with a clean cloth and let the dough rest for 30 minutes. This resting period, known as autolyse, allows the flour to absorb the water, resulting in better gluten development and a more elastic dough.

After 30 minutes, sprinkle 10 grams of salt over the dough and mix it in thoroughly. Knead the dough for about 10-15 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. You can perform a windowpane test to check if the gluten has developed properly. Take a small piece of dough and stretch it gently. If it becomes thin and translucent without tearing, you’re good to go!

Step 3: Bulk Fermentation

After kneading, it’s time for the bulk fermentation stage. Place the dough in a clean, lightly oiled bowl and cover it with a clean cloth. Let it sit at room temperature for about 4-6 hours or until it doubles in size. The duration of the fermentation process depends on the ambient temperature and the strength of your starter.

During bulk fermentation, the wild yeast in the starter will consume the carbohydrates in the dough, producing carbon dioxide. This creates air pockets and gives the dough its characteristic airy texture.

Step 4: Shaping and Final Proofing

Once the dough has doubled in size, it’s time to shape it. Gently deflate the dough by folding it a few times, then shape it into a round loaf or any desired shape. Place the shaped dough onto a well-floured surface or a proofing basket, seam side up.

Cover the shaped dough with a clean cloth and let it proof for another 1-2 hours. This final proofing allows the dough to relax and rise slightly, resulting in a lighter and more tender crumb.

Once the oven is hot, carefully transfer the proofed dough into the preheated Dutch oven or onto the baking stone. If using a Dutch oven, remove the lid and bake for 20 minutes. Then, reduce the heat to 400째F (205째C) and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes with the lid off, or until the bread develops a deep golden brown crust.

If using a baking stone, slide the proofed dough directly onto the stone and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Step 6: Cool and Enjoy!

Once the bread is baked, remove it from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 1 hour before slicing. This cooling period allows the bread to set and develop its final texture. If you slice it too soon, the bread may become gummy.

Finally, slice a piece of your homemade sourdough bread, spread some butter, and enjoy the fruits of your labor! The tangy flavor and chewy texture will make you wonder why you ever settled for store-bought bread.

Remember, making homemade sourdough bread requires practice and patience, so don’t be discouraged if your first few loaves don’t turn out perfect. Keep experimenting, refining your technique, and soon you’ll become a sourdough pro!

Start your sourdough journey today and experience the joy of baking your own delicious and nutritious bread from scratch. Your taste buds will thank you!
