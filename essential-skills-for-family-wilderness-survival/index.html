Essential Skills for Family Wilderness Survival


When venturing into the great outdoors with your family, it’s essential to be prepared for any situation that may arise. Whether you’re planning a camping trip or embarking on a hiking adventure, having the right skills can make all the difference in ensuring a safe and enjoyable experience. In this blog post, we will explore some essential skills for family wilderness survival.

1. Navigation: Knowing how to read a map and use a compass is crucial when exploring unfamiliar terrain. Teach your family members how to navigate using these tools, as well as how to identify landmarks and follow trails.

2. Shelter Building: In the wilderness, having a shelter can protect you from the elements and provide a safe haven. Teach your family how to construct a basic shelter using natural materials such as branches, leaves, and rocks. This skill can come in handy during unexpected weather changes or emergencies.

3. Fire Starting: Fire is not only a source of warmth but also a means to cook food and signal for help. Teach your family members different fire-starting techniques, such as using a fire starter, flint and steel, or friction-based methods like the bow drill. Emphasize the importance of fire safety and responsible fire management.

4. Water Sourcing and Purification: Finding a clean water source is vital for survival. Teach your family how to identify potential water sources in the wilderness, such as streams or lakes, and how to purify water using methods like boiling, filtration, or chemical treatment. This knowledge can prevent dehydration and waterborne illnesses.

5. First Aid: Accidents can happen even in the wilderness. Teach your family basic first aid skills, such as treating cuts, burns, sprains, and insect bites. Additionally, ensure you have a well-stocked first aid kit with essential supplies for any potential injuries.

6. Food Foraging: Familiarize your family with edible plants and berries that can be found in the wilderness. Teach them how to identify these plants correctly and safely consume them. However, it’s crucial to emphasize the importance of proper identification to avoid consuming poisonous plants.

7. Wildlife Awareness: Teach your family how to coexist with wildlife and minimize encounters. Educate them on how to store food properly, dispose of waste responsibly, and respect the natural habitats of animals. Additionally, teach them how to react in case of an encounter with potentially dangerous wildlife.

Remember, these skills are meant to enhance your family’s safety and enjoyment in the wilderness. Practice them in controlled environments and gradually increase the level of difficulty as your family becomes more proficient. By equipping your family with these essential skills, you’ll be better prepared to handle unexpected situations and create lasting memories in the great outdoors.
