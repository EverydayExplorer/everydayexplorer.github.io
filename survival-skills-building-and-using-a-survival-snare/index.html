Survival Skills: Building and Using a Survival Snare


In today’s fast-paced and unpredictable world, having survival skills is more important than ever. Whether you find yourself in a wilderness scenario or facing a natural disaster, knowing how to build and use a survival snare can greatly increase your chances of survival. In this article, we will explore the art of constructing a survival snare and provide some useful tips on setting it up effectively.

A survival snare is a simple yet effective tool used to catch small game for food in survival situations. It consists of a looped wire or cord that is strategically placed in an area where animals are likely to pass through. When triggered, the snare tightens around the animal, trapping it until you can come and secure your catch. Here’s a step-by-step guide on building and using a survival snare:

Step 1: Choose the Right Material
To build a survival snare, you will need a strong yet flexible wire or cord. Ideally, opt for a material that is lightweight and easy to carry, such as braided fishing line or stainless steel wire. Make sure the material is durable enough to withstand the weight and struggle of the animal you are targeting.

Step 2: Determine the Location
The success of your snare largely depends on choosing the right location. Look for signs of animal activity, such as tracks, droppings, or chewed vegetation. Animals tend to follow predictable paths, so set up your snare along these routes. It’s also essential to consider the size of the animal you are targeting and adjust the height and width of your snare accordingly.

Step 3: Construct the Snare
Start by creating a loop with your chosen material. The loop should be large enough to fit around the animal’s neck or leg but small enough to tighten when triggered. Secure the ends of the loop to a tree or a sturdy anchor point, ensuring it is at the appropriate height. Be careful not to make the snare too tight, as you want the animal to be able to trigger it easily.

Step 4: Set the Trigger Mechanism
To effectively catch your target animal, you will need a reliable trigger mechanism. One popular option is the twitch-up trigger, which uses a bent sapling or branch to create tension. When the animal disturbs the bait or triggers the snare, the sapling is released, causing the snare to tighten around the animal. Experiment with different trigger mechanisms to find what works best for you.

Step 5: Bait and Camouflage
To entice the animal into the snare, use bait such as berries, nuts, or any food source that is appealing to your target species. Place the bait near the snare but not directly in it, as this might make the animal cautious. Additionally, camouflage the snare by covering it with leaves, twigs, or other natural materials to make it blend in with its surroundings.

Step 6: Regularly Check and Reset
It’s crucial to check your snare regularly to see if you’ve caught anything. Remember, the purpose of a survival snare is to provide you with food, so you must attend to it frequently. If you’ve caught an animal, approach it cautiously to avoid injury. To reset the snare, release the animal, remove the bait, and carefully reset the loop and trigger mechanism.

In conclusion, mastering the art of building and using a survival snare is a valuable skill for anyone venturing into the great outdoors. By understanding the basics of constructing a snare, choosing the right location, and employing effective trigger mechanisms, you can significantly increase your chances of capturing small game for sustenance. However, always remember to check local regulations and ethical considerations before using snares in any survival situation. Stay safe, be prepared, and never stop learning the essential survival skills that could save your life.
