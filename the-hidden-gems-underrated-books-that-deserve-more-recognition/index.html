The Hidden Gems: Underrated Books That Deserve More Recognition


When it comes to literature, we often find ourselves focusing on the popular bestsellers and classic works that have stood the test of time. While these books certainly deserve the recognition they receive, there are countless underrated books out there that are equally deserving of our attention. In this blog post, we will explore some of these hidden gems in the literary world that often go unnoticed, but certainly shouldn’t.

1. ‘The Shadow of the Wind’ by Carlos Ruiz Zafón:
Set in post-war Barcelona, this captivating novel takes readers on a journey through the mysterious and magical Cemetery of Forgotten Books. It tells the story of a young boy named Daniel who becomes obsessed with a forgotten author and sets out to uncover the truth behind his identity. With its intriguing plot, richly drawn characters, and atmospheric setting, ‘The Shadow of the Wind’ is a true masterpiece that deserves more recognition.

2. ‘Kindred’ by Octavia Butler:
Octavia Butler is a highly respected author in the science fiction genre, yet ‘Kindred’ remains an underrated gem. This powerful novel seamlessly blends elements of time travel, historical fiction, and social commentary. It follows the story of Dana, a modern-day African American woman who is repeatedly transported back to the pre-Civil War era, where she must confront the harsh realities of slavery. Butler’s gripping storytelling and thought-provoking exploration of race and identity make ‘Kindred’ a must-read for any book lover.

3. ‘The House in the Cerulean Sea’ by TJ Klune:
This heartwarming fantasy novel is a hidden gem that deserves more recognition. It follows the story of Linus Baker, a mild-mannered caseworker who is sent to inspect an orphanage for magical children. As Linus gets to know the unique and loveable children, he begins to question his own preconceived notions and discovers the true meaning of family. With its whimsical world-building, well-rounded characters, and themes of acceptance and love, ‘The House in the Cerulean Sea’ is a delightful read that will leave you with a warm heart.

4. ‘Homegoing’ by Yaa Gyasi:
Spanning over multiple generations, ‘Homegoing’ is an epic historical fiction novel that traces the lives of two half-sisters and their descendants. Set in Ghana and the United States, this powerful narrative explores themes of identity, race, and the lasting legacy of slavery. Gyasi’s stunning prose and ability to weave together multiple storylines make ‘Homegoing’ an underrated gem that should be on everyone’s reading list.

5. ‘Station Eleven’ by Emily St. John Mandel:
While dystopian novels have gained popularity in recent years, ‘Station Eleven’ remains a hidden gem that deserves more recognition. Set in a post-apocalyptic world, the novel follows a group of interconnected characters as they navigate a world devastated by a deadly pandemic. Mandel’s beautiful writing and exploration of humanity and the power of art make ‘Station Eleven’ a thought-provoking and memorable read.

In a literary world that is often saturated with popular bestsellers, it’s important to shine a light on the lesser-known books that deserve more recognition. Whether it’s through their captivating storytelling, thought-provoking themes, or masterful prose, these hidden gems have the power to captivate readers and leave a lasting impact. So, the next time you’re in search of a new book to read, don’t overlook the underrated titles that are waiting to be discovered.
