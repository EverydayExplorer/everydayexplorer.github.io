How to Make Your Own Soy Candles


Are you looking for a creative and sustainable way to enhance the ambiance of your home while also enjoying the benefits of aromatherapy? Look no further than making your own soy candles! In this step-by-step guide, we will explore the art of soy candle making and help you create personalized and eco-friendly candles that will fill your space with beautiful scents and create a warm and inviting atmosphere.

Why Choose Soy Wax?

Before we dive into the process, let’s first discuss why soy wax is an excellent choice for candles. Unlike paraffin wax, which is derived from petroleum, soy wax is made from soybeans, making it a natural and renewable resource. Soy wax is known for its clean burn and lower soot production, which not only helps keep the air in your home cleaner but also ensures that your candles burn for a longer period. Moreover, soy wax has a lower melting point, which means that your candles will last even longer.

Materials You’ll Need

To get started on your soy candle making journey, you’ll need a few essential materials. Here’s a comprehensive list:

1. Soy Wax: Choose a high-quality soy wax from a reputable supplier. Make sure it is specifically designed for candle making.
2. Candle Wicks: Opt for natural cotton wicks, preferably pre-waxed and pre-tabbed for ease of use.
3. Candle Containers: Select heat-resistant containers that can withstand the heat of the melting wax. Mason jars, glass tumblers, or metal tins are popular choices.
4. Fragrance Oils: Invest in high-quality fragrance oils in your preferred scents. It’s always a good idea to refer to the supplier’s guidelines for appropriate usage rates.
5. Colorants (optional): If you’d like to add a touch of color to your candles, consider using liquid or dye chips suitable for candle making.
6. Thermometer: A candle-making thermometer will help you monitor the temperature of the wax accurately.
7. Double Boiler or Wax Melting Pot: To melt the wax, you’ll need a heat-resistant container, such as a double boiler or a wax melting pot.
8. Heat Source: A heat source like a stove, hot plate, or electric wax melter will be necessary to melt the wax.
9. Stirring Utensil: Choose a dedicated utensil, such as a metal spoon or a heat-resistant spatula, for stirring the wax and incorporating the fragrance.
10. Adhesive: A glue gun or adhesive dots are useful for securing the wicks to the bottom of the candle containers.
11. Wick Centering Device (optional): This tool helps keep the wick centered as the wax cools and solidifies.

Step-by-Step Candle Making Process

1. Prepare Your Workspace: Before you start, ensure you have a clean and organized workspace. Cover the surface with newspaper or a dedicated craft mat to catch any spills or drips.

2. Measure and Melt the Wax: Using a kitchen scale, measure the desired amount of soy wax flakes based on the size of your candle containers. As a general guideline, multiply the volume of your container by 0.9 to determine the amount of wax needed. Place the wax flakes in the double boiler or wax melting pot and melt them over low to medium heat. Stir occasionally until the wax reaches a temperature of around 180-185°F (82-85°C).

3. Add Fragrance and Color (if desired): Once the wax has melted, remove it from the heat source and let it cool for a few minutes. Add your desired fragrance oil, following the recommended usage rate provided by the supplier. If you wish to add color, now is the time to incorporate liquid colorants or dye chips. Stir gently but thoroughly to evenly distribute the fragrance and color throughout the wax.

4. Prepare the Candle Containers: While the wax is cooling, prepare your candle containers. Place a small dot of hot glue at the bottom of the container and firmly press the tabbed end of the wick onto it, ensuring it is centered. Alternatively, you can use adhesive dots specifically designed for wick attachment. Use a wick centering device, if available, to hold the wick in place while pouring the wax.

5. Pour the Wax: Once the wax has cooled to the desired temperature (around 135-140°F or 57-60°C), carefully pour it into the prepared candle containers. Be cautious and avoid splashing, as hot wax can cause burns. Leave some space at the top of the container to avoid overflow when the wax solidifies.

6. Cool and Cure: Allow the candles to cool and solidify completely, which can take several hours. Avoid touching or moving the candles during this time to ensure a smooth and even finish.

7. Trim the Wicks: Once the candles have fully solidified, trim the wicks to a length of about ¼ inch (0.6 cm) using sharp scissors or a wick trimmer. This helps ensure a clean and controlled burn when you light your candles.

Enjoy Your Handmade Soy Candles

Congratulations! You have successfully made your own soy candles.

When using your soy candles, always remember to trim the wicks before lighting them and keep an eye on burning candles to ensure safety. Never leave burning candles unattended and always place them on a heat-resistant surface.

Making your own soy candles not only allows you to customize the scent, color, and container, but it also gives you the satisfaction of crafting something with your own hands. Light up your living spaces with the warm glow of your handmade soy candles and delight in the comforting scents that will create a serene and inviting atmosphere. Happy candle making!
