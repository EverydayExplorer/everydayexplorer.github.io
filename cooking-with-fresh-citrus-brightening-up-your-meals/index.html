Cooking with Fresh Citrus: Brightening up Your Meals


Citrus fruits are a kitchen staple that can be used in a variety of ways to brighten up your meals. From oranges to lemons to limes, there’s a citrus fruit for every type of dish. Not only do they add a burst of flavor, but they also offer a host of health benefits.

One way to incorporate fresh citrus into your cooking is through marinades and dressings. Citrus juice can add tang and acidity to any marinade, making meats and seafood more tender while also adding flavor. A simple marinade consisting of lemon juice, olive oil, garlic, and salt can elevate grilled chicken or fish to a whole new level.

Fresh citrus can also be used as a salad dressing base. Simply whisk together some lemon or lime juice, olive oil, honey, and Dijon mustard for a bright and tangy salad dressing. You can also experiment with different citrus fruits to create unique and flavorful dressing combinations.

When it comes to cooking with citrus, don’t forget about the zest. Citrus zest is the outer layer of the fruit that contains oils and flavor compounds. It can be grated and added to dishes as a way to enhance the citrus flavor. Try adding lemon zest to your favorite baked goods or incorporating orange zest into your stir-fry dish.

In addition to providing flavor, citrus fruits are also a great source of nutrients. They are high in vitamins C and A, as well as antioxidants. Studies have also shown that consuming citrus fruits may help reduce the risk of certain diseases, such as heart disease and stroke.

So, next time you’re in the kitchen, consider incorporating some fresh citrus into your cooking. Whether it’s through marinades, dressings, or zest, citrus fruits can add a burst of flavor and an abundance of health benefits to any meal.
