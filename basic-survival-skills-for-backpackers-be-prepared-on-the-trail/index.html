Basic Survival Skills for Backpackers: Be Prepared on the Trail


Backpacking is an adventurous and thrilling activity that allows you to connect with nature and get away from the hustle and bustle of city life. It offers a unique sense of freedom and self-reliance as you explore remote and untouched areas. However, venturing into the wilderness brings with it its own set of challenges and potential dangers. That’s why it is important to equip yourself with basic survival skills before embarking on any backpacking trip.

In this blog post, we will guide you through some essential techniques and knowledge that every backpacker should possess. These skills will not only enhance your overall experience but also help ensure your safety in unforeseen circumstances. So, let’s dive in and explore the world of basic survival skills for backpackers.

1. Navigation Skills One of the most important skills a backpacker should have is the ability to navigate. Whether you are hiking through dense forests or traversing open meadows, having the knowledge to read a map and use a compass is crucial. Familiarize yourself with topographic maps and practice using a compass to determine your location and direction. In addition, learning how to use GPS devices can provide an additional layer of safety and accuracy.

2. Shelter Building Knowing how to build a shelter in the wilderness is a skill that could potentially save your life. Even if you have a tent, situations may arise where it becomes unusable or gets left behind. Learning how to construct a makeshift shelter using natural materials such as branches, leaves, and debris will protect you from the elements and keep you warm during the night.

3. Fire Starting Fire is not only a source of warmth but also a means to cook food, purify water, and signal for help. Carrying waterproof matches or a lighter is essential, but what if you run out of matches or lose your lighter? That’s why learning alternative fire-starting methods such as using a fire striker or creating a fire bow drill will prove invaluable in survival situations.

4. Water Procurement Water is essential for survival, and finding a clean and reliable source becomes a top priority when backpacking. While some water sources may appear clear, they can still be contaminated with harmful bacteria or parasites. Learning how to purify water through boiling, using water filters, or chemical treatments like iodine tablets will ensure you have safe drinking water on the trail.

5. First Aid Accidents happen, and being prepared to handle medical emergencies while in the wilderness is crucial. Equip yourself with a basic first aid kit and learn how to treat common injuries such as cuts, sprains, and burns. Knowing how to perform CPR and handle more serious injuries such as fractures or snake bites can be lifesaving in remote areas where medical help is not readily available.

6. Food Procurement While backpackers generally carry their supplies, unexpected situations such as getting lost or an extended trip may require you to procure food from the environment. Basic knowledge of edible plants, fishing, and primitive trapping techniques will provide you with alternative food sources when needed. However, it is important to thoroughly research the local flora and fauna beforehand to avoid ingesting anything poisonous or endangered.

7. Leave No Trace Being a responsible backpacker means preserving the beauty of nature for future generations. The Leave No Trace principles emphasize minimizing our impact on the environment. This includes properly disposing of waste, using camping stoves instead of open fires, respecting wildlife, and staying on designated trails. When practicing Leave No Trace, we can protect pristine ecosystems and ensure a positive backpacking experience for both ourselves and others.

In conclusion, being equipped with basic survival skills is essential for any backpacking adventure. From navigation and shelter building to fire starting and water procurement, these skills will not only enhance your experience but also ensure your safety in the wilderness. Remember, being prepared is key, and investing time in learning and practicing these skills will give you the confidence to tackle any challenges that may arise. So, pack your backpack, lace up your hiking boots, and embark on a thrilling adventure with the knowledge that you are well-prepared for whatever comes your way.
