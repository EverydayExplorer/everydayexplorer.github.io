The Best Plants for a Coastal Garden


Living near the coast can bring a sense of calm and tranquility. The gentle sound of crashing waves and the fresh sea breeze are enough to make anyone feel at peace. If you’re lucky enough to have a garden in a coastal location, you have the opportunity to create a stunning outdoor space that captures the essence of the ocean. To achieve the perfect coastal garden, it’s important to choose plants that can tolerate the unique conditions found in such an environment. In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the best plants for a coastal garden.

  1. Sea Thrift (Armeria maritima): This perennial, also known as Sea Pink, is a fantastic choice for a coastal garden. It produces clusters of pink or white flowers on sturdy stems, adding a touch of color to your garden. Sea Thrift is highly adaptable and can tolerate salt spray and sandy soil, making it a perfect choice for coastal regions.

  2. Beach Rose (Rosa rugosa): This tough, salt-tolerant shrub is a classic choice for a coastal garden. It features beautiful, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, followed by attractive red hips in the fall. Beach Rose is also highly drought-tolerant, making it an ideal choice for gardens with limited water availability.

  3. Lavender (Lavandula): With its aromatic foliage and beautiful purple flowers, lavender is a staple in many gardens. It is also well-suited to coastal areas due to its ability to tolerate dry, sandy soil and salt spray. Lavender thrives in sunny locations and can be used as a border plant, in containers, or to create a fragrant, low-growing hedge.

  4. Salvia (Salvia): Salvia is a diverse genus that includes many different species and cultivars, all of which are known for their stunning flowers and ability to attract pollinators. Many varieties, such as the Salvia greggii (Autumn Sage), are well-suited to coastal gardens as they can tolerate salt spray and thrive in well-drained soil. Salvia’s vibrant colors and long bloom time make it a great addition to any coastal garden.

  5. Ice Plant (Delosperma): If you’re looking for a groundcover that can withstand the harsh conditions of a coastal garden, look no further than the Ice Plant. This low-growing succulent features fleshy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, yellow, and white. Ice Plant is highly drought-tolerant and can be an excellent choice for stabilizing slopes and preventing soil erosion.

  6. Agapanthus (Agapanthus): Known for their striking blue or white flowers, agapanthus is a popular choice for coastal gardens. These hardy perennials are tolerant of salt spray, windy conditions, and poor soil. Agapanthus adds a touch of elegance to any garden and can be planted en masse or used as a focal point.

  7. Cordyline (Cordyline australis): This architectural plant with its long, sword-like leaves adds a dramatic touch to any coastal garden. Cordyline is highly tolerant of coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It can be grown in containers or planted directly into the ground, adding vertical interest to your garden.

  8. Yucca (Yucca filamentosa): With its spiky foliage and towering flower spikes, yucca is a tough and resilient plant that can thrive in a coastal garden. Yucca is drought-tolerant and can tolerate sandy, well-drained soil. Its architectural form makes it an excellent focal point, especially when planted against a backdrop of the ocean.

When designing a coastal garden, it’s important to remember that the conditions can be harsh. The wind, salt spray, and sandy soil can pose challenges for many plants. By selecting the right plant species, you can ensure that your coastal garden remains beautiful and thriving all year round.

In addition to the plants mentioned above, other suitable options for a coastal garden include seaside goldenrod (Solidago sempervirens), Beach aster (Aster furcatus), and seashore mallow (Kosteletzkya virginica). Be sure to take into account the specific microclimate of your garden and choose plants that are adapted to your particular coastal region.

Creating a coastal garden allows you to embrace the beauty of the ocean and bring a touch of seaside charm to your outdoor space. With the right selection of plants, your coastal garden can become a true oasis, providing a serene and peaceful retreat right in your own backyard.
