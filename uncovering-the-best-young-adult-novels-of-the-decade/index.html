Uncovering the Best Young Adult Novels of the Decade


The past decade has witnessed a remarkable surge in the popularity of young adult novels. These captivating literary works have captured the hearts and minds of readers of all ages, transporting them to magical worlds, exploring complex themes, and delving into the tumultuous journey of adolescence. With so many exceptional books to choose from, it can be overwhelming to uncover the best young adult novels of the decade. Fear not! In this blog post, we will embark on an exciting journey to unearth some of the most outstanding young adult novels that have graced the literary landscape in the past ten years.

1. ‘The Hunger Games’ Trilogy by Suzanne Collins: Kicking off our list is the electrifying dystopian series that took the world by storm. Set in a post-apocalyptic nation, this gripping tale follows the courageous Katniss Everdeen as she fights for survival in the brutal Hunger Games. With its powerful social commentary and unforgettable characters, this trilogy has become a modern classic.

2. ‘The Fault in Our Stars’ by John Green: Get ready to be moved to tears by this heart-wrenching story of young love and terminal illness. John Green’s poignant novel explores the lives of Hazel and Gus, two teenagers who embark on a journey that will forever change their lives. With its raw emotions and beautiful prose, this book is a must-read for anyone seeking an emotional rollercoaster.

3. ‘Six of Crows’ Duology by Leigh Bardugo: Prepare to be swept away by this thrilling heist fantasy set in the Grishaverse. Follow a band of misfits as they navigate the treacherous city of Ketterdam, aiming to pull off an impossible heist. Bardugo’s rich world-building and complex characters make this duology a standout in the young adult genre.

4. ‘The Hate U Give’ by Angie Thomas: Tackling issues of race, identity, and police brutality, this powerful novel has made a profound impact on readers worldwide. Through the eyes of Starr Carter, we witness the aftermath of a police shooting and the ensuing struggle for justice. Thomas’s debut novel is a timely and thought-provoking exploration of contemporary society.

5. ‘A Court of Thorns and Roses’ Series by Sarah J. Maas: Enter a world of faeries, romance, and epic battles in this addictive series. Follow Feyre Archeron as she navigates the dangerous realm of the High Fae and discovers her own hidden powers. Maas’s lush storytelling and intricate world-building make this series a must-read for fantasy enthusiasts.

6. ‘The Book Thief’ by Markus Zusak: Set during World War II, this mesmerizing novel tells the story of Liesel Meminger, a young girl who finds solace in books amidst the horrors of Nazi Germany. With its unique narrative voice and unforgettable characters, Zusak’s masterpiece is a testament to the power of words and the resilience of the human spirit.

7. ‘Eleanor & Park’ by Rainbow Rowell: Fall in love with the unconventional romance between two misfit teenagers in this heartwarming and nostalgic story. Set in the 1980s, Rowell beautifully captures the complexities of first love, friendship, and the challenges of growing up.

8. ‘The Maze Runner’ Series by James Dashner: Prepare for a thrilling ride through a deadly maze in this fast-paced dystopian series. Join Thomas and his fellow Gladers as they navigate a mysterious and dangerous world, fighting for their lives. Dashner’s gripping storytelling will keep you on the edge of your seat until the very end.

9. ‘The Raven Boys’ Series by Maggie Stiefvater: Delve into a world of magic, mystery, and ancient prophecies in this enchanting series. Follow Blue Sargent and a group of boys from the prestigious Aglionby Academy as they search for a long-lost Welsh king. Stiefvater’s lyrical prose and captivating characters make this series a true gem.

10. ‘The Giver’ by Lois Lowry: This timeless dystopian novel takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through a seemingly utopian society. Follow Jonas as he discovers the dark secrets lurking beneath the surface and questions the cost of a world without pain and suffering. Lowry’s powerful storytelling and exploration of complex themes make this novel a must-read for all ages.

These are just a few of the remarkable young adult novels that have left an indelible mark on readers over the past decade. Whether you’re seeking heart-pounding adventures, thought-provoking social commentary, or captivating romances, the young adult genre has something for everyone. So grab a book, settle into a cozy corner, and get ready to embark on unforgettable literary journeys in the company of these exceptional young adult novels.
