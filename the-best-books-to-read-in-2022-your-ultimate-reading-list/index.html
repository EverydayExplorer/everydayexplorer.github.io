The Best Books to Read in 2022: Your Ultimate Reading List


As we step into a new year, it’s the perfect time to refresh our reading lists and dive into captivating stories, insightful non-fiction, and transformative self-help books. Whether you’re a seasoned bookworm or looking to cultivate a new reading habit, there’s something for everyone in the literary landscape of 2022. From gripping novels to thought-provoking essays, here’s a curated selection of the best books to add to your reading list this year.

1. ‘The Midnight Library’ by Matt Haig
Dive into the enchanting world of ‘The Midnight Library,’ where every book represents a different life that could have been lived. Matt Haig weaves a poignant tale of regrets, second chances, and the power of choice in this captivating novel that will leave you contemplating the infinite possibilities of existence.

2. ‘Klara and the Sun’ by Kazuo Ishiguro
Nobel Prize-winning author Kazuo Ishiguro returns with a mesmerizing exploration of artificial intelligence, love, and humanity in ‘Klara and the Sun.’ Through the eyes of Klara, an artificial friend, Ishiguro invites readers to ponder what it truly means to be alive and the complexities of human relationships in an increasingly technologically driven world.

3. ‘Crying in H Mart’ by Michelle Zauner
In this deeply personal memoir, Michelle Zauner, the acclaimed musician behind Japanese Breakfast, reflects on grief, identity, and the enduring bond between food and family. ‘Crying in H Mart’ is a raw and tender exploration of loss and belonging that resonates long after the final page.

4. ‘Project Hail Mary’ by Andy Weir
From the author of ‘The Martian’ comes another exhilarating sci-fi adventure that will keep you on the edge of your seat. ‘Project Hail Mary’ follows astronaut Ryland Grace as he embarks on a desperate mission to save humanity from extinction. With its blend of humor, suspense, and scientific ingenuity, this novel is a must-read for fans of hard science fiction.

5. ‘Ace of Spades’ by Faridah Àbíké-Íyímídé
Taut, gripping, and thought-provoking, ‘Ace of Spades’ is a powerful debut that tackles issues of race, privilege, and institutionalized racism in a prestigious academy. Faridah Àbíké-Íyímídé crafts a chilling narrative that explores the lengths some will go to maintain their status quo and the courage it takes to dismantle systems of oppression.

6. ‘Empire of Pain: The Secret History of the Sackler Dynasty’ by Patrick Radden Keefe
Delve into the dark and compelling saga of one of the wealthiest and most controversial families in America. In ‘Empire of Pain,’ Patrick Radden Keefe exposes the ruthless business practices behind the Sackler dynasty’s pharmaceutical empire and the devastating human cost of the opioid crisis.

7. ‘The Anthropocene Reviewed’ by John Green
With his trademark wit and insight, John Green offers a collection of essays that celebrate the beauty and complexity of the human experience. From Hawaiian pizza to the Taco Bell breakfast menu, ‘The Anthropocene Reviewed’ is a poignant exploration of the ways in which we shape and are shaped by the world around us.

8. ‘The Push’ by Ashley Audrain
Ashley Audrain delivers a chilling psychological thriller that explores the dark underbelly of motherhood and the legacy of trauma. ‘The Push’ is a gripping page-turner that will keep you guessing until the very end, raising questions about nature vs. nurture and the enduring power of maternal love.

9. ‘The Code Breaker: Jennifer Doudna, Gene Editing, and the Future of the Human Race’ by Walter Isaacson
In this illuminating biography, Walter Isaacson tells the remarkable story of Jennifer Doudna, the Nobel Prize-winning scientist who pioneered CRISPR technology. ‘The Code Breaker’ offers a fascinating glimpse into the ethical dilemmas and revolutionary potential of gene editing, raising crucial questions about the future of humanity.

10. ‘How to Avoid a Climate Disaster: The Solutions We Have and the Breakthroughs We Need’ by Bill Gates
Drawing on his expertise as a technologist and philanthropist, Bill Gates presents a compelling roadmap for addressing the climate crisis in ‘How to Avoid a Climate Disaster.’ Through accessible language and concrete solutions, Gates outlines the urgent steps we must take to mitigate the impact of climate change and build a sustainable future for generations to come.

As you embark on your literary journey in 2022, may these diverse and thought-provoking books inspire, challenge, and enrich your mind and soul. Happy reading!
