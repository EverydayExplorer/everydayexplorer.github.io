10 Must-Read Books of the Decade


In the past decade, literature has witnessed an array of remarkable works that have captivated readers’ hearts and minds. These books have not only entertained us but have also challenged our perspectives and expanded our understanding of the world. Without further ado, here are 10 must-read books of the decade that have left an indelible mark on the literary landscape.

1. ‘The Underground Railroad’ by Colson Whitehead
Colson Whitehead takes readers on a gripping journey through the Underground Railroad, reimagined as an actual subterranean railway. This powerful novel explores the horrors of slavery and the resilience of those who fought for freedom.

2. ‘The Goldfinch’ by Donna Tartt
Donna Tartt’s Pulitzer Prize-winning novel tells the story of Theo Decker, a young boy who clings to a painting called ‘The Goldfinch’ after a tragic event. Through Theo’s tumultuous life, Tartt explores themes of loss, love, and the power of art.

3. ‘Educated’ by Tara Westover
Tara Westover’s memoir is a testament to the transformative power of education. Raised in a strict and abusive household in rural Idaho, Westover defied all odds to pursue higher education and find her own path.

4. ‘Normal People’ by Sally Rooney
Sally Rooney’s critically acclaimed novel delves into the complex relationship between Connell and Marianne, two young people from different social backgrounds who navigate love, friendship, and the challenges of adulthood.

5. ‘The Hate U Give’ by Angie Thomas
Angie Thomas’s groundbreaking debut novel tackles timely issues of racial injustice and police brutality through the eyes of Starr Carter, a young African American girl who becomes the witness to a fatal shooting.

6. ‘A Little Life’ by Hanya Yanagihara
Hanya Yanagihara’s emotionally devastating novel follows the lives of four friends over several decades. ‘A Little Life’ explores trauma, friendship, and the enduring power of love amidst unimaginable pain.

7. ‘The Girl on the Train’ by Paula Hawkins
Paula Hawkins’s gripping psychological thriller takes readers on a twisted journey through the life of Rachel Watson, an alcoholic who becomes entangled in a missing person investigation.

8. ‘The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks’ by Rebecca Skloot
Rebecca Skloot’s non-fiction book tells the incredible true story of Henrietta Lacks, a woman whose cells were taken without her consent and became instrumental in countless medical breakthroughs.

9. ‘Station Eleven’ by Emily St. John Mandel
In a post-apocalyptic world, Emily St. John Mandel weaves together multiple narratives to explore the power of art, the resilience of humanity, and the interconnectedness of our lives.

10. ‘Big Little Lies’ by Liane Moriarty
Liane Moriarty’s gripping page-turner follows a group of mothers whose seemingly perfect lives unravel after a mysterious death occurs in their community. This addictive novel delves into secrets, lies, and the complexities of female friendships.

These 10 must-read books of the decade have touched the hearts of millions, sparking conversations, and leaving a lasting impact on readers. Whether you’re looking for thought-provoking literature, gripping thrillers, or powerful memoirs, these books offer something for every reader. So, grab a cup of tea, find a cozy spot, and immerse yourself in the captivating worlds crafted by these talented authors. Happy reading!
