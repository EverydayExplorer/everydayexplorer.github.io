Sartrean Existentialism: Embracing Authenticity and Freedom


Existentialism is a philosophical movement that emerged in the 20th century, focusing on the meaning of human existence. Among its prominent figures, Jean-Paul Sartre stands out as one of the leading voices of this school of thought. Sartrean existentialism emphasizes the concepts of authenticity and freedom as fundamental aspects of human existence.

Understanding Existentialism

To truly comprehend Sartrean existentialism, it is essential to understand the broader framework of existentialist philosophy. Existentialism rejects traditional ideas of essentialism and determinism, instead emphasizing individual freedom and personal responsibility.

Existentialists believe that existence precedes essence, meaning that humans do not possess any predetermined purpose or meaning in life. Instead, individuals have the freedom to create their own meaning through their choices and actions.

Authenticity: Embracing One’s True Self

One of the central ideas of Sartrean existentialism is the concept of authenticity. Authenticity refers to the ability to live in accordance with one’s true self, free from the influence of external factors such as societal expectations or the norms of others.

Sartre argues that authenticity requires individuals to confront the harsh reality of their existence and make choices based on their own values and beliefs, rather than conforming to the expectations imposed upon them by society. In embracing authenticity, one takes full responsibility for their choices, actions, and ultimately, their own existence.

Overcoming Bad Faith

Sartre identifies ‘bad faith’ as a common pitfall in which individuals deny their freedom and instead choose to live inauthentically. Bad faith manifests when individuals deceive themselves into believing that they are not free and that their actions are determined by external circumstances or societal expectations.

Choosing to ignore their freedom, individuals may adopt predefined roles and identities or conform to societal norms, effectively suppressing their own desires and values. Sartre argues that embracing authenticity necessitates recognizing one’s freedom and taking responsibility for the choices made in life.

Freedom: The Essence of Existence

Central to Sartrean existentialism is the concept of freedom. Sartre posits that freedom is an intrinsic aspect of human existence and that individuals always have the freedom to choose their actions, regardless of external constraints.

However, this freedom is not without its challenges. Sartre recognizes that humans are unavoidably burdened by the weight of their choices, as every decision has consequences. The recognition of this responsibility can lead to anxiety and anguish, a concept he terms ’existential dread.’ Nevertheless, Sartre argues that it is precisely this freedom and responsibility that define our humanity.

Authenticity in Practice

In order to live authentically, individuals must examine their values, beliefs, and desires and make choices that reflect their true selves. This includes the willingness to face the uncertainty and anxiety inherent in decision-making.

Embracing authenticity also means being true to one’s own passions and desires, rather than trying to fit into predefined societal roles or expectations. It involves living a life that aligns with one’s core principles and values, regardless of external judgments or pressures.

Embracing Existentialism in Today’s World

In our modern society, it is all too easy to lose sight of our authentic selves. External pressures, societal expectations, and the influence of others can often lead us astray from living a genuine and fulfilling life. However, embracing Sartrean existentialism can serve as a guide to reclaiming our authenticity and freedom.

By recognizing our inherent freedom and the responsibility that comes with it, we can make choices that align with our true selves. By embracing authenticity, we can resist the temptation to conform to societal norms and instead create our own meaning and purpose in life.

Conclusion

Sartrean existentialism offers a profound perspective on the nature of human existence, emphasizing the importance of authenticity and freedom. By embracing our freedom and taking responsibility for our choices, we can live an authentic life aligned with our true selves.

In a world where external pressures and societal expectations often dominate our lives, Sartrean existentialism provides a philosophy that encourages us to break free and embrace our individuality. By accepting the inherent uncertainty and anxiety that accompany freedom, we can find meaning and purpose in our own unique journey.
