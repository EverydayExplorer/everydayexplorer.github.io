The Truth About Credit Scores: Myths vs. Facts


In today’s financial landscape, credit scores hold significant importance. Whether you’re applying for a loan, renting an apartment, or even getting a new job, your credit score can have a significant impact on your life. However, despite its significance, credit scores remain shrouded in mystery. There are countless myths and misconceptions surrounding credit scores, making it difficult for individuals to understand the truth behind these three-digit numbers. In this blog post, we aim to debunk some common myths and shed light on the facts about credit scores.

Myth #1: Closing credit card accounts will improve your credit score.
Fact: Contrary to popular belief, closing credit card accounts can actually harm your credit score. One crucial factor that affects your credit score is your credit utilization ratio, which is the amount of credit you’re using compared to the total credit available to you. By closing a credit card account, you decrease your available credit, which can increase your credit utilization ratio. It’s generally advisable to keep credit card accounts open, even if you’re not actively using them, to maintain a healthy credit utilization ratio.

Myth #2: Checking your credit score will lower it.
Fact: Many people avoid checking their credit scores out of fear that it will negatively impact their score. However, this is far from the truth. When you check your own credit score, it is considered a ‘soft inquiry’ and does not affect your credit score. On the other hand, ‘hard inquiries’ made by lenders when you apply for credit can have a slight impact on your score. Regularly monitoring your credit score is a responsible practice that can help you identify any errors or fraudulent activity.

Myth #3: Having a high income guarantees a high credit score.
Fact: Your credit score is not determined by your income level. While having a steady and substantial income can help you manage your debts and maintain a good credit score, it is not the sole factor that creditors consider. Your credit score is influenced by various factors, including your payment history, credit utilization, length of credit history, types of credit, and new credit applications. It’s essential to manage your credit responsibly, regardless of your income level.

Myth #4: Paying off debts will immediately improve your credit score.
Fact: While paying off debts is a responsible financial practice, it may not have an immediate impact on your credit score. Your payment history, which includes any late or missed payments, plays a significant role in determining your credit score. It takes time to build a positive payment history and demonstrate consistent financial responsibility. It’s crucial to make timely payments and maintain a good credit utilization ratio over an extended period to see an improvement in your credit score.

Myth #5: Closing old credit card accounts will remove them from your credit report.
Fact: Closing old credit card accounts does not remove them from your credit report. Your credit report is a detailed record of your credit history, including accounts that you’ve opened and closed. Closed accounts can remain on your credit report for up to ten years, depending on the credit bureau’s policies. It’s important to note that the age of your credit accounts is a factor in calculating your credit score. Closing old accounts can potentially shorten your credit history and impact your score.

Understanding the truth about credit scores is essential for making informed financial decisions. By debunking these common myths, we hope to empower individuals with accurate information about credit scores. Remember, maintaining a good credit score requires responsible credit management, timely payments, and a thorough understanding of how credit scores work. Stay informed, stay proactive, and take control of your financial well-being.
