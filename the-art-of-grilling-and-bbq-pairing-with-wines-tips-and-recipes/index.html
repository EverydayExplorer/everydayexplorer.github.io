The Art of Grilling and BBQ Pairing with Wines: Tips and Recipes


When it comes to the art of grilling and BBQ, there is a magical alchemy that happens when smoky flavors meet perfectly charred meats. But have you ever thought about pairing your grilled masterpieces with the right wines? Yes, that’s right - wine and barbecue can be a match made in culinary heaven. In this post, we will explore the nuances of pairing wines with grilled dishes, from juicy steaks to tender ribs, and provide you with tips and recipes to elevate your next backyard BBQ to gourmet status.

To start off, let’s debunk a common myth: red wine is not the only option for pairing with grilled foods. While it’s true that the tannins in red wine can complement the bold flavors of grilled meats, there are plenty of white and rosé wines that can hold their own against the smokiness of barbecue. When choosing a wine to pair with your grilled dish, consider the dominant flavors of the dish. For example, if you’re grilling seafood with a citrus marinade, a crisp Sauvignon Blanc or a dry Riesling would be an excellent choice. For grilled chicken with herbs, a light-bodied Pinot Noir or a Chardonnay would be a great match.

Now, let’s talk about some general guidelines for pairing wines with grilled dishes. First and foremost, consider the intensity of flavors in both the food and the wine. A heavily seasoned steak might overpower a delicate white wine, while a robust red wine could clash with a lightly seasoned chicken breast. Secondly, think about the cooking method - grilled foods often have a smoky char that can be enhanced by wines with oak influences, such as Chardonnay or Cabernet Sauvignon aged in oak barrels. Lastly, don’t forget about the sauce! Sweet and tangy BBQ sauces pair well with fruity red wines like Zinfandel or Shiraz, while spicy rubs call for wines with a touch of sweetness to balance the heat.

To help you put these tips into practice, here are two mouth-watering recipes for grilled dishes paired with wines:

1. Grilled Ribeye Steak with Garlic Butter Sauce
- Ingredients:
- 2 ribeye steaks
- Salt and pepper
- 4 tbsp butter
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- Fresh parsley, chopped
- Instructions:
1. Season the steaks with salt and pepper.
2. Preheat the grill to high heat.
3. Grill the steaks to your desired doneness.
4. In a small saucepan, melt the butter and sauté the garlic until fragrant.
5. Drizzle the garlic butter sauce over the grilled steaks and sprinkle with fresh parsley.
- Wine Pairing: Cabernet Sauvignon

2. BBQ Pulled Pork Sliders with Coleslaw
- Ingredients:
- 2 lbs pork shoulder
- 1 cup BBQ sauce
- Slider buns
- Coleslaw mix
- Mayonnaise
- Apple cider vinegar
- Sugar
- Instructions:
1. Rub the pork shoulder with salt and pepper and slow-cook until tender.
2. Shred the pork and mix with BBQ sauce.
3. Toast the slider buns on the grill.
4. Toss the coleslaw mix with mayonnaise, apple cider vinegar, and sugar.
5. Assemble the sliders with pulled pork and coleslaw.
- Wine Pairing: Zinfandel

In conclusion, mastering the art of grilling and BBQ pairing with wines is all about balancing flavors and creating harmonious combinations. Whether you prefer a juicy steak or fall-off-the-bone ribs, there is a wine out there waiting to enhance your dining experience. So fire up the grill, uncork a bottle of your favorite vintage, and get ready to impress your guests with a feast fit for a sommelier. Cheers to the perfect union of barbecue and wine!
