Why Do Avocados Turn Brown? Unveiling the Oxidation Process in Foods


Avocados are a popular superfood known for their creamy texture, distinct flavor, and numerous health benefits. Whether it’s adding slices to a salad, making guacamole, or spreading it on toast, avocados have become a staple in many people’s diets. However, one common issue that avocado lovers face is the frustrating phenomenon of browning. It can be disappointing to cut open an avocado, only to find that it has turned an unappetizing brown color. But why does this happen? In this blog post, we will delve into the science behind avocado browning and the oxidation process in foods.

The Culprit: Enzymatic Browning

The primary reason why avocados turn brown is a process called enzymatic browning. Enzymatic browning is a chemical reaction that occurs when an enzyme called polyphenol oxidase (PPO) interacts with oxygen in the air. This reaction leads to the formation of brown pigments known as melanin. PPO is present in many fruits and vegetables, including avocados, and is responsible for their browning when exposed to air.

Polyphenols: The Key Players

Polyphenols are natural compounds found in various plant-based foods, such as fruits, vegetables, and even coffee and tea. They contribute to the color, flavor, and nutritional properties of these foods. In the case of avocados, polyphenols are concentrated in the skin and the seed. When the avocado is cut or damaged, these polyphenols come into contact with oxygen, triggering the enzymatic browning reaction.

Oxidation: The Chemical Reaction

When an avocado is sliced or bruised, enzymes in the fruit’s tissues come into contact with oxygen from the air, initiating the oxidation process. As a result, PPO enzymes convert certain chemicals, known as phenolic compounds, into reactive quinones. These quinones then undergo further reactions, leading to the production of melanin pigments, which are responsible for the brown color.

How to Slow Down Avocado Browning

While it may be challenging to completely prevent avocado browning, several strategies can help slow down the process:

1. Acidic Solution: The acid in substances like lemon juice or vinegar can help prevent enzymatic browning. Simply sprinkle lemon juice or vinegar over the exposed areas of the avocado to slow down browning. The acid denatures the enzymes, preventing them from interacting with oxygen and delaying the browning process.

2. Refrigeration: Storing cut avocados in the refrigerator can help slow down browning. The colder temperature slows down the enzymatic reaction, providing more time before the avocado turns brown. It is important to note that refrigeration does not completely stop the browning, but it can significantly extend the avocado’s freshness.

3. Plastic Wrap: Another method to prevent avocado browning is by covering the exposed surface tightly with plastic wrap. This prevents oxygen from reaching the avocado, inhibiting the enzymatic browning reaction. Be sure to press the plastic wrap directly against the avocado to minimize air contact.

4. Onion Method: Some avocado aficionados swear by the ‘onion method’ to prevent browning. The principle behind this method is that onions release sulfur compounds that can inhibit the enzymatic browning process. Simply place a slice of onion on top of the cut avocado before storing it. However, note that the onion’s taste might seep into the avocado, altering its flavor.

5. Brush with Oil: Brushing the exposed areas of the avocado with oil, such as extra virgin olive oil, can create a physical barrier between the fruit and oxygen, slow down the browning process, and help preserve its freshness for a bit longer.

Understanding the role of enzymatic browning and oxidation in avocados can help us appreciate the science behind this natural process. While it may not always be possible to prevent avocado browning entirely, these simple techniques can help prolong the freshness and visual appeal of this cherished fruit. Whether it’s enjoying a vibrant green slice of avocado on your toast or incorporating it into your favorite recipes, knowing how to slow down browning will enhance your avocado experience. So, the next time you indulge in this nutritious and delicious fruit, you can enjoy it without the worry of untimely browning.
