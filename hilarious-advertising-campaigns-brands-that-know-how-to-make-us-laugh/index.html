Hilarious Advertising Campaigns: Brands that Know How to Make us Laugh


Note: The following blog post is dedicated to exploring hilarious advertising campaigns and how various brands have successfully used humor to captivate and entertain their audience.

When it comes to advertising, the ultimate goal is to grab the attention of consumers and leave a lasting impression. In today’s fast-paced digital world, it can be challenging for brands to stand out from the competition. However, some brands have figured out the secret recipe to success - humor. In this blog post, we will explore some of the funniest and most successful advertising campaigns that have not only garnered attention but have also achieved high levels of engagement.

  1. Old Spice ‘The Man Your Man Could Smell Like’

Old Spice, the iconic men’s grooming brand, revolutionized its advertising strategy with the launch of ‘The Man Your Man Could Smell Like’ campaign. This series of commercials featured a handsome and charismatic spokesperson delivering humorous one-liners while showcasing the brand’s various products. The campaign immediately went viral, attracting millions of views on YouTube and creating a massive online buzz. By combining humor, creativity, and memorable catchphrases, Old Spice successfully reinvented its image and connected with a younger and broader audience.

  1. Dollar Shave Club ‘Our Blades Are F***ing Great’

Dollar Shave Club, a subscription-based razor company, made a splash in the advertising world with their memorable commercial titled ‘Our Blades Are F***ing Great’. The ad featured the company’s founder, Michael Dubin, delivering a humorous and irreverent monologue while showcasing the brand’s affordable and high-quality razors. The raw and comedic style of the commercial resonated with viewers, leading to an influx of online orders and skyrocketing brand awareness. Dollar Shave Club’s bold and hilarious approach proved that engaging content combined with a touch of edginess can go a long way in capturing the attention of consumers.

  1. Geico ‘Hump Day Camel’

Geico, an insurance company known for its witty and entertaining commercials, struck gold with their ‘Hump Day Camel’ commercial. This ad featured a talking camel enthusiastically promoting the fact that it is ‘hump day’ - the middle of the workweek. The whimsical and humorous nature of the commercial resonated with viewers, leading to widespread popularity and countless parodies on social media. Geico’s ability to inject humor into an inherently unexciting industry like insurance has set them apart from their competitors and made them a household name.

  1. Snickers ‘You’re Not You When You’re Hungry’

Snickers, the popular chocolate bar brand, has built an entire advertising campaign around the idea that people are not themselves when they’re hungry. The commercials feature famous celebrities and athletes portraying grumpy and out-of-character versions of themselves, only to transform back into their usual selves after eating a Snickers bar. The humor in these commercials lies in the stark contrast between the hungry and satisfied personalities, creating a relatable and amusing scenario for viewers. Snickers’ clever use of humor has not only boosted brand recognition but has also strengthened the association between the brand and satisfaction.

  1. Skittles ‘Taste the Rainbow’

Skittles, the beloved fruit-flavored candy brand, has never shied away from quirky and humorous advertising. Their famous ‘Taste the Rainbow’ campaign features a series of bizarre and often hilarious scenarios involving Skittles candies. From a man made of Skittles being licked by a giraffe to a woman giving birth to a Skittles baby, these commercials are as entertaining as they are perplexing. Skittles’ mastery of absurdity and their willingness to embrace the unconventional have made them a memorable and beloved brand.

In conclusion, humor has proven to be a powerful tool for brands looking to make a lasting impression in the advertising world. The campaigns mentioned above are just a few examples of how humor can captivate an audience, generate buzz, and ultimately drive sales. By infusing their brand messaging with wit, cleverness, and relatability, these brands have successfully differentiated themselves from their competitors and established a strong connection with consumers.

So the next time you find yourself laughing out loud at a commercial, remember that behind the humor lies a carefully crafted strategy to entertain and engage. By embracing humor, brands can tap into the universal language of laughter and create a memorable experience that will keep customers coming back for more.
