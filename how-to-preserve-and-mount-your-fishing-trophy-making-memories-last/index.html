How to Preserve and Mount Your Fishing Trophy: Making Memories Last


Fishing can be an exciting hobby that brings you closer to nature and allows you to engage in a thrilling activity. When you have a successful fishing trip, it’s only natural to want to preserve the memory of your achievement. One way to do this is by preserving and mounting your fishing trophy. In this blog post, we will discuss the step-by-step process of preserving and mounting your fishing trophy, allowing you to make your memories last a lifetime!

Step 1: Choosing the Right Fish

Before you can begin the preservation and mounting process, you need to catch the right fish. Look for a trophy-worthy species that exhibits beautiful coloring and impressive size. Keep in mind any local regulations regarding the type or size of fish you can keep. Catching a standout fish will make the final product even more visually appealing.

Step 2: Proper Handling and Care

Once you’ve caught your trophy fish, it’s crucial to handle it with care to maintain its quality. Avoid touching the fish with bare hands, as it can remove the protective slime on their scales. Instead, use a soft, wet cloth or gloves to handle the fish gently. This step is important because damaged scales or fins can diminish the overall appearance of the mounted trophy.

Step 3: Document the Measurements

Before preserving the fish, it’s essential to document its measurements accurately. Measuring the length, girth, and weight of the fish will help create a realistic mount. Take clear photographs from various angles to assist the taxidermist in recreating the fish’s natural appearance.

Step 4: Choosing the Preservation Technique

There are several methods for preserving your fish trophy, depending on personal preference and budget. The most common methods are skin mounts, fiberglass reproductions, and replicas. A skin mount involves preserving the actual skin and scales of the fish. Fiberglass reproductions are made from molds of the fish, while replicas are entirely synthetic renditions of the fish. Consider the pros and cons of each method before making the final decision.

Step 5: Finding a Professional Taxidermist

To ensure the best possible outcome for your fishing trophy, it’s crucial to find a reputable taxidermist who specializes in fish mounts. Ask for recommendations from fellow anglers or visit local taxidermy shops to review their work. A skilled taxidermist will be able to replicate the natural posture, coloration, and details of your fish, creating a beautiful and lifelike trophy.

Step 6: Preparing the Trophy for Mounting

Once you’ve chosen a taxidermist, you will need to prepare the fish for mounting. This involves properly cleaning the fish to remove any debris, blood, or slime that may interfere with the preservation process. It’s important to follow the taxidermist’s instructions carefully to ensure the best results. Remember, an adequately prepared trophy will yield better outcomes.

Step 7: Mounting the Fish

The actual mounting process is best left to the taxidermist, as it requires expert knowledge and skill. They will use the measurements and photographs you provided to recreate the fish’s natural appearance. The fish will be carefully positioned and set on a sturdy base to achieve the desired pose. The taxidermist will also apply various techniques to preserve the coloration and texture of the fish, making it look as realistic as possible.

Step 8: Displaying Your Mounted Fish Trophy

Once your fishing trophy has been preserved and mounted, it’s time to display it proudly. Choose a prominent location in your home or office where it can be admired by all. Make sure to clean and dust the trophy regularly to maintain its appearance. Remember, your mounted fish trophy is not only a testament to your angling skills but also a beautiful piece of artwork that deserves proper care and attention.

Step 9: Capturing the Moment

In addition to preserving and mounting your fishing trophy, don’t forget to document the moment with photographs or videos. Share your achievement with family and friends, and reminisce about the incredible experience. These memories will become even more precious as time passes, and having visual reminders will help keep them alive.

Conclusion

Preserving and mounting your fishing trophy is an excellent way to relive the excitement of your fishing adventures. By following the steps outlined in this blog post, you will be able to create a lasting memory and a beautiful piece of artwork that showcases your angling skills. Remember, choosing the right fish, handling it with care, finding a skilled taxidermist, and properly maintaining your trophy are all vital steps in making your memories last a lifetime.
