Tips for Getting Upgraded to a Suite at Your Hotel


As travelers, we all love to be upgraded to a suite at our hotel. It’s that little touch of luxury that can make our stay even more memorable. But how do you increase your chances of getting upgraded? Are there any tips or tricks that you can use to score a suite without breaking the bank? In this blog post, we will explore some strategies that may help you secure that coveted upgrade.

1. Join the loyalty program: One of the simplest and most effective ways to increase your chances of getting upgraded is by joining the hotel’s loyalty program. Hotels often prioritize their loyal customers when it comes to upgrades. By signing up for the loyalty program, you are showing your commitment to the brand, which could earn you some extra brownie points during check-in.

2. Book directly with the hotel: When booking your hotel, it’s always best to book directly with the hotel instead of using third-party websites. Hotels are more likely to offer upgrades to guests who book directly with them as they have a higher profit margin on these reservations. Additionally, booking directly allows for better communication between you and the hotel, making it easier to negotiate an upgrade if necessary.

3. Travel during the off-season: Another strategy to increase your chances of getting an upgrade is to travel during the hotel’s off-peak season. Hotels are more likely to have available suites during these quieter times, making it easier for them to upgrade guests. Plus, traveling during the off-season often means lower rates, so it’s a win-win situation.

4. Arrive early and be polite: When you arrive at the hotel for check-in, it’s essential to be courteous and polite to the front desk staff. Remember, they have the power to upgrade you or not. Being friendly, engaging in small talk, and expressing your excitement about your stay can go a long way in receiving a positive response. Additionally, arriving early increases your chances of getting an upgrade as hotels tend to allocate rooms and upgrades in the morning before they fill up.

5. Special occasions or celebrations: If you are celebrating a special occasion such as a birthday, anniversary, or honeymoon, don’t forget to mention it to the hotel. Hotels often go the extra mile to make such moments memorable for their guests. By informing them in advance, they may surprise you with a complimentary upgrade as a gesture of goodwill.

6. Become a social media influencer: In recent years, hotels have become more active on social media platforms. If you have a significant following or are known for your travel content, hotels may be more willing to provide you with an upgrade in exchange for exposure on your social media channels. Consider reaching out to the hotel in advance and proposing a collaboration.

7. Maintain a positive online reputation: Hotels often research their guests before they check-in, especially if you have a reservation under your name. By maintaining a positive online reputation through review sites, social media, or travel blogs, you could increase your chances of getting an upgrade. Hotels are more likely to reward guests who have a good track record of leaving positive reviews or engaging positively with their brand.

8. Offer to pay for an upgrade: If all else fails, you can try the direct approach and offer to pay for an upgrade. While this may not be the most cost-effective solution, it does give you a higher chance of securing a suite. Be sure to negotiate the price with the hotel as they may be willing to offer you a discounted rate.

In conclusion, it is possible to increase your chances of getting upgraded to a suite at your hotel. By joining the loyalty program, booking directly, traveling during the off-season, being polite, mentioning special occasions, leveraging your social media presence, maintaining a positive online reputation, or offering to pay for an upgrade, you can potentially enhance your hotel experience and enjoy the luxury of a suite. Remember to approach these strategies with respect and gratitude, as upgrades are not guaranteed but rather offered as a gesture of goodwill by the hotel. Happy travels!
