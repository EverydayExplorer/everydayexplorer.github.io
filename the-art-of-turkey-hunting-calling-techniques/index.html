The Art of Turkey Hunting: Calling Techniques


Turkey hunting is a popular sport among outdoor enthusiasts, and for good reason. It combines the thrill of the chase with the opportunity to commune with nature. But success in turkey hunting is not solely dependent on your shooting skills and knowledge of the terrain. One crucial aspect of turkey hunting that often separates the successful hunters from the frustrated ones is the art of calling.

Calling is the act of imitating turkey sounds to attract them towards your location. It is an essential technique that can significantly increase your chances of a successful hunt. In this blog post, we will explore various calling techniques that you can use to improve your turkey hunting game.

1. The Turkey Language: Before we dive into specific calling techniques, it’s essential to understand the basics of turkey language. Turkeys have a wide range of vocalizations that they use to communicate. These include clucks, purrs, yelps, cuts, cackles, gobbles, and more. Each vocalization serves a unique purpose and can be used in different hunting situations.

2. Master the Basics: The first step to mastering turkey calling is becoming proficient in the basic calls. The primary calls to learn are the yelp, cluck, and purr. The yelp is a versatile call that turkeys use to communicate with each other. It can be used to locate, calm, or call in a turkey. The cluck is a short, staccato sound that is often used to get a turkey’s attention. The purr is a soft, rolling sound that turkeys use when they are content and relaxed.

To master these calls, practice with various turkey calls until you can imitate them accurately. There are a variety of turkey calls available, including box calls, slate calls, diaphragm calls, and push-button calls. Experiment with different calls and find the one that feels most comfortable for you.

3. The Art of Gobbling: Gobbling is a vocalization that male turkeys use to attract hens during the mating season. It is a powerful sound that can be used effectively to locate gobblers. However, gobbling is an advanced calling technique that requires practice and caution. It is crucial to use gobbling sparingly and in a specific situation, as it can also scare off turkeys if overused.

To imitate the gobble, you can use a gobble tube or a mouth call. Start by practicing the gobble in a controlled environment, gradually increasing your volume and intensity. Remember, gobbling should be used strategically and sparingly, as it can attract both turkeys and other hunters.

4. Calling Tactics: Once you have mastered the basic calls, it’s time to put them into action. The key to effective turkey calling is to mimic the sounds and behavior of real turkeys. Start by listening to recordings of turkey vocalizations to understand the cadence and rhythm of their calls. Pay attention to the timing and spacing between calls, as this can make a significant difference in attracting turkeys.

When calling, start with soft, subtle calls and gradually increase the volume and intensity. Use various calls, such as yelps, clucks, and purrs, to create a realistic scenario. Remember to be patient and give the turkey ample time to respond. Avoid overcalling, as it can alert turkeys to your presence and make them wary.

5. Decoy Placement: Calling techniques are not just about the sounds you make; they also involve visual cues. Using turkey decoys can significantly enhance your calling strategy. Place your decoys strategically to create a realistic scene. Position them in open areas where they can be easily seen by approaching turkeys. Use different types of decoys, such as hens, jakes, or full-body tom decoys, to add realism to your setup.

6. The Power of Silence: While turkey calling is an effective technique, sometimes silence can be equally powerful. Turkeys are naturally inquisitive, and they will often investigate when they hear nothing. Use periods of silence during your calling sequence to create intrigue and make approaching turkeys curious about the source of the sound.

In conclusion, mastering the art of turkey hunting requires a deep understanding of calling techniques. By imitating turkey vocalizations accurately, using different calls strategically, and incorporating visual cues, you can significantly increase your chances of a successful hunt. Practice regularly, experiment with different calls, and study the behavior of turkeys to refine your calling skills. With patience and persistence, you’ll become a proficient caller and improve your turkey hunting game. Happy hunting!
