Top 10 Must-Watch Documentaries on Netflix


Netflix has become a treasure trove of documentaries that capture the intricacies of human life, society, and the world around us. From eye-opening investigations to heartwarming stories, these films offer a diverse range of topics that can educate, inspire, and entertain. Below, we delve into the top 10 must-watch documentaries available on Netflix that you simply cannot miss.

1. The Social Dilemma

In an age where social media dominates our daily lives, “The Social Dilemma” explores the dangerous human impact of social networking. Featuring interviews with tech experts and former employees of major tech companies, this documentary reveals how social media algorithms are designed to manipulate users. It serves as a wake-up call to examine our relationship with technology and the consequences of our digital footprints. The film artfully blends real-life testimonials with dramatizations, making it both informative and engaging.

2. My Octopus Teacher

“My Octopus Teacher” takes viewers on an extraordinary journey into the underwater world of the Great African Sea Forest. Documentarian Craig Foster forms an unlikely bond with an octopus, providing a unique perspective on the intelligence and emotional depth of these creatures. As Foster spends a year observing the octopus’s life, the film beautifully captures the themes of connection, vulnerability, and the wonders of nature. This documentary not only showcases marine life but also serves as a poignant reminder of our need to reconnect with the natural world.

3. 13th

A powerful exploration of race and the criminal justice system in the United States, “13th” highlights the systemic racism that has shaped the nation’s history. Directed by Ava DuVernay, this documentary examines the Thirteenth Amendment, which abolished slavery, and its loophole that allows for involuntary servitude as punishment for a crime. Through a combination of historical footage, expert interviews, and personal stories, the film provocatively addresses the mass incarceration of African Americans and the ongoing impact of racial discrimination. “13th” is a must-watch for anyone seeking a deeper understanding of social justice issues.

4. Wild Wild Country

This captivating docuseries chronicles the rise and fall of the controversial Indian guru Bhagwan Shree Rajneesh and his community in Wasco County, Oregon. “Wild Wild Country” dives into the conflict between the Rajneeshis and the local residents, exploring themes of power, spirituality, and the clash of cultures. With its mix of archival footage and current interviews, the series paints a vivid picture of an extraordinary and tumultuous chapter in American history. The complex characters and dramatic events make it a riveting watch.

5. The Keepers

“The Keepers” is a gripping true-crime documentary that investigates the unsolved murder of Sister Cathy Cesnik, a beloved nun and teacher in Baltimore. As the series unfolds, it delves into the dark secrets of the Catholic Church and the abuse that occurred within its institutions. The documentary is not only an exploration of the murder mystery but also a poignant narrative of the survivors’ quest for justice. With its emotional depth and thorough investigation, “The Keepers” is a haunting yet essential watch.

6. Our Planet

Narrated by the beloved Sir David Attenborough, “Our Planet” is a visually stunning documentary series that showcases the beauty of the Earth’s ecosystems while addressing the urgent issues of climate change. Each episode explores a different habitat, from the frozen Arctic to the lush jungles, highlighting the interconnectedness of life on Earth. The breathtaking cinematography and powerful storytelling aim to inspire viewers to take action in preserving our planet. “Our Planet” is not just a documentary; it’s a call to arms for environmental conservation.

7. Making a Murderer

“Making a Murderer” is a groundbreaking true-crime series that captivated audiences with its exploration of the controversial case of Steven Avery, a man wrongfully convicted of sexual assault. The series follows Avery’s legal battles as he seeks justice while also delving into the complexities of the criminal justice system. With its intricate storytelling and compelling characters, this docuseries raises important questions about morality, justice, and the potential for systemic failures in law enforcement. Its impact on public discourse surrounding criminal justice is undeniable.

8. Icarus

“Icarus” begins as an exploration of doping in sports but quickly evolves into a thrilling investigation into a state-sponsored doping scandal in Russia. Filmmaker Bryan Fogel sets out to expose the dark side of competitive sports, only to uncover a web of corruption that reaches the highest levels of government. The documentary is both a captivating thrill ride and a critical examination of ethics in sports, making it a must-watch for anyone interested in the intersection of politics and athletics.

9. Jiro Dreams of Sushi

This documentary offers a mouthwatering glimpse into the world of Jiro Ono, an esteemed sushi chef in Tokyo. Renowned for his meticulous craftsmanship and unwavering dedication to his craft, Jiro’s story is one of passion, discipline, and the pursuit of perfection. The film not only showcases the artistry of sushi-making but also delves into themes of family, legacy, and the relentless pursuit of excellence. “Jiro Dreams of Sushi” is a feast for the senses and an inspiring look at what it means to dedicate oneself to a craft.

10. The Great Hack

In an era where data is the new oil, “The Great Hack” explores the dark side of social media and its influence on democracy. This documentary delves into the Cambridge Analytica scandal, where the personal data of millions of Facebook users was harvested for political campaigns. Through personal stories and expert insights, the film raises critical questions about privacy, consent, and the power of information in shaping public opinion. “The Great Hack” serves as a cautionary tale about the complexities of the digital age and the need for accountability.

These ten documentaries represent just a fraction of the incredible content available on Netflix. Each film and series not only entertains but also challenges viewers to think critically about the world around them. Whether you’re interested in social justice, nature, true crime, or the art of cooking, there’s something for everyone in this diverse selection of must-watch documentaries. Grab your popcorn, settle in, and prepare to be inspired and informed by these powerful stories.
