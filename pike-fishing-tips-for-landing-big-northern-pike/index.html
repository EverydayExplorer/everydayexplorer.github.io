Pike Fishing: Tips for Landing Big Northern Pike


Pike fishing is a thrilling and challenging sport that attracts anglers from all over the world. Known for their aggressive strikes and powerful fighting abilities, northern pike are a highly sought-after species. However, landing a big northern pike requires patience, skill, and the right techniques. In this blog post, we will discuss some valuable tips that can improve your chances of landing that trophy-sized pike.

1. Choose the Right Fishing Gear:
When targeting big northern pike, it’s essential to have the right fishing gear. A medium to heavy-action spinning or baitcasting rod paired with a sturdy reel is recommended. Opt for a reel with a high line capacity and a strong drag system as pike can put up a fierce fight. Use a braided fishing line with a higher pound test to handle the pike’s sharp teeth and minimize the risk of getting your line cut.

2. Target Pike in the Right Locations:
Knowing where to find pike is crucial for a successful fishing trip. These apex predators inhabit shallow weedy areas, rocky reefs, submerged vegetation, and drop-offs. Look for areas with adequate cover, such as fallen trees, weed beds, or underwater structure, as pike often use these areas as ambush points. Pay attention to water temperature and focus your efforts in spring and fall when pike are more active.

3. Make Use of Effective Lures:
Using the right lures can significantly increase your chances of enticing a big northern pike to strike. Pike are aggressive predators that are attracted to large, flashy baits that mimic their favorite prey. Popular lure choices include spoons, crankbaits, spinnerbaits, and topwater lures. Experiment with different colors, sizes, and retrieve speeds to determine what pike are responding to on that particular day.

4. Master the Art of Pike Fishing Techniques:
To effectively target northern pike, it’s crucial to master some specialized fishing techniques. One popular technique is ‘jigging’ where you vertically present a jig or soft plastic bait near structures or drop-offs. Another effective technique is ’twitching’ where you jerk the bait with quick, erratic movements to mimic a wounded fish. Additionally, mastering the cast and retrieve technique with spinners or crankbaits can be highly effective for covering a large area of water.

5. Practice Proper Hookset and Landing Techniques:
When a big northern pike strikes, it’s important to execute a proper hookset to ensure the fish is firmly hooked. When using treble-hook lures, it’s recommended to wait until you feel the weight of the fish before setting the hook with a solid upward motion. Once hooked, maintain steady pressure on the fish to tire it out before attempting to land it. Use a landing net with a wide opening to secure the pike safely and avoid any potential injuries.

6. Practice Catch and Release:
Conservation is crucial for keeping the pike population healthy. Catch and release practices help maintain the balance in the ecosystem and ensure future generations of anglers can enjoy pike fishing. Handle the fish carefully, minimizing their time out of the water, and use appropriate tools like long-nose pliers to safely remove the hook. Take quick photographs if desired and release the pike back into the water gently.

Conclusion:
Landing a big northern pike is an exhilarating experience that requires skill, knowledge, and the right techniques. By choosing the right gear, targeting the right locations, using effective lures, mastering specialized techniques, and practicing proper hookset and landing techniques, you can significantly enhance your chances of success. Remember to always practice catch and release to preserve the sport for future generations. So, grab your fishing gear, head to your favorite pike fishing spot, and put these tips into action to potentially land your next trophy-sized pike!
