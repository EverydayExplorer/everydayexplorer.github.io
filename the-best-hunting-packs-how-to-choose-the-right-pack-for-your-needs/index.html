The Best Hunting Packs: How to Choose the Right Pack for Your Needs


When it comes to hunting, having the right gear is absolutely crucial. And one of the most important gear items for any serious hunter is a hunting pack. A hunting pack is not just a bag to carry your equipment; it is an essential tool that can make or break your hunting experience. It needs to be durable, comfortable, and functional, while also being able to accommodate all your gear. But with so many options available in the market, how do you choose the right hunting pack for your needs? In this blog post, we will discuss the key factors to consider when selecting a hunting pack and highlight some of the best options on the market.

1. Size and Capacity

The first thing you need to determine when choosing a hunting pack is its size and capacity. This will depend on the type of hunting you will be doing and the amount of gear you will need to carry. If you are planning on doing day hunts, a smaller pack with a capacity of 20 to 30 liters may be sufficient. However, if you are going on extended hunting trips or carrying bulky gear, you may need a larger pack with a capacity of 50 liters or more. Make sure to consider both the main compartment size and the availability of additional pockets and storage options.

2. Design and Features

The design and features of a hunting pack can greatly impact its functionality and usability. Look for a pack that is designed specifically for hunting and has features that align with your needs. Some key features to consider include:

  • Weapon carry system: If you need to carry a rifle or a bow, look for a pack with a dedicated weapon carry system. This will ensure your weapon stays secure and easily accessible.

  • Compression straps: Compression straps are essential for keeping your pack tight and eliminating any excess movement. They also allow you to adjust the pack’s volume depending on your load.

  • Access points: Look for a pack with multiple access points, including top-loading, front-loading, and side-loading options. This will make it easier to access different parts of your gear without having to empty the entire pack.

  • Hydration compatibility: Staying hydrated is crucial during a hunt. Look for a pack that is compatible with a hydration bladder, so you can easily drink water on the go.

3. Comfort and Fit

Comfort is another important factor to consider when choosing a hunting pack. You will be carrying the pack for long periods of time, so it needs to be comfortable and ergonomic. Look for a pack with padded shoulder straps and a well-padded hip belt. The shoulder straps should be adjustable to fit your body properly, and the hip belt should distribute the weight evenly to reduce strain on your back and shoulders. Additionally, many hunting packs come with a suspension system that helps to keep the weight stable and close to your body.

4. Durability and Weather Resistance

Hunting packs are subjected to rough conditions, so durability is crucial. Look for a pack made from high-quality materials such as nylon or polyester. These materials are not only durable but also water-resistant, which is important for protecting your gear from rain, snow, or accidental spills.

5. Price and Brand Reputation

Lastly, consider your budget and the reputation of the brand. While it is tempting to go for the cheapest option, investing in a high-quality hunting pack will likely be more cost-effective in the long run. Look for reputable brands that are known for their quality and customer satisfaction. Read reviews and seek recommendations from experienced hunters to ensure you are choosing a pack that will stand the test of time.

Now that we have covered the key factors to consider, let’s highlight some of the best hunting packs available on the market:

  • Mystery Ranch Metcalf: Known for its exceptional quality and durability, this pack offers a generous capacity of 71 liters and is designed for multi-day hunts.

  • Sitka Mountain Hauler 4000: If you need a pack with a large capacity for packing out meat, this is an excellent choice. It has a unique frame design that allows you to carry heavy loads comfortably.

  • Eberlestock X2: This versatile pack is perfect for both day hunts and overnight trips. It has a unique back panel that allows you to carry a bow or a rifle securely.

  • Badlands 2200: Renowned for its comfort and ample storage options, this pack is ideal for day hunts. It also features a built-in meat shelf for hauling out game.

  • Alps OutdoorZ Pursuit: If you are on a budget, this pack offers great value for the price. It has a capacity of 44 liters and features a drop-down pocket for carrying a bow or a rifle.

In conclusion, choosing the right hunting pack is essential for a successful and enjoyable hunt. Consider factors such as size and capacity, design and features, comfort and fit, durability and weather resistance, as well as price and brand reputation. By taking these factors into account and exploring some of the top options available on the market, you can find a hunting pack that meets your needs and enhances your hunting experience. Happy hunting!
