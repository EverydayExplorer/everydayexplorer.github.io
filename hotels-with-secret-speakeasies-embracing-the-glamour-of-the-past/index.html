Hotels with Secret Speakeasies: Embracing the Glamour of the Past


Step back in time and immerse yourself in the hidden world of secret speakeasies. These underground bars, made famous during the Prohibition era, offer a unique and glamorous experience that transports you to the Roaring Twenties. In this blog post, we will explore some of the top hotels that have embraced the allure of the past by featuring secret speakeasies within their walls. Get ready to don your best attire, sip on expertly crafted cocktails, and embrace the nostalgia of a bygone era.

  1. The Beekman, New York City Tucked away inside The Beekman hotel in downtown Manhattan lies a hidden gem known as the Alley Cat Amateur Theatre. This speakeasy pays homage to the building’s rich history as a theater district hotspot in the 1920s. To gain access, guests must find the unmarked door leading to a dimly lit staircase. Once inside, they are transported to a world of vintage glamour and jazz-inspired vibes. The Alley Cat offers a menu of classic cocktails with a modern twist, paired with live jazz performances that set the perfect ambience for an unforgettable evening.

  2. The Savoy, London The American Bar at The Savoy is an iconic institution that has been serving up its signature cocktails since 1893. However, hidden behind a nondescript door within the bar is the secret Beaufort Bar. In the 1920s, this space was the meeting place for the high society of London, and today it continues to exude an air of elegance and sophistication. The Beaufort Bar features Art Deco styling, plush velvet seating, and a dazzling black-and-gold color scheme. Step inside and enjoy a curated menu of exquisite libations while being serenaded by live music in an atmosphere that truly captures the spirit of the Roaring Twenties.

  3. The Langham, Chicago At The Langham, Chicago, guests can embark on a journey back in time with a visit to the hidden speakeasy known as The Tippling Hall. This intimate and secretive bar is accessible only through a discreet entrance disguised as a bookcase within the hotel’s main lobby. Once inside, patrons are transported to a world of opulence and exclusivity. The Tippling Hall boasts an extensive list of craft cocktails, premium spirits, and rare liquors, all served in a setting reminiscent of a private club from the Prohibition era. Indulge in the luxurious surroundings and savor the flavors of the past in this hidden gem.

  4. Hotel Del Coronado, San Diego The Hotel Del Coronado is not only a historic landmark but also home to a hidden speakeasy known as The Club Room. Tucked away in the basement, this secret bar pays homage to the hotel’s colorful past during the Prohibition era. The Club Room features a vintage-inspired decor, complete with reclaimed wood, leather banquettes, and a cozy fireplace. Sample handcrafted cocktails made with carefully selected ingredients, and let the ambiance whisk you away to a time when the illicit thrill of a secret gathering added an extra layer of excitement to every sip.

  5. The Ritz Paris, France The Ritz Paris is a bastion of luxury and refinement, and hidden within its walls is the legendary Bar Hemingway. Named after the famed writer who frequented the bar during his time in Paris, this secret speakeasy offers a glimpse into the city’s glamorous past. The Bar Hemingway is renowned for its elegant decor, attentive service, and expertly crafted cocktails. Sip on a perfectly mixed drink while surrounded by vintage photographs, leather armchairs, and a warm, inviting atmosphere that harkens back to the golden age of Parisian nightlife.

In conclusion, hotels with secret speakeasies allow guests to step back in time and embrace the glamour and excitement of the Prohibition era. From hidden entrances to vintage-inspired decor, these underground bars offer a unique and immersive experience that transports you to another era. So, if you’re looking for a touch of nostalgia and a taste of the past, venture into these secret speakeasies within hotels and let yourself be captivated by the allure of a bygone time.
