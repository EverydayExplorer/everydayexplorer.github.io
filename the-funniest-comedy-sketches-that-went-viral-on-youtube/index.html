The Funniest Comedy Sketches That Went Viral on YouTube


Comedy has always been a popular genre within the entertainment industry. From television sitcoms to stand-up specials, there are countless ways for people to get their laughter fix. However, in recent years, one platform has risen to the forefront as a hub for comedic content - YouTube.

YouTube has become a breeding ground for comedians and actors to showcase their talent and connect with a global audience. The platform’s accessibility and vast user base have made it the perfect place for comedy sketches to go viral. In this blog post, we will take a look at some of the funniest comedy sketches that have become internet sensations on YouTube.

1. ‘The Ultimate Meme Mashup’ by Smosh:
Smosh, one of the earliest comedy sketch channels on YouTube, struck gold with their hilarious ‘The Ultimate Meme Mashup’ video. In this sketch, they seamlessly weave together various internet memes, creating an uproarious and endlessly quotable video that resonated with viewers across the globe. From ‘Rickrolling’ to ‘Nyan Cat,’ this sketch took memes to a whole new level of entertainment.

2. ‘Key & Peele - Substitute Teacher’ by Comedy Central:
The comedy duo Key & Peele became a household name with their brilliant sketch series on Comedy Central. One sketch, in particular, ‘Substitute Teacher,’ took the internet by storm. In this sketch, Jordan Peele plays a substitute teacher who struggles to pronounce his students’ names correctly, leading to hilarious situations. The sketch’s clever writing, impeccable timing, and brilliant performances catapulted it to viral status and solidified Key & Peele’s place in comedic history.

3. ‘The Lonely Island - I’m on a Boat’ featuring T-Pain:
‘The Lonely Island,’ a comedy trio consisting of Andy Samberg, Akiva Schaffer, and Jorma Taccone, gained massive popularity on YouTube with their catchy and hilarious music videos. Their sketch ‘I’m on a Boat,’ featuring Grammy-winning artist T-Pain, became an internet sensation. The combination of the ridiculous lyrics, infectious autotune melodies, and over-the-top visuals made this sketch an instant hit and cemented The Lonely Island’s reputation as masters of comedy music.

4. ‘SNL Digital Short - Lazy Sunday’ by Saturday Night Live:
Saturday Night Live has been a breeding ground for comedic talent for decades. In 2005, they revolutionized the digital sketch game with their sketch ‘Lazy Sunday.’ This hilarious music video, featuring SNL cast members Andy Samberg and Chris Parnell rapping about their love for cupcakes and ‘The Chronicles of Narnia,’ became a viral sensation. It showcased the power of YouTube as a platform for comedy and propelled Samberg’s career to new heights.

5. ‘Epic Rap Battles of History - Barack Obama vs. Mitt Romney’:
‘Epic Rap Battles of History’ is a YouTube series that pits historical figures against each other in hilarious rap battles. The sketch featuring Barack Obama and Mitt Romney garnered millions of views and widespread acclaim. The clever lyrics, spot-on impressions, and witty banter between the two characters made this sketch a standout and showcased the talent and creativity of the creators, Nice Peter and EpicLLOYD.

In conclusion, YouTube has become the go-to platform for comedy sketches to reach an enormous audience and attain viral status. The sketches mentioned above are just a few examples of the countless hilarious videos that have taken the internet by storm. From established comedians to up-and-coming creators, YouTube continues to be a breeding ground for comedy talent, providing a platform for laughter and entertainment for people around the world.
