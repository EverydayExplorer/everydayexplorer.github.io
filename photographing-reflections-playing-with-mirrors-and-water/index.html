Photographing Reflections: Playing with Mirrors and Water


Photography is an art form that allows us to capture the beauty of the world around us in unique and creative ways. One fascinating aspect of photography is capturing reflections, whether it’s through the use of mirrors or the natural surface of water. In this blog post, we will explore the techniques and creative possibilities of photographing reflections, and how to play with mirrors and water to create stunning images.

Understanding Reflections

Reflections occur when light bounces off a surface and returns to the camera or the observer’s eyes. When it comes to photography, reflections can add depth, interest, and a sense of surrealism to an image. By understanding how reflections work, photographers can use this phenomenon to their advantage, creating captivating and thought-provoking compositions.

Playing with Mirrors

Mirrors are versatile tools for photographers, offering endless creative possibilities for capturing reflections. When using mirrors in photography, consider experimenting with angles and perspectives to create unique compositions. Reflective surfaces can be used to create symmetrical images, or to distort reality and challenge the viewer’s perception. Additionally, mirrors can be used to reflect light onto a subject, adding a dramatic and ethereal quality to the photograph.

When photographing reflections using mirrors, it’s important to pay attention to the surrounding environment and how it interacts with the reflection. Incorporating elements such as architecture, nature, or urban landscapes can add depth and context to the image, turning a simple reflection into a storytelling element.

Exploring Water Reflections

Water, whether it’s a calm lake, a flowing river, or a gentle puddle, provides a natural canvas for capturing mesmerizing reflections. When photographing water reflections, consider the time of day and weather conditions, as they can greatly influence the appearance of the reflection. For example, still water on a windless day can produce a mirror-like reflection, while ripples or waves can create a more abstract and dynamic effect.

To enhance water reflections in photography, experiment with long exposure techniques to capture the movement of the water and the reflection simultaneously. This can create a dreamy and surreal atmosphere in the photograph, blurring the line between reality and illusion. Additionally, incorporating elements such as colorful foliage, buildings, or the sky into the water reflection can add visual interest and context to the image.

Creative Tips for Photographing Reflections

1. Experiment with different angles and perspectives to find the most captivating reflection.
2. Utilize natural and artificial light to enhance the appearance of the reflection and create a mood.
3. Look for interesting juxtapositions and compositions within the reflection and its surroundings.
4. Consider using a polarizing filter to reduce glare and enhance the clarity of the reflection, especially when photographing water.

In conclusion, photographing reflections by playing with mirrors and water offers a wealth of creative opportunities for photographers. Whether capturing the symmetry of a mirrored surface or the fluidity of a water reflection, exploring the world of reflections can lead to captivating and visually stunning images. By understanding the principles of reflections and experimenting with creative techniques, photographers can elevate their work and create compelling visual stories that resonate with viewers on a deeper level.
