Haunted Hotels: Where Guests Encounter the Unexplained


Have you ever wondered what it’s like to stay in a haunted hotel? To experience the thrill of encountering the unexplained while being surrounded by history and mystery? If you’re a fan of the supernatural or enjoy a good scare, then haunted hotels might be right up your alley. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most famous haunted hotels around the world, where guests have reported eerie encounters and unexplained phenomena.

1. The Stanley Hotel - Estes Park, Colorado

The Stanley Hotel is perhaps one of the most famous haunted hotels in the United States, thanks to its connection with Stephen King’s novel, ‘The Shining.’ Located in Estes Park, Colorado, this historic hotel has a rich history and countless stories of paranormal activity. From the sounds of children running and laughing in the hallways to furniture moving on its own, staying at The Stanley Hotel is not for the faint of heart.

2. The Fairmont Banff Springs - Banff, Alberta, Canada

Nestled in the heart of the Canadian Rockies, The Fairmont Banff Springs is widely regarded as one of the most haunted hotels in Canada. With a history dating back to 1888, this grand hotel is said to be haunted by several spirits, including a bride who mysteriously died on her wedding day. Guests have reported seeing apparitions, hearing footsteps in empty corridors, and feeling cold spots throughout the hotel.

3. The Queen Mary - Long Beach, California

Once a luxurious ocean liner, The Queen Mary now resides permanently in Long Beach, California, where it has been transformed into a hotel. However, this ship-turned-hotel has a dark and haunted past. The Queen Mary is believed to be haunted by the spirits of those who perished on board during its time as a transatlantic liner. Visitors and guests have reported encounters with apparitions, unexplained knocking sounds, and objects moving seemingly on their own.

4. The Langham Sydney - Sydney, Australia

Located in the heart of Sydney, The Langham Sydney is a stunning hotel that also happens to be haunted. With a history dating back to 1851, this hotel is said to be haunted by the ghost of a former guest who tragically fell to his death from one of the upper floors. Guests have reported seeing his ghostly figure wandering the hallways and experiencing strange sensations in their rooms.

5. Hotel Burchianti - Florence, Italy

If you’re planning a trip to Italy and looking for a unique and haunted accommodation option, look no further than Hotel Burchianti in Florence. This historic hotel is not only known for its exquisite Renaissance architecture but also for its ghostly inhabitants. Guests have reported encountering apparitions, hearing strange noises, and feeling an eerie presence in their rooms. One of the most famous ghostly residents is a young girl who has been seen playing on the grand staircase.

6. The Savoy Hotel - London, England

Situated in the heart of London, The Savoy Hotel is not only known for its luxurious accommodations but also for its haunted reputation. Over the years, guests have reported numerous ghostly encounters, including sightings of famous historical figures such as poet Oscar Wilde and actress Sarah Bernhardt. The hotel’s history and its connection to these famous personalities make The Savoy a must-visit for paranormal enthusiasts.

7. The Crescent Hotel - Eureka Springs, Arkansas

Tucked away in the picturesque town of Eureka Springs, Arkansas, The Crescent Hotel is a stunning Victorian-era hotel that has gained a reputation as one of America’s most haunted hotels. Built in 1886, this hotel has a dark past, including an alleged fraudulent cancer treatment center in the 1930s. It is said that the spirits of those who died during this time still linger within the hotel’s walls, making it a hotspot for paranormal activity.

8. The Hollywood Roosevelt Hotel - Los Angeles, California

Last but not least, we have The Hollywood Roosevelt Hotel in Los Angeles, California. With its rich history and proximity to Hollywood’s entertainment industry, it’s no wonder that this hotel has a haunted reputation. According to legends, the spirit of Marilyn Monroe herself has been seen in a full-length mirror that was once in her suite. Guests have also reported encounters with other ghostly spirits throughout the hotel’s corridors and rooms.

In conclusion, if you’re looking for a unique and spine-chilling experience, staying in a haunted hotel might be just the thing for you. From the historic Stanley Hotel in Colorado to the glamorous Hollywood Roosevelt Hotel in California, these haunted accommodations offer a thrilling blend of history, mystery, and the paranormal. Whether you’re a believer or a skeptic, these hotels provide an unforgettable experience that is sure to leave you questioning the boundaries between the world of the living and the world of the dead.
