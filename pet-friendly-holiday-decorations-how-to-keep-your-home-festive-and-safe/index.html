Pet-Friendly Holiday Decorations: How to Keep Your Home Festive and Safe


The holiday season is a time for joy, festivities, and spending quality time with loved ones—including our furry friends. As you deck the halls with boughs of holly and trim the tree with twinkling lights, it’s important to consider the safety and comfort of your pets amidst the seasonal decor. After all, nothing puts a damper on the holidays quite like a trip to the vet because of a mishap with holiday decorations.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore pet-friendly holiday decorations and how you can keep your home festive and safe for your four-legged family members.

1. Opt for Pet-Safe Materials

When choosing holiday decorations, opt for items made from pet-safe materials. Avoid decorations that are made of glass, fragile materials, or small pieces that can easily be chewed or swallowed by curious pets. Instead, look for decorations made from pet-friendly materials such as fabric, wood, or non-toxic materials.

2. Secure Your Christmas Tree

The Christmas tree is often the centerpiece of holiday decor, but it can pose several hazards to pets. To keep your tree pet-friendly, make sure it is securely anchored to prevent it from toppling over if your pet decides to investigate. Consider using a sturdy tree stand or even tethering the tree to the wall for added stability.

Additionally, be mindful of the decorations you use on your tree. Avoid using tinsel, as it can be tempting for pets to play with and can cause serious health issues if ingested. Opt for pet-friendly ornaments made of sturdy materials that won’t break easily if knocked off the tree.

3. Keep Wires and Cords Out of Reach

Holiday lights can add a festive touch to your home, but they also pose a potential danger to pets. Keep wires and cords out of reach of curious pets to prevent them from chewing on them and risking electric shock. Consider using cord covers or hiding cords behind furniture to keep them out of sight and out of reach.

4. Choose Pet-Safe Plants

Many popular holiday plants, such as poinsettias, mistletoe, and holly, can be toxic to pets if ingested. When decorating with plants, choose pet-safe options such as Christmas cactus, spider plants, or Boston ferns. If you’re unsure if a plant is safe for pets, do some research or consult with your veterinarian before bringing it into your home.

5. Create a Safe Haven

With all the hustle and bustle of the holiday season, it’s important to create a safe haven for your pets where they can retreat and relax away from the excitement. Set up a cozy bed or blanket in a quiet corner of your home where your pets can escape to when they need a break from the festivities.

6. Avoid Scented Candles and Potpourri

While scented candles and potpourri can add a lovely aroma to your home, they can also pose a risk to pets. Some scents can be irritating or even toxic to pets if inhaled or ingested. Opt for unscented candles or pet-safe alternatives to keep your home smelling festive without putting your pets at risk.

7. Supervise Holiday Gatherings

If you’re hosting holiday gatherings or parties, make sure to supervise your pets around guests and food. Some holiday foods, such as chocolate, nuts, and certain fruits, can be toxic to pets if ingested. Keep an eye on your pets to prevent them from sneaking any forbidden treats and consider keeping them in a separate room or crate during the festivities if they are easily stressed or overwhelmed.

By following these tips, you can create a festive and pet-friendly environment in your home this holiday season. With a little planning and consideration, you can ensure that your furry friends stay safe and happy amidst the holiday cheer.
