Writing Fantasy for Children: Tips and Tricks


Writing fantasy stories for children can be a tremendously rewarding experience. It allows you to unleash your imagination and create magical worlds where anything is possible. However, writing for children comes with its own set of challenges. You need to strike the right balance between enchantment and clarity, captivating young minds while still delivering a well-constructed narrative. In this blog post, we will explore some helpful tips and tricks to guide you on your journey of writing fantasy for children.

1. Understand Your Audience

Before diving into the magical realms of your fantasy story, it is crucial to have a deep understanding of your target audience. Consider the age range of children you are writing for, as this will influence the complexity of your language, themes, and plot. Younger children may require simpler language and more straightforward storytelling, while older children can handle more intricate plots and complex ideas.

2. Create Engaging Characters

Children are drawn to characters they can relate to and root for. When crafting your characters, make them relatable, likable, and age-appropriate. Develop their personalities, quirks, and motivations in a way that resonates with your target audience. Give them unique abilities, flaws, and challenges that young readers can connect with and learn from.

3. Build a Fascinating World

The world in which your story unfolds is a crucial aspect of writing fantasy. Develop a vivid and detailed setting that sparks the imagination of young readers. Consider creating a map, designing fantastical creatures, and establishing the rules that govern your magical world. Descriptions should be vivid and engaging, transporting children to a place they can envision and explore in their minds.

4. Use Simple and Clear Language

While fantasy stories allow for imaginative language and unconventional concepts, it is important to keep the language simple and clear when writing for children. Avoid using overly complicated words or convoluted sentences that may confuse or frustrate young readers. Stick to age-appropriate vocabulary and use descriptive language that paints a vivid picture without overwhelming their comprehension.

5. Maintain a Fast-Paced Storytelling

Children have shorter attention spans, so it’s important to maintain a fast-paced and engaging narrative throughout your fantasy story. Avoid unnecessary tangents or lengthy descriptions that can slow down the pace. Each chapter should end with a cliffhanger or a significant event to keep children eagerly turning the pages.

6. Introduce Moral Lessons

Fantasy stories for children can offer valuable moral lessons and teach important life values. Integrate themes of friendship, bravery, kindness, and perseverance within your narrative. These moral lessons can be subtly woven into the story, allowing children to learn and reflect upon them while enjoying the enchantment of the fantasy world.

7. Incorporate Humor

Humor is an effective tool for engaging young readers. Children love to laugh, so injecting humor into your writing can make your story more appealing. Create humorous situations, witty dialogues, or funny characters that will bring smiles to children’s faces and keep them entertained.

8. Test Your Story with Children

After completing your fantasy story, it is highly beneficial to get feedback from your target audience - children. Allow them to read your manuscript and observe their reactions. Do they understand the story? Are they captivated? Do they connect with the characters? Such feedback will help you identify any areas for improvement and ensure your story resonates with its intended readership.

9. Edit and Revise

The process of writing doesn’t end with the completion of the first draft. Revision and editing are essential for crafting a polished and engaging fantasy story. Pay attention to pacing, dialogue, character development, and overall flow. Remove any unnecessary elements or inconsistencies. Polish your language to make it more captivating and engaging for young readers.

10. Tap into Your Inner Child

Finally, one of the most important tips for writing fantasy for children is to tap into your own inner child. Remember the wonder and excitement you experienced as a child when diving into imaginary worlds. Allow your imagination to run wild and bring forth the magic that will captivate young readers. By connecting with the child within you, you can create a story that truly resonates with children.

As you embark on the journey of writing fantasy for children, remember to have fun and let your imagination soar. By following these tips and tricks, you can create a captivating and enchanting story that will transport young readers to a world of magic and wonder. So go ahead, start writing, and unleash your creative powers!
