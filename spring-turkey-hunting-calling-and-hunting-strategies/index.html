Spring Turkey Hunting: Calling and Hunting Strategies


Spring turkey hunting is an exhilarating outdoor activity that attracts millions of hunters each year. With its combination of exciting calling techniques and strategic hunting strategies, it’s no wonder why so many hunters are drawn to this sport. In this blog post, we will delve into the world of spring turkey hunting, focusing on the art of calling and effective hunting strategies. Whether you are a beginner or an experienced hunter, this guide will provide valuable insights to help you up your game and increase your chances of success.

The Importance of Calling in Spring Turkey Hunting

Calling is one of the most critical elements of spring turkey hunting. Effective calling techniques can be the difference between a successful hunt and a missed opportunity. Understanding the various types of calls and when to use them can greatly enhance your chances of luring in a mature gobbler.

Types of Turkey Calls

There are several different types of turkey calls, each serving a specific purpose:

  1. Box Calls: A box call is a popular choice for many hunters due to its versatility and ease of use. It consists of a wooden box with a movable paddle that creates different turkey sounds when rubbed against the side of the box.

  2. Slate Calls: Slate calls, also known as pot calls, are made of a circular slate or glass surface and are played with a striker. They produce a range of realistic turkey sounds, making them a favorite among experienced hunters.

  3. Diaphragm Calls: Diaphragm calls, also called mouth calls, are inserted into the mouth and rely on air pressure and tongue placement to produce a wide array of turkey sounds. While they require some practice to use effectively, they offer hands-free operation and are ideal for close-quarters hunting.

  4. Push/Pull Calls: Push/pull calls are compact and user-friendly, making them a great choice for beginners. They consist of a peg that is pushed or pulled to create turkey sounds.

  5. Locator Calls: Locator calls, such as owl hooters and crow calls, are not used to imitate turkeys but rather to locate them. These calls can be effective in locating gobblers in the early morning or late afternoon.

Understanding Turkey Vocalizations

To effectively call in turkeys, it is crucial to understand and replicate their vocalizations. Here are some common turkey sounds and their meanings:

  1. Gobbles: Gobbles are the unmistakable sound of a mature male turkey, also known as a gobbler. Gobbles are used to attract hens and to establish territory.

  2. Yelps: Yelps are versatile calls that can be used by both male and female turkeys. They are commonly used to locate other turkeys and maintain contact within a flock.

  3. Clucks: Clucks are short, staccato-like vocalizations used by both male and female turkeys. They can convey a variety of emotions and are frequently employed when turkeys are in close proximity to one another.

  4. Purrs: Purrs are soft, rolling sounds made by contented turkeys. They are often associated with feeding and can be used to calm a wary gobbler or communicate with other turkeys.

  5. Cutts: Cutts are sharp, rapid calls that indicate excitement or agitation. They are commonly made when a turkey is alarmed or is attempting to assert dominance.

Mastering Turkey Calling Techniques

To master the art of turkey calling, it is essential to practice regularly and learn the intricacies of different calls. Here are some tips for effective turkey calling:

  1. Start with the Basics: Begin by learning simple yelps, clucks, and purrs, as these are the foundational calls for turkey hunting. Once you have mastered these, you can move on to more advanced techniques.

  2. Match the Mood: Pay attention to the turkey’s mood and adjust your calling accordingly. Soft purrs and gentle yelps work well when turkeys are calm, while aggressive yelps and cutts can be effective when a gobbler is fired up.

  3. Use Decoys: Decoys can be powerful tools in conjunction with your calling. Adding realistic turkey decoys to your setup can help draw in curious gobblers and provide a visual target for them.

  4. Be Patient: Sometimes, it takes time for turkeys to respond to your calls. Don’t be discouraged if you aren’t getting immediate results. Sit tight and wait for the turkeys to come to you.

Effective Spring Turkey Hunting Strategies

In addition to mastering turkey calling, employing effective hunting strategies can greatly increase your chances of success. Here are some valuable tips to consider:

  1. Scouting: Prior to the season, spend time scouting your hunting area to locate roosting sites, feeding areas, and travel routes. Knowing the turkeys’ patterns will help you position yourself in the best possible location.

  2. Set up Early: Arriving at your hunting location well before daybreak allows you to get in position before the turkeys start moving. Finding a good vantage point and setting up your decoys early can give you a better chance of intercepting the turkeys.

  3. Blend in with Your Surroundings: Turkeys have exceptional eyesight, so it is crucial to blend in with your surroundings. Wear camouflage clothing that matches the vegetation in your hunting area and use a facemask or face paint to conceal your face.

  4. Practice Patience and Stealth: Once you are set up and ready to hunt, patience is key. Avoid unnecessary movements and minimize noise, as turkeys can be easily spooked. Wait for the perfect shot opportunity before taking aim.

  5. Utilize Natural Land Features: Take advantage of natural land features, such as hills, ridges, and draws, to get closer to turkeys undetected. Using these features to your advantage can allow for a more successful and rewarding hunt.

Conclusion

Spring turkey hunting is an incredible pursuit that requires a combination of calling skills and effective hunting strategies. By mastering different turkey calls and understanding their vocalizations, you can attract gobblers and increase your chances of success. Remember to practice regularly and be patient, as turkey hunting can be a challenging endeavor. Employing effective hunting strategies, such as scouting, early setups, and blending in with your surroundings, will further enhance your chances of a successful hunt. So prepare yourself for an exciting adventure in the woods, and may your spring turkey hunting season be full of thrilling moments and bountiful harvests!
