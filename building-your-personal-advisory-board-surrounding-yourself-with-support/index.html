Building Your Personal Advisory Board: Surrounding Yourself with Support


In the journey of life, navigating through its twists and turns can often feel like a solitary endeavor. Whether you’re pursuing personal goals or striving for professional success, having a strong support system can make all the difference. While friends and family are undoubtedly valuable sources of support, there’s another resource that can provide unique insights and guidance: a personal advisory board.

What exactly is a personal advisory board, you ask? Think of it as a handpicked group of individuals who bring diverse perspectives, expertise, and experience to the table to help you navigate life’s challenges and opportunities. Much like a company’s board of directors, your personal advisory board serves as a trusted counsel, offering wisdom, encouragement, and accountability as you pursue your goals.

But how do you go about building your own personal advisory board? Here are some steps to help you assemble a team of advisors who will support and empower you on your journey:

  1. Identify Your Needs and Goals: Before you start recruiting members for your personal advisory board, take some time to reflect on your needs and goals. What areas of your life or career could benefit from additional guidance or support? Whether you’re looking to advance in your career, navigate a major life transition, or improve your overall well-being, clarifying your objectives will help you identify the right advisors to help you achieve them.

  2. Diversify Your Board: Just like a well-rounded investment portfolio, your personal advisory board should include individuals with diverse backgrounds, skills, and perspectives. Consider including people from different industries, professions, age groups, and cultural backgrounds to ensure that you receive a wide range of insights and advice. Diversity fosters creativity, innovation, and problem-solving, making your advisory board more effective and robust.

  3. Choose Wisely: When selecting members for your personal advisory board, prioritize quality over quantity. Look for individuals who possess the expertise, integrity, and willingness to support and challenge you in constructive ways. Seek out people who have achieved success in areas that are relevant to your goals, whether it’s entrepreneurship, leadership, personal development, or wellness. Consider reaching out to mentors, colleagues, former teachers, or community leaders who inspire you and whose values align with yours.

  4. Build Genuine Relationships: Building a personal advisory board is not just about assembling a group of advisors; it’s about cultivating meaningful relationships based on trust, mutual respect, and reciprocity. Take the time to get to know each member of your advisory board on a personal level, sharing your goals, aspirations, and challenges openly and honestly. Show genuine interest in their lives and experiences, and be willing to offer support and guidance in return.

  5. Establish Clear Expectations: To ensure that your personal advisory board operates effectively, it’s essential to establish clear expectations and boundaries from the outset. Clearly communicate your goals, objectives, and the role you envision for each member of your advisory board. Discuss how often you’ll meet, the format of your meetings (in person, virtual, or a combination of both), and any specific topics or issues you’d like to address. Setting clear expectations upfront will help prevent misunderstandings and ensure that everyone is on the same page.

  6. Regularly Review and Refresh Your Board: As your goals and circumstances evolve, so too should your personal advisory board. Regularly review the composition of your board to ensure that it remains relevant and aligned with your current needs and aspirations. Be open to adding new members or rotating out existing ones as needed to ensure that you continue to receive the support and guidance you require.

  7. Show Gratitude and Appreciation: Last but not least, don’t forget to show gratitude and appreciation to the members of your personal advisory board for their time, wisdom, and support. Acknowledge their contributions, celebrate their successes, and express your gratitude for the role they play in helping you achieve your goals. A little appreciation goes a long way in strengthening relationships and fostering a sense of camaraderie and mutual respect.

In conclusion, building your personal advisory board is a powerful way to surround yourself with support, guidance, and inspiration as you navigate the ups and downs of life. By assembling a diverse team of advisors who share your values and aspirations, you’ll have access to a wealth of wisdom, experience, and perspective to help you achieve your goals and fulfill your potential. So take the time to identify your needs, choose your advisors wisely, and invest in cultivating meaningful relationships that will support and empower you on your journey toward success and fulfillment.
