Exploring Wine Auction Etiquette: Dos and Don'ts for Bidders


Wine auctions are a thrilling experience for wine enthusiasts and collectors alike. As you step into the world of wine auctions, it’s important to familiarize yourself with the proper etiquette to ensure a seamless and enjoyable bidding process. In this blog post, we will delve into the dos and don’ts of wine auction etiquette, providing you with valuable insights to navigate this unique world confidently.

1. Do Your Research:
Before participating in a wine auction, it’s crucial to conduct thorough research. Familiarize yourself with the wines that will be featured, the auction house’s reputation, and the estimated prices of the bottles you are interested in. This will help you set realistic expectations and make informed decisions during the bidding process.

2. Don’t Overspend:
One of the most common mistakes bidders make at wine auctions is getting caught up in the excitement and overspending. Set a budget for yourself beforehand and stick to it. Remember, there will always be more opportunities to acquire exceptional wines, so it’s essential to exercise self-control and avoid getting carried away.

3. Do Inspect the Bottles:
Prior to bidding, take the time to inspect the bottles you are interested in. Look for signs of damage, such as leaks, mold, or compromised labels. Auction houses usually provide a preview period where you can examine the bottles up-close. If you have any doubts about a bottle’s condition, consult with an expert or ask for additional information before placing your bid.

4. Don’t Be Shy to Ask Questions:
Wine auctions can be intimidating, especially for newcomers. However, it’s important to remember that auction house staff is there to assist you. If you have any questions about the bidding process, the wines, or the auction house’s policies, don’t hesitate to ask. Clearing up any uncertainties beforehand will help you feel more confident and ensure a smoother experience.

5. Do Be Courteous:
Respecting the auction house’s rules and showing courtesy towards fellow bidders is a fundamental aspect of wine auction etiquette. Avoid disruptive behavior, such as talking loudly or using your phone during the bidding process. Additionally, refrain from making derogatory comments about the wines or other bidders, as this can create an uncomfortable atmosphere for everyone involved.

6. Don’t Forget About Additional Costs:
When participating in a wine auction, it’s important to factor in additional costs beyond the bid price. Auction houses usually charge a buyer’s premium, which is a percentage added to the final bid amount. Additionally, there may be shipping or storage fees, depending on the auction house’s policies. Be sure to factor in these costs when determining your maximum bid.

7. Do Take Notes:
During the auction, it can be challenging to keep track of all the wines, their lot numbers, and their estimated prices. Taking notes will help you stay organized and make more informed decisions. Jot down any relevant information or bidding patterns that may influence your bidding strategy.

In conclusion, attending a wine auction can be an exhilarating experience for wine enthusiasts and collectors. By following these dos and don’ts of wine auction etiquette, you’ll be well-equipped to navigate this unique world with confidence. Remember to do your research, set a budget, inspect the bottles, ask questions, be courteous, consider additional costs, and take notes. With these guidelines in mind, you’re ready to raise your paddle and immerse yourself in the fascinating world of wine auctions. Cheers!
