10 DIY Crafts for a Coastal-Inspired Home


If you love the beach and dream of living beside the sea, why not bring the coastal vibes into your home? Creating a coastal-inspired home doesn’t have to break the bank. With a bit of creativity and some DIY crafts, you can transform your space into a tranquil seaside retreat. In this blog post, we will explore 10 DIY crafts for a coastal-inspired home that will help you infuse your living space with the beauty and serenity of the ocean.

1. Driftwood Wall Art

One of the simplest and most effective ways to add a coastal touch to your home is by incorporating driftwood into your decor. Head to your local beach and collect pieces of driftwood of various shapes and sizes. Once you have your driftwood collection, clean and dry the pieces thoroughly. Then, arrange them in an appealing pattern on a canvas or frame and secure them using a hot glue gun. Hang your unique driftwood wall art piece in your living room or bedroom to add a touch of coastal charm.

2. Seashell Candles

Seashell candles are not only beautiful but also emit a lovely coastal scent when lit. Start by collecting seashells, preferably in various sizes and shapes. Clean the shells thoroughly to remove any sand or debris. Melt candle wax in a heat-resistant container and add a few drops of your favorite essential oil with a coastal aroma. Carefully pour the melted wax into the seashells, ensuring the wick stays in the center. Let the candles cool and solidify before trimming the wick. Display these seashell candles on a side table or coffee table to create a cozy coastal ambiance.

3. Rope Coasters

Capture the essence of coastal living with some DIY rope coasters. You’ll need a pack of small, circular cork coasters and some thick nautical rope. Start by applying adhesive to the cork coaster and carefully wrap the rope around it, securing it with more adhesive as needed. Continue wrapping until the entire coaster is covered. Trim any excess rope and allow the adhesive to dry thoroughly. These rope coasters not only serve a practical purpose but also add a touch of nautical flair to your living or dining area.

4. Beach-Inspired Mason Jar Lanterns

Create a soft and cozy coastal atmosphere by making beach-inspired mason jar lanterns. Start with some clean, empty mason jars of various sizes. Fill them up to one-third with sand or small pebbles for stability. Add a tea light candle to each jar and secure the lid tightly. Customize your lanterns by tying twine or rope around the neck of the jar and attaching seashells or starfish using hot glue. These lanterns can be placed on your patio or even hung from trees during outdoor gatherings for a touch of coastal charm.

5. Coastal Painted Terra Cotta Pots

Transform ordinary terra cotta pots into stunning pieces of coastal decor. Start by cleaning the pots and giving them a coat of primer to ensure proper adhesion. Then, choose coastal-inspired colors like hues of blue, green, or white and paint the pots using a sponge or a brush. Once the paint has dried, use a fine-tip brush or a paint pen to add coastal-themed designs such as seahorses, starfish, or anchors. These painted pots can be used to showcase your favorite indoor plants or flowers and are perfect for adding a coastal touch to any room.

6. Seashell Wind Chime

Bring the soothing sound of the ocean breeze inside your home by crafting a seashell wind chime. Start by gathering various seashells of different shapes and sizes. Clean and dry them thoroughly before attaching them to a hoop or a piece of driftwood using fishing line or thin wire. Space the shells out evenly and add small bells or metal charms for additional sound. Hang your seashell wind chime near a window or on your porch to enjoy the tranquil sound of coastal living.

7. Coastal-inspired Pillow Covers

Refresh your living room or bedroom decor with some DIY coastal-inspired pillow covers. Begin by choosing fabric with coastal patterns, such as seashells, anchors, or beach waves. Cut the fabric into square or rectangular pieces, allowing room for seam allowance. Sew the fabric together, leaving one side open to insert the pillow form. Once the pillow insert is inside, sew the opening closed. These custom pillow covers instantly add a coastal vibe to your space while giving it a fresh new look.

8. Beach Glass Vases

Create stunning beach glass vases to hold your favorite blooms. Start by collecting glass bottles or jars of different sizes. Remove any labels and clean the glass thoroughly. Apply a layer of adhesive to the outside of the glass, carefully pressing pieces of beach glass onto the adhesive. Let the adhesive dry and your beach glass vases are ready to display. Use them as centerpieces or decorative accents around your home for a coastal touch.

9. Seashell Picture Frame

Give your favorite memories a touch of the beach by creating seashell picture frames. Start with a plain wooden frame and cover it with a coat of white or pastel paint for a coastal look. Use a hot glue gun to attach small seashells, starfish, or sand dollars to the frame, ensuring they are evenly distributed. Allow the glue to dry and insert your favorite photo. These seashell picture frames make for lovely coastal-inspired decor pieces and also serve as great gifts for friends and family.

10. Ocean-Inspired Mobile

Create a captivating ocean-inspired mobile to hang in your baby’s nursery or as a decorative piece in any room. Start by cutting out fish, jellyfish, seahorse, and other ocean-themed shapes from cardstock or colorful craft paper. Paint or color them using your preferred shades of blue, green, and purple. Attach string, fishing line, or ribbon to each cut-out shape and secure them to a wooden hoop or a piece of driftwood. Hang the mobile from the ceiling and watch as it adds a whimsical touch to your coastal-inspired space.

Conclusion

Bringing a touch of coastal charm into your home doesn’t have to be complicated or expensive. With these 10 DIY crafts, you can infuse your living space with the beauty and serenity of the ocean. From driftwood wall art to seashell candles, each craft showcases the natural elements that define coastal living. So get creative, grab some supplies, and start transforming your home into a coastal-inspired haven.
