The Best Cartoon Shows for Young Adults


In recent years, the world of animation has undergone a remarkable transformation. No longer confined to the realm of children, cartoons have evolved to cater to a more mature audience, particularly young adults. These shows not only entertain but also tackle complex themes, explore nuanced characters, and provide social commentary that resonates with an older demographic. In this post, we delve into some of the best cartoon shows for young adults that have garnered acclaim for their creativity, humor, and depth.

1. Bojack Horseman

Bojack Horseman is often hailed as one of the greatest animated series of all time. The show follows the life of Bojack, a washed-up actor who happens to be an anthropomorphic horse. Set in a vibrant Hollywood universe, it explores themes of depression, addiction, and the quest for identity. What sets Bojack apart is its ability to balance humor with poignant moments that resonate deeply with viewers. The show’s unique animation style, coupled with its clever writing, makes it a must-watch for young adults seeking both entertainment and introspection.

Why It’s Great:

  • Deep Character Development: Each character is multi-dimensional, allowing viewers to empathize with their struggles.
  • Cultural Commentary: The series brilliantly satirizes Hollywood and celebrity culture, offering a reflective lens on fame.
  • Realistic Themes: Tackling issues like mental health and personal failure, Bojack Horseman sparks important conversations.

2. Rick and Morty

Rick and Morty has taken the animation world by storm since its debut. This sci-fi comedy follows the adventures of an eccentric and often reckless scientist, Rick Sanchez, and his good-hearted but easily influenced grandson, Morty. The series is known for its dark humor, complex storytelling, and philosophical undertones. Each episode presents a new adventure that often includes existential questions, making it a favorite among young adults who appreciate a show that challenges their thinking.

Why It’s Great:

  • Innovative Storytelling: The show’s non-linear narratives and unexpected twists keep viewers engaged.
  • Rich Lore: Over time, Rick and Morty has developed a vast universe filled with intriguing characters and concepts.
  • Philosophical Depth: It encourages viewers to contemplate deeper issues such as morality, free will, and the nature of existence.

3. Adventure Time

Initially aimed at a younger audience, Adventure Time has evolved to appeal to all ages, particularly young adults. The series follows Finn, a human boy, and Jake, his shape-shifting dog, as they embark on whimsical adventures in the Land of Ooo. Beneath its colorful exterior, Adventure Time delves into themes of friendship, love, loss, and the complexities of growing up. The show’s unique art style and imaginative storytelling have garnered a dedicated fan base.

Why It’s Great:

  • Emotional Resonance: The series adeptly balances lighthearted fun with profound emotional moments.
  • World-Building: The Land of Ooo is rich with lore and diverse characters, creating an immersive viewing experience.
  • Life Lessons: Adventure Time is filled with valuable lessons about relationships and self-discovery.

4. Big Mouth

Big Mouth is a bold and unapologetic look at the trials and tribulations of adolescence. The series follows a group of middle school friends navigating the awkwardness of puberty, complete with hormone monsters and a plethora of relatable experiences. With its frank discussions about sexuality, mental health, and growing up, Big Mouth has struck a chord with young adults who appreciate its honesty and humor.

Why It’s Great:

  • Relatable Content: The show tackles real-life issues that resonate with anyone who has experienced adolescence.
  • Humor and Heart: While it’s often hilarious, Big Mouth also takes time to explore the emotional aspects of growing up.
  • Diverse Representation: The series features a diverse cast of characters, showcasing various experiences and backgrounds.

5. F Is for Family

Inspired by comedian Bill Burr’s childhood experiences, F Is for Family offers a nostalgic yet raw portrayal of family life in the 1970s. The show centers around the Murphy family and delves into the dynamics of parenting, societal expectations, and personal struggles. Its humor is often dark, reflecting the era’s cultural landscape, making it relatable for young adults who appreciate a more grounded approach to animation.

Why It’s Great:

  • Nostalgic Themes: The show captures the essence of growing up in a different time, appealing to those who appreciate nostalgia.
  • Authentic Humor: Its comedic style is heavily influenced by real-life experiences, making it relatable and genuine.
  • Character Complexity: The characters are flawed yet lovable, creating an engaging viewing experience.

6. Final Space

Final Space is an epic space opera that follows Gary Goodspeed, an astronaut on a quest to save the universe. With a mix of humor, action, and heartfelt moments, the series explores themes of friendship, sacrifice, and the battle between good and evil. The animation is visually stunning, and the character development is compelling, making it a standout choice for young adults.

Why It’s Great:

  • Engaging Plot: The overarching narrative is captivating, keeping viewers invested in the fate of the characters.
  • Diverse Cast: Each character brings a unique perspective, adding depth to the story.
  • Emotional Moments: Final Space masterfully balances humor with emotional stakes, making for a well-rounded series.

7. Solar Opposites

From one of the co-creators of Rick and Morty, Solar Opposites follows an alien family that crash-lands on Earth. The show combines science fiction with humor as it explores the challenges of adapting to human life. With its witty writing and engaging characters, Solar Opposites provides a fresh take on the alien-invasion trope.

Why It’s Great:

  • Clever Humor: The writing is sharp and often subversive, appealing to a discerning audience.
  • Cultural Commentary: The show critiques human society through an outsider’s perspective, sparking thought-provoking conversations.
  • Unique Characters: Each alien character has distinct personalities and quirks, making for entertaining dynamics.

8. The Midnight Gospel

The Midnight Gospel is a visually stunning series created by Pendleton Ward, the mind behind Adventure Time. The show features a spacecaster named Clancy who travels to different worlds while exploring deep philosophical questions through his podcast interviews. With its unique animation style and thought-provoking dialogue, The Midnight Gospel appeals to young adults seeking a blend of creativity and introspection.

Why It’s Great:

  • Philosophical Themes: The discussions cover a wide range of topics, encouraging viewers to engage in introspective thinking.
  • Stunning Visuals: The animation is vibrant and imaginative, creating an immersive experience.
  • Emotional Depth: Despite its surreal nature, the show addresses real-life issues such as mortality and relationships.

9. Tuca & Bertie

Tuca & Bertie is a delightful exploration of friendship between two bird-women living in the same apartment building. The series tackles various themes, including mental health, relationships, and the complexities of adulthood. Its unique animation style and relatable storytelling make it a refreshing addition to the landscape of adult animation.

Why It’s Great:

  • Empowering Themes: The show celebrates female friendship and individuality, promoting positive messages.
  • Visual Creativity: The animation is colorful and imaginative, enhancing the viewing experience.
  • Realistic Challenges: It addresses issues that young adults face, making it relatable and poignant.

10. Undone

Undone takes a unique approach to animation by blending real-life action with rotoscope animation. The series follows Alma, a young woman who discovers she has a new relationship with time following a near-fatal accident. As she navigates her newfound abilities, she uncovers dark family secrets. The show’s blend of reality and fantasy creates a captivating narrative that resonates with young adults.

Why It’s Great:

  • Innovative Animation: The rotoscope technique creates a visually striking experience unlike traditional animations.
  • Complex Narrative: The intertwining of reality and fantasy keeps viewers guessing and engaged.
  • Emotional Storytelling: The show explores themes of grief, family dynamics, and self-discovery, making it an impactful watch.

Conclusion

The landscape of animation for young adults is rich and diverse, offering a plethora of shows that entertain while provoking thought. From the emotional depth of Bojack Horseman to the whimsical adventures in Adventure Time, these series capture the complexities of life in a way that resonates with a mature audience. As animation continues to evolve, it is clear that there is no shortage of exceptional content for young adults to enjoy. Whether you’re looking for humor, introspection, or a combination of both, these cartoon shows are sure to leave a lasting impression. So grab some popcorn, settle in, and get ready to experience the best that animated storytelling has to offer!
