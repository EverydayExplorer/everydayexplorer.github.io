The Best TV Shows Based on Books That You Need to Watch


In today’s golden age of television, the fusion of literature and screen has yielded some of the most captivating and immersive storytelling experiences. With streaming platforms offering a plethora of options, it can be daunting to sift through the vast array of TV shows vying for your attention. Fear not, bibliophiles and cinephiles alike, for I have curated a list of the best TV shows based on books that are bound to enthrall and enchant.

  1. Game of Thrones: Based on George R.R. Martin’s epic fantasy series ‘A Song of Ice and Fire,’ this HBO juggernaut needs no introduction. Set in the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos, the show intertwines intricate political intrigue with visceral battles and supernatural elements. With its morally ambiguous characters and shocking plot twists, ‘Game of Thrones’ redefined television storytelling and became a cultural phenomenon.

  2. The Handmaid’s Tale: Margaret Atwood’s dystopian novel serves as the foundation for this hauntingly relevant Hulu series. Set in a totalitarian society where women are subjugated and fertility is prized above all else, ‘The Handmaid’s Tale’ explores themes of oppression, resistance, and the resilience of the human spirit. Its chilling portrayal of a possible future is both captivating and chilling.

  3. The Witcher: Based on the book series by Polish author Andrzej Sapkowski, ‘The Witcher’ follows the adventures of Geralt of Rivia, a monster hunter with supernatural abilities. With its rich world-building, complex characters, and breathtaking action sequences, this Netflix original has garnered a dedicated fanbase and renewed interest in the source material.

  4. Outlander: Diana Gabaldon’s time-traveling romance novels provide the inspiration for this Starz series. Set against the backdrop of 18th-century Scotland, ‘Outlander’ follows Claire Randall, a World War II nurse who finds herself transported back in time. As she navigates the treacherous political landscape and finds love in the arms of the dashing Jamie Fraser, Claire must reconcile her modern sensibilities with the realities of the past.

  5. Big Little Lies: Liane Moriarty’s bestselling novel receives the HBO treatment in this star-studded adaptation. Set in the affluent community of Monterey, California, ‘Big Little Lies’ explores the lives of three women whose seemingly perfect lives unravel in the wake of a tragic event. With its stellar cast, sharp dialogue, and gripping storytelling, this series delves into themes of motherhood, friendship, and the facade of perfection.

  6. The Haunting of Hill House: Shirley Jackson’s classic horror novel gets a modern update in this Netflix series created by Mike Flanagan. Blending supernatural scares with emotional depth, ‘The Haunting of Hill House’ follows the fractured Crain family as they confront the ghosts of their past both literal and metaphorical. With its intricate storytelling and powerhouse performances, this series is a must-watch for horror aficionados and fans of psychological drama alike.

  7. Mindhunter: Inspired by the true-crime book ‘Mindhunter: Inside the FBI’s Elite Serial Crime Unit’ by John E. Douglas and Mark Olshaker, this Netflix series delves into the early days of criminal profiling at the FBI. Set in the late 1970s, ‘Mindhunter’ follows agents Holden Ford and Bill Tench as they interview incarcerated serial killers in an effort to understand the minds of murderers. With its chilling atmosphere and thought-provoking subject matter, this series offers a fascinating glimpse into the psychology of evil.

  8. His Dark Materials: Philip Pullman’s beloved fantasy trilogy serves as the basis for this HBO/BBC co-production. Set in a parallel universe where every human has a daemon, a manifestation of their soul in animal form, ‘His Dark Materials’ follows young Lyra Belacqua as she embarks on a journey to uncover the truth about a mysterious substance known as Dust. With its dazzling visuals, rich mythology, and compelling characters, this series is a treat for fans of epic fantasy.

From sprawling epics to intimate character studies, these TV shows based on books offer something for every viewer. Whether you’re a fan of fantasy, drama, horror, or mystery, you’re sure to find a new favorite among these adaptations. So grab your remote, settle in with a cozy blanket, and prepare to be transported to worlds both familiar and fantastical.
