How to Achieve the Perfect Cat Eye Makeup


Are you looking to add a touch of glamour to your everyday makeup look? Look no further than the classic cat eye. The cat eye makeup look is a timeless trend that can instantly elevate your appearance. Whether you’re going for a bold and dramatic look or a subtle and sophisticated one, the cat eye is sure to make a statement.

But mastering the cat eye technique can be a bit intimidating, especially if you’re a beginner. But fear not! In this comprehensive guide, we’ll break down the step-by-step process to help you achieve the perfect cat eye makeup. So grab your favorite eyeliner and let’s get started!

Step 1: Prep and Prime

Before you begin creating your cat eye, it’s essential to prepare your eyelids. Start by applying an eyeshadow primer to ensure that your eye makeup lasts throughout the day. This step is particularly crucial if you have oily eyelids.

Next, apply a neutral eyeshadow base to even out the skin tone on your eyelids. This will create a smooth canvas for your cat eye look and help the colors pop. Use a fluffy eyeshadow brush to blend the eyeshadow base seamlessly.

Step 2: Choose the Right Eyeliner

The choice of eyeliner is vital for achieving the perfect cat eye. There are various types of eyeliners available, including pencil, gel, and liquid liners. For a precise and sharp cat eye, opt for a liquid eyeliner with a fine tip applicator. The liquid formula allows for easy control and creates a clean, defined line.

Step 3: Start with the Flick

Begin by drawing a small flick at the outer corner of your eye. This flick will serve as a guide for the rest of your cat eye. Angle the flick upwards towards the tail end of your eyebrow for an elongated look.

Step 4: Create the Outline

Once you’ve drawn the flick, it’s time to create the outline of your cat eye. Starting from the inner corner of your eye, draw a thin line along your lash line towards the flick. Make sure to keep the line as close to the lashes as possible for a seamless finish.

Step 5: Fill in the Wing

Now that you have the outline, it’s time to fill in the wing. Start from the tip of the flick and draw a line towards the middle of your eyelid. Connect the line to the eyeliner on your lash line and fill in any gaps. The key is to maintain a smooth and even line.

Step 6: Tightline and Mascara

To complete the cat eye look, tightline your upper waterline. This will define your lash line and create a fuller appearance. Use a waterproof eyeliner for long-lasting results.

Finish off your cat eye by applying a coat or two of your favorite mascara to your upper and lower lashes. This will give your eyes a wide-eyed, fluttery effect.

Tips and Tricks

  • Practice makes perfect! Don’t get discouraged if your first attempts at creating a cat eye aren’t flawless. Keep practicing, and you’ll get better with time.
  • Concealer can be your best friend when it comes to cleaning up any mistakes or sharpening the edges of your cat eye.
  • If you find it challenging to create a straight line, try using small dots along your lash line and then connect them.
  • Experiment with different styles and variations of the cat eye. You can make it thick and bold or thin and subtle, depending on your preference and occasion.

With these simple steps and a little bit of practice, you’ll be able to achieve the perfect cat eye makeup. Remember, confidence is key when rocking this timeless look. So go ahead, channel your inner feline, and slay the cat eye game!
