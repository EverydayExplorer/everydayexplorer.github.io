The Influence of Cartoons on Music and Musical Theatre


Cartoons have been a beloved form of entertainment for both children and adults for decades. From the catchy theme songs to the memorable soundtracks, cartoons have had a profound influence on music and musical theatre. In this blog post, we will explore how cartoons have shaped the world of music and how they continue to impact the musical landscape today.

One of the most significant ways that cartoons have influenced music is through their theme songs. Think about the iconic tunes from shows like ‘The Flintstones,’ ‘Scooby-Doo,’ and ‘The Simpsons.’ These theme songs have become ingrained in popular culture and have transcended the boundaries of the cartoon world. They have become recognizable melodies that evoke nostalgia and bring back fond memories of childhood. In fact, many of these theme songs have been covered and reimagined by various artists, further solidifying their influence on music.

Cartoons have also played a vital role in introducing young audiences to various music genres. Take, for example, ‘Looney Tunes.’ This classic cartoon series featured a wide range of musical styles, from classical compositions to jazz and swing. Through the use of music, cartoons have exposed children to different sounds and rhythms, fostering an appreciation for music diversity from an early age. This exposure has undoubtedly contributed to the development of many musicians and artists who draw inspiration from these cartoon soundtracks.

Furthermore, cartoons have been instrumental in introducing musical theatre to younger audiences. Shows like ‘Beauty and the Beast,’ ‘The Lion King,’ and ‘Aladdin’ were not only successful as animated films but also made successful transitions to the Broadway stage. These adaptations brought the magic of musical theatre to a whole new generation, sparking a renewed interest in this art form. The success of these musicals has not only created a new fan base but has also paved the way for other animated films to follow suit, such as ‘Frozen’ and ‘Moana.’

In recent years, cartoons have continued to influence the world of music through collaborations between animators and musicians. For example, the critically acclaimed Adult Swim series ‘Rick and Morty’ has featured original songs by various artists, including the popular band, ‘Chaos Chaos.’ These collaborations have not only created unique musical moments within the series but have also brought attention to lesser-known artists and introduced their music to a wider audience.

In conclusion, cartoons have had a significant impact on music and musical theatre. From the memorable theme songs that have become part of our cultural fabric to the introduction of different music genres to young audiences, cartoons have shaped the way we perceive and appreciate music. Furthermore, the crossover between cartoons and music continues to evolve, with new collaborations and adaptations keeping the influence alive. So, the next time you find yourself humming a cartoon theme song or enjoying a Broadway musical inspired by an animated film, remember the profound influence that cartoons have had on the world of music.
