The Healing Properties of Reiki Symbols


Reiki is a gentle and powerful healing system that originated in Japan. It focuses on balancing and harmonizing the energy within our bodies to promote physical, emotional, and spiritual well-being. One of the key elements of Reiki is the use of symbols, which are sacred and hold immense healing potential.

In Reiki, symbols are considered to be gateways to specific energies or frequencies. By drawing or visualizing these symbols, Reiki practitioners tap into the universal life force energy, channeling it to facilitate healing and transformation. Each symbol has its own unique properties and purposes, making them valuable tools in the practice of Reiki.

1. The Power Symbol (Cho Ku Rei)

Cho Ku Rei is perhaps the most widely used symbol in Reiki. It is often described as the ’light switch’ symbol, as it amplifies the healing energy and increases its intensity. This symbol can be used to clear energy blockages, enhance the effectiveness of healing treatments, and provide overall balance and grounding. It is particularly useful for physical healing and protection.

2. The Mental/Emotional Symbol (Sei He Ki)

Sei He Ki is commonly referred to as the symbol for emotional and mental healing. It has a calming effect on the mind and helps release negative emotions, trauma, and mental patterns. This symbol is often used to promote emotional stability, harmonize relationships, and address issues related to addiction and self-destructive behaviors. It is also beneficial for enhancing memory and learning abilities.

3. The Distant/Connection Symbol (Hon Sha Ze Sho Nen)

Hon Sha Ze Sho Nen is known as the symbol for distant healing and connection. It bridges the gap of time and space, allowing Reiki energy to be sent across distances, even to the past or future. This symbol is commonly used for healing past traumas, sending healing energy to loved ones who are not physically present, and connecting with higher realms of consciousness. It is a powerful tool for facilitating healing on a spiritual level.

4. The Master Symbol (Dai Ko Myo)

Dai Ko Myo is the symbol that represents the master level of Reiki. It embodies the highest level of consciousness and spiritual enlightenment. This symbol is often associated with inner peace, wisdom, and spiritual growth. It is used for deep healing, profound spiritual transformation, and aligning with one’s highest purpose and potential. It is sometimes referred to as the symbol of perfect harmony.

5. The Harmony Symbol (Raku)

Raku is a symbol that is not traditionally taught in all Reiki lineages, but it is gaining popularity due to its powerful healing properties. This symbol represents the completion of a healing session or energy work. It helps integrate the energies received during a treatment, ensuring their harmonious integration in the recipient’s energy system. It is often described as the symbol of grounding and sealing.

Using Reiki Symbols for Healing

To activate the healing properties of Reiki symbols, practitioners can draw or visualize the symbols in the air, on their hands, or over specific areas of the body. They can also use them for distance healing by drawing or visualizing the symbols on a photograph of the recipient or simply by intention. The symbols can be combined and used in conjunction with the traditional hand positions or in any way guided by intuition.

It is important to note that the true power of Reiki symbols lies in the intention and connection to the universal life force energy. They act as reminders and anchoring points for the practitioner to access and direct the healing energy. While symbols are essential in Reiki, it is the practitioner’s connection and understanding of the universal energy that truly brings about healing.

In conclusion, Reiki symbols are sacred and powerful tools that enhance the healing potential of the Reiki practice. They open doors to specific energies and frequencies, allowing practitioners to access deeper levels of healing and transformation. By understanding and utilizing the properties of these symbols, Reiki practitioners can facilitate profound healing on physical, emotional, and spiritual levels.
