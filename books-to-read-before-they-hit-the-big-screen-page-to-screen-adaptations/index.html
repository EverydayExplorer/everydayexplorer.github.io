Books to Read Before They Hit the Big Screen: Page to Screen Adaptations


In recent years, we have seen an increasing trend of books being adapted into movies and TV shows. Filmmakers and producers are constantly on the lookout for compelling stories that can capture the imaginations of millions of viewers. This begs the question - what books should we read before they hit the big screen? In this blog post, we will explore some of the most highly anticipated page to screen adaptations that are sure to excite book lovers and movie enthusiasts alike.

  1. The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald: Set in the Roaring Twenties, this timeless classic tells the story of Jay Gatsby, a mysterious millionaire, and his obsession with the beautiful Daisy Buchanan. Baz Luhrmann’s visually stunning adaptation stars Leonardo DiCaprio as Gatsby and has garnered much attention for its lavish production design. Before watching the movie, readers should immerse themselves in Fitzgerald’s lyrical prose and discover the intricate layers of this tragic love story.

  2. Dune by Frank Herbert: Considered one of the greatest science fiction novels ever written, ‘Dune’ follows the story of Paul Atreides as he navigates the treacherous politics of a distant planet. Directed by Denis Villeneuve, the upcoming adaptation boasts an all-star cast and promises a visually stunning and faithful retelling of Herbert’s complex and expansive world. Dive into this epic tale of politics, religion, and ecology before experiencing its cinematic adaptation.

  3. The Power of the Dog by Don Winslow: Set in the world of drug cartels, ‘The Power of the Dog’ is a gripping crime thriller that explores the dark underbelly of the drug trade. Directed by Ridley Scott and starring Jake Gyllenhaal and Benicio del Toro, this adaptation has generated immense buzz for its gritty and intense portrayal of the novel’s characters. Winslow’s raw and powerful storytelling delves deep into the complexities of corruption and the consequences it has on both sides of the law.

  4. The Nightingale by Kristin Hannah: This heart-wrenching historical fiction novel tells the tale of two sisters in World War II-occupied France. Their struggle for survival and resistance against the Nazis is both gripping and deeply emotional. With Michelle MacLaren set to direct and Dakota Fanning and Elle Fanning to star as the two sisters, the adaptation of ‘The Nightingale’ promises to be a poignant exploration of the strength of the human spirit.

  5. The Invisible Life of Addie LaRue by V.E. Schwab: A captivating blend of fantasy, romance, and historical fiction, ‘The Invisible Life of Addie LaRue’ follows the story of a young woman who makes a Faustian bargain to live forever, but with the consequence of being forgotten by everyone she meets. The book’s unique premise and beautifully crafted characters have already captivated readers, and the highly anticipated adaptation will undoubtedly breathe life into Schwab’s enchanting world.

  6. The Marrying of Chani Kaufman by Eve Harris: This critically acclaimed debut novel tells the story of an orthodox Jewish community in London and the strict traditions and expectations that shape the lives of its inhabitants. With its exploration of love, faith, and rebellion, the adaptation of ‘The Marrying of Chani Kaufman’ has the potential to shine a light on a lesser-known community and their struggles and triumphs.

Reading these books before their adaptations hit the big screen allows us to experience the stories in their original form and fully appreciate the artistry and depth of the written word. As page to screen adaptations continue to gain popularity, it is essential to embrace the unique qualities that make books so beloved in the first place. So, grab a copy of these novels, immerse yourself in their pages, and get ready to see them come alive on the silver screen.
