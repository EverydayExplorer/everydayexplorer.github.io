Hunting in Thick Cover: Strategies for Success


Please note that the content and opinions expressed in this blog are for informational purposes only. Hunting laws and regulations vary by state and it’s important to adhere to all local hunting regulations. Safety should always be the top priority in any hunting activity.

When it comes to hunting, different terrains present unique challenges and opportunities. Thick cover, often encountered in dense forests or areas with heavy vegetation, can prove to be particularly challenging for hunters. However, with the right strategies and techniques, success can be achieved. In this blog post, we will explore some effective strategies for hunting in thick cover.

Understanding Thick Cover

Thick cover refers to areas where visibility is limited due to densely packed vegetation or thick foliage. It can be challenging to navigate through this environment, let alone spot game. However, many game animals seek out these areas for shelter and protection, making it an ideal hunting ground if approached correctly.

To successfully hunt in thick cover, it is crucial to understand how game animals behave in these environments. For example, deer tend to stick to well-worn trails or game paths when moving through dense vegetation. Taking advantage of this knowledge can increase your chances of success.

Pre-Season Preparation

Preparing for a hunt in thick cover starts long before you set foot in the hunting ground. Here are some crucial steps to consider:

  1. Scouting: Familiarize yourself with the terrain by scouting the area before the actual hunt. Look for game trails, feeding areas, bedding areas, or any other signs of animal activity. This will help you identify the best spots to set up your stand or blind.

  2. Clear Shooting Lanes: Once you have identified potential hunting spots, clear shooting lanes to increase your visibility and ensure a clear shot should an opportunity arise. Remember to only remove enough vegetation to create a window for shooting, as excessive cutting may alert game animals to your presence.

  3. Scent Control: Thick cover can amplify scents, making it easier for game animals to pick up on human odors. Practice scent control by washing your hunting clothes with scent-free detergent, using scent-eliminating sprays, and taking precautions to minimize your scent while in the field.

Effective Hunting Strategies

When it comes to hunting in thick cover, adopting the right strategies can significantly improve your chances of success. Here are some methods to consider:

  1. Still Hunting: Still hunting involves moving slowly and silently through the woods, scanning the surroundings for game. Take your time and be observant, as animals may be bedded down or moving stealthily in thick cover. Use binoculars to spot game before they spot you, and take advantage of available cover to mask your movements.

  2. Ambushing: Another effective strategy in thick cover is setting up an ambush. Identify trails or travel corridors where game animals are likely to pass through. Set up a ground blind or treestand, ensuring that you have a clear view of shooting lanes. Remain patient, quiet, and alert, as it may take some time for animals to appear.

  3. Calling Techniques: Utilizing calling techniques can also be effective in thick cover. Familiarize yourself with the calls of the game species you are targeting and use them strategically. Deer, for example, can be attracted using grunts or rattling antlers. However, ensure that you are well-concealed and using calls sparingly, as overcalling can spook wary animals.

  4. Tracking: Tracking is particularly useful when hunting in thick cover. Look for fresh tracks, scat, or rubs to determine the direction and proximity of game animals. Carefully follow the trail, staying low and utilizing cover to remain undetected. Eventually, you may come within shooting range.

Safety Considerations

While hunting in thick cover can be exciting and rewarding, it’s important to prioritize safety. Here are some crucial safety considerations:

  1. Know Your Target: Always positively identify your target before taking a shot. Thick cover can limit visibility and it’s essential to ensure you’re aiming at the intended game animal.

  2. Wear Blaze Orange: In areas where it’s required by law, wear at least one article of blaze orange clothing to increase visibility to other hunters.

  3. Communicate with Others: If hunting in a group, establish clear communication protocols and ensure all members are aware of each other’s locations at all times.

  4. Practice Firearm Safety: Always treat firearms with the utmost respect and adhere to proper firearm safety practices. Be aware of your surroundings and never take a shot unless you have a clear line of fire.

  5. Use a Safety Harness: If hunting from a treestand, always use a safety harness to prevent falls and injury.

Conclusion

Hunting in thick cover presents a unique set of challenges, but with careful planning, preparation, and adopting the right strategies, success can be achieved. Understanding animal behavior, scouting effectively, and employing methods such as still hunting, ambushing, and tracking can significantly increase your chances of a successful hunt. Remember to prioritize safety at all times and adhere to local hunting regulations. Happy hunting!
