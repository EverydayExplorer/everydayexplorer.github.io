Caring for Your Suede Shoes: Tips and Tricks


Suede shoes are a stylish and fashionable choice of footwear that can elevate any outfit. However, they also require special care and attention to keep them looking their best. In this blog post, we will share with you some valuable tips and tricks for caring for your suede shoes, ensuring that they stay in pristine condition for years to come.

1. Brushing is Key:
One important step in caring for suede shoes is regular brushing. A suede brush is specially designed with bristles that are gentle on the delicate suede material. Brushing your shoes regularly not only helps to remove dirt and dust but also revives the nap of the suede, keeping it looking fresh and vibrant. Remember to brush in the same direction to maintain the natural appearance of the material.

2. Protect with Waterproof Spray:
Suede shoes are more vulnerable to stains and damage compared to other types of shoes. To safeguard them, apply a waterproof spray specifically formulated for suede. This protective layer helps to repel liquid and prevent stains from setting in. It is advisable to reapply the spray every few months, or after cleaning your shoes, to maintain its effectiveness.

3. Tackle Stains with a Stain Eraser:
Despite your best efforts, accidents happen, and your suede shoes may encounter unsightly stains. The good news is that most stains on suede can be removed with a suede stain eraser. Gently rub the eraser over the affected area, and watch as the stain begins to disappear. Remember to work the eraser in a circular motion to avoid leaving visible marks on the suede.

4. No Water, Please:
Unlike leather shoes, suede does not react well to water. Water can leave permanent marks and cause the suede to lose its texture and shape. If your suede shoes do get wet, stuff them with newspaper or a shoe tree to maintain their shape, and allow them to air dry away from direct heat sources. Once dry, use a suede brush to fluff up the nap again and restore the texture.

5. Take Precautions on Rainy Days:
Rainy weather can be a nightmare for suede shoe owners. However, you can protect your suede shoes by wearing waterproof shoe covers or opting for water-resistant footwear on rainy days. If your shoes do get wet, follow the steps mentioned in tip number 4 to minimize damage and restore their appearance.

6. Beware of Color Transfer:
Suede shoes, especially those in lighter colors, are prone to color transfer. Be cautious when wearing them with dark-colored clothing items, as the dye from these garments may rub off onto your shoes. To avoid this, try to wear light-colored suede shoes with light-colored clothing, and dark-colored suede shoes with dark-colored clothing.

7. Store Properly:
To maintain the shape and quality of your suede shoes, it is essential to store them properly. Avoid stacking or piling shoes on top of each other to prevent unnecessary pressure that can cause the suede to lose its shape. Instead, store them individually in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight, and with shoe trees inserted to help maintain their structure.

8. Professional Cleaning:
While regular home maintenance is important, there are times when you may need to seek professional help to clean your suede shoes. Professional cleaners have the expertise and tools to remove stubborn stains and restore the original look of your shoes. Before choosing a cleaner, ensure they have experience and positive reviews in handling suede footwear.

By following these tips and tricks, you can extend the lifespan of your suede shoes and keep them looking fabulous. Remember, caring for your suede shoes involves regular brushing, applying a protective spray, using a suede stain eraser for stains, and avoiding water whenever possible. Additionally, take precautions on rainy days, prevent color transfer, store your shoes properly, and consider professional cleaning when necessary.

Investing time and effort into the proper care of your suede shoes will not only protect your investment but also ensure that you always step out in style. So go ahead, pull out your favorite pair of suede shoes, and show them the love and care they deserve.
