The Best Survival Books to Expand Your Knowledge


In a world where natural disasters, economic uncertainties, and unexpected emergencies can strike at any moment, having a solid understanding of survival skills is essential. Whether you are an avid outdoorsman, a prepper, or simply someone who wants to be better prepared for unforeseen circumstances, diving into survival literature can greatly enhance your knowledge and skills. Below, we explore some of the best survival books that offer invaluable insights, practical techniques, and life-saving strategies.

1. “SAS Survival Handbook” by John ‘Lofty’ Wiseman

Considered a classic in survival literature, the “SAS Survival Handbook” is a comprehensive resource that covers a wide array of survival topics. Written by former SAS soldier John ‘Lofty’ Wiseman, this book is packed with practical advice on everything from building shelters and finding food to navigating in the wilderness. The detailed illustrations and real-life anecdotes make it an engaging read, while the systematic approach to survival scenarios empowers readers to think critically in emergency situations.

Key Features:

  • Extensive coverage of survival situations, including urban and wilderness environments.
  • Clear, concise instructions paired with illustrative diagrams.
  • Tips on psychological resilience and decision-making under pressure.

2. “The Trapper’s Bible: Traps, Snares & Pathguards” by Dale Martin

For those interested in the art of trapping, “The Trapper’s Bible” serves as an essential guide. Dale Martin provides a thorough exploration of various trapping techniques, including how to construct and set traps effectively. Whether in a survival scenario or simply for outdoor recreation, this book offers insights into the historical significance of trapping and its applications in modern survival situations.

Key Features:

  • Step-by-step instructions for crafting and using different types of traps.
  • Information on animal behavior to increase trapping success.
  • Emphasis on ethical trapping practices and sustainability.

3. “Emergency War Surgery: The Survivalist’s Medical Desk Reference” by the U.S. Department of the Army

In emergencies, medical knowledge can be as critical as wilderness survival skills. “Emergency War Surgery” is a military manual that provides detailed instructions for treating traumatic injuries when professional medical help is not available. This book is particularly useful for those who want to prepare for scenarios where access to healthcare may be limited.

Key Features:

  • Comprehensive guidance on managing severe injuries and trauma.
  • Focus on surgical procedures, wound care, and infection prevention.
  • A valuable resource for both preppers and medical professionals.

Key Features:

  • Focus on primitive skills and how to utilize natural resources.
  • Tips on crafting tools and building shelters using local materials.
  • Insightful anecdotes that inspire a connection with nature.

5. “The Complete Guide to Edible Wild Plants” by the U.S. Department of the Army

Understanding what nature has to offer in terms of nutrition is a crucial part of survival. “The Complete Guide to Edible Wild Plants” provides detailed descriptions of various edible plants, including their habitats, seasons, and preparation methods. This book is an excellent reference for anyone looking to enhance their foraging skills and integrate wild edibles into their diet.

Key Features:

  • Clear illustrations for easy identification of plants.
  • Nutritional information and potential uses for each plant.
  • Safety tips for avoiding poisonous plants.

6. “Survival Poaching” by Ragnar Benson

“Survival Poaching” takes a controversial yet practical approach to survival skills. Ragnar Benson provides readers with insights into hunting and trapping in desperate situations, focusing on techniques that may not necessarily align with legal hunting practices. While the book may raise ethical questions, it offers a raw perspective on survival when traditional resources are unavailable.

Key Features:

  • Techniques for hunting and gathering food in survival scenarios.
  • Discussions on the moral implications of poaching.
  • Real-life stories that highlight the urgency of survival situations.

7. “The Ultimate Guide to Wilderness Living: Surviving with Nothing But Your Bare Hands” by John McPherson and Geri McPherson

For those who want to challenge themselves, “The Ultimate Guide to Wilderness Living” offers a deep dive into primitive skills. The authors, John and Geri McPherson, emphasize the importance of self-reliance and creativity in survival situations. This book covers everything from crafting tools to building shelters without modern conveniences.

Key Features:

  • In-depth exploration of primitive skills and self-sufficiency.
  • Practical exercises to build confidence and expertise.
  • Encouragement to develop a personal connection with nature.
