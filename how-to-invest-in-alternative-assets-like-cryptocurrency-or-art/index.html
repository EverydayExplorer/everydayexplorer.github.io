How to invest in alternative assets, like cryptocurrency or art


As the world continues to shift and evolve, there has been a growing interest in alternative asset classes, mirroring a shift in traditional investment strategies. Traditionally, stocks and bonds were considered the only place for investors looking for a solid yield, but now people are exploring a broader range of investment opportunities. Here, we’ll take a look at investing in alternative assets such as cryptocurrency or art, which have become increasingly attractive to both big and small investors.

First off, let’s talk about cryptocurrency. The controversial digital currency, Bitcoin, had a wild ride in 2017 when its value skyrocketed from roughly $1,000 to almost $20,000 by year-end. While it’s down 60% from its peak, investors were enticed by Bitcoin in its early days because its blockchain technology promised to simplify transactions, eliminate intermediaries and reduce costs for everyone involved. Its success has led to the rise of other cryptocurrencies such as Ethereum, Litecoin, and Ripple. It’s important to note that investing in cryptocurrencies is not for everyone, despite its reputation as the “digital gold rush”. Cryptocurrencies are still unregulated and highly volatile, and investors should only invest money that they can afford to lose.

When it comes to cryptocurrency, the first and foremost step to take is to do your research. Find out what cryptocurrencies are available and their market capitalization, history, and price fluctuations. Then, find a reputable exchange to buy and sell cryptocurrencies such as Coinbase, Binance, or Kraken. Be sure to keep your cryptocurrency investments in a secure digital wallet, preferably protected by two-factor authentication. Be aware that there may be fees associated with buying, selling, and transferring cryptocurrency.

Another popular alternative investment is art. It’s important to note that investing in art is quite different from investing in cryptocurrencies, stocks, or real estate. The value of art doesn’t come from its intrinsic value, but from its rarity and the demand for it. Its value is also highly subjective and fluctuates depending on market conditions, fads, and trends. Nonetheless, investing in art can offer investors unique investment opportunities, as well as diversify their portfolio.

When it comes to art collection, it’s important to note that this is not an investment for the faint-hearted. The art market is volatile and largely unregulated, which makes it difficult for individuals to convert their holdings into cash. To invest in art, investors should take time to learn about the art market and the different types of art available. Consider working with an art adviser or consultant, or investing in art funds that hire professionals to manage their collections.

Another option is to invest in art-related assets such as art funds, art indices, or even shares in companies that deal in art. These can offer multiple benefits, including diversification and professional management.

In conclusion, investing in alternative assets like cryptocurrency or art can be an exciting and rewarding opportunity, but it’s important to understand the risks and take proper precautions. Investing in both art and cryptocurrency require significant research and a willingness to take on risk. Investors should carefully consider their options and always seek the advice of a qualified financial professional before making a commitment.
