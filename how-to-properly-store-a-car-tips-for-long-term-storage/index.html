How to Properly Store a Car: Tips for Long-Term Storage


Welcome to our blog post where we’ll be diving into the important topic of properly storing a car for the long-term. Whether you’re going on an extended vacation or not planning to use your vehicle for an extended period, knowing how to store it correctly is crucial. By following these tips, you’ll ensure that your car remains in top condition, ready for your return. Let’s get started!

Cleanliness is Key:
Before putting your car into storage, it’s essential to give it a thorough cleaning. Washing and waxing the exterior will protect the paintwork, while vacuuming and cleaning the interior will help prevent any odors or damage from developing over time. Don’t forget to remove any food crumbs or items that could attract pests as well.

Fluid Check:
Check all the fluids in your car and change them if necessary. Old and dirty fluids can cause damage to various components of your vehicle, so it’s best to start with fresh oil, coolant, and brake fluid. Topping up the fuel tank and adding a fuel stabilizer will prevent the fuel from deteriorating while in storage.

Tire Care:
Properly inflating the tires is crucial to prevent flat spots from forming during long-term storage. Consider investing in a set of jack stands to take the weight off the tires, or rotate them periodically to distribute the weight evenly. This will help maintain their shape and integrity.

Battery Maintenance:
If your car will be stored for an extended period, it’s advisable to disconnect the battery to prevent it from draining. Alternatively, you can use a battery maintainer or trickle charger to keep it charged throughout the storage duration. Just remember to follow the manufacturer’s instructions when using these devices.

Protective Cover:
Investing in a high-quality, breathable car cover is a wise decision to protect your vehicle from dust, moisture, and any potential scratches. Ensure that the cover fits properly, and consider using a soft cloth or towels to cover areas prone to scratches, such as the side mirrors or door handles.

Ideal Storage Environment:
Finding the right storage location is crucial to ensure the longevity of your car. Ideally, you want a cool, dry, and well-ventilated space that is shielded from direct sunlight. If possible, avoid outdoor storage as it exposes your vehicle to the elements. A garage or a dedicated storage facility with climate control is a perfect choice.

Regular Check-Ups:
Even during storage, it’s important to periodically check on your car. Every few weeks, start the engine and let it run for a few minutes to prevent any mechanical components from seizing up. Additionally, check for any signs of pests, leaks, or damage that may have occurred during storage.

Insurance and Legal Considerations:
Remember to review your insurance policy to ensure that your car is adequately covered during storage. Some insurance providers offer specialized coverage options for vehicles in long-term storage. Also, check with local authorities for any regulations or requirements related to keeping an unattended vehicle in your area.

By following these tips, you can ensure that your car remains in top condition during its time in storage. When the time comes to retrieve it, you’ll be ready to hit the road without any unexpected surprises.
