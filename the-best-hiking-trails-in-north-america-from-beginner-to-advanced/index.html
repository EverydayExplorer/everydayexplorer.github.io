The Best Hiking Trails in North America: From Beginner to Advanced


Hiking is a popular outdoor activity that allows people to explore and connect with nature while challenging themselves physically. North America is home to numerous breathtaking hiking trails, offering something for every level of hiker. From beginners looking for a leisurely stroll through scenic landscapes to experienced adventurers seeking thrilling, rugged terrains, there is a trail for everyone. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best hiking trails in North America, catering to hikers of all skill levels and preferences.

Beginner Level Trails

  1. The Appalachian Trail

The Appalachian Trail is one of the most iconic hiking routes in North America, spanning approximately 2,190 miles along the eastern coast of the United States. While completing the entire trail can take several months, beginners can opt for shorter day hikes or weekend trips, experiencing the beauty of the Appalachian Mountains. Hiking through dense forests, across picturesque meadows, and along stunning ridgelines, beginners will be captivated by the scenic beauty and tranquility of this trail.

  1. Angel’s Landing

Located in Zion National Park, Utah, Angel’s Landing is a popular trail known for its stunning panoramic views. Although it can be challenging at times, the well-maintained trail offers chains along steep sections to assist hikers. The hike is approximately five miles round trip and rewards hikers with breathtaking vistas of the Zion Canyon.

  1. Calf Creek Falls

Situated in Grand Staircase-Escalante National Monument, Utah, the Calf Creek Falls trail is perfect for beginners looking to experience the beauty of a peaceful desert oasis. The 6-mile round trip hike takes you to a stunning 126-foot waterfall, passing through beautiful red rock canyons and offering plenty of photo opportunities. The trail is relatively flat, making it ideal for families and those looking for a less strenuous hike.

Intermediate Level Trails

  1. The Pacific Crest Trail

Stretching approximately 2,650 miles from Mexico to Canada, the Pacific Crest Trail (PCT) is a dream for many experienced hikers. While thru-hiking the entire trail may not be feasible for everyone, there are plenty of shorter sections that offer breathtaking views and challenging terrain. The PCT passes through diverse landscapes, including deserts, mountains, and forests, providing hikers with a unique experience every step of the way.

  1. The West Coast Trail

Located on Vancouver Island, British Columbia, the West Coast Trail is a 47-mile hiking route that showcases the rugged beauty of the Canadian west coast. This challenging trail takes hikers through remote rainforests, across sandy beaches, and along towering cliffs. With its challenging terrain and unpredictable weather conditions, this trail is perfect for intermediate level hikers seeking a thrilling adventure.

  1. The Presidential Traverse

Situated in New Hampshire’s White Mountains, the Presidential Traverse is a strenuous but incredibly rewarding hike. Spanning approximately 23 miles, hikers will encounter challenging rocky terrain, steep ascents, and unpredictable weather. The trail offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains, including the opportunity to summit several peaks such as Mount Washington. This hike is not for the faint of heart but is a favorite among experienced hikers looking for a challenging day or multi-day adventure.

Advanced Level Trails

  1. The Grand Canyon Rim-to-Rim

Crossing from one side of the Grand Canyon to the other, the Rim-to-Rim hike is a bucket list experience for many hikers. Covering approximately 24 miles, this trail offers stunning views of the vast and awe-inspiring canyon. The hike is challenging due to steep descents and ascents, extreme temperatures, and limited water sources. Proper preparation and physical fitness are essential for tackling this advanced level trail.

  1. Mount Rainier’s Wonderland Trail

Circumnavigating Washington’s Mount Rainier, the Wonderland Trail is a challenging and stunningly beautiful 93-mile trail. With dramatic mountain views, glacial rivers, and alpine meadows, this hike offers a wilderness experience like no other. Completing the entire trail requires several weeks of hiking and extensive preparation, making it a perfect adventure for advanced hikers looking for a multi-day expedition.

  1. The Long Range Traverse

In Newfoundland and Labrador’s Gros Morne National Park, the Long Range Traverse is a remote and demanding hike that spans approximately 35 miles. With no marked trails and challenging terrain, this trail is strictly for experienced hikers with advanced navigational skills. Hikers will face steep climbs, rugged coastlines, and potentially challenging weather conditions. However, the stunning landscapes and untouched wilderness make this trail an unforgettable experience for those seeking a true adventure.

Conclusion

North America offers a diverse range of hiking trails, catering to the needs and skill levels of hikers from beginners to advanced. Whether you are looking for a leisurely stroll through breathtaking scenery or a challenging adventure in remote wilderness, these trails provide opportunities for everyone to connect with nature and explore their limits. Remember, regardless of the level of difficulty, always come prepared, respect the environment, and hike responsibly. Lace up your boots, pack your essentials, and set off on an unforgettable journey through some of the best hiking trails North America has to offer.
