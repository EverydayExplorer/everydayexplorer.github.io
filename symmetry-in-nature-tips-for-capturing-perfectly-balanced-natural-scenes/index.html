Symmetry in Nature: Tips for Capturing Perfectly Balanced Natural Scenes


As humans, we are naturally drawn to symmetry. We find beauty and harmony in balanced compositions and perfectly mirrored designs. And where better to find symmetry than in nature? From the delicate wings of a butterfly to the intricately patterned petals of a flower, symmetry is abundant in the natural world. In this blog post, we will explore the concept of symmetry in nature and provide some tips for capturing perfectly balanced natural scenes in your photographs.

First and foremost, it is important to understand what symmetry is. Symmetry refers to a balanced arrangement of elements on either side of an axis or a central point. It can be classified into two types - bilateral symmetry and radial symmetry. Bilateral symmetry is the most commonly observed type and is characterized by a mirror-like reflection on either side of a central axis. Radial symmetry, on the other hand, involves a central point from which identical patterns radiate outwards.

One of the most popular examples of bilateral symmetry in nature is the butterfly. Their delicate wings are often perfectly mirrored, with intricate patterns and vibrant colors on each side. When photographing butterflies, try to capture their wingspan with both sides fully visible. Position yourself to capture the butterfly from a frontal perspective, ensuring that both sides of the wings are showcased in your composition.

Flowers are another prime example of symmetry in nature. The petals of a flower can exhibit both bilateral and radial symmetry. For bilateral symmetry, focus on capturing the flower head-on, ensuring that the petals on both sides are evenly displayed. To highlight radial symmetry, position yourself directly above the flower and photograph it from a top-down perspective. This will showcase the flower’s concentric patterns radiating outwards.

Water reflections offer a unique opportunity to capture natural symmetry. Find a calm body of water such as a lake or pond, and position yourself to capture the reflection of a subject with striking symmetry, such as a mountain or a tree. Ensure that the reflection is crisp and clear to emphasize the symmetry in your composition. Additionally, consider using a polarizing filter to reduce glare and further enhance the reflection.

Patterns in nature provide another avenue for capturing symmetry. Look for repetitive patterns found in rocks, tree bark, or even in the arrangement of leaves. By positioning your camera to capture the pattern in its entirety, you can create a visually compelling image that showcases the inherent symmetry in nature. Pay attention to lines, shapes, and textures to enhance the overall impact of the pattern.

When composing your photographs, keep the rule of thirds in mind. This fundamental rule of photography involves dividing the frame into a grid of nine equal parts and placing key elements along these lines or at their intersections. By aligning symmetrical elements with these lines, you can achieve a harmonious balance in your composition. Experiment with the placement of your subject within the frame to find the most pleasing arrangement.

Lastly, remember that lighting plays a crucial role in capturing symmetry in nature. Soft, even light is generally the most flattering for showcasing symmetry. Overcast days or the soft light of sunrise or sunset can help minimize harsh shadows and bring out the details in your subject. However, don’t be afraid to experiment with backlighting or other dramatic lighting conditions to create unique and visually striking images.

In conclusion, symmetry is a fundamental concept that can be found abundantly in nature. By understanding the different types of symmetry and employing various techniques, you can capture perfectly balanced natural scenes in your photographs. Whether it’s the wings of a butterfly, the petals of a flower, or the reflections in a body of water, nature offers endless opportunities to explore and celebrate symmetry. So, grab your camera, venture into the great outdoors, and let nature’s symmetry inspire your photography!
