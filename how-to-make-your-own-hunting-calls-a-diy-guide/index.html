How to Make Your Own Hunting Calls: A DIY Guide


Creating your own hunting calls can be a rewarding experience that enhances your hunting adventures. Not only does it allow you to customize sounds to suit specific hunting situations, but it also adds a personal touch to your outdoor experience. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore various types of hunting calls, the materials you need, and step-by-step instructions to craft your own calls for game such as ducks, deer, and turkeys.

Understanding Hunting Calls

Hunting calls are tools used to mimic the sounds of animals, attracting them closer to the hunter. Different species respond to different sounds, and understanding these nuances is key to successful hunting. Let’s dive into the types of hunting calls you can create.

Types of Hunting Calls

  1. Duck Calls: Mimic the quacks and whistles of various duck species.
  2. Deer Calls: Replicate the sounds of does, fawns, or bucks to attract deer.
  3. Turkey Calls: Imitate the distinct sounds of turkeys, such as clucks, purrs, and yelps.

Materials Needed

Before embarking on your DIY hunting call project, gather the necessary materials. Depending on the type of call you want to create, the materials will vary slightly.

General Materials

  • Wood: Cedar, mahogany, or walnut are excellent choices for crafting calls.
  • Plastic: PVC pipes can be used for duck calls.
  • Rope or String: Useful for some types of calls, especially for turkey calls.
  • Drill: For making holes and shaping your call.
  • Saw: To cut wood or plastic to the desired size.
  • Sandpaper: For smoothing edges and surfaces.
  • Finish: Wood stain or sealant to protect your finished call.

Crafting a Duck Call

Step 1: Choose Your Material

Select a piece of wood or a PVC pipe. For a traditional wooden duck call, a piece of hardwood about 6 inches long will work well.

Step 2: Shape the Call

  1. Cut the Material: If using wood, cut it to a length of about 6 inches and a diameter of about 1.5 inches. For PVC, a 1.5-inch diameter pipe will suffice.
  2. Drill the Insert Hole: At one end, drill a hole that’s about 3/4 inch wide and 1.5 inches deep.
  3. Create the Tone Board: Cut a thin piece of wood or plastic to fit snugly inside the call. This will be your tone board. Make sure it’s about 1/8 inch thick.

Step 3: Assemble the Call

  1. Insert the Tone Board: Place it in the drilled hole, ensuring it fits tightly.
  2. Seal the End: If using PVC, cap the end. For wood, use a wood plug to seal it.

Step 4: Finishing Touches

Sand the exterior to remove any rough edges and apply a finish to protect your call from the elements.

Step 5: Testing the Call

To test your duck call, blow gently into the opening. Adjust the tone board if needed to produce the desired quack sound.

Crafting a Deer Call

Step 1: Gather Your Materials

A simple deer grunt call can be made using a small piece of wood or a plastic bottle.

Step 2: Create the Call

  1. Choose a Bottle: A small plastic bottle will work well. Cut a small hole in the side of the bottle near the bottom to create the sound chamber.
  2. Make a Reed: Cut a small piece of plastic or thin wood to serve as a reed. This should fit over the hole you’ve created.

Step 3: Assemble the Call

  1. Attach the Reed: Secure the reed over the hole with tape or glue, ensuring it can vibrate freely.
  2. Seal the Bottle: Make sure the top of the bottle is sealed to maintain air pressure.

Step 4: Finishing Touches

You can paint or camouflage the bottle for a more natural look.

Step 5: Testing the Call

Blow into the bottle, adjusting the pressure until you achieve a deep grunt sound that mimics a buck.

Crafting a Turkey Call

Step 1: Gather Your Materials

A turkey box call is a great project for beginners. You’ll need a wooden box, a lid, and a wooden stick for the striker.

Step 2: Shape the Call

  1. Create the Box: Cut a rectangle of wood that is about 8 inches long, 4 inches wide, and 2 inches high for the box.
  2. Create the Lid: Cut a piece of wood that is slightly larger than the box’s top for the lid.

Step 3: Assemble the Call

  1. Attach the Lid: Use hinges to attach the lid to one side of the box.
  2. Create the Striker: Use a thin piece of wood, about 6 inches long, for the striker.

Step 4: Finishing Touches

Sand the edges and apply a finish to protect the wood.

Step 5: Testing the Call

To test, gently slide the striker across the lid to produce a clucking or yelping sound.

Tips for Success

  • Practice: Like any skill, practice is vital. Spend time honing your technique with each call.
  • Experiment: Don’t be afraid to try different materials and designs. Each modification can yield unique sounds.
  • Research: Listen to recordings of real animal sounds to get a better understanding of what you’re trying to replicate.

Conclusion

Making your own hunting calls is an exciting and fulfilling project that can enhance your hunting experience. By crafting personalized calls, you not only save money but also gain a deeper connection to the art of hunting. Whether you’re calling ducks, deer, or turkeys, your DIY calls can make all the difference in attracting game. Remember to practice and refine your techniques, and soon you’ll be enjoying the fruits of your labor in the great outdoors. Happy hunting!
