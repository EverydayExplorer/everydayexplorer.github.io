Hunting Turkeys: Calls and Decoys for a Successful Hunt


There’s nothing quite like the thrill of turkey hunting. It’s a sport that requires patience, skill, and a deep understanding of turkey behavior. Whether you’re a seasoned hunter or just starting out, mastering the art of turkey calls and decoys can make all the difference between a successful hunt and going home empty-handed.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the different types of turkey calls and decoys available, as well as tips and techniques for using them effectively in the field.

Understanding Turkey Calls

Turkey calls come in a variety of forms, each designed to mimic the sounds of a turkey in different situations. Here are some of the most common types of turkey calls:

  1. Box Calls: Box calls are perhaps the most versatile turkey calls available. They consist of a small wooden box with a hinged lid that, when rubbed against the side of the box, produces realistic turkey sounds. Box calls are relatively easy to use and are great for beginners.

  2. Slate Calls: Also known as pot calls, slate calls consist of a small piece of slate or glass set into a wooden or plastic pot. By running a striker across the surface of the slate, you can produce a wide range of turkey sounds, from soft purrs to loud yelps.

  3. Mouth Calls: Mouth calls, or diaphragm calls, are placed inside the hunter’s mouth and operated by the tongue and airflow. While mouth calls can be more challenging to master, they offer hands-free operation, making them ideal for situations where you need to remain still.

  4. Locator Calls: Locator calls are used to locate turkeys in the wild. These calls mimic the sounds of other animals, such as owls, crows, or coyotes, which can trigger a response from nearby turkeys.

Choosing the Right Decoys

In addition to calls, decoys are another essential tool for turkey hunters. Decoys are lifelike replicas of turkeys that are used to attract birds into shooting range. Here are some tips for choosing and using decoys effectively:

  1. Selecting Decoy Types: There are several types of turkey decoys available, including hen decoys, jake decoys, and tom (or gobbler) decoys. Hen decoys are typically used to attract male turkeys looking for a mate, while jake and tom decoys can be used to provoke a response from dominant males.

  2. Placement is Key: When setting up decoys, it’s essential to consider the natural behavior of turkeys. Place decoys in open areas where they can be easily seen from a distance, and position them facing away from your blind or hiding spot to draw approaching turkeys into shooting range.

  3. Use Realism to Your Advantage: The more lifelike your decoys appear, the more likely they are to attract turkeys. Look for decoys with realistic colors, textures, and movement features, such as swiveling heads or flapping wings.

  4. Don’t Overdo It: While decoys can be highly effective, it’s essential not to overuse them. In some situations, a single decoy may be all you need to attract nearby turkeys. Experiment with different setups and configurations to see what works best in your area.

Tips for Success

In addition to mastering turkey calls and decoys, there are several other tips and techniques that can help increase your chances of success in the field:

  1. Practice, Practice, Practice: Like any skill, mastering turkey calling takes practice. Spend time practicing different calls and techniques until you feel confident in your abilities.

  2. Be Patient: Turkey hunting requires patience and perseverance. Be prepared to spend long hours in the field waiting for the perfect opportunity to present itself.

  3. Stay Concealed: Turkeys have excellent eyesight and can easily spot movement in their surroundings. Use natural cover or a well-camouflaged blind to conceal your presence and avoid spooking approaching birds.

  4. Know When to Call: Knowing when to call and when to remain silent is crucial for success in turkey hunting. Pay attention to the behavior of nearby birds and adjust your calling strategy accordingly.

By mastering the art of turkey calls and decoys and employing these tips and techniques in the field, you can increase your chances of a successful hunt and enjoy an unforgettable hunting experience. So grab your gear, head out into the woods, and get ready for an adventure you’ll never forget!
