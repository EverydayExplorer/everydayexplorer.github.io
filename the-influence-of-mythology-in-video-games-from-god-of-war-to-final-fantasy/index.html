The Influence of Mythology in Video Games: From God of War to Final Fantasy


Video games have become a major form of entertainment in the modern world. With their immersive gameplay and captivating storylines, video games have the power to transport players to fantastical realms filled with mythical creatures, gods, and heroes. One of the key elements that have had a significant influence on video game development is mythology. From ancient Greek gods to Japanese folklore, mythology has served as a rich source of inspiration for game designers and developers. In this blog, we will explore the influence of mythology in video games, specifically focusing on two iconic franchises: God of War and Final Fantasy.

*God of War: An Epic Journey through Greek Mythology*

One of the most prominent examples of mythology in video games is the God of War series. Developed by Santa Monica Studio, God of War takes players on an epic journey through the world of Greek mythology. The game follows the story of Kratos, a vengeful Spartan warrior who battles against gods, monsters, and titans of Greek mythology.

The game seamlessly blends elements of ancient mythology with intense action-packed gameplay. Players encounter iconic gods such as Zeus, Poseidon, and Hades, as well as mythical creatures like the Hydra and the Minotaur. The Greek mythology-inspired setting provides a fertile ground for the game’s narrative and creates a sense of grandeur and scale that is unique to the series.

The God of War franchise has also delved into Norse mythology with the release of God of War (2018). This installment introduces players to the world of Norse gods and explores the relationship between Kratos and his son, Atreus. By incorporating different mythological elements, the game not only expands the lore of the series but also adds an exciting new dimension to the gameplay experience.

*Final Fantasy: Blending Eastern and Western Mythology*

The Final Fantasy series has been captivating gamers since its inception in 1987. Developed by Square Enix, the franchise has explored various themes and settings, often drawing inspiration from mythology. The game’s unique blend of Eastern and Western mythologies creates a distinct world that players can immerse themselves in.

Each entry in the Final Fantasy series features a different universe with its own mythology. From the crystals in Final Fantasy I to the summon creatures and gods in later installments, the games incorporate mythological elements that enrich the gameplay experience. For example, Final Fantasy VI introduces players to the concept of Espers, magical beings that are reminiscent of traditional summon creatures from mythology.

Final Fantasy games also draw on folklore and mythos from different cultures. In Final Fantasy X, the game’s setting is heavily influenced by Polynesian mythology, incorporating elements like the Zanarkand Ruins and references to mythical creatures such as the Leviathan.

The extensive use of mythology in the Final Fantasy series provides players with a sense of familiarity and wonder. By incorporating elements from different mythological traditions, the games create a unique and immersive world that resonates with players from various cultural backgrounds.

*Impact of Mythology on Video Game Design*

The integration of mythology in video games serves multiple purposes. Firstly, it adds depth and richness to the game’s lore. By drawing from ancient myths and legends, game developers can create intricate and immersive worlds that engage players on a creative and intellectual level.

Secondly, mythology provides developers with a vast pool of inspiration for character and creature designs. The gods, monsters, and heroes from mythological stories offer a diverse range of aesthetics and abilities that can be incorporated into game design. This not only adds variety to the gameplay but also sparks the player’s imagination.

Moreover, mythology in video games facilitates cultural exchange. By including mythologies from different cultures, games expose players to diverse belief systems and folklore. This cross-cultural exchange promotes understanding and appreciation of different traditions, fostering a more inclusive and interconnected gaming community.

*Conclusion*

The influence of mythology in video games, as exemplified by the God of War and Final Fantasy series, is undeniable. These games provide players with an opportunity to explore ancient myths and legends in an interactive and engaging manner. The fusion of mythology and video game design creates a captivating experience that appeals to a wide audience.

Furthermore, the integration of mythology in video games enhances the depth and complexity of the game worlds, adding a layer of authenticity and cultural richness. By drawing inspiration from different mythological traditions, game developers can create unique and immersive experiences that resonate with players.

As video games continue to evolve as an art form, the influence of mythology will undoubtedly remain a significant aspect of their development. Whether it’s battling gods in God of War or embarking on epic quests in Final Fantasy, mythology will continue to shape the way we experience and interact with virtual worlds. So, next time you pick up a controller, remember the enduring influence of mythology that has made video games the epic adventures they are today.
