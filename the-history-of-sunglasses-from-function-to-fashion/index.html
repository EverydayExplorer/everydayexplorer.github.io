The History of Sunglasses: From Function to Fashion


Sunglasses have become an iconic fashion accessory that not only protects our eyes but also adds a touch of style to any outfit. But have you ever wondered about the history of sunglasses and how they evolved from being purely functional to becoming a fashion statement? In this blog post, we will take a journey through time to explore the fascinating history of sunglasses and their transformation from a practical tool to a symbol of fashion.

The origins of sunglasses can be traced back to ancient civilizations. It is believed that the Inuit people of the Arctic region were the first to use flattened walrus ivory as a form of eye protection, reducing the glare from the snow. Similarly, the Roman Emperor Nero was known to watch gladiator fights through polished gemstones to shield his eyes from the sun’s rays.

Fast forward to the 18th century, and we see the emergence of tinted lenses as a means of vision correction. James Ayscough, an English optician, experimented with blue and green lenses to aid in treating certain vision impairments. While the primary purpose was still functionality, these lenses laid the foundation for the future development of sunglasses.

It wasn’t until the early 20th century that sunglasses began to gain popularity as a fashion accessory. In 1929, Sam Foster, the founder of Foster Grant, introduced affordable sunglasses to the mass market. Initially marketed as a means of protecting the eyes from the sun’s harmful rays, sunglasses quickly became a symbol of sophistication and glamour.

The 1930s and 1940s saw Hollywood stars embracing sunglasses as a way to shield their eyes from the paparazzi and maintain an air of mystery. Iconic figures like Audrey Hepburn and Marilyn Monroe turned sunglasses into a must-have fashion item.

In the 1960s, sunglasses experienced a revolution with the introduction of polarized lenses. Polarization technology helped reduce glare and enhance visual clarity, making sunglasses even more functional and appealing. Ray-Ban, a brand known for its timeless designs, released the iconic Wayfarer sunglasses, which became a symbol of rebellion and counterculture.

As the years went by, sunglasses continued to evolve in terms of style, design, and materials. From oversized frames in the 1970s to mirrored lenses in the 1980s, sunglasses became an integral part of self-expression. Designers like Gucci, Prada, and Chanel started incorporating sunglasses into their collections, further cementing their status as a fashion statement.

Today, sunglasses come in a wide variety of styles, shapes, and colors, catering to every individual’s taste and preference. Whether you prefer classic aviators, trendy cat-eye frames, or sporty wraparound styles, there is a pair of sunglasses out there for everyone.

In conclusion, the history of sunglasses showcases their journey from being purely functional to becoming a fashion accessory that defines personal style. From their humble beginnings as eye protection to their transformation into a symbol of glamour and self-expression, sunglasses have come a long way. So the next time you put on a pair of sunglasses, remember that they are not just a tool to shield your eyes from the sun, but also a fashion statement that has stood the test of time.
