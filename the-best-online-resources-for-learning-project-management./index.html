The Best Online Resources for Learning Project Management.


Welcome, fellow project management enthusiasts! Today, we are diving into the vast world of online resources dedicated to helping you expand your knowledge and skills in the art of project management. Whether you’re a seasoned professional looking to stay up-to-date with the latest industry trends, or a beginner looking to embark on a new and exciting career, this blog post will guide you through some of the best online resources available.

1. Project Management Institute (PMI):
Let’s start with the mother of all project management resources - PMI. PMI provides a wealth of knowledge, tools, and resources for project management professionals. Their website offers a plethora of articles, webinars, and exclusive member-only content. They also offer globally recognized certifications such as PMP (Project Management Professional) and CAPM (Certified Associate in Project Management), giving you the credibility you need to excel in your career.

2. Coursera:
Coursera is a popular online learning platform that partners with top universities and organizations to offer a wide range of project management courses. From introductory courses to advanced specializations, Coursera has something for everyone. What sets Coursera apart is its interactive and collaborative learning environment, allowing you to engage with a global community of learners and experts.

3. ProjectManager.com:
If you’re looking for a comprehensive project management tool with built-in learning resources, ProjectManager.com is worth considering. Not only does it provide powerful project management features, but it also offers a library of training videos, tutorials, and templates to help you sharpen your skills. With ProjectManager.com, you can apply what you learn directly to your projects, making it a practical and efficient learning experience.

4. LinkedIn Learning:
Formerly known as Lynda.com, LinkedIn Learning is a leading platform for online learning. It offers a vast library of project management courses taught by industry experts. LinkedIn Learning allows you to learn at your own pace with its flexible course structure. Additionally, it seamlessly integrates with your LinkedIn profile, making it easy to showcase your newfound project management skills to potential employers.

5. A Girl’s Guide to Project Management:
Dedicated to empowering women in project management, Elizabeth Harrin’s blog, “A Girl’s Guide to Project Management,” is a gem for both women and men looking to enhance their project management knowledge. Elizabeth shares her insights and experiences, providing practical tips, tools, and resources that are applicable to any project manager. Her blog is a breath of fresh air in a male-dominated field.

6. Project Management Podcasts:
For those who prefer to learn on the go, project management podcasts are a fantastic resource. “The Project Management Podcast” by Cornelius Fichtner is a well-loved podcast that covers a wide variety of project management topics. Additionally, “PM Point of View” by Kendall Lott and “Project Management Happy Hour” by Kim Essendrup and Kate Anderson offer valuable insights and discussions on project management best practices.

7. ProjectManagement.com:
ProjectManagement.com, powered by PMI, is an online community and resource hub for project management professionals. It offers a wealth of articles, webinars, templates, and tools to help you succeed in your projects. The community forums provide an opportunity to connect with like-minded individuals, ask questions, and share experiences, fostering a sense of camaraderie amongst project management enthusiasts.

In conclusion, the world of project management is constantly evolving, and keeping up with the latest trends and best practices is essential. Thankfully, the internet provides an abundance of resources to support your learning journey. From industry-leading organizations like PMI to interactive platforms like Coursera, the options are endless. So, embrace the power of online resources, broaden your horizons, and take your project management skills to new heights. Happy learning!
