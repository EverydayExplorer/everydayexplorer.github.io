Hunting for Small Game with Airguns: An Alternative Approach


As the seasons change and the weather cools, many hunters begin to think about the upcoming small game hunting seasons. While rifles and shotguns are often the go-to choices for taking down larger game, airguns can be a great option for hunting small game. In this post, we’ll explore the benefits of using airguns for small game hunting and provide some tips and techniques for getting started.

The Benefits of Airgun Hunting

Airguns are often overlooked as a viable option for hunting small game, but they offer several benefits that make them an attractive choice. Firstly, airguns are generally quieter than traditional firearms, which can be a major advantage when hunting in areas where noise needs to be kept to a minimum. This is particularly important when hunting in close proximity to residential areas or during early morning and late evening hunts when noise can carry further.

Another benefit of airgun hunting is the lack of recoil. Airguns are powered by compressed air or CO2, which means that they don’t produce the same level of recoil as traditional firearms. This can make them more comfortable to shoot, particularly for younger or older hunters who may not be able to handle the recoil of a traditional firearm.

Airguns are also relatively inexpensive to operate and maintain. There are no shells to purchase, and the only maintenance required is to clean and lubricate the gun periodically. This makes them a great option for hunters on a budget or those who want to try out small game hunting without breaking the bank.

Choosing the Right Airgun

When choosing an airgun for small game hunting, there are several factors to consider. The first thing to think about is the power and range of the gun. Airguns come in a variety of power levels, ranging from low-powered guns that are suitable for hunting small game like rodents and birds, to high-powered guns that are suitable for hunting larger game like deer and coyotes.

The type of airgun you choose will also depend on the type of small game you plan to hunt. For example, if you’re hunting rabbits and hares, a gun with a high-powered scope and a fast follow-up shot is ideal. If you’re hunting birds, a gun with a lower-powered scope and a more precise shot is better.

Another important factor to consider is the accuracy of the gun. Airguns can be very accurate, but they can also be affected by a variety of factors such as temperature, humidity, and air pressure. Look for a gun with a high-quality scope and a consistent trigger pull to ensure that you’re getting the most accurate shot possible.

Tips and Techniques for Hunting Small Game with Airguns

Once you’ve chosen the right airgun, it’s time to start thinking about tips and techniques for hunting small game. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Stalking: Small game is often most active at dawn and dusk, when temperatures are cooler and cover is more abundant. Use these times to your advantage by stalking your prey quietly and patiently.
  • Silence: Remember that airguns are quieter than traditional firearms, but they’re still not silent. Be careful not to make any sudden noises that might spook your prey.
  • Patience: Small game can be skittish, so be prepared to wait for long periods of time before getting a shot. Keep your eyes and ears open, and be ready to pounce when the opportunity arises.
  • Follow-up shots: Airguns are generally more accurate than traditional firearms, but they can still be affected by factors like temperature and air pressure. Practice your follow-up shots to ensure that you’re getting the most accurate shot possible.
  • Cleaning and maintenance: Airguns require regular cleaning and maintenance to ensure that they’re functioning properly. Take the time to clean your gun regularly, and perform any necessary maintenance tasks.

Conclusion

Hunting small game with airguns can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it requires patience, practice, and the right equipment. By choosing the right airgun and following the tips and techniques outlined above, you can increase your chances of success and enjoy a fun and challenging hunting experience. Remember to always follow local laws and regulations, and to respect the environment and the animals you’re hunting. Happy hunting!
