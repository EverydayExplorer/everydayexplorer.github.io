Predator Hunting Tips: Tactics for Pursuing Coyotes, Foxes, and More


Predator hunting has gained popularity among hunters in recent years, thanks to the thrill and challenge it offers. Pursuing predators such as coyotes, foxes, and other cunning creatures requires a different set of skills and strategies compared to hunting big game. In this blog post, we will explore some effective predator hunting tips and tactics that will help increase your success in the field.

1. Scout the Area

Before embarking on your predator hunting adventure, it is crucial to thoroughly scout the hunting area. Look for signs of predator activity such as tracks, scat, and kill sites. Understanding the movement patterns and habits of your target species will give you a significant advantage when it comes to setting up your hunting strategy.

2. Use Predator Calls

Predator calls are an essential tool in attracting predators to your location. Electronic calls that mimic the sounds of distressed prey, injured or mating animals can be highly effective. Experiment with different calls and sounds to find the ones that work best for the predators in your area. It’s important to note that predators have keen senses, so make sure to use calls at appropriate volumes and frequencies.

3. Choose the Right Hunting Gear

Predator hunting often requires specialized gear to maximize your chances of success. Opt for a rifle or shotgun chambered in a caliber suitable for predator hunting, such as .223 Remington or .22-250 Remington. These calibers offer flat trajectories and sufficient energy to take down predators effectively. Additionally, invest in a high-quality scope with good low-light capabilities, as predators are most active during dawn and dusk.

4. Set Up Effective Ambush Points

Once you’ve scouted the area and identified potential hotspots, it’s time to set up your ambush points. Predators like coyotes and foxes tend to follow prominent features such as ridgelines, fence lines, and the edges of fields. Position yourself downwind or crosswind to increase your chances of remaining undetected.

5. Camouflage and Concealment

Predators have exceptional eyesight, so it’s crucial to blend in with your surroundings. Wear camouflage clothing that matches the terrain and use natural cover, such as brush or trees, to conceal your position. Situating yourself in natural shadows or using natural blinds like rock formations can also help you stay hidden and increase your chances of a successful hunt.

6. Opt for Decoys

Using decoys can be a game-changer in predator hunting. Predator decoys, such as those imitating injured prey or other predators, can pique the curiosity of predators and bring them within shooting range. Combined with calling techniques, decoys can significantly increase your chances of a successful hunt.

7. Hunt During Optimal Times

Predators are most active during low light conditions, especially during dawn and dusk. Plan your hunting trips accordingly, as these time periods offer the best chances of spotting and targeting predators. Consider the moon phase as well; hunting during a full moon can provide better visibility for both predator and hunter.

8. Practice Patience and Persistence

Predator hunting requires patience and persistence. Unlike big game hunting, which often involves long periods of sitting and waiting, predator hunting can be more dynamic. It may require frequent moves to different locations based on the response of the predators. Be prepared to adapt your strategy and stay patient until you find success.

9. Use Thermal Imaging Technology

Investing in thermal imaging technology can give you a considerable advantage when hunting predators, especially during low light conditions or when concealed by dense cover. Thermal scopes or thermal monoculars allow you to spot predators by detecting their body heat, making it easier to locate them even in challenging conditions.

10. Practice Ethical Hunting

Lastly, always prioritize ethical hunting practices when pursuing predators. Ensure that your shooting skills are proficient, and take clean and ethical shots to ensure a quick and humane kill. Respect the land and its wildlife, and follow all local hunting regulations and laws.

In conclusion, predator hunting can provide an exciting and challenging experience for hunters looking to test their skills. By following these predator hunting tips and tactics, you’ll greatly increase your chances of success in the field. Remember, hunting predators requires preparation, patience, and adapting to the ever-changing dynamics of the hunt. Happy hunting!
