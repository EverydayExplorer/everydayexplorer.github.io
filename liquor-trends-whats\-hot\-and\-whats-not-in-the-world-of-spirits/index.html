Liquor Trends: Whats\ Hot\ and\ Whats Not in the World of Spirits


As we step into a new year, it’s time to take a look at the evolving trends in the world of spirits. From craft distilleries to unusual flavor combinations, the industry is constantly changing and adapting to the ever-growing demands of consumers. In this blog post, we will explore some of the hottest and not-so-hot trends in the world of liquor, providing you with insights into what’s popular and what’s losing its appeal.

Craft Distilleries:

One of the biggest trends in the spirits industry is the rise of craft distilleries. Consumers are increasingly interested in the story behind the spirit, as well as the craftsmanship that goes into creating it. Craft distilleries offer unique and small-batch spirits that are often made using traditional distilling methods. This trend has been fueled by a desire for authenticity and a focus on supporting local businesses. Consumers are willing to pay a premium for spirits that are handcrafted and produced in small quantities, making craft distilleries a hot trend to watch.

Unusual Flavor Combinations:

Gone are the days when spirits were limited to traditional flavors. We are now witnessing a surge in unusual flavor combinations that cater to the adventurous palate. From bacon-infused vodka to chili-flavored tequila, the possibilities seem endless. Distilleries are experimenting with different ingredients, creating unique flavor profiles that appeal to a variety of tastes. Consumers are becoming more open to trying unconventional flavors, and this trend is expected to continue to grow.

Sustainable Practices:

In recent years, there has been a growing emphasis on sustainability in the spirits industry. From organic and biodynamic ingredients to eco-friendly packaging, distilleries are making efforts to reduce their environmental footprint. Consumers are increasingly mindful of the impact their choices have on the planet, and they are seeking out spirits that align with their values. This trend goes beyond the product itself and extends to the entire production process, making sustainability a hot topic in the world of spirits.

Low and No Alcohol Options:

Another trend that is gaining traction in the world of spirits is the rise of low and no alcohol options. While the demand for alcohol-free beverages has been steadily increasing, it has now extended to the spirits category as well. Distilleries are responding to this demand by creating alcohol-free spirits that mimic the flavors and aromas of traditional spirits. This trend appeals to those who are looking to enjoy the experience of drinking spirits without the alcohol content.

Classic Cocktails with a Twist:

While new and innovative creations are in the spotlight, classic cocktails are not going anywhere. However, what’s changing is the way these cocktails are presented. Mixologists are putting their own spin on classic recipes, using unique ingredients and techniques to create modern and exciting versions of the old favorites. This trend appeals to both the traditionalists who appreciate the familiarity of a classic cocktail, as well as the adventurous drinkers who are looking for something new and different.

What’s Not:

Now that we have covered some of the hot trends in the world of spirits, let’s take a look at what’s not gaining much traction. Despite the popularity of flavored spirits, there seems to be a decline in artificially flavored spirits. Consumers are increasingly seeking out natural flavors and are turned off by artificial additives. Similarly, overly sweet and syrupy cocktails are losing their appeal, as consumers are gravitating towards more balanced and nuanced flavors.

In conclusion, the world of spirits is constantly evolving, with new trends emerging and fading away. Craft distilleries, unusual flavor combinations, sustainable practices, and low and no alcohol options are some of the hottest trends in the industry. At the same time, artificial flavors and overly sweet cocktails are losing their appeal. As consumers become more educated and discerning, distilleries will continue to push the boundaries of creativity, striving to meet the ever-changing tastes of their audience. So, whether you’re a spirits enthusiast or a casual drinker, next time you’re at a bar or liquor store, keep an eye out for these trends and broaden your horizons by trying something new and exciting. Happy sipping!
