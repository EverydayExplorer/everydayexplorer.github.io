Achieve the Perfect Winged Eyeliner: Techniques for All Eye Shapes


Winged eyeliner is a timeless beauty trend that adds a touch of glamour and sophistication to any makeup look. However, many people struggle to create the perfect wing, as it can be challenging to get those crisp, sharp lines. The good news is that with the right techniques and practice, anyone can achieve the perfect winged eyeliner, regardless of their eye shape. In this blog post, we will explore various techniques and tips that will help you master this classic makeup look.

Before we dive into the techniques, it’s crucial to understand how to choose the right eyeliner for your needs. There are various types of eyeliners available, such as pencil, gel, and liquid. While all can be used to create a winged look, liquid eyeliner tends to offer the most precise and sharp lines, making it the preferred choice for many. When choosing a liquid eyeliner, opt for one with a fine, pointed applicator brush for better control.

Now, let’s discuss the different techniques for achieving the perfect winged eyeliner based on your eye shape.

1. Almond Eyes:
Almond-shaped eyes are considered the most versatile when it comes to eyeliner application. Start by drawing a thin line along your upper lash line, starting from the inner corner and extending towards the outer corner of your eye. Then, create a small flick at the outer corner, angling it slightly upwards. Follow the natural shape of your eye for the most flattering wing.

2. Round Eyes:
For round eyes, the goal is to elongate and create the illusion of an almond shape. Begin by drawing a thin line along your upper lash line, gradually thickening it as you reach the outer corner. Instead of flicking the wing upwards, angle it slightly downwards to create a more elongated shape. This will help counterbalance the roundness of your eyes and achieve a more lifted appearance.

3. Hooded Eyes:
Hooded eyes have a naturally limited visible lid space, so the focus should be on creating a subtle wing that doesn’t overpower the eye. Start by applying eyeliner along your upper lash line, making sure to keep the line thin and close to the lashes. When creating the wing, keep it short and slightly angled upwards to lift the eye without covering the lid space entirely. Also, avoid extending the wing too far outwards, as it can make the eyes appear droopy.

4. Monolid Eyes:
Monolid eyes have little to no crease, making it essential to create a wing that adds dimension and depth. Start by applying eyeliner along your upper lash line, making it slightly thicker as you move towards the outer corner. To create the wing, draw a diagonal line extending from the outer corner towards the end of your eyebrow. Then, connect the diagonal line to the upper lash line, filling in the shape to create a soft winged effect.

Regardless of your eye shape, there are a few general tips that can help you achieve a flawless winged eyeliner look:

- Practice makes perfect: Winged eyeliner requires precision and practice. Don’t be discouraged if it doesn’t turn out perfectly the first few times. Keep practicing, and you’ll get better with time.
- Use tape or a spoon as a guide: If you’re struggling with getting a straight wing, you can use scotch tape or the edge of a spoon as a guide. Place it diagonally against the outer corner of your eye and use it as a stencil to create a crisp wing.
- Clean up mistakes with a concealer: If you make a mistake or your lines aren’t as precise as you’d like, dip a thin brush into a concealer that matches your skin tone. Use the concealer to clean up any uneven lines or smudges, giving you a clean and polished winged eyeliner look.
- Curl your eyelashes: To enhance the overall effect of your winged eyeliner, don’t forget to curl your eyelashes before applying mascara. This will open up your eyes and make the winged liner pop even more.

In conclusion, achieving the perfect winged eyeliner is achievable with the right techniques and a little practice. Regardless of your eye shape, understanding how to adapt the wing to complement your features is key. Experiment with different thicknesses, lengths, and angles until you find what works best for you. Remember, makeup is about self-expression, so have fun, get creative, and rock that winged eyeliner with confidence!
