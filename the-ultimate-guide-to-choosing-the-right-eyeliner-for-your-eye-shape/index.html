The Ultimate Guide to Choosing the Right Eyeliner for Your Eye Shape


Eyeliner is a staple in every makeup lover’s beauty arsenal. It has the power to transform your eyes and elevate your overall look. But with so many options available, choosing the right eyeliner for your eye shape can be a daunting task. Fear not, for today, we are here to guide you through the process and help you find the perfect eyeliner that will enhance your natural beauty.

When it comes to eyeliner, one size does not fit all. Different eye shapes require different techniques and products to achieve the desired effect. To make the selection process easier for you, we have compiled the ultimate guide to choosing the right eyeliner for your eye shape. So without further ado, let’s dive in!

1. Almond Eyes:
Almond-shaped eyes are considered the most versatile and can pull off various eyeliner styles. For a classic look, opt for a thin line along the lash line, gradually thickening it towards the outer corner. If you want to make your eyes appear larger, try a cat-eye flick. Gel or liquid eyeliners work best for achieving precision with almond-shaped eyes.

2. Round Eyes:
To create the illusion of elongated eyes, go for a winged eyeliner. Start from the inner corner of the eye, applying a thin line along the lash line and extending it slightly past the outer corner. This will help elongate the eyes and give them a more defined shape. A felt-tip or liquid eyeliner with a fine tip will make the process easier.

3. Hooded Eyes:
Hooded eyes have a fold of skin that partially covers the crease, making the eyelid appear smaller. To enhance hooded eyes, use a thin, smudge-proof eyeliner and create a slightly thicker line closer to the lash line. Extend the line slightly past the outer corner to create a lifted effect. Gel or pencil eyeliners are great options for hooded eyes.

4. Monolid Eyes:
Monolid eyes lack a visible crease, so it’s important to create depth and dimension with eyeliner. Apply a thin line along the lash line, gradually thickening it towards the outer corner. For a more dramatic look, extend the line upwards into a winged shape. Liquid or gel eyeliners that dry quickly and are smudge-proof are ideal for monolid eyes.

5. Downturned Eyes:
Downturned eyes have a natural droop at the outer corners, and the goal is to lift and elongate them. Begin by applying a thin line along the lash line, gradually thickening it towards the outer corner. Extend the line upwards, creating a subtle winged effect. A waterproof pencil or gel eyeliner works best for downturned eyes.

Remember, practice makes perfect when it comes to applying eyeliner. Experiment with different techniques and products to find what works best for you. And don’t forget to complete your eyeliner look with mascara and eyeshadow to enhance your eye shape even further.

Now that you have the ultimate guide to choosing the right eyeliner for your eye shape, go ahead and experiment with different styles to find your signature look. Embrace your unique features and let your eyes do the talking!
