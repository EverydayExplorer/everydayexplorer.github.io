How Hotels are Adapting to the New Normal - COVID-19 Safety Measures


The COVID-19 pandemic has brought about significant changes in all aspects of life, including the way we travel and stay in hotels. As governments and health organizations continue to emphasize the importance of safety and cleanliness, hotels around the world have been proactively implementing new measures to ensure the health and wellbeing of their guests and staff.

In this blog post, we will explore the various ways hotels have adapted to the new normal, focusing on the COVID-19 safety measures they have put in place.

  1. Enhanced Cleaning and Sanitization Protocols: One of the most noticeable changes in hotels is the increased emphasis on cleaning and sanitization. Hotel staff are now trained to follow enhanced cleaning protocols, including frequent disinfection of high-touch surfaces, such as doorknobs, elevator buttons, and countertops. In addition, common areas and guest rooms are thoroughly cleaned using hospital-grade disinfectants. Many hotels have also implemented electrostatic sprayers and UV-C disinfection technology to further enhance the sanitization process.

  2. Social Distancing Measures: To promote social distancing, hotels have implemented various measures, such as rearranging furniture in common areas to ensure adequate space between guests, limiting the number of people allowed in elevators, and providing markers or floor decals to guide guests in maintaining a safe distance. Hotel restaurants and gyms have also rearranged seating and equipment to allow for proper distancing.

  3. Contactless Check-In and Check-Out: To minimize physical contact, many hotels have introduced contactless check-in and check-out procedures. Guests can now check-in online prior to arrival, receive a digital key, and avoid crowded front desks. Similarly, during check-out, guests can settle their bills electronically, eliminating the need for physical credit card transactions.

  4. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) for Staff: Hotels are prioritizing the safety of their staff by providing them with appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) such as masks, gloves, and face shields. Staff members, especially those in direct guest contact areas, are required to wear PPE at all times. Regular health screenings and temperature checks are also conducted to ensure the wellbeing of the staff.

  5. Improved Ventilation and Air Quality: Hotels are implementing measures to improve ventilation and air quality to reduce the risk of airborne transmission. This includes increasing outdoor air circulation, upgrading filtration systems, and regularly inspecting and maintaining HVAC systems to ensure optimal performance.

  6. Modified Food and Beverage Services: Hotel restaurants and bars have adapted their service offerings to align with the new safety measures. Self-serve buffets and communal tables have been replaced with individually packaged or à la carte options. Room service has become more popular, with contactless delivery to guest rooms. In some cases, hotels have also partnered with local restaurants or food delivery services to offer a wider range of dining options without compromising safety.

  7. Educational and Training Initiatives: Hotels are conducting extensive educational and training programs to ensure that all staff members are well-equipped to implement and adhere to the new safety measures. This includes training on proper cleaning protocols, the correct use of PPE, and effective communication with guests regarding safety guidelines.

  8. Flexibility in Booking and Cancellation Policies: Hotels have recognized the uncertainty brought about by the pandemic and have implemented more flexible booking and cancellation policies. Many hotels now offer fully refundable or amendable reservations to accommodate changes in travel plans or unforeseen circumstances.

It is worth highlighting that these safety measures are continuously evolving as more information and guidelines are provided by health authorities. Hotels are actively monitoring the situation to ensure they stay up to date with the latest recommendations and provide the highest level of safety to their guests.

In conclusion, hotels have taken comprehensive steps to adapt to the new normal and prioritize the health and safety of their guests and staff. With enhanced cleaning protocols, social distancing measures, contactless procedures, and various other measures, hotels are demonstrating their commitment to providing a safe and comfortable environment for travelers during these challenging times. By implementing these COVID-19 safety measures, hotels are helping to restore confidence in the hospitality industry and ensure a positive and secure accommodation experience for all.
