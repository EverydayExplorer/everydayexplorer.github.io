Top 10 Legal Resources for Small Business Owners


When starting a small business, there are numerous legal tasks that need to be tackled to ensure compliance and protect your business. From understanding the legal structure to drafting contracts and navigating intellectual property laws, small business owners often find themselves in need of legal resources to help them navigate the complex world of business law.

To assist small business owners in finding the necessary legal resources, we have compiled a list of the top 10 resources that are essential for small business owners. These resources cover a wide range of legal aspects and provide valuable information to help you make informed decisions for your business.

1. Small Business Administration (SBA): The SBA is a valuable resource for all small business owners. They provide information on all aspects of starting, growing, and managing a small business, including legal guidance. The SBA offers workshops, online courses, and one-on-one counseling to help small business owners understand the legal requirements and processes of running a business.

2. SCORE: SCORE is a nonprofit organization that offers free mentoring services to small business owners. They have a pool of experienced business professionals who can provide guidance on legal matters. Whether you need assistance with contracts, trademarks, or employment law, SCORE mentors can help you navigate the legal complexities of running a small business.

3. U.S. Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO): If you have an innovative product or a unique business name, protecting your intellectual property is crucial. The USPTO website provides guidance on patenting inventions, registering trademarks, and protecting copyrights. It also offers resources to help you search existing patents and trademarks to avoid infringement.

4. LegalZoom: LegalZoom is an online legal service that provides easy access to a range of legal documents and services tailored for small businesses. From forming an LLC to drafting contracts and registering trademarks, LegalZoom simplifies legal processes by providing customizable templates and access to legal professionals.

5. State Small Business Development Centers (SBDCs): SBDCs are local centers that provide free or low-cost assistance to small business owners. These centers are often affiliated with universities or state governments and offer legal resources such as workshops, counseling, and resources specific to each state’s laws.

6. FindLaw: FindLaw is an online resource that provides legal information and interactive tools tailored for small business owners. Their website covers a wide range of legal topics, including business formation, contracts, intellectual property, employment law, and more. FindLaw offers articles, legal guides, and a directory to help you find an attorney specializing in business law in your area.

7. NOLO: NOLO is a legal publishing and software company that provides a wealth of legal information and resources for small business owners. Their books, articles, and online tools cover various topics, including starting a business, contracts, legal disputes, and more. NOLO also offers do-it-yourself legal forms and software to help you save on legal costs.

8. American Bar Association (ABA): The ABA offers a variety of legal resources for small business owners. Their website provides articles, webinars, and legal guides covering business law and its various aspects. The ABA’s directory can help you find a local attorney specializing in small business law.

9. IRS Small Business and Self-Employed Tax Center: As a small business owner, understanding your tax obligations is of paramount importance. The IRS Small Business and Self-Employed Tax Center provides resources to help you understand and navigate tax requirements for your business. From keeping proper records to understanding deductions and credits, this resource can help you stay compliant and minimize your tax liability.

10. Local Bar Associations: Local bar associations often have resources dedicated to assisting small businesses. These associations can provide referrals to attorneys specializing in small business law, as well as educational events and workshops related to legal issues faced by small businesses. Connecting with your local bar association can provide you with access to a network of legal professionals and valuable information specific to your region.

By utilizing these top 10 legal resources for small business owners, you can arm yourself with the knowledge and support needed to navigate the legal challenges of running a small business. Remember, each business is unique, and it’s always advisable to consult with an attorney for personalized legal advice. With the right legal resources at your disposal, you can protect your business and focus on its growth and success.
