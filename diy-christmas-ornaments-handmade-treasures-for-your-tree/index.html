DIY Christmas Ornaments: Handmade Treasures for Your Tree


Christmas is a time for joy, family, and giving. What better way to celebrate than by creating your own unique Christmas ornaments? DIY ornaments are a great way to add a personal touch to your tree and create lasting memories. Plus, they’re a fun and easy way to get into the holiday spirit.

In this blog post, we’ll share 10 of our favorite DIY Christmas ornament ideas. These ornaments are all easy to make and require minimal supplies. So gather your friends and family and get ready to create some festive holiday cheer!

1. Salt Dough Ornaments

Salt dough ornaments are a classic Christmas craft that is fun for all ages. To make salt dough ornaments, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup salt
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Cookie cutters
  • Straw or toothpick
  • Ribbon or string

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 200 degrees F (93 degrees C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt.
  3. Add water and stir until dough forms.
  4. Knead dough on a lightly floured surface for several minutes until it is smooth and elastic.
  5. Roll out dough to 1/4-inch thickness.
  6. Use cookie cutters to cut out shapes from dough.
  7. Use a straw or toothpick to make a hole at the top of each ornament for hanging.
  8. Place ornaments on a baking sheet and bake for 2-3 hours, or until hard.
  9. Let ornaments cool completely before decorating.
  10. Decorate ornaments with paint, markers, glitter, or other embellishments.
  11. Thread ribbon or string through the hole at the top of each ornament for hanging.

2. Paper Plate Ornaments

Paper plate ornaments are another easy and fun Christmas craft for kids. To make paper plate ornaments, you’ll need:

  • Paper plates
  • Markers, crayons, or paint
  • Scissors
  • Glue
  • Ribbon or string

Instructions:

  1. Decorate paper plates with markers, crayons, or paint.
  2. Cut out shapes from paper plates.
  3. Glue shapes together to create ornaments.
  4. Thread ribbon or string through the hole at the top of each ornament for hanging.

3. Popsicle Stick Ornaments

Popsicle stick ornaments are a great way to use up leftover popsicle sticks. To make popsicle stick ornaments, you’ll need:

  • Popsicle sticks
  • Glue
  • Markers, crayons, or paint
  • Ribbon or string

Instructions:

  1. Glue popsicle sticks together to create shapes.
  2. Decorate ornaments with markers, crayons, or paint.
  3. Thread ribbon or string through the hole at the top of each ornament for hanging.

4. Felt Ornaments

Felt ornaments are a soft and cuddly way to decorate your tree. To make felt ornaments, you’ll need:

  • Felt
  • Scissors
  • Needle and thread
  • Embroidery floss or yarn
  • Stuffing

Instructions:

  1. Cut out shapes from felt.
  2. Sew shapes together, leaving a small opening for stuffing.
  3. Stuff ornaments with stuffing.
  4. Sew opening closed.
  5. Embroider or stitch on details, such as eyes, noses, and mouths.

5. Button Ornaments

Button ornaments are a fun and easy way to add a touch of whimsy to your tree. To make button ornaments, you’ll need:

  • Buttons
  • Glue
  • Ribbon or string

Instructions:

  1. Glue buttons together to create shapes.
  2. Thread ribbon or string through the hole at the top of each ornament for hanging.

6. Pinecone Ornaments

Pinecone ornaments are a natural and rustic way to decorate your tree. To make pinecone ornaments, you’ll need:

  • Pinecones
  • Glue
  • Ribbon or string
  • Embellishments (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Glue pinecones to ribbon or string.
  2. Add embellishments, such as glitter, beads, or small ornaments.

7. Cinnamon Stick Ornaments

Cinnamon stick ornaments are a fragrant and festive way to decorate your tree. To make cinnamon stick ornaments, you’ll need:

  • Cinnamon sticks
  • Glue
  • Ribbon or string
  • Embellishments (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Glue cinnamon sticks together to create shapes.
  2. Thread ribbon or string through the hole at the top of each ornament for hanging.
  3. Add embellishments, such as glitter, beads, or small ornaments.

8. Gingerbread Ornaments

Gingerbread ornaments are a classic Christmas treat that you can also use to decorate your tree. To make gingerbread ornaments, you’ll need:

  • Gingerbread cookie dough
  • Cookie cutters
  • Straw or toothpick
  • Ribbon or string

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Roll out gingerbread cookie dough to 1/4-inch thickness.
  3. Use cookie cutters to cut out shapes from dough.
  4. Use a straw or toothpick to make a hole at the top of each ornament for hanging.
  5. Place ornaments on a baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until golden brown.
  6. Let ornaments cool completely before decorating.
  7. Decorate ornaments with frosting, sprinkles, or other embellishments.
  8. Thread ribbon or string through the hole at the top of each ornament for hanging.

9. Candy Cane Ornaments

Candy cane ornaments are a sweet and simple way to decorate your tree. To make candy cane ornaments, you’ll need:

  • Candy canes
  • Ribbon or string

Instructions:

  1. Thread ribbon or string through the hook at the top of each candy cane.
  2. Hang candy canes on your tree.

10. Popcorn Garland

Popcorn garland is a fun and festive way to decorate your tree. To make popcorn garland, you’ll need:

  • Popcorn
  • Needle and thread
  • Ribbon or string

Instructions:

  1. Pop popcorn and let it cool completely.
  2. Thread needle with thread and knot the end.
  3. Thread popcorn onto needle and thread.
  4. Continue threading popcorn until garland is desired length.
  5. Tie the ends of the garland together with ribbon or string.
