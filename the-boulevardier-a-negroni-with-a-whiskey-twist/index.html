The Boulevardier: A Negroni with a Whiskey Twist


If you’re a fan of classic cocktails, you’ve probably heard of the Negroni. This timeless drink, made with equal parts gin, Campari, and sweet vermouth, has been a favorite among cocktail enthusiasts for decades. But what if you’re not a fan of gin? Fear not, because there’s a twist on the Negroni that’s perfect for whiskey lovers - the Boulevardier.

The Boulevardier is essentially a Negroni with a whiskey twist. Instead of gin, this variant uses bourbon or rye whiskey as the base spirit, resulting in a bolder and more robust flavor profile. The combination of the bitter Campari and the sweet vermouth with the rich and oaky whiskey creates a complex and sophisticated cocktail that is sure to impress.

One of the great things about the Boulevardier is its versatility. Much like the Negroni, there are endless variations and recipes to experiment with. Some bartenders like to use different types of whiskey, such as smoky scotch or spicy Irish whiskey, to add an extra dimension to the drink. Others prefer to play around with the ratios of the ingredients to achieve their desired balance of flavors. Regardless of how you make it, the Boulevardier is a cocktail that can be tailored to suit your personal taste.

To make a classic Boulevardier, start by gathering the following ingredients:

- 1 ½ oz bourbon or rye whiskey
- 1 oz Campari
- 1 oz sweet vermouth
- Orange twist, for garnish

To prepare the cocktail, simply combine the whiskey, Campari, and sweet vermouth in a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir vigorously for about 20-30 seconds to chill the drink properly and to allow the flavors to meld together. Then strain the mixture into a chilled glass filled with fresh ice. Finally, garnish with an orange twist, expressing the oils over the drink for an aromatic touch.
