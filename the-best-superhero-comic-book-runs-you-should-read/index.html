The Best Superhero Comic Book Runs You Should Read


The world of comic books is vast and exciting, filled with colorful characters and thrilling adventures. With so many superhero comic book runs to choose from, it can be overwhelming to decide where to start. But fear not, because we’ve compiled a list of the best superhero comic book runs that every comic book fan should read. These runs not only showcase incredible storytelling but also leave a lasting impact on the comic book industry.

  1. The Dark Knight Returns by Frank Miller - This iconic Batman story redefined the superhero genre, showing a gritty and aging Batman coming out of retirement. Miller’s striking artwork and mature storytelling made this run a must-read for any fan of the Dark Knight.

  2. Watchmen by Alan Moore - Often hailed as one of the greatest graphic novels of all time, Watchmen revolutionized the superhero genre by deconstructing the typical superhero tropes. Moore’s complex narrative and intricate characters explore the moral ambiguity of being a hero, leaving a lasting impact on the comic book landscape.

  3. The Amazing Spider-Man by Stan Lee and Steve Ditko - The dynamic duo of Lee and Ditko introduced readers to the wonderful world of Spider-Man. This run captures the essence of the character, from Peter Parker’s struggles as a teenager to his responsibilities as a superhero. The Amazing Spider-Man laid the foundation for one of Marvel’s most beloved and enduring characters.

  4. The Dark Phoenix Saga by Chris Claremont and John Byrne - This X-Men storyline is considered one of the greatest superhero stories ever told. The arc explores the internal struggle of Jean Grey as she becomes the powerful and volatile Dark Phoenix. Claremont’s writing combined with Byrne’s exceptional artwork make this run a masterpiece.

  5. All-Star Superman by Grant Morrison and Frank Quitely - Morrison’s love letter to the Man of Steel showcases a Superman at his best. This stand-alone series captures the essence of Superman’s unwavering optimism and his never-ending quest to protect the world. Quitely’s stunning artwork complements Morrison’s heartfelt storytelling, making this run an absolute must-read for Superman fans.

  6. Batman: Year One by Frank Miller and David Mazzucchelli - This Batman origin story revitalized the character for a new generation of readers. Miller’s gritty writing and Mazzucchelli’s atmospheric artwork bring Gotham City to life, showcasing Bruce Wayne’s journey from an inexperienced vigilante to becoming the Dark Knight.

  7. Daredevil: Born Again by Frank Miller and David Mazzucchelli - Another collaboration between Miller and Mazzucchelli, this run delves into the depths of Matt Murdock’s life as Daredevil. The story follows Murdock as he faces a series of challenges that push him to the brink. Dark, complex, and beautifully illustrated, Daredevil: Born Again is a standout run in the Daredevil mythos.

  8. Saga by Brian K. Vaughan and Fiona Staples - While not a traditional superhero comic book, Saga explores a fascinating universe filled with extraordinary characters. Vaughan’s masterful storytelling, combined with Staples’ breathtaking artwork, creates an epic space opera that defies expectations. Saga is a breath of fresh air in the comic book industry and deserves a spot on every fan’s reading list.

  9. The Infinity Gauntlet by Jim Starlin and George Perez - This cosmic event changed the Marvel Universe forever. The Mad Titan, Thanos, seeks to impress the embodiment of Death by assembling the all-powerful Infinity Gauntlet. The storyline features a multitude of Marvel’s greatest heroes and villains fighting for the fate of the universe. Starlin’s epic narrative is brilliantly complemented by Perez’s stunning artwork.

  10. The Sandman by Neil Gaiman - While not centered around traditional superheroes, The Sandman is a seminal work that has had a profound influence on the comic book medium. Gaiman’s imaginative storytelling and the distinctive artwork by various artists create a dark and enchanting world filled with dreams, mythology, and complex characters.

These superhero comic book runs represent some of the best storytelling in the industry. Whether you’re a longtime fan or new to the world of comics, these runs are sure to captivate and entertain. So grab a cup of coffee, curl up with these iconic runs, and prepare to be transported to the thrilling world of superheroes and villains. Happy reading!
