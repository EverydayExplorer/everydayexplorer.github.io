How to Properly Store Your Car for Long-Term Storage


When it comes to storing your car for long-term storage, proper preparation is key to ensure its safety and longevity. Whether you’re going on an extended trip or simply have an extra vehicle that requires storage, it’s important to follow a few essential steps to protect your investment. In this article, we’ll explore the best practices for storing your car for an extended period, ensuring that it remains in optimal condition until you’re ready to hit the road again.

1. Find the Right Storage Facility: The first step in storing your car long-term is to find a suitable storage facility. Look for a secure, climate-controlled facility that offers protection against the elements, as well as adequate security measures to prevent theft or damage. A reputable storage facility will provide a clean and dry environment, helping to preserve your car’s paint, interior, and mechanical components.

2. Clean and Detail Your Car: Before storing your car, give it a thorough cleaning, both inside and out. Remove any dirt, dust, or debris that may have accumulated, as these contaminants can cause corrosion over time. Waxing the exterior can offer an extra layer of protection against moisture and harmful UV rays. Don’t forget to clean the interior as well, including vacuuming the carpets and upholstery, and wiping down surfaces with a mild cleaner.

3. Change Fluids and Filters: To prevent any potential issues during storage, it’s recommended to change the engine oil and filter before storing your car. Old oil can contain contaminants that may damage the engine if left sitting for an extended period. Additionally, consider topping up other fluids, such as coolant, brake fluid, and windshield washer fluid, to ensure they’re at the appropriate levels.

4. Protect the Battery: If you’re planning to store your car for more than a few weeks, it’s wise to disconnect the battery or use a battery maintainer to prevent it from draining. A fully charged battery can help avoid potential issues when you’re ready to start the vehicle again. Additionally, consider removing the battery and storing it in a cool, dry place to prolong its lifespan.

5. Preserve the Tires: To prevent flat spots and uneven wear, inflate the tires to the manufacturer’s recommended pressure before storing your car. Consider using jack stands to take the weight off the tires, especially if storing for an extended period. If possible, roll the car slightly every few weeks to distribute the weight and minimize tire damage.

6. Protect Against Pests: To keep unwanted guests away, such as rodents or insects, take precautions to seal off any potential entry points. Plug exhaust pipes, air intakes, and any other openings that pests could use to access your car. Additionally, consider using pest deterrents like mothballs or repellent sprays in the storage area.

7. Cover Your Car: Finally, invest in a high-quality, breathable car cover that fits your vehicle properly. A car cover will protect your vehicle from dust, dirt, and potential scratches. Ensure the cover is made of a breathable material to prevent moisture buildup, which can lead to mold and mildew.

By following these steps, you can ensure that your car remains in excellent condition during long-term storage. When the time comes to bring your vehicle back on the road, it will be ready for your next adventure. Remember, proper preparation and care are essential for preserving the value and performance of your car, even when it’s not in use.
