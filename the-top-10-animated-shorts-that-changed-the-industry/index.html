The Top 10 Animated Shorts That Changed the Industry


The first-ever animated feature film, ‘The Adventures of Prince Achmed,’ was created by German artist Lotte Reiniger. This groundbreaking work utilized silhouette animation, with intricately crafted paper cutouts that were carefully lit and photographed frame by frame. The film’s innovative techniques and storytelling laid the foundation for future animated shorts and features.

2. Steamboat Willie (1928)

Walt Disney and Ub Iwerks introduced the world to Mickey Mouse in ‘Steamboat Willie,’ a groundbreaking animated short that featured synchronized sound. The film’s combination of animation and music revolutionized the industry and established Disney as a major player in the world of animation.

3. Flowers and Trees (1932)

‘Flowers and Trees’ was a pioneering work in the field of synchronized sound in animation. The film, directed by Walt Disney and Ub Iwerks, featured a unique blend of live-action and animation, with the characters coming to life through the power of music. This innovative approach to storytelling inspired future generations of animators and filmmakers.

4. Three Little Pigs (1933)

‘Three Little Pigs’ is a classic animated short that showcased the potential of sound in animation. The film’s use of music, dialogue, and sound effects brought the characters to life in a way that had never been seen before. The story’s enduring popularity and cultural impact cemented its status as a landmark in the history of animation.

5. Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs (1937)

Disney’s first feature-length animated film, ‘Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs,’ was a technical marvel that pushed the boundaries of what was possible in animation. The film’s groundbreaking use of color, depth, and character design set a new standard for the industry and paved the way for future animated features.

6. Pinocchio (1940)

‘Pinocchio’ was another groundbreaking achievement for Disney, as it was the first animated feature to utilize a multiplane camera. This innovative technology allowed for a greater sense of depth and perspective in the film’s scenes, adding a level of realism that had never been seen before in animation. The film’s story and characters continue to resonate with audiences today.

7. Fantasia (1940)

‘Fantasia’ was an ambitious experiment in blending animation and classical music. The film’s eight segments, each set to a different piece of music, showcased the artistic potential of animation and introduced a new generation of audiences to the world of classical music. The film’s success demonstrated the power of animation as a medium for storytelling and expression.

8. Cinderella (1950)

‘Cinderella’ was a major milestone for Disney, as it was the first animated feature to be filmed in the widescreen format of CinemaScope. The film’s stunning visuals and memorable characters captivated audiences and solidified Disney’s reputation as a leader in the animation industry.

9. Toy Story (1995)

Pixar’s ‘Toy Story’ was the first feature-length film to be created entirely with computer-generated imagery (CGI). The film’s groundbreaking use of technology allowed for a level of detail and realism in animation that was previously unimaginable. The success of ‘Toy Story’ ushered in a new era of animation and paved the way for future CGI films.

10. Spirited Away (2001)

Hayao Miyazaki’s ‘Spirited Away’ is a masterpiece of animation that pushed the boundaries of the medium. The film’s intricate hand-drawn artwork, combined with its immersive storytelling, created a unique and unforgettable cinematic experience. The success of ‘Spirited Away’ helped to introduce Japanese animation to a wider audience and solidified Miyazaki’s status as a visionary in the world of animation.
