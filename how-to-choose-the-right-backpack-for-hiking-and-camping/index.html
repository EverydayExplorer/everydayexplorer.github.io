How to Choose the Right Backpack for Hiking and Camping


When it comes to hiking and camping, having the right backpack can make all the difference. A well-fitting, durable backpack not only ensures that you can carry all your essentials comfortably, but it also keeps your gear organized and easily accessible. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the key factors to consider when choosing the perfect backpack for your outdoor adventures.

1. Capacity

The first thing to consider is the capacity of the backpack. This refers to the volume of space available for storing your gear. The capacity you choose depends on the duration of your trips and the amount of gear you need to carry. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Daypacks (under 30 liters): Ideal for day hikes or overnight trips where you only need to carry essentials like water, snacks, and a lightweight jacket.

  • Weekend packs (30-50 liters): Suitable for two to three-day trips, allowing you to carry extra clothing, food, and some camping equipment.

  • Multi-day packs (50-70 liters): Designed for longer trips, these backpacks can accommodate all the essentials needed for several days in the wilderness.

  • Expedition packs (70 liters and above): If you’re planning an extended expedition or carrying specialized equipment, such as climbing gear, opt for a larger pack with ample storage space.

2. Fit and Comfort

A well-fitting backpack is crucial for a comfortable hiking and camping experience. Look for a backpack that suits your body type and provides adjustable straps for a customizable fit. Consider the following aspects:

  • Torso length: Measure your torso length from your C7 vertebra (the bony protrusion at the base of your neck) to your iliac crest (the top of your hip bone). Choose a backpack with an adjustable torso length or select a size that matches your measurement.

  • Shoulder straps: Look for padded shoulder straps that distribute the weight evenly and prevent discomfort.

  • Hip belt: A sturdy hip belt helps transfer the weight of the pack to your hips, relieving strain on your shoulders. Ensure the belt is adjustable and padded for added comfort.

  • Back panel: Opt for a backpack with a well-padded and ventilated back panel to prevent sweat buildup and improve airflow.

3. Features and Organization

Having a backpack with well-designed compartments and pockets can greatly enhance your outdoor experience. Consider the following features:

  • Main compartment: Look for a backpack with a large, easily accessible main compartment. A top-loading design allows for efficient packing, while a front-loading zipper provides quick access to your gear.

  • External pockets: Side pockets, hip belt pockets, and mesh pockets are great for storing frequently used items like water bottles, snacks, and maps.

  • Compression straps: These straps help stabilize your load, prevent shifting, and allow you to adjust the pack’s volume based on your needs.

  • Attachment points: If you need to carry additional gear like trekking poles or a sleeping bag, look for backpacks with external attachment points or daisy chains.

4. Durability

Your backpack will face rough terrains, unpredictable weather, and constant use. Look for a backpack made from durable materials like nylon or polyester, with reinforced stitching and robust zippers. Waterproof or water-resistant fabrics are also desirable to protect your gear during rain showers or river crossings.

5. Try It On

Lastly, before making a purchase, try the backpack on and walk around with it. Pay attention to any pressure points, discomfort, or limitations in movement. A good outdoor gear store will allow you to load the pack with weight to simulate the actual conditions of your hikes.

Choosing the right backpack for hiking and camping is a personal decision. Consider your specific needs, preferences, and budget to find the perfect fit. Remember, a comfortable and well-organized backpack will enhance your outdoor adventures and make your trips more enjoyable. Happy hiking and camping!
