10 Unconventional Instruments Used in Modern Music


Music is a universal language that has been evolving for centuries. As technology advances and musicians push boundaries, they are constantly seeking new ways to create unique sounds and add innovative elements to their compositions. In recent years, there has been a rise in the use of unconventional instruments in modern music. These instruments, often non-traditional or electronic, bring a fresh perspective and add a distinctive flavor to songs. In this blog post, we’ll explore 10 unconventional instruments that have made their mark in the world of modern music.

1. Theremin

Originating in the 1920s, the theremin is an electronic instrument that uses hand movements to create sound. It produces otherworldly, eerie sounds that have been featured in various genres, from classical to electronic music. The theremin’s unique sound has made it a favorite among experimental musicians.

2. Steel Drum

The steel drum, also known as the steel pan, is a musical instrument that originated in Trinidad and Tobago. It was initially created from oil drums and is now a staple in Caribbean music. With its vibrant and infectious sound, the steel drum adds a tropical vibe to any song.

3. Hang Drum

The hang drum, an idiophone created in Switzerland, is an unusual percussion instrument known for its soothing and mesmerizing tones. It has gained popularity in alternative and New Age music for its ability to create ambient and introspective atmospheres.

4. Didgeridoo

Originating from Indigenous Australian culture, the didgeridoo is one of the world’s oldest wind instruments. Its distinctive low, resonant sound has found its way into contemporary music, particularly in genres like ambient, trance, and world music.

5. Kalimba

Also known as a thumb piano, the kalimba is a small, handheld instrument that originated in Africa. It consists of metal tines that are plucked to produce melodic sounds. The kalimba’s soft and gentle tones have made it popular in folk and indie music.

6. Shamisen

The shamisen is a three-stringed instrument from Japan. It has a sharp, distinctive sound that can be both haunting and energetic. In recent years, the shamisen has been incorporated into various genres like rock and jazz, adding a unique cultural flavor to modern music.

7. Waterphone

The waterphone is a percussive instrument that creates haunting sounds reminiscent of ethereal water droplets or howling winds. It is often used in film scores and ambient music to create tension and evoke emotions.

8. Kazoo

The kazoo is a small, inexpensive instrument that is played by humming into a small opening. Its quirky, buzzing sound has been used in a wide range of musical genres, including rock, jazz, and even classical music. The kazoo’s playful nature adds a fun and unexpected element to songs.

9. Chapman Stick

The Chapman Stick is a unique instrument that blends elements of guitar, bass, and piano. It is played by tapping the strings, producing a dynamic and rich sound. Its versatility has made it popular in progressive rock and jazz fusion.

10. Musical Saw

As the name suggests, the musical saw is a regular handsaw that is played with a bow. Its ethereal and haunting sound has been utilized in various genres, including folk, experimental, and even orchestral music.

Conclusion

The use of unconventional instruments in modern music allows musicians to experiment with new sounds and push the boundaries of traditional music. Whether it’s the eerie theremin, the Caribbean flavor of the steel drum, or the ethereal sounds of the waterphone, these instruments bring a unique character to songs. As technology continues to advance, we can expect to see even more unconventional instruments being integrated into the world of music, creating exciting and innovative sounds for years to come.
