Crafting Belgian Ales: Trappist Brews and Abbey Styles


Belgium, a country known for its rich brewing tradition, has given birth to some of the world’s finest and most revered beers. Amongst the multitude of brews that Belgium has to offer, Trappist ales and Abbey styles stand out as true gems of the craft beer world. In this blog post, we will explore the art of crafting Belgian ales, with a particular focus on Trappist brews and Abbey styles.

Trappist Brews: A Divine Tradition

Trappist ales hold a special place in the hearts of beer enthusiasts worldwide. These beers are brewed within the walls of Trappist monasteries, where monks follow centuries-old brewing techniques that have been passed down from generation to generation. The term ‘Trappist’ is a designation that ensures the beer is brewed by monks or under their supervision.

The brewing process of Trappist ales is a labor of love, with meticulous attention to detail at every step. From selecting the finest ingredients to fermenting the beer in oak barrels, Trappist monks imbue their brews with a sense of spirituality and devotion. The result is a range of beers that are complex, full-bodied, and often higher in alcohol content.

Each Trappist monastery has its own unique brewing style and recipes, resulting in a diverse array of Trappist ales. Some of the most renowned Trappist breweries include Westvleteren, Chimay, and Orval. Each brewery has its own distinct flavor profiles and brewing techniques, making every sip of a Trappist ale a truly exceptional experience.

Abbey Styles: Honoring Tradition

Abbey styles, on the other hand, are beers that are inspired by the brewing traditions of the Trappist monasteries. While they may not be brewed within the walls of monasteries, these beers still pay homage to the craftsmanship and heritage of the Trappist brewers.

Abbey styles encompass a wide range of beer types, including Dubbels, Tripels, and Quadrupels. These beers are characterized by their rich malt profiles, complex flavors, and often higher alcohol content. Abbey styles offer a more accessible alternative to Trappist ales, allowing beer enthusiasts to appreciate the Belgian brewing tradition without the exclusivity of the Trappist designation.

Crafting Belgian Ales: A Journey of Flavors

Crafting Belgian ales, whether Trappist or Abbey styles, requires a deep understanding of the brewing techniques and ingredients that make these beers so unique. The use of Belgian yeast strains, which produce fruity and spicy esters, is a hallmark of these styles. Additionally, the incorporation of specialty malts, such as caramel and roasted malts, adds depth and complexity to the final product.

One cannot overlook the importance of fermentation in the brewing process of Belgian ales. The unique fermentation methods employed by Trappist brewers, such as open fermentation and bottle conditioning, contribute to the distinctive flavors and aromas found in these beers. The patience and skill required to achieve the desired results are truly a testament to the art of brewing.

In conclusion, crafting Belgian ales, specifically Trappist brews and Abbey styles, is a journey that combines tradition, expertise, and a touch of divine inspiration. Whether you’re sipping on a Trappist ale brewed by monks in the solitude of their monastery or enjoying an Abbey-style beer crafted by passionate brewers, each sip is an invitation to explore the rich tapestry of flavors that Belgium has to offer. So, raise your glass and immerse yourself in the world of Belgian brewing excellence. Santé!
