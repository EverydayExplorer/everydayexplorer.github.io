Discover the Hidden Gems: 7 Underrated Books You Need to Read


In a world filled with bestsellers and literary classics, it’s easy for some truly remarkable books to get lost in the shuffle. These hidden gems often go unnoticed, tucked away on dusty shelves or buried beneath the weight of more popular titles. But fear not, fellow bookworms, for today we embark on a journey to discover these underrated treasures. Here are seven underrated books that deserve a spot on your reading list.

1. ‘The Golem and the Jinni’ by Helene Wecker
Set in New York City at the turn of the 20th century, this enchanting tale weaves together the stories of a golem and a jinni, two mythical creatures from different cultural backgrounds. As they navigate the challenges of immigrant life, their paths intertwine in unexpected ways, exploring themes of identity, friendship, and the power of choice. Wecker’s prose is rich and evocative, transporting readers to a world where magic and reality seamlessly coexist.

2. ‘The Night Circus’ by Erin Morgenstern
Step into the mesmerizing world of Le Cirque des Rêves, a mysterious circus that appears without warning and disappears just as mysteriously. Within its black and white striped tents lie wonders beyond imagination, and at the heart of it all, a fierce competition between two young magicians. Morgenstern’s lyrical prose and intricate storytelling create a spellbinding atmosphere that will leave you longing for more.

3. ‘The Shadow of the Wind’ by Carlos Ruiz Zafón
In the dark labyrinth of post-war Barcelona, young Daniel Sempere discovers a hidden book in the Cemetery of Forgotten Books, setting off a chain of events that will unravel secrets, love, and the power of literature. Zafón’s gothic tale is a tribute to the magic of storytelling, weaving together mystery, romance, and a love letter to books themselves.

4. ‘Station Eleven’ by Emily St. John Mandel
In a world devastated by a pandemic, a traveling symphony and Shakespearean troupe roam the wastelands, keeping the flame of art and humanity alive. Mandel’s hauntingly beautiful novel explores the fragility of civilization, the resilience of the human spirit, and the enduring power of art in the face of adversity.

5. ‘The Book of Lost Things’ by John Connolly
Enter the twisted and enchanting realm of fairy tales in this dark coming-of-age story. Young David, grieving the loss of his mother, finds solace in books that transport him to a world where stories are alive. Connolly’s atmospheric writing takes readers on a journey through familiar tales with a sinister twist, exploring themes of grief, courage, and the power of imagination.

6. ‘The Sparrow’ by Mary Doria Russell
Part science fiction, part philosophical exploration, ‘The Sparrow’ follows a group of Jesuit priests who embark on a mission to a distant planet after receiving a mysterious transmission of beautiful music. What they find there challenges their faith, their humanity, and the very nature of the universe. Russell’s thought-provoking novel delves into themes of religion, morality, and the consequences of first contact.

7. ‘The Alchemist’ by Paulo Coelho
A modern classic, ‘The Alchemist’ tells the tale of Santiago, a shepherd boy who embarks on a journey to find his personal legend. Along the way, he encounters wise mentors, magical omens, and learns valuable lessons about the pursuit of one’s dreams. Coelho’s allegorical novel inspires readers to listen to their hearts, follow their passions, and embrace the unknown.

These seven underrated books are just a glimpse into a world of hidden literary treasures waiting to be discovered. So, venture beyond the bestseller lists and explore the lesser-known realms of literature. Who knows what wonders await you? Happy reading!
