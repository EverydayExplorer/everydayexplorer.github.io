Chasing the Northern Lights: Where to See the Aurora Borealis


The Aurora Borealis, a celestial spectacle that has captured the imagination of humans for centuries, is a phenomenon that truly epitomizes the beauty and wonder of the natural world. Also known as the Northern Lights, this breathtaking display of colorful lights dancing across the night sky is caused by charged particles from the sun colliding with the Earth’s atmosphere. For those who are fortunate enough to witness it, chasing the Northern Lights can be a once-in-a-lifetime experience filled with awe and wonder.

But where exactly can you see the Aurora Borealis? While the Northern Lights are predominantly visible in regions close to the Arctic Circle, such as Alaska, Canada, Iceland, Norway, Sweden, Finland, and Russia, there are certain factors to consider when planning your aurora adventure.

One of the key factors in seeing the Northern Lights is the level of solar activity. The auroras are most commonly visible during periods of high solar activity, known as solar maximums. During these times, the sun is more active, sending out increased solar flares and charged particles that interact with the Earth’s magnetic field, creating spectacular displays of light. Conversely, during periods of low solar activity, such as solar minimums, the auroras may be less frequent and less intense.

Another important consideration is the time of year. While the Northern Lights can technically be seen year-round in some regions, the best time to witness them is typically during the winter months when the nights are longest and the skies are darkest. This is especially true in the high-latitude regions near the Arctic Circle, where the polar nights provide optimal conditions for viewing the auroras.

In addition to solar activity and time of year, weather conditions also play a crucial role in aurora hunting. Clear, dark skies free of light pollution are essential for spotting the Northern Lights, so be sure to check the weather forecast and choose a location away from city lights for the best chance of seeing the auroras in all their glory.

Now that we’ve covered some of the key factors to consider when planning your Northern Lights adventure, let’s take a closer look at some of the best destinations for aurora chasing around the world.

  1. Iceland: With its dramatic landscapes, geothermal hot springs, and vibrant cultural scene, Iceland is a popular destination for aurora hunters. The country’s location just below the Arctic Circle makes it an ideal spot for witnessing the Northern Lights, especially during the winter months.

  2. Norway: From the rugged fjords of the west coast to the remote wilderness of the far north, Norway offers a variety of stunning locations for viewing the auroras. The city of Tromsø, often referred to as the ‘Gateway to the Arctic,’ is a particularly popular destination for aurora enthusiasts.

  3. Canada: With its vast wilderness and northern latitude, Canada is another excellent destination for aurora chasing. The Yukon Territory, in particular, offers some of the best opportunities for viewing the Northern Lights, thanks to its clear skies and minimal light pollution.

  4. Alaska: The Last Frontier is a prime location for experiencing the magic of the Northern Lights. From Fairbanks to Denali National Park, there are plenty of spots throughout the state where you can witness the auroras in all their splendor.

  5. Sweden: The remote wilderness of Swedish Lapland provides the perfect backdrop for aurora hunting. Whether you’re dog sledding through snow-covered forests or staying in a cozy log cabin under the northern lights, Sweden offers a truly unforgettable aurora experience.

In conclusion, chasing the Northern Lights is a bucket-list-worthy adventure that offers the chance to witness one of nature’s most awe-inspiring phenomena. By considering factors such as solar activity, time of year, and weather conditions, you can maximize your chances of seeing the auroras in all their splendor. Whether you’re exploring the wilds of Alaska, soaking in a hot spring in Iceland, or dog sledding through Swedish Lapland, the Northern Lights are sure to leave you spellbound and inspired by the beauty of the natural world.
