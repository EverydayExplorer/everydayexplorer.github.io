Unconventional Instruments: Beyond the Guitar and Piano


Hey there music enthusiasts! Are you tired of hearing the same old tunes played on the guitar and piano? If you’re ready to dive into the world of unconventional instruments, then you’ve come to the right place. In this blog, we’ll be uncovering a treasure trove of unique and fascinating instruments that go beyond the traditional guitar and piano. Get ready to expand your musical horizons and discover some truly extraordinary sounds. Let’s jump right in!

1. Hang Drum: The hang drum, also known as the handpan, is a mesmerizing instrument that produces ethereal and enchanting sounds. Its steel construction and unique design allow for a wide range of melodic possibilities. From soothing and meditative tones to playful and upbeat beats, the hang drum is a captivating instrument that will transport you to another world.

2. Theremin: Prepare to step into the realm of the strange and otherworldly with the theremin. This instrument is played without any physical contact, as it utilizes electromagnetic fields to generate sound. With its eerie and haunting sound, the theremin has been featured in many sci-fi movies and adds an otherworldly touch to any musical composition.

3. Didgeridoo: Journey to the land down under with the powerful and resonant sounds of the didgeridoo. Originating from Indigenous Australian cultures, this wind instrument is made from a hollowed-out tree trunk and produces deep, earthy tones that are both primal and mesmerizing. With a bit of practice, you can create rhythmic patterns and even imitate the sounds of nature.

4. Hangul: If you’re looking to make music using your voice, why not explore the world of overtone singing? This technique, also known as throat singing, allows you to produce multiple pitches simultaneously. The Hangul, originating from Mongolia and parts of Central Asia, is a prime example of overtone singing. Mastering this technique can create a truly mesmerizing and harmonically rich musical experience.

5. Handpan: Similar to the hang drum, the handpan is a modern-day marvel that has gained popularity in recent years. With its unique construction and layout of notes, the handpan offers a wide range of melodic possibilities. Whether you’re using mallets or your hands, this instrument produces beautiful, resonant tones that will captivate anyone who listens.

6. Kalimba: Looking for something portable and versatile? The kalimba, also known as the thumb piano, is a small instrument with a big sound. Often made from wood and metal, the kalimba is played by plucking the metal tines with your thumbs. With its mellow and melodic tones, the kalimba is perfect for creating soothing and introspective music.

7. Glass Armonica: Invented by Benjamin Franklin, the glass armonica is a truly unique instrument that produces sound by rubbing wet fingers along the rims of glass bowls of varying sizes. The resulting ethereal and haunting tones make this instrument a perfect addition to any experimental or ambient composition.

So there you have it, a glimpse into the world of unconventional instruments that go beyond the guitar and piano. These extraordinary instruments offer endless possibilities for musical exploration and creativity. Whether you’re drawn to the soothing tones of the hang drum or the strange allure of the theremin, there’s a unique instrument waiting to be discovered. So why not step outside the box and dive into a musical adventure like no other? The world of unconventional instruments awaits you!
