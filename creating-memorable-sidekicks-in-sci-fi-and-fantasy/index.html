Creating Memorable Sidekicks in Sci-Fi and Fantasy


If you ask any fan of sci-fi and fantasy what makes a great story, they’ll likely mention memorable characters. From the epic hero to the wicked villain, these characters shape the narrative and leave a lasting impression on readers. But what about sidekicks? Often overlooked, these supporting characters play a crucial role in bringing the story to life. In this blog, we’ll explore the art of creating memorable sidekicks in sci-fi and fantasy.

1. Give them a unique personality

The first step in creating a memorable sidekick is to give them a distinct personality. Think about what sets them apart from the main character. Are they quirky, sarcastic, or loyal to a fault? Give them traits that make them instantly recognizable and relatable to the readers. By making them stand out, you’ll ensure that they won’t be easily forgotten.

2. Develop their backstory

Just like the main characters, sidekicks should have a backstory that informs their current actions and motivations. Dive into their past, explore their experiences and relationships, and add depth to their character. By understanding where they come from, readers will be more invested in their journey alongside the main character.

3. Establish a complex dynamic with the main character

The relationship between the main character and their sidekick is crucial to creating a memorable duo. It can be a mentor-mentee relationship, a sibling-like bond, or a comedic duo. Whatever dynamic you choose, ensure that it complements the main character’s strengths and weaknesses, and allows for growth and development on both sides. This interplay will keep readers engaged and invested in the story.

4. Give them a unique skillset

Sidekicks often have their own unique set of skills that contribute to the overall mission or quest. Whether it’s their ability to hack into the most secure systems or their extraordinary strength, their skills should complement the main character’s abilities. This not only enhances their utility within the story but also provides opportunities for exciting plot developments and unexpected twists.

5. Create a compelling arc for the sidekick

Just like the main character, sidekicks should undergo their own character arc. Give them challenges to overcome, let them face their own insecurities, and allow them to grow throughout the story. By giving them a compelling arc, you’ll make them more relatable, and readers will become invested in their journey as much as the main character’s.

6. Use humor to add depth and charm

Incorporating humor is a great way to add depth and charm to sidekick characters. They can serve as comic relief or use humor to cope with difficult situations. Through their wit and banter, sidekicks can bring levity to serious moments, provide a momentary escape from tension, and create memorable interactions with other characters.

7. Make them integral to the plot

Sidekicks should never just be there for the sake of filling a role. They should have their own contribution to the overall plot and be instrumental in advancing the story. Give them tasks that matter, decisions to make, and obstacles to overcome. By allowing sidekicks to have a meaningful impact on the outcome of events, you’ll ensure that readers see them as more than just supporting characters.

8. Allow them to challenge the main character

While sidekicks are often seen as support for the main character, it can be interesting to give them the opportunity to challenge the protagonist’s perspectives and decisions. By providing them with their own voice and agency, sidekicks become more than mere followers. This dynamic can lead to intense and thought-provoking conversations, adding depth to both the sidekick and the main character.

9. Avoid stereotypes and clichés

When creating sidekicks, it’s important to avoid falling into the trap of stereotypes and clichés. Sidekicks shouldn’t be there solely for comedic relief or to be a damsel in distress. Give them agency, a sense of purpose, and multidimensional personalities. By breaking away from stereotypes, you’ll create sidekicks that feel fresh and unique to readers.

10. Make them memorable through actions and choices

Ultimately, what makes sidekicks truly memorable is the impact they leave on readers through their actions and choices. Make them courageous, selfless, or even morally ambiguous, but ensure that their choices resonate with readers. Whether they make a sacrifice, face a difficult decision, or exhibit unexpected heroism, these actions will solidify their place in readers’ hearts and minds.

Creating memorable sidekicks in sci-fi and fantasy is a delicate balance of unique personalities, complex dynamics, and compelling character arcs. By investing time and effort into crafting these supporting characters, you’ll enrich your story, captivate readers, and leave a lasting impression on fans of the genre. So, as you embark on your own storytelling journey, don’t forget the power of a memorable sidekick.
