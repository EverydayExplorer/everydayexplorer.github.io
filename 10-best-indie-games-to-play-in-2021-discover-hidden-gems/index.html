10 Best Indie Games to Play in 2021: Discover Hidden Gems


Sure, I’d be happy to help you write a blog about the 10 Best Indie Games to Play in 2021. Here’s a draft for you:

-–

Are you tired of playing the same old mainstream games? Looking for something fresh and exciting to dive into? Well, you’re in luck! 2021 is shaping up to be a fantastic year for indie games, with a plethora of hidden gems just waiting to be discovered. In this blog post, we’ll be exploring the 10 best indie games that you should consider adding to your collection this year.

1. ‘Hades’ - Developed by Supergiant Games, ‘Hades’ has taken the gaming world by storm. This action-packed rogue-like dungeon crawler combines stunning visuals, engaging storytelling, and addictive gameplay to deliver an unforgettable experience.

2. ‘Spiritfarer’ - If you’re in the mood for a heartwarming adventure with a touch of melancholy, then ‘Spiritfarer’ is the game for you. Developed by Thunder Lotus Games, this indie gem allows you to explore a beautiful hand-drawn world while managing a ship and taking care of spirits on their journey to the afterlife.

3. ‘Ori and the Will of the Wisps’ - A sequel to the critically acclaimed ‘Ori and the Blind Forest,’ this mesmerizing platformer developed by Moon Studios takes players on a breathtaking journey through a mystical forest. With its gorgeous visuals, emotionally captivating story, and challenging gameplay, ‘Ori and the Will of the Wisps’ is a must-play indie game this year.

4. ‘Carrion’ - If you’ve ever wondered what it’s like to be the villain, ‘Carrion’ will satisfy your curiosity. Developed by Phobia Game Studio, this reverse horror game allows you to control a grotesque creature as it terrorizes its human prey. With its unique gameplay mechanics and atmospheric pixel art, ‘Carrion’ offers a refreshing twist on the genre.

5. ‘Streets of Rage 4’ - For fans of classic beat ’em up games, ‘Streets of Rage 4’ is a nostalgic trip down memory lane. Developed by Dotemu, Lizardcube, and Guard Crush Games, this modern take on the iconic franchise features beautifully hand-drawn visuals, a rocking soundtrack, and addictive multiplayer gameplay.

6. ‘Fall Guys: Ultimate Knockout’ - Looking for a game that will make you laugh out loud while testing your skills? Look no further than ‘Fall Guys: Ultimate Knockout.’ Developed by Mediatonic, this hilarious battle royale game pits you against other players in a series of wacky and chaotic challenges. It’s perfect for both casual gamers and competitive players alike.

7. ‘Creaks’ - Developed by Amanita Design, the studio behind the critically acclaimed ‘Machinarium’ and ‘Botanicula,’ ‘Creaks’ is a puzzle-platformer with a unique hand-drawn art style. Dive into a mysterious world filled with strange creatures and mind-bending puzzles as you unravel the secrets of the game’s beautifully crafted universe.

8. ‘Phasmophobia’ - If you’re a fan of horror games and enjoy the thrill of the unknown, ‘Phasmophobia’ is a game you won’t want to miss. Developed by Kinetic Games, this cooperative psychological horror game puts you in the shoes of a paranormal investigator. Use your equipment and wits to uncover the truth behind haunted locations and survive encounters with vengeful spirits.

9. ‘Raji: An Ancient Epic’ - Immerse yourself in a captivating tale inspired by ancient Hindu and Balinese mythology with ‘Raji: An Ancient Epic.’ Developed by Nodding Heads Games, this action-adventure game features breathtaking hand-painted visuals, challenging combat, and a compelling story of a young girl’s journey through a war-torn world to rescue her brother.

10. ‘Risk of Rain 2’ - Developed by Hopoo Games, ‘Risk of Rain 2’ is a fast-paced, multiplayer roguelike third-person shooter. Team up with friends or tackle the game solo as you fight your way through hordes of enemies, unlock powerful abilities, and discover hidden secrets in ever-changing environments.

These are just a few of the best indie games to play in 2021. Whether you’re into action, adventure, puzzles, or horror, the indie game scene has something for everyone. So, why not take a break from the mainstream and discover these hidden gems? You might just find your new favorite game.
