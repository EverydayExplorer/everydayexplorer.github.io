The Curse of the Omen: Movie Production Tragedies


In the world of movie production, there have been countless tragedies that have struck sets and crews throughout history. From accidents, deaths, and mysterious incidents, some films seem to have a curse hanging over them. One such movie is ‘The Curse of the Omen,’ a horror classic that gained notoriety for the string of misfortunes that plagued its production. In this blog post, we will dive deep into the dark history of ‘The Curse of the Omen’ and explore the various tragedies that befell those involved.

The Beginnings of ‘The Curse of the Omen’

‘The Curse of the Omen,’ released in 1976, is a supernatural horror film directed by Richard Donner. A sequel to the highly successful ‘The Omen,’ it tells the story of a young boy, Damien Thorn, who is revealed to be the Antichrist. While the film achieved critical acclaim and commercial success, it also became infamous due to the tragic events that took place during its production.

The Tragedies Unveiled

  1. The Accident That Started It All

One of the most well-known incidents associated with ‘The Curse of the Omen’ occurred during the filming of the movie’s opening sequence, which takes place in a church. On January 20th, 1975, a few months before the film’s release, a powerful storm struck London. Lightning struck the church location where the scene was being shot, causing significant damage. Miraculously, no crew members were injured, but this incident was seen by many as a sign of the curse to come.

  1. The Car Accident

Another chilling event took place on Friday the 13th of June, 1975. While flying home from filming, the film’s lead actor, Gregory Peck, canceled his reservation for a flight on Iberia Airlines. Unfortunately, tragedy struck when that very flight crashed, killing everyone on board. Many believed that had Peck taken that flight, the curse would have claimed another victim.

  1. The Shoot That Cost a Life

During the production of ‘The Curse of the Omen,’ a memorable scene involves a character being impaled by an iron rod. However, tragedy struck when camera operator John Richardson and his assistant Liz Moore were involved in a horrific car accident on Friday the 13th of August, 1976 – only two months after the film’s release. Moore was instantly killed, and Richardson, who was sitting next to her, survived but sustained a serious injury. Coincidentally, the nearest road sign at the accident site indicated the distance to the town of Ommen – another eerie connection to the film’s title.

  1. The Airliner Struck by Tragedy

Even the marketing campaign for ‘The Curse of the Omen’ was marred by tragedy. Twentieth Century Fox arranged a publicity event for the film, which involved flying a promotional plane around Europe. However, the aircraft was struck by lightning, just like in the film’s opening sequence and suffered significant damage. Luckily, none of the 62 passengers on board were harmed, but rumors of the curse were fueled by this chilling event.

The Search for Explanations

With such a dark sequence of tragedies surrounding ‘The Curse of the Omen,’ many began to question whether there was indeed a curse at play. Skeptics often attribute the events to mere coincidences, while others believe that the film’s subject matter and symbolism invited supernatural forces. However, regardless of the cause, the numerous misfortunes surrounding the film have made it a haunting legend within the world of cinema.

Conclusion

‘The Curse of the Omen’ stands as a chilling reminder of the mysterious and tragic events that can plague a movie production. From accidents to deaths, the incidents that occurred during the creation of this horror classic have left an indelible mark on its legacy. Whether the tragedies were caused by a curse or were simply coincidental, the story of ‘The Curse of the Omen’ continues to fascinate and disturb both fans of the film and skeptics alike.
