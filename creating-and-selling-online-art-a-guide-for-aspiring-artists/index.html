Creating and Selling Online Art: A Guide for Aspiring Artists


As an aspiring artist, you already know that the world of art is vast and vibrant. With the advent of the internet, it has become easier to showcase, create and sell your artwork online. With the right mindset, strategy and some hard work, you can build your brand and create a loyal following of customers who adore your art. In this guide, we will share some tips on how you can start creating and selling your art online.

Create Your Online Presence

The first step to selling your art online is to create your online presence. It’s important to set up a website or social media account to showcase your art and connect with potential customers. Consider investing in a website builder or hiring a professional to help you set up your website. Your website should include a bio, your portfolio, and a contact page. If you’re not comfortable with creating a website, you can create social media accounts on platforms that best suit your art.

Choosing Your Niche

Choosing your niche is key to establishing yourself as an artist, helping you to build an online presence, and to attract new customers. Knowing your niche also gives you focus and helps you to create art that your customers are interested in. There are many niches to choose from, such as landscapes, portraits, abstract, still-life, etc. Spend some time researching your niche and find examples of successful artists in the same niche.

Promoting Your Art

Your website and social media accounts are platforms to showcase your art, but you also need to find ways to promote your art to reach new customers. One way to do this is to join online art fairs, communities, or groups. Some art fairs allow artists to participate online, making it easy for you to showcase and sell your art without the expense or hassle of traveling. Another way to promote your art is to join online galleries and marketplaces for artists such as Etsy, Saatchi Art, and ArtFinder. These platforms offer artists the opportunity to sell their artwork to a larger audience.

Pricing Your Art

Knowing how to price your art is important, especially if you want to make a living from your artwork. When pricing your art, consider your niche, quality of artwork, materials used, size and complexity, and how long it took you to create the artwork. Researching prices for art of a similar style and size can also help you determine the right price for your art. You can also offer discounts and sales to attract new customers and create demand for your art.

Shipping and Packaging

Shipping and packaging your art is important for ensuring that your artwork arrives safely at its destination. Invest in sturdy packaging materials, such as bubble wrap or padding, and have a system for labeling and tracking your artwork. Research the best carriers that can deliver your art safely and on time and be sure to include insurance on your shipments.

In conclusion, selling your art online may seem daunting, but it is possible with hard work, patience, and a solid strategy. By creating your online presence, choosing your niche, promoting your art, pricing your art correctly, and ensuring your artwork arrives safely, you can build a successful business selling your art online. Investing in your art is an investment in yourself. With dedication, talent, and hard work, you can carve out your place in the online art world and share your creativity with the world.
