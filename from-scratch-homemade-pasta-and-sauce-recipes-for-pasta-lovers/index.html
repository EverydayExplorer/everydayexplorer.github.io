From Scratch: Homemade Pasta and Sauce Recipes for Pasta Lovers


Pasta is one of the most beloved dishes all around the world, and it’s not hard to see why. There’s something undeniably comforting about a steaming bowl of pasta, coated in a rich and flavorful sauce. But while it’s easy to reach for a box of store-bought noodles and a jar of sauce, there’s something truly special about making your own pasta and sauce from scratch.

Not only is homemade pasta and sauce delicious, but it’s also a great way to impress your guests, or simply treat yourself to a special meal. And contrary to popular belief, it’s not as difficult as you might think.

In this blog, we’ll explore how to make homemade pasta and sauce recipes that will satisfy even the most discerning pasta lovers.

Homemade Pasta Recipes

Making fresh pasta from scratch may seem daunting, but it’s actually quite simple, and the end result is well worth the effort. Here are a few of our favorite homemade pasta recipes:

Classic Homemade Pasta

This is a simple and traditional recipe that yields delicious, flavorful pasta. Here’s what you’ll need:

- 2 cups of flour
- 3 eggs
- 1/2 teaspoon of salt
- 1 tablespoon of olive oil

To start, mix the flour and salt together in a large bowl. Make a well in the center of the flour and crack the eggs into it. Use a fork to whisk the eggs together and begin incorporating the flour gradually until a rough dough forms.

Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Cover the dough with a clean dish towel and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Once rested, cut the dough into thirds, and roll each piece out with a rolling pin or pasta machine until it’s thin enough to see through (about 1/16th of an inch). Cut the pasta into your desired shape, cook for 2-3 minutes in boiling salted water, and enjoy!

Spinach Pasta

This recipe is a great way to add some nutrients to your pasta dish while still keeping it delicious. Here’s what you’ll need:

- 2 cups of flour
- 3 eggs
- 1/2 teaspoon of salt
- 1 tablespoon of olive oil
- 1 cup of fresh spinach

Start by steaming the spinach until it’s wilted, then squeeze out as much excess water as you can. Puree the spinach in a blender or food processor until it’s smooth.

Follow the same steps for making classic homemade pasta, but add the pureed spinach to the egg mixture before whisking in the flour.

Once you’ve mixed and kneaded the dough, proceed as you would with classic pasta recipe. This spinach pasta is perfect for serving with a cream sauce or a simple garlic and olive oil coating.

Homemade Sauce Recipes

Now that you’ve got some homemade pasta recipes under your belt, it’s time to tackle the sauce. Homemade sauce is much simpler than you might think, and you can customize it to your taste preferences. Here are a few recipes to get you started:

Tomato Sauce

This classic tomato sauce recipe requires only a handful of ingredients and is perfect for a variety of pasta shapes. Here’s what you’ll need:

- 2 cans of whole peeled tomatoes
- 1/4 cup of olive oil
- 1 onion, chopped
- 4 cloves of garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon of salt
- 1 teaspoon of sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon of red pepper flakes

Start by heating the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic and cook until softened (about 5 minutes). Crush the tomatoes by hand and add them to the pot, along with the salt, sugar, and red pepper flakes.

Bring the sauce to a simmer and let it cook, stirring occasionally, for about 30-40 minutes. The longer you let it cook, the more flavorful it will become. Once the sauce has thickened, adjust the seasoning as needed and serve over your favorite pasta.

Alfredo Sauce

This rich and creamy sauce is perfect for coating long noodles like fettuccine or linguine. Here’s what you’ll need:

- 1/2 cup of unsalted butter
- 1 cup of heavy cream
- 2 cups of grated Parmesan cheese
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Pinch of nutmeg (optional)

Melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat, then whisk in the heavy cream. Add the Parmesan cheese and cook, stirring constantly, until the cheese has melted and the sauce has thickened (about 5 minutes). Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg if desired. Serve over cooked pasta.

In Conclusion

Homemade pasta and sauce may sound intimidating, but once you’ve tried it, there’s no going back to the store-bought stuff. These recipes are just a starting point, and you can customize them as much as you’d like to suit your taste preferences. So, roll up your sleeves and start cooking!
