How to Train Your Dog to Sit, Stay, and Come


Training your dog is an essential part of being a responsible pet owner. It not only helps keep your dog safe and well-behaved, but it also strengthens the bond between you and your furry friend. In this blog post, we will discuss three fundamental commands every dog should know: sit, stay, and come. These commands are not only basic obedience commands, but they are also useful in various situations. Whether you have a new puppy or an adult dog, it’s never too late to start training them!

The Basics of Positive Reinforcement Training

Before we dive into the specifics of training your dog to sit, stay, and come, let’s discuss the basics of positive reinforcement training. Positive reinforcement is the most effective and humane way to train dogs. It involves rewarding your dog with treats, praise, and affection when they exhibit the desired behavior. This type of training builds trust and encourages your dog to willingly engage in the training process.

Training Your Dog to Sit

Training your dog to sit is one of the first commands you should teach them. It’s a foundational behavior that sets the groundwork for other commands and behaviors. Here’s a step-by-step guide to training your dog to sit:

  1. Start by holding a treat close to your dog’s nose, allowing them to sniff it.
  2. Slowly move the treat upwards, over their head. As you do this, their bottom will automatically lower into a sitting position.
  3. As soon as your dog is in the sitting position, say ‘sit’ and reward them with the treat.
  4. Repeat this exercise several times, gradually phasing out the treat until your dog responds to the verbal command alone.
  5. Practice in different locations and gradually add distractions to solidify the behavior.

Remember to be patient and practice short training sessions several times a day. Consistency is key!

Teaching Your Dog to Stay

Once your dog has mastered the sit command, it’s time to move on to the stay command. This command is crucial for keeping your dog safe in various situations. Follow these steps to train your dog to stay:

  1. Start with your dog in the sitting position.
  2. Give the ‘stay’ command while holding your hand out in front of you, like a stop sign.
  3. Take a step back, but stay close to your dog to prevent them from moving.
  4. If your dog stays in the sitting position, reward them with praise and a treat.
  5. Gradually increase the distance and duration of the stay, always rewarding your dog for successful responses.
  6. Practice in different environments and with distractions to reinforce the behavior.

Remember, consistency and patience are essential when teaching your dog to stay. Be sure to release your dog from the stay command with a specific verbal cue, such as ‘okay.’

Training Your Dog to Come

The recall command, or ‘come,’ is crucial for keeping your dog safe in potentially dangerous situations or when you need them to return to you. Here’s how to train your dog to come reliably:

  1. Start by calling your dog’s name in an enthusiastic tone.
  2. When they start to come towards you, encourage them further by using the ‘come’ command.
  3. Reward your dog lavishly with treats and praise when they reach you.
  4. Practice the recall command in different environments, gradually increasing distractions.
  5. Avoid calling your dog to come for negative experiences, such as bath time or leaving the park, as this may undermine their trust in the command.

Remember not to punish your dog if they don’t respond to the recall command. Instead, make it more rewarding and engaging for them to come to you.

Wrapping Up

Training your dog to sit, stay, and come is a rewarding process that strengthens your bond with your furry companion. Remember to always use positive reinforcement and reward-based training methods, as they are the most effective and humane. By following the steps outlined in this blog post, you’ll be well on your way to having a well-behaved dog that can confidently respond to these important commands. So grab some treats, be patient, and start training today!
