Survival Skills: Creating and Using Snares and Traps


In a world where unexpected situations can arise, knowing survival skills is essential. One such skill that can greatly increase your chances of survival is the ability to create and use snares and traps. These simple yet effective tools can be used to catch small game, providing a valuable source of food in a survival situation. In this blog post, we will explore the art of creating and using snares and traps, equipping you with the knowledge you need to thrive in the wilderness.

Before we delve into the specifics, it is important to understand the purpose of snares and traps. These devices are designed to capture animals by restraining them or causing them to become entangled. Snares are typically made from a loop of wire or cord, while traps can be constructed using various materials such as sticks, rocks, and natural fibers. By strategically placing these devices in areas where animals frequent, you can increase your chances of catching prey.

When it comes to creating snares, it is crucial to use the right materials and techniques. Start by selecting a strong and flexible wire or cord, ensuring that it is long enough to form a loop that can easily capture the intended prey. The loop should be small enough to trap the animal’s neck or foot, but not so tight that it causes harm. To secure the loop, tie it to a stable anchor point such as a tree or branch. Finally, camouflage the snare by disguising it with foliage or natural materials to increase its effectiveness.

Traps, on the other hand, offer a broader range of possibilities. One of the simplest and most effective types of traps is the deadfall trap. This trap consists of a heavy object, such as a large rock, that is propped up by sticks or other supports. When an animal disturbs the trigger mechanism, the weight of the object causes it to fall, trapping the animal underneath. Another type of trap is the pitfall trap, which involves digging a hole and covering it with vegetation. When an animal steps on the disguised surface, it falls into the pit, making it easy to capture.

Now that you understand the basics of creating snares and traps, it’s time to discuss their usage. When setting up snares, it is important to place them in areas where animals are likely to pass, such as game trails or near water sources. Additionally, consider the size and weight of the animal you are targeting when selecting the appropriate snare size. Remember to check your snares regularly to ensure they are still in working order and to prevent unnecessary suffering of captured animals.

As for traps, proper placement is key. Look for signs of animal activity, such as tracks or droppings, and set your traps accordingly. It is also crucial to exercise caution and respect for the animals you are targeting. Always prioritize humane methods and avoid causing unnecessary harm. Remember, survival is about coexisting with nature, not exploiting it.

In conclusion, mastering the art of creating and using snares and traps can significantly improve your chances of survival in the wilderness. By learning the proper techniques, selecting suitable materials, and understanding animal behavior, you can effectively catch small game for sustenance. However, it is important to approach this skill with responsibility and a deep respect for nature. So go out, practice, and embrace the art of survival. Your newfound skills may just save your life one day.
