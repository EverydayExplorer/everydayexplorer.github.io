How Online Banking is Changing Fundraising and Crowd-funding


Online banking has taken over the world in a very short span of time, and its influence has spread from regular financial transactions to areas like fundraising and crowd-funding. The rise of online banking has brought about significant changes in the way people donate their money. In this blog, we shall explore how online banking is changing the face of fundraising and crowd-funding while discussing the various advantages and challenges it creates.

Online banking has facilitated various digital payment methods that have revolutionized the way funds are raised for charitable causes and crowdfunding. Gone are the days when people had to physically travel to the bank to make a donation, which was time-consuming and quite cumbersome, to say the least. Today, with online banking, people can quickly donate their funds from their mobile devices and computers, with only a few clicks. This has strengthened the capacity to collect funds and has addressed the challenges posed by limited physical access to banks.

Online fundraising platforms have taken off in the past few years, allowing people to donate money quickly and easily to charities and other worthy causes. These online platforms enable a vast number of individuals to collectively give funds to a cause, creating a crowd-funding effect of sorts. For instance, platforms like GoFundme, JustGiving, and Fundly, allow people to contribute small amounts to specific projects or goals. The power of these platforms is such that they can raise a considerable amount of funds in a shorter time than traditional fundraising methods, thanks to the ease of sharing and the ability to reach a wider pool of potential donors.

Another way online banking has transformed fundraising and crowd-funding is through the introduction of digital wallets. Digital wallets are virtual accounts that allow for secure and simplified transactions. Charities and other organizations involved in fundraising and crowd-funding can set up digital wallets, enabling donors to deposit funds directly into them with ease. The digital wallets also offer donors a degree of transparency, allowing them to track the progress of their donations and how they are being utilized to achieve set targets.

Alongside the many benefits of online banking and fundraising, there are also challenges unique to this model. One significant challenge is that of security as the ease of access and high volume of online transactions give rise to fraudulent activities. Charities and organizations must ensure that they have robust security measures in place to secure the online donations from being hacked or misused or lost. These measures could include two-factor authentication, encryption, and firewalls amongst many others.

Another challenge that organizations utilizing online banking must face is that of donor fatigue. With so many fundraisers, crowd-funding platforms, and appeals vying for attention through multiple channels online, it is possible that donors may feel overwhelmed and disenchanted. Organizers and charities must employ strategies to cut out the noise and make their appeals personal and relatable to potential donors to make a more significant impact.

In conclusion, the rise of online banking and fundraising has had a significant impact on fundraising and crowd-funding, giving a boost to charities and other organizations working on social, cultural, or historic initiatives. The ease of access, speed of transaction, and convenience have opened up new horizons for fundraising efforts. However, like any new technological development, there remain risks and challenges, which must be overcome. Overall, the impact of online banking on fundraising and crowd-funding has been overwhelmingly positive, broadening the horizon of charitable efforts and personifying the meaning of financial inclusivity.
