How to Read Tarot Cards for Beginner Astrologers


When it comes to exploring the mystical world of divination, few practices are as captivating as tarot reading. For beginner astrologers, incorporating tarot into their toolkit can deepen their understanding of astrology and enhance their intuitive skills. This blog post serves as a comprehensive guide to help novice astrologers learn how to read tarot cards effectively, bridging the gap between these two ancient arts.

Understanding the Tarot Deck

The tarot deck consists of 78 cards divided into two main categories: the Major Arcana and the Minor Arcana.

Major Arcana

The Major Arcana comprises 22 cards, each representing significant life themes, spiritual lessons, and archetypal energies. Cards like The Fool, The Magician, and The Empress offer profound insights into life’s journey and serve as pivotal points in readings.

Minor Arcana

The Minor Arcana consists of 56 cards, which are further divided into four suits: Cups, Pentacles, Swords, and Wands. Each suit reflects different aspects of human experience:

  • Cups: Emotions, relationships, and connections.
  • Pentacles: Material aspects, finances, and career.
  • Swords: Intellect, conflict, and communication.
  • Wands: Creativity, passion, and inspiration.

Understanding the structure of the tarot deck is essential for interpreting the cards effectively.

Preparing for Your Tarot Reading

Before diving into the intricacies of tarot reading, it is vital to create a conducive environment. Here are some steps to prepare for your reading:

1. Find Your Space

Choose a quiet, comfortable space where you feel at ease. This will help you concentrate and connect with your intuition. Consider lighting candles or burning incense to create an inviting atmosphere.

2. Grounding and Centering

Take a few moments to ground yourself. You might meditate, take deep breaths, or visualize roots growing from your feet into the earth. Grounding helps you center your energy and opens the pathway for intuitive guidance.

3. Set Your Intention

Before you begin your reading, clarify your intention. What question or topic do you want to explore? Setting a clear intention will guide your reading and help you focus on the relevant cards.

Shuffling and Drawing Cards

The way you shuffle and draw cards can influence the energy of your reading. Here’s a straightforward method to follow:

1. Shuffle the Deck

While shuffling, focus on your intention. Allow your thoughts and feelings to flow into the cards. You can shuffle in various ways, such as traditional overhand shuffling or a more intricate riffle shuffle.

2. Cut the Deck

After shuffling, cut the deck into three piles. This act symbolizes the division of past, present, and future energies. Reassemble the deck in any order that feels right for you.

3. Draw Cards

Depending on the spread you choose, draw the appropriate number of cards. For beginners, a simple three-card spread representing the past, present, and future is an excellent starting point.

Learning Tarot Card Meanings

Each card in the tarot deck has its own unique meaning, and these meanings can vary based on the context of the reading. As a beginner astrologer, here are some approaches to learning card meanings:

1. Use a Tarot Guidebook

Many tarot decks come with guidebooks that offer detailed explanations of each card. Use these resources to familiarize yourself with the imagery, symbols, and meanings associated with each card.

2. Create Your Own Tarot Journal

Maintaining a tarot journal can enhance your learning process. Write down your interpretations of each card, along with your personal reflections and experiences. Over time, your understanding will deepen, and you may discover unique insights that resonate with you.

3. Explore Symbolism and Numerology

Tarot cards are rich in symbolism and numerological significance. Take time to explore the meanings behind the symbols, colors, and numbers associated with each card. For example, understanding that the number 3 often symbolizes growth and creativity can add depth to your readings.

Connecting Tarot with Astrology

Integrating tarot and astrology can significantly enhance your readings. Here are some ways to establish this connection:

1. Astrological Associations

Each tarot card is associated with specific astrological signs and planets. For example, The Emperor card relates to Aries energy, representing authority and structure. Familiarize yourself with these associations to deepen your readings.

2. Use Birth Charts

Incorporating birth charts into your tarot readings can provide additional layers of insight. Consider how the positions of planets in a client’s birth chart may influence the cards drawn. This combination can offer a more comprehensive understanding of the individual’s life circumstances.

3. Timing and Transits

Astrology is intrinsically tied to timing. When conducting a tarot reading, consider the current astrological transits and how they may affect the interpretations of the cards. For instance, a reading during a Mercury retrograde might highlight themes of communication challenges.

Interpreting the Cards in a Reading

Once you’ve drawn your cards, it’s time to interpret their meanings. Here’s how to approach the interpretation process:

1. Read Intuitively

Begin by observing your initial feelings and impressions of the cards. Pay attention to any emotions or thoughts that arise. Intuition plays a significant role in tarot reading, and your gut feelings can provide valuable insights.

2. Consider Card Positions

In a spread, the position of each card can influence its meaning. For example, in a three-card spread, the first card may represent past influences, the second the present situation, and the third the potential outcome. Take note of how the positions inform your interpretation.

3. Look for Patterns

As you interpret the cards, look for patterns or connections between them. Are there recurring themes or symbols? How do the cards interact with one another? Recognizing these relationships can enhance your overall understanding of the reading.

Practicing Tarot Reading

The key to becoming proficient in tarot reading is practice. Here are some effective ways to hone your skills:

1. Daily Card Pulls

Start your day by pulling a single card. Reflect on its meaning and how it may influence your day ahead. This practice helps you build familiarity with the cards and enhances your intuitive abilities.

2. Read for Friends and Family

Once you feel comfortable, offer to read for friends or family members. This experience will expose you to different perspectives and questions, challenging you to think critically about your interpretations.

3. Join Tarot Communities

Engaging with tarot communities, either online or in-person, can be incredibly beneficial. Sharing experiences, asking questions, and learning from others can accelerate your growth as a tarot reader.

Ethical Considerations in Tarot Reading

As a tarot reader, it’s essential to approach your practice with integrity and respect. Here are some ethical considerations to keep in mind:

1. Confidentiality

Always maintain the confidentiality of your clients’ readings. Trust is fundamental in this practice, and respecting privacy fosters a safe environment.

2. Empowerment

Aim to empower your clients rather than create dependency. Encourage them to trust their intuition and capabilities. Tarot should serve as a guide, not a crutch.

3. Avoid Predicting Absolute Outcomes

Tarot readings offer insights and guidance, but they are not definitive predictions of the future. Remind clients that they have the power to shape their destinies through their choices.

Conclusion

Reading tarot cards can be a transformative experience for beginner astrologers, deepening their understanding of both astrology and intuitive practices. By familiarizing yourself with the tarot deck, setting intentions, interpreting cards, and integrating astrology, you can unlock the profound wisdom of tarot.

As you embark on your tarot journey, remember that practice is key. Embrace your intuition, explore the rich symbolism of the cards, and connect with the astrological energies that surround you. With patience and dedication, you’ll become a skilled tarot reader, offering insightful guidance to yourself and others. Happy reading!
