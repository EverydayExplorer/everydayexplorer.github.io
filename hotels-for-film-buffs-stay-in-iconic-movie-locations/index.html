Hotels for Film Buffs: Stay in Iconic Movie Locations


Lights, camera, action! Calling all film buffs! Are you tired of the same old hotel experiences? Well, we’ve got just the thing for you. Imagine staying in the very locations where your favorite movies were filmed. That’s right, we’re talking about hotels that have been immortalized on the silver screen. In this blog post, we’ll take you on a journey through some of the most iconic movie locations where you can actually book a room and live out your cinematic dreams.

1. The Overlook Hotel - The Shining:
Let’s start with a classic. If you’re a fan of horror, you’ll be thrilled to know that you can stay in the infamous Overlook Hotel from Stanley Kubrick’s ‘The Shining.’ Located in Estes Park, Colorado, The Stanley Hotel served as the inspiration for Stephen King’s novel and was also featured in the movie. Get ready to explore the hedge maze and maybe even encounter a ghost or two.

2. The Plaza Hotel - Home Alone 2:
Remember when Kevin McCallister got lost in New York City? Well, you can experience the same luxury as he did by staying at The Plaza Hotel. Located on Fifth Avenue in Manhattan, this iconic hotel has been featured in numerous films, but it gained extra fame when it played a central role in ‘Home Alone 2: Lost in New York.’ From the elegant decor to the stunning views, The Plaza Hotel offers a truly magical experience.

3. The Grand Budapest Hotel - The Grand Budapest Hotel:
Wes Anderson’s whimsical masterpiece, ‘The Grand Budapest Hotel,’ introduced us to a world of pastel colors and eccentric characters. While the fictional hotel itself doesn’t exist, you can still immerse yourself in its aesthetic by staying at the Görlitzer Warenhaus in Görlitz, Germany. This stunning building served as the exterior for the film and offers a unique blend of history and charm.

4. The Fontainebleau Miami Beach - Goldfinger:
If you’re a fan of James Bond, you can’t miss the opportunity to stay at the iconic Fontainebleau Miami Beach. This luxurious hotel was featured in the classic Bond film, ‘Goldfinger.’ Relive the glamour of the 1960s and imagine yourself as a secret agent as you explore the stunning architecture and soak up the sun on the beautiful Miami Beach.

5. The Biltmore Hotel - Scarface:
For those who love crime dramas, the Biltmore Hotel in Coral Gables, Florida is a must-visit. This historic hotel played a prominent role in Brian De Palma’s ‘Scarface.’ Feel like a gangster as you walk through the opulent corridors and imagine yourself in Tony Montana’s shoes.

These are just a few examples of the many hotels around the world that have become famous thanks to their appearances in movies. So, why settle for a regular hotel when you can have a cinematic experience? Whether you’re a horror enthusiast, a fan of romantic comedies, or an action movie lover, there’s a movie location hotel just waiting for you to check-in.

Remember, this is just the tip of the iceberg. The world is full of hotels that have made their mark on the big screen. So, go ahead, pack your bags, and get ready to step into the world of your favorite movies. Lights, camera, check-in!
