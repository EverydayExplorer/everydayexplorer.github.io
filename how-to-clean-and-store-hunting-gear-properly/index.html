How to Clean and Store Hunting Gear Properly


Hunting is not just a hobby; it’s a way of life for many outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you enjoy hunting deer, waterfowl, or other game, having the right hunting gear is crucial to your success in the field. However, acquiring high-quality hunting gear is only part of the equation. In order to maximize the lifespan and performance of your gear, proper care and maintenance are essential. This includes cleaning and storing your hunting gear correctly.

Importance of Cleaning and Storing Hunting Gear

Cleaning and storing your hunting gear properly may not seem like a top priority, especially after a long and exhausting day in the field. However, neglecting this critical step can have negative consequences. Here are a few reasons why cleaning and storing your hunting gear should be a priority:

  1. Extend the Lifespan: Hunting gear can be expensive, so maximizing its lifespan is essential. Regular cleaning helps remove dirt, stains, and odors that can degrade the materials over time. Storing gear properly also prevents it from getting damaged or becoming a breeding ground for mold and mildew.

  2. Maintain Performance: Hunting gear, such as clothing and footwear, often comes with special features like waterproof membranes and scent control technologies. Regular cleaning ensures that these features remain intact and effective, allowing you to stay comfortable and undetected on your hunts.

  3. Prevent Disease Spread: Properly cleaning your hunting gear can help reduce the spread of diseases among wildlife. By removing any potential contaminants, such as blood, feces, or ticks, you can minimize the risk of introducing or transmitting diseases to different areas.

Now that we understand the importance of cleaning and storing hunting gear, let’s dive into the step-by-step process to ensure your gear remains in top condition.

Cleaning Hunting Gear

  1. Read and Follow Manufacturer’s Instructions: Before you start cleaning your hunting gear, be sure to read the manufacturer’s instructions. They may have specific guidelines for cleaning different pieces of gear.

  2. Start with Spot Cleaning: Spot cleaning is an effective way to remove dirt, stains, and debris from your gear. Use a damp cloth or sponge with mild soap to gently scrub the affected areas. Avoid using harsh chemicals that may damage the materials.

  3. Machine Wash When Appropriate: If your hunting clothing is machine washable, follow the manufacturer’s instructions for washing. Use a scent-free, non-detergent hunting-specific laundry detergent to maintain the effectiveness of scent control technologies. Be sure to wash your gear separately from other clothing items.

  4. Remove Scents: Scent control is crucial for hunting success. Use scent-eliminating sprays or ozone-generating devices specifically designed for hunting gear to remove odors. Pay special attention to your clothing, boots, and accessories.

  5. Dry Properly: After cleaning, ensure that your gear is thoroughly dried before storing. Hang clothing and use a fan or dehumidifier to speed up the process. Avoid using high heat or direct sunlight, as it can damage the materials.

Storing Hunting Gear

  1. Clean Before Storage: Always store clean hunting gear. Any dirt, blood, or other contaminants left on the gear can attract pests or lead to mold growth during storage.

  2. Separate and Organize: Properly organize and separate your hunting gear based on categories such as clothing, accessories, and optics. This not only makes it easier to find what you need but also prevents damage caused by items rubbing or pressing against each other.

  3. Use Appropriate Storage Containers: Invest in high-quality storage containers that are moisture-resistant and airtight. This will protect your gear from dust, moisture, pests, and other elements that can cause damage.

  4. Utilize Desiccants: To prevent moisture buildup, place desiccant packets or moisture-absorbing products inside the storage containers. This helps maintain the integrity of your hunting gear, especially if you live in a humid climate.

  5. Store in a Cool and Dry Area: Find a cool, dry, and well-ventilated area to store your hunting gear. Avoid storing in areas prone to temperature fluctuations, such as attics or garages, as extreme temperature changes can damage the materials.

Regular Maintenance

Cleaning and storing your hunting gear properly should be part of your regular maintenance routine. Additionally, here are a few other maintenance tips to keep in mind:

  • Inspect and Repair: Regularly inspect your gear for any signs of wear and tear. Repair or replace damaged components promptly to ensure your gear’s functionality and safety.

  • Reapply Waterproofing: If your hunting gear has waterproofing features, reapply waterproofing treatments as recommended by the manufacturer. This will help maintain the gear’s water repellency and keep you dry during wet hunting conditions.

  • Check Equipment: Stay on top of equipment maintenance, such as sharpening knives, checking bowstrings, and properly maintaining firearms. These small steps can make a big difference in the field.

By following these cleaning, storing, and maintenance practices, you can ensure that your hunting gear remains in excellent condition for many hunting seasons to come.

Remember, taking care of your hunting gear is not just about preserving its value; it also demonstrates respect for the sport and the environment. Happy hunting!
