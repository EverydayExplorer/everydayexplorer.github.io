15 Healthy and Delicious Recipes for Vegetarian BBQ


Barbecues are a favorite summer pastime for many people around the world. However, for vegetarians, finding delicious and satisfying options can sometimes be a challenge. But fear not! We have got you covered with this amazing collection of 15 healthy and delicious recipes for vegetarian BBQs. Whether you are a vegetarian looking for new and exciting recipes for your backyard cookout or a meat-eater wanting to try something different, these recipes are sure to impress!

1. Grilled Veggie Skewers

Grilled Veggie Skewers are a classic BBQ staple. They are versatile, easy to make and bursting with flavors. Load up skewers with an assortment of your favorite vegetables like colorful bell peppers, zucchini, mushrooms, and cherry tomatoes. Brush them with a marinade of balsamic vinegar, olive oil, garlic, and herbs for an extra layer of flavor.

2. Portobello Mushroom Burger

Who says you need meat for a delicious burger? Portobello Mushroom Burgers are a hearty and satisfying alternative. Marinate large portobello mushroom caps in a mixture of soy sauce, garlic, and balsamic vinegar. Grill them until tender and juicy, and serve on a whole grain bun with all your favorite toppings – lettuce, tomato, onions, and condiments of your choice.

3. Grilled Halloumi Skewers

Halloumi cheese, known for its salty and slightly tangy taste, is a fantastic option for skewers. Cut the cheese into cubes and thread them onto skewers with colorful vegetables like cherry tomatoes and bell peppers. Grill until the cheese is golden brown and slightly crispy. Serve as a standalone dish or as a delicious addition to any BBQ spread.

4. Quinoa Stuffed Bell Peppers

Quinoa Stuffed Bell Peppers are a healthy and flavorful option for a vegetarian BBQ. Cut bell peppers in half and remove the seeds. Stuff them with a mixture of cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, and spices. Bake them in the oven or grill until the peppers are tender and the filling is heated through. Top with fresh herbs and a dollop of Greek yogurt for added creaminess.

5. Grilled Veggie Pizza

Take your pizza game to the grill with a delicious grilled veggie pizza. Roll out pizza dough and grill it on one side until it gets those beautiful char marks. Flip it over, add your favorite sauce, cheese, and an array of grilled vegetables like eggplant, zucchini, and bell peppers. Close the lid and cook until the cheese is bubbly and the crust is golden brown.

6. BBQ Cauliflower Wings

Cauliflower wings are an excellent substitute for chicken wings at a BBQ. Dip cauliflower florets into a flavorful BBQ sauce and bake or grill until crispy and caramelized. Serve with your favorite dipping sauce, and you won’t miss the meat!

7. Grilled Tofu Skewers

Tofu, when properly marinated and grilled, takes on a smoky flavor that is simply irresistible. Cut firm tofu into cubes and marinate in a mixture of soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger. Thread them onto skewers with vegetables of your choice and grill until golden brown. Serve with a drizzle of teriyaki sauce for an extra kick.

8. Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

No BBQ is complete without fresh corn on the cob. Grill corn on the cob until the kernels are tender and slightly charred. Spread a generous amount of herb butter made with fresh herbs like parsley, thyme, and garlic. The combination of sweet corn and herb butter is simply mouthwatering!

9. Barbecue Sweet Potato Wedges

Forget about regular fries when you can have Barbecue Sweet Potato Wedges. Cut sweet potatoes into wedges and toss them in a mixture of BBQ seasoning and olive oil. Grill them until crispy and golden brown. These wedges are the perfect side dish to complement any vegetarian BBQ feast.

10. Grilled Caprese Skewers

Everyone loves Caprese salad, so why not put a grilled spin on it? Thread cherry tomatoes, fresh basil leaves, and bite-sized pieces of mozzarella onto skewers. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and grill until the cheese starts to melt. These skewers are bursting with flavor and make for an elegant yet easy appetizer or side dish.

11. Grilled Watermelon Salad

Grilling watermelon may sound unusual, but it takes this refreshing fruit to a whole new level. Slice watermelon into thick wedges and grill them for a few minutes on each side until grill marks appear. Toss with feta cheese, fresh mint, and a drizzle of olive oil for a unique and delicious salad that will wow your guests.

12. Grilled Romaine Lettuce

Grilling romaine lettuce may seem unconventional, but it adds a smoky flavor and a pleasant charred texture. Cut a head of romaine lettuce in half lengthwise, brush with olive oil, and grill for a minute or two on each side until slightly wilted. Serve with your favorite dressing, croutons, and shaved Parmesan for a twist on the classic Caesar salad.

13. Veggie Kebabs with Peanut Sauce

Create mouthwatering veggie kebabs by using a variety of vegetables like bell peppers, red onions, cherry tomatoes, and zucchini. Thread them onto skewers and grill until tender. Serve with a creamy peanut sauce made with peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, garlic, and ginger for an added depth of flavor.

14. Grilled Pineapple Salsa

Take your salsa game up a notch with Grilled Pineapple Salsa. Grill pineapple slices until caramelized and dice them into small pieces. Combine with diced tomatoes, red onion, jalapeño, cilantro, lime juice, and a pinch of salt. This vibrant and tangy salsa is the perfect accompaniment to grilled tofu, veggie burgers, or as a refreshing dip with tortilla chips.

15. Grilled Banana S’mores

End your vegetarian BBQ on a sweet note with Grilled Banana S’mores. Cut a banana in half lengthwise, leaving the skin on. Grill until the skin turns black and the flesh becomes soft and caramelized. Remove from the grill, peel back the skin, and stuff the bananas with chocolate chips and marshmallows. Close the skin back up and let the residual heat melt the chocolate and marshmallows. It’s a sinfully delicious and healthy treat!

Conclusion

Vegetarian BBQs don’t have to be boring! With these 15 healthy and delicious recipes, you can enjoy a mouthwatering spread that will please both vegetarians and meat-eaters alike. From grilled veggie skewers and Portobello mushroom burgers to creative dishes like grilled pineapple salsa and grilled banana s’mores, there is no shortage of flavor and excitement for your next summer cookout. So fire up your grill, invite your friends and family, and get ready to savor the incredible flavors of these vegetarian BBQ delights!
