The Best Surfing Spots in Bali, Indonesia


Bali, Indonesia is a tropical paradise known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant culture, and incredible surfing spots. Whether you’re a beginner looking to catch your first wave or an experienced surfer seeking the thrill of challenging breaks, Bali offers a diverse range of surf spots that cater to all levels of expertise. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best surfing spots in Bali, highlighting their unique characteristics and why they should be on every surfer’s bucket list.

1. Uluwatu: Located on the southwestern tip of Bali, Uluwatu is famous for its world-class waves and breathtaking cliffside views. Home to the iconic Uluwatu Temple, this surf spot offers consistent swells, powerful waves, and long rides. Intermediate to advanced surfers will delight in the challenging breaks and the opportunity to ride some of the best barrels in Bali. With its stunning scenery and vibrant surf culture, Uluwatu is a must-visit destination for any surfer.

2. Padang Padang: Just a short distance away from Uluwatu lies Padang Padang, a reef break renowned for its epic left-hand barrels. This spot gained popularity after being featured in the movie ‘Eat, Pray, Love’ and has since become a favorite among surfers from around the world. With its consistent waves and picturesque surroundings, Padang Padang provides an unforgettable surfing experience for intermediate and advanced surfers.

3. Canggu: Located on the southwestern coast of Bali, Canggu has emerged as a popular surfing destination in recent years. This laid-back coastal town offers a variety of surf breaks suitable for all levels of surfers. Beginners can enjoy the mellow waves at Batu Bolong or Echo Beach, while more experienced surfers can challenge themselves at Berawa Beach or Old Man’s. Canggu’s vibrant surf scene, trendy cafes, and stunning sunsets make it a must-visit for surfers looking for a more relaxed atmosphere.

4. Dreamland: Situated on the Bukit Peninsula, Dreamland is a hidden gem that offers a less crowded surfing experience compared to other popular spots in Bali. With its sandy beach break and consistent waves, Dreamland is perfect for surfers of all levels. Beginners can take advantage of the gentle, rolling waves, while more advanced surfers can find bigger, more challenging breaks further out. Don’t miss the opportunity to catch a wave at this picturesque spot and enjoy its tranquil beauty.

5. Medewi: If you’re looking for a more remote and uncrowded surfing experience, head to Medewi on Bali’s west coast. Known for its long, peeling left-hand point break, Medewi offers a unique and rewarding surfing experience. This spot is ideal for intermediate surfers looking to improve their skills and enjoy long rides on the gentle waves. With its peaceful atmosphere and stunning scenery, Medewi provides a refreshing escape from the hustle and bustle of Bali’s more popular surf spots.

In conclusion, Bali, Indonesia is a surfer’s paradise, offering a wide array of surf spots that cater to all levels of expertise. From the world-class waves of Uluwatu to the mellow breaks of Canggu, Bali has something to offer every surfer. Whether you’re seeking an adrenaline-pumping adventure or a more laid-back surfing experience, Bali should be at the top of your surfing destination list. So grab your board, wax it up, and get ready to ride the waves in this tropical haven. Surf’s up in Bali!
