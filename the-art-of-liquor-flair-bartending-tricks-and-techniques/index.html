The Art of Liquor Flair Bartending: Tricks and Techniques


Bartending is no longer just about mixing drinks, it has evolved into a form of entertainment, with flair bartending taking center stage. Flair bartending is the art of performing tricks and techniques while making cocktails, adding a touch of excitement and spectacle to the bar experience. In this blog post, we’ll explore the world of flair bartending, from its origins to the various tricks and techniques used by professional flair bartenders.

Origins of Flair Bartending

Flair bartending gained popularity in the 1980s, when it emerged as a distinctive style of bartending in the United States. It started as a way for bartenders to showcase their skills and entertain customers while preparing their drinks. The trend quickly spread across the globe, captivating audiences with impressive displays of bottle flipping, juggling, and precision pouring.

The Basic Techniques

To become a flair bartender, one must master the basic techniques that form the foundation of the craft. These techniques include working with multiple bottles, shakers, and other bar tools, as well as mastering various pours, flips, and catches. Let’s take a closer look at some of the most common techniques used in flair bartending:

  1. Bottle Flipping: This is perhaps the most iconic move in flair bartending. It involves flipping bottles in the air and catching them with precision. Bottle flipping can create a real wow-factor and adds an element of danger and excitement to the performance.

  2. Shaker Tricks: Another popular technique is performing tricks with cocktail shakers. Flair bartenders often showcase their skills by tossing shakers behind their backs, throwing them in the air, and catching them effortlessly.

  3. Pouring Techniques: Flair bartenders are skilled in executing various pouring techniques, such as the ‘free pour,’ which involves pouring liquor from one bottle to another without using a jigger or measuring device. The ability to pour with accuracy and style is what sets flair bartending apart from traditional bartending.

The Tricks of the Trade

Flair bartending is all about entertaining the audience, and this is achieved through a repertoire of impressive tricks. Here are some of the most popular flair bartending tricks:

  1. The Working Flair: This trick involves incorporating flair moves while simultaneously serving customers. It requires impeccable coordination and multitasking skills, as the bartender must perform complex tricks while making drinks efficiently.

  2. The Fire Show: Fire is often used as a prop in flair bartending to create a visually stunning performance. Bartenders often light up their drinks or bar tools to add an element of danger and drama, capturing the attention of the audience.

  3. The Bottle Stall: A classic move in flair bartending, the bottle stall involves balancing and resting a bottle on various parts of the body, such as the elbow, shoulder, or even the head. It requires a combination of balance, control, and confidence.

The Importance of Practice

Becoming a skilled flair bartender takes time and practice. It requires mastering the techniques and tricks mentioned above, but also developing showmanship and a unique style. Flair bartenders must invest hours into perfecting their moves, refining their timing, and creating seamless routines that captivate the audience.

Incorporating Flair into the Bar Experience

Flair bartending is not just limited to competitions or special events. Many bars and restaurants have embraced the art and incorporated it into their daily operations. By having flare bartenders behind the bar, establishments can create a unique and memorable experience for their patrons. Watching a bartender perform amazing tricks while preparing a cocktail adds an extra level of excitement and entertainment to a night out.

Conclusion

The art of liquor flair bartending is a captivating spectacle that adds a touch of excitement and entertainment to the world of mixology. With its origins in the 1980s, flair bartending has become a global phenomenon, captivating audiences with impressive tricks and techniques. From bottle flipping to fire shows, flair bartending is an art form that requires skill, practice, and showmanship. So, the next time you’re at a bar, keep an eye out for the skilled flair bartenders behind the counter, and enjoy the mesmerizing performance that accompanies your drink. Cheers!
