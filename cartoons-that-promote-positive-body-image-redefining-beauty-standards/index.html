Cartoons That Promote Positive Body Image: Redefining Beauty Standards


In today’s society, there is an increasing awareness of the impact media has on our perception of beauty. From airbrushed models on billboards to unrealistic body standards portrayed in movies and television shows, it can be difficult for individuals, particularly young children, to develop a healthy body image. However, there is one form of media that has the power to challenge these societal norms and promote a more positive outlook on body image: cartoons.

Cartoons have long been a source of entertainment for children and adults alike. They provide an escape from reality, allowing viewers to enter fantastical worlds filled with colorful characters and imaginative storylines. Yet, beneath the surface, many cartoons are doing much more than just entertaining their audience. They are promoting positive body image by redefining beauty standards.

One such cartoon that stands out is ‘Steven Universe.’ Created by Rebecca Sugar, this groundbreaking series features a diverse cast of characters with varying body shapes and sizes. From the main protagonist, Steven, who is depicted as a pleasantly plump and cheerful young boy, to the Crystal Gems, a group of strong and powerful women who come in all shapes and forms, ‘Steven Universe’ challenges the notion that beauty is limited to a specific body type.

Not only does ‘Steven Universe’ showcase characters with diverse body types, but it also explores themes of self-acceptance and self-love. The characters navigate their own personal journeys of discovering their true worth, emphasizing the importance of loving oneself regardless of societal expectations. By promoting these messages, the cartoon encourages viewers to embrace their own unique bodies and appreciate the beauty within themselves.

Another cartoon that champions positive body image is ‘She-Ra and the Princesses of Power.’ This reimagining of the classic 1980s cartoon features a diverse cast of female characters, each with their own unique body shape, size, and abilities. The show highlights the strength and power that comes in different forms, challenging the notion that a specific body type defines one’s worth.

‘She-Ra and the Princesses of Power’ also emphasizes the importance of teamwork and unity. The princesses come together, embracing their individual strengths, and supporting each other through their journey to protect their world. This message of unity promotes the idea that beauty and strength can be found in diversity, encouraging viewers to celebrate their own unique qualities.

In addition to these two cartoons, there are several others that also promote positive body image and redefine beauty standards. ‘The Loud House’ features a diverse set of characters within a large family, each with their own distinct personalities and body types. ‘Miraculous: Tales of Ladybug & Cat Noir’ showcases a female superhero who takes pride in her abilities rather than her appearance. ‘Big Hero 6: The Series’ celebrates friendship, teamwork, and the idea that looks don’t define who we are on the inside.

These cartoons are just a few examples of how media can promote positive body image and challenge societal beauty standards. By showcasing diverse characters with realistic body types and emphasizing messages of self-acceptance, self-love, and the celebration of unique qualities, these shows encourage viewers of all ages to develop a healthy and positive outlook on their own bodies.

It’s important to note that while cartoons can play a significant role in promoting positive body image, the responsibility goes beyond the media. Parents, guardians, and educators also have a role to play in fostering a healthy body image in children. By discussing these topics and providing children with positive role models, we can help shape a generation that respects and values diversity in body types.

In conclusion, cartoons that promote positive body image are redefining beauty standards and challenging the societal pressures surrounding body image. Through the portrayal of diverse characters, messages of self-acceptance, self-love, and the celebration of unique qualities, these cartoons encourage viewers to embrace their own bodies and appreciate the beauty within themselves. By combining the influence of media with the efforts of parents, guardians, and educators, we can help create a society that values and respects all body types.
