Survival Skills for People with Chronic Illness: Managing Health in Crisis


Living with a chronic illness can be an immense challenge, as it often involves managing symptoms on a daily basis. However, when faced with a crisis or emergency situation, such as a natural disaster or a global pandemic like we have experienced with COVID-19, the difficulties multiply exponentially. Suddenly, the typical coping mechanisms and routines that were in place may no longer be accessible or effective, leaving individuals with chronic illness feeling even more vulnerable and uncertain about their health.

In such circumstances, it becomes crucial for those with chronic illness to develop and enhance their survival skills to effectively manage their health in times of crisis. By having a well-thought-out plan and strategies in place, individuals can be better prepared for any unforeseen eventuality, ensuring that their health needs are met even under the most challenging circumstances.

Here are some essential survival skills that individuals with chronic illness can rely on during a crisis:

1. Emergency Preparedness: One of the first steps in managing health during a crisis is to be prepared in advance. Create an emergency kit that includes necessary medications, medical supplies, and important documents such as medical records and contact details of healthcare providers. Keep this kit easily accessible, and consider having duplicates in different locations to ensure access in case of evacuation or displacement.

2. Support Network: During a crisis, it is vital to have a strong support network that understands your needs and can provide assistance if necessary. Reach out to family, friends, neighbors, and support groups to communicate your situation and establish a reliable system of support. Consider having a designated emergency contact who can advocate for your needs and communicate with healthcare providers on your behalf.

3. Communication: Effective communication becomes paramount during a crisis. Ensure that you have a comprehensive list of your healthcare providers’ contact information, as well as alternative methods of communication in case traditional means are unavailable. Stay updated with local news and emergency alerts to stay informed about resources and facilities available in your area.

4. Adaptability: With the sudden disruption of routines and resources during a crisis, learning to adapt becomes crucial. Be flexible and open to alternative solutions for managing your health. Explore virtual healthcare options such as telemedicine or virtual support groups to maintain continuity in your care. Create backup plans for obtaining medications and supplies in case your usual sources become unavailable.

5. Mental Health Support: The impact of a crisis on mental health cannot be underestimated. It is essential to prioritize self-care and seek mental health support when needed. Engage in activities that promote relaxation and stress reduction. Take breaks from news updates and social media overload to maintain mental well-being. Reach out to mental health professionals or helplines for additional support during challenging times.

6. Self-Advocacy: In a crisis, healthcare systems and resources may become overwhelmed. It is crucial for individuals with chronic illness to become their own advocates and assertively communicate their health needs. Be familiar with your medical history, medications, and treatment plans, and advocate for yourself to ensure you receive the appropriate care. Stay informed about your rights and available support services in your community.

7. Emergency Financial Planning: A crisis can often have financial implications. Establish an emergency fund and explore insurance options that can provide coverage for healthcare needs during a crisis. Consider creating a budget and identifying areas where expenses can be reduced to ensure financial stability during difficult times.

8. Wellness and Lifestyle Management: Despite the challenges posed by a crisis, it is important to prioritize general wellness and lifestyle management. This includes maintaining a nutritious diet, participating in physical activity within the limitations of your health condition, and getting adequate sleep. Such lifestyle modifications can play a significant role in supporting overall health and managing chronic illness effectively.

By developing these survival skills, individuals with chronic illness can navigate through times of crisis with greater confidence and resilience. Remember that knowledge is power, so stay informed about potential crises and develop contingency plans accordingly. Reach out to healthcare providers and support networks for guidance and assistance. Most importantly, practice self-compassion and be kind to yourself during challenging times. Together, we can adapt, overcome, and manage our health in the face of any crisis.
