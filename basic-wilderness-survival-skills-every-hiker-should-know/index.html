Basic Wilderness Survival Skills Every Hiker Should Know


Picture this: you’re deep in the heart of nature, surrounded by towering trees and a symphony of birdsong. The sun is shining, and the trail beckons you further into the wilderness. As a hiker, it’s essential to be prepared for the unexpected. Whether you’re a seasoned backpacker or just starting your outdoor adventures, knowing basic wilderness survival skills is crucial for your safety and well-being. In this blog post, we’ll explore some essential skills that every hiker should know.

1. Navigation:
When venturing into the wilderness, it’s important to have a good sense of direction. Carry a detailed map and compass with you, and learn how to use them effectively. Familiarize yourself with landmarks and trail markings, so you can confidently find your way back to civilization if needed.

2. Shelter Building:
Knowing how to create a suitable shelter is vital in case you get caught in unexpected weather conditions or need to spend the night outdoors. Learn how to construct a basic lean-to or debris hut using natural materials such as branches, leaves, and moss. These simple structures can provide protection from rain, wind, and cold temperatures.

3. Fire Starting:
Fire not only provides warmth but also helps with cooking food and signaling for help. Learn different methods of fire starting, such as using a ferro rod, flint and steel, or friction-based techniques like the bow drill or hand drill. Practice these skills before your hike, and always carry waterproof matches or a lighter as a backup.

4. Water Sourcing and Purification:
Water is essential for survival, but it’s not always readily available in the wilderness. Learn how to find water sources like streams, lakes, or even dew on plants. However, it’s crucial to purify any water you collect to avoid waterborne illnesses. Carry a water filter or purification tablets, and learn how to build a simple water filter using natural materials if needed.

5. Food foraging:
While it’s always best to carry enough food for your hiking trip, knowing how to identify edible wild plants can be a valuable skill. Research and learn about local flora, and only consume plants that you’re absolutely sure are safe to eat. Avoid mushrooms and berries unless you have expert knowledge in their identification.

6. First Aid:
Accidents can happen even on the most well-planned hikes. Knowing basic first aid skills can make a significant difference in responding to injuries or emergencies. Take a first aid course, and carry a well-stocked first aid kit that includes essentials like bandages, antiseptic ointment, pain relievers, and insect repellent.

7. Signaling for Help:
In case you find yourself lost or injured, knowing how to signal for help can be a lifesaver. Carry a whistle, a signal mirror, or a brightly colored bandana to attract attention. Learn how to create visible distress signals, such as building a signal fire or using reflective materials to catch the attention of search and rescue teams.

Remember, these are just a few basic wilderness survival skills every hiker should know. It’s crucial to continue expanding your knowledge and skills through practice and further education. By being prepared and equipped with the necessary skills, you can confidently explore the wilderness while ensuring your safety and enjoyment of the great outdoors. Stay safe and happy hiking!
