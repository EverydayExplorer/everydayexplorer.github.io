Planting for Pollinators: Flowers that Attract Bees and Butterflies


Pollinators play a crucial role in maintaining the biodiversity of our ecosystems. Bees and butterflies, in particular, are essential for the reproduction of many flowering plants. By attracting these pollinators to your garden, you not only create a beautiful space but also contribute to the health and sustainability of our planet. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best flowers to plant in your garden to attract bees and butterflies.

Why Attract Bees and Butterflies?

Before we dive into the specific flowers, let’s understand why attracting bees and butterflies is so important. Bees and butterflies are both pollinators, meaning they transfer pollen from the male parts of a flower to the female parts, enabling fertilization and the production of seeds. This process is crucial for the reproduction of many plants, including those that provide us with food.

Flowers that Attract Bees

Bees are attracted to flowers with bright colors, especially shades of blue, purple, and yellow. They also prefer flowers with a tubular shape, as it allows them to easily access the nectar. Here are some bee-friendly flowers you should consider planting:

  1. Lavender: Not only does lavender produce beautiful purple flowers, but its fragrance is also irresistible to bees. Planting lavender in your garden will not only attract bees but also add a pleasant aroma to the air.

  2. Sunflowers: These iconic flowers are not only visually stunning but also a favorite of bees. Their large, open faces provide easy access to nectar, making them a popular choice for pollinators.

  3. Borage: Borage is a herb with vibrant blue flowers that bees absolutely love. It’s easy to grow and can be a great addition to your garden, attracting bees throughout the blooming season.

  4. Coneflowers: Coneflowers, also known as echinaceas, are not only attractive to bees but also to butterflies. Their daisy-like flowers come in various colors and provide a long-lasting source of nectar.

  5. Salvia: With its tall spikes of blue, purple, or red flowers, salvia is a favorite among bees. This low-maintenance plant is not only beautiful but also drought-tolerant, making it perfect for any garden.

Flowers that Attract Butterflies

Butterflies are attracted to flowers with bright colors, particularly shades of red, orange, and pink. They also prefer flowers with a flat or shallow shape, allowing them to land and access nectar easily. Here are some butterfly-friendly flowers to consider:

  1. Butterfly Bush: As its name suggests, the butterfly bush is a magnet for butterflies. Its long clusters of flowers, available in various colors, produce abundant nectar, attracting butterflies from far and wide.

  2. Milkweed: Milkweed is not only essential for monarch butterflies but also attracts a variety of other butterfly species. Its colorful flowers and abundant nectar make it a must-have for any butterfly garden.

  3. Zinnias: Zinnias come in a wide range of vibrant colors and are a favorite of many butterfly species. Planting zinnias in your garden will not only attract butterflies but also add a pop of color to your landscape.

  4. Verbena: Verbena is a versatile plant that comes in various colors, including shades of pink, purple, and red. Its clusters of small flowers provide a rich source of nectar for butterflies, making it a valuable addition to any butterfly garden.

  5. Black-eyed Susans: Black-eyed Susans, with their golden yellow petals and dark centers, are not only attractive to bees but also to butterflies. These hardy flowers are easy to grow and can thrive in a variety of conditions.

Creating a Pollinator-Friendly Garden

To create a truly pollinator-friendly garden, it’s important to provide a variety of flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. This ensures a continuous food source for bees and butterflies. Additionally, avoid using pesticides and herbicides, as they can be harmful to pollinators.

Remember to provide water sources, such as shallow dishes with pebbles, for butterflies to drink from. Creating sheltered areas, such as shrubs or small trees, can also provide resting spots for butterflies.

By incorporating these flowers into your garden, you can create a haven for bees and butterflies while enjoying a vibrant and colorful landscape. Together, let’s take small steps to protect our pollinators and preserve the beauty of nature.
