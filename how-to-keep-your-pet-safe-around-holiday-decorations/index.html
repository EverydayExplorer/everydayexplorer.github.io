How to Keep Your Pet Safe Around Holiday Decorations


The holiday season is a wonderful time of year, filled with festive decorations and joyful celebrations. As pet owners, it’s important to keep our furry friends safe amidst the excitement. Holiday decorations can pose potential hazards for pets if we’re not careful. To ensure a happy and safe holiday season for both you and your beloved pet, here are some essential tips on how to keep your pet safe around holiday decorations.

1. Secure Your Christmas Tree: The centerpiece of many holiday decorations is undoubtedly the Christmas tree. Ensure that your tree is securely anchored to prevent it from toppling over if your curious pet decides to investigate. Consider using a sturdy tree stand, tying the tree to a fixed object, or even placing a baby gate around the tree to create a barrier.

2. Choose Pet-Friendly Ornaments: While shiny and delicate ornaments may be visually appealing, they can also entice pets to play and explore. Opt for pet-friendly ornaments made of non-toxic materials that won’t break easily. Avoid using tinsel and edible decorations, as they can be harmful if ingested by your pet.

3. Avoid Hanging Low: Hang ornaments higher on the tree where your pet can’t reach them. Pets, especially cats, are naturally drawn to dangling objects and may be tempted to swat at low-hanging ornaments. By keeping decorations out of reach, you can prevent accidents and potential choking hazards.

4. Secure Electrical Cords: Holiday lights and electrical cords can be hazardous to pets if chewed on. Keep cords out of your pet’s reach, tucking them away or covering them with cord protectors. Additionally, unplugging decorations when you’re not around can provide an extra level of safety.

5. Use Pet-Friendly Christmas Lights: When choosing Christmas lights, opt for pet-friendly options that are labeled as such. These lights are typically insulated and have thicker cords, making them less likely to cause harm if chewed on. LED lights are an excellent choice, as they stay cool to the touch and use less energy.

6. Mindful Candle Placement: Candles create a warm and cozy ambiance, but they can also pose a fire hazard if knocked over by a playful pet. Place candles in secure, elevated holders or consider using flameless LED candles instead. This way, you can enjoy the flickering glow without worrying about your pet’s safety.

7. Keep Gift Wrapping Supplies Away: Ribbons, bows, and gift wrap may be fun for us, but they can be dangerous if ingested by pets. Keep all gift wrapping supplies stored safely out of your pet’s reach. After unwrapping presents, dispose of any ribbons or plastic pieces promptly.

8. Be Wary of Plants: Many popular holiday plants, such as poinsettias, mistletoe, and holly, can be toxic to pets if ingested. Consider using artificial versions of these plants to decorate your home or placing them in areas inaccessible to your pet. If you must have live plants, research safe alternatives that won’t harm your furry friend.

9. Provide a Safe Haven: With guests and increased activity during the holidays, your pet may become overwhelmed or anxious. Create a quiet, designated space where your pet can retreat to if needed. Fill this space with their bed, toys, and familiar scents to help them feel secure and comfortable.

10. Supervise and Monitor: Ultimately, the best way to keep your pet safe around holiday decorations is to supervise them closely. Keep an eye on their behavior and intervene if necessary. If you’re hosting gatherings, consider assigning a family member or friend to be the designated pet watcher, ensuring that your pet remains safe and content.

By following these essential tips, you can create a festive and pet-friendly environment during the holiday season. Remember, the well-being and safety of your pet should always be a top priority. Wishing you and your furry companion a joyous and hazard-free holiday season!
