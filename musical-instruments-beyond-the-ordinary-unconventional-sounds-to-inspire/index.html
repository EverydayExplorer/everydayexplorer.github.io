Musical Instruments Beyond the Ordinary: Unconventional Sounds to Inspire


Music is an art form that has been practiced for centuries and has evolved into an industry that provides entertainment for millions of people around the world. The beauty of music lies in its variety - the ability to create different sounds and emotions using a range of instruments. While traditional instruments like the guitar, piano, and drums remain popular, there are several unconventional instruments that can inspire musical creativity.

In this blog post, we will take a look at some of the unconventional musical instruments that can help you create unique and inspiring sounds.

1. The Theremin

The Theremin is an electronic musical instrument that is played without being touched. It consists of two metal antennas that sense the position of the player’s hands - one controlling pitch and the other controlling volume. The Theremin produces a haunting sound that has been used in numerous movies, including ‘The Day the Earth Stood Still’ and ‘The Birdcage’.

2. The Waterphone

The Waterphone is a unique instrument that consists of a cylindrical resonator with a number of bronze rods of different lengths and thicknesses attached to it. The Waterphone produces a range of eerie and ethereal sounds that have been used in movies like ‘The Matrix’ and ‘Twilight’.

3. The Hang

The Hang is a steel percussion instrument that produces a distinctive sound similar to that of a steelpan. It has a unique shape that resembles an inverted wok and is played by striking the different areas of the instrument with the hands. The Hang has been used in contemporary music and can add a touch of exoticism to your compositions.

4. The Ondes Martenot

The Ondes Martenot is an electronic musical instrument that has been around since the 1920s. It produces a wide range of sounds by varying the frequency and amplitude of the waveforms produced by oscillators. The Ondes Martenot has been used in film soundtracks like ‘Lawrence of Arabia’ and ‘V for Vendetta’.

5. The Hang Drum

The Hang Drum is a relatively new percussion instrument that is a cross between a steelpan and a drum. It has a unique design that allows it to produce a range of different tones and can be played with the hands or with a variety of mallets. The Hang Drum has found its way into world music and has been used by artists like Bjork and Peter Gabriel.

6. The Glass Harp

The Glass Harp is an instrument that consists of a number of wine glasses filled with water at different levels. The player runs their finger around the rim of the glasses to produce a range of different tones. The Glass Harp produces a beautiful, ethereal sound that can add a touch of magic to your music.

Using unconventional musical instruments can help to inspire creativity in your compositions. By incorporating these unique sounds into your music, you can add a touch of personality and uniqueness to your creations.

In conclusion, music is a beautiful art form that is constantly evolving. Musical instruments are the tools that musicians use to create the wonderful sounds that we love to hear. While traditional instruments will always have their place in music, there are several unconventional instruments that can inspire creativity and add a touch of magic to your compositions. So why not try using some of these unconventional musical instruments to create your next masterpiece?
