Hunting Small Game with Air Rifles


Hunting small game with air rifles has become a popular and exciting activity for many hunters. Not only is it a cost-effective alternative to using traditional hunting rifles, but it also provides a unique challenge that many find appealing.

Air rifles are especially suitable for hunting small game such as rabbits, squirrels and birds. They are relatively quiet, which allows the hunter to blend into the surrounding environment without disturbing the prey. Air rifles are also lighter and easier to carry, making them ideal for hunters who prefer to move around a lot during their hunt.

Before you begin hunting with an air rifle, it’s essential to make sure you have the right equipment. Here are some tips to help you prepare for your next small game hunting trip.

Choose the right air rifle

There are several types of air rifles on the market, and each one has unique features that make them suitable for specific hunting conditions. The most common types of air rifles are pre-charged pneumatic, spring-powered, and gas-ram rifles. If you are new to air rifles, it’s best to start with a spring-powered model as they are more straightforward to operate and require less maintenance. However, if you are an experienced shooter and want a more powerful rifle, then a gas-ram or pre-charged pneumatic rifle may be a better option.

Also, choose an air rifle with a caliber that will allow you to take down your intended quarry humanely. Most air rifles come in 0.177 and 0.22 calibers, but there are also other options, such as 0.20 and 0.25 calibers. For small game hunting, a 0.177 or 0.22 would be a good choice, depending on the size of your target.

Select the right pellets

Once you have selected your air rifle, the next step is to choose the right pellets. Pellets come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and weights, and the one you choose will depend on the type of air rifle you are using and the game you will be hunting.

For small game hunting, it’s essential to choose a pellet that will provide the most accurate shot possible. Hollow point pellets are an excellent choice for hunting small game because they expand on impact, causing more significant tissue damage. However, wadcutter pellets may be a better option for shooting small birds that require a more precise shot.

Practice your shot

Before you head out on your small game hunting trip, it’s crucial to practice your shot with your air rifle. The more comfortable you are with your weapon, the more accurate your shots will be. Set up targets at different ranges and practice shooting until you feel confident with your aim.

Be aware of the law

It’s essential to check your local hunting laws before heading out on your small game hunting trip. Some areas may have specific regulations regarding the use of air rifles, so it’s best to be informed before venturing out.

In conclusion, hunting small game with an air rifle can be a fun and exciting experience for hunters of all skill levels. Choosing the right equipment, practicing your shot, and being aware of the hunting laws in your area are all essential factors to keep in mind when hitting the field. With these tips in mind, you will be well on your way to a successful small game hunting trip with your trusty air rifle.
