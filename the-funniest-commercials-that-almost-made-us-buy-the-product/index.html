The Funniest Commercials That Almost Made Us Buy the Product


Commercials are a ubiquitous part of our modern lives. We see them on TV, streaming platforms, social media, and even before watching a YouTube video. While most commercials aim to inform and persuade us to buy a product, some take a different approach and focus on humor to capture our attention. These funny commercials not only entertain us but also leave a lasting impression, making us almost consider purchasing the advertised product. In this blog post, we’ll take a look at some of the funniest commercials that almost made us reach for our wallets.

1. Old Spice: “The Man Your Man Could Smell Like”

Old Spice, a brand known for its men’s grooming products, created a commercial that quickly became a viral sensation. Featuring a suave and humorous spokesman, the commercial took viewers on a fast-paced journey through various scenes, all while delivering witty one-liners. The ad’s humor and memorable catchphrases such as “I’m on a horse” made it a hit. Even though the commercial was selling body wash, it left a lasting impression on viewers, making them consider trying out the product.

2. Doritos: “Pug Attack”

Doritos has a history of creating hilarious commercials for the Super Bowl, and “Pug Attack” was no exception. The commercial featured a man taunting a pug with a bag of Doritos, only to be hilariously attacked by a group of pugs in retaliation. The unexpected and comical nature of the ad made it a standout, and while viewers may not have rushed to buy Doritos immediately, the commercial certainly left a positive association with the brand.

3. Geico: “Hump Day”

Geico’s “Hump Day” commercial became a cultural phenomenon with its portrayal of a talking camel wandering through an office, exuberantly reminding everyone that it’s “hump day.” The commercial’s humor and catchy tagline made it a hit, and even though it was promoting insurance, it left a mark on viewers and became a conversation starter. The commercial’s success showed that humor can be a powerful tool in advertising, even for seemingly mundane products.

4. Snickers: “You’re Not You When You’re Hungry”

Snickers’ “You’re Not You When You’re Hungry” campaign is a prime example of using humor to sell a product. The commercial series featured various scenarios where individuals behaved out of character due to hunger, only to return to normal after eating a Snickers bar. The humorous take on the effects of hunger resonated with audiences and made them consider reaching for a Snickers during their next craving.

5. Skittles: “Taste the Rainbow”

Skittles has a history of creating offbeat and funny commercials, and “Taste the Rainbow” is a standout example. The commercial depicted a man with a beard made entirely of Skittles, leading to absurd and humorous situations. The quirky and unexpected nature of the ad made it memorable and almost compelled viewers to pick up a pack of Skittles just to experience the brand’s unique and playful approach.

Conclusion

While the primary goal of commercials is to sell products, the funniest ones often achieve much more. They entertain us, make us laugh, and leave a lasting impression that almost compels us to consider purchasing the advertised product. By leveraging humor effectively, brands can create a strong emotional connection with their audience and stand out in a crowded advertising landscape. So, the next time a funny commercial makes you chuckle, remember that it’s not just about the laughs – it’s also about the lasting impact on your buying decisions.
