Natural Shelter Building: Using What's Around You for Protection


In today’s modern world, where we are surrounded by concrete walls and glass windows, the concept of natural shelter building may seem like a distant memory. However, the ability to create a shelter using what’s around you for protection is an essential skill that dates back to our ancestors. In this blog post, we will explore the art of natural shelter building, the materials you can use, and the techniques that will help you create a safe and cozy sanctuary in the wilderness.

When it comes to natural shelter building, one of the first things you need to consider is the location. Look for a spot that is elevated and away from potential hazards such as flooding or falling branches. It’s also important to choose an area where the materials you need are readily available.

Now that you have found the perfect location, let’s talk about the materials you can use for your natural shelter. Mother nature has provided us with a wide range of resources that can be utilized for this purpose. Fallen branches, leaves, moss, and rocks are just a few examples of the materials that can be used to create a sturdy and protective shelter.

The next step is to decide on the type of shelter you want to build. One popular option is the lean-to shelter. This involves propping a long branch against a tree and using smaller branches and leaves to create a slanted roof. This type of shelter provides excellent protection against wind and rain.

If you’re feeling more adventurous, you can try building a debris hut. This involves creating a framework using branches and then covering it with leaves, moss, and other natural materials. The result is a cozy shelter that provides insulation and protection from the elements.

Another option is the teepee shelter, which involves using long branches to create a cone-shaped structure. The branches are then covered with leaves or other available materials. This type of shelter is not only effective but also visually appealing.

Now that you know the basics of natural shelter building, let’s discuss some tips and techniques to ensure that your shelter is sturdy and reliable. First and foremost, make sure to gather enough materials before you start building. Having everything you need within reach will save you time and energy.

When constructing your shelter, it’s important to pay attention to the angles and joints. Make sure that the branches are securely connected and that the structure is stable. Adding additional support, such as rocks or logs, can further enhance the stability of your shelter.

Lastly, don’t forget about insulation. Layering leaves, moss, or even animal fur inside your shelter will help retain heat and keep you warm during cold nights.

In conclusion, natural shelter building is a valuable skill that allows us to connect with nature and protect ourselves in the wild. By using what’s around us, we can create comfortable and reliable shelters that provide protection from the elements. So next time you find yourself in the great outdoors, embrace the art of natural shelter building and create your own sanctuary amidst the wilderness.
