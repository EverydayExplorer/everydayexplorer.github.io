Astrology and Tarot: Interpreting Card Combinations


Welcome to the fascinating world of Astrology and Tarot! If you’re new to these subjects or a seasoned enthusiast, you might be curious about how the two complement each other. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of interpreting card combinations in Tarot readings, using the principles of astrology to enhance our understanding. So, without further ado, let’s delve into the magical realm of Astrology and Tarot!

Astrology and Tarot are both ancient divination practices that have been used for centuries to gain insights into the past, present, and future. While astrology focuses on the movements and positions of celestial bodies to make predictions, Tarot uses a deck of cards with symbolic imagery to tap into the subconscious mind and offer guidance. When combined, these powerful tools can provide profound insights into various aspects of our lives.

Interpreting card combinations in Tarot is a skill that requires practice, intuition, and knowledge of the symbolism embedded in each card. By incorporating astrological influences, we can deepen our understanding of the energies at play within a reading. The zodiac signs, planetary associations, and astrological houses can add layers of meaning to the individual cards and their combinations.

For example, let’s say you pull the Empress card, which signifies nurturing and abundance, paired with the High Priestess card, representing intuition and mysticism. With a basic understanding of astrology, you can consider the zodiac signs associated with these cards. The Empress is connected to Venus, the planet of love and beauty, while the High Priestess corresponds to the Moon, representing intuition and the subconscious. Understanding these planetary influences can help you interpret this combination as a call to tap into your intuitive and nurturing abilities to manifest abundance in your life.

Moreover, the astrological houses can shed light on the specific areas of life that are being influenced by a particular card combination. Each house represents a different aspect of our lives, such as relationships, career, or personal growth. By considering the house associated with a card combination, you can gain deeper insights into the specific area where these energies are most strongly influencing you.

As an example, imagine you draw the Death card, symbolizing transformation and rebirth, combined with the King of Cups card, representing emotional maturity and empathy. By knowing that the Death card is associated with the eighth astrological house, which deals with transformation, shared resources, and intimacy, you can interpret this combination as a sign that a significant emotional transformation is occurring in your intimate relationships or shared resources.

Incorporating astrology into your Tarot practice allows for a more nuanced interpretation of card combinations, making your readings more insightful and accurate. Understanding the astrological correspondences of the cards adds depth and symbolism to your readings, helping you uncover hidden messages and tap into the cosmic energies at play.

It is important to remember that Astrology and Tarot are not fortune-telling methods. They are tools for self-reflection and guidance, offering perspectives and insights that can help you make informed decisions and navigate life’s challenges.

In conclusion, the combination of Astrology and Tarot is a powerful amalgamation that can enhance the depth and accuracy of your readings. By incorporating the astrological correspondences of the cards, such as zodiac signs, planetary influences, and astrological houses, you can gain a deeper understanding of the energies at play within a reading. Whether you are exploring your own life’s path or seeking to unravel the mysteries of the universe, Astrology and Tarot provide a captivating journey of self-discovery.

So, dive into the world of Astrology and Tarot, explore the interconnectedness of the cosmos, and let the cards guide you on your spiritual journey. Happy reading!
