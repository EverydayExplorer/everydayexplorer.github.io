The World of Craft Tequila: From Highland to Lowland


Tequila is more than just a drink; it is a cultural icon that represents the essence of Mexico. While most people are familiar with tequila as a party drink, there is a whole world of craft tequila waiting to be discovered. In this blog post, we will take a journey through the two primary regions where tequila is produced: the Highland and Lowland regions of Mexico.

The Highland region, also known as Los Altos, is situated in the state of Jalisco, Mexico. This area is characterized by its higher elevation, which influences the flavor profile of the tequila produced here. The Highland tequilas are known for their sweeter, fruitier, and more floral notes. The agave plants cultivated in this region benefit from the volcanic soil and cooler temperatures, resulting in a more nuanced and complex taste.

One of the most famous Highland tequila brands is Casa Noble. Their tequilas are crafted using traditional methods and aged in French white oak barrels, imparting a smooth and refined character. The Casa Noble Reposado, with its hints of vanilla and caramel, is a perfect example of the quality and craftsmanship found in Highland tequilas.

On the other hand, the Lowland region, also called Los Bajos, is located closer to the coast, in the state of Jalisco and parts of the neighboring states. The soil in this region is rich in minerals and has a higher clay content, resulting in a distinctive flavor profile. Lowland tequilas are known for their earthy, herbal, and savory notes. The agave plants grown here have a shorter maturation period due to the warmer climate, giving Lowland tequilas a unique character.

One notable brand that showcases the flavors of the Lowland region is El Tesoro. Their tequilas are crafted using traditional methods, including stone ovens and tahona mills, which crush the agave to extract the juice. The El Tesoro Añejo, aged for four years in oak barrels, offers a deep and rich flavor with hints of roasted agave and spices.

In addition to the geographical differences, the production process also plays a crucial role in the final flavor profile of craft tequilas. From the harvesting and cooking of the agave to the fermentation and distillation, each step is meticulously executed to ensure the highest quality.

Craft tequila is gaining popularity among enthusiasts who appreciate the artistry and attention to detail that goes into its production. By exploring the diverse flavors of tequila from the Highland to the Lowland regions, one can truly appreciate the richness and complexity of this iconic Mexican spirit.

In conclusion, the world of craft tequila offers a fascinating journey through the Highland and Lowland regions of Mexico. From the sweet and floral notes of the Highland tequilas to the earthy and savory flavors of the Lowland tequilas, there is something for every palate. So, next time you raise a glass of tequila, take a moment to savor the craftsmanship and heritage behind this extraordinary spirit. Salud!
