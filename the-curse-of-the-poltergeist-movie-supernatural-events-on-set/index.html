The Curse of the Poltergeist Movie: Supernatural Events on Set


The Poltergeist franchise, a cinematic masterpiece of horror, has captivated audiences with its spine-tingling scares and haunting imagery. However, beyond the silver screen, the production of these films was shrouded in an eerie aura of supernatural occurrences that left an enduring mark on the cast and crew.

The Amityville Connection

The genesis of the Poltergeist saga lies in the infamous Amityville Horror house, where a series of chilling events had allegedly occurred in the 1970s. The producers of Poltergeist sought to tap into this notoriety by filming the first installment in the historic abode. However, the decision proved fateful as strange and unsettling incidents began to plague the set.

Crew members reported unexplained noises, flickering lights, and the sensation of being watched. One night, a camera operator claimed to have witnessed a ghostly figure in the window of the house. Director Tobe Hooper was so unnerved by these occurrences that he refused to return to the property and the production was relocated.

Tragic Loss and Untimely Deaths

As filming progressed, a tragic shadow fell over the production. Dominique Dunne, the young actress who played Dana Freeling, was brutally murdered by her ex-boyfriend shortly after filming wrapped. This heartbreaking event cast a pall over the cast and crew, and it is believed that Dunne’s spirit remained on the set, causing further disturbances.

In the years that followed, several other cast and crew members associated with the Poltergeist films met untimely deaths. Heather O’Rourke, who played Carol Anne Freeling, died at the age of 12 from septic shock. Will Sampson, who portrayed the medicine man Taylor, passed away from complications of a heart transplant. Julian Beck, who played the demonic Kane, succumbed to stomach cancer.

Recurring Nightmares and Psychological Trauma

Many of the actors who participated in the Poltergeist films experienced recurring nightmares and psychological trauma long after production ended. Craig T. Nelson, who played Steven Freeling, reported having terrifying dreams in which he was haunted by the ghost of Dominique Dunne. Zelda Rubinstein, who portrayed the psychic Tangina, claimed to have been plagued by demonic forces that attached themselves to her during filming.

Paranormal Investigations

In the wake of the tragic events surrounding the Poltergeist films, paranormal investigators have conducted numerous investigations at the various filming locations. They have reported capturing EVPs (electronic voice phenomena), witnessing unexplained apparitions, and experiencing intense feelings of dread and unease.

One particularly chilling investigation took place at the Cuesta Grade Road tunnel, where the infamous ‘face in the tree’ scene was filmed. Investigators claimed to have recorded the sound of a young girl’s voice whispering, ‘Help me.’ They also captured footage of a shadowy figure that appeared to be floating in the air.

The Curse of Poltergeist: Fact or Fiction?

Whether the supernatural occurrences associated with the Poltergeist films were genuine or merely the product of overactive imaginations remains a matter of debate. However, the sheer volume and consistency of the reported incidents suggest that there may have been something truly unsettling at play.

Some skeptics dismiss the events as elaborate hoaxes or coincidences. Others believe that the production team inadvertently opened a portal to the supernatural realm, unleashing a malevolent force that continues to haunt the legacy of the films.

A Haunting Legacy

The Poltergeist franchise has left an enduring mark on the horror genre and popular culture. Its chilling scares and haunting imagery have terrified generations of moviegoers. However, the supernatural events that plagued the production have also cast a long shadow over the films, creating a legend that continues to fascinate and disturb.

Whether one believes in the curse of Poltergeist or not, there is no denying the eerie aura that surrounds these films. They serve as a cautionary tale about the potential dangers of delving too deeply into the realm of the unknown.
