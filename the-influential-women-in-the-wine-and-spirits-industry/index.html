The Influential Women in the Wine and Spirits Industry


The wine and spirits industry has long been dominated by men, but over the years, there has been a significant rise in the influence and impact of women in this field. These women have not only shattered glass ceilings but have also played a pivotal role in shaping the industry and its trends. In this blog post, we will explore some of the influential women who have made their mark in the wine and spirits industry, revolutionizing the way we appreciate and consume these beverages.

1. Julia Jackson: As the proprietor of Jackson Family Wines, Julia Jackson has been instrumental in expanding the family’s wine empire. Her passion for wine and dedication to sustainable farming practices have earned her recognition as a leading figure in the industry. Julia is an advocate for empowering women in the wine industry and has launched initiatives to support women winemakers and vineyard managers.

2. Marianne Eaves: Marianne Eaves is the first female master distiller in Kentucky since Prohibition. Her expertise in crafting fine bourbons has garnered her accolades and respect from her peers. Marianne’s commitment to quality and innovation has pushed the boundaries of traditional bourbon production and has solidified her position as a trailblazer in the spirits industry.

3. Cathy Corison: Known as the ‘Cabernet Queen,’ Cathy Corison has been producing exceptional Napa Valley wines for over three decades. Her dedication to crafting elegant and balanced wines has earned her a loyal following. Cathy’s wines have consistently received critical acclaim, and her commitment to showcasing the unique terroir of Napa Valley has made her one of the most influential women in the wine industry.

4. Lynne Sherriff MW: Lynne Sherriff is a Master of Wine and one of the most respected wine judges in the world. Her expertise and palate have made her a sought-after judge at prestigious wine competitions. Lynne’s deep knowledge of wine regions and varietals has made her an authority in the industry, and she continues to inspire and educate wine enthusiasts around the globe.

5. Helen Turley: Helen Turley is a renowned winemaker and consultant who has played a pivotal role in shaping the modern California wine scene. Her commitment to crafting small-production, high-quality wines has earned her a cult following. Helen’s influence extends beyond winemaking, as she has mentored and inspired a new generation of winemakers who are pushing the boundaries of innovation and quality in the industry.

These are just a few examples of the influential women in the wine and spirits industry who have challenged norms, broken barriers, and left an indelible mark on the field. Their passion, expertise, and dedication have not only elevated the quality of wines and spirits but have also opened doors for aspiring women in the industry. As we celebrate their achievements, let us raise a glass to these trailblazers and toast to a future where gender equality in the wine and spirits industry becomes the norm.
