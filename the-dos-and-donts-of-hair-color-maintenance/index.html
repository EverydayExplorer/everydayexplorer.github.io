The Dos and Don'ts of Hair Color Maintenance


Maintaining vibrant and healthy hair color can be a challenge, especially if you’ve recently dyed your locks or have been sporting a new hue for a while. To help you navigate the world of hair color maintenance, we’ve put together a comprehensive list of dos and don’ts that will keep your color looking fresh and fabulous for as long as possible. Whether you’re a seasoned color chameleon or a newbie to the world of hair dye, these tips will ensure your color stays on point.

1. DO invest in color-safe products: Using hair care products specifically formulated for color-treated hair is crucial. These products are designed to protect your color from fading and can help prolong its vibrancy. Look for shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that are labeled ‘color-safe’ or ‘color-protecting’ to maintain your hair color’s longevity.

2. DON’T wash your hair too frequently: Washing your hair too often can strip away the natural oils that help lock in color. Aim to wash your hair every two to three days instead of daily. If you’re worried about greasiness between washes, try using dry shampoo to refresh your roots.

3. DO use cool water when rinsing: Hot water can open up the hair cuticle and cause color to fade more quickly. After shampooing and conditioning, rinse your hair with cool water to help seal the cuticle and lock in your color.

4. DON’T forget to use heat protectant: Heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons can cause damage to your hair and fade your color. Before using any heat styling tools, make sure to apply a heat protectant spray or serum to shield your hair from damage and color loss.

5. DO schedule regular touch-ups: Depending on the type of hair color you have, you may need to schedule regular touch-up appointments with your stylist. This will help maintain your color’s vibrancy and prevent any visible regrowth from showing. Consult with your stylist to determine the best frequency for touch-ups based on your hair color and desired look.

6. DON’T overexpose your hair to the sun: Sun exposure can cause your hair color to fade faster, especially if you have lighter shades. Whenever you’re spending extended periods outside, protect your hair by wearing a hat or using a UV-protecting spray to shield your locks from the sun’s harmful rays.

7. DO deep condition regularly: Coloring your hair can cause it to become dry and brittle. To combat this, incorporate regular deep conditioning treatments into your hair care routine. Look for deep conditioning masks or treatments specifically designed for color-treated hair to help restore moisture and maintain the health of your strands.

8. DON’T use harsh styling products: Certain styling products, like gels or hairsprays that contain alcohol, can strip away your hair color and leave it looking dull. Opt for styling products that are alcohol-free and specifically formulated for color-treated hair to avoid any unwanted color fading.

9. DO protect your hair color while swimming: Chlorine and saltwater can be harsh on colored hair, leading to fading and dryness. Before taking a dip, wet your hair with clean water and apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to create a protective barrier. Additionally, consider wearing a swim cap to minimize exposure to chlorine or saltwater.

10. DON’T DIY major color changes: While it can be tempting to experiment with different hair colors at home, major color changes are best left to professionals. They have the expertise to achieve the desired results while minimizing damage and maintaining the integrity of your hair. Consult with a professional colorist to discuss your desired color change and ensure it’s done safely and effectively.

By following these dos and don’ts of hair color maintenance, you’ll be able to enjoy vibrant and beautiful hair for longer periods. Remember, taking care of your colored tresses requires a little extra effort, but the results are definitely worth it. Embrace your hair color and flaunt your locks with confidence!
