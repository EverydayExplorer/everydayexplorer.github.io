Top 20 Dog Breeds for Apartment Living


Living in an apartment doesn’t mean you have to give up on the idea of having a furry friend as a companion. Many dog breeds are perfectly suited for apartment living, as they have low exercise requirements, are small in size, and have a calm temperament. In this blog post, we’ll go over the top 20 dog breeds that thrive in apartment settings, making them perfect choices for city dwellers.

1. French Bulldog: With their small size and easygoing nature, French Bulldogs are a popular choice for apartment living. They don’t require much exercise and are generally well-behaved indoors.

2. Cavalier King Charles Spaniel: These small and affectionate dogs are known for their friendly and adaptable nature, making them great companions for apartment living.

3. Bichon Frise: Bichon Frises are small, cheerful dogs that don’t shed much, which is a bonus for apartment dwellers. They adapt well to indoor living and have a playful and friendly temperament.

4. Boston Terrier: Boston Terriers are compact and low-maintenance dogs that adapt well to apartment living. They are known for their friendly and sociable personalities.

5. Shih Tzu: Shih Tzus are small and affectionate dogs that thrive in apartment environments. They are low-energy and enjoy being indoors, making them a great choice for apartment dwellers.

6. Pomeranian: Pomeranians may be small, but they have big personalities. Despite their fluffy appearance, they require minimal exercise and are well-suited for apartment living.

7. Maltese: Maltese dogs are small and easy to manage, making them perfect for apartment living. They are gentle and affectionate, and their hypoallergenic coat is a plus for those with allergies.

8. Pug: Pugs are small and sturdy dogs that are well-suited for apartment living. They are low-energy and adapt well to indoor environments.

9. Yorkshire Terrier: Yorkshire Terriers, or Yorkies, are small and adaptable dogs that make great apartment companions. They are easy to train and don’t require much exercise.

10. Dachshund: Dachshunds may have a long body, but they don’t need a lot of space to roam. They are active and playful, but their exercise needs can be easily met in an apartment setting.

11. Corgi: While Corgis are known for their herding instincts, they can adapt well to apartment living if their exercise needs are met. Their small size makes them suitable for smaller living spaces.

12. Chihuahua: Chihuahuas are some of the smallest dog breeds, which makes them ideal for apartment living. They are loyal and affectionate, and their exercise requirements can easily be met indoors.

13. Shetland Sheepdog: Shetland Sheepdogs, or Shelties, may be larger than other breeds on this list, but they are well-suited for apartment living due to their adaptable nature and moderate exercise needs.

14. Pembroke Welsh Corgi: Like their larger counterparts, Pembroke Welsh Corgis can adapt well to apartment living as long as they get enough exercise. They are intelligent and easy to train.

15. Cocker Spaniel: Cocker Spaniels are medium-sized dogs, but they are well-suited for apartment living due to their calm temperament and low exercise requirements.

16. Chinese Crested: Chinese Cresteds are hairless or low-shedding dogs that are excellent choices for apartment dwellers with allergies. They are affectionate and don’t require much space.

17. Toy Poodle: Toy Poodles are intelligent and adaptable dogs that make great companions for apartment living. They are low-energy and don’t require a lot of exercise.

18. Italian Greyhound: Italian Greyhounds are graceful and elegant dogs that adapt well to apartment environments. Despite their slender appearance, they don’t require much exercise.

19. Pekingese: Pekingese dogs are small and low-maintenance, which makes them ideal for apartment living. They are affectionate and adapt well to indoor environments.

20. English Bulldog: Although English Bulldogs may be larger in size compared to other breeds on this list, they are well-suited for apartment living due to their low exercise needs and calm personality.

When selecting a dog breed for apartment living, it’s important to consider their size, exercise requirements, and temperament. These 20 dog breeds are known for their compatibility with apartment living, making them great choices for those living in smaller spaces.

Remember, though, that every dog is an individual, and their behavior can vary depending on their upbringing and training. Regardless of the breed, it’s crucial to provide your furry friend with regular exercise, mental stimulation, and love to ensure they are happy and content in their apartment home.

Now that you have a list of the top 20 dog breeds for apartment living, you can make an informed decision and find the perfect companion to share your apartment life with.
