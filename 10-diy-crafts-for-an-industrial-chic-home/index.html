10 DIY Crafts for an Industrial-Chic Home


Are you tired of the same old home decor styles and looking to add some unique flair to your living space? Why not try incorporating the industrial-chic trend into your home? Industrial-chic is a popular style that combines the raw, unfinished look of industrial elements with the warmth and comfort of modern decor. In this blog post, we will explore 10 DIY crafts that can help you achieve an industrial-chic look for your home, without breaking the bank.

1. Pipe Shelves

One of the easiest ways to add an industrial touch to your home is by installing pipe shelves. All you need is some black iron pipes, flanges, and wooden planks. Simply assemble the pipes and flanges, attach them to the wall, and place the wooden planks on top. These shelves are not only functional but also add a unique industrial vibe to any room.

2. Concrete Planters

Incorporate the industrial aesthetic into your garden or indoor plants by making concrete planters. Mix concrete and pour it into molds of various shapes and sizes. Once the concrete sets, remove the molds, and you’ll have stylish planters that perfectly complement the industrial-chic theme.

3. Metal Wall Art

Metal wall art can instantly give your space an industrial feel. Find some scrap metal or metal sheets and cut out your desired shapes, such as gears, arrows, or abstract designs. Then, hang them on the wall for a striking focal point.

4. Upcycled Pallet Furniture

Pallets are versatile and can be transformed into unique furniture pieces. Sand down the pallets, paint or stain them in a rustic finish, and add some cushions for comfort. You can create coffee tables, shelves, or even bed frames with a touch of industrial style.

5. Edison Bulb Chandelier

Nothing says industrial-chic more than an Edison bulb chandelier. Purchase some vintage-style Edison bulbs and hang them from a sturdy wooden or metal base. This DIY craft will create a warm and inviting atmosphere with a touch of vintage industrial charm.

6. Steampunk Bookends

Give your bookshelf a steampunk twist with these DIY bookends. Find old gears, clock parts, or other metal objects and glue them onto wooden blocks. Paint the blocks in metallic colors for an authentic steampunk look. These bookends will surely be a conversation starter.

7. Industrial Pipe Curtain Rods

Upgrade your curtains with industrial pipe curtain rods. Simply choose the desired length and width of the pipes, then assemble and mount them on your wall. This unique alternative to traditional curtain rods will add an edgy industrial vibe to any window treatment.

8. Concrete Coasters

Create custom-made coasters with a touch of industrial elegance. Mix concrete and pour it into molds. Once dry, seal the coasters and add felt pads to protect your surfaces. These coasters will not only protect your furniture but also double as decorative pieces.

9. Repurposed Vintage Suitcase Side Table

Turn an old, vintage suitcase into a stylish side table. Remove the lid, add four legs, and secure them to the bottom of the suitcase. This repurposed piece will add a touch of nostalgia and industrial chic to your living room or bedroom.

10. Galvanized Pipe Paper Towel Holder

Ditch your ordinary paper towel holder and make a statement with a galvanized pipe version. Purchase the necessary pipe fittings and assemble them into a unique paper towel holder design. This simple DIY craft will add an industrial touch to your kitchen or bathroom.

In conclusion, incorporating industrial-chic elements into your home decor doesn’t have to be complicated or expensive. With these 10 DIY crafts, you can easily add an industrial edge to your living space. From pipe shelves to concrete planters, each project offers a unique and stylish way to embrace the industrial-chic trend. So, unleash your creativity, roll up your sleeves, and start transforming your home into an industrial-chic haven.
