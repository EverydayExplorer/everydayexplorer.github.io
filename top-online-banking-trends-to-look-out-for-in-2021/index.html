Top Online Banking Trends to Look Out for in 2021


With the advancements in technology and the ever-growing popularity of digital banking, online banking has become an integral part of our lives. From convenience to security, online banking offers a range of benefits that have revolutionized the way we manage our finances. As we enter 2021, online banking is expected to continue its upward trajectory with several emerging trends that are set to shape the industry. In this blog post, we will explore the top online banking trends to look out for in 2021.

1. Mobile Banking Dominance:
Mobile banking has been on the rise for the past few years, and this trend shows no signs of slowing down. With the increasing adoption of smartphones and the ease of use they provide, more and more people are turning to mobile banking as their primary means of managing their finances. In 2021, we can expect to see even more features and functionalities added to mobile banking apps, making it easier for users to access their accounts, transfer funds, pay bills, and even apply for loans, all from the comfort of their smartphones.

2. Contactless Payments:
The global pandemic has accelerated the adoption of contactless payments, and this trend is expected to continue in 2021. With health and safety concerns driving consumer behavior, contactless payments offer a convenient and hygienic solution for transactions. Online banking platforms are likely to enhance their contactless payment capabilities by incorporating new technologies such as near-field communication (NFC) and biometrics, further simplifying the payment process and ensuring a seamless user experience.

3. Enhanced Security Measures:
As digital banking continues to gain popularity, safety and security remain a top concern for both banks and users. In response to growing cyber threats, online banking platforms are continuously investing in advanced security measures. In 2021, we can expect to see the implementation of robust authentication methods such as biometrics (fingerprint, face recognition), multi-factor authentication, and behavioral analytics. These technologies will ensure that only authorized individuals can access and transact on their online banking platforms, providing users with peace of mind and elevating the overall security of digital banking.

4. Personalized Banking Services:
With the vast amount of data available today, banks are now better equipped to provide personalized banking services tailored to the individual needs of their customers. Through advanced analytics and AI algorithms, banks can analyze customer behavior and offer customized financial products, advice, and recommendations. In 2021, we can expect to see more banks leveraging these technologies to provide hyper-personalized experiences for their customers, ultimately deepening customer loyalty and engagement.

5. Open Banking and API Integration:
Open banking is a concept that enables banks to share customer data with third-party financial service providers through the use of APIs (Application Programming Interfaces). This allows for the development of innovative financial applications and services that can enrich the overall banking experience. In 2021, we can expect to see an increased focus on open banking, with more banks embracing API integrations to offer their customers a broader range of financial products and services, such as loan comparisons, budgeting apps, and investment tools.

6. Voice Banking:
Voice assistants such as Amazon’s Alexa, Google Assistant, and Apple’s Siri have become commonplace in our daily lives. With the increasing use of voice-enabled devices, voice banking is expected to gain prominence in 2021. Voice banking allows customers to perform a range of banking tasks using voice commands, including checking account balances, transferring funds, and making payments. This convenient and hands-free banking experience is set to become more prevalent as banks integrate voice recognition technologies into their online platforms.

7. Digital Identity Verification:
Traditional methods of identity verification, such as passwords and security questions, are no longer secure enough to protect against identity theft and fraud. In 2021, we can expect to see the widespread adoption of digital identity verification technologies, such as biometrics and blockchain, which offer a higher level of security and trust. Digital identity verification will not only enhance security but also streamline the onboarding process for new customers, making it easier and faster to open online bank accounts.

In conclusion, the online banking landscape is evolving rapidly, driven by advancements in technology and changing consumer preferences. In 2021, we can expect to see a continued dominance of mobile banking, an increased focus on security, and the adoption of emerging technologies such as contactless payments, voice banking, and digital identity verification. These trends will undoubtedly shape the future of online banking, providing users with convenient, secure, and personalized banking experiences. As customers, it is important for us to stay informed about these trends and leverage them to make the most of our online banking experiences.
