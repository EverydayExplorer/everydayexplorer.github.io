Survival Skills for Urban Explorers: Navigating Abandoned Spaces


Urban exploration, or urbex, has gained popularity in recent years as an exciting way to discover hidden corners of cities and experience the thrill of exploring abandoned spaces. From old factories and hospitals to derelict train stations and forgotten underground tunnels, urban explorers set out to unravel the stories of these forgotten places. But venturing into abandoned spaces comes with a set of challenges and risks that should not be underestimated. In this blog post, we will discuss essential survival skills to ensure a safe and successful urban exploration experience.

1. Research and Preparation

Before embarking on an urbex adventure, thorough research and preparation are crucial. Identify the specific location you wish to explore and learn about its history, potential hazards, and legal restrictions. Research can provide valuable insights into the layout of the site, any dangers to be aware of, and even stories or rumors surrounding the place. It’s also essential to check if permission is required to access the location to avoid legal issues.

2. Safety Equipment

When exploring abandoned spaces, personal safety should always be a top priority. Make sure to pack the necessary safety equipment before heading out. Some essential items include:

  • Protective Clothing: Wear durable clothing that covers your body, including long sleeves, pants, and sturdy closed-toe shoes. This will protect you from sharp objects, broken glass, or potential exposure to harmful substances.
  • Headlamp: A reliable headlamp is essential for navigating dark areas and tunnels, leaving your hands free to explore. Remember to bring extra batteries or a backup light source in case the batteries run out.
  • Protective Gloves: Thick gloves will shield your hands from potential injuries, especially when dealing with rusty metals, broken glass, or hazardous materials.
  • Respirator or Dust Mask: Abandoned spaces often have poor air quality due to mold, dust, or chemical pollutants. Protect your lungs by wearing a respirator or dust mask.
  • First Aid Kit: Accidents can happen, so it’s crucial to have a well-stocked first aid kit on hand. Bandages, antiseptic wipes, pain relievers, and any necessary medication should be included.

3. Navigation Tools

Abandoned spaces can be complex and disorienting, making navigation challenging. Be sure to bring a few tools to help you find your way:

  • Map and Compass: Carry a reliable map of the location and a compass to orient yourself. GPS signals may not always be reliable in enclosed spaces.
  • Markers or Chalk: Marking your path along the way can help you avoid getting lost. Use colorful markers or chalk to leave a trail that is visible and easy to follow.

4. Group Safety

Exploring abandoned spaces alone can be risky, so it’s wise to go with a reliable group of fellow urban explorers. A group can provide extra support, share knowledge, and help in case of an emergency. Safety in numbers is especially crucial when navigating unfamiliar and potentially dangerous environments.

5. Situational Awareness

Maintaining situational awareness is key when exploring abandoned spaces. Always be alert and observant of your surroundings. Look out for structural instability, loose floors, weak ceilings, or any signs of recent collapse. Avoid potentially dangerous areas such as rooftops, unsafe staircases, or areas with visible signs of decay.

6. Respect for the Environment

Urban exploration can be a fascinating way to appreciate forgotten history, but it’s essential to explore responsibly and show respect for the environment. Do not vandalize or damage any property, and take care not to disturb any artifacts or sentimental items that might be left behind. Leave the site as you found it, ensuring that future visitors can also appreciate its beauty and history.

7. Legal Considerations

While urban exploration can be an exhilarating adventure, it’s crucial to respect the law and any associated restrictions. Some abandoned spaces are on private property or protected by law, and gaining access without permission could result in legal consequences. Always check local regulations and seek proper authorization if necessary.

8. Trust Your Instincts and Be Prepared for the Unexpected

Finally, trust your instincts. If something feels unsafe or if your intuition tells you to retreat, listen to it. Always have an emergency plan in place and inform someone of your exploration plans, including the location and estimated duration of your exploration. Carry a fully charged cell phone in case of emergencies and familiarize yourself with emergency exit routes.

Urban exploration can be a thrilling and rewarding experience for those with a sense of adventure. By following these survival skills and safety guidelines, you can enhance your urban exploring experience while minimizing risks and ensuring your wellbeing. Remember, preparation and caution are key. So go out and discover the hidden treasures of abandoned spaces, but do so with respect, responsibility, and safety in mind.
