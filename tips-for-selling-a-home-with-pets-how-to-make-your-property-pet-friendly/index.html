Tips for Selling a Home with Pets: How to Make Your Property Pet-Friendly


When it comes to selling your home, there are many factors to consider in order to attract potential buyers. One often overlooked aspect is how pet-friendly your property is. With a significant portion of the population owning pets, making your home appealing to pet owners can greatly increase your chances of selling quickly and at a good price. In this guide, we’ll explore some tips for selling a home with pets and making your property more attractive to both pet owners and non-pet owners alike.

  1. Deep Clean Your Home: Before putting your home on the market, it’s essential to give it a thorough cleaning, especially if you have pets. Pet odors and hair can be off-putting to potential buyers. Consider hiring a professional cleaning service that specializes in removing pet odors to ensure your home smells fresh and clean.

  2. Repair Any Pet Damage: Pets, especially dogs, can sometimes cause damage to floors, walls, and furniture. Take the time to repair any damage caused by your pets, such as scratches on hardwood floors or chew marks on baseboards. Investing in these repairs upfront can help increase the value of your home and prevent potential buyers from being deterred by visible damage.

  3. Create a Pet-Friendly Outdoor Space: If you have a backyard or outdoor space, consider making it more pet-friendly. Install a fence to provide a safe area for pets to roam, and add features like a dog run or pet-friendly landscaping. A well-maintained outdoor space can be a major selling point for pet owners looking for a home that accommodates their furry friends.

  4. Highlight Pet-Friendly Features: When marketing your home, be sure to highlight any pet-friendly features it may have. This could include built-in pet doors, a designated pet washing station, or easy-to-clean flooring materials like laminate or tile. Including these features in your listing can attract pet owners who are specifically looking for homes that cater to their pets’ needs.

  5. Remove Pet Items During Showings: While it’s important to make your home pet-friendly, it’s also important to temporarily remove any visible signs of pets during showings. Put away food and water bowls, litter boxes, and pet toys to create a clean and clutter-free environment for potential buyers. This allows them to envision themselves living in the space without being distracted by pet-related items.

  6. Be Transparent About Pets: If you currently have pets living in your home, be upfront about it with potential buyers. Disclose any information about the type and number of pets you have, as well as any related issues such as barking or allergies. Honesty is key when selling a home, and being transparent about your pets can help build trust with potential buyers.

  7. Consider Professional Staging: Hiring a professional stager can help showcase your home in the best possible light, including making it more appealing to pet owners. A stager can rearrange furniture, add decorative touches, and create a welcoming atmosphere that appeals to a wide range of buyers, including those with pets.

  8. Offer Pet-Friendly Incentives: To sweeten the deal for pet owners, consider offering incentives such as covering pet deposit fees or including pet-related amenities like a doggy door or fenced-in yard. These incentives can help set your home apart from others on the market and attract more potential buyers.

  9. Provide Information About Nearby Pet Amenities: Highlight any nearby pet-friendly amenities in your listing, such as dog parks, walking trails, or pet-friendly restaurants and cafes. This information can be especially appealing to pet owners who want to ensure their furry friends will have plenty of opportunities for exercise and socialization in the area.

  10. Work With a Pet-Friendly Realtor: Finally, consider working with a real estate agent who understands the importance of marketing to pet owners. A pet-friendly realtor will know how to effectively showcase your home’s pet-friendly features and attract buyers who are looking for a home that accommodates their pets’ needs.

By following these tips, you can make your home more attractive to pet owners and increase your chances of selling quickly and at a good price. With a little extra effort, you can turn your property into a haven for both humans and their four-legged friends.
