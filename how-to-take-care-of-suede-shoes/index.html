How to Take Care of Suede Shoes


Suede shoes are trendy and fashionable, lending a touch of sophistication to any outfit. However, they require special care to maintain their luxurious texture and appearance. With the right care and maintenance routine, you can keep your suede shoes looking fabulous for years to come. In this blog post, we will guide you through the essential steps on how to take care of your suede shoes and keep them looking as good as new.

Step 1: Protecting your Suede Shoes

Before you even step out of the house wearing your new pair of suede shoes, it’s crucial to protect them from potential damage. Suede is highly prone to staining, dirt, and water damage. Therefore, applying a protective spray designed specifically for suede shoes is essential. This spray forms a protective barrier that repels water and helps prevent stains from setting in.

To apply the protective spray, follow these steps:

  1. Ensure your shoes are clean and dry.
  2. Shake the can well before use.
  3. Hold the spray approximately 6 inches away from the shoes.
  4. Apply an even coat, covering the entire shoe surface.
  5. Allow the spray to dry for the recommended time.

By applying a protective spray, you create a barrier against stains and water, increasing the longevity of your suede shoes.

Step 2: Regular Cleaning

Regular cleaning is crucial to maintain the appearance of your suede shoes. Neglecting this step can result in a buildup of dirt and stains, which can be challenging to remove later.

Here is a simple cleaning routine for your suede shoes:

  1. Purchase a suede brush or a suede eraser.
  2. Brush your shoes gently to remove any loose dirt or debris.
  3. For stubborn stains, use a suede eraser to gently rub the affected area.
  4. Brush the shoes again to restore the suede’s original texture.
  5. For grease stains, place a small amount of cornstarch or talcum powder on the stain and leave it overnight. In the morning, brush away the residue.

Remember to brush your shoes in one particular direction, following the natural grain of the suede, to prevent damaging the material.

Step 3: Treating Stains

Stains are inevitable when it comes to suede shoes. However, with the right technique and products, you can effectively treat most common stains.

Here’s how to treat different types of stains on suede shoes:

  1. Oil and grease stains: Use a suede brush or an eraser to try and lift the stain gently. If the stain persists, consider using a specialized suede cleaner.
  2. Water stains: Dampen the entire shoe, carefully using a clean cloth or sponge. Avoid saturating the suede. Allow the shoes to dry naturally, away from direct heat sources. Once dry, brush the shoes to restore the suede’s texture.
  3. Ink stains: Use a suede cleaner or a specialized suede stain remover. Follow the product instructions carefully and avoid excessive rubbing, as it may cause discoloration.

Remember, quick action is the key when it comes to treating stains on suede shoes. The sooner you tackle a stain, the higher the chances of successful removal.

Step 4: Conditioning and Restoring Suede

Suede shoes require regular conditioning to maintain their softness and prevent them from drying out or cracking. Conditioning also helps to restore the natural oils of the suede, keeping it supple and preventing it from becoming stiff.

To condition and restore your suede shoes:

  1. Purchase a suede conditioner or a specialized suede oil.
  2. Apply a small amount to a clean, soft cloth.
  3. Gently rub the conditioner into the suede, focusing on areas that appear dull or dry.
  4. Allow the shoes to air dry naturally.
  5. Once dry, brush the shoes to restore the suede’s texture.

Regular conditioning will keep your suede shoes looking their best and help extend their lifespan.

Step 5: Proper Storage

Proper storage of suede shoes is crucial to prevent damage and maintain their shape. Here are some tips for storing your suede shoes:

  1. Clean your shoes thoroughly before storing them to remove any dirt or debris.
  2. Stuff the shoes with acid-free tissue paper to help them maintain their shape.
  3. Store the shoes in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat sources.
  4. Avoid storing suede shoes in plastic bags, as they can cause the material to become damp and moldy.
  5. If possible, store suede shoes in a breathable shoe bag or a shoebox with ventilation holes.

Following these storage tips will ensure that your suede shoes remain in excellent condition, ready to be worn whenever you desire.

Conclusion

Caring for suede shoes might seem demanding, but the effort is well worth it. By following the steps outlined in this blog post, you can ensure that your suede shoes remain a fashionable and elegant addition to your wardrobe. Remember to protect them, clean them regularly, treat stains promptly, condition and restore them when needed, and store them properly. With a little extra care, your suede shoes will continue to turn heads and make a lasting impression wherever you go.
