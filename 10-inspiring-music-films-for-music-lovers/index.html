10 Inspiring Music Films for Music Lovers


Music has the power to move us, to inspire us, and to bring us together. For music lovers, there is nothing quite like immersing ourselves in the stories and emotions that music can evoke. Film has long been a medium for sharing these stories, and over the years, numerous films have captured the essence of music in ways that resonate with audiences. In this blog post, we will explore 10 inspiring music films that every music lover should watch. These films span genres, time periods, and styles, but they all share one common thread: their ability to inspire and move us through the power of music.

1. Amadeus (1984)

Based on the life of Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, Amadeus is a captivating film that delves into the world of classical music in 18th-century Vienna. The story follows the life of Mozart, played by Tom Hulce, as he navigates the complexities of court life and the competitive world of composers. The film is a testament to the power of music to transcend social boundaries and bring people together, regardless of their backgrounds.

2. La La Land (2016)

A modern-day musical that has captured the hearts of audiences around the world, La La Land tells the story of an aspiring actress and a jazz pianist who fall in love while pursuing their dreams in Los Angeles. The film’s enchanting score, featuring songs like ‘City of Stars’ and ‘Audition (The Fools Who Dream),’ has won the hearts of music lovers and earned the film numerous awards, including a record-tying 14 Academy Award nominations.

3. 20 Feet from Stardom (2013)

This inspiring documentary explores the world of backup singers, who have long played a crucial role in shaping the sound of some of the most iconic songs in music history. Featuring interviews with legendary backup singers like Darlene Love, Merry Clayton, and Lisa Fischer, the film showcases the immense talent and dedication of these often unsung heroes of the music industry.

4. Whiplash (2014)

A powerful drama about the world of competitive jazz, Whiplash tells the story of a young drummer, played by Miles Teller, who is pushed to his limits by his demanding conductor, portrayed by J.K. Simmons. The film’s intense portrayal of the pursuit of musical perfection will leave audiences both inspired and questioning the true cost of success.

5. The Beatles: Eight Days a Week - The Touring Years (2016)

This critically acclaimed documentary offers an in-depth look at the Beatles’ early years as they toured the world, captivating audiences with their unique blend of music and charisma. Featuring never-before-seen footage and interviews with the band members, the film is a must-watch for any Beatles fan or music lover looking to gain a deeper understanding of the band’s impact on the world of music.

6. Selma (2014)

Though not a traditional music film, Selma’s powerful soundtrack, featuring songs like ‘Glory’ by John Legend and Common, plays a crucial role in the film’s portrayal of the civil rights movement in the 1960s. The film’s use of music to evoke the emotions and struggles of the time is a testament to the power of music as a tool for social change.

7. The Sound of Music (1965)

A classic musical that has captivated audiences for decades, The Sound of Music tells the inspiring true story of Maria, a young woman who becomes a governess for a large family in Nazi-occupied Austria. The film’s unforgettable songs, including ‘Do-Re-Mi’ and ‘My Favorite Things,’ have become timeless classics that continue to inspire and entertain generations of music lovers.

8. Inside Llewyn Davis (2013)

Set in the Greenwich Village folk scene of the early 1960s, Inside Llewyn Davis follows the story of a struggling folk singer, played by Oscar Isaac, as he navigates the competitive world of music. The film’s soundtrack, featuring original songs written by T Bone Burnett, is a tribute to the golden age of folk music and is sure to resonate with music lovers of all genres.
