The Dos and Don'ts of Wearing Ruffles


Ruffles have long been a fashion statement, adding a touch of femininity and playfulness to any outfit. However, mastering the art of wearing ruffles can be a bit tricky. From choosing the right style to pairing it with the perfect accessories, there are certain dos and don’ts that every fashionista should keep in mind. In this blog post, we will explore the dos and don’ts of wearing ruffles, helping you rock this trend with confidence and style.

DO choose the right size and placement of ruffles. Ruffles come in various sizes and can be placed strategically on different parts of a garment. If you have a smaller frame, opt for smaller ruffles to avoid overwhelming your silhouette. On the other hand, if you have a taller or curvier figure, larger ruffles can help create balance and add dimension. Experiment with different placements, such as ruffled sleeves or a ruffled hemline, to find the look that flatters your body shape the most.

DO pair ruffles with simpler pieces. When wearing ruffles, it’s important to let them take center stage. Avoid overwhelming your outfit by pairing ruffled pieces with simpler, more streamlined garments. For example, a ruffled blouse can be paired with tailored trousers or a pencil skirt, while a ruffled dress can be complemented with minimal accessories. This creates a balanced and cohesive look that allows the ruffles to shine.

DO play with textures and patterns. Ruffles add texture and movement to an outfit, making them the perfect element to mix and match with other textures and patterns. Experiment with combining ruffles with lace, silk, or even leather for a bold and unique look. Similarly, don’t be afraid to mix different patterns, such as pairing a striped ruffled top with a floral skirt. Just ensure that the colors complement each other to avoid overwhelming the eye.

DON’T overdo it with ruffles. While ruffles can be a fun and playful addition to any outfit, it’s important to know when to stop. Too many ruffles can make your look appear overly busy and chaotic. Instead, choose one statement ruffled piece and keep the rest of your outfit simple. This allows the ruffles to make a statement without overpowering your overall look.

DON’T forget about proportion. Proportion is key when wearing ruffles. If you’re opting for a voluminous ruffled blouse, balance it out with a fitted bottom like skinny jeans or a pencil skirt. On the other hand, if you’re wearing a ruffled skirt, pair it with a more fitted top to create a well-balanced silhouette. Paying attention to proportion ensures that your outfit looks intentional and put together.

DON’T be afraid to experiment. Fashion is all about self-expression, so don’t be afraid to experiment with ruffles in different ways. Mix and match different styles, colors, and textures to create your own unique look. Whether you prefer a romantic, bohemian, or edgy style, there are endless possibilities when it comes to incorporating ruffles into your wardrobe.

In conclusion, wearing ruffles can be a fun and stylish way to elevate your outfit. By following these dos and don’ts, you can confidently embrace this trend and make a fashion statement that is uniquely you. Remember to choose the right size and placement of ruffles, pair them with simpler pieces, play with textures and patterns, avoid overdoing it, pay attention to proportion, and most importantly, have fun experimenting with different ruffled styles. Happy ruffling!
