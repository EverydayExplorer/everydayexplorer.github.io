Tips for Safe Hiking in Bear Country


When venturing into bear country, it’s essential to take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable hiking experience. With proper preparation and knowledge, you can minimize the risk of encountering bears and reduce the likelihood of an attack.

Before You Head Out

Before embarking on your hike, research the area you plan to visit and learn about the types of bears that inhabit the region. Familiarize yourself with their behavior, habitat, and any specific regulations or guidelines in place.

Packing Essentials

When packing for your trip, make sure to include the following essential items:

  • Bear spray: a powerful deterrent that can help protect you from aggressive bears
  • Food storage containers: keep food and trash in airtight containers to prevent attracting bears
  • Bear-resistant bags: use these bags to store food and trash when camping
  • Noise-making devices: bells, whistles, or other noise-making devices can help alert bears to your presence
  • First aid kit: a basic kit should include items such as bandages, antiseptic wipes, and pain relievers

Hiking Techniques

When hiking in bear country, it’s crucial to adopt certain techniques to minimize the risk of encountering bears:

  • Make noise: as you hike, make noise by talking, singing, or wearing bear bells to alert bears of your presence
  • Stay alert: keep an eye out for signs of bears, such as tracks, scat, or digging
  • Avoid attractants: avoid areas with known bear activity, and keep a safe distance from any attractants like food, trash, or pet food
  • Hike in groups: hiking with a group can help deter bears from approaching you
  • Avoid hiking at dawn, dusk, or night: these are the times when bears are most active

What to Do in a Bear Encounter

In the unlikely event of a bear encounter, it’s essential to remain calm and follow proper protocol:

  • Stay calm: keep your voice low and speak calmly to avoid panicking the bear
  • Give the bear space: back away slowly and give the bear a clear path to leave the area
  • Avoid eye contact: looking a bear in the eye can be perceived as a challenge or threat
  • Do not run: running can trigger a bear’s chase instinct, so it’s essential to stay calm and move slowly
  • Use bear spray: if the bear approaches you and you’re within spraying distance, use your bear spray

Camping and Food Storage

When camping in bear country, it’s crucial to store food and trash properly to prevent attracting bears:

  • Store food and trash in airtight containers: keep food and trash in containers that are airtight and odor-proof
  • Use bear-resistant bags: use bear-resistant bags to store food and trash when camping
  • Keep a clean campsite: keep your campsite clean and free of food and trash to avoid attracting bears

What to Do in Case of a Bear Attack

In the rare event of a bear attack, it’s essential to know what to do to minimize the risk of injury or fatality:

  • Play dead: if a bear attacks, play dead by curling into a ball on your side, with your knees tucked into your chest and your hands over your neck and ears
  • Use bear spray: if a bear attacks and you’re within spraying distance, use your bear spray
  • Protect your vital organs: if a bear attacks, use any available objects to protect your vital organs, such as your head, neck, or chest

By following these tips and taking necessary precautions, you can minimize the risk of encountering bears and ensure a safe and enjoyable hiking experience in bear country. Remember to always be prepared, stay alert, and know what to do in case of a bear encounter or attack.
