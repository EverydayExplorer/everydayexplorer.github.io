Turkey Hunting: Calling, Decoying, and Other Effective Techniques


Turkey hunting is a thrilling and challenging sport that requires skill, patience, and knowledge of effective techniques. Whether you’re a seasoned hunter or a beginner, mastering the art of calling and decoying can greatly enhance your chances of a successful hunt. In this blog post, we will explore some tried and tested techniques that will help you become a more effective turkey hunter.

Calling is an essential aspect of turkey hunting, as it allows you to communicate with the birds and lure them within range. There are various types of turkey calls, each producing distinct sounds that imitate different turkey vocalizations. The most common types of turkey calls include box calls, slate calls, diaphragm calls, and locator calls.

Box calls are often recommended for beginners, as they are easy to use and produce realistic sounds. They consist of a wooden box and a paddle that is moved across the box to create turkey sounds. Slate calls, on the other hand, are made of a slate or glass surface and a striker. By dragging the striker across the surface, you can produce a variety of turkey calls, from soft purrs to loud yelps.

Diaphragm calls, also known as mouth calls, require more practice to master but offer the advantage of hands-free operation. They consist of a latex or rubber reed that is placed in the roof of the mouth. By manipulating your tongue and airflow, you can create a range of turkey sounds. Diaphragm calls are ideal for creating realistic hen calls, which can entice gobblers to come closer.

Locator calls are used to locate turkeys before the hunt begins. They imitate the sounds of other animals, such as owls, crows, or coyotes, which can trigger a turkey’s gobble response. By listening for gobbling sounds in response to your locator call, you can identify the general location of the turkeys and plan your approach accordingly.

While calling is an effective technique, combining it with decoying can significantly increase your chances of success. Decoys mimic the appearance of real turkeys and can attract both dominant gobblers and curious hens. There are various types of turkey decoys available, including hen decoys, jake decoys, and tom decoys.

Hen decoys are the most commonly used decoys, as they can attract gobblers looking for companionship. They should be positioned in a way that makes them visible to approaching turkeys, while leaving enough space for the hunter to take a shot. Jake decoys, which represent young male turkeys, can be used to provoke a dominant gobbler’s territorial instincts, making him more likely to approach. Tom decoys, which represent mature gobblers, can be effective during the breeding season, as they can attract both hens and rival gobblers.

In addition to calling and decoying, there are other effective techniques that can improve your turkey hunting success. One such technique is scouting, which involves locating turkey roosts, feeding areas, and travel routes before the hunt. This allows you to set up in strategic positions and increases your chances of encountering turkeys.

Another technique is using natural cover to your advantage. Turkeys have excellent eyesight and can easily detect movement, so it’s important to blend into your surroundings. Wear camouflage clothing that matches the environment and use natural cover, such as trees or bushes, to break up your silhouette.

Lastly, patience is key in turkey hunting. Turkeys can be wary and may take their time approaching your calls or decoys. Stay still, remain hidden, and be prepared for a shot when the opportunity presents itself.

In conclusion, turkey hunting is a thrilling and rewarding sport that requires skill, patience, and knowledge of effective techniques. Mastering the art of calling, decoying, and other techniques can greatly increase your chances of a successful hunt. Remember to practice your calls, choose the right decoys, scout the area, blend into your surroundings, and be patient. Happy hunting!
