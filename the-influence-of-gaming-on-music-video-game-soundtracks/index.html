The Influence of Gaming on Music: Video Game Soundtracks


Video games have become a cultural phenomenon, captivating millions of players around the world. From the immersive graphics to the thrilling gameplay, gaming has evolved into a multi-billion dollar industry. But there’s one aspect of gaming that often goes unnoticed - its influence on music, specifically video game soundtracks.

Video game soundtracks have come a long way since the days of simple bleeps and bloops. Today, they are intricate compositions that enhance the gaming experience and evoke emotions in players. These soundtracks have become an integral part of the gaming industry, with some even rivaling the popularity of mainstream music.

One of the most notable influences of gaming on music is the rise of the video game composer. These talented individuals are responsible for creating the captivating melodies and memorable tunes that accompany gamers on their virtual adventures. With their expertise in both music theory and technology, video game composers have pushed the boundaries of what is possible in sound design.

The influence of gaming on music can be seen in various genres. From orchestral scores to electronic beats, video game soundtracks encompass a wide range of musical styles. Take, for example, the iconic soundtrack of ‘The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time,’ composed by Koji Kondo. The melodic and adventurous tunes of this game have become synonymous with the franchise and have even been performed by orchestras around the world.

Another example is the critically acclaimed ‘Final Fantasy’ series, known for its epic and emotionally charged soundtracks composed by Nobuo Uematsu. These compositions have transcended the gaming world and have gained recognition as standalone pieces of music. Uematsu’s work on the series has even been performed in concert halls, further cementing the influence of gaming on the music industry.

But it’s not just the composition of video game soundtracks that has influenced music. The technological advancements in gaming have also played a significant role. With the advent of more powerful consoles and gaming platforms, developers now have the ability to create immersive and realistic audio experiences. This has led to collaborations between video game composers and mainstream musicians, resulting in unique and innovative soundtracks.

One such collaboration is the partnership between Swedish DJ Avicii and the video game ‘Invector.’ Avicii worked closely with the game’s developers to create an electrifying soundtrack that perfectly complements the fast-paced gameplay. This collaboration not only introduced Avicii’s music to a new audience but also showcased the potential for video game soundtracks to break into the mainstream music scene.

The influence of gaming on music extends beyond just the composition and technology. It has also shaped the way we consume and discover music. With platforms like Spotify and Apple Music, gamers can now easily access and stream their favorite video game soundtracks. This has opened up a whole new avenue for video game composers to gain recognition and for gamers to explore new musical genres.

In conclusion, the influence of gaming on music, particularly video game soundtracks, cannot be underestimated. From the rise of video game composers to the collaborations between gaming and mainstream musicians, gaming has revolutionized the way we experience and appreciate music. So, the next time you find yourself immersed in a virtual world, take a moment to appreciate the incredible musical journey that accompanies your gameplay.
