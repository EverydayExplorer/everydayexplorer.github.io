The Connection Between Astrology and Tarot


Astrology and Tarot have long been intertwined in the realm of divination and spiritual practices. Both of these ancient art forms offer us a glimpse into the mysteries of the cosmos and our place within it. While Astrology focuses on the alignment and positions of celestial bodies, Tarot taps into the power of symbolism and the subconscious mind. In this blog post, we will explore the fascinating connection between Astrology and Tarot and how they can work together to provide guidance, insight, and self-discovery.

Astrology, often referred to as the language of the stars, is based on the belief that the positions of celestial bodies at the time of our birth can influence our character, behavior, and life events. By studying the positions of the Sun, Moon, planets, and other celestial bodies, astrologers can create birth charts, which act as a snapshot of the heavens at the moment of our birth. These birth charts reveal valuable information about our personality traits, strengths, weaknesses, and potential life paths.

On the other hand, Tarot is a mystical deck of cards that has been used for centuries to gain insight into the past, present, and future. The 78 cards in a Tarot deck represent archetypal energies and universal themes that are deeply rooted in human experiences. The images on these cards, combined with the reader’s intuition and interpretation, provide guidance and clarity on various aspects of life, such as relationships, career, and personal growth.

When we look closely at both Astrology and Tarot, we start to notice several similarities. Both systems rely heavily on symbolism and interpretation. Astrologers interpret the positions of celestial bodies, taking into account the sign they are in, their aspects to other planets, and their placement in different houses of the birth chart. Similarly, Tarot readers interpret the images and symbols on the cards, applying their knowledge of traditional meanings and their intuition to provide meaningful insights.

Another interesting connection between Astrology and Tarot is the concept of archetypes. In Astrology, each zodiac sign represents a specific archetype or energy pattern. For example, Aries is associated with assertiveness and leadership, while Pisces embodies compassion and spirituality. In Tarot, the major arcana cards also represent archetypal energies, such as The Fool, The Magician, and The Empress. These archetypes resonate with our subconscious mind and can provide valuable insights into our own journey and life lessons.

By combining Astrology and Tarot, we can access a deeper level of self-awareness and understanding. Astrology can help identify our strengths, challenges, and life purpose, while Tarot can offer guidance and clarity on how to navigate through different situations and choices. Together, these practices provide a holistic approach to self-discovery and personal growth.

For example, let’s say someone is going through a challenging time in their career. They consult their birth chart to understand their natural inclinations and talents. Perhaps they discover that they have a strong placement of Mars, which signifies ambition and drive. Using Tarot, they can pull a card and receive guidance on how to best channel their energy and take advantage of career opportunities.

Alternatively, someone could use Astrology and Tarot to gain insight into their relationships. By analyzing their birth chart, they can understand their compatibility with others based on the positions of Venus and Mars. They can then use Tarot to explore their emotional patterns and communication style, providing valuable insights into their relationships and how they can improve them.

In conclusion, the connection between Astrology and Tarot is undeniable. Both systems tap into the ancient wisdom of symbolism and interpretation to provide guidance and insight. Whether used separately or in combination, Astrology and Tarot offer us the tools to explore our potential, understand our life’s purpose, and make informed decisions. So, why not embrace the wisdom of the stars and the cards to embark on a journey of self-discovery and personal growth?
