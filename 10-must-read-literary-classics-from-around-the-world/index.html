10 Must-Read Literary Classics from Around the World


Literature, like a vast ocean, encompasses a myriad of cultures, voices, and stories from around the world. Delving into the literary classics of different countries not only offers a glimpse into their history and society but also provides an enriching experience that transcends borders. Whether you’re an avid reader or just starting your literary journey, here are ten must-read literary classics from around the globe that deserve a place on every bookshelf.

  1. ‘One Hundred Years of Solitude’ by Gabriel García Márquez (Colombia): Dive into the magical realism of Macondo, a town suspended between reality and myth, as the Buendía family navigates love, solitude, and the cyclical nature of history. García Márquez’s masterpiece is a testament to the power of storytelling and the complexities of human existence.

  2. ‘War and Peace’ by Leo Tolstoy (Russia): Embark on an epic journey through the Napoleonic Wars and Russian society with Tolstoy’s magnum opus. With its sprawling cast of characters and profound philosophical insights, ‘War and Peace’ is a timeless exploration of the human condition and the pursuit of meaning in a world torn apart by conflict.

  3. ‘The Master and Margarita’ by Mikhail Bulgakov (Russia): Blurring the lines between reality and fantasy, Bulgakov’s satirical masterpiece takes readers on a wild ride through 1930s Moscow, where the Devil himself pays a visit. Filled with wit, dark humor, and philosophical depth, ‘The Master and Margarita’ is a literary tour de force that continues to captivate readers worldwide.

  4. ‘Don Quixote’ by Miguel de Cervantes (Spain): Join the delusional yet endearing Don Quixote and his loyal squire, Sancho Panza, on their quest for chivalric glory in this timeless Spanish classic. Cervantes’ masterpiece is a poignant exploration of idealism, madness, and the power of imagination that has inspired generations of readers and writers alike.

  5. ‘The Tale of Genji’ by Murasaki Shikibu (Japan): Step into the mesmerizing world of Heian-era Japan with this poetic masterpiece, often considered the world’s first novel. Follow the life and loves of Prince Genji as he navigates the intricacies of courtly life and grapples with the ephemeral nature of beauty and desire.

  6. ‘Anna Karenina’ by Leo Tolstoy (Russia): Explore the complexities of love, marriage, and societal norms in 19th-century Russia with Tolstoy’s tragic tale of Anna Karenina. With its richly drawn characters and profound moral insights, this classic novel remains as relevant today as it was when it was first published.

  7. ‘The Odyssey’ by Homer (Greece): Embark on an epic journey across the ancient Mediterranean with Odysseus as he struggles to find his way home after the Trojan War. Homer’s timeless tale of adventure, heroism, and the power of perseverance continues to captivate readers of all ages.

  8. ‘Crime and Punishment’ by Fyodor Dostoevsky (Russia): Delve into the tortured psyche of Raskolnikov, a destitute student who commits a heinous crime in the name of his twisted ideology. Dostoevsky’s exploration of guilt, redemption, and the nature of evil is as gripping and relevant today as it was when it was first published.

  9. ‘Pride and Prejudice’ by Jane Austen (England): Enter the world of Regency-era England with Austen’s timeless comedy of manners, where the headstrong Elizabeth Bennet clashes with the proud Mr. Darcy in a battle of wits and wills. With its sharp social commentary and unforgettable characters, ‘Pride and Prejudice’ remains a beloved classic of English literature.

  10. ‘The Brothers Karamazov’ by Fyodor Dostoevsky (Russia): Journey into the depths of the human soul with Dostoevsky’s sprawling masterpiece, which explores the moral and spiritual dilemmas of three brothers: the intellectual Ivan, the passionate Dmitri, and the saintly Alyosha. A profound meditation on faith, doubt, and the search for meaning, ‘The Brothers Karamazov’ is a towering achievement of world literature.

These ten literary classics from around the world offer readers a passport to different cultures, perspectives, and time periods. Whether you’re drawn to epic adventures, intimate character studies, or philosophical inquiries into the nature of existence, there’s something for everyone in the rich tapestry of global literature. So why not embark on a literary journey of your own and discover the timeless treasures waiting to be found within the pages of these extraordinary works?
