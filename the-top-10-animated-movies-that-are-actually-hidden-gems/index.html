The Top 10 Animated Movies That Are Actually Hidden Gems


Animated movies have long been celebrated for their ability to captivate audiences of all ages with their stunning visuals, heartfelt stories, and memorable characters. While blockbusters like ‘Frozen’ and ‘Toy Story’ dominate the box office, there are plenty of hidden gems in the world of animation that deserve recognition. In this blog post, we’ll shine a spotlight on 10 animated movies that may have flown under the radar but are definitely worth seeking out.

1. ‘The Secret of Kells’ (2009) - This Irish-French-Belgian animated fantasy film tells the story of a young boy named Brendan who embarks on a magical adventure to complete the Book of Kells and save his village from invaders. With its breathtaking hand-drawn animation inspired by Celtic art and mythology, ‘The Secret of Kells’ is a visually stunning masterpiece that has earned widespread acclaim for its artistry and storytelling.

2. ‘Song of the Sea’ (2014) - From the same creators as ‘The Secret of Kells,’ ‘Song of the Sea’ is a mesmerizing tale inspired by Irish folklore. The film follows a young boy named Ben and his mute sister Saoirse as they journey through a magical world to return to their home by the sea. With its enchanting animation and poignant storytelling, ‘Song of the Sea’ is a hidden gem that will tug at your heartstrings.

3. ‘The Triplets of Belleville’ (2003) - This French-Belgian-Canadian animated comedy-drama follows the journey of Madame Souza and her loyal dog Bruno as they embark on a quest to rescue her grandson Champion, a cyclist kidnapped by the French mafia. With its unique visual style, quirky characters, and inventive storytelling, ‘The Triplets of Belleville’ is a delightful and offbeat gem that defies categorization.

4. ‘The Illusionist’ (2010) - Directed by Sylvain Chomet, the creator of ‘The Triplets of Belleville,’ ‘The Illusionist’ is a bittersweet tale of a struggling magician who befriends a young girl while performing in Scotland. Set against the backdrop of 1950s Europe, the film is a poignant meditation on the passage of time, the power of illusions, and the beauty of fleeting moments.

5. ‘Ernest & Celestine’ (2012) - Based on the beloved children’s books by Gabrielle Vincent, ‘Ernest & Celestine’ is a heartwarming tale of friendship between a bear named Ernest and a mouse named Celestine. Despite their differences, the unlikely duo forms a bond that defies societal norms and teaches them valuable lessons about acceptance and understanding.

6. ‘The Red Turtle’ (2016) - This dialogue-free animated film, co-produced by Studio Ghibli, tells the story of a man shipwrecked on a deserted island who forms a deep connection with a mysterious red turtle. Through its breathtaking visuals and meditative storytelling, ‘The Red Turtle’ explores themes of isolation, resilience, and the beauty of the natural world.

7. ‘Mary and Max’ (2009) - This Australian stop-motion animated film tells the unlikely friendship between Mary, a lonely eight-year-old girl living in Australia, and Max, a middle-aged man with Asperger syndrome living in New York City. Through their correspondence, Mary and Max navigate life’s challenges and form a bond that transcends distance and difference.

8. ‘The Breadwinner’ (2017) - Directed by Nora Twomey and executive produced by Angelina Jolie, ‘The Breadwinner’ is a powerful and poignant tale of a young girl named Parvana who disguises herself as a boy to provide for her family in Taliban-controlled Afghanistan. With its stunning animation and gripping storytelling, ‘The Breadwinner’ sheds light on the resilience of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

9. ‘Wrinkles’ (2011) - This Spanish animated drama follows the friendship between two elderly men living in a nursing home: Emilio, a former bank manager grappling with Alzheimer’s disease, and Miguel, a charming con artist. Through its sensitive portrayal of aging and friendship, ‘Wrinkles’ offers a poignant meditation on the passage of time and the importance of human connection.

10. ‘Kubo and the Two Strings’ (2016) - From the acclaimed animation studio Laika, ‘Kubo and the Two Strings’ is a visually stunning epic inspired by Japanese folklore. The film follows the journey of a young boy named Kubo as he embarks on a quest to unlock the secret of his past and fulfill his destiny. With its breathtaking stop-motion animation and rich storytelling, ‘Kubo and the Two Strings’ is a hidden gem that deserves to be discovered.

In conclusion, these 10 animated movies may not have received the same level of attention as blockbuster hits, but they are true hidden gems that deserve a spot in any animation lover’s collection. With their stunning visuals, heartfelt stories, and memorable characters, these films prove that great storytelling knows no boundaries and that animation has the power to touch hearts and inspire minds in unexpected ways. Whether you’re a fan of traditional hand-drawn animation, stop-motion, or computer-generated imagery, there’s something on this list for everyone to enjoy. So why not take a break from the mainstream and dive into the enchanting world of these hidden treasures? You won’t be disappointed.
