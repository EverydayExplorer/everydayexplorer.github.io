10 Must-Watch Documentaries for Sports Fans


Documentaries have long been a source of inspiration and enlightenment for people across the globe. They offer a unique perspective on various subjects, including sports. For sports fans, documentaries provide a behind-the-scenes look at the triumphs, hardships, and untold stories of athletes and teams. If you’re a sports enthusiast looking to delve deeper into the world of sports through the lens of documentaries, we’ve got you covered. Here are ten must-watch documentaries that will leave you inspired, entertained, and craving for more.

1. ‘Senna’ (2010): This critically acclaimed documentary tells the story of the legendary Brazilian Formula One driver, Ayrton Senna. Through a combination of archival footage and interviews, ‘Senna’ explores the intense rivalry, dedication, and tragic end of one of the greatest racing drivers in history.

2. ‘Hoop Dreams’ (1994): This groundbreaking documentary follows the lives of two African-American high school students, William Gates and Arthur Agee, as they pursue their dreams of playing professional basketball. Through their struggles and triumphs, ‘Hoop Dreams’ offers a poignant portrayal of the realities of inner-city life and the pursuit of basketball stardom.

3. ‘When We Were Kings’ (1996): Set against the backdrop of the iconic ‘Rumble in the Jungle’ boxing match between Muhammad Ali and George Foreman, this Academy Award-winning documentary captures the energy and significance of the event. ‘When We Were Kings’ provides a fascinating look at the larger-than-life personalities of Ali and Foreman and the cultural impact of their historic bout.

4. ‘The Last Dance’ (2020): This captivating documentary series chronicles the final championship season of the Chicago Bulls in 1997-1998, led by the iconic Michael Jordan. Through never-before-seen footage and interviews, ‘The Last Dance’ offers an in-depth exploration of Jordan’s unparalleled competitiveness and the team’s journey to their sixth NBA title.

5. ‘Senna vs. Brundle’ (2016): This gripping documentary explores the intense rivalry between Ayrton Senna and Martin Brundle during their time as Formula One drivers in the 1980s. Through interviews with both drivers and archival footage, ‘Senna vs. Brundle’ showcases the fierce competition and mutual respect between these two titans of the sport.

6. ‘O.J.: Made in America’ (2016): This five-part documentary series provides a comprehensive examination of O.J. Simpson’s rise to fame as a football player and the subsequent murder trial that captivated the world. ‘O.J.: Made in America’ delves into the complex issues of race, celebrity, and justice, making it a must-watch for sports and true crime enthusiasts.

7. ‘Maradona’ (2019): Directed by Academy Award-winning filmmaker Asif Kapadia, ‘Maradona’ tells the story of Argentine soccer legend Diego Maradona. Through a combination of archival footage and personal interviews, the documentary offers an intimate portrayal of Maradona’s rise to fame, his struggles with addiction, and his enduring impact on the sport.

8. ‘Free Solo’ (2018): This heart-stopping documentary follows rock climber Alex Honnold as he attempts to become the first person to free solo climb El Capitan in Yosemite National Park. ‘Free Solo’ offers a thrilling and awe-inspiring look at Honnold’s daring pursuit and the mental and physical challenges he faces along the way.

9. ‘The Two Escobars’ (2010): This ESPN 30 for 30 documentary explores the intertwined stories of Colombian soccer player Andrés Escobar and drug lord Pablo Escobar. Through interviews with players, journalists, and family members, ‘The Two Escobars’ examines the impact of drug violence on Colombian society and the tragic fate of Andrés Escobar, who was murdered after scoring an own goal in the 1994 FIFA World Cup.

10. ‘Icarus’ (2017): This Academy Award-winning documentary takes an unexpected turn as filmmaker Bryan Fogel sets out to uncover the truth about doping in professional cycling. Through his interactions with Russian scientist Grigory Rodchenkov, ‘Icarus’ uncovers a massive state-sponsored doping scandal that shakes the world of sports to its core.

These ten must-watch documentaries for sports fans offer a diverse range of stories, emotions, and insights. Whether you’re a fan of motorsports, basketball, boxing, or soccer, there’s something for everyone in this list. So grab some popcorn, get comfortable, and prepare to be enthralled by the remarkable tales that unfold onscreen. These documentaries will leave you with a newfound appreciation for the athletes, the sports they play, and the human spirit that drives them to greatness. Enjoy the journey!
