Survival Medicine: Treating Common Illnesses in the Wild


When venturing into the great outdoors, it’s essential to come prepared for any situation. While most people remember to pack the necessary gear, it’s equally important to have the knowledge and resources to handle medical emergencies and common illnesses that may arise in the wilderness. In this blog post, we will explore various survival medicine techniques that can help you manage and treat common illnesses when medical assistance is not readily available.

One of the most common illnesses encountered in the wild is dehydration. In the harsh conditions of the outdoors, it’s easy to become dehydrated quickly, which can lead to fatigue, dizziness, confusion, and even heatstroke. To prevent and treat dehydration, it’s crucial to drink plenty of water and replenish electrolytes regularly. If you don’t have access to clean water, you can use water purification tablets or boil water to make it safe for consumption.

Another common ailment that outdoor enthusiasts may face is gastrointestinal issues such as diarrhea and food poisoning. Contaminated water or improper food handling can cause these problems. To treat diarrhea, it’s important to stay hydrated and consume foods that are easily digestible, such as the BRAT diet (bananas, rice, applesauce, and toast). Activated charcoal tablets can also help absorb toxins and alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort.

Insect bites and stings are another prevalent issue in the wild. Mosquitoes, ticks, and other insects can carry diseases or cause allergic reactions. To prevent insect bites, wear long-sleeved shirts, pants, and use insect repellents containing DEET. After getting bitten, clean the affected area with soap and water and apply an insect bite ointment or cream. If you have an allergic reaction, such as difficulty breathing or severe swelling, seek immediate medical assistance.

Abrasions, cuts, and wounds are common injuries that can occur during outdoor activities. To treat such injuries, it’s crucial to clean the wound thoroughly with clean water and disinfect it with antiseptic solutions. Apply antibiotic ointment and cover the wound with a sterile dressing to prevent infection. If sutures are required, clean the wound as best as you can and use sterile strips or butterfly closures to bring the edges together until you can receive proper medical attention.

Additionally, it’s vital to know how to manage common allergic reactions, such as those caused by poison ivy, poison oak, or bee stings. If you come into contact with these plants, wash the affected area immediately with soap and water. Applying calamine lotion or a hydrocortisone cream can help relieve itching and soothe the skin. For severe allergic reactions, such as difficulty breathing or anaphylaxis, seek immediate medical assistance.

Lastly, it’s essential to have a basic understanding of wilderness first aid and CPR in case of any medical emergencies. Knowing how to perform CPR and deliver basic first aid can make a significant difference in saving someone’s life until professional help arrives. Wilderness first aid courses are available and highly recommended for individuals who frequently engage in outdoor activities.

In conclusion, being well-prepared to handle common illnesses and medical emergencies in the wild is crucial for outdoor enthusiasts. Remember to pack essential medical supplies, and have the knowledge to treat dehydration, gastrointestinal issues, insect bites, wounds, allergic reactions, and perform basic first aid and CPR. By having the necessary skills and resources, you can ensure a safer and more enjoyable outdoor experience. Stay safe, be prepared, and have fun exploring the beauty of the wilderness!
