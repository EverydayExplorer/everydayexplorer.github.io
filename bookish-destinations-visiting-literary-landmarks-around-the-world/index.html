Bookish Destinations: Visiting Literary Landmarks Around the World


When it comes to traveling, why not combine your love for literature with your wanderlust? Bookish destinations offer a unique and immersive experience for avid readers and literary enthusiasts. From quaint bookshops to iconic author’s homes, these literary landmarks around the world are sure to transport you into the pages of your favorite books. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most enchanting bookish destinations that every book lover should add to their travel bucket list.

1. Edinburgh, Scotland: Known as the birthplace of Harry Potter, Edinburgh is a must-visit for any fan of J.K. Rowling’s magical series. Start your journey at The Elephant House, a cozy café where Rowling wrote parts of the first book. Then, head to the stunning Edinburgh Castle, which inspired the Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Finally, take a stroll through the charming streets of the city to discover various bookshops that showcase Scotland’s rich literary heritage.

2. Paris, France: Often called the City of Love, Paris is also a literary paradise. Visit the iconic Shakespeare and Company bookstore, where famous authors like Ernest Hemingway and James Joyce once spent their time. Don’t forget to take a walk along the Seine River, just like the characters in Victor Hugo’s ‘Les Misérables.’ For a touch of mystery, explore the world of ‘The Da Vinci Code’ by Dan Brown, unraveling the secrets hidden within the Louvre Museum.

3. Dublin, Ireland: Immerse yourself in the literary legacy of Dublin, the hometown of renowned authors such as James Joyce, Oscar Wilde, and Bram Stoker. Begin your literary journey at Trinity College Library, which houses the famous Book of Kells. Then, pay a visit to the Dublin Writers Museum, showcasing the lives and works of Ireland’s literary greats. Wrap up your trip with a pint of Guinness at the historic pubs that were once frequented by literary legends.

4. New York City, USA: The Big Apple is not only a hub for art and culture but also a literary haven. Start your literary exploration at the iconic New York Public Library, where you can marvel at the grandeur of its architecture and peruse its extensive collection of books. For fans of F. Scott Fitzgerald, take a stroll through the streets of the Upper East Side, retracing the steps of the characters in ‘The Great Gatsby.’ Finally, make a pilgrimage to the former apartment of the Beat Generation’s Jack Kerouac and Allen Ginsberg in the vibrant Greenwich Village.

5. Tokyo, Japan: Japan’s capital offers a blend of tradition and modernity, with a vibrant literary scene to boot. Begin your literary tour at the Hongo Campus of the University of Tokyo, where Natsume Soseki, one of Japan’s greatest writers, taught. Then, visit Jimbocho, Tokyo’s book district, where you can lose yourself amidst countless bookstores and cafes. For manga enthusiasts, don’t miss a visit to Akihabara, the electric town, where you can find manga and anime-related bookshops and merchandise.

These are just a few examples of the countless bookish destinations around the world. Whether you’re an avid reader, a literature student, or simply someone who appreciates the magic of words, these literary landmarks offer an unforgettable experience. So, pack your bags, grab your favorite book, and embark on a literary adventure that will transport you to the heart of your most cherished stories.
