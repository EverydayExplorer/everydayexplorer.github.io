Unique Hotels around the World: From Treehouses to Ice Hotels


Looking for a unique and unforgettable hotel experience? Look no further! In this blog post, we’ll be exploring some of the most extraordinary hotels around the world. From treehouses perched high up in the treetops to ice hotels nestled in frozen landscapes, these accommodations offer a truly exceptional stay. So, pack your bags and get ready for a one-of-a-kind adventure!

1. Treehouse hotels: Are you a nature lover? If so, staying in a treehouse hotel is the perfect choice for you. Imagine waking up amidst lush greenery, surrounded by the soothing sounds of birds chirping. From South Africa’s Tsala Treetop Lodge to Sweden’s Treehotel, these properties offer a unique blend of luxury and wilderness, allowing you to reconnect with nature in the most remarkable way.

2. Ice hotels: For those who aren’t afraid of the cold, a stay in an ice hotel is an experience like no other. These architectural marvels are built entirely out of ice and snow, providing a stunning and ethereal atmosphere. From Sweden’s iconic ICEHOTEL to Canada’s Hôtel de Glace, these hotels offer cozy ice beds, ice sculptures, and even ice bars. Don’t worry, warm thermal sleeping bags will keep you snug throughout the night!

3. Cave hotels: Take a step back in time and experience life as our ancestors did by staying in a cave hotel. Cappadocia in Turkey is famous for its cave dwellings turned luxury accommodations. These unique hotels feature beautifully restored caves with modern amenities, creating a harmonious blend of history and comfort. The otherworldly landscapes surrounding these cave hotels only add to the enchantment.

4. Underwater hotels: Dive into a surreal underwater world by booking a stay in one of the few underwater hotels around the world. Imagine falling asleep while surrounded by vibrant coral reefs and exotic marine life. The Manta Resort in Zanzibar and the Atlantis, The Palm in Dubai are just a couple of examples that offer this extraordinary experience. Get ready to explore the depths of the ocean without even leaving your room!

5. Train hotels: If you’re a train enthusiast, why not take your passion to the next level by staying in a train hotel? These beautifully restored vintage trains have been transformed into luxurious accommodations. The Venice Simplon-Orient-Express and the Train Suite Shiki-Shima in Japan are perfect examples of this unique concept. Journey through breathtaking landscapes while enjoying world-class service and comfort.

6. Treetop hotels: Imagine staying in a hotel perched high up in the canopy of the world’s tallest trees. Treetop hotels offer an unparalleled connection to nature and breathtaking views of the surrounding forests. The Costa Rican property, Finca Bellavista, and the Lion Sands Game Reserve Treehouses in South Africa are just a couple of examples of these extraordinary accommodations.

7. Castle hotels: Fulfill your childhood dreams of being a prince or princess by staying in a castle hotel. Europe is home to many stunning castle hotels that offer a glimpse into a bygone era. From Ashford Castle in Ireland to Neuschwanstein Castle in Germany, these accommodations provide a unique blend of history, elegance, and luxury. Get ready to be treated like royalty!

8. Underground hotels: For an unconventional experience, why not stay in an underground hotel? Set in former mines or caves, these hotels offer a unique and secluded atmosphere. The Sala Silver Mine Hotel in Sweden and the Kokopelli’s Cave Bed & Breakfast in New Mexico are perfect examples of underground accommodations that are sure to make your stay memorable.

9. Wildlife reserve lodges: If you’re a wildlife enthusiast, consider a stay in a lodge located within a wildlife reserve. These lodges offer an unparalleled opportunity to observe majestic creatures up close and personal. From the Singita Grumeti Reserves in Tanzania to the Sabi Sabi Private Game Reserve in South Africa, these accommodations combine luxury with thrilling wildlife encounters.

10. Floating hotels: Make your stay even more memorable by booking a room in a floating hotel. From the floating villas in the Maldives to the floating cabins of Sweden’s Arctic Bath, these unique accommodations let you wake up surrounded by water and stunning natural scenery. Enjoy a tranquil and captivating experience unlike any other.

Remember, when booking these unique hotels, it’s important to plan well in advance due to their popularity and limited availability. Whether you’re seeking a connection with nature, a journey through time, or a surreal underwater experience, these extraordinary hotels will leave you with memories to last a lifetime.

So, which one of these unique hotels will you be adding to your bucket list? Let us know in the comments below!
