The Best Exercises for Building Strength and Power in Football


Football is a physically demanding sport that requires strength, power, and explosive movements. To excel on the field, football players must have a solid foundation of strength and power to help them tackle opponents, sprint down the field, and dominate the game. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best exercises for building strength and power in football.

1. Squats: Squats are a staple exercise for building lower-body strength and power. They target the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and core muscles. By performing squats with proper form and gradually increasing the weight, football players can develop stronger legs and improve their ability to generate explosive movements.

To perform a squat, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointed slightly outward. Lower your body as if you’re sitting back into a chair while keeping your chest up and your core engaged. Make sure your knees track over your toes and avoid letting them cave inward. Push through your heels to return to the starting position.

2. Deadlifts: Deadlifts are another essential exercise for football players as they help build overall strength and power. Deadlifts primarily target the glutes, hamstrings, quadriceps, and lower back muscles. They also engage the core muscles, promoting stability and balance.

To perform a deadlift, place a barbell on the ground in front of you. Stand with your feet hip-width apart and grasp the bar with your hands shoulder-width apart, palms facing down. Keep your back flat, engage your core, and drive through your heels as you stand up, lifting the barbell with your legs. Lower the bar back to the ground in a controlled manner and repeat.

3. Power Cleans: Power cleans are a dynamic exercise that focuses on explosive power and full-body strength. This exercise targets the legs, hips, back, and shoulders. It mimics the explosive movements required in football, such as jumping, tackling, and sprinting.

To perform a power clean, start by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a barbell at thigh level with an overhand grip. Lower into a quarter squat, then explosively extend your hips, shrug your shoulders, and pull the barbell upward. As the bar rises, drop into a quarter squat and catch the bar at shoulder height while rotating your elbows forward. Stand up to complete the movement, then lower the barbell back down to the starting position.

4. Bench Press: While football is primarily a lower-body dominated sport, upper-body strength is also crucial. The bench press is an excellent exercise for developing strength in the chest, shoulders, and triceps, which are vital for blocking opponents and pushing past defenders.

To perform a bench press, lie flat on a bench with your feet firmly planted on the ground. Grasp the barbell with a grip slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower the barbell down to your chest with control, then press it back up towards the starting position, keeping your elbows at a 45-degree angle to your body. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

5. Sled Push/Pull: Sled pushes and pulls are functional exercises that mimic the movements required in football, such as pushing an opponent or pulling away from a tackle. This exercise targets the legs, core, and upper body, promoting overall strength and power.

To perform a sled push, set up a sled with weight plates on it and push it forward using a low, athletic stance. Lean slightly forward, keep your core engaged, and drive with your legs to move the sled across the desired distance.

To perform a sled pull, attach a harness or handles to the sled, and walk backward while pulling the sled towards you. Maintain a strong grip, engage your core, and use your legs and upper body to generate power and pull the sled towards you.

Incorporating these exercises into your training routine can significantly improve your strength and power on the football field. Remember to start with lighter weights and focus on proper form before gradually increasing the weight. Additionally, consult with a coach or trainer to ensure you’re performing the exercises correctly and safely.

By consistently incorporating these exercises into your training regimen, you can develop the strength and power needed to excel in the game of football. Whether you’re a quarterback looking to throw farther, a lineman aiming to overpower opponents, or a running back striving to break tackles, building your strength and power will undoubtedly elevate your performance on the field.
