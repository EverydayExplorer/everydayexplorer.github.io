Surviving in Volcanic Areas: Coping with Ashfall and Lava Flows


Living in proximity to a volcano is both exciting and dangerous. Volcanic eruptions bring about a spectacle of nature’s power, but they also pose significant threats to human life, property, and the environment. In this blog post, we will explore the measures one can take to survive in volcanic areas and cope with the two primary hazards: ashfall and lava flows.

Ashfall: The Silent Threat

When a volcano erupts, it releases a mixture of volcanic ash, gases, and rock fragments into the atmosphere. This ash can travel great distances, blanketing surrounding areas and even neighboring countries. While it may seem harmless, volcanic ash poses serious health risks and can cause considerable damage.

Health Risks

Volcanic ash is made up of tiny particles that can be inhaled deep into the lungs, causing respiratory problems. These particles can irritate the eyes, nose, and throat, leading to coughing, wheezing, and difficulty breathing. People with pre-existing respiratory conditions such as asthma are particularly vulnerable.

To protect yourself from the health hazards of ashfall:

  1. Stay indoors: Close windows and doors to prevent ash from entering your home, and use air purifiers or high-efficiency filters to improve indoor air quality.
  2. Wear protective gear: When venturing outside, wear an N95 respirator mask, goggles, and long-sleeved clothing to minimize exposure to airborne ash particles.
  3. Stay hydrated: Breathing in ash can cause dehydration, so make sure to drink plenty of water or non-caffeinated fluids to stay hydrated.

Property Damage

Besides health risks, volcanic ashfall can cause significant damage to homes, cars, and infrastructure. The weight of accumulated ash can collapse roofs, clog drainage systems, and damage machinery and electronics.

To protect your property from ashfall:

  1. Clear roofs and gutters: Regularly remove ash accumulation from roofs and gutters to prevent structural damage and maintain proper drainage.
  2. Cover sensitive equipment: Use tarps or plastic covers to shield machinery, vehicles, and outdoor electronics from ashfall.
  3. Avoid using water: Do not use water to clean ash as it can solidify, causing blockages in pipes and drains. Instead, use brooms and shovels to clear ash from surfaces.

Lava Flows: A Force of Nature

Lava flows are another significant threat associated with volcanic eruptions. These rivers of molten rock can rapidly advance, engulfing everything in their path. While it is challenging to redirect or stop a lava flow, there are steps you can take to increase your chances of survival.

Stay Informed

Stay updated on volcano monitoring and early warning systems. Local authorities often provide regular updates on volcanic activity and issue evacuation orders if necessary. Pay attention to these alerts and make sure to have an emergency plan in place for yourself and your family.

Be Prepared to Evacuate

In the event of an impending lava flow:

  1. Pack essential supplies: Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, medications, important documents, and other essentials that can sustain you and your family for at least 72 hours.
  2. Plan evacuation routes: Identify multiple evacuation routes in case one becomes blocked or compromised. Familiarize yourself with local maps and landmarks for easy navigation.
  3. Follow instructions: If authorities issue evacuation orders, follow them promptly and stay informed about designated evacuation centers.

Remember, your safety and the safety of your loved ones should be the top priority during a volcanic eruption. While it may be tempting to document the event or get closer for a better view, it is critical to prioritize your well-being over any desire for a first-hand experience.

Conclusion

Living in volcanic areas requires a combination of preparedness, awareness, and vigilance. By understanding the risks associated with ashfall and lava flows and taking the necessary precautions, you can increase your chances of surviving such natural disasters. Stay informed, stay prepared, and stay safe.
