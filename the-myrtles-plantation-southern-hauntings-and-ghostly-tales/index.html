The Myrtles Plantation: Southern Hauntings and Ghostly Tales


Nestled in the heart of St. Francisville, Louisiana, lies a place shrouded in mystery and intrigue - The Myrtles Plantation. This historic Southern mansion, with its rich history and chilling tales, has captivated the imaginations of locals and visitors alike for decades. Join us as we delve into the haunting legends and ghostly tales that surround this iconic plantation.

Dating back to the late 18th century, The Myrtles Plantation has witnessed a myriad of joyous occasions and tragic events. It is said to be home to at least 12 restless spirits, each with their own unique story to tell. From the lingering presence of Chloe, a former slave girl, to the ghostly apparitions that roam the halls, this plantation is a hotbed for paranormal activity.

One of the most famous legends associated with The Myrtles Plantation is the tale of Chloe. According to local lore, Chloe was a slave who was caught eavesdropping on her master’s conversations. As punishment, her ear was severed, and she was forced into hard labor. Seeking revenge, Chloe added poison to a cake intended for her master’s family, resulting in the deaths of two of his children. Legend has it that her spirit still wanders the property, seeking forgiveness for her actions.

Visitors to The Myrtles Plantation have reported a range of spine-chilling experiences. From unexplained footsteps echoing through the corridors to flickering lights and shadowy figures, the paranormal encounters are enough to send shivers down one’s spine. Many have even captured eerie photographs and recordings that seem to confirm the presence of supernatural entities.

The Myrtles Plantation also boasts a unique feature - the haunted mirror. This antique mirror, which hangs in the foyer, is said to hold the spirits of Sara Woodruff and her two young daughters, who tragically died at the hands of a slave. Locals believe that if you stare into the mirror long enough, the ghostly images of the Woodruff family will appear before your eyes.

For those brave enough to spend the night, The Myrtles Plantation offers overnight accommodations. Known as one of America’s most haunted bed and breakfasts, guests can immerse themselves in the rich history and eerie ambiance of this iconic location. From themed ghost tours to candlelit dinners, the experience is sure to leave a lasting impression.

Whether you’re a believer in the supernatural or simply intrigued by the mysteries of the past, The Myrtles Plantation offers an unforgettable journey into the Southern hauntings and ghostly tales that have made it famous. Explore the haunted corridors, listen to the whispers of the past, and prepare to be both thrilled and chilled at this historic gem.

In conclusion, The Myrtles Plantation is more than just a historic landmark - it is a portal to another world. Its haunted history and ghostly tales continue to captivate visitors from near and far. So, if you’re seeking an unforgettable adventure into the unknown, make sure to add The Myrtles Plantation to your list of must-visit destinations. Just remember, you may not be alone during your stay.
