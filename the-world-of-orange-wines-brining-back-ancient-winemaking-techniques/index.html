The World of Orange Wines: Brining Back Ancient Winemaking Techniques


Wine enthusiasts are always on the lookout for new and exciting experiences. In recent years, there has been a resurgence of interest in orange wines. These unique and intriguing wines, sometimes referred to as amber wines, are made using ancient winemaking techniques that date back thousands of years.

What exactly are orange wines? They are white wines that are made using the same process as red wines. While most white wines are made by fermenting the juice and discarding the skins, orange wines are made by fermenting the juice together with the skins and sometimes even the seeds and stems of the grapes. This extended contact with the skins gives the wines their distinct orange color and unique flavor profile.

The origins of orange wines can be traced back to the country of Georgia, where winemaking has been a cherished tradition for over 8,000 years. Traditional Georgian winemakers would bury clay vessels, known as qvevris, in the ground, and use them to ferment and store their wines. The qvevris are large clay pots lined with beeswax and covered with a clay lid. This traditional winemaking method allowed for extended skin contact and natural fermentation, resulting in the production of orange wines.

In recent years, the revival of ancient winemaking techniques has gained traction in other parts of the world as well. Winemakers in Italy, Slovenia, Austria, and even the United States have started experimenting with orange wines, bringing this unique style to a wider audience.

So, what sets orange wines apart from other types of wines? One of the most notable characteristics of orange wines is their texture. The extended skin contact during fermentation gives the wines a richer and more tannic mouthfeel, similar to that of red wines. This makes orange wines a great option for those seeking a white wine with more complexity and structure.

In terms of flavor, orange wines can vary greatly depending on the grape variety, winemaking technique, and aging process. They often exhibit aromas of baked fruits, honey, and spices, with a subtle tannic grip and a distinct acidity. The flavor profiles can range from crisp and refreshing to bold and rustic, making orange wines versatile and suitable for a wide range of pairings.

Pairing orange wines with food is an exciting adventure. The complexity and structure of these wines make them a great match for a variety of dishes. They pair exceptionally well with savory and earthy flavors like roasted poultry, grilled vegetables, and aged cheeses. The tannins in orange wines also make them a perfect companion for fatty and rich foods like charcuterie and paté. The acidity in the wines helps to cut through the richness of these dishes, creating a harmonious balance of flavors.

Another reason for the increasing popularity of orange wines is their organic and natural winemaking approach. Many winemakers who produce orange wines follow organic or biodynamic farming practices and refrain from using chemicals or additives during the winemaking process. This commitment to producing wines in a more natural and sustainable way resonates with consumers who value environmentally conscious products.

From a marketing standpoint, orange wines also provide an opportunity for winemakers to differentiate themselves in a crowded market. With their unique color and flavor profile, orange wines stand out on the shelf and pique the curiosity of wine enthusiasts. This, coupled with the growing interest in natural and ancestral winemaking methods, has contributed to the increase in demand for orange wines.

In conclusion, orange wines offer a unique and captivating experience for wine lovers. By embracing ancient winemaking techniques and extending skin contact during fermentation, these wines have carved a niche for themselves in the ever-evolving world of wine. Whether you’re a novice wine drinker or an experienced connoisseur, orange wines are worth exploring. So, the next time you’re looking to expand your wine horizons, reach for a bottle of orange wine and embark on a journey back in time to the world of ancient winemaking techniques. Cheers!
