10 Healthy Homemade Treat Recipes for Dogs


You love your furry friend and want to give them the best, so why not treat them to some healthy homemade goodies? Making your own dog treats allows you to control the ingredients and ensure that your pup is getting the nutrition they need. Plus, it’s a fun and rewarding way to show your love. In this post, we’ll share 10 delicious and nutritious recipes for homemade dog treats that are sure to make your pup’s tail wag!

1. Peanut Butter and Banana Biscuits

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 2 ½ cups whole wheat flour

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mash the bananas in a bowl until smooth.
  3. Add the peanut butter and flour to the mashed bananas and mix well.
  4. Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut into shapes using a cookie cutter.
  5. Place the biscuits on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  6. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.
  7. Allow the biscuits to cool completely before serving.

2. Sweet Potato and Chicken Jerky

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 250°F (120°C).
  2. Slice the sweet potatoes and chicken breasts into thin strips.
  3. Place the strips on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Bake for 2-3 hours or until the strips are dry and crispy.
  5. Allow the jerky to cool before serving.

3. Carrot and Oatmeal Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups grated carrots
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • ½ cup unsweetened applesauce
  • ½ cup water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix together the grated carrots, rolled oats, applesauce, and water.
  3. Drop spoonfuls of the mixture onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Flatten the cookies with the back of a spoon.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the cookies are firm.
  6. Allow the cookies to cool completely before serving.

4. Pumpkin and Cinnamon Bites

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup canned pumpkin
  • 2 ½ cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix together the pumpkin, flour, and cinnamon until well combined.
  3. Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut into bite-sized pieces.
  4. Place the bites on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until firm.
  6. Allow the bites to cool before serving.

5. Blueberry and Yogurt Frozen Treats

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 1 cup plain yogurt

Instructions:

  1. Blend the blueberries and yogurt together until smooth.
  2. Pour the mixture into ice cube trays or silicone molds.
  3. Freeze for at least 4 hours or until solid.
  4. Pop the frozen treats out of the trays or molds before serving.

6. Apple and Cheddar Pupcakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup grated apple
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • ½ cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 2 eggs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix together the grated apple, cheddar cheese, applesauce, flour, baking powder, and eggs until well combined.
  3. Spoon the batter into a greased muffin tin.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  5. Allow the pupcakes to cool before serving.

7. Salmon and Sweet Potato Bites

Ingredients:

  • 1 can salmon, drained and flaked
  • 1 cup mashed sweet potato
  • 1 ½ cups oat flour

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix together the salmon, mashed sweet potato, and oat flour until well combined.
  3. Roll the mixture into small balls.
  4. Place the balls on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.
  6. Allow the bites to cool before serving.

8. Spinach and Cheese Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh spinach, finely chopped
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 ½ cups whole wheat flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 eggs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix together the spinach, cheese, flour, and baking powder.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, olive oil, and eggs.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  5. Spoon the batter into a greased muffin tin.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the muffins to cool before serving.

9. Turkey and Cranberry Balls

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked turkey, shredded
  • ½ cup dried cranberries
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 egg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix together the turkey, cranberries, oats, and egg until well combined.
  3. Roll the mixture into small balls.
  4. Place the balls on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until firm.
  6. Allow the balls to cool before serving.

10. Beef and Carrot Sticks

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 cup grated carrots
  • ½ cup oats

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix together the ground beef, grated carrots, and oats until well combined.
  3. Shape the mixture into sticks.
  4. Place the sticks on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until cooked through.
  6. Allow the sticks to cool before serving.

These 10 healthy homemade treat recipes are a fantastic way to spoil your furry friend while ensuring they’re getting the nutrition they need. Remember to always consult with your veterinarian before making any changes to your dog’s diet, especially if they have specific dietary requirements or health conditions. Enjoy making these delicious treats, and watch your pup’s excitement as they indulge in these homemade goodies!
