The Kitchen Garden: 25 Tools and Supplies for Growing Herbs Indoors


When it comes to growing herbs indoors, having the right tools and supplies can make all the difference. Whether you’re a seasoned gardener or just starting out, having a well-equipped kitchen garden is essential for successful herb cultivation. In this blog post, we’ll explore 25 tools and supplies that every indoor herb gardener should have on hand. From basic essentials to handy gadgets, we’ve got you covered.

1. Planting Pots: Start off with a collection of small to medium-sized pots that are suitable for herb cultivation. Opt for pots with good drainage to prevent waterlogging.

2. Seed Trays: Seed trays are perfect for starting herb seeds indoors. They provide ample space for seeds to germinate and can be easily transferred to individual pots once the seedlings are ready.

3. Seed Starting Mix: A high-quality seed starting mix is crucial for healthy seed germination. Look for a mix that is lightweight, well-draining, and nutrient-rich.

4. Grow Lights: Since herbs require ample sunlight to thrive, investing in a good set of grow lights is essential for indoor gardening. LED grow lights are energy-efficient and provide the right spectrum of light for optimal plant growth.

5. Heat Mat: Some herbs require warmer temperatures to germinate. A heat mat placed under the seed trays can help maintain the ideal temperature for successful seed germination.

6. Watering Can: A small, easy-to-handle watering can is a must-have for indoor herb gardening. Look for one with a long spout that allows for precise watering without causing waterlogging.

7. Pruning Shears: Keep your herbs tidy and promote healthy growth with a pair of sharp pruning shears. Regular pruning helps prevent overcrowding and encourages bushier growth.

8. Plant Labels: Keep track of your herbs with plant labels. These can be made from popsicle sticks, plastic markers, or even repurposed wine corks. Labeling helps you identify herbs and their specific care requirements.

9. Spray Bottle: Herbs love humidity. A spray bottle filled with water is handy for misting the leaves and creating a humid environment, especially during dry winter months.

10. Organic Fertilizer: Keep your herbs well-nourished with an organic fertilizer. Look for a balanced formula specifically designed for herbs, or opt for homemade compost tea.

11. Herb Scissors: Herb scissors are specially designed with multiple blades that allow for quick and precise herb harvesting. They make chopping herbs a breeze.

12. Herb Grinder: If you plan on using your freshly harvested herbs for cooking or tea, a herb grinder is a handy tool to have. It helps release the flavors and aromas of the herbs.

13. Mason Jars: Preserve your herbs by drying them in mason jars. Not only do they keep your herbs fresh, but they also add a rustic touch to your kitchen decor.

14. Herb Drying Rack: If you have a large herb harvest, a drying rack is essential. Hang your herbs upside down to dry them naturally, preserving their flavors and medicinal properties.

15. Microgreens Starter Kit: Microgreens are young and tender herb shoots that pack a punch of flavor and nutrition. Invest in a microgreens starter kit to grow these miniature wonders.

16. pH Test Kit: Maintain the right pH levels for your herbs with a pH test kit. Most herbs prefer slightly acidic soil, so regular testing can help you make necessary adjustments.

17. Neem Oil: Keep pests at bay with a natural insecticide like neem oil. It’s safe to use on herbs and effectively controls common pests like aphids and spider mites.

18. Plant Mister: For herbs that prefer a more humid environment, a plant mister is a handy tool. It allows you to provide a fine mist of water directly to the leaves.

19. Plant Stand: Make the most of your indoor space by investing in a plant stand. It allows you to create vertical gardens, giving your herbs ample sunlight and making them a focal point in your kitchen.

20. Herb Infuser: Enjoy the flavors and health benefits of herbal teas by using a herb infuser. Simply place your fresh or dried herbs in the infuser and steep in hot water.

21. Herb Preserver: Extend the shelf life of your harvested herbs with a herb preserver. These storage containers are designed to keep herbs fresh for longer periods.

22. Garden Gloves: Protect your hands while gardening with a pair of durable and comfortable garden gloves. Look for gloves that provide a good grip and allow for dexterity.

23. Herb Book: Expand your knowledge of herbs with a comprehensive herb book. It can provide valuable information on growing, harvesting, and using herbs for culinary and medicinal purposes.

24. Herb Calendar: Keep track of important dates and tasks with a herb calendar. It helps you stay organized and ensures you don’t miss any essential steps in your herb gardening journey.

25. Herb Tea Infuser Mug: Sip on your favorite herbal teas in style with a herb tea infuser mug. These mugs come with built-in infusers, making it easy to brew and enjoy your herbal concoctions.

So there you have it - 25 essential tools and supplies for growing herbs indoors in your kitchen garden. With the right equipment, you’ll be well on your way to enjoying a bountiful harvest of fresh and flavorful herbs throughout the year. Happy gardening!
