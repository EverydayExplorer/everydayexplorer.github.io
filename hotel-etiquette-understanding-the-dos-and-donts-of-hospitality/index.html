Hotel Etiquette: Understanding the Do's and Don'ts of Hospitality


When staying in a hotel, it’s important to remember that you are a guest in someone else’s space. Practicing proper hotel etiquette not only ensures a pleasant experience for yourself but also shows respect for the hotel staff and other guests. From check-in to check-out, here are some do’s and don’ts of hotel etiquette to keep in mind during your stay.

Do’s:

  1. Be Polite and Courteous: Treat the hotel staff with kindness and respect. Say ‘please’ and ’thank you,’ and use polite language when interacting with the front desk, housekeeping, and other staff members. A friendly attitude goes a long way in creating a positive atmosphere.

  2. Follow Check-In Procedures: When checking in, have your identification and reservation details ready. Provide any necessary information promptly, such as your credit card for incidentals or a deposit. Listen attentively to any instructions given by the front desk staff, and ask any questions you may have about the hotel facilities or services.

  3. Respect Quiet Hours: Be mindful of quiet hours, typically observed during the late evening and early morning. Keep noise levels to a minimum, especially in shared areas such as hallways and elevators. Avoid slamming doors, speaking loudly, or playing loud music in your room.

  4. Keep Common Areas Clean: Be considerate of others by keeping common areas clean and tidy. Dispose of trash properly, and clean up after yourself in shared spaces like the lobby or breakfast area. If you notice any spills or messes, alert the hotel staff so they can address it promptly.

  5. Conserve Resources: Practice eco-friendly habits by conserving resources during your stay. Turn off lights, TV, and air conditioning when leaving the room, and avoid excessive water usage. Reuse towels and bed linens when possible, indicating your preference to the housekeeping staff if desired.

  6. Follow Smoking Policies: If the hotel is a non-smoking property, adhere to the policy and refrain from smoking in your room or other designated non-smoking areas. If you’re a smoker, utilize designated smoking areas and dispose of cigarette butts properly.

  7. Check-Out on Time: Respect the hotel’s check-out time and ensure you vacate the room by the specified hour. If you need extra time, inquire about late check-out options in advance. Leave the room in good condition, removing any personal belongings and disposing of trash appropriately.

Don’ts:

  1. Don’t Make Excessive Noise: Avoid noisy behavior that may disturb other guests. This includes loud conversations, playing loud music or TV, or engaging in rowdy activities in your room or public areas.

  2. Don’t Abuse Hotel Property: Treat the hotel property with care and respect. Don’t damage or remove any items from the room or common areas. If something is not working or needs attention, report it to the hotel staff rather than trying to fix it yourself.

  3. Don’t Skimp on Tips: When receiving exceptional service from hotel staff, show appreciation by leaving an appropriate tip. This includes bellhops, valet parking attendants, housekeeping, and any other staff members who go above and beyond to assist you.

  4. Don’t Ignore Safety Precautions: Follow any safety guidelines and regulations provided by the hotel, such as using the room safe for valuables or wearing a mask in shared spaces during times of illness outbreaks. Familiarize yourself with emergency exits and procedures in case of an evacuation.

  5. Don’t Overstay Without Notice: If you need to extend your stay or change your reservation, inform the front desk in advance. Avoid overstaying without notifying the hotel, as this may inconvenience both the staff and other guests.

  6. Don’t Be Demanding or Rude: Remember that hotel staff are there to assist you, but they also have their limitations. Avoid being demanding or rude when making requests, and understand that not all requests can be accommodated. Approach any concerns or issues with a calm and respectful demeanor.

  7. Don’t Forget to Leave a Review: After your stay, consider leaving a review of your experience on online platforms. This helps future guests make informed decisions and provides valuable feedback to the hotel management.

By following these do’s and don’ts of hotel etiquette, you’ll contribute to a harmonious and enjoyable atmosphere for everyone. Remember that practicing good manners and respect goes a long way in creating a positive experience during your hotel stay. Enjoy your trip and make the most out of your hospitality experience!
