10 Delicious Ways to Cook with Fresh Seafood


Seafood has long been a staple in many cuisines around the world, and for good reason. Not only is it delicious, but it’s also packed with essential nutrients like omega-3 fatty acids, protein, and various vitamins and minerals. If you’re lucky enough to have access to fresh seafood, whether you live near the coast or have a reliable fishmonger nearby, you’re in for a treat. Here are 10 mouthwatering ways to make the most of fresh seafood in your kitchen.

  1. Grilled Lemon Garlic Shrimp: Start by marinating jumbo shrimp in a mixture of minced garlic, fresh lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Thread them onto skewers and grill until they’re cooked through and slightly charred. Serve with a squeeze of lemon for a simple yet flavorful dish.

  2. Pan-Seared Scallops with Brown Butter: Pat dry large sea scallops and season them with salt and pepper. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and add a tablespoon of butter. Once the butter starts to brown, add the scallops and sear for a few minutes on each side until they develop a golden crust. Finish with a squeeze of lemon juice and chopped parsley.

  3. Crab Cakes with Remoulade Sauce: Mix lump crabmeat with breadcrumbs, minced onion, mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, Old Bay seasoning, and a beaten egg. Form the mixture into patties and pan-fry until golden brown and crispy. Serve with a homemade remoulade sauce made with mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, capers, pickles, and herbs.

  4. Grilled Whole Fish with Herb Butter: Rub a whole fish, such as branzino or trout, with olive oil, salt, pepper, and your favorite herbs, like thyme, rosemary, and parsley. Grill the fish over medium heat until the skin is crispy and the flesh is opaque and flakes easily with a fork. Top with a dollop of herb butter before serving.

  5. Lobster Risotto: Cook Arborio rice in a mixture of seafood stock and white wine until creamy and tender. Stir in cooked lobster meat, freshly grated Parmesan cheese, chopped chives, and a knob of butter until everything is heated through and well combined. This luxurious dish is perfect for special occasions.

  6. Miso Glazed Black Cod: Marinate black cod fillets in a mixture of miso paste, mirin, sake, soy sauce, and sugar for at least 30 minutes. Broil the fish until the glaze is caramelized and the fish is cooked through. Garnish with sliced green onions and toasted sesame seeds for an elegant presentation.

  7. Ceviche: Marinate diced fresh fish, such as halibut or snapper, in a mixture of lime juice, lemon juice, orange juice, diced onion, jalapeño, cilantro, and salt until the fish is ‘cooked’ by the acidity of the citrus juices. Serve the ceviche chilled with tortilla chips or crispy plantain chips for a refreshing appetizer.

  8. Seafood Paella: Sauté onions, bell peppers, and garlic in olive oil until softened. Add Arborio rice and cook until translucent. Stir in saffron-infused broth, diced tomatoes, and a variety of seafood such as shrimp, mussels, clams, and squid. Simmer until the rice is cooked through and the seafood is tender. Garnish with lemon wedges and chopped parsley before serving.

  9. Grilled Swordfish with Mango Salsa: Season swordfish steaks with salt, pepper, and a drizzle of olive oil. Grill the steaks until they’re charred on the outside and cooked to your desired doneness. Serve with a vibrant mango salsa made with diced mango, red onion, jalapeño, cilantro, lime juice, and a pinch of salt.

  10. Baked Stuffed Sole: Stuff sole fillets with a mixture of crabmeat, breadcrumbs, minced shallots, garlic, parsley, lemon zest, and melted butter. Roll up the fillets and place them in a baking dish. Drizzle with white wine and bake until the fish is cooked through and the stuffing is golden brown and crispy.

These are just a few delicious ways to showcase the incredible flavors of fresh seafood. Whether you prefer delicate fish, succulent shellfish, or briny crustaceans, there’s a dish here to satisfy every seafood lover’s cravings. So head to your local fish market, pick up some fresh catches, and get cooking! Your taste buds will thank you.
