Photographing Abstract Art: Elevating Everyday Objects


Abstract art has been a powerful outlet for self-expression and creative exploration for many artists throughout history. With its emphasis on shapes, colors, and forms, abstract art allows artists to break away from traditional representation and delve into the realm of the intangible and the subjective. This unique genre of art presents photographers with a myriad of opportunities to capture intriguing images that elevate everyday objects into extraordinary works of art.

Photographing abstract art requires a keen eye for detail, an understanding of the artistic principles that underpin this genre, and a willingness to experiment and push the boundaries of conventional photography. This article aims to guide photographers on how to approach photographing abstract art and capture images that evoke emotions, spark imagination, and leave a lasting impact.

1. Explore Unique Perspectives

When photographing abstract art, it is important to cultivate a fresh and unique perspective. Look at everyday objects from a new angle, and experiment with different vantage points and compositions. Play with light and shadows to create dynamic contrasts that enhance the abstract qualities of the subject. By approaching your subjects from unexpected angles, you can draw attention to the often-overlooked beauty in ordinary objects.

2. Pay Attention to Shapes and Textures

Abstract art is characterized by its emphasis on shapes and textures. When photographing abstract art, focus on capturing the intricate details and patterns created by these shapes and textures. Experiment with different lens choices, such as macro lenses, to capture the fine details of objects up close. Look for interesting textures, patterns, and lines that can transform mundane objects into captivating abstract compositions.

3. Experiment with Colors

Color plays a significant role in abstract art, as it can convey emotions and create visual impact. When photographing abstract art, pay attention to the colors present in your subject and experiment with different lighting techniques to enhance or subdue specific hues. Play with color contrasts and complementary color combinations to create visually compelling images that evoke strong emotions.

4. Embrace Minimalism

Abstract art often embraces minimalism, focusing on simplicity and reducing subjects to their essential elements. By adopting a minimalist approach, you can create photos with a sense of elegance and tranquility. Look for objects with clean lines and uncluttered backgrounds. Keep the composition simple and eliminate any extraneous elements that may distract from the main subject. The concept of less is more is particularly relevant in abstract photography.

5. Use Reflections and Distortions

Reflections and distortions can add a surreal and ethereal quality to abstract photographs. Experiment with mirrors, glass, water, and other reflective surfaces to capture captivating and distorted reflections of everyday objects. This technique can result in surreal and dream-like images that push the boundaries of reality and challenge the viewer’s perception.

6. Experiment with Camera Movement

One way to add an element of dynamism and energy to abstract photographs is by incorporating camera movement. Experiment with panning, long exposures, and intentional camera movement to create blurred lines and streaks of color. This technique can result in abstract images that convey a sense of motion and fluidity, evoking a sense of energy and vitality.

7. Post-Processing

Post-processing is an essential part of the abstract photography process. Experiment with different editing techniques, such as adjusting the contrast, saturation, and sharpness of your images. Consider converting your images to black and white to emphasize the shapes and textures. Play with different filters and effects to further enhance the abstract qualities of your photographs.

In conclusion, photographing abstract art offers photographers the opportunity to create visually captivating images by elevating everyday objects into works of art. By exploring unique perspectives, embracing shapes, textures, colors, and minimalism, and experimenting with reflections, distortions, camera movement, and post-processing techniques, photographers can unlock their creativity and capture abstract photographs that intrigue and inspire viewers. So grab your camera, step outside your comfort zone, and embark on a journey to discover the abstract art hidden within the world around you.
