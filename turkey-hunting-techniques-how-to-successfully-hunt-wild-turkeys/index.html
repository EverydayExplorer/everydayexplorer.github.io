Turkey Hunting Techniques: How to Successfully Hunt Wild Turkeys


When it comes to hunting, few species are as challenging and rewarding to pursue as the wild turkey. Known for their keen senses, elusive behavior, and remarkable speed, turkeys can provide hunters with a thrilling and exhilarating experience. However, to be successful in hunting these magnificent birds, it is crucial to understand and employ effective turkey hunting techniques. In this article, we will explore some proven strategies that can help you increase your chances of a successful turkey hunt.

Equipment and Gear

Before embarking on your turkey hunting adventure, it is essential to ensure that you have the right equipment and gear. The following items are considered essential for a successful turkey hunt:

  1. Shotgun: When it comes to turkey hunting, a shotgun is the weapon of choice. A 12-gauge shotgun is the most common option, but a 20-gauge shotgun can also be effective at shorter distances. Ensure that your shotgun is properly sighted in and patterned for turkey loads.

  2. Ammunition: It is crucial to use the right ammunition for turkey hunting. Choose turkey-specific loads with tight patterns and heavy shot, such as #4, #5, or #6 shot. These loads deliver sufficient energy and penetrate the turkey’s feathers at extended ranges.

  3. Calls: Turkey calls are excellent tools that allow hunters to mimic the sounds made by turkeys. There are various types of calls available, including box calls, slate calls, and mouth calls. Practice using different calls to master their sounds and be able to effectively communicate with the turkeys.

  4. Camouflage Clothing: Turkeys have excellent eyesight and can detect the slightest movements. Wearing camouflage clothing that matches the surroundings is crucial for blending in and staying undetected. Opt for patterns that mimic the natural environment, such as Mossy Oak or Realtree.

  5. Decoys: Turkey decoys can be incredibly effective in attracting the birds within range. There are a wide variety of decoys available, including lifelike full-body decoys, foam decoys, and collapsible decoys. Experiment with different decoy setups and positions to find what works best for you.

Scouting and Locating Turkeys

One of the most critical steps in turkey hunting is scouting and identifying turkey habitats. The following tips will help you locate turkeys before your hunt:

  1. Field Scouting: Look for turkey sign such as tracks, feathers, dusting areas, and droppings in fields and open areas. Turkeys often feed in these locations, and their presence can be a good indicator of their activity.

  2. Roosting Sites: Turkeys roost in trees overnight for safety. Look for large trees with flattened leaves and droppings underneath them, as these are common signs of roosting sites. Turkeys tend to return to the same roosting areas, so identifying them can increase your chances of a successful hunt.

  3. Listening for Gobbling: During the early morning and late afternoon, male turkeys (known as toms or gobblers) will often gobble to announce their presence to hens. Listen for gobbling sounds to pinpoint their location. Gobbling can help you determine where the turkeys are and even the number of birds in the area.

  4. Using Trail Cameras: Trail cameras can be valuable tools for scouting turkeys. Set up cameras near known turkey habitats or feeding areas and review the images to identify patterns and determine when and where the turkeys are active.

Calling and Turkey Behavior

Mastering turkey calls and understanding turkey behavior is essential for a successful hunt. Here are some tips to enhance your calling techniques and attract turkeys:

  1. Learn the Calls: Practice various turkey calls to imitate different sounds, such as yelps, clucks, purrs, and gobbles. Each call mimics a specific turkey vocalization and can be used to elicit different responses. Learning and perfecting these calls will increase your chances of enticing turkeys.

  2. Call Softly and Sparingly: Turkeys have excellent hearing and can pinpoint the source of a sound. Start with soft calls and gradually increase the volume if needed. Avoid overcalling, as this can make the turkeys suspicious.

  3. Use Decoys: Positioning decoys strategically can attract turkeys and bring them within shooting range. Pairing a hen decoy with a jake or gobbler decoy can create a realistic setup that entices toms into range. Experiment with different decoy setups and positions to determine what works best for your hunting area.

  4. Concealment and Patience: Set up in a concealed location, using natural cover or a blind to remain hidden from the keen eyesight of turkeys. Patience is crucial when turkey hunting, as it can take time for a turkey to respond to your calls or decoys.

Setting Up and Shot Placement

Properly setting up and knowing where to aim when a turkey is within range will greatly increase your chances of making a clean and ethical kill.

  1. Create a Concealed Setup: When selecting a hunting location, choose an area with good visibility and cover. Set up your decoys within range, facing towards your position. Ensure that your position allows for a clear shooting lane and provides opportunities for a safe shot.

  2. Aim for the Vital Area: The vital area of a turkey is located around the base of the neck and upper chest. When the head and neck are extended, aim just below the turkey’s wattle for an effective shot. This area provides a high chance of hitting vital organs and stopping the turkey in its tracks.

  3. Wait for a Clear Shot: It is crucial to wait for a clear, ethical shot opportunity when hunting turkeys. Patience is key, as turkeys can be extremely wary and may require careful positioning for a clean shot. Practice shot placement and make sure you are comfortable with your shotgun’s effective range.

Final Thoughts

Turkey hunting can be a thrilling and rewarding pursuit for hunters of all skill levels. By equipping yourself with the right gear, scouting effectively, mastering turkey calls, and employing proper shot placement, you can significantly increase your chances of success in the field. Remember to always prioritize safety and ethical hunting practices.

So, what are you waiting for? Get out there and embrace the challenge of hunting wild turkeys. Happy hunting!
