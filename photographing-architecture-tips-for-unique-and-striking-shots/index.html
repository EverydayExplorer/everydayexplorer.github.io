Photographing Architecture: Tips for Unique and Striking Shots


Architecture has always fascinated photographers. The way a building is designed, the curves, lines, and shapes, all create a visual language that can be captured through the lens. If you have a passion for photography and an interest in architecture, then experimenting with architectural photography can be a fantastic way to combine your two passions.

In this blog post, we will provide you with some valuable tips and techniques to capture unique and striking shots of architecture. Whether you’re shooting historic landmarks, modern skyscrapers, or even residential buildings, these tips will help you take your architectural photography skills to the next level.

1. Research and Plan: Before heading out with your camera, it’s essential to do some research about the architecture you’re planning to photograph. Learn about the history and significance of the building. Understanding its design, purpose, and unique features will give you a better perspective on how to capture it effectively. Additionally, plan your shoot by considering the time of day, lighting conditions, and the angles you want to shoot from. This preparation will help you save time and ensure you get the shots you envision.

2. Choose the Right Equipment: While you can capture stunning architectural shots with any camera, a DSLR or mirrorless camera with interchangeable lenses will give you more flexibility. Wide-angle lenses such as 16-35mm or even ultra-wide-angle lenses like 14-24mm are commonly used in architectural photography as they allow you to capture the entire building without distortion. A sturdy tripod is also essential to keep your camera steady and enable long exposure shots if required.

3. Pay Attention to Lighting: Lighting plays a pivotal role in architectural photography. Consider shooting during the golden hour, which is roughly an hour after sunrise or an hour before sunset. The warm, soft light during these times will beautifully illuminate your subject. If you’re shooting during other times of the day, pay attention to the direction and quality of light. Experiment with shooting in different lighting conditions to capture unique perspectives and moods.

4. Utilize Lines and Shapes: Architecture is all about lines, shapes, and patterns. Utilize these elements to create visually striking compositions. Look for leading lines that draw the viewer’s eye into the frame. Experiment with symmetry and asymmetry to add a sense of balance or tension to your shots. Incorporate curves and diagonals to add dynamism and depth to your images. By understanding how to use lines and shapes, you can create compelling visual narratives that make your architectural shots stand out.

5. Explore Perspectives and Angles: Don’t be afraid to experiment with different perspectives and angles. Sometimes the most ordinary buildings can become extraordinary when captured from unique viewpoints. Get up high and shoot from a rooftop or balcony for an aerial perspective. Get down low and shoot upwards to emphasize the height and grandeur of a structure. Play with different angles and compositions to find the most interesting and engaging viewpoints that highlight the architecture’s essence.

6. Capture Details: While capturing the entire building is essential, don’t forget to focus on the smaller details as well. Architectural elements like doors, windows, staircases, and textures can add depth and character to your shots. Zoom in and capture these details up close to showcase the craftsmanship and intricate design. These architectural details often tell a story of their own, and capturing them can result in visually captivating images.

7. Consider People and Scale: Including people in your architectural shots can add a sense of scale and a human element to your photographs. It helps viewers to connect with the architecture on a more personal level and understand the purpose and function of the building. Look for interesting interactions between people and the architecture, whether it’s someone walking through a grand entrance or a group of friends enjoying a public space. Including people can also bring life and dynamism to your compositions.

8. Post-processing: After capturing your architectural shots, spend some time post-processing them to enhance their visual impact. Adjust the exposure, contrast, and colors to bring out the textures and details in your images. Correct any perspective distortions if necessary. Use tools like dodge and burn to selectively enhance specific areas. However, be cautious not to overdo it and maintain the authenticity and integrity of the architectural subject.

9. Never Stop Exploring and Practicing: Architecture is always evolving, and there is no shortage of stunning architectural marvels around the world. Explore different cities, countries, and cultures to expand your architectural photography portfolio. Visit famous landmarks as well as lesser-known structures, as both can offer unique photographic opportunities. The more you practice, the more you’ll develop an eye for capturing architectural beauty.

In conclusion, photographing architecture is a rewarding and creative genre of photography. By following these tips and techniques, you can capture unique and striking shots that showcase the beauty and significance of architectural wonders. Remember to research and plan, choose the right equipment, pay attention to lighting, utilize lines and shapes, explore perspectives and angles, capture details, consider people and scale, post-process your images, and never stop exploring and practicing. So grab your camera and go out there to capture the beauty of architecture through your lens!

Remember, the key to successful architectural photography lies in your ability to see the beauty and uniqueness in the structures around you. Whether you’re shooting classic designs or modern masterpieces, it’s all about capturing the essence and spirit of the architecture through your lens. Happy shooting!
