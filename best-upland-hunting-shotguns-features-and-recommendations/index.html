Best Upland Hunting Shotguns: Features and Recommendations


When it comes to upland hunting, having the right shotgun is crucial. Upland game birds like pheasants, grouse, and quails can be quick and elusive, requiring a shotgun that is both reliable and versatile. In this blog, we will be discussing some of the best upland hunting shotguns on the market, their features, and our top recommendations for your next hunting adventure.

Before we dive into our recommendations, let’s take a moment to understand what features make a shotgun suitable for upland hunting. Firstly, it is important to consider the gauge of the shotgun. For upland hunting, a 12 or 20 gauge shotgun is a popular choice. These gauges provide a good balance between power and recoil, making them perfect for taking down upland game birds.

Next, the weight and balance of the shotgun are important factors to consider. Upland hunting often involves hours of walking through fields and cover, so having a lightweight shotgun that is easy to carry can make a big difference. Additionally, a shotgun with good balance can help improve accuracy and reduce fatigue during long hunting sessions.

Another important feature to look for in an upland hunting shotgun is reliability. Upland hunting can be unpredictable, with quick and unexpected shots. Having a shotgun that is known for its reliability and durability is essential to ensure a successful hunt. Look for shotguns that are built with quality materials and have a proven track record of performance in the field.

Now that we have a better understanding of the features to look for, let’s dive into our top recommendations for upland hunting shotguns.

1. Browning Citori 725 Field: The Browning Citori 725 Field is a top-choice for many upland hunters. It features a lightweight design, thanks to its steel receiver and Grade III Walnut stock. The shotgun is available in both 12 and 20 gauge, allowing hunters to choose the gauge that best suits their preferences. The Citori 725 Field also boasts a mechanical trigger system, ensuring consistent and reliable shot breaks. Additionally, its Invector-DS choke system provides versatility and enhances shot patterns.

2. Beretta A400 Upland: Beretta is a well-known and respected brand in the shotgun industry, and their A400 Upland shotgun is a testament to their expertise. This shotgun is available in 12 gauge and features a traditional walnut stock that adds to its aesthetics. The Beretta A400 Upland incorporates advanced technologies like the Blink operating system, which allows for fast and reliable cycling of rounds. The gun’s Micro-Core recoil pad also helps reduce felt recoil, allowing for more comfortable shooting.

3. Winchester SX4 Upland Field: The Winchester SX4 Upland Field is an excellent choice for upland hunting, combining affordability with quality. It features a lightweight alloy receiver, making it easy to carry during long hunts. The shotgun is available in both 12 and 20 gauge, offering options for hunters of all preferences. The SX4 Upland Field also incorporates an Active Valve gas system, ensuring reliable cycling no matter the conditions. Its Inflex Technology recoil pad helps reduce recoil and improve shooting comfort.

4. Benelli Montefeltro: The Benelli Montefeltro is a popular choice among upland hunters due to its lightweight design and reliability. This shotgun is available in 12 and 20 gauge, offering options for various hunting scenarios. It incorporates the Inertia Driven System, which ensures consistent and reliable cycling of rounds. The Montefeltro features a weather-resistant synthetic stock, making it suitable for hunting in all conditions. Its slim and ergonomic design provides a comfortable grip, enhancing control and accuracy.

5. Remington V3 Field Sport: The Remington V3 Field Sport is a versatile and affordable option for upland hunting. It boasts a lightweight design, thanks to its aluminum receiver, making it easy to carry during long hunting trips. The shotgun is available in 12 gauge and features a synthetic stock that is resistant to various weather conditions. The V3 Field Sport incorporates the VersaPort gas system, which provides reliable cycling and reduced felt recoil. Its adjustable length of pull and drop and cast shims allow for customization to fit various shooters.

In conclusion, choosing the right shotgun for upland hunting is crucial for a successful and enjoyable hunting experience. By considering factors such as gauge, weight, balance, reliability, and overall performance, you can find the perfect shotgun that suits your needs and preferences. The Browning Citori 725 Field, Beretta A400 Upland, Winchester SX4 Upland Field, Benelli Montefeltro, and Remington V3 Field Sport are all excellent options to consider. So, gear up with one of these shotguns, and get ready for an exciting upland hunting adventure!
