Rum Cocktails: From Daiquiris to Zombies


Rum is a beloved spirit that has been enjoyed for centuries, and it’s no wonder why. This versatile liquor offers a wide range of flavors, from light and fruity to rich and complex, making it a go-to choice for mixologists and cocktail enthusiasts alike. Whether you’re sipping a classic Daiquiri on a tropical beach or indulging in the mysterious depths of a Zombie cocktail, rum has something for everyone. In this blog, we’ll take a journey through the world of rum cocktails, exploring their history, ingredients, and some exciting variations you can try at home.

The History of Rum Cocktails

Rum has a storied history that dates back to the 17th century when sugarcane was first cultivated in the Caribbean. As sugarcane plantations flourished, so did the production of rum. Early rum was a rough and unrefined spirit enjoyed by sailors and pirates, but over time, it evolved into a sophisticated and diverse category of liquor.

The concept of cocktails, in general, emerged in the 18th century, and rum quickly found its place in these concoctions. Sailors mixed it with citrus fruits to prevent scurvy, and bartenders began experimenting with various ingredients to create the delightful drinks we know today.
