Iconic Music Videos That Redefined the Visual Aspect of Music


Music has always been a powerful form of self-expression, but when combined with visual storytelling, it has the ability to create an even more profound impact. Throughout the history of music, there have been certain music videos that have not only captured our attention but have also redefined the way we perceive music visually. These iconic music videos have pushed boundaries, challenged norms, and left an indelible mark on popular culture. In this blog post, we will explore some of these groundbreaking music videos that have redefined the visual aspect of music.

1. ‘Thriller’ by Michael Jackson:
Released in 1983, ‘Thriller’ is often hailed as one of the greatest music videos of all time. Directed by John Landis, the video showcased Michael Jackson’s incredible dance moves and storytelling abilities. It revolutionized the music video industry by introducing complex narrative structures, cinematic production values, and high-quality special effects. With its iconic dance sequences and horror-inspired storyline, ‘Thriller’ set a new standard for music videos and paved the way for future visual masterpieces.

2. ‘Take on Me’ by A-ha:
Released in 1985, ‘Take on Me’ by A-ha combined live-action and animation to create a visually stunning music video. Directed by Steve Barron, the video tells the story of a young woman who is pulled into a comic book world by a mysterious man. The use of rotoscoping, a technique that involves tracing over live-action footage frame by frame, gave the video a unique and captivating visual style. ‘Take on Me’ showcased the potential of animation in music videos and inspired countless artists to experiment with different visual techniques.

3. ‘Sledgehammer’ by Peter Gabriel:
Directed by Stephen R. Johnson, ‘Sledgehammer’ is a groundbreaking music video that utilized stop-motion animation, claymation, and various visual effects. Released in 1986, the video featured Peter Gabriel interacting with various surreal and imaginative scenes, including a dancing chicken, a singing flower, and his own face melting. The innovative use of animation techniques and the meticulous attention to detail made ‘Sledgehammer’ a visual feast for the eyes. It pushed the boundaries of what was possible in a music video and inspired future generations of artists to think outside the box.

4. ‘Bad’ by Michael Jackson:
Directed by Martin Scorsese, ‘Bad’ is another iconic music video that redefined the visual aspect of music. Released in 1987, the video tells the story of a young man from a disadvantaged background who gets caught up in a world of crime. With its gritty urban setting and powerful choreography, ‘Bad’ showcased Michael Jackson’s ability to tell compelling stories through music videos. It also highlighted the importance of narrative and character development in visual storytelling.

5. ‘Formation’ by Beyoncé:
Released in 2016, ‘Formation’ by Beyoncé is a visually stunning music video that celebrates black culture and addresses social issues. Directed by Melina Matsoukas, the video showcases powerful imagery, including scenes of Beyoncé submerged in water, dancers forming powerful formations, and references to Hurricane Katrina. ‘Formation’ not only pushed the boundaries of visual storytelling but also sparked important conversations about race, identity, and empowerment.

These are just a few examples of the many music videos that have redefined the visual aspect of music. Each of these videos brought something unique and innovative to the table, leaving a lasting impact on the music industry. From complex narratives to groundbreaking animation techniques, these videos have shown us the power of combining music with visual storytelling. As artists continue to push boundaries and experiment with new ideas, we can only imagine what the future holds for the visual aspect of music.
