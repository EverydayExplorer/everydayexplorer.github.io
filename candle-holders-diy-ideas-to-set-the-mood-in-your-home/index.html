Candle Holders: DIY Ideas to Set the Mood in Your Home


When it comes to decorating your home, there are plenty of ways you can add personality and set the mood. One of the easiest and most affordable ways to do this is by incorporating candle holders into your decor. Candle holders can add warmth, ambiance, and a touch of elegance to any room. Plus, with a little bit of creativity, you can make your own DIY candle holders that are both unique and functional.

In this blog, we’ll take a look at some DIY ideas to help you create your own candle holders that will set the mood in your home.

1. Mason Jar Candle Holder

Mason jars are a versatile and affordable option for creating DIY candle holders. You can use a variety of items to decorate your mason jars, such as ribbons, lace, burlap, or even paint. You can also fill your jars with decorative stones or sand to add some weight and stability.

To create a mason jar candle holder, simply place a votive candle in the center of the jar and light it. The jar will reflect the light and create an enchanting ambiance in any room.

2. Wine Bottle Candle Holder

If you have an empty wine bottle lying around, why not turn it into a candle holder? You will need to purchase a taper candle holder that fits snugly in the neck of the bottle. You can also add some decorative elements to the bottle such as twine, ribbon, or beads.

To create a wine bottle candle holder, fill the bottom of the bottle with decorative stones or sand to add some weight and stability. Then, insert the taper candle holder into the neck of the bottle. Place a taper candle in the holder and light it. Your wine bottle candle holder will add a touch of sophistication to any room.

3. Seashell Candle Holder

For a beach-inspired candle holder, why not try making your own seashell candle holder? You can pick up some seashells from a craft store or collect them yourself during a beach vacation.

To create a seashell candle holder, choose a few larger shells that can hold a votive candle. Clean the shells thoroughly and then place a votive candle in the center of each shell. You can also add some sand or pebbles to the bottom of the shell to add some weight and stability.

4. Birch Log Candle Holder

If you are looking for a rustic and natural look, a birch log candle holder may be the perfect option for you. You’ll need to find a birch log that is slightly larger in diameter than your votive candles.

To create a birch log candle holder, start by cutting the log into sections that are just slightly taller than your votive candles. You’ll then need to hollow out the top of each section so that the votive candle can sit securely inside. You can also carve out some decorative patterns into the birch log to add some visual interest.

5. Tea Cup Candle Holder

What better way to repurpose old tea cups than by turning them into candle holders? You’ll need to find tea cups that are slightly larger than your votive candles.

To create a tea cup candle holder, start by placing a small amount of decorative stones or sand in the bottom of the cup to add some weight and stability. Then, place a votive candle in the center and light it. You can also add some decorative elements to the tea cup, such as a ribbon or lace.

Conclusion

Decorating your home with candle holders can be both fun and functional. With these DIY ideas, you can create unique candle holders that will set the mood in any room. From mason jars to birch logs, there are plenty of options to choose from to suit your personal style. So why not get started on your very own candle holders today?
