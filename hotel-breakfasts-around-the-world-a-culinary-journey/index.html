Hotel Breakfasts Around the World: A Culinary Journey


Eating breakfast at a hotel can be a delightful experience, especially when you’re traveling and eager to explore new cultures and cuisines. Hotel breakfasts can offer a glimpse into the local cuisine, providing travelers with a culinary journey without having to step foot outside the hotel.

From delicious pastries to traditional dishes, hotel breakfasts around the world can truly be a feast for the senses. In this blog post, we will take you on a gastronomic adventure as we explore different hotel breakfasts from various countries.

Let’s start our culinary journey in France, where breakfast is considered an important meal. French hotel breakfasts typically consist of an assortment of pastries, such as croissants, pain au chocolat, and brioche. These light, flaky pastries are often accompanied by butter and jams, and of course, a freshly brewed cup of coffee.

Jumping over to the United States, we find a different take on breakfast. American hotel breakfasts often include a selection of cereals, toasts, and bagels. You’ll also find a variety of hot dishes like bacon, eggs, sausages, and pancakes. This hearty breakfast is perfect for those who prefer a filling start to the day.

Next up, we head to Japan, where breakfast is a delicate and beautifully presented affair. Japanese hotel breakfasts usually feature steamed rice, miso soup, grilled fish, and pickled vegetables. The emphasis is on fresh, seasonal ingredients and a balanced meal. This nutritious breakfast provides a great start to the day and gives a glimpse into the traditional Japanese cuisine.

Moving on to Mexico, breakfast takes on a spicy twist. Mexican hotel breakfasts often include dishes such as chilaquiles, a traditional breakfast dish made with tortilla chips, salsa, cheese, and eggs. You might also find tamales, stuffed with savory fillings like chicken or cheese. To wash it all down, a refreshing glass of freshly squeezed fruit juice or a cup of Mexican coffee is a must.

Heading across to the Middle East, we arrive in Israel, where breakfast is a sensational affair. Israeli hotel breakfasts feature a variety of local specialties such as hummus, falafel, labneh, and shakshuka. Alongside these delicious dishes, you’ll find an array of freshly baked bread, olives, and salad. This breakfast spread is a food lover’s dream and offers a unique blend of Mediterranean and Middle Eastern flavors.

Now, let’s travel to South Korea, where breakfast is all about the variety and balance. Korean hotel breakfasts typically include a range of options including rice, soup, kimchi, and various banchan (side dishes). You may also find popular street food items like hotteok (sweet pancakes filled with brown sugar) or gyeran-ppang (egg bread). This breakfast is a true reflection of the diverse flavors of Korean cuisine.

Last but not least, we land in Egypt, where breakfast is a grand affair. Egyptian hotel breakfasts often include a combination of local dishes like ful medames (a dish made with fava beans), taameya (Egyptian falafel), and pickled vegetables. To satisfy your sweet tooth, you can indulge in a serving of basbousa (a sweet semolina cake) or konafa (a cheese-filled pastry soaked in syrup). These hearty and flavorful breakfasts are sure to leave you satisfied.

As you can see, hotel breakfasts around the world offer a wide range of flavors and experiences. Whether you prefer a light and flaky croissant in France or a spicy chilaquiles in Mexico, there is something for everyone. These breakfasts not only provide nourishment but also offer a window into the local culture and culinary traditions.

So, the next time you find yourself staying in a hotel abroad, make sure to savor the hotel breakfast spread. It is an opportunity to embark on a culinary journey without even stepping outside the hotel.
