How to Clean and Maintain Your Fishing Reel


Fishing reels are an essential piece of equipment for any angler. They help to control the line and provide smooth operation when reeling in fish. However, like any other piece of equipment, fishing reels require regular cleaning and maintenance to ensure optimal performance and longevity.

In this blog post, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to clean and maintain your fishing reel, along with some helpful tips and techniques. Following these steps will not only extend the lifespan of your reel but also improve its overall performance and functionality.

Why is Cleaning and Maintaining Your Fishing Reel Important?

Regular cleaning and maintenance of your fishing reel are crucial for several reasons:

  1. Preventing Corrosion: Exposure to saltwater, dirt, sand, and other contaminants can cause corrosion on the reel’s metal components. Cleaning and lubricating the reel will help prevent this corrosion and ensure the smooth operation of the reel.

  2. Maintaining Performance: Over time, dirt, debris, and fishing line residue can accumulate on the reel’s internal parts, such as the gears and bearings. Regular cleaning will help remove these particles, which can affect the reel’s performance.

  3. Extending Lifespan: By properly cleaning and maintaining your fishing reel, you can extend its lifespan, saving you money in the long run. Well-maintained reels are less likely to suffer from mechanical failures and require costly repairs or replacements.

Step-by-Step Guide to Clean and Maintain Your Fishing Reel

Now, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of cleaning and maintaining your fishing reel:

1. Gather the Necessary Tools and Materials

Before you start cleaning your fishing reel, gather the following tools and materials:

  • Clean cloth or towel: Use a soft, lint-free cloth to wipe away dirt and debris.
  • Reel oil and grease: Choose a reputable brand of reel oil and grease to lubricate the moving parts.
  • Toothbrush or small brush: Use a toothbrush or a small brush with soft bristles to clean hard-to-reach areas.
  • Mild soap or detergent: Use a mild soap or detergent for cleaning the reel’s exterior.
  • Warm water: You’ll need warm water to rinse the reel and remove any soap residue.
  • Screwdriver or Allen wrench: Depending on your reel, you may need a screwdriver or Allen wrench to disassemble certain parts.

2. Disassemble Your Fishing Reel

Carefully disassemble your fishing reel, following the manufacturer’s instructions or referencing any provided diagrams or videos. Note how each part fits together and take pictures if necessary for reference during reassembly.

3. Clean the Exterior of the Reel

Use a mild soap or detergent and warm water to clean the exterior of the reel. Gently scrub away dirt, grime, and salt residues using a soft brush or cloth. Be sure to clean all sides, including the handle, spool, and drag system. Rinse the reel thoroughly with warm water to remove any soap residue.

4. Clean the Internal Components

Using a toothbrush or small brush, carefully clean the internal components of the reel. Pay close attention to the gears, anti-reverse mechanism, and bearings, as these are crucial for the reel’s performance. Remove any dirt, debris, or fishing line residue that may have accumulated on these parts.

5. Lubricate the Reel Components

Apply reel oil and grease to the necessary parts of the reel. Follow the manufacturer’s recommendations for the specific areas that require lubrication. Common areas include the gears, bearings, bail arm, and handle. Avoid over-lubricating, as excessive grease can attract dirt and clog the reel.

6. Reassemble the Reel

Once you’ve cleaned and lubricated the components, carefully reassemble the reel in the reverse order of disassembly. Refer to the pictures or diagrams you took earlier to ensure everything is put back correctly. Tighten all screws or fasteners to the appropriate tension, but be careful not to overtighten and risk damaging the reel.

7. Perform a Test Run

After reassembling the reel, perform a test run by spooling some fishing line and reeling it in. Pay attention to any unusual noises, vibrations, or resistance. If anything seems amiss, disassemble the reel again to identify and fix the issue before proceeding.

8. Regular Maintenance Schedule

To keep your fishing reel in top condition, establish a regular maintenance schedule. Depending on your usage and fishing environment, you may need to clean and maintain your reel after every fishing trip or at least once a season. Regularly check the reel for signs of wear, corrosion, or damage and address any issues promptly.

Additional Tips for Cleaning and Maintenance

  • Avoid submerging the reel: While cleaning, avoid submerging the entire reel in water, as this can damage the internal components. Instead, focus on the individual parts that require cleaning.
  • Protect the drag system: When cleaning the drag system, be careful not to get oil or grease on the drag washers. This can reduce their friction and affect the reel’s drag performance.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals: Stick to mild soaps and detergents when cleaning your reel. Harsh chemicals can damage the reel’s finish and internal components.
  • Store your reel properly: When not in use, store your fishing reel in a cool, dry place to prevent dust, moisture, and humidity from affecting its performance.
  • Seek professional help if needed: If you’re unsure about disassembling or cleaning specific parts of your reel, consult the manufacturer’s manual or seek professional assistance from a local tackle shop.

By following these cleaning and maintenance steps, you’ll ensure that your fishing reel remains in excellent condition, providing you with reliable performance and a seamless fishing experience for years to come.

Remember, regular cleaning and maintenance are key to extending the lifespan of your reel, preventing corrosion, and optimizing its performance. Take care of your fishing gear, and it will take care of you on your fishing adventures!
