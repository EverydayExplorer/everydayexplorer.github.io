How to Properly Care for and Preserve Your Hunting Trophies


As hunters, we all understand the thrill of the chase, the adrenaline rush as we patiently wait for our target, and the sense of accomplishment when we finally bag our trophy. Hunting trophies are not only symbols of our successful hunts but also cherished mementos that hold sentimental value. However, these trophies require proper care and preservation to ensure their longevity and appeal. In this blog, we will delve into the steps on how to properly care for and preserve your hunting trophies, so they can be enjoyed for years to come.

Field Care:  
The first step in preserving your hunting trophies starts in the field. After successfully harvesting your game, it is crucial to handle it with care to minimize damage. Make sure to clean the animal properly by removing any excess blood or debris from the exterior. Additionally, take care to not cut or damage any areas that will need to be taxidermied or preserved.

Quick Cooling:  
Once the animal has been field-dressed, it's vital to cool it down as soon as possible. This step helps minimize bacteria growth, which can cause deterioration and negatively impact the overall quality of the trophy. Consider packing the cavity with ice or placing cold packs around the carcass to maintain a cool temperature until you can properly store it.

Choosing a Taxidermist:  
Selecting a skilled and reputable taxidermist is a critical aspect of preserving your hunting trophy. Research and ask fellow hunters for recommendations to find a taxidermist with a proven track record. Consult with them to discuss your specific requirements and ensure they have experience working with the type of animal you've hunted.

Proper Storage:  
Before handing over your trophy to the taxidermist, it's important to store it correctly. Avoid exposing the trophy to excessive heat, humidity, or direct sunlight as these factors can cause damage and deterioration. Choose a cool, dry, and well-ventilated area for temporary storage until you can hand it over to the taxidermist.

Care during Transport:  
If you plan to transport your hunting trophy over long distances, take extra precautions to prevent any damage. Wrap the trophy carefully in plastic or a protective covering to shield it from dust, moisture, and potential physical impact during transit. Secure it in a sturdy container that offers full protection against shocks or jostling.

Proper Documentation:  
To comply with hunting regulations and demonstrate your ethical hunting practices, ensure that you have all the necessary permits and documentation required for your trophies. Familiarize yourself with local laws governing the transportation of animal remains and obtain permits accordingly. Adhering to these regulations not only prevents legal issues but also ensures that your trophies can be enjoyed without any reservation.

Regular Cleaning and Maintenance:  
Once your hunting trophy is completed and proudly displayed, it's crucial to regularly clean and maintain it to preserve its appearance. Dust the trophy gently using a soft, lint-free cloth, avoiding excessive pressure or harsh cleaners that could damage the fur or feathers. Occasionally, consult your taxidermist for specific cleaning recommendations based on the type of trophy and materials used.

Protection from Pests:  
Nothing can be more disheartening than discovering that pests have damaged your prized hunting trophy. To safeguard against this, take preventive measures such as using mothballs or cedar chips near displays or storage areas. Regularly inspect the trophy for any signs of pest infestation and take immediate action if detected.

Avoid Excessive Handling:  
While it's natural to want to proudly show off your hunting trophies, excessive handling can lead to wear and tear. Limit the amount of touching or handling to preserve the original condition. Encourage others to admire from a distance and avoid high-traffic areas where accidental damage is more likely to occur.

Consider Professional Restoration:  
Over time, hunting trophies may require minor repairs or restoration due to natural aging or accidental damage. If you notice any deterioration or issues with your trophy, consult a professional taxidermist or restoration specialist. They possess the necessary skills and knowledge to restore your trophy to its former glory.

In conclusion, properly caring for and preserving your hunting trophies is essential to maintaining their beauty and longevity. From field care to choosing a skilled taxidermist and practicing regular maintenance, every step is crucial. By following these guidelines, your hunting trophies will continue to be a source of pride and a lasting testament to your hunting adventures for generations to come.
