The Best Music Podcasts to Tune Into for Music Lovers


Are you a music enthusiast looking for new ways to indulge in your passion for music? Podcasts have become a treasure trove for music lovers, offering a diverse array of shows that cater to different musical tastes, genres, and interests. Whether you’re into discovering new artists, exploring the history of music, or simply want to stay updated with the latest music trends, there’s a podcast out there for you. To help you navigate the sea of music podcasts, we’ve curated a list of the best music podcasts that are worth tuning into.

1. Song Exploder

Delve into the art of music creation with Song Exploder. Each episode features an artist breaking down their song, unraveling the intricate layers of composition, lyrics, and production. This podcast provides a fascinating insight into the creative process, allowing listeners to gain a deeper appreciation for the music they love.

2. Dissect

For those who crave in-depth analysis and interpretation of iconic albums, Dissect is a must-listen. Host Cole Cuchna meticulously dissects one album per season, exploring the themes, production, and lyrics in a way that unveils new layers of meaning and significance.

3. All Songs Considered

NPR’s All Songs Considered is a goldmine for music discovery. Hosts Bob Boilen and Robin Hilton curate an eclectic mix of new music, interviews, and live performances, providing a platform for emerging artists alongside established ones. If you’re looking to broaden your musical horizons, this podcast is an essential listen.

4. The Bobbycast

Country music fans, look no further than The Bobbycast hosted by Bobby Bones. This podcast offers candid conversations with country music artists, songwriters, and industry insiders, providing an intimate glimpse into the world of country music and its vibrant community.

5. Switched on Pop

Get ready to dive deep into the mechanics of pop music with Switched on Pop. Musicologist Nate Sloan and songwriter Charlie Harding dissect the intricacies of popular songs, exploring the cultural and musical significance behind the catchiest tunes of our time.

6. The Trap Set

For those fascinated by the art of drumming and the stories behind the drummers, The Trap Set is a captivating podcast. Host Joe Wong engages in intimate and revealing conversations with esteemed drummers, uncovering the personal and professional journeys that have shaped their craft.

7. Tiny Desk Concerts

While not a traditional podcast, NPR’s Tiny Desk Concerts are an audiovisual feast for music lovers. Featuring intimate performances by a diverse range of artists, these concerts offer a front-row seat to raw, stripped-down musical performances that are as intimate as they are captivating.

8. The Jasta Show

Metalheads, rejoice! The Jasta Show, hosted by Jamey Jasta of Hatebreed fame, is a no-holds-barred podcast featuring conversations with metal and hardcore artists. Expect unfiltered discussions and behind-the-scenes insights into the world of heavy music.

9. Broken Record

Join host Rick Rubin, legendary music producer, in conversations with artists, producers, and songwriters as they share personal stories, insights, and reflections on the art of music-making. Broken Record offers a rare glimpse into the minds of music icons and the creative processes that drive their work.

10. The 500 with Josh Adam Meyers

Explore the Rolling Stone magazine’s list of the 500 greatest albums with comedian Josh Adam Meyers. Each episode is dedicated to dissecting and celebrating a specific album from the iconic list, offering a blend of humor, music trivia, and genuine appreciation for the timeless classics.

Whether you’re seeking musical enlightenment, behind-the-scenes stories, or a platform for new discoveries, these podcasts cater to a wide spectrum of musical interests. So, grab your headphones, immerse yourself in the world of music podcasts, and let the melodies and stories transport you to a realm of endless musical fascination. Happy listening!
