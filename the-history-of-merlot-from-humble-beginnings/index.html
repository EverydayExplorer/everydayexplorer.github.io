The History of Merlot: From Humble Beginnings


Merlot is a red wine varietal that has gained immense popularity over the years. Known for its smoothness, richness, and versatility, Merlot has become a favorite choice for wine enthusiasts around the world. But have you ever wondered about the history of this beloved wine? How did it come to be, and what makes it so special? Join us as we delve into the fascinating journey of Merlot, from its humble beginnings to its current status as a renowned wine.

The origins of Merlot can be traced back to France, specifically the Bordeaux region. The word Merlot itself is derived from the French word ‘merle,’ which means blackbird. This is likely due to the grape’s dark blue color, reminiscent of the black feathers of a blackbird. The first recorded mention of Merlot dates back to the late 18th century when it was referred to as ‘Merlau’ in the region.

Merlot’s journey to popularity began in the 19th century when it started to be recognized as a grape variety worthy of attention. It gained acclaim for its ability to produce wines with soft tannins, a supple texture, and delightful flavors. The wines crafted from Merlot grapes were seen as approachable with a wide appeal and quickly found favor among wine lovers.

In Bordeaux, Merlot became an integral part of the wine blends alongside other grape varieties such as Cabernet Sauvignon and Cabernet Franc. Its plush fruitiness and smoothness balanced out the tannins of these other grapes, enhancing the overall complexity and drinkability of the wines. This blending practice remains prevalent to this day, with some of the most celebrated wines from Bordeaux containing a significant proportion of Merlot.

While Bordeaux played a significant role in Merlot’s history, it was the 20th century that saw the grape’s true rise to worldwide recognition. In the 1960s and 1970s, Merlot began to gain traction beyond Bordeaux’s borders. It found success in the New World wine regions, particularly in California and Chile, where winemakers recognized its potential and started cultivating it extensively.

Merlot’s popularity skyrocketed in the 1990s, fueled in part by the cult-classic movie ‘Sideways.’ The film, centered around the wine country of California, brought Merlot into the mainstream consciousness. However, it also inadvertently led to a decline in Merlot’s reputation when the lead character famously proclaimed, ‘I am not drinking any [expletive] Merlot!’ Despite this setback, Merlot persevered and continued to be cherished by wine lovers around the world.

Today, Merlot is grown in various wine regions across the globe, from France and Italy to the United States, Australia, Chile, and beyond. Its adaptability to different climates and soil types, combined with its smooth and approachable character, has made it a favorite for winemakers worldwide. Merlot wines can vary in style, ranging from rich and full-bodied to soft and fruity, offering a vast array of options for wine enthusiasts to explore.

In recent years, Merlot has experienced a resurgence in popularity, as more consumers rediscover its elegance and charm. The focus has shifted towards showcasing the grape’s unique characteristics rather than blending it into the background. Single-varietal Merlot wines are now highly sought after and admired for their depth, complexity, and age-worthiness.

The history of Merlot is one of evolution and resilience. From its humble beginnings in Bordeaux to its widespread cultivation and recognition worldwide, this grape has come a long way. Its smoothness, versatility, and likability have solidified its place as one of the most cherished red wine varieties.

So, the next time you uncork a bottle of Merlot, take a moment to appreciate the rich history behind every sip. From the vineyards of Bordeaux to the rolling hills of California, each glass tells a story of passion, dedication, and the enduring love for this remarkable wine. Cheers to Merlot, a true testament to the magic captured in each bottle.
