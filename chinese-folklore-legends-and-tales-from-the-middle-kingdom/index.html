Chinese Folklore: Legends and tales from the Middle Kingdom


China, with its rich history and diverse culture, is home to a fascinating collection of folklore and legends. Passed down through generations, these tales offer a glimpse into the traditional belief systems, values, and customs of the Chinese people. In this article, we will explore some of the most popular legends and tales from the Middle Kingdom.

The Legend of the White Snake

The Legend of the White Snake is one of the most famous Chinese folktales that dates back to the Tang Dynasty (618-907 AD). This timeless love story tells the tale of a white snake spirit named Bai Suzhen and a young scholar named Xu Xian. Their forbidden love, challenged by the powerful monk Fahai, symbolizes the eternal struggle between good and evil. This legend serves as a reminder of the importance of love, forgiveness, and compassion in overcoming adversity.

The Monkey King and Journey to the West

Another iconic legend in Chinese folklore is the tale of the Monkey King and his adventure in the epic novel ‘Journey to the West.’ This tale, written by Wu Cheng’en during the Ming Dynasty (1368-1644 AD), follows the journey of the mischievous and powerful Monkey King as he accompanies the Buddhist monk, Xuanzang, on a pilgrimage to India. Along the way, they face various challenges and encounter mythical creatures, showcasing the Monkey King’s bravery, wit, and eventual transformation from a troublemaker to a revered hero.

The Eight Immortals

The Eight Immortals, or Ba Xian in Chinese, are a group of legendary Daoist figures known for their ability to grant immortality. Each immortal possesses unique supernatural powers and represents a specific virtue. These tales have been a significant influence in Chinese culture, inspiring art, literature, and even popular TV series. The stories of the Eight Immortals serve as a moral guide, teaching values such as kindness, humility, and perseverance.

Legends of the Three Kingdoms

The period of the Three Kingdoms (AD 220-280) marked a tumultuous time in ancient Chinese history, filled with political intrigue and military warfare. The legends surrounding this era provide a glimpse into the lives of notable historical figures such as Liu Bei, Guan Yu, and Cao Cao. These tales highlight the virtues of loyalty, righteousness, and valor, with heroes and villains vying for power in a chaotic landscape. The ‘Romance of the Three Kingdoms,’ a renowned Chinese novel written by Luo Guanzhong, is a classic literary masterpiece that immortalizes these legends.

The Legend of the Zhong Kui

The legend of Zhong Kui, also known as the Ghostcatcher, is a popular folklore tale in China. Zhong Kui, a deformed scholar, was banished from the imperial examinations due to his appearance. After committing suicide, he became a ghost and was appointed the task of driving away evil spirits. Often depicted as a fierce-looking deity with a sword, Zhong Kui represents justice, bravery, and the triumph of good over evil. His image can be found on traditional Chinese paintings and artifacts, and he is also considered a guardian against evil spirits and demons.

Legends of the Dragon and the Phoenix

The dragon and the phoenix are two mythical creatures deeply rooted in Chinese folklore and culture. The dragon, symbolizing power, strength, and good luck, is one of the most prominent symbols in Chinese mythology. The phoenix, a symbol of beauty and grace, represents femininity and prosperity. Legends often depict the dragon and phoenix as a pair, representing the harmonious union of yin and yang. These legends are often portrayed in traditional Chinese art, architecture, and celebrations, reflecting their significance in Chinese culture.

In conclusion, Chinese folklore is a treasure trove of legends and tales that reflect the country’s rich cultural heritage. These stories not only entertain but also teach valuable lessons about morality, bravery, loyalty, and the pursuit of love and justice. As we delve into the realms of these mythical creatures and legendary heroes, we gain a deeper understanding of Chinese traditions and values. Exploring the folklore of the Middle Kingdom opens our eyes to a world of magic, mystique, and wisdom - a world that continues to captivate and inspire us.
