Gardening in Small Apartments: Microgreens and More


Living in a small apartment doesn’t mean you have to miss out on the joys of gardening. With a little creativity and the right knowledge, you can transform your limited living space into a mini garden paradise. One of the most popular and rewarding gardening techniques for small spaces is growing microgreens.

Microgreens are young vegetable or herb seedlings that are harvested when they are just a couple of inches tall. They are packed with flavor, nutrition, and can be grown in just a few weeks. The best part is that you can grow them indoors, right on your kitchen countertop or windowsill.

To get started with microgreens, you’ll need a few basic supplies. First, choose a shallow container with drainage holes. Plastic salad containers or seed trays work well for this purpose. Fill the container with a mixture of soil and potting mix, about an inch deep. Moisten the soil, but be careful not to overwater it.

Next, choose the type of microgreens you want to grow. Popular choices include arugula, kale, radish, and sunflower. These seeds can be easily purchased online or at your local gardening store. Sprinkle the seeds evenly over the soil, ensuring that they are not overcrowded. Gently press them into the soil, but do not cover them completely.

Place the container in a location that receives plenty of indirect sunlight or install a grow light if natural light is limited. Microgreens need at least 4-6 hours of light per day to grow properly. Keep the soil moist by misting it with water or using a spray bottle. Within a few days, you’ll start to see sprouts emerging from the soil.

As your microgreens grow, make sure to continue watering them regularly and monitor their progress. In about 1-2 weeks, they will be ready to harvest. Simply snip off the greens just above the soil line using a pair of clean scissors or sharp knife. Rinse the harvested microgreens and enjoy them fresh in salads, sandwiches, or as nutritious toppings for your favorite dishes.

Microgreens are not only a delicious addition to your meals but also a great way to introduce gardening into your small apartment. They require minimal space, time, and effort to grow, making them perfect for urban dwellers with limited resources. Plus, they provide a fresh and healthy food source that can be enjoyed year-round.

In addition to microgreens, there are other gardening techniques you can explore to maximize your apartment’s green space. Vertical gardening is a popular choice for small apartments as it allows you to grow plants upwards instead of outwards. You can hang pots on walls, install shelves for potted plants, or even create a vertical herb garden using repurposed materials like pallets.

Another option for small apartment gardening is container gardening. Choose containers of various sizes and shapes to accommodate different types of plants. Herbs like basil, rosemary, and thyme thrive in containers and can provide a continuous supply of fresh flavors for your cooking endeavors. Flowers like pansies or petunias can add a pop of color and beauty to your living space.

To ensure the success of your apartment garden, pay attention to the environmental conditions in your home. Consider factors like temperature, humidity, and airflow. Some plants may require specific environments to grow well, so it’s essential to choose plants that are suitable for your apartment’s microclimate.

In conclusion, gardening in a small apartment is not only possible but also a rewarding experience. Microgreens, vertical gardening, and container gardening are excellent options to explore. They allow you to bring nature into your living space, enhance your cooking with fresh flavors, and create a soothing ambiance. So why not start your small apartment garden today and enjoy the many benefits it brings?
