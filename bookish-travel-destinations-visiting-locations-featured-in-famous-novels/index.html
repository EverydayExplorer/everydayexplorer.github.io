Bookish Travel Destinations: Visiting Locations Featured in Famous Novels


Visiting locations that have been featured in famous novels is a dream come true for book lovers and travel enthusiasts alike. The magic of literature is not limited to words on a page; it can transport us to different worlds and landscapes, allowing us to experience the setting of our favorite stories firsthand. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most iconic and enchanting bookish travel destinations around the globe.

1. Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry - United Kingdom üè∞
If you’re a fan of the Harry Potter series by J.K. Rowling, a visit to the United Kingdom is a must. Start your journey by visiting the Warner Bros. Studio Tour in London, where you can explore the sets, costumes, and props used in the films. Don’t forget to take a trip to Edinburgh, where Rowling began writing the series and drew inspiration for Hogwarts.

2. Prince Edward Island - Canada üçÅ
If you have ever dreamed of walking in the footsteps of Anne Shirley from ‘Anne of Green Gables’ by Lucy Maud Montgomery, a trip to Prince Edward Island is a must. Explore the beautiful landscapes and quaint villages that inspired the fictional town of Avonlea.

3. Paris - France üá´üá∑
Paris has been a favorite setting for many renowned authors, including Victor Hugo and Ernest Hemingway. Lose yourself in the streets of Montmartre, which inspired the world-famous novel ‘The Hunchback of Notre-Dame’ by Victor Hugo. For Hemingway fans, a visit to the Latin Quarter and the iconic Shakespeare and Company bookstore is a must.

4. Chatsworth House - United Kingdom üè∞
Jane Austen fans will fall in love with Chatsworth House in Derbyshire, England. This grand estate is believed to have inspired Mr. Darcy’s residence, Pemberley, in ‘Pride and Prejudice.’ Explore the magnificent gardens and opulent interiors that Austen described so vividly in her novel.

5. Maycomb - United States üá∫üá∏
For fans of ‘To Kill a Mockingbird’ by Harper Lee, a trip to Monroeville, Alabama is a must. Monroeville is said to be the inspiration behind the fictional town of Maycomb. Visit the Old Courthouse Museum, where you can learn about the historical events that influenced Lee’s Pulitzer Prize-winning novel.

6. The Hobbiton Movie Set - New Zealand üèûÔ∏è
J.R.R. Tolkien fans cannot miss a trip to the Hobbiton Movie Set in Matamata, New Zealand. This picturesque location served as the Shire in the ‘Lord of the Rings’ and ‘The Hobbit’ trilogies. Take a guided tour and immerse yourself in the enchanting world of Middle-earth.

7. St. Petersburg - Russia üá∑üá∫
Literature lovers will appreciate a visit to St. Petersburg, the setting for Fyodor Dostoevsky’s famous novel, ‘Crime and Punishment.’ Explore the city’s canals, bridges, and historic buildings, which vividly come to life in Dostoevsky’s masterpiece.

8. Bath - United Kingdom üá¨üáß
Known for its Roman-built baths and Georgian architecture, Bath served as the backdrop for Jane Austen’s novels, including ‘Northanger Abbey’ and ‘Persuasion.’ Take a stroll through Royal Crescent and the Pump Room, and experience the charm of the Regency era.

9. Rome - Italy üáÆüáπ
Follow in the footsteps of Robert Langdon, the protagonist of Dan Brown’s ‘Angels & Demons,’ on a journey through Rome. Visit notable landmarks such as St. Peter’s Square, the Vatican, and the Pantheon, which play a significant role in the thrilling plot of the novel.

10. Marrakech - Morocco üá≤üá¶
Experience the vibrant atmosphere of Marrakech, the setting for ‘The Alchemist’ by Paulo Coelho. Lose yourself in the winding streets of the Medina, explore the bustling souks, and visit the famous Jardin Majorelle, a stunning garden that once belonged to French painter Jacques Majorelle.

Visiting locations inspired by famous novels allows us to connect more deeply with the stories and characters we love. Whether you’re a fan of Harry Potter, classics like ‘Pride and Prejudice,’ or modern works like ‘Harry Potter,’ these bookish travel destinations offer a chance to make our literary dreams a reality. So grab a book, pack your bags, and embark on a literary adventure that will leave you with memories to cherish for a lifetime.
