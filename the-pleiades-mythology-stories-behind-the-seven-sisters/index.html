The Pleiades Mythology: Stories Behind the Seven Sisters


In the vast expanse of the night sky, there exists a captivating cluster of stars known as the Pleiades. Also referred to as the Seven Sisters, these celestial gems have mesmerized and inspired countless cultures throughout history. The Pleiades mythology is a tapestry of captivating stories that unravel the origins and adventures of these seven sisters. Join me on a journey through the cosmos as we explore the enchanting tales behind the Pleiades.

In Greek mythology, the Pleiades were the daughters of Atlas, the Titan who held the heavens on his shoulders, and Pleione, an Oceanid nymph. The story goes that Zeus, the king of the gods, transformed the sisters into stars to protect them from the advances of the giant hunter Orion. The Pleiades became a beacon of hope and guidance for sailors, as their appearance in the night sky marked the beginning of the sailing season.

In Japanese folklore, the Pleiades are known as Subaru, which means ‘unite’ or ‘gather together’ in Japanese. The story revolves around seven sisters who were transformed into stars after they were pursued by a powerful and jealous hunter. The Subaru constellation has been a symbol of unity and harmony in Japanese culture, and the Pleiades Festival is celebrated every year to honor the bond between siblings.

The Maori people of New Zealand have their own captivating tale of the Pleiades. According to Maori mythology, the seven sisters are called Matariki, which means ’eyes of god.’ The appearance of Matariki in the sky signals the start of the Maori New Year. Each sister represents a different aspect of life, such as health, prosperity, and family. The Maori people gather to celebrate and honor their ancestors during this time, reflecting on the past and looking forward to the future.

In Hindu mythology, the Pleiades are associated with the goddess Krittika, who is believed to have raised the seven sisters as her own. The story tells of Krittika’s nurturing and protective nature, as she guided the sisters through their journey in the heavens. The Pleiades hold great significance in Hindu astrology, and their alignment is believed to influence the lives and destinies of individuals.

Across different cultures and civilizations, the Pleiades mythology continues to captivate and inspire. From the ancient Greeks to the Maori people of New Zealand, these seven sisters have left an indelible mark on our collective imagination. Their stories remind us of the power of family, unity, and protection in the face of adversity.

As we gaze up at the night sky, let us marvel at the beauty and wonder of the Pleiades. Each star represents a story, a connection to our past, and a reminder of the countless tales that have been woven around these celestial beings. Whether we see them as guiding lights or symbols of familial love, the Pleiades will forever hold a special place in the tapestry of human mythology.

So, the next time you find yourself under a blanket of stars, take a moment to search for the Pleiades. As you admire their brilliance, remember the stories that have been passed down through generations. The Pleiades mythology is a testament to the enduring power of storytelling and the profound impact the cosmos can have on our lives. Let the tales of the Seven Sisters inspire you to reach for the stars and embrace the magic that lies within the vastness of the universe.
