What to Do if Your Pet Has a Seizure: First Aid and Support


Pets are cherished members of our families, bringing joy, love, and companionship into our lives. However, just like humans, pets can experience health issues, including seizures. Witnessing your beloved pet have a seizure can be a distressing and alarming experience. It’s crucial to stay calm and take immediate action to ensure their safety and well-being. In this blog post, we’ll discuss what to do if your pet has a seizure, providing essential first aid and support.

1. Stay Calm:
When your pet experiences a seizure, it’s essential to remain calm. While it can be frightening to witness, remember that your pet is relying on you for support. Panicking can increase their stress levels, so take a deep breath and focus on helping them.

2. Ensure Safety:
During a seizure, your pet may involuntarily thrash around, potentially injuring themselves or those nearby. Clear the immediate area of any sharp objects, furniture, or obstacles to prevent accidents. Create a safe space by gently guiding your pet away from stairs or dangerous areas.

3. Time the Seizure:
It’s crucial to note the duration of the seizure. Although it may feel like an eternity, most seizures last anywhere from a few seconds to a few minutes. Timing the seizure can provide valuable information to your veterinarian later.

4. Do Not Restrain:
While it’s natural to want to hold or restrain your pet during a seizure, it’s important not to do so. Restraining them can cause additional stress or lead to accidental injuries. Allow your pet to move freely while ensuring their safety.

5. Protect Their Airway:
During a seizure, pets may drool or produce excess saliva, which can pose a risk of choking. Gently roll your pet onto their side to prevent saliva from obstructing their airway. This position can also help prevent them from inhaling vomit.

6. Dim the Lights and Minimize Noise:
Seizures can be disorienting for pets, and bright lights or loud noises can exacerbate their discomfort. Dim the lights in the room and reduce any sources of noise to create a calm environment. This can help your pet recover more smoothly after the seizure.

7. Monitor Vital Signs:
During and after the seizure, monitor your pet’s vital signs. Check their breathing and heart rate to ensure they are stable. If you notice any abnormalities or if the seizure lasts longer than five minutes, contact your veterinarian immediately.

8. Document the Seizure:
To help your veterinarian make an accurate diagnosis, document the seizure and any relevant details. Note the time it started and ended, the behavior exhibited, and any specific triggers that may have preceded the seizure. This information can aid in determining the cause and appropriate treatment.

9. Contact Your Veterinarian:
After the seizure, contact your veterinarian to discuss the episode and seek further guidance. They may recommend bringing your pet in for an examination or provide instructions on how to manage the situation at home.

10. Follow-Up Care:
Depending on the underlying cause of the seizure, your veterinarian may recommend further tests or treatments to manage your pet’s condition. Follow their instructions diligently and provide any necessary medications or lifestyle changes to ensure your pet’s well-being.

Remember, witnessing your pet have a seizure can be distressing, but by staying calm, ensuring their safety, and seeking prompt veterinary care, you can support them through this challenging experience. Your love and support are essential in helping your pet recover and live a happy, healthy life.
