Exploring Orange Wine Grape Varieties: Skin-Contact Wines


Orange wine, also known as skin-contact wine, is a unique and intriguing category within the world of winemaking. With its deep amber hue and complex flavor profile, orange wine has gained popularity among wine enthusiasts and sommeliers alike. In this blog post, we will explore the various grape varieties used to make orange wine and delve into the fascinating process behind its creation.

Orange wine is made by fermenting white grape juice with the grape skins, seeds, and stems. This extended maceration period gives the wine its distinctive color and robust characteristics. While orange wine has been around for centuries, it has recently experienced a resurgence in popularity, thanks to its natural, artisanal production methods and its ability to pair well with a wide range of foods.

One of the most widely used grape varieties for making orange wine is Pinot Grigio. Originating in Italy, Pinot Grigio is known for its delicate flavors and crisp acidity. When transformed into orange wine, Pinot Grigio exhibits a rich, full-bodied texture with notes of dried fruit and spice. It pairs wonderfully with roasted meats, aged cheeses, and hearty vegetarian dishes.

Another grape variety commonly used for orange wine production is Gewürztraminer. This aromatic grape brings intense floral and tropical fruit flavors to the wine, creating a sensory experience like no other. Gewürztraminer-based orange wines often display a deep golden color and a pronounced spiciness on the palate. They are a fantastic match for spicy Asian cuisine, bold cheeses, and even chocolate-based desserts.

Riesling, a beloved grape variety known for its versatility, also lends itself well to orange wine production. When fermented on the skins, Riesling develops a beautiful orange hue and a distinct complexity. The resulting wine showcases a delicate balance between acidity and sweetness, with flavors of citrus, stone fruit, and honey. It pairs exceptionally well with seafood, spicy dishes, and fresh salads.

Moving away from Europe, let’s explore a grape variety from the United States that is gaining recognition in the orange wine scene: Chardonnay. While Chardonnay is typically associated with buttery, oaked white wines, when made into orange wine, it takes on a whole new personality. The skin-contact process adds depth and texture to Chardonnay, resulting in a wine with flavors of baked apple, toasted nuts, and a hint of vanilla. It pairs beautifully with roasted poultry, creamy pasta dishes, and rich seafood.

These are just a few examples of the grape varieties that can be used to create orange wine. Each variety brings its own unique characteristics to the final product, offering a diverse range of aromas, flavors, and textures. Exploring orange wine grape varieties is an exciting journey that allows wine lovers to experience a whole new dimension of winemaking.

In conclusion, orange wine, with its striking color and bold flavors, is a category of wine that continues to captivate and intrigue. By experimenting with different grape varieties and embracing the skin-contact process, winemakers have created a truly unique and exciting style of wine. Whether you are a seasoned wine connoisseur or a curious beginner, exploring orange wine grape varieties is a journey that is sure to expand your palate and introduce you to a world of flavors waiting to be discovered. So, the next time you’re looking to try something new, why not uncork a bottle of orange wine and embark on a sensory adventure like no other?
