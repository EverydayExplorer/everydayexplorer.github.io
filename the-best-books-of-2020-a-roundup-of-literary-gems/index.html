The Best Books of 2020: A Roundup of Literary Gems


As another year comes to a close, it’s time to reflect on the literary landscape of 2020. Despite the numerous challenges the world faced, authors continued to produce remarkable works that captivated readers and provided an escape from reality. From thought-provoking non-fiction to gripping novels, 2020 was a year that saw a plethora of literary gems. In this blog post, we will delve into a roundup of the best books that graced our shelves this year.

1. Fiction

Fiction took us on an extraordinary journey in 2020, offering a wide range of stories that explored the depths of human emotion and imagination. Amongst the standout novels was ‘The Vanishing Half’ by Brit Bennett. This mesmerizing tale explores themes of identity, race, and family secrets through the lives of twin sisters who choose to live in different racial worlds. Bennett’s beautiful prose and compelling narrative make this book an absolute must-read.

Another noteworthy fiction novel of the year was ‘Deacon King Kong’ by James McBride. Set in 1969 Brooklyn, this captivating story revolves around the shooting of a drug dealer and the ensuing ripple effect it has on the neighborhood. McBride skillfully weaves together humor, tragedy, and justice, creating a novel that is both poignant and memorable.

2. Non-Fiction

Non-fiction offered a wealth of knowledge and insight in 2020. One exceptional book that stood out was ‘Uncanny Valley’ by Anna Wiener. In this memoir, Wiener provides a firsthand account of her time working in Silicon Valley during the height of the tech boom. She explores the disillusionment she experienced and the ethical dilemmas of the industry. Wiener’s sharp observations and witty writing style make this book a thought-provoking read for anyone interested in the intersection of technology and society.

Another remarkable non-fiction book of the year was ‘Hidden Valley Road’ by Robert Kolker. This gripping true story tells the tale of the Galvin family, who had six out of their twelve children diagnosed with schizophrenia. Kolker delves into the history of mental illness, intertwining it with the Galvin family’s personal journey. By offering a compassionate and intimate portrayal, this book sheds light on the complexities surrounding mental health.

3. Memoirs and Autobiographies

The year 2020 also showcased powerful memoirs and autobiographies that gave readers an intimate glimpse into the lives of extraordinary individuals. Tara Westover’s ‘Educated’ continued to garner acclaim this year. This memoir depicts Westover’s upbringing in a strict and abusive household in rural Idaho, and her journey towards education and self-discovery. It is a story of resilience, determination, and the power of knowledge.

Another notable memoir released this year was ‘The Splendid and the Vile’ by Erik Larson. Providing a captivating account of Winston Churchill’s leadership during World War II, Larson brings history to life with his meticulous research and engaging writing style. This book offers readers a chance to understand the man behind the iconic leader, showcasing the triumphs and tribulations of Churchill’s wartime strategy.

Conclusion

The year 2020 may have been challenging in many ways, but it also gifted us with a plethora of remarkable books that provided solace, inspiration, and escape. Whether you are a fan of fiction, non-fiction, or memoirs, there was something for everyone to enjoy. From ‘The Vanishing Half’ to ‘Hidden Valley Road,’ these literary gems captured readers’ hearts and minds, igniting important conversations and transporting us to worlds filled with wonder and intrigue. So, as we bid farewell to 2020, let’s relish in the power of literature and look forward to the literary treasures that await us in the years to come.
