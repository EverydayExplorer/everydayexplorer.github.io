Hunting Wild Turkeys: Calling and Decoy Strategies


If you’re an avid hunter looking for a thrilling challenge, hunting wild turkeys might just be the adventure you’ve been seeking. These majestic birds are known for their elusive nature, sharp senses, and impressive speed. To successfully hunt wild turkeys, you’ll need to employ effective calling and decoy strategies that mimic their natural behavior and entice them within range. In this blog post, we’ll explore some tried and tested techniques that will help you become a skilled turkey hunter.

Understanding Turkey Behavior

Before diving into calling and decoy strategies, it’s essential to understand the behavior of wild turkeys. Turkeys are highly social creatures, and their communication is crucial for their survival. They use a variety of vocalizations and body language to communicate with each other, especially during the breeding season. By learning to interpret their behavior, you’ll be better equipped to attract them during your hunts.

Turkey Calling Techniques

Calling is perhaps the most critical aspect of turkey hunting. It involves using various calls to mimic the sounds turkeys make, thereby attracting them closer. Here are some popular turkey calls and when to use them:

  1. Cluck: The cluck is a versatile call that mimics the sound of a contented turkey. It can be used throughout the hunting season and is particularly effective when turkeys are nearby but not actively gobbling.

  2. Purrs: Purrs are soft, rolling sounds that turkeys make when they’re relaxed and content. This call is useful when trying to calm down a wary turkey or to imitate feeding turkeys.

  3. Yelps: Yelps are the most common and versatile turkey vocalizations. They can be used to locate turkeys, get their attention, and provoke a response. Yelps can be made in a variety of pitches and volumes, so experiment with different combinations to find what works best for you.

  4. Cutting: Cutting is a series of fast, irregular yelps that turkeys make when they’re excited or agitated. It can be an effective call during the peak of the breeding season when turkeys are most responsive.

Remember, practice makes perfect when it comes to turkey calling. Invest time in mastering these calls to sound as natural as possible, increasing your chances of success in the field.

Decoy Strategies

Decoys are another valuable tool in a turkey hunter’s arsenal. They help create a visual attraction and give turkeys a sense of security, making them more likely to approach your hunting setup. Here are some decoy strategies to consider:

  1. Hen Decoys: Hen decoys are the most common type and should be used throughout the hunting season. They imitate female turkeys and can be positioned in feeding, breeding, or alert postures. Placing a hen decoy near your hunting blind or setup can pique the interest of nearby gobblers.

  2. Jake Decoys: Jake decoys represent young male turkeys and are effective during the early part of the breeding season. Mature gobblers may feel threatened by jakes, which can draw them closer to investigate.

  3. Tom Decoys: Tom decoys, also known as strutters, imitate dominant male turkeys. They are most effective during the peak of the breeding season when gobblers are highly territorial. Placing a tom decoy in a strutting posture can trigger a dominant response from nearby gobblers.

Remember to position your decoys strategically, creating a natural-looking scene that mimics turkey behavior. Experiment with different setups and decoy combinations to find what works best in your hunting area.

Conclusion

Successfully hunting wild turkeys requires a combination of skill, patience, and an understanding of turkey behavior. By mastering turkey calling techniques and utilizing effective decoy strategies, you can increase your chances of a successful hunt. Remember, practice is key, so spend time honing your skills in the field. Good luck, and happy hunting!

Note: Hunting regulations and laws vary by location. Always ensure you are familiar with and adhere to local hunting regulations and ethical hunting practices.
