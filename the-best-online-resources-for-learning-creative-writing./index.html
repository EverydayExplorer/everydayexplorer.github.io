The Best Online Resources for Learning Creative Writing.


In today’s digital age, there is an overwhelming abundance of online resources available for individuals looking to learn and master creative writing. Whether you’re a seasoned writer aiming to refine your skills or a novice yearning to unleash your creativity, the internet offers a myriad of platforms and tools that can cater to your specific needs. In this blog post, we will be exploring some of the best online resources for learning creative writing, helping you embark on an enriching journey towards becoming a proficient wordsmith.

1. Writer’s Digest University:
Writer’s Digest University is an exceptional resource for aspiring writers with courses covering various aspects of creative writing. From character development and plot construction to honing your editing skills, this platform offers in-depth lessons facilitated by industry professionals. The structured approach of these courses ensures that you receive comprehensive guidance that fosters growth in your writing abilities.

2. Coursera:
Coursera is another renowned platform that offers a wide array of creative writing courses. Partnering with prestigious universities, such as Stanford and Yale, Coursera provides access to top-tier writing instruction. Whether you’re interested in poetry, fiction, or non-fiction, Coursera’s catalog boasts an extensive collection of courses to accommodate various genres and skill levels. Moreover, their interactive learning experience and peer review systems allow for valuable feedback and engagement with fellow aspiring writers.

3. Skillshare:
Skillshare stands as a popular online learning platform that transcends multiple disciplines, including creative writing. With a vibrant community of creative enthusiasts, Skillshare provides a vast collection of classes facilitated by renowned authors. From storytelling techniques to mastering the art of suspense, these classes offer practical insights and hands-on exercises to polish your writing skills. Skillshare also encourages collaborative learning, with opportunities to share and receive feedback on your work.

4. The Writers’ Workshop:
The Writers’ Workshop is an online resource that caters specifically to aspiring writers. Through their online courses, workshops, and manuscript assessment services, this platform offers tailored guidance to help writers refine their craft. With an emphasis on personalized feedback and mentorship, The Writers’ Workshop instills a supportive environment that nurtures growth and inspiration.

5. National Novel Writing Month (NaNoWriMo):
Every November, writers from around the globe participate in NaNoWriMo — a month-long challenge to write a 50,000-word novel. While this event emphasizes quantity over quality, it serves as an excellent opportunity for writers to cultivate discipline, unleash creativity, and embrace their storytelling instincts. NaNoWriMo provides a sense of community and hosts various online resources, including forums, podcasts, and pep talks from established authors.

6. Medium:
For writers seeking a platform to share their work and receive feedback from a wide audience, Medium is an ideal choice. With its user-friendly interface and vast community of readers and writers, Medium allows you to publish your creative writing pieces and engage in meaningful discussions. The platform also offers writing prompts and curated collections that can serve as valuable sources of inspiration.

7. Reedsy:
Reedsy is a comprehensive online marketplace connecting writers with professional editors, designers, and marketers. In addition to their services, Reedsy hosts a blog packed with valuable writing advice, industry insights, and interviews with successful authors. Their resources cover a wide range of topics, from character development and world-building to marketing and self-publishing tips.

8. Podcasts:
Podcasts dedicated to creative writing can be a convenient and entertaining way to absorb knowledge on the go. Some noteworthy options include “Writing Excuses,” a podcast hosted by renowned authors who delve into various aspects of writing, and “The Creative Penn,” hosted by bestselling author Joanna Penn, which covers self-publishing and marketing strategies in addition to craft-focused episodes.

In conclusion, the internet offers a treasure trove of online resources for learning creative writing. From structured courses, like those offered by Writer’s Digest University and Coursera, to interactive platforms like Skillshare and Medium, you have ample opportunities to enhance your writing skills and find a supportive writing community. Whether through participating in NaNoWriMo, exploring valuable writing advice on Reedsy’s blog, or listening to podcasts like “Writing Excuses” and “The Creative Penn,” there are countless avenues to learn, grow, and become the best creative writer you can be. So, seize the digital tools at your disposal, embrace the wealth of knowledge available, and unlock your full potential in the world of creative writing.
