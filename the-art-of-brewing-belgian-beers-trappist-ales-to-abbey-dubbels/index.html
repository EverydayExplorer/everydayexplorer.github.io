The Art of Brewing Belgian Beers: Trappist Ales to Abbey Dubbels


Belgian beers have long been revered for their rich history, intricate flavors, and unique brewing techniques. Among the many exceptional styles that Belgium has to offer, Trappist Ales and Abbey Dubbels stand out as true masterpieces of the brewing craft. In this blog post, we will delve into the art of brewing these Belgian beers, exploring their origins, characteristics, and the secrets behind their unparalleled taste.

Trappist Ales, brewed exclusively within the walls of Trappist monasteries, are known for their deep-rooted tradition and commitment to quality. These beers are crafted by monks who dedicate their lives to prayer, manual work, and brewing. The Trappist monasteries strictly adhere to the principles set forth by the International Trappist Association, ensuring that the beers they produce meet the highest standards of authenticity and excellence.

One of the most famous Trappist Ales is the Westvleteren 12, often regarded as one of the best beers in the world. This dark, complex ale boasts flavors of dark fruits, caramel, and spice, with a velvety smooth mouthfeel. The brewing process for Trappist Ales involves carefully selected ingredients, including specialty malts, hops, and unique yeast strains that contribute to their distinctive flavors.

Abbey Dubbels, on the other hand, are similar to Trappist Ales but are brewed by secular breweries inspired by the monastic traditions. These beers mimic the rich flavors and complexity of Trappist Ales, offering a wide range of aromas and taste profiles. The Abbey Dubbel style typically features notes of dried fruit, caramel, and a hint of spice, creating a harmonious balance between sweetness and bitterness.

To brew Trappist Ales and Abbey Dubbels, brewers employ traditional methods that have been passed down for centuries. The use of specific Belgian yeast strains, which often have fruity and spicy characteristics, is crucial in achieving the desired flavors. Fermentation temperatures are carefully controlled to allow the yeast to work its magic, resulting in the unique profiles that define these beers.

The malt bill for both Trappist Ales and Abbey Dubbels typically includes a combination of base malts, specialty malts, and, in some cases, candi sugar. This carefully crafted blend of malts imparts the rich, malty backbone that complements the yeast characteristics and adds complexity to the final brew.

When it comes to food pairings, Trappist Ales and Abbey Dubbels are incredibly versatile. Their robust flavors make them ideal companions for hearty dishes such as stews, roasted meats, and aged cheeses. The caramel and dried fruit notes beautifully complement the flavors found in traditional Belgian cuisine.

In conclusion, the art of brewing Trappist Ales and Abbey Dubbels is a testament to Belgium’s rich brewing heritage. These beers, crafted with passion and dedication, offer a sensory experience unlike any other. From the centuries-old monastic traditions to the meticulous brewing techniques, every aspect of their production contributes to their exceptional quality. So, the next time you have the opportunity to enjoy a Trappist Ale or an Abbey Dubbel, take a moment to appreciate the craftsmanship and history behind each sip.
