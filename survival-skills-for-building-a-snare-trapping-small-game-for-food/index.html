Survival Skills for Building a Snare: Trapping Small Game for Food


When it comes to survival situations, one of the most essential skills to have is the ability to procure food. While hunting with firearms or catching fish may not always be an option, building a snare can be a highly effective way to trap small game for sustenance. In this blog post, we will explore the art of snaring and provide you with some valuable tips to help you master this vital survival skill.

1. Understanding the Basics
Before diving into the specifics of building a snare, it’s important to have a basic understanding of how they work. A snare is essentially a simple, yet effective, mechanism that captures animals by using a noose or loop. When an animal passes through the snare, the loop tightens, effectively trapping the animal. It’s important to note that snaring is legal in some areas and illegal in others, so be sure to check local regulations before utilizing this method.

2. Choosing the Right Location
Selecting the right location for your snare is crucial. Look for areas with signs of animal activity, such as tracks, droppings, or chewed vegetation. Paths near water sources or game trails are often ideal spots to set up your snare. Remember that animals are creatures of habit, so setting your snare in an area they frequent will increase your chances of success.

3. Building the Snare
Now that you’ve found the perfect location, it’s time to build your snare. Start by finding a sturdy sapling or branch that is about as thick as your thumb. This will serve as the main support for your snare. Next, fashion a loop using a strong cord or wire, ensuring that it is large enough to fit the intended target animal’s head or body through. Attach one end of the loop to the sapling or branch, leaving enough slack for the snare to close when triggered.

4. Triggering Mechanism
To trigger your snare, you’ll need to create a mechanism that will release the loop when an animal disturbs it. This can be achieved by utilizing a bent sapling, a toggle stick, or even a simple twig. Experiment with different methods to find what works best for you, keeping in mind that the trigger should be sensitive enough to respond to the slightest touch.

5. Patience and Persistence
Once your snare is set up, it’s important to exercise patience and persistence. Check your snare regularly to ensure that it remains in good working order and to increase your chances of a successful catch. Remember that animals can be unpredictable, so it may take some time before you achieve your desired outcome. Stay focused and adapt your approach as needed.

6. Safety Considerations
While snaring can be an effective survival technique, it’s important to prioritize safety. Always handle materials and tools with care, and be aware of potential hazards in your surrounding environment. Additionally, be mindful of local regulations and ethical considerations when using snaring as a method for procuring food.

In conclusion, mastering the art of snaring is a valuable skill for anyone interested in survival. By understanding the basics, choosing the right location, building the snare correctly, creating an effective triggering mechanism, and exercising patience and persistence, you can greatly increase your chances of successfully trapping small game for food. Always prioritize safety and be mindful of local regulations to ensure responsible and ethical hunting practices. With practice and experience, you’ll become a proficient snarer, adding an invaluable tool to your survival skill set.
