How to Make Homemade Bread from Scratch


Homemade bread has a distinct taste and aroma that store-bought bread simply cannot replicate. There is something truly satisfying about the process of making bread from scratch – the feel of the dough in your hands, the anticipation of watching it rise, and the reward of savoring a warm slice fresh out of the oven. If you’ve always wanted to learn how to bake homemade bread, you’ve come to the right place. In this comprehensive guide, we will walk you through the steps of making homemade bread from scratch, covering everything from selecting the right ingredients to mastering the art of kneading and shaping the dough.

Step 1: Gather Your Ingredients

To make homemade bread, you will need a few basic ingredients that you probably already have in your kitchen pantry. These include:

  1. Flour: All-purpose flour or bread flour are the most common choices for making homemade bread. Bread flour has a higher protein content, which helps create a chewier texture.

  2. Yeast: There are different types of yeast available, including active dry yeast and instant yeast. Active dry yeast needs to be proofed before using, while instant yeast can be added directly to the dough.

  3. Water: The temperature of the water is crucial for activating the yeast. It should be warm, around 105°F to 110°F (40°C to 43°C).

  4. Salt: Salt not only enhances the flavor but also helps regulate the yeast’s activity.

  5. Sugar or honey: These help feed the yeast and promote fermentation, resulting in better rise and flavor.

Step 2: Mixing the Dough

Once you have gathered your ingredients, it’s time to mix the dough. Follow these steps:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the warm water, yeast, and sugar/honey. Stir gently and allow the mixture to sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.

  2. Add the flour and salt to the yeast mixture. Use a wooden spoon or your hands to mix everything together until a shaggy dough forms.

  3. Once the dough comes together, transfer it to a lightly floured surface. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic. This process develops the gluten in the dough, which gives bread its structure.

Step 3: Rising and Proofing

After kneading the dough, it’s time to let it rise. Here’s what to do:

  1. Place the dough in a clean, lightly oiled bowl, covering it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap.

  2. Allow the dough to rise in a warm, draft-free place for about 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size. This process is known as the first rise.

  3. Once the dough has risen, gently deflate it with your hands and shape it into the desired form - a loaf, rolls, or even a baguette.

  4. Place the shaped dough on a parchment-lined baking sheet or in a loaf pan, cover it, and let it rise again for another 30-60 minutes. This is called the second rise or proofing.

Step 4: Baking the Bread

Before you start baking, preheat your oven to the required temperature, typically between 375°F to 425°F (190°C to 220°C). Follow these steps:

  1. If desired, brush the shaped dough with an egg wash or sprinkle it with seeds like sesame or poppy for added flavor and texture.

  2. Carefully transfer the dough to the preheated oven and bake for the recommended time. The baking time will vary depending on the type and size of the bread. As a general rule, most loaves take around 30-45 minutes.

  3. To check if the bread is done, tap the bottom – it should sound hollow. If needed, continue baking for a few more minutes.

  4. Once the bread is baked, remove it from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack before slicing.

Conclusion

Making homemade bread from scratch is a rewarding experience that anyone can enjoy. By following these steps, you’ll be able to consistently produce delicious, freshly baked bread right in your own kitchen. Experiment with different types of flour, add-ins like herbs or cheese, and even shape your dough into unique designs. With practice and patience, you’ll soon become a master bread baker, impressing your family and friends with your homemade creations. So, why wait? Roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and begin your bread-making journey today!
