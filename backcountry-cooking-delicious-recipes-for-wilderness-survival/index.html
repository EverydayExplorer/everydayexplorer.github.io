Backcountry Cooking: Delicious Recipes for Wilderness Survival


When embarking on a backcountry adventure, one of the most important considerations is food. A well-prepared meal can not only provide sustenance but also boost morale and enhance the overall experience. However, cooking in the wilderness can present its own set of challenges. Limited ingredients, lack of modern cooking equipment, and the need to conserve resources make backcountry cooking a unique and rewarding experience.

In this blog post, we will explore delicious recipes specifically designed for wilderness survival. These recipes are not only flavorful but also easy to prepare with minimal equipment and ingredients. Whether you’re hiking, camping, or backpacking, these ideas will help you create nourishing and delicious meals that will fuel your adventures.

1. One-Pot Pasta Primavera

Pasta is a staple in many households, and it can also be a versatile option for backcountry cooking. With just a single pot and a few simple ingredients, you can create a hearty and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pasta
  • 1/2 cup dehydrated mixed vegetables
  • 1/4 cup dehydrated mushrooms
  • 1/4 cup dehydrated parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil.
  2. Add pasta and cook until al dente.
  3. Drain the water, reserving a small amount (approximately 1/4 cup) for later.
  4. In the pot, add olive oil, garlic powder, and reserved water. Stir to combine.
  5. Add dehydrated vegetables and mushrooms to the pot. Mix well.
  6. Simmer the mixture for a few minutes until the vegetables have rehydrated.
  7. Remove from heat and stir in the parmesan cheese.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Let the pasta sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  10. Serve and enjoy!

2. Trail Mix Energy Bars

When you’re on the go, having a quick and energizing snack can make all the difference. These trail mix energy bars are packed with nutrients and easy to carry in your backpack.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dates, pitted
  • 1/2 cup almonds
  • 1/4 cup cashews
  • 1/4 cup pumpkin seeds
  • 1/4 cup shredded coconut
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon cocoa powder
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Put the dates, almonds, cashews, pumpkin seeds, and shredded coconut in a food processor.
  2. Process the ingredients until they are finely chopped and well combined.
  3. Add honey, cocoa powder, chia seeds, and salt to the mixture.
  4. Pulse a few times until all the ingredients are evenly distributed.
  5. Line a baking dish with parchment paper and press the mixture firmly into the dish.
  6. Place the dish in the refrigerator for at least one hour to set.
  7. Once firm, cut into bars or squares.
  8. Store the bars in an airtight container, ready to grab and go on your next adventure.

3. Campfire Pizza

Who says you can’t have pizza in the wilderness? With a few simple ingredients and some creativity, you can whip up a delicious campfire pizza that will leave you craving for more.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (mozzarella, cheddar, or a blend)
  • Toppings of your choice (such as sliced pepperoni, mushrooms, bell peppers, onions)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your campfire or grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a flat surface, dusted with flour.
  3. Spread pizza sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border for the crust.
  4. Sprinkle shredded cheese on top of the sauce.
  5. Add your choice of toppings, distributing them evenly across the pizza.
  6. Carefully transfer the pizza onto a greased baking sheet or pizza stone.
  7. Place the baking sheet or stone on the grill or over the campfire.
  8. Cook for approximately 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is crisp and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  9. Remove from heat and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.
  10. Serve and enjoy your delicious campfire pizza!

Conclusion

Backcountry cooking doesn’t have to be a bland and boring affair. With a little creativity and the right recipes, you can enjoy delicious and nutritious meals while exploring the great outdoors. The recipes shared in this blog post are just a starting point - feel free to experiment and adapt them to suit your tastes and dietary preferences.

Remember, when cooking in the wilderness, it’s important to practice Leave No Trace principles. Clean up after yourself, dispose of waste properly, and be mindful of your impact on the environment. By doing so, you can enjoy the beauty of nature while satisfying your taste buds with these delicious backcountry recipes.
