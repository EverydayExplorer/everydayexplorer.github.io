Why We Need to Protect Our Mountains and Forests


Mountains and forest are an integral part of our natural environment, and they are critical for our survival as a species. They not only provide us with fresh air and clean water, but they also play a crucial role in regulating climate, preserving biodiversity, and supporting ecosystem services that sustain human life. However, in recent years, both mountains and forests are facing unprecedented destruction due to human activities. Therefore, it is essential that we take measures to protect our mountains and forests to ensure that we can continue to enjoy their many benefits for generations to come.

One of the most critical roles that mountains play in our environment is regulating water cycles. Mountains are the source of many of our rivers, which are essential for agriculture, drinking water, and supporting wildlife. However, human activities like deforestation, mining, and other destructive practices are harming the watersheds and eroding soil, leading to flooding during the rainy season and droughts during the dry season. Protecting our mountains’ forests is critical in mitigating these impacts, as trees help retain water through their roots, reducing erosion, and increasing soil stability.

Forests, too, are critical to our environment. They are home to biodiversity, with thousands of different plant and animal species living within them. Deforestation, logging, and forest fires are putting this biodiversity at risk, causing extinction and habitat loss, which ultimately leads to the collapse of complex ecosystems. This, in turn, can have disastrous consequences for human communities that rely on these ecosystems for their livelihoods and food supply.

Forests also play a vital role in mitigating climate change. Trees absorb carbon dioxide, one of the greenhouse gases that contribute to global warming, while producing oxygen through photosynthesis. Massive deforestation, which contributes to around 10% of the world’s greenhouse gas emissions, is exacerbating this problem and rapidly accelerating climate change. In addition, the burning of forests causes even more carbon to be released into the atmosphere, making the climate crisis even worse.

Moreover, both mountains and forests are critical in protecting soil biodiversity. Mountains can mitigate soil erosion by providing a natural barrier against heavy rains and winds. Forests help to retain soil moisture, stabilizing soil structure and, most importantly, building an environment that is viable for soil organisms. The forest floor is home to fungi, bacteria, and other microorganisms that play a critical role in planting, decomposition, and nutrient cycling. With the reduction in forest cover, soil biodiversity is also at risk of being destroyed.

In conclusion, protecting our mountains and forests is not just critical for our survival, but it is also important to help preserve our planet. By safeguarding our forests and mountains, we can help ensure that we have clean air, clean water, and healthy biodiversity. We should work to promote sustainable practices that prioritize the conservation of natural environments, discourage deforestation and mining, and promote tree-planting initiatives. With collective action and individual efforts, we can secure the future of our planet for generations to come.
