The Pleiades in Mythology: Stories from Around the World


When we gaze up at the night sky, we are often captivated by the celestial wonders that twinkle above us. One such wonder is the Pleiades, a cluster of stars that has fascinated cultures around the world for centuries. In mythology, the Pleiades hold a special place, with various tales and legends weaving their way into our collective consciousness. Join us on a journey as we explore the myths and stories surrounding the Pleiades from different cultures across the globe.

Greek Mythology:
In Greek mythology, the Pleiades were known as the seven daughters of Atlas and Pleione. These sisters - Maia, Electra, Taygete, Alcyone, Celaeno, Sterope, and Merope - were beautiful nymphs who caught the attention of the gods. Zeus, the king of the gods, transformed them into stars to protect them from the pursuit of Orion, the great hunter. Today, we can still see these stars shining brightly in the night sky, forming the Pleiades constellation.

Native American Mythology:
Native American tribes also have their own stories about the Pleiades. The Cherokee people, for example, believed that the Pleiades were the seven sons of the Sun, who were placed in the sky to guide and protect their people. The Hopi tribe, on the other hand, saw the Pleiades as the home of the Kachina spirits, powerful beings who brought rain and fertility to the land.

Australian Aboriginal Mythology:
In Australian Aboriginal mythology, the Pleiades are known as the Seven Sisters. According to their stories, the sisters were pursued by a man named Warramurrungundji, who wanted to marry them. In order to escape his advances, the sisters transformed themselves into stars and flew into the sky. To this day, the Aboriginal people use the rising and setting of the Pleiades as a calendar, marking the change of seasons.

Japanese Mythology:
Japanese mythology also has its own interpretation of the Pleiades. In Japan, the Pleiades are known as Subaru, which means ‘unite’ or ‘gather together.’ According to legend, the Subaru constellation represents a group of celestial beings who came together to protect the Earth against evil forces. This story is reflected in the logo of the automobile company Subaru, which features a cluster of stars.

These are just a few examples of the rich tapestry of myths and legends surrounding the Pleiades. From Greek mythology to Native American folklore, the Pleiades have captured the imaginations of people from all corners of the globe. Their twinkling presence in the night sky continues to inspire awe and wonder, reminding us of the power of storytelling and the enduring legacy of ancient myths.

Next time you find yourself gazing up at the stars, take a moment to appreciate the stories that have been passed down through generations. The Pleiades, with their timeless beauty, serve as a reminder of the human desire to understand and connect with the vast universe that surrounds us. So, the next time you spot the Pleiades, remember the myths that have shaped our understanding of these celestial wonders, and let your imagination take flight.
