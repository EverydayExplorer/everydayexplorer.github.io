Hidden Gems: Underrated Superhero Comics You Should Be Reading


As a comic book enthusiast, I understand the struggle of finding new, exciting titles to read outside of the mainstream superhero comics. Although the iconic characters like Batman, Superman, and Spider-Man are a staple in the comic book industry, there are many hidden gems or underrated superhero comics out there that are worth the read.

In this article, I am going to introduce you to some of the most underrated superhero comics that you should definitely check out.

1. Black Hammer

First on the list is Black Hammer, a comic book series by writer Jeff Lemire and artist Dean Ormston. The story follows a group of superheroes who are trapped in a mysterious small town and cannot escape. The characters are reminiscent of classic heroes like Superman, Batman, and Wonder Woman, but the story questions what happens to superheroes after their prime and what happens when their greatest enemy, Anti-God, has already won.

The series has gained critical acclaim for its unique storyline, vivid characters, and compelling art style. Black Hammer is a must-read for any superhero fan seeking something new and fresh.

2. The Vision

The Vision is a limited series written by Tom King and illustrated by Gabriel Hernandez Walta. It follows the titular character, Vision, as he attempts to create the perfect family and live a peaceful life outside of his superhero duties. But as with any good story, things don’t go as planned, and the family’s attempt to integrate into society takes a dark turn. The series explores themes of grief, family, and what it means to be human.

The Vision received critical acclaim and won multiple awards for its unique storytelling, character development, and art style. It can be a good starting point for readers who want to dip their toes into Marvel’s cosmic universe but don’t know where to start.

3. Invincible

Invincible, created by writer Robert Kirkman and artist Cory Walker, is a superhero comic book series about teenager Mark Grayson, who develops superpowers and must balance life as a high school student and a superhero. The series examines the difficult decisions that Mark has to make, balancing his desire to save the world with his responsibilities to his family and friends.

Invincible combines the teenage angst of Spider-Man with the epic action of Superman comics, providing readers with a unique story that is both relatable and exciting.

4. East of West

East of West is a post-apocalyptic, sci-fi western written by Jonathan Hickman and illustrated by Nick Dragotta. The story takes place in an alternate version of America, where the Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse have been reborn as humans and are seeking to bring about the end of the world. A mysterious, undead cowboy named Death defies his former allies and embarks on a journey to stop the Horsemen and save the world.

The series has gained critical acclaim for its unique worldbuilding, intricate plot, and stunning artwork. East of West is a must-read for anyone who enjoys action-packed, thought-provoking comics.

5. Gotham Academy

Gotham Academy is a coming-of-age story set in the famous city of Batman. The series follows a group of students attending the prestigious Gotham Academy as they uncover the mysteries behind the school’s past. It features a diverse cast of characters, from the school’s scholarship student Olive Silverlock to her bubbly roommate Maps Mizoguchi.

Gotham Academy offers a fresh take on the Batman universe, presenting a story that is both entertaining and touching. It can be a perfect starting point for those who want to explore Gotham beyond the Dark Knight.

Final Words

These are just a few of the many hidden gems and underrated superhero comics worth checking out. Whether you’re a longtime fan of the genre or just starting your journey, these titles can provide a fresh new perspective and enjoyable read.

Don’t be afraid to venture outside of the popular comics to discover something new and exciting. You never know what hidden gem you might find.
