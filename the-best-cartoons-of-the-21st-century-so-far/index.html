The Best Cartoons of the 21st Century So Far


The 21st century has seen a boom in animated content, with a wide range of cartoons captivating audiences of all ages. From heartwarming tales to action-packed adventures, these cartoons have left a lasting impact on viewers around the world. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best cartoons of the 21st century so far.

1. Avatar: The Last Airbender (2005-2008)
This critically acclaimed series follows the journey of Aang, the last surviving Airbender, as he learns to master the elements and save the world from the Fire Nation. With its rich storytelling, complex characters, and stunning animation, Avatar: The Last Airbender has become a beloved classic.

2. Adventure Time (2010-2018)
Adventure Time takes viewers on a whimsical journey through the Land of Ooo with Finn the Human and his shape-shifting dog, Jake. This unique and surreal series combines humor, adventure, and heartfelt moments, exploring themes of friendship, love, and personal growth.

3. Rick and Morty (2013-present)
This adult animated series follows the misadventures of an eccentric scientist, Rick, and his naive grandson, Morty. Known for its dark humor, sci-fi references, and complex storytelling, Rick and Morty has gained a dedicated fanbase and critical acclaim.

4. Steven Universe (2013-2019)
Steven Universe tells the story of a young boy who discovers he is part of an intergalactic team of magical guardians known as the Crystal Gems. This groundbreaking series explores themes of identity, love, and acceptance, while challenging traditional gender roles and societal norms.

5. Gravity Falls (2012-2016)
Set in the mysterious town of Gravity Falls, this animated series follows twins Dipper and Mabel as they uncover supernatural secrets and encounter strange creatures. With its clever writing, engaging mysteries, and memorable characters, Gravity Falls has become a fan favorite.

6. BoJack Horseman (2014-2020)
BoJack Horseman is a darkly comedic series that delves into the life of a washed-up sitcom star, who also happens to be a horse. Through its exploration of addiction, mental health, and the pursuit of happiness, BoJack Horseman offers a poignant and thought-provoking portrayal of human struggles.

7. The Legend of Korra (2012-2014)
A sequel to Avatar: The Last Airbender, The Legend of Korra follows the next Avatar, a young woman named Korra, as she faces new challenges and battles against forces that threaten the world’s balance. With its stunning animation and complex characters, this series has continued to captivate audiences.

8. The Simpsons (1989-present)
Although The Simpsons began in the late 20th century, it has continued to entertain audiences well into the 21st century. This iconic animated sitcom has become a cultural phenomenon, blending satire, humor, and social commentary to reflect the ever-changing world we live in.

These are just a few examples of the best cartoons of the 21st century so far. Each of these shows offers something unique, whether it’s compelling storytelling, memorable characters, or thought-provoking themes. As we continue to journey through the 21st century, we can look forward to even more remarkable cartoons that will leave a lasting impact on generations to come.
