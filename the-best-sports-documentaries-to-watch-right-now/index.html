The Best Sports Documentaries to Watch Right Now


Sports documentaries have become increasingly popular in recent years, offering viewers an inside look into the lives of athletes and the stories behind their triumphs and struggles. Whether you’re a die-hard sports fan or simply interested in compelling storytelling, these are the best sports documentaries to watch right now.

1. ‘The Last Dance’ (2020): This Emmy-winning documentary series chronicles the rise of basketball legend Michael Jordan and the Chicago Bulls during their historic 1997-98 season. With never-before-seen footage and interviews, ‘The Last Dance’ provides a captivating behind-the-scenes look at one of the greatest dynasties in sports history.

2. ‘Senna’ (2010): This critically acclaimed documentary tells the story of Brazilian Formula One driver Ayrton Senna, who became a national hero and an international icon before his tragic death at the age of 34. ‘Senna’ offers an intimate portrait of a fiercely talented athlete and explores the complexities of his life both on and off the track.

3. ‘Hoop Dreams’ (1994): Widely regarded as one of the greatest documentaries of all time, ‘Hoop Dreams’ follows the journeys of two inner-city African-American teenagers as they navigate the world of high school basketball and pursue their dreams of playing in the NBA. This groundbreaking film offers an unflinching look at the realities of race, class, and the pursuit of athletic success.

4. ‘O.J.: Made in America’ (2016): This five-part documentary series explores the rise and fall of football star O.J. Simpson, as well as the racial tensions and media circus surrounding his infamous murder trial. ‘O.J.: Made in America’ delves deep into the complexities of celebrity, race relations, and the criminal justice system, making it a thought-provoking and timely watch.

5. ‘Free Solo’ (2018): This Academy Award-winning documentary follows rock climber Alex Honnold as he attempts to free solo climb El Capitan, one of the most challenging and dangerous climbs in the world. With breathtaking cinematography and a nail-biting narrative, ‘Free Solo’ offers a thrilling and awe-inspiring exploration of human determination and the pursuit of greatness.

6. ‘The Two Escobars’ (2010): This ESPN 30 for 30 documentary examines the intersecting lives of Colombian soccer player Andrés Escobar and drug lord Pablo Escobar. Through their stories, ‘The Two Escobars’ explores the intertwined worlds of sports and organized crime, shedding light on the dark underbelly of the beautiful game.

7. ‘Undefeated’ (2011): This Academy Award-winning documentary follows the Manassas Tigers, a high school football team in Memphis, Tennessee, as they strive to overcome adversity and achieve success on and off the field. ‘Undefeated’ is a powerful and inspiring film that captures the resilience and determination of these young athletes.

8. ‘Icarus’ (2017): Filmmaker Bryan Fogel sets out to uncover the truth about doping in sports, but his investigation takes an unexpected turn when he becomes embroiled in the Russian doping scandal. ‘Icarus’ is a gripping and eye-opening documentary that exposes the widespread corruption and cover-ups in the world of professional sports.

9. ‘The Barkley Marathons: The Race That Eats Its Young’ (2014): This offbeat and fascinating documentary explores the Barkley Marathons, one of the most grueling and secretive ultramarathons in the world. With its quirky characters and unconventional format, ‘The Barkley Marathons’ offers a unique perspective on the limits of human endurance.

10. ‘Rising Phoenix’ (2020): This documentary tells the inspiring stories of Paralympic athletes from around the world, highlighting their triumphs, struggles, and the impact they have on society. ‘Rising Phoenix’ celebrates the power of the human spirit and challenges our perceptions of disability and athleticism.

These sports documentaries offer a wide range of stories, from the legendary careers of iconic athletes to the untold tales of underdogs and the exploration of larger societal issues. Whether you’re a sports enthusiast or simply appreciate compelling storytelling, these documentaries are sure to captivate and inspire. So grab some popcorn, sit back, and immerse yourself in the world of sports through these must-watch films.
