Survival Hunting: Techniques for Finding and Trapping Food


In a world where modern conveniences have become the norm, it’s easy to forget that our ancestors once relied on their hunting skills to survive. While hunting for food is not a necessity for most of us today, understanding the techniques and strategies used by our forefathers can still be valuable knowledge. Whether you’re an avid outdoors enthusiast or simply want to be prepared for any situation, learning survival hunting techniques can prove to be an essential skill.

1. Tracking and Scouting

Tracking is a fundamental skill in survival hunting. To successfully track animals, you need to be able to read signs and clues left behind by them. Look for footprints, broken twigs, disturbed vegetation, and droppings. Pay attention to the direction of tracks and the freshness of signs to determine if the animal is nearby. Scouting involves observing the area for potential hunting grounds, such as game trails, feeding areas, or watering holes. Familiarize yourself with the habits and behaviors of the animals you want to hunt, as this will give you an advantage in finding them.

2. Camouflage and Concealment

Animals have highly developed senses, and it’s important to blend into your surroundings to avoid detection. Camouflage clothing and gear that match the environment you’re in can help you go unnoticed. Use natural materials, such as branches, leaves, or mud, to further conceal yourself. Take note of wind direction to prevent your scent from giving away your presence. Mastering the art of staying hidden will increase your chances of getting close enough for a successful hunt.

3. Primitive Traps and Snares

When it comes to survival hunting, traps and snares can be effective tools for catching small game. Familiarize yourself with different types of traps and snares, such as deadfall traps, snare loops, or figure-four traps. These can be constructed using natural materials found in the wilderness. Remember to place your traps in areas where you’ve observed animal activity. Patience is key when using traps, as it may take some time before an animal is caught.

4. Hunting with Primitive Weapons

While firearms are the go-to choice for modern hunters, survival hunting often requires a more primitive approach. Learning to use traditional weapons, such as a bow and arrow or a slingshot, can be a valuable skill. These weapons require practice and precision to master. Additionally, understanding the anatomy of your target animal is crucial to ensure an ethical and successful kill. Aim for vital areas, such as the heart or lungs, to minimize suffering and improve your chances of a clean kill.

5. Edible Plants and Insects

In a survival situation, finding food can be challenging. Therefore, it’s important to expand your knowledge beyond hunting animals. Familiarize yourself with edible plants, berries, and insects that can provide sustenance when game is scarce. However, be cautious and ensure you can correctly identify these food sources to avoid consuming something toxic.

Remember, survival hunting is not about sport or entertainment but about ensuring your survival in a challenging environment. Always prioritize safety, respect for nature, and ethical hunting practices. While these techniques may not be necessary in our modern lives, having a basic understanding of survival hunting can give you a sense of self-reliance and preparedness. So, take the time to learn these skills, practice them responsibly, and gain a deeper connection with the natural world.
