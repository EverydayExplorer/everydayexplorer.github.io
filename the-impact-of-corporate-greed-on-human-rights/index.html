The Impact of Corporate Greed on Human Rights


As we journey through the 21st century, the rise of corporate greed is one of the most pressing issues that we face. In an era where big corporations are more powerful than ever before, the impact of corporate greed on human rights is increasingly evident. For years, businesses have put their interests over those of society and its members, leading to gross human rights violations.

The greed for profit over the well-being and dignity of human beings has led to some of the most significant violations of human rights, including environmental degradation, forced labor, and exploitation of natural resources. Corporations show little regard for human life and the wider community in which they operate. In most cases, corporate leaders focus on short-term gains at the expense of long-term sustainability.

For instance, the fast fashion industry is notorious for exploiting cheap labor in developing countries, employing children, and exposing workers to hazardous working conditions. Sweatshops and factories producing clothes for fast fashion brands have been linked to modern-day slavery, with workers being underpaid and overworked. Corporate leaders turn a blind eye to these violations, turning a blind eye to the plight of those who manufacture the clothes.

Unfortunately, such corporate greed has far-reaching effects, spreading from industries such as fashion and agriculture to medical and pharmaceuticals. The cost of prescription drugs, for instance, has increased exponentially over the years, with the pharmaceutical industry focusing solely on profit rather than ensuring access to medication, especially in developing countries. Agribusinesses, on the other hand, prioritize profits over feeding the world, leading to the takeover of small family-owned farms globally. They also produce genetically modified crops which are harmful to the environment and human health.

The concept of corporate social responsibility (CSR) was introduced in the 1970s as a voluntary measure by companies to ensure that their actions did not harm people or the planet. However, this concept has failed to achieve significant results. Aligned with a business strategy, CSR can be practiced against ethical standards to show that corporations have the welfare of human beings at heart. But it is just a side effect of CEOs chasing profits for shareholders.

In conclusion, it is high time businesses adopt ethical conduct beyond rules of compliance and profit maximization. Any business aimed at producing goods and services for human consumption is obliged to prioritize socially responsible practices over making profits. From the fast fashion and food industry to the medical and tech industry, companies need to prioritize humanity over their bottom line. It is time for us to build partnerships for a better world, one where corporate greed is minimized in favor of human rights, sustainability, and respect for our planet. It’s time for corporate and individual responsibility to ensure that anyone responsible for violating human rights is held accountable.
