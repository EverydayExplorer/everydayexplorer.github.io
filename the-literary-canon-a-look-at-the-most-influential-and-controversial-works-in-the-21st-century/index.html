The Literary Canon: A Look at the Most Influential and Controversial Works in the 21st Century


The 21st century has been a time of immense change and growth in the world of literature. With the rise of digital technology and the internet, the literary landscape has become more diverse and inclusive than ever before. This has led to the emergence of a new literary canon, one that is both influential and controversial. In this article, we will explore some of the most significant works of the 21st century, examining their impact on the literary world and the debates they have sparked.

1. The Road by Cormac McCarthy

Published in 2006, The Road is a post-apocalyptic novel that tells the story of a father and his son as they navigate a world ravaged by an unspecified catastrophe. The novel has been praised for its powerful exploration of human resilience and the bonds of family in the face of adversity. However, it has also been criticized for its bleak portrayal of humanity and its lack of hope for the future.

2. The Goldfinch by Donna Tartt

Winner of the 2014 Pulitzer Prize for Fiction, The Goldfinch is a coming-of-age story that follows the life of a young man named Theo Decker after the death of his mother in a bombing at the Metropolitan Museum of Art. The novel has been lauded for its intricate plot and rich characterization, but it has also faced criticism for its length and perceived lack of originality.

3. The Sellout by Paul Beatty

Published in 2015, The Sellout is a satirical novel that follows the story of a man named Bonbon who attempts to reinstitute slavery and segregation in order to prove that racism no longer exists. The novel has been praised for its biting humor and its exploration of race and identity in America, but it has also been criticized for its controversial subject matter and its potential to reinforce racial stereotypes.

4. The Sympathizer by Viet Thanh Nguyen

Winner of the 2016 Pulitzer Prize for Fiction, The Sympathizer is a novel that follows the story of a Vietnamese spy working for the South Vietnamese government during the Vietnam War. The novel has been praised for its complex narrative structure and its exploration of identity, loyalty, and the consequences of war. However, it has also been criticized for its graphic violence and its portrayal of women.

5. The Underground Railroad by Colson Whitehead

Published in 2016, The Underground Railroad is a historical fiction novel that tells the story of a young slave named Cora as she escapes from a Georgia plantation and travels north on an actual underground railroad. The novel has been praised for its powerful portrayal of slavery and its innovative use of magical realism, but it has also been criticized for its lack of historical accuracy and its potential to oversimplify the complex reality of slavery.

6. Sing, Unburied, Sing by Jesmyn Ward

Published in 2017, Sing, Unburied, Sing is a novel that follows the story of a young boy named Jojo and his family as they navigate the challenges of race, poverty, and loss in the American South. The novel has been praised for its lyrical prose and its exploration of the black experience in America, but it has also been criticized for its perceived lack of originality and its heavy reliance on magical realism.

7. Normal People by Sally Rooney

Published in 2018, Normal People is a novel that follows the story of two young people named Connell and Marianne as they navigate the complexities of love, class, and identity in Ireland. The novel has been praised for its realistic portrayal of modern relationships and its exploration of mental health, but it has also been criticized for its perceived lack of depth and its reliance on clichéd tropes.

Conclusion

The 21st-century literary canon is a diverse and ever-evolving collection of works that reflect the complexities of our modern world. These influential and controversial works have sparked important debates about race, gender, identity, and the human experience, and they will undoubtedly continue to shape the literary landscape for years to come.
