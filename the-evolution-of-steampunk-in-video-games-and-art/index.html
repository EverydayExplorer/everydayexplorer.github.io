The Evolution of Steampunk in Video Games and Art


Steampunk is a genre that has captured the imaginations of many worldwide. With its blend of Victorian aesthetics and steam-powered technology, it has inspired countless works of art and literature. In recent years, steampunk has also made its mark in the world of video games, allowing players to immerse themselves in fantastical worlds filled with mechanical marvels and intricate clockwork designs. This article will explore the evolution of steampunk in video games and art, highlighting its impact on the gaming industry and showcasing some of the best examples of steampunk-inspired games and artworks.

But first, let’s take a step back and delve into the origins of steampunk. The term itself was coined in the late 1980s by author K.W. Jeter as a way to describe a subgenre of science fiction that draws inspiration from the steam-powered machinery of the 19th century. Steampunk worlds are often set in an alternate history where steam power is still the dominant form of technology, leading to a unique blend of Victorian aesthetics and futuristic elements.

In video games, steampunk has become a popular genre, offering players a chance to experience a world unlike anything they’ve seen before. One of the earliest examples of steampunk in gaming is the classic point-and-click adventure game ‘Myst’ (1993). While not explicitly labeled as steampunk, it features a visually stunning setting filled with intricate devices and imaginative technology. Players navigate through a series of puzzles in a mysterious island, uncovering the secrets of a long-lost civilization.

Fast forward to the early 2000s, and we see the rise of more explicitly steampunk games. ‘Arcanum: Of Steamworks and Magick Obscura’ (2001) is a notable example. This RPG blends traditional fantasy elements with steampunk technology, allowing players to create characters that wield both magic and technology. The game introduces innovative mechanics like a complex crafting system and a morality system that impacts gameplay and story.

As technology advanced, so did the complexity and detail of steampunk in video games. ‘Bioshock’ (2007) is a prime example of this evolution. Set in an underwater city called Rapture, the game features a blend of art deco and steampunk aesthetics. Players uncover the secrets of this fallen city while encountering various genetically-altered enemies and utilizing unique steampunk-inspired weapons and powers.

Another standout in the realm of steampunk gaming is the ‘Dishonored’ series (2012-2016). Set in a Victorian-inspired city called Dunwall, the game offers a richly detailed world brimming with industrial steampunk elements. Players assume the role of a supernatural assassin and navigate through a dystopian city filled with political intrigue and steampunk technology. The series beautifully combines stealth gameplay, compelling narratives, and a visually stunning steampunk art style.

Apart from video games, steampunk has also made a significant impact in the world of art. From paintings and sculptures to cosplay and fashion, artists have embraced the steampunk aesthetic and created breathtaking works that capture the essence of this genre. Steampunk art often focuses on intricate details, utilizing gears, clockwork mechanisms, and Victorian-inspired elements to create visually stunning pieces.

One artist who has made a name for himself in the steampunk art world is Brian Kesinger. Known for his whimsical and charming steampunk illustrations, Kesinger seamlessly blends classic Disney characters with steampunk elements, creating a unique and captivating style. His art showcases the perfect balance between nostalgia and innovation, appealing to both fans of the original characters and enthusiasts of the steampunk genre.

In addition to visual art, steampunk-inspired music has also gained popularity. Bands like Abney Park and Steam Powered Giraffe infuse their music with steampunk themes and aesthetics, creating a soundscape that complements the visual world of steampunk.

In conclusion, the evolution of steampunk in video games and art has brought a fresh and unique experience to gamers and art enthusiasts alike. From the early days of simple point-and-click adventures to the immersive and visually stunning worlds of today, steampunk has captured the hearts of many. Through its intricate designs, innovative technology, and rich narratives, it has carved a niche in the gaming industry and become an iconic genre in art. Whether in video games or artworks, steampunk continues to inspire and captivate audiences with its blend of Victorian elegance and fantastical possibilities.
