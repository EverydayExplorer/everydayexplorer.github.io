The Best Fantasy Video Games: A Comprehensive Guide


Fantasy video games have captivated players for decades, transporting them to magical worlds filled with epic quests, mythical creatures, and unforgettable characters. Whether it’s slaying dragons, exploring enchanted forests, or embarking on legendary adventures, the fantasy genre offers an immersive experience that allows players to escape reality. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore some of the best fantasy video games available, discussing their unique features, gameplay mechanics, and what makes them stand out in the genre.

1. The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim

Release Date: November 11, 2011
Platform: PC, PS4, Xbox One, Nintendo Switch

Skyrim is a cornerstone of fantasy gaming and is often hailed as one of the greatest video games of all time. Set in the sprawling province of Skyrim, players take on the role of the Dragonborn, a hero destined to save the world from an ancient dragon. The game’s open-world design allows for unparalleled exploration, offering players the freedom to follow the main quest or delve into countless side quests and activities.

Key Features:

  • Expansive Open World: Explore diverse landscapes, including mountains, forests, and cities.
  • Character Customization: Create your own character with various races and skill sets.
  • Dynamic Quests: Experience a living world where your choices affect the storyline.

Why It Stands Out:

Skyrim’s depth and richness draw players into its world. The modding community has further enhanced the game, ensuring its longevity and replayability. The game’s atmospheric soundtrack and engaging lore make it an unforgettable experience.

2. The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt

Release Date: May 19, 2015
Platform: PC, PS4, Xbox One, Nintendo Switch

The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt is a masterclass in storytelling and world-building. Players assume the role of Geralt of Rivia, a monster hunter searching for his missing adopted daughter. The game features a vast open world with a rich narrative and morally complex choices that affect the outcome of the story.

Key Features:

  • Narrative Depth: Multiple branching storylines and complex characters.
  • Rich Side Quests: Engaging side quests that often rival the main story.
  • Gwent Card Game: A fun mini-game that adds depth to the overall experience.

Why It Stands Out:

The Witcher 3 combines beautiful graphics with a compelling narrative, captivating players from start to finish. Its expansive world feels alive, with dynamic weather and day-night cycles that enhance immersion.

3. Dark Souls Series

Release Dates:

  • Dark Souls: September 22, 2011
  • Dark Souls II: March 11, 2014
  • Dark Souls III: March 24, 2016
    Platform: PC, PS4, Xbox One, Nintendo Switch

The Dark Souls series is renowned for its challenging gameplay and atmospheric storytelling. Set in a dark fantasy universe, players navigate treacherous environments filled with formidable enemies and intricate lore. The series is known for its punishing difficulty, requiring players to learn from their mistakes and adapt their strategies.

Key Features:

  • Challenging Combat: Skill-based gameplay that rewards patience and precision.
  • Intricate Level Design: Interconnected worlds that encourage exploration.
  • Rich Lore: Subtle storytelling that encourages players to piece together the narrative.

Why It Stands Out:

Dark Souls has created a dedicated community of players who appreciate its depth and challenge. The satisfaction of overcoming difficult bosses and uncovering the game’s lore keeps players coming back for more.

4. Final Fantasy VII

Release Date: August 15, 1997 (Original)
Platform: PC, PS4, Xbox One, Nintendo Switch (Various Remakes)

Final Fantasy VII is a landmark title in the RPG genre, known for its engaging story and memorable characters. Players follow Cloud Strife as he teams up with a group of rebels to fight against the oppressive Shinra Corporation and the enigmatic Sephiroth. The game’s combination of turn-based combat and exploration has left a lasting impact on the gaming industry.

Key Features:

  • Iconic Characters: A cast of memorable heroes and villains.
  • Engaging Storyline: A deep narrative with themes of identity, loss, and redemption.
  • Limit Breaks and Materia System: Unique combat mechanics that allow for strategic gameplay.

Why It Stands Out:

The emotional weight of Final Fantasy VII’s story and its unforgettable soundtrack have solidified its status as a classic. The recent remake has introduced a new generation to its compelling world.

5. Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild

Release Date: March 3, 2017
Platform: Nintendo Switch, Wii U

Breath of the Wild reinvented the Legend of Zelda franchise, offering a vast open world filled with adventure and discovery. Players control Link as he awakens from a long slumber to defeat Calamity Ganon and save Hyrule. The game’s emphasis on exploration and player choice has set a new standard for open-world design.

Key Features:

  • Open-Ended Exploration: Players can approach challenges in multiple ways.
  • Physics-Based Gameplay: Creative solutions to puzzles and combat situations.
  • Engaging Crafting System: Gather resources to create food, elixirs, and weapons.

Why It Stands Out:

Breath of the Wild’s freedom and creativity allow players to engage with the world in unique ways. Its beautiful art style and captivating music enhance the overall experience, making it a must-play for fans of fantasy gaming.

6. World of Warcraft

Release Date: November 23, 2004
Platform: PC

World of Warcraft (WoW) has become synonymous with fantasy MMORPGs. Set in the expansive world of Azeroth, players create characters and embark on quests, engage in player-versus-environment (PvE) and player-versus-player (PvP) content, and explore a rich lore that spans decades.

Key Features:

  • Immersive Lore: A deep and evolving storyline with countless quests.
  • Community Engagement: Join guilds and participate in raids and events.
  • Regular Content Updates: Expansions and patches that keep the game fresh.

Why It Stands Out:

WoW’s community-driven gameplay and social aspects create a unique experience. The ever-expanding world, combined with rich lore and engaging gameplay, has kept players invested for years.

7. Dragon Age: Inquisition

Release Date: November 18, 2014
Platform: PC, PS4, Xbox One

Dragon Age: Inquisition invites players into Thedas, a world filled with political intrigue and magical conflict. As the Inquisitor, players must unite factions to combat a new threat arising from the Fade. The game features a blend of real-time combat and strategic decision-making.

Key Features:

  • Character-Driven Storytelling: Deep relationships with party members.
  • Tactical Combat: Pause-and-play mechanics allowing for strategic planning.
  • Open-World Exploration: Vast environments filled with quests and secrets.

Why It Stands Out:

Dragon Age: Inquisition’s emphasis on player choice and character development creates a rich narrative experience. The game’s stunning visuals and engaging combat ensure that players remain immersed in its world.

8. Hollow Knight

Release Date: February 24, 2017
Platform: PC, PS4, Xbox One, Nintendo Switch

Hollow Knight takes players on a journey through Hallownest, a vast, interconnected world filled with challenging enemies and intricate lore. This Metroidvania-style game combines platforming with combat, offering a unique experience in the fantasy genre.

Key Features:

  • Beautiful Art Style: Hand-drawn visuals and atmospheric design.
  • Challenging Gameplay: Intense combat and exploration that test player skills.
  • Rich Lore: Discover the history of Hallownest through environmental storytelling.

Why It Stands Out:

Hollow Knight’s blend of challenging gameplay, stunning visuals, and deep lore make it a standout title in the indie gaming scene. Its intricate world encourages exploration and rewards curiosity.

9. Divinity: Original Sin 2

Release Date: September 14, 2017
Platform: PC, PS4, Xbox One, Nintendo Switch

Divinity: Original Sin 2 is a critically acclaimed RPG that combines deep storytelling with tactical turn-based combat. Players create their character and embark on a quest filled with choices that shape the world around them.

Key Features:

  • Rich Character Customization: Choose from various races and backgrounds.
  • Tactical Combat: A strategic turn-based system that encourages creative solutions.
  • Multiplayer Options: Play cooperatively with friends or compete against them.

Why It Stands Out:

Divinity: Original Sin 2 is celebrated for its intricate writing, engaging gameplay mechanics, and the freedom it offers players to approach challenges in unique ways. The game’s world feels alive, with consequences for every choice made.

10. Monster Hunter: World

Release Date: January 26, 2018
Platform: PC, PS4, Xbox One

Monster Hunter: World redefined the franchise with its expansive world and accessible gameplay. Players take on the role of hunters tasked with tracking down and defeating massive monsters in a variety of environments.

Key Features:

  • Diverse Ecosystems: Explore dynamic habitats filled with unique monsters.
  • Cooperative Multiplayer: Team up with friends to take on challenging hunts.
  • Customizable Gear: Craft and upgrade weapons and armor based on the monsters hunted.

Why It Stands Out:

Monster Hunter: World’s blend of action, strategy, and cooperative gameplay creates an engaging experience. Its mesmerizing graphics and dynamic environments draw players into a beautifully crafted world.
