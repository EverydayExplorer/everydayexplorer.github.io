How to Transition your Skincare Routine for Winter


As winter approaches, our skin goes through a lot of changes due to the cold and dry air. The cold weather leads to reduced humidity, which leaves our skin dry and itchy. This cold weather wind and dry air affects our skin texture, moisture, and natural oils. Especially in winter, our regular skincare routine may not be enough. So, we need to make some changes in our skincare regimen to combat the winter season.

Thus, let’s dive into the tips for transitioning skincare routine for winters:

1. Decrease Use of Exfoliants

During winters, our skin becomes prone to becoming dry and flaky. Hence, the usage of exfoliating products, such as face scrubs, should reduce to avoid over-exfoliating your skin which could lead to irritation. Instead, use gentle exfoliants like chemical exfoliants, which gently dissolve the dead skin cells, or opt for a gentle face wash.

2. Use Hydrating Cleanser

Using a hydrating cleanser can be beneficial for your skin in winter. These cleansers remove dirt, pollutants, and impurities without stripping your skin’s natural oils, leaving your skin soft, hydrated, and supple. Additionally, you may switch to cream-based cleansers or oil-based cleansers during winters for more hydration.

3. Increase Moisture

In the winter season, our skin lacks moisture due to the dry air. Therefore, it is crucial to provide extra moisture to our skin through the use of a hydrating moisturizer. You can opt for a moisturizer that contains ingredients such as hyaluronic acid, glycerin, urea, or ceramides, which help to lock-in moisture and keep your skin hydrated throughout the day. If your skin is extra-dry, you can layer your moisturizer by using a hydrating serum or oil in the evening.

4. Don’t Skip Sunscreen

UV rays can damage your skin, and the intensity of UV rays is not any less during winter. Hence, it’s vital to use sunscreen, even during winter. You could choose a daily moisturizer with SPF protection or a dedicated sunscreen for added protection.

5. Use a Humidifier

Winter air is dry, which sucks the moisture out of your skin and leaves it dry, tight, and irritated. One way to combat this is by using a humidifier at home. A humidifier releases moisture into the air, which helps to maintain the natural moisture balance of your skin. This is an excellent remedy for those living in extreme cold areas where the temperature goes below zero.

6. Apply lip balm

Our lips are susceptible to cracking and peeling due to the dry and cold weather of winters. To keep your lips healthy and moisturized, use a lip balm regularly. Opt for a lip balm that contains nourishing ingredients like beeswax, shea butter, and vitamin E to repair chapped lips.

7. Use Omega Fatty Acids

Finally, to maintain your skin’s natural oils, you can consume foods rich in omega fatty acids, such as flaxseeds, salmon, chia seeds, or walnuts. These foods can provide nourishment to your skin from the inside-out and maintain the skin’s natural oils, making it healthy and glowing.

Final Thoughts

Winter calls for a change in our skincare regimen. Follow the tips mentioned above to transition your skincare routine for winters. These tips will help you keep your skin healthy, hydrated, and glowing during this dry and harsh winter season. Remember, consistency is key, so, stick to a routine that works best for you. Keep your skin healthy and beautiful!
