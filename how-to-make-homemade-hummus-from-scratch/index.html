How to Make Homemade Hummus from Scratch


Hummus has become an incredibly popular dip and spread in recent years, and for good reason. Made from a blend of chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, and garlic, hummus is not only delicious, but also nutritious and versatile. It can be enjoyed as a dip with fresh vegetables, pita bread, or chips, or used as a spread in sandwiches and wraps. The best part? You can easily make it at home from scratch with just a few simple ingredients. In this blog post, we will guide you through the step-by-step process of making homemade hummus from scratch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas (15 ounces), drained
  • 3 tablespoons of tahini
  • 3 tablespoons of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt (adjust to taste)

Step 1: Prepare the Chickpeas

Start by draining and rinsing the chickpeas. If you prefer a smoother hummus, you can also remove the skins from the chickpeas by pinching them gently between your fingers. While this step is not necessary, it helps to achieve a creamier texture.

Step 2: Blend the Ingredients

In a food processor or blender, combine the chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, minced garlic, olive oil, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides if needed. If the mixture seems too thick, you can add a tablespoon of water at a time until you reach your desired consistency.

Step 3: Adjust the Seasonings

Once the hummus is smooth, taste and adjust the seasonings as needed. You can add more salt, lemon juice, or even a pinch of cumin for extra flavor. Remember, cooking is all about personal preference, so feel free to experiment and find the perfect balance of flavors for your taste buds.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

Transfer the homemade hummus to a serving bowl and drizzle with a little extra olive oil. You can also garnish it with a sprinkle of paprika, chopped fresh parsley, or toasted sesame seeds for added visual appeal. Serve with your favorite dippers such as carrot sticks, cucumber slices, or warm pita bread, and enjoy!

Tips and Tricks:

  • Use good quality tahini: Tahini is a key ingredient in hummus and can greatly impact its flavor. Opt for a high-quality tahini made from toasted sesame seeds for the best results.
  • Customize your hummus: While the classic hummus recipe calls for the ingredients mentioned above, you can get creative and add your own twist. You can experiment with roasted red peppers, sun-dried tomatoes, or even herbs like cilantro or basil to create unique flavor variations.
  • Don’t be afraid to adjust the consistency: If your hummus is too thick, you can thin it out by adding a little water or extra lemon juice until it reaches the desired creaminess.
  • Store it properly: Homemade hummus can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to one week. Just give it a good stir before serving.

The Benefits of Homemade Hummus

Making hummus from scratch not only allows you to control the quality of the ingredients, but it also gives you the freedom to customize it to your liking. Plus, it’s a healthier alternative to store-bought hummus that often contains preservatives and added sugars. Chickpeas, the main ingredient in hummus, are packed with protein, fiber, and essential vitamins and minerals, making homemade hummus a nutritious addition to your diet.

Final Thoughts

If you’re a hummus lover, why not give homemade hummus a try? It’s an easy and delicious way to enjoy a nutritious snack or meal. With just a few simple ingredients and a blender or food processor, you can whip up a batch of creamy, flavorful hummus that is sure to impress your friends and family. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to experience the joy of homemade hummus!
