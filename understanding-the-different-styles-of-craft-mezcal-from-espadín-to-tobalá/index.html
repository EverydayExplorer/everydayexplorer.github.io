Understanding the Different Styles of Craft Mezcal: From Espadín to Tobalá


If you consider yourself a lover of mezcal, the traditional Mexican liquor made from the agave plant, you are probably familiar with the fact that there are various styles of craft mezcal available on the market. Each style offers a unique taste and experience, making it an exciting journey for those who appreciate this ancient beverage.

In this blog post, we will explore the different styles of craft mezcal, from the widely known Espadín to the more rare and exotic Tobalá. By understanding these styles, you will be able to appreciate the diversity and richness of this fascinating spirit.

1. Espadín: The Classic Mezcal

Espadín is the most widely produced and consumed style of craft mezcal. It is made from the agave Espadín, a type of agave that takes around 7-10 years to mature before it can be harvested. This style of mezcal is known for its smooth and smoky flavor profile, making it a favorite among mezcal enthusiasts. Espadín mezcal can be enjoyed on its own or used as a base for mezcal cocktails.

2. Tobalá: The Rare Gem

Tobalá mezcal is highly sought after due to its rarity and unique flavor profile. It is made from the wild Tobalá agave, which takes anywhere from 10-30 years to reach maturity and can only be found in specific regions of Mexico. Because of its scarcity, Tobalá mezcal is often more expensive than other styles. It is characterized by its complex flavors, including notes of tropical fruits, spices, and floral undertones. Tobalá is often enjoyed neat to fully appreciate its distinct taste.

3. Tobaziche: The Earthy Delight

Tobaziche mezcal is made from the Tobaziche agave, a variety that belongs to the Karwinskii family. This style of mezcal has a unique earthy flavor profile, with hints of spices, herbs, and citrus. The agave Tobaziche usually takes around 12-25 years to reach maturity. Its distinct taste makes it a favorite among mezcal connoisseurs who appreciate its rustic and aromatic qualities.

4. Arroqueño: The Bold and Robust

Arroqueño mezcal is made from the Arroqueño agave, which can take up to 25-35 years to mature. This style of mezcal is known for its bold and robust flavor profile, with smoky undertones and a long-lasting finish. Arroqueño mezcal is often described as having complex flavors, including notes of caramel, tobacco, and leather. It is best enjoyed on its own to fully appreciate its rich and intense characteristics.

5. Madrecuixe: The Herbal Elixir

Made from the Madrecuixe agave, this style of mezcal offers a unique herbal and botanical flavor profile. The Madrecuixe agave typically takes around 12-20 years to mature. Mezcal made from this agave variety often exhibits strong aromas of green herbs, mint, and eucalyptus. Its distinctive taste makes it a popular choice for those who enjoy the freshness and complexity of herbal flavors.

6. Coyote: The Wild Spirit

Coyote mezcal is made from the Coyote agave, an agave variety that can be found growing wild in the mountains of Mexico. This style of mezcal is characterized by its wild and untamed nature, with a flavor profile that showcases both earthy and fruity notes. Coyote mezcal is often described as having a smoky and peppery taste, making it a favorite among mezcal enthusiasts looking for a bold and adventurous experience.

7. Cuixe: The Exotic Choice

Cuixe mezcal is made from the rare Cuixe agave, a variety that can take up to 20-25 years to mature. This style of mezcal is known for its exotic and complex flavor profile, with notes of tropical fruits, spices, and hints of smoke. Cuixe mezcal is often described as having a velvety texture and a long-lasting finish. It is a favorite among mezcal aficionados who appreciate the unique and uncommon characteristics of this agave variety.

In conclusion, understanding the different styles of craft mezcal allows you to explore the world of this ancient Mexican spirit in a more meaningful way. From the classic and widely available Espadín to the rare and exotic Tobalá, each style offers a unique taste experience that reflects the variety and richness of the agave plant. Whether you prefer the smooth and smoky flavors of Espadín or the bold and robust characteristics of Arroqueño, there is a craft mezcal style out there to satisfy your palate and ignite your imagination. So, next time you enjoy a glass of mezcal, take a moment to appreciate the craftsmanship and diversity that goes into each bottle, and savor the journey it takes you on.
