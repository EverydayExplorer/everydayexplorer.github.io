Mastering the Winged Eyeliner: Tips for a Flawless Flick


The winged eyeliner is a classic and timeless makeup trend that has been around for decades. It is known for its ability to instantly enhance and define the eyes, giving them a seductive and captivating look. However, achieving a flawless and symmetrical flick can be challenging for many people. In this blog post, we will share some expert tips and tricks to help you master the art of winged eyeliner and create a flawless flick every time.

1. Start with the Right Tools

Having the right tools is crucial for achieving a flawless winged eyeliner. Invest in a high-quality liquid eyeliner or gel eyeliner that has a fine precision tip. This will allow for better control and precision when creating your wing. Additionally, make sure to have a clean and thin makeup brush or an angled eyeliner brush for a more precise application.

2. Prep Your Eyelids

Before applying the winged eyeliner, it is important to prep your eyelids. Start by cleansing your face and removing any excess oil or makeup. Then, apply an eyeshadow primer to create a smooth and even canvas for your eyeliner. This will help the eyeliner to last longer and prevent smudging.

3. Map Out Your Wing

Creating a symmetrical wing can be tricky, especially if you are new to winged eyeliner. To make it easier, use an angled brush or a thin makeup brush to map out the shape of your wing before applying the eyeliner. Start by drawing a small line from the outer corner of your eye, angling it upwards towards the end of your eyebrow. Then, connect the line to your upper lash line to create a triangle shape. This will serve as a guide for creating your wing.

4. Use Short Strokes

When applying the winged eyeliner, use short strokes instead of trying to create a perfect line in one go. This will give you better control and precision. Start by drawing small dots or dashes along your upper lash line and then connect them with short strokes to create a smooth and even line. Extend the line towards the outer corner of your eye to create the wing.

5. Clean Up with a Concealer

Even the most skilled makeup artists make mistakes when applying winged eyeliner. If you make a mistake or if your lines aren’t as precise as you’d like them to be, don’t worry! You can easily clean up any smudges or uneven lines with a concealer. Use a small, flat brush and apply a tiny amount of concealer along the edges of the wing to sharpen and perfect the lines.

6. Practice Makes Perfect

Mastering the winged eyeliner takes time and practice. Don’t get discouraged if your first attempts aren’t perfect. Keep practicing and experimenting with different techniques and products until you find what works best for you. Remember, even professional makeup artists had to start somewhere.

7. Experiment with Different Styles

Once you have mastered the basic winged eyeliner, don’t be afraid to experiment with different styles and variations. You can try an exaggerated wing for a more dramatic look, or a subtle and thin wing for a more natural look. Play around with different colors and textures, such as metallic or glitter eyeliners, to add a fun and glamorous twist to your winged eyeliner.

In conclusion, mastering the winged eyeliner is all about practice, patience, and having the right tools. By following these tips and tricks, you will be on your way to creating a flawless flick every time. So, grab your eyeliner and get ready to unleash your inner winged goddess!
