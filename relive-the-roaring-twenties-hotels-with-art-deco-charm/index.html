Relive the Roaring Twenties: Hotels with Art Deco Charm


The Roaring Twenties was a time of great social and cultural change, characterized by economic prosperity, technological advancements, and a flourishing of art and design. One of the most iconic artistic styles of this era was Art Deco, which was characterized by its bold geometric shapes, lavish ornamentation, and luxurious materials. The Art Deco style was seen in everything from architecture to fashion to interior design, and its influence can still be seen today in buildings and objects from the 1920s and 1930s.

One of the best ways to relive the glamour and sophistication of the Roaring Twenties is to stay in a hotel with Art Deco charm. Many hotels from this era have been lovingly preserved or restored, allowing guests to step back in time and experience the elegance of the Jazz Age. From the moment you walk through the doors of these hotels, you are transported to a world of glittering chandeliers, gleaming marble floors, and sumptuous furnishings.

One of the most famous Art Deco hotels in the world is the Waldorf Astoria in New York City. Built in 1931, this iconic hotel is a masterpiece of Art Deco design, with its sleek lines, geometric patterns, and glamorous detailing. From the moment you enter the grand lobby with its soaring ceilings and intricate friezes, you are surrounded by the opulence and elegance of the Roaring Twenties. The guest rooms are equally luxurious, with custom-designed furnishings, plush bedding, and all the modern amenities you could desire.

Another stunning example of an Art Deco hotel is the Fontainebleau in Miami Beach. Built in 1954, this historic hotel is a prime example of the Miami Modern style, which drew inspiration from Art Deco and blended it with elements of Streamline Moderne and Tropical Deco. The Fontainebleau is a true architectural masterpiece, with its curving lines, porthole windows, and sweeping terraces overlooking the ocean. The interiors are equally breathtaking, with marble floors, crystal chandeliers, and vibrant colors that evoke the glamour and excitement of the Roaring Twenties.

If you prefer a more intimate and boutique experience, there are also many smaller Art Deco hotels that capture the spirit of the era. For example, the Avalon Hotel in Los Angeles is a hidden gem that offers guests a glimpse into Hollywood’s golden age. Built in 1949, this charming hotel features retro-chic furnishings, vintage artwork, and a glamorous rooftop pool with panoramic views of the city. Staying at the Avalon Hotel is like stepping into a classic movie set, where every detail reflects the elegance and style of the Roaring Twenties.

Whether you are a history buff, an architecture enthusiast, or just someone who appreciates good design, staying in a hotel with Art Deco charm is a truly memorable experience. These hotels offer a unique opportunity to immerse yourself in the glamour and sophistication of the Roaring Twenties, while still enjoying all the modern amenities and comforts of a luxury hotel. So why not step back in time and relive the magic of the Jazz Age at one of these fabulous Art Deco hotels?
