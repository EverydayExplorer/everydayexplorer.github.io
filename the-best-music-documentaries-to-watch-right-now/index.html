The Best Music Documentaries to Watch Right Now


Documentaries have always been a great way to explore and discover new aspects of the world we live in. When it comes to music, there’s something truly captivating about getting an intimate look into the lives of our favorite artists, the creative process behind their music, and the impact they’ve had on the industry. Whether you’re a die-hard music fan or simply curious about the stories behind the songs, here are some of the best music documentaries that you should watch right now.

1. “Searching for Sugar Man”

“Searching for Sugar Man” is a mesmerizing documentary that tells the incredible story of Sixto Rodriguez, a forgotten American folk musician who became a legend in South Africa. Directed by Malik Bendjelloul, this film takes you on a journey to uncover the enigmatic life of Rodriguez and the mystery surrounding his disappearance from the music scene. With breathtaking cinematography and a captivating soundtrack, “Searching for Sugar Man” is a must-watch for any music lover.

2. “Amy”

Directed by Asif Kapadia, “Amy” is a powerful and heartbreaking documentary that chronicles the life and tragic death of British singer-songwriter Amy Winehouse. Through a combination of archival footage, interviews, and personal recordings, this film offers an intimate portrayal of the talented artist and the struggles she faced in the music industry and her personal life. “Amy” is a poignant reminder of the price of fame and the impact it can have on an individual’s well-being.

3. “20 Feet from Stardom”

“20 Feet from Stardom” shines a spotlight on the incredible voices behind some of the greatest songs in music history – the backup singers. Directed by Morgan Neville, this documentary explores the lives and careers of talented singers who have spent their lives in the shadows, supporting renowned artists on stage and in the studio. Through interviews and breathtaking performances, “20 Feet from Stardom” celebrates the unsung heroes of the music industry and highlights their immense talent and contribution.

4. “The Last Waltz”

“The Last Waltz” is a classic music documentary directed by Martin Scorsese that captures the final concert of the legendary rock band, The Band. Filmed on Thanksgiving Day in 1976, this documentary features electrifying performances by The Band, as well as guest appearances by iconic musicians such as Bob Dylan, Eric Clapton, and Joni Mitchell. With its raw energy and unforgettable music, “The Last Waltz” is a time capsule of a pivotal moment in rock and roll history.

5. “Stop Making Sense”

Directed by Jonathan Demme, “Stop Making Sense” documents the iconic performances of the American rock band Talking Heads during their 1983 tour. Known for their innovative and energetic live shows, Talking Heads deliver a captivating performance that seamlessly blends music, visuals, and stage presence. With its infectious rhythm and infectious energy, “Stop Making Sense” is a must-watch for anyone who wants to experience the magic of a live concert.

Conclusion

These are just a few of the incredible music documentaries that are worth watching right now. Each film offers a unique perspective on the music industry and provides a deeper understanding of the artists and their craft. So grab some popcorn, turn up the volume, and immerse yourself in the world of music through these captivating documentaries. Happy watching!
