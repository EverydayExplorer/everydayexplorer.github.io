Surviving a Lightning Strike: Dos and Don'ts for Electrical Storms


Lightning strikes are a powerful and dangerous force of nature. They can cause significant damage to property and pose a serious threat to human life. In order to survive a lightning strike, it’s important to know the dos and don’ts for electrical storms. In this blog post, we will provide you with valuable information on how to stay safe during these unpredictable events.

Dos:

  1. Seek Shelter: When an electrical storm is approaching, it is crucial to find a safe place to take cover. Look for a sturdy building or a fully enclosed vehicle. Avoid seeking shelter under tall trees, open fields, or any other exposed areas.

  2. Stay Inside: Once you have found shelter, stay indoors until the storm has passed. Avoid using electrical appliances, landline phones, or any other equipment that is connected to the electrical grid. Lightning can travel through these conductive materials and pose a risk of electrocution.

  3. Close Windows and Doors: Close all windows and doors in your shelter to minimize the risk of lightning entering the building. This will also help protect you from strong winds and flying debris that are often associated with electrical storms.

  4. Unplug Electronic Devices: Unplug all electronic devices from power outlets to prevent damage caused by power surges. Lightning can cause power fluctuations that may damage sensitive electronics, so it’s important to disconnect them until the storm has passed.

  5. Stay Away from Water: Avoid using showers, sinks, or any other water source during an electrical storm. Lightning can travel through plumbing systems and pose a risk of electrocution. It’s best to wait until the storm has passed before using any water-related activities.

  6. Stay Informed: Keep an eye on weather forecasts and listen to local news updates to stay informed about the progress of the storm. This will help you make informed decisions and take appropriate action to ensure your safety.

Don’ts:

  1. Don’t Use Corded Phones: Avoid using corded phones during an electrical storm, as they can conduct electricity from lightning strikes. Instead, use a mobile phone or wait until the storm has passed to make calls.

  2. Don’t Stand Near Windows: Standing near windows during an electrical storm puts you at risk of injury from shattered glass in case of a lightning strike. Stay away from windows and move to the central part of your shelter.

  3. Don’t Touch Metal Objects: Refrain from touching metal objects such as fences, railing, or any other conductive materials during an electrical storm. Lightning can travel through these objects and pose a risk of electrocution.

  4. Don’t Take Shelter Under Trees: Avoid seeking shelter under trees during an electrical storm, as they attract lightning. Instead, find a safer location such as a building or a vehicle.

  5. Don’t Ignore Warning Signs: If you notice signs of an approaching thunderstorm, such as darkening skies, distant rumbling, or static electricity in the air, take immediate action to seek shelter. Ignoring these warning signs can put you at unnecessary risk.

  6. Don’t Assume You’re Safe: Even if you are indoors during an electrical storm, it’s important to remain cautious. Lightning can still strike buildings and travel through electrical systems, so it’s best to follow the dos and don’ts outlined in this post.

Remember, lightning strikes can be unpredictable and dangerous. By following these dos and don’ts, you can significantly increase your chances of surviving an electrical storm. Stay safe and stay informed!
