Tips for Successful Predator Hunting: Coyotes and Bobcats


Predator hunting can be an exciting and challenging endeavor. Whether you’re targeting coyotes or bobcats, having a successful hunt requires the right knowledge, skills, and equipment. In this blog, we will discuss some essential tips for successful predator hunting, focusing specifically on coyotes and bobcats. These tips will help you improve your chances of a successful hunt and ensure a more rewarding experience in the field.

**Understanding Coyotes and Bobcats**

Before we delve into the tips for successful predator hunting, it’s important to understand the characteristics and behaviors of the specific animals you are targeting. Coyotes and bobcats are both extremely cunning and elusive predators.

Coyotes are known for their adaptability and intelligence, which makes them challenging to hunt. They are primarily nocturnal, although they can also be active during the day. They have keen senses, including excellent hearing and a keen sense of smell, which they use to avoid danger and find their prey.

On the other hand, bobcats are highly skilled predators with remarkable agility and patience. They are solitary animals and prefer to hunt at night. Like coyotes, bobcats are stealthy and rely heavily on their senses to navigate their environment and hunt effectively.

**Tip 1: Scout Your Hunting Area**

Scouting is essential before any hunt, particularly when it comes to predator hunting. Understanding the terrain, locating prey, and identifying potential ambush locations is crucial for a successful hunt. Spend time researching and scouting your hunting area to determine the prevalent movement patterns of coyotes and bobcats.

Look for tracks, scat, and signs of recent activity such as hair caught on branches or scratch marks on trees. Additionally, utilizing trail cameras in strategic locations can provide valuable information on the presence and behavior of these predators.

**Tip 2: Master Your Calling Technique**

Calling predators is a vital aspect of successful predator hunting. Coyotes and bobcats are attracted to distress calls, particularly sounds resembling injured prey. Invest in a high-quality electronic predator caller and practice different calls to mimic various distress sounds.

It’s essential to match the calls to the specific prey species found in your hunting area. Additionally, incorporating realistic pauses and sequences in your calling technique can make it more convincing and enticing for the coyotes and bobcats.

**Tip 3: Concealment and Scent Control**

Since coyotes and bobcats have exceptional senses, it’s crucial to focus on concealment and scent control during your hunt. Dress in camouflage that matches the environment you will be hunting in, and use natural cover to break up your outline.

Take precautions to minimize your scent by thoroughly washing your hunting clothes with scent-free detergents and using scent-eliminating sprays. The use of cover scents, such as ones mimicking local vegetation, may also help mask your presence from these wary predators.

**Tip 4: Utilize Decoys**

Adding decoys to your predator hunting setup can significantly increase your chances of success. Both coyotes and bobcats are naturally curious animals that investigate potential prey. By placing a lifelike decoy, such as a rabbit or a bird, you can attract their attention and draw them closer to your calling location.

Ensure that the decoy is positioned within your effective shooting range and set it up in a natural pose to increase its effectiveness. The movement of the decoy, such as a tail wagging or a slight head movement, can make it even more enticing and convincing.

**Tip 5: Patience and Persistence**

Predator hunting, especially for coyotes and bobcats, requires patience and persistence. These animals are intelligent and cautious, making them challenging targets. It may take multiple attempts before encountering success.

Avoid giving up too quickly and be prepared to spend several hours in the field. Sometimes, waiting out the animals until the opportune moment presents itself is the key to a successful hunt. Remember, predator hunting is a game of patience, so embrace the process and enjoy the entire experience.

**Conclusion**

Successful predator hunting requires a combination of knowledge, skill, and preparation. By understanding the behaviors and patterns of coyotes and bobcats, scouting your hunting area, mastering your calling technique, practicing concealment and scent control, utilizing decoys, and embracing patience and persistence, you increase your chances of a successful hunt.

Remember to always comply with local hunting laws and regulations, respect the wildlife, and practice ethical hunting practices. With these tips in mind, you’re well on your way to a rewarding and successful predator hunting experience. Happy hunting!
