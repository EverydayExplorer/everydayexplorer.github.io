The Art of Calling Turkeys: Techniques and Gear


As the crisp autumn air settles and the leaves transform into vibrant shades of red and gold, hunters across the country eagerly prepare for one of the most exhilarating hunting experiences - turkey hunting. It’s an art form that requires skill, patience, and a deep understanding of turkey behavior. Today, we’ll be delving into the world of turkey hunting, exploring the techniques and gear that can aid you in your pursuit.

1. Understanding Turkey Behavior
Before we dive into the techniques and gear, it’s crucial to have a solid understanding of turkey behavior. Turkeys are intelligent creatures with keen senses, making them a challenging quarry. They have exceptional eyesight, acute hearing, and an innate ability to detect any slight movement. To outsmart these majestic birds, hunters must study their habits, vocalizations, and even their preferred habitats.

2. Mastering the Art of Calling
Calling is an essential skill when it comes to turkey hunting. It involves mimicking the various vocalizations of turkeys to lure them closer. The most common calls include yelps, clucks, purrs, and gobbles. There are different types of calls, such as box calls, slate calls, diaphragm calls, and push-button calls. Each call produces distinct sounds that can be used to imitate different turkey vocalizations, depending on the situation.

3. Selecting the Right Gear
Having the right gear can make a world of difference in your turkey hunting experience. Here are some key items to consider:

a. Shotgun: When it comes to turkey hunting, a 12-gauge shotgun is the most popular choice. It provides sufficient power and range to effectively take down a turkey. Opt for a shotgun with a tight choke and consider using turkey-specific loads for maximum effectiveness.

b. Camouflage Clothing: Turkeys have exceptional eyesight, so blending into your surroundings is crucial. Invest in high-quality camouflage clothing that matches the terrain you’ll be hunting in. This will help you stay hidden and increase your chances of success.

c. Decoys: Decoys can be a game-changer when it comes to turkey hunting. They attract turkeys by creating a visual representation of other turkeys, enticing them to come closer. There are various types of decoys available, including hen and jake decoys, as well as full strut gobblers.

d. Turkey Vest: A well-designed turkey vest can serve as your mobile hunting base. It provides storage for calls, ammunition, and other essentials, while also offering a comfortable seat for those long hours of waiting.

4. Techniques for Success
To maximize your chances of success, consider these techniques:

a. Scouting: Before your hunt, scout the area to identify turkey roosting sites, feeding areas, and travel routes. This will help you position yourself in the best possible spot and increase your chances of encountering a turkey.

b. Setting Up: When choosing your hunting spot, look for natural cover that will conceal your movements. Create a comfortable setup with a good field of view, using your decoys strategically to attract approaching turkeys.

c. Patience and Persistence: Turkey hunting requires patience and persistence. Be prepared to spend long hours in the field, waiting for the perfect opportunity. Remember, turkeys can be unpredictable, so staying focused and alert is essential.

In conclusion, turkey hunting is an art form that demands skill, knowledge, and the right gear. By understanding turkey behavior, mastering the art of calling, and selecting the appropriate gear, you can enhance your chances of success. So, gear up, hone your skills, and embark on an unforgettable turkey hunting adventure this season. Happy hunting!
