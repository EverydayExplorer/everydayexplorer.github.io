Survival Fishing Techniques: Catching Food in Waterways


In a survival situation, knowing how to catch your own food can make the difference between life and death. While land animals may not always be readily available, waterways, such as rivers, lakes, and streams, often teem with fish, offering a potential source of sustenance. In this blog post, we will explore various survival fishing techniques that can help you catch food in waterways.

  1. Hand Fishing - Hand fishing, also known as noodling or grabbling, is a technique where you catch fish using your bare hands. This method is most effective for catching catfish. Look for underwater holes or crevices where catfish like to hide. Slowly and carefully insert your hand into the hole, and when the fish bites, grab onto it tightly. Be cautious of sharp teeth or spines and wear protective gloves if possible.

  2. Spearfishing - Spearfishing is an ancient technique that involves using a spear to impale fish underwater. You can fashion a simple spear from a sturdy stick or branch by sharpening one end. Look for shallow areas or pools where fish tend to congregate. Slowly approach your target, making as little noise and movement as possible, and thrust your spear with precision. Practice is key to mastering this technique, as it requires good hand-eye coordination and excellent aim.

  3. Trapping - Constructing fish traps is an effective passive method to catch fish. Whether using traditional fish traps made from woven materials or improvising with available resources, the concept is the same. Create a funnel shape with an opening large enough for fish to swim in but narrow enough to prevent their escape. Bait the trap with food that fish are attracted to, such as worms or small insects. Place the trap in a strategic location in the waterway and wait for the fish to enter. Check the trap regularly to collect your catch.

  4. Improvised Fishing Gear - In a survival situation, you may not have access to conventional fishing gear. However, you can improvise with materials you find in your surroundings. For example, loops of fishing line or parachute cord can be used as makeshift fishing lines, and sticks or branches can be fashioned into fishing poles. Hooks can be created from safety pins, paperclips, or even thorns. Don’t forget to find suitable bait such as insects, worms, or small pieces of meat to attract fish.

  5. Net Fishing - Net fishing is a versatile method that can be used in various water environments. Create a net from available materials, such as clothing, rope, or even vines. Weave a net with small mesh sizes, wide enough to allow fish to enter but narrow enough to prevent their escape. Find a suitable location where fish frequently pass by, such as river bends or channels. Submerge the net, securing it firmly, and wait for fish to swim into it. Pull the net out of the water swiftly to entangle the fish inside.

  6. Fish Traps - If you have access to containers or bottles, you can quickly construct a fish trap. Cut off the top portion of the container and invert it, creating a funnel-shaped entrance. Bait the trap and submerge it partially or completely in the water, ensuring the opening faces downstream. The fish will be lured into the trap while trying to reach the bait. Check the traps regularly to assess your success.

  7. Using Natural Bait - When traditional bait is not readily available, using natural bait can be advantageous. Observe your environment for potential food sources that fish feed on, such as insects, worms, or small fish. Carefully select the bait and present it on a hook or trap to attract fish. Understanding the local ecosystem and the preferences of different fish species will increase your chances of success.

Remember, survival fishing requires patience, skill, and knowledge about the local environment. Practice these techniques in non-emergency situations to hone your skills and gain confidence. Ensure you are aware of local fishing regulations and obtain the necessary permits or licenses where required.

Survival fishing can sustain you during challenging times when food is scarce. With the right techniques and resourcefulness, you can utilize waterways effectively to catch food and increase your chances of survival. Stay prepared, stay vigilant, and stay safe!
