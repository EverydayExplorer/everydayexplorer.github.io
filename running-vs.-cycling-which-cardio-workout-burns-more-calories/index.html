Running vs. Cycling: Which Cardio Workout Burns More Calories?


When it comes to cardio workouts, running and cycling are two of the most popular choices for fitness enthusiasts. Both activities offer numerous health benefits, including improved cardiovascular endurance, weight management, and stress reduction. However, many people wonder which of these two exercises burns more calories. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the calorie-burning potential of running and cycling, and explore the factors that can influence the calorie expenditure for each activity.

Calories Burned During Running

Running is a high-impact, weight-bearing exercise that engages multiple muscle groups, making it an excellent calorie-burning workout. The number of calories burned during running depends on various factors such as speed, distance, terrain, and individual body weight. Generally, a person weighing around 155 pounds can burn approximately 300 calories during a 30-minute run at a moderate pace of 5 mph. However, increasing the running speed or tackling challenging terrains like hills can significantly elevate the calorie-burning potential of this activity.

Calories Burned During Cycling

Cycling is a low-impact, non-weight-bearing exercise that also offers an effective calorie-burning workout. Similar to running, the number of calories burned while cycling varies based on factors such as speed, resistance, duration, and body weight. On average, a person weighing 155 pounds can burn about 260 calories during a 30-minute bike ride at a moderate pace of 12-14 mph. Utilizing a stationary bike, incorporating interval training, or biking uphill can intensify the calorie-burning effects of cycling.

Comparing Calorie Expenditure

When comparing the calorie expenditure of running and cycling, several considerations come into play. Running generally burns more calories per minute than cycling due to the higher intensity and weight-bearing nature of the activity. However, cycling allows for longer workouts with less impact on the joints, potentially resulting in a greater overall calorie burn during extended sessions. Additionally, the enjoyment and sustainability of each activity for the individual should also be taken into account, as adherence to a workout routine is crucial for achieving long-term fitness goals.

Maximizing Calorie Burn

For those seeking to maximize calorie burn through running or cycling, several strategies can be employed. Interval training, which involves alternating between high-intensity bursts and recovery periods, can boost calorie expenditure during both activities. Additionally, incorporating resistance or incline work, such as hill sprints for running or cycling uphill, can elevate the intensity and calorie burn of the workouts. Engaging in longer duration sessions or increasing the frequency of workouts can also contribute to greater overall calorie expenditure.

In conclusion, both running and cycling offer effective calorie-burning workouts with their own unique advantages. The choice between the two ultimately depends on individual preferences, fitness goals, and physical capabilities. Whether it’s the exhilarating rush of a run or the scenic tranquility of a bike ride, both activities have the potential to help individuals achieve their fitness and weight management objectives. By understanding the factors that influence calorie burn during running and cycling, individuals can make informed decisions about which activity best aligns with their fitness aspirations. So, lace up those running shoes or hop on that bike, and embark on a calorie-burning journey towards a healthier, fitter you.
