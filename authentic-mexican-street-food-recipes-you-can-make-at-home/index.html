Authentic Mexican Street Food Recipes You Can Make at Home


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine, you’ve probably indulged in the mouthwatering flavors of authentic Mexican street food. From tacos to elotes, the vibrant and bold flavors of these dishes are hard to resist. But what if you could recreate these delicious treats in the comfort of your own kitchen? In this blog post, we’ll share some authentic Mexican street food recipes that you can easily make at home. So grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

1. Tacos al Pastor

Tacos al Pastor are a staple of Mexican street food culture. These flavorful tacos are traditionally made with marinated pork that’s cooked on a vertical spit, similar to shawarma. To make them at home, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound of pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of pineapple juice
  • 2 tablespoons of achiote paste
  • 1 tablespoon of dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon of ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas
  • Chopped onions, cilantro, and pineapple for garnish

Marinate the pork in the garlic, pineapple juice, achiote paste, oregano, cumin, salt, and pepper for at least 2 hours, or overnight if possible. Once marinated, grill the pork until it’s cooked through and slightly charred. Warm the corn tortillas, fill them with the grilled pork, and top with onions, cilantro, and pineapple. Enjoy the explosion of flavors in every bite!

2. Elotes (Mexican Street Corn)

Elotes are a popular street food snack that combines the sweetness of corn with savory and tangy flavors. To make this delicious treat, you’ll need:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/4 cup of mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup of sour cream
  • 1/2 cup of crumbled cotija cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon of chili powder
  • Fresh lime wedges

Grill the corn until it’s slightly charred. In a small bowl, mix together the mayonnaise and sour cream. Spread this mixture evenly over the grilled corn. Sprinkle the cotija cheese and chili powder on top, then squeeze fresh lime juice over the elotes. Dig in and savor the perfect balance of flavors!

3. Churros

No Mexican street food experience is complete without churros. These deep-fried pastries are crispy on the outside, soft on the inside, and coated in cinnamon sugar. To make churros at home, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 2 quarts of vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon

In a saucepan, bring the water, sugar, salt, and vegetable oil to a boil. Reduce the heat and add the flour, stirring until the mixture forms a ball. Remove from heat and let it cool for a few minutes. In a separate bowl, mix together the sugar and cinnamon.

Heat the vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 375°F (190°C). Transfer the dough to a piping bag fitted with a star tip. Pipe strips of dough directly into the hot oil, cutting them with scissors to the desired length. Fry until golden brown, then remove from the oil and drain on paper towels. Roll the churros in the cinnamon sugar mixture and serve them warm. Dip them in chocolate sauce for an extra treat!

Conclusion

These three authentic Mexican street food recipes are just the tip of the iceberg when it comes to the culinary wonders of Mexico. By recreating these dishes at home, you can bring the vibrant flavors of the streets right to your own kitchen. So why wait? Put on some Mexican music, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure that will transport you to the colorful streets of Mexico. ¡Buen provecho!
