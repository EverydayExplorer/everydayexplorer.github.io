Hotel Stay for Film Buffs: Iconic Movie Locations and Cinematic Settings


Are you a film enthusiast looking to combine your love for movies with your next hotel stay? We’ve got you covered! In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the most iconic movie locations and cinematic settings that you can experience firsthand during your hotel stay. From luxurious hotels featured in blockbuster films to quaint inns that transport you to the golden age of cinema, get ready for a cinematic adventure like no other.

1. The Plaza Hotel, New York City (Featured in Home Alone 2: Lost in New York)
Located at the heart of Manhattan, The Plaza Hotel is not only a luxurious accommodation but also an iconic movie location. Step into the shoes of Kevin McCallister as you explore the hotel’s stunning lobby and relive the famous scenes from Home Alone 2: Lost in New York. Whether you’re enjoying a gourmet meal at The Palm Court or indulging in a spa treatment at The Plaza Beauty by Warren-Tricomi, be prepared for a truly cinematic experience.

2. The Grand Budapest Hotel, Germany (Featured in The Grand Budapest Hotel)
If you’re a fan of Wes Anderson’s quirky and whimsical films, then a stay at The Grand Budapest Hotel is a must. While the actual hotel portrayed in the film is fictional, you can still find inspiration in the stunning architecture and vibrant color palette. Choose a hotel with a similar aesthetic and immerse yourself in the enchanting world of Gustave H., Zero Moustafa, and the charming fictional Republic of Zubrowka.

3. The Timberline Lodge, Oregon (Featured in The Shining)
Take a thrilling trip to the Timberline Lodge in Oregon, where Stanley Kubrick’s horror masterpiece, The Shining, was filmed. Nestled amidst the picturesque landscapes of Mount Hood, this historic hotel offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cinematic history. Explore the corridors and rooms that once housed Jack Torrance’s madness, and if you’re feeling brave, join a guided tour to learn more about the film’s production.

4. The Millennium Biltmore Hotel, Los Angeles (Featured in Ghostbusters)
For fans of the supernatural comedy classic Ghostbusters, a stay at the Millennium Biltmore Hotel in Los Angeles is a must. This historic hotel not only served as the filming location for scenes in the original movie but has also hosted numerous Hollywood stars throughout the years. Immerse yourself in the glamour of old Hollywood as you wander through the opulent halls and grand ballrooms of this iconic hotel.

5. The Park Hyatt Tokyo, Japan (Featured in Lost in Translation)
Experience the same sense of isolation and cultural immersion as Bob Harris and Charlotte in the award-winning film Lost in Translation by staying at the Park Hyatt Tokyo. This luxury hotel offers stunning views of Tokyo’s vibrant skyline, along with the same sophisticated ambiance portrayed in the film. Sip on a cocktail at the New York Bar, just like Bob and Charlotte, and soak in the breathtaking panoramic views of the city.

These are just a few examples of how you can combine your passion for movies with a memorable hotel stay. So, why not make your next trip extra special by choosing a hotel that transports you to your favorite cinematic moments? From romantic dramas to thrilling adventures, the world of film is filled with iconic locations waiting to be explored. Lights, camera, action - your next cinematic adventure awaits!
