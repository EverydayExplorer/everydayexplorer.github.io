The Secrets to Achieving the Perfect Cat Eye for Your Eye Shape


If there’s one makeup look that never goes out of style, it’s the classic cat eye. The cat eye is a timeless beauty trend that can instantly make your eyes appear larger, more defined, and more glamorous. Whether you are a makeup enthusiast or a beginner, mastering the cat eye can be a bit of a challenge. However, with the right techniques and a little bit of practice, anyone can achieve the perfect cat eye for their eye shape. In this blog post, we will uncover the secrets to achieving the perfect cat eye for your eye shape.

Understanding Your Eye Shape

Before we delve into the techniques, it’s important to understand your eye shape. There are several different eye shapes, including almond, round, hooded, monolid, and downturned. Each eye shape requires a slightly different approach when it comes to applying eyeliner and creating the cat eye. Once you determine your eye shape, you can choose the most flattering technique to enhance your natural features.

Prep and Prime

To achieve a flawless cat eye, it’s crucial to start with a clean and primed eyelid. Begin by removing any excess oil or residue using a gentle cleanser or makeup remover. Next, apply an eyeshadow primer to create a smooth canvas for your eyeliner application. This will help your eyeliner go on smoothly and stay in place throughout the day.

Choosing the Right Eyeliner

When it comes to cat eye makeup, the type of eyeliner you use plays a significant role. There are several options to choose from, including liquid, gel, and pencil eyeliners. Liquid eyeliner is often the go-to choice for achieving a precise and sharp cat eye. Gel eyeliners can also be a great choice for those who prefer a more versatile formula that can be easily smudged or softened. Pencil eyeliners work best for creating a softer, more smoky cat eye look.

The Technique

Now that you have prepped your eyelids and chosen the right eyeliner, it’s time to master the technique. Here is a step-by-step guide to achieving the perfect cat eye for your specific eye shape:

  1. Almond-Shaped Eyes: If you have almond-shaped eyes, you’re in luck! This eye shape is the most versatile and can pull off almost any cat eye look. Start by applying a thin line along your upper lash line, gradually increasing the thickness towards the outer corner of your eye. Once you reach the desired thickness, extend the line slightly past the outer corner of your eye, angling it slightly upwards.

  2. Round Eyes: To create the illusion of longer, more elongated eyes, start by applying a thin line along your upper lash line, going from the inner corner to the outer corner. Extend the line slightly past the outer corner of your eye, flicking it upwards to create a subtle wing.

  3. Hooded Eyes: Hooded eyes can sometimes make it challenging to achieve a visible cat eye. To combat this, start by applying a thin line along your upper lash line, keeping it as close to the lash line as possible. Gradually increase the thickness towards the outer corner of your eye, flicking it slightly upwards. Next, with your eyes open, look straight ahead and draw a line that connects the flick to the midpoint of your eyelid. This will create a more visible cat eye when your eyes are open.

  4. Monolid Eyes: Monolid eyes have little to no visible crease, so creating a cat eye can take a bit of skill. Start by applying a thin line along your upper lash line, gradually increasing the thickness towards the outer corner of your eye. Extend the line slightly past the outer corner of your eye, flicking it upwards. To create depth, lightly blend a matte brown eyeshadow into the crease of your eye, emphasizing the outer corner.

  5. Downturned Eyes: Downturned eyes have a natural droop at the outer corners, so it’s essential to focus on lifting and elongating the eyes. Start by applying a thin line along your upper lash line, going from the inner corner to the outer corner. Extend the line slightly past the outer corner of your eye, flicking it upwards at a more exaggerated angle.

Finishing Touches

To complete your cat eye look, there are a few finishing touches you can add to enhance the overall effect:

  1. Mascara: Apply two coats of mascara to your upper lashes to add volume and length. This will further emphasize the cat eye effect and make your eyes pop.
  2. Lower Lash Line: For a more dramatic look, you can also line your lower lash line with a thin line of eyeliner, connecting it to the outer corner of your cat eye.
  3. Concealer: Use a small brush or a cotton swab dipped in concealer to clean up any mistakes or smudges. This will help create a clean and precise cat eye.

Practice Makes Perfect

Remember, achieving the perfect cat eye takes practice. Don’t be discouraged if your first attempts are not up to par. Experiment with different techniques, angles, and products until you find what works best for your eye shape. With time and patience, you will master the cat eye and be able to create the perfect winged liner look effortlessly.

In conclusion, the cat eye is a classic makeup look that can instantly elevate your eye makeup game. By understanding your eye shape, prepping your eyelids, choosing the right eyeliner, and mastering the technique, you can achieve the perfect cat eye for your specific eye shape. With a little bit of practice and some finishing touches, you’ll be rocking the cat eye like a pro in no time. Happy practicing!
