Craft Gin Distilleries You Need to Try


Craft gin has taken the world by storm in recent years, with an explosion of small-batch distilleries popping up all over the globe. These artisanal spirits offer a unique and flavorful experience that can’t be found in mass-produced gins. If you’re a gin enthusiast or simply looking to explore the world of craft spirits, here are some distilleries you absolutely need to try.

1. The Botanical Garden Distillery - Edinburgh, Scotland
Nestled in the heart of Scotland’s capital, The Botanical Garden Distillery is a haven for gin lovers. They pride themselves on using hand-picked, locally sourced botanicals to create their award-winning gins. The distillery offers tours where you can learn about the gin-making process and even create your own bespoke bottle of gin.

2. Four Pillars Gin - Yarra Valley, Australia
Located in the stunning Yarra Valley, Four Pillars Gin is known for its innovative approach to gin-making. They experiment with unique botanicals, resulting in gins that are bursting with flavor. Their distillery offers a range of experiences, including guided tastings and cocktail masterclasses.

3. Monkey 47 - Black Forest, Germany
Nestled deep in Germany’s Black Forest, Monkey 47 is a gin lover’s paradise. Their gin is made with an astounding 47 botanicals, resulting in a complex and aromatic spirit. The distillery offers guided tours, where you can learn about the rich history of the brand and sample their exceptional gins.

4. The Kyoto Distillery - Kyoto, Japan
Bringing a touch of Japanese elegance to the world of gin, The Kyoto Distillery is renowned for its artisanal gins inspired by the country’s rich cultural heritage. They use traditional Japanese botanicals, such as yuzu and green tea, to create gins that are both delicate and distinctive. A visit to their distillery is a sensory experience like no other.

5. Sipsmith - London, England
Located in the heart of London, Sipsmith is credited with kickstarting the craft gin revolution in the UK. They are known for their traditional and handcrafted approach to gin-making, using copper stills and time-honored techniques. A visit to their distillery offers a glimpse into the history of gin in London and the chance to sample their exquisite spirits.

6. Aviation American Gin - Portland, Oregon
Aviation American Gin is a true pioneer in the craft gin scene in the United States. Located in Portland, Oregon, they produce a gin that is smooth, balanced, and full of character. Their distillery offers tours and tastings, allowing visitors to see firsthand how their gin is made and learn about the brand’s commitment to quality.

7. Gin Mare - Vilanova i la Geltrú, Spain
Situated in the coastal town of Vilanova i la Geltrú, Gin Mare is a Mediterranean-style gin that captures the essence of the region. They use botanicals such as olives, thyme, and rosemary to create a gin that is fresh, herbaceous, and perfect for warm summer evenings. A visit to their distillery is a journey through the flavors of the Mediterranean.

8. The Cape Byron Distillery - Byron Bay, Australia
Nestled in the picturesque hinterland of Byron Bay, The Cape Byron Distillery is a hidden gem in the world of craft gin. They create their gin using native Australian botanicals, resulting in a spirit that is uniquely Australian. The distillery offers tours and tastings, allowing visitors to discover the beauty of the surrounding rainforest while enjoying their exceptional gins.

These are just a few of the many craft gin distilleries around the world that are pushing the boundaries of what gin can be. Each distillery offers a unique experience and a chance to explore the artistry and passion behind craft gin-making. So, whether you’re a gin aficionado or simply looking for a new spirit to try, these distilleries should be at the top of your list. Cheers to the wonderful world of craft gin!
