Breaking Down the Best TV Shows of the Decade


The past decade has been a golden era for television, with an explosion of creativity and innovation that has given us some of the most memorable and impactful shows in recent memory. From groundbreaking dramas to hilarious comedies, there’s been something for everyone to enjoy. As we reflect on the past ten years of television, let’s break down some of the best shows that have left an indelible mark on our screens and in our hearts.

1. Breaking Bad (2008-2013)

Kicking off our list is arguably one of the greatest television dramas of all time, ‘Breaking Bad.’ Created by Vince Gilligan, this series follows the transformation of Walter White, a high school chemistry teacher turned methamphetamine manufacturer. With its gripping storyline, complex characters, and impeccable performances, ‘Breaking Bad’ kept viewers on the edge of their seats from start to finish. Bryan Cranston’s portrayal of Walter White earned him multiple Emmy Awards, solidifying his place in television history.

2. Game of Thrones (2011-2019)

Love it or hate it, ‘Game of Thrones’ undeniably made a significant impact on the television landscape during its eight-season run. Based on George R.R. Martin’s ‘A Song of Ice and Fire’ novels, this epic fantasy series captivated audiences with its intricate political intrigue, sprawling ensemble cast, and jaw-dropping plot twists. While the final season received mixed reviews, there’s no denying the cultural phenomenon that ‘Game of Thrones’ became, setting the bar high for fantasy television.

3. Stranger Things (2016-present)

Taking us back to the 1980s with a mix of nostalgia, supernatural mystery, and heartfelt coming-of-age drama, ‘Stranger Things’ quickly became a worldwide sensation upon its release. Created by the Duffer Brothers, this Netflix original series follows a group of kids in the fictional town of Hawkins, Indiana, as they encounter otherworldly creatures and government conspiracies. With its lovable characters, thrilling storyline, and homage to ’80s pop culture, ‘Stranger Things’ has captured the hearts of audiences young and old.

4. The Crown (2016-present)

Offering a glimpse behind the closed doors of the British monarchy, ‘The Crown’ has been praised for its lavish production values, stellar performances, and nuanced exploration of power and duty. Created by Peter Morgan, this Netflix original series chronicles the reign of Queen Elizabeth II, from her ascension to the throne in the early 1950s to the present day. With each season covering a different period in history, ‘The Crown’ offers a fascinating look at the personal and political challenges faced by the royal family.

5. Fleabag (2016-2019)

Written by and starring the immensely talented Phoebe Waller-Bridge, ‘Fleabag’ is a darkly comedic exploration of love, loss, and female identity. Originally conceived as a one-woman stage show, the series follows the titular character, a witty and self-destructive young woman navigating life in London. With its sharp writing, breakout performances, and unapologetically honest portrayal of modern relationships, ‘Fleabag’ earned widespread critical acclaim and a legion of devoted fans.

6. The Mandalorian (2019-present)

In a galaxy far, far away, ‘The Mandalorian’ has revitalized the ‘Star Wars’ franchise with its compelling storytelling, stunning visual effects, and breakout character, the adorable Baby Yoda (officially known as Grogu). Created by Jon Favreau, this Disney+ original series follows a lone bounty hunter as he navigates the outer reaches of the galaxy, far from the authority of the New Republic. With its blend of Western-inspired storytelling and space opera aesthetics, ‘The Mandalorian’ has captured the imagination of fans old and new.

7. Chernobyl (2019)

Rounding out our list is ‘Chernobyl,’ a harrowing miniseries that chronicles the catastrophic nuclear disaster that occurred in Soviet Ukraine in April 1986. Created by Craig Mazin and directed by Johan Renck, this HBO production offers a chilling portrayal of the events leading up to and following the explosion at the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant. With its meticulous attention to detail, haunting atmosphere, and powerhouse performances, ‘Chernobyl’ stands as a stark reminder of the dangers of hubris and the importance of truth and accountability.

As we bid farewell to the past decade of television, these shows serve as a testament to the power of storytelling and the enduring impact of great television. Whether you’re a fan of gripping dramas, laugh-out-loud comedies, or mind-bending mysteries, the past ten years have delivered a treasure trove of unforgettable television experiences. Here’s to the next decade of groundbreaking storytelling and the shows yet to come.
