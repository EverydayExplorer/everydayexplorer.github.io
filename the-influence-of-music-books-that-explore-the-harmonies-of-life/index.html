The Influence of Music: Books That Explore the Harmonies of Life


Music has the power to touch our souls, evoke emotions, and transport us to different realms. From the catchy tunes that get stuck in our heads to the haunting melodies that bring tears to our eyes, music has a profound influence on our lives. In this blog post, we will explore the harmonies of life through the lens of literature. Join us as we delve into a curated list of books that beautifully capture the transformative power of music.

1. ‘The Music Shop’ by Rachel Joyce: Set in the 1980s, this heartwarming novel follows the story of Frank, the owner of a failing record store. Frank has an uncanny ability to find the perfect song for every person, even if they don’t know what they need. Through his love for music, Frank navigates the complexities of relationships and teaches us that melodies have the power to heal wounds and mend broken hearts.

2. ‘The Sound of a Wild Snail Eating’ by Elisabeth Tova Bailey: In this memoir, the author finds solace in the presence of a small snail while she battles a debilitating illness. As she observes the snail’s delicate movements and listens to the gentle sounds it makes, she discovers the beauty and rhythm of life. This captivating tale reminds us that even the smallest creatures can create symphonies of their own.

3. ‘The Song of Achilles’ by Madeline Miller: Drawing inspiration from Greek mythology, this novel explores the bond between Achilles, the legendary hero, and Patroclus, his best friend and lover. As their relationship unfolds, music becomes a powerful tool for expressing their love and sorrow. Miller’s poetic prose will transport you to ancient Greece, where music intertwined with love and war.

4. ‘The Music of Dolphins’ by Karen Hesse: This enchanting young adult novel tells the story of Mila, a girl who was raised by dolphins and later reintegrated into human society. As she struggles to find her place in the world, Mila discovers her voice through music. Hesse beautifully explores the universal language of music and its ability to bridge the gap between different worlds.

5. ‘Bel Canto’ by Ann Patchett: Set in an unnamed South American country, this captivating novel revolves around a hostage situation at a high-profile opera performance. As the hostages and captors spend days together, music becomes a unifying force that transcends language and cultural barriers. Patchett’s lyrical prose will immerse you in a world where music has the power to create unexpected connections.

6. ‘The Chimes’ by Anna Smaill: In a dystopian world where memories are wiped away every day, music is the only way to preserve history and personal identity. Smaill’s novel follows the journey of Simon, a boy who discovers his musical talent and joins a group of rebels fighting against the oppressive regime. Through music, Simon learns to navigate a world where harmony is both fragile and essential.

These books offer a glimpse into the profound impact that music can have on our lives. They celebrate the melodies that shape our experiences, heal our wounds, and connect us with others. So, the next time you find yourself lost in a song, remember the harmonies of life and let the music guide you on a transformative journey.
