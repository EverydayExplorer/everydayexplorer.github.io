Human Rights in the Age of Surveillance Capitalism


Human rights are the basic rights that every individual is entitled to pursue their life and achieve their well-being without any obstruction or impediments. Historically, human rights have been fought for in order to ensure that everyone is treated equally and justly. However, in the age of surveillance capitalism, many argue that these rights are being infringed upon. Today, human rights activists are concerned that our right to privacy is being compromised by modern surveillance technology being employed by large-scale companies. In this blog, we’ll delve into the problem of surveillance capitalism and the impact it has on human rights.

What is Surveillance Capitalism?

Surveillance capitalism refers to the extraction of personal data and information through digital surveillance technologies. Companies across the world are using technologies such as smartphones, tablets, and laptops to collect and analyze consumers’ data. The gathered data is then sold to advertisers to target and influence consumer behavior. Though this business model is lucrative, it has created plenty of problems.

How is Surveillance Capitalism Affecting Human Rights?

Surveillance capitalism has an enormous impact on human rights, particularly privacy. The right of humans to make free choices, often called the autonomy principle, is eroding due to depleting privacy. Private data, once revealed, cannot be retrieved or controlled ever again. Consequently, violating a person’s right to privacy can be detrimental to their reputation, finances, and some times physical security.

Online surveillance by large-scale companies has become so rampant that people are finding it hard to make informed choices without interference. The worst part is that these surveillance activities are often carried out without the user’s knowledge or consent. Hence, it is safe to say that human rights are being disregarded to feed the growing business interests of these tech giants.

What Are the Possible Solutions to These Issues?

There is a growing need to challenge the current surveillance norms that infringe upon human rights. To achieve this, there needs to be a way to ensure transparency and accountability in the data harvesting process. This can be done by providing more oversight and regulation in the handling of personal data. Additionally, businesses should be made to educate users on the types of data that will be collected before permission to store it is given. Users should also be notified of the purpose of harvesting the data before giving consent.

Furthermore, some pioneering business models can change the way data is harvested while still allowing sufficient revenue. An example of such a model is the open-source culture. This culture rewards contributors for their content’s value and integrity, rather than simply collecting data and profiting financially. This could reduce the necessity of data surveillance.

Conclusion:

Human rights are non-negotiable privileges, which should not be sacrificed for any reason. As we continue to confront the challenge of surveillance capitalism, it’s vital that we prioritize human rights and take steps towards creating and maintaining secure and equitable digital spaces. As we navigate this quickly transforming digital landscape, any development must consider the value of personal data security and privacy to our lives. Ultimately, we must work together across a vast array of sectors, including platforms, policymakers, and advocacy groups, to create a fair and safe online environment.
