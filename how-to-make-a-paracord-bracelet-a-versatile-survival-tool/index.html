How to Make a Paracord Bracelet: A Versatile Survival Tool


Are you a fan of survival tools and outdoor activities? If so, you may have heard about paracord bracelets. These versatile accessories are not only trendy but also functional, making them an essential part of any adventurer’s arsenal. Whether you are exploring the wilderness or camping in the great outdoors, having a paracord bracelet can be a lifesaver in certain situations. In this blog post, we will explore how to make a paracord bracelet and delve into the various ways it can serve as a versatile survival tool.

To begin with, let’s understand what paracord is. Originally used in parachute suspension lines during World War II, paracord is a lightweight nylon rope known for its strength and durability. It can support a significant amount of weight, making it ideal for various survival situations.

Now, let’s dive into the process of making a paracord bracelet. You will need the following materials:

1. Parachute cord (also known as paracord)
2. A buckle
3. Scissors
4. A lighter

Step 1: Measure the size of your wrist.

To ensure that your paracord bracelet fits perfectly, measure your wrist using a flexible tape measure. Make a note of the measurement, as it will determine how much paracord you will need.

Step 2: Cut the paracord.

Once you have measured your wrist, cut the paracord according to that measurement. It’s better to have excess cord than to fall short, so add a few extra inches to be safe.

Step 3: Attach the buckle.

Take one end of the paracord and loop it through one side of the buckle. Pull the paracord through the loop and tighten it, creating a snug fit. Repeat this process for the other side of the buckle.

Step 4: Start knotting.

This is where the magic happens! Start by holding the buckle end of the paracord in your hand. Take the loose end of the paracord and pass it over the cord held in your hand. Then, take the loose end and pass it under the buckle’s loop, creating a loop of its own.

Step 5: Keep knotting.

Continue knotting the paracord by repeating the process described in step 4. Make sure to keep the knots tight and close together, as this will determine the strength of your bracelet.

Step 6: Measure the length.

As you continue knotting, periodically measure the length of your bracelet against your wrist to ensure it’s the right size. Adjust the knots accordingly if needed.

Step 7: Finishing touches.

Once you reach the end of your bracelet, make a final knot and trim any excess cord. Use the scissors to cut the remaining cord, leaving a small tail to melt. Carefully use the lighter to melt the end of the cord, preventing it from unraveling.

Congratulations! You have successfully made your own paracord bracelet. Now, let’s discuss the various ways it can be used as a versatile survival tool:

1. Emergency cord: The most obvious use of a paracord bracelet is its function as an emergency cord. In situations where you need to secure a tent, make a shelter, or repair equipment, the durability of paracord comes into play.

2. Fishing line: If you find yourself in a survival situation near a body of water, your bracelet can serve as a makeshift fishing line. Unravel the cord, tie a hook or make-shift lure, and try your luck at catching dinner.

3. Tourniquet: In the case of a severe injury or bleeding, a paracord bracelet can be used as an improvised tourniquet. Although this should only be considered as a last resort, having the option available can make a life-saving difference.

4. Fire starter: Paracord is highly flammable, making it an excellent fire-starting tool. Separate the threads of the cord and use a fire starter or matches to ignite it. The inner threads can burn for a significant amount of time, providing you with enough heat to start a fire.

5. Gear repair: Should any of your camping or survival gear break, the incredible strength of paracord comes in handy once again. Use it to repair backpack straps, tent poles, or any other equipment that requires a temporary fix.

6. Clothesline: Need a place to dry your clothes or hang items to dry? Unravel your paracord bracelet and use it as a clothesline to save space in your pack.

These are just a few examples of how a paracord bracelet can serve as a versatile survival tool. The possibilities are endless, and its lightweight design makes it easy to carry wherever you go.

In conclusion, making your own paracord bracelet is a fun and rewarding activity. Not only does it serve as a stylish accessory, but it also provides you with a valuable survival tool. Whether you are an avid outdoor enthusiast or someone who values preparedness, adding a paracord bracelet to your gear collection is a wise decision. So, why not give it a try? You’ll be amazed at the many ways it can come in handy during your adventures. Stay safe, and happy knotting!
