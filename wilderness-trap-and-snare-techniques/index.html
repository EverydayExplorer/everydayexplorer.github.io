Wilderness Trap and Snare Techniques


When it comes to surviving in the wilderness, having knowledge of trap and snare techniques can be invaluable. Whether you’re lost in the woods or trying to catch food for sustenance, knowing how to set traps effectively can greatly increase your chances of survival. In this blog post, we will be exploring some popular wilderness trap and snare techniques, providing you with a comprehensive guide to mastering this essential skill.

1. Deadfall traps: Deadfall traps are one of the most basic yet effective ways to catch small game in the wilderness. To set up a deadfall trap, you will need a heavy object, such as a large rock or log, that is balanced precariously on a triggering device. When the animal touches or tries to retrieve bait from the trap, it will cause the object to fall and crush the prey. Be sure to camouflage the trap with leaves, sticks, and other natural materials to increase its effectiveness.

2. Snare traps: Snare traps are another commonly used method for catching small animals. They consist of a looped wire or cord that tightens around the animal’s neck or body when it moves through the snare. To set up a snare, find a game trail or an area where you have observed animal activity. Attach one end of the snare to a stationary object, such as a tree or a stake, and create a loop on the other end. Position the loop at the appropriate height for the target animal and secure it in place. Remember to place bait near the loop to entice the animal to enter the snare.

3. Spring snare traps: Spring snare traps are similar to regular snare traps but utilize a branch or sapling as a spring mechanism. This type of trap can be particularly effective for catching larger game. To set up a spring snare trap, find a flexible sapling or branch and bend it down towards the ground. Create a snare loop and attach it to the sapling or branch, then secure the other end to a stationary object. When the animal enters the loop, it will release the tension on the sapling or branch, causing it to snap back and tighten the snare.

4. Tension traps: Tension traps are designed to be triggered by the animal’s movement, capturing it through mechanical advantage. One popular type of tension trap is the figure-4 trap. This trap involves interlocking sticks in a particular configuration, with one stick acting as the trigger. When the prey touches the trigger stick, it releases tension, causing the trap to snap shut. Tension traps can be versatile and can be customized based on the target animal’s size and behavior.

5. Paiute deadfall trap: The Paiute deadfall trap is a more complex variation of the deadfall trap mentioned earlier. It involves creating a stable structure with multiple sticks and rocks that will collapse when triggered. This trap requires some practice to perfect, but it can prove highly effective for catching small game.

It’s important to note that when using any type of trap or snare, it’s crucial to follow local laws and regulations. Some areas may prohibit the use of certain traps, especially those that can cause harm to endangered species or non-target animals. Additionally, always prioritize the ethical treatment of animals and ensure that traps are set up in a humane manner.

In conclusion, wilderness trap and snare techniques are essential skills for anyone venturing into the wild. They provide a means of procuring food and increasing your chances of survival. Mastering these techniques takes practice, patience, and a deep understanding of animal behavior. Remember to always prioritize safety, legality, and ethical considerations when using traps or snares in the wilderness. By doing so, you can enhance your wilderness survival skills and increase your chances of successfully catching game.
