Beginner's Guide to Speedrunning: How to Get Started


Speedrunning is a captivating and thrilling way to engage with video games, transforming the experience from casual play into a competitive and skill-based pursuit. As a beginner, diving into the world of speedrunning can feel daunting, but with the right guidance and resources, you can start your journey towards mastering your favorite games. This comprehensive guide will provide you with everything you need to know to get started in speedrunning, from understanding the basics to developing your skills and joining the community.

What is Speedrunning?

At its core, speedrunning involves completing a video game as quickly as possible. Players often utilize various techniques, glitches, and strategies to optimize their playthroughs. Speedruns can be categorized into different types, such as:

  • Any%: Complete the game as fast as possible, using any means necessary, including glitches.
  • 100%: Complete the game while achieving all objectives, collectibles, or achievements.
  • Glitchless: Finish the game without exploiting any glitches or bugs.
  • Category Extensions: These are specialized categories within a game that might focus on specific challenges or goals.

Understanding these categories is crucial for finding your niche within the speedrunning community.

Choosing Your Game

The first step in your speedrunning journey is selecting a game that resonates with you. Here are some tips for choosing the right game:

  1. Love for the Game: Choose a game you genuinely enjoy. Speedrunning requires significant practice, and it’s easier to stay motivated if you love the game you’re playing.

  2. Community Support: Look for games with active speedrunning communities. A supportive community can provide resources, tips, and motivation.

  3. Accessibility: Consider games that are easy to access and play, whether they are available on PC, consoles, or through emulation.

  4. Game Length: Beginners might want to start with shorter games that can be completed in under an hour. This allows for quicker practice and improvement.

Some popular games for beginners include Super Mario Bros., Celeste, and The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time. These games have well-established communities and abundant resources.

Setting Up Your Environment

Once you’ve chosen a game, it’s time to set up your environment for speedrunning:

  1. Hardware: Ensure that your gaming setup is capable of running the game smoothly. This includes a reliable console or PC and a good controller.

  2. Recording Software: Speedrunning often requires recording your runs to share with the community or to submit for leaderboard placements. Free software like OBS Studio or Twitch Studio can help you capture your gameplay.

  3. Timer: Use a timer to track your runs. Software like LiveSplit is widely used in the speedrunning community and allows for detailed timing and split tracking.

Learning the Game

Before you can speedrun effectively, you need to master the game itself. Here are some strategies for learning:

  1. Playthrough: Start by playing through the game casually. Familiarize yourself with the controls, mechanics, and story.

  2. Watch Speedruns: Observing experienced speedrunners can provide insights into strategies, techniques, and routing. Websites like Twitch or YouTube offer countless speedrun videos and live streams.

  3. Resources and Guides: Many games have dedicated speedrunning guides available on sites like Speedrun.com or community forums. These can help you learn optimal routes and strategies.

  4. Practice: Once you understand the game, practice specific segments repeatedly. Focus on mastering difficult sections before attempting full runs.

Understanding Speedrun Techniques

Speedrunning employs various techniques to optimize gameplay. Familiarize yourself with common methods:

  • Glitches: Learn about useful glitches that can save time. However, remember that some categories may require you to avoid glitches.

  • Routing: Develop an optimal route through the game. This involves planning the best sequence of actions to achieve the fastest completion time.

  • Movement Techniques: Mastering movement can significantly affect your speedrun. Techniques like wall jumps, dashes, and precise jumps can save precious seconds.

  • Resource Management: Efficiently managing in-game resources, such as health or items, is crucial for maintaining speed and avoiding delays.

Practicing and Improving

Improvement in speedrunning comes from consistent practice and analysis. Here are some tips for progressing:

  1. Record Your Runs: Regularly record and review your runs. Analyzing your gameplay can help identify mistakes and areas for improvement.

  2. Join a Community: Engage with the speedrunning community through forums, Discord servers, or social media. Sharing experiences and tips with others can enhance your learning.

  3. Set Goals: Establish achievable goals for your runs, such as improving your personal best time or mastering a specific segment.

  4. Participate in Events: Look for speedrunning events or marathons, such as Games Done Quick. Participating can provide motivation and connect you with other speedrunners.

Submitting Your Run

Once you feel confident in your speedrunning abilities, you may want to submit your run for leaderboard consideration. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Check Rules: Each game may have specific rules for submission, including category definitions and timing methods. Be sure to understand these before submitting.

  2. Record a Valid Run: Ensure that your recorded run meets all submission criteria. This often includes using the correct version of the game and following category rules.

  3. Upload and Share: Use platforms like Speedrun.com to submit your run. Provide adequate information, including your video link and any relevant details about your gameplay.

The Community Aspect

Speedrunning is not just about personal achievement; it’s also a vibrant community. Engaging with others can enhance your experience:

  • Forums and Discord: Join forums and Discord channels related to your chosen game. These platforms are great for asking questions, sharing tips, and finding practice partners.

  • Twitch and YouTube: Watching and interacting with speedrunners on Twitch or YouTube can expose you to different styles and techniques. Consider streaming your own runs to connect with others.

  • Events and Competitions: Participate in community events or competitions to challenge yourself and meet fellow speedrunners. These can provide valuable experience and motivation.

Overcoming Challenges

Like any competitive pursuit, speedrunning comes with its challenges. Here are some common obstacles and how to overcome them:

  1. Frustration: It’s common to feel frustrated when you can’t achieve a desired time. Remember that improvement takes time and persistence.

  2. Burnout: Speedrunning can sometimes lead to burnout, especially if you’re pushing too hard. Take breaks and ensure that you’re still enjoying the process.

  3. Comparison: It’s easy to compare yourself to more experienced runners, but focus on your own progress. Everyone’s speedrunning journey is unique.

Conclusion

Embarking on your speedrunning journey can be an exhilarating experience filled with challenges, growth, and community. By choosing a game you love, mastering its mechanics, and engaging with fellow speedrunners, you can improve your skills and enjoy the thrill of racing against the clock. Remember to embrace the learning process, set achievable goals, and most importantly, have fun. Whether you aim to break records or simply enjoy the art of speedrunning, the journey is what truly matters. Happy speedrunning!
