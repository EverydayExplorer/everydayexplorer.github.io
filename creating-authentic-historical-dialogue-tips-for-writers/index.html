Creating Authentic Historical Dialogue: Tips for Writers


As a writer, one of the key challenges you may encounter when working on a historical fiction or non-fiction piece is creating authentic historical dialogue. It’s essential to capture the essence of the time period you are writing about and ensure that your characters speak in a way that reflects the language, dialects, and customs of that era. In this blog post, we’ll explore some tips to help you master the art of creating authentic historical dialogue.

1. Research, Research, Research!

Before you dive into writing historical dialogue, it’s crucial to conduct thorough research about the time period you are focusing on. Read books, articles, and primary sources from that era to become familiar with the language, slang, idioms, and vocabulary used by the people of that time. By immersing yourself in the historical context, you will be better equipped to create authentic dialogue that feels true to the period.

2. Study Letters, Diaries, and Personal Accounts

Letter writing was more common in the past, and diaries and personal accounts provide invaluable insights into how people actually spoke and expressed themselves during a specific period. Reading these primary sources can give you a better understanding of the speech patterns, grammar, nuances, and informal expressions used by people at that time. Incorporating these elements into your dialogue will add depth and authenticity to your characters’ conversations.

3. Pay Attention to Regional Differences

Language and dialects can vary significantly across regions and time periods. Local dialects, accents, and speech patterns are essential to consider when crafting dialogue. Take into account the geography of your story and the historical events that may have influenced language variations in different areas. By accurately representing regional differences, you can bring your readers into the specific setting and time period you are portraying in your work.

4. Avoid Anachronisms

Anachronisms can be a significant pitfall in historical writing, as they jar readers out of the time period you are trying to faithfully recreate. Steer clear of using phrases, terms, or expressions that did not exist during your chosen era. Poring over historical dictionaries, etiquette books, and other references specific to your time period can help you identify anachronistic language and avoid using it in your dialogue.

5. Balance Authenticity with Readability

While authenticity is crucial in historical writing, it’s also essential to strike a balance between authenticity and readability. Historical dialogue doesn’t have to be impenetrable or verbose. It should engage and captivate readers without overwhelming them with unnecessary details. Use a blend of historical accuracy and contemporary readability to keep your dialogue accessible while still evoking the spirit of the time period.

6. Consider the Social Status and Education Level of Your Characters

In the past, education levels, social status, and occupation had a significant impact on how people spoke. A nobleman would have had an entirely different way of speaking compared to a lower-class laborer. Take into account the background, education, and personalities of your characters, as this will influence their choice of words, grammar, and speech patterns. This attention to detail will add depth and authenticity to your dialogue.

7. Use Dialogue to Reflect Historical Context

Dialogue is an excellent tool to bring historical context to life. Use it to showcase the attitudes, beliefs, and social dynamics of the time period you are writing about. By incorporating relevant historical references and events into your dialogue, you can create a more immersive experience for your readers, allowing them to understand and connect with the historical setting on a deeper level.

8. Read and Seek Feedback

Finally, read widely within the genre of historical fiction or non-fiction to gain inspiration and learn from successful authors. Pay attention to how they handle dialogue, and analyze the techniques they employ to create authentic historical conversations. Additionally, seek feedback from beta readers or historical experts who can provide insights and help you refine your dialogue for accuracy and authenticity.

In conclusion, crafting authentic historical dialogue requires diligent research, attention to detail, and an understanding of the historical context. By immersing yourself in primary sources, carefully considering regional differences, avoiding anachronisms, and balancing authenticity with readability, you can create dialogue that brings your historical setting to life. Let your characters speak the language of their time and allow your readers to journey back with you into history.
