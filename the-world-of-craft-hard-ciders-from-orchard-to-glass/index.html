The World of Craft Hard Ciders: From Orchard to Glass


In recent years, the craft cider industry has experienced a remarkable resurgence, captivating the palates of beverage enthusiasts around the globe. With its rich history dating back centuries, cider has evolved from a humble farmyard drink to a sophisticated libation enjoyed in trendy bars and breweries worldwide. Join us on a journey through the world of craft hard ciders, from the orchard to your glass, as we explore the fascinating process behind this beloved beverage.

The Roots of Cider: A Brief History

Cider, often referred to as ’the nectar of the gods,’ boasts a history as old as civilization itself. Tracing its origins to ancient times, cider was enjoyed by the Greeks and Romans, who prized its refreshing taste and purported health benefits. In medieval Europe, cider became a staple drink, particularly in regions where grapes were scarce. English settlers brought cider to the New World, where it flourished alongside apple orchards in colonial America.

From Orchard to Press: The Art of Cider Making

At the heart of every glass of cider lies the orchard, where rows of apple trees bask in the sunlight, their branches heavy with ripe fruit. Craft cider makers carefully select a diverse array of apple varieties, each prized for its unique flavor profile and acidity levels. From tart Granny Smiths to sweet Honeycrisps, the blending of different apple varieties is akin to crafting a fine wine, with each ingredient contributing to the cider’s overall complexity and balance.

Once harvested, the apples are washed, crushed, and pressed to extract their precious juices. This juice, known as ‘must,’ undergoes fermentation, where natural yeasts or carefully selected strains are added to convert sugars into alcohol. The length and temperature of fermentation play a crucial role in shaping the final flavor profile of the cider, with some producers opting for slow, cool fermentation to preserve delicate aromas, while others favor a faster, warmer approach for a more robust character.

The Art of Blending: Creating Complexity and Depth

Craft cider makers often employ the art of blending to create ciders with layers of complexity and depth. By combining juices from different apple varieties, each with its own unique characteristics, cider makers can achieve a harmonious balance of sweetness, acidity, and tannins. Some ciders may be aged in oak barrels or infused with additional fruits, herbs, or spices to impart subtle nuances and aromas, elevating the drinking experience to new heights.

From Barrel to Bottle: The Final Touches

After fermentation and aging, the cider is carefully filtered and carbonated before being bottled or kegged for distribution. Some craft cider makers choose to bottle-condition their ciders, allowing them to undergo a secondary fermentation in the bottle, resulting in natural carbonation and a more complex flavor profile.

The labeling and branding of craft ciders often reflect the passion and creativity of the producers, with colorful artwork and whimsical names evoking the spirit of the orchard. Whether enjoyed on its own or paired with a variety of foods, craft cider offers a versatile and refreshing alternative to more traditional beverages, with styles ranging from dry and crisp to sweet and effervescent.

Exploring the World of Craft Cider

As the craft cider movement continues to gain momentum, cider enthusiasts have a wealth of options to explore, from traditional English scrumpies to innovative American blends infused with exotic fruits and spices. Craft cider festivals and tasting events provide opportunities for cider lovers to discover new favorites and connect with fellow enthusiasts, while cider bars and taprooms offer curated selections of the finest artisanal ciders from around the world.

Whether you’re a seasoned cider aficionado or new to the world of craft beverages, there has never been a better time to explore the diverse and exciting landscape of craft hard ciders. From the orchard to your glass, each sip of cider tells a story of tradition, innovation, and the timeless appeal of nature’s bounty. So raise a glass, savor the flavors, and toast to the boundless possibilities of the world of craft cider. Cheers!
