Functional Movement Screening: Assessing Mobility and Stability


In the realm of fitness and physical wellness, the quest for optimum performance and injury prevention is a perpetual journey. Whether you’re a seasoned athlete, a weekend warrior, or simply someone who prioritizes their health, understanding the intricacies of your body’s movement patterns is paramount. Enter Functional Movement Screening (FMS), a comprehensive system designed to assess and address movement deficiencies that could compromise performance and increase the risk of injury.

Understanding Functional Movement Screening

Functional Movement Screening (FMS) is not just another fitness fad; it’s a scientifically backed methodology that aims to evaluate fundamental movement patterns. Developed by physical therapist Gray Cook and strength and conditioning expert Lee Burton, FMS provides a systematic approach to identifying limitations or asymmetries in seven fundamental movement patterns.

These movement patterns include:

  1. Deep Squat: Evaluates bilateral, symmetrical, functional mobility and stability of the hips, knees, and ankles.
  2. Hurdle Step: Assesses bilateral functional mobility and stability of the hips, knees, and ankles.
  3. Inline Lunge: Examines contralateral (opposite sides) mobility and stability of the hips, knees, and ankles.
  4. Shoulder Mobility: Tests bilateral upper extremity mobility and stability, specifically in the shoulders and thoracic spine.
  5. Active Straight-Leg Raise: Assesses bilateral active hamstring and gastrocnemius flexibility while maintaining a stable pelvis.
  6. Trunk Stability Push-Up: Evaluates symmetrical upper extremity stability while the core is engaged in a push-up position.
  7. Rotary Stability: Assesses multi-planar pelvic, core, and shoulder girdle stability during a combined upper and lower extremity movement.

The Importance of Mobility and Stability

Mobility and stability are the pillars of functional movement. Mobility refers to the ability of a joint to move freely through its full range of motion, while stability pertains to the ability to control that movement. Both are crucial for efficient movement patterns and injury prevention.

When mobility is compromised or stability is lacking in certain areas, the body compensates by relying on other muscles and joints to perform tasks, leading to inefficient movement patterns and increased stress on tissues. Over time, these compensations can result in overuse injuries, chronic pain, and decreased performance.

How FMS Works

Functional Movement Screening involves a series of simple tests that can be administered by trained professionals, such as physical therapists, athletic trainers, or certified FMS specialists. During the assessment, the individual performs each movement pattern, and the evaluator scores any limitations, asymmetries, or compensations observed.

Based on the results, the evaluator can identify areas of dysfunction and develop a targeted corrective exercise program to address these issues. Corrective exercises typically focus on improving mobility, stability, and motor control in specific areas, ultimately restoring optimal movement patterns and reducing the risk of injury.

Benefits of Functional Movement Screening

  1. Injury Prevention: By identifying movement deficiencies early on, FMS helps individuals address potential risk factors before they lead to injury.

  2. Improved Performance: Optimal movement patterns are essential for achieving peak performance in sports, fitness, and daily activities. FMS helps athletes and fitness enthusiasts optimize their movement efficiency, leading to improved performance outcomes.

  3. Individualized Programming: FMS results provide valuable insights into an individual’s unique movement profile, allowing for the development of personalized corrective exercise programs tailored to address specific needs.

  4. Long-Term Health: By promoting proper movement mechanics and addressing dysfunctional patterns, FMS supports long-term musculoskeletal health and overall well-being.

Conclusion

Functional Movement Screening is not just a tool for elite athletes or fitness professionals; it’s a valuable resource for anyone committed to optimizing their movement quality and reducing the risk of injury. By assessing fundamental movement patterns and addressing any limitations or asymmetries, individuals can enhance their performance, improve their quality of life, and pursue their fitness goals with confidence. Whether you’re an athlete striving for greatness or someone simply looking to move better and feel better, FMS offers a roadmap to success on your journey to optimal movement and health.
