Nutrition for Growing Kids: Balancing Healthy Eating and Treats


When it comes to the nutrition of growing kids, finding the right balance between healthy eating and indulging in treats can be a challenge for parents. On one hand, it’s important to provide children with the nutrients they need for optimal growth and development. On the other hand, completely depriving them of treats can lead to feelings of restriction and may result in unhealthy eating habits later in life.

So, how can parents navigate this delicate balance and ensure that their children are getting the right nutrition while still enjoying occasional treats? In this blog post, we will explore some practical tips and strategies that can help parents strike a healthy balance between healthy eating and treats for their growing kids.

1. Focus on Nutrient-Dense Foods: Growing kids have higher nutrient needs, so it’s crucial to provide them with nutrient-dense foods that are rich in vitamins, minerals, and other essential nutrients. Include a variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and dairy products in their diet. These foods will not only meet their nutritional needs but also promote healthy growth and development.

2. Introduce a Rainbow of Colors: Encourage your kids to eat a wide range of colorful fruits and vegetables. Each color represents different types of nutrients, so the more variety, the better. Get creative with food presentation by cutting veggies into fun shapes or making fruit skewers. Engaging your child’s senses can make healthy eating more appealing.

3. Involve Kids in Meal Planning and Preparation: By involving your kids in meal planning and preparation, you can empower them to make healthier choices. Take them grocery shopping and let them choose a few fruits and vegetables they’d like to try. In the kitchen, let them help with washing, cutting, and cooking. Studies have shown that children are more likely to eat foods they have played a role in preparing.

4. Set a Good Example: Children often mimic the eating behaviors and preferences of their parents. If they see you enjoying a wide variety of nutritious foods and treats in moderation, they are more likely to follow suit. Be a role model by practicing what you preach and demonstrating healthy eating habits yourself.

5. Practice the 80/20 Rule: The 80/20 rule suggests that 80% of a child’s diet should be focused on healthy, nutrient-dense foods, while the remaining 20% can be reserved for treats and occasional indulgences. This approach allows for flexibility and teaches kids the value of moderation.

6. Create a Positive Food Environment: Avoid labeling foods as ‘good’ or ‘bad’ and promote a positive food environment at home. Encourage open discussions about nutrition and the importance of nourishing the body. Instead of banning certain foods completely, teach your child about making balanced food choices and the concept of ‘sometimes foods.’

7. Be Mindful of Portion Sizes: While treats can be part of a balanced diet, it’s essential to be mindful of portion sizes. Teach your child to listen to their hunger and fullness cues, and encourage them to savor each bite. By teaching portion control early on, you can help your child develop healthy eating habits that will last a lifetime.

8. Reinforce Healthy Habits Outside of Home: While it’s crucial to provide a healthy food environment at home, it’s equally important to reinforce healthy habits outside of the home. Teach your child about making smart choices when dining out or attending social events. Encourage them to opt for healthier options but also allow them to enjoy treats in moderation.

9. Encourage Physical Activity: Alongside a balanced diet, regular physical activity is crucial for growing kids. Encourage your child to participate in activities they enjoy, such as sports, dancing, or swimming. Not only does it promote a healthy lifestyle, but physical activity also helps to stimulate a healthy appetite and creates a positive relationship with food.

10. Seek Professional Advice: If you’re unsure about your child’s nutritional needs or how to strike a proper balance, consider seeking advice from a registered dietitian or pediatrician. These professionals can provide personalized guidance and address any specific concerns or challenges you may be facing.

In conclusion, achieving a balance between healthy eating and treats for growing kids is all about moderation, education, and setting a positive food environment. By providing nutrient-dense foods, involving kids in meal planning, and practicing the 80/20 rule, parents can ensure their children receive proper nutrition while still enjoying occasional treats. Remember, it’s about teaching kids to make balanced food choices and instilling healthy eating habits that will benefit them throughout their lives.
