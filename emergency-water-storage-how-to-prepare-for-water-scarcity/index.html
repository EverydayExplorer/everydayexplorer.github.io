Emergency Water Storage: How to Prepare for Water Scarcity


Water is the most precious resource on our planet. It sustains all forms of life, and without it, we cannot survive. However, with increasing population growth and climate change, water scarcity is becoming a pressing issue in many parts of the world. To ensure our own well-being and that of our communities, it is crucial to be prepared for emergencies and have a plan in place for storing water. In this blog post, we will discuss the importance of emergency water storage and provide some practical tips on how to prepare for water scarcity.

Why Emergency Water Storage Matters

Water scarcity can occur due to a variety of factors such as drought, contamination, or infrastructure failure. During such situations, access to clean and safe drinking water may become limited or completely unavailable. It is estimated that an average person needs a minimum of one gallon of water per day for drinking, cooking, and personal hygiene. Without access to clean water, our health and well-being are at risk.

By having an emergency water storage plan in place, you can ensure that you and your loved ones will have a sufficient supply of water to survive during times of scarcity. It is important to note that during emergencies, public water supplies may become contaminated or disrupted, making it necessary to rely on your own stored water.

Tips for Emergency Water Storage

1. Calculate your water needs: Before you start storing water, it is important to calculate how much you and your family will need during an emergency. The general rule of thumb is to store at least one gallon of water per person per day for a minimum of three days. However, it is advisable to store water for a longer duration, such as two weeks, to ensure you have an adequate supply.

2. Choose appropriate storage containers: Selecting the right water storage containers is crucial. Avoid using containers that have held chemicals or beverages, as they can contaminate the water. Opt for food-grade plastic containers, glass containers, or stainless steel containers. Ensure that the containers are clean, have tight-fitting lids, and are kept in a cool, dark place to prevent bacterial growth.

3. Properly sanitize and fill containers: Before filling your water storage containers, wash them with dish soap and rinse them thoroughly. Then sanitize the containers by mixing 1 teaspoon of unscented bleach per gallon of water and filling each container with this solution. Let it stand for a few minutes and then empty the container. Finally, fill the containers with clean water from a safe and reliable water source.

4. Rotate your water supply: Water stored for emergencies should be rotated every six months to ensure freshness and prevent bacterial contamination. When the time comes to rotate your water supply, use the stored water for non-potable purposes such as watering plants and refill the containers with fresh water. Remember to properly sanitize the containers before refilling.

5. Consider additional water sources: In addition to storing water, it is wise to identify and plan for potential alternative water sources during emergencies. This may include rainwater harvesting, well water, or nearby natural water bodies. It is important to have the necessary filtration systems in place to purify the water from these sources before consumption.

6. Educate yourself and your family: Make sure everyone in your family understands the importance of emergency water storage and knows how to access and use the stored water. Keep a copy of your emergency water storage plan and instructions in a readily accessible location. Also, educate yourself on various water treatment methods and basic emergency water purification techniques.

Conclusion

Water is an essential resource that we often take for granted. However, as water scarcity becomes a growing concern, it is important to be prepared for emergencies. By following the tips mentioned above and having a well-thought-out plan for emergency water storage, you can ensure the safety and well-being of yourself and your loved ones during water scarcity. Remember, it is better to be prepared now than to face a crisis unready. Stay informed, stay prepared, and stay hydrated!
