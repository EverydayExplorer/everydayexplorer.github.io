The Hidden Gems: Underrated Books You Need to Read


Are you tired of reading the same old books that everyone else is talking about? Looking to discover some hidden gems in the vast world of literature? Well, you’ve come to the right place! In this blog post, we will explore some of the most underrated books that you absolutely need to read.

One of the first books that comes to mind is ‘The Book Thief’ by Markus Zusak. This historical fiction novel takes place during World War II and is narrated by Death itself. It follows the story of a young girl named Liesel who discovers the power of words and storytelling in the midst of a war-torn Germany. This book beautifully captures the resilience of the human spirit and is often overlooked in favor of more popular war-themed novels.

Another hidden gem is ‘Station Eleven’ by Emily St. John Mandel. This science fiction novel takes place in a post-apocalyptic world and revolves around a traveling symphony that performs Shakespearean plays. It explores themes of survival, art, and the interconnectedness of humanity. Despite its gripping storyline and thought-provoking themes, ‘Station Eleven’ remains relatively unknown compared to other dystopian novels.

If you’re a fan of magical realism, then ‘The Night Circus’ by Erin Morgenstern is a must-read. This enchanting novel follows the story of two young magicians who are locked in a duel, only to fall in love with each other. Set in a mysterious circus that only operates at night, this book will transport you to a world filled with wonder and intrigue.

For those who enjoy historical fiction with a twist, ‘The Blind Assassin’ by Margaret Atwood is a perfect choice. This novel weaves together multiple narratives, including a love story and a science fiction novel within a novel. With its complex storytelling and rich characters, ‘The Blind Assassin’ showcases Atwood’s masterful writing ability.

If you’re in the mood for a heartwarming coming-of-age story, look no further than ‘A Tree Grows in Brooklyn’ by Betty Smith. This classic novel follows the life of Francie Nolan, a young girl growing up in the slums of Brooklyn in the early 20th century. Despite the harsh realities of her life, Francie finds solace in books and education. This book is often overshadowed by other literary classics, but its powerful portrayal of the immigrant experience and the importance of perseverance make it a must-read.

Last but not least, ‘Never Let Me Go’ by Kazuo Ishiguro is a hauntingly beautiful novel that will stay with you long after you’ve finished reading. Set in a dystopian alternate reality, the story follows three friends who grow up in a boarding school and eventually discover a dark secret about their future. Ishiguro’s writing is atmospheric and thought-provoking, making ‘Never Let Me Go’ a truly underrated gem.

So, there you have it - a list of underrated books that deserve more recognition. These hidden gems offer unique perspectives, captivating storylines, and unforgettable characters. Whether you’re searching for historical fiction, science fiction, magical realism, or coming-of-age tales, these books have it all. Expand your literary horizons and dive into these underrated reads. Happy reading!
