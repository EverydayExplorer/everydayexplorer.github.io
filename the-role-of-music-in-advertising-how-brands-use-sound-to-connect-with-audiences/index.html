The Role of Music in Advertising: How Brands Use Sound to Connect with Audiences


Music has always been a powerful tool to evoke emotion and create connection. It has the ability to transport us back in time, evoke nostalgia, and elicit strong emotional responses. With this in mind, it’s no surprise that music plays a significant role in advertising.

When done right, music can enhance a brand’s message, create a memorable experience, and ultimately foster a deeper connection with audiences. In this blog post, we will explore the various ways that brands use music to connect with their target audience and the impact it has on their overall advertising campaigns.

One of the most obvious ways brands use music in advertising is through the use of jingles. Jingles are short, catchy melodies or songs that are specifically created for a brand. They often act as earworms, getting stuck in our heads and associating the brand with a particular melody. Think of the iconic McDonald’s ‘I’m Lovin’ It’ jingle or the Rice Krispies ‘Snap, Crackle, Pop’ tune. These jingles serve as a way to reinforce brand recognition and create a lasting impression.

In addition to jingles, brands also utilize popular songs to enhance their advertisements. By selecting a well-known song that resonates with their target audience, brands can tap into the emotional connection people already have with that particular song. This can trigger nostalgia or positive feelings associated with the song, which in turn can transfer to the brand itself. For example, think of Coca-Cola’s use of the song ‘Holidays are Coming’ during their Christmas campaigns. The song has become synonymous with the brand’s holiday adverts and has created a strong emotional connection with audiences.

Furthermore, music can also help establish a brand’s identity and differentiate it from competitors. By carefully selecting specific genres or styles of music to accompany their adverts, brands can convey a certain mood or image. For instance, luxury brands often use classical music to evoke a sense of elegance and sophistication, whereas energetic and upbeat music is more commonly found in commercials for sports brands or energy drinks. This strategic use of music helps to solidify a brand’s positioning and make it more memorable to consumers.

Music can also be used to tell a story or amplify the narrative of an advertisement. It can enhance the visual storytelling by adding emotional depth and creating a sense of anticipation or excitement. By synchronizing the visuals with the music, brands can create a cohesive and impactful message that resonates with viewers on a deeper level. A prime example of this is Nike’s ‘Find Your Greatness’ campaign, which featured a powerful and inspiring soundtrack that reinforced the message of pushing boundaries and achieving greatness.

Moreover, music can play a vital role in cross-cultural advertising. Different cultures have unique musical preferences and associations, and by incorporating music that resonates with a specific culture, brands can establish a deeper connection with that audience. This can be achieved by adapting a popular song to the local language or by selecting a genre that is popular in the targeted region. By doing so, brands can bridge the gap between cultures and create a sense of familiarity and relatability.

In today’s digital age, music continues to be a vital component of advertising, with brands now taking advantage of online platforms and social media to connect with audiences. Short videos or commercials shared on platforms like YouTube or Instagram often rely on catchy tunes or background music to capture attention and make an impact in just a few seconds. Additionally, brands also collaborate with popular musicians or influencers to enhance their digital advertising efforts. By leveraging the fanbase of these individuals, brands can tap into their reach and create a buzz around their products or services.

In conclusion, the role of music in advertising should not be underestimated. It has the power to evoke emotion, establish brand identity, and create a lasting connection with audiences. Whether it be through jingles, popular songs, or original compositions, music serves as an integral part of a brand’s advertising strategy. By selecting the right music that aligns with their brand’s message and target audience, brands can effectively engage and connect with their consumers, driving brand loyalty and ultimately contributing to their overall success.
