10 Iconic Music Videos That Broke the Internet


Music videos have always been a powerful medium for artists to express their creativity and captivate audiences. Over the years, there have been numerous music videos that have not only captured the attention of viewers but also broke the internet. From scandalous visuals to groundbreaking concepts, these videos have left a lasting impact on pop culture. In this blog post, we will explore 10 iconic music videos that broke the internet, taking a trip down memory lane and reliving the moments that made these videos go viral.

1. ‘Thriller’ by Michael Jackson: Released in 1983, ‘Thriller’ is undoubtedly one of the most iconic music videos of all time. Directed by John Landis, the video combined horror elements with dance choreography, creating a cinematic experience that was unlike anything seen before. With its groundbreaking visuals and captivating storyline, ‘Thriller’ set a new standard for music videos and propelled Jackson to superstardom.

2. ‘Baby One More Time’ by Britney Spears: Britney Spears burst onto the music scene in 1998 with her debut single ‘Baby One More Time.’ The music video, featuring Spears as a schoolgirl, sparked controversy and became a cultural phenomenon. The video’s provocative imagery and catchy choreography helped launch Spears’ career and solidified her status as a pop princess.

3. ‘Gangnam Style’ by Psy: In 2012, South Korean artist Psy took the world by storm with his infectious hit ‘Gangnam Style.’ The music video, filled with quirky dance moves and vibrant visuals, quickly became a global sensation. It became the first video to reach one billion views on YouTube, breaking the internet and proving the power of viral videos.

4. ‘Wrecking Ball’ by Miley Cyrus: Miley Cyrus caused a stir in 2013 with her provocative music video for ‘Wrecking Ball.’ Directed by Terry Richardson, the video showcased Cyrus swinging on a wrecking ball and appearing nude. The video generated controversy and sparked discussions about artistic expression and boundaries in the music industry.

5. ‘Formation’ by Beyoncé: Beyoncé’s ‘Formation’ took the world by storm in 2016. The music video, released a day before her Super Bowl halftime performance, celebrated black culture and addressed social issues. With striking visuals and powerful messaging, ‘Formation’ became a rallying cry for the Black Lives Matter movement and solidified Beyoncé’s status as a cultural icon.

6. ‘Gentleman’ by PSY: Following the massive success of ‘Gangnam Style,’ PSY released ‘Gentleman’ in 2013. The music video featured PSY engaging in mischievous behavior and comedic antics. Once again, PSY’s unique style and catchy dance moves captured the attention of viewers worldwide and became an internet sensation.

7. ‘Bad Romance’ by Lady Gaga: Known for her avant-garde style, Lady Gaga pushed boundaries with her music video for ‘Bad Romance’ in 2009. The video showcased Gaga in elaborate costumes and surreal visuals, highlighting her artistic vision and fearless approach. ‘Bad Romance’ became one of Gaga’s most successful videos and solidified her status as a pop culture icon.

8. ‘This Is America’ by Childish Gambino: In 2018, Childish Gambino released ‘This Is America,’ a music video that addressed issues of racism and violence in America. Directed by Hiro Murai, the video featured powerful imagery and symbolism, sparking conversations and debates about the state of the nation. ‘This Is America’ went viral and became a cultural touchstone.

9. ‘Hotline Bling’ by Drake: Drake’s ‘Hotline Bling’ video became an internet sensation in 2015. The video showcased Drake’s unique dance moves, sparking countless memes and parodies. With its colorful visuals and catchy hooks, ‘Hotline Bling’ became a cultural phenomenon and solidified Drake’s status as one of the biggest names in music.

10. ‘Hello’ by Adele: Adele’s comeback in 2015 was marked by the release of her music video for ‘Hello.’ Directed by Xavier Dolan, the video showcased Adele’s emotive performance, capturing the hearts of viewers worldwide. The video broke records for the most views in 24 hours and showcased Adele’s ability to connect with audiences on a deep emotional level.

These 10 iconic music videos have not only broken the internet but also shaped the music industry and pop culture as a whole. From Michael Jackson’s groundbreaking visuals to Adele’s heartfelt performances, these videos have left an indelible mark on our collective consciousness. As technology and social media continue to evolve, we can only imagine what future music videos will break the internet and captivate audiences around the world.
