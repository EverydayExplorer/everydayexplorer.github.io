Cartoon Character Redesigns: Hits and Misses


Cartoons have been a significant part of our lives since childhood. We grew up watching classic animated shows featuring iconic characters that have become beloved symbols of pop culture. However, as time goes on, animation studios often choose to redesign these characters to keep up with the times or to attract a new audience. While some cartoon character redesigns have been successful, others have faced criticism and backlash from fans. In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the best and worst cartoon character redesigns and analyze why they were hits or misses.

Hits

1. Mickey Mouse - Disney’s beloved mascot, Mickey Mouse, has undergone several redesigns throughout the years. However, one of the most successful redesigns was the addition of his classic red shorts and white gloves, which he still wears to this day. This simple change helped to solidify Mickey’s iconic look and made him more visually appealing to a wider audience.

2. Bugs Bunny - Another classic cartoon character, Bugs Bunny, has also seen a few redesigns over the years. However, one redesign that received positive feedback was the removal of his gloves and carrot-shaped body. This change made Bugs Bunny look more natural and relatable, while still retaining his mischievous personality.

3. Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles - The Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles have had numerous adaptations, each with their own unique redesigns. One of the most successful redesigns was the transition from the original comic book style to the animated TV series in the late 1980s. The redesign made the turtles more light-hearted and marketable, appealing to both kids and adults.

4. Batman - Batman has had countless incarnations, each with their own distinct design. One successful redesign was seen in Batman: The Animated Series, where the character was given a sleek, animated look. This redesign perfectly captured the dark and mysterious essence of Batman while still maintaining a kid-friendly appeal.

Misses

1. Sonic the Hedgehog - One example of a cartoon character redesign that faced heavy criticism was the initial design of Sonic the Hedgehog in the 2019 live-action film adaptation. Fans were shocked and disappointed by Sonic’s unrealistic and unsettling appearance, particularly his overly human-like teeth and small, beady eyes. The backlash was so significant that the studio decided to completely redesign the character, showcasing the power of social media and fan feedback.

2. The Powerpuff Girls - The Powerpuff Girls, a popular cartoon from the late 90s, received a redesign in a 2016 reboot series titled ‘The Powerpuff Girls: Dance Pantsed.’ Unfortunately, this redesign was met with widespread criticism and disappointment from fans. The new art style was seen as lacking the charm and simplicity of the original series, ultimately resulting in lower viewer ratings.

3. ThunderCats Roar - ThunderCats Roar, a reboot of the classic ThunderCats series, faced immense backlash when the redesign was revealed. Fans were unhappy with the shift towards a more comedic and cartoony art style, as it deviated too far from the original show’s action-packed and serious tone.

4. Teen Titans Go! - Teen Titans Go!, a comedic spin-off of the original Teen Titans animated series, received mixed reactions when it first premiered. While some viewers appreciated the lighthearted humor and self-awareness of the show, others felt that the redesign and comedic direction diminished the depth and complexity of the characters and storytelling.

In conclusion, cartoon character redesigns can be a hit or miss. Successful redesigns often enhance the characters’ visual appeal while staying true to their core essence. On the other hand, poor redesigns can alienate and disappoint fans, resulting in negative reception and lower viewer ratings. The key to a successful redesign lies in understanding the character’s established identity and finding a balance between relevancy and respecting the nostalgia of the original design.
