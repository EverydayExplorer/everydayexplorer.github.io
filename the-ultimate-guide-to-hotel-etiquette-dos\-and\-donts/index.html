The Ultimate Guide to Hotel Etiquette: Dos\ and\ Donts


Welcome to our comprehensive guide on hotel etiquette! Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or planning your very first hotel stay, understanding the do’s and don’ts of hotel etiquette can greatly enhance your overall experience. From check-in to check-out, we’ve got you covered with our ultimate guide. So let’s dive right in and discover the key to being a well-mannered and considerate guest.

1. Do: Be polite and respectful to the hotel staff

One of the most important aspects of hotel etiquette is treating the hotel staff with kindness and respect. Remember, they are there to assist you and make your stay as comfortable as possible. A simple smile and a ‘please’ and ’thank you’ can go a long way in creating a positive atmosphere.

2. Don’t: Ignore noise regulations

Hotels are designed to provide a peaceful and relaxing environment for all guests. Be mindful of noise levels, especially during quiet hours. Avoid slamming doors, playing loud music, or having loud conversations in hallways or common areas. Respecting the tranquility of others will ensure a harmonious atmosphere for everyone.

3. Do: Keep your room tidy

Maintaining cleanliness in your room is not only courteous to the hotel staff but also contributes to your own comfort. Keep your belongings organized and dispose of trash properly. Additionally, if you require extra housekeeping services, it’s best to communicate your needs with the front desk in a polite manner.

4. Don’t: Take home hotel property

While it may be tempting to take home those plush bathrobes or a stack of hotel stationery, it’s important to remember that these items are provided for your convenience during your stay. Taking them home as souvenirs is considered theft. Enjoy the amenities while you’re there, but leave them behind when you check out.

5. Do: Follow the hotel’s guidelines for dining areas

When dining in hotel restaurants or using the breakfast area, it’s vital to follow any dress codes or guidelines that may be in place. Remember to be considerate of other guests and avoid lingering for an extended period if there’s a waitlist. These small gestures ensure that everyone has a pleasant dining experience.

6. Don’t: Smoke in non-smoking areas

In consideration of other guests and the hotel’s policies, it’s crucial to adhere to designated smoking areas. Smoking in non-smoking rooms or common areas can result in cleaning fees or fines, and it also infringes on the comfort of other guests who may have allergies or sensitivities.

7. Do: Use hotel amenities responsibly

Hotels often provide various amenities such as pools, fitness centers, and spas for guests’ enjoyment. It’s essential to use these facilities responsibly and follow any rules or regulations posted. This ensures that all guests can enjoy these amenities safely and comfortably.

8. Don’t: Overuse or misuse hotel supplies

While it’s perfectly acceptable to use the toiletries and supplies provided by the hotel, taking excessive amounts or using them for purposes other than intended is discouraged. To avoid unnecessary waste, take only what you need and respect the hotel’s resources.

9. Do: Tip appropriately

Recognize exceptional service by tipping the hotel staff, including bellhops, housekeeping, and room service. Tipping is not mandatory, but it is a gesture of appreciation for their hard work and dedication. The amount you tip may vary depending on the region and the level of service you receive.

10. Don’t: Forget to check out on time

To avoid any inconvenience, make sure to check out on time. Hotels often have specific check-out times to allow for room preparation for incoming guests. If you need extra time, it’s best to communicate with the front desk in advance to arrange a late check-out if available.

By following these do’s and don’ts of hotel etiquette, you’ll ensure a pleasant and respectful experience for yourself and your fellow guests. Remember, a little courtesy goes a long way in creating a positive atmosphere and fostering good relationships with hotel staff. So, go ahead and enjoy your stay while being the epitome of a well-mannered guest. Safe travels!
