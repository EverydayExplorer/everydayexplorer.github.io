The Truth About Credit Scores: Debunking Common Myths


Introduction Having a good credit score is essential in today’s economy. It can determine whether you’ll be approved for a loan, credit card, or even a rental application. However, there is a lot of confusion and misinformation surrounding credit scores. In this blog post, we aim to debunk common myths and shed light on the truth about credit scores.

Myth 1: Checking Your Credit Score Lowers It One of the most common myths is that checking your credit score frequently can harm your credit. However, this is far from the truth. When you check your own credit score, it’s considered a ‘soft inquiry’ and doesn’t impact your credit. It’s only when a lender or credit card issuer makes a ‘hard inquiry’ to assess your creditworthiness that there may be a slight impact on your score. Regularly monitoring your credit score is a responsible habit that helps you stay aware of your financial health.

Myth 2: Closing Old Credit Accounts Improves Your Score Some people believe that closing old credit accounts will boost their credit score. However, this is a misconception. In reality, closing old accounts can actually harm your credit score. Credit age is an important factor in determining your score, and older accounts with a long history of on-time payments contribute positively to your creditworthiness. Closing them can decrease your credit age and potentially lower your score. It’s generally advisable to keep old accounts open, especially if they have no annual fees.

Myth 3: Paying Off Debts Instantly Boosts Your Credit While paying off your debts is undoubtedly a responsible financial move, it doesn’t guarantee an instant boost to your credit score. Credit scoring models take into account various factors, including payment history, credit utilization, length of credit history, and types of credit. It’s important to maintain a consistent track record of making timely payments and keeping your credit utilization ratio in check. Over time, responsible credit behavior will positively impact your credit score.

Myth 4: Closing Credit Cards With High Limits Helps Your Score Some individuals believe that closing credit cards with high credit limits can improve their credit score. However, this is another common myth. Credit utilization is a key factor in determining your credit score, and it’s calculated by dividing your total credit card balances by your total available credit. Closing a card reduces your available credit, which can increase your credit utilization ratio and potentially lower your score. It’s generally better to keep your credit cards open unless they have excessive annual fees.

Myth 5: Only Income Affects Your Credit Score Contrary to popular belief, your income does not directly affect your credit score. Credit scoring models do not consider your income when calculating your creditworthiness. However, your income indirectly impacts your credit score when it comes to managing your debts and paying them off on time. The key factors that influence your credit score include payment history, credit utilization, length of credit history, types of credit, and new credit applications.

Conclusion It’s important to separate fact from fiction when it comes to credit scores. Checking your own credit score doesn’t harm it, and closing old accounts can actually have a negative impact. Paying off debts doesn’t guarantee an instant boost, and closing credit cards with high limits may harm your credit utilization ratio. Finally, remember that income is not a direct factor in calculating your credit score. By understanding these truths about credit scores, you can make informed decisions to improve and maintain a positive credit history.
