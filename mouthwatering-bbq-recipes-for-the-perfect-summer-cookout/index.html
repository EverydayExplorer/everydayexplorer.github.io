Mouthwatering BBQ Recipes for the Perfect Summer Cookout


Summer is here, and that means it’s time to fire up the grill and indulge in some mouthwatering BBQ recipes. Whether you’re hosting a backyard cookout or simply enjoying a sunny day with your loved ones, these recipes are sure to impress your guests and satisfy your taste buds. From juicy burgers to tender ribs, here are some BBQ recipes that will take your summer cookout to the next level.

Grilled BBQ Chicken Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, mix together the BBQ sauce, olive oil, minced garlic, paprika, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Cut the chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces and thread them onto the soaked wooden skewers.
  4. Brush the BBQ sauce mixture onto the chicken skewers, making sure to coat them evenly.
  5. Place the skewers on the preheated grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred.
  6. Serve the grilled BBQ chicken skewers with extra BBQ sauce for dipping.

Smoky Pulled Pork Sandwiches

Ingredients:

  • 3 pounds pork shoulder
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • Hamburger buns

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to indirect heat, around 225°F (107°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the smoked paprika, brown sugar, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper to create a dry rub.
  3. Rub the dry rub all over the pork shoulder, making sure to coat it evenly.
  4. Place the seasoned pork shoulder on the grill, away from direct heat, and cook for about 6-8 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 195°F (90°C).
  5. Once the pork shoulder is cooked, remove it from the grill and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  6. Using two forks, shred the pork shoulder into small pieces.
  7. Mix the shredded pork with BBQ sauce, ensuring it is well coated.
  8. Serve the smoky pulled pork on hamburger buns and enjoy!

Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husks removed
  • 4 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh herbs (such as parsley, chives, or basil), chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the softened butter, fresh herbs, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Brush the herb butter mixture onto each ear of corn, making sure to coat them evenly.
  4. Place the corn on the preheated grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are tender and slightly charred.
  5. Remove the grilled corn from the grill and let it cool for a few minutes.
  6. Serve the grilled corn on the cob with extra herb butter on the side.

Grilled Pineapple with Cinnamon Sugar

Ingredients:

  • 1 pineapple, peeled and cored
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Vanilla ice cream (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Slice the pineapple into rings, about 1/2 inch thick.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together the brown sugar and ground cinnamon.
  4. Sprinkle the cinnamon sugar mixture onto each pineapple ring, making sure to coat them evenly.
  5. Place the pineapple rings on the preheated grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes per side, until they are caramelized and slightly charred.
  6. Remove the grilled pineapple from the grill and let it cool for a few minutes.
  7. Serve the grilled pineapple on its own or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a delightful summer dessert.

With these mouthwatering BBQ recipes, you’re ready to host the perfect summer cookout. From tender grilled chicken skewers to smoky pulled pork sandwiches, these dishes will impress your guests and leave them craving for more. Don’t forget to pair them with grilled corn on the cob and finish off the meal with some grilled pineapple for a sweet and satisfying ending. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and enjoy the flavors of summer!
