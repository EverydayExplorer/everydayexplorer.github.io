Pet-Friendly Thanksgiving: Tips for Including Your Pet in the Festivities


As Thanksgiving approaches, families across the country are eagerly anticipating the joyous festivities and delicious feasts. For many, this holiday is a time for gratitude, togetherness, and indulgence in delectable dishes. But amidst all the excitement, there’s one member of the family that often gets overlooked – our beloved pets.

Including your furry friend in the Thanksgiving celebrations can be a rewarding experience for both you and your pet. After all, they’re part of the family too! However, it’s essential to ensure that their involvement is safe, enjoyable, and stress-free. To help you make this Thanksgiving a memorable one for your pet, here are some tips for including them in the festivities:

  1. Plan Pet-Friendly Activities: Incorporate activities that your pet will enjoy throughout the day. Whether it’s a leisurely walk in the morning, a game of fetch in the backyard, or a cozy cuddle session while watching the Thanksgiving Day parade, make sure to carve out time for your pet amidst the hustle and bustle.

  2. Create a Safe Space: With guests coming and going, loud noises, and tempting aromas wafting through the air, your home can quickly become overwhelming for your pet. Set up a quiet, comfortable space where they can retreat if they need a break from the festivities. Make sure this area is equipped with their favorite toys, bedding, and water bowl.

  3. Mind the Menu: While it may be tempting to share your Thanksgiving feast with your pet, many traditional holiday foods can be harmful to them. Avoid feeding them foods such as turkey bones, stuffing, gravy, onions, garlic, chocolate, grapes, and raisins, as these can be toxic to pets. Instead, offer them pet-safe treats or prepare a special Thanksgiving meal just for them.

  4. Stick to Routine: Amidst all the excitement, try to maintain your pet’s regular routine as much as possible. Stick to their usual feeding and walking schedule to help them feel secure and comfortable. This consistency can help alleviate any stress or anxiety they may experience during the festivities.

  5. Be Mindful of Decorations: Thanksgiving decorations can add a festive touch to your home, but some decorations can pose a risk to your pet. Keep candles, potpourri, and decorative plants out of reach to prevent accidental ingestion or injury. Additionally, be cautious with decorative items that your pet may see as toys, such as small decorations or table centerpieces.

  6. Supervise Interactions: If you’re hosting guests who are unfamiliar with your pet, make sure to supervise interactions between them to ensure everyone’s safety and comfort. Some guests may not be comfortable around animals, while others may inadvertently do things that could frighten or harm your pet. Keep a close eye on how your pet is responding to the company and intervene if necessary.

  7. Prepare for Travel: If you’re traveling for Thanksgiving and bringing your pet along, be sure to plan ahead. Pack their essentials, including food, water, medications, bedding, and any comfort items they may need. Additionally, make sure your pet is properly secured during travel to ensure their safety.

By following these tips, you can ensure that your pet feels included, safe, and loved during your Thanksgiving celebrations. After all, there’s no better feeling than sharing gratitude and joy with the ones we hold dear – furry friends included. So this Thanksgiving, take the time to show your pet how thankful you are for their unconditional love and companionship.
