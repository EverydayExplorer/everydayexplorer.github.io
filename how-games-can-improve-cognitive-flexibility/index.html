How games can improve cognitive flexibility


In today’s digital age, video games have revolutionized the way we entertain ourselves. But did you know that games can also have a positive impact on our cognitive abilities? One particular aspect that games can enhance is cognitive flexibility - the ability to think flexibly and adapt to new situations. In this blog post, we will explore the ways in which games can improve cognitive flexibility, as well as provide some examples of games that can help develop this valuable skill.

What is Cognitive Flexibility?

Cognitive flexibility refers to the mental ability to switch between thinking about different concepts, ideas, or perspectives. It involves being able to adapt to new information, solve problems creatively, and respond to changing circumstances. Cognitive flexibility is a crucial skill in various aspects of life, such as problem-solving, decision-making, and social interactions.

The Benefits of Cognitive Flexibility

Enhancing cognitive flexibility can bring numerous benefits to our everyday lives. For example, the ability to think flexibly allows us to approach problems from different angles and come up with innovative solutions. It also helps us to adapt quickly in new or unfamiliar situations, enabling us to navigate through challenging or uncertain circumstances effectively. Moreover, cognitive flexibility plays a vital role in enhancing creativity, as it allows us to consider alternative ideas and perspectives.

How Games Foster Cognitive Flexibility

Games are an excellent medium for developing cognitive flexibility due to their interactive nature and the challenges they present. Here are some ways in which games can improve cognitive flexibility:

  1. Complex problem-solving: Many games involve complex puzzles and challenges that require players to think critically and find unconventional solutions. By engaging in these types of games, players enhance their cognitive flexibility by exploring different problem-solving strategies and adapting their thinking to overcome various obstacles.

  2. Adapting to new environments: In video games, players often find themselves in unfamiliar virtual worlds with unique rules and dynamics. This requires players to quickly adapt their strategies and approaches to navigate and succeed in these environments. This constant need for adaptation enhances cognitive flexibility by fostering flexible thinking and quick decision-making.

  3. Embracing perspective-taking: Some games allow players to take on different roles or perspectives, which encourages them to think from alternative viewpoints. By embodying different characters or playing through different storylines, players develop the ability to think flexibly and consider diverse ideas and perspectives.

  4. Multi-tasking: Many games require players to juggle multiple tasks simultaneously. This not only challenges their ability to manage different objectives but also enhances cognitive flexibility by training their minds to switch between tasks and adapt their strategies accordingly.

Examples of Games That Improve Cognitive Flexibility

Now that we understand how games can improve cognitive flexibility, let’s explore some examples of games that have been specifically designed to enhance this skill:

  1. Portal Series - These puzzle-platformer games require players to think critically and approach problems from various angles. Players control a character equipped with a ‘portal gun’ that can create portals allowing for unique ways of navigating through levels. The game’s complex puzzles demand players to think flexibly and adapt their portal placements to progress.

  2. Civilization Series - This strategy game allows players to build and manage their own civilization, making decisions that impact their society’s growth and development. In Civilization, players need to analyze multiple variables, adapt their strategies to changing circumstances, and anticipate the actions of other civilizations.

  3. Braid - Braid is a platformer puzzle game that challenges players to manipulate time in order to solve puzzles. It requires players to think outside the box and consider different ways of using time manipulation to progress through the game.

  4. The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild - This open-world adventure game provides players with a vast and dynamic environment to explore and conquer. The game encourages players to think flexibly by presenting them with different obstacles and puzzles that require creative problem-solving and adaptation.

In conclusion, games can have a significant impact on cognitive flexibility by providing players with complex problem-solving, adapting to new environments, embracing perspective-taking, and practicing multitasking. By engaging in these types of games, players can enhance their cognitive flexibility, which in turn can improve their problem-solving skills, decision-making abilities, and overall creativity. So the next time you pick up a controller, remember that gaming not only provides entertainment but also an opportunity to develop valuable cognitive skills.
