The Best Road Trips in Europe: From the Amalfi Coast to the Scottish Highlands


Europe is undoubtedly one of the most picturesque and diverse continents in the world. With its rich history, stunning landscapes, and charming cities, it’s no wonder that road trips have become increasingly popular for travelers looking to explore the hidden gems that Europe has to offer. From the breathtaking Amalfi Coast in Italy to the rugged Scottish Highlands, there are endless possibilities for an unforgettable road trip adventure. In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the best road trips in Europe that will leave you in awe of the beauty and diversity of this continent.

1. Amalfi Coast, Italy:
Known for its dramatic coastal cliffs, turquoise waters, and charming pastel-colored towns, the Amalfi Coast is a dream road trip destination. Start your journey from Naples and drive along the winding coastal road, passing picturesque towns like Positano, Amalfi, and Ravello. Enjoy the stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea, visit historic sites, indulge in delicious Italian cuisine, and soak up the sun on the beautiful beaches along the way.

2. Ring of Kerry, Ireland:
The Ring of Kerry is a famous circular route that takes you through some of the most spectacular landscapes in Ireland. Starting and ending in Killarney, this 179-kilometer journey will take you through rugged mountains, glacial lakes, picturesque villages, and breathtaking coastal cliffs. Don’t forget to visit the Skellig Islands, an UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ancient monastic settlement and stunning bird colonies.

3. Transfagarasan Highway, Romania:
Considered one of the most scenic drives in the world, the Transfagarasan Highway is a must-do road trip for adventure seekers. This winding road takes you through the beautiful Carpathian Mountains, offering breathtaking views of valleys, waterfalls, and snow-capped peaks. Make sure to stop at Balea Lake, a glacial lake surrounded by majestic mountains, and visit the iconic Poenari Castle, associated with the legendary Dracula.

4. Scottish Highlands, Scotland:
For nature lovers, a road trip through the Scottish Highlands is an absolute must. Starting from Edinburgh or Glasgow, explore the rugged landscapes, ancient castles, and mystical lochs that have inspired countless stories and legends. Drive through Glencoe, a stunning valley with dramatic mountain scenery, visit the iconic Eilean Donan Castle, and explore the Isle of Skye, known for its rugged coastline and fairy pools.

5. The Atlantic Road, Norway:
The Atlantic Road is a truly unique road trip experience that will take you along one of Norway’s most scenic coastal routes. Starting from Kristiansund, this 8-kilometer stretch of road winds its way across a series of islands and causeways, offering breathtaking views of the Norwegian Sea. Don’t miss the chance to stop at the viewpoints, visit the charming fishing villages, and marvel at the mesmerizing architecture of the iconic Storseisundet Bridge.

6. Grossglockner High Alpine Road, Austria:
Nestled in the heart of the Austrian Alps, the Grossglockner High Alpine Road is a dream road trip destination for nature enthusiasts. This panoramic mountain road takes you through the spectacular Hohe Tauern National Park, offering breathtaking views of alpine meadows, glacial valleys, and towering peaks. Make sure to visit the Kaiser Franz Josef’s Hohe, a visitor center located at the foot of Austria’s highest mountain, Grossglockner.

7. Causeway Coastal Route, Northern Ireland:
The Causeway Coastal Route is a scenic road trip that takes you along the stunning coastline of Northern Ireland. Starting from Belfast, drive through charming coastal villages, marvel at the famous Giant’s Causeway, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and explore the beautiful Glens of Antrim. Don’t forget to visit the Carrick-a-Rede Rope Bridge for a thrilling experience and enjoy the stunning views of the rugged coastline.

8. Trollstigen, Norway:
Trollstigen, meaning ’the Troll’s Path,’ is a dramatic mountain road that winds its way through the Norwegian countryside. This 20-kilometer road is famous for its hairpin bends, steep gradients, and panoramic views. Drive through the scenic Rauma Valley, admire the powerful Stigfossen waterfall, and enjoy the breathtaking views of the mountains and fjords from the viewpoint at the top.

9. Cinque Terre, Italy:
Cinque Terre, meaning ‘Five Lands,’ is a picturesque coastal region in Liguria, Italy. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its colorful cliffside villages, charming vineyards, and scenic hiking trails. Take a leisurely drive along the winding coastal road, visit the five beautiful villages – Monterosso al Mare, Vernazza, Corniglia, Manarola, and Riomaggiore, and soak up the charming atmosphere of this unique destination.

10. The Romantic Road, Germany:
The Romantic Road is a famous scenic route that stretches over 350 kilometers, connecting picturesque towns and fairytale castles in southern Germany. Starting from Würzburg and ending in Füssen, this road trip will take you through the heart of Bavaria, passing through charming towns like Rothenburg ob der Tauber, Dinkelsbühl, and Nördlingen. Don’t miss the chance to visit Neuschwanstein Castle, the inspiration behind Disney’s Sleeping Beauty castle.

In conclusion, Europe is a continent that offers some of the most captivating and diverse road trip experiences in the world. From the stunning Amalfi Coast in Italy to the wild and rugged beauty of the Scottish Highlands, there’s something for every type of traveler. So grab a map, hit the open road, and get ready for an adventure of a lifetime as you explore the best road trips Europe has to offer.
