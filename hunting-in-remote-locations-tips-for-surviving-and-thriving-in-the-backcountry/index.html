Hunting in Remote Locations: Tips for Surviving and Thriving in the Backcountry


When it comes to hunting, there is an undeniable thrill in venturing out into remote locations. The backcountry offers not only the challenge of tracking and capturing game, but also the chance to connect with nature on a deeper level. However, hunting in these remote locations requires careful planning, preparation, and knowledge. In this blog post, we will provide you with valuable tips to ensure your safety, success, and enjoyment while hunting in the backcountry.

1. Research and Scout the Area

Before embarking on a hunting trip in a remote location, it is crucial to thoroughly research and scout the area. Study maps, topography, and satellite images to familiarize yourself with the terrain. Look for signs of game habitats, water sources, and potential travel corridors. Understanding the area will help you plan your hunting strategy more effectively.

2. Prepare a Detailed Itinerary

Creating a detailed itinerary is essential when venturing into remote locations. Share your itinerary with a reliable person back home, providing them with your expected return date and contact details. In case of an emergency, this information will be crucial for search and rescue teams.

3. Pack the Right Gear

Packing the right gear is essential for surviving and thriving in the backcountry. Some essential items include:

  • Quality hunting rifle or bow
  • Ammunition or arrows
  • Topographic maps and compass
  • GPS device
  • Quality hunting knife
  • Binoculars and rangefinder
  • Appropriate clothing and layering options for varying weather conditions
  • First aid kit
  • Water purification system
  • High-calorie, nutritious food
  • Shelter options (tent, tarp, sleeping bag)

Ensure that you pack light but prioritizethe essentials. Weight and space limitations in remote locations make it crucial to carry only what is necessary.

4. Be Prepared for Challenging Weather

When hunting in remote locations, you must prepare for various weather conditions that can change rapidly. Dressing in layers allows you to adjust your clothing as needed. Invest in quality waterproof and breathable outerwear to protect yourself from rain, snow, and wind. Additionally, pack extra socks and a waterproof cover for your backpack to keep your gear dry.

5. Develop Navigation Skills

Navigating through unfamiliar terrain is a skill every hunter should possess. Learn how to use a compass and topographic maps before your trip. Familiarize yourself with using a GPS device and download relevant offline maps. Having these skills will help you find your way back to camp or locate specific hunting spots.

6. Carry an Emergency Communication Device

In remote locations, it is essential to have a reliable means of communication in case of emergencies. Carrying a satellite phone, a personal locator beacon (PLB), or a two-way radio can be a lifeline. It allows you to call for help if needed and stay in contact with your hunting partners.

7. Prioritize Water and Food Management

In the backcountry, access to clean water and adequate food is essential for survival. Carry a water filtration system to purify water from streams or lakes. Plan your meals and snacks wisely, opting for lightweight, high-calorie foods. Be sure to pack enough supplies for unexpected delays or emergencies.

8. Practice Stealth and Patience

Hunting in remote locations requires stealth and patience. Be mindful of noise, scent, and visual cues that may alert game to your presence. Use natural cover and stay downwind when approaching your target. Patience is key as you wait for the opportune moment to take a shot.

9. Respect the Environment and Wildlife

When hunting in remote locations, it is vital to respect the environment and wildlife. Observe ethical hunting practices and local regulations. Pack out any trash or waste, leaving no trace of your presence. Treat wildlife with respect and appreciate the beauty of nature that surrounds you.

10. Never Compromise on Safety

Above all, prioritize your safety when hunting in remote locations. Be aware of potential hazards such as steep terrain, unpredictable weather, and dangerous wildlife. Trust your instincts and avoid taking unnecessary risks.

By following these tips, you can ensure a safe and successful hunting experience in remote locations. Remember, preparation, knowledge, and respect for the environment are crucial. Happy hunting!

Disclaimer: Hunting regulations and guidelines may vary by location. Always check and abide by local hunting laws and regulations before proceeding with any hunting activities.
