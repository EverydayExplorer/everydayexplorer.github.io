The Influence of Music in Advertising: Case Studies


In the world of advertising, capturing the attention of consumers is crucial. But what makes an ad memorable? What makes it stand out from the sea of other commercials? One of the key elements that can make or break an advertisement is the use of music.

Music has a powerful influence on our emotions and can evoke specific moods and feelings. Advertisers have long recognized the potential of utilizing music to connect with their audience on a deeper level. The right soundtrack can help to create a memorable and emotional experience that resonates with viewers.

In this blog post, we will explore various case studies that highlight the impact of music in advertising. These examples demonstrate how the choice of music can make a significant difference in the effectiveness and success of an advertisement.

1. Apple’s ‘Silhouette’ Campaign

One of the most iconic examples of music’s influence in advertising is Apple’s ‘Silhouette’ campaign. The series of commercials featured silhouettes of people dancing against vibrant, colorful backgrounds while listening to iPods. One of the key factors that set these ads apart was the music selection.

Apple chose popular upbeat songs from artists like Jet, The Ting Tings, and The Black Eyed Peas, creating a high-energy atmosphere that perfectly complemented the energetic movements of the silhouettes. The catchy tunes and infectious beats not only appealed to the target audience but also helped to establish a connection between the product and a sense of fun and excitement.

2. Volkswagen’s ‘The Force’

Volkswagen’s ‘The Force’ commercial during the Super Bowl in 2011 became an instant hit. The ad features a young boy dressed as Darth Vader attempting to use the Force on various objects around the house, without success. However, when his father arrives home in a Volkswagen Passat, the boy tries one more time, and to his surprise, the car starts up.

The choice of music was pivotal in making this commercial memorable. The use of John Williams’ iconic ‘Imperial March’ from the Star Wars soundtrack added humor and excitement to the ad, as well as creating an emotional connection with the audience. The music instantly transports viewers back to the Star Wars universe, creating a sense of nostalgia and delight.

3. Nike’s ‘Jogger’

Another example of the influential power of music in advertising is Nike’s ‘Jogger’ commercial. The ad features a runner on a deserted city street, accompanied by an intense and powerful soundtrack. The music, ‘Out of the Black’ by Royal Blood, sets the tone for the ad by creating a sense of determination, struggle, and triumph.

The combination of the powerful visuals and the driving music evokes a strong emotional response from viewers. It conveys the message that Nike products are designed for those who are prepared to push their limits and overcome obstacles. The music in this case enhances the impact of the ad, making it more memorable and impactful.

4. Coca-Cola’s ‘I’d Like to Buy the World a Coke’

One of the most enduring and influential ad campaigns of all time is Coca-Cola’s ‘I’d Like to Buy the World a Coke.’ The commercial features a multicultural group of people singing together on a hilltop, with the iconic melody and lyrics of the song ‘I’d Like to Teach the World to Sing (In Perfect Harmony).’

This campaign was revolutionary because it used music and a universal message of harmony and unity to connect with a global audience. The song’s catchy and uplifting melody, combined with the visual representation of people from diverse backgrounds coming together, created a powerful emotional experience that resonated with viewers worldwide. The song became a hit in its own right and has since become synonymous with Coca-Cola.

Conclusion

These case studies demonstrate the immense influence that music can have on advertising. The right choice of music can elevate an advertisement from being forgettable to being memorable and impactful. It can create an emotional connection between the brand and the audience, evoke specific emotions, and enhance the overall message of the ad.

Advertisers must carefully consider the target audience, the desired emotional response, and the brand identity when choosing music for their commercials. They must select music that aligns with their product or message and creates a harmonious and memorable experience for viewers.

While these case studies represent only a fraction of the countless examples of music’s influence in advertising, they serve as powerful reminders of the transformative power of music in the world of marketing. Music has the ability to captivate, inspire, and connect with consumers in ways that words and visuals alone cannot. It can be the secret ingredient that makes an ad truly unforgettable.
