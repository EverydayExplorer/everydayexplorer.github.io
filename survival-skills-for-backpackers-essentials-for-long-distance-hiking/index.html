Survival Skills for Backpackers: Essentials for Long-Distance Hiking


Heading out into the great outdoors on a long-distance hiking trip is an exhilarating adventure. It offers you the opportunity to disconnect from the hustle and bustle of everyday life and immerse yourself in nature. However, it is important to remember that hiking in remote areas comes with its own set of challenges and risks. That’s why having the right survival skills is essential for backpackers. In this article, we will explore some of the most important survival skills that every backpacker should possess to ensure a safe and successful journey.

1. Navigation Skills:
One of the first and most crucial survival skills for backpackers is navigation. When you are hiking in unfamiliar territory, it is essential to know how to navigate using a map and compass. Familiarize yourself with reading topographic maps, understanding contour lines, and identifying key landmarks. Additionally, consider carrying a GPS device as a backup, but do not solely rely on it.

2. Shelter Building:
Knowing how to build a shelter can be a lifesaver in unpredictable weather conditions or emergencies. Even if you are carrying a tent, accidents can happen, and knowing how to create a makeshift shelter from natural materials such as branches, leaves, or rocks will provide you with a safe place to rest and protect yourself from the elements.

3. Fire Building:
Fire is not only essential for warmth but also for cooking food, purifying water, and signaling for help. Learn different fire-building techniques, such as the bow drill or flint and steel, and practice them before your hike. Carry fire-starting tools like waterproof matches or a lighter, and always ensure you adhere to local fire regulations and safety precautions.

4. Water Sourcing and Purification:
In the wilderness, finding a reliable source of clean water is crucial. Learn how to identify potential water sources, such as streams or lakes, and carry a water filtration system or water purification tablets. Avoid drinking untreated water, as it can contain harmful bacteria and parasites that could lead to illness.

5. First Aid Skills:
Accidents can happen anytime, anywhere, so having basic first aid skills is essential. Carry a comprehensive first aid kit with supplies for treating cuts, blisters, sprains, burns, and insect bites. Additionally, take a first aid course to familiarize yourself with emergency procedures and CPR techniques. Remember to also carry any personal medications you may require.

6. Food foraging:
While carrying sufficient food is a priority, it is essential to have knowledge of edible plants and berries in case of emergency or unexpected situations. Learn to identify edible wild plants and berries native to the area you are hiking in. Carry a field guide that specifically highlights edible plants, and research which plants are safe to consume based on your location and season.

7. Wildlife Safety:
Encountering wildlife while hiking can be a breathtaking experience, but it is important to understand how to coexist with them safely. Research the wildlife species native to the area you are hiking in and learn how to react in case of an encounter. Carry bear spray or other wildlife deterrents, and always store your food securely to avoid attracting unwanted animal attention.

8. Leave No Trace Principles:
As backpackers, it is our responsibility to minimize our impact on the environment. Familiarize yourself with the Leave No Trace principles which include disposing of waste properly, camping on durable surfaces, and respecting wildlife. By adhering to these principles, we can ensure that future generations can also enjoy the beauty of the wilderness.

In conclusion, acquiring and honing these survival skills is essential for backpackers embarking on long-distance hiking trips. The ability to navigate, build a shelter, start a fire, source and purify water, provide basic first aid, forage for food, practice wildlife safety, and follow Leave No Trace principles will greatly enhance your safety and enjoyment during your outdoor adventures. Remember to always prepare, research, and practice these skills before setting out on your journey. Stay safe, stay prepared, and make memories that will last a lifetime.
