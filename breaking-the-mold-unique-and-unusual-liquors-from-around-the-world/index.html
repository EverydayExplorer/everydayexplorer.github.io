Breaking the Mold: Unique and Unusual Liquors from Around the World


Do you consider yourself an adventurous spirit when it comes to trying new things? Are you tired of the same old vodka, rum, and whiskey? If so, then you have come to the right place. In this blog post, we will take you on a journey to discover unique and unusual liquors from around the world. These unconventional spirits are sure to break the mold and bring a whole new level of excitement to your drinking experience.

Let’s start our journey in South Korea with a liquor called Soju. Soju is a traditional Korean spirit that has gained popularity worldwide in recent years. What sets Soju apart is its smooth and clean taste, similar to vodka but with a slightly sweeter flavor. Made primarily from rice, wheat, or barley, Soju is often enjoyed neat or mixed in cocktails. With its low alcohol content, it’s a great option for those who prefer a lighter, more refreshing drink.

From Korea, we move to Japan, where we discover Shochu. Shochu is a traditional Japanese distilled spirit made from various ingredients such as rice, barley, sweet potatoes, or even buckwheat. What makes Shochu unique is its versatility. It can be enjoyed straight, on the rocks, or mixed in cocktails. With its earthy and delicate flavors, Shochu is a fantastic choice for those looking to experiment with new taste profiles.

Next, we head over to Mexico, where we encounter Mezcal. Mezcal is a smoky and savory liquor made from the agave plant. Unlike its more popular cousin, tequila, which is made solely from blue agave, Mezcal can be made from various species of agave, each producing a unique flavor profile. The production process of Mezcal involves roasting the agave hearts in underground pit ovens, giving it a rich, smoky taste. Mezcal is often sipped neat or used as a base for craft cocktails, providing a distinct and unforgettable experience.

Our journey wouldn’t be complete without a stop in Eastern Europe, where we stumble upon Biska. Biska is a traditional Croatian liqueur known for its fruity and herbal flavors. It is made by infusing mistletoe berries in brandy and honey, resulting in a distinctive taste that is both sweet and slightly bitter. Biska is often served as a digestif or used in cocktails to add a unique twist to classic recipes. This uncommon liquor is sure to surprise and delight your taste buds.

Finally, we end our adventure in the Caribbean, where we discover Falernum. Falernum is a sweet and spicy liqueur that originated in Barbados. It is made by blending rum with lime, almond, and various spices like ginger, cloves, and allspice. Falernum is a popular ingredient in many tropical cocktails, adding a complex and aromatic flavor. With its warm and exotic taste, Falernum is a must-try for anyone looking to delve into the world of Caribbean-inspired libations.

Now that we have explored some of the unique and unusual liquors from around the world, it’s time for you to embark on your own journey of discovery. Break free from the traditional and venture into the realm of unconventional spirits. Whether you choose to sip Soju in a Korean-inspired cocktail, experiment with Shochu’s delicate flavors, savor the smokiness of Mezcal, savor the fruity notes of Biska, or indulge in the exotic taste of Falernum, these unique liquors are guaranteed to take your drinking experience to new and exciting heights.

So, raise your glass and toast to the world of unconventional spirits. Cheers to breaking the mold and embracing the extraordinary. With these unique liquors in hand, the possibilities are endless, and the adventure continues.
