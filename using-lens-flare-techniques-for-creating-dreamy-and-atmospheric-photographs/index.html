Using Lens Flare: Techniques for Creating Dreamy and Atmospheric Photographs


Photography is a powerful medium that allows us to capture moments and evoke emotions through visual storytelling. One technique that can add a dreamy and atmospheric quality to our photographs is the use of lens flare. Lens flare occurs when light enters the camera lens and creates bright, hazy shapes or streaks in the final image. While some photographers may see lens flare as a technical flaw to be avoided, others embrace it as a creative tool to enhance their photographs. In this blog post, we will explore various techniques for using lens flare to create captivating and ethereal images.

1. Understand the Nature of Lens Flare
Before we dive into the techniques, it’s important to understand the nature of lens flare. As mentioned earlier, lens flare occurs when light enters the lens and interacts with the internal elements. The shape, intensity, and color of the flare depend on factors such as the angle of the light source, the type of lens, and the position of the sun or other light sources. By intentionally incorporating lens flare into our compositions, we can add an element of intrigue and whimsy to our photographs.

2. Find the Right Light Source
To create lens flare, you need a strong light source such as the sun or artificial lights. The positioning of the light source relative to the lens will determine the intensity and shape of the flare. Experiment with different angles and positions, and observe how the flare interacts with the elements in your scene. The ideal placement of the light source will vary depending on the desired effect, so don’t be afraid to move around and explore different perspectives.

3. Use a Narrow Aperture
To enhance lens flare, it is recommended to use a narrow aperture (higher f-stop). A smaller aperture increases the chances of the light source creating flare within the image. Keep in mind that a narrower aperture also means less light entering the camera, so you may need to adjust other settings like ISO or shutter speed to compensate for the reduced exposure.

4. Composition is Key
When incorporating lens flare into your photographs, pay careful attention to composition. Think about how the flare interacts with the other elements in the frame and how it guides the viewer’s eye. Look for opportunities to use flare to draw attention to your subject or create a sense of depth. Remember that lens flare can add a mystical and dreamy quality to your images, so consider how it complements the mood and story you want to convey.

5. Experiment with Different Lenses
Not all lenses produce lens flare in the same way. Some lenses are more susceptible to flare due to their construction or coatings, while others may suppress or modify it. Experiment with different lenses to discover how each one interacts with the light source and adds flare to your images. Vintage lenses or lenses with lower-quality coatings often produce interesting and unique flare patterns, which can give your photographs a distinct aesthetic.

6. Embrace Imperfections
Lens flare is often accompanied by other imperfections such as reduced contrast, ghosting, or chromatic aberration. Rather than trying to eliminate these imperfections entirely, consider embracing them as part of the overall artistic effect. Sometimes, these flaws can add character and enhance the dreamy atmosphere of your photographs.

7. Control the Flare
While lens flare can add beauty to your images, excessive flare can also overpower the scene and distract from your subject. To control the flare, use a lens hood to block direct light from entering the lens. Additionally, you can try positioning your hand or another object near the lens to create shadows and shape the flare. By experimenting with these techniques, you can exert more control over the flare and achieve the desired effect.

8. Post-Processing Enhancements
In addition to capturing lens flare in-camera, you can also enhance the effect during post-processing. Some photo editing software allows you to add artificial lens flare or adjust the intensity, color, and position of existing flare. However, it’s important to use these enhancements sparingly and thoughtfully to ensure your images maintain a natural and authentic feel.

In conclusion, lens flare can be a powerful tool for creating dreamy and atmospheric photographs. By understanding the nature of lens flare, finding the right light source, using a narrow aperture, and experimenting with composition and different lenses, you can harness its potential to enhance your images. Remember to embrace imperfections, control the flare when necessary, and use post-processing enhancements judiciously. Incorporating lens flare into your photography repertoire can add a touch of magic to your images and transport viewers into a dreamlike world. Happy shooting!
