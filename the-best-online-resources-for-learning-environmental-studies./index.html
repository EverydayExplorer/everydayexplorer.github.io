The Best Online Resources for Learning Environmental Studies.


Are you passionate about the environment and want to learn more about it? Are you interested in pursuing a career in environmental studies? Look no further! In this blog post, we will explore the best online resources for learning environmental studies. Whether you are a student, a professional, or simply an individual who wants to expand their knowledge on environmental issues, these resources will provide you with valuable information and insights.

1. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) Website:
The EPA website is a treasure trove of information on environmental studies. It offers a wide range of resources, including research papers, reports, and educational materials. The website covers various topics such as air quality, water pollution, climate change, and sustainability. The EPA also provides interactive tools and databases that allow users to explore environmental data and trends.

2. National Geographic:
National Geographic is renowned for its stunning photography and in-depth articles on environmental issues. Their website features a dedicated section on the environment, where you can find articles, videos, and interactive maps. National Geographic also offers online courses and webinars on environmental topics, providing a comprehensive learning experience.

3. Coursera:
Coursera is a popular online learning platform that offers a wide range of courses, including environmental studies. You can find courses from top universities and institutions around the world, taught by experts in the field. These courses cover various aspects of environmental studies, such as conservation, ecology, and sustainable development. Coursera also provides certificates upon completion, which can be a valuable addition to your resume.

4. TED Talks:
TED Talks are known for their inspiring and thought-provoking presentations. Many renowned environmentalists and scientists have given TED Talks on topics related to the environment. These talks provide a unique perspective and can help you gain a deeper understanding of environmental issues. The TED website allows you to search for talks based on specific keywords, making it easy to find talks on environmental studies.

5. Yale Environment 360:
Yale Environment 360 is an online magazine that covers a wide range of environmental topics. It features articles, interviews, and opinion pieces from leading experts in the field. The magazine covers issues such as biodiversity loss, renewable energy, and environmental policy. Yale Environment 360 also offers a podcast series, where you can listen to in-depth discussions on environmental issues.

6. United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP):
The UNEP website provides a wealth of information on global environmental issues. It offers reports, publications, and educational resources on topics such as climate change, biodiversity, and sustainable development. The UNEP also hosts webinars and online events, allowing you to engage with experts and learn from their experiences.

7. Environmental Studies Online:
Environmental Studies Online is a comprehensive resource for anyone interested in environmental studies. It offers a curated collection of online courses, textbooks, and research papers. The website also features a blog with articles on various environmental topics. Environmental Studies Online aims to provide accessible and up-to-date information to help individuals deepen their understanding of environmental issues.

8. Khan Academy:
Khan Academy is a non-profit organization that offers free online courses on a wide range of subjects, including environmental studies. Their courses are designed to be accessible to learners of all ages and backgrounds. Khan Academy provides video lessons, practice exercises, and quizzes to help you grasp key concepts in environmental studies.

9. Environmental Science.org:
Environmental Science.org is a website dedicated to providing information and resources on environmental science and studies. It offers articles, career guides, and a directory of environmental science programs. The website also features interviews with professionals in the field, giving you insights into potential career paths in environmental studies.

10. YouTube Channels:
YouTube is not just for entertainment; it is also a valuable resource for learning. Many environmental organizations and experts have YouTube channels where they share educational videos on environmental topics. Some popular channels include “SciShow” and “CrashCourse,” which cover a wide range of scientific subjects, including environmental studies.
