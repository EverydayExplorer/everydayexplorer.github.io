Travel through Pages: Best Books Set in Exotic Locations


Traveling to exotic locations is a dream for many, but sometimes, circumstances prevent us from embarking on these adventures. However, with the help of books, we can transport ourselves to far-off lands and experience the magic of these exotic locations from the comfort of our own homes. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best books set in exotic locations that will take you on a literary journey like no other. So, grab your passport and let’s dive into the world of travel through pages!

1. ‘The Alchemist’ by Paulo Coelho - Andalusia, Spain:

Set in the enchanting region of Andalusia, Spain, ‘The Alchemist’ tells the story of Santiago, a young shepherd who embarks on a journey to find his personal legend. With vivid descriptions of Andalusian landscapes, readers will be transported to the vibrant markets of Tangier, the mystical deserts of Egypt, and the majestic pyramids of Giza. Coelho’s beautiful prose captures the essence of this exotic location, making it a must-read for any travel enthusiast.

2. ‘The Beach’ by Alex Garland - Thailand:

For those yearning for the tropical paradise of Thailand, ‘The Beach’ is the perfect escape. Set on a secluded island, this gripping novel follows the adventures of Richard, a young traveler in search of the ultimate beach paradise. As Richard explores the hidden coves, turquoise waters, and dense jungles of Thailand, readers will be immersed in the mystique and allure of this exotic destination.

3. ‘The Kite Runner’ by Khaled Hosseini - Afghanistan:

Afghanistan may not be the first place that comes to mind when dreaming of exotic locations, but ‘The Kite Runner’ offers a unique glimpse into this war-torn yet captivating country. Through the eyes of Amir, the protagonist, readers will witness the beauty of Kabul’s ancient streets, the rugged landscapes of the Afghan countryside, and the cultural richness that lies within. Hosseini’s poignant storytelling will transport you to a world that is both foreign and familiar.

4. ‘Shantaram’ by Gregory David Roberts - India:

India, with its vibrant colors, bustling streets, and rich cultural heritage, has always been a popular destination for travelers. ‘Shantaram’ takes readers on an unforgettable journey through the chaotic streets of Mumbai, the serene beaches of Goa, and the spiritual depths of Varanasi. Roberts’ vivid descriptions and intimate portrayal of India’s diverse landscape will leave you yearning to explore this exotic country firsthand.

5. ‘The God of Small Things’ by Arundhati Roy - Kerala, India:

Another gem set in India, ‘The God of Small Things’ paints a vivid picture of the lush backwaters and enchanting landscapes of Kerala. Roy’s lyrical prose captures the essence of this southern Indian state, known for its serene houseboat cruises, verdant tea plantations, and tranquil beaches. Through the lives of the characters, readers will be transported to the heart of Kerala, immersing themselves in its beauty and cultural nuances.

Conclusion:

Books have the power to transport us to far-off lands, allowing us to experience the wonders of exotic locations without leaving our homes. Whether it’s the sun-soaked beaches of Thailand, the mystical deserts of Egypt, or the vibrant streets of Mumbai, these books will take you on a literary journey like no other. So, if you’re craving an adventure but can’t hop on a plane, grab one of these books and let your imagination take flight. Happy reading and bon voyage!
