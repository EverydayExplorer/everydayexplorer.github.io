Bookish Travel: Literary Destinations to Visit Around the World


Are you a book lover with a passion for travel? If so, then you’re in for a treat! There are countless literary destinations around the world that offer the perfect blend of culture, history, and literature. From the cobblestone streets of Dublin to the vibrant neighborhoods of New Orleans, the world is full of places that have inspired and been inspired by great works of literature. In this blog post, we will take you on a journey through some of the most captivating literary destinations around the globe.

Dublin, Ireland
Dublin is a city steeped in literary history and has been home to some of the most renowned literary figures, such as James Joyce, Oscar Wilde, and W.B. Yeats. Take a stroll through Trinity College and visit the Long Room of the Old Library, which houses the famous Book of Kells. Immerse yourself in the world of ‘Ulysses’ by following the footsteps of Leopold Bloom and exploring the city’s iconic landmarks mentioned in the novel.

New Orleans, Louisiana
New Orleans is a city known for its rich literary heritage and vibrant cultural scene. The French Quarter, with its charming architecture and lively atmosphere, has been the backdrop for many works of literature, including Anne Rice’s ‘Interview with the Vampire’ and Tennessee Williams’ ‘A Streetcar Named Desire.’ Explore the city’s literary history by visiting the Faulkner House Books, where William Faulkner wrote his first novel, and take a literary walking tour to discover the city’s literary landmarks.

Edinburgh, Scotland
Edinburgh, often referred to as the ‘City of Literature,’ is a haven for book lovers. Walk in the footsteps of J.K. Rowling and explore the magical world of Harry Potter by visiting The Elephant House cafe, where she penned the early chapters of the series. Delve into the city’s rich literary past by visiting the Writers’ Museum, which celebrates the lives and works of Scotland’s literary greats, including Robert Burns, Sir Walter Scott, and Robert Louis Stevenson.

Key West, Florida
Key West has long been a haven for writers seeking inspiration and solitude. Visit the Ernest Hemingway Home and Museum, where the Nobel Prize-winning author lived and wrote some of his most famous works. The island’s laid-back atmosphere and breathtaking sunsets have attracted literary legends such as Tennessee Williams and Shel Silverstein, making it a must-visit destination for bookish travelers.

Tokyo, Japan
Tokyo may not be the first place that comes to mind when thinking about literary destinations, but this bustling metropolis has a thriving literary scene waiting to be explored. Dive into the world of Japanese literature by visiting bookstores in Jimbocho, Tokyo’s renowned ‘Book Town,’ and immerse yourself in the works of Haruki Murakami and other influential Japanese authors. Take a stroll through Yanaka, a neighborhood known for its traditional charm and literary connections, and visit the grave of Lafcadio Hearn, an influential writer who introduced the world to Japanese folklore and ghost stories.

These are just a few examples of the many literary destinations waiting to be discovered around the world. Whether you’re a fan of classic literature, contemporary fiction, or poetry, there’s a literary destination out there that will capture your imagination and provide a deeper understanding of the books and authors you love. So pack your bags, grab a book, and embark on a literary journey that will inspire and enrich your love for both literature and travel.
