Holiday Crafts for Kids: Fun and Festive Projects for the Little Ones


It’s that time of year again - the holiday season is upon us! And what better way to get into the festive spirit than by engaging in some fun and creative holiday crafts with your kids? Not only will these projects keep them busy and entertained, but they will also help create lasting memories and decorations that you can cherish for years to come. So, grab your glue sticks, paint brushes, and glitter, and let’s dive into the world of holiday crafts for kids!

1. Handprint Wreath:

Create a beautiful wreath using your child’s handprints. Start by tracing and cutting out several handprints on green construction paper. Then, arrange and glue them in a circular shape to form a wreath. Add some colorful ribbon or bows for an extra festive touch. This craft is not only adorable but also a great way to document your child’s growth.

2. Santa’s Milk and Cookie Plate:

Get ready for Santa’s visit by decorating a special milk and cookie plate with your little ones. Use a plain white ceramic plate and let your child unleash their creativity by painting their own designs on it. Don’t forget to add a message for Santa too! Once the plate is dry, it will be ready to use on Christmas Eve when Santa comes to visit.

3. Sparkling Snow Globes:

Capture the magic of a winter wonderland with DIY snow globes. Collect various small figurines, glitter, and a clean glass jar with a tight-fitting lid. Glue the figurine to the inside of the jar lid and fill it with water. Add a sprinkle of glitter, screw the lid back on tightly, and give it a good shake. Your kids will love watching the snow flurry around their own personalized snow globe.

4. Reindeer Handprint Ornaments:

These adorable reindeer handprint ornaments are not only cute but also make for great keepsakes. Trace and cut out your child’s handprint on brown construction paper. Attach googly eyes, a red pompom for the nose, and pipe cleaners for the antlers. Punch a hole at the top of the ornament and thread a ribbon through it for hanging.

5. Paper Plate Christmas Tree:

Transform a simple paper plate into a festive Christmas tree. Cut out the center of the plate, leaving just the outer rim. Let your child decorate the rim with paint, markers, or even glued-on sequins. To complete the tree, cut out a smaller triangle shape from green construction paper and glue it to the back of the plate. Now your little one has their own mini Christmas tree!

6. Santa Hat Chair Covers:

Add a touch of holiday cheer to your dining room by making Santa hat chair covers. Start by sewing or using fabric glue to attach red and white felt together in the shape of Santa hats. Ensure there is an opening at the bottom to slip over the top of each chair. Your kids will love seeing their chairs transformed into Santa’s favorites!

7. Snowflake Window Clings:

If you live in an area without real snow, create your own winter wonderland using snowflake window clings. Cut out snowflake shapes from white craft foam or paper. Help your child attach double-sided tape or static cling window film to the back of the snowflakes. Now they can decorate the windows with their own unique snowflake designs.

8. Marshmallow Snowmen:

Get creative and edible with marshmallow snowmen. Using toothpicks, stack three marshmallows on top of each other to create the snowman’s body. Use mini chocolate chips for the eyes and buttons, a small carrot piece for the nose, and pretzel sticks for the arms. Your child can assemble and enjoy their tasty snowman creation.

9. Hanukkah Dreidel Spin Art:

Add a spin (literally!) to traditional Hanukkah festivities with dreidel spin art. Decorate a dreidel-shaped piece of paper with various colors and patterns. Place it on a spinning top and watch as the paint splatters and creates unique spin art. This is a fun and messy activity that kids of all ages will enjoy.

10. Candy Cane Reindeer:

Turn simple candy canes into reindeer buddies. Use pipe cleaners to create antlers and glue on googly eyes, a red pompom for the nose, and any other decorative touches you desire. These cute reindeer can be used as ornaments or given as gifts.

The holiday season is all about spending quality time with your loved ones. This year, create cherished memories by engaging in holiday crafts with your kids. From wreaths and ornaments to edible creations and window decorations, there are endless possibilities for fun and festive projects. So gather your supplies, get creative, and let the holiday crafting adventures begin!
