Survival Fishing Techniques: Catching Food in Rivers and Lakes


In a survival situation, being able to find and catch food is crucial. While there are various methods to obtain food, fishing in rivers and lakes can be an effective way to secure sustenance. This blog will explore different survival fishing techniques that can be employed to catch food in these water bodies. By mastering these techniques, you can increase your chances of survival by having a reliable food source.

1. Assessing the Water Source

Before embarking on your fishing expedition, it is essential to assess the water source carefully. Look for signs of fish activity, such as ripples on the water’s surface or jumping fish. Observing where birds, such as herons or kingfishers, are feeding can also provide valuable information. Additionally, take note of any potential hiding spots and areas with underwater vegetation, as these are often where fish tend to congregate.

2. Hand Fishing

Hand fishing, sometimes referred to as noodling, is a popular survival fishing technique used to catch fish in rivers and lakes. This technique primarily targets bottom-dwelling fish, such as catfish. To practice hand fishing, locate a fish’s hiding spot, which is often a rock crevice or a submerged log. Slowly insert your hand into the hiding spot and wiggle your fingers like bait. When the fish bites onto your hand, firmly grasp its mouth and lift it out of the water.

3. Using Natural Bait

Another effective technique for catching fish in rivers and lakes is using natural bait. This method involves finding and using local bait sources to attract fish. Common natural baits include worms, insects, small crustaceans, and even smaller fish. By presenting these baits on a hook, you can entice fish to bite. You can either cast the bait using a fishing rod or set up a simple handline by attaching the bait to a line and dropping it into the water.

4. Building Fish Traps

Fish traps are useful tools for catching fish passively. They can be constructed using various materials found in the surrounding environment, such as rocks, sticks, and vines. The basic idea is to create a channel that leads fish into a confined space, making it difficult for them to escape. By strategically placing the trap near areas with high fish activity, you can maximize your chances of catching fish without actively monitoring the trap.

5. Improvised Fishing Gear

In a survival situation, you may not have access to traditional fishing gear. However, you can improvise by creating simple fishing equipment using readily available materials. For example, you can fashion a fishing hook by straightening a paperclip or using a safety pin. Attach this makeshift hook to a piece of fishing line or even dental floss. To increase your chances of success, use natural bait as mentioned earlier.

Conclusion

When it comes to survival fishing in rivers and lakes, mastering various techniques can significantly increase your chances of catching food. Whether it is hand fishing, using natural bait, building fish traps, or improvising fishing gear, each technique has its advantages and unique situations where it is most effective. By being prepared and knowledgeable, you can ensure a steady and reliable source of food in a survival scenario. Remember, practice makes perfect, so it is essential to familiarize yourself with these techniques before finding yourself in an actual survival situation.
