The Best Animated Shorts That You Need to Watch


Animated shorts are a unique and captivating form of storytelling that often go underappreciated. These short films have the power to evoke a wide range of emotions, from laughter to tears, and can deliver profound messages in just a few minutes. If you’re a fan of animation, or even if you’re just looking for something new and interesting to watch, here are some of the best animated shorts that you need to add to your must-watch list.

1. “Paperman” (2012)

“Paperman” is a romantic black-and-white short film produced by Walt Disney Animation Studios. The story follows a young man who uses paper airplanes to get the attention of a woman he meets during his morning commute. The animation style is a beautiful blend of traditional hand-drawn and computer-generated imagery, creating a visually stunning and heartwarming experience. The film won the Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film in 2013 and is a perfect choice for anyone who appreciates a good love story.

2. “Piper” (2016)

“Piper” is a short film produced by Pixar Animation Studios that accompanied the theatrical release of “Finding Dory.” This visually stunning film follows the story of a young sandpiper learning to overcome her fear of the ocean. The animation is incredibly detailed and realistic, immersing the viewer in the world of the tiny bird and the challenges she faces. The film received critical acclaim for its breathtaking visuals and heartwarming story, making it a must-watch for animation enthusiasts of all ages.

3. “La Maison en Petits Cubes” (2009)

“La Maison en Petits Cubes,” or “The House of Small Cubes,” is a Japanese animated short film that won the Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film in 2009. The film tells the story of an old man who reminisces about his life as he adds more layers to his house to escape the rising floodwaters. The animation style is simple yet incredibly effective, and the emotional depth of the story is sure to leave a lasting impression on the viewer. This film is a testament to the power of animation to convey deep, meaningful narratives in a short amount of time.

4. “The Lost Thing” (2010)

“The Lost Thing” is a 2010 animated short film based on the picture book by Shaun Tan. The story follows a boy who discovers a bizarre creature and sets out on a journey to find its rightful home. The film’s unique visual style and thought-provoking narrative earned it the Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film in 2011. “The Lost Thing” is a perfect example of the way animation can transport viewers to imaginative worlds and encourage them to contemplate themes of belonging and acceptance.

5. “Feast” (2014)

“Feast” is a heartwarming animated short film produced by Walt Disney Animation Studios. The film follows the story of a Boston Terrier named Winston and his owner’s evolving love life, as seen through the eyes of the food Winston is fed. The animation is vibrant and charming, and the emotional journey of the characters is beautifully portrayed in just a few minutes. “Feast” won the Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film in 2015 and is a delightful choice for anyone looking for a feel-good story.

Conclusion

Animated shorts have the power to captivate audiences and deliver powerful messages in a short amount of time. Whether you’re a fan of animation or just looking for something new to watch, these short films offer a wide range of emotional experiences and thought-provoking narratives. From heartwarming love stories to profound reflections on life, the best animated shorts have something to offer to viewers of all ages. So, grab some popcorn, find a cozy spot, and immerse yourself in the captivating world of animated shorts. You won’t be disappointed!
