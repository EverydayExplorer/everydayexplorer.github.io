The Thrill of Varmint Hunting: Tips and Tricks


Varmint hunting is one of the most exhilarating activities a hunter can engage in. With its versatility and the thrill of successfully hitting a moving target, it’s no wonder why varmint hunting has become a popular pastime for many hunters worldwide. However, to be successful in varmint hunting, you need to know the right techniques and approach to increase your chances of landing a prey.

Here are some tips and tricks to help you get that prized varmint:

1. Research and scout the area beforehand

Before heading out, it’s essential to research the area to understand the type of varmints found there. You can speak to the locals, read hunting guides, or look at maps to identify the best spots to hunt. Once you have an idea of where the varmints are likely to be, scout the area beforehand to determine the best position to set up and ensure your safety.

2. Use the right equipment

Varmint hunting requires specialized equipment, including a good quality rifle, scope, and the right ammunition. It’s best to invest in a long-range rifle, preferably with a scope with clear magnification capabilities and zero retention. A bipod or tripod can also be useful to help stabilize your aim, especially for long-range shooting.

3. Be mindful of the wind

Wind direction can significantly affect your success in varmint hunting. Animals can quickly detect your presence from upwind and run away. That is why it’s best to position yourself downwind, where the smell will not give away your location, providing you with the advantage of surprise.

4. Use game calls

Varmints are smart animals that can detect danger with ease. Using game calls to mimic animal sounds and distress calls can lure them out of hiding. It’s best to use high-quality, electronic game calls that are durable, sound authentic, and can broadcast over long distances. You can also use decoys, such as a rabbit or a coyote to further entice the varmints out of hiding.

5. Be patient and wait for the right shot

Patience is key to successful varmint hunting. Wait for the animal to present a clear shot, preferably broadside to increase your chances of making a clean and lethal shot. It’s always better to take a shot when you’re confident of making a kill and not just a wound that can cause unnecessary harm and suffering.

In conclusion, varmint hunting takes time, skill, and lots of patience. However, with the right equipment, approach, and mindset, you can enjoy the thrill of the hunt. Remember to always prioritize safety, respect for nature, and moral hunting practices. With these tips and tricks for varmint hunting, you’re sure to have an enjoyable and successful hunting experience. Get started today and experience the thrill of varmint hunting for yourself!
