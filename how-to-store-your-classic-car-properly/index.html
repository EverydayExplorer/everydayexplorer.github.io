How to Store Your Classic Car Properly

If you’re a classic car enthusiast, you know that your prized possession requires special care and attention. Proper storage is essential to preserve its value and maintain its pristine condition. In this blog post, we will discuss some tips and guidelines on how to store your classic car properly.

1. Find a Suitable Storage Facility:
Choosing the right storage facility is crucial for the long-term preservation of your classic car. Look for a storage facility that offers climate control to protect your car from extreme temperature fluctuations. It should also have good ventilation to prevent moisture buildup and adequate security measures to protect against theft and vandalism.

2. Clean and Detail the Car:
Before storing your classic car, give it a thorough clean and detail. This includes washing the exterior, waxing it to create a protective barrier, and cleaning the interior. Use high-quality, non-abrasive cleaning products to avoid damaging the paint and upholstery. Additionally, consider applying a coat of paint protection film on vulnerable areas to prevent scratches and chips.

3. Change Fluids and Filters:
To prevent potential damage caused by old and dirty fluids, it’s essential to change the oil, coolant, transmission fluid, and brake fluid before storing your classic car. Also, replace the fuel filter and perform a fuel system flush to remove any contaminants that could cause problems down the line. This step will ensure that your car’s mechanical components remain in good condition even during long periods of inactivity.

4. Maintain the Battery:
Classic cars are notorious for having draining batteries if left unused for extended periods. To avoid this, disconnect the battery or adopt a trickle-charging system to keep it charged while in storage. This will prevent the battery from dying and preserve its lifespan.

5. Protect the Tires:
Flat spots can develop on tires that remain stationary for a long time. To prevent this, inflate your classic car’s tires to the recommended pressure and consider using tire cradles or jacks to take the weight off the tires. Alternatively, you can periodically move the car during storage, ensuring that the tires are not sitting in the same spot for an extended period.

6. Use a Car Cover:
Invest in a high-quality car cover that provides protection against dust, moisture, and UV rays. Choose a cover specifically designed for classic cars, as generic covers may not fit properly or can potentially scratch the paint. Ensure that the car is clean and dry before covering it to avoid trapping moisture underneath.

7. Regular Inspection:
Even though your classic car is stored, it’s essential to inspect it regularly. Check for any signs of damage, such as leaks, mold, or rodent infestation. This will allow you to identify and address any potential issues promptly.

8. Implement Pest Control:
Critters love to make themselves at home in classic cars. To keep them out, place mothballs or cotton balls soaked in peppermint oil inside the vehicle. Additionally, consider using traps or deterrents specifically designed for cars to keep rodents away.

9. Keep Documentation:
Maintain proper documentation of your classic car’s storage, including dates, services performed, and any issues noticed. This will not only help you stay organized but also will be valuable if you decide to sell your car in the future.

10. Drive It Occasionally:
While your classic car may spend most of its time in storage, it’s important to take it out for a spin occasionally. Plan short drives to keep the engine running, prevent fluid stagnation, and maintain proper lubrication. This will also give you the opportunity to enjoy your classic car on the road.

In conclusion, storing a classic car properly is essential to maintain its value and preserve its condition. By following these tips, you can ensure that your beloved car remains in top-notch shape for years to come. Remember that taking the time and effort to store your classic car correctly will be well worth it when you see it shine and perform flawlessly when you’re ready to hit the open road. Happy car storing!
