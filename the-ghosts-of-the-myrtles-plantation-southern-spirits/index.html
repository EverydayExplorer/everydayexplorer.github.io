The Ghosts of the Myrtles Plantation: Southern Spirits


Once dubbed ‘one of America’s most haunted houses,’ the Myrtles Plantation in St. Francisville, Louisiana, has a reputation for being home to numerous restless spirits. With a history dating back to 1796, this antebellum plantation has experienced tragedy and turmoil, leaving behind an eerie atmosphere that continues to captivate visitors from near and far. Join us on a journey through the ghostly tales and Southern spirits that haunt the Myrtles Plantation.

Legend has it that the plantation’s haunting began with the murder of Judge Clarke Woodruff and two of his children in 1817. The judge’s killer, a slave named Chloe, allegedly poisoned them with oleander flowers. Some believe that Chloe had hoped to gain favor with the Woodruff family, but her actions led to her own demise. It is said that Chloe was subsequently hanged by her fellow slaves, and her spirit has been seen wandering the grounds ever since.

Chloe’s ghost is not the only one said to roam the Myrtles Plantation. Visitors have reported encountering the spirit of a young girl named ‘Theodora’ who died from yellow fever, as well as a ghostly maid who has been seen on the property. The Myrtles Plantation has become a hotspot for paranormal enthusiasts seeking to connect with the otherworldly.

But why is the Myrtles Plantation so haunted? Some believe that the residual energy left behind by the plantation’s tumultuous past contributes to its supernatural activity. In addition to the murders, the plantation has witnessed countless tragedies, including the deaths of numerous slaves and the burdens of slave ownership itself. This dark legacy has left its mark on the Myrtles, making it a magnet for ghostly apparitions.

Visitors to the Myrtles Plantation can choose to stay overnight in one of the plantation’s luxurious suites, with the hope of experiencing their own paranormal encounter. Many guests have reported hearing unexplained noises, seeing shadowy figures, and feeling a chilling presence in their rooms. The Myrtles Plantation offers ghost tours where visitors can learn more about the haunted history of the property and get a firsthand taste of the supernatural.

The Myrtles Plantation has also attracted the attention of paranormal investigators over the years. Numerous TV shows and documentaries, such as ‘Ghost Hunters’ and ‘Paranormal State,’ have explored the plantation’s hauntings, adding to its legend and allure. The Myrtles has, without a doubt, become a focal point for those fascinated by the mysteries of the afterlife.

Whether you believe in ghosts or not, the Myrtles Plantation’s reputation as a haunted house cannot be denied. Its rich history and tragic events have undoubtedly left a supernatural imprint on the property. Visitors flock to the Myrtles in search of a spine-tingling experience or a chance to connect with the spirits of the past. Whether you’re a skeptic or a believer, the Myrtles Plantation offers a fascinating glimpse into Southern spirits and the enduring power of ghostly tales.
