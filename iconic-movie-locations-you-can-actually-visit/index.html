Iconic Movie Locations You Can Actually Visit


Lights, camera, action! If you’re a movie buff, there’s nothing quite like visiting iconic movie locations in person. Stepping foot where your favorite films were shot can be a thrilling and immersive experience, allowing you to relive your favorite moments and connect with the magic of cinema. In this blog post, we’ll take you on a virtual journey to some of the most iconic movie locations you can actually visit. Get ready to explore the world of film!

Let’s start our adventure in New York City, the backdrop of countless movies throughout the years. From the bustling streets of Times Square to the elegant Grand Central Terminal, this city has served as a stunning backdrop for many iconic films. Remember the famous ‘I’m walking here!’ scene from Midnight Cowboy? You can walk the same streets that Jon Voight and Dustin Hoffman did in this classic film.

Moving across the pond, we find ourselves in the enchanting city of Paris, France. This city has graced the silver screen in countless romantic movies, and for good reason. Who could forget the picturesque bridges and charming cafés from the film ‘Amélie’? Take a stroll along the Seine River and you’ll find yourself in the same locations where Audrey Tautou’s character embarked on her whimsical adventures.

Speaking of romantic destinations, let’s hop on a plane and head to Italy. Remember the charming village in ‘Under the Tuscan Sun’ that stole our hearts? Well, it’s a real place called Cortona, located in the stunning region of Tuscany. Immerse yourself in the breathtaking landscapes and wander through the streets that Diane Lane’s character fell in love with.

Now, let’s venture into the realm of fantasy and visit the iconic Hobbiton in Matamata, New Zealand. This magical movie set, created for ‘The Lord of the Rings’ and ‘The Hobbit’ trilogies, is an absolute must-visit for any fan of J.R.R. Tolkien’s work. Explore the lush green hills, visit the hobbit holes, and imagine yourself as part of the epic adventure that unfolded on-screen.

If you’re a fan of classic movies, the next location will surely make your heart skip a beat. The famous Bodega Bay from Alfred Hitchcock’s ‘The Birds’ is located in California. Feel the thrill as you visit the town where Tippi Hedren’s character battled the menacing avian creatures. Just be sure to keep an eye on the skies!

Last but not least, let’s dive into the magical world of Harry Potter. While Hogwarts may be an imaginary place, you can still visit the real-life locations that brought the wizarding world to life. Head to London and visit King’s Cross Station, where you can find Platform 9¾, the gateway to the Hogwarts Express. Take a stroll through the streets of Oxford and you’ll discover the iconic Bodleian Library, which served as Hogwarts’ library in the films.

These are just a few examples of the many iconic movie locations you can actually visit. From bustling cities to picturesque landscapes, there’s something for every film enthusiast. So grab your camera, pack your sense of adventure, and embark on a cinematic journey like no other. Who knows, you might even stumble upon a film shoot or catch a glimpse of your favorite actors in action. Lights, camera, let the adventure begin!
