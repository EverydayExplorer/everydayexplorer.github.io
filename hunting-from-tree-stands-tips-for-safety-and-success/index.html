Hunting from Tree Stands: Tips for Safety and Success


Hunting from tree stands has become increasingly popular among hunters, especially those who prefer bow hunting. Tree stands can provide many advantages to hunters such as better visibility, improved angles, increased scent control, and enhanced camouflage. However, it’s crucial to note that tree stands can also be dangerous if not used safely. In this blog, we will discuss some essential tips for hunting from tree stands to ensure both safety and success.

1. Know the Tree Stand Laws and Regulations

One of the most crucial aspects of hunting from a tree stand is ensuring that you are following all the state and local laws and regulations. Every state has different laws for hunters using tree stands, so it’s best to research the regulations specific to your location. Violating these laws can result in hefty fines, loss of hunting privileges, and potentially even legal consequences.

2. Inspect Your Tree Stand Regularly

Before stepping into a tree stand, ensure that you thoroughly inspect all parts of it. This includes the stand’s straps, seat, bolts, and cables. If any of these parts are rusted, corroded, or worn, do not risk your safety by using it. It’s essential to put up a new stand or fix the old one before your hunt.

3. Use a Full Body Safety Harness

One of the most critical safety features you can use while hunting from a tree stand is a full-body safety harness. It’s crucial to understand that falling from a tree stand can result in serious, even fatal injuries. An excellent quality safety harness can protect you from a fall, and some harnesses even have suspension relief systems that help reduce the impact of a fall.

4. Choose the Right Tree

When selecting the perfect tree to set up your stand, there are a few essential factors to consider. A sturdy tree that is at least 12 inches in diameter and has no signs of rot or damage is necessary. Make sure the tree you choose is straight and free from large, low-hanging branches. The last thing you want to do is draw attention to yourself or make it difficult to get into your stand.

5. Stay Focused and Patient

When hunting from a tree stand, patience is vital, and staying focused is equally important. You will likely be sitting in your stand for hours, waiting for the right opportunity to present itself. It might be challenging to stay still, quiet and remain alert for an extended period, but it’s essential to remain focused and patient.

6. Wear the Right Clothing

Wearing the right clothing is crucial when hunting from a tree stand. Camouflage clothing is essential to blend in with your surroundings, but it’s also critical that you dress appropriately for the weather conditions. Layering your clothing can help keep you warm on cold days, and it allows you to remove layers as the temperature rises. Wearing insulated boots and gloves is also necessary to keep your hands and feet warm and dry.

7. Choose the Right Time to Hunt

The best time to hunt from a tree stand is typically early in the morning and late in the afternoon. These are the times of day when animals are typically most active, and the light conditions make it easier to spot them. It’s also essential to consider the wind conditions and make sure you are hunting with the wind in your favor.

Conclusion

Tree stands can provide hunters with many advantages, but it’s crucial to remember that safety should always come first. By following these tips, you’ll not only improve your chances of a successful hunt but also increase your safety while hunting from a tree stand. Remember, a successful hunt is a great achievement, but it’s essential to return home safely to your loved ones.
