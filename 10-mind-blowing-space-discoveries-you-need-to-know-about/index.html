10 Mind-Blowing Space Discoveries You Need to Know About


The universe is an infinite expanse that still holds so much mystery and wonder that we’ve yet to uncover. The universe continuously astounds us, and we are often blown away by the discoveries made in space. These fascinating space discoveries are not only groundbreaking but also are a reminder of how little we truly know about the universe. Here are ten mind-blowing space discoveries that you need to know about.

1. Gravitational Waves
Gravitational waves are ripples in space-time caused by the acceleration of massive objects. The first detection of gravitational waves occurred in 2015, when scientists observed the collision of two black holes. This breakthrough not only confirmed Albert Einstein’s theory of general relativity but also opened up a new way of studying the cosmos.

2. Trappist-1 System
In 2017, scientists discovered seven Earth-sized planets orbiting a red dwarf star called Trappist-1, just 39 light-years away from us. These planets are in the habitable zone, where conditions may be suitable for life as we know it. The discovery brings us closer to finding habitable worlds beyond our solar system.

3. Water on Mars
Scientists have long suspected that water exists on Mars, but it wasn’t until 2015 that NASA announced definitive evidence of liquid water on the planet. NASA’s Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter found dark streaks on the planet’s surface, which indicated that liquid water was flowing on Mars.

4. Dark Matter
Dark matter is a mysterious form of matter that can’t be seen or detected, but scientists believe that it makes up about 27% of the universe. Through studying the movements of galaxies, researchers have gathered evidence to support the existence of dark matter, even if we can’t see it.

5. Hubble Deep Field
The Hubble Deep Field is one of the most iconic images in the history of astronomy. In 1995, astronomers pointed the Hubble Space Telescope at a seemingly empty patch of space and captured an image that revealed thousands of galaxies. This discovery revealed that the universe is far more vast and complex than we’d ever imagined.

6. Black Holes
Black holes are objects so massive and dense that their gravity makes it impossible for even light to escape. Even as a result of purely gravitational influences, black holes have been discovered lurking in our galaxy and beyond; however, there’s still plenty for us to learn.

7. Exoplanets
Exoplanets are planets outside our solar system. In just the past couple of decades, astronomers have confirmed the existence of thousands of exoplanets and are continuing to discover more. These planets come in all shapes and sizes, and even more fascinatingly, some may be habitable.

8. The Age of the Universe
In 2013, researchers determined the age of the universe to be 13.8 billion years old. This number was calculated by measuring the cosmic microwave background radiation, the leftover heat from the Big Bang, and studying its patterns.

9. Proxima Centauri b
In 2016, astronomers found a planet orbiting Proxima Centauri, the closest star to our sun. Proxima Centauri b is an Earth-sized planet that lies in the habitable zone of its star, raising hopes that it may be capable of supporting life.

10. Voyager 1 and 2
The Voyager missions, launched in 1977, are the most extended-running space mission to date. Voyager 1 and 2 have reached the edge of our solar system and have been beaming back data to Earth since their launch.

These ten discoveries and many others remind us of how little we know about our universe. They show that the universe is an ever-evolving mystery ripe for exploration and discovery. Our fascination with space and what lies beyond is unlikely to diminish anytime soon, as we continue to uncover new and incredible things about the universe we inhabit.
