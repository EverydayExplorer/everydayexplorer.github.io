Survival Water Storage: Options for Emergency Preparedness


Water is one of the most crucial resources for survival, yet it’s often taken for granted until it’s in short supply. In emergency situations, access to clean drinking water may be compromised, making it essential to have a reliable water storage plan in place. Whether you’re preparing for natural disasters, power outages, or other emergencies, having an adequate supply of stored water can make all the difference. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore various options for survival water storage to help you prepare for the unexpected.

Importance of Water Storage

Before delving into the different water storage options, let’s first understand why it’s so critical to have a plan for storing water in emergency situations:

  1. Hydration: The human body can survive for only a few days without water. Adequate hydration is essential for maintaining bodily functions and overall health, especially during stressful situations.

  2. Cooking and Hygiene: In addition to drinking, water is necessary for cooking food, washing dishes, and maintaining personal hygiene. Having enough stored water ensures you can meet these basic needs even if your regular water supply is disrupted.

  3. Medical and First Aid: Clean water is vital for cleaning wounds, diluting medications, and practicing proper sanitation, all of which are crucial for preventing infections and promoting healing during emergencies.

Water Storage Options

Now that we understand why water storage is essential, let’s explore some of the most common options for storing water for emergency preparedness:

  1. Water Containers: One of the simplest and most versatile options for storing water is using dedicated water containers. These can range from large storage tanks and barrels to smaller jugs and bottles. Choose containers made from food-grade materials, such as BPA-free plastic or stainless steel, to ensure water safety. Make sure to rotate water periodically to prevent stagnation and contamination.

  2. Water Barrels: For long-term water storage, consider investing in water barrels specifically designed for emergency preparedness. These barrels are typically made from durable materials and come in various sizes, ranging from 15 to 55 gallons. Place them in a cool, dark area away from direct sunlight to prevent algae growth and degradation of the barrel material.

  3. WaterBOB: The WaterBOB is a unique emergency water storage solution that turns your bathtub into a temporary water reservoir. Simply lay the WaterBOB liner in your bathtub, fill it with water from the tap, and secure the top with the included zip tie. In emergency situations, you’ll have access to up to 100 gallons of clean water stored in your bathtub.

  4. Water Filtration and Purification: In addition to storing water, it’s also essential to have means of purifying water from alternative sources, such as rivers, lakes, and rainwater. Portable water filters and purification tablets can remove contaminants and make water safe for drinking. However, it’s still recommended to store a sufficient supply of clean water whenever possible.

  5. Rainwater Harvesting: Another sustainable option for supplementing your water supply is rainwater harvesting. Install rain barrels or cisterns to collect rainwater from your roof, which can then be filtered and treated for drinking and other household uses. Check local regulations and guidelines before setting up a rainwater harvesting system.

Tips for Water Storage

Regardless of the water storage option you choose, here are some additional tips to ensure the safety and effectiveness of your emergency water supply:

  • Regular Maintenance: Inspect water storage containers regularly for signs of damage or contamination. Clean and sanitize containers before refilling them with fresh water.
  • Labeling and Rotation: Label water containers with the date of storage and rotation schedule to ensure you’re using the oldest water first. Rotate stored water every six months to maintain freshness.
  • Emergency Plan: Develop a comprehensive emergency plan that includes provisions for water storage, purification, and conservation. Educate family members on the importance of water safety and proper storage practices.

Conclusion

Survival water storage is a critical aspect of emergency preparedness, ensuring you have access to clean drinking water when regular water sources are unavailable. By exploring different water storage options and implementing a comprehensive water storage plan, you can enhance your resilience and readiness to face unexpected challenges. Remember to prioritize water safety, regular maintenance, and proper rotation to ensure the effectiveness of your emergency water supply. With careful planning and preparation, you can rest assured knowing you’re equipped to handle whatever comes your way.
