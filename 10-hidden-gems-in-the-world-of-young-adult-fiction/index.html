10 Hidden Gems in the World of Young Adult Fiction


When it comes to the world of young adult fiction, there are so many incredible books to choose from. From dystopian fantasies to contemporary romances, the genre is filled with captivating stories that have touched the hearts and minds of readers around the world. While everyone is familiar with popular young adult novels like ‘The Hunger Games’ and ‘Harry Potter,’ there are some hidden gems that often go unnoticed. In this blog post, we will uncover 10 hidden gems in the world of young adult fiction that deserve more recognition.

1. ‘The Scorpio Races’ by Maggie Stiefvater
Set on the remote island of Thisby, this book tells the story of a dangerous annual race where riders attempt to tame water horses. With its lush prose and powerful storytelling, ‘The Scorpio Races’ grips readers from start to finish. Combining elements of mythology and adventure, Stiefvater creates a world full of exhilarating risk and heart-stopping moments.

2. ‘Aristotle and Dante Discover the Secrets of the Universe’ by Benjamin Alire Sáenz
This beautiful coming-of-age story explores the friendship between two Mexican-American teenage boys, Aristotle and Dante. Through their shared experiences, readers witness the complexities of identity, self-discovery, and love. Sáenz’s lyrical writing style and authentic character development make this book a must-read for fans of contemporary young adult fiction.

3. ‘Bone Gap’ by Laura Ruby
‘Bone Gap’ is a magical realism novel that follows the story of Finn O’Sullivan, who witnesses the kidnapping of his girlfriend, Roza. Set in a small town in Illinois, this captivating book delves into themes of perception, love, and loss. With its stunning prose and intricate plot, Ruby weaves together a tale that will keep readers guessing until the very end.

4. ‘The Love That Split the World’ by Emily Henry
In this enchanting debut novel, Natalie Cleary experiences strange occurrences that lead her to uncover secrets about her town and her own identity. With elements of time travel and romance, ‘The Love That Split the World’ explores themes of self-discovery and the power of love. Henry’s writing is immersive and evocative, making this book a hidden gem in the young adult fiction genre.

5. ‘A Great and Terrible Beauty’ by Libba Bray
This historical fantasy novel introduces readers to Gemma Doyle, a young girl sent to a British boarding school in the late 19th century. As Gemma discovers her magical powers and unravels the mysteries surrounding her heritage, readers are taken on a thrilling journey filled with romance, mystery, and the battle between light and darkness. Bray’s vivid prose and well-developed characters make this book an underrated gem in the young adult fantasy genre.

6. ‘The Diviners’ by Libba Bray
Another hidden gem by Libba Bray, ‘The Diviners’ is a dark and atmospheric novel set in 1920s New York City. With a diverse cast of complex characters and a gripping plot that intertwines supernatural elements with a murder mystery, this book is a captivating read. Bray’s meticulous attention to historical detail and her ability to create a sense of suspense make ‘The Diviners’ a standout in the genre.

7. ‘I’ll Give You the Sun’ by Jandy Nelson
‘I’ll Give You the Sun’ explores the complicated relationship between fraternal twins, Noah and Jude. Nelson’s book captivates readers with its rich and poetic language, exploring themes of family, grief, and self-discovery. The alternating perspectives and timelines add depth and complexity to the story, making it a must-read for fans of young adult contemporary fiction.

8. ‘The Strange and Beautiful Sorrows of Ava Lavender’ by Leslye Walton
Told from the perspective of Ava Lavender, a girl born with wings, this whimsical and magical tale explores themes of identity, love, and the pain of being different. Walton’s lyrical writing style and memorable characters breathe life into this enchanting story, capturing the hearts of readers seeking a unique and captivating read.

9. ‘The Weight of Feathers’ by Anna-Marie McLemore
‘The Weight of Feathers’ tells the story of two rival families, one of traveling performers and the other of mermaid descendants. Written in beautiful and atmospheric prose, McLemore explores themes of love, nature, and the complexities of family. This hidden gem blends magical realism with a touching romance, leaving readers enchanted from beginning to end.

10. ‘The Astonishing Color of After’ by Emily X.R. Pan
Combining magical realism with a poignant exploration of grief and identity, ‘The Astonishing Color of After’ tells the story of teenager Leigh Chen Sanders, who believes that her mother has transformed into a bird after her suicide. Pan’s lyrical writing and sensitive portrayal of mental health make this book a hidden gem that deserves more recognition in the young adult fiction world.

In conclusion, the world of young adult fiction is filled with hidden gems that often go unnoticed. From magical realism to historical fantasy, these 10 books offer unique and captivating stories that delve deep into the human experience. Whether you’re a fan of epic adventures or heartfelt coming-of-age tales, these hidden gems are sure to satisfy your reading appetite. So, the next time you’re browsing for a new young adult fiction read, don’t forget to seek out these overlooked treasures.
