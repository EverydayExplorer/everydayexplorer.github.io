Photography Podcasts: Learning from Experts in the Field


In today’s digital age, podcasts have emerged as a valuable resource for photographers looking to expand their knowledge, gain inspiration, and connect with like-minded individuals. Whether you’re a seasoned professional or just starting out on your photography journey, there’s a wealth of information waiting to be discovered in the world of photography podcasts. From technical tips and gear reviews to interviews with industry experts and discussions on artistic vision, these podcasts offer something for every photographer. In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the top photography podcasts that are worth tuning into.

1. The Candid Frame

Hosted by photographer and educator Ibarionex Perello, The Candid Frame is a long-running podcast that features insightful interviews with photographers from around the world. Each episode delves into the creative process, exploring the stories behind the images and offering valuable lessons on technique, storytelling, and the business of photography. With guests ranging from established professionals to up-and-coming talents, The Candid Frame provides a diverse and inspiring look at the world of photography.

2. The Art of Photography

Hosted by renowned photographer and educator Ted Forbes, The Art of Photography is a podcast dedicated to all things photographic. From discussions on the history of photography to practical tips on composition and lighting, each episode offers valuable insights into the art and craft of making images. Ted’s passion for photography shines through in every episode, making The Art of Photography a must-listen for anyone looking to deepen their understanding of the medium.

3. Master Photography Podcast

Hosted by a team of experienced photographers, including Jeff Harmon, Brent Bergherm, and Connor Hibbs, the Master Photography Podcast covers a wide range of topics related to photography. From gear reviews and post-processing techniques to interviews with industry professionals, each episode is packed with actionable advice and practical tips for photographers of all skill levels. Whether you’re a beginner looking to master the basics or a seasoned pro seeking to stay up-to-date on the latest trends, the Master Photography Podcast has something for everyone.

4. The Grid

Hosted by Scott Kelby and Erik Kuna, The Grid is a live talk show dedicated to all things photography and design. Each week, Scott and Erik are joined by special guests to discuss a variety of topics, ranging from photography techniques and gear reviews to industry news and trends. With its interactive format and engaging hosts, The Grid offers a fun and informative look at the world of photography, making it a favorite among photographers worldwide.

5. The Photo Banter

Hosted by photographer Alex Gagne, The Photo Banter is a podcast that features in-depth interviews with photographers, creatives, and industry professionals. Each episode provides a behind-the-scenes look at the lives and careers of the guests, offering valuable insights into their creative process, influences, and experiences. From fine art photographers to commercial shooters, The Photo Banter covers a wide range of genres and styles, making it a must-listen for anyone passionate about photography.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to improve your technical skills, gain inspiration, or connect with fellow photographers, podcasts offer a convenient and accessible way to learn from experts in the field. With their wide range of topics and diverse range of guests, the photography podcasts mentioned above are sure to provide valuable insights and inspiration for photographers of all levels. So grab your headphones, tune in, and start learning from the best in the business.
