The Best Gear for Ski Touring: A Comprehensive Guide


Winter is a magical time of year, especially for outdoor enthusiasts. When the snow-covered mountains beckon, there’s nothing quite like the thrill of ski touring. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or a beginner, having the right gear can make a world of difference in your experience on the slopes. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best gear for ski touring, ensuring that you’re well-prepared for your next adventure.

1. Skis: The foundation of any ski touring setup starts with the right skis. Look for lightweight, backcountry-specific skis that offer a good balance between uphill efficiency and downhill performance. Consider factors such as width, length, and rocker profiles to suit your skiing style and the terrain you’ll be tackling.

2. Bindings: Ski touring bindings are designed to provide efficient uphill climbs while maintaining the ability to ski downhill with confidence. Look for lightweight bindings with a touring mode that allows for free heel movement and a secure locking mechanism for descents. Consider tech bindings for weight savings or frame bindings for added downhill performance.

3. Boots: Ski touring boots should strike a balance between uphill mobility and downhill performance. Look for boots with a walk mode that offers a wide range of motion for climbing. Additionally, consider boots with a sturdy construction, good insulation, and customizable fit for maximum comfort and control.

4. Skins: Skins are essential for ski touring as they provide the traction needed for uphill climbs. Choose skins that are tailored to the dimensions of your skis and feature efficient glide and grip characteristics. Look for skins with durable materials and easy-to-use attachment systems for hassle-free transitions.

5. Poles: Ski touring poles should be lightweight, adjustable, and durable. Look for poles with comfortable grips, adjustable lengths, and powder baskets for added stability in deep snow. Consider carbon fiber or aluminum constructions for optimal weight savings without compromising on strength.

6. Avalanche Safety Gear: Safety should always be a top priority when venturing into the backcountry. Invest in an avalanche safety kit, which typically includes a beacon, shovel, and probe. Before heading out, ensure you have the necessary knowledge and training to use these tools effectively.

7. Backpack: A well-designed backpack is crucial for carrying all your essential gear. Look for a pack with dedicated compartments for avalanche safety gear, hydration compatibility, and backcountry-specific features like ski and helmet carry systems. Opt for a pack with a comfortable suspension system for long days on the mountain.

8. Clothing: Dressing in layers is key to staying comfortable during ski touring. Choose moisture-wicking base layers, insulating mid-layers, and a waterproof and breathable outer shell. Don’t forget accessories like gloves, goggles, and a helmet for added protection.

9. Navigation and Communication: A GPS device or a smartphone app with offline maps can be invaluable for navigation in unfamiliar terrain. Additionally, consider carrying a two-way radio or satellite communication device for emergencies or group communication.

10. Training and Education: Lastly, investing in avalanche safety courses and backcountry skiing training is essential for responsible ski touring. These courses will provide you with the knowledge and skills needed to make informed decisions in the backcountry.

Remember, gear is just one aspect of ski touring. Always prioritize your safety, know your limits, and be prepared for changing weather and snow conditions. With the right gear and knowledge, ski touring can be an unforgettable and rewarding experience. So, get out there, explore the untouched slopes, and embrace the freedom of ski touring. Happy skiing!
