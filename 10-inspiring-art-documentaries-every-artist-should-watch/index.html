10 Inspiring Art Documentaries Every Artist Should Watch


Art documentaries are a fantastic way for artists to gain inspiration and insight into the creative process. Whether you’re a professional artist or a beginner, these documentaries can provide valuable perspectives and insights into the art world. In this blog post, we will explore 10 inspiring art documentaries that every artist should watch. So grab your popcorn and get ready for a visual feast of creativity!

1. ‘Exit Through the Gift Shop’ (2010):

This cult-classic documentary directed by the elusive street artist, Banksy, explores the world of street art and its relation to the commercialization of art. The film takes the viewer on a rollercoaster ride through the life of an aspiring artist turned art dealer, blurring the lines between reality and artifice.

2. ‘Waste Land’ (2010):

‘Waste Land’ follows Brazilian artist Vik Muniz as he collaborates with garbage pickers from the Jardim Gramacho landfill in Rio de Janeiro to create artwork from recycled materials. This powerful documentary showcases the transformative power of art and its ability to bring hope and dignity to marginalized communities.

3. ‘Rivers and Tides’ (2001):

Directed by Thomas Riedelsheimer, ‘Rivers and Tides’ is a visually captivating documentary that delves into the creative process of Scottish artist Andy Goldsworthy. Through mesmerizing installations made from natural materials, Goldsworthy explores the ephemeral nature of art and its connection to the natural world.

4. ‘Cutie and the Boxer’ (2013):

Directed by Zachary Heinzerling, ‘Cutie and the Boxer’ chronicles the tumultuous relationship between Japanese artist Ushio Shinohara and his wife, Noriko. This intimate portrayal of their lives explores the sacrifices artists make in the pursuit of their passions and the complexities of love and art.

5. ‘Leaning Into the Wind’ (2017):

A follow-up to ‘Rivers and Tides,’ this documentary by Thomas Riedelsheimer provides further insight into Andy Goldsworthy’s creative process. With stunning visuals and poetic narration, the film takes viewers on a journey through Goldsworthy’s site-specific installations in various locations around the world.

6. ‘Herb and Dorothy’ (2008):

This heartwarming documentary directed by Megumi Sasaki tells the extraordinary story of Herb and Dorothy Vogel, a New York City postal worker and librarian, respectively, who amassed an impressive art collection on their modest salaries. Their passion for art and unwavering support for emerging artists will inspire any creative soul.

7. ‘The Salt of the Earth’ (2014):

Directed by Wim Wenders and Juliano Ribeiro Salgado, ‘The Salt of the Earth’ showcases the breathtaking photography of Sebastião Salgado. The film explores the intersection of art, social activism, and the environment, highlighting the power of visual storytelling to raise awareness and inspire change.

8. ‘Cave of Forgotten Dreams’ (2010):

In this mesmerizing documentary directed by Werner Herzog, viewers are taken on a journey into the Chauvet-Pont-d’Arc Cave in France, home to some of the oldest cave paintings in the world. Herzog’s exploration of this hidden treasure gives artists a unique glimpse into the origins of artistic expression.

9. ‘Marina Abramović: The Artist Is Present’ (2012):

Directed by Matthew Akers, this documentary offers an intimate look into the provocative performance art of Marina Abramović. Through her groundbreaking work, Abramović challenges the boundaries of art and the relationship between artist and audience.

10. ‘Jiro Dreams of Sushi’ (2011):

While not strictly focused on visual art, this critically acclaimed documentary directed by David Gelb tells the story of Jiro Ono, an 85-year-old sushi master. Jiro’s relentless pursuit of perfection in his craft serves as a powerful reminder of the dedication and discipline required to excel in any artistic endeavor.

Conclusion:

These 10 inspiring art documentaries offer a diverse range of artistic perspectives, shedding light on the lives of artists and the power of creativity. Whether you’re seeking inspiration, looking for new artistic techniques, or simply want to immerse yourself in the world of art, these documentaries are a must-watch for any artist. So, grab your sketchbook, sit back, and let these films ignite your artistic passion!
