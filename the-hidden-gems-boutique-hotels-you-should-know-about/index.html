The Hidden Gems: Boutique Hotels You Should Know About


Boutique hotels have been gaining popularity over the years, as travelers seek out unique and personalized experiences. These hotels are characterized by their smaller size, attention to detail, and distinctive design elements. They are often independently owned and provide a more intimate and personalized stay than larger chain hotels. In this blog post, we will be highlighting some hidden gems in the world of boutique hotels that you should know about.

First up, we have The Merchant House in Bahrain. This hotel may be small in size with only twelve suites, but it certainly makes a big impact. The building dates back to the 1800s and was once a family residence. It has since been restored and transformed into a stunning boutique hotel that seamlessly blends old-world charm with modern luxury. The rooftop terrace is a particular highlight, offering breathtaking views of Manama’s skyline.

Next, we jet off to South Africa to discover Cape View Clifton. This hotel is situated on Cape Town’s trendy Atlantic Seaboard and offers panoramic views of the ocean and surrounding mountains. The hotel’s rooms are spread across three villas and are each uniquely decorated with a mix of contemporary and traditional African art. The hotel also features a stunning infinity pool, perfect for soaking up the sun while taking in the views.

Our third hidden gem is found in the heart of Paris. Hotel des Grands Boulevards is a beautifully designed hotel that exudes Parisian chic. The hotel’s rooms are spacious and feature elegant decor that pays homage to the building’s history as a 1700s private mansion. The hotel also features a stunning rooftop bar and restaurant, perfect for enjoying a glass of wine while taking in views of the city’s stunning skyline.

Nestled in the heart of Seville, Spain lies our fourth hidden gem, Corral del Rey. This hotel boasts just thirteen rooms and suites, each uniquely designed with a mix of Moorish and Andalusian-style architecture. The hotel’s rooftop terrace is also a highlight, offering stunning views of the city’s skyline and iconic cathedral.

Last but certainly not least, we have the stunning Jasvilas in Jaipur, India. This boutique hotel is situated in a heritage building and features eleven luxurious rooms and suites. The hotel’s decor pays tribute to Rajasthan’s rich history with traditional Indian furnishings and intricate hand-painted murals. The hotel also offers a tranquil courtyard and a stunning rooftop terrace, perfect for enjoying a cup of chai while taking in views of the city’s bustling streets.

In conclusion, these hidden gems are just a few examples of the many boutique hotels that offer personalized and unique experiences for travelers. These hotels may be smaller in size, but they certainly make up for it in charm, attention to detail, and stunning design elements. Whether you’re looking for a romantic getaway or a culturally immersive experience, these boutique hotels are sure to provide a memorable stay.
