The Art of Trapping: Capture Food in Survival Situations


In a survival situation, one of the most critical skills to have is the ability to procure food. However, hunting can be a challenging and energy-intensive task. That’s where the art of trapping comes into play. Trapping is a method used by many outdoor enthusiasts and survivalists to capture food efficiently and with minimal effort. In this blog, we will explore the various types of traps, their construction, and how to effectively use them to increase your chances of survival in the wild.

Understanding the Purpose of Trapping:

Trapping is an ancient practice that has served as a reliable means of procuring food for centuries. Its primary purpose is to provide sustenance in situations where hunting or gathering may not yield fruitful results. Traps help you conserve energy while maximizing your ability to capture game, making them an invaluable tool in survival scenarios.

Types of Traps:

1. Deadfall Traps:

Deadfall traps are simple yet effective devices that rely on gravity to capture prey. They consist of a heavy object, such as a rock, suspended above a food bait. When the prey investigates the bait, it triggers the release mechanism, causing the weight to fall and crush the animal. Deadfall traps can be constructed using natural materials found in the wilderness, making them highly accessible for survival situations.

2. Snare Traps:

Snare traps are designed to catch animals by wrapping around their bodies once triggered. They can be constructed using cordage or wire, forming a loop that tightens when tension is applied. Snares are effective for capturing small to medium-sized game, such as rabbits or birds. Placement is crucial, as they should be set in areas with evidence of animal activity or along game trails to increase chances of trapping success.

3. Pitfall Traps:

Pitfall traps are excellent for capturing smaller animals that tend to burrow or tunnel. These traps involve digging a hole and concealing it with natural materials, such as leaves or branches. As the animal falls into the pit, it becomes trapped, unable to escape. Pitfall traps work best for creatures like snakes, mice, or insects, making them an ideal option for acquiring additional sources of protein during a survival situation.

4. Fish Traps:

When near bodies of water, fish traps can provide a reliable source of food. Enclose a pool of water with rocks or logs, leaving only a small opening for fish to swim in. Once inside, fish find it challenging to navigate back out, making them easy to catch and collect for sustenance. Fish traps are low effort, requiring minimal maintenance, allowing you to concentrate on other survival-related tasks.

Using Traps Effectively:

Placement and camouflage are essential when setting traps. It’s vital to identify signs of animal activity and strategically place traps in high-traffic areas or near potential food sources. To increase the chances of success, consider setting multiple traps of different types to target a wider range of prey. Monitor and adjust your traps regularly to maximize your chances of capturing food.

Safety Considerations:

While trapping is an effective survival technique, it’s essential to prioritize safety when setting and checking traps. Avoid placing traps near your campsite to minimize the risk of trapping unintended targets, including domestic animals or fellow campers. Always handle traps with caution, especially when dealing with Deadfall traps, which may cause injury if mishandled.

Conclusion:

In survival situations, trapping can be a game-changer when it comes to procuring food efficiently. By understanding the different types of traps and employing strategic placement and camouflage, you increase your chances of capturing game while conserving energy for other essential survival tasks. Remember to prioritize safety and always check local regulations before using traps in a non-emergency situation. Mastering the art of trapping can be a valuable skill that enhances your ability to survive and thrive in the wild.
