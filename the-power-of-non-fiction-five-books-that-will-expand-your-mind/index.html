The Power of Non-Fiction: Five Books That Will Expand Your Mind


Non-fiction books can change your outlook on life and help you look at the world in new ways. They’re an essential tool for personal growth and education. Non-fiction books inspire us to think critically, develop empathy, and improve our understanding of the world around us. Here are five must-read non-fiction books that will expand your mind.

1. Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind

Yuval Noah Harari’s book Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind is a breathtaking exploration of the human experience and how we’ve progressed over time. It’s a journey through the history of our species, starting from the early days of our existence to the present day. It covers topics such as agriculture, religion, and science, and introduces cutting-edge theories about the future of humankind. Harari presents a compelling argument that the most significant events in human history are the ones that helped us create cohesive societies.

2. Thinking, Fast and Slow

Daniel Kahneman’s Thinking, Fast and Slow is a fascinating book that takes the reader on a journey through the workings of the human mind. Drawing on decades of research, this book offers insights into how our brains make decisions. Kahneman delves deep into behavioural economics and the mechanisms that govern our thinking processes. He highlights the role of cognitive biases and how they can impact our decision-making and judgement.

3. The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks

Rebecca Skloot’s bestseller The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks tells the amazing story of Henrietta Lacks, whose cells have been used in research for decades. The book investigates the medical ethics of using human tissue for research without consent, and it explores Lacks’s legacy and how it impacted her family. Skloot’s writing is superb, and she creates a powerful narrative that has helped to raise awareness of the need for greater transparency in medical research.

4. The New Jim Crow

Michelle Alexander’s book The New Jim Crow documents the history of the war on drugs in America and how it has helped to create a form of institutionalised racism. Alexander argues that the war on drugs has become a tool for oppressing people of colour and that it has resulted in the mass incarceration of African Americans. The book shines a light on the need for reform in the criminal justice system and has played a significant role in raising awareness of issues of social justice.

5. The Sixth Extinction

Elizabeth Kolbert’s The Sixth Extinction is an intense and thought-provoking book that explores the impact of humans on the natural world. Kolbert argues that human activity is driving a sixth mass extinction, with the potential to wipe out a significant portion of the world’s biodiversity. The book examines how various species have been impacted by human activities such as deforestation, climate change, and ocean acidification. It’s a sobering reminder of the importance of protecting our planet for future generations.

In conclusion, non-fiction books offer an unparalleled opportunity to learn, grow and expand our minds. Reading these books can help us understand ourselves and the world around us in new ways. These five books are highly recommended for anyone who wants to enrich their mind and expand their understanding of the world. Whether you’re looking to improve your understanding of human history, psychology, social justice, or the natural world, this list is a great starting point. So go out and explore the world of non-fiction, and who knows, what you learn could change your life.
