Jewelry Tips for Brides: Completing Your Wedding Look


Your wedding day is one of the most special and memorable days of your life, and as a bride, you want to look absolutely stunning from head to toe. While the dress is undoubtedly the centerpiece of your bridal look, it’s the accessories that truly complete your ensemble. In this blog post, we will share some valuable jewelry tips for brides to help you put together the perfect finishing touches for your wedding look.

1. Consider Your Dress Style: Before diving into the world of jewelry, it’s important to consider the style and neckline of your wedding dress. If you have a strapless or sweetheart neckline, a statement necklace can add a touch of glamour and draw attention to your décolletage. For a dress with intricate details, such as lace or beading, opt for simpler, more delicate jewelry pieces that won’t overpower the dress.

2. Match Metals: When it comes to choosing your jewelry, consider the metal tones of your engagement and wedding rings. If you have a platinum or white gold ring, silver or diamond jewelry will complement it beautifully. If you have a yellow gold ring, opt for warmer-toned accessories. Mixing metals can create a stylish and modern look, but make sure they harmonize well together.

3. Balance is Key: When selecting your jewelry, it’s important to strike a balance between the different pieces. If you’re wearing a statement necklace, keep the earrings and bracelet more understated. On the other hand, if you’re planning to wear bold, chandelier earrings, opt for a simpler necklace or skip it altogether. The goal is to create a cohesive and harmonious look that doesn’t overwhelm.

4. Consider Your Hairstyle: The way you style your hair can greatly impact the type of jewelry you choose. If you’re wearing an updo, consider statement earrings or a hairpiece to add some sparkle and drama. For loose, flowing locks, delicate earrings and a statement necklace can beautifully frame your face. Experiment with different hairstyles and jewelry combinations during your dress fittings to find the perfect pairing.

5. Don’t Forget the Veil: If you’ll be wearing a veil, take it into consideration when selecting your jewelry. If your veil is heavily embellished, opt for simpler jewelry that won’t compete for attention. On the other hand, if your veil is more minimalistic, you can afford to be a bit bolder with your jewelry choices.

6. Personalize Your Look: Your wedding day is all about showcasing your unique style and personality. Consider incorporating sentimental pieces into your bridal jewelry, such as heirloom earrings or a necklace that has been passed down through generations. This will not only add a sentimental touch but also make your look even more special.

7. Take Time for Trials: Just like with your dress fittings, it’s essential to have jewelry trials as well. Try on different combinations of jewelry with your dress to see what works best for you. Take pictures from different angles to get a better idea of how everything looks together. Don’t be afraid to mix and match until you find the perfect combination that makes you feel like the most beautiful bride.

Remember, your wedding day is about celebrating love and embarking on a new chapter of your life. The jewelry you choose should enhance your natural beauty and make you feel confident and radiant. By following these jewelry tips for brides, you’ll be able to complete your wedding look with style and grace, leaving a lasting impression on your special day.
