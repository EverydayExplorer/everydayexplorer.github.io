The Art of Topwater Fishing: Tips for Success


If you’re an angler looking to up your fishing game, topwater fishing is a technique that you should definitely consider. There’s something truly exciting about watching a fish explode on the surface of the water as it goes for your lure. Topwater fishing is not only thrilling but can also be highly effective in enticing fish to bite. In this blog post, we will dive deep into the art of topwater fishing and provide you with some valuable tips for success.

1. Choose the right time and place:
Timing and location are crucial when it comes to topwater fishing. The best times for topwater action are early morning and late evening when fish tend to feed near the surface. Pay attention to the weather conditions as well. Overcast days, when light levels are lower, can often result in more aggressive fish behavior. Look for areas with structure, such as weeds, lily pads, or submerged logs, as these are prime spots for fish to hide and ambush their prey.

2. Select the appropriate lure:
The key to successful topwater fishing is selecting the right lure for the job. There are various types of topwater lures available, such as poppers, walk-the-dog baits, and buzzbaits. Poppers create a popping sound when twitched, imitating a wounded baitfish or insect. Walk-the-dog baits, like a Zara Spook, create a side-to-side darting action when retrieved, mimicking a struggling baitfish. Buzzbaits have a propeller-like blade that creates a buzzing sound on the water’s surface, attracting fish from a distance. Experiment with different types and sizes of lures to see what works best for the species you are targeting.

3. Master the retrieval technique:
The way you retrieve your lure is crucial to enticing fish to strike. Start by casting your lure beyond your desired fishing spot, giving it time to settle on the water’s surface. Once your lure is in position, use a combination of twitching, popping, or reeling to create movement on the surface. Vary the speed and cadence of your retrieval until you start getting bites. Remember, sometimes a slow, subtle retrieve can be just as effective as a fast and aggressive one. Be patient and adjust your technique based on the fish’s response.

4. Be stealthy and observant:
When it comes to topwater fishing, a stealthy approach is key. Approach the fishing spot quietly and avoid making sudden movements that could spook the fish. Keep an eye out for any signs of activity on the water, such as boils, swirls, or baitfish jumping. These indicators can help you identify where the fish are congregating and increase your chances of success.

5. Stay focused and react quickly:
Topwater strikes can happen in an instant, so it’s important to stay focused and react quickly when a fish hits your lure. As soon as you see or feel a strike, resist the urge to immediately set the hook. Instead, wait for a split second to ensure that the fish has fully taken the lure before giving a firm hookset. It can be challenging to resist the excitement, but a little patience can make all the difference between landing the fish or losing it.

6. Play with colors and sizes:
If you’re not having luck with your current lure, don’t be afraid to switch things up. Fish can be picky, and their preferences can vary depending on the conditions. Experiment with different colors and sizes of topwater lures to see what works best on any given day. Sometimes a subtle change can make a big difference in enticing fish to strike.

7. Practice catch and release:
Lastly, as responsible anglers, it’s essential to practice catch and release whenever possible. Topwater fishing often attracts larger fish, which are vital for maintaining healthy fish populations. Handle the fish with care, using wet hands or gloves to minimize damage to their protective slime coating. If you are planning to take a photo of your catch, be sure to do it quickly and then release the fish promptly back into the water.

In conclusion, topwater fishing is an art that requires patience, skill, and careful observation. By applying the tips mentioned above and practicing your topwater techniques, you can greatly increase your chances of success. So get out there, enjoy the excitement, and experience the thrill of seeing fish strike on the surface of the water. Happy fishing!
