The Influence of Ancient Egypt on Fantasy Literature


Of all the ancient civilizations, few have captured the imagination quite like Ancient Egypt. Its mysterious pyramids, intricate hieroglyphics, and fascinating pantheon of gods and goddesses have been a source of inspiration for artists, writers, and filmmakers for centuries. But perhaps one of the most interesting ways in which Ancient Egypt has influenced popular culture is through fantasy literature.

Fantasy literature is a genre that allows authors to create imaginative worlds filled with magic, mythical creatures, and epic quests. And it is within this genre that we can find countless examples of Ancient Egypt’s influence. From the iconic character of Imhotep in Bram Stoker’s ‘The Jewel of Seven Stars’ to the richly detailed world of N.K. Jemisin’s ‘The Hundred Thousand Kingdoms,’ Ancient Egypt has provided a wealth of inspiration for fantasy authors.

One of the most obvious connections between Ancient Egypt and fantasy literature is the concept of magic. In Ancient Egypt, magic played a central role in everyday life. Spells, incantations, and rituals were used to protect against evil spirits, heal the sick, and control the forces of nature. This tradition of magic and mysticism has been carried over into many fantasy novels.

Take, for example, the popular Harry Potter series by J.K. Rowling. The magical world she has created draws heavily from Ancient Egyptian mythology and symbolism. The Egyptian god Ra, the sun god, is featured prominently in the story, and many spells and potions have Egyptian roots. Similarly, the ‘Kane Chronicles’ by Rick Riordan centers around Egyptian mythology and follows the adventures of modern-day magicians descended from the pharaohs.

Another way in which Ancient Egypt has influenced fantasy literature is through its gods and goddesses. The pantheon of Egyptian deities is diverse and fascinating, with each god or goddess representing different aspects of life and nature. This rich mythology has been incorporated into numerous fantasy novels, often with a unique twist.

An excellent example of this is Neil Gaiman’s ‘American Gods,’ where ancient deities from different cultures, including Ancient Egypt, live in modern-day America. The character of Anubis, the god of death, plays a significant role in the story, reminding readers of the enduring power and influence of Ancient Egyptian mythology.

Additionally, Ancient Egypt’s iconic symbols and architecture have made their way into fantasy literature. The towering pyramids, majestic temples, and intricate hieroglyphics have become synonymous with mystery and adventure. These symbols have been used to great effect in countless fantasy novels, creating a sense of awe and wonder in readers.

An example of this can be seen in the critically acclaimed ‘The Fifth Sacred Thing’ by Starhawk. Set in a post-apocalyptic future, the novel incorporates ancient Egyptian symbols and themes to create a world where magic and spirituality coexist. The use of these symbols adds depth and authenticity to the story, drawing readers deeper into the narrative.

In conclusion, the influence of Ancient Egypt on fantasy literature is undeniable. Its rich mythology, magical traditions, and iconic symbols have captivated authors and readers alike, providing a treasure trove of inspiration. From the use of Egyptian gods and goddesses to the incorporation of ancient symbols and architecture, Ancient Egypt continues to shape the worlds and characters that inhabit the realm of fantasy literature. So, the next time you delve into a fantasy novel and find yourself transported to a world of magic and adventure, take a moment to appreciate the enduring influence of Ancient Egypt.
