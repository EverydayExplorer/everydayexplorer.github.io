The Top 10 Must-Read Books of 2021


Are you a book lover? Are you constantly on the lookout for the next great read? Well, you’ve come to the right place! In this blog post, we will be sharing our top picks for the must-read books of 2021. Whether you enjoy fiction, non-fiction, thriller, or romance, there’s something for everyone on this list. So, let’s dive right in and discover some amazing books that are sure to captivate you!

1. *The Invisible Life of Addie LaRue* by V.E. Schwab

This book takes readers on an extraordinary journey through time. Addie LaRue makes a deal with the devil to live forever but is cursed to be forgotten by everyone she meets. The story explores themes of love, identity, and the desire for a lasting impact on the world. It’s a beautifully written and enchanting novel that will leave you pondering the meaning of life.

2. *The Vanishing Half* by Brit Bennett

Brit Bennett’s second novel is a powerful exploration of race, identity, and family. The story follows the lives of twin sisters who choose different paths—one living as a black woman, the other passing as white. Through their diverging experiences, Bennett delves into the complexities of racial identity and the profound impact it has on our lives.

3. *The Push* by Ashley Audrain

If you love psychological thrillers, then this book is for you. *The Push* tells the story of a mother grappling with the belief that something is wrong with her daughter. As the tension builds, the line between reality and paranoia becomes blurred. Ashley Audrain’s debut novel is a gripping and thought-provoking exploration of motherhood and the nature versus nurture debate.

4. *Klara and the Sun* by Kazuo Ishiguro

Nobel laureate Kazuo Ishiguro once again delivers a stunning piece of literature with *Klara and the Sun*. Set in a dystopian future where artificial intelligence is indistinguishable from humans, the story follows Klara, an AI friend, as she navigates the complexities of human emotions. Ishiguro delves into themes of love, friendship, and what it means to be human, crafting a haunting and beautiful tale.

5. *Project Hail Mary* by Andy Weir

From the author of *The Martian* comes another thrilling science fiction novel. *Project Hail Mary* follows an astronaut who wakes up on a spaceship with no memory of who he is or how he got there. As he slowly pieces together his past, he realizes that the fate of humanity rests on his shoulders. Andy Weir’s gripping storytelling and scientific accuracy make this a must-read for any sci-fi fan.

6. *The Gilded Ones* by Namina Forna

Steeped in West African mythology, *The Gilded Ones* is a powerful fantasy novel that explores themes of patriarchy, identity, and the price of freedom. The story follows Deka, who discovers that she is an immortal and must fight for her place in a world that sees her as a monster. Namina Forna weaves a captivating tale filled with magic, adventure, and a strong female protagonist.

7. *Empire of Pain: The Secret History of the Sackler Dynasty* by Patrick Radden Keefe

This gripping non-fiction work uncovers the dark history behind the Sackler family, owners of Purdue Pharma, the company responsible for the opioid crisis in the United States. Patrick Radden Keefe meticulously reveals the elaborate web of deceit and greed that allowed the Sacklers to amass their immense wealth while millions suffered from addiction. It’s a thought-provoking and important read.

8. *The Sanatorium* by Sarah Pearse

Looking for a chilling thriller to keep you on the edge of your seat? Look no further than *The Sanatorium*. Set in an isolated hotel once a sanatorium, the story follows a detective investigating a series of mysterious deaths. As the tension builds and secrets are revealed, Sarah Pearse keeps readers guessing until the very end. Perfect for fans of the genre who enjoy a good mystery.

9. *No One Is Talking About This* by Patricia Lockwood

*No One Is Talking About This* is a unique and genre-defying novel that explores the intersection of the digital world and real life. The story follows a woman, famous for her viral internet presence, as her life spirals into chaos after a family tragedy. Patricia Lockwood’s sharp and witty prose takes the reader on a rollercoaster ride of emotions, offering a fresh perspective on our interconnected world.

10. *The Code Breaker: Jennifer Doudna, Gene Editing, and the Future of the Human Race* by Walter Isaacson

This illuminating non-fiction book explores the revolutionary technology of CRISPR and the scientist behind it, Jennifer Doudna. Walter Isaacson delves into the ethical implications of gene editing and its potential to reshape humanity. *The Code Breaker* is both a biography and a science book, offering readers a deep understanding of this groundbreaking field.

So, there you have it! Our top 10 must-read books of 2021. Whether you’re a fan of fiction or non-fiction, these books offer something for everyone. From gripping thrillers to thought-provoking explorations of identity and humanity, these books will keep you entertained and engaged throughout the year. Happy reading!
