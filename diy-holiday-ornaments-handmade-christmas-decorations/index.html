DIY Holiday Ornaments: Handmade Christmas Decorations

The holiday season is right around the corner, and what better way to get into the festive spirit than by making your own DIY holiday ornaments? Handmade Christmas decorations add a personal touch to your home decor and can be a fun activity for the whole family. In this blog post, we’ll explore some creative ideas and techniques for creating beautiful ornaments that will make your Christmas tree sparkle.

1. Painted Wood Slice Ornaments

Wood slice ornaments are rustic and charming, and they are incredibly easy to make. Start by gathering some small wood slices, which can be found at craft stores or easily cut from fallen tree branches. Sand the rough edges, and then grab some acrylic paints in your favorite festive colors. Paint holiday-themed designs such as snowflakes, reindeer, or Christmas trees onto the wood slices. Once dry, add a thin layer of varnish to protect the paint and tie a ribbon through the top for hanging.

2. Salt Dough Ornaments

Salt dough ornaments are a classic choice for handmade Christmas decorations. To make the dough, mix together 1 cup of all-purpose flour, 1/2 cup of salt, and 1/2 cup of water until a smooth dough forms. Roll out the dough and use cookie cutters to create shapes like stars, hearts, or candy canes. Before baking, use a straw to create a small hole for hanging. Bake the ornaments in a preheated oven at 200°F (93°C) for about 2-3 hours or until fully hardened. Once cooled, you can paint them with acrylic paints or decorate them with glitter.

3. Paper Quilled Ornaments

Paper quilling is a beautiful and intricate art form that can be used to create stunning holiday ornaments. Start by cutting thin strips of colored paper, around 1/8 inch wide. Use a quilling tool to curl the strips into tight coils, and then shape them into various designs such as snowflakes, stars, or Christmas wreaths. Once you have assembled your design, glue the strips together and add a loop of string for hanging. Paper quilled ornaments are a time-consuming but rewarding DIY project that will impress your guests.

4. Felt Gingerbread Ornaments

Gingerbread ornaments are a festive and delicious addition to your Christmas tree. To make these cute decorations, you’ll need some brown and white craft felt, as well as embroidery floss and stuffing. Cut out gingerbread shapes from the brown felt, and then decorate them with white felt icing and colored embroidery floss for details like eyes and buttons. Sew the front and back together, leaving a small opening for stuffing. Once stuffed, sew the opening closed and add a ribbon loop for hanging.

5. Mason Jar Lid Ornaments

If you have some spare mason jar lids lying around, why not turn them into unique ornaments? Start by painting the lids with metallic spray paint or acrylic paint in your desired color. Once dry, glue a small holiday-themed figurine or ornament to the center of the lid. You can also add glitter or small beads for extra sparkle. Attach a ribbon or string to the top of the lid for hanging, and your mason jar lid ornaments are ready to adorn your tree.

6. Cinnamon Stick Ornaments

Cinnamon stick ornaments not only look beautiful but also smell amazing. Simply gather some cinnamon sticks and tie them together with a ribbon to create a bundle. You can also attach small ornaments, bows, or dried flowers to the ribbon for added decoration. Hang these fragrant ornaments on your tree or around your home to create a warm and cozy holiday atmosphere.

7. Pinecone Ornaments

Pinecones are a versatile material for DIY holiday ornaments. You can leave them in their natural state for a rustic look or add some paint, glitter, or even fake snow for a touch of sparkle. Attach a loop of string or ribbon to the top of the pinecone for hanging. You can also glue small ornaments or miniature figurines to the pinecone for extra charm. Pinecone ornaments are an easy and budget-friendly way to bring the beauty of nature into your holiday decor.

-–

By creating your own DIY holiday ornaments, you not only add a personal touch to your Christmas decorations but also unleash your creativity. Whether you prefer a rustic, elegant, or whimsical theme, there’s a handmade ornament idea to suit your style. So gather your supplies, put on some holiday music, and make this season extra special with your very own handmade Christmas decorations.

*Note: Remember to always supervise children when working with small objects or using hot glue guns.*
