How to Make Healthier Choices When Dining Out


The modern lifestyle often keeps us on the go, with little time to cook meals at home. As a result, dining out has become a common occurrence for many individuals. While this can be convenient, it also poses a challenge when it comes to making healthier choices. Restaurant menus are often filled with tempting options that are high in calories, sodium, and unhealthy fats. However, with a little awareness and planning, you can make healthier choices when dining out without sacrificing taste. In this blog post, we will explore some strategies and tips to help you navigate the world of dining out while staying on track with your wellness goals.

1. Do your research before you go

One of the best ways to make healthier choices when dining out is to research the menu beforehand. Most restaurants have their menus available online, allowing you to review your options and make a decision ahead of time. Look for dishes that are grilled, baked, or steamed, as these cooking methods tend to use less oil and are generally healthier than fried options. Also, keep an eye out for menu items labeled as ’low-fat,’ ’low-sodium,’ or ‘healthy choice,’ as they are designed with health-conscious individuals in mind.

2. Start with a plan

Going into a restaurant with a plan can help you resist the temptation of ordering less healthy options. Decide on what types of foods you want to focus on, such as lean proteins, whole grains, and fresh vegetables. This will help you narrow down your choices when you’re faced with an extensive menu. Additionally, consider your portion sizes. Many restaurants serve large portions that exceed the recommended serving sizes. You can either share a meal with a friend or ask for a to-go box at the beginning of the meal to portion out half of your dish for later.

3. Mindful ordering

When it comes to making healthier choices, it’s important to pay attention to how the food is prepared and cooked. Opt for grilling, baking, or steaming instead of frying. Choose dishes that are rich in vegetables and lean protein sources like chicken, fish, or tofu. Avoid dishes with heavy sauces or dressings, as they can be high in fat and calories. Ask for dressings and sauces on the side, so you can control the amount you use. It’s also a good idea to choose whole grain options when available, such as whole wheat bread or brown rice, as they provide more fiber and nutrients compared to their refined counterparts.

4. Be aware of portion sizes

Portion sizes at restaurants are often larger than what we truly need. It’s easy to get carried away and overeat. To avoid this, you can try ordering from the appetizer or side dish section of the menu. These smaller portions can still be satisfying and allow you to enjoy a variety of flavors without going overboard. Another option is to share your meal with a dining partner or ask for a half portion. This way, you can enjoy the food without consuming excessive calories.

5. Be mindful of beverages

Beverages can be a hidden source of calories and sugar. It’s best to stick to water, unsweetened iced tea, or sparkling water instead of sugary sodas, juices, or alcoholic beverages. If you do choose to have an alcoholic drink, opt for a light beer or a glass of wine instead of cocktails that are often high in added sugars and calories.

6. Pay attention to your body’s hunger and fullness cues

While dining out, it’s important to listen to your body’s signals of hunger and fullness. Take your time while eating, and pay attention to how your body feels. Stop eating when you’re satisfied, not when you’re overly full. It’s easy to get caught up in the delicious flavors and socializing, but being mindful of your body’s cues can help prevent overeating.

7. Don’t be afraid to make special requests

Restaurants are often willing to accommodate special requests. If you’re not sure how a dish is prepared, don’t hesitate to ask your server. Request that your food be prepared with less oil, salt, or butter, or ask for vegetables to be cooked without any added fats. Most importantly, don’t be afraid to advocate for your health and make requests that align with your dietary needs and preferences.

By following these strategies and tips, you can enjoy dining out while making healthier choices that support your overall well-being. Remember that it’s all about balance and moderation. You don’t have to deprive yourself of occasional treats, but making mindful choices most of the time can contribute to a healthier lifestyle. So the next time you find yourself dining out, apply these principles and enjoy your meal guilt-free!
