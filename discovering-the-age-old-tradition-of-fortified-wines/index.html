Discovering the Age-Old Tradition of Fortified Wines


Fortified wines have a long and storied history, dating back centuries. These wines, known for their higher alcohol content and unique flavor profiles, have been enjoyed by wine enthusiasts around the world for ages. From the legendary Ports of Portugal to the rich Sherries of Spain, fortified wine production has become an age-old tradition that continues to thrive to this day.

What sets fortified wines apart from other types of wine is the addition of distilled spirits, typically brandy, during the fermentation process. This fortification process not only increases the alcohol content but also imparts a distinctive richness and complexity to the finished product. Fortified wines can range in style from dry to sweet, offering a wide range of options to suit different palates.

One of the most well-known categories of fortified wine is Port, which hails from the Douro Valley in Portugal. Port has a rich history dating back to the late 17th century when the British first began importing wine from Portugal. Port is traditionally made from a blend of grape varieties, including Touriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, and Tinta Roriz, among others. These grapes are grown in the rugged and terraced vineyards of the Douro Valley, where the unique microclimate and soil characteristics contribute to the wine’s distinct flavor profile.

There are several styles of Port wine, including Ruby, Tawny, Vintage, and Late Bottled Vintage (LBV). Ruby Port is typically young and vibrant, with pronounced fruit flavors and a deep, ruby-red color. Tawny Port, on the other hand, is aged in wooden barrels, resulting in a lighter hue and a nuttier, more complex flavor profile. Vintage Port is considered the pinnacle of Port production, made from the best grapes of a single exceptional vintage. These wines require decades of aging before they are released, resulting in a bold and intensely flavored wine. Lastly, LBV is a style of Port that is often considered a more approachable and affordable alternative to Vintage Port. LBV wines are aged for a shorter period than Vintage Ports and are ready to drink upon release.

Another iconic fortified wine is Sherry, which originates from the Andalusia region of southern Spain. Sherry is made from a variety of white grape called Palomino Fino, which is grown in the unique albariza soil. Sherry production is a labor-intensive process that involves a solera system, where barrels of different vintages are blended together to create a consistent and complex wine.

Sherry is known for its wide range of styles and flavors, from bone-dry Fino and Manzanilla to rich and sweet Pedro Ximénez and Cream Sherry. Fino and Manzanilla Sherries are aged under a layer of yeast called ‘flor,’ which imparts unique flavors and helps protect the wine from oxidation. These Sherries are pale in color, with delicate nutty and saline notes. On the other hand, Pedro Ximénez Sherries are made from sun-dried grapes and are intensely sweet and viscous, with flavors of raisins, figs, and caramel.

Apart from Port and Sherry, there are other notable fortified wines from various regions around the world. Madeira, a Portuguese fortified wine, has a unique aging process that involves exposure to heat, resulting in a wine that is rich, complex, and long-lasting. Marsala, an Italian fortified wine, is produced in Sicily and comes in both dry and sweet styles. Vermouth, a fortified and aromatized wine, is widely used as an ingredient in cocktails and comes in various styles depending on whether it is red or white and sweet or dry.

In recent years, there has been a resurgence of interest in fortified wines, as wine lovers discover the depth and complexity that these wines offer. They are not only enjoyed on their own but are also versatile when it comes to pairing with a wide range of foods. Ports and Sherries, in particular, are known for their ability to pair well with both sweet and savory dishes, making them a favorite choice for food and wine pairing enthusiasts.

Whether you are savoring a glass of aged Tawny Port after dinner or enjoying a crisp glass of Fino Sherry as an aperitif, fortified wines provide a unique and unforgettable experience. The age-old tradition of fortifying wines has stood the test of time, and wine lovers around the world continue to appreciate the artistry and complexity that these wines offer. So the next time you’re looking to elevate your wine tasting experience, consider exploring the world of fortified wines and discovering the rich history and tradition that goes into each bottle.
