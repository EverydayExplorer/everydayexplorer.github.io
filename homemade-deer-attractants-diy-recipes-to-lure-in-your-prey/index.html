Homemade Deer Attractants: DIY Recipes to Lure in Your Prey


If you’re an avid hunter looking to improve your chances of a successful deer hunt, using homemade deer attractants is a smart move. Not only are these attractants cost-effective and easy to make, but they can also be customized to suit your specific hunting needs. In this blog post, we will explore some DIY recipes for homemade deer attractants that will help you lure in your prey and maximize your hunting experience.

Why Use Homemade Deer Attractants?

Before we dive into the DIY recipes, it’s important to understand why homemade deer attractants are a valuable tool for hunters. Using attractants allows you to bring deer to a specific location, increasing the likelihood of a successful hunt. Whether you are setting up a deer stand or hunting blind, the right attractant can make all the difference.

Another advantage of homemade deer attractants is that you have complete control over the ingredients and scent. This means you can tailor the attractant to the specific deer species in your area, as well as the time of year you’ll be hunting. By experimenting with different ingredients, you can find the perfect recipe that will attract more deer to your hunting spot.

DIY Recipes for Homemade Deer Attractants

Now that we understand the benefits of homemade deer attractants, let’s explore some popular recipes that you can make at home.

1. Apple and Corn Mixture

Apple and corn have long been known to attract deer, making this recipe a classic choice for many hunters. To make this attractant, you’ll need:

  • 5 pounds of dried corn
  • 5 pounds of fresh apples
  • 5 gallons of water

Instructions: 1. Chop the apples into small pieces, discarding the cores. 2. In a large pot, combine the chopped apples and dried corn. 3. Add water to the pot and bring it to a boil. 4. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for an hour, stirring occasionally. 5. Allow the mixture to cool completely before transferring it into containers. 6. Place the containers near your hunting spot or use them to bait trails.

2. Molasses and Peanut Butter Blend

Molasses and peanut butter are both highly attractive to deer due to their sweet scent and rich flavor. To make this recipe, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup of molasses
  • 1 cup of peanut butter
  • 1 cup of vegetable oil
  • 2 cups of powdered sugar

Instructions: 1. In a mixing bowl, combine the molasses, peanut butter, vegetable oil, and powdered sugar. 2. Stir the ingredients until they are well blended and form a thick paste. 3. Transfer the mixture into airtight containers for storage. 4. Spread the attractant onto logs, stumps, or rocks near your hunting spot.

3. Acorn and Persimmon Mixture

Acorns and persimmons are natural food sources for deer, making this recipe highly effective. To make this attractant, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound of acorns
  • 1 pound of ripe persimmons
  • 1 cup of apple juice
  • 1 tablespoon of salt

Instructions: 1. In a food processor or blender, grind the acorns and ripe persimmons until they form a smooth paste. 2. Transfer the paste into a mixing bowl and add apple juice and salt. 3. Stir the ingredients until they are well combined. 4. Store the mixture in airtight containers. 5. Place the attractant near your hunting spot or use it to create a feeding station.

Using Homemade Deer Attractants Responsibly

While homemade deer attractants can significantly enhance your hunting experience, it’s important to use them responsibly. Here are a few guidelines to keep in mind:

  • Check local hunting regulations: Before using attractants, make sure they are legal in your area. Some states have specific restrictions on baiting and attractant use.

  • Avoid overusing attractants: By using attractants sparingly, you can prevent deer from becoming too reliant on them. Over time, deer may become less responsive to the attractant if they find it readily available.

  • Keep scent control in mind: When using attractants, be mindful of your own scent. Deer have a keen sense of smell, so it’s essential to practice scent control techniques to avoid alerting them to your presence.

  • Maintain ethical hunting practices: Remember to always hunt responsibly and abide by ethical hunting practices. Attractants should be used as a tool to improve your hunting success, not as a means to lure in deer and take unfair advantage.

In conclusion, homemade deer attractants can be a game-changer for hunters looking to increase their chances of a successful hunt. By experimenting with different recipes and ingredients, you can create attractants that are tailored to your specific hunting needs. However, it’s crucial to use them responsibly and in accordance with local regulations. Good luck on your next deer hunting adventure!
