Top 10 Outrageous Celebrity Fashion Fails That Will Make You LOL


Fashion is a realm where creativity knows no bounds, and celebrities often push the envelope to make bold statements on the red carpet or in their day-to-day lives. However, sometimes these attempts at avant-garde fashion end up falling flat, leaving us scratching our heads and scrolling for the nearest LOL emoji. From questionable outfit choices to downright bizarre ensembles, here are the top 10 outrageous celebrity fashion fails that will have you double-checking your own wardrobe choices.

  1. Lady Gaga’s Meat Dress: Let’s start with a classic. Back in 2010, Lady Gaga made headlines (and stomachs turn) when she showed up to the MTV Video Music Awards wearing a dress made entirely of raw meat. While she may have been trying to make a statement about animal rights, it left many wondering if she had taken the theme of ‘meatpacking chic’ a little too far.

  2. Björk’s Swan Dress: Another unforgettable moment in fashion faux pas history comes courtesy of Björk at the 2001 Oscars. The Icelandic singer-songwriter arrived in a swan-inspired dress complete with a beak-shaped headpiece. While she’s known for her eccentric style, this ensemble had viewers squawking with disbelief.

  3. Rihanna’s Sheer CFDA Dress: Rihanna is no stranger to turning heads on the red carpet, but her sheer, Swarovski crystal-encrusted gown at the 2014 CFDA Fashion Awards left little to the imagination. While some praised her confidence, others couldn’t help but wonder if she forgot to wear a slip.

  4. Kanye West’s Met Gala Look: Kanye West is known for his boundary-pushing fashion choices, but his 2016 Met Gala ensemble had many scratching their heads. With ripped jeans, a bedazzled denim jacket, and color contacts that turned his eyes a shade of icy blue, Kanye’s look was more confusing than avant-garde.

  5. Celine Dion’s Backwards Suit: At the 1999 Oscars, Celine Dion attempted to shake up the red carpet by wearing a backwards white suit. While she may have been trying to channel her inner rebel, the end result was more reminiscent of a wardrobe malfunction than a fashion statement.

  6. Nicki Minaj’s Red Riding Hood Look: Nicki Minaj is known for her fearless approach to fashion, but her Little Red Riding Hood-inspired ensemble at the 2012 Grammy Awards missed the mark. With a red cloak, matching gloves, and what appeared to be a pope lookalike as her date, Nicki’s outfit had viewers wondering if they had stumbled into a fairy tale gone wrong.

  7. Katy Perry’s Muppet Dress: Katy Perry is no stranger to quirky fashion, but her Muppet-inspired dress at the 2019 Met Gala left many wondering if she had raided the Sesame Street costume department. Complete with googly eyes and feathers galore, Katy’s outfit was more fuzzy than fabulous.

  8. Miley Cyrus’ VMAs Performance Outfit: Miley Cyrus is no stranger to controversy, and her performance at the 2013 MTV Video Music Awards was no exception. Clad in a flesh-colored latex bikini and wielding a foam finger like a prop, Miley’s look had viewers questioning whether they had accidentally tuned into a risqué circus act.

  9. Solange Knowles’ Puffy Cloud Dress: Solange Knowles is known for her avant-garde fashion sense, but her puffy cloud-inspired dress at the 2017 Met Gala had many scratching their heads. With layers of billowing fabric in shades of white and blue, Solange looked less like a fashion icon and more like she was auditioning for a role in a school play.

  10. Kim Kardashian’s Floral Couch Dress: Kim Kardashian is no stranger to turning heads on the red carpet, but her floral couch-inspired dress at the 2013 Met Gala had many wondering if she had raided her grandmother’s living room for inspiration. With a high neckline, long sleeves, and an abundance of floral print, Kim’s outfit was more upholstery than haute couture.

In conclusion, while celebrities may have access to the world’s top designers and unlimited resources, even they are not immune to the occasional fashion fail. Whether it’s a questionable outfit choice or a bizarre red carpet ensemble, these moments remind us that when it comes to fashion, sometimes it’s better to play it safe than risk ending up on a ‘worst dressed’ list.
