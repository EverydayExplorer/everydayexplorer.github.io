The Best Teen Movies of the Last Decade


The past decade has been a remarkable period for teen cinema, showcasing a diverse range of stories that resonate with young audiences. From coming-of-age tales to heartwarming romances and thrilling adventures, these films have explored the complexities of adolescence in unique and engaging ways. In this blog post, we will delve into some of the best teen movies from the last ten years, highlighting their themes, characters, and cultural impact.

1. The Edge of Seventeen (2016)

Directed by Kelly Fremon Craig, “The Edge of Seventeen” stands out as a quintessential coming-of-age film. Starring Hailee Steinfeld as Nadine, the story delves into the struggles of a high school junior grappling with the challenges of growing up. Nadine feels isolated and misunderstood, particularly after her best friend starts dating her older brother. The film brilliantly captures the awkwardness of adolescence, showcasing the ups and downs of teenage life. Its sharp writing, relatable characters, and humor make it a must-watch for anyone who remembers the trials of high school.

2. Lady Bird (2017)

Greta Gerwig’s “Lady Bird” is a poignant exploration of the mother-daughter relationship set against the backdrop of a high school senior year. Saoirse Ronan delivers a captivating performance as Christine “Lady Bird” McPherson, a teenager yearning for independence while navigating her complicated relationship with her mother, played by Laurie Metcalf. The film is celebrated for its authentic portrayal of teenage angst, aspirations, and the bittersweet nature of growing up. “Lady Bird” received critical acclaim and multiple award nominations, solidifying its place as one of the best teen films of the decade.

3. To All the Boys I’ve Loved Before (2018)

Based on Jenny Han’s bestselling novel, “To All the Boys I’ve Loved Before” became a cultural phenomenon upon its release on Netflix. The film follows Lara Jean Covey, played by Lana Condor, whose secret love letters are accidentally sent to her past crushes. This romantic comedy captures the innocence and excitement of first love while also addressing themes of identity and self-discovery. The chemistry between Condor and Noah Centineo, who plays Peter Kavinsky, adds depth to the film, making it a delightful watch for teens and adults alike.

4. Eighth Grade (2018)

Bo Burnham’s “Eighth Grade” is an unflinching look at the trials of middle school. The film follows Kayla, played by Elsie Fisher, as she navigates her last week of eighth grade. With its realistic portrayal of social media’s impact on young lives, “Eighth Grade” resonates with today’s teens, capturing the anxiety and awkwardness of adolescence. The film’s raw honesty and relatability have earned it critical acclaim, making it one of the most significant teen films of the decade.

5. The Hate U Give (2018)

Based on Angie Thomas’s bestselling novel, “The Hate U Give” tackles pressing social issues through the eyes of a teenager. Amandla Stenberg stars as Starr Carter, a girl who witnesses the police shooting of her best friend. The film bravely addresses themes of racial injustice, identity, and activism, making it an essential watch for teens. With its powerful message and compelling storytelling, “The Hate U Give” has sparked important conversations about race and inequality in America.

6. Booksmart (2019)

Olivia Wilde’s directorial debut, “Booksmart,” reimagines the classic high school comedy. The film follows two overachieving best friends, Amy and Molly, played by Kaitlyn Dever and Beanie Feldstein, as they realize they’ve spent their high school years focused solely on academics. Determined to make up for lost time, they embark on a wild night of partying before graduation. “Booksmart” is celebrated for its witty script, strong performances, and progressive representation, making it a fresh addition to the teen movie genre.

7. Palm Springs (2020)

While “Palm Springs” straddles the line between romantic comedy and science fiction, its exploration of love and self-discovery resonates with a youthful audience. Starring Andy Samberg and Cristin Milioti, the film follows two wedding guests who find themselves trapped in a time loop. As they relive the same day repeatedly, they confront their insecurities and discover what truly matters in life. The film’s clever premise and engaging performances have made it a standout in recent teen cinema.

8. The Half of It (2020)

Alice Wu’s “The Half of It” provides a fresh take on the classic Cyrano de Bergerac story, focusing on friendship, love, and identity. The film follows Ellie, a shy and introverted high school student, who is hired by a jock to help him woo a girl. However, Ellie finds herself falling for the same girl, leading to a poignant exploration of unrequited love and self-acceptance. With its beautiful storytelling and relatable characters, “The Half of It” has garnered praise for its authentic representation of LGBTQ+ experiences.

9. Cruella (2021)

Though primarily a villain origin story, “Cruella” offers a unique perspective on teenage rebellion and identity. Set in the 1970s, it follows a young Estella, played by Emma Stone, who navigates the fashion world while embracing her darker instincts. The film’s vibrant aesthetic, strong performances, and themes of individuality make it a captivating watch for teens. “Cruella” effectively combines humor, drama, and style, appealing to a broad audience.

10. Everything Everywhere All at Once (2022)

While not strictly a teen film, “Everything Everywhere All at Once” has gained a substantial following among younger audiences due to its innovative storytelling and themes of identity. The film follows Evelyn Wang, played by Michelle Yeoh, who discovers that she must connect with parallel universe versions of herself to save the multiverse. Its blend of action, humor, and emotional depth resonates with viewers of all ages, making it a standout film of the decade.

Conclusion

The last decade has produced a wealth of exceptional teen movies that reflect the diverse experiences of adolescence. From heartfelt dramas to lighthearted comedies, these films have captured the essence of what it means to be a teenager in today’s world. As we celebrate these cinematic gems, it’s clear that the stories of young people continue to evolve, offering fresh perspectives and inspiring narratives that resonate with audiences everywhere. Whether you’re a teen navigating the complexities of growing up or an adult reminiscing about your own experiences, these films provide an engaging and relatable glimpse into the teenage experience. So, grab some popcorn, settle in, and explore the best teen movies of the last decade—there’s something for everyone!
