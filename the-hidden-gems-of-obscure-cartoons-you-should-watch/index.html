The Hidden Gems of Obscure Cartoons You Should Watch


When it comes to animated cartoons, most people instantly think of popular shows like ‘The Simpsons’, ‘SpongeBob SquarePants’, or ‘Adventure Time’. While these shows have certainly left a mark on the animation industry, there are many hidden gems of obscure cartoons that often go unnoticed by the general public. These hidden gems are unique, creative, and deserving of some recognition. In this blog post, we will explore some of these lesser-known cartoons that you should watch.

1. ‘The Maxx’:
‘The Maxx’ is a groundbreaking animated series that aired on MTV in the late 1990s. Based on the comic book of the same name, this show follows the exploits of a man named Dave (aka The Maxx) as he navigates between the real world and a dreamlike dimension called the Outback. The animation style is dark and surreal, with fluid movements and striking visuals. ‘The Maxx’ explores themes of identity, trauma, and the struggle between reality and fantasy. If you’re a fan of psychological storytelling and visually stunning animation, ‘The Maxx’ is a must-watch.

2. ‘Gargoyles’:
‘Gargoyles’ is an American animated series that premiered in 1994. The show centers around a clan of creatures known as gargoyles who turn to stone during the day and come to life at night to defend their castle. The series combines elements of fantasy, mythology, and Shakespearean drama to create a captivating and complex storyline. The writing is intelligent, mature, and not afraid to tackle serious topics. ‘Gargoyles’ is known for its intricate character development and has gained a cult following over the years. If you’re looking for a cartoon that goes beyond the typical animated fare, ‘Gargoyles’ is a must-watch.

3. ‘Samurai Jack’:
‘Samurai Jack’ is a critically acclaimed animated series created by Genndy Tartakovsky. The show originally aired on Cartoon Network from 2001 to 2004 and recently returned for a fifth season in 2017. ‘Samurai Jack’ follows the journey of a time-displaced samurai who battles the evil shape-shifting demon known as Aku. The series is known for its unique art style, fluid animation, and innovative storytelling techniques. ‘Samurai Jack’ blends elements of action, adventure, and science fiction to create a visually stunning and emotionally resonant series. If you’re a fan of epic tales and stunning visual storytelling, ‘Samurai Jack’ deserves a spot on your watchlist.

4. ‘As Told by Ginger’:
‘As Told by Ginger’ is an animated series that aired on Nickelodeon from 2000 to 2006. The show revolves around the life of Ginger Foutley, a middle schooler dealing with the ups and downs of adolescence. ‘As Told by Ginger’ tackles serious themes such as family dynamics, friendship, and identity, while also infusing humor and relatability. The series is a coming-of-age story that resonates with viewers of all ages. ‘As Told by Ginger’ gained a cult following and is praised for its realistic portrayal of teenage life. If you’re looking for a heartfelt and authentic animated series, ‘As Told by Ginger’ is a hidden gem worth discovering.

5. ‘Over the Garden Wall’:
‘Over the Garden Wall’ is a charming and whimsical animated miniseries created by Patrick McHale. The show originally aired on Cartoon Network in 2014 and has since gained a dedicated fanbase. ‘Over the Garden Wall’ follows the adventures of two brothers, Wirt and Greg, as they navigate a mysterious forest known as the Unknown. The series is known for its beautiful animation, atmospheric storytelling, and hauntingly beautiful soundtrack. ‘Over the Garden Wall’ explores themes of friendship, loss, and self-discovery in a way that appeals to both children and adults. If you’re a fan of fairy tales and atmospheric storytelling, ‘Over the Garden Wall’ is a hidden gem that shouldn’t be missed.

In conclusion, while popular cartoons often capture our attention, there is a rich world of lesser-known animated series that deserve to be seen. From the dark and surreal ‘The Maxx’ to the Shakespearean drama of ‘Gargoyles’ and the visually stunning epic ‘Samurai Jack’, these hidden gems offer unique storytelling and unforgettable characters. ‘As Told by Ginger’ and ‘Over the Garden Wall’ remind us that animation can tackle serious topics and touch the hearts of viewers of all ages. So, the next time you’re in the mood for something different, consider exploring these obscure cartoons to discover the hidden treasures of the animation world.
