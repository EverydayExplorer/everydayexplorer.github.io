The Haunting of the Myrtles Plantation: Ghostly Encounters in the Deep South


Deep in the heart of the enchanting Deep South lies a place that is rumored to be haunted by restless spirits. The Myrtles Plantation, nestled amidst the lush greenery of St. Francisville, Louisiana, has gained a reputation as one of the most haunted locations in America. With its rich history and eerie legends, the Myrtles Plantation is a magnet for those seeking ghostly encounters.

The Myrtles Plantation, with its imposing Greek Revival architecture, stands as a testament to the antebellum era of the Deep South. Its origins can be traced back to the late 18th century when General David Bradford, also known as ‘Whiskey Dave,’ acquired the land and built a small cottage. Over the years, the property changed hands, and in 1796, it became the grand plantation we know today.

Legend has it that the Myrtles Plantation is haunted by the spirits of those who suffered within its walls. The most famous ghostly resident is Chloe, a slave who met a tragic end. According to the story, Chloe, who was known for her beauty, overheard a conversation between the plantation owner’s wife and her husband. Fearing that she would be sent away, Chloe eavesdropped from behind a door. Unfortunately, she was caught, and her punishment was severe. Her ear was severed, and she was forced to wear a green turban to hide her disfigurement.

Chloe’s spirit is said to wander the plantation, wearing her green turban and seeking forgiveness for her transgressions. Guests and staff have reported sightings of a woman in a green turban, and some have even claimed to feel her presence and hear her whispers in the dead of night.

But Chloe is not the only spirit said to haunt the Myrtles Plantation. The ghost of a young girl, believed to be the daughter of a former plantation owner, has also been spotted. She is often seen playing on the front porch or swinging from the branches of the ancient oak trees that surround the property. Her laughter echoes through the night, creating an eerie atmosphere that sends shivers down the spines of those who dare to visit.

In addition to these well-known apparitions, there have been countless reports of unexplained phenomena at the Myrtles Plantation. Guests have reported doors slamming shut on their own, disembodied voices whispering in their ears, and the sensation of being touched by an unseen presence. Some have even captured ghostly figures in their photographs, providing further evidence of the supernatural activity that permeates the plantation.

For those brave enough to stay overnight, the Myrtles Plantation offers a unique opportunity to experience the paranormal firsthand. The property has been transformed into a bed and breakfast, welcoming guests from around the world. Visitors can choose to stay in one of the haunted rooms, such as the Judge Clarke Woodruff Suite, where Chloe is said to have met her gruesome fate.

Whether you believe in ghosts or not, a visit to the Myrtles Plantation is sure to leave you with a sense of intrigue and wonder. The rich history, coupled with the countless ghostly encounters, make this Southern gem a must-see for paranormal enthusiasts. So, if you’re ready to explore the realm of the supernatural, pack your bags and prepare for a ghostly adventure in the Deep South. But beware, for you may just encounter more than you bargained for at the infamous Myrtles Plantation.
