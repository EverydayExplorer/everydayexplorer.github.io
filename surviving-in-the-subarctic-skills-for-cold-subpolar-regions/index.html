Surviving in the Subarctic: Skills for Cold Subpolar Regions


When it comes to surviving in extreme cold climates, few places can rival the challenges of the subarctic regions. These subpolar areas, characterized by long, harsh winters and short, cool summers, require unique skills and preparations to ensure one’s safety and well-being. In this blog post, we will explore some essential skills for surviving in the subarctic, offering valuable insights for adventurers and explorers venturing into these frigid lands.

1. Cold Weather Clothing: Dressing appropriately is the first line of defense against the subarctic cold. Layering is key, as it helps trap body heat and allows for easy adjustment as temperatures fluctuate. Start with a moisture-wicking base layer to keep your skin dry, then add insulating layers such as fleece or down jackets. Finally, top it off with a windproof and waterproof outer shell. Don’t forget to cover your extremities with warm gloves, hats, and insulated boots.

2. Fire Building: Mastering fire-building techniques is crucial in subarctic regions, as fire provides warmth, light, and a means of cooking food. Collect dry wood and kindling before starting, and ensure you have a reliable fire starter, such as matches or a lighter. Choose a sheltered spot, away from wind and snow, and build a fire pit. Use a combination of small twigs, larger branches, and logs to create a sustainable fire. Remember to follow all safety precautions and never leave a fire unattended.

3. Shelter Construction: In subarctic regions, finding or building suitable shelter is vital for survival. If you don’t have a tent or other portable shelter, consider constructing a snow cave or an igloo. These structures provide excellent insulation against the cold and wind. Alternatively, learn how to build a debris shelter using branches, leaves, and other natural materials. Ensure your shelter is well-ventilated and can withstand the weight of snow.

4. Navigation Skills: Navigating through subarctic landscapes can be challenging, especially when landmarks are obscured by snow and ice. Carry a reliable compass and map, and learn how to use them effectively. Familiarize yourself with basic orienteering techniques, such as triangulation and dead-reckoning, to navigate without relying solely on electronic devices. Consider taking a navigation course or hiring a local guide if you’re unfamiliar with the region.

5. Food and Water Procurement: In subarctic regions, finding food and water sources can be difficult. Learn how to identify safe sources of water, such as flowing streams or melted snow. Consider carrying a portable water filter to remove impurities. For food, study the local flora and fauna to determine edible plants and safe hunting practices. Always adhere to local regulations and ethical guidelines when hunting or foraging.

6. Cold Weather First Aid: Understanding how to treat cold-related injuries and illnesses is crucial in the subarctic. Learn how to recognize and manage conditions like frostbite and hypothermia. Carry a well-stocked first aid kit specifically tailored for cold weather emergencies. Consider taking a wilderness first aid course to enhance your knowledge and preparedness.

Surviving in the subarctic requires a combination of practical skills, preparation, and respect for the environment. By mastering these essential skills, you can embark on your subarctic adventure with confidence and enjoy the breathtaking beauty of these challenging regions. Stay safe, stay warm, and embrace the wild wonders of the subarctic!
