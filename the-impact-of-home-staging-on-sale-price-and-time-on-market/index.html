The Impact of Home Staging on Sale Price and Time on Market


When it comes to selling a home, first impressions matter. Home staging has emerged as a powerful tool for sellers looking to maximize their property’s appeal and attract potential buyers. But what exactly is home staging, and how does it impact the sale price and time on the market? In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the ins and outs of home staging and its effects on the real estate transaction process.

What is Home Staging?

Home staging is the process of preparing a property for sale by strategically arranging furniture, decor, and other elements to showcase its best features and create an inviting atmosphere. The goal of home staging is to help buyers envision themselves living in the space and to highlight the property’s potential.

The Impact on Sale Price

Numerous studies have shown that staged homes tend to sell for higher prices than non-staged homes. According to the National Association of Realtors (NAR), staged homes typically sell for 1% to 5% more than unstaged homes. This increase in sale price can translate to thousands of dollars in additional profit for sellers.

But why do staged homes command higher prices? The answer lies in perception and presentation. Staged homes appear more attractive, well-maintained, and move-in ready, which can lead to increased buyer interest and competition. Additionally, staging helps to highlight the property’s best features and potential, allowing buyers to envision themselves living in the space and emotionally connect with the property.

The Impact on Time on Market

In addition to boosting sale prices, home staging can also help reduce the time a property spends on the market. Staged homes tend to sell faster than non-staged homes, often spending less time languishing on the market and receiving more offers.

According to the Real Estate Staging Association (RESA), staged homes spend 73% less time on the market on average compared to non-staged homes. This reduced time on the market can be attributed to the increased appeal and marketability of staged homes, which attract more interested buyers and lead to quicker sales.

Key Considerations for Home Staging

While home staging offers numerous benefits, it’s essential to approach the process strategically to maximize its effectiveness. Here are some key considerations for sellers:

Professional vs. DIY Staging: While some sellers opt to stage their homes themselves, hiring a professional stager can yield superior results. Professional stagers have the expertise and resources to create compelling, market-ready spaces that appeal to a wide range of buyers.

Targeted Staging: Not all rooms require staging, and sellers should prioritize staging areas that have the most significant impact on buyers, such as the living room, kitchen, master bedroom, and bathrooms. Targeted staging allows sellers to allocate their resources effectively and focus on areas that will yield the highest return on investment.

Neutralizing Decor: When staging a home, it’s essential to appeal to a broad audience of potential buyers. This often means removing personal items, decluttering, and neutralizing decor to create a blank canvas that allows buyers to envision themselves living in the space.

Curbside Appeal: The exterior of a home plays a crucial role in shaping buyers’ perceptions and first impressions. Sellers should invest in enhancing curb appeal by maintaining the landscaping, repairing any exterior damage, and adding inviting touches such as potted plants or a fresh coat of paint.

Conclusion

Home staging has a significant impact on both the sale price and time on the market of a property. By strategically showcasing a home’s best features and creating an inviting atmosphere, sellers can attract more buyers, command higher prices, and sell their homes faster. Whether you’re preparing to sell your home or working with clients as a real estate agent, home staging is a valuable investment that can pay dividends in the form of a quicker sale and increased profit.
