The Survivalist's Library: Essential Books for Outdoor Living


Are you an outdoor enthusiast looking to enhance your survival skills? Whether you’re an experienced survivalist or just starting your journey into outdoor living, having the right knowledge is crucial. One of the best ways to acquire the necessary skills is by building a survivalist’s library, filled with essential books that cover various aspects of outdoor living. In this blog post, we will explore some must-have books for your survivalist library, covering topics such as wilderness survival, foraging, shelter-building, and more.

1. “SAS Survival Handbook” by John ‘Lofty’ Wiseman

Considered a classic in the survivalist community, the “SAS Survival Handbook” is a comprehensive guide that covers a wide range of survival skills. From building shelters to finding food and water sources, this book provides valuable knowledge that can be applied in any outdoor setting. The author, John ‘Lofty’ Wiseman, draws from his extensive experience as a former British Special Air Service (SAS) soldier, making this book a trusted resource for survivalists worldwide.

2. “Bushcraft 101: A Field Guide to the Art of Wilderness Survival” by Dave Canterbury

Written by renowned survival expert Dave Canterbury, “Bushcraft 101” is a practical guide that focuses on the art of wilderness survival. This book covers essential skills such as fire-making, shelter construction, foraging, and navigation. Canterbury’s easy-to-follow instructions and detailed illustrations make this book a valuable resource for both beginners and experienced outdoor enthusiasts.

3. “Edible Wild Plants: A North American Field Guide” by Thomas Elias and Peter Dykeman

When it comes to outdoor living, knowing which plants are safe to eat is essential. “Edible Wild Plants” is a comprehensive field guide that helps you identify and harvest edible plants found in North America. The book includes detailed descriptions, color photographs, and information on the nutritional value and potential uses of each plant. Whether you’re foraging for food or simply expanding your knowledge of the natural world, this guide is a valuable addition to your survivalist library.

4. “The Complete Guide to Hunting, Butchering, and Cooking Wild Game” by Steven Rinella

For those interested in hunting and gathering their own food, “The Complete Guide to Hunting, Butchering, and Cooking Wild Game” is a must-have resource. Written by renowned outdoorsman and host of the “MeatEater” television series, Steven Rinella, this book covers everything from selecting the right firearms and gear to field dressing, butchering, and cooking wild game. Rinella’s expertise and passion for the wilderness shine through in this comprehensive guide.

5. “Primitive Technology: A Survivalist’s Guide to Building Tools, Shelters, and More in the Wild” by John Plant

If you’re interested in primitive survival skills and learning to live off the land with minimal tools, “Primitive Technology” is the book for you. Author John Plant, known for his popular YouTube channel of the same name, shares step-by-step instructions on how to build tools, shelters, and other essential items using only natural materials. This book offers a unique perspective on survival and provides valuable insights into ancient techniques that are still relevant today.

Conclusion

Building a survivalist’s library is an investment in your outdoor living skills and knowledge. The books mentioned in this blog post are just a starting point, but they cover a wide range of essential topics for survivalists. Remember, reading about survival is just the first step; practice and hands-on experience are equally important. So, grab a book, head outdoors, and start honing your skills as you embark on your own outdoor adventures.
