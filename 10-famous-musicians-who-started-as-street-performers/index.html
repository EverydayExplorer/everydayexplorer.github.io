10 Famous Musicians Who Started as Street Performers


Introduction Street performers have always held a special place in the world of music. Many famous musicians had humble beginnings, starting their careers by busking on the streets. Despite the lack of resources, these talented individuals managed to captivate audiences with their raw talent and perseverance. In this blog post, we will explore the stories of 10 famous musicians who started as street performers and went on to achieve global stardom.

1. Ed Sheeran One of the most successful musicians of our time, Ed Sheeran began his career as a street performer in London. Armed with his guitar and a loop pedal, he would entertain passersby with his soulful voice and heartfelt lyrics. Sheeran’s persistence paid off as he built a loyal fan base and eventually signed a record deal, skyrocketing him to fame with hits like ‘Shape of You’ and ‘Thinking Out Loud.’

2. Tracy Chapman Famous for her powerful voice and profound lyrics, Tracy Chapman started her music career as a street performer in Harvard Square, Cambridge. Often seen playing her acoustic guitar and singing on the streets, Chapman’s talent attracted the attention of a record label, leading to the release of her iconic debut album, ‘Fast Car.’

3. Seal Before becoming a Grammy-winning artist, Seal used his incredible vocal range to captivate audiences as a street performer in London. Known for his soulful voice and charismatic stage presence, Seal’s career took off after he released his self-titled debut album, featuring the hit song ‘Kiss from a Rose.’

4. Passenger English singer-songwriter Michael David Rosenberg, better known as Passenger, began his musical journey as a street performer in Brighton, England. With his emotional and introspective songs, he quickly gained popularity and eventually achieved international success with his chart-topping ballad, ‘Let Her Go.’

5. BB King The legendary blues guitarist BB King, whose distinctive playing style influenced generations of musicians, started his career by playing on street corners in Memphis. King’s stellar guitar skills and heartfelt voice eventually brought him fame, earning him the nickname ‘The King of Blues.’

6. Robin Williams Before his successful acting career, the late Robin Williams started his creative journey as a street performer in San Francisco. Armed with his quick wit and comedic talents, Williams entertained both locals and tourists alike, eventually transitioning to television and film.

7. Benjamin Clementine British singer-songwriter Benjamin Clementine rose to prominence after many years of busking on the streets of Paris. Known for his captivating voice and emotionally charged performances, he won the prestigious Mercury Prize in 2015 for his debut album, ‘At Least for Now.’

8. KT Tunstall Scottish singer-songwriter KT Tunstall began her music career as a street performer in Edinburgh. With her unique blend of folk and pop, she gained recognition and achieved international success with her debut album, ‘Eye to the Telescope,’ which featured the hit song ‘Black Horse and the Cherry Tree.’

9. Jewel Jewel Kilcher, known by her stage name Jewel, honed her skills as a street performer in San Diego. Her heartfelt folk songs and poetic lyrics attracted audiences, leading to the release of her multi-platinum debut album, ‘Pieces of You,’ which featured the hits ‘Foolish Games’ and ‘You Were Meant for Me.’

10. Rod Stewart Before becoming a rock icon, Rod Stewart showcased his musical talent as a street performer in London. Stewart’s distinctive raspy voice and dynamic stage presence eventually led him to become one of the best-selling musicians of all time, with hits like ‘Maggie May’ and ‘Do Ya Think I’m Sexy?’

Conclusion Street performers often face numerous challenges on their journey to success, but these 10 musicians prove that talent and determination can lead to incredible achievements. From humble beginnings, they managed to captivate audiences worldwide and leave an indelible mark on the music industry. So, the next time you encounter a street performer showcasing their skills, remember that they might just be the next big thing in music.
