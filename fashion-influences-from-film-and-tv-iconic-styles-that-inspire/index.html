Fashion Influences from Film and TV: Iconic Styles that Inspire


Film and television are not only sources of entertainment, but also powerful influencers of fashion. Over the years, countless iconic styles from the big and small screens have made a lasting impact on the fashion industry and continue to inspire trends today. From the glamorous Old Hollywood era to the edgy streetwear of modern TV shows, the fashion influences from film and TV are undeniable.

One of the most influential eras in film fashion is undoubtedly the Golden Age of Hollywood in the 1920s to 1950s. Actresses like Audrey Hepburn, Marilyn Monroe, and Grace Kelly defined elegance and sophistication with their on-screen wardrobes. Hepburn’s iconic little black dress from ‘Breakfast at Tiffany’s’ or Kelly’s timeless style in Alfred Hitchcock’s ‘Rear Window’ are still referenced and emulated by fashion enthusiasts today. These actresses were not only stars on screen, but also style icons who set trends that have stood the test of time.

Moving into the 1960s and 1970s, films like ‘Bonnie and Clyde’ and ‘Saturday Night Fever’ brought a new wave of fashion influences. Faye Dunaway’s berets and tweed suits in ‘Bonnie and Clyde’ captured the rebellious spirit of the era and still inspire today’s modern fashion. Meanwhile, John Travolta’s white suit and disco-inspired outfits in ‘Saturday Night Fever’ influenced the disco fashion trends of the time. The wide-leg trousers, platform shoes, and shiny, sequined outfits from the film are still referenced in contemporary fashion collections.

Moving on to more recent times, television has become a major source of fashion inspiration. Shows like ‘Sex and the City’ and ‘Gossip Girl’ have had a tremendous impact on women’s fashion. ‘Sex and the City’ showcased a wide range of styles, with each character having her own distinct fashion personality. Carrie Bradshaw, played by Sarah Jessica Parker, was known for her eclectic, avant-garde style, inspiring women to experiment with bold and unexpected fashion choices. Similarly, ‘Gossip Girl’ portrayed a world of luxury and high fashion, with characters like Blair Waldorf and Serena Van Der Woodsen becoming style icons in their own right. The Preppy, Upper East Side aesthetic of the show influenced a generation of young fashionistas, with headbands, statement jewelry, and designer clothing gaining popularity.

Superhero movies and TV shows have also made a significant impact on fashion trends. From the classic ‘Superman’ and ‘Batman’ films to the Marvel Cinematic Universe, superhero costumes have become a popular source of inspiration for cosplay and streetwear alike. The sleek, form-fitting suits worn by heroes like Iron Man and Black Widow have influenced the athleisure and minimalist trends of recent years. Additionally, the reinvention of Wonder Woman’s costume in the 2017 film brought a refreshing and empowering take on female superhero fashion.

In conclusion, the fashion influences from film and TV are far-reaching and have left an indelible mark on the industry. From the glamorous styles of Old Hollywood to the edgy streetwear featured in modern shows, these iconic styles continue to inspire and shape current fashion trends. Whether it’s a classic Hollywood movie, a period drama, or a contemporary TV series, the fashion choices of characters on screen have the power to influence and captivate audiences, making fashion an integral part of the storytelling process. As we continue to look to the big and small screens for entertainment and inspiration, we can expect the worlds of fashion and film to remain deeply intertwined for years to come.
