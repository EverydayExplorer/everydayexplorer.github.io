Wilderness Survival Skills for LGBTQ+ Individuals: Inclusivity Outdoors


Nature has always been a place of solace and adventure for many individuals. The wilderness provides an escape from the everyday hustle and bustle, allowing us to reconnect with ourselves and the world around us. However, for LGBTQ+ individuals, venturing into the wilderness may come with its own unique set of challenges. In this blog post, we will explore wilderness survival skills specifically tailored to the LGBTQ+ community, promoting inclusivity and ensuring that everyone can enjoy the great outdoors.

1. Safety and Awareness:
When venturing into the wilderness, safety should always be a top priority. LGBTQ+ individuals may face additional safety concerns due to potential discrimination or harassment. It’s important to research and choose LGBTQ+ friendly camping sites and outdoor organizations that prioritize inclusivity. Additionally, staying aware of your surroundings and practicing situational awareness can help mitigate potential risks.

2. Navigation and Orientation:
Knowing how to navigate your way through the wilderness is crucial for any outdoor enthusiast. LGBTQ+ individuals may find comfort in joining LGBTQ+ outdoor groups or attending LGBTQ+ inclusive wilderness workshops. These resources can provide a sense of community and camaraderie, while also teaching valuable navigation and orientation skills.

3. Shelter and Campsite Selection:
Creating a safe and inclusive campsite is essential for LGBTQ+ individuals. When selecting a campsite, consider factors such as privacy, proximity to water sources, and accessibility. Look for LGBTQ+ friendly campsites or remote areas where you can set up camp without fear of discrimination or harassment.

4. Fire Building and Cooking:
Mastering the art of fire building and cooking is not only practical but also a great way to bond with fellow outdoor enthusiasts. LGBTQ+ individuals can connect with LGBTQ+ inclusive outdoor cooking classes or LGBTQ+ camping groups to learn these skills while fostering a sense of community.

5. First Aid and Emergency Preparedness:
Accidents can happen in the wilderness, and being prepared is crucial. Knowing basic first aid skills, such as treating cuts, burns, and sprains, can make a significant difference in emergencies. LGBTQ+ individuals can also benefit from LGBTQ+ specific first aid workshops, where they can learn essential skills while being surrounded by a supportive and understanding community.

6. Leave No Trace Principles:
Respecting and preserving the environment is essential for all outdoor enthusiasts. LGBTQ+ individuals can engage in LGBTQ+ inclusive wilderness clean-up events, where they can contribute to environmental conservation while connecting with like-minded individuals. Remember to follow the Leave No Trace principles and leave the wilderness as pristine as you found it.

By embracing inclusivity and promoting LGBTQ+ specific wilderness survival skills, we can ensure that everyone feels welcome and safe in the great outdoors. Whether you identify as LGBTQ+ or as an ally, it is our collective responsibility to create an inclusive outdoor community that celebrates diversity. Let’s explore the wilderness together, embracing the beauty of nature and the beauty of our differences.
