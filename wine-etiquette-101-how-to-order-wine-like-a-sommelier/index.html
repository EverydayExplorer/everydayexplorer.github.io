Wine Etiquette 101: How to Order Wine Like a Sommelier


Wine has long been a symbol of sophistication and elegance, enjoyed by many for its complex flavors and cultural significance. Whether you’re at a fancy restaurant or hosting a dinner party at home, knowing how to order wine like a sommelier can elevate your dining experience and impress those around you. In this comprehensive guide, we will cover everything you need to know about wine etiquette 101.

First and foremost, it’s essential to understand the basics of wine tasting and selection. When perusing a wine list, take note of the different categories of wines available, such as red, white, rosé, and sparkling. Familiarize yourself with popular grape varieties and regions, so you can confidently make informed decisions when ordering. If you’re unsure about a specific wine, don’t hesitate to ask the sommelier for recommendations based on your preferences.

When ordering wine at a restaurant, there are several key etiquette tips to keep in mind. Always defer to the host or lead guest to select the wine, unless they specifically ask for your input. If you’re hosting the meal, take charge of selecting the wine pairings based on the menu and guest preferences. Remember to consider the occasion and budget when choosing a bottle of wine, as prices can vary significantly.

One of the most crucial aspects of ordering wine like a sommelier is properly communicating your preferences to the server or sommelier. Be specific about the style of wine you enjoy, such as dry or sweet, full-bodied or light, and fruity or earthy. Providing this information will help the sommelier recommend a wine that aligns with your taste preferences. Additionally, don’t be afraid to ask questions about the wine list or seek guidance on food and wine pairings – sommeliers are there to assist you and enhance your dining experience.

Another essential aspect of wine etiquette is handling the wine bottle and glass correctly. When presented with the wine bottle at the table, allow the sommelier to pour a small sample for you to taste. Swirl the wine in your glass, take a sniff to assess the aroma, and then taste the wine to ensure it meets your expectations. If you approve, the sommelier will proceed to pour glasses for the rest of the table. Remember to hold your wine glass by the stem to prevent warming the wine with your hands and to better observe the wine’s color and clarity.

In addition to ordering and tasting wine like a sommelier, it’s essential to understand proper wine service etiquette. When serving wine at a dinner party, always start with lighter wines before progressing to heavier or more intense varieties. Ensure that each guest has a clean glass for each wine served and offer to refill glasses throughout the meal. Properly storing and serving wine at the correct temperature can also enhance the flavors and aromas of the wine, so be mindful of these factors when hosting a wine-centric event.

In conclusion, mastering wine etiquette 101 and learning how to order wine like a sommelier can elevate your dining experiences and showcase your appreciation for fine wine. By understanding the basics of wine tasting, selection, communication, and service, you can confidently navigate wine lists, make informed decisions, and impress your companions with your knowledge and sophistication. Cheers to expanding your wine knowledge and enhancing your enjoyment of this timeless beverage!
