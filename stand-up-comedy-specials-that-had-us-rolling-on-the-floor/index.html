Stand-Up Comedy Specials That Had Us Rolling on the Floor


Stand-up comedy has been a source of entertainment for decades, and comedy specials have become a popular way for comedians to showcase their talent and connect with audiences on a much larger scale. These specials, whether they are released on major television networks or streaming platforms, have given us some of the most memorable and laugh-out-loud moments in comedy history. In this blog post, we will take a look at stand-up comedy specials that have had us rolling on the floor with laughter.

  1. Eddie Murphy: Delirious Considered one of the greatest stand-up comedy specials of all time, ‘Delirious’ catapulted Eddie Murphy to superstardom. Filmed in 1983, this special features Murphy’s trademark raw and raunchy style, tackling topics such as racism, relationships, and his childhood. From his iconic red leather suit to hilarious impressions of his family members, this comedy special still holds up today and continues to make audiences laugh uncontrollably.

  2. Dave Chappelle: Killin’ Them Softly Known for his sharp wit and social commentary, Dave Chappelle’s ‘Killin’ Them Softly’ is a masterpiece in the world of stand-up comedy. Filmed in 2000, Chappelle takes on a wide range of topics such as race, politics, and relationships with his trademark humor and intelligence. His ability to tackle sensitive subjects with a unique blend of honesty and comedy is what makes this special a must-watch for comedy enthusiasts.

  3. Hannah Gadsby: Nanette ‘Hannah Gadsby: Nanette’ is a groundbreaking special that challenges the traditional boundaries of stand-up comedy. Released in 2018, Gadsby tackles important themes such as gender, sexuality, trauma, and mental health in a way that is both funny and deeply impactful. This special goes beyond the realm of traditional jokes and provides a thought-provoking and emotional experience that has resonated with audiences worldwide.

  4. John Mulaney: Kid Gorgeous at Radio City John Mulaney’s ‘Kid Gorgeous at Radio City’ is a masterclass in storytelling and observational comedy. Filmed in 2017, Mulaney takes the audience on a hilarious journey through his childhood, marriage, and life as a comedian. His ability to craft intricate and relatable anecdotes, combined with his impeccable timing and delivery, makes this special a must-watch for anyone in need of a good laugh.

  5. Ali Wong: Baby Cobra Ali Wong’s ‘Baby Cobra’ is a fearless and unapologetic comedy special that tackles topics like pregnancy, motherhood, and feminism with both humor and honesty. Released in 2016, Wong’s sharp wit and unfiltered perspective provide an entertaining and refreshing take on these often-taboo subjects. Her candid delivery and relatable stories make this special an instant classic.

  6. Kevin Hart: Laugh at My Pain Kevin Hart’s ‘Laugh at My Pain’ is a high-energy and hilarious comedy special that showcases Hart’s unique comedic style. Filmed in 2011, Hart’s ability to connect with his audience through relatable stories and exaggerated physical comedy is what makes this special so memorable. His infectious energy and relatable tales of personal struggle had audiences in stitches from start to finish.

  7. Trevor Noah: Son of Patricia Released in 2018, Trevor Noah’s ‘Son of Patricia’ is a brilliant blend of storytelling, humor, and social commentary. Noah, known for his role as the host of ‘The Daily Show,’ shares his experiences growing up in South Africa, his unique perspective on American culture, and his thoughts on race and identity. This special is a testament to Noah’s ability to navigate difficult subjects with intelligence and comedic grace.

These comedy specials have provided us with countless hours of laughter and entertainment. Each comedian brings their own unique style and perspective to the stage, making these specials a testament to the artistry and brilliance of stand-up comedy. Whether you’re a fan of raw and raunchy humor, sharp social commentary, or relatable storytelling, these specials are sure to leave you rolling on the floor with laughter.

In conclusion, stand-up comedy specials have given us some of the most unforgettable moments in comedy history. From Eddie Murphy’s iconic red leather suit in ‘Delirious’ to Dave Chappelle’s thought-provoking social commentary in ‘Killin’ Them Softly,’ these specials have made us laugh, think, and appreciate the artistry of comedians. So sit back, relax, and prepare to roll on the floor with laughter as you dive into these comedy specials.
