Books That Inspire Travel: Literary Journeys Around the World


There’s nothing quite like the feeling of getting lost in a good book, especially when the story takes you on a journey to far-off lands and exotic locales. For those with a love of both reading and travel, there are certain books that can inspire a deep sense of wanderlust and a desire to explore the world. From the bustling streets of Tokyo to the rugged landscapes of the Scottish Highlands, the following literary works have the power to transport readers to distant corners of the globe without ever leaving the comfort of their own home.

1. ‘Eat, Pray, Love’ by Elizabeth Gilbert
This memoir takes readers on a transformative journey through Italy, India, and Indonesia as the author sets out to find herself and discover the true meaning of happiness. From savoring mouth-watering pasta dishes in Rome to meditating in the serene ashrams of India, this book is sure to ignite a sense of adventure and self-discovery in anyone who reads it.

2. ‘The Alchemist’ by Paulo Coelho
A timeless tale of self-discovery and following one’s dreams, ‘The Alchemist’ follows the journey of Santiago, a shepherd boy who travels from his homeland in Spain to the Egyptian desert in search of treasure. Along the way, Santiago encounters a cast of fascinating characters and experiences the beauty of the desert landscape, making this book a must-read for anyone yearning to embark on a soul-stirring adventure.

3. ‘Wild’ by Cheryl Strayed
In this powerful memoir, the author recounts her solo hike along the Pacific Crest Trail, a challenging and breathtaking journey that spans over one thousand miles. As Cheryl confronts her inner demons and grapples with the untamed wilderness, readers are taken on a visceral and awe-inspiring expedition through the rugged terrain of the American West.

4. ‘Shantaram’ by Gregory David Roberts
Set in the bustling city of Mumbai, ‘Shantaram’ is a sprawling epic that follows the adventures of an escaped Australian convict who finds himself immersed in the vibrant and chaotic world of India. With its vivid descriptions of Mumbai’s streets, slums, and hidden corners, this novel is a captivating exploration of the city’s rich tapestry of culture, history, and humanity.

5. ‘Outlander’ by Diana Gabaldon
This beloved series of historical fiction novels whisks readers away to the rugged and romantic landscapes of 18th century Scotland. Filled with vivid descriptions of the Scottish Highlands and the bustling streets of Edinburgh, ‘Outlander’ is a captivating blend of adventure, history, and romance that will leave readers yearning to visit the breathtaking landscapes that serve as the backdrop for the story.

Whether you’re seeking inspiration for your next travel adventure or simply looking to escape into a different world through the pages of a book, these literary works offer a passport to a diverse array of destinations. From the sun-drenched streets of Italy to the misty moors of Scotland, the power of storytelling has the ability to transport us to far-off lands and ignite a sense of wanderlust that lingers long after the final page has been turned. So, grab a book, pack your bags, and embark on a literary journey around the world - the adventure awaits!
