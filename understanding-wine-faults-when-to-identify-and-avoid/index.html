Understanding Wine Faults: When to Identify and Avoid


Welcome to our blog, where we uncover the fascinating world of understanding wine faults and how to identify and avoid them! Whether you’re a seasoned wine connoisseur or just beginning to explore the delightful realm of wines, this article will be your comprehensive guide to recognizing and steering clear of common wine flaws.

Wine, with its myriad of complex flavors and nuances, can sometimes exhibit faults that affect its taste, aroma, and overall quality. It’s important to note that not all wine faults render the wine undrinkable or spoiled. In fact, some faults can even be appreciated by certain enthusiasts as unique characteristics. Nevertheless, having a basic understanding of wine faults will equip you to make informed decisions when selecting and enjoying your favorite vino.

1. Cork Taint or ‘Corked’ Wine:
One of the most common wine faults encountered is cork taint, caused by a compound called TCA (2,4,6-trichloroanisole). This fault occurs when natural cork interacts with chlorine compounds present in the winery environment. A ‘corked’ wine exhibits musty, moldy aromas, and a flat, muted taste. If you notice these characteristics, it’s likely you’ve encountered cork taint and should consider returning or exchanging the bottle.

2. Oxidation:
Oxidation, the process by which wine is exposed to too much oxygen, can result in a wine fault. When a wine oxidizes, it loses its freshness and vibrant flavors, turning dull and flat. If you detect notes of vinegar or sherry-like aromas, it may indicate oxidation. Proper storage, with airtight seals and temperature control, can help prevent this flaw.

3. Reduction:
On the opposite end of the spectrum, reduction occurs when wine lacks exposure to enough oxygen. This can lead to off-putting aromas reminiscent of rotten eggs or burnt rubber. A simple solution is to decant the wine, allowing it to breathe and release some of the volatile compounds causing the reduction.

4. Volatile Acidity (VA):
Volatile acidity refers to the presence of acetic acid in wine, which can cause a vinegary aroma and taste. While low levels of VA can contribute to complexity and enhance certain wine styles, excessive amounts can be considered a fault. High VA is often associated with poor winemaking practices or microbial contamination.

5. Brettanomyces:
Brettanomyces, commonly referred to as ‘Brett,’ is a yeast strain that can infect wine during production. In moderate amounts, it can add complexity and earthy aromas, but in excess, it can lead to faults. Brett can give wine a barnyard-like odor or medicinal taste, which some wine enthusiasts enjoy, while others find it off-putting.

6. Sulfur Compounds:
Sulfur dioxide (SO2) is an additive used in winemaking to prevent oxidation and microbial growth. However, when used excessively or combined with other compounds, it can result in sulfur-related faults. These faults can manifest as unpleasant odors, such as rotten eggs or burnt matches. Winemakers strive to find the delicate balance between preserving the wine and avoiding sulfur faults.

7. Heat Damage:
Excessive exposure to heat can cause wine faults, altering the wine’s flavor and aging potential. Heat damage often leads to cooked or stewed fruit aromas, flatness on the palate, and a lack of freshness. It’s essential to store wine in a cool, consistent environment to prevent this fault.

By arming yourself with knowledge about wine faults, you can confidently navigate the diverse world of wines and make more informed choices. Remember, wine faults are not always synonymous with undrinkable wine, as personal taste preferences vary. However, being able to identify and understand these faults empowers you to appreciate the true quality and potential of the wines you encounter.

So, the next time you open a bottle of wine, take a moment to observe its aromas and flavors. If something seems amiss, refer back to this guide to help pinpoint the potential fault. Cheers to exploring the world of wines, avoiding common faults, and enjoying the remarkable craftsmanship of winemakers!
