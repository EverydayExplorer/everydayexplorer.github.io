10 Famous Bands That Started in High School


Throughout history, there have been numerous bands that have achieved great success and left a lasting impact on the music industry. Many of these bands started their journey right from their high school days, honing their skills and developing a unique sound that would eventually propel them to stardom. In this blog post, we will take a closer look at 10 famous bands that got their start in high school.

1. **The Beatles**: One of the most iconic bands of all time, The Beatles formed in 1957 while its members were still attending Liverpool’s Quarry Bank High School. John Lennon, Paul McCartney, and George Harrison initially came together as a skiffle group called The Quarrymen and later went on to become the Fab Four, revolutionizing popular music forever.

2. **Nirvana**: Seattle-based grunge band, Nirvana, was formed in 1987 by Kurt Cobain, Krist Novoselic, and Aaron Burckhard while they were attending high school. These influential musicians went on to define a generation with their raw sound and introspective lyrics, making them one of the most celebrated bands of the 90s.

3. **Red Hot Chili Peppers**: Another band that originated in high school halls is the Red Hot Chili Peppers. Formed in 1983 in Los Angeles, the band initially consisted of Anthony Kiedis, Flea (Michael Balzary), Hillel Slovak, and Jack Irons. Their unique blend of funk, rock, and rap earned them worldwide recognition and a spot in the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

4. **Radiohead**: The British rock band, Radiohead, was born out of Abingdon School in Oxfordshire. The group, initially called On a Friday, formed in 1985 and consisted of Thom Yorke, Jonny Greenwood, Colin Greenwood, Ed O’Brien, and Phil Selway. Radiohead’s unconventional sound and thought-provoking lyrics catapulted them to critical acclaim and commercial success.

5. **AC/DC**: The Australian hard rock band AC/DC was founded by brothers Malcolm and Angus Young in 1973. The Young brothers were just teenagers when they started playing together and soon recruited vocalist Bon Scott. Known for their high-energy performances and timeless hits, AC/DC became one of the best-selling bands in the world.

6. **Guns N’ Roses**: Axl Rose and Izzy Stradlin formed Guns N’ Roses in 1985 while attending high school in Los Angeles. The band’s explosive combination of hard rock, punk, and blues resonated with fans worldwide. With iconic tracks like ‘Sweet Child o’ Mine’ and ‘Welcome to the Jungle,’ Guns N’ Roses became one of the most influential bands of the late 80s and early 90s.

7. **U2**: U2, one of the most successful rock bands of all time, was formed when its members were still in high school in Dublin, Ireland. Paul Hewson (Bono), David Howell Evans (The Edge), Adam Clayton, and Larry Mullen Jr. joined forces in 1976. U2’s anthemic sound and socially conscious lyrics have earned them a dedicated fanbase and countless accolades over the years.

8. **The Beach Boys**: The Beach Boys, known for their harmonious tunes and surfer image, were originally a group of friends from Hawthorne High School in California. Brian Wilson, Dennis Wilson, Carl Wilson, Mike Love, and Al Jardine formed the band in 1961. Their songs, such as ‘Surfin’ USA’ and ‘Good Vibrations,’ helped define the sounds of the 60s and 70s.

9. **The Ramones**: Considered one of the pioneers of punk rock, The Ramones formed in 1974 in Forest Hills, Queens, New York. Although not formal high school classmates, Joey Ramone, Johnny Ramone, Dee Dee Ramone, and Tommy Ramone came together in their youth, creating a fast and aggressive sound that would have a lasting impact on the punk movement.

10. **The Rolling Stones**: While not exclusively a high school band, The Rolling Stones came together while its members were attending various schools in London. Mick Jagger, Keith Richards, Brian Jones, Ian Stewart, Mick Taylor, and Charlie Watts all met in the early 1960s and went on to become one of the greatest rock bands in history.

These are just a few examples of the countless bands that started in high school and went on to achieve worldwide fame. The shared experiences and creative collaboration during those formative years undoubtedly helped shape their sound and set them on a path to musical greatness. So, the next time you’re attending a high school music performance, remember that you may be witnessing the birth of the next big thing in the music world!
