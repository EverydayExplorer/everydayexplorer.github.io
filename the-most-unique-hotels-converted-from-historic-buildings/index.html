The Most Unique Hotels Converted from Historic Buildings


When it comes to choosing a hotel for your next vacation, why settle for the ordinary when you can stay in a truly unique and historic building? The world is full of hotels that have been converted from historic buildings, each with its own story to tell. From former palaces to old factories, these hotels offer a glimpse into the past while providing modern-day luxury and comfort. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most unique hotels converted from historic buildings that are sure to satisfy your wanderlust.

1. The Taj Lake Palace, Udaipur, India:
Located in the heart of Udaipur’s famous Lake Pichola, The Taj Lake Palace is a stunning hotel that was once a royal palace. Built in 1746, the palace was originally used as a summer retreat for the royal family. Today, it offers luxurious accommodations with breathtaking views of the lake and the surrounding Aravalli hills. With its intricate architecture, beautiful gardens, and opulent interiors, staying at The Taj Lake Palace feels like stepping into a fairytale.

2. The Liberty Hotel, Boston, USA:
If you’re looking for a unique hotel in the heart of Boston, look no further than The Liberty Hotel. This elegant property was formerly the Charles Street Jail, which operated from 1851 to 1990. The hotel has successfully integrated modern amenities while preserving the historic charm of the building. With its spacious rooms, high-end restaurants, and a rooftop bar offering panoramic views of the city, The Liberty Hotel is a perfect blend of luxury and history.

3. The Belmond Hotel das Cataratas, Iguazu National Park, Brazil:
Located just steps away from the world-famous Iguazu Falls, the Belmond Hotel das Cataratas is a historic hotel that offers an unforgettable experience. Originally built in 1939, the hotel is a classic example of Portuguese colonial architecture. Staying at this hotel allows you to experience the beauty of the falls after all the tourists have left for the day. With its lush gardens, elegant rooms, and a swimming pool overlooking the falls, the Belmond Hotel das Cataratas promises a truly unique stay.

4. The Four Seasons Hotel Gresham Palace, Budapest, Hungary:
Situated on the banks of the Danube River, The Four Seasons Hotel Gresham Palace is a remarkable example of Art Nouveau architecture. Built in 1906 as an office space, the building was later transformed into a luxury hotel in 2004. The hotel’s interior is a stunning display of intricate details, including stained glass windows, mosaics, and ironwork. With its impeccable service, luxurious amenities, and panoramic views of the Danube and the Budapest skyline, The Four Seasons Hotel Gresham Palace offers a truly exceptional experience.

5. The Alila Fort Bishangarh, Jaipur, India:
Perched atop a granite hill, The Alila Fort Bishangarh is a fortress turned hotel that offers a unique blend of history and luxury. Originally built in the 18th century, the property has been meticulously restored to its former glory, with all modern amenities seamlessly integrated. The hotel’s 59 suites and rooms feature traditional Rajasthani decor, and the rooftop restaurant offers panoramic views of the Aravalli hills. Staying at The Alila Fort Bishangarh allows you to step back in time while enjoying top-notch hospitality.

6. The Tambo del Inka, Sacred Valley, Peru:
Nestled in the heart of Peru’s Sacred Valley, The Tambo del Inka is a luxurious hotel that combines modern comfort with ancient Incan heritage. The property was built on the site of a 16th-century colonial hacienda and incorporates traditional Incan design elements. The hotel offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains, a world-class spa, and access to the nearby Machu Picchu. Staying at The Tambo del Inka allows you to immerse yourself in the rich history and natural beauty of the region.

In conclusion, if you’re looking for a hotel experience that goes beyond the ordinary, consider staying in one of these unique hotels converted from historic buildings. From royal palaces to former jails, each of these properties offers a glimpse into the past while providing luxurious accommodations and modern amenities. Whether you choose to stay in India, the USA, Brazil, Hungary, or Peru, these hotels are sure to leave you with unforgettable memories. So why settle for a standard hotel when you can have a truly unique experience?
