Books for the Wanderlust: 10 Travel Memoirs That Take You Around the World


‘Embarking on a journey through the pages of a travel memoir is like stepping into a world of wanderlust and adventure. These captivating narratives transport readers to far-flung destinations, immersing them in the sights, sounds, and cultures of different corners of the globe. If you’re a travel enthusiast seeking literary inspiration, here are 10 travel memoirs that will ignite your wanderlust and leave you yearning for new horizons.

1. ‘Eat, Pray, Love’ by Elizabeth Gilbert: Join Gilbert as she embarks on a soul-searching journey across Italy, India, and Indonesia, discovering the true meaning of love, spirituality, and self-discovery.

2. ‘Wild’ by Cheryl Strayed: Follow Strayed’s transformative hike along the Pacific Crest Trail, as she confronts her inner demons and finds solace in the untamed wilderness.

3. ‘Into the Wild’ by Jon Krakauer: Delve into the gripping true story of Christopher McCandless, who abandoned civilization to live in the Alaskan wilderness, seeking a deeper connection with nature.

4. ‘The Alchemist’ by Paulo Coelho: Although not strictly a memoir, Coelho’s enchanting tale of a young shepherd’s journey to find his personal legend resonates with wanderers and dreamers alike.

5. ‘Tracks’ by Robyn Davidson: Join Davidson as she treks across the Australian Outback with her camels, defying societal norms and embracing the solitude of the desert.

6. ‘A Walk in the Woods’ by Bill Bryson: Laugh out loud as Bryson recounts his humorous and often challenging attempt to hike the Appalachian Trail, showcasing the beauty and quirks of America’s great outdoors.

7. ‘In a Sunburned Country’ by Bill Bryson: Join Bryson on his hilarious and informative journey through the vast and diverse landscapes of Australia, uncovering its unique wildlife, history, and culture.

8. ‘The Lost City of Z’ by David Grann: Immerse yourself in the gripping account of British explorer Percy Fawcett’s quest to find a mythical city in the Amazon rainforest, a tale of adventure, obsession, and mystery.

9. ‘Travels with Charley’ by John Steinbeck: Accompany Steinbeck and his faithful poodle, Charley, as they embark on a cross-country road trip, capturing the essence of America in the 1960s.

10. ‘Into Thin Air’ by Jon Krakauer: Experience the harrowing firsthand account of Krakauer’s ill-fated Mount Everest expedition, a chilling reminder of the power and unpredictability of nature.

These travel memoirs offer a glimpse into the transformative power of travel, inspiring readers to explore the world and embrace the unknown. Whether you’re seeking tales of self-discovery, thrilling adventures, or cultural immersion, these books will transport you to distant lands and ignite your wanderlust. Happy reading and may your next journey be filled with unforgettable experiences!’
