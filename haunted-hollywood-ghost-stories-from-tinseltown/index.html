Haunted Hollywood: Ghost Stories from Tinseltown


Hollywood: a land of glitz, glamour, and… ghosts? Indeed, behind the camera flashes and star-studded premieres lies another, more spectral side of Hollywood. Over the years, many of Tinseltown’s most famous landmarks and estates have become the subjects of eerie ghost stories and paranormal encounters, making the entertainment capital of the world also one of the most haunted.

The Ghosts of Hollywood’s Golden Age

Perhaps no other place in Hollywood is as synonymous with ghost stories as the Hollywood Roosevelt Hotel. Opened in 1927, this iconic hotel has been a hotspot for celebrity sightings—both living and deceased. It is said that Marilyn Monroe, who once lived in a suite on the property, still haunts the building. Guests and staff report seeing her reflection in a mirror that once hung in her room. Similarly, the spirit of Montgomery Clift is rumored to roam the ninth floor, particularly room 928, where he stayed while filming ‘From Here to Eternity.’ Visitors claim to hear him playing the trumpet or reciting lines from various scripts.

The Haunting of the TCL Chinese Theatre

The TCL Chinese Theatre, a landmark recognized around the world, is famous not only for its celebrity handprints and footprints but also for its spectral activities. The ghost of Victor Kilian, an actor who met a tragic end after a violent burglary in his nearby apartment, is said to wander the forecourt, perhaps revisiting one of his favorite places in Hollywood. Others have reported eerie occurrences during screenings, including cold spots, disembodied voices, and flickering lights—typical ghostly mischief.

Paramount Pictures’ Phantoms

Paramount Studios, one of the oldest operating film studios in the world, has its fair share of ghost stories. The most chilling tales come from Stage 19, which is allegedly haunted by the ghost of Heather O’Rourke, the young actress from the ‘Poltergeist’ film series who died at a tragically young age. Employees report seeing shadows and hearing strange sounds, and some even claim to feel a presence watching them as they work late into the night.

The Ghosts of Griffith Park

Griffith Park, a large municipal park at the eastern end of the Santa Monica Mountains, has more than its fair share of ghost stories. One of the most famous involves the ghost of Peg Entwistle, a young actress who committed suicide by jumping off the Hollywood Sign in 1932. Hikers and tourists often report seeing a disoriented woman dressed in 1930s clothing, who suddenly vanishes when approached. Additionally, the old Griffith Park Zoo, now abandoned, is said to be haunted by the spirits of animals once displayed there, with visitors hearing unexplained animal noises and feeling eerie presences.

The Curson Ave Curse

On Curson Ave in West Hollywood, a string of tragic deaths and misfortunes have led many to believe the street is cursed. The House of Blues, before its demolition, was a hotspot for paranormal activity, with staff witnessing levitating objects and ghostly apparitions in the Voodoo Room. Nearby, at the Comedy Store, comedians and patrons have encountered the spirit of a mobster in the basement, believed to be a remnant from the building’s days as a nightclub with rumored underworld connections.

Warner Bros. Studio’s Haunted Soundstage

Warner Bros. Studio’s Stage 4 is reputedly haunted by the ghost of a film technician who died when a sound baffle fell and struck him on the head. Since then, workers on the soundstage have reported tools moving on their own, strange sounds when no one else is around, and sudden, unexplained drops in temperature—a common indicator of paranormal activity.

Whether these tales are true hauntings or merely the products of Hollywood’s penchant for drama and storytelling, they add a fascinating layer to the history of America’s film capital. These stories offer a glimpse into a Hollywood that, despite its bright lights, is not immune to the shadows that history inevitably casts. For the curious, the thrill-seekers, or the downright skeptical, these haunted hotspots provide a unique lens through which to view the famed city, blending the allure of stardom with the mysteries of the supernatural.
