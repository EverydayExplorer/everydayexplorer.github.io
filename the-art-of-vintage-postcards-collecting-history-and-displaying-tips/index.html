The Art of Vintage Postcards: Collecting, History, and Displaying Tips


In this digital age, we often forget about the charm and beauty of vintage postcards. These small pieces of art not only carry a sense of nostalgia but also serve as valuable historical artifacts. Whether you are a seasoned collector or just starting out, this blog will guide you through the art of vintage postcards, from collecting to history, and provide valuable tips on displaying your collection.

Collecting Vintage Postcards: Where to Start

Collecting vintage postcards can be an exciting and rewarding hobby. If you’re new to the world of postcard collecting, here are a few tips to help you get started:

  1. Define Your Focus: Determine what type of postcards you are interested in collecting. This could be based on a particular theme (such as landscapes, architecture, or holidays) or a specific era (such as the early 20th century or the Victorian era).

  2. Research and Learn: Familiarize yourself with the different types of vintage postcards available. There are various printing techniques, including lithography, etching, and chromolithography, which result in different styles and qualities of postcards. Understanding these techniques will help you appreciate the beauty and value of each card.

  3. Join Collectors’ Communities: Engage with other collectors by joining online forums or attending postcard collectors’ conventions. These communities are a great resource for learning, buying, and selling vintage postcards. You can gain invaluable knowledge and insights from experienced collectors.

  4. Start Small: Begin your collection with a few postcards that capture your interest. Look for local antique shops, flea markets, or online marketplaces to find reasonably priced vintage postcards. As you gain experience and knowledge, you can expand your collection to include rare and valuable pieces.

The History of Vintage Postcards

Vintage postcards offer a unique glimpse into the past, allowing us to explore different cultures, landmarks, and even daily life from bygone eras. Understanding the history behind these postcards can deepen your appreciation for their beauty and significance.

Early Days (Late 19th Century): The concept of sending picture postcards emerged in the late 19th century when photography and printing techniques advanced. These early postcards were known as ‘cartes postales’ and were blank on one side for the message, while the other side featured a photograph or illustration.

Golden Age (1900-1914): The early 20th century witnessed the peak of postcard popularity. During this period, ‘Golden Age’ postcards were created, characterized by vibrant colors and intricate designs. These postcards depicted various themes, including scenic views, local landmarks, holidays, and historical events.

War and Social Change (1914-1945): The outbreak of World War I and subsequent social and political changes had a significant impact on postcard production. While the popularity of postcards continued, their designs became more subdued and reflected the somber nature of the times. Many postcards during World War II were used for propaganda or to boost morale.

Post-War Era (1945-1970s): After World War II, the popularity of postcards declined due to the rise of other forms of communication, such as telephone and television. However, this era saw the rise of ‘chrome’ or ‘modern’ postcards, which featured high-quality color printing and glossy finishes.

Displaying Your Vintage Postcard Collection

Once you’ve built your collection of vintage postcards, it’s time to think about displaying them in a way that showcases their beauty and preserves their condition. Here are a few tips for displaying your postcards:

  1. Use Acid-Free Materials: When mounting or framing your postcards, ensure that you use acid-free materials, such as mounting corners, mats, and acid-free paper. Acidic materials can damage the postcards over time, causing discoloration and deterioration.

  2. Consider a Postcard Album: Postcard albums are specifically designed to store and display postcards. Choose albums with acid-free pages and clear sleeves to protect your postcards while allowing you to easily view and enjoy them. Organize your collection by theme, era, or location.

  3. Frame and Hang: Frame your favorite postcards and hang them on your wall to create a visually stunning display. Choose frames that complement the style and theme of the postcards. Consider grouping them by theme or creating a gallery wall for a more dynamic look.

  4. Rotate Your Collection: To prevent fading and wear, rotate the postcards you display every few months. This allows you to enjoy different parts of your collection while keeping the rest safely stored.

Conclusion

The art of vintage postcards is a fascinating and rewarding hobby that allows us to appreciate the beauty, history, and cultural significance of the past. Whether you are a seasoned collector or just starting out, this guide has provided valuable insights on collecting, the history behind vintage postcards, and tips for displaying your collection. Embrace the charm of vintage postcards and enjoy the journey of collecting these small pieces of art that connect us to a bygone era.
