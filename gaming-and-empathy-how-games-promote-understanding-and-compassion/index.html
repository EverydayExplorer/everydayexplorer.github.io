Gaming and Empathy: How Games Promote Understanding and Compassion


For years, video games have been a hot topic of debate. Many argue that they promote violence and antisocial behavior, while others believe that they can actually have positive effects on players. One of the positive aspects that often goes unnoticed is the ability of games to promote empathy and compassion. In this blog post, we will explore how games can foster understanding and compassion among players, and why it is important to acknowledge this impact.

Firstly, we need to understand what empathy and compassion are. Empathy is the ability to understand and share the feelings of others, while compassion is the concern for the well-being of others and the desire to alleviate their suffering. These qualities are important for building strong social connections and creating a more compassionate society.

Video games provide a unique platform for players to experience different perspectives and situations. Games often present players with complex narratives and characters that require them to make choices that have consequences. By immersing players in these virtual worlds, games can encourage them to think about the impact of their actions and consider the feelings and perspectives of others.

Take, for example, the game ‘Life is Strange.’ In this episodic adventure game, players follow the story of Max Caulfield, a young photography student who discovers she has the ability to rewind time. Throughout the game, players are presented with various choices that can significantly alter the outcome of the story. As players make these choices, they are confronted with moral dilemmas and have to consider the potential consequences of their actions.

The game’s narrative tackles issues such as bullying, mental health, and the struggles of adolescence. By presenting players with these challenging themes, ‘Life is Strange’ prompts them to empathize with the characters and reflect on their own experiences. Through their interactions with the virtual world, players can gain a deeper understanding of the emotions and challenges that others face in their lives.

Another game that highlights the potential for empathy is ‘That Dragon, Cancer.’ This autobiographical game tells the story of a young boy named Joel, who was diagnosed with terminal cancer. Developed by Ryan and Amy Green, parents who experienced the loss of their own son to the disease, the game offers players an intimate and emotional journey.

As players navigate through the game, they are exposed to the struggles, fears, and hopes of Joel and his family. By experiencing their journey alongside them, players can gain a profound sense of empathy for the characters. ‘That Dragon, Cancer’ reminds players of the importance of compassion and understanding in the face of adversity.

Besides narrative-driven games, multiplayer games also have the potential to foster empathy and compassion. In games like ‘Overwatch’ or ‘League of Legends,’ players are required to work together as a team to achieve a common goal. Effective teamwork and communication are vital for success. This collaborative aspect of multiplayer games can promote cooperation and empathy among players, as they learn to understand each other’s strengths and weaknesses and work together towards a common objective.

Moreover, the online nature of multiplayer games allows players to interact with individuals from diverse backgrounds and cultures. Through these interactions, players can gain exposure to different perspectives and develop empathy towards others who may be different from themselves. This can ultimately contribute to a more inclusive and understanding society both within and outside the gaming community.

In conclusion, video games have the potential to promote empathy and compassion among players. By immersing players in different narrative experiences and encouraging cooperative gameplay, games can foster deeper understanding and appreciation for the feelings and perspectives of others. It is important to recognize the positive impacts of gaming in promoting empathy and compassion, as it can lead to a more compassionate and empathetic society overall.
