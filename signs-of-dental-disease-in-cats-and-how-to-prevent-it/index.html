Signs of Dental Disease in Cats and How to Prevent it


Dental disease is a common problem that affects cats of all ages. It is estimated that around 70% of cats over the age of three have some form of dental disease. Just like humans, cats can develop a range of oral health problems, including gingivitis, periodontal disease, and tooth decay. The good news is that with proper care and attention, dental disease in cats can be prevented. In this blog post, we will discuss the signs of dental disease in cats and provide you with practical tips on how to prevent it.

Signs of Dental Disease in Cats

Cats are notorious for hiding their pain and discomfort, and dental disease is no exception. However, there are some common signs you can look out for that may indicate your cat is suffering from dental problems:

  1. Bad breath: A foul odor emanating from your cat’s mouth is one of the most common signs of dental disease. If your cat’s breath smells particularly unpleasant, it could be due to the buildup of bacteria and food debris in their mouth.

  2. Red or swollen gums: Healthy gums should be a pale pink color. If you notice that your cat’s gums are red, swollen, or bleeding, it could be a sign of gingivitis or periodontal disease.

  3. Drooling: Excessive drooling or drooling that is accompanied by difficulty eating can be a sign that your cat is experiencing some degree of oral pain.

  4. Loss of appetite: Cats with dental disease may have difficulty eating due to pain or discomfort. If you notice that your cat is having trouble chewing or seems reluctant to eat, it could be a red flag.

  5. Pawing at the mouth: Cats in pain may paw at their mouths in an attempt to alleviate the discomfort. If you notice your cat constantly rubbing or pawing at their mouth, it could be a sign of dental disease.

  6. Visible tartar or plaque: Take a look inside your cat’s mouth and check for the presence of tartar or plaque on their teeth. These hard deposits can lead to dental disease if left untreated.

How to Prevent Dental Disease in Cats

Preventing dental disease in cats involves a combination of proper oral hygiene and regular veterinary care. Here are some practical tips to help keep your cat’s teeth and gums healthy:

  1. Brush your cat’s teeth: Yes, you read that right! Brushing your cat’s teeth regularly can significantly reduce the risk of dental disease. Use a soft-bristled toothbrush and veterinary-approved toothpaste to gently brush your cat’s teeth. Start slowly and gradually increase the duration of brushing sessions.

  2. Provide dental-friendly toys and treats: There are plenty of dental-friendly toys and treats available on the market designed to promote better oral health. These toys and treats encourage chewing and help remove plaque and tartar from your cat’s teeth.

  3. Consider a dental diet: Dental diets, also known as dental care kibble, have been specifically formulated to promote oral health. These diets often have a larger kibble size and a special texture that helps to remove plaque from your cat’s teeth while they eat.

  4. Regular veterinary check-ups: Regular dental check-ups are essential for maintaining your cat’s oral health. Your veterinarian can perform a thorough dental examination and identify any dental issues before they become more serious. They may also recommend professional dental cleanings under anesthesia if necessary.

  5. Encourage chewing: Chewing is a natural behavior for cats and can help keep their teeth clean. Provide your cat with appropriate chew toys or treats to promote clean teeth and healthy gums.

  6. Monitor your cat’s diet: Feeding your cat a balanced diet that is appropriate for their age and life stage is important for overall health, including dental health. Avoid feeding them sugary or sticky foods that can contribute to plaque buildup.

Conclusion

Dental disease in cats is a common problem that can cause pain and discomfort if left untreated. By being vigilant and proactive, you can help prevent dental disease in your feline friend. Look out for signs of dental disease, such as bad breath, swollen gums, drooling, loss of appetite, pawing at the mouth, and visible tartar or plaque. Implementing a good oral hygiene routine, providing dental-friendly toys and treats, and scheduling regular veterinary check-ups are all essential steps in keeping your cat’s teeth and gums healthy. Remember, prevention is key when it comes to dental disease in cats, so start taking care of your cat’s oral health today!
