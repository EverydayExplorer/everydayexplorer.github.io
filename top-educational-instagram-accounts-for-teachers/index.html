Top Educational Instagram Accounts for Teachers


Instagram has become more than just a platform for sharing personal photos and updates. It has evolved into a powerful tool for education, connecting teachers with valuable resources, lesson ideas, and like-minded educators from all around the world. If you’re a teacher looking to enhance your teaching methods and gain inspiration, Instagram is the place to be. In this blog post, we will explore some of the top educational Instagram accounts that every teacher should follow.

1. @WeAreTeachers: With over 1 million followers, We Are Teachers is one of the most popular educational Instagram accounts. They share a wide range of content, including lesson plans, teaching strategies, classroom management tips, and inspiring quotes. Their posts are visually appealing and cater to teachers of all subjects and grade levels.

2. @TeachThought: TeachThought is another great Instagram account for teachers. They focus on innovative teaching methods, critical thinking, and technology integration in the classroom. They regularly share articles, infographics, and videos that challenge traditional teaching practices and promote creativity and student engagement.

3. @CultOfPedagogy: Cult of Pedagogy, run by Jennifer Gonzalez, provides practical advice and resources for teachers. She covers topics such as classroom management, instructional strategies, and professional development. Her content is research-based and easy to implement, making it a go-to resource for many educators.

4. @ThePrimaryGal: Ashley Schroeder, known as The Primary Gal, shares ideas specifically for primary school teachers. Her account is filled with colorful and engaging activities, anchor charts, and classroom organization tips. She also offers free resources and collaborates with other educators to share their experiences.

5. @Edutopia: Edutopia is the Instagram account of the George Lucas Educational Foundation. They share inspirational stories, teaching practices, and research-backed strategies to improve learning outcomes. Edutopia also highlights creative projects happening in schools and showcases the work of innovative educators.

6. @TeachingIdeas: As the username suggests, this account is a treasure trove of teaching ideas and inspiration. They provide quick and easy activities that can be implemented in any classroom, along with helpful tips for assessment and differentiation. Teaching Ideas is perfect for teachers who need fresh ideas to keep their students engaged.

7. @MathWithMrsM: Math teachers, rejoice! Mrs. M shares creative and fun ways to teach mathematics. Her posts include interactive lesson ideas, math games, and resources that help make math enjoyable for students. Whether you’re an elementary teacher or a high school math enthusiast, this account has something for everyone.

8. @ReadingWithMrsReed1: For literacy-focused teachers, Reading with Mrs. Reed is a must-follow account. The posts are filled with book recommendations, reading strategies, and ideas for building a love of reading in young learners. Mrs. Reed’s passion for literacy shines through her content, making it a valuable resource for English language arts teachers.

9. @TeachAndTransform: Dena Simmons, the creator behind Teach and Transform, focuses on social-emotional learning and equity in education. She shares empowering messages, resources, and strategies to create inclusive and supportive classrooms. Dena’s work is essential for teachers who aim to create safe and welcoming spaces for all students.

10. @PocketfulofPrimary: Michelle Ferré, known as Pocketful of Primary, takes a holistic approach to teaching. Her account covers topics like self-care, well-being, and work-life balance for educators. She shares practical tips for managing stress, finding joy in teaching, and maintaining a healthy lifestyle while making a positive impact in the classroom.

Following these top educational Instagram accounts will provide you with a wealth of knowledge, inspiration, and support throughout your teaching journey. From classroom management to engaging lesson ideas and professional development, these accounts cover a wide variety of topics that will help you become a better educator. So, open up Instagram, hit that follow button, and let the learning begin!
