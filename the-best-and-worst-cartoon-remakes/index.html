The Best and Worst Cartoon Remakes


Welcome to the wonderful world of cartoons! From timeless classics to modern masterpieces, the animation industry has always had a special place in our hearts. Over the years, we have witnessed various remakes of beloved cartoon shows, some of which have been celebrated and others that have left fans scratching their heads. In this blog, we will explore the best and worst cartoon remakes, taking you on a nostalgic journey through the ups and downs of animated series reimagination.

The Best:

1. “Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles” (2012-2017): Cowabunga! This remake of the iconic ’80s show captured the essence of the original while injecting fresh energy and modern storytelling. With dynamic animation, complex character development, and engaging storylines, it successfully appealed to both new and old fans of the franchise.

2. “DuckTales” (2017-present): Life is like a hurricane in Duckburg! This reboot of the beloved ’80s series brought back the charm and adventure of the original, while adding a contemporary twist. The animation is vibrant, the humor is on point, and the voice cast is superb. Join Scrooge McDuck and his nephews on a treasure hunting adventure that will make you feel like a kid again!

3. “Voltron: Legendary Defender” (2016-2018): Form Voltron! This reimagining of the ’80s show breathed new life into the giant robot genre. With stunning animation, compelling character arcs, and an epic space opera storyline, it captivated audiences of all ages. The remake honored the spirit of the original while expanding the mythology, making it a hit among old and new fans alike.

4. “She-Ra and the Princesses of Power” (2018-2020): By the power of Grayskull! This reboot of the ’80s series gave the iconic character She-Ra a fresh and empowering makeover. With its diverse and complex characters, strong storytelling, and beautiful animation, it struck a chord with viewers. This remake showcased the importance of friendship, courage, and standing up for what is right.

The Worst:

1. “The Powerpuff Girls” (2016-2019): Sugar, spice, and everything not so nice. This remake of the beloved ’90s show failed to capture its charm and wit. The animation style changed drastically, losing the unique appeal of the original, while the writing felt flat. It left fans disappointed and longing for the magic of the original series.

2. “ThunderCats Roar” (2020-present): ThunderCats, ho! This controversial remake took a drastic departure from the original, trading in the action-packed adventure for slapstick comedy. The animation style, resembling a cartoon for younger children, alienated longtime fans who were expecting a more serious and epic revival of the beloved ’80s show.

3. “Scooby-Doo! Mystery Incorporated” (2010-2013): Zoinks! While not a direct remake, this incarnation of the classic mystery-solving gang didn’t quite hit the mark. The show took a darker and more serialized approach, which didn’t resonate with all viewers. The departure from the lighthearted and episodic nature of the original series left some fans feeling disconnected.

4. “The Looney Tunes Show” (2011-2014): What’s up, Doc? This remake of the iconic Looney Tunes characters tried to modernize them by placing them in sitcom-style situations. While the animation remained true to the original, the writing and humor fell short. The absence of the fast-paced and zany comedy that made the originals beloved left fans feeling disappointed.

In the world of cartoon remakes, there are hits and misses, successes, and disappointments. But one thing is for sure, the power of nostalgia and the love for these beloved animated characters will continue to endure. Whether a remake captures the spirit of the original or falls flat, cartoons will always hold a special place in our hearts, reminding us of our childhood and bringing joy to both young and old.
