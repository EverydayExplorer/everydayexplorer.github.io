Creating Engaging Side Characters in Science Fiction and Fantasy


Science fiction and fantasy have long been beloved genres for their ability to transport readers to new worlds and ignite their imagination. While the main characters often take center stage, it is the side characters that can really add depth and nuance to a story. Creating engaging side characters is an art form that can elevate a sci-fi or fantasy novel to new heights. In this blog post, we will explore some tips and techniques for crafting captivating and memorable side characters in these genres.

1. Unique Abilities and Characteristics

One way to make your side characters stand out is by giving them unique abilities or characteristics. This could be anything from a special power to an unusual appearance. These traits can add intrigue and contribute to the overall world-building of your story. Think about how these abilities or characteristics can play into the plot or support the main character’s journey. The key is to make sure they are not just gimmicks, but rather integral to the development of the character and the story.

2. Personal Goals and Motivations

Side characters shouldn’t just exist to serve the main character’s storyline. They should have their own personal goals and motivations that drive their actions. This adds complexity and depth to their character, making them more relatable and interesting for readers. Consider the side character’s backstory and what drives them. Are they seeking revenge, fighting for justice, or simply trying to survive in a harsh world? Understanding their motivations will help you develop more well-rounded and engaging side characters.

3. Relationships and Interactions

The relationships your side characters have with other characters, including the main protagonist, can greatly impact the overall dynamics of the story. Creating meaningful and authentic interactions between side characters and the main character can deepen their impact and make them more memorable. Think about the history they share, the conflicts they may have, and how they influence each other’s growth. By giving your side characters a strong presence in these relationships, readers will be more invested in their journey.

4. A Unique Perspective

Side characters often provide a different perspective on the events and themes explored in your sci-fi or fantasy world. They can offer fresh insights and challenge the main character’s beliefs, leading to meaningful character development. Consider giving your side characters a unique background, culture, or worldview that sets them apart from the main character. This will not only make them more interesting but also allow you to explore different facets of your story’s world.

5. Emotional Arcs and Growth

Just like the main character, side characters should undergo their own emotional arcs and growth throughout the story. They should face challenges and overcome obstacles, experiencing their own personal transformations. This adds depth and realism to their character and makes them more relatable to readers. Consider how the events of the story impact your side characters and how they evolve as a result. By giving them their own narrative arcs, you can create a more well-rounded and engaging cast of characters.

In conclusion, creating engaging side characters in science fiction and fantasy is an essential aspect of writing a compelling story. By giving them unique abilities and characteristics, personal goals and motivations, meaningful relationships and interactions, a unique perspective, and emotional arcs, you can craft side characters that will captivate readers and add depth to your narrative. Remember, side characters should not be an afterthought, but rather an integral part of the world you have created. So let your imagination run wild and create side characters that will leave a lasting impact on readers for years to come.
