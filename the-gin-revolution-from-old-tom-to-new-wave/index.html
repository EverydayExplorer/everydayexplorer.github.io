The Gin Revolution: From Old Tom to New Wave


In the realm of spirits, few beverages have undergone as dramatic a transformation as gin. Once relegated to dusty shelves and the memories of elderly relatives, gin has experienced a remarkable renaissance in recent years. From the traditional styles of Old Tom to the innovative creations of the New Wave, the gin revolution has captivated palates and ignited a fervor for experimentation. Join me on a journey through the colorful history and exciting future of this beloved botanical elixir.

The Origins of Gin

To truly understand the gin revolution, we must first delve into its origins. Gin’s story begins in the Netherlands in the 17th century, where it was initially crafted as a medicinal tonic. Dutch physician Franciscus Sylvius is often credited with creating the precursor to gin, known as genever, which was distilled from malt wine and flavored with juniper berries to mask its less-than-pleasant taste.

Genever’s popularity quickly spread throughout Europe, reaching England by way of English soldiers returning from the Dutch War of Independence. It was here that gin truly found its footing, thanks in part to the passage of the Gin Act in 1751, which made gin production more accessible and affordable to the masses. This era, known as the Gin Craze, saw an explosion of gin consumption, with distillers experimenting with various botanicals to create unique flavor profiles.

Old Tom: The Classic Style

During the 18th and 19th centuries, gin production soared in England, leading to the creation of several distinct styles. One of the most iconic of these is Old Tom gin. Named after the wooden plaques shaped like a black cat (or ‘Old Tom’) that once adorned London’s gin houses, Old Tom gin is slightly sweeter and less dry than London Dry gin, with a subtle maltiness that sets it apart.

Old Tom gin fell out of favor in the 20th century, overshadowed by the rise of London Dry gin and the prohibition era in the United States. However, in recent years, there has been a resurgence of interest in this classic style, with craft distillers putting their own modern spin on this historical recipe.

The Rise of New Wave Gin

While Old Tom gin pays homage to tradition, the New Wave of gin represents a bold departure from convention. Characterized by innovative flavor combinations and unconventional production methods, New Wave gin pushes the boundaries of what gin can be.

One of the hallmarks of New Wave gin is its emphasis on locally sourced botanicals, allowing distillers to showcase the unique flavors of their region. From foraged herbs and fruits to exotic spices and flowers, the possibilities are endless. This focus on terroir and sustainability has resonated with consumers who value authenticity and craftsmanship.

Another defining feature of New Wave gin is its experimental approach to distillation and flavoring. Distillers are harnessing cutting-edge techniques such as vacuum distillation and cold infusion to extract the purest essence of their botanicals, resulting in gins that are vibrant, aromatic, and bursting with flavor.

The Future of Gin

As we look to the future, it’s clear that the gin revolution is far from over. With consumers thirsting for new experiences and flavors, the demand for innovative gins will only continue to grow. From small-batch artisanal producers to established distilleries experimenting with new recipes, the gin landscape is more diverse and exciting than ever before.

Whether you prefer the timeless elegance of Old Tom gin or the daring creativity of the New Wave, one thing is certain: gin has cemented its place as a staple of the modern cocktail renaissance. So raise a glass to the spirit of innovation and discovery, and toast to the endless possibilities of the gin revolution. Cheers!
