Predator Hunting: Techniques for Bagging Coyotes and Foxes


Predator hunting is a thrilling and challenging pursuit that requires a combination of skill, knowledge, and patience. In this blog post, we will explore various techniques that can help you become successful in bagging coyotes and foxes during your hunting expeditions.

1. Scout the Territory:
Before heading out into the field, it’s crucial to familiarize yourself with the hunting area. Look for signs of predator activity such as tracks, scat, and kill sites. Understanding their patterns and preferred habitats will give you a strategic advantage when setting up your hunting spots.

2. Use Effective Calling Techniques:
Calling is an essential aspect of predator hunting, as it helps attract coyotes and foxes within range. There are various types of calls available, including electronic calls, mouth calls, and hand calls. Experiment with different sounds like rabbit distress, coyote howls, and rodent squeaks to entice predators. Remember to practice your calling skills to sound as realistic as possible.

3. Employ Camouflage and Concealment:
Predators have keen senses, especially when it comes to spotting movement. Wearing camouflage clothing that matches the surrounding environment and using natural cover to hide can greatly increase your chances of remaining undetected. Consider using a ground blind or a ghillie suit for added concealment.

4. Optimize Night Hunting:
Predators are most active during low-light conditions, making night hunting a popular choice. Invest in quality night vision or thermal imaging equipment to level the playing field. Set up your hunting area near open fields or water sources where predators are likely to forage or hunt for prey.

5. Master Shot Placement:
Accuracy is crucial when it comes to predator hunting. Knowing the anatomy of coyotes and foxes is essential for making clean and ethical kills. Aim for the vitals, which include the heart and lung area. Practice shooting from various positions and distances to ensure you can make accurate shots when the opportunity presents itself.

6. Employ Decoys and Scents:
Predators are curious creatures, and using decoys or scent attractants can help draw them closer. A motion decoy, such as a wounded rabbit or a fluttering bird, can be effective in piquing their interest. Additionally, using predator urine or glandular scents can create a realistic environment that predators find hard to resist.

7. Hunt with a Partner:
Predator hunting can be more successful and enjoyable when done with a hunting partner. Having someone to watch your back and help with calling or spotting can significantly increase your chances of bagging a coyote or fox. Remember to communicate effectively and establish a safe hunting strategy.

8. Practice Ethical Hunting:
Responsible predator hunting involves respecting wildlife and adhering to local hunting regulations. Ensure you have the necessary permits and licenses before embarking on your hunting adventure. Additionally, always practice ethical hunting practices, including proper disposal of carcasses and minimizing disturbance to the ecosystem.

In conclusion, predator hunting is an exhilarating activity that requires dedication, skill, and a deep understanding of the animals you are pursuing. By employing effective scouting, calling, concealment, and shot placement techniques, you can increase your chances of successfully bagging coyotes and foxes. Remember to hunt ethically and responsibly, always prioritizing the well-being of wildlife and the environment. Happy hunting!
