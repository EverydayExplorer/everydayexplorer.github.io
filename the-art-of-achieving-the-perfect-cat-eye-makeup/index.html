The Art of Achieving the Perfect Cat Eye Makeup


Cat eye makeup is a classic and timeless look that has been favored by makeup enthusiasts for decades. It’s a versatile style that can be both subtle and dramatic, depending on your preference and the occasion. Mastering the art of the perfect cat eye can be a bit challenging, but with the right techniques and a little practice, you can achieve a flawless cat eye every time. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to achieve the perfect cat eye makeup, from choosing the right products to mastering the application.

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary supplies on hand. Here’s a list of what you’ll need:

  1. Eyeliner: Liquid or gel eyeliners work best for creating a precise cat eye.

  2. Eyeliner Brush: If you’re using gel eyeliner, a fine, angled brush is essential for precise application.

  3. Concealer: To clean up any mistakes and create sharp lines.

  4. Eyeshadow: Optional but can be used to enhance and blend the cat eye look.

  5. Mascara: To finish off the look with long, voluminous lashes.

Step 2: Prep Your Eyelids

Start by applying an eyeshadow primer or concealer to your eyelids. This will create a smooth canvas for your eyeliner and help it stay in place throughout the day. Make sure to blend it evenly and set it with a translucent powder to prevent creasing.

Step 3: Create the Wing

Now comes the trickiest part – creating the wing. Here’s a step-by-step process to achieve that perfect cat eye wing:

  1. Start at the Outer Corner: Begin by drawing a small dot or line at the outer corner of your eye. This will serve as a guide for the wing’s angle and length.

  2. Connect the Dot: Draw a line from the dot towards the center of your eyelid. The angle of this line will determine the sharpness of your cat eye.

  3. Complete the Wing: Connect the end of the line to the upper lash line, creating a triangular shape. Make sure the line is smooth and even.

  4. Fill in the Wing: Once you have the outline of your wing, fill it in with your chosen eyeliner. Keep the line as close to your lashes as possible for a clean look.

Step 4: Line the Upper Lash Line

With the wing in place, it’s time to line the upper lash line. Start from the inner corner and gradually thicken the line as you move towards the outer corner, where it meets the wing. The line should be thickest at the outer corner for that classic cat eye effect.

Step 5: Clean Up and Perfect

To achieve a flawless cat eye, use a small concealer brush and concealer to clean up any uneven lines or mistakes. This step is crucial for creating sharp, precise edges.
