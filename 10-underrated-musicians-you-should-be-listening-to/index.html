10 Underrated Musicians You Should Be Listening To


As a music enthusiast, it’s always exciting to discover new artists and expand one’s musical horizons. In this blog post, we will explore 10 underrated musicians who deserve more recognition for their incredible talent and unique sound. These artists represent a wide range of genres, from indie rock to electronic, and their music is sure to captivate listeners who are open to exploring new sounds.

1. serpentwithfeet
Serpentwithfeet is an R&B artist known for his emotive vocals and heart-wrenching lyrics. His music is a blend of gospel, soul, and experimental R&B, creating a sound that is both intimate and powerful. His debut album, ‘soil,’ showcases his ability to create a sonic landscape that is both haunting and beautiful.

2. Tune-Yards
Tune-Yards is an indie pop duo consisting of Merrill Garbus and Nate Brenner. Their music is characterized by Garbus’ unique vocal style, which incorporates elements of African rhythms and vocal techniques. Their albums ‘Whokill’ and ‘Nikki Nack’ are perfect starting points for those looking to dive into their eclectic sound.

3. Big Thief
Big Thief is an indie rock band from Brooklyn, New York, led by singer-songwriter Adrianne Lenker. Their music is characterized by introspective lyrics, intricate guitar work, and a raw, emotional sound. Their latest album, ‘Two Hands,’ is a testament to their growing prowess as songwriters and musicians.

4. Snail Mail
Snail Mail is the project of 19-year-old indie rock musician Lindsey Jordan. Her debut album, ‘Lush,’ showcases her impressive songwriting skills and her ability to create a lush, immersive sound. Her music is a perfect blend of 90s-inspired indie rock and modern pop sensibilities.

5. Moses Sumney
Moses Sumney is a multi-instrumentalist and singer-songwriter known for his atmospheric sound and incredible vocal range. His music is a mix of indie rock, R&B, and experimental pop, and his debut album, ‘Aromanticism,’ is a masterful exploration of love, loss, and self-discovery.

6. Soccer Mommy
Soccer Mommy is the stage name of 21-year-old singer-songwriter Sophie Allison. Her music is characterized by her introspective lyrics, lo-fi production, and catchy melodies. Her album ‘Clean’ is a coming-of-age narrative that showcases her growth as a musician and storyteller.

7. Tank and the Bangas
Tank and the Bangas is a New Orleans-based band known for their high-energy live shows and eclectic sound. Their music is a mix of soul, jazz, hip-hop, and spoken word, and their album ‘Green Balloon’ is a testament to their versatility and talent.

8. Wye Oak
Wye Oak is an indie rock duo from Baltimore, Maryland, consisting of Jenn Wasner and Andy Stack. Their music is characterized by Wasner’s ethereal vocals and Stack’s intricate guitar work. Their album ‘Shriek’ is a perfect example of their ability to create a lush, immersive sound.

9. EMA
EMA is the stage name of electronic musician and singer-songwriter Erika M. Anderson. Her music is characterized by her haunting vocals, experimental production, and introspective lyrics. Her album ‘The Future’s Void’ is a powerful exploration of the darker aspects of human nature.

10. Big K.R.I.T.
Big K.R.I.T. is a rapper, singer, and producer from Mississippi. His music is characterized by his soulful production and introspective lyrics, which often explore themes of race, identity, and perseverance. His album ‘4 Eva Is a Mighty Long Time’ is a testament to his growth as an artist and his ability to create a unique sound within the hip-hop genre.

In conclusion, these 10 underrated musicians represent a diverse range of styles and sounds, each with their unique voice and perspective. By exploring their music, you’ll not only discover new artists to love, but you’ll also expand your musical horizons and appreciation for the incredible talent that exists in the world of music.
