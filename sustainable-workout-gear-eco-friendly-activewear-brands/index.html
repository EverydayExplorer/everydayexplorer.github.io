Sustainable Workout Gear: Eco-Friendly Activewear Brands


Sustainability has become a central topic in many aspects of our lives, and workout gear is no exception. Exercise is an integral part of our daily routine, and it’s essential to have clothing that is comfortable, breathable, and functional. However, the production of activewear comes at a cost, and many brands are now taking initiatives to reduce their carbon footprint and use eco-friendly materials for their clothing.

If you are someone who is environmentally conscious and looking for sustainable workout gear, this blog is for you. We’ve compiled a list of the top eco-friendly activewear brands that prioritize sustainability and ethical production practices.

1. Patagonia

Patagonia is a well-known outdoor clothing brand that has been at the forefront of sustainability in the fashion industry. Their activewear line is no exception. The company uses recycled fabrics, including polyester made from plastic bottles, to produce their clothing. They have also introduced a program called Worn Wear, which allows customers to repair and recycle their Patagonia clothing.

2. Girlfriend Collective

Girlfriend Collective is a sustainable activewear brand that uses recycled water bottles to create their leggings and bras. The company is transparent about their production process and ensures that their workers are paid fair wages and operate in a safe environment. They also offer a recycling program that allows customers to send in their old clothing for recycling.

3. tentree

Tentree is a Canadian activewear brand that is committed to sustainability and ethical practices. The company uses organic cotton, recycled polyester, and sustainable materials like Tencel for their clothing. Tentree plants ten trees for every item purchased, and they have already planted over 47 million trees worldwide.

4. Wolven

Wolven is a sustainable activewear brand that uses recycled water bottles and OEKO-TEX certified printing methods to create their clothing. The company is dedicated to reducing waste and carbon emissions by producing their clothing in small batches. They donate a portion of their profits to environmental organizations.

5. prAna

PrAna is a sustainable activewear brand that is committed to fair trade practices and reducing their environmental impact. They use sustainable materials like organic cotton, hemp, and recycled polyester to create their clothing. They also partner with sustainable initiatives like the Responsible Down Standard and Bluesign to ensure ethical practices throughout their supply chain.

6. Outerknown

Outerknown is a sustainable activewear brand that focuses on using recycled materials like ECONYL to create their clothing. The company is transparent about their production practices and works with fair trade factories to ensure that their workers are paid fair wages and operate in safe conditions.

7. Athleta

Athleta is a sustainable athleisure brand that is committed to ethical production practices and sustainability. They use sustainable materials like recycled polyester and organic cotton, and they are transparent about their supply chain and production practices. They also partner with organizations like Fair Trade USA to ensure ethical practices throughout their supply chain.

8. PACT

PACT is a sustainable activewear brand that uses organic cotton and recycled materials to create their clothing. They are dedicated to using sustainable and ethical production processes and ensuring fair wages and safe working conditions for their workers. They also donate a portion of their profits to environmental and social initiatives.

In conclusion, the fashion industry has a significant impact on the environment, and it’s essential to support brands that prioritize sustainability and ethical production practices. These eco-friendly activewear brands create high-quality clothing that is both functional and eco-friendly. By choosing sustainable workout gear, you’re not only protecting the environment, but you’re also supporting companies that are committed to making a difference.
