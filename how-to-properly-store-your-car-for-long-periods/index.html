How to Properly Store Your Car for Long Periods


Is your car going to be sitting idle for an extended period of time? Whether you’re going on a long vacation, leaving for work overseas, or simply have an extra vehicle that you won’t be using for a while, it’s crucial to store your car properly to ensure it stays in good condition.

While it may seem like storing a car is as simple as parking it in a garage and leaving it, there are several important steps you need to take to ensure that your car is protected and ready to go when you decide to use it again. In this blog post, we will discuss the proper techniques for storing your car for long periods of time, including both indoor and outdoor storage options.

Indoor Car Storage

If possible, storing your car indoors is the best option as it provides the most protection against the elements. Here are some steps to follow when storing your car indoors:

1. Clean your car thoroughly

Before storing your car, it’s important to give it a thorough cleaning, both inside and out. This will help prevent any dirt or debris from causing damage during the storage period. Make sure to remove any personal items, vacuum the interior, and wash the exterior.

2. Change the oil and fluids

Changing the oil and other fluids is an essential step in preparing your car for long-term storage. Old oil can become acidic over time, which can lead to damage to the engine. It is also advisable to top off other fluids, such as coolant and brake fluid, to prevent any issues.

3. Fill up the fuel tank

Before storing your car, fill up the gas tank to prevent condensation from forming inside the tank. Adding a fuel stabilizer is also a good idea to prevent the gas from deteriorating.

4. Remove the battery or use a battery maintainer

To prevent draining the battery, it is recommended to either remove it and store it in a cool, dry place or use a battery maintainer to keep it charged. This will ensure that your car starts up easily when you decide to use it again.

5. Protect the tires

Place your car on jack stands to take weight off the tires and prevent flat spots. If you don’t have jack stands, overinflate the tires slightly to help maintain their shape.

6. Cover your car

Lastly, use a car cover made of breathable material to protect your car from dust and potential scratches.

Outdoor Car Storage

If you don’t have access to indoor storage, outdoor car storage can still be a viable option. However, it requires extra precautions to protect your vehicle from the elements. Here are some additional steps to take when storing your car outdoors:

1. Find a suitable location

Look for a space that is sheltered from direct sunlight to minimize exposure to ultraviolet rays. Additionally, find a location that is away from trees to avoid potential damage from falling branches or bird droppings.

2. Cover your car with a weatherproof car cover

Invest in a high-quality weatherproof car cover that is UV-resistant and breathable. This will protect your car from rain, snow, and other elements.

3. Use a car tent or carport

Consider using a car tent or carport to provide an added layer of protection against the elements. These structures offer shelter and can help prevent damage from hail, heavy rain, and snow.

4. Apply a coat of wax

Before storing your car outside, apply a coat of wax to help protect the paint from fading and oxidation caused by exposure to the sun.

5. Inflate the tires properly

Make sure the tires are properly inflated before storing your car. Overinflated tires can be prone to damage, while underinflated tires can develop flat spots.

6. Check on your car regularly

If possible, check on your car periodically to ensure that everything is in order and that no damage has occurred. This is especially important during extreme weather conditions.

Conclusion

Properly storing your car is essential to maintain its condition and ensure that it is ready to go when you need it again. Whether you choose indoor or outdoor storage, following these steps will help protect your vehicle from damage and prolong its lifespan.

Remember to clean your car thoroughly, change the oil and fluids, fill up the fuel tank, and remove or maintain the battery. If storing your car outdoors, find a suitable location, invest in a weatherproof car cover, and consider using a car tent or carport for additional protection. By taking these steps, you can have peace of mind knowing that your car is stored properly and will be in top shape when you’re ready to use it again.
