Hunting Predators with Calling: Techniques for Bringing Them in Close


Are you an avid hunter looking to up your predator hunting game? If so, you’ve come to the right place! In this blog post, we will be discussing the thrilling and effective technique of hunting predators with calling. Whether you’re going after coyotes, foxes, or bobcats, using calls to mimic prey can be a game-changer in bringing them within shooting range. We’ll explore various calling techniques, gear essentials, and tips to ensure successful hunts. So, read on to learn more about this exciting hunting strategy and how to bring predators in close.

The Basics of Predator Calling

Predator calling is a hunting technique that involves using specific calls to imitate the sounds of distressed prey animals. The goal is to attract predators into close range, giving you a better opportunity for a clean and ethical shot. These calls can mimic wounded rabbits, birds, rodents, or other small prey species commonly hunted by predators.

Choosing the Right Calls

When it comes to choosing predator calls, you have several options available. Electronic calls are popular among hunters due to their versatility and realistic sounds. They often come preloaded with a wide range of different animal distress calls. Hand calls, on the other hand, offer a more hands-on approach and require practice to master. They can be effective in the right hands and offer greater control over the call’s volume and tone. Experimenting with both electronic and hand calls will help you determine which one works best for your hunting style and environment.

Mastering the Art of Calling

Calling predators effectively requires practice, patience, and a good understanding of your target species. Here are a few techniques to consider:

  1. Start with low volume: Begin calling softly and gradually increase the volume as the hunt progresses. This mimics an injured or distressed animal that might initially be too weak to make much noise.

  2. Vary the pitch and tone: Predators can distinguish different pitches and tones, so experiment with variations to make your calls more realistic. Mimic the natural sounds of prey animals in distress to grab their attention.

  3. Add movement: Pair your calling with realistic decoys or motion devices to make the scene more convincing. A motion decoy effectively attracts predators by creating a visual element to complement the sounds.

  4. Utilize wind in your favor: Consider the wind direction when setting up your hunting spot. Predators rely heavily on their sense of smell, so positioning yourself downwind will help prevent them from detecting your presence.

  5. Stay patient and observant: Predators are known for their cautious nature. It may take time for them to approach, so be patient and maintain a constant watch. Use binoculars to scan the surroundings for any sign of movement.

Gear Essentials for Predator Calling

To increase your chances of success when hunting predators with calling, consider investing in the following gear:

  1. Quality camouflage clothing: Blend into your environment to avoid detection by predators. Choose camouflage patterns that match the terrain you’ll be hunting in.

  2. Decoys and motion devices: As mentioned earlier, realistic motion decoys can be highly effective in bringing predators within range. Choose decoys that match the prey species you’re imitating.

  3. High-quality optics: A good pair of binoculars or a spotting scope helps you scan the area for any sign of predators approaching.

  4. Predator calls: Invest in high-quality predator calls that produce realistic sounds. Electronic calls offer convenience and a wide range of options, while hand calls provide more control and versatility.

Safety Considerations

As with any hunting activity, safety should always be a top priority. Here are some essential safety tips to keep in mind when hunting predators with calling:

  1. Know your surroundings: Familiarize yourself with the area where you’ll be hunting. Be aware of any potential hazards or obstacles that could pose a risk.

  2. Always use a light: When predator hunting during low-light conditions, make sure to use a reliable light source to identify your target and ensure a safe shot.

  3. Practice firearm safety: Always treat your firearm as if it’s loaded, even when you know it isn’t. Never point your weapon at anything you don’t intend to shoot, and keep your finger off the trigger until you’re ready to fire.

  4. Communicate with others: If hunting in a group, establish a clear communication system to avoid accidents or misunderstandings.

Conclusion

Hunting predators with calling is an exhilarating and effective hunting strategy. By mastering the art of predator calling, choosing the right gear, and staying patient and vigilant, you can greatly increase your chances of success. Remember, practice makes perfect, so spend ample time honing your calling skills before heading out into the field. Stay safe, be ethical, and enjoy the excitement of bringing predators in close for a clean shot. Happy hunting!
