The Influence of Eastern Mythology on Science Fiction and Fantasy


Science fiction and fantasy are two popular genres that have captivated readers for decades. They take us on incredible journeys to far-off worlds, introduce us to fascinating characters, and explore themes that are often grounded in the reality of our own lives. One of the key elements that has shaped these genres is the influence of mythology, specifically Eastern mythology.

Eastern mythology encompasses a wide range of belief systems, including those from China, Japan, India, and other Asian countries. These myths and legends are often steeped in symbolism, spirituality, and a deep understanding of the human condition. They provide a rich tapestry that science fiction and fantasy authors have drawn upon to create truly remarkable stories.

One of the most prominent examples of Eastern mythology in science fiction and fantasy is the concept of the hero’s journey. This is a common trope in both genres, and it can be traced back to ancient mythological tales from Eastern cultures. In these stories, the hero embarks on a transformative journey, facing various trials and challenges along the way. Through these experiences, the hero grows and evolves, ultimately becoming a better version of themselves.

In many Eastern myths, the hero’s journey is intricately connected to concepts such as destiny, karma, and the cyclical nature of life. These themes have found their way into science fiction and fantasy stories, adding depth and meaning to the characters and their quests. For example, in the popular video game series ‘The Legend of Zelda,’ the protagonist, Link, often embarks on a journey to save the world from darkness. His journey mirrors the traditional hero’s journey found in Eastern mythology, where he must overcome various obstacles and face his own limitations to fulfill his destiny.

Another aspect of Eastern mythology that has influenced science fiction and fantasy is the idea of interconnectedness. Eastern philosophies often emphasize the interconnectedness of all living beings and the world around us. This concept has been explored in numerous works of science fiction and fantasy, particularly in stories that involve sentient beings and advanced civilizations.

In works such as Isaac Asimov’s ‘Foundation’ series or Frank Herbert’s ‘Dune,’ the concept of interconnectedness is explored through themes of unity, harmony, and the balance between different factions or species. These stories often draw parallels to Eastern philosophies such as Taoism or Zen Buddhism, where the interconnectedness of all things is a fundamental principle.

Furthermore, Eastern mythology has also influenced the portrayal of supernatural and mythical creatures in science fiction and fantasy. From dragons and phoenixes to spirits and gods, these creatures often originate from Eastern myths. They add a sense of wonder and mystique to these genres, captivating readers with their unique characteristics and powers.

A prime example of this influence can be seen in Hayao Miyazaki’s animated films, such as ‘Spirited Away’ and ‘Princess Mononoke.’ These films feature a diverse range of fantastical creatures drawn from Japanese folklore. Miyazaki’s films not only entertain audiences but also serve as a bridge between Eastern mythology and the modern world, introducing a new generation to these ancient tales.

In conclusion, the influence of Eastern mythology on science fiction and fantasy cannot be overstated. The rich tapestry of symbols, themes, and beliefs found in these myths has provided authors and creators with a vast array of ideas and concepts to draw upon. From the hero’s journey to the interconnectedness of all things, Eastern mythology has shaped the very fabric of these genres, allowing readers to embark on extraordinary adventures and explore the depths of human imagination. So, the next time you dive into a science fiction or fantasy novel, take a moment to appreciate the deep-rooted influence of Eastern mythology that lies within its pages.
