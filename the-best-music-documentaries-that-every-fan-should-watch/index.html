The Best Music Documentaries That Every Fan Should Watch


Music documentaries offer a unique insight into the lives and careers of some of the most influential artists in the industry. They provide a backstage pass to the creative process, the struggles, and the triumphs of musicians and bands that have shaped the music landscape. Whether you’re a die-hard fan or just looking to explore the stories behind the music, here are some of the best music documentaries that you shouldn’t miss.

1. “Searching for Sugar Man” (2012)

This Academy Award-winning documentary follows the incredible story of Rodriguez, a forgotten folk musician from Detroit who became a legend in South Africa without even knowing it. The film delves into the mysterious circumstances surrounding Rodriguez’s disappearance from the music scene and his unexpected resurgence decades later. It’s a compelling and heartwarming journey that showcases the power of music to transcend borders and generations.

2. “Amy” (2015)

“Amy” is a poignant and intimate portrayal of the life and tragic downfall of Amy Winehouse, one of the most soulful voices of her generation. Through rare and unseen footage, the documentary unravels the complexities of her talent, her personal struggles, and the overwhelming pressures of fame. It’s a raw and honest tribute to a remarkable artist whose music continues to resonate with audiences worldwide.

3. “The Beatles: Eight Days a Week” (2016)

Directed by Ron Howard, this documentary chronicles the touring years of the iconic band, The Beatles. With electrifying live performances and behind-the-scenes glimpses, the film captures the frenzy and excitement of Beatlemania while also shedding light on the toll that constant touring took on the band members. It’s a must-watch for both devoted fans and those looking to understand the cultural impact of The Beatles.

4. “What Happened, Miss Simone?” (2015)

Nina Simone’s incomparable talent and unapologetic activism are at the forefront of this documentary, which offers a captivating exploration of her life and legacy. Through archival footage and interviews, “What Happened, Miss Simone?” delves into the complexities of Simone’s artistry, her role in the civil rights movement, and the personal challenges she faced. It’s a powerful tribute to a fearless artist who defied convention and left an indelible mark on music history.

5. “Gimme Shelter” (1970)

This landmark documentary captures the tumultuous journey of The Rolling Stones during their 1969 US tour, culminating in the tragic events at the Altamont Free Concert. With its raw portrayal of the era’s social and political upheaval, “Gimme Shelter” offers a gripping glimpse into the darker side of rock ’n’ roll and the seismic shifts taking place in society. It’s a riveting and sobering account of a pivotal moment in music history.

6. “Whitney” (2018)

Directed by Kevin Macdonald, “Whitney” delves into the extraordinary life and career of Whitney Houston, one of the most celebrated voices in the history of music. Through archival footage and interviews with family members and colleagues, the documentary paints a multifaceted portrait of Houston, exploring the soaring heights of her success and the personal turmoil that ultimately led to her tragic end. It’s a moving tribute to a trailblazing artist whose impact reverberates through the generations.

7. “The Last Waltz” (1978)

Directed by Martin Scorsese, “The Last Waltz” captures the farewell concert of The Band and features guest performances by music legends such as Bob Dylan, Eric Clapton, and Joni Mitchell. The documentary is a masterful celebration of the camaraderie and musical prowess of The Band, serving as a time capsule of an era defined by its revolutionary sound and spirit. It’s a timeless ode to the power of live music and the enduring influence of The Band.

Music documentaries have the power to transport us into the inner sanctum of our favorite artists, allowing us to witness their triumphs, struggles, and everything in between. They offer a deeper appreciation for the music we love and the human stories behind it. So, grab some popcorn, turn up the volume, and immerse yourself in these captivating tales that showcase the indelible impact of music on our lives.
