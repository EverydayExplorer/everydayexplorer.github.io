Cartoons That Tackle Mental Health Issues: Breaking the Stigma


In recent years, there has been a noticeable shift in the way mental health is addressed in various forms of media. While television shows and movies have started to incorporate more realistic portrayals of mental health issues, one medium that often gets overlooked is cartoons. Yes, you read that right - cartoons.

When we think of cartoons, we often picture light-hearted and fun animated shows that are meant to entertain children. However, these animated series can also delve into deeper and more complex themes, including mental health. In fact, there are several cartoons that have started to tackle mental health issues head-on, aiming to break the stigma surrounding these conditions.

One such cartoon is ‘BoJack Horseman.’ This critically acclaimed Netflix series follows the life of the titular character, an anthropomorphic horse who was once a famous sitcom star. The show provides a raw and honest portrayal of BoJack’s struggle with depression, addiction, and self-destructive behavior. By showcasing the character’s inner demons, the show highlights the importance of seeking help and confronts the societal stigma associated with mental health issues.

Another cartoon that addresses mental health is ‘Steven Universe.’ While primarily a show for children, ‘Steven Universe’ has been praised for its exploration of complex topics, including anxiety and PTSD. The series follows the adventures of a young boy named Steven and his friends, who are crystal gems with unique abilities. As the show progresses, Steven’s anxiety and PTSD stemming from past traumatic events are subtly and realistically portrayed. The show teaches children that it is okay to ask for help and that their emotions are valid, breaking down barriers and normalizing discussions around mental health.

Furthermore, the popular cartoon ‘The Simpsons’ has also tackled mental health issues throughout its extensive run. In various episodes, the show bravely addresses topics such as depression, anxiety, and bipolar disorder. By incorporating mental health storylines into animated episodes, ‘The Simpsons’ reaches a vast audience and helps shed light on these issues in a relatable and approachable way.

So why are these cartoons important? Firstly, cartoons have a wide reach, appealing to both children and adults alike. By addressing mental health in a medium that is typically associated with carefree entertainment, these shows have the power to reach individuals who may not otherwise engage in conversations about mental health. Cartoons create an opportunity to educate and raise awareness about these topics in a non-threatening manner.

Secondly, by portraying characters who experience mental health issues, these cartoons provide viewers with relatable and empathetic role models. Seeing beloved characters like BoJack Horseman or Steven Universe navigate their mental health challenges reminds us that we are not alone in our struggles. These characters’ journeys encourage viewers to seek help and challenge the stigma that often surrounds mental health.

Moreover, the inclusion of mental health storylines in cartoons can spark important discussions between parents and children. By watching these shows together, families can have open and constructive conversations about mental health, promoting understanding and empathy from an early age.

In conclusion, cartoons have become a powerful vehicle to address and challenge the stigma surrounding mental health issues. By weaving these themes into animated series, shows like ‘BoJack Horseman,’ ‘Steven Universe,’ and ‘The Simpsons’ are breaking down barriers and creating a more inclusive and empathetic society. These cartoons not only entertain but also educate and inspire viewers to seek help and understand the importance of mental well-being. As we continue to navigate the complexities of mental health, it is encouraging to see more and more cartoons striving to tell authentic stories that resonate with audiences of all ages.
