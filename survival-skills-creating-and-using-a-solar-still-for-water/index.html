Survival Skills: Creating and Using a Solar Still for Water


In a survival situation, one of the most crucial skills to possess is the ability to find and purify water. When faced with limited resources, it’s essential to know how to create and use a solar still to obtain clean drinking water. In this blog post, we will explore the step-by-step process of building a solar still and highlight its effectiveness in water purification.

Firstly, let’s understand what a solar still is. A solar still is a simple, yet ingenious, device that utilizes the sun’s energy to extract pure water from any available source, such as seawater, contaminated water, or even moisture in the air. This method is particularly useful in arid environments or situations where access to clean water is limited.

To create a solar still, you’ll need a few basic materials that are typically easy to find or scavenge. These include a clear plastic sheet, a container, rocks or small pebbles, and a collection vessel. The first step is to dig a hole in the ground, deep enough to fit your container. Place the container in the center of the hole and surround it with rocks or pebbles, leaving enough space between them for water to flow.

Next, find a clear plastic sheet and position it securely over the hole, ensuring that it forms a slight concave shape. The edges of the sheet should be sealed with rocks or soil to prevent any air from entering or escaping. Make sure the sheet is taut but not touching the container.

Now, the solar still is ready to collect water. Place a small weight in the center of the plastic sheet, directly above the container. This will create a slight depression, facilitating the condensation process. As the sun’s rays heat the ground and the water within it, condensation occurs on the inner surface of the plastic sheet. The water droplets then trickle down towards the center, where they collect and drip into the container.

It’s important to note that the water collected through a solar still may not be immediately drinkable. While the distillation process removes many impurities, it may not eliminate all harmful substances. If possible, filter the collected water through a cloth or purifying tablets to ensure its safety.

Using a solar still for water is not only effective but also sustainable and environmentally friendly. With minimal effort and reliance on natural resources, you can consistently produce drinkable water in survival situations. The versatility of this method makes it invaluable for hikers, campers, or anyone who ventures into remote areas.

In conclusion, mastering the skill of creating and using a solar still for water is an essential survival skill that can make a significant difference in life-threatening situations. By harnessing the power of the sun, you can obtain clean drinking water from various sources, ensuring your hydration and overall well-being. Remember, preparation and knowledge are key, so practice building and utilizing a solar still before you find yourself in a survival scenario. Stay safe and stay hydrated!
