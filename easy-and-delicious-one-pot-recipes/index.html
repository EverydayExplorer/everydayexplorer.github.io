Easy and Delicious One-Pot Recipes


Are you tired of spending hours in the kitchen cooking and cleaning up afterward? Do you crave delicious meals without the hassle of multiple pots and pans? Look no further! In this blog post, we will explore a collection of easy and delicious one-pot recipes that will save you time and effort in the kitchen, while still satisfying your taste buds.

One-Pot Pasta Primavera

Preparation time: 10 minutes
Cooking time: 20 minutes
Servings: 4

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces of penne pasta
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup of broccoli florets
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup of vegetable broth
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for garnish
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil and cook the penne pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Add the cherry tomatoes, broccoli florets, and sliced zucchini to the pot. Cook for 5 minutes, or until the vegetables are slightly tender.
  4. Pour in the vegetable broth and heavy cream, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Allow the mixture to come to a simmer.
  5. Add the cooked penne pasta to the pot and toss everything together until well combined.
  6. Reduce the heat to low and let the pasta simmer for another 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  7. Serve the One-Pot Pasta Primavera hot, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil leaves.

One-Pot Chicken and Rice

Preparation time: 15 minutes
Cooking time: 30 minutes
Servings: 6

Ingredients:

  • 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup of frozen peas
  • 2 cups of long-grain white rice
  • 4 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chicken thighs, skin side down, and cook until golden brown, about 5 minutes. Flip the chicken and cook for an additional 3 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  4. Add the diced red and green bell peppers to the pot, and cook for 3-4 minutes, until slightly softened.
  5. Stir in the frozen peas, long-grain white rice, chicken broth, paprika, and dried thyme. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Place the chicken thighs back into the pot, skin side up, on top of the rice mixture.
  7. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the chicken is cooked through.
  8. Remove the pot from the heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes before serving.
  9. Serve the One-Pot Chicken and Rice hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

One-Pot Chili Mac

Preparation time: 10 minutes
Cooking time: 30 minutes
Servings: 6

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1 can (14 ounces) of diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 ounces) of kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 ounces) of corn, drained
  • 2 cups of beef broth
  • 2 cups of elbow macaroni
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Shredded cheddar cheese for garnish
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess grease.
  2. Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the pot, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, and paprika, and cook for 1 minute, until the spices are fragrant.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes, kidney beans, corn, beef broth, elbow macaroni, salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir everything together until well combined.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the macaroni is cooked to your desired tenderness.
  6. Remove the pot from the heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  7. Serve the One-Pot Chili Mac hot, garnished with shredded cheddar cheese and chopped green onions.

Conclusion

With these easy and delicious one-pot recipes, cooking flavorful meals has never been easier. Whether you’re a busy parent, a college student, or simply looking to simplify your cooking routine, these recipes will save you time and effort in the kitchen. So why not give them a try and enjoy the convenience without compromising on taste? Happy cooking!
