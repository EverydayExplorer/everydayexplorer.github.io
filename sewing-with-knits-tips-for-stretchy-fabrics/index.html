Sewing with Knits: Tips for Stretchy Fabrics


Sewing with knits can be both exciting and challenging. Knit fabrics offer stretch and comfort, making them perfect for garments like t-shirts, dresses, and activewear. However, their stretchy nature can also make them tricky to work with, especially for beginners. But fear not! With the right techniques and a few helpful tips, you can master the art of sewing with knits and create beautiful, professional-looking garments.

Choose the Right Needle and Thread

When sewing with knits, it’s essential to use the right tools for the job. Opt for a ballpoint or stretch needle, which is specifically designed for knit fabrics. These needles have rounded tips that glide between the fabric’s fibers without causing snags or runs. Additionally, choose a polyester or cotton-wrapped polyester thread, which offers stretch and durability, ensuring your seams withstand the fabric’s movement.

Use the Right Stitch

Selecting the appropriate stitch is crucial when sewing with knits. While a straight stitch may work for some projects, it can break when the fabric stretches. Instead, opt for a stretch stitch, such as a zigzag or triple stretch stitch, which allows the seam to stretch with the fabric without popping. Many modern sewing machines have built-in stretch stitches, making it easy to achieve professional-quality results.

Invest in Quality Interfacing

Adding interfacing to knit garments can provide stability and structure, preventing stretching and distortion around areas like necklines, hems, and cuffs. Look for lightweight knit interfacing specifically designed for stretch fabrics. Fusible interfacing is the easiest to work with, as it adheres to the fabric with heat from an iron, eliminating the need for sewing.

Use Pins Sparingly

Traditional sewing wisdom often advises using plenty of pins to hold fabric pieces together before sewing. However, when working with knits, pins can stretch and distort the fabric, leading to misaligned seams and puckering. Instead, use clips or fabric weights to secure your fabric layers, allowing them to maintain their shape while sewing. If you must use pins, place them perpendicular to the seam to minimize distortion.

Test Your Stitches

Before sewing your garment’s final seams, it’s essential to test your stitches on a scrap piece of fabric. This allows you to adjust your machine’s tension, stitch length, and width to achieve the perfect seam. Test different combinations of settings until you find the one that produces smooth, even stitches with the right amount of stretch. Taking the time to test your stitches will save you frustration and ensure professional-looking results.

Finish Seam Allowances

To prevent fraying and ensure durability, finish the seam allowances of your knit garments. You can use a serger or overlock machine to trim and encase the edges in one step, creating clean, professional-looking seams. If you don’t have access to a serger, consider using a zigzag stitch or a mock overlock stitch on your sewing machine to finish the raw edges.

Experiment with Hemming Techniques

Hemming knit garments can be challenging due to their stretchy nature. Traditional turned and stitched hems may cause puckering and distortion, especially on curved or circular hems. Instead, try using a twin needle or a coverstitch machine to create professional-looking, stretchy hems. These techniques mimic the finish of store-bought knit garments and allow the fabric to stretch without popping stitches.

Conclusion

Sewing with knits may seem intimidating at first, but with the right techniques and a bit of practice, you can create beautiful, comfortable garments that rival store-bought options. By choosing the right needles and thread, using appropriate stitches, and experimenting with different techniques, you’ll be well on your way to mastering the art of sewing with knits. So grab your favorite knit fabric, fire up your sewing machine, and let your creativity soar!
