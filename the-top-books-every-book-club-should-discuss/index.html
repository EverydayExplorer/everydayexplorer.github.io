The Top Books Every Book Club Should Discuss


Welcome to our blog where we will explore the fascinating world of book clubs! Book clubs are a fantastic way to connect with fellow book lovers, engage in thought-provoking discussions, and discover new perspectives through literature. If you’re part of a book club or thinking about starting one, we’ve got you covered. In this article, we’ll be delving into the top books that every book club should consider discussing. From classic masterpieces to contemporary gems, there’s something for everyone. So, grab a cup of tea, cozy up, and let’s dive in!

1. ‘To Kill a Mockingbird’ by Harper Lee:
This timeless classic tackles themes of racial injustice, morality, and the loss of innocence. It’s a must-read for any book club seeking to explore deep-rooted societal issues and the power of compassion.

2. ‘The Great Gatsby’ by F. Scott Fitzgerald:
Transport yourself to the glamorous and decadent world of the Roaring Twenties. This novel explores themes of wealth, love, and the American Dream. Perfect for engaging discussions about materialism and the pursuit of happiness.

3. ‘The Handmaid’s Tale’ by Margaret Atwood:
Dystopian novels often spark captivating conversations, and this one is no exception. Atwood’s masterpiece offers a chilling glimpse into a society dominated by misogyny and oppression, making it an ideal book club pick for exploring gender roles and the dangers of totalitarianism.

4. ‘The Alchemist’ by Paulo Coelho:
A true gem for those seeking spiritual and philosophical discussions. This allegorical novel follows a young shepherd boy on a journey of self-discovery, imparting powerful lessons about destiny, purpose, and the pursuit of dreams.

5. ‘Educated’ by Tara Westover:
This memoir takes readers on a harrowing journey of a woman’s quest for education and independence. A thought-provoking exploration of the importance of education, family dynamics, and the power of resilience.

6. ‘The Kite Runner’ by Khaled Hosseini:
Hosseini’s debut novel offers a poignant exploration of friendship, betrayal, and redemption set against the backdrop of Afghanistan’s tumultuous history. It’s a book that will leave you emotionally stirred and ready for a captivating book club discussion.

7. ‘Beloved’ by Toni Morrison:
Morrison’s Pulitzer Prize-winning novel delves into the haunting legacy of slavery and its impact on individuals and communities. A powerful and complex work that raises profound questions about identity, memory, and the human spirit.

8. ‘Big Little Lies’ by Liane Moriarty:
For book clubs seeking a thrilling and addictive page-turner, look no further. This suspenseful novel delves into the secrets and lies that can lurk behind seemingly perfect lives, making it an ideal choice for lively discussions about friendship, motherhood, and the complexities of human relationships.

9. ‘The Lord of the Rings’ by J.R.R. Tolkien:
For fans of epic fantasy, Tolkien’s masterpiece is a must-read. Dive into Middle-earth and explore themes of friendship, heroism, and the struggle between good and evil. It’s a book that will inspire passionate discussions and spark the imagination.

10. ‘The Underground Railroad’ by Colson Whitehead:
Whitehead’s novel reimagines the historical Underground Railroad as a physical network of secret tunnels and railways. This powerful work confronts the horrors of slavery, racism, and the enduring quest for freedom. A book that will leave you with a deep appreciation for the resilience and strength of the human spirit.

There you have it, a diverse selection of books that are sure to enrich your book club discussions. From classics to contemporary works, there’s something for everyone’s taste. So, gather your fellow bookworms, embark on literary journeys, and let the conversations unfold. Happy reading!
