How to Navigate the Music Industry: Insights from Industry Professionals


When it comes to navigating the music industry, there’s no shortage of advice out there. Whether you’re an up-and-coming musician, a music manager, or just someone with an interest in the industry, there’s no doubt that you’ve heard a lot about what it takes to succeed. But with so much information available, it can be hard to know where to start.

To help you out, we’ve compiled insights from a range of industry professionals, including musicians, managers, and executives. Whether you’re just starting out or you’re looking to take your career to the next level, there’s something here for everyone.

Build Your Network

One thing that almost everyone we talked to emphasized is the importance of building a strong network. Whether it’s through attending events, reaching out to other musicians, or simply being active on social media, having connections in the industry can make all the difference.

‘Networking is key,’ says Leah McFall, a singer-songwriter who rose to fame after appearing on The Voice UK. ‘It’s important to get yourself out there, meeting other artists, and building relationships.’

This sentiment is echoed by Jason Tighe, a music manager who has worked with some of the biggest names in the industry. ‘You never know who you’ll meet or how they might be able to help you,’ he says. ‘Don’t be afraid to put yourself out there and make connections.’

Stay Focused

Of course, building a network is just the first step. To succeed in the music industry, you also need to stay focused on your goals and keep pushing forward, even when things get tough.

For example, Chuka Nwanazia, an A&R consultant who has worked with major labels like Atlantic Records, emphasizes the importance of persistence. ‘You have to be willing to put in the work and keep pushing, even when you’re facing rejection,’ he says.

Similarly, Jordan Carter, the founder of music promotion company DRAMA, advises aspiring musicians to stay focused on their craft. ‘At the end of the day, it all comes down to the music,’ he says. ‘If you’re not putting in the work and constantly trying to improve, it’s going to be tough to make it in this industry.’

Be Authentic

At the same time, it’s important to recognize that the music industry is also a highly competitive and often cutthroat business. In this environment, it’s crucial to be authentic and true to yourself.

As Matthew Rook, co-founder of independent music publisher Modern Works, puts it, ‘You have to be true to your art and who you are as a person. Don’t try to be something you’re not just because you think it will be more marketable.’

Similarly, Khamaal Montgomery, a music marketing consultant who has worked with major labels like Universal Music Group, advises artists not to get caught up in trends or trying to fit into a particular genre. ‘At the end of the day, fans can smell fake a mile away,’ he says. ‘Just be true to yourself and let the music speak for itself.’

Embrace Technology

Finally, it’s impossible to talk about navigating the music industry today without acknowledging the pivotal role that technology plays. From social media to streaming platforms like Spotify and Apple Music, the way we consume and promote music has changed dramatically in the past decade.

According to Alex Rainbird, founder of the popular YouTube channel and record label AlexrainbirdMusic, embracing technology is key. ‘Social media has completely leveled the playing field, so you have to be on top of your game when it comes to promoting your music online,’ he says.

Kwasi Lee, a music producer and mixing engineer who has worked with artists like Chance the Rapper, agrees. ‘You have to understand how to use technology to your advantage, whether it’s through social media, streaming platforms, or even tools like GarageBand.’

In Conclusion

There’s no one-size-fits-all guide to navigating the music industry, but by taking the advice of these industry professionals, you can start to build a roadmap to success. From building a strong network to staying true to yourself, there are certain principles that apply to almost anyone looking to break through in this competitive field.

And of course, it’s important to keep in mind that the music industry is also constantly evolving, so it’s crucial to stay up-to-date on the latest trends and technologies. But by staying focused, authentic, and open to new opportunities, there’s no limit to what you can achieve in this exciting and constantly-changing industry.
