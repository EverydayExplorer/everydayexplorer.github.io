The Joy of Plein Air Pastel: Capturing the Atmosphere of Outdoor Scenes


As the warm sun kisses your skin and a gentle breeze rustles through the leaves, there’s something magical about being outdoors. The world comes alive with vibrant colors, captivating scents, and a sense of freedom that can only be found in nature. And for artists, there’s no better way to capture this essence than through the captivating medium of plein air pastel.

Plein air pastel is an art form that involves creating vibrant and expressive artwork outdoors, directly from nature. Unlike studio work, plein air pastel allows artists to immerse themselves in the atmosphere of their surroundings, capturing the ever-changing light, colors, and textures with every stroke of their pastels.

One of the greatest joys of plein air pastel is the opportunity to witness firsthand the beauty and intricacies of outdoor scenes. Whether it’s a serene landscape, a bustling cityscape, or a quaint village, plein air pastel allows artists to breathe life into their artwork, infusing it with a sense of immediacy and authenticity that can’t be replicated in the studio.

The process of plein air pastel begins with carefully selecting a scene that speaks to the artist’s soul. It could be a breathtaking sunset over a tranquil lake, a bustling market filled with vibrant colors, or a quiet alleyway bathed in soft, dappled light. Once the scene is chosen, the artist sets up their easel, selects their pastels, and takes a moment to connect with their surroundings.

As the artist begins to sketch the initial outlines of their composition, they are transported into a world of colors. With each stroke of their pastels, they capture the essence of the scene, carefully observing the interplay of light and shadow, the subtle nuances of color, and the delicate balance of shapes and forms.

But plein air pastel is not just about capturing the visual beauty of outdoor scenes. It’s about immersing oneself in the entire sensory experience. The artist listens to the sounds of nature, the rustling of leaves, the chirping of birds, and the distant hum of traffic. They feel the warmth of the sun on their skin, the coolness of the breeze, and the texture of the earth beneath their feet. All of these elements find their way into the artwork, creating a rich tapestry of sensations that transport the viewer into the very heart of the scene.

For those who have never tried plein air pastel, it may seem daunting. But the joy lies in the process itself. It’s about embracing the unpredictability of nature, the ever-changing light and weather, and the challenges that come with working outdoors. It’s about letting go of control and allowing the scene to guide the artist’s hand.

Plein air pastel is not just a medium for professional artists. It’s a form of self-expression that can be enjoyed by anyone with a love for art and the outdoors. Whether you’re a seasoned artist or a beginner, plein air pastel offers a unique opportunity to connect with nature, to slow down, and to truly see the world around you.

So, the next time you find yourself captivated by the beauty of an outdoor scene, why not grab your pastels, set up your easel, and let the joy of plein air pastel guide your artistic journey? Step outside, embrace the atmosphere, and let nature be your muse. You may just discover a whole new world of creativity and inspiration waiting to be explored.
