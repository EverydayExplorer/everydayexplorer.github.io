From Atlantis to Lemuria: Lost Civilizations of Fantasy


From ancient myths and legends to modern-day fantasy novels, the concept of lost civilizations has always captivated human imagination. Two of the most famous and intriguing lost civilizations in popular culture are Atlantis and Lemuria. Despite their mythical origins, the legends surrounding these civilizations have endured for centuries, inspiring countless works of fiction, scientific theories, and spiritual beliefs.

Atlantis, according to Plato’s account, was a powerful and advanced city-state that existed more than 11,000 years ago somewhere in the Atlantic Ocean. It was said to have been a utopian society, with its people living in luxury and harmony, having access to advanced technology and possessing incredible knowledge of the natural world. However, due to their excessive pride and hubris, the Atlanteans angered the gods, who punished them by causing a great cataclysm that flooded their city and buried it beneath the waves.

Throughout history, many scholars and explorers have attempted to locate the real Atlantis, with some suggesting that it might have been an actual historical civilization. However, no conclusive evidence has been found to support such claims, leaving Atlantis firmly in the realm of myth and fantasy.

On the other hand, Lemuria is a lost continent that is said to have existed in the Indian Ocean around 50,000 years ago. According to the Theosophical Society, founded by Madame Blavatsky in the late 19th century, Lemuria was a spiritual and highly evolved civilization that was inhabited by giant, telepathic beings known as Lemurians. They were said to have communicated with each other through mental telepathy and lived for thousands of years in perfect harmony with nature.

The theory of Lemuria was based on the discovery of fossil records of extinct species of primates, which appeared to have originated from a landmass that once connected Africa and Asia. While this theory has been debunked by modern scientific evidence, the concept of Lemuria has continued to inspire a range of spiritual and new age beliefs.

Both Atlantis and Lemuria have served as fertile ground for writers and artists of all genres. From Jules Verne’s adventure novel ‘20,000 Leagues Under the Sea’ to Edgar Rice Burroughs’ ‘At the Earth’s Core,’ countless works of fiction have been written about lost civilizations that draw inspiration from these mythical lands.

In recent years, the popularity of fantasy literature and role-playing games has renewed interest in these lost civilizations. Fans of Dungeons & Dragons, for example, will recognize Lemuria as the setting for the ‘Blackmoor’ campaign, while Atlantis has inspired books like ‘The Atlantis Revelation’ by Thomas Greanias and the Disney animated movie ‘Atlantis: The Lost Empire.’

Beyond their use in works of fiction, the legends of Atlantis and Lemuria continue to fascinate people around the world, fueling ongoing debates about their existence and legacy. Some believe that these lost civilizations were real historical societies that were wiped out by natural disasters, while others view them as symbolic representations of mankind’s lost spirituality and higher consciousness.

In the end, whether real or fictional, Atlantis and Lemuria serve as reminders of humanity’s desire to explore the unknown and uncover the mysteries of our past. As long as people continue to dream and wonder, these lost civilizations will continue to live on in our imaginations, inspiring and intriguing us for generations to come.
