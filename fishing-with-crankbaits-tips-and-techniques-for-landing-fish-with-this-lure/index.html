Fishing with Crankbaits: Tips and Techniques for Landing Fish with This Lure


If you are an avid angler or someone who enjoys spending time on the water, then you are probably familiar with crankbaits. These versatile lures are a go-to for many fishermen, as they have proven to be effective in catching a wide variety of fish species. In this article, we will explore the benefits of using crankbaits and provide you with some tips and techniques to help you increase your success rate when fishing with this lure.

Crankbaits are designed to mimic the movement and appearance of baitfish or other prey that fish like to eat. They usually feature a realistic crankbait body shape with a diving lip that allows them to dive and swim at various depths when retrieved. This diving action is what sets crankbaits apart from other types of lures, making them highly appealing to fish.

One of the key benefits of fishing with crankbaits is their ability to cover a large area of water. Whether you are fishing in a lake, river, or reservoir, crankbaits can be casted long distances and retrieved at different speeds to explore different depths. This makes them particularly effective for locating active fish and triggering strikes from predators, such as bass, walleye, pike, and muskie.

Now that we understand the basics of crankbaits, let’s dive into some tips and techniques to help you improve your chances of landing fish with this lure:

1. Choose the right crankbait: Crankbaits come in various sizes, shapes, and colors, so it’s important to select the right one for the conditions you are fishing in. Consider the depth of the water, the type of fish you are targeting, and the prevalent forage in the area. Match the size and color of your crankbait to closely resemble the local baitfish.

2. Use the appropriate gear: When fishing with crankbaits, it’s crucial to have the right fishing gear. Choose a medium to medium-heavy spinning or casting rod that can handle the weight and action of the crankbait. Pair it with a reel that has a high gear ratio to quickly retrieve the lure.

3. Pay attention to water temperature: Fish are cold-blooded creatures, and their activity levels are influenced by water temperature. In colder water, slow down your retrieve and use a crankbait with a tighter wobble. In warmer water, speed up your retrieve and choose a crankbait with a wider wobble.

4. Experiment with retrieval speed: Crankbaits can be retrieved at varying speeds to imitate different types of prey. Start with a steady retrieve and experiment with pauses, jerks, and speed changes to trigger fish into striking. Sometimes a quick burst of speed or a sudden stop can entice a reaction bite.

5. Target structure and cover: Fish often hide around structure and cover, such as submerged rocks, fallen trees, weed beds, and drop-offs. Cast your crankbait near these areas, allowing it to bump into the structure or cover. This can trigger a predatory response from fish, causing them to strike.

6. Pay attention to vibrations and deflections: Crankbaits generate vibrations and deflections when in contact with objects underwater. Pay close attention to your rod tip and line for any sudden changes or subtle vibrations. These signals can indicate that a fish has taken the bait or is following it closely.

7. Consider the seasons: Fish behavior and preferred prey change with the seasons. In the spring, fish are often in shallow water and are more aggressive. Use shallow-running crankbaits to target these active fish. In the summer and fall, switch to deeper-diving crankbaits as fish move to deeper water.

8. Practice proper hookset technique: When a fish strikes your crankbait, it’s essential to execute a proper hookset to ensure a solid connection. Instead of a forceful jerk, maintain a steady pressure and reel down to set the hook firmly into the fish’s mouth. This reduces the chances of the fish throwing the hook.

By following these tips and techniques, you can enhance your success when fishing with crankbaits. However, it’s important to note that patience and persistence are key to mastering any fishing technique. Don’t be discouraged if you don’t receive instant success; keep experimenting, learning, and adapting to different fishing conditions.

In conclusion, crankbaits are a versatile and effective lure for catching a wide range of fish species. By selecting the right crankbait, using the appropriate gear, paying attention to water temperature, experimenting with retrieval speed, targeting structure and cover, and practicing proper hookset technique, you can increase your chances of landing fish with crankbaits. Remember to enjoy the process and appreciate the peacefulness of being out on the water. Happy fishing!
