Comedy in Advertising: Memorable Commercials That Tickle Our Funny Bone


Welcome to our blog post all about the wonderful world of comedy in advertising! Today, we’re going to dive into the realm of memorable commercials that never fail to tickle our funny bone. Get ready to laugh, because these advertisements are sure to bring a smile to your face!

Comedy has been a powerful tool in advertising for decades, primarily because it serves as a universal language that can connect with people from all walks of life. A well-executed comedic commercial can leave a lasting impression, making us remember not just the brand, but also the positive emotions associated with it.

One example of a hilarious commercial that has stood the test of time is the Old Spice ‘The Man Your Man Could Smell Like’ campaign. This series of ads features a confident, bare-chested man who effortlessly transitions from one scene to another while delivering clever one-liners. The absurdity of the situations, combined with the actor’s deadpan delivery, creates a truly unforgettable experience for the viewers. Even years after its initial release, this campaign is still referenced and parodied, proving that laughter truly is timeless.

Another commercial that has become a staple in the world of advertising is the Geico ‘Hump Day’ commercial. This gem features a talking camel walking through an office, enthusiastically asking employees what day it is, only to be met with responses of ‘It’s hump day!’ The humor lies in the camel’s infectious enthusiasm and the unexpectedness of a talking animal in a corporate setting. This commercial has become so popular that ‘hump day’ has now become a widely recognized phrase used to celebrate the middle of the workweek.

Of course, we cannot forget the iconic Budweiser ‘Wassup’ campaign. Originating in the late 90s, this series of commercials featured a group of friends casually greeting each other with a simple ‘Wassup?’ The catchphrase quickly caught on and became a cultural phenomenon, with people across the globe adopting it into their everyday vocabulary. The simplicity of this campaign is what makes it so brilliant – sometimes, all it takes is a relatable and memorable phrase to make an impact.

One more example that showcases the power of comedy in advertising is the Doritos ‘Crash the Super Bowl’ campaign. This clever concept encouraged fans to create their own Doritos commercials for a chance to have their ad aired during the Super Bowl. The result was a series of hilarious and often outrageous commercials that not only entertained viewers but also created a sense of community and engagement. By involving the audience directly in the creation process, Doritos was able to establish a connection with consumers that went beyond the typical passive viewing experience.

Comedy in advertising is a powerful tool that can break through the noise and capture our attention. It helps brands to stand out, create a positive association in our minds, and ultimately drive sales. The key to a successful comedic commercial is to strike the right balance – it should be funny, relatable, and memorable, all while effectively conveying the brand’s message.

In conclusion, comedy in advertising is a winning combination that never fails to leave a lasting impression. Whether it’s through hilarious characters, unexpected punchlines, or relatable situations, these memorable commercials have managed to tickle our funny bone and create a lasting bond with consumers. So, the next time you find yourself laughing at a commercial, remember that there’s more to it than just a good joke – behind the humor lies a brilliant marketing strategy that aims to win your heart and your loyalty.
