The Top 10 Cartoon Cameos: When Famous Actors Voiced Animated Roles


In the world of animation, cameos are a delightful surprise for fans. When famous actors lend their voices to animated roles, it adds an extra layer of excitement to the viewing experience. Today, we’re taking a look at the top 10 cartoon cameos featuring famous actors voicing animated roles. These appearances have left a lasting impression on audiences and showcase the incredible talent of these actors.

1. Robin Williams as Genie in Disney’s Aladdin

Robin Williams’ unforgettable performance as the Genie in Disney’s 1992 animated film Aladdin is a testament to his comedic genius. His improvisational skills and ability to bring life to the character made the Genie one of the most loved Disney characters of all time.

2. Christopher Walken as King Adam Sack in Robot Chicken

Christopher Walken’s distinctive voice and acting style make him a perfect fit for the role of King Adam Sack in Robot Chicken, an Adult Swim stop-motion animated series. His cameo in the show is a delightful treat for fans of the veteran actor.

3. Ellen DeGeneres as Diane in Finding Nemo

Ellen DeGeneres lent her voice to the character Diane, a regal moor hen, in the 2003 animated film Finding Nemo. Her comedic timing and unique vocal style added depth and humor to the character, making her a memorable part of the film.

4. Brad Pitt as Mr. Incredible in The Simpsons

In a 2004 episode of The Simpsons, Brad Pitt voiced the character of Mr. Incredible, a parody of his role in the movie The Incredibles. His cameo was a delightful homage to the popular Pixar film and showcased his ability to poke fun at his own fame.

5. Elijah Wood as Ryan in Robot Chicken

Elijah Wood, best known for his role as Frodo Baggins in The Lord of the Rings trilogy, voiced the character Ryan in an episode of Robot Chicken. His cameo showcased his versatility as an actor and added a touch of humor to the show.

6. Hugh Jackman as Wolverine in Ultimate Spider-Man

Hugh Jackman, who portrayed Wolverine in the X-Men film series, lent his voice to the character in the animated series Ultimate Spider-Man. His cameo was a treat for fans of both the comic book and animated worlds, and it showcased his ability to bring the character to life in any medium.

7. Kiefer Sutherland as Voice in The Simpsons

Kiefer Sutherland, known for his role as Jack Bauer in the television series 24, lent his voice to a character in a 2011 episode of The Simpsons. His cameo was a delightful surprise for fans of both the actor and the long-running animated series.

8. Anna Faris as Jessica in Family Guy

Anna Faris, known for her roles in comedies like Scary Movie and The House Bunny, voiced the character Jessica in an episode of Family Guy. Her cameo was a fun addition to the show and showcased her ability to bring humor to any role she takes on.

9. Stephen Colbert as Himself in Robot Chicken

Stephen Colbert, the former host of The Colbert Report, voiced himself in an episode of Robot Chicken. His cameo was a hilarious addition to the show and showcased his ability to poke fun at his own persona.

10. Danny DeVito as Pengu in Madagascar: Escape 2 Africa

Danny DeVito, known for his roles in films like Twins and Matilda, lent his voice to the character Pengu in the 2008 animated film Madagascar: Escape 2 Africa. His cameo added a touch of humor and charm to the film, making it a memorable addition to his impressive acting career.

In conclusion, these top 10 cartoon cameos featuring famous actors voicing animated roles have left a lasting impression on audiences. The talent and versatility of these actors have added depth and excitement to the world of animation, making these cameos a delightful treat for fans of both animation and live-action film.
