The Philosophy of Creativity: What Does it Mean to Be Creative?


Creativity is referred to as the ability to bring something new and original into existence, as well as the capacity to use imagination to produce something that was not there before. It is an important value that pervades every aspect of human life. Creativity is an innate human attribute that has allowed us to generate new products, inventions, and concepts, from works of art to groundbreaking scientific discoveries.

Creativity takes many forms, including visual art, music, writing, dance, theatre, fashion design, and architecture. It also includes creativity in problem-solving, innovation, perception, and communication. However, the philosophy of creativity involves understanding the nature of creativity, its importance, and the methods of enhancing and cultivating it.

At its core, creativity is a complex and dynamic process involving various cognitive and affective activities such as divergent thinking, imagination, problem-solving, and intuition. It requires cognitive and emotional flexibility and the ability to generate multiple possibilities and solutions. Creativity is a natural inclination for most people, but it needs to be trained and developed through practice and exposure to diverse experiences and cultures.

The significance of creativity lies in its capacity to promote personal growth, enhance problem-solving skills, encourage innovation, and enable progress in various areas of human endeavour. It also has an impact on health and well-being because it is linked to happiness, self-expression, and meaning-making. Creativity is essential for thriving in today’s fast-paced, dynamic, and complex world as it offers an opportunity to find new solutions to old problems.

There isn’t a precise formula for being creative. However, some philosophers, psychologists, and educators have suggested that various factors impact creativity. One factor is the environment, where creativity is encouraged and supported, and the other is one’s mindset and attitudes towards creativity.

For instance, Teresa Amabile, a psychologist, suggests that three conditions increase creativity; a combination of expertise, task motivation, and an environment in which creativity is supported. She also observed that creative thinking is enhanced by intrinsic motivation more than extrinsic ones.

Other philosophers suggest that creativity is linked to curiosity and openness to diverse experiences and cultures. Creativity is also said to be linked to failure and the notion that it is a necessary part of the creative process. Some creatives believe in outlining a certain problem to tackle before working on it.

In conclusion, creativity is a critical aspect of human life that allows us to produce new and innovative concepts, products, and ideas. It has a considerable impact on our personal, social, and cultural development. Creativity is an innate human quality that can be nurtured and developed through specific interventions that encourage curiosity, openness to experience, and a supportive environment. To be creative is to be open-minded, curious, and willing to take risks while trusting the process and embracing failure as part of the journey. With these values in mind, anyone can harness their creative spirit and embody the philosophy of creativity.
