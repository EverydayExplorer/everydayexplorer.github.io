Mountain Biking Trails Around the World: Epic Rides and Stunning Views


Mountain biking is a thrilling outdoor activity that allows riders to explore beautiful landscapes, conquer challenging terrain, and experience an adrenaline rush like no other. From lush forests to rugged mountains, there are countless trails around the world that cater to mountain biking enthusiasts of all skill levels. Whether you’re a beginner looking for a leisurely ride or an experienced rider seeking an epic adventure, these mountain biking trails offer stunning views and unforgettable experiences.

1. Moab, Utah, USA
Moab is a mecca for mountain bikers, with its red rock landscape and diverse terrain offering endless possibilities for exploration. The Slickrock Bike Trail is a must-ride for experienced riders, featuring challenging slickrock formations and steep descents. For a more mellow ride with stunning views, the Porcupine Rim Trail offers a mix of technical sections and scenic overlooks of the Colorado River.

2. Whistler Bike Park, British Columbia, Canada
Whistler is known for its world-class mountain biking trails, with the Whistler Bike Park offering over 80 kilometers of downhill trails for riders of all skill levels. From flowy jump lines to technical rock gardens, the park has something for everyone. The stunning alpine views and lush forests make Whistler a must-visit destination for mountain biking enthusiasts.

3. Queenstown, New Zealand
Queenstown is a mountain biking paradise, with its rugged landscapes and stunning views of Lake Wakatipu. The Queenstown Bike Park offers a variety of trails for all abilities, from gentle cross-country rides to adrenaline-pumping downhill tracks. The Skyline Queenstown gondola provides easy access to the park’s trails, allowing riders to enjoy breathtaking views before descending back down the mountain.

4. Finale Ligure, Italy
Located on the Italian Riviera, Finale Ligure is a hidden gem for mountain bikers, with its network of trails winding through lush forests and rocky cliffs. The 24-hour Finale Ligure Enduro World Series race attracts riders from around the world, showcasing the area’s diverse terrain and stunning coastal views. Whether you’re seeking technical descents or scenic cross-country routes, Finale Ligure has something for every rider.

5. Rotorua, New Zealand
Rotorua is a volcanic wonderland, with its geothermal features and lush forests creating a unique mountain biking experience. The Whakarewarewa Forest offers over 100 kilometers of purpose-built trails, ranging from easy loops to challenging downhill runs. The Redwoods Treehouse Trail is a must-ride for its stunning views of the towering trees and geothermal vents, providing a truly memorable riding experience.

6. Verbier, Switzerland
Verbier is a playground for mountain bikers, with its high-altitude trails and panoramic views of the Swiss Alps. The Verbier Bike Park features a mix of flow trails and technical descents, catering to riders of all abilities. The iconic Mont-Fort descent is a bucket-list ride, offering breathtaking views of the Matterhorn and Mont Blanc as riders navigate steep switchbacks and rocky terrain.

7. Cairns, Australia
Cairns is a tropical paradise for mountain biking, with its lush rainforests and stunning coastal views. The Smithfield Mountain Bike Park hosts international competitions, showcasing its world-class trails and challenging features. The iconic Wooroonooran National Park offers a mix of cross-country and downhill trails, allowing riders to explore the region’s diverse ecosystem and breathtaking scenery.

8. Zermatt, Switzerland
Zermatt is a mountain biking destination like no other, with its iconic views of the Matterhorn and endless alpine trails to explore. The Gornergrat Trail is a highlight for its stunning vistas of the surrounding peaks and glaciers, offering a mix of technical descents and flowy singletrack. The high-altitude trails around Zermatt provide a unique riding experience, with panoramic views at every turn.

9. Sedona, Arizona, USA
Sedona is a desert oasis for mountain biking, with its red rock formations and scenic vistas attracting riders from around the world. The Sedona Mountain Bike Trail System offers over 250 kilometers of trails, ranging from easy scenic rides to technical challenges. The Hiline Trail is a must-ride for its challenging terrain and stunning views of Cathedral Rock, providing a thrilling experience for advanced riders.

10. Andermatt, Switzerland
Andermatt is a hidden gem for mountain bikers, with its rugged terrain and stunning alpine scenery. The Andermatt Mountain Bike Park offers a variety of trails for all abilities, from gentle forest paths to technical descents. The Oberalp Pass Trail is a highlight for its panoramic views of the Swiss Alps and challenging climbs, making it a must-do ride for experienced riders.

In conclusion, mountain biking trails around the world offer epic rides and stunning views that cater to riders of all abilities. From the red rocks of Moab to the alpine trails of Verbier, each destination provides a unique and memorable experience for mountain biking enthusiasts. Whether you’re seeking a leisurely ride through lush forests or an adrenaline-pumping descent down a rocky cliff, these trails offer endless opportunities for adventure and exploration. So grab your bike, hit the trails, and experience the thrill of mountain biking in some of the most beautiful locations on earth.
