The Best Grilled Vegetable Recipes for Summer


Summer is here, and that means it’s time to fire up the grill and enjoy some delicious grilled vegetable recipes. Grilling vegetables not only enhances their flavors, but it also adds a smoky and charred element that takes them to a whole new level. Whether you’re a vegetarian, vegan, or simply looking to incorporate more veggies into your diet, these grilled vegetable recipes are sure to please everyone at your summer cookouts.

1. Grilled Portobello Mushrooms with Balsamic Glaze:
Portobello mushrooms are perfect for grilling because of their meaty texture. To prepare this recipe, marinate the mushrooms in a mixture of balsamic vinegar, olive oil, garlic, and herbs for about 30 minutes. Then, grill them over medium heat until they are tender and slightly charred. Serve them with a drizzle of balsamic glaze for a tangy and savory flavor.

2. Grilled Corn on the Cob with Chili Lime Butter:
Nothing says summer like grilled corn on the cob. To make this recipe even more flavorful, spread a mixture of melted butter, lime juice, and chili powder onto the corn before grilling. The heat from the grill will caramelize the butter and infuse the corn with a smoky and zesty taste. Sprinkle some fresh cilantro on top for an extra burst of freshness.

3. Grilled Zucchini and Yellow Squash Skewers:
Zucchini and yellow squash are abundant during the summer months, and grilling them on skewers is a simple yet delicious way to enjoy them. Slice the vegetables into rounds and thread them onto skewers. Brush them with a mixture of olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and Italian seasoning. Grill them over medium-high heat until they are tender and lightly charred. These skewers make a colorful and healthy addition to any summer meal.

4. Grilled Eggplant with Tahini Sauce:
Eggplant is a versatile vegetable that becomes incredibly flavorful when grilled. To make this recipe, slice the eggplant into thick rounds and brush them with olive oil. Grill them over medium heat until they are soft and have grill marks. Serve the grilled eggplant with a creamy tahini sauce made from tahini paste, lemon juice, garlic, and salt. The combination of the smoky eggplant and the tangy tahini sauce is simply irresistible.

5. Grilled Bell Peppers Stuffed with Quinoa and Feta:
For a hearty and nutritious grilled vegetable dish, try stuffing bell peppers with quinoa and feta cheese. Start by grilling the bell peppers until they are slightly charred and softened. Meanwhile, cook quinoa according to package instructions and mix it with crumbled feta cheese, cherry tomatoes, fresh herbs, and a drizzle of olive oil. Stuff the mixture into the grilled bell peppers and return them to the grill for a few more minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly. These stuffed peppers are not only delicious but also make for an impressive presentation.

In conclusion, grilling vegetables is a fantastic way to elevate their flavors and create delicious and healthy summer dishes. Whether you’re a seasoned griller or just starting out, these grilled vegetable recipes are sure to impress your friends and family at your next summer gathering. So fire up the grill, get creative with your vegetable selection, and enjoy the smoky and charred goodness of summer!
