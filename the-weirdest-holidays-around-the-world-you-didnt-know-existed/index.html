The Weirdest Holidays Around the World You Didn't Know Existed


In a world filled with traditional holidays like Christmas, New Year’s, and Halloween, there are some truly bizarre and peculiar celebrations that often go unnoticed. These obscure holidays, celebrated in various corners of the world, are a testament to the diversity of human culture and the wacky creativity that exists within us. Join me as we explore some of the weirdest holidays around the world that you probably didn’t know existed.

1. La Tomatina - Bunol, Spain:
Imagine a street filled with people, all armed with ripe tomatoes, ready to engage in one giant food fight. La Tomatina, held on the last Wednesday of August in the small town of Bunol, Spain, is exactly that. What started as a spontaneous tomato fight in 1945 has now become a full-blown festival attracting thousands of locals and tourists. Participants hurl tomatoes at each other, transforming the streets into a sea of red pulp. It’s messy, it’s bizarre, and it’s definitely one for the bucket list.

2. Kanamara Matsuri - Kawasaki, Japan:
Prepare yourself for a celebration that is as peculiar as it is colorful. Kanamara Matsuri, also known as the ‘Festival of the Steel Phallus,’ takes place on the first Sunday of April in Kawasaki, Japan. This unique festival celebrates fertility and is dedicated to the deity of fertility and childbirth. The festival features giant phallus-shaped floats, phallus-shaped candies, and even phallus-shaped decorations. It’s a lively and lighthearted event that embraces sexuality and breaks down taboos.

3. Day of the Dead - Mexico:
While not as obscure as the other holidays on this list, the Day of the Dead (Dia de los Muertos) in Mexico is a fascinating celebration that deserves a mention. This multi-day holiday, celebrated from October 31st to November 2nd, honors deceased loved ones. Families create altars adorned with photographs, favorite foods, and personal belongings of the departed. They believe that during this time, the spirits of their loved ones return to the world of the living to be with them. The streets come alive with vibrant parades, decorative sugar skulls, and marigolds, symbolizing death and rebirth.

4. Cheese Rolling - Gloucestershire, England:
In the picturesque hills of Gloucestershire, England, a centuries-old tradition takes place every year. Known as the Cheese Rolling Festival, participants chase a wheel of cheese down a steep hill. The objective is simple - to catch the cheese. However, with the cheese reaching speeds of up to 70 miles per hour, this race is not for the faint-hearted. It’s a thrilling and adrenaline-fueled event that attracts both locals and visitors alike.

5. Baby-Jumping Festival - Castrillo de Murcia, Spain:
If you thought running with the bulls was extreme, wait until you hear about the Baby-Jumping Festival. Taking place in the small town of Castrillo de Murcia, Spain, this unusual festival involves men dressed as the devil leaping over rows of infants lying on mattresses. The event is believed to cleanse the babies of original sin and bring them good fortune. It’s a spectacle that is both fascinating and controversial, but it continues to be celebrated year after year.

These are just a few examples of the many weird and wonderful holidays that exist around the world. From food fights to fertility festivals, each celebration showcases the unique traditions and customs of different cultures. So the next time you’re planning your travels, consider incorporating one of these unusual holidays into your itinerary. You never know what strange and unforgettable experiences await you.
