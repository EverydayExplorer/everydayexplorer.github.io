Exploring the Subgenres of Fantasy: From High to Low and Everything In Between


The world of fantasy is a rich and diverse one, filled with endless possibilities and captivating adventures. From the mythical lands of elves and dragons to the dark, gritty realms of urban fantasy, there is something for every reader to delve into and get lost in. But within the broader genre of fantasy, there are also multiple subgenres that cater to different tastes and preferences. In this blog post, we will be exploring the various subgenres of fantasy and discussing what makes them unique.

High Fantasy:

High fantasy is perhaps the most classic and well-known subgenre of fantasy. It is characterized by its epic scope, sprawling world-building, and larger-than-life characters. In high fantasy, the setting is often a completely fictional secondary world, distinct from our own. Think J.R.R. Tolkien’s Middle Earth or George R.R. Martin’s Westeros. High fantasy stories are often characterized by quests, prophecies, and grand battles between good and evil. Magic is prevalent and the stakes are high. If you’re looking for an immersive and epic adventure, high fantasy is the subgenre for you.

Low Fantasy:

In contrast to high fantasy, low fantasy is characterized by a more grounded and realistic setting. It takes place in our own world or a world very similar to ours, but with the addition of fantastical elements. These elements could be magical creatures, supernatural powers, or hidden realms. Low fantasy often focuses on the interactions between the fantastical and the everyday, blurring the line between reality and magic. Neil Gaiman’s ‘Neverwhere’ and Lev Grossman’s ‘The Magicians’ are prime examples of low fantasy. If you’re intrigued by the idea of the fantastical lurking just beneath the surface of our world, low fantasy is the subgenre to explore.

Urban Fantasy:

Urban fantasy is a subgenre that brings the fantastical into a contemporary urban setting. It often features protagonists who are immersed in a modern world but find themselves dealing with supernatural forces and creatures. From vampires and werewolves to witches and fae, urban fantasy takes the familiar elements of city life and gives them a magical twist. Works such as Jim Butcher’s ‘Dresden Files’ series and Patricia Briggs’ ‘Mercy Thompson’ series exemplify this subgenre. If you crave a mix of the ordinary and extraordinary, urban fantasy will scratch that itch.

Historical Fantasy:

Historical fantasy combines elements of fantasy with real-world historical settings. It takes historical events and figures and adds a magical or fantastical element to them. This subgenre allows readers to experience familiar historical periods in a whole new way. From Arthurian legends to ancient Rome or medieval Europe, historical fantasy blends history and magic to create a unique reading experience. Mary Robinette Kowal’s ‘Glamourist Histories’ series and Guy Gavriel Kay’s ‘The Lions of Al-Rassan’ are excellent examples of this subgenre. If you enjoy a dash of history with your fantasy, historical fantasy is the perfect choice.

Steampunk:

Steampunk is a subgenre of fantasy that combines elements of the Victorian era with steam-powered technology and futuristic concepts. It often features an alternative history where steam power remained dominant, resulting in a world filled with airships, clockwork contraptions, and elaborate machinery. Steampunk stories are known for their intricate world-building and attention to detail. Authors such as Gail Carriger and Philip Reeve have crafted immersive steampunk worlds in their respective works. If you’re drawn to the aesthetic of the Victorian era and enjoy imaginative technology, steampunk is a subgenre you won’t want to miss.

These are just a few examples of the many subgenres that fall under the umbrella of fantasy. Each subgenre offers a unique reading experience and allows readers to explore different facets of the fantastical. Whether you prefer epic quests in fictional worlds or everyday life with a touch of magic, there is a subgenre of fantasy that will capture your imagination.

In conclusion, the world of fantasy is vast and varied, with subgenres to suit every reader’s taste. From high fantasy to low fantasy, urban fantasy to historical fantasy, and even steampunk, there is a subgenre for everyone. By exploring these subgenres, readers can uncover new and exciting worlds, characters, and adventures. So, grab a book and get ready to embark on a journey full of magic, wonder, and endless possibilities. Happy reading!
