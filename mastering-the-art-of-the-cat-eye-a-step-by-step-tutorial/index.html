Mastering the Art of the Cat Eye: A Step-By-Step Tutorial


The cat eye makeup look is a classic and timeless beauty trend that has been popular for decades. It is a versatile style that can be worn for any occasion, from a casual day out to a glamorous evening event. If you’ve always wanted to master the art of the cat eye but found it challenging, fear not! In this step-by-step tutorial, we will guide you through the process of achieving the perfect cat eye look.

Step 1: Prep and Prime

Before you start creating your cat eye, it’s essential to prep your eyelids. Start by applying an eyeshadow primer to ensure that your eye makeup stays in place all day. This will also help to intensify the color of your eyeshadow and eyeliner.

Step 2: Choose Your Eyeliner

The key to a flawless cat eye is selecting the right eyeliner. You can use a gel eyeliner, liquid liner, or a pencil liner, depending on your preference and skill level. For beginners, a pencil liner might be easier to control, while a gel or liquid liner will provide a more precise and defined look.

Step 3: Create the Wing

To start creating your cat eye, begin at the outer corner of your eye. Imagine a line extending from the corner of your eye towards the tail of your eyebrow. Using short, feathery strokes, draw a diagonal line that follows this imaginary line.

Next, connect this line to your upper lash line, creating a triangle shape. Fill in the triangle with your eyeliner, ensuring a smooth and even application. Take your time and build up the thickness of the line gradually to achieve your desired intensity.

Step 4: Fill in the Eyeliner

Once you have created the wing, it’s time to fill in the rest of your eyeliner along your upper lash line. Start from the inner corner of your eye and work your way towards the outer corner, following the natural curve of your eye.

If you prefer a more dramatic cat eye, you can extend the line slightly beyond the outer corner of your eye. However, be careful not to make it too long, as it can create a droopy effect.

Step 5: Clean Up and Perfect

To ensure a crisp and polished cat eye, clean up any mistakes or uneven lines using a cotton swab dipped in makeup remover. You can also use a concealer or a nude eyeshadow shade to tidy up the edges and create a seamless finish.

Step 6: Finishing Touches

To complete your cat eye look, apply a few coats of mascara to your lashes to add volume and length. You can also curl your lashes before applying mascara for an extra eye-opening effect. Consider adding a subtle eyeshadow shade to your crease to enhance the dimension of your eye makeup.

Final Thoughts

Mastering the art of the cat eye takes practice, but with the right techniques and tools, you can achieve a flawless result. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don’t be discouraged if your first attempts aren’t perfect. Experiment with different eyeliner types and styles to find what works best for you.

Whether you’re going for a subtle and understated cat eye or a bold and dramatic statement, this classic makeup look will never go out of style. So grab your eyeliner, take a deep breath, and let your creativity soar as you master the art of the cat eye.

Now that you have the step-by-step guide, it’s time to unleash your inner feline and rock that cat eye with confidence!
