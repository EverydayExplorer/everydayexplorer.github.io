Surviving Extreme Weather: Skills for Coping with Storms and Hurricanes


Welcome to our blog post dedicated to helping you survive extreme weather, specifically storms and hurricanes! Mother Nature can be unpredictable and it’s crucial to be prepared for anything she throws our way. Whether you’re a seasoned storm chaser or new to experiencing extreme weather, we’ve got you covered with a comprehensive guide on how to cope with storms and hurricanes.

1. Stay Informed: Knowledge is power when it comes to surviving extreme weather. Stay updated with the latest weather forecasts and warnings from reliable sources such as national weather services, meteorological websites, and local news channels. Understanding the intensity and trajectory of a storm or hurricane allows you to make informed decisions regarding your safety and preparedness.

2. Create an Emergency Kit: Assemble an emergency kit that includes essential items such as non-perishable food, drinking water, first aid supplies, flashlights, batteries, a battery-powered radio, a whistle, a multipurpose tool, and any necessary medications. Keep the kit in a designated place that is easily accessible to all family members.

3. Secure Your Home: Protect your home from potential damage by securing loose objects in your yard, such as furniture, potted plants, and outdoor equipment. Reinforce windows and doors, or consider installing storm shutters. In regions prone to hurricanes, it may be worth investing in impact-resistant windows and doors for added protection.

4. Have an Evacuation Plan: Familiarize yourself with local evacuation routes and shelters in your area. Create a detailed evacuation plan with your family members, including a designated meeting point in case you get separated. Keep important documents, such as identification and insurance papers, in a waterproof bag that you can easily grab in case of an evacuation.

5. Stock Up on Supplies: Before a storm or hurricane hits, stock up on necessary supplies such as batteries, candles, fuel for generators (if applicable), and non-perishable food items. Having these supplies readily available will ensure that you can sustain yourself and your family if you lose power or become isolated for an extended period.

6. Practice Electrical Safety: During storms, power outages are common. Minimize the risk of electrical accidents by turning off and unplugging unnecessary appliances and electronics. Avoid using candles as a primary light source to prevent fire hazards, and instead, opt for battery-powered lanterns or flashlights.

7. Stay Indoors and Seek Shelter: When extreme weather strikes, it’s best to stay indoors and seek shelter in a sturdy building. Stay away from windows and doors, and if possible, find a small interior room on the lowest level of your home, such as a basement or bathroom. If you’re in a region prone to hurricanes, consider installing a safe room that provides additional protection during severe storms.

8. Communicate and Check on Loved Ones: Stay in touch with your loved ones during extreme weather events. Share your plans and keep each other updated on your well-being. If you have elderly or vulnerable family members or neighbors, make sure to check on them and offer assistance if needed.

9. After the Storm: Once the storm or hurricane has passed, exercise caution when returning home. Be mindful of fallen power lines, debris, and standing water, which may hide potential dangers. Take photographs of any damage to your property for insurance purposes, and contact your insurance provider promptly to initiate the claims process.

Remember, surviving extreme weather is a combination of preparedness, vigilance, and resilience. By following these tips and staying informed, you’ll be better equipped to handle storms and hurricanes. Stay safe, stay prepared, and take care of yourself and your loved ones!
