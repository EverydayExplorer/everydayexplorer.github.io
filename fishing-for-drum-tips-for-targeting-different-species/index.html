Fishing for Drum: Tips for Targeting Different Species


When it comes to fishing, targeting different species of fish can be an exciting challenge. One species of fish that many anglers seek out is the drum. The drum is a popular game fish that is known for its strong fighting ability and tasty flesh. In this blog post, we will provide you with some tips and techniques for targeting different species of drum, including the black drum, red drum, and spotted drum.

1. Black Drum:

The black drum, also known as the freshwater drum, is a species of fish that is commonly found in bodies of water such as rivers, lakes, and ponds. Black drum are known for their dark coloration and distinctive drumming noise that they produce by vibrating their swim bladder. Here are some tips for targeting black drum:

- Location: Black drum can often be found near structure such as submerged logs, fallen trees, and underwater rock formations. Look for areas with slow-moving water and a sandy or muddy bottom.

- Bait: Black drum are not picky eaters and will readily take a variety of baits. Some popular bait options include earthworms, nightcrawlers, minnows, and cut bait such as mullet or shrimp.

- Tackle: When targeting black drum, it is recommended to use medium to heavy action rods with a spinning reel or baitcasting reel. Use a weight appropriate for the current and consider using a Carolina rig or a drop shot rig.

2. Red Drum:

Red drum, also known as redfish, are a sought-after species of fish that can be found along the Atlantic coast and Gulf of Mexico. Red drum are known for their striking copper coloration and powerful fighting ability. Here are some tips for targeting red drum:

- Location: Red drum can be found near marshes, grass beds, and oyster bars. Look for areas with submerged vegetation and structure such as rocks or fallen trees.

- Bait: Red drum are opportunistic feeders and will eat a variety of baits. Some effective bait options include live shrimp, finger mullet, blue crabs, and artificial lures such as spoons, soft plastics, and topwater plugs.

- Tackle: When targeting red drum, it is recommended to use medium to heavy action rods with a spinning reel or baitcasting reel. Use a weight appropriate for the current and consider using a popping cork rig or a Carolina rig.

3. Spotted Drum:

The spotted drum, also known as the speckled drum, is a species of fish that is commonly found in the tropical waters of the Caribbean and the western Atlantic Ocean. Spotted drum are known for their distinct black and white coloration and can be a thrilling catch for anglers. Here are some tips for targeting spotted drum:

- Location: Spotted drum can be found near coral reefs, rocky bottoms, and seagrass beds. Look for areas with clear water and a lot of reef structure.

- Bait: Spotted drum are predatory fish and will eat a variety of baits. Some recommended bait options include small live fish, shrimp, crabs, and squid.

- Tackle: When targeting spotted drum, it is recommended to use medium action rods with a spinning reel. Use a weight appropriate for the current and consider using a jig or a live bait setup.

In conclusion, targeting different species of drum can provide an exciting and rewarding fishing experience. Whether you are targeting black drum, red drum, or spotted drum, the tips and techniques mentioned in this blog post will help increase your chances of success. Remember to do your research on the specific species you are targeting and always follow local fishing regulations. Happy fishing!
