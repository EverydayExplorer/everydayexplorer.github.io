Top 5 Scenic Drives in the United States


There’s something absolutely magical about hitting the open road and embarking on a scenic drive, isn’t there? The wind in your hair, the freedom to explore at your own pace, and the breathtaking beauty of the landscapes around you. The United States is blessed with countless stunning scenic drives that cater to every taste and preference. Whether you’re a nature lover, adventure seeker, or simply someone who appreciates the beauty of the open road, there’s a scenic drive in the US that’s perfect for you. In this blog post, we’ll explore the top 5 scenic drives in the United States that you simply cannot miss.

1. Pacific Coast Highway, California

Stretching for approximately 655 miles along the coastline of California, the Pacific Coast Highway (PCH) is without a doubt one of the most iconic and breathtaking scenic drives in the United States. Starting from Dana Point in Orange County and ending at Leggett in northern Mendocino County, this drive offers breathtaking views of rugged cliffs, pristine beaches, and picturesque coastal towns. As you cruise along the PCH, don’t forget to make stops at renowned sites like the Bixby Bridge, Big Sur, and the famous 17-Mile Drive in Monterey.

2. Blue Ridge Parkway, North Carolina and Virginia

Connecting the Great Smoky Mountains National Park in North Carolina to Shenandoah National Park in Virginia, the Blue Ridge Parkway is a 469-mile long road that takes you through some of the most stunning landscapes in the eastern United States. Driving along this scenic route, you’ll be treated to panoramic views of the Blue Ridge Mountains, awe-inspiring waterfalls, and vibrant autumn foliage during the fall season. Be sure to make a stop at the picturesque Mabry Mill and take in the views from the popular overlooks like Chimney Rock and Grandfather Mountain.

3. Going-to-the-Sun Road, Montana

Located in Glacier National Park, Montana, the Going-to-the-Sun Road is an engineering marvel that provides visitors with unparalleled access to the park’s dramatic landscapes and breathtaking mountain vistas. This 50-mile-long road takes you through the heart of the park, crossing the Continental Divide and offering magnificent views of glaciers, alpine meadows, and crystal-clear lakes. Allow plenty of time to stop at Logan Pass, Jackson Glacier Overlook, and the Weeping Wall to fully appreciate the beauty of this scenic drive.

4. The Million Dollar Highway, Colorado

Don’t let the name fool you - the Million Dollar Highway is worth every penny. This 25-mile stretch of US Route 550 in southwestern Colorado is known for its hairpin turns, steep cliffs, and jaw-dropping views of the San Juan Mountains. As you navigate the serpentine curves, you’ll be treated to panoramic vistas of rugged peaks, deep canyons, and vibrant wildflowers. Make sure to stop at the historic mining towns of Silverton and Ouray along the way and soak in their old-world charm.

5. Overseas Highway, Florida

The Overseas Highway is a true gem among scenic drives, offering a unique experience unlike any other on this list. Stretching for 113 miles from Miami to Key West, this highway takes you across a series of bridges and causeways through the Florida Keys, allowing you to drive over the stunning turquoise waters of the Atlantic Ocean. Be prepared to encounter stunning ocean views, sailboats, and opportunities for water activities such as snorkeling and fishing. Stop at the iconic Seven Mile Bridge, visit the beautiful beaches of Islamorada, and indulge in the famous Key lime pie along the way.

Now that we’ve explored the top 5 scenic drives in the United States, it’s time for you to start planning your next epic road trip. Whether you choose the breathtaking Pacific Coast Highway, the picturesque Blue Ridge Parkway, the awe-inspiring Going-to-the-Sun Road, the thrilling Million Dollar Highway, or the unique Overseas Highway, you’re guaranteed an unforgettable journey filled with natural wonders and stunning landscapes. So, pack your bags, grab your camera, and hit the open road to embark on the adventure of a lifetime. Happy road tripping!
