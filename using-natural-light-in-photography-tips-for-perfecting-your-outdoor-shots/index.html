Using Natural Light in Photography: Tips for Perfecting Your Outdoor Shots


Photography is an art form that allows us to capture and preserve moments in time. Whether you’re just starting out or have been shooting for years, understanding how to use natural light can greatly improve your outdoor photography. Natural light has a beautiful and flattering quality that artificial lighting simply cannot replicate. In this blog post, we will share some tips and techniques for using natural light to perfect your outdoor shots.

1. Time of day can make a difference

The first tip is to pay attention to the time of day when shooting outdoors. The quality of natural light varies throughout the day, and different times will create different moods in your photos. The golden hour, which occurs shortly after sunrise and before sunset, is often considered the best time for outdoor photography. During this time, the sun is low in the sky, creating soft and warm hues that can add a magical touch to your images. Avoid shooting in the harsh midday sun, as it can create harsh shadows and blow out highlights.

2. Understand the direction of light

The direction of light is another important aspect to consider when shooting outdoors. Front lighting, where the sun is behind you and illuminating the subject from the front, is the most common and can create a well-lit, evenly exposed image. However, experimenting with side lighting and backlighting can add depth and drama to your photos. Side lighting will create shadows and highlights that can add texture, while backlighting will create a halo effect around the subject. Play around with different directions of light to find the style that suits your vision.

3. Utilize diffusers and reflectors

Sometimes, the natural light may not be ideal for the look you’re trying to achieve. In these situations, diffusers and reflectors can come in handy. A diffuser is a translucent material that softens and spreads the light, creating a more even and flattering illumination. You can use a commercial diffuser or even a simple white sheet to achieve this effect. Reflectors, on the other hand, bounce light back onto the subject, filling in shadows and adding a gentle glow. Reflectors come in various colors, such as silver, gold, and white, each producing a different effect. Experiment with these tools to enhance the natural light and create stunning outdoor shots.

4. Pay attention to the background

When shooting outdoors, it’s important to consider the background as it can greatly impact the overall composition and mood of your photos. Look for clean, uncluttered backgrounds that won’t distract from your subject. Avoid busy or messy backgrounds that may take away from the main focal point. Additionally, be mindful of the colors in the background and how they interact with the natural light. The right background choice can help create a harmonious and visually pleasing image.

5. Embrace the blue hour

The blue hour is a beautiful time of day that occurs shortly before sunrise and after sunset. It is characterized by a soft, cool, and blueish light that can add an ethereal and dreamy quality to your outdoor shots. During the blue hour, the world takes on a surreal and magical feel. Experiment with long exposures during this time to capture stunning cityscapes, landscapes, or even portraits. Just like the golden hour, the blue hour offers a unique and captivating natural light to enhance your photography.

In conclusion, learning to utilize natural light is essential for any outdoor photographer. By understanding the time of day, direction of light, and utilizing diffusers and reflectors, you can create captivating and professional-looking images. Don’t forget to pay attention to the background and embrace the unique qualities of the blue hour. With practice and experimentation, you will become more proficient in using natural light to perfection in your outdoor shots. So grab your camera, head outside, and let the beauty of natural light guide you in capturing stunning and memorable moments.
