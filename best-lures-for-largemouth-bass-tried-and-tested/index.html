Best Lures for Largemouth Bass: Tried and Tested


Largemouth bass is one of the most popular freshwater game fish and is highly sought after by anglers around the world. Catching this elusive species requires the right set of skills, knowledge, and of course, the best lures. In this blog post, we will explore some tried and tested lures for largemouth bass that have proven to be successful in reeling in these hard-fighting fish.

Understanding Largemouth Bass Behavior

Before diving into the best lures for largemouth bass, it’s important to understand their behavior and feeding patterns. Largemouth bass are known for their aggressive nature and their preference for ambush-style attacks on their prey. They tend to hide around structures such as rocks, fallen trees, weed beds, and lily pads, waiting for an opportunity to strike.

Taking these habits into consideration, it’s crucial to choose lures that mimic the appearance and movement of the bass’s natural prey.

Top Lures for Largemouth Bass

  1. Jigs: Jigs are versatile lures that can be used in various fishing conditions. They consist of a weighted hook with a painted head and a silicone or rubber skirt. Jigs are effective in imitating both crawfish and baitfish, which are common prey for largemouth bass. When retrieving a jig, making it hop and bounce off the bottom mimics the action of a prey trying to escape.

  2. Plastic Worms: Plastic worms are a classic choice for targeting largemouth bass. These soft baits can be rigged Texas or Carolina style and come in various colors and sizes. Their lifelike wriggling action, especially when rigged with a worm weight, can entice even the most finicky bass. Slowly retrieving a plastic worm along the bottom near structures can trigger a strike from lurking bass.

  3. Crankbaits: Crankbaits are designed to imitate injured or fleeing baitfish. These lures have a diving lip that allows them to dive to a specific depth when retrieved. Their realistic swimming action coupled with the vibrations they produce can trigger a reaction strike from bass. Crankbaits are particularly effective when bass are actively feeding near the surface or around mid-depth structures.

  4. Spinnerbaits: Spinnerbaits are excellent lures for covering a large area quickly. They consist of a metal blade that spins as it’s retrieved, creating flash and vibration, and a skirted hook. The unique design of spinnerbaits makes them ideal for fishing in weedy and brushy areas. Retrieving a spinnerbait at various speeds allows you to imitate both slow-moving and fast-fleeing prey.

  5. Topwater Lures: For an exhilarating fishing experience, nothing beats the explosive strikes that topwater lures can elicit. These lures are designed to mimic injured or struggling prey that remains on or near the water’s surface. Topwater lures such as poppers, buzzbaits, and frogs create commotion and noise that grab the attention of bass. Using these lures in low light conditions or when bass are actively feeding near the surface can yield fantastic results.

Tips for Using Lures Effectively

To maximize your chances of catching largemouth bass, here are a few additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Match the hatch: Pay attention to the prey species present in the water you’re fishing and try to choose lures that closely resemble them.

  • Vary your retrieve: Experiment with different retrieval speeds, pauses, and jerks to imitate different prey behaviors and trigger strikes.

  • Target structures: Bass love to hide around structures, so aim your casts near rocks, fallen trees, weed beds, and other potential hiding spots.

  • Pay attention to water temperature: Largemouth bass tend to be more active and aggressive in warmer water, so adjust your lure choice and presentation accordingly.

  • Have confidence in your lures: Trust your instincts and have faith in the lures you choose. A confident angler is more likely to attract fish.

In conclusion, selecting the right lures is essential for a successful day of largemouth bass fishing. The lures mentioned above - jigs, plastic worms, crankbaits, spinnerbaits, and topwater lures - have stood the test of time and have proven to be effective in attracting and hooking largemouth bass. Remember to adapt your lure choice based on the prevailing conditions and be persistent in your fishing efforts. With the right technique and the right lure, you’ll increase your chances of catching some trophy largemouth bass!
