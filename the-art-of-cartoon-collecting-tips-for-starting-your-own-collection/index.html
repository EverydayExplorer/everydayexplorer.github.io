The Art of Cartoon Collecting: Tips for Starting Your Own Collection


If you’re a fan of cartoons, then you probably know how exciting it can be to start collecting your favorite pieces of art. Not only does collecting cartoons give you a chance to showcase your love for a particular show or character, but it also allows you to create a unique and personalized collection that reflects your tastes and personality.

But where do you start when it comes to building your own cartoon collection? From deciding what types of cartoons to focus on to finding the best pieces to add to your collection, there are a lot of factors to consider. To help you get started on your cartoon collecting journey, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  1. Define Your Focus: The world of cartoons is vast and diverse, so it’s important to determine what specific types of cartoons you want to collect. Whether you’re a fan of classic animations like Mickey Mouse and Bugs Bunny, or prefer more modern cartoons like Steven Universe and Adventure Time, having a clear focus will help you narrow down your options and build a cohesive collection.

  2. Set a Budget: Collecting cartoons can be a fun and rewarding hobby, but it can also be expensive. Before you start adding pieces to your collection, it’s important to set a budget that works for you. This will help you avoid overspending and ensure that you’re able to enjoy your collection without breaking the bank.

  3. Research: Once you’ve decided on your focus and set a budget, it’s time to start researching potential pieces for your collection. Whether you’re looking for original animation cels, limited edition prints, or collectible figurines, there are a variety of options available to cartoon collectors. Take the time to explore different sources, such as online marketplaces, specialty stores, and conventions, to find the perfect pieces for your collection.

  4. Network with Other Collectors: Building a network of fellow collectors can be a valuable resource when it comes to growing your collection. Not only can other collectors provide recommendations and insights on where to find the best pieces, but they can also help you connect with sellers and artists who may have rare or unique items for sale.

  5. Display Your Collection: Once you’ve started adding pieces to your collection, it’s important to think about how you want to display them. Whether you choose to showcase your cartoons in a dedicated display case, frame them for hanging on the wall, or rotate them throughout your home, displaying your collection in a way that reflects your personal style and preferences will help you enjoy and appreciate your pieces even more.

  6. Protect Your Investment: Collecting cartoons can be a valuable investment, so it’s important to take steps to protect your collection. Whether you’re storing your pieces in archival-quality sleeves, keeping them out of direct sunlight, or insuring them against damage or loss, taking the time to care for your collection will help ensure that it remains in top condition for years to come.

Ultimately, collecting cartoons is a fun and rewarding hobby that allows you to express your love for your favorite shows and characters in a creative and personalized way. By following these tips and staying true to your own tastes and interests, you can create a unique and vibrant collection that brings you joy and excitement for years to come.
