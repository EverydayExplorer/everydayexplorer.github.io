How to Make Homemade Sourdough Bread: A Beginner's Guide


In recent years, there has been a resurgence in the popularity of homemade bread. And one type of bread that has captured the attention of many baking enthusiasts is sourdough bread. The tangy flavor, chewy texture, and rustic appearance make it a favorite among bread lovers. If you’ve ever wondered how to make this delicious bread from scratch, you’re in luck! In this beginner’s guide, we’ll take you through the step-by-step process of making homemade sourdough bread.

Before we dive into the process, let’s understand what makes sourdough bread unique. Unlike regular bread, which relies on commercial yeast for fermentation, sourdough bread is made using a natural leavening agent called a sourdough starter. This starter is a mixture of flour and water that captures wild yeast and beneficial bacteria from the environment. Over time, this mixture becomes a living organism, creating a distinct sourdough flavor.

To begin your sourdough bread journey, you’ll need to create a sourdough starter. Start by mixing equal parts of flour and water in a clean, glass jar. Cover the jar loosely with a cloth or plastic wrap and leave it at room temperature. Every day, discard half of the mixture and feed it with fresh flour and water. After a few days, you’ll notice bubbles forming and a slightly tangy aroma. Congratulations, you now have a sourdough starter!

Once your starter is active and bubbly, it’s time to make the dough. In a large bowl, mix together 500 grams of bread flour, 350 grams of water, and 100 grams of your sourdough starter. Add 10 grams of salt and mix until all the ingredients are well combined. Cover the bowl with a kitchen towel and let it rest for about 30 minutes.

After the initial rest, it’s time to begin the process of fermentation and dough development. This is where the magic happens! Using the ‘stretch and fold’ technique, gently stretch the dough from one side and fold it over the top. Rotate the bowl and repeat this process three more times, ensuring that all sides of the dough have been stretched and folded. This process helps to strengthen the gluten and develop the dough’s structure.

Now it’s time for the first rise. Place the dough back into the bowl, cover it, and let it rest at room temperature for about 3-4 hours. During this time, the dough will double in size, thanks to the activity of the wild yeast in your sourdough starter.

Once the dough has risen, it’s time to shape your bread. Gently transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and fold it in on itself, creating tension on the surface. Place the shaped dough into a proofing basket or a bowl lined with a floured kitchen towel. Cover it and let it rise for another 2-3 hours, or until it has visibly puffed up.

While the dough is undergoing its final rise, preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C) and place a Dutch oven or a baking stone inside to heat up. When the dough is ready, carefully transfer it into the preheated Dutch oven or onto the hot baking stone. Score the top of the bread with a sharp knife to allow for steam to escape during baking.

Bake the bread covered for the first 20 minutes, then remove the lid or cover and continue baking for another 20-25 minutes, or until the bread is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom. Allow the bread to cool completely on a wire rack before slicing into it.

Congratulations! You’ve successfully made your very own homemade sourdough bread. The process may seem intimidating at first, but with practice and patience, you’ll soon become a sourdough master. Experiment with different flours, add-ins like seeds or herbs, and enjoy the endless possibilities that sourdough bread has to offer. Happy baking!

Note: Remember to maintain your sourdough starter by regularly feeding it with fresh flour and water. This will keep it active and ready for your next baking adventure.
