Exploring the World of Bitters: From Classic to Artisanal Varieties


Bitters have been an essential ingredient in the world of cocktails for centuries. They add depth, complexity, and balance to drinks, elevating them to new heights of flavor. From classic bitters to artisanal varieties, there is a whole world of options to explore and discover. In this blog post, we’ll take a deep dive into the fascinating world of bitters, learn about their history, production methods, and how to use them in cocktails.

The History of Bitters

Bitters have a rich history that dates back to ancient times. The first known use of bitters can be traced back to ancient Egypt, where medicinal herbal preparations were infused with wine to create healing tonics. Over time, these tonics evolved into what we now know as bitters.

In the 18th and 19th centuries, bitters became widely popular as a remedy for various ailments, including digestive issues. They were typically made by infusing a blend of botanicals, herbs, roots, and barks in high-proof spirits. Soon, cocktail pioneers started experimenting with these herbal concoctions in their drinks, ultimately giving birth to classic cocktails like the Old Fashioned and the Manhattan.

Classic Bitters

Classic bitters are the foundation of the bitters world. They are essential in classic cocktail recipes and are often used to add balance and complexity to drinks. Some of the most well-known classic bitters include angostura bitters, aromatic bitters, and orange bitters.

Angostura bitters, created by Dr. Johann Gottlieb Benjamin Siegert in the early 19th century, are an iconic example of classic bitters. Made with a secret blend of herbs and spices, angostura bitters add a unique and unmistakable flavor profile to cocktails. They are particularly famous for their use in the classic Whiskey Sour and Pink Gin cocktails.

Aromatic bitters, such as Peychaud’s bitters, have a long-standing history in the world of cocktails. They are typically characterized by their strong, bitter flavor and their ability to bring balance to sweet or citrusy cocktails. Originally created by Antoine Amedie Peychaud in the 19th century, Peychaud’s bitters became an essential ingredient in the classic Sazerac cocktail, which is a cornerstone of New Orleans’ cocktail culture.

Orange bitters, as the name suggests, infuse cocktails with a bright and citrusy flavor. The use of orange bitters can take a cocktail to new heights, adding a refreshing and aromatic twist to the drink. Regan’s Orange Bitters No. 6, created by bartender Gary Regan, is a popular choice among bartenders and cocktail enthusiasts alike.

Artisanal Bitters

In recent years, there has been a resurgence of interest in artisanal bitters. Craft producers have embraced the world of bitters, experimenting with unique botanical combinations and innovative production methods. This has led to a plethora of artisanal bitters that offer a wide range of flavors and aromas.

Artisanal bitters often use small-batch production methods, allowing producers to focus on quality and attention to detail. They often incorporate locally sourced botanicals and unique flavor profiles that set them apart from classic bitters. From barrel-aged bitters to floral and fruity infusions, there is no shortage of options to explore in the world of artisanal bitters.

One notable example is Bittered Sling from Canada. Created by bartenders Lauren Mote and Jonathan Chovancek, Bittered Sling offers a diverse range of flavors, from plum & rootbeer to Malagasy chocolate. These innovative bitters are sought after by mixologists around the world, as they bring a new level of complexity and creativity to cocktails.

Using Bitters in Cocktails

When it comes to using bitters in cocktails, the possibilities are endless. Bitters can be used to enhance the flavor of classic cocktails, create new and innovative recipes, or simply elevate a classic spirit and mixer combination.

To use bitters in cocktails, a few dashes are typically added alongside the other ingredients. The right amount of bitters can make a significant difference in the overall flavor profile of a drink. It’s important to note that bitters are highly concentrated and should be used sparingly to avoid overpowering the other flavors in the cocktail.

Experimentation is key when using bitters in cocktails. Try different combinations of spirits, mixers, and bitters to discover new flavor profiles and create unique drinks. Whether you’re making a classic Old Fashioned or inventing your own signature cocktail, bitters will undoubtedly play a crucial role in taking your drink to the next level.

Conclusion

The world of bitters is vast and diverse, offering a myriad of flavors and possibilities. From classic bitters with a rich history to artisanal varieties that push the boundaries of flavor, exploring the world of bitters is an adventure worth embarking on. Whether you’re a seasoned mixologist or a cocktail enthusiast, bitters will undoubtedly add depth, complexity, and balance to your creations. So, next time you’re behind the bar or enjoying a drink, don’t forget to raise a glass to the fascinating world of bitters. Cheers!
