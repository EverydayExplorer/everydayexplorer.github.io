Wonders of the Ancient World: Fantasy Epics That Transport You to Forgotten Realms


In the vast expanse of literature, fantasy epics have always held a special place, enchanting readers with tales of heroism, magic, and mythical realms. And when it comes to transporting readers to forgotten realms, few genres achieve it as masterfully as fantasy. Within this genre lie echoes of the wonders of the ancient world, offering readers a chance to explore imagined landscapes that mirror the grandeur and mystery of civilizations long past.

Ancient wonders have captivated human imagination for millennia, from the towering pyramids of Egypt to the majestic Colosseum of Rome. These architectural marvels, along with their rich histories, have inspired countless authors to create fantastical worlds that evoke a sense of wonder and awe. In this blog post, we’ll delve into some of the most iconic wonders of the ancient world and explore how they have been reimagined in fantasy literature.

The Great Library of Alexandria, one of the most famous centers of learning in antiquity, has been immortalized in numerous fantasy epics as a repository of forbidden knowledge and arcane secrets. In works such as ‘The Name of the Wind’ by Patrick Rothfuss and ‘The Lies of Locke Lamora’ by Scott Lynch, the concept of a vast library filled with ancient tomes and mystical texts serves as a focal point for adventure and intrigue. These fictional libraries, much like their real-world counterpart, represent the pursuit of knowledge and the mysteries of the universe waiting to be unraveled.

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon, often described as one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, have been depicted in fantasy literature as enchanted gardens teeming with magical flora and fauna. In ‘The Garden of Evening Mists’ by Tan Twan Eng and ‘The Grace of Kings’ by Ken Liu, lush and vibrant gardens play a central role in the narrative, serving as symbols of beauty, tranquility, and hidden power. These fantastical gardens transport readers to a world of wonder and enchantment, where the ordinary rules of nature are suspended and anything is possible.

The Colossus of Rhodes, a colossal statue of the sun god Helios, has inspired authors to create larger-than-life characters and mythical beings imbued with divine power. In George R.R. Martin’s ‘A Song of Ice and Fire’ series, characters like Daenerys Targaryen and her dragons evoke the awe-inspiring presence of the Colossus, commanding the attention and admiration of all who behold them. These larger-than-life figures symbolize the triumph of the human spirit over adversity and the enduring legacy of ancient legends.

The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus, a monumental tomb built for the Carian king Mausolus, has been reimagined in fantasy literature as a haunted crypt or labyrinthine catacombs filled with undead horrors and ancient curses. In works such as ‘The Bone Clocks’ by David Mitchell and ‘The City of Brass’ by S.A. Chakraborty, protagonists venture into the depths of these ancient tombs in search of lost treasures and forbidden knowledge, facing perilous traps and malevolent spirits along the way. These dark and atmospheric settings evoke a sense of mystery and danger, inviting readers to explore the hidden depths of the past.

The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus, dedicated to the goddess of the hunt, has inspired authors to create mystical sanctuaries and sacred groves where gods and mortals alike seek refuge and guidance. In Neil Gaiman’s ‘American Gods’ and Madeline Miller’s ‘Circe,’ ancient deities walk among mortals, their divine presence infusing the natural world with magic and wonder. These mythical realms blur the boundaries between reality and fantasy, inviting readers to suspend their disbelief and embark on a journey into the unknown.

In conclusion, the wonders of the ancient world continue to captivate and inspire us, serving as timeless symbols of human creativity and ingenuity. Through the lens of fantasy literature, these iconic landmarks are reimagined and transformed into fantastical realms where anything is possible. Whether exploring the labyrinthine catacombs beneath a forgotten tomb or wandering through enchanted gardens teeming with magical creatures, readers are transported to a world of wonder and imagination, where the echoes of the past resonate with the promise of adventure and discovery.
