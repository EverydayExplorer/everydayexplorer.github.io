10 Underrated Musical Instruments Worth Exploring


When it comes to music, many people are familiar with the mainstream instruments like the guitar, piano, and drums. These instruments have become iconic symbols of the music industry and have gained immense popularity over the years. However, there are many other musical instruments that often go unnoticed and are highly underrated. In this blog post, we will explore 10 underrated musical instruments that are definitely worth exploring.

1. Hang drum:

The Hang drum, also known as the hang, is a unique musical instrument that resembles a UFO. It was created in Switzerland in the early 2000s and has gained popularity for its mesmerizing sound. The Hang drum produces a beautiful melodic tone when played and is often used in meditation and relaxation music.

2. Theremin:

The Theremin is one of the oldest electronic musical instruments, invented in the 1920s by Russian physicist Lev Theremin. It is played without any physical contact, as the player’s hands control the pitch and volume by hovering near the two antennas. The Theremin produces an eerie and haunting sound that has been used in many science fiction films and soundtracks.

3. Oud:

The Oud is a pear-shaped string instrument commonly used in traditional Middle Eastern music. It is often regarded as the ancestor of the guitar and has been played for centuries. The Oud has a unique and captivating sound that adds a rich cultural flavor to any musical composition.

4. Hangul:

The Hangul is a traditional Korean instrument that is similar to a zither. It has a beautiful and delicate sound that is reminiscent of the traditional Korean music. The Hangul is often used in classical and folk music and adds a unique element to any composition.

5. Duduk:

The Duduk is an ancient Armenian double reed woodwind instrument that has been played for over 3,000 years. It has a haunting and mournful sound that is often associated with sadness and melancholy. The Duduk has gained popularity in recent years and has been featured in several Hollywood film soundtracks.

6. Nyckelharpa:

The Nyckelharpa is a traditional Swedish instrument that dates back to the 14th century. It has a distinctive sound produced by pressing keys on a keyboard while bowing the strings. The Nyckelharpa is often associated with Swedish folk music and adds a unique and enchanting element to any composition.

7. Hangpan:

The Hangpan is a modern variation of the Hang drum and is relatively new to the musical instrument scene. It has a similar shape and sound to the Hang drum but incorporates additional notes and a more refined playing technique. The Hangpan has gained popularity for its versatility and is often used in various genres of music.

8. Hammered Dulcimer:

The Hammered Dulcimer is a traditional string instrument that is played by striking the strings with small hammers. It has a unique and beautiful sound that is often associated with Celtic and folk music. The Hammered Dulcimer is relatively easy to learn and is a great instrument for beginners.

9. Guzheng:

The Guzheng is a traditional Chinese instrument that has a history of over 2,500 years. It is a plucked zither with 21 or more strings and has a delicate and ethereal sound. The Guzheng is often played in solo performances and is known for its intricate and complex melodies.

10. Chapman Stick:

The Chapman Stick is a unique instrument that combines elements of a guitar and a bass. It has multiple strings that can be tapped or strummed, allowing the player to create complex polyphonic arrangements. The Chapman Stick is a versatile instrument that can be used in various genres of music, from jazz to rock.

In conclusion, these 10 underrated musical instruments offer a unique and captivating sound that often goes unnoticed in the mainstream music industry. If you’re looking to explore new sounds and expand your musical horizons, consider giving these instruments a try. Each instrument has its own distinct characteristics and cultural significance, making them worth exploring for any music enthusiast. So, go ahead and delve into the world of underrated musical instruments, and who knows, you might just discover your new favorite sound!
