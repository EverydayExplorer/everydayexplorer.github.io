How to Adult: A Comedic Journey of Failures


Adulting is hard, folks. Like, really hard. It’s like trying to solve a puzzle blindfolded while being attacked by a swarm of bees. But, hey, someone’s gotta do it, right? And if you’re anything like me, you’re probably still figuring things out as you go along. I mean, who needs experience and wisdom when you can just make it up as you go, right?

I remember when I first moved out of my parents’ house. I was so excited to finally be independent and start my own life. But, boy, was I in for a rude awakening. I quickly realized that adulting is not all it’s cracked up to be. In fact, it’s a lot of hard work, responsibility, and… well, let’s just say, a lot of failures.

One of my first major failures was trying to cook a decent meal. I mean, who doesn’t love a good ol’ fashioned microwaveable mac and cheese, am I right? But, no, I had to go and try to make something “gourmet” like chicken parmesan. Let’s just say it was a disaster. The chicken was overcooked, the breading was soggy, and the sauce was straight out of a can. I mean, what even is the point of calling it “homemade” if you’re just going to use store-bought sauce? But, hey, at least I learned my lesson: stick to the microwaveable mac and cheese, folks.

Another major failure of mine was trying to do my own laundry. I mean, who needs a washing machine when you can just throw your clothes in the sink and scrub them with a toothbrush, right? Just kidding, that’s not a thing. Or is it? Okay, maybe it is, but it’s definitely not the most efficient way to do laundry. Let’s just say it took me a while to figure out that whole “separating your clothes” thing. Who knew that cotton and polyester didn’t mix? But, hey, at least I learned my lesson: do your research before attempting to do your own laundry.

And then there was the time I tried to pay my bills on time. I mean, who doesn’t love the feeling of being on top of their finances, right? But, no, I had to go and try to be all responsible and stuff. Let’s just say it didn’t go so well. I mean, who knew that the due date for your electricity bill was actually a day earlier than you thought? But, hey, at least I learned my lesson: always double-check your due dates before paying your bills.

But, despite all these failures, I’ve learned a thing or two about adulting. I mean, it’s all about trial and error, folks. And, let’s be real, a lot of error. But, hey, at least I’m not alone in this struggle. I mean, who else out there has tried to cook a decent meal, done their own laundry, and paid their bills on time? Okay, maybe that’s a stretch, but you get the point.

So, if you’re anything like me and you’re still figuring out this whole adulting thing, don’t worry. You’re not alone. We’re all in this together, folks. And, hey, at least we can all commiserate about our failures and laugh about them together. Because, let’s be real, laughter is the best medicine, right?

In conclusion, adulting is hard, folks. But, hey, at least we can all laugh about our failures and learn from them. So, the next time you’re trying to cook a decent meal, doing your own laundry, or paying your bills on time, just remember: it’s okay to fail. And, hey, if all else fails, just stick to the microwaveable mac and cheese. Trust me, it’s the way to go.
