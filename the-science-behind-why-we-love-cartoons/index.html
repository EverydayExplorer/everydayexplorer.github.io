The Science Behind Why We Love Cartoons


Ever wondered why we love cartoons so much? Whether it’s a childhood favorite, or a modern show that speaks to us in a different way, cartoons have a way of drawing us in and keeping us entertained for hours on end. But what is it about them that captures our attention so effectively?

One reason why we love cartoons can be attributed to the way our brains are wired. Our brains are constantly looking for patterns, and cartoons provide a predictable structure that we can easily follow along with. Whether it’s the repeated use of gags or the familiar character archetypes, cartoons give our brains a sense of familiarity that we find comforting and enjoyable.

Another reason why we love cartoons is the emotions they evoke in us. From joy and excitement to sadness and fear, cartoons can make us feel a wide range of emotions. When we watch our favorite cartoon character go through a tough time, we empathize with them and feel a sense of connection to their struggles. This emotional investment in the characters and their stories keeps us coming back for more.

Additionally, cartoons often explore complex themes and ideas that are not only entertaining, but also thought-provoking. Shows like Avatar: The Last Airbender and Steven Universe tackle issues of identity, acceptance, and morality in a way that is accessible to viewers of all ages. The ability of cartoons to explore such deep and meaningful topics makes them not only entertaining, but also a valuable educational tool.

But what about the visual appeal of cartoons? Why do we love the bright colors and exaggerated features that are often present in animated shows? The answer lies in a concept known as the uncanny valley. The uncanny valley refers to the discomfort we feel when something looks almost, but not quite, human. Cartoons, with their exaggerated features and unrealistic settings, fall firmly outside of the uncanny valley. This allows us to appreciate the visuals without feeling any unease.

It’s also worth noting that cartoons have come a long way since their inception. With advances in technology, animators have been able to create increasingly realistic and immersive worlds. Shows like BoJack Horseman and Rick and Morty make use of cutting-edge animation techniques to create truly stunning visuals that are a joy to behold.

In conclusion, there are many reasons why we love cartoons. From their predictable structure to the emotional investment we have in the characters, cartoons provide a unique form of entertainment that captures our attention in ways that other mediums can’t. With their ability to explore complex themes and ideas, as well as their stunning visual appeal, it’s no wonder that cartoons continue to be a beloved part of our cultural landscape.
