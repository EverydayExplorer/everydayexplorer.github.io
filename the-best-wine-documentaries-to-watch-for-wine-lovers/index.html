The Best Wine Documentaries to Watch for Wine Lovers


Are you a wine enthusiast looking to expand your knowledge and deepen your appreciation for the world of wine? If so, you’re in luck! There’s a wealth of captivating wine documentaries out there that offer a behind-the-scenes look at winemaking, explore the history and culture of wine-producing regions, and introduce you to the passionate individuals who dedicate their lives to the craft. In this blog post, we’ll highlight some of the best wine documentaries that are sure to delight and inspire any wine lover.

1. SOMM

If you’ve ever been curious about what it takes to become a Master Sommelier - one of the most prestigious titles in the world of wine - then ‘SOMM’ is a must-watch. This documentary follows a group of aspiring sommeliers as they embark on a grueling journey to pass the notoriously difficult Master Sommelier exam. With its intense training sessions, high-stakes blind tastings, and intimate portraits of the candidates, ‘SOMM’ offers a fascinating glimpse into the world of wine education and the pursuit of excellence.

2. Mondovino

Directed by Jonathan Nossiter, ‘Mondovino’ takes a sweeping look at the global wine industry and the clash between tradition and modernity. From the vineyards of Burgundy to the boardrooms of California, the film explores the impact of globalization, corporate consolidation, and the rise of mass-produced wines on small-scale producers and traditional winemaking practices. With its thought-provoking commentary and stunning cinematography, ‘Mondovino’ offers a nuanced perspective on the complex forces shaping the world of wine today.

3. Sour Grapes

For a real-life tale of deception and intrigue in the world of fine wine, look no further than ‘Sour Grapes.’ This documentary chronicles the rise and fall of Rudy Kurniawan, a charismatic conman who fooled collectors and auction houses with counterfeit bottles of rare and expensive wines. Through interviews with key players in the wine world and archival footage, ‘Sour Grapes’ unravels the elaborate scheme that shook the wine industry to its core and raises important questions about authenticity, trust, and the allure of luxury.

4. A Year in Burgundy

Step into the picturesque vineyards of Burgundy with ‘A Year in Burgundy,’ a visually stunning documentary that captures the beauty and complexity of winemaking in one of France’s most revered wine regions. Over the course of a year, director David Kennard follows seven winemaking families as they navigate the challenges of weather, pests, and the relentless pursuit of perfection. With its intimate portraits of winemakers and breathtaking cinematography, ‘A Year in Burgundy’ offers a captivating glimpse into the rhythm of life in this hallowed wine country.

5. Decanted

‘Decanted’ offers a behind-the-scenes look at the burgeoning wine scene in Napa Valley, California, and the passionate individuals who are shaping its future. From visionary winemakers to pioneering viticulturists, the documentary celebrates the spirit of innovation and experimentation that defines this dynamic wine region. With its vibrant imagery and heartfelt storytelling, ‘Decanted’ is a love letter to Napa Valley and a testament to the power of wine to bring people together.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a seasoned wine aficionado or just starting your journey into the world of wine, these documentaries offer a captivating and educational glimpse into the culture, history, and passion behind every bottle. So pour yourself a glass, settle in, and prepare to be entertained, enlightened, and inspired by these extraordinary stories from the world of wine. Cheers!
