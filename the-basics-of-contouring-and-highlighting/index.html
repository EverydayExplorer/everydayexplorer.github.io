The Basics of Contouring and Highlighting


Contouring and highlighting have become popular techniques in the world of makeup. These techniques help to enhance facial features and create a more sculpted and defined look. Whether you’re a makeup enthusiast or just starting out, understanding the basics of contouring and highlighting is essential. In this blog post, we will explore the fundamentals of these techniques and how to apply them effectively.

What is contouring?

Contouring involves using makeup products to create shadows and depth on the face. The goal is to sculpt and shape the facial features, bringing out the natural bone structure. Contouring is done using shades that are darker than your skin tone and is usually applied to areas such as the hollows of the cheeks, the sides of the nose, and the jawline.

What is highlighting?

Highlighting, on the other hand, involves using light-reflecting products to bring areas of the face forward and create a radiant, glowing effect. Highlighter is typically applied to areas such as the tops of the cheekbones, the bridge of the nose, and the cupid’s bow (the area above the upper lip).

Choosing the Right Products

When it comes to contouring and highlighting, choosing the right products is crucial. For contouring, opt for a matte bronzer or contour powder that is one or two shades darker than your skin tone. Avoid products with shimmer as they can create an unnatural effect. For highlighting, use a luminous powder or a liquid highlighter with a reflective finish. Experiment with different shades to find the one that complements your skin tone the best.

Step-by-Step Guide to Contouring and Highlighting

  1. Start with a clean, moisturized face. Apply a primer to create a smooth canvas for your makeup.

  2. Apply your foundation as you normally would, ensuring an even base.

  3. Take your contour product and apply it to the hollows of your cheeks, blending it towards your temples. You can also apply it to the sides of your nose and along your jawline if desired.

  4. Blend the contour product using a blending brush or a damp makeup sponge. Make sure there are no harsh lines and the product is seamlessly blended into your skin.

  5. Next, take your highlighter and apply it to the tops of your cheekbones, the bridge of your nose, and the cupid’s bow. You can also apply it to your brow bone and the inner corners of your eyes for an extra glow.

  6. Blend the highlighter using a clean brush or your fingers. Be gentle to avoid smudging your foundation.

  7. Set your contour and highlight with a translucent powder to ensure longevity.

Tips and Tricks

  • Less is more: Start with a light hand and build up the intensity gradually. It’s easier to add more product than to remove it.

  • Consider your face shape: The placement of contour and highlight can vary depending on your face shape. For example, if you have a round face, focus on contouring the cheeks and temples to create a more defined look.

  • Use the right tools: Investing in good quality brushes and sponges can make a significant difference in the application and blending of contour and highlight products.

  • Practice makes perfect: Don’t be discouraged if your first attempts aren’t perfect. Like any skill, it takes practice to master contouring and highlighting. Keep experimenting and adjusting until you find the techniques that work best for you.

Final Thoughts

Contouring and highlighting are powerful techniques that can transform your makeup look and enhance your features. With the right products, tools, and techniques, you can achieve a beautifully sculpted and glowing complexion. Remember to start with a light hand, practice, and don’t be afraid to experiment. So go ahead, embrace your inner artist, and have fun mastering the basics of contouring and highlighting!
