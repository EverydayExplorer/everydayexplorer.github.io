Navigating with a GPS: Essential Skills for Wilderness Survival


In today’s digital age, we have become accustomed to relying on technology for various aspects of our lives. When it comes to wilderness survival, one tool that can prove to be invaluable is a GPS (Global Positioning System). With its ability to provide precise location information in real-time, a GPS can significantly enhance your navigation skills in the wilderness and increase your chances of survival. In this blog post, we will explore the essential skills necessary for navigating with a GPS in a wilderness setting.

1. Understanding GPS Basics:

To effectively navigate with a GPS, it is crucial to first understand how it works. A GPS receiver communicates with a network of satellites orbiting the Earth to determine your precise location. By triangulating signals from multiple satellites, the GPS receiver can pinpoint your coordinates on a map. Some key terms to familiarize yourself with include latitude, longitude, waypoints, and tracks. Knowing these basics will set a solid foundation for using a GPS device effectively.

2. Choosing the Right GPS Device:

Not all GPS devices are created equal, so it’s important to choose the right one for your needs. Factors to consider include battery life, durability, screen size, user interface, and the availability of maps and additional features. Some devices are specifically designed for outdoor navigation and offer features such as topographic mapping, weather updates, and geocaching. Researching different models and reading customer reviews will help you find the most suitable GPS device for your wilderness adventures.

3. Becoming Familiar with Navigation Features:

GPS devices come equipped with various navigation features that can aid in your wilderness survival. Learning how to set waypoints, create routes, and follow tracks will allow you to navigate accurately and efficiently. Imagine being able to mark specific locations such as campsites, water sources, or emergency rendezvous points. With a GPS device, you can easily save and recall these waypoints, ensuring you always know where you are or where you need to go.

4. Utilizing Mapping and Route Planning:

Modern GPS devices often include detailed topographic maps, which can be invaluable for wilderness navigation. Take the time to understand how to utilize these maps effectively, zooming in and out, and customizing the display options to suit your needs. Additionally, many GPS units allow you to plan routes and calculate estimated travel times. By studying the terrain and planning your routes in advance, you can avoid potential obstacles and make informed decisions during your wilderness expeditions.

5. Navigating in Challenging Conditions:

In wilderness survival scenarios, you may encounter challenging conditions such as dense forests, deep canyons, or low visibility due to bad weather. In these situations, knowing how to utilize your GPS effectively can be a lifesaver. Understanding concepts like ‘dead reckoning’ and ‘resection’ can help you navigate when there is no clear line of sight to the satellites. Familiarize yourself with your GPS device’s compass and altimeter functions to aid navigation when GPS signal strength is compromised.

6. Regularly Practicing GPS Skills:

Like any skill, using a GPS device effectively requires practice. Don’t wait until you find yourself in a survival situation to bring out your GPS device for the first time. Take regular hikes or outdoor excursions and use your GPS to navigate your way. This practice will not only help you hone your skills but also familiarize yourself with your device’s intricacies and limitations. Remember to keep your device up to date with the latest software updates and regularly calibrate your compass to ensure accuracy.

7. Carrying Backup Navigational Tools:

While GPS devices are incredibly helpful for wilderness navigation, it is essential to have a backup plan in case of device failure or battery depletion. Carrying a physical map and compass should be standard practice for any wilderness explorer. Learning how to read maps and navigate using a compass is a fundamental skill that should not be overlooked. These backup tools will not rely on batteries or satellite signals and can save your life in emergency situations.

In conclusion, navigating with a GPS is an essential skill for wilderness survival. By understanding the basics of GPS technology, choosing the right device, and becoming proficient in navigation features, you can greatly enhance your ability to navigate in the wilderness. Remember to practice regularly and carry backup navigational tools to ensure you are prepared for any situation that may arise. Whether you are an experienced hiker or an outdoor enthusiast, a GPS device can be the difference between getting lost and finding your way back to safety.
