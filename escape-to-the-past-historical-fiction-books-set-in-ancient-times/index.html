Escape to the Past: Historical Fiction Books Set in Ancient Times


Are you ready to embark on an enchanting journey through time? Get ready to immerse yourself in the rich tapestry of ancient civilizations with our curated list of historical fiction books set in ancient times. From the grandeur of Egyptian pharaohs to the mystery of the Roman Empire, these captivating tales will transport you to a bygone era where the past comes alive.

1. “Cleopatra’s Lost Tomb” by Stephanie Dray and Laura Kamoie:
Step into the shoes of Cleopatra, the legendary queen of Egypt, as she navigates political intrigue, romance, and war in ancient Alexandria. This mesmerizing tale weaves together fact and fiction, unraveling the secrets of Cleopatra’s life while taking you on a thrilling adventure through the Nile Valley.

2. “The Pillars of the Earth” by Ken Follett:
Travel back to 12th-century England and witness the construction of a magnificent cathedral. Ken Follett masterfully intertwines the lives of a diverse cast of characters, capturing the essence of medieval society with its political turmoil, religious fervor, and architectural wonders.

3. “Memoirs of a Geisha” by Arthur Golden:
Delve into the captivating world of geishas in pre-World War II Japan. Arthur Golden’s exquisite prose invites us into the life of Sayuri, a young girl sold into a geisha house. This evocative tale explores the complexities of tradition, love, and personal sacrifice against the backdrop of a rapidly changing society.

4. “I, Claudius” by Robert Graves:
Unearth the fascinating story of the Roman Empire through the eyes of Claudius, a historian turned emperor. Robert Graves breathes life into Claudius’s memoirs, revealing the dark underbelly of the Roman political scene, filled with conspiracies, power struggles, and the relentless pursuit of dominance.

5. “The Song of Achilles” by Madeline Miller:
Discover the mythological world of ancient Greece through the eyes of Patroclus, companion and lover of the mighty warrior Achilles. Madeline Miller’s lyrical prose brings the Trojan War to life, exploring themes of honor, friendship, and the complexities of destiny.

6. “The Egyptian” by Mika Waltari:
Step into the shoes of Sinuhe, an Egyptian physician, as he navigates the political and religious upheavals of ancient Egypt. Mika Waltari’s meticulously researched novel provides a vivid portrayal of the pharaohs, priests, and court intrigue that shaped the fate of the Nile civilization.

7. “The Clan of the Cave Bear” by Jean M. Auel:
Transport yourself to prehistoric times and follow the journey of Ayla, a young girl raised by Neanderthals. Jean M. Auel’s epic series explores the clash of cultures and the resilience of the human spirit in a world ruled by primal instincts.

8. “Empress Orchid” by Anchee Min:
Enter the forbidden halls of the Forbidden City in 19th-century China through the eyes of Orchid, a young concubine who rises to become the Empress Dowager. Anchee Min’s lush narrative captures the opulence and treachery of the imperial court, a world filled with political intrigue and dazzling ceremonies.

As you delve into these historical fiction books set in ancient times, prepare to be transported to majestic civilizations and experience the trials and triumphs of characters who shaped the course of history. Let the vivid imagery, intricate plots, and rich historical details ignite your imagination and take you on an unforgettable adventure through time.
