Trolling Techniques: Catching Fish on the Move


When it comes to fishing, there are many techniques that anglers use to increase their chances of success. One such technique is trolling, which involves dragging a baited line behind a moving boat. Trolling allows anglers to cover a larger area of water and target fish that are on the move. In this blog post, we will explore some trolling techniques that can help you catch fish on the move.

1. Choosing the Right Lures

The key to successful trolling is selecting the right lures. Different fish species have different preferences when it comes to bait, so it’s important to choose lures that mimic the natural prey of your target fish. For example, if you’re targeting trout, consider using small spoons or spinners that imitate small fish or insects. If you’re after larger game fish like salmon or muskie, opt for larger lures such as plugs or crankbaits.

2. Vary Your Speed

Fish are not always moving at the same speed, so it’s important to vary your trolling speed to match their activity level. Start by trolling at a slow speed, around 1-2 miles per hour, and observe how the fish respond. If you’re not getting any bites, try increasing your speed gradually until you find the sweet spot. Some fish, like walleye, prefer a slow trolling speed, while others, like tuna, may be more active at higher speeds.

3. Use Planer Boards

Planer boards are a great tool for trolling, especially in areas with heavy boat traffic or when targeting fish that are close to the surface. These boards are attached to your fishing line and are designed to spread out your lines, allowing you to cover a wider area of water. By using planer boards, you can avoid spooking the fish and increase your chances of hooking into a bite.

4. Consider Depth Control

When trolling, it’s important to consider the depth at which the fish are feeding. This can vary depending on factors such as water temperature, time of day, and the species you’re targeting. To control the depth of your lures, you can use a variety of techniques, such as adding weight to your line, using diving plugs, or adjusting the length of your line. Experiment with different depths until you find where the fish are actively feeding.

5. Pay Attention to Your Surroundings

While trolling, it’s crucial to pay attention to your surroundings and look for signs of fish activity. Keep an eye out for birds diving or fish jumping, as these are indicators that fish are feeding in the area. Additionally, use your fishfinder to locate underwater structures, such as drop-offs or submerged vegetation, where fish may be hiding. By staying aware of your surroundings, you can adjust your trolling patterns to target the most productive areas.

Conclusion

Trolling is a versatile fishing technique that can be highly effective for catching fish on the move. By choosing the right lures, varying your speed, using planer boards, considering depth control, and paying attention to your surroundings, you can increase your chances of success while trolling. Remember to always practice catch-and-release and follow local fishing regulations to ensure the sustainability of fish populations. So, grab your gear, hop in your boat, and give trolling a try – you might just reel in your biggest catch yet!
