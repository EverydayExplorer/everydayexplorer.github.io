The Influence of Mythology on Modern Video Games: From God of War to World of Warcraft


Mythology has been a source of fascination for humans since time immemorial. Ancient civilizations crafted rich and intricate myths to explain the world around them, to understand the human condition, and to explore the realms of gods and heroes. These myths have left a profound impact on our collective conscious, shaping our culture, art, and literature. In recent years, mythology has found a new medium through which to captivate and inspire: video games.

Video games have become an increasingly popular form of entertainment, attracting millions of players from around the world. With advances in technology and graphics, video games have evolved into immersive and interactive experiences that transport players into fantastical worlds. It is within these worlds that mythology has found a new home, influencing and shaping the narratives, characters, and settings of modern video games.

One of the most prominent examples of mythology in video games is the ‘God of War’ series. Developed by Sony Santa Monica, this franchise draws heavily from ancient Greek mythology. Players assume the role of Kratos, a Spartan warrior who embarks on a quest for revenge against the gods of Olympus. Throughout the series, players encounter iconic figures from mythology such as Zeus, Poseidon, and Hades, as well as creatures like Medusa, Minotaurs, and Cyclops. The game seamlessly weaves these mythological elements into its storyline and gameplay, creating a captivating and immersive experience for players.

Another popular video game franchise heavily influenced by mythology is ‘World of Warcraft’ (WoW). Developed by Blizzard Entertainment, WoW is a massively multiplayer online role-playing game set in the fantasy world of Azeroth. In this vast and sprawling universe, players can choose from different races and classes, each drawing inspiration from various mythological cultures. The night elves, for example, are reminiscent of Celtic mythology with their connection to nature and druidic practices. The Norse-inspired dwarves and the ancient Egyptian-inspired trolls further expand the mythological tapestry of the game. From epic quests to mythical creatures and legendary artifacts, WoW encompasses a rich and diverse mythological experience.

Beyond these two examples, mythology influences numerous other video games, both in terms of narrative and aesthetics. Greek and Roman mythology are often utilized due to their widespread popularity and familiarity among players. Norse mythology has also gained prominence in recent years, thanks in part to games like ‘God of War’ and ‘Assassin’s Creed: Valhalla.’ Eastern mythologies, such as those from Japan and China, have also found their way into video games, adding a unique flavor to the gaming world.

So why do video game developers incorporate mythology into their games? One reason is the universal appeal and recognition of these mythological stories and characters. Mythology offers a vast trove of rich narratives, epic battles, and larger-than-life characters that captivate players. Additionally, mythology provides a sense of authenticity and depth to the game worlds. By drawing from real-world mythologies, developers can create immersive and believable environments that resonate with players.

Moreover, mythology provides a thematic framework through which games can explore complex themes and ideas. Ancient myths often touch upon universal themes such as power, morality, heroism, and the consequences of one’s actions. Through their narratives, video games can delve into these themes, offering players a chance to reflect and engage with profound questions in a captivating and interactive way.

From a marketing perspective, incorporating mythology into video games also has its advantages. Mythological themes and characters have a built-in fan base and cultural cachet, attracting players who are already familiar with these stories. By tapping into these pre-existing interests, video game developers can attract a larger audience and generate more interest in their games.

In conclusion, mythology has become an integral part of modern video games, influencing and shaping their narratives, characters, and settings. Whether it be the majestic and awe-inspiring world of ‘God of War’ or the sprawling mythological tapestry of ‘World of Warcraft,’ mythology adds depth, authenticity, and universal appeal to video game experiences. As players continue to embark on epic quests, battle mythical creatures, and engage with legendary figures, the influence of mythology on video games will only continue to grow. It is a truly exciting time for both mythology enthusiasts and gamers alike, as these two worlds intertwine to create memorable and immersive gaming experiences.
