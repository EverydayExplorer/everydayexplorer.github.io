How to Conduct a Title Search for a Property


When purchasing a property, it’s essential to ensure that the title is clear and free of any outstanding liens or claims. A title search is a crucial step in the process, as it helps to identify any potential issues that could affect the ownership or sale of the property. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process of conducting a title search for a property, highlighting the key steps and considerations to keep in mind.

What is a Title Search?

A title search is an examination of the public records to determine the ownership of a property and to identify any potential obstacles that could impact the sale or transfer of the property. The search typically involves reviewing documents such as deeds, mortgages, and court records to ensure that the title is clear and free of any liens or encumbrances.

Why is a Title Search Necessary?

Conducting a title search is crucial for several reasons:

  • Prevents Unexpected Liens: A title search helps to identify any outstanding liens or claims that could affect the ownership or sale of the property. This ensures that the buyer is aware of any potential issues and can take steps to resolve them.
  • Protects the Buyer: A title search protects the buyer by ensuring that they are purchasing a property that is free of any hidden liabilities or encumbrances.
  • Saves Time and Money: Conducting a title search early in the buying process can save time and money in the long run by identifying any potential issues and allowing the buyer to address them before the sale is completed.

The Title Search Process

The title search process typically involves the following steps:

  • Ordering the Search: The buyer or their agent orders the title search from a title insurance company or a title search provider.
  • Reviewing the Search Results: The title search provider reviews the search results and identifies any potential issues or obstacles that could affect the title.
  • Reporting the Findings: The title search provider reports the findings to the buyer or their agent, highlighting any issues that need to be addressed.
  • Addressing the Issues: The buyer or their agent works with the title search provider to address any issues that were identified during the search.

What Does a Title Search Typically Include?

A title search typically includes the following:

  • Deed Search: A search of the public records to determine the ownership of the property and identify any outstanding liens or claims.
  • Mortgage Search: A search of the public records to identify any outstanding mortgages or liens on the property.
  • Court Records Search: A search of court records to identify any outstanding judgments or liens that could affect the title.
  • Tax Records Search: A search of tax records to identify any outstanding taxes or assessments on the property.

What Are the Common Issues Found in a Title Search?

Common issues found during a title search include:

  • Unpaid Taxes: Outstanding taxes or assessments on the property.
  • Unpaid Mortgages: Outstanding mortgages or liens on the property.
  • Unrecorded Liens: Liens or claims that have not been recorded in the public records.
  • Forged Documents: Documents that have been altered or forged to deceive the buyer or seller.
  • Missing or Incomplete Records: Missing or incomplete records that could affect the title.

How to Address Issues Found in a Title Search

If issues are found during a title search, the buyer or their agent can take the following steps:

  • Negotiate with the Seller: The buyer can negotiate with the seller to resolve the issue or to obtain a credit at closing.
  • Pay the Outstanding Amount: The buyer can pay the outstanding amount to resolve the issue.
  • Work with the Title Company: The title company can work with the buyer or seller to resolve the issue and clear the title.

Conclusion

Conducting a title search is a critical step in the process of purchasing a property. By understanding the importance of a title search and the steps involved in the process, buyers and sellers can ensure that the title is clear and free of any outstanding liens or claims. Remember to always work with a reputable title insurance company or title search provider to ensure that the title search is conducted accurately and efficiently.
