The Pleiades Mythology: Stories and Legends of the Seven Sisters


In the vast expanse of the night sky, a cluster of stars shimmer with an ethereal beauty that has captivated humanity for centuries. Known as the Pleiades, or the Seven Sisters, these celestial beings have inspired countless myths and legends across different cultures throughout history. In this blog post, we delve into the captivating world of Pleiades mythology, exploring the stories and legends that surround these enchanting stars.

The Pleiades, named after the seven daughters of Atlas and Pleione in Greek mythology, have been revered by various civilizations around the world. Let us embark on a journey through time and space, uncovering the diverse tales that have been woven around these celestial entities.

In Greek mythology, the story of the Pleiades begins with their birth. The mighty hunter Orion pursued the sisters relentlessly, until the gods took pity on them and transformed them into stars. To this day, it is said that Orion can be seen chasing the Pleiades across the night sky.

In Native American folklore, the Pleiades hold great significance. Among the Cherokee people, the stars represent seven young hunters who pursued a white stag into the sky, becoming the Pleiades as they ascended. The Hopi tribe sees the Pleiades as the kachina spirits, symbolic of fertility and renewal.

Moving eastward, we encounter the Japanese myth of Subaru, which means ‘unite’ in Japanese. According to the legend, a young girl named Subaru fell in love with a prince who was banished to the stars. Determined to reunite with her beloved, Subaru embarked on a journey to find him, eventually transforming into the beautiful Pleiades constellation.

The Pleiades also find their place in Hindu mythology. In the epic Mahabharata, the story of the Pandavas unfolds, with each brother representing a different star in the Pleiades cluster. Their mother, Kunti, prayed to the gods for their protection, and they were blessed with immortality, becoming the Pleiades.

These are just a few examples of the rich tapestry of mythology that surrounds the Pleiades. From ancient Greece to Native American tribes, from Japan to India, the Seven Sisters have left an indelible mark on cultures across the globe.

In addition to their mythological significance, the Pleiades have also been a source of fascination for astronomers and scientists. These stars, located in the Taurus constellation, have been studied for their unique properties and have provided valuable insights into the formation and evolution of stars.

In conclusion, the Pleiades Mythology is a captivating subject that intertwines the realms of myth, culture, and science. From the tales of Greek gods to the beliefs of Native American tribes, the stories and legends surrounding the Seven Sisters have endured for centuries. As we gaze up at the night sky, let us remember the enchanting tales that have been woven around these celestial beings, and marvel at the wonder and mystery they continue to evoke.
