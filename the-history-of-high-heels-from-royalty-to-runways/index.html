The History of High Heels: From Royalty to Runways


High heels have been a fashion statement for centuries, evolving from their practical origins to becoming a symbol of glamour, power, and self-expression. These iconic shoes have a rich history that spans across cultures, centuries, and social classes. In this blog post, we’ll take a journey through time to explore the fascinating history of high heels, from their humble beginnings among the nobility to their prominent place on today’s runways.

Ancient Roots: The Birth of Elevated Footwear

High heels may seem like a modern invention, but their history dates back to ancient civilizations. The earliest known high heels can be traced to ancient Egypt, where both men and women wore elevated sandals. These early heels served a practical purpose, elevating the foot above the hot sands of the desert.

Similarly, ancient Greek actors often wore platform shoes called ‘kothorni’ to add height and presence to their performances. These early examples of elevated footwear were more functional than fashionable.

China and Persia: Elevated Style and Social Status

Around the 10th century, high heels started to take on a more fashionable role in China and Persia. Chinese nobility wore heels made of stacked leather or wood, while in Persia, mounted warriors used heels to secure their feet in stirrups. These heeled shoes were both functional and symbolic of elevated social status.

The Renaissance: A European Love Affair

The true transformation of high heels into a fashion statement began during the Renaissance in Europe. It was in Venice, Italy, that the high heel gained popularity as a symbol of prestige. In the 16th century, Catherine de’ Medici, an Italian noblewoman, married King Henry II of France and introduced high heels to the French court. She was known for her diminutive stature and used heels to add height and presence to her appearance.

As high heels became more popular in European courts, they began to signify social status and nobility. The height of the heel also became a symbol of wealth, as the cost of creating taller heels increased with their height. Red heels, in particular, were associated with power and privilege.

The Practical Turn: Riding and Warfare

In the 17th century, high heels took on a practical role in both men’s and women’s fashion. For men, heels were used in riding boots to help secure the foot in stirrups. This practical use persisted in military uniforms well into the 18th century.

Women, on the other hand, embraced high heels not just for practicality but also for aesthetics. The shoes became narrower, more ornate, and featured intricate decorations. High heels became a symbol of femininity, elegance, and refinement.

The Industrial Revolution: Mass Production and Accessibility

The 19th century saw the emergence of mass production techniques, making high heels more accessible to a broader range of people. With the advent of the sewing machine, it became easier and more cost-effective to produce fashionable footwear. High heels started to be marketed to a wider audience.

The 20th Century: A Platform for Change

The 20th century brought significant changes to the high heel. The two World Wars disrupted fashion trends, and practicality often took precedence over style. However, after World War II, high heels made a comeback with a vengeance.

In the 1950s, the stiletto heel was introduced, and it quickly became a symbol of feminine allure and sexuality. Icons like Marilyn Monroe and Audrey Hepburn popularized the stiletto, and it became a staple in every fashion-conscious woman’s wardrobe.

The 1960s saw the emergence of the platform heel, which was associated with the counterculture movement and embraced by musicians and rebels. The 1970s brought about chunky heels and disco-inspired styles, while the 1980s were marked by bold, high, and often colorful heels.

Contemporary High Heels: Art, Expression, and Empowerment

Today, high heels continue to be a dynamic and evolving fashion statement. While they remain symbols of femininity and style, they have also been reclaimed by women as symbols of empowerment and self-expression. Women wear high heels not because they have to but because they want to. Heels are a way to express their individuality and confidence.

High heels have also become a form of art, with designers pushing the boundaries of creativity. Runways showcase avant-garde designs that challenge traditional notions of footwear, incorporating innovative materials, shapes, and heights.

In conclusion, high heels have come a long way from their practical origins in ancient civilizations to their current status as a symbol of style, empowerment, and self-expression. The history of high heels is a testament to the ever-changing nature of fashion and its ability to reflect and shape societal norms and values. Whether you wear them for fashion, comfort, or both, high heels continue to leave an indelible mark on the world of footwear and fashion.

So, the next time you slip on a pair of high heels, remember the rich history behind these iconic shoes and the journey they’ve taken from royalty to runways.
