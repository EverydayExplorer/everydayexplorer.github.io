The Influence of Architecture in Literature: Setting the Scene


Architecture is not just about buildings and structures; it is an art form that has the power to influence and shape our surroundings. From towering skyscrapers to quaint cottages, every architectural style carries its own unique charm and character. But did you know that architecture also plays a crucial role in literature? In this blog post, we will explore the fascinating connection between architecture and literature, specifically focusing on how architecture sets the scene and influences the narrative in various literary works.

When we think about literature, we often associate it with vivid descriptions of landscapes, characters, and emotions. However, architecture is an integral part of the literary world, as it helps create a sense of time, place, and atmosphere. Whether it’s a dilapidated mansion, a bustling cityscape, or a humble cottage, the architectural elements within a story can transport readers to a different world, making the narrative more immersive and engaging.

One of the most iconic examples of architecture in literature is the grand manor house in Emily Brontë’s masterpiece, Wuthering Heights. The isolated and hauntingly beautiful house serves as the central setting for the turbulent love story between Heathcliff and Catherine. The dark and brooding atmosphere of the house reflects the complex and tumultuous emotions of the characters, adding depth and intensity to the narrative. Brontë’s vivid descriptions of the house’s decaying walls, creaking stairs, and wild moors that surround it create an eerie and gothic ambiance, which becomes an integral part of the story itself.

In F. Scott Fitzgerald’s The Great Gatsby, architecture plays a pivotal role in portraying the stark contrast between the opulent and extravagant lifestyles of the wealthy elite and the ordinary lives of the working class. The grand mansions and lavish party scenes in East Egg and West Egg serve as symbols of the characters’ wealth and social status. The descriptions of the magnificent houses, with their sprawling lawns, marble fountains, and ornate interiors, highlight the excesses and superficiality of the Jazz Age society. Through architecture, Fitzgerald effectively captures the essence of the roaring twenties and critiques the emptiness of the American Dream.

Architecture can also be used to create a sense of nostalgia and evoke a particular time period. In Harper Lee’s To Kill a Mockingbird, the iconic courthouse in the fictional town of Maycomb becomes a symbol of justice, morality, and racial inequality. The detailed descriptions of the courthouse’s Greek revival architecture and the iconic oak tree in the front yard set the stage for the racial tensions and injustices that unfold within the story. The courthouse becomes a physical representation of the town’s deeply rooted prejudices and the challenges faced by the characters in their pursuit of justice.

Beyond these classic examples, architecture continues to play a significant role in contemporary literature as well. In J.K. Rowling’s Harry Potter series, the magical architecture of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry captures the imagination of readers worldwide. The moving staircases, hidden passages, and towering turrets create a sense of enchantment and mystery, mirroring the fantastical world of wizards and witches. The unique architectural elements of Hogwarts, such as the Gryffindor Tower and the Chamber of Secrets, become integral to the plot, providing a sense of adventure and discovery.

In conclusion, architecture is not merely a backdrop in literature; it is a powerful tool that authors use to set the scene, evoke emotions, and enhance the overall narrative. From Gothic mansions to opulent mansions and magical castles, architecture adds depth, symbolism, and atmosphere to the stories we love. The way an author describes a building or a structure can transport us to a different time, place, or state of mind. So, the next time you dive into a great work of literature, pay attention to the architectural details, for they hold the key to unlocking a whole new dimension within the story.
