The Rebirth of the Boulevardier: Rediscovering a Classic


The world of cocktails is constantly evolving, with new drinks and innovative creations being introduced regularly. However, amidst all the new trends, it’s always refreshing to rediscover a classic cocktail that has stood the test of time. Today, we’re diving into the fascinating history and resurgence of one such cocktail - the Boulevardier.

The Boulevardier is a timeless cocktail that combines the bold flavors of whiskey, sweet vermouth, and Campari, creating a symphony of tastes that is hard to resist. It is essentially a Negroni with whiskey instead of gin, and it brings a whole new level of complexity to the drink. The Boulevardier is a true testament to the art of mixology, blending together ingredients in perfect harmony.

Despite its current popularity, the Boulevardier had humble beginnings. The cocktail was first mentioned in Harry McElhone’s 1927 book ‘Barflies and Cocktails,’ where he claimed that the recipe came from a man named Erskine Gwynne. Gwynne was an American writer and expatriate who published a monthly magazine in Paris called ‘Boulevardier.’ It was during his time in Paris that he supposedly created this cocktail, hence the name.

The Boulevardier fell out of favor over the years, as cocktail enthusiasts started to focus on more glamorous and trendy drinks. The Negroni, a close cousin of the Boulevardier, became the preferred choice for many, overshadowing this forgotten gem. However, like all true classics, the Boulevardier couldn’t stay away for too long. In recent years, bartenders and mixologists have begun rediscovering and reviving this forgotten cocktail, introducing it to a new generation of cocktail enthusiasts.

One of the reasons behind the Boulevardier’s resurgence is its versatility. The cocktail can be easily customized to suit different tastes and preferences. Want a sweeter drink? Add a dash of maple syrup or honey. Prefer a smokier profile? Use a peaty Scotch instead of bourbon. The possibilities are endless, making it a favorite among both home bartenders and professionals alike.

Additionally, the Boulevardier’s rich history and connection to the literary world have also contributed to its renewed popularity. When sipping a Boulevardier, one can’t help but feel a sense of nostalgia for a bygone era, a time when Hemingway, Fitzgerald, and Gwynne himself roamed the streets of Paris, enjoying this elegant concoction.

Another factor that has propelled the Boulevardier back into the spotlight is the ongoing craft cocktail movement. In recent years, there has been a resurgence of interest in classic cocktails, artisanal spirits, and the revival of forgotten recipes. This movement has been instrumental in bringing back cocktails like the Boulevardier, once considered relics of the past, and reintroducing them as modern classics.

Social media has undoubtedly played a significant role in the Boulevardier’s resurgence as well. Platforms like Instagram have provided a platform for mixologists and cocktail enthusiasts to showcase their creations and exchange ideas. The aesthetically pleasing nature of the Boulevardier, with its deep red hue and elegant garnishes, makes it an instant hit on these platforms, further fueling its popularity.

In conclusion, the rebirth of the Boulevardier is a testament to the timelessness of classic cocktails. Despite being overshadowed by newer trends and momentarily forgotten, the Boulevardier has emerged as a favorite among cocktail enthusiasts once again. Its rich history, versatility, and connection to the literary world have all contributed to its resurgence. Whether enjoyed in a speakeasy, a high-end cocktail bar, or in the comfort of your own home, the Boulevardier is a drink that demands your attention and appreciation. So, next time you have the chance, take a step back in time and rediscover the charm of this classic cocktail.
