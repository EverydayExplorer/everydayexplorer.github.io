The Best Animated Series for Adults


In recent years, animated series have transcended their traditional boundaries, evolving from children’s entertainment to a sophisticated medium that tackles complex themes, societal issues, and adult humor. This blog post explores some of the best animated series for adults, highlighting their unique storytelling, memorable characters, and cultural impact. Whether you’re a seasoned fan or a newcomer to adult animation, this comprehensive guide will help you navigate the vibrant landscape of animated shows designed for a mature audience.

1. BoJack Horseman

Premiere Date: August 22, 2014
Platform: Netflix

“BoJack Horseman” is a groundbreaking series that delves into the life of a washed-up actor who happens to be a horse. This dark comedy brilliantly encapsulates themes of depression, addiction, and the quest for redemption. With its rich character development and sharp social commentary, the show resonates with audiences on multiple levels. The animation style is unique, using vibrant colors and exaggerated designs to create a world that mirrors the absurdity of Hollywood. Over six seasons, “BoJack Horseman” has garnered critical acclaim, earning several awards and a devoted fanbase.

2. Rick and Morty

Premiere Date: December 2, 2013
Platform: Adult Swim

“Rick and Morty” follows the interdimensional escapades of an eccentric scientist, Rick Sanchez, and his good-hearted but fretful grandson, Morty. This series is known for its innovative storytelling, blending science fiction with dark humor and philosophical musings. Each episode takes viewers on a wild ride through bizarre worlds, exploring themes of existentialism, family dynamics, and the consequences of one’s actions. With its quick-witted dialogue and intricate plotlines, “Rick and Morty” has become a cultural phenomenon, inspiring memes and a dedicated fan community.

3. Archer

Premiere Date: September 17, 2009
Platform: FX

“Archer” is a spy comedy that follows the exploits of Sterling Archer and his colleagues at the International Secret Intelligence Service (ISIS). The series is characterized by its unique animation style, rapid-fire humor, and clever writing. Each episode parodies classic spy tropes, combining absurd scenarios with sharp character interactions. Over the years, “Archer” has evolved, exploring various themes such as love, loyalty, and workplace dynamics while maintaining its signature humor. The show has received numerous accolades, including several Primetime Emmy Awards.

4. Futurama

Premiere Date: March 28, 1999
Platform: Fox, Comedy Central

“Futurama,” created by Matt Groening, the mind behind “The Simpsons,” transports viewers to the 31st century, where a delivery boy named Fry awakens from cryogenic sleep. The show cleverly blends science fiction with satire, addressing contemporary societal issues through its futuristic lens. With its memorable characters, such as the one-eyed Leela and the unintentionally hilarious Bender, “Futurama” balances humor with poignant commentary on topics like consumerism, politics, and the human condition. The series has gained a cult following and remains a staple in adult animation.

5. The Boondocks

Premiere Date: November 6, 2005
Platform: Adult Swim

Based on the comic strip by Aaron McGruder, “The Boondocks” is a thought-provoking animated series that addresses race, culture, and politics in America. The story follows two young African American brothers, Huey and Riley Freeman, who move to a predominantly white neighborhood with their grandfather. The show is known for its sharp social critique, blending humor with serious discussions about identity and inequality. With its unique animation style and powerful storytelling, “The Boondocks” has left a lasting impact on the landscape of animated series for adults.

6. Big Mouth

Premiere Date: September 29, 2017
Platform: Netflix

“Big Mouth” is a coming-of-age animated series that explores the tumultuous journey of adolescence. Through its exaggerated portrayal of puberty, the show tackles topics such as sexuality, identity, and friendship with humor and heart. The series features a diverse cast of characters, each navigating the challenges of growing up. With its frank discussions about the awkwardness of adolescence, “Big Mouth” has resonated with audiences, sparking conversations about the complexities of teenage life. Its bold approach and relatable themes have earned it critical acclaim and a passionate following.

7. Solar Opposites

Premiere Date: May 8, 2020
Platform: Hulu

From one of the co-creators of “Rick and Morty,” “Solar Opposites” follows a family of aliens who crash-land on Earth and must navigate human culture. As they attempt to adapt, the show explores themes of assimilation, identity, and the absurdity of human behavior. With its unique blend of humor and sci-fi elements, “Solar Opposites” has captivated audiences with its engaging storytelling and quirky characters. The series has received positive reviews for its sharp wit and entertaining premise, making it a worthy addition to the realm of adult animation.

8. Tuca & Bertie

Premiere Date: April 13, 2019
Platform: Netflix

“Tuca & Bertie” is a whimsical animated series that chronicles the friendship between two bird-women in their 30s. The show tackles themes of mental health, relationships, and the challenges of adulthood with humor and empathy. Its vibrant animation style and surreal visual metaphors create a unique viewing experience. The series has been praised for its authentic portrayal of female friendship and the complexities of navigating life in your thirties. Despite its short-lived run, “Tuca & Bertie” has developed a dedicated fanbase and has sparked discussions about representation in animation.

9. Final Space

Premiere Date: February 26, 2018
Platform: TBS

“Final Space” follows the adventures of Gary Goodspeed, an astronaut who teams up with an alien named Mooncake to save the universe. This space opera combines humor, action, and emotional depth, exploring themes of friendship, sacrifice, and the search for purpose. The animation is visually stunning, featuring vibrant colors and imaginative designs. With its engaging storytelling and well-developed characters, “Final Space” has garnered a loyal following and critical acclaim, making it a standout series in the realm of adult animation.

10. Castlevania

Premiere Date: July 7, 2017
Platform: Netflix

Based on the popular video game series, “Castlevania” is a dark fantasy animated series that follows the battle between Dracula and a group of unlikely heroes. The show is known for its stunning animation, complex characters, and mature themes, including morality, love, and revenge. With its richly detailed world-building and compelling storytelling, “Castlevania” has captivated audiences and critics alike. The series has received praise for its character development and intricate plotlines, solidifying its place as one of the best animated series for adults.

Conclusion

The world of adult animated series has expanded to include a diverse array of storytelling styles and themes, appealing to mature audiences with its unique blend of humor, social commentary, and emotional depth. Each series mentioned in this blog post has contributed to the evolution of animation, proving that the medium is not limited to children’s entertainment. From the darkly comedic insights of “BoJack Horseman” to the whimsical adventures of “Tuca & Bertie,” these shows offer something for everyone. As the landscape of animated series continues to evolve, viewers can look forward to more innovative and thought-provoking content that pushes the boundaries of storytelling. Whether you’re seeking laughter, introspection, or a mix of both, the best animated series for adults are sure to provide an unforgettable viewing experience.
