How to Support Local Music Venues and Keep the Scene Alive


This blog post is dedicated to all the music lovers out there who want to support their local music venues and keep the vibrant music scene alive.

If you’ve ever attended a local music show, you know there’s a unique energy that can’t be replicated in bigger arenas or stadiums. Local music venues are the lifeblood of the music scene, providing a platform for emerging artists to showcase their talent and for fans to discover new artists. However, these venues often face numerous challenges, including financial difficulties, competition from larger venues, and a general lack of recognition. In this blog post, we’ll explore some practical ways you can support your local music venues and help sustain the music scene in your community.

  1. Attend Local Shows: The first and most obvious way to support your local music venue is to attend shows regularly. By buying tickets and showing up to local gigs, you’re not only supporting the artists but also contributing to the venue’s revenue. Spread the word about upcoming shows among your friends and social networks to encourage more people to attend.

  2. Buy Merchandise: Another way to support local music venues is by purchasing merchandise from the artists performing. Most bands and artists have merchandise tables set up at their shows, selling CDs, t-shirts, stickers, and other goodies. Buying these items not only helps the artists financially but also contributes to the overall success of the show.

  3. Become a Member or Donate: Many local music venues offer membership programs or accept donations to help cover their operating costs. Consider becoming a member of your favorite venue or making a one-time donation. These small contributions can go a long way in keeping the lights on and supporting the local music scene.

  4. Spread the Word: Use your social media platforms to promote local shows and venues. Share the event details, tag the artists, and encourage your friends to attend. Tagging the venue in your posts will also help increase their visibility among music enthusiasts. Additionally, writing positive reviews and sharing your experiences on websites and social media platforms can motivate others to check out the venue.

  5. Follow and Engage on Social Media: Support local music venues by following them on social media platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter. Engage with their content by liking, commenting, and sharing their posts. This not only helps boost their online presence but also shows that there’s a community of fans supporting the venue.

  6. Write Reviews: After attending a show, take a few minutes to write a positive review of the venue on websites like Yelp, Google, or TripAdvisor. Not only will this help raise awareness of the venue, but it will also provide valuable feedback for potential visitors.

  7. Host Fundraisers or Benefit Shows: If you’re particularly passionate about supporting a local music venue, consider organizing a fundraiser or benefit show. Collaborate with the venue and local artists to create an event that not only raises funds but also showcases the talent in your community.

  8. Attend Community Meetings: Keep an eye out for community meetings or town hall discussions that address local music venues and their challenges. Attend these meetings and voice your support for the venues. Showing up in numbers will demonstrate the importance of these venues and encourage local authorities to take action.

  9. Support Local Music Blogs: Many cities have local music blogs that cover shows, events, and feature emerging artists. Follow these blogs, read their articles, and share their content. By supporting local music blogs, you’re indirectly supporting the venues and artists they promote.

  10. Be Respectful: Lastly, when attending a show at a local music venue, remember to be respectful of the space and the people around you. Follow the venue’s rules, be mindful of the artists’ performances, and treat the staff and fellow attendees with kindness.

By implementing these simple yet effective strategies, you can make a significant impact in supporting your local music venues and ensuring that the music scene in your community thrives. Remember, small actions can lead to big changes, so let’s come together and keep the local music culture alive and vibrant for years to come!
