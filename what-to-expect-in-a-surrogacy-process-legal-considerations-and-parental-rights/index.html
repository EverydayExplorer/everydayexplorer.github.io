What to Expect in a Surrogacy Process: Legal Considerations and Parental Rights


For couples struggling with infertility or same-sex couples who want to start a family, surrogacy can be an incredible option. It allows intended parents to have a biologically connected child through a gestational surrogate. However, navigating the surrogacy process can be complex and may involve numerous legal considerations and parental rights. In this blog post, we will explore what you can expect in a surrogacy process, focusing specifically on the legal aspects.

**Understanding Surrogacy**

Surrogacy is a process in which a woman, known as the gestational surrogate, carries a pregnancy for intended parents. There are two types of surrogacy: traditional surrogacy and gestational surrogacy. In traditional surrogacy, the surrogate’s own eggs are utilized, making her the biological mother of the child. In gestational surrogacy, on the other hand, the surrogate carries an embryo created using the intended parents’ eggs and sperm or through the use of a sperm or egg donor.

**Legal Considerations in Surrogacy**

Before embarking on a surrogacy journey, it is vital for intended parents to understand the legal implications involved. Surrogacy laws vary from country to country, and sometimes even within different states or provinces. It is essential to research and seek legal guidance specific to your jurisdiction to ensure compliance with local regulations.

1. **Choosing the Right Surrogacy Arrangement**

There are various surrogacy arrangements available, including traditional surrogacy, gestational surrogacy, and altruistic surrogacy. Each type of arrangement comes with its own set of legal considerations, rights, and responsibilities. Understanding the differences between them is crucial before making any decisions.

2. **Pre-Birth and Post-Birth Orders**

In many jurisdictions, intended parents are required to obtain pre-birth and post-birth orders to establish their legal parentage. These orders are typically issued by a court and can provide legal protection for the intended parents, ensuring that their rights as parents are recognized.

3. **Surrogacy Contracts**

Surrogacy contracts are an essential component of any surrogacy arrangement. These contracts outline the rights, responsibilities, and obligations of all parties involved, including the surrogate, intended parents, and sometimes even the egg or sperm donor. Surrogacy contracts typically cover matters such as compensation, medical expenses, health and lifestyle requirements, and confidentiality clauses.

4. **Consent and Termination of Parental Rights**

In some jurisdictions, it may be necessary for the gestational surrogate and her partner (if applicable) to give their consent to the intended parents adopting the child or obtaining legal parental rights. Additionally, the termination of parental rights of the surrogate may need to be addressed, ensuring a smooth and legally recognized transfer of parental rights to the intended parents.

5. **International Surrogacy Considerations**

For those considering surrogacy in a foreign country, it is crucial to understand the legal framework governing surrogacy in that particular country. International surrogacy can present additional complexities, such as immigration or citizenship issues, and ensuring compliance with both the home country and the host country’s legal requirements is of utmost importance.

**Conclusion**

Entering into a surrogacy process comes with numerous legal considerations and parental rights that must be carefully navigated. Understanding the legal framework, choosing the right surrogacy arrangement, drafting comprehensive surrogacy contracts, and obtaining necessary court orders are crucial steps in protecting the rights and ensuring a legally recognized parental relationship. Seeking legal guidance from professionals experienced in surrogacy law is crucial in embarking on a successful surrogacy journey. By understanding the legal aspects, intended parents can focus on what truly matters – building their dream family.
