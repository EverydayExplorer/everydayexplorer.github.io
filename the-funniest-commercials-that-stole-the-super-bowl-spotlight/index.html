The Funniest Commercials That Stole the Super Bowl Spotlight


Every year, the Super Bowl not only brings together millions of sports fans but also captivates the attention of viewers around the world with its highly entertaining commercials. These advertisements are an integral part of the game, and they have become an iconic aspect of one of the most-watched television events globally. Over the years, a handful of commercials have managed to steal the Super Bowl spotlight with their humor and wit, leaving an indelible mark on pop culture.

1. The Old Spice Man

One of the most memorable and hilarious commercials to come out of the Super Bowl is the Old Spice ‘The Man Your Man Could Smell Like’ campaign. The ad features a suave and confident spokesman who delivers humorous one-liners to capture the attention of both men and women. The clever writing and over-the-top humor turned this commercial into an instant sensation, and it became one of the most talked-about ads of the year.

2. Doritos’ Crash the Super Bowl

Doritos is no stranger to creating memorable Super Bowl commercials. In their ‘Crash the Super Bowl’ campaign, Doritos invited fans to submit their own commercial ideas for a chance to have their ad featured during the game. The result was a collection of incredibly funny and creative ads that showcased both the brand’s sense of humor and the creativity of its fans. This user-generated content approach not only engaged the audience but also generated significant buzz and social media shares.

3. Volkswagen’s ‘The Force’

Volkswagen’s ‘The Force’ commercial stole the show at the Super Bowl with its adorable and humorous storyline. The ad features a young boy dressed as Darth Vader, attempting to use the force on various objects around the house. When the father arrives home in his Volkswagen Passat, he discreetly activates the car’s remote start feature, surprising the boy and making him believe he actually used the force. This heartwarming and witty commercial left a lasting impression on viewers and quickly went viral.

4. Budweiser’s Clydesdales

Budweiser’s commercials featuring their iconic Clydesdale horses have become a beloved Super Bowl tradition. These ads often tug at the heartstrings while still managing to incorporate humor. Whether showcasing the special bond between a trainer and his horse or telling a heartwarming story of friendship, Budweiser commercials have consistently entertained and inspired audiences. These commercials are a perfect combination of humor, emotion, and brand messaging.

5. E-Trade’s Dancing Baby

E-Trade’s popular ‘Talking Baby’ campaign made its Super Bowl debut with a hilarious commercial featuring a baby dancing and singing. The ad humorously addresses the audience’s doubts about investing and portrays the baby as a stock market savvy character. The catchy jingle, amusing dance moves, and comedic script made this commercial a standout during the Super Bowl, and it quickly became a viral hit.

Conclusion

The Super Bowl commercials that steal the spotlight are not only entertaining but also have a lasting impact on viewers. From the Old Spice Man’s humor to the heartwarming Clydesdale ads, these commercials have become part of pop culture, generating significant buzz and becoming the talk of the town. Their ability to balance humor, creativity, and messaging has made them standouts in a sea of advertisements. As we eagerly await the next Super Bowl, we can’t help but wonder which commercials will steal the show and become the talk of the town once again.
