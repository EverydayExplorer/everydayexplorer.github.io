Fishing with Artificial Lures: Techniques That Work


Fishing with artificial lures is an exciting and effective method to target a wide range of fish species in freshwater and saltwater environments. Unlike natural bait, artificial lures offer versatility, durability, and a range of techniques to attract fish. If you’re looking to improve your success rate and enhance your fishing skills, understanding how to use artificial lures effectively is crucial. This guide explores various techniques and tips for fishing with artificial lures.

Choosing the Right Lure for the Job

The first step in successful lure fishing is selecting the right lure for your target species, fishing location, and conditions. Here’s a breakdown of the most common types of artificial lures and when to use them:

1. Soft Plastic Lures

Soft plastic lures are highly versatile and can mimic various prey, from worms and grubs to baitfish and crawfish. They come in a range of shapes, sizes, and colors, making them suitable for different fishing situations.

  • Technique: Use a slow, steady retrieve or a jigging motion to imitate natural prey. Soft plastics are great for bass, walleye, and other predatory fish.

2. Crankbaits

Crankbaits are designed to dive and wobble, simulating a distressed fish or baitfish. They have a lip that determines the depth they dive to, making them effective for various water levels.

  • Technique: Crankbaits are best used with a steady retrieve, varying the speed to trigger strikes. They are ideal for covering large areas and attracting aggressive fish.

3. Spinnerbaits

Spinnerbaits feature one or more spinning blades that create flash and vibration in the water, attracting fish from a distance. They are popular for bass fishing and can be used in various environments.

  • Technique: Use a steady retrieve or a stop-and-go pattern to simulate injured prey. Spinnerbaits are effective in murky water and around cover.

4. Topwater Lures

Topwater lures float on the water’s surface and create noise and commotion to attract fish. They are exciting to use because you can see the fish strike.

  • Technique: Use a popping or walking motion to create splashes and vibrations. Topwater lures are excellent for early morning or late evening fishing, especially for bass and pike.

5. Jigs

Jigs are versatile lures that can be used in various situations. They consist of a weighted head and a hook, often paired with a soft plastic or feathered trailer.

  • Technique: Jigs are best used with a bouncing motion to mimic prey. They are ideal for fishing in deep water, around structure, and for targeting species like bass, walleye, and crappie.

Effective Techniques for Fishing with Artificial Lures

Once you’ve selected the right lure, it’s time to focus on techniques that will increase your chances of success. Here are some effective methods for fishing with artificial lures:

1. Vary Your Retrieve Speed

Changing the speed at which you retrieve your lure can make a significant difference in attracting fish. A slower retrieve can be effective in cold water or when fish are less active, while a faster retrieve can trigger aggressive strikes. Experiment with different speeds to see what works best.

2. Use a Stop-and-Go Pattern

The stop-and-go technique involves pausing your retrieve periodically to create a sudden change in lure movement. This can trigger reaction strikes from fish that are following but hesitant to bite. Use this technique with crankbaits, spinnerbaits, and jigs for best results.

3. Experiment with Depth

Fish can be found at different depths depending on the time of day, water temperature, and other factors. Use lures with different diving depths or adjust your jigging technique to explore various water levels. This can help you locate fish that might be hiding deeper in the water column.

4. Work Around Structure

Fish often seek shelter around structures like rocks, logs, submerged trees, and vegetation. Cast your lure near these structures and work it through or around them to attract fish. Be cautious to avoid snags, and consider using weedless lures when fishing in heavy cover.

5. Match the Hatch

Matching the hatch means choosing a lure that resembles the natural prey in the area. Observe the types of baitfish or insects present in the water and select lures that mimic them in size, color, and movement. This can increase your chances of attracting fish that are feeding on specific prey.

6. Fish at the Right Time

The time of day can impact fish activity. Early morning and late evening are often prime times for fishing, as fish are more active during these periods. Adjust your fishing schedule to take advantage of these peak times, and consider using topwater lures for added excitement.

7. Use Scented Lures

Scented lures can be effective in attracting fish by appealing to their sense of smell. Some soft plastic lures come pre-scented, while others can be enhanced with additional scents or attractants. This technique is especially useful in murky water or when fish are less active.

Tips for Success with Artificial Lures

To maximize your success when fishing with artificial lures, consider the following tips:

1. Keep Your Hooks Sharp

Sharp hooks are essential for setting the hook and landing fish. Check your hooks regularly and sharpen or replace them as needed to ensure a solid hookset.

2. Use the Right Gear

Choosing the right rod, reel, and line is crucial for successful lure fishing. Consider the type of lure you’re using, the size of the fish you’re targeting, and the fishing environment to select the appropriate gear.

3. Practice Patience and Persistence

Fishing with artificial lures can require patience, especially when experimenting with different techniques. Be persistent and willing to try different approaches until you find what works.

4. Observe the Environment

Pay attention to environmental cues, such as water temperature, weather conditions, and bird activity. These factors can provide insights into fish behavior and help you make informed decisions about where and how to fish.

5. Learn from Others

Don’t hesitate to ask other anglers for advice or tips on fishing with artificial lures. Sharing knowledge and learning from experienced anglers can improve your skills and increase your success rate.

Fishing with artificial lures is a rewarding and versatile method to target a variety of fish species. By choosing the right lure, applying effective techniques, and following these tips, you’ll be well on your way to a successful and enjoyable fishing experience. Whether you’re fishing in freshwater lakes, rivers, or saltwater environments, artificial lures offer endless opportunities to explore and catch the fish you’re after.
