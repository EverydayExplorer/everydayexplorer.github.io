The Ultimate Guide to Achieving a Perfect Winged Eyeliner


Achieving the perfect winged eyeliner can be a daunting task, but with the right techniques and a little bit of practice, anyone can master this classic makeup look. Whether you’re a makeup novice or a seasoned pro, this ultimate guide will provide you with all the tips and tricks you need to create flawless winged eyeliner every time. From choosing the right products to mastering the technique, we’ve got you covered.

First things first, let’s talk about the tools you’ll need. To achieve a precise and clean winged eyeliner, invest in a good quality liquid eyeliner or a gel eyeliner with a fine brush. These formulas offer better control and precision compared to pencil eyeliners. Additionally, having a cotton swab and some makeup remover handy will help you clean up any mistakes along the way.

Now that you have your tools, it’s time to prep your eyes. Start by applying a primer or an eyeshadow base to ensure that your eyeliner stays in place all day. This step is especially important if you have oily eyelids. Next, apply a neutral eyeshadow all over your eyelids to create a smooth canvas for your eyeliner.

When it comes to the actual application, there are a few different techniques you can try. One popular method is the tape trick. Simply place a small piece of tape diagonally from the outer corner of your eye towards the end of your eyebrow. This will act as a guide and help you create a sharp and even wing. Once the tape is in place, start by lining your upper lash line, gradually thickening the line as you move towards the outer corner. When you reach the end, use the tape as a stencil to create a straight wing. Remove the tape gently, and voila – a perfect winged eyeliner!

If you prefer a more freehand approach, start by drawing a thin line along your upper lash line using short, precise strokes. Once you reach the outer corner of your eye, imagine an invisible line extending from your lower lash line towards the end of your eyebrow. This will serve as a guide for the wing. Using the same short strokes, connect the line you just drew with the imaginary line, creating a triangle shape. Fill in the triangle, making sure to keep the line as thin and precise as possible. If needed, use a cotton swab dipped in makeup remover to clean up any mistakes or smudges.

Practice makes perfect, so don’t get discouraged if your first few attempts aren’t spot-on. Remember, even the most skilled makeup artists started somewhere. Take your time, be patient, and most importantly, have fun with it! Experiment with different eyeliner styles and shapes to find the one that suits your eye shape and personal style.

To finish off your winged eyeliner look, curl your lashes and apply a coat or two of mascara. This will help your eyes appear more open and enhance the overall effect of your winged eyeliner.

In conclusion, achieving a perfect winged eyeliner is all about practice and patience. With the right tools, techniques, and a little bit of confidence, you’ll be rocking flawless wings in no time. So go ahead, grab your eyeliner and start practicing – the perfect wing awaits you!
