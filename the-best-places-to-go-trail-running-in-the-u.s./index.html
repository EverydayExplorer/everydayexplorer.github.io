The Best Places to Go Trail Running in the U.S.


Welcome to our exciting blog post where we explore the best places to go trail running in the United States! Strap on your running shoes and get ready to immerse yourself in some of the most breathtaking landscapes this beautiful country has to offer. From rugged mountains to lush forests, we’ve got you covered with a variety of trail running destinations that will leave you invigorated and inspired. So without further ado, let’s dive into the adventure!

1. Moab, Utah: Known for its jaw-dropping red rock formations, Moab offers trail runners a unique experience. With trails like the famous Slickrock Trail and the scenic Fisher Towers Trail, Moab is a paradise for those seeking a challenging yet awe-inspiring run.

2. Boulder, Colorado: Nestled in the Rocky Mountains, Boulder is a trail runner’s dream come true. With miles upon miles of well-maintained trails, you can explore the beautiful Chautauqua Park or challenge yourself on the famous Mesa Trail, which offers breathtaking views of the Flatirons.

3. Lake Tahoe, California and Nevada: This stunning alpine lake straddles the border between California and Nevada, offering trail runners a diverse range of landscapes to explore. From the Tahoe Rim Trail, which circumnavigates the entire lake, to the exhilarating Flume Trail with its panoramic views, Lake Tahoe is a trail runner’s paradise.

4. Appalachian Trail, Eastern United States: For those seeking an epic adventure, look no further than the legendary Appalachian Trail. Spanning over 2,000 miles from Georgia to Maine, this iconic trail offers an unparalleled experience for long-distance trail runners. Lace up your shoes and prepare for an unforgettable journey through diverse terrains and charming rural communities.

5. Sedona, Arizona: With its otherworldly red rock formations and breathtaking desert landscapes, Sedona is a trail runner’s haven. Trails like the Cathedral Rock Trail and the Bell Rock Pathway offer a thrilling combination of challenging terrain and awe-inspiring views, making it a must-visit destination for enthusiasts.

6. Mount Rainier National Park, Washington: Nature lovers and trail runners alike will find solace in the stunning Mount Rainier National Park. Offering a wide range of trails, from the popular Wonderland Trail that encircles the entire mountain to the Skyline Trail with its panoramic views, this destination promises a memorable trail running experience in the Pacific Northwest.

7. Great Smoky Mountains National Park, Tennessee and North Carolina: Immerse yourself in the beauty of the Great Smoky Mountains National Park, where you’ll find an abundance of trails to explore. From the challenging Alum Cave Trail to the scenic Clingmans Dome Trail, this park offers a magical experience for trail runners of all levels.

8. Acadia National Park, Maine: Seaside cliffs, rocky shores, and lush forests await you in the picturesque Acadia National Park. With trails like the iconic Jordan Pond Path and the challenging Precipice Trail, this destination offers a unique blend of coastal beauty and rugged landscapes for an unforgettable trail running experience.

9. Olympic National Park, Washington: From the wild and rugged coastline to the lush rainforests, Olympic National Park offers a diverse range of trails for every trail runner. Whether you explore the Hoh Rainforest or tackle the challenging High Divide Trail, this park will leave you in awe of its natural wonders.

10. Grand Teton National Park, Wyoming: Last but certainly not least, Grand Teton National Park showcases the majestic beauty of the Teton Range. Trail runners can experience the stunning vistas along the Teton Crest Trail or challenge themselves with the exhilarating Cascade Canyon Trail. Prepare to be inspired by the dramatic mountain peaks and crystal-clear alpine lakes.

There you have it, folks! Our top picks for the best places to go trail running in the United States. Whether you’re seeking a challenging adventure or a scenic escape, these destinations offer something for everyone. So grab your running gear, pack your sense of adventure, and embark on a trail running journey you’ll never forget. Happy trails!
