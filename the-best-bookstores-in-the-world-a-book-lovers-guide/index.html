The Best Bookstores in the World: A Book Lover's Guide


Bookstores have always been a sanctuary for book lovers, a place where the aroma of fresh pages and the promise of new adventures fill the air. For those who live and breathe books, exploring the best bookstores in the world is like embarking on a literary pilgrimage. From cozy independent shops tucked away in quiet corners to grandiose establishments that have become landmarks in their own right, these bookstores are a testament to the enduring love affair between readers and the written word. So, grab your reading glasses and join me on a journey through some of the most extraordinary bookstores around the globe.

1. Shakespeare and Company - Paris, France
Nestled in the heart of Paris, Shakespeare and Company has become an iconic landmark for bibliophiles. This legendary bookstore, which first opened its doors in 1919, has been frequented by literary giants such as Ernest Hemingway and F. Scott Fitzgerald. With its cozy reading nooks and a wide selection of English language books, stepping into Shakespeare and Company is like stepping into a literary time capsule.

2. El Ateneo Grand Splendid - Buenos Aires, Argentina
Housed in a beautifully restored theater, El Ateneo Grand Splendid is a book lover’s paradise. The grandeur of the theater’s original architecture has been preserved, with the stage transformed into a café where visitors can sip coffee while enjoying a good read. With its towering shelves and ornate décor, El Ateneo Grand Splendid is a true feast for the eyes and the soul.

3. Powell’s City of Books - Portland, Oregon, USA
Spanning an entire city block, Powell’s City of Books is a book lover’s dream come true. Boasting over one million new and used books, this sprawling bookstore has become a mecca for book enthusiasts from around the world. Lose yourself in the labyrinthine aisles, explore the carefully curated sections, and discover literary gems you never knew existed.

4. Livraria Lello - Porto, Portugal
Livraria Lello is more than just a bookstore; it is a work of art. Its stunning neo-gothic façade and intricate woodwork transport visitors to a bygone era. Once inside, you’ll find a magical staircase that seems straight out of a fairytale. This enchanting bookstore has inspired authors such as J.K. Rowling, who frequented Livraria Lello while living in Porto. It’s no wonder that this bookstore has become a pilgrimage site for Harry Potter fans.

5. Atlantis Books - Santorini, Greece
Perched on the picturesque island of Santorini, Atlantis Books is a hidden gem for book lovers. With its breathtaking views of the Aegean Sea, this bookstore offers a unique reading experience. Browse through a carefully curated selection of books, attend literary events, or simply relax on the terrace with a book and let the beauty of the surroundings inspire you.

6. Bart’s Books - Ojai, California, USA
Bart’s Books is a one-of-a-kind bookstore that will transport you back in time. Located in the charming town of Ojai, this open-air bookstore is an ode to the love of reading. With its shelves spilling out onto the sidewalks, you can peruse the books both inside and outside the store. Bart’s Books is a cozy haven where you can lose yourself in the pages of a book while enjoying the California sunshine.

7. Libreria Acqua Alta - Venice, Italy
In the enchanting city of Venice, Libreria Acqua Alta offers a truly unique bookstore experience. With its gondolas filled with books and stacks of books forming makeshift staircases, this quirky bookstore exudes charm and eccentricity. As you wander through the labyrinth of books, be prepared to stumble upon hidden corners and unexpected surprises.

These are just a few of the best bookstores in the world that every book lover should visit at least once in their lifetime. Whether you’re seeking a literary treasure or simply looking for a cozy reading nook, these bookstores offer a haven for the literary-minded. So, pack your bags, grab a good book, and embark on a journey to these extraordinary destinations that celebrate the joy of reading.
