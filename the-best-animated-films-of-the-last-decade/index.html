The Best Animated Films of the Last Decade


Animation has transformed dramatically over the past decade, evolving into a rich tapestry of storytelling that captivates audiences of all ages. From Pixar’s heartwarming tales to the bold artistry of independent films, the last ten years have gifted us with a plethora of animated gems that embody creativity, emotional depth, and innovative techniques. In this post, we’ll take a closer look at some of the best animated films released in the last decade, exploring their themes, artistry, and cultural impact.

1. Coco (2017)

Directed by Lee Unkrich and Adrian Molina, Coco is a vibrant celebration of Mexican culture, particularly the Day of the Dead. The film follows Miguel, a young boy with dreams of becoming a musician despite his family’s generational ban on music. When he accidentally finds himself in the Land of the Dead, Miguel embarks on an enchanting journey to discover his family’s history and follow his passion.

The film’s stunning visuals, combined with its heartfelt narrative, resonate deeply with audiences. The musical score, featuring the captivating song “Remember Me,” enhances the emotional weight of the story. Coco not only showcases the importance of family and memory but also serves as a bridge connecting different cultures through its universal themes.

2. Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (2018)

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse redefined superhero animation with its groundbreaking visual style and innovative storytelling. Directed by Bob Persichetti, Peter Ramsey, and Rodney Rothman, this film introduces audiences to Miles Morales, a teenager who becomes Spider-Man in a multiverse filled with various Spider-People.

The film’s unique comic book aesthetic, characterized by vibrant colors and dynamic visuals, sets it apart from traditional animation. With a rich narrative that explores identity, responsibility, and heroism, Into the Spider-Verse garnered widespread acclaim, winning the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature. Its influence has opened doors for more diverse representations in animation, inspiring a new generation of fans.

3. Toy Story 4 (2019)

The beloved Toy Story franchise continued its legacy with Toy Story 4, directed by Josh Cooley. Reuniting audiences with Woody, Buzz Lightyear, and their friends, this installment focuses on Woody’s journey of self-discovery as he grapples with his purpose in a world where he’s no longer the favorite toy.

Toy Story 4 masterfully balances humor and emotional resonance, exploring themes of friendship, loyalty, and what it means to let go. With stunning animation and a poignant storyline, the film invites viewers to reflect on their own experiences with change and growth. The film’s ability to evoke nostalgia while also introducing new characters, like Forky, makes it a standout in the series.

4. Frozen II (2019)

The sequel to the phenomenon Frozen, Frozen II, directed by Chris Buck and Jennifer Lee, expands on the original’s themes of love, sisterhood, and self-discovery. The story follows Elsa and Anna as they embark on a quest to uncover the truth about Elsa’s powers and their family’s past.

With breathtaking animation and a powerful soundtrack, including the anthem “Into the Unknown,” Frozen II captivates audiences while delving deeper into its characters’ emotional landscapes. The film emphasizes the importance of facing one’s fears and embracing change, resonating with audiences both young and old.

5. Soul (2020)

Pixar’s Soul, directed by Pete Docter and Kemp Powers, takes viewers on a philosophical journey through the concepts of passion and purpose. The film follows Joe Gardner, a middle school music teacher who dreams of becoming a professional jazz musician. After an unexpected accident, Joe finds himself in the Great Before, where souls are prepared for life on Earth.

Soul is a visually stunning exploration of what it means to truly live. Its jazz-infused soundtrack, vibrant animation, and thought-provoking narrative invite audiences to reflect on their own lives and passions. The film’s celebration of creativity and the joy of living resonates deeply, making it a standout in Pixar’s illustrious catalog.

6. Wolfwalkers (2020)

This beautifully animated film from Cartoon Saloon, directed by Tomm Moore and Ross Stewart, is set in 17th-century Ireland. Wolfwalkers tells the story of Robyn, a young apprentice hunter, who befriends a girl named Mebh, who can transform into a wolf. The film explores themes of nature, community, and the clash between progress and tradition.

The hand-drawn animation style, with its intricate details and vibrant colors, creates a mesmerizing visual experience. With a narrative that emphasizes the importance of understanding and respecting nature, Wolfwalkers has garnered critical acclaim and stands as a testament to the power of storytelling in animated cinema.

7. Raya and the Last Dragon (2021)

Disney’s Raya and the Last Dragon, directed by Don Hall and Carlos López Estrada, is a breathtaking fantasy adventure set in the mystical land of Kumandra. The film follows Raya, a skilled warrior on a quest to find the last dragon, Sisu, and unite the fractured lands to save her people from an ancient evil.

With stunning animation and a strong emphasis on themes of trust and unity, Raya and the Last Dragon showcases the importance of working together despite differences. The film’s diverse representation and richly developed characters contribute to its significance in contemporary animation.

8. Encanto (2021)

Encanto, directed by Byron Howard and Jared Bush, invites audiences into the magical world of the Madrigal family, who live in a vibrant, enchanted house in Colombia. Each family member possesses a unique gift—except for Mirabel, who embarks on a journey to save her family’s magic.

The film’s lush animation, memorable characters, and catchy musical numbers, including “We Don’t Talk About Bruno,” resonate with viewers. Encanto celebrates family, identity, and the beauty of individuality, making it a culturally rich addition to Disney’s animated repertoire.

9. The Mitchells vs. The Machines (2021)

Directed by Mike Rianda and Jeff Rowe, The Mitchells vs. The Machines is a hilarious and heartfelt exploration of family dynamics and technology’s impact on our lives. The story follows the Mitchell family as they embark on a road trip, only to find themselves facing a robot apocalypse.

The film’s unique animation style and clever humor set it apart, while its exploration of family relationships and the importance of connection resonates deeply in an increasingly digital world. With relatable characters and a message about embracing imperfections, The Mitchells vs. The Machines offers a fresh perspective on animation.

10. Turning Red (2022)

Pixar’s Turning Red, directed by Domee Shi, is a coming-of-age story that explores themes of adolescence, identity, and cultural heritage. The film follows Mei Lee, a 13-year-old girl who transforms into a giant red panda whenever she experiences strong emotions.

With its vibrant animation and relatable narrative, Turning Red tackles the challenges of growing up while highlighting the importance of family and self-acceptance. The film’s unique premise and heartfelt storytelling make it a significant entry in the realm of animated cinema.

Conclusion

The last decade has witnessed an extraordinary evolution in animated filmmaking, with stories that resonate deeply with audiences worldwide. From the vibrant cultures depicted in Coco and Raya and the Last Dragon to the introspective journeys of self-discovery in Soul and Turning Red, these films have left an indelible mark on the landscape of animation.

As technology continues to advance, the possibilities for storytelling in animation are boundless. The films mentioned above exemplify the artistry and emotional depth that modern animated cinema can achieve. They invite viewers to reflect on their own lives while celebrating the power of imagination and creativity.

As we look ahead, it’s exciting to anticipate what the future holds for animated films. With diverse voices and innovative storytelling at the forefront, the next decade promises to bring even more captivating narratives and breathtaking animation to life.
