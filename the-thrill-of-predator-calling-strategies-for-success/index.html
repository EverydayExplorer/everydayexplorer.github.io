The Thrill of Predator Calling: Strategies for Success


Predator calling is an exciting and popular hunting technique that involves mimicking the cries and sounds of prey animals to attract predators. It can be a thrilling and effective way to hunt coyotes, bobcats, foxes, and other predators. However, it requires a lot of practice, patience, and knowledge of predator behavior.

If you are new to predator calling, you might feel a bit overwhelmed with all the equipment, techniques, and strategies involved. But don’t worry, in this blog post, we will share some tips and tricks to help you become a successful predator caller.

1. Choose the Right Equipment
Choosing the right predator calling equipment is crucial for success. You will need a predator call that mimics the sounds of distressed or dying prey animals, such as rabbits, mice, or birds. There are a variety of predator calls available on the market, including electronic calls, mouth-blown calls, and handheld calls. Electronic calls are the most popular and effective right now due to their convenience, realistic sounds, and extended range.

You will also need a camouflage outfit, a good pair of binoculars, and a rifle or shotgun.

2. Scout the Area
Scouting the area is essential before you start predator calling. You need to analyze the terrain, look for signs of prey animals, and study the predator behavior. Look for tracks, droppings, and scratch marks to identify the predator’s pattern. Predators are smart and cautious animals, and you need to know their habits to outsmart them.

3. Plan Your Setup
Once you have identified a good location to call predators, it is time to plan your setup. The idea is to position yourself in an area where the predator will not spot your movements but still have a clear shot if it approaches. Use the wind to your advantage, and try to blend in with the surroundings. Predators have excellent eyesight, and if they spot you, they will disappear in a flash.

4. Choose the Right Time
Choosing the right time to predator call is also crucial. Typically, the best time to hunt predators is during their active periods, which are usually at dawn and dusk. Predators are nocturnal animals, and they become more active and hungry when the sun goes down. Try to call early in the morning or late in the evening, and avoid calling during the middle of the day when predators are usually resting.

5. Be Patient
Patience is the key to success in predator calling. It can take anywhere from a few minutes to several hours for a predator to approach after a call. Keep in mind that predators are cautious and smart animals, and they will not rush into danger. Give them time to feel safe and curious, and they will eventually come out of hiding.

6. Vary Your Calls
Varying your calls is essential to attract different types of predators. Use different calls, sequences, and volumes to mimic different prey animals and their reactions. Predators are skilled hunters, and they can decipher whether a call is authentic or not. Try to sound as natural as possible, and use different calls according to the predator’s behavior.

7. Know When to End the Setup
Knowing when to end the setup is also crucial. Typically, you should end the setup after 20 to 30 minutes of calling or if you see signs of predator activity. If you don’t attract any predators after a reasonable amount of time, move on to another location. Try not to overcall, as this can scare away predators and decrease your chances of success.

Conclusion

Predator calling can be a thrilling and rewarding hunting technique if done correctly. It requires patience, practice, and knowledge of predator behavior. By choosing the right equipment, scouting the area, planning your setup, choosing the right time, being patient, varying your calls, and knowing when to end the setup, you can increase your chances of success. Remember to be safe, ethical, and respectful of the environment, and enjoy the thrill of predator calling.
