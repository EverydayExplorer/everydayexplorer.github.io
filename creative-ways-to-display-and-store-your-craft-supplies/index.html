Creative Ways to Display and Store Your Craft Supplies


Are you a craft enthusiast? Do you find yourself surrounded by a sea of colorful ribbons, buttons, and beads? If so, you’re not alone! Crafters often accumulate a vast collection of supplies over time, and finding creative ways to display and store these materials can be a fun and practical challenge. Not only does it help keep your craft space organized, but it can also inspire you to get creative in your crafting. In this blog, we’ll explore some ingenious and visually pleasing methods to display and store your craft supplies.

  1. Mason Jar Magic 🌟

Mason jars are a versatile and cost-effective solution for organizing small craft items. Fill them with buttons, brads, or tiny beads, and arrange them on shelves or in a decorative tray. You can also paint the lids or add labels for a pop of color and easy identification.

  1. Pegboards for Practicality 🪝

Pegboards are a classic choice for organizing craft supplies. Mount a pegboard on your wall and hang hooks, baskets, and shelves to store various items like scissors, washi tape, and paintbrushes. The best part is that you can easily rearrange the components to suit your evolving needs.

  1. Vintage Suitcases 👝

Old suitcases, whether inherited or found at thrift stores, can make charming storage solutions for craft supplies. Stack them, open them up, and use the compartments to store fabrics, yarn, or sewing notions. Plus, they add a touch of nostalgia to your craft space.

  1. Rolling Carts 🛒

Rolling carts are incredibly versatile and can be moved around your crafting area as needed. These carts come in various sizes and styles, making them suitable for storing anything from paper and pens to sewing machines and fabric scraps. Some even have built-in compartments and drawers for better organization.

  1. Clear Containers for Clarity 📦

Clear storage containers with lids are perfect for keeping your craft supplies visible and protected from dust. Use labels or colored tape to identify the contents, making it easy to find what you need. Stack them neatly on shelves or in cubbies for a streamlined look.

  1. Drawer Dividers 🗂️

If you have drawers in your craft room, invest in drawer dividers to create designated spaces for your supplies. This is particularly handy for sorting and storing items like markers, pens, and colored pencils.

  1. Creative Wall Displays 🎨

Transform your craft supplies into wall art by displaying them creatively. For example, you can frame your fabric swatches or embroidery hoops to showcase your collection while keeping them organized and easily accessible.

  1. PVC Pipe Storage 🌀

PVC pipes can be repurposed into unique storage solutions for your craft room. Cut them into different lengths and mount them horizontally or vertically on the wall. These makeshift shelves are excellent for holding rolls of wrapping paper, vinyl, or even knitting needles.

  1. Hanging Shoe Organizers 👟

Don’t let the name fool you; hanging shoe organizers are versatile and work well for storing a variety of craft supplies. Hang one on the back of a door or on a closet rod, and use the pockets to hold scissors, tape, glue sticks, and more.

  1. Repurposed Furniture 🪑

Give old furniture a new lease on life by turning it into craft supply storage. An old hutch, bookshelf, or dresser can be transformed into a stunning craft cabinet with a fresh coat of paint and some creative reconfiguration.

  1. Upcycled Jars and Cans 🍶

Get eco-friendly by upcycling glass jars, tin cans, or coffee containers into charming craft supply holders. Decorate them with paint, fabric, or paper, and use them to store paintbrushes, markers, or knitting needles.

  1. Floating Shelves 📚

Floating shelves not only provide a stylish display but also offer practical storage for your craft supplies. Arrange them in an aesthetically pleasing manner and adorn them with your favorite crafting materials or decorative trinkets.

  1. Repurpose Spice Racks 🌶️

Repurpose spice racks as compact storage solutions for small craft supplies. The individual compartments are ideal for storing beads, buttons, or embroidery floss, and you can hang them on the wall or place them on a shelf for easy access.

  1. Vintage Toolboxes 🔧

Vintage toolboxes make for unique and nostalgic craft supply storage. The segmented compartments are perfect for sorting and organizing smaller items like jewelry findings, sewing notions, or even colored pencils.
