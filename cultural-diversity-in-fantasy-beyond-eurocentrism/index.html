Cultural Diversity in Fantasy: Beyond Eurocentrism


In recent years, there has been a growing demand for more diverse representation in literature and popular culture. From race and ethnicity to gender and sexuality, people are realizing the importance of telling stories that reflect the multifaceted nature of our society. This demand for diversity has extended to the world of fantasy, a genre that has long been dominated by Eurocentric narratives. In this blog post, we will explore the concept of cultural diversity in fantasy and discuss how it goes beyond Eurocentrism.

When we think of fantasy, it is often accompanied by images of knights, castles, and dragons. These elements have become synonymous with the genre, primarily due to the influence of European folklore and mythology. However, there is a whole world of rich and diverse cultures that can be explored within the realms of fantasy.

The first step to going beyond Eurocentrism in fantasy is to diversify the settings and inspirations for our stories. Instead of drawing solely from Western mythology, authors can look to different cultures and incorporate their unique mythologies and folklore into their narratives. By doing so, we can create a tapestry of fantastical worlds that celebrate the diversity of human experiences.

For example, we can explore the mythology of various African cultures like the Yoruba or the Ashanti. These mythologies are rich in gods and spirits, offering a vast array of stories to be told. By incorporating these elements into our fantasy narratives, we not only provide representation for underrepresented cultures but also introduce readers to new and exciting mythologies they may not be familiar with.

Another way to promote cultural diversity in fantasy is to create diverse and authentic characters. By developing characters with diverse racial and ethnic backgrounds, we can challenge the stereotypical depiction of fantasy heroes and heroines. This not only provides readers with relatable protagonists but also reinforces the idea that heroes can come from all walks of life.

Furthermore, it is essential to ensure that characters from diverse backgrounds are portrayed authentically and respectfully. This means conducting thorough research and consulting with cultural experts to avoid perpetuating harmful stereotypes or misrepresentations. By doing so, we can create dynamic characters who defy expectations and challenge assumptions.

In addition to diversifying settings and characters, it is crucial to address themes that are relevant to different cultures in fantasy literature. By exploring topics like colonialism, cultural assimilation, or the power dynamics between different groups, authors can shed light on real-world issues through the lens of fantasy. This not only adds depth to the storytelling but also encourages readers to think critically and reflect on their own beliefs and biases.

Finally, the promotion of cultural diversity in fantasy extends beyond the pages of a book. It involves amplifying and supporting voices from marginalized communities within the publishing industry. By actively seeking out and promoting works by authors from diverse backgrounds, we can ensure that a wide range of perspectives is represented in the fantasy genre.

In conclusion, cultural diversity in fantasy goes beyond Eurocentrism. By diversifying settings, incorporating authentic characters, addressing relevant themes, and amplifying diverse voices, we can create a genre that celebrates the richness and complexity of our world. By doing so, we not only provide representation for underrepresented cultures but also offer readers a chance to explore new perspectives and expand their horizons. So, let’s embrace cultural diversity in fantasy and create a genre that reflects the beauty of our diverse world.
