Comic Book Events: The Biggest Storylines and Crossovers


Comic books have always been a source of excitement and wonder for fans around the world. From the incredible artwork to the captivating stories, comic book events have been a source of fascination for both casual readers and die-hard fans. In this blog post, we will dive into the biggest storylines and crossovers in comic book history.

1. Crisis on Infinite Earths

One of the most significant events in comic book history is the Crisis on Infinite Earths. Published by DC Comics in 1985, this 12-issue limited series aimed to streamline DC’s vast and convoluted multiverse. Written by Marv Wolfman and illustrated by George Perez, Crisis on Infinite Earths brought together heroes and villains from different dimensions to combat the Anti-Monitor in an effort to save the entire universe.

2. Secret Wars

Marvel’s Secret Wars, published in 1984-85, was another groundbreaking event in the comic book world. Written by Jim Shooter and illustrated by Mike Zeck, Secret Wars brought together the most popular heroes and villains from the Marvel Universe to battle on a distant planet called Battleworld. The series not only introduced new characters but also had long-lasting effects on the Marvel Universe.

3. Infinity Gauntlet

In 1991, Marvel released Infinity Gauntlet, a six-issue limited series written by Jim Starlin and illustrated by George Perez and Ron Lim. This cosmic event saw the Mad Titan, Thanos, acquire all six Infinity Gems and become omnipotent. Heroes from across the Marvel Universe, including the Avengers, the X-Men, and the Fantastic Four, united to stop Thanos and save the universe.

4. Civil War

Civil War, published by Marvel in 2006-2007, is a crossover event that divided the superhero community. Written by Mark Millar and illustrated by Steve McNiven, Civil War explored the ethical and moral dilemmas of government oversight of superheroes. The event pitted hero against hero as characters like Iron Man and Captain America took opposing sides on the issue, ultimately resulting in a major shift in the Marvel Universe.

5. Flashpoint

Flashpoint, published by DC Comics in 2011, was a game-changer for the DC Universe. Written by Geoff Johns and illustrated by Andy Kubert, Flashpoint altered the timeline, leading to significant changes in the DC Universe and setting the stage for DC’s New 52 relaunch. The event not only impacted The Flash but also affected characters like Batman, Wonder Woman, and Superman.

6. Secret Invasion

Secret Invasion, published by Marvel in 2008-2009, was a crossover event that revealed that Skrulls, a shape-shifting alien race, had infiltrated Earth’s population for years. Written by Brian Michael Bendis and illustrated by Leinil Francis Yu, Secret Invasion had major implications for the Marvel Universe, forever changing the dynamics between heroes and leaving a lasting impact on various characters.

7. Blackest Night

Blackest Night, published by DC Comics in 2009-2010, was a crossover event that explored the concept of death in the DC Universe. Written by Geoff Johns and illustrated by Ivan Reis, Blackest Night brought back deceased heroes and villains as Black Lanterns, creating a devastating threat to the living. The event delved into the emotional struggles of characters and reshaped the DC Universe.

8. Age of Apocalypse

Age of Apocalypse, published by Marvel in 1995, was a major crossover event that presented an alternate reality where Professor X died before forming the X-Men. Written by Scott Lobdell, Mark Waid, and others, Age of Apocalypse showcased a dystopian world ruled by mutants, with familiar characters taking on new roles. This event had long-lasting effects on the X-Men and remains a fan-favorite storyline.

9. House of M

House of M, published by Marvel in 2005, was a crossover event that focused on the reality-altering powers of the mutant Scarlet Witch. Written by Brian Michael Bendis and illustrated by Olivier Coipel, House of M explored the fallout of Scarlet Witch’s mental breakdown, leading to a world where mutants were the dominant species. This event had significant consequences for the Marvel Universe and its mutant population.

10. Crisis on Infinite Earths (TV)

While not a comic book event per se, Crisis on Infinite Earths deserves mention for its impact on the television landscape. The Arrowverse crossover event, aired in 2019-2020, brought together characters from various DC shows, including Arrow, The Flash, Supergirl, and more. Packed with nods to the source material, Crisis on Infinite Earths left fans excited for future crossovers and showcased the potential of bringing comic book events to the small screen.

In conclusion, comic book events and crossovers have thrilled fans for decades. These major storylines have pushed the boundaries of storytelling, united heroes and villains, and forever changed the comic book landscape. Whether in the pages of a comic or on the screen, these events have left a lasting impact on the industries and the hearts of fans everywhere.
