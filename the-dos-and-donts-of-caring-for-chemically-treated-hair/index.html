The Dos and Don'ts of Caring for Chemically Treated Hair


Chemically treated hair requires special care to maintain its health, strength, and shine. Whether you’ve recently undergone a chemical treatment or are considering one, it’s important to understand the dos and don’ts of caring for chemically treated hair. In this blog post, we will explore the best practices to keep your hair looking and feeling its best.

Dos:

1. Use sulfate-free shampoos: Sulfates can strip away the natural oils from your hair, leaving it dry and brittle. Opt for sulfate-free shampoos that are gentle on chemically treated hair. Look for products that are specifically formulated for your hair type and the chemical treatment you’ve had.

2. Deep condition regularly: Chemically treated hair often lacks moisture and can become prone to breakage. To combat this, incorporate deep conditioning treatments into your hair care routine. These treatments help to restore moisture, strengthen the hair shaft, and prevent further damage. Look for deep conditioners that are rich in natural oils, proteins, and vitamins.

3. Protect your hair from heat: Heat styling tools like straighteners, curling irons, and blow dryers can cause damage to chemically treated hair. When using these tools, always apply a heat protectant spray or serum to minimize heat damage. Additionally, try to limit the use of heat styling tools and opt for heatless hairstyles whenever possible.

4. Trim regularly: Chemical treatments can weaken the hair, leading to split ends and breakage. Regular trims every 6-8 weeks will help to prevent split ends from traveling up the hair shaft and keep your hair looking healthy and fresh.

5. Sleep on a satin pillowcase or use a satin bonnet: The friction between your hair and a cotton pillowcase can cause breakage and frizz. Sleeping on a satin pillowcase or using a satin bonnet will help to reduce friction, protect your hair from damage, and preserve your hairstyle for longer.

Don’ts:

1. Overwash your hair: Washing your hair too frequently can strip away the natural oils, leaving it dry and dull. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days or as needed, depending on your hair type and the chemical treatment you’ve had.

2. Use harsh styling products: Avoid using styling products that contain alcohol, as they can further dry out your hair. Opt for lightweight, moisturizing products that provide hold without causing damage.

3. Brush wet hair: Wet hair is more fragile and susceptible to breakage. Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it’s wet, as it can lead to unnecessary damage. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle gently.

4. Skip regular touch-ups: If you have chemically relaxed or straightened hair, it’s essential to schedule regular touch-ups to maintain the desired look. Neglecting touch-ups can lead to new growth that is more prone to breakage and can affect the overall health of your hair.

5. Overload on heat styling: While it’s important to protect your hair from heat, it’s also crucial to limit the use of heat styling tools. Excessive heat can cause damage to chemically treated hair, leading to dryness, breakage, and loss of luster. Embrace natural hairstyles and try heatless styling methods to give your hair a break.

Caring for chemically treated hair requires a combination of gentle products, regular deep conditioning, and mindful styling practices. By following these dos and don’ts, you can maintain the health and beauty of your chemically treated hair, allowing it to thrive and shine. Remember, healthy hair is beautiful hair!
