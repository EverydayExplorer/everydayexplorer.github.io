The Art of Eyeliner Application: Winged and Smudged


When it comes to eye makeup, the perfect eyeliner can create a dramatic and captivating look. Among the different eyeliner techniques, the winged and smudged styles have gained immense popularity. From celebrities strutting the red carpet to beauty influencers showcasing their skills, the art of eyeliner application has become an essential part of every makeup lover’s routine.

The Winged Eyeliner:

The winged eyeliner is a classic and timeless look that adds a touch of elegance and femininity to any makeup look. This technique involves creating a thin line along the upper lash line that extends slightly past the outer corner of the eye, forming a flick or wing-like shape. It can be sharp and bold, or soft and subtle, depending on personal preference.

To achieve the perfect winged eyeliner, start by drawing a thin line along your upper lash line, starting from the inner corner and gradually increasing the thickness towards the outer corner. Once you reach the end, angle the line slightly upwards towards the tail of your brow. Then, connect the angled line to the outer corner of your eye, creating a flick or wing. Fill in any gaps and ensure a smooth application.

There are various tools you can use to create the winged eyeliner, such as liquid, gel, or pencil liners. Liquid liners are ideal for precise lines and sharp wings, while gel liners offer a more forgiving application and can easily be smudged for a softer look. Pencil liners are great for beginners or when you want a more smudged and smokey effect.

The Smudged Eyeliner:

While the winged eyeliner is known for its clean and precise lines, the smudged eyeliner offers a more sultry and edgy vibe. This technique involves applying the eyeliner along the upper and lower lash lines and then smudging it for a softer and more diffused effect. It’s perfect for achieving a seductive smokey eye look.

To create a smudged eyeliner look, start by applying the liner along your upper and lower lash lines, as close to the roots of your lashes as possible. You can opt for a pencil or gel liner for easy smudging. Once you have applied the liner, use a smudge brush or a cotton swab to gently blend and soften the edges, creating a smoky effect. You can also add eyeshadow on top of the liner to intensify the smudged look.

The key to a successful smudged eyeliner application is to use the right tools and techniques. Pencil liners work best for smudging as they tend to be softer and easier to blend. Gel liners can also be smudged, but they may require some practice to achieve the desired effect.

Tips for Perfect Eyeliner Application:

To master the art of eyeliner application, here are a few tips that will help you achieve flawless results:

1. Practice makes perfect: Like any skill, mastering eyeliner application requires practice. Don’t be discouraged if your first attempts aren’t perfect. Keep practicing, and you will eventually develop the necessary skills and techniques.

2. Invest in good quality products: Using high-quality eyeliner products will make a significant difference in the application process. Opt for long-lasting and smudge-proof formulas that will stay put throughout the day.

3. Prep your eyelids: Before applying eyeliner, it’s crucial to prep your eyelids. Cleanse your lids to remove any oils or residue, and apply an eyeshadow primer to ensure a smooth and long-lasting application.

4. Start thin and build up: It’s easier to add more eyeliner if needed than to remove excess. Start with a thin line, and gradually build up the thickness as desired.

5. Use tape or a spoon for a precise wing: If you struggle with creating a symmetrical wing, try using a strip of tape or a spoon as a guide. Place the tape or the handle of the spoon diagonally from the outer corner of your eye, and use it as a stencil for your wing.

6. Fix mistakes with concealer: If you make any mistakes while applying your eyeliner, don’t worry! Use a small, flat brush dipped in concealer to gently clean up any uneven lines.

In conclusion, the art of eyeliner application allows you to enhance your eyes and create various looks, from a classic winged eyeliner to a smudged and smokey effect. By practicing the techniques mentioned above and using the right tools and products, you can effortlessly achieve the perfect eyeliner look. So, grab your favorite eyeliner and get creative with your eye makeup!
