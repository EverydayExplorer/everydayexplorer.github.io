The Influence of C.S. Lewis on Christian Allegory in Fantasy Literature


As a spiritual and literary figure who has stood the test of time, C.S. Lewis has been an influential writer for decades. Known for his extensive literary works, ranging from medieval literature to Christian allegory, Lewis’s influence on the world of fantasy fiction cannot be ignored, especially for Christian writers who have used fantasy as a medium to convey their thoughts and beliefs. C.S. Lewis’s influence on Christian allegory in fantasy literature can be seen in many contemporary writers who have adopted his style and approach in their works. In this blog post, we will explore the impact that C.S. Lewis has had on the world of Christian allegory in fantasy literature.

C.S. Lewis was a British writer who was born in 1898 and died in 1963. Throughout his prolific career, he wrote several works that continue to resonate with readers across generations. Lewis’s most popular work is The Chronicles of Narnia, a seven-book series that is considered a modern classic of children’s literature. The books follow the adventures of children in a magical land called Narnia, which is populated by various mythical creatures, including fauns, centaurs, and talking animals.

C.S. Lewis was a devout Christian, and his faith influenced his writing significantly. Narnia is full of Christian symbolism, and the series is seen as an allegory for the Christian faith. Many Christian writers who came after Lewis have used his approach to allegory to convey their ideas and beliefs.

One of the most significant ways in which C.S. Lewis has influenced Christian allegory in fantasy literature is through his use of fantasy as a medium for conveying moral and ethical lessons. Through his works, Lewis explored various themes such as love, friendship, forgiveness, and redemption. His characters and their experiences reflect these themes, and his readers can draw meaningful lessons from them.

Most significantly, Lewis’s use of Christian symbolism in his work has had a lasting impact on the genre. His stories are deeply rooted in Christian theology and use elements from the Bible as a basis for their narrative. For example, Aslan, the lion who is the king of Narnia, is seen as a Christ figure who dies and comes back to life, just as Christ did in the Bible. Other characters in the series also represent Christian figures or themes, such as the White Witch who represents Satan and the Tree of Life that symbolizes the Garden of Eden.

The use of Christian symbolism in fantasy literature is now a staple of the genre, and contemporary writers continue to adopt this approach in their works. J.K. Rowling’s Harry Potter series, for instance, has been praised for its use of Christian symbolism, specifically the concept of self-sacrifice and the idea that love is the most potent force in the world. The books are also seen as an allegory for the fight between good and evil, which is a central theme in Christianity.

Another way in which C.S. Lewis has influenced Christian allegory in fantasy literature is through his focus on character development. Lewis’s characters are often flawed and undergo a transformation throughout the course of the story. This approach has been adopted by many contemporary writers who use their characters to convey complex moral and ethical issues. For example, George R.R. Martin’s A Song of Ice and Fire series explores the themes of power, corruption, and morality through the development of complex, multi-dimensional characters.

In conclusion, C.S. Lewis’s influence on Christian allegory in fantasy literature cannot be overstated. His use of Christian symbolism and his focus on character development have had a lasting impact on the genre, and his legacy continues to resonate with contemporary writers. His contribution to the genre has allowed writers to use fantasy as a medium for exploring complex ethical and moral issues, which is no small feat. Christian writers continue to take inspiration from Lewis’s approach to allegory, and his influence will undoubtedly be felt for generations to come.
