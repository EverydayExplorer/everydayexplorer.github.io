Building a Bug Out Bag: What to Pack for Survival


In today’s uncertain world, it’s more important than ever to be prepared for emergencies. Whether it’s a natural disaster, a power outage, or a sudden evacuation, having a bug out bag can make all the difference in your survival. A bug out bag, also known as a go bag or 72-hour kit, is a portable kit that contains essential items to sustain you for at least 72 hours. In this blog post, we will discuss the importance of building a bug out bag and provide you with a comprehensive list of what to pack for survival.

Why Do You Need a Bug Out Bag?

Emergencies can happen at any time and often without warning. Having a bug out bag ready to go ensures that you have essential supplies readily available in case you need to evacuate quickly or survive on your own for an extended period. It is particularly crucial if you live in an area prone to natural disasters such as hurricanes, earthquakes, or wildfires. When disaster strikes, emergency services may be overwhelmed, and access to food, water, and medical supplies may be limited.

What Should You Consider When Building a Bug Out Bag?

When building a bug out bag, it’s important to consider the following factors:

1. Duration: Plan for at least 72 hours of self-sufficiency. However, it’s advisable to pack enough supplies that can sustain you for a more extended period, such as a week or even two if possible.

2. Environment: Consider the climate and terrain in your area. If you live in a cold climate, pack warm clothing and sleeping gear. If you live in a coastal area, include items such as a life vest or water purification tablets.

3. Personal Needs: Take into account any specific needs you may have or those of your family members. This could include medications, baby supplies, or items for pets.

What to Pack for Survival

Now let’s dive into the essential items you should pack in your bug out bag:

1. Water: Pack at least one liter of water per person per day. Opt for lightweight, portable water filters or purification tablets to ensure a continuous supply of safe drinking water.

2. Food: Include non-perishable food items that are high in calories and nutrition. Granola bars, energy bars, canned goods, and dehydrated meals are great options. Don’t forget a can opener or a multi-tool with a can opener function.

3. Shelter: Pack a compact tent or tarp, along with a sleeping bag or emergency blanket. These items will provide protection from the elements and help you stay warm.

4. Clothing: Include a change of clothes suitable for the climate in your region. Don’t forget to pack extra socks, underwear, and sturdy footwear.

5. First Aid Kit: A well-stocked first aid kit is essential. Make sure it includes bandages, antiseptic wipes, pain relievers, tweezers, and any necessary prescription medications.

6. Tools and Equipment: A multi-tool, duct tape, a flashlight with extra batteries, a whistle, and a portable radio can come in handy during emergencies.

7. Personal Hygiene Items: Include items like toothpaste, toothbrushes, soap, hand sanitizer, toilet paper, and feminine hygiene products.

8. Communication: Pack a fully charged mobile phone with an extra battery pack or a solar charger. Include important contact numbers, maps, and emergency preparedness guides.

9. Personal Documents: Make copies of important documents like identification cards, insurance policies, passports, and medical records. Store them in a waterproof and portable container.

10. Cash: Include some cash in your bug out bag, as ATMs and credit card systems may not be operational during emergencies.

Remember to periodically check and update your bug out bag’s contents. Rotate food and water supplies to ensure freshness and replace expired items in your first aid kit. Additionally, practice using the equipment in your bug out bag to ensure you are familiar with how it works.

In conclusion, building a bug out bag is an essential step towards preparedness and self-sufficiency during emergencies. By following the guidelines and packing the recommended items, you can have peace of mind knowing that you are ready to face any unexpected situation. Stay safe, be prepared, and remember that in times of crisis, having a bug out bag can make all the difference.
