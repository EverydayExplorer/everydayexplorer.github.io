Understanding Lens Flare: Embracing or Avoiding this Photographic Phenomenon


Lens flare is a captivating and often debated phenomenon in photography that occurs when light enters the camera lens and creates unwanted artifacts or streaks of light in the final image. Some photographers view lens flare as a flaw to be avoided at all costs, while others embrace it as a creative element that can add a unique and ethereal quality to their photos. In this blog post, we will dive deep into the world of lens flare, exploring its causes, effects, and how you can effectively use or avoid it in your own photography.

Understanding the Causes of Lens Flare

Lens flare is caused by the scattering of light inside the camera lens, typically due to internal reflections between lens elements or the camera sensor. When light enters the lens from a strong source, such as the sun or artificial lighting, it can bounce around inside the lens, creating unwanted artifacts in the final image. The shape and appearance of lens flare can vary depending on the number and arrangement of lens elements in your camera, as well as the angle and intensity of the light source.

Embracing Lens Flare as a Creative Element

While some photographers go to great lengths to avoid lens flare in their images, others see it as a creative opportunity to enhance the mood and atmosphere of their photos. Lens flare can add a dreamy, otherworldly quality to your images, lending them a sense of depth and warmth. By deliberately positioning your camera to capture lens flare, you can create visually striking compositions that draw the viewer’s eye and evoke a sense of wonder.

Tips for Embracing Lens Flare in Your Photography

If you’re interested in incorporating lens flare into your photography, there are a few tips you can follow to achieve the desired effect. Firstly, experiment with different angles and positions relative to the light source to find the most flattering and aesthetically pleasing lens flare. Additionally, consider using a lens hood or your hand to block out excess light and control the intensity of the flare in your images.

Avoiding Lens Flare in Your Photography

On the flip side, if you prefer to minimize or eliminate lens flare in your photographs, there are several techniques you can employ to achieve this goal. Using a lens hood or a lens filter can help reduce the amount of stray light entering your lens, thereby decreasing the likelihood of lens flare appearing in your images. Additionally, adjusting your shooting angle or position relative to the light source can help you avoid undesirable flare patterns and maintain the clarity and sharpness of your photos.

In conclusion, whether you choose to embrace or avoid lens flare in your photography is ultimately a matter of personal preference and artistic vision. By understanding the causes and effects of lens flare and experimenting with different techniques, you can harness the power of this photographic phenomenon to enhance your images and create compelling visual narratives. So, the next time you pick up your camera, don’t shy away from lens flare – embrace it as a tool to elevate your photography to new heights.
