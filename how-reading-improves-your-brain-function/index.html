How Reading Improves Your Brain Function


Reading has long been known to be beneficial for the mind, and science has now given us more concrete reasons why. Research has shown that reading can improve various aspects of brain function. Whether you prefer fiction, non-fiction, or anything in between, the following are some of the reasons why you should pick up a book and get lost in its pages.

Improves Cognitive Functioning

Cognitive functioning refers to the processes in the brain that are necessary for learning, thinking, and problem-solving. Reading has been shown to improve these processes by stimulating the areas of the brain that are responsible for them. According to a study conducted at Stanford University, reading can increase the connectivity of different areas of the brain, specifically those involved in language and sensory processing.

Improves Memory

Reading helps to exercise the brain, and like any other muscle, it gets stronger with exercise. Reading helps to improve memory and the ability to recall information. When you read, you’re constantly trying to keep track of different plotlines, characters, settings, and so on. All of this requires you to remember details and make connections between them. The more you do this, the more you train your brain to be better at processing and retaining information.

Reduces Stress

Reading can serve as a form of escapism when the stress of life becomes overwhelming. A study by the University of Sussex showed that reading can reduce stress levels by up to 68%.
