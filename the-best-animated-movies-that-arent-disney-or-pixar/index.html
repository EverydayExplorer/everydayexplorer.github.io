The Best Animated Movies That Aren't Disney or Pixar


When it comes to animated movies, Disney and Pixar often steal the spotlight with their magical worlds, captivating characters, and heartwarming stories. However, there’s a whole world of animated gems beyond the realm of Disney and Pixar waiting to be discovered. From breathtaking visuals to thought-provoking narratives, these non-Disney and non-Pixar animated movies showcase the diverse talent and creativity within the animation industry. Whether you’re a seasoned animation enthusiast or simply looking for something new to watch, here’s a curated list of some of the best animated movies that aren’t Disney or Pixar.

  1. ‘The Secret of Kells’ (2009) - Directed by Tomm Moore and Nora Twomey, ‘The Secret of Kells’ is a visually stunning Irish animated film that tells the story of a young boy named Brendan who embarks on a magical adventure to complete the legendary Book of Kells and save his village from invaders. With its unique art style inspired by Celtic illustrations and its enchanting tale of bravery and friendship, ‘The Secret of Kells’ is a must-watch for animation aficionados.

  2. ‘Song of the Sea’ (2014) - Another masterpiece from director Tomm Moore, ‘Song of the Sea’ is a mesmerizing Irish animated film that weaves together elements of Celtic mythology and folklore. The story follows a young boy named Ben and his mute sister Saoirse as they embark on a journey to save the spirit world and rediscover their own identities. Featuring breathtaking hand-drawn animation and a hauntingly beautiful score, ‘Song of the Sea’ is a captivating cinematic experience for audiences of all ages.

  3. ‘Kubo and the Two Strings’ (2016) - Directed by Travis Knight and produced by Laika Studios, ‘Kubo and the Two Strings’ is a visually stunning stop-motion animated film that combines Japanese folklore with breathtaking animation and heartfelt storytelling. The film follows a young boy named Kubo as he sets out on a quest to unlock the secret of his magical heritage and defeat the vengeful Moon King. With its meticulous craftsmanship and emotionally resonant narrative, ‘Kubo and the Two Strings’ is a true work of art.

  4. ‘The Breadwinner’ (2017) - Directed by Nora Twomey and produced by Cartoon Saloon, ‘The Breadwinner’ is a powerful animated film that sheds light on the plight of women in Afghanistan under Taliban rule. The story follows a young girl named Parvana who disguises herself as a boy to provide for her family after her father is wrongfully imprisoned. Through its stunning animation and poignant storytelling, ‘The Breadwinner’ offers a compelling glimpse into the resilience of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

  5. ‘Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse’ (2018) - Directed by Bob Persichetti, Peter Ramsey, and Rodney Rothman, ‘Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse’ is a groundbreaking animated film that reimagines the Spider-Man mythos in a bold and innovative way. With its vibrant animation style inspired by comic books, its diverse cast of characters, and its heartfelt exploration of heroism and identity, ‘Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse’ has earned its place as one of the greatest superhero movies of all time.

  6. ‘Wolfwalkers’ (2020) - Directed by Tomm Moore and Ross Stewart, ‘Wolfwalkers’ is an enchanting Irish animated film that follows a young girl named Robyn as she befriends a mysterious girl with the ability to transform into a wolf. Set against the backdrop of 17th century Ireland, the film explores themes of friendship, freedom, and the conflict between civilization and nature. With its breathtaking hand-drawn animation and timeless storytelling, ‘Wolfwalkers’ is a modern animated classic.

These are just a few examples of the many incredible animated movies that exist beyond the realm of Disney and Pixar. From the lush landscapes of Ireland to the bustling streets of New York City, each of these films offers a unique and unforgettable cinematic experience that is sure to delight audiences of all ages. So the next time you’re in the mood for a movie night, why not venture outside the Disney-Pixar bubble and discover the magic of these hidden animated gems?
