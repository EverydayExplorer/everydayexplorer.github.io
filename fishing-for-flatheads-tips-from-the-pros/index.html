Fishing for Flatheads: Tips from the Pros


Are you looking to catch big flatheads on your next fishing trip? Look no further! In this blog post, we will be sharing some tips from the pros that will help increase your chances of landing a trophy flathead. So grab your fishing gear, stay tuned, and let’s dive into the world of flathead fishing!

1. Location, location, location

When it comes to flathead fishing, finding the right location is key. Flatheads are generally found in deeper waters, particularly around structures such as trees, rocks, and logs. Look for areas with a slow-moving current and plenty of cover. These fish are ambush predators, so they prefer to hide in structures and wait for their prey to come by.

2. Time your fishing trip

Flatheads are most active during dusk and dawn. These are the prime feeding times for these elusive fish. Plan your fishing trip accordingly and be at your desired fishing spot well before the sun rises or sets. This will give you the best chance of hooking into a big flathead.

3. Choose the right bait

Flatheads are opportunistic feeders and will eat almost anything. Live bait such as large shiners, bluegills, or suckers work well when targeting big flatheads. They prefer live bait over artificial lures, so it’s crucial to have a good supply of lively baitfish on hand. Make sure to hook your bait properly to ensure it stays alive and looks natural in the water.

4. Be patient and persistent

Fishing for flatheads can be challenging and requires patience and persistence. These fish don’t actively chase down their prey; instead, they wait for it to come to them. So it’s essential to present your bait in a way that looks natural and irresistible to the flatheads. Keep your line tight and be prepared for a long wait. Sometimes it can take hours before you get a bite, but when you do, it’s often worth the wait.

5. Keep your gear in top condition

To increase your chances of landing a trophy flathead, it’s essential to keep your fishing gear in top condition. Check your lines regularly for any signs of wear or damage, and replace them if necessary. Make sure your hooks are sharp and your knots are secure. Flatheads are powerful fighters, so you need to have gear that can withstand their strength.

6. Stay quiet and stealthy

When fishing for flatheads, it’s crucial to be quiet and stealthy. These fish have excellent hearing and can easily be spooked by loud noises or vibrations in the water. Avoid banging or dropping anything on your boat, and try to move around as quietly as possible. Additionally, use a fishing kayak or a small boat to access hard-to-reach spots and increase your chances of hooking into a trophy flathead.

7. Practice catch and release

Lastly, it’s essential to practice catch and release when targeting flatheads. These fish are slow-growing and take several years to reach trophy size. By releasing them back into the water, you are ensuring the longevity of the species and allowing future generations to enjoy the thrill of catching big flatheads.

In conclusion, fishing for flatheads can be an exciting and rewarding experience. By following these tips from the pros, you will increase your chances of hooking into a trophy flathead. Remember to find the right location, use the right bait, be patient and persistent, keep your gear in top condition, stay quiet and stealthy, and practice catch and release. So grab your fishing gear, head out to your favorite fishing spot, and get ready for an adventure like no other!
