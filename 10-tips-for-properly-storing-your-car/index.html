10 Tips for Properly Storing Your Car


Properly storing your car is crucial to maintain its performance and ensure its longevity. Whether you’re going on an extended vacation, live in an area with harsh weather conditions, or simply have an extra vehicle that needs safekeeping, following the right storage techniques is essential. In this blog post, we will share ten tips to help you store your car properly, keeping it in excellent condition until you’re ready to hit the road again.

1. Clean and Detail Your Car:

Before storing your car, give it a thorough cleaning inside and out. Remove any debris, dirt, or food particles that may attract pests. Also, consider getting a professional detailing service to protect the paint and interior surfaces.

2. Change Fluids and Filters:

To prevent any potential damage, it’s advisable to change the oil and filter before storing your vehicle. Old oil can become acidic over time, leading to corrosion and engine issues. Additionally, replace the coolant, brake fluid, and other necessary fluids to ensure optimal performance when you retrieve your car.

3. Fill Up the Tank:

A full gas tank helps prevent moisture build-up inside the fuel tank, which can lead to rust and other damage. Add a fuel stabilizer to keep the gasoline fresh during storage, and run the engine for a few minutes to circulate the treated fuel throughout the system.

4. Remove the Battery:

Disconnect the battery and store it in a cool, dry place. If you’re storing your car for an extended period, consider using a battery tender to maintain its charge. This prevents battery drain and ensures it will be ready to start when you return.

5. Protect the Tires:

Inflate the tires to the manufacturer’s recommended pressure and consider using jack stands to relieve the weight on the suspension. This prevents flat spots and keeps the tires in good condition. If possible, move the car slightly every few weeks to avoid prolonged pressure on a single spot.

6. Choose the Right Storage Location:

When selecting a storage location, opt for a clean, dry, and well-ventilated space. If available, a climate-controlled garage or storage unit is ideal, as it helps maintain a consistent temperature and prevents moisture-related issues. Ensure the area is secure and has proper pest control measures in place.

7. Cover Your Car:

Invest in a high-quality, breathable car cover to shield your vehicle from dust, debris, and potential scratches. Avoid using plastic or vinyl covers, as they can trap moisture and cause damage to the paintwork.

8. Perform Periodic Checks:

Even though your car is in storage, it’s essential to perform periodic checks to ensure everything remains in good condition. Every few weeks, inspect the vehicle for any signs of leaks, pests, or damage. Start the engine and let it run for a few minutes to circulate fluids and lubricate internal components.

9. Maintain Insurance and Registration:

Don’t forget to keep your car’s insurance and registration up to date, even when it’s in storage. This ensures you remain protected against any unforeseen circumstances, such as theft, vandalism, or natural disasters.

10. Plan for Long-Term Storage:

If you’re planning to store your car for an extended period, consider taking additional precautions. Consult your vehicle’s manufacturer guidelines for specific instructions, such as removing certain parts, applying rust inhibitors, or elevating the vehicle to relieve tire pressure.

Conclusion:

Properly storing your car is essential to preserve its condition and performance. By following these ten tips, you can ensure that your vehicle remains in excellent shape while it’s not in use. From cleaning and detailing to choosing the right storage location, every step plays a crucial role in maintaining your car’s longevity. So, take the time to properly store your vehicle, and it will be ready to hit the road when you are. Safe travels!
