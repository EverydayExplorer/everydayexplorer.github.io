Bourbon 101: A Beginner's Guide to America's Native Spirit


When it comes to iconic American spirits, few can compare to bourbon. With its rich history, complex flavors, and devoted fan base, bourbon has become a staple in bars and liquor cabinets across the country. If you’re new to the world of bourbon, fear not! This beginner’s guide will help you navigate the world of America’s native spirit and discover what makes it so special.

What is Bourbon?

Bourbon is a type of whiskey that is primarily made from corn. To be considered bourbon, a whiskey must meet certain strict criteria set forth by the American government. These criteria include being made from a grain mixture that is at least 51% corn, aged in new charred oak barrels, distilled to no more than 160 proof, and bottled at no less than 80 proof. Additionally, bourbon must be produced in the United States to earn the coveted title of America’s native spirit.

The Origins of Bourbon

The exact origins of bourbon are somewhat murky, but it is widely believed to have been invented in Kentucky in the late 18th century. Many credit Elijah Craig, a Baptist minister and distiller, with creating the first bourbon whiskey. Legend has it that Craig accidentally charred a batch of whiskey barrels, giving the spirit its signature smoky flavor. Whether or not this story is true, there is no denying that bourbon has deep roots in Kentucky and has become synonymous with the state.

Types of Bourbon

Bourbon comes in many different varieties, from small-batch to single-barrel to straight bourbon. Each type of bourbon has its own unique characteristics and flavor profiles. Small-batch bourbons are typically made in limited quantities and are known for their higher quality and attention to detail. Single-barrel bourbons are bottled from a single, carefully selected barrel, resulting in a more consistent flavor profile. Straight bourbons are aged for at least two years and do not contain any added colors or flavors.

How to Drink Bourbon

Now that you know a bit about the history and types of bourbon, it’s time to learn how to drink it. While some people enjoy sipping bourbon neat, others prefer it on the rocks or in a classic cocktail like a Manhattan or Old Fashioned. When tasting bourbon, be sure to take your time and savor the flavors. Swirl the bourbon in your glass to release its aromas, take a small sip, and let it linger on your palate. Pay attention to the different flavors and notes that you detect, such as caramel, vanilla, oak, and spice.

Bourbon Tasting Tips

If you’re new to bourbon tasting, there are a few tips to keep in mind to enhance your experience. Start by tasting bourbon in a tulip-shaped glass, which will help concentrate the aromas. Take small sips and let the bourbon coat your palate to fully appreciate the flavors. You can also add a few drops of water to your bourbon to open up the flavors and aromas. And most importantly, have fun experimenting with different bourbons and finding the ones that you enjoy the most.

In conclusion, bourbon is a truly unique and versatile spirit that has captivated drinkers for centuries. Whether you’re a seasoned bourbon aficionado or a beginner just starting to explore the world of whiskey, there is always something new to discover and appreciate about America’s native spirit. So pour yourself a glass, raise a toast to tradition and craftsmanship, and enjoy the rich flavors of bourbon. Cheers!
