Surviving a Blizzard: Essential Preparations


Blizzards can be fierce and unforgiving, leaving people stranded and homes without power for days on end. It is crucial to be prepared for these harsh winter storms to ensure the safety and well-being of yourself and your loved ones. In this blog post, we will discuss the essential preparations you need to make to survive a blizzard.

Stay Informed

Before a blizzard hits, it is essential to stay informed about weather conditions and any storm warnings in your area. Sign up for local weather alerts, follow reliable weather sources, and keep a battery-powered radio handy. Being aware of the approaching storm will give you time to make necessary preparations and take appropriate action.

Stock Up on Supplies

When a blizzard is on its way, it’s important to stock up on essential supplies to last you through the storm. Here’s a list of items you should have on hand:

  • Non-perishable food items: Stock your pantry with canned goods, dried fruits, nuts, and other non-perishable food items that can provide sustenance during a power outage.
  • Water: Fill containers with clean drinking water, as water supply may be compromised during a blizzard.
  • Medications and first aid supplies: Ensure you have an ample supply of any necessary medications, as well as a well-stocked first aid kit.
  • Extra blankets and warm clothing: Layer up to stay warm in case of a power outage. Have extra blankets, sweaters, and warm clothing readily accessible.
  • Flashlights and batteries: Keep flashlights and extra batteries handy to navigate through the darkness during a power outage.
  • Portable phone charger: A fully charged portable phone charger can be a lifesaver during a blizzard, enabling you to stay connected and call for help if needed.
  • Alternative heating source: If you have a fireplace or a wood-burning stove, make sure you have enough firewood to keep warm.

Prepare Your Home

To protect your home from the harsh winter elements, take these essential steps:

  • Insulate windows and doors: Use weather stripping and insulation to prevent drafts and keep the cold air out.
  • Clear gutters and drains: Remove any debris from gutters and drains to prevent ice dams from forming.
  • Insulate pipes: Wrap exposed pipes with insulation to prevent freezing and bursting.
  • Trim trees: Prune tree branches that may pose a risk of falling on your home or power lines during a blizzard.
  • Have an emergency generator: If possible, invest in a portable generator to provide backup power during an outage.
  • Keep a supply of rock salt or ice melt: Sprinkle rock salt or ice melt on walkways and driveways to prevent ice buildup and increase traction.

Create an Emergency Plan

Having an emergency plan in place can make all the difference during a blizzard. Here are some key points to consider:

  • Establish a communication plan: Designate a meeting place and ensure everyone in your household knows how to reach each other in case of separation.
  • Create an emergency kit: Put together a comprehensive emergency kit that includes essential items like a battery-powered radio, extra batteries, a first aid kit, non-perishable food, water, and blankets.
  • Know how to shut off utilities: Familiarize yourself with how to shut off gas, water, and electricity in case of an emergency.
  • Plan for pets: Make arrangements for your pets’ safety and comfort during a blizzard. Have enough food, water, and medication to last them through the storm.

Stay Safe During the Blizzard

When the blizzard hits, it’s important to prioritize safety:

  • Stay indoors: Avoid unnecessary travel and stay indoors until it is safe to venture outside.
  • Dress in layers: Layer your clothing to stay warm and prevent frostbite.
  • Avoid carbon monoxide poisoning: If using a generator or alternative heating source, ensure proper ventilation to prevent carbon monoxide buildup.
  • Check on vulnerable individuals: Keep an eye on elderly neighbors or individuals with special needs to ensure their safety.

By following these essential preparations, you will be better equipped to survive a blizzard and keep yourself and your loved ones safe. Remember, it’s always better to be over-prepared than caught off guard. Stay informed, stock up on supplies, prepare your home, and create an emergency plan. With these precautions in place, you can face the winter storms with confidence and resilience. Stay safe!
