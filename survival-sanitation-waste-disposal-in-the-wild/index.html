Survival Sanitation: Waste Disposal in the Wild


When it comes to survival in the wild, one aspect that is often overlooked is waste disposal. Proper sanitation is crucial not only for our own health and well-being but also for the environment. In this blog post, we will explore the various methods of waste disposal in the wild, focusing on eco-friendly techniques that minimize our impact on nature.

The Importance of Waste Disposal

In a survival situation, proper waste disposal becomes even more critical. Improper waste management can lead to the spread of diseases, attract unwanted wildlife, and contaminate water sources. It is essential to remember that we are guests in nature, and we should strive to leave as little trace as possible.

Carry Out What You Carry In

The simplest and most effective method of waste disposal in the wild is to practice the ‘Carry Out What You Carry In’ principle. This means that any waste you generate while in the wilderness, whether it be food scraps, packaging, or personal hygiene items, should be collected and taken back with you.

Carrying out your waste not only ensures that you are leaving no trace but also allows for proper disposal in designated waste bins once you reach civilization. It may seem cumbersome, but it is a responsible and respectful way to treat the environment.

Digging a Cathole

When nature calls and there are no civilized facilities nearby, digging a cathole is a suitable option for waste disposal. A cathole is a small hole dug in the ground, away from water sources, and at least 200 feet from any campsites. The hole should be around 6 to 8 inches deep.

After using the cathole, it is crucial to cover it completely with the soil, leaving no trace behind. This method is effective for disposing of solid waste, but it should not be used for disposing of liquids or other non-biodegradable items.

Pack Out Toilet

For more extended trips and situations where digging a cathole is not possible or practical, using a pack-out toilet is a viable option. A pack-out toilet, also known as a portable toilet or wag bag, is a lightweight and compact solution for waste disposal in the wild.

These portable toilets consist of a sturdy bag lined with a biodegradable gel or powder that helps neutralize odors, solidify waste, and facilitate safe and sanitary disposal. Once you have finished using the pack-out toilet, seal the bag and carry it out with you until you can dispose of it properly.

Other Considerations for Survival Sanitation

In addition to waste disposal techniques, there are some other important considerations for survival sanitation in the wild:

Hygiene and Personal Care

Maintaining good hygiene and personal care practices is key to staying healthy in the wilderness. Carry biodegradable soap and use it sparingly to wash your hands, face, and any other body parts as necessary. Remember to use water sources responsibly, avoiding contamination.

Keep Water Sources Clean

Water is essential for survival, but it can also become a carrier of diseases if contaminated by waste. Always collect water from flowing sources and filter it before drinking. Avoid disposing of waste near water sources and take care not to contaminate them during bathing or washing.

Dispose of Hygiene Products Properly

Toilet paper, feminine hygiene products, and other personal care items should never be left behind in the wilderness. These items should be packed out and disposed of properly in designated waste bins once you return to civilization. Using biodegradable options or reusable alternatives can also reduce waste.

Respect Local Regulations

Different areas may have specific regulations and guidelines for waste disposal in the wild. It is essential to research and respect these regulations to minimize our impact on the environment and ensure the safety and well-being of ourselves and others.

Conclusion

Survival sanitation is a crucial aspect of wilderness survival that is often overlooked. By practicing responsible waste disposal techniques such as carrying out what you carry in, digging catholes, and using pack-out toilets, we can minimize our impact on the environment and protect ourselves and others from potential health risks.

Remember to prioritize hygiene and personal care, keep water sources clean, and respect local regulations. By doing so, we can ensure a more enjoyable and sustainable experience in the wild, all while preserving the beauty of nature for future generations.
