All You Need to Know about Ice Wine: Harvesting the Frozen Grapes


Ice wine, also known as Eiswein in German, is a luxurious and rare type of dessert wine that is highly prized by wine connoisseurs around the world. Unlike traditional wines made from ripe grapes, ice wine is produced from grapes that have been left on the vine until they freeze. This unique harvesting process results in a concentrated and intensely flavored wine that pairs exceptionally well with desserts. In this blog post, we will delve into the fascinating world of ice wine and explore the meticulous process of harvesting the frozen grapes.

The Origins of Ice Wine

The origins of ice wine can be traced back to Germany in the late 18th century. Legend has it that a winemaker in Franconia, Germany, discovered the magic of frozen grapes when severe winter weather struck the region. The winemaker noticed that the grapes left on the vines had frozen and decided to press them, producing a sweet and delicious wine. This accidental discovery soon spread throughout Germany and eventually made its way across the ocean to Canada.

The Ice Wine Process

The key to making ice wine lies in the process of allowing the grapes to freeze on the vine. In order to produce high-quality ice wine, several factors must align perfectly. First and foremost, the grapes must be of excellent quality and perfectly ripe. Typically, white grape varieties such as Riesling, Vidal Blanc, and Gewürztraminer are used for ice wine production, as they have the ideal sugar and acid balance.

The process begins in the vineyard, where the grapes are left on the vine well into the winter months. Winemakers carefully monitor the weather conditions, as the grapes must experience sustained temperatures of -8 degrees Celsius (17.6 degrees Fahrenheit) or lower for an extended period of time. Once the grapes have frozen, usually in the early hours of the morning, they are carefully handpicked and pressed immediately.

Harvesting the frozen grapes is a labor-intensive process. As the grapes have frozen solid, they become much harder to handle and pick. However, the effort is worth it, as the frozen grapes result in more concentrated flavors and higher sugar levels, which are essential for producing exceptional ice wine.

The Pressing Process

After the frozen grapes are harvested, they are transported quickly to the winery to undergo the pressing process. The grapes are carefully sorted to remove any leaves, stems, or frozen berries that may have been picked along with them. The pressing is done gently and slowly, allowing the concentrated juice to be extracted without extracting any of the frozen water. This process is crucial for maintaining the integrity and concentration of flavors in the ice wine.

Fermentation and Aging

Once the juice is extracted, it is then fermented at a low temperature to preserve the delicate aromas and flavors. The fermentation process can take several weeks or even months, depending on the winemaker’s preference. After fermenting, the ice wine is typically aged in stainless steel tanks or oak barrels to further develop its complexity and character.

The Characteristics of Ice Wine

Ice wine is renowned for its distinct characteristics, which set it apart from other dessert wines. Due to the freezing process, the grapes’ water content freezes, leaving behind a highly concentrated juice. This results in a wine with an intense sweetness balanced by high acidity, creating a harmonious and complex flavor profile. Ice wine often exhibits flavors of tropical fruits, honey, apricots, and citrus, with a rich and luscious mouthfeel.

Food Pairings and Serving Recommendations

Ice wine’s sweet profile makes it an excellent companion to a variety of desserts. Its high acidity cuts through rich flavors, making it an ideal pairing with creamy desserts, fruit tarts, and even dark chocolate. Additionally, ice wine can be enjoyed on its own as a flavorful and indulgent drink. It is best served chilled in small glassware to fully appreciate its unique aroma and flavors.

Conclusion

Ice wine is a true testament to the skill and patience of winemakers. The meticulous process of harvesting frozen grapes results in a wine that is luxurious, rare, and highly sought after. From its humble beginnings in Germany to its present-day acclaim in countries like Canada and the United States, ice wine continues to captivate wine enthusiasts with its intense flavors and exquisite sweetness. Whether enjoyed on its own or paired with delightful desserts, ice wine is sure to provide a memorable and indulgent experience. Cheers!
