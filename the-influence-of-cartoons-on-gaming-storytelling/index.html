The Influence of Cartoons on Gaming Storytelling


Cartoons and video games have been two popular forms of entertainment for decades. While they may seem like separate entities, there is a significant overlap between the two, particularly when it comes to storytelling. In recent years, the influence of cartoons on gaming storytelling has become increasingly evident. Cartoons have provided inspiration for storytelling techniques, character development, art styles, and even plotlines in video games. This blog post explores this fascinating relationship and how cartoons have shaped the way stories are told in gaming.

One of the most noticeable influences of cartoons on gaming storytelling is the art style. Many video games have adopted a cel-shaded or animated aesthetic, which is directly derived from cartoons. Cel-shading is a technique that involves applying shades of color to give a flat, cartoony appearance to characters and environments, mimicking traditional 2D animation. This art style has become popular in games like The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker and Borderlands, creating a unique and visually distinctive experience for players.

Aside from the art style, cartoons have also influenced character development in video games. In cartoons, characters are often portrayed with exaggerated features and personalities, which can be seen in various video game characters. For example, the iconic plumber, Mario, has a larger-than-life personality with his cheerful demeanor and comedic antics. Similarly, Sonic the Hedgehog is known for his super-fast speed and cocky attitude, reminiscent of the classic cartoon hero archetype. These examples demonstrate how cartoons have shaped video game characters by presenting them in a larger-than-life, memorable way.

Furthermore, cartoons have influenced the storytelling techniques utilized in video games. Cartoons often follow a serialized format, where each episode forms a smaller part of a larger narrative. This structure has been adapted into video games, particularly in the form of episodic games. Episodic games are released in smaller, self-contained portions, allowing players to experience the story in a similar fashion to watching a cartoon series. Games like The Walking Dead by Telltale Games and Life is Strange by Dontnod Entertainment have successfully employed this storytelling technique, providing players with a unique and engaging narrative experience.

In addition to these influences, cartoons have also contributed to video game plotlines. Many video games have borrowed story elements from cartoons, whether it be in the form of comedic scenarios, epic adventures, or even nostalgic references. Games like Cuphead pay homage to classic cartoons like Popeye and Steamboat Willie, incorporating their visual style and humor into the gameplay. This fusion of mediums creates a sense of familiarity and nostalgia while also expanding the storytelling possibilities in gaming.

The influence of cartoons on gaming storytelling extends beyond visual and narrative elements. Cartoons have also played a role in shaping the tone and atmosphere of video games. Just as cartoons have different genres, from light-hearted comedies to dark and intense dramas, video games have also embraced a wide range of tones. Some games draw inspiration from cartoons like Adventure Time or SpongeBob SquarePants, creating a whimsical and lighthearted atmosphere. On the other hand, games like Bioshock or The Last of Us adopt a darker, more mature tone, reminiscent of gritty and emotionally charged cartoons like Batman: The Animated Series. These tonal influences add depth and variety to the storytelling in video games.

The influence of cartoons on gaming storytelling is undeniable. From the art style to character development, storytelling techniques, and plotlines, cartoons have shaped and enhanced the way stories are told in video games. As both mediums continue to evolve, we can expect to see even more cross-pollination between cartoons and gaming, leading to exciting and innovative storytelling experiences for players.

In conclusion, the impact of cartoons on gaming storytelling is far-reaching. Cartoons have influenced the art style, character development, storytelling techniques, plotlines, and tone in video games. The interplay between these two mediums has created a rich and diverse gaming landscape, with games that are visually striking, narratively engaging, and emotionally resonant. By embracing the influence of cartoons, video game developers have been able to push the boundaries of storytelling and provide players with unforgettable experiences. So next time you pick up a controller and dive into a video game, take a moment to appreciate the profound influence that cartoons have had on the storytelling within that game.
