Why Cats Scratch Furniture and How to Redirect Their Behavior


Cats scratching furniture is a common issue faced by many cat owners. It can be frustrating and can cause significant damage to your beloved pieces of furniture. However, it’s crucial to understand that scratching is a natural behavior for cats. In this blog post, we will delve into why cats scratch furniture and provide effective ways to redirect this behavior.

Why Do Cats Scratch Furniture

  1. Marking Territory: Cats have scent glands in their paws, and when they scratch, they leave both visual and olfactory marks, marking their territory. This helps them establish a sense of ownership and security in their environment.

  2. Maintenance: Scratching helps cats shed the outer layers of their claws, keeping them sharp and healthy. As a result, it helps to remove the dead keratin and stimulates the growth of new claws.

  3. Stretching: Cats love to stretch their muscles, and scratching provides an excellent opportunity for them to do so. When they stretch while scratching, it allows them to exercise and keep their muscles toned.

  4. Emotional Release: Scratching can also serve as a means of emotional release for cats. It helps them relieve stress, frustration, and anxiety. It can act as a form of physical and mental therapy for them.

Now that we understand why cats scratch furniture, let’s explore some ways to redirect their behavior.

How to Redirect Your Cat’s Scratching Behavior

  1. Provide Appropriate Scratching Surfaces: Cats need a place to scratch, so it’s essential to provide them with suitable alternatives. Invest in several scratching posts or boards that are tall and sturdy. These should be covered in materials like sisal rope, which are appealing to cats and provide a satisfying scratching experience.

  2. Place Scratching Posts Strategically: Position the scratching posts in locations where your cat spends the most time. Cats often scratch after napping or waking up, so placing a scratching post near their favorite sleeping spot can encourage them to use it.

  3. Make the Furniture Unattractive: To deter your cat from scratching furniture, make it less appealing. You can try covering the furniture with aluminum foil or double-sided tape, which cats dislike the texture of. Alternatively, you can use furniture covers or throws to protect your furniture temporarily.

  4. Positive Reinforcement: Whenever your cat uses the scratching post instead of the furniture, reward them with praise or treats. Positive reinforcement can help reinforce this desirable behavior and encourage them to continue using the appropriate scratching surfaces.

  5. Trim Their Claws: Regularly trim your cat’s claws to minimize the damage caused by scratching. Be sure to use proper cat nail clippers and consult with your veterinarian or a professional groomer for guidance on how to trim them safely.

  6. Provide Enrichment and Playtime: Boredom can contribute to furniture scratching, so make sure your cat has plenty of mental and physical stimulation. Interactive toys, regular play sessions, and environmental enrichment can help keep your cat entertained and reduce the urge to scratch furniture out of boredom.

  7. Consider Using Deterrents: If your cat continues to scratch furniture despite your best efforts, you can try using deterrent sprays or synthetic pheromone products that are designed to discourage scratching behavior. These can be sprayed on the furniture or applied to the appropriate scratching surfaces to make them more appealing to your cat.

Redirecting your cat’s scratching behavior requires patience and consistency. It’s essential to remember that punishment is not an effective solution, as it can create fear and anxiety in your cat. Instead, focus on providing them with appropriate alternatives and reinforcing positive behavior.

In conclusion, scratching is a natural behavior for cats, but with the right approach, you can redirect their behavior away from your furniture. By providing suitable scratching surfaces, making furniture unattractive, and using positive reinforcement, you can help your cat develop healthy scratching habits. Remember, understanding your cat’s needs and providing them with a stimulating environment is key to fostering positive behavior.
