The Dos and Don’ts of Tipping Hotel Staff


Tipping is an essential part of the hospitality industry and plays a significant role in ensuring excellent service at hotels. However, it can sometimes be confusing to know when, who, and how much to tip. In this blog post, we will discuss the dos and don’ts of tipping hotel staff to help you navigate this social custom with ease.

DOs:

  1. Tip for exceptional service: If a staff member goes above and beyond to make your stay memorable, it is appropriate to show your appreciation by leaving a generous tip. This could include acts such as providing extra amenities, arranging special requests, or offering personalized recommendations.

  2. Tip the housekeeping staff: Housekeeping staff work tirelessly to ensure a clean and comfortable environment for your stay. It is customary to leave a daily tip of to per day, depending on the hotel’s level of service. Leaving the tip daily ensures that it reaches the person responsible for your room each day.

  3. Tip the bellhop and concierge: When bellhops assist you with your bags or concierges provide valuable information or bookings, it is customary to show your gratitude with a tip. For bellhops, a tip of to per bag is customary, while concierges should be tipped based on the level of service provided.

  4. Tip the valet: If you utilize the hotel’s valet parking service, it is customary to tip the valet attendant. Usually, a tip of to should be given when your vehicle is returned to you.

  5. Tip the shuttle driver: If the hotel offers shuttle services and the driver assists you with your luggage or provides excellent service, it is appropriate to tip them. Tips of to per bag are generally appreciated.

DON’Ts:

  1. Don’t forget to consider the destination: Tipping customs may vary by country, and it’s important to research the local customs before traveling. In some countries, tipping is not expected, while in others, it may be insulting to offer a tip. Awareness of local customs ensures respectful behavior toward hotel staff.

  2. Don’t assume that tips are included: While some hotels include a service charge in your bill, it is always a good idea to check. If no service charge is noted, it is expected to provide individual tips for hotel staff based on the services received.

  3. Don’t tip excessively for basic services: While it is important to acknowledge good service, tipping should be proportionate to the level of service received. Excessive tipping for basic tasks can sometimes be seen as overcompensating or even embarrassing to the staff.

  4. Don’t tip on every interaction: It’s unnecessary to tip every staff member you come in contact with during your stay. Focus on tipping those who directly impact your experience, such as those mentioned in the ‘DOs’ section.

  5. Don’t forget to leave a note or thank you: When leaving a tip, it is a nice gesture to accompany it with a handwritten note or a simple ’thank you.’ This personal touch can make a lasting impression and further express your gratitude for the excellent service received.

In conclusion, tipping hotel staff is a common practice that ensures appreciation for their hard work and dedication. By following these dos and don’ts, you can navigate the tipping customs with confidence. Remember to consider the destination, tip appropriately based on the level of service, and always express your gratitude. Happy travels!
