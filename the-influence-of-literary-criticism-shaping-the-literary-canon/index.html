The Influence of Literary Criticism: Shaping the Literary Canon


Literary criticism has long been a topic of fascination within the field of literature. It is the practice of analyzing and interpreting literary works, dissecting its themes, symbolism, and overall meaning. However, beyond mere analysis, literary criticism has a profound influence on the creation and shaping of the literary canon. In this blog, we will explore how literary criticism shapes the literary canon and why it is an important aspect of the literary world.

To understand the influence of literary criticism, we must first define what the literary canon is. The literary canon refers to a collection of works that are considered to be of great value and significance in a particular culture or society. These works often form the foundation of literature and are studied, analyzed, and celebrated for their artistic merit and cultural relevance. The canon can vary across time periods, regions, and even different literary genres.

One of the ways in which literary criticism shapes the canon is through the process of evaluation. As literary critics evaluate and analyze works, they determine which ones have enduring value and should be included in the canon. Through their discerning eye, critics recognize the brilliance and innovation in certain works, elevating them to canonical status. By championing these works through their analysis, critics influence the broader literary community to recognize and appreciate their significance.

Furthermore, literary criticism helps to establish standards of quality within the literary world. By studying and critiquing various works, critics develop a set of criteria by which works can be judged. This criteria can include elements such as language proficiency, narrative structure, thematic depth, and overall originality. These standards then become benchmarks for evaluating works and determining their worthiness for inclusion in the canon. By setting these standards, literary critics shape the literary landscape and guide future generations of writers and readers.

Another way in which literary criticism shapes the canon is through the exploration of marginalized voices and perspectives. Historically, the literary canon has often been dominated by works from a particular social, cultural, or gendered perspective. However, literary criticism has played a crucial role in challenging this exclusivity and amplifying the voices that have been historically marginalized. By highlighting works from diverse backgrounds and experiences, critics expand the canon, making it more inclusive and representative. This process allows for a greater diversity of voices to be heard and celebrated within the literary world.

In addition to expanding the canon through the inclusion of marginalized voices, literary criticism also helps to reassess and reinterpret existing works within the canon. As time passes and societal perspectives evolve, the interpretation of literary works can change. Critics engage with these works from new perspectives, uncovering new meanings, and shedding light on previously overlooked themes and subtexts. Through their reinterpretation, critics breathe new life into these works, keeping them relevant and resonant for contemporary readers.

Literary criticism, therefore, plays a vital role in shaping the literary canon and ensuring its continual evolution. By championing works of merit, establishing standards of quality, showcasing marginalized voices, and reevaluating existing works, critics contribute to the dynamic nature of the canon. This ongoing process ensures that the literary world remains vibrant and reflective of the diverse human experience.

In conclusion, literary criticism has a significant influence on shaping the literary canon. Through evaluation, set standards of quality, amplification of marginalized voices, and reinterpretation of existing works, critics shape the landscape of literature. They play a crucial role in recognizing and celebrating works of enduring value, expanding the canon to include diverse perspectives, and keeping the canon relevant for future generations. Literary criticism is an essential aspect of the literary world, fostering growth, innovation, and inclusivity.
