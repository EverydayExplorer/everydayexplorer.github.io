Black Panther: Wakanda Forever and the Black Excellence


The world was left in shock when Chadwick Boseman, the lead actor of Black Panther, passed away on August 28th, 2020. His passing was a huge loss to the film industry, and particularly to the Black community, as Boseman’s iconic role as T’Challa, the Wakandan king in the Marvel Cinematic Universe, gave them representation on the big screen.

The Black Panther movie was a cultural phenomenon beyond anything Marvel had ever released before. It was a celebration of Black excellence, showcasing the talents of Black actors, directors, writers, and creatives. The film captured the hearts of audiences worldwide, grossing .3 billion at the box office. Its success proved that stories being told by and about Black people are not only profitable but increasingly necessary.

As we await the release of the highly anticipated sequel, Black Panther: Wakanda Forever, fans are eager to see how the story will continue in a post-Boseman world. The film’s director, Ryan Coogler, has assured fans that the sequel will honor Boseman’s legacy and the character’s impact on the world.

But what does Black excellence mean and why is it important? Black excellence refers to Black people achieving success, making groundbreaking contributions to their fields, and paving the way for future generations. The term is not only used to describe individuals but also to celebrate the collective achievements of Black people as a whole.

Black excellence is crucial because it shows the world what Black people are capable of achieving despite systemic racism and injustice. It challenges negative stereotypes and dismantles the false narrative that Black people are inferior. It also encourages young Black people to strive for excellence and shows them that their dreams are possible.

Black Panther: Wakanda Forever provides a unique opportunity to celebrate Black excellence once again. The film will not only showcase the talents of its Black cast and crew but also honor the legacy of Chadwick Boseman. It will continue to tell the story of Wakanda, a fictional African nation that is rich with culture, innovation, and resources. Wakanda is a symbol of hope for the Black community, representing what Africa could have been without colonization and exploitation.

In addition to honoring Boseman and showcasing Black excellence, Black Panther: Wakanda Forever has a responsibility to address issues affecting the Black community. The first movie tackled themes of racism, neo-colonialism, and African diaspora identity. The sequel has an opportunity to delve further into these themes, particularly in light of the racial reckoning happening worldwide.

In conclusion, Black Panther: Wakanda Forever has enormous expectations to live up to. The film has to honor the legacy of Chadwick Boseman, showcase Black excellence, and address pressing issues affecting the Black community. But we have faith in the film’s director, Ryan Coogler, and the talented cast and crew who brought Wakanda to life. We believe that the sequel will continue the cultural phenomenon started by the first movie, inspiring and empowering Black people worldwide.
