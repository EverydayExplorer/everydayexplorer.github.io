The Dos and Don'ts of Wearing Jewelry to the Office


When it comes to dressing for the office, many of us focus on choosing the right outfit and shoes, but we often overlook the impact of jewelry. The right jewelry can elevate your professional look, while the wrong choices can detract from your overall appearance. In this post, we’ll explore the dos and don’ts of wearing jewelry to the office, helping you make a polished and professional statement.

The Dos

1. Keep it Simple

Less is more is the golden rule when it comes to office jewelry. Opt for simple, understated pieces that complement your outfit without overpowering it. Delicate stud earrings, a dainty necklace, or a classic watch can add a touch of elegance without being distracting.

2. Consider Your Industry

Different industries have different dress codes, and this extends to jewelry. If you work in a creative environment, you may have more freedom to express your personal style through statement pieces. However, in conservative industries such as finance or law, it’s best to stick to more traditional and subdued jewelry choices.

3. Quality Over Quantity

Invest in quality jewelry pieces that will stand the test of time. Quality jewelry doesn’t necessarily mean expensive, but rather well-crafted pieces that won’t tarnish easily. Opt for materials like sterling silver, gold, or stainless steel for longevity and durability.

4. Coordinate with Your Outfit

Your jewelry should complement your outfit, not compete with it. If you’re wearing a patterned blouse or dress, stick to simple, coordinating jewelry to avoid creating a visually overwhelming look. Similarly, if your outfit is monochromatic, you can add a pop of color with a tasteful gemstone piece.

5. Know When to Remove Jewelry

Certain tasks, such as typing on a keyboard or handling paperwork, can be hindered by excessive jewelry. Know when to remove bracelets or rings to ensure you can work comfortably and efficiently.

The Don’ts

1. Avoid Noisy Jewelry

Jingling bracelets or clunky bangles can be distracting in a quiet office environment. Opt for quiet, subtle jewelry that won’t create unnecessary noise during meetings or while working at your desk.

2. Say No to Overly Flashy Pieces

While a little sparkle can add a touch of glamour, avoid overly flashy or gaudy pieces that can be perceived as unprofessional. Save your bold, statement jewelry for social events and opt for more refined pieces in the office.

3. Mind Your Piercings

If you have multiple ear piercings, consider choosing a single pair of earrings for the office to maintain a polished look. While personal style is important, too many visible piercings can be distracting in a professional setting.

4. Don’t Mix Metals Excessively

While mixing metals can be a stylish choice, excessive mixing can appear chaotic in a corporate environment. Stick to one or two coordinated metals to maintain a cohesive and professional look.

5. Avoid Overwhelming Jewelry

Large, chunky jewelry can overpower a professional outfit and detract from your overall appearance. Opt for smaller, more refined pieces that add a touch of sophistication without being visually overwhelming.

By following these dos and don’ts, you can navigate the world of office-appropriate jewelry with confidence, ensuring that your accessories enhance your professional image rather than detract from it. Remember, the key is to strike a balance between personal style and professional polish, allowing your jewelry to complement your overall look in the workplace.
