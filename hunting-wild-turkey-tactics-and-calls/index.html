Hunting Wild Turkey: Tactics and Calls


Are you an avid hunter looking for a challenging and thrilling hunting experience? If so, hunting wild turkey might be the perfect adventure for you. Wild turkey hunting requires a unique set of skills and knowledge, and in this blog post, we will delve into the tactics and calls that can help you become a successful wild turkey hunter. Let’s get started!

Understanding the Wild Turkey

Before we dive into the tactics and calls, it’s important to understand the wild turkey’s behavior and habitat. Wild turkeys inhabit forests and woodlands across North America and are known for their keen senses and sharp vision. They are also highly vocal creatures, and their various calls play a crucial role in their mating and communication rituals.

Essential Tactics for Wild Turkey Hunting

Scouting and Locating

The first step in a successful turkey hunt is scouting and locating potential hunting grounds. Look for areas with abundant food sources, such as open fields, clearings, or agricultural lands. Wild turkeys are known to frequent these areas, especially during their feeding times in the early morning and late afternoon.

Concealment and Stealth

Wild turkeys have exceptional eyesight, so it’s essential to blend in with your surroundings and remain concealed. Wear camouflage clothing that matches the environment you’re hunting in, and consider using a ground blind or natural cover to conceal your movements.

Additionally, practice stealth when approaching or moving around in the hunting area. Slow movements, avoiding unnecessary noise, and being mindful of wind direction will increase your chances of getting close to a turkey undetected.

Decoy Setup

Using decoys can be highly effective when hunting wild turkeys. Decoys can be placed strategically to attract turkeys within range, making them an essential tool for luring in these cautious birds. Consider using different types of decoys, such as a hen decoy or a tom decoy, to mimic realistic turkey behavior.

When setting up your decoys, position them within your shooting range and try to create a natural-looking scene. Placing them near feeding or strutting areas can increase the chances of attracting turkeys.

Calling Techniques

Calling is an integral part of wild turkey hunting, as it helps hunters establish communication and provoke a response from the turkeys. Here are some essential calls that every turkey hunter should be familiar with:

  1. The Yelp: The yelp is the most common call used by wild turkeys. It is a series of high-pitched, rhythmic notes that turkeys make to call out to one another. The yelp is versatile and can be used to locate birds, maintain contact, or draw them closer.

  2. The Cluck: The cluck is a short, staccato call that turkeys make to express contentment or get the attention of other turkeys. It can be used to reassure turkeys or to entice them closer.

  3. The Purr: The purr is a soft, rolling call that turkeys make when they are relaxed or content. It mimics the sound of feeding turkeys and can be effective in luring in curious birds.

  4. The Gobble: The gobble is the classic call of a male turkey. It is a loud, booming sound used mainly for establishing dominance and attracting hens during the mating season. Using a gobble call can be effective when trying to provoke a response from a distant tom.

Patience and Proper Shooting Technique

As with any form of hunting, patience is key when hunting wild turkeys. These birds are known for being cautious and easily spooked, so it’s important to remain still and wait for the right moment to take a shot. Make sure you have a clear line of sight, proper shooting form, and aim for the vital areas to ensure a humane and successful kill.

Conclusion

Hunting wild turkey requires a combination of knowledge, skill, and patience. By understanding turkey behavior, employing essential tactics, and mastering various calling techniques, you can enhance your chances of a successful hunt. Remember to prioritize safety and respect for the environment while enjoying this thrilling hunting experience. Happy hunting!
