10 Inspiring Photography Documentaries Every Photographer Should Watch


Hello there, photography enthusiasts and aspiring shutterbugs! Today, I am thrilled to share with you an exciting list of 10 inspiring photography documentaries that will ignite your passion for capturing moments, telling stories, and exploring the world through your lens. These documentaries have been carefully selected to educate, entertain, and inspire photographers of all levels. So, grab your camera, popcorn, and let’s get started on this captivating visual journey!

1. ‘Finding Vivian Maier’ (2013): This fascinating documentary delves into the life and work of Vivian Maier, an undiscovered street photographer whose remarkable talent only came to light after her death. Prepare to be mesmerized by Maier’s captivating images and the journey to uncover the artist behind the lens.

2. ‘The Salt of the Earth’ (2014): Directed by Wim Wenders and Juliano Ribeiro Salgado, this visually stunning documentary follows the life and work of Sebastião Salgado, one of the most acclaimed photographers of our time. Prepare to be awestruck by Salgado’s breathtaking black and white images, as he explores themes of social injustice and the beauty of the natural world.

3. ‘McCullin’ (2012): Immerse yourself in the powerful and often harrowing images of legendary war photographer Don McCullin. This documentary offers a deep insight into McCullin’s career, his experiences on the front lines, and the profound impact his work has made on the world.

4. ‘Bill Cunningham New York’ (2010): Take a peek into the fascinating life of street fashion photographer Bill Cunningham. This heartwarming documentary showcases Cunningham’s tireless dedication to capturing the essence of New York City’s vibrant street fashion scene and his unwavering passion for his craft.

5. ‘The Woodmans’ (2010): Dive into the captivating yet tragic world of the Woodman family, exploring the life and work of the renowned photographer Francesca Woodman. Through interviews and her hauntingly beautiful photographs, this documentary delves into the complexities of art, family, and mental health.

6. ‘War Photographer’ (2001): Brace yourself for an unflinching and thought-provoking journey into the world of war photojournalism. James Nachtwey, one of the most accomplished war photographers, takes you behind the lens and offers a glimpse into the dangers and realities faced by those who document the horrors of conflict.

7. ‘The Many Lives of William Klein’ (2012): Discover the multifaceted career of William Klein, an influential photographer, filmmaker, and painter. This documentary explores Klein’s avant-garde approach to photography and his impact on the fashion and art world.

8. ‘Annie Leibovitz: Life Through a Lens’ (2008): Go behind the scenes with iconic portrait photographer Annie Leibovitz. This documentary offers a glimpse into Leibovitz’s creative process, her iconic celebrity portraits, and the stories behind her most memorable shots.

9. ‘Everybody Street’ (2013): Step into the vibrant and diverse world of New York City street photography. This documentary features interviews with legendary street photographers, showcasing the power and beauty of capturing the essence of a city and its people.

10. ‘National Geographic: The Photographers’ (2016): Embark on a visual adventure as National Geographic photographers share their incredible stories behind capturing some of the most awe-inspiring images ever seen. From wildlife to landscapes, this documentary offers a glimpse into the diverse world of National Geographic photography.

These ten inspiring photography documentaries will not only fuel your creativity but also offer a deeper understanding of the art and craft of photography. Whether you are an enthusiast or a professional, each of these films will leave you inspired, motivated, and ready to embark on new photographic adventures.

So, grab a cozy spot on your couch, prepare to be captivated by the power of visual storytelling, and let these documentaries take you on a journey that will forever change the way you view the world through your lens. Happy watching and happy shooting!
