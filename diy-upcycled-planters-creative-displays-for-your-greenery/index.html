DIY Upcycled Planters: Creative Displays for Your Greenery


In today’s fast-paced world, where sustainability is becoming increasingly important, upcycling has emerged as a popular trend. Upcycling not only allows us to reduce waste but also gives us an opportunity to flex our creative muscles. One area where upcycling truly shines is in the realm of planters. By repurposing everyday objects, you can create unique and eye-catching displays for your greenery. In this blog post, we will explore some DIY upcycled planters that will add a touch of creativity and charm to your indoor or outdoor space.

1. Tin Can Planters:
One of the simplest and most versatile upcycled planters is the trusty tin can. Before tossing those empty soup or bean cans into the recycling bin, consider giving them a new lease on life. Clean the cans thoroughly, remove any labels, and paint them in vibrant colors to match your decor. Punch a few drainage holes in the bottom and fill them with soil. These tin can planters are perfect for small herbs or succulents and can be hung on walls or arranged on shelves.

2. Teacup Terrariums:
If you have a collection of mismatched teacups lying around, why not turn them into charming terrariums? Fill the teacups with a layer of small rocks for drainage, followed by a layer of activated charcoal to keep the soil fresh. Add a mixture of potting soil and sand, and then carefully arrange small succulents or air plants. These teacup terrariums will add a whimsical touch to any corner of your home.

3. Wooden Pallet Planters:
Wooden pallets are a versatile and abundant source of upcycled materials. With a little bit of creativity, you can transform them into unique planters for your garden or balcony. Simply dismantle the pallet and use the wooden planks to construct a box-like structure. Line the bottom with landscaping fabric to prevent soil from falling out, fill it with potting soil, and then plant your favorite flowers or herbs. Hang the pallet planter on a wall or prop it up against a fence for a rustic and charming display.

4. Mason Jar Herb Garden:
Mason jars have become a staple in DIY projects, and turning them into herb gardens is no exception. Collect a few mason jars, clean them thoroughly, and fill them with potting soil. Plant your favorite herbs, such as basil, parsley, or mint. Attach the jars to a wooden board or hang them individually using twine. Not only will you have a fresh supply of herbs at your fingertips, but you’ll also have a beautiful and functional display for your kitchen.

5. Bicycle Wheel Planter:
For a truly unique and eye-catching upcycled planter, consider repurposing an old bicycle wheel. Spray paint the wheel in a vibrant color, then attach small pots or baskets to the spokes. Fill each pot with soil and plant colorful flowers or trailing vines. Hang the bicycle wheel planter on a wall or fence, and watch as it becomes a conversation starter in your garden.

These are just a few examples of the endless possibilities for DIY upcycled planters. Get creative, think outside the box, and let your imagination run wild. Not only will you be reducing waste and embracing sustainability, but you’ll also have a stunning and personalized display for your greenery. So go ahead, grab some everyday objects, and start upcycling your way to a more beautiful and eco-friendly space.
